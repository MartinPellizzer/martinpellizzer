Eryngium montanum

Eryngium montanum, commonly known as mountain thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, often found in alpine environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
