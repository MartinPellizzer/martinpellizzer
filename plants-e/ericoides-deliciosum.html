Ericoides deliciosum

Ericoides deliciosum, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

Ericoides deliciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
