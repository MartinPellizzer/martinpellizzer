Ehretia laevis var. platyphylla

Ehretia laevis var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
