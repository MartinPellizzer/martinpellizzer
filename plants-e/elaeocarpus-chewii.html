Elaeocarpus chewii

Elaeocarpus chewii, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue.

Elaeocarpus chewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
