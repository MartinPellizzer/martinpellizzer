Eulophia thunbergii

Eulophia thunbergii, commonly known as the Thunberg's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple shoots and leaves. Its flowers are usually arranged in a loose raceme and are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant structure.

Eulophia thunbergii is notable for its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions and its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
