Eriophyllum trichocarpum

Eriophyllum trichocarpum, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has silvery, fuzzy leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Eriophyllum trichocarpum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
