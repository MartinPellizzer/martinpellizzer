Eugenia santosii

Eugenia santosii, commonly known as the Santosii Eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to habitat loss, Eugenia santosii is considered a rare and endangered species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts in its native ecosystem.
