Eria euryloba

Eria euryloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Eria euryloba is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
