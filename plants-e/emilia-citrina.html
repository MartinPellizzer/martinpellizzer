Emilia citrina

Emilia citrina, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive greenish tinge. Emilia citrina is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal medicine.
