Eupatorium maculatum f. tegulosum

Eupatorium maculatum f. tegulosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, almost silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

It is commonly found in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.
