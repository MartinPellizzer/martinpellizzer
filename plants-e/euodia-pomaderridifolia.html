Euodia pomaderridifolia

Euodia pomaderridifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, white flowers. The tree has rough, fissured bark and is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability.
