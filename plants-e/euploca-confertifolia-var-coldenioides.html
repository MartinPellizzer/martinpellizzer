Euploca confertifolia var. coldenioides

Euploca confertifolia var. coldenioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf euploca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in dense tufts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
