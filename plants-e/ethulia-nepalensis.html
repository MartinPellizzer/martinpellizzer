Ethulia nepalensis

Ethulia nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ethulia nepalensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
