Emilia juncea

Emilia juncea, commonly known as flossflower, is an annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, fluffy, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble cotton. The plant grows up to 1 to 2 meters tall and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening for their delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Emilia juncea is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
