Eriocaulon sclerophyllum

Eriocaulon sclerophyllum, commonly known as the hard-leaved spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences and tough, sclerophyllous leaves. This plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are often narrow and arranged in a tight spiral, contributing to its structural rigidity.

Eriocaulon sclerophyllum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
