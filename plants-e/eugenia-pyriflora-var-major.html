Eugenia pyriflora var. major

Eugenia pyriflora var. major, commonly known as the Chilean guava, is a botanical variant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits resembling small pineapples. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible fruit.
