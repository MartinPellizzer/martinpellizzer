Elaphoglossum fimbriatum

Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a specialized structure called a indusium.

Elaphoglossum fimbriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
