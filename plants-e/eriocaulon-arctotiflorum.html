Eriocaulon arctotiflorum

Eriocaulon arctotiflorum, commonly known as the Arctic flowering rush, is a flowering plant native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet, peaty soils and is often found in tundra and moist meadow habitats. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Eriocaulon arctotiflorum is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.
