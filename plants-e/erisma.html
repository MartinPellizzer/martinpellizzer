Erisma

Erisma, commonly known as the snowbell, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Erisma glabrum, which is native to the western United States. The plant is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Erisma thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or mountainous regions.

Its foliage is typically evergreen, with small, leathery leaves that contribute to its ornamental value.
