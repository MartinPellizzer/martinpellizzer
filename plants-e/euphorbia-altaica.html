Euphorbia altaica

Euphorbia altaica, commonly known as the Altai spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and shield-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia altaica is adapted to withstand harsh climates and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
