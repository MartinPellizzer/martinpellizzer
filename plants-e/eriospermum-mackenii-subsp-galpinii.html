Eriospermum mackenii subsp. galpinii

Eriospermum mackenii subsp.

galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, herbaceous growth habit.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
