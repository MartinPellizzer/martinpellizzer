Eugenia pubiflora

Eugenia pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that are covered in fine, hairy glands. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface.

Eugenia pubiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
