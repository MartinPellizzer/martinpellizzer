Eupatorium occidentale var. subroseum

Eupatorium occidentale var. subroseum, commonly known as the pink boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pinkish hue on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
