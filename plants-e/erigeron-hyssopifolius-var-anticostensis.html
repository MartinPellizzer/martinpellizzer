Erigeron hyssopifolius var. anticostensis

Erigeron hyssopifolius var. anticostensis, commonly known as the Anticosti blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the island of Anticosti in the Gulf of Saint Lawrence.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
