Eupatorium gouanii

Eupatorium gouanii, commonly known as the Guanin bush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Eupatorium gouanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
