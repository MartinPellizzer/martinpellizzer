Encholirium pierre-braunii

Encholirium pierre-braunii, commonly known as the Pierre-Braun's torch cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Encholirium pierre-braunii is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
