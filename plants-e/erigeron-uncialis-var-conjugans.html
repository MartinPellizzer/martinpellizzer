Erigeron uncialis var. conjugans

Erigeron uncialis var. conjugans, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has linear, gray-green leaves and produces yellow-centered white flowers that bloom in summer.

Its structure and growth habits make it an attractive plant for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
