Eulophia goetzeana

Eulophia goetzeana, commonly known as the Goetze's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia goetzeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
