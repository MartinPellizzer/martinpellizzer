Ericoides velitare

Ericoides velitare, commonly known as the velvet erica, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

Ericoides velitare is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environmental conditions.
