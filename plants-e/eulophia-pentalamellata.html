Eulophia pentalamellata

Eulophia pentalamellata, commonly known as the five-lobed eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in two rows. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, with five distinct lobes on each petal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Eulophia pentalamellata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
