Erysimum grandiflorum var. minus

Erysimum grandiflorum var. minus, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy, linear leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
