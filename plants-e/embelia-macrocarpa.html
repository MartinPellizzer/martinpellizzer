Embelia macrocarpa

Embelia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like capsules that contain several small seeds.

Embelia macrocarpa is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
