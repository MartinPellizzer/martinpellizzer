Eupatorium ivifolium var. genuinum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a serrated margin, and they are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils in full sun to partial shade, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
