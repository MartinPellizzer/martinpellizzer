Epipactis latifolia f. pseudovarians

Epipactis latifolia f. pseudovarians, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a variant of the broad-leaf helleborine, a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Native to Europe, it thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and often grows in association with other orchids. The plant has a pseudobulb-like structure and produces a single, erect flowering stem.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
