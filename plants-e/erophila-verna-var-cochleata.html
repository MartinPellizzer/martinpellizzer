Erophila verna var. cochleata

Erophila verna var. cochleata, commonly known as the early purple orchid, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is considered a rare and protected plant in many regions due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
