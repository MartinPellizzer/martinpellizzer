Eriocaulon elenorae

Eriocaulon elenorae, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized reproductive structures. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its slender stems are covered in fine, hair-like structures, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
