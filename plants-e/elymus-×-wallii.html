Elymus × wallii

Elymus × wallii, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Elymus × wallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid landscapes.
