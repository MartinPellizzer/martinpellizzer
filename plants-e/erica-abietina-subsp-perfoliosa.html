Erica abietina subsp. perfoliosa

Erica abietina subsp.

perfoliosa, commonly known as the bog heather, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form.

The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers. It thrives in acidic, nutrient-poor soils, often found in wet or boggy habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting diverse insect life.
