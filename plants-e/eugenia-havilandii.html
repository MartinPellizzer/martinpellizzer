Eugenia havilandii

Eugenia havilandii, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its flowers are typically five-petaled and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Eugenia havilandii is adapted to the tropical climate of Hawaii and thrives in well-drained soil.
