Echinodorus cordifolius var. berteroanus

Echinodorus cordifolius var. berteroanus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. This variety thrives in slow-moving or still freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively through underground stems.

It is often cultivated in aquariums and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to improve water quality.
