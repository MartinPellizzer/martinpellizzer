Epacris microphylla var. rivularis

Epacris microphylla var. rivularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, pinkish-white flowers. This variant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rock faces and forest floors. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators like bees and birds.
