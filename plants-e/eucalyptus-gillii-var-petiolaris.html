Eucalyptus gillii var. petiolaris

Eucalyptus gillii var. petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It has a woody habit with a multi-stemmed structure, often forming dense clumps.

This variety is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is known for its aromatic foliage.
