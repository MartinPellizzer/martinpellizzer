Eriosyce heinrichiana subsp. simulans

Eriosyce heinrichiana subsp.

simulans, commonly known as the "Furrowed Pincushion Cactus," is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, cushion-like structure.

The plant produces small, pale green or white flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
