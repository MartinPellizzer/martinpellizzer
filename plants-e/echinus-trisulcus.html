Echinus trisulcus

Echinus trisulcus, commonly known as the three-spined hollyhock, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, adorned with large, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The flowers are typically arranged in racemes and have five petals with a distinctive three-lobed structure. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Echinus trisulcus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
