Eryngium × dalla-torrei

Eryngium × dalla-torrei, commonly known as the hybrid sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles, which are surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 60–120 cm and has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive foliage and long-lasting blooms that attract pollinators.
