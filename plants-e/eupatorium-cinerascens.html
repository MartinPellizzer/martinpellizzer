Eupatorium cinerascens

Eupatorium cinerascens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, silvery hairs.

Eupatorium cinerascens is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
