Excoecaria glaucescens

Excoecaria glaucescens, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas, particularly in mangrove ecosystems. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy coating. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

The plant is adapted to saline environments and plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils.
