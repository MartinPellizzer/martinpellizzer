Epipactis helleborine var. herbacea

Epipactis helleborine var. herbacea, commonly known as the common helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form colonies.

The species is notable for its ability to hybridize with other epipactis species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
