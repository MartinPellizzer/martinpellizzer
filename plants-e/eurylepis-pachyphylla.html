Eurylepis pachyphylla

Eurylepis pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved eurylepis, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to full sun exposure. Its flowering stems produce clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
