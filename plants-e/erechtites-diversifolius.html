Erechtites diversifolius

Erechtites diversifolius, commonly known as the diverse-leaved tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters, making it a notable presence in its native wetland ecosystems.
