Erythraea minima

Erythraea minima, commonly known as the dwarf erythraea, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or white. It is often found in coastal or coastal plain habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
