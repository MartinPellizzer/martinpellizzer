Eleocharis mamillata subsp. ussuriensis

Eleocharis mamillata subsp.

ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats.

The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
