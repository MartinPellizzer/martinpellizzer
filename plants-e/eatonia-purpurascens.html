Eatonia purpurascens

Eatonia purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet and pink petals, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers bloom in clusters and emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
