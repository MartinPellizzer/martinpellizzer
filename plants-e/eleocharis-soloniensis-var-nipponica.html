Eleocharis soloniensis var. nipponica

Eleocharis soloniensis var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese spike-rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and pond edges. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with small, spiky inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense mats in its native habitat.

This variety is native to East Asia and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
