Echinodorus ranunculoides f. terrestris

Echinodorus ranunculoides f. terrestris, commonly known as the terrestrial echinodorus, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green color, often with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that emerge above the water surface, adding ornamental value to ponds and water gardens. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving water with moderate to high light exposure.

Its robust growth makes it a popular choice for aquascaping and natural water feature enhancement.
