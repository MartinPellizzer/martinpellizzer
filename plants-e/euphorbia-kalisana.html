Euphorbia kalisana

Euphorbia kalisana, commonly known as the Kalisana spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
