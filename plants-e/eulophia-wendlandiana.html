Eulophia wendlandiana

Eulophia wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive dappled sunlight. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually arranged in a raceme and are characterized by their intricate, often fragrant structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
