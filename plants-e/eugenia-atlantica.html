Eugenia atlantica

Eugenia atlantica, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-do-brejo*, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Eugenia atlantica thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitats.
