Erica galiiflora

Erica galiiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Erica galiiflora produces small, dark purple to reddish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
