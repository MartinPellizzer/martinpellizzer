Eleocharis schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered and bear small, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces spike-like inflorescences.

Eleocharis schaffneri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
