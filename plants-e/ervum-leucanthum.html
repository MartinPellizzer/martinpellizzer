Ervum leucanthum

Ervum leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several oval seeds. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
