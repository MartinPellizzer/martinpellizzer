Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp. collinus

Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, fleshy seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This species is often found in shaded, moist habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
