Elodea canadensis var. gigantea

Elodea canadensis var. gigantea, commonly known as giant waterweed, is a variant of the common waterweed species native to North America.

It is a submerged aquatic plant characterized by its long, flexible stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. Its rapid growth rate allows it to form dense underwater vegetation, which can impact water quality and aquatic ecosystems.

The plant reproduces through vegetative propagation, with new shoots emerging from the nodes along its stem.
