Elephantopus violaceus

Elephantopus violaceus, commonly known as purple elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, velvety purple leaves that resemble the shape of an elephant's foot, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its striking appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
