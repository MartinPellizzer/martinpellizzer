Eriogonum vimineum subsp. molestum

Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

molestum, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its wiry, creeping stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces showy, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardening.
