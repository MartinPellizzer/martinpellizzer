Eucalyptus botryoides

Eucalyptus botryoides, commonly known as the globular eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and coastal climates. The plant is characterized by its distinctive globular-shaped fruit, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside, and it produces fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and aromatic properties.
