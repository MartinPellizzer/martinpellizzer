Eruca pinnatifida

Eruca pinnatifida, commonly known as wild rocket or Italian arugula, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed, with a peppery, spicy aroma due to its high concentration of glucosinolates. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a common weed in cultivated fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution across temperate climates.
