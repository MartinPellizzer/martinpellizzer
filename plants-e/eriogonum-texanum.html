Eriogonum texanum

Eriogonum texanum, commonly known as the Texas buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a branched stem that can reach up to 1 meter in height.

Eriogonum texanum is adapted to withstand drought and heat, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
