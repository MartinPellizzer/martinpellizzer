Euphorbia hammeri

Euphorbia hammeri, commonly known as the Hammer's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower structures. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia hammeri is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal.
