Eragrostis wilmaniae

Eragrostis wilmaniae, commonly known as Wilman's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis wilmaniae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry regions.
