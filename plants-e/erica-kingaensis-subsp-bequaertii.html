Erica kingaensis subsp. bequaertii

Erica kingaensis subsp.

bequaertii, commonly known as the Kingaensis heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
