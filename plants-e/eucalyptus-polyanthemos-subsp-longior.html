Eucalyptus polyanthemos subsp. longior

Eucalyptus polyanthemos subsp.

longior, commonly known as the long-leaf polyanthemos, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often coated with a whitish bloom.

The flowers are typically arranged in umbellate clusters and are usually pale or creamy white in color. This subspecies is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
