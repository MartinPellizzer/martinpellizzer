Epidendrum chalmersii

Epidendrum chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces racemose inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Epidendrum chalmersii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
