Erica mackayana subsp. andevalensis

Erica mackayana subsp.

andevalensis, commonly known as the Andalucian heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of bright red or pink flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.
