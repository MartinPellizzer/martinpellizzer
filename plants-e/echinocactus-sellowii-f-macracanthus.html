Echinocactus sellowii f. macracanthus

Echinocactus sellowii f. macracanthus, commonly known as the Sowden's Hedgehog Cactus, is a distinctive cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features dense, white to yellowish spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the tips of the stems during the summer months.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
