Elleanthus longibracteatus var. latifolius

Elleanthus longibracteatus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf elleanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
