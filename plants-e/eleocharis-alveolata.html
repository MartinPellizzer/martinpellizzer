Eleocharis alveolata

Eleocharis alveolata, commonly known as the hollow spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, hollow stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Eleocharis alveolata is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability.
