Epidendrum strobiliferum var. swartzii

Epidendrum strobiliferum var. swartzii, commonly known as the Swartz's spike orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces striking, elongated inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
