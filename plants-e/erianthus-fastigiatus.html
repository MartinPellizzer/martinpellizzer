Erianthus fastigiatus

Erianthus fastigiatus, commonly known as the tall reed or narrowleaf reed, is a tall grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
