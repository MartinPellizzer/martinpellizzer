Escallonia pugae

Escallonia pugae, commonly known as the Tasmanian snowball, is a shrub native to Tasmania, Australia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Escallonia pugae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide winter interest in gardens.
