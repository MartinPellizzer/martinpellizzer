Eragrostis tricuspis

Eragrostis tricuspis, commonly known as three-lobed lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, three-lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis tricuspis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
