Eleocharis obtusa var. engelmannii

Eleocharis obtusa var. engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann's spike-rush, is a sedge species native to North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its small, spike-like inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is characterized by its narrow leaves and is often found in moist, acidic soils.
