Eucalyptus retinens

Eucalyptus retinens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin layers, revealing a mottled appearance.

Eucalyptus retinens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
