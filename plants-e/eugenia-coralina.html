Eugenia coralina

Eugenia coralina, commonly known as the coral tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking coral-colored flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant visual appeal to its surroundings. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green appearance.

Eugenia coralina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
