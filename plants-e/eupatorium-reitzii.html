Eupatorium reitzii

Eupatorium reitzii, commonly known as Reitz's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped with prominent veins, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Reitz's boneset is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
