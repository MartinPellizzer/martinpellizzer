Erucastrum pubescens

Erucastrum pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent erucaceous, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

Erucastrum pubescens is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate foliage and modest floral display.
