Esterhuysenia inclaudens

Esterhuysenia inclaudens, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is a small, deciduous shrub characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, typically found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces.

Esterhuysenia inclaudens is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in local biodiversity.
