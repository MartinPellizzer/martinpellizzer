Euphorbia vaalputsiana

Euphorbia vaalputsiana, commonly known as the Vaalputs spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellowish-green flower clusters, known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
