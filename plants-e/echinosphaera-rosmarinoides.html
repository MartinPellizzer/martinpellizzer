Echinosphaera rosmarinoides

Echinosphaera rosmarinoides, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small hedgehogs. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has silvery-gray, aromatic leaves similar to those of rosemary. Its flowers are small and blue or purple, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Echinosphaera rosmarinoides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
