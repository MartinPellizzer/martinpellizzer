Eupatorium grossidentatum

Eupatorium grossidentatum, commonly known as the broad-leaved eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Eupatorium grossidentatum is also known for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in some cultures for its purported health benefits.
