Eucalyptus obliqua var. discocarpa

Eucalyptus obliqua var. discocarpa, commonly known as the red mahogany gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in long strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a white underside. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in umbels, typically appearing in spring.

The tree is valued for its durable, aromatic timber and its ecological importance in native habitats.
