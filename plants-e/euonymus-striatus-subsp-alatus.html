Euonymus striatus subsp. alatus

Euonymus striatus subsp.

alatus, commonly known as the winged euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slight pubescence.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a valuable addition to landscape designs.
