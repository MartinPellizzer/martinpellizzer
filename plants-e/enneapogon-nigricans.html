Enneapogon nigricans

Enneapogon nigricans, commonly known as blackleaf dropseed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often pink or reddish in color, which give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Enneapogon nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in native landscapes.
