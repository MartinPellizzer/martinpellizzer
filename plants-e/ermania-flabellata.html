Ermania flabellata

Ermania flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate ermania, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its showy yellow flowers are arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, resembling a fan shape.

Ermania flabellata is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
