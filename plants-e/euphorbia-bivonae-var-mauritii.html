Euphorbia bivonae var. mauritii

Euphorbia bivonae var. mauritii, commonly known as the Mauritius spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
