Eugenioides lachnobotryum

Eugenioides lachnobotryum, commonly known as the silverleaf eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
