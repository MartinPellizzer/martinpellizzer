Euonymus dolichopus

Euonymus dolichopus, commonly known as the long-leaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 6 feet. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often dark green with a slightly waxy texture. Its showy, red-orange flowers appear in late spring, followed by small, berry-like fruits that are blue-black in color.

It is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
