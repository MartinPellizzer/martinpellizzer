Epipactis persica subsp. gracilis

Epipactis persica subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender Persian helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Central Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

The plant features long, grass-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and specialized pollination mechanisms.

This subspecies is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
