Euphorbia Chamaesyce F. Glabra

Euphorbia chamaesyce f. glabra is a unique variant of the common spurge known for its smooth, hairless foliage. This plant is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its small, green leaves are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant is popular among gardeners for its low maintenance and decorative appeal. Despite its beauty, it can be toxic and should be handled with care.
