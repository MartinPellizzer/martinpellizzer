Erophila affinis

Erophila affinis, commonly known as the small water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with small white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and colonize new areas.

Erophila affinis plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
