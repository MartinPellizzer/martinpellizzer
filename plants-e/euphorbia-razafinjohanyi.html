Euphorbia razafinjohanyi

Euphorbia razafinjohanyi, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
