Espeletia boyacensis

Espeletia boyacensis, commonly known as the Boyacá heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and showy flower heads. The plant typically produces small, tubular flowers that are often arranged in a spherical or conical shape. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and páramo ecosystems.

Espeletia boyacensis is adapted to cold, moist conditions and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
