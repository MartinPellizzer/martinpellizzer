Eragrostis magna

Eragrostis magna, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
