Eugenia schunkei

Eugenia schunkei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Schunke's eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
