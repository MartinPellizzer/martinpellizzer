Erigeron sionis var. trilobatus

Erigeron sionis var. trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

The flowers are usually blue to purple in color, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
