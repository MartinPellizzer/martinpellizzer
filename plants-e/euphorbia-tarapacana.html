Euphorbia tarapacana

Euphorbia tarapacana, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces unique yellowish-green flowers with a central gelatinous structure, which is a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
