Erica purialis

Erica purialis, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica purialis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
