Epidendrum aclandiae

Epidendrum aclandiae, commonly known as the Acland's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, branching inflorescence that bears numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a distinctive yellow lip that attracts pollinators.

Epidendrum aclandiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
