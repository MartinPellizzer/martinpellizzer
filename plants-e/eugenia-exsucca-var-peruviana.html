Eugenia exsucca var. peruviana

Eugenia exsucca var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular plant in horticulture and traditional medicine.
