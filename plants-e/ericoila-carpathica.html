Ericoila carpathica

Ericoila carpathica, commonly known as the Carpathian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, and it produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Ericoila carpathica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environmental conditions.
