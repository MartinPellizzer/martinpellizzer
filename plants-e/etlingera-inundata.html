Etlingera inundata

Etlingera inundata, commonly known as the water ginger, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a rhizomatous herb that thrives in wet, marshy environments, often found near water bodies. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, and it has a distinctive, branched inflorescence.

Etlingera inundata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its traditional use in herbal medicine.
