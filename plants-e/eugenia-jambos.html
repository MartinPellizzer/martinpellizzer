Eugenia jambos

Eugenia jambos, commonly known as the Java apple or tamarillo, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces edible berries that are rich in vitamins and antioxidants. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruit develops from the flower and is typically harvested when it turns a bright red or yellow color.

Eugenia jambos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
