Escobaria sneedii subsp. villardii

Escobaria sneedii subsp.

villardii, commonly known as the Desert Globemallow, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming low, rounded mounds. Its leaves are typically green and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water.
