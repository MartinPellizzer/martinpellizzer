Eclipta erecta var. prostrata

Eclipta erecta var. prostrata, commonly known as prostrate false dandelion, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Americas.

It features a spreading growth habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive white or grayish underside.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
