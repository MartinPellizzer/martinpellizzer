Eriocaulon fenshamii

Eriocaulon fenshamii, commonly known as the Fensham's spike-rush, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. This species is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a spike.

Eriocaulon fenshamii plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environment by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
