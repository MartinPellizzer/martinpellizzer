Erucastrum varium subsp. barbei

Erucastrum varium subsp.

barbei, commonly known as the Barbe's bladderpod, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its small, pungent leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is found in regions of southern Europe and North Africa. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
