Eria coriacea

Eria coriacea, commonly known as the rough eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, South America, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eria coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
