Eugenia ledophylla

Eugenia ledophylla, commonly known as the satin leaf or satinwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that give it a luxurious appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Eugenia ledophylla is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and cabinetry.
