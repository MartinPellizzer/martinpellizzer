Erica hirsuta

Erica hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces dark, leathery berries.

Erica hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, hardy thickets.
