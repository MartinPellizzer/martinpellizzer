Exacum sutaepense f. gracile

Exacum sutaepense f. gracile, commonly known as the slender African violet, is a compact, evergreen perennial plant native to Ethiopia.

It features small, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a slight silver sheen. The plant produces clusters of tiny, tubular flowers in shades of violet, pink, or white, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is often used in container gardening and as a decorative plant.
