Excoecaria biglandulosa var. moritziana

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's spurge, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features smooth, succulent stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is limited due to its toxic properties, but it is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
