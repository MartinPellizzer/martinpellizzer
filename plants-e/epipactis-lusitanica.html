Epipactis lusitanica

Epipactis lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and north Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of broad, dark green leaves and produces a single spike of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Epipactis lusitanica is known for its ability to form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.
