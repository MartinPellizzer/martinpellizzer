Erodium cinereum

Erodium cinereum, commonly known as common erodium, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other plants such as buckwheat and sorrel.
