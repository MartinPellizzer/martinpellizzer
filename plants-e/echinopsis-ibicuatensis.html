Echinopsis ibicuatensis

Echinopsis ibicuatensis, commonly known as the Ibicuan cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick stem that often branches near the base. Its most distinctive feature is its large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically during the summer months. The plant has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.

It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.
