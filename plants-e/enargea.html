Enargea

Enargea, commonly known as the yellow wood or yellowheart, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fabaceae.

These plants are native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. Enargea species are characterized by their showy yellow flowers and distinctive heart-shaped leaves. They often grow as shrubs or small trees, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The wood of Enargea is highly valued for its durability and golden color, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.
