Echites brasiliensis

Echites brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or purple in color.

Its fruits are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it a plant with both ecological and potential medicinal significance.
