Eragrostis remotiflora

Eragrostis remotiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish-brown flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it is often found in association with other native grasses.

Eragrostis remotiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
