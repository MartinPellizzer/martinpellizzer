Epipactis cucullata

Epipactis cucullata, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure formed by the sepals and petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a unique hooded shape that protects the reproductive structures.

Epipactis cucullata is known for its subtle fragrance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
