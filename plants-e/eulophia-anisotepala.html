Eulophia anisotepala

Eulophia anisotepala, commonly known as the variable lip orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its leaves are linear and arranged in two rows, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Eulophia anisotepala is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and adaptability to various growing conditions.
