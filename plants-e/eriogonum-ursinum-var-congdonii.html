Eriogonum ursinum var. congdonii

Eriogonum ursinum var. congdonii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, oval-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system adapted to arid conditions.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
