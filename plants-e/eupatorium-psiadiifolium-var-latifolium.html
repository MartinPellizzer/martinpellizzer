Eupatorium psiadiifolium var. latifolium

Eupatorium psiadiifolium var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and its flowering period occurs in the summer months. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
