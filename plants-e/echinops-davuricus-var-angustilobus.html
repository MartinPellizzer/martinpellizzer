Echinops davuricus var. angustilobus

Echinops davuricus var. angustilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardens.
