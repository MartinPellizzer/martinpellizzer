Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. violaciflorus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. violaciflorus, commonly known as the violet-flowered echinofossulocactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and produces striking violet-pink flowers during the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments. Its foliage is typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable and delicate species in botanical collections.
