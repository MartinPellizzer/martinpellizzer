Exochaenium clavatum subsp. clavatum

Exochaenium clavatum subsp.

clavatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its distinctive seed heads and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
