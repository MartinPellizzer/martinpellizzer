Eragrostis tenella var. insularis

Eragrostis tenella var. insularis, commonly known as the island hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain island regions.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in coastal or rocky habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
