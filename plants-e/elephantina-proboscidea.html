Elephantina proboscidea

Elephantina proboscidea, commonly known as the African elephant's nose plant, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that resembles an elephant's trunk, which stores water to help it survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers during the rainy season, adding a delicate contrast to its robust structure. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a waxy coating, reducing water loss and enhancing its drought tolerance.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
