Eugenia subviridis

Eugenia subviridis, commonly known as the green eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dull green. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are clustered in groups, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Eugenia subviridis is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is adapted to thrive in regions with mild, temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
