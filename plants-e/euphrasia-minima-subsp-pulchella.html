Euphrasia minima subsp. pulchella

Euphrasia minima subsp.

pulchella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features delicate, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent yellow spots. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
