Euonymus sanguineus var. brevipedunculatus

Euonymus sanguineus var. brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the red-fruit euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that persist into winter. Its compact growth habit and ornamental fruits make it a popular choice for garden use.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
