Eremophila spectabilis

Eremophila spectabilis, commonly known as the showy emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and striking, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila spectabilis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
