Eupatorium cujabense

Eupatorium cujabense, commonly known as the Cuja feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a pale blue or white color, and it is known for its aromatic properties.

Eupatorium cujabense is also valued for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
