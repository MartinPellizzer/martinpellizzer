Eulophia austrooccidentalis

Eulophia austrooccidentalis, commonly known as the southern western eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, elongated inflorescence with several flowers, each featuring a distinctive three-lobed lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Eulophia austrooccidentalis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or grassland habitats.
