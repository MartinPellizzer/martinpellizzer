Elephantopsis

Elephantopsis, commonly known as the elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, spiky flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It is often cultivated for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Elephantopsis is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
