Erica macrotrema

Erica macrotrema, commonly known as the giant heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica macrotrema typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
