Eragrostis kinshasaensis

Eragrostis kinshasaensis, commonly known as the Kinshasa grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing a robust growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often thriving in regions with high rainfall.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to withstand moderate grazing pressure.
