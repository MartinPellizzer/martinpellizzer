Echium italicum subsp. scaettae

Echium italicum subsp.

scaettae, commonly known as the Sicilian thorned gentian, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in gardens and botanical collections.
