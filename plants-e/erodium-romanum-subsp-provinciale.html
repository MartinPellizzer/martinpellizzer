Erodium romanum subsp. provinciale

Erodium romanum subsp.

provinciale, commonly known as the provincial bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

The plant features slender stems and small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are opposite and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This subspecies is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, where it thrives in dry, sunny conditions.
