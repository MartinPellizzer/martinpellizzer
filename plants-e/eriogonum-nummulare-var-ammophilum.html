Eriogonum nummulare var. ammophilum

Eriogonum nummulare var. ammophilum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low, spreading growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal dunes and inland deserts.

Its botanical features make it an important component of xerophytic plant communities.
