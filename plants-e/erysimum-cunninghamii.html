Erysimum cunninghamii

Erysimum cunninghamii, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum cunninghamii is often found in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats.
