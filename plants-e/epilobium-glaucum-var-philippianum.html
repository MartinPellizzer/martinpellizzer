Epilobium glaucum var. philippianum

Epilobium glaucum var. philippianum, commonly known as the Philippine willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
