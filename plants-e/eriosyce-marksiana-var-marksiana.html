Eriosyce marksiana var. marksiana

Eriosyce marksiana var. marksiana, commonly known as the Marks' globose cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
