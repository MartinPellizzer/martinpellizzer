Eugenia librevillensis

Eugenia librevillensis, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, white or pale pink flowers and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, round berries that are typically yellow or red when mature.

Eugenia librevillensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
