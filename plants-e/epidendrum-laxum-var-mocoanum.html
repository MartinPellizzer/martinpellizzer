Epidendrum laxum var. mocoanum

Epidendrum laxum var. mocoanum, commonly known as the Mocoan Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
