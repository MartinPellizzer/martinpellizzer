Euonymus subsessilis var. latifolius

Euonymus subsessilis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically broad and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round visual interest in gardens.
