Eucalyptus camaldulensis var. pendula

Eucalyptus camaldulensis var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping eucalypt, is a striking tree characterized by its drooping branches that create a cascading effect.

It is native to Australia and thrives in a variety of soil types, including saline and waterlogged conditions. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a blue-green hue, and they are aromatic when crushed.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal environments.
