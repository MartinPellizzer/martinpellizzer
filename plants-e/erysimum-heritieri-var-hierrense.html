Erysimum heritieri var. hierrense

Erysimum heritieri var. hierrense, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features pinnately divided, hairy leaves that help it withstand harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
