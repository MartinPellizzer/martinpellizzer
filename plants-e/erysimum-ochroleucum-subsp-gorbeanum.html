Erysimum ochroleucum subsp. gorbeanum

Erysimum ochroleucum subsp.

gorbeanum, commonly known as the Gorbean wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in open habitats such as coastal cliffs and sandy shores. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, and the leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
