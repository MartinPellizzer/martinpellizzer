Erythrodes oroensis

Erythrodes oroensis, commonly known as the Oro Valley redleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to conserve water in its native habitat.

Erythrodes oroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
