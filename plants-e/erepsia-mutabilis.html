Erepsia mutabilis

Erepsia mutabilis, commonly known as the variable erica, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erepsia mutabilis is notable for its ability to change flower color, a trait that makes it visually striking and ecologically significant.
