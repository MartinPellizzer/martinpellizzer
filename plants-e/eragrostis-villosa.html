Eragrostis villosa

Eragrostis villosa, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

Eragrostis villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
