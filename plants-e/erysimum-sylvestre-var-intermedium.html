Erysimum sylvestre var. intermedium

Erysimum sylvestre var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate wild brassica, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as meadows and woodland edges. It has hairy, alternate leaves and produces yellow or white flowers in early spring.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential ornamental use.
