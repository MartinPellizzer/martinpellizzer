Eurya acuminatissima

Eurya acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed eurya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, where it grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
