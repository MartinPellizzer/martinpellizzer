Erythroxylum nitidulum var. leptophyllum

Erythroxylum nitidulum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. It is also known for its role in traditional medicine and its potential ecological importance in its native habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable subject for studies in plant ecology and conservation.
