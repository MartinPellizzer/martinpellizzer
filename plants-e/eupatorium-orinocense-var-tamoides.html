Eupatorium orinocense var. tamoides

Eupatorium orinocense var. tamoides, commonly known as the Tamoides boneset, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly found in countries like Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. It has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its foliage and inflorescences are often harvested for medicinal purposes.
