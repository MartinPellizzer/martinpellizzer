Eragrostis eichingeri

Eragrostis eichingeri, commonly known as Eichinger's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis eichingeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
