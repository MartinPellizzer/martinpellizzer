Ectadium

Ectadium, commonly known as the "common nettle," is a flowering plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its opposite, serrated leaves and the presence of stinging hairs on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions across the globe. Ectadium produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its life cycle includes both vegetative and reproductive stages, allowing it to thrive in a variety of environments.
