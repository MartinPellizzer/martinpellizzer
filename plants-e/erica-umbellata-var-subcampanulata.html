Erica umbellata var. subcampanulata

Erica umbellata var. subcampanulata, commonly known as the bell heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
