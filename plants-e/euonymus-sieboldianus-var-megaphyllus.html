Euonymus sieboldianus var. megaphyllus

Euonymus sieboldianus var. megaphyllus, commonly known as the giant-leaf euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, inflated fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its foliage.

The variety is noted for its robust growth and adaptability to various soil types.
