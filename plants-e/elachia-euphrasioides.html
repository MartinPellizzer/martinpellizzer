Elachia euphrasioides

Elachia euphrasioides, commonly known as the common bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Elachia euphrasioides is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
