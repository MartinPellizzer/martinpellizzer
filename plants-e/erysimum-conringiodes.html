Erysimum conringiodes

Erysimum conringiodes, commonly known as the Conring's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are characterized by four petals.

Erysimum conringiodes is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing a range of morphological features that aid in its survival in such conditions.
