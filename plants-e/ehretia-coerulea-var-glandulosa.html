Ehretia coerulea var. glandulosa

Ehretia coerulea var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular ehretia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
