Erodium glandulosum subsp. glandulosum

Erodium glandulosum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native environment.
