Eupatorium berlandieri

Eupatorium berlandieri, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium berlandieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
