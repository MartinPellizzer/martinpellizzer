Erythroxylum petrae-caballi

Erythroxylum petrae-caballi, commonly known as the rock copal or rock holly, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, adapting to high-altitude conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
