Erythrina crista-galli var. longiflora

Erythrina crista-galli var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cranesbill, is a tropical legume characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers.

It features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and branched. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain flat, lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is closely related to other erythrina species and shares similar ecological and medicinal properties.
