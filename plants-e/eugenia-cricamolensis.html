Eugenia cricamolensis

Eugenia cricamolensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia cricamolensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical regions.
