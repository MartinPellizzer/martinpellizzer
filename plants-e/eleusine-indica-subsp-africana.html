Eleusine indica subsp. africana

Eleusine indica subsp.

africana, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

This species is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop in arid regions.
