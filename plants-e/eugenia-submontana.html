Eugenia submontana

Eugenia submontana, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a reddish or purple hue when young.

The flowers are small, fragrant, and clustered in inflorescences, producing white or pale pink blooms that attract pollinators.
