Echium hispidissimopustulatum

Echium hispidissimopustulatum, commonly known as the hispid and pustulate echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
