Eclipta longifolia var. linearis

Eclipta longifolia var. linearis, commonly known as slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, exhibiting a herbaceous growth habit. Its linear leaves are usually arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
