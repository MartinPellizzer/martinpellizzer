Epidendrum pachoi

Epidendrum pachoi, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety pink flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive white throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum pachoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
