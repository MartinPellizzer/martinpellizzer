Eucalyptus conveniens

Eucalyptus conveniens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect shrubs or small trees with smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
