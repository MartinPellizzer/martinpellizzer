Ehrharta longiflora var. longiseta

Ehrharta longiflora var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
