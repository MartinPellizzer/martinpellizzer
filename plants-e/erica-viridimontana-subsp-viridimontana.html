Erica viridimontana subsp. viridimontana

Erica viridimontana subsp.

viridimontana, commonly known as the green mountain heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Erica viridimontana subsp.

viridimontana is a hardy species that is relatively low maintenance once established.
