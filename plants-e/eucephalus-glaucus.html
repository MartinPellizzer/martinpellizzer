Eucephalus glaucus

Eucephalus glaucus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that blooms from late summer to early fall.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant and low-maintenance plants.
