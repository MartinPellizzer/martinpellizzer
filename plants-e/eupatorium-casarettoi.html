Eupatorium casarettoi

Eupatorium casarettoi, commonly known as the Casaretto's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are small and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

Eupatorium casarettoi is often found in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic foliage and potential medicinal properties.
