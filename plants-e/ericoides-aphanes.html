Ericoides aphanes

Ericoides aphanes, commonly known as the small heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the rocky and acidic soils of the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces tiny, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, and it is adapted to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Ericoides aphanes is often found in mountainous regions and is an important component of the local flora in its habitat.
