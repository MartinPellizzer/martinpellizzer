Eriocaulon robustobrownianum

Eriocaulon robustobrownianum, commonly known as the robust brownian rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures are typically small and inconspicuous, contributing to its classification within the rush family.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
