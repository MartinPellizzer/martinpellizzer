Eleocharis hatschbachii

Eleocharis hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in Central and South America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and colonize new areas within its preferred wetland environments.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically important species in its native range.
