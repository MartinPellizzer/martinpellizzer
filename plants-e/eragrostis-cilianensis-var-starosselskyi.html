Eragrostis cilianensis var. starosselskyi

Eragrostis cilianensis var. starosselskyi, commonly known as the Starosselskyi lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear a bluish-gray sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, awned florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control and livestock forage.
