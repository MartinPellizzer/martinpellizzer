Euphorbia denisiana var. denisiana

Euphorbia denisiana var. denisiana, commonly known as the Denisian spurge, is a succulent plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and may be covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
