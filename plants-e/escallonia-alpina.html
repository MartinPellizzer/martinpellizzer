Escallonia alpina

Escallonia alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Escallonia, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Escallonia alpina is also valued for its resilience to harsh climates and its role in supporting local wildlife.
