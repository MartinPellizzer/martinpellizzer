Echeveria globulosa

Echeveria globulosa, commonly known as the Globular Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating called farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces striking pink or purple flowers in late spring or early summer.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
