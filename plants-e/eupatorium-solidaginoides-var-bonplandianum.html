Eupatorium solidaginoides var. bonplandianum

Eupatorium solidaginoides var. bonplandianum, commonly known as the Bonplandian boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its flowering structure is typical of the Asteraceae family, with composite flower heads made up of numerous tiny florets.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wetlands.
