Erica praestans

Erica praestans, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica praestans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
