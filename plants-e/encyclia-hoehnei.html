Encyclia hoehnei

Encyclia hoehnei, commonly known as the Hoehne's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. Encyclia hoehnei is typically found in shaded, moist environments and requires specific soil conditions for optimal growth.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered threatened and is of interest for conservation efforts.
