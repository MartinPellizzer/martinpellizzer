Epilobium tetragonum var. billardierianum

Epilobium tetragonum var. billardierianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
