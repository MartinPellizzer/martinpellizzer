Epigaea repens f. rubicunda

Epigaea repens f. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-flowered trailing arum, is a variant of the trailing arum lily.

It is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking red berries that contrast with its waxy, white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and a preference for moist, acidic soils.

The red variant is distinguished by its vibrant, fleshy berries, which are a notable feature in its appearance.
