Erica triflora var. triflora

Erica triflora var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

Erica triflora var. triflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
