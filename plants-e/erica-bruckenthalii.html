Erica bruckenthalii

Erica bruckenthalii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Erica bruckenthalii typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
