Erythroxylum acutum

Erythroxylum acutum, commonly known as the acuteleaf copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its simple, acute leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody capsules containing seeds. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The plant's ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
