Ehrharta schlechteri

Ehrharta schlechteri, commonly known as the African carpet grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Ehrharta schlechteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
