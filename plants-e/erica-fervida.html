Erica fervida

Erica fervida, commonly known as the fiery heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range from red to orange, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Erica fervida is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
