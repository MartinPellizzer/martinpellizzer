Erica banksiana

Erica banksiana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardiness.

Erica banksiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
