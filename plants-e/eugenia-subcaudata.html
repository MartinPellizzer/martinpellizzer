Eugenia subcaudata

Eugenia subcaudata, commonly known as the narrow-tailed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia subcaudata is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
