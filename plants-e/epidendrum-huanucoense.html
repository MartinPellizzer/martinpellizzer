Epidendrum huanucoense

Epidendrum huanucoense, commonly known as the Huanuco orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

Epidendrum huanucoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
