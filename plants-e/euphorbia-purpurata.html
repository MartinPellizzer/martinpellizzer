Euphorbia purpurata

Euphorbia purpurata, commonly known as the purple spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by colorful bracts that give it its striking appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia purpurata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience.
