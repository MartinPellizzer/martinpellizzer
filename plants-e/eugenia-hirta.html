Eugenia hirta

Eugenia hirta, commonly known as the hairy evergreen or hairy guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia hirta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
