Eriosyce napina subsp. duripulpa

Eriosyce napina subsp.

duripulpa, commonly known as the "Papery Pincushion Cactus," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white, woolly bristles that give it a puffy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted coloration. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with a shallow root system and thickened stems that store water.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry environments.
