Euodia tenuifolia

Euodia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed euodia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Euodia tenuifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.
