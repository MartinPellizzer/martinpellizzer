Eugenia roseopetala

Eugenia roseopetala, commonly known as the rose-petaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, is edible and has a tart, aromatic flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
