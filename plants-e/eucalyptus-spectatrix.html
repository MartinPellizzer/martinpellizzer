Eucalyptus spectatrix

Eucalyptus spectatrix, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub or tree form, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are small and usually white or pale blue, arranged in clusters along the branches. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus spectatrix plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
