Eupatorium ferrugineum f. parvifolium

Eupatorium ferrugineum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the redstem boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a reddish tint on the underside.

The species is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
