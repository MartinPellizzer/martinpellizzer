Epacris impressa var. campanulata

Epacris impressa var. campanulata, commonly known as the Victorian honey mint, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial shrub that produces nectar-rich blooms, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual appeal.
