Eupatorium oxylepis var. typicum

Eupatorium oxylepis var. typicum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium oxylepis var. typicum is a valuable addition to native gardens due to its ecological benefits and ornamental value.
