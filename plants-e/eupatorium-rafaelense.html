Eupatorium rafaelense

Eupatorium rafaelense, commonly known as Rafael's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica and other nearby islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Eupatorium rafaelense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
