Eupatorium amygdalinum var. oxychlaenum

Eupatorium amygdalinum var. oxychlaenum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.
