Euphorbia qarad

Euphorbia qarad, commonly known as the Red-veined Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Middle East.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are typically small and may have a reddish or purple hue, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Euphorbia qarad is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its toxic sap that can cause skin irritation.
