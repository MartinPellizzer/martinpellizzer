Encephalartos msinganus

Encephalartos msinganus, commonly known as the Msingane cycad, is a rare and endangered cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, pinnate leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody, orange-brown cones. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a warm, dry climate.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in its native range.
