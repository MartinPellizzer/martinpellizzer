Erythrophleum ordale

Erythrophleum ordale, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical tree belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree also plays a role in ecological systems by fixing nitrogen in the soil through its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
