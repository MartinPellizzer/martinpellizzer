Eleocharis emarginata

Eleocharis emarginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Eleocharis emarginata plays a role in providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
