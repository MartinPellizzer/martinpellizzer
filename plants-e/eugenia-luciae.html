Eugenia luciae

Eugenia luciae, commonly known as the silky mango, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small mangoes. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Eugenia luciae thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
