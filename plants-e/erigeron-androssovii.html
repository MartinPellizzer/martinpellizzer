Erigeron androssovii

Erigeron androssovii, commonly known as the Andros Island fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the island of Andros in the Bahamas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, sunny environments. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
