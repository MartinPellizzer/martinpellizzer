Emodiopteris elwesii

Emodiopteris elwesii, commonly known as the Elwes' water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomes are submerged, anchoring the plant while absorbing nutrients from the water.

Emodiopteris elwesii plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
