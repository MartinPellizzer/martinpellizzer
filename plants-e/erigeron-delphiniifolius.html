Erigeron delphiniifolius

Erigeron delphiniifolius, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are generally narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
