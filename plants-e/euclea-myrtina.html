Euclea myrtina

Euclea myrtina, commonly known as the red stinkwood, is a South African shrub or small tree belonging to the Rhamnaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round, black berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. Euclea myrtina is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.

Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
