Eucalyptus foecunda

Eucalyptus foecunda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or tree form, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white tomentum. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are typically clustered in groups of three. Its bark is rough and flaky, often peeling in strips, and it is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus foecunda is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their potential antimicrobial properties.
