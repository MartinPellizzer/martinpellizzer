Eschscholzia incisa

Eschscholzia incisa, commonly known as the narrowleaf California poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to California.

It features delicate, bright orange to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
