Echinocereus pectinatus var. candicans

Echinocereus pectinatus var. candicans, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems and colorful, tubular flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has spiny stems and produces vibrant red, orange, or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Its unique blooming cycle makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and homes.
