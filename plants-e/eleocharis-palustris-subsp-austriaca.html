Eleocharis palustris subsp. austriaca

Eleocharis palustris subsp.

austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian marsh sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dry, three-angled fruits.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and nutrient-poor soils.
