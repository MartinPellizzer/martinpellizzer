Erica × veitchii

Erica × veitchii, commonly known as the Veitch's heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant produces small, dark brown, woody capsules as fruits, which contain several seeds. Erica × veitchii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its hardiness and attractive floral display.
