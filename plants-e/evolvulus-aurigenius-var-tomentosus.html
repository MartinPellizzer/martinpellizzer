Evolvulus aurigenius var. tomentosus

Evolvulus aurigenius var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, velvety stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing vines.
