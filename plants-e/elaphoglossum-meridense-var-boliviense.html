Elaphoglossum meridense var. boliviense

Elaphoglossum meridense var. boliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian holly fern, is a fern species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, leathery fronds with a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky outcrops. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
