Eucalyptus × jugalis

Eucalyptus × jugalis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Eucalyptus × jugalis is valued for its hardiness and is often used in horticulture and reforestation projects.
