Eugenia haeckeliana

Eugenia haeckeliana, commonly known as the Haeckel's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is typically red when ripe and contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic qualities.
