Elongatia sijmii

Elongatia sijmii, commonly known as the Sijmii's elongated fern, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender fronds that can reach several meters in length, making it a striking feature in its natural habitat. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in dense tropical forests. Its leaves are bioluminescent, a rare trait among ferns, which helps it attract pollinators at night.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a vulnerable species.
