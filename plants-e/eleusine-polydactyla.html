Eleusine polydactyla

Eleusine polydactyla, commonly known as the many-fingered grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Eleusine polydactyla is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in some regions.
