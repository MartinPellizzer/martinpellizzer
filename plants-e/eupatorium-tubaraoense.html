Eupatorium tubaraoense

Eupatorium tubaraoense, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and coastal dunes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium tubaraoense is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
