Enkianthus quinqueflorus var. ciliatoserrulatus

Enkianthus quinqueflorus var. ciliatoserrulatus, commonly known as the serrulate enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in woodland environments and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
