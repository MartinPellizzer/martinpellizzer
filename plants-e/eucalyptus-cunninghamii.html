Eucalyptus cunninghamii

Eucalyptus cunninghamii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, inland environments.

Eucalyptus cunninghamii is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
