Episcia lineata

Episcia lineata, commonly known as the silver lace plant, is a tropical herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns of silver and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Episcia lineata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
