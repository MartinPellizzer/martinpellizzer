Euphorbia sachetiana

Euphorbia sachetiana, commonly known as the Sachet Spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a latex-like sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
