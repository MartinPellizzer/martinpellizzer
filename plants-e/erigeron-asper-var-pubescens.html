Erigeron asper var. pubescens

Erigeron asper var. pubescens, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and leaves. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white to pale pink petals. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and roadsides.

Its pubescent (hairy) texture is a distinguishing feature of this variant.
