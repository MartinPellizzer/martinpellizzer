Erigeron semirasus

Erigeron semirasus, commonly known as the Japanese blueweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
