Eugenia bartonii

Eugenia bartonii, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is typically round and about 3-5 cm in diameter, has a thick, purple rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
