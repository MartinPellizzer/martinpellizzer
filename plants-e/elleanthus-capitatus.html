Elleanthus capitatus

Elleanthus capitatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded inflorescence composed of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Elleanthus capitatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
