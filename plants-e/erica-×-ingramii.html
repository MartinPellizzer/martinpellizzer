Erica × ingramii

Erica × ingramii, commonly known as the hybrid heather, is a shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica × ingramii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.

Its hybrid origin results from crosses between Erica coccinea and Erica baltica, contributing to its unique growth habits and floral characteristics.
