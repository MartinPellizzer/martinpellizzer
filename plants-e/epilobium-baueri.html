Epilobium baueri

Epilobium baueri, commonly known as Bauer's willowherb, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
