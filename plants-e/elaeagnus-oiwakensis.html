Elaeagnus oiwakensis

Elaeagnus oiwakensis, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has a spreading habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden environments.

Additionally, it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which can improve soil fertility.
