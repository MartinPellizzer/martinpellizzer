Eugenioides theifolium

Eugenioides theifolium, commonly known as the false pepper tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, elliptical fruits that turn red when mature. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
