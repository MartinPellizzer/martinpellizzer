Elaeodendron albivenosum

Elaeodendron albivenosum, commonly known as the white-veined elaeodendron, is a rare tropical plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green foliage with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
