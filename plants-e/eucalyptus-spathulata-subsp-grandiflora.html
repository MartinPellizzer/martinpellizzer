Eucalyptus spathulata subsp. grandiflora

Eucalyptus spathulata subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces large, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in dry climates.
