Evolvulus glomeratus var. albicans

Evolvulus glomeratus var. albicans, commonly known as the white bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces inflated, bladder-like fruits that are a distinctive feature of the species. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical name reflects its clustered flowering and the white coloration of its flowers and fruits.
