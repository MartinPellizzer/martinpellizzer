Epidendrum frons-bovis

Epidendrum frons-bovis, commonly known as the Boar's Head Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often resemble a boar's head, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing support for its heavy floral display.

The orchid produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, making it a popular choice for horticultural collections.
