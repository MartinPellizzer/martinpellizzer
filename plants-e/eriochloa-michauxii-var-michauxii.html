Eriochloa michauxii var. michauxii

Eriochloa michauxii var. michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering heads are small, spike-like clusters that produce light green to brown seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
