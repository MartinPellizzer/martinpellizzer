Eria ramosa

Eria ramosa, commonly known as the branched orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, typically growing in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with numerous branches, from which erect flowering stems arise. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often grouped in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Eria ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
