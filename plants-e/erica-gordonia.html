Erica gordonia

Erica gordonia, commonly known as the western heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica gordonia is evergreen, with dense, leathery leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
