Erythrina poeppigiana

Erythrina poeppigiana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The plant is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
