Eugenia siamensis

Eugenia siamensis, commonly known as the Siamese guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
