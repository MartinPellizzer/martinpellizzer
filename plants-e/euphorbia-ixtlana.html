Euphorbia ixtlana

Euphorbia ixtlana, commonly known as the Mexican spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia ixtlana is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
