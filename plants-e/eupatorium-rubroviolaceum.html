Eupatorium rubroviolaceum

Eupatorium rubroviolaceum, commonly known as the red-violet eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to red flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with the upper leaves often displaying a reddish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
