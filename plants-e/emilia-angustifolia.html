Emilia angustifolia

Emilia angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a slightly hairy surface.

Emilia angustifolia is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
