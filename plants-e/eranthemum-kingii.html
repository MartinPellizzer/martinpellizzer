Eranthemum kingii

Eranthemum kingii, commonly known as the King's eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
