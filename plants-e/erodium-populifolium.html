Erodium populifolium

Erodium populifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers and heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of poplar trees. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Erodium populifolium is often found in temperate climates and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
