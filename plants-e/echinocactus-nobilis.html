Echinocactus nobilis

Echinocactus nobilis, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom from the areoles. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
