Eugenia maclurei

Eugenia maclurei, commonly known as the Maclure's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers and its small, edible, citrus-scented fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist forest understories.

The leaves are usually elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma when crushed.
