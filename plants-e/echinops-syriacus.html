Echinops syriacus

Echinops syriacus, commonly known as the Syrian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its rugged habitat.

Echinops syriacus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
