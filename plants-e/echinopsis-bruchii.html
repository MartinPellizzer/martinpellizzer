Echinopsis bruchii

Echinopsis bruchii, commonly known as the Bruch's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its globular, rounded shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens and collections.
