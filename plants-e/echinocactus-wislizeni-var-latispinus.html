Echinocactus wislizeni var. latispinus

Echinocactus wislizeni var. latispinus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in long, white spines that give it a distinctive, almost feathery appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
