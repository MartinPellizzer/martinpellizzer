Elymus karataviensis

Elymus karataviensis, commonly known as Karatavian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous florets.

Elymus karataviensis is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock in its native range.
