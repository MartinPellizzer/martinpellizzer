Eruca sylvestris

Eruca sylvestris, commonly known as wild mustard, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and broccoli. The plant features upright stems with lobed, hairy leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its seeds are enclosed in siliques, a type of dry fruit characteristic of the mustard family.

Eruca sylvestris is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in some regions.
