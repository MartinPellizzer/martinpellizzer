Eria ornata

Eria ornata, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and it produces showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Eria ornata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
