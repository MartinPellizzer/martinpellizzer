Eriosyce

Eriosyce, commonly known as the woolly cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the cactus family, Cactaceae.

These plants are native to South America, particularly in regions of Argentina and Chile. They are characterized by their dense, woolly covering of white or pale hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in arid environments. Eriosyce species typically have short, cylindrical stems and produce small, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red.

They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
