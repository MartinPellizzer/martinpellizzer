Eleocharis palustris subvar. calva

Eleocharis palustris subvar. calva, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spiky inflorescences that produce small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Due to its ability to thrive in wet conditions, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
