Eragrostis bequaertii

Eragrostis bequaertii, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend at the base. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis bequaertii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
