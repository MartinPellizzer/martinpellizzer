Erica petiveri var. willdenowii

Erica petiveri var. willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
