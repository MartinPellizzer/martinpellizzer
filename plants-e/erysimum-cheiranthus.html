Erysimum cheiranthus

Erysimum cheiranthus, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed areas.

Erysimum cheiranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
