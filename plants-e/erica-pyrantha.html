Erica pyrantha

Erica pyrantha, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Erica pyrantha thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
