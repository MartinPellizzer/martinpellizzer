Erysimum lanceolatum

Erysimum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum lanceolatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
