Eragrostis longipaniculata

Eragrostis longipaniculata, commonly known as the long-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, elongated panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Eragrostis longipaniculata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
