Erigeron thunbergii subsp. komarovii

Erigeron thunbergii subsp.

komarovii, commonly known as the Komarovii fleabane, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features finely divided, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
