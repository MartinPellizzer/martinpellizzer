Epilobium paniculatum var. jucundum

Epilobium paniculatum var. jucundum, commonly known as the showy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed sites.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
