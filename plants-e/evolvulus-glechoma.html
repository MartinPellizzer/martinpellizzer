Evolvulus glechoma

Evolvulus glechoma, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Evolvulus glechoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread easily in suitable conditions.
