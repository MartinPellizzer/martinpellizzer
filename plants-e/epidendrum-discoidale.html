Epidendrum discoidale

Epidendrum discoidale, commonly known as the discoid orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features strap-like leaves and bears showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum discoidale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
