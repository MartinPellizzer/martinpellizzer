Ericoides colorans

Ericoides colorans, commonly known as the colorful eric, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericoides colorans thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a border plant in horticultural settings.
