Euonymus ilicifolius

Euonymus ilicifolius, commonly known as the evergreen euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage with elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and attractive appearance make it a valued plant in both landscape design and horticultural collections.
