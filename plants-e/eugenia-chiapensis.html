Eugenia chiapensis

Eugenia chiapensis, commonly known as Chiapas myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
