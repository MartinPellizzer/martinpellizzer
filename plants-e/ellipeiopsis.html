Ellipeiopsis

Ellipeiopsis, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their vibrant, showy flowers and compact, rosette-like foliage. Ellipeiopsis species typically have hairy stems and leaves that are often velvety to the touch. They thrive in moist, well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light to flourish.

The genus is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation indoors.
