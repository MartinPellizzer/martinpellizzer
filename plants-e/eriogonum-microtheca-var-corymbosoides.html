Eriogonum microtheca var. corymbosoides

Eriogonum microtheca var. corymbosoides, commonly known as the Sierra goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
