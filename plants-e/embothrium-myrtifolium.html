Embothrium myrtifolium

Embothrium myrtifolium, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Embothrium myrtifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
