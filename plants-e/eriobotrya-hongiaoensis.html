Eriobotrya hongiaoensis

Eriobotrya hongiaoensis, commonly known as the Hongiao loquat, is a species of evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by yellow, juicy berries in late summer. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
