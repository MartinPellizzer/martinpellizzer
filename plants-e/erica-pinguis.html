Erica pinguis

Erica pinguis, commonly known as the fat heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. Erica pinguis is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions, where it withstands mild frost and salt spray.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
