Elaeagnus simonii

Elaeagnus simonii, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, edible berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus simonii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, enhancing soil fertility.
