Euphorbia ramosa var. villosior

Euphorbia ramosa var. villosior, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its branching stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are its modified inflorescences containing both male and female flowers. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
