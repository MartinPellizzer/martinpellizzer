Eupatorium nitidum

Eupatorium nitidum, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is commonly found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its medicinal properties have been explored in traditional medicine, though it is toxic to livestock.
