Epidendrum rhomboglossum

Epidendrum rhomboglossum, commonly known as the rhomboid-tongue orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a short, erect stem and fleshy, elliptic leaves. The plant produces racemose inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum rhomboglossum is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
