Erica obcordata

Erica obcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica obcordata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
