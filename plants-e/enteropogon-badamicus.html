Enteropogon badamicus

Enteropogon badamicus, commonly known as the red grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including India and China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish-brown inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
