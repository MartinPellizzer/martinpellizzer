Erica plukenetii subsp. breviflora

Erica plukenetii subsp.

breviflora, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a low-growing shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa. It features dense, evergreen foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
