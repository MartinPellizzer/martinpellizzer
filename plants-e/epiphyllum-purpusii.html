Epiphyllum purpusii

Epiphyllum purpusii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is native to Mexico and parts of Central America. Its stems are fleshy and often segmented, and it lacks true leaves, relying on its stems for photosynthesis.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet fragrance.
