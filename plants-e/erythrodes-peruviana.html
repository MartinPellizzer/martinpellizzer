Erythrodes peruviana

Erythrodes peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian redwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a tall, evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing small, round seeds. It thrives in humid, temperate climates and is often found in cloud forests, where it plays a significant role in the local ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
