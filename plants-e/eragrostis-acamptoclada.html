Eragrostis acamptoclada

Eragrostis acamptoclada, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth habit.

It is often found in association with other native grasses and forbs in its ecological community.
