Eriope obovata var. gracilis

Eriope obovata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender eriope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, obovate leaves that are densely covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique foliage and compact growth habit make it an attractive addition to native plant collections.
