Epidendrum brachybotrys

Epidendrum brachybotrys, commonly known as the short-stalked epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

The flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
