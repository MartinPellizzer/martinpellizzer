Enekbatus stowardii

Enekbatus stowardii, commonly known as the Stoward's ghost fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling ghostly silhouettes. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination for propagation.

Due to habitat destruction, Enekbatus stowardii is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts to preserve its dwindling population.
