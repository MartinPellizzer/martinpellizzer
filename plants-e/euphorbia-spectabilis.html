Euphorbia spectabilis

Euphorbia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, spiny stems and lobed, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking yellow-orange cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid landscapes.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
