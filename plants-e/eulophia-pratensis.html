Eulophia pratensis

Eulophia pratensis, commonly known as the marsh eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to moist meadows and wetlands in Europe.

It typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and features slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces striking, pale yellow to greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the damp soil.

Eulophia pratensis is a relatively rare species, often found in specific habitats with high humidity and nutrient-rich soil.
