Epilobium palustre var. adenophorum

Epilobium palustre var. adenophorum, commonly known as the bog willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in northern regions.

It is characterized by its slender, often reddish stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. Its flowers are tubular and are adapted for pollination by insects, particularly bees.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and other wetland environments.
