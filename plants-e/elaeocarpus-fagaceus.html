Elaeocarpus fagaceus

Elaeocarpus fagaceus, commonly known as the Queensland nut, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, spherical fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell. The seeds inside are highly nutritious and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal and lowland rainforests.
