Eriogonum umbellatum var. polyanthum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. polyanthum, commonly known as the showy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, edible seeds that are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

The species is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
