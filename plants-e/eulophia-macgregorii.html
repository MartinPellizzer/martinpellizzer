Eulophia macgregorii

Eulophia macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and long, strap-like leaves. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
