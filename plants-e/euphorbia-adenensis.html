Euphorbia adenensis

Euphorbia adenensis, commonly known as the Aden's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which store water to help it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Euphorbia adenensis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
