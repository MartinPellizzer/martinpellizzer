Erica plukenetii subsp. penicillata

Erica plukenetii subsp.

penicillata, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, forming a star-like pattern. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation. Erica plukenetii subsp.

penicillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
