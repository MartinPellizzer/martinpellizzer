Eucalyptus goniocalyx

Eucalyptus goniocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are usually white or pale blue, appearing in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
