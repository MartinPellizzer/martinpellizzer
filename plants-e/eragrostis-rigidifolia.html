Eragrostis rigidifolia

Eragrostis rigidifolia, commonly known as rigid beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, rigid leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Eragrostis rigidifolia is often found in grasslands, rocky outcrops, and along roadsides, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
