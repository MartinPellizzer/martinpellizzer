Epilobium hornemannii var. lactiflorum

Epilobium hornemannii var. lactiflorum, commonly known as the milkflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and forest edges.

The milkflower is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
