Erigeron simplex

Erigeron simplex, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, daisy-like, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Erigeron simplex is often found in dry to moist environments and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
