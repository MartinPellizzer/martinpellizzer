Echinospermum squarrosum

Echinospermum squarrosum, commonly known as the rough-hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, rough-textured stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
