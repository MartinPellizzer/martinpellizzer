Epilobium crassifolium var. steinbergiae

Epilobium crassifolium var. steinbergiae, commonly known as Steinberg's fireweed, is a perennial herb native to the arctic and alpine regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and subarctic environments. Its leaves are broad and thick, adapted to withstand cold temperatures and strong winds.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh ecological conditions.
