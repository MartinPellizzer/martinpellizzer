Erigeron divergens f. incomptus

Erigeron divergens f. incomptus, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a rough or hairy texture.

The inflorescence consists of both ray and disk flowers, contributing to its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
