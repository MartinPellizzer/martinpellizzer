Eragrostis rigida

Eragrostis rigida, commonly known as rigid lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Eragrostis rigida is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant with drought tolerance.

It is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
