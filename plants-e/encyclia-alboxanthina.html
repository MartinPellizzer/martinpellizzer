Encyclia alboxanthina

Encyclia alboxanthina, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Encyclia alboxanthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
