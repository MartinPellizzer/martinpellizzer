Eleusine tocussa var. flavocarpa

Eleusine tocussa var. flavocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to orange-colored seeds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
