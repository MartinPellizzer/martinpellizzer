Eucalyptus × silvestris

Eucalyptus × silvestris, commonly known as the silver gum, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus gunnii and Eucalyptus viminalis.

It is native to southeastern Australia and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, silver-gray bark. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Eucalyptus × silvestris is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in reforestation efforts due to its adaptability and rapid growth.
