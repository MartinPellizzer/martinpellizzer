Eleocharis nudipes

Eleocharis nudipes, commonly known as the naked sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually smooth and lack the prominent veins seen in many other sedges.

Eleocharis nudipes plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
