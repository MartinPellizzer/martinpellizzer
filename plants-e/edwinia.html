Edwinia

Edwinia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage. The leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like segments. Edwinia species are often found in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to arid conditions.

They produce small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, contributing to their ecological role in their native environments.
