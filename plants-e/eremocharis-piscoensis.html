Eremocharis piscoensis

Eremocharis piscoensis, commonly known as the Pisco orchid, is a species of orchid native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically blooms during the warmer months, showcasing inflorescences that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. Its foliage consists of broad, leathery leaves that help it thrive in its arid environment.

Eremocharis piscoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
