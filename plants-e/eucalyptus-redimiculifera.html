Eucalyptus redimiculifera

Eucalyptus redimiculifera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale pink, which attract various pollinators. Its bark is typically smooth and may shed in strips, revealing a fibrous inner layer.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
