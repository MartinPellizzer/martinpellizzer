Eupatorium sachalinense

Eupatorium sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the Far East, including parts of Russia, Korea, and Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium sachalinense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
