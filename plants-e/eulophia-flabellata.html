Eulophia flabellata

Eulophia flabellata, commonly known as the fan-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, each having a distinctive, fan-shaped lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia flabellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
