Elaphoglossum sporadolepis

Elaphoglossum sporadolepis, commonly known as the sporadolepis holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a small, delicate fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance. Its spores are produced in sori that are clustered along the margins of the fertile leaves.

Elaphoglossum sporadolepis is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
