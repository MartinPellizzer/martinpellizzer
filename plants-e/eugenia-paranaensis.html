Eugenia paranaensis

Eugenia paranaensis, commonly known as the Parana myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they are an important food source for various wildlife.

Eugenia paranaensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and aesthetic qualities.
