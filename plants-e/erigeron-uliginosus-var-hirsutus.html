Erigeron uliginosus var. hirsutus

Erigeron uliginosus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, often disturbed soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
