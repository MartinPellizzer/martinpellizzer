Eugenia malaccensis

Eugenia malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a fragrant, sweet flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
