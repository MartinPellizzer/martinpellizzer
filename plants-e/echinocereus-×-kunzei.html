Echinocereus × kunzei

Echinocereus × kunzei, commonly known as the klondike diamond, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a dense covering of spines and produces yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
