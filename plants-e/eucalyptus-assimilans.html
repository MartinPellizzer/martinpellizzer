Eucalyptus assimilans

Eucalyptus assimilans, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub or small tree form, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The flowers are small and typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds. This plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

It is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
