Epiblastus tuberculatus

Epiblastus tuberculatus, commonly known as the hairy epiblastus, is a tropical climbing plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves that often have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically enclosed within a spadix, a common feature in the Araceae family. Epiblastus tuberculatus is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique growth habit.
