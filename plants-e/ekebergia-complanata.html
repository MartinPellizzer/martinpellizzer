Ekebergia complanata

Ekebergia complanata, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by winged fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Ekebergia complanata is often used in landscaping due to its fast growth and ability to provide shade.
