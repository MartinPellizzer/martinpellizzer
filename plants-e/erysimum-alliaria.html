Erysimum alliaria

Erysimum alliaria, commonly known as winter purslane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and coastal areas. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by triangular, seed-like fruits. Its leaves are succulent and have a mild, peppery flavor, making it edible in some regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
