Euphorbia dioica

Euphorbia dioica, commonly known as spurge or common spurge, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring upright stems covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while its roots can be milky and toxic.

Euphorbia dioica is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats.
