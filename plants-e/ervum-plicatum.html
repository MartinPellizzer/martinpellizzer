Ervum plicatum

Ervum plicatum, commonly known as the broad bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, inflated pods that contain the edible seeds. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high protein content in the seeds. Its root system has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

Ervum plicatum is an important crop in both traditional and modern agriculture.
