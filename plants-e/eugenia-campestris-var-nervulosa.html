Eugenia campestris var. nervulosa

Eugenia campestris var. nervulosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
