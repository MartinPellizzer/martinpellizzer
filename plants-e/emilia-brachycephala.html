Emilia brachycephala

Emilia brachycephala, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically arranged in a compact, rounded shape. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Emilia brachycephala thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
