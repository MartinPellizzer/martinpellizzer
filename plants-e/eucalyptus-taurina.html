Eucalyptus taurina

Eucalyptus taurina, commonly known as the Tasmanian gum or mountain gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to Tasmania and is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow. Eucalyptus taurina is adapted to cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its essential oils, particularly eucalyptol, have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
