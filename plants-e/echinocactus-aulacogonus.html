Echinocactus aulacogonus

Echinocactus aulacogonus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, with a dense covering of yellowish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
