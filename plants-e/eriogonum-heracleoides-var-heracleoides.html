Eriogonum heracleoides var. heracleoides

Eriogonum heracleoides var. heracleoides, commonly known as desert parsley, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a taproot and hairy, basal leaves that are often divided into lobes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky soils.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and has a long blooming period, typically from spring to early summer.
