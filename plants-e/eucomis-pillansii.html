Eucomis pillansii

Eucomis pillansii, commonly known as the Zulu torch or Zulu grass, is a striking ornamental plant native to South Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that resemble torches, rising from a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Eucomis pillansii is valued for its unique appearance and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice in xeriscaping.
