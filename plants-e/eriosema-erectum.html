Eriosema erectum

Eriosema erectum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or yellow bell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal umbels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.

Eriosema erectum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
