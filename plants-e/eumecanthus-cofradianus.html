Eumecanthus cofradianus

Eumecanthus cofradianus, commonly known as the golden spire orchid, is a rare and exotic flowering plant native to the high-altitude cloud forests of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescence, which consists of tightly packed, spire-like flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its intricate, fragrant blooms that bloom during the dry season. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited habitat and habitat destruction, it is considered a critically endangered species.
