Eupatorium oblongifolium var. tucumanense

Eupatorium oblongifolium var. tucumanense, commonly known as Tucuman feverfew, is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and medicinal studies.
