Elodes chinensis

Elodes chinensis, commonly known as Chinese waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it relies on water for propagation. Its leaves are narrow and arranged alternately along the stems, aiding in its ability to photosynthesize underwater.

Elodes chinensis is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.
