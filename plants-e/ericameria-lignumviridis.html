Ericameria lignumviridis

Ericameria lignumviridis, commonly known as greenwood grevillea, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

Ericameria lignumviridis is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant in xeriscaped gardens.
