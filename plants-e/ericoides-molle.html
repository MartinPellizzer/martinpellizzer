Ericoides molle

Ericoides molle, commonly known as the soft ericoid, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in pine forests and rocky habitats.

Ericoides molle is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
