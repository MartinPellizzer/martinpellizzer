Eriogonum reniforme var. playanum

Eriogonum reniforme var. playanum, commonly known as Playa Basin buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, often lobed leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as playas, salt flats, and disturbed soils. It has a short growing season and is adapted to arid environments with limited water availability.

Its seed capsules dehisce to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in suitable habitats.
