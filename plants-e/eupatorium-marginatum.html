Eupatorium marginatum

Eupatorium marginatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its name "boneset" is derived from its historical use in treating ailments, including those related to the skeletal system.
