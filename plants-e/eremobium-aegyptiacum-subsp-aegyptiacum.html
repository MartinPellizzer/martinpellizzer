Eremobium aegyptiacum subsp. aegyptiacum

Eremobium aegyptiacum subsp.

aegyptiacum, commonly known as the Egyptian eremobium, is a flowering plant species native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small scales, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique adaptations to harsh environments.
