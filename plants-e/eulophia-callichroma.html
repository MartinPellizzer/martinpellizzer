Eulophia callichroma

Eulophia callichroma, commonly known as the golden eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces a single, erect stem. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green to orange flowers arranged in a raceme, which are characteristic of the Orchidaceae family. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Eulophia callichroma thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
