Elsholtzia ciliata var. brevipes

Elsholtzia ciliata var. brevipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
