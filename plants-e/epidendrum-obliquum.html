Epidendrum obliquum

Epidendrum obliquum, commonly known as the crooked lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or other surfaces in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum obliquum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
