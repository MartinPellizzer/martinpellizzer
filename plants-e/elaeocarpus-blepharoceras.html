Elaeocarpus blepharoceras

Elaeocarpus blepharoceras, commonly known as the horned elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, woody fruits that resemble small horns. The plant belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
