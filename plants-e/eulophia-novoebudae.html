Eulophia novoebudae

Eulophia novoebudae, commonly known as the New Guinea eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid lowland forests of New Guinea.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense undergrowth. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually white to pale yellow, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals.

Eulophia novoebudae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
