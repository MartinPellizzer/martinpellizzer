Elaeocarpus viguieri

Elaeocarpus viguieri, commonly known as the Madagascar olive, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The tree produces woody fruits that contain a single, edible seed surrounded by a fleshy, yellowish pulp.

It is often found in moist, shaded forest habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
