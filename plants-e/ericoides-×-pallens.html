Ericoides × pallens

Ericoides × pallens, commonly known as the hybrid ericaceous plant, is a species that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species, typically from the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in forested or heathland environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, providing year-round interest.

Ericoides × pallens is valued in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
