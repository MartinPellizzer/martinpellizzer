Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp. aurigeranum

Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp.

aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a fern-like appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more elongated and golden-tinged flowers compared to other Erysimum species.
