Euterpe megalochlamys

Euterpe megalochlamys, commonly known as the giant palm or large-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruits are edible, containing a sweet, creamy pulp that is often consumed by local communities.

This palm plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
