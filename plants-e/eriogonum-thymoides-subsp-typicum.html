Eriogonum thymoides subsp. typicum

Eriogonum thymoides subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. It has hairy, glandular leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

This subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and is often used in restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological importance.
