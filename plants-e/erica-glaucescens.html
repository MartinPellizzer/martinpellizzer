Erica glaucescens

Erica glaucescens, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

Erica glaucescens is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
