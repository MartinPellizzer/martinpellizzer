Epidendrum glumibracteum

Epidendrum glumibracteum, commonly known as the woolly epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with prominent, hairy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum glumibracteum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
