Eriochloa pulchella

Eriochloa pulchella, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, often tufted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow with a slightly hairy texture, and the flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Eriochloa pulchella plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
