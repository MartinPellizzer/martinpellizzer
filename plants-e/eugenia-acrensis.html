Eugenia acrensis

Eugenia acrensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia acrensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia acrensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
