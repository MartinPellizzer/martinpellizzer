Embothrium myricoides

Embothrium myricoides, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile and western Argentina.

It is characterized by its bright red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure.

Embothrium myricoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
