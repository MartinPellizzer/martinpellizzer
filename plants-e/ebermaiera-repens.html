Ebermaiera repens

Ebermaiera repens, commonly known as the creeping evergreen shrub, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense, dark green foliage that forms a mat-like structure. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
