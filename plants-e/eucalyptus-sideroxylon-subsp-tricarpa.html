Eucalyptus sideroxylon subsp. tricarpa

Eucalyptus sideroxylon subsp.

tricarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, spherical flowers that are typically pale or greenish in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
