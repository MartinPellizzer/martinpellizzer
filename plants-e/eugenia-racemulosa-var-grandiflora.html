Eugenia racemulosa var. grandiflora

Eugenia racemulosa var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered eugenia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

The fruit, which is edible, is small, round, and has a sweet, tart flavor.
