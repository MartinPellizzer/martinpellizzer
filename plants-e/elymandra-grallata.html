Elymandra grallata

Elymandra grallata, commonly known as the silky rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Elymandra grallata is often found in open habitats such as spinifex plains and is a valuable component of local ecosystems.
