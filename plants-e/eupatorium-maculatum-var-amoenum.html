Eupatorium maculatum var. amoenum

Eupatorium maculatum var. amoenum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems covered in white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological gardens.
