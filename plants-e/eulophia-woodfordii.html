Eulophia woodfordii

Eulophia woodfordii, commonly known as the Woodford's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and strap-like leaves. The plant produces inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
