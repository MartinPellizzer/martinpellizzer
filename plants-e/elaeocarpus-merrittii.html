Elaeocarpus merrittii

Elaeocarpus merrittii, commonly known as the New Guinea elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a species of conservation concern in some areas.
