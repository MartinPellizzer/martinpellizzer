Eupatorium molinae

Eupatorium molinae, commonly known as Molina's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium molinae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
