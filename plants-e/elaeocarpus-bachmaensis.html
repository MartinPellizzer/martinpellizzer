Elaeocarpus bachmaensis

Elaeocarpus bachmaensis, commonly known as the Bachma elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
