Evolvulus alsinoides f. rotundifolius

Evolvulus alsinoides f. rotundifolius, commonly known as the rounded-leaf morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, rounded leaves and clusters of small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and wild areas.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
