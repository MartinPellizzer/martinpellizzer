Euonymus latifolius elegantissimus

Euonymus latifolius elegantissimus, commonly known as the glossy euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy sheen. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
