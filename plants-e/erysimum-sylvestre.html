Erysimum sylvestre

Erysimum sylvestre, commonly known as wild wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, often arranged in a cross shape typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The leaves are basal and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
