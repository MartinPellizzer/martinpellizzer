Eragrostis curvula var. conferta

Eragrostis curvula var. conferta, commonly known as weeping lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fountain-like growth habit with long, arching leaves that give it a soft, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish to brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
