Ensete glaucum var. wilsonii

Ensete glaucum var. wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson's banana, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its large, elongated leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of the genus Ensete. Its fibrous pseudostem is valued for its durability and has been traditionally used in construction and crafts.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
