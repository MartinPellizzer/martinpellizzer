Echeveria × sayulensis

Echeveria × sayulensis, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a hybrid succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves that range from green to blue, red, or purple. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or orange flowers during the spring or summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its coloration.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
