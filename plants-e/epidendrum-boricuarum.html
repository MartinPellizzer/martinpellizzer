Epidendrum boricuarum

Epidendrum boricuarum, commonly known as the Boricua Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Epidendrum boricuarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
