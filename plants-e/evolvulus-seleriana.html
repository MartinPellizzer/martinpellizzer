Evolvulus seleriana

Evolvulus seleriana, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
