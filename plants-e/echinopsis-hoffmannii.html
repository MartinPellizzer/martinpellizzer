Echinopsis hoffmannii

Echinopsis hoffmannii, commonly known as the Hoffmann's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to survive. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

Echinopsis hoffmannii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
