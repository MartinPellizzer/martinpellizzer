Echinodorus punctatus

Echinodorus punctatus, commonly known as the Mexican sword plant, is a popular aquatic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in length. The plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often used in pond and aquarium settings for its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Echinodorus punctatus is relatively easy to care for, making it a favored choice for both novice and experienced aquatic gardeners.
