Elaeocarpus vitiensis

Elaeocarpus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Fiji.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

Elaeocarpus vitiensis plays a significant ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and forest structure.
