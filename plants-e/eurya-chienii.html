Eurya chienii

Eurya chienii, commonly known as Chinese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Eurya chienii is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in urban environments.
