Euphorbia illirica subsp. semivillosa

Euphorbia illirica subsp.

semivillosa, commonly known as the Illyrian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers.

The plant has a low-growing, shrub-like habit and thrives in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking floral structures.
