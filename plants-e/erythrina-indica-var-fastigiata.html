Erythrina indica var. fastigiata

Erythrina indica var. fastigiata, commonly known as the Indian coral tree or the red torch tree, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in racemes. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant has also been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
