Epidendrum latibracteum

Epidendrum latibracteum, commonly known as the broad-bract epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid environments. The plant produces fragrant, often colorful flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

It is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
