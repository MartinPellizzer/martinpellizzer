Euphorbia maryrichardsiae

Euphorbia maryrichardsiae, commonly known as Mary Richards' spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and collections.
