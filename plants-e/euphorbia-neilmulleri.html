Euphorbia neilmulleri

Euphorbia neilmulleri, commonly known as the Miller's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its spiky, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flower clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. It produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

Euphorbia neilmulleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and striking appearance.
