Euphorbia virgata var. orientalis

Euphorbia virgata var. orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Spurge, is a succulent plant native to regions of Asia, including parts of China and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fleshy, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has modified leaves that are often reduced to spines, a feature common in many euphorbias for protection against herbivores. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant.
