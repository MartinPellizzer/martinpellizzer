Episcia melittifolia var. guadelupensis

Episcia melittifolia var. guadelupensis, commonly known as the Guadeloupe wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. The plant typically grows as a trailing or shrub-like species, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
