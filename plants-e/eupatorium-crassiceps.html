Eupatorium crassiceps

Eupatorium crassiceps, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often ribbed and have a distinctive, somewhat woody texture. Boneset typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used to treat fever and digestive ailments.
