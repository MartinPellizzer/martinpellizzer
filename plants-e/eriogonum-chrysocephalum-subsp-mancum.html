Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp. mancum

Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp.

mancum, commonly known as goldenhead buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, nut-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
