Endolepis Phyllostegia

False indigo, scientifically known as Endolepis phyllostegia, is a striking flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is prized for its vibrant blue-violet blooms that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. This perennial species thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens. Its unique foliage, which features narrow, lance-shaped leaves, adds visual interest throughout the growing season. Despite its beauty, the plant is relatively uncommon in cultivation, often overlooked for its lesser-known medicinal properties.
