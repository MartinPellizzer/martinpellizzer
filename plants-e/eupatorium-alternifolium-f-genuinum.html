Eupatorium alternifolium f. genuinum

Eupatorium alternifolium f. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
