Euodia lasioneura

Euodia lasioneura, commonly known as the silky ash or silky tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Euodia lasioneura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
