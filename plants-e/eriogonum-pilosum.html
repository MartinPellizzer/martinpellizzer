Eriogonum pilosum

Eriogonum pilosum, commonly known as the softleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and hillsides. It produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriogonum pilosum is an important species for pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
