Erysimum transsilvanicum

Erysimum transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress species. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of pinnately divided leaves, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
