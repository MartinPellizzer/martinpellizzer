Eugenia lourteigiae

Eugenia lourteigiae, commonly known as the Lourteig's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
