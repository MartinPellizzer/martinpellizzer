Eucalyptus foecunda var. loxophleba

Eucalyptus foecunda var. loxophleba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or red, which develop into woody capsules. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
