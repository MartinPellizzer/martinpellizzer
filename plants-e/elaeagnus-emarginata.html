Elaeagnus emarginata

Elaeagnus emarginata, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Elaeagnus emarginata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant for soil enrichment.
