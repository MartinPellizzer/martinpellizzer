Epilobium brevistylum var. ursinum

Epilobium brevistylum var. ursinum, commonly known as the Alaska willow, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, often alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense, showy inflorescences.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in stabilizing rocky or disturbed soils.
