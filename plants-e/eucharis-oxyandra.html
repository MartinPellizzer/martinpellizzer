Eucharis oxyandra

Eucharis oxyandra, commonly known as the white cyclamen, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family, characterized by its showy, spatulate-shaped flowers that resemble small, white cyclamen blooms. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne on a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

Eucharis oxyandra is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
