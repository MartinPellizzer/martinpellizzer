Eugenia mexicana

Eugenia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Mexican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces edible, red, cherry-like berries that are often used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies. Eugenia mexicana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and aromatic properties make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
