Erythroxylum longipes

Erythroxylum longipes, commonly known as the longipes coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small, white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found growing in moist, shaded environments.

This species is related to the coca plant and has been studied for its potential botanical and pharmacological properties.
