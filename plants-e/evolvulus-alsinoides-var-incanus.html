Evolvulus alsinoides var. incanus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. incanus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, five-petaled, and usually pale blue or white. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is cultivated as an ornamental plant in some regions.
