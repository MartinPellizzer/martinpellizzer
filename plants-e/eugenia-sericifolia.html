Eugenia sericifolia

Eugenia sericifolia, commonly known as the silky eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. These berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Eugenia sericifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.
