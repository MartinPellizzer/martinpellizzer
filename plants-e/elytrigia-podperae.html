Elytrigia podperae

Elytrigia podperae, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Elytrigia podperae is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and genetic uniqueness.
