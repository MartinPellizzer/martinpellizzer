Equisetum ramosissimum var. huegelii

Equisetum ramosissimum var. huegelii, commonly known as the Hugel's horse-tail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, spore-bearing cone at the top of its stems. This variety is typically found in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its stems are often segmented and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The plant plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
