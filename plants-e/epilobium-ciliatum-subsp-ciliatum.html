Epilobium ciliatum subsp. ciliatum

Epilobium ciliatum subsp.

ciliatum, commonly known as the silky willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spike-like clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
