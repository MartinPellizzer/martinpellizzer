Erigeron philadelphicus var. quercinus

Erigeron philadelphicus var. quercinus, commonly known as the oakleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble oak leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple-blue petals and a yellow central disk, which bloom from late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and ecologically valuable plant for pollinators and wildlife.
