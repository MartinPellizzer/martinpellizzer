Erigeron griseus

Erigeron griseus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces blue to purple ray flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils.

Erigeron griseus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
