Eriogonum annuum subsp. chihuahuaense

Eriogonum annuum subsp.

chihuahuaense, commonly known as the Chihuahua morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Chihuahua region of Mexico. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It has small, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
