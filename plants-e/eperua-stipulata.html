Eperua stipulata

Eperua stipulata, commonly known as the velvetwood tree, is a tropical species native to parts of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves with small stipules. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
