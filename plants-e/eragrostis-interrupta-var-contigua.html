Eragrostis interrupta var. contigua

Eragrostis interrupta var. contigua, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching foliage that forms a weeping or cascading appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
