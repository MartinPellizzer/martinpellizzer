Eugenia albanensis

Eugenia albanensis, commonly known as the Albanian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
