Escallonia stricta

Escallonia stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf escallonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or red flowers in the spring, which are followed by red berries. Its leaves are narrow and elliptical, with a slightly serrated edge.

Escallonia stricta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
