Espeletia emmanuelis

Espeletia emmanuelis, commonly known as the Emilio Páramo plant, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude grasslands of the Sierra Nevada de Santa Marta in Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth form and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adapted to attract pollinators in its harsh environment. Espeletia emmanuelis is well-suited to the cold, arid conditions of its habitat, with thick leaves that help retain moisture.

It plays a vital ecological role in the cloud forests and páramo ecosystems of the region.
