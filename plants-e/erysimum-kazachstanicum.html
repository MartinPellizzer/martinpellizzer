Erysimum kazachstanicum

Erysimum kazachstanicum, commonly known as the Kazakh wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage is characterized by narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
