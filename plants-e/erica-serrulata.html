Erica serrulata

Erica serrulata, commonly known as the serrated heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erica serrulata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
