Euclea racemosa subsp. schimperi

Euclea racemosa subsp.

schimperi, commonly known as the African wild cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of tannins.
