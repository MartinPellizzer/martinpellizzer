Eustoma russellianum var. flavum

Eustoma russellianum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow larkspur, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
