Euphorbia esula subsp. mosana

Euphorbia esula subsp.

mosana, commonly known as the Canadian tansy, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.
