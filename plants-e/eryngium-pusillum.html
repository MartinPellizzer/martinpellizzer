Eryngium pusillum

Eryngium pusillum, commonly known as the dwarf thistle, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of only a few inches, with dense, spiny leaves that are often blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Eryngium pusillum is a hardy perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens with suitable conditions.
