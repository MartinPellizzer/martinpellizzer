Erica embothriifolia var. longiflora

Erica embothriifolia var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
