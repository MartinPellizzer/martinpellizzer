Euthyra

Euthyra, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often blue or purple, ray florets that resemble holly leaves, giving it its common name. The plants are typically found in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline environments. Euthyra species are perennial herbs with a rosette of basal leaves and tall flowering stems.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often used in gardens and coastal landscaping.
