Epilobium × fallacinum

Epilobium × fallacinum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Epilobium × fallacinum is also known for its ability to colonize areas quickly, making it both a pioneer species and a potential weed.
