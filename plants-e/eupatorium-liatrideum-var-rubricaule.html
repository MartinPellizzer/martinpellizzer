Eupatorium liatrideum var. rubricaule

Eupatorium liatrideum var. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-stemmed thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that often display a reddish hue, particularly during the growing season. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
