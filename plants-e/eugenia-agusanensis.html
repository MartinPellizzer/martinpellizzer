Eugenia agusanensis

Eugenia agusanensis, commonly known as the Agusan ebony, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically found in the provinces of Agusan and Davao. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
