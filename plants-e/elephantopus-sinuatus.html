Elephantopus sinuatus

Elephantopus sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuous Elephant's Foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
