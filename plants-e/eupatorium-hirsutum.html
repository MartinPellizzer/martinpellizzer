Eupatorium hirsutum

Eupatorium hirsutum, commonly known as roughstem boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium hirsutum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
