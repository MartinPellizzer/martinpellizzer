Eriocaulon similischimperi

Eriocaulon similischimperi, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various aquatic invertebrates and birds.
