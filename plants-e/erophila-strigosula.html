Erophila strigosula

Erophila strigosula, commonly known as the woolly erophila, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, usually appearing in shades of pink or purple.

Erophila strigosula is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
