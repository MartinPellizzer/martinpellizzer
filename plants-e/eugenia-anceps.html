Eugenia anceps

Eugenia anceps, commonly known as the velvetleaf guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antioxidant properties.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
