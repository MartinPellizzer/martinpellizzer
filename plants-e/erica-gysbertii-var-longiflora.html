Erica gysbertii var. longiflora

Erica gysbertii var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica gysbertii var. longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
