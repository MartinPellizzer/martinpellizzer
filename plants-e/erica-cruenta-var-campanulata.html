Erica cruenta var. campanulata

Erica cruenta var. campanulata, commonly known as the bell heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
