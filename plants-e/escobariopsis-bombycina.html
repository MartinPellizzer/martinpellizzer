Escobariopsis bombycina

Escobariopsis bombycina, commonly known as the silky cushion lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, temperate environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and a pale green to yellowish thallus. The plant's surface is covered with a fine, silky layer of vegetative tissue, which gives it its common name. It is typically found on soil, rocks, or decaying wood in shaded areas.

Escobariopsis bombycina plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms.
