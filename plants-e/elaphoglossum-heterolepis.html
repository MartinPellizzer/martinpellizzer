Elaphoglossum heterolepis

Elaphoglossum heterolepis, commonly known as the variable holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically divided into multiple pinnae. The plant produces spores on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in clusters called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Elaphoglossum heterolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
