Echinacea paradoxa var. paradoxa

Echinacea paradoxa var. paradoxa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking purple petals and a prominent, spiky yellow to orange central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has fibrous roots and is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinacea paradoxa var. paradoxa is also valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies.
