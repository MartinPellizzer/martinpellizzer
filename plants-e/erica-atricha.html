Erica atricha

Erica atricha, commonly known as the black heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, dark purple to black flowers. Erica atricha typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
