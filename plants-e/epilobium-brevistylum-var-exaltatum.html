Epilobium brevistylum var. exaltatum

Epilobium brevistylum var. exaltatum, commonly known as the tall willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pinkish-purple flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
