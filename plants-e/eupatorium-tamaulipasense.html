Eupatorium tamaulipasense

Eupatorium tamaulipasense, commonly known as Tamaulipan eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Tamaulipas region of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Eupatorium tamaulipasense is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
