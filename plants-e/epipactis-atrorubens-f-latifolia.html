Epipactis atrorubens f. latifolia

Epipactis atrorubens f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved green helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

This orchid is noted for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi, aiding in nutrient uptake.
