Ermania saposhnikovii

Ermania saposhnikovii, commonly known as the Siberian angelica, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a distinctive purple tint. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a compound umbel, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a strong, aromatic scent, similar to other members of its family.

The roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
