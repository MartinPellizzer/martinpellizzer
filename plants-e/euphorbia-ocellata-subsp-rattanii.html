Euphorbia ocellata subsp. rattanii

Euphorbia ocellata subsp.

rattanii, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features striking, spiky leaves with a distinctive red or orange coloration, often marked with contrasting patterns.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
