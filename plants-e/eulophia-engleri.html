Eulophia engleri

Eulophia engleri, commonly known as the Engle's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a distinctive coloration.

Eulophia engleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
