Eucalyptus opimiflora

Eucalyptus opimiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

Eucalyptus opimiflora is adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils in its native habitat.
