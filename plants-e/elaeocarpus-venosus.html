Elaeocarpus venosus

Elaeocarpus venosus, commonly known as the veiny elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by round, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
