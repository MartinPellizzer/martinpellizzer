Elsholtzia taiwanensis

Elsholtzia taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone-rich soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a strong, mint-like scent.

The flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls, usually blooming in late spring to early summer.
