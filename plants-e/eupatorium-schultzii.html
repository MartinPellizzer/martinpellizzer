Eupatorium schultzii

Eupatorium schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

Its common name refers to the brittle, joint-like appearance of its stems.
