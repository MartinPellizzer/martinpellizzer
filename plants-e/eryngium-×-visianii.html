Eryngium × visianii

Eryngium × visianii, commonly known as the blueweed or sea holly, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showing a high tolerance for salt and wind. Its ornamental value comes from its striking appearance, making it a popular choice in gardens.

Eryngium × visianii is also known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun conditions.
