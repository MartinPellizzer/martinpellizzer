Equisetum telmateia f. caespitosum

Equisetum telmateia f. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted horse tail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems that bear whorls of small, needle-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

The species is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
