Encyclia thrombodes

Encyclia thrombodes, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, erect leaf and produces a raceme of golden-yellow flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is typically found in pine flatwoods and wetlands, often near water sources.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
