Eriogonum atrorubens var. rupestre

Eriogonum atrorubens var. rupestre, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often gray-green in color, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. It is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands, slopes, and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
