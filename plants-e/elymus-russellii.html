Elymus russellii

Elymus russellii, commonly known as Russell's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid conditions and can tolerate drought.

Its seeds are a food source for various wildlife species.
