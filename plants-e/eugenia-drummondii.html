Eugenia drummondii

Eugenia drummondii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Eugenia drummondii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.
