Erythroxylum mocquerysii var. beando

Erythroxylum mocquerysii var. beando, commonly known as the Beando coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that contain seeds, and it is adapted to grow in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures.

It is notable for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Andean societies.
