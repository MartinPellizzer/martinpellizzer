Erica leucopelta var. epheboides

Erica leucopelta var. epheboides, commonly known as the white-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, often blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
