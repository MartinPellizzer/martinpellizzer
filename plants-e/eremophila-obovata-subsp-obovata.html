Eremophila obovata subsp. obovata

Eremophila obovata subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants.
