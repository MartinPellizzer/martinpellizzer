Erica didyma

Erica didyma, commonly known as the pink heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica didyma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to form dense, compact shrubs.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience in coastal and sandy environments.
