Erigeron lorentzii

Erigeron lorentzii, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is often found in the central and western United States, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
