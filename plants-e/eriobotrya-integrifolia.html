Eriobotrya integrifolia

Eriobotrya integrifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear or pearsap, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces white, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, and juicy pears in autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses, often cultivated in gardens and orchards.
