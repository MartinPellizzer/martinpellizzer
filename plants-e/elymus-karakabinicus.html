Elymus karakabinicus

Elymus karakabinicus, commonly known as the Karakabinsk grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Elymus karakabinicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
