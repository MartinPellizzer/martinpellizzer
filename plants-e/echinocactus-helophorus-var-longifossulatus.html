Echinocactus helophorus var. longifossulatus

Echinocactus helophorus var. longifossulatus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a variety of the hedgehog cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, sharp spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
