Eragrostis curvula

Eragrostis curvula, commonly known as weeping lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis curvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some regions for forage and erosion control.
