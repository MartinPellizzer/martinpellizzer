Eugenia levinervis

Eugenia levinervis, commonly known as the Lemon Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are typically followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits. Its leaves contain high levels of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive citrus aroma.

Eugenia levinervis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
