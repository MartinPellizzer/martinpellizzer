Eucalyptus pyriformis var. elongata

Eucalyptus pyriformis var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its bark is usually smooth and peels in thin, flaky layers, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
