Eulophia sooi

Eulophia sooi, commonly known as the Singapore orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically has broad, strap-like leaves and bears inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Eulophia sooi thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical gardens.
