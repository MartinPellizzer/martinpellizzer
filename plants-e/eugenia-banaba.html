Eugenia banaba

Eugenia banaba, commonly known as the banana shrub, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that give way to edible, yellow-orange berries. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine for their health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
