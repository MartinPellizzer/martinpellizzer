Echinopsis pectinata var. reichenbachii

Echinopsis pectinata var. reichenbachii, commonly known as the Reichenbach's moon cactus, is a striking cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and vibrant, colorful flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a short, thick stem with a dense covering of spines, and its flowers are typically pink, red, or purple. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
