Escobaria dasyacantha var. duncanii

Escobaria dasyacantha var. duncanii, commonly known as Duncan's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or yellowish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
