Eragrostis pilosa subsp. multicaulis

Eragrostis pilosa subsp.

multicaulis, commonly known as hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical panicle, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
