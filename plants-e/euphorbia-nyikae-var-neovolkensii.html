Euphorbia nyikae var. neovolkensii

Euphorbia nyikae var. neovolkensii, commonly known as the Kigezi euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the mountains of Uganda.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
