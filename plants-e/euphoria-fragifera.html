Euphoria fragifera

Euphoria fragifera, commonly known as the sweet cheese tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces a unique fruit that resembles a small, round cheese-like structure, hence its common name. The fruit has a mild, sweet aroma and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes a variety of plants with diverse ecological roles.
