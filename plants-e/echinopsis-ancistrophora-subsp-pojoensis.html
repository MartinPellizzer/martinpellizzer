Echinopsis ancistrophora subsp. pojoensis

Echinopsis ancistrophora subsp.

pojoensis, commonly known as the Pojoensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and conservation efforts.
