Echinops globifer

Echinops globifer, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble small globes, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers in early summer, which are followed by the distinctive, prickly seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Echinops globifer is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
