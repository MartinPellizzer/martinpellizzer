Epidendrum polybulbon var. luteoalbum

Epidendrum polybulbon var. luteoalbum, commonly known as the Yellow White Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually white or yellowish in color, with a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
