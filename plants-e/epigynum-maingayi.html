Epigynum maingayi

Epigynum maingayi, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking, large, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers emit a strong fragrance at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
