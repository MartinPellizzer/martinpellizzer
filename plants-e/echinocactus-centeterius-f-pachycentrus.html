Echinocactus centeterius f. pachycentrus

Echinocactus centeterius f. pachycentrus, commonly known as the thick-centred hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
