Echinocereus bacanorensis

Echinocereus bacanorensis, commonly known as the Bacanora cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often pollinated by bats and insects. Echinocereus bacanorensis is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

It is a relatively small cactus, usually growing to a height of less than one meter.
