Encyclia alborubra

Encyclia alborubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a pseudobulb with a reddish stem and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the soil.

Encyclia alborubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
