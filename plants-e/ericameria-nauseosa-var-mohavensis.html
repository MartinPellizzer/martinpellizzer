Ericameria nauseosa var. mohavensis

Ericameria nauseosa var. mohavensis, commonly known as Mohave greasewood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

This species plays a role in stabilizing soils in desert ecosystems.
