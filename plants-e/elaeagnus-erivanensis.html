Elaeagnus erivanensis

Elaeagnus erivanensis, commonly known as the Persian silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to regions of Central Asia, including Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry landscapes. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Elaeagnus erivanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in ecological restoration projects.
