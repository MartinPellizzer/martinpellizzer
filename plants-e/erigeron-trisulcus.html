Erigeron trisulcus

Erigeron trisulcus, commonly known as the three-lobed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a distinctive three-lobed shape that gives the plant its common name.
