Efossus lamellosus

Efossus lamellosus, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It features a fibrous root system and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has alternate, narrow, and slightly hairy leaves that are often lobed or divided. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some areas.

Efossus lamellosus is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of forage for livestock.
