Euchaetis ericoides

Euchaetis ericoides, commonly known as the heath shrub, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands and coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, which bloom in early spring. Euchaetis ericoides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
