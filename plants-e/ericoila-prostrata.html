Ericoila prostrata

Ericoila prostrata, commonly known as the creeping heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and densely arranged along its stems. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late winter to early spring. Ericoila prostrata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens.
