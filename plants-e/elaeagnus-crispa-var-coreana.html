Elaeagnus crispa var. coreana

Elaeagnus crispa var. coreana, commonly known as Korean silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
