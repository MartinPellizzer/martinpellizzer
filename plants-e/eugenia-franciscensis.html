Eugenia franciscensis

Eugenia franciscensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or frangipani, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
