Echinocactus mazanensis

Echinocactus mazanensis, commonly known as the Mazan's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Echinocactus mazanensis is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
