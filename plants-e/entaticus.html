Entaticus

Entaticus, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a perennial plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing toxic compounds such as solanine. Entaticus is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
