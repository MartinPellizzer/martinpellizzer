Euphorbia rothrockii

Euphorbia rothrockii, commonly known as Rothrock's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
