Eriosema zuluense

Eriosema zuluense, commonly known as Zulu's fuzzy legume, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features slender, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets.

Eriosema zuluense is often found in grassy areas and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
