Eupatorium patagonicum

Eupatorium patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian boneset, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium patagonicum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in local remedies.
