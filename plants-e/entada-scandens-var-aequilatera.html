Entada scandens var. aequilatera

Entada scandens var. aequilatera, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features a straight trunk with smooth, grey bark and can grow up to 20 meters in height. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters.

The wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and construction.
