Endymion lingulatus

Endymion lingulatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf yarrow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Endymion lingulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
