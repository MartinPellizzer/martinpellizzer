Eriochilus fimbriatus

Eriochilus fimbriatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed everlasting or narrow-leafed dianthus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It features linear, grass-like leaves and produces showy, pink or red flowers in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens. It is often used in native plant landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

Eriochilus fimbriatus is also known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even when dried.
