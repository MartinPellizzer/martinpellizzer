Echium cyrenaicum

Echium cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Cyprus flax or sea spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in coastal and sandy environments. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in spikes. It is characterized by its fibrous stems and is often found in disturbed soils or along shorelines.

Echium cyrenaicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically for its fibers.
