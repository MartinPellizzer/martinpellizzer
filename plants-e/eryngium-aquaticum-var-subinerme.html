Eryngium aquaticum var. subinerme

Eryngium aquaticum var. subinerme, commonly known as water thistle, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features a rosette of spiny, blue-green leaves that are adapted for water retention. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming dense, thistle-like inflorescences.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.
