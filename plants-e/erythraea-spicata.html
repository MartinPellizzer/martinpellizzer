Erythraea spicata

Erythraea spicata, commonly known as the red spurge or red sandmat, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by red bracts. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky or sandy soils.
