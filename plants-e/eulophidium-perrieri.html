Eulophidium perrieri

Eulophidium perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's euphorbia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences known as cyathia. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
