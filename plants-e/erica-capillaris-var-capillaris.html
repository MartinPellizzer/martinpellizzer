Erica capillaris var. capillaris

Erica capillaris var. capillaris, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It features dense, compact foliage with fine, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
