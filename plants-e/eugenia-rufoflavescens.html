Eugenia rufoflavescens

Eugenia rufoflavescens, commonly known as the red-flowered eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds.

Eugenia rufoflavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
