Erigeron podophyllus

Erigeron podophyllus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its blue-violet flower heads bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to the landscape.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
