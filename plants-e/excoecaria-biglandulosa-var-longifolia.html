Excoecaria biglandulosa var. longifolia

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical to ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, often with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange to red berries. Its leaves are characterized by their long, slender shape and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has a moderate level of toxicity.
