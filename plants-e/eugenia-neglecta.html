Eugenia neglecta

Eugenia neglecta, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. Eugenia neglecta is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
