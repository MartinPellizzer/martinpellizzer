Erica tetralix var. glandulosa

Erica tetralix var. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular heath, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly referred to as the heath or heather family. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environmental conditions.
