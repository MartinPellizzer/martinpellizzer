Echinopsis ancistrophora subsp. arachnacantha

Echinopsis ancistrophora subsp.

arachnacantha, commonly known as the spider-thorned cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny tubercles that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for cultivation in dry climates.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
