Eragrostis boehmii

Eragrostis boehmii, commonly known as Boehm's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing forage for livestock.
