Eperua campestris

Eperua campestris, commonly known as the broad-leaved eperua, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by woody, elongated pods containing flat, edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
