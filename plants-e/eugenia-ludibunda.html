Eugenia ludibunda

Eugenia ludibunda, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, while its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds.

Eugenia ludibunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
