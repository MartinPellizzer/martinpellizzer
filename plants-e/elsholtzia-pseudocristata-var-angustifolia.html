Elsholtzia pseudocristata var. angustifolia

Elsholtzia pseudocristata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural cultivation in suitable climates.
