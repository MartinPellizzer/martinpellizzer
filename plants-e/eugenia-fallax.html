Eugenia fallax

Eugenia fallax, commonly known as the Australian native myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional Australian medicine. It produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Eugenia fallax is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
