Ericoides bruniades

Ericoides bruniades, commonly known as the Brun's ericoid, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
