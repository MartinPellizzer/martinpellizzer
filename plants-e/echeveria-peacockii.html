Echeveria peacockii

Echeveria peacockii, commonly known as the 'Peacock Plant,' is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green with red or purple margins. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or lavender flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Echeveria peacockii is popular as a decorative plant due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
