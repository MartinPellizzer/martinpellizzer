Echinops grandiflorus

Echinops grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that become prominent in late summer and autumn. The plant features gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops grandiflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
