Eugenia itacarensis

Eugenia itacarensis, commonly known as the Itacare myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

Eugenia itacarensis is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
