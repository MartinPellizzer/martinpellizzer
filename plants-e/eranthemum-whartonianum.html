Eranthemum whartonianum

Eranthemum whartonianum, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. It blooms throughout the year, particularly in spring and summer, and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
