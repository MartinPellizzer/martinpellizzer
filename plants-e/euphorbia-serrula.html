Euphorbia serrula

Euphorbia serrula, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving it its common name.

Euphorbia serrula is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and should be handled with care.
