Elatostema appendiculatum

Elatostema appendiculatum, commonly known as the appendiculate elatostemum, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as understorey forests or along stream banks. The plant features a compact rosette of leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in humid conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in greenish-white or pale yellow hues.

Elatostema appendiculatum is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
