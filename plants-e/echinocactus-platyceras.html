Echinocactus platyceras

Echinocactus platyceras, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, helmet-shaped stem that is often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiky structure. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
