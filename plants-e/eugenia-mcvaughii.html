Eugenia mcvaughii

Eugenia mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
