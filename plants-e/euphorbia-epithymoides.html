Euphorbia epithymoides

Euphorbia epithymoides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems and spiny, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a green, fleshy capsule that turns orange when mature, containing numerous seeds.

Euphorbia epithymoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
