Eupatorium obtusissimum

Eupatorium obtusissimum, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
