Eucalyptus × coniophloia

Eucalyptus × coniophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus coniophloia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions of Australia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a shrub or small tree that often forms dense thickets, providing habitat for various native wildlife.

The bark is usually smooth and peels in strips, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
