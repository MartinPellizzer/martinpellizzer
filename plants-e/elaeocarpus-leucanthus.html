Elaeocarpus leucanthus

Elaeocarpus leucanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
