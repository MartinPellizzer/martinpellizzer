Echium angustifolium var. parviflorum

Echium angustifolium var. parviflorum, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are clustered in dense, elongated inflorescences called racemes.

The plant is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid conditions.
