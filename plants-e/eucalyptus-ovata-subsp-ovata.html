Eucalyptus ovata subsp. ovata

Eucalyptus ovata subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikes, typically during the autumn and winter months. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
