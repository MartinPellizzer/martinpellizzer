Erianthus macratherus

Erianthus macratherus, commonly known as the tall grass reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated. It is adapted to thrive in wet or marshy environments, making it a common species in swampy areas.

Its robust growth and ecological resilience make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
