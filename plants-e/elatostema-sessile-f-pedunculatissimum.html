Elatostema sessile f. pedunculatissimum

Elatostema sessile f. pedunculatissimum, commonly known as the long-stemmed sessile elatostema, is a variant of the Elatostema genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on elongated pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
