Euphorbia brittingeri subsp. flavicoma

Euphorbia brittingeri subsp.

flavicoma, commonly known as the yellow-hair euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
