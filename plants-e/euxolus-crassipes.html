Euxolus crassipes

Euxolus crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its stems are often succulent, adapted to store moisture.

Euxolus crassipes is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
