Ehretia philippinensis

Ehretia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that split open to release seeds.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
