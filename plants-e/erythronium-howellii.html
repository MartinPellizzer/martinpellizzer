Erythronium howellii

Erythronium howellii, commonly known as Howell's dog's mercury, is a member of the Colocasiaceae family and is native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant features a rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. In spring, it produces a single, nodding flower with a distinctive yellow corona surrounding a white tepal.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
