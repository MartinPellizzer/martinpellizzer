Epidendrum gracillimum

Epidendrum gracillimum, commonly known as the slender epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance.
