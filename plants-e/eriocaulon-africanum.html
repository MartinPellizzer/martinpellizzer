Eriocaulon africanum

Eriocaulon africanum, commonly known as African threadgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Eriocaulon africanum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
