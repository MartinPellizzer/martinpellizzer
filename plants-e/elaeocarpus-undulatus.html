Elaeocarpus undulatus

Elaeocarpus undulatus, commonly known as the wavy-leaf elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are yellowish-green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
