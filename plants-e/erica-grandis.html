Erica grandis

Erica grandis, commonly known as the common heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or acidic soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Erica grandis is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in areas with limited rainfall.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
