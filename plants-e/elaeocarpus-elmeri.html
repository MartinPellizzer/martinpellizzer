Elaeocarpus elmeri

Elaeocarpus elmeri, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous rainforests.

The plant has limited commercial use but is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
