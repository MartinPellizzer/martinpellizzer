Eucalyptus oleosa subsp. corvina

Eucalyptus oleosa subsp.

corvina, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hair.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but thrives in well-drained soils. Eucalyptus oleosa subsp.

corvina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
