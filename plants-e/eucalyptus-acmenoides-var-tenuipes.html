Eucalyptus acmenoides var. tenuipes

Eucalyptus acmenoides var. tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are often visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring sandy or rocky soils.

This variant is part of the Eucalyptus genus, known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in Australian ecosystems.
