Euphorbia siguatepequensis

Euphorbia siguatepequensis, commonly known as the Pequén Pequén, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Euphorbia siguatepequensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
