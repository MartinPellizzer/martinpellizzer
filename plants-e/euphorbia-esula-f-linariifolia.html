Euphorbia esula f. linariifolia

Euphorbia esula f. linariifolia, commonly known as the Linaria leaf euphorbia, is a variant of the leafy spurge, characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and parts of Europe. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with thick, waxy leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family, and are usually yellowish-green in color.

This plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
