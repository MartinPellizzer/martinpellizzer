Epilobium pyrricholophum f. japonicum

Epilobium pyrricholophum f. japonicum, commonly known as the Japanese willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It is native to East Asia and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
