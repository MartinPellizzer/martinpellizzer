Epilobium × pseudocollinum

Epilobium × pseudocollinum, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect or decumbent stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated margin.

Epilobium × pseudocollinum is often found in damp meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
