Erythroxylum davidii

Erythroxylum davidii, commonly known as the David's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its woody structure and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in well-drained soils.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance.
