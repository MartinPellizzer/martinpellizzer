Eucalyptus dumosa var. rhodophloia

Eucalyptus dumosa var. rhodophloia, commonly known as the red-wooded malleeflower, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown bark, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often in sandy or rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, and it produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
