Euphorbia balsamifera subsp. adenensis

Euphorbia balsamifera subsp.

adenensis, commonly known as the腺状 euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
