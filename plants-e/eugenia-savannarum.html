Eugenia savannarum

Eugenia savannarum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
