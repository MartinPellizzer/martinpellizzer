Echinocactus sessiliflorus var. tetracanthus

Echinocactus sessiliflorus var. tetracanthus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with distinctive spines and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is characterized by its dense, spiny appearance and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
