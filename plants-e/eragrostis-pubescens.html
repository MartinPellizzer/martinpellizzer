Eragrostis pubescens

Eragrostis pubescens, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
