Elodes virginica var. japonica

Elodes virginica var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually found near the water's surface. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls, aiding in efficient light absorption in aquatic environments.

It is often used in pond ecosystems for water filtration and as a habitat for aquatic organisms.
