Eryngium dichotomum var. ramosissimum

Eryngium dichotomum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the branched thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and spherical, blue-gray flower heads that resemble small umbrellas. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its unique morphology and adaptive features make it well-suited for arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
