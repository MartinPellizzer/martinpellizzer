Eupatorium ageratoides

Eupatorium ageratoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium ageratoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
