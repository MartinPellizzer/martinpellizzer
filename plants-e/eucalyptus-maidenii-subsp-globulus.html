Eucalyptus maidenii subsp. globulus

Eucalyptus maidenii subsp.

globulus, commonly known as the Sydney blue gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that turn red or yellow when mature. It is a tall tree, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters, and is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils.

The subspecies is notable for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern medicinal applications.
