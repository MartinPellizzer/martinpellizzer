Eulophia chrysops

Eulophia chrysops, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the showy, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings.

Eulophia chrysops is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
