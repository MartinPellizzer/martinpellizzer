Eugenia polyneura

Eugenia polyneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several small, edible seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
