Eryngium vaseyi var. oblanceolatum

Eryngium vaseyi var. oblanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Texas.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal rosettes of oblanceolate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open prairies and disturbed sites.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
