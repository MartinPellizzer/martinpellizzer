Eriosema chrysadenium

Eriosema chrysadenium, commonly known as the golden-eyed eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, hairy stipules, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a distinctive, spurred structure. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
