Etlingera heliconiifolia

Etlingera heliconiifolia, commonly known as the Heliconia ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking, inflorescence-like structures that resemble heliconias, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
