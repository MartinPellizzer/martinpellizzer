Elymus franchetii subsp. pacificus

Elymus franchetii subsp.

pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to western North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as streambanks, meadows, and forest edges.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
