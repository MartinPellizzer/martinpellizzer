Encholirium sazimae

Encholirium sazimae, commonly known as the Sazimae torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of western Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and clusters of bright red flowers that bloom during the summer months. The plant has spiny areoles and produces small, elongated fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
