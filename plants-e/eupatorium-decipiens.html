Eupatorium decipiens

Eupatorium decipiens, commonly known as false boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium decipiens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
