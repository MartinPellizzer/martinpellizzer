Echinopsis cotacajesii

Echinopsis cotacajesii, commonly known as the Tepui Candelabra Cactus, is a species native to the tepuis of northern South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive, spiny appearance and produces yellow flowers during the dry season. The plant has a ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its roots are adapted to anchor in rocky substrates, and it thrives in full sun.

Echinopsis cotacajesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
