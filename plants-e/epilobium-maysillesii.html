Epilobium maysillesii

Epilobium maysillesii, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Epilobium maysillesii is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.
