Eleocharis uniglumis var. halophila

Eleocharis uniglumis var. halophila, commonly known as salt marsh spike-rush, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in coastal wetlands, estuaries, and salt marshes, where it tolerates high salinity levels. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
