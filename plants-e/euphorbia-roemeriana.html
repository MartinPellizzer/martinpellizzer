Euphorbia roemeriana

Euphorbia roemeriana, commonly known as the Roemer's spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
