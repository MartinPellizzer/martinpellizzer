Eragrostis pilosa var. bahiensis

Eragrostis pilosa var. bahiensis, commonly known as Bahian hairy grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, grasslands, and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
