Erica petrophila

Erica petrophila, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Erica petrophila is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and hardy species.
