Epiphyllum ruckerianum

Epiphyllum ruckerianum, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that resemble leaves. The plant produces striking, large, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and brief blooming period.

Epiphyllum ruckerianum thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
