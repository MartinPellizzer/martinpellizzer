Eleocharis attenuata

Eleocharis attenuata, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Eleocharis attenuata is also used in phytoremediation due to its ability to absorb heavy metals from contaminated water.
