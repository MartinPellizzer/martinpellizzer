Eulalia argentea

Eulalia argentea, commonly known as silver flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that reflect light, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eulalia argentea is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
