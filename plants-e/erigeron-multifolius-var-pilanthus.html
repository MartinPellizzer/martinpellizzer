Erigeron multifolius var. pilanthus

Erigeron multifolius var. pilanthus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features hairy, gray-green leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for pollinators and a hardy addition to native plant gardens.
