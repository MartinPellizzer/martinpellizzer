Eupatorium helianthifolium

Eupatorium helianthifolium, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, often found in marshy areas and along waterways.

Its showy inflorescences make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
