Elatine serpyllifolia

Elatine serpyllifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ditches, ponds, and moist soil near water. The plant has slender, ribbon-like leaves that are often less than 1 cm in width, arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered, with four petals that are usually pink or white in color.

Elatine serpyllifolia is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to thrive in nutrient-rich, wet habitats.
