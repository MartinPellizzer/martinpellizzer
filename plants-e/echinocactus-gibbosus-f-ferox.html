Echinocactus gibbosus f. ferox

Echinocactus gibbosus f. ferox, commonly known as the Mexican lion's claw, is a distinctive cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, globular shape, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its dense, spiny exterior provides protection against herbivores and harsh weather conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
