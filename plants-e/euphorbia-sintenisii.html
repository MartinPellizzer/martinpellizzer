Euphorbia sintenisii

Euphorbia sintenisii, commonly known as the Sintenis's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent gardens.
