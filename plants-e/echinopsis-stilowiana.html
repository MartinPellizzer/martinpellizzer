Echinopsis stilowiana

Echinopsis stilowiana, commonly known as the Stilowiana cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive green body and spines that are often reddish or yellowish in color. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
