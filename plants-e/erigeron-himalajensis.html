Erigeron himalajensis

Erigeron himalajensis, commonly known as the Himalayan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its foliage is usually gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which are blue to purple in color, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
