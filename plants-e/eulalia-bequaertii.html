Eulalia bequaertii

Eulalia bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purplish in color. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Eulalia bequaertii is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
