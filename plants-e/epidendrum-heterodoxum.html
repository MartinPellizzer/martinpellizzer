Epidendrum heterodoxum

Epidendrum heterodoxum, commonly known as the heterodox epidendron, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a rhizomatous structure with fleshy, elliptical leaves that are dark green and covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences, displaying vivid colors ranging from yellow to red, with intricate patterns on the petals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
