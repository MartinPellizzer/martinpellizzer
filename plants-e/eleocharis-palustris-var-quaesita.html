Eleocharis palustris var. quaesita

Eleocharis palustris var. quaesita, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This variety is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
