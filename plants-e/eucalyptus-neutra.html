Eucalyptus neutra

Eucalyptus neutra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale green, appearing in clusters.

Eucalyptus neutra is adapted to withstand drought and is often found in open woodlands or shrublands.
