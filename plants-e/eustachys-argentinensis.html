Eustachys argentinensis

Eustachys argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas, including grasslands and disturbed soils, and is well adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Eustachys argentinensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
