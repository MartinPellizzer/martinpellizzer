Eupatorium cuneatum

Eupatorium cuneatum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Eupatorium cuneatum is often found in the eastern United States and is part of the Asteraceae family.
