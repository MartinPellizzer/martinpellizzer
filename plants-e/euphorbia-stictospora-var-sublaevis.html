Euphorbia stictospora var. sublaevis

Euphorbia stictospora var. sublaevis, commonly known as the smooth euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a spiny stem that serves as a defense mechanism.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
