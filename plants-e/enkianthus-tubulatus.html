Enkianthus tubulatus

Enkianthus tubulatus, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
