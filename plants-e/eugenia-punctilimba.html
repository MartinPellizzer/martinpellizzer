Eugenia punctilimba

Eugenia punctilimba, commonly known as the red-eyed ash or red-eyed tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
