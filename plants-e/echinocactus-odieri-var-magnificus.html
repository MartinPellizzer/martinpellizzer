Echinocactus odieri var. magnificus

Echinocactus odieri var. magnificus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that bear yellow to red bristles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its thick, ribbed stem stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
