Eriocaulon pardinum

Eriocaulon pardinum, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a small, herbaceous plant native to sandy and coastal regions.

It typically grows in wet, saline soils and is often found in dune environments. The plant has a compact, low-growing habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing white or pale pink blooms.

Eriocaulon pardinum plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
