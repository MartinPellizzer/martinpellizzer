Eriocaulon raipurense

Eriocaulon raipurense, commonly known as the Raipura rush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters and has slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming dense clusters near the water's surface.

Eriocaulon raipurense plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
