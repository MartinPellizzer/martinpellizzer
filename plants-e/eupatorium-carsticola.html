Eupatorium carsticola

Eupatorium carsticola, commonly known as Carsticola eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas, such as forest floors and stream banks. Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a rough texture, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium carsticola is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
