Ervum tetraphyllum

Ervum tetraphyllum, commonly known as the four-leafed vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which often have a fourth small leaflet, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its seed pods are typically hairy and contain several elliptical seeds.

Ervum tetraphyllum is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal practices.
