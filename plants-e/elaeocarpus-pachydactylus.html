Elaeocarpus pachydactylus

Elaeocarpus pachydactylus, commonly known as the thick-fingered elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. Its fruits are an important food source for various birds and mammals in its native habitat.

The wood of Elaeocarpus pachydactylus is hard and durable, making it valuable for local crafts and construction.
