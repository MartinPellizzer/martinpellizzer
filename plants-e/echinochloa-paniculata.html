Echinochloa paniculata

Echinochloa paniculata, commonly known as panicled fingergrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green color.

Echinochloa paniculata is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a food source for various herbivorous insects and livestock.
