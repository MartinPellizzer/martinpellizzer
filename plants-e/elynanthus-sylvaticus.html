Elynanthus sylvaticus

Elynanthus sylvaticus, commonly known as the European water dropwort, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, hollow stems and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet soils and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
