Erythroxylum nitidum

Erythroxylum nitidum, commonly known as the glossy copal tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries containing seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its resin and leaves.
