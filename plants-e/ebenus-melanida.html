Ebenus melanida

Ebenus melanida, commonly known as black sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually succulent and can store water, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.

Ebenus melanida is native to regions of Europe and Asia, where it plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local biodiversity.
