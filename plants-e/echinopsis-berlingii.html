Echinopsis berlingii

Echinopsis berlingii, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to produce striking, bright-colored flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines that provide protection from herbivores. Its flowering period typically occurs in the summer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Echinopsis berlingii is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight to thrive.
