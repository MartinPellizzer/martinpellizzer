Eugenia craveniana

Eugenia craveniana, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight waxy coating. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia craveniana thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
