Eleocharis acicularis f. longiseta

Eleocharis acicularis f. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is notable for its elongated, thread-like bracts that give it its distinguishing appearance. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in aquatic gardens.
