Elymus trachycaulus var. latiglumis

Elymus trachycaulus var. latiglumis, commonly known as the rough false wheat, is a grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are generally rough and hairy, contributing to its common name. This variety is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
