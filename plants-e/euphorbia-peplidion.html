Euphorbia peplidion

Euphorbia peplidion, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy, while its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia peplidion is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
