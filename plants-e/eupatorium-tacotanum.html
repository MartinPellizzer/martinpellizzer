Eupatorium tacotanum

Eupatorium tacotanum, commonly known as the Tacotanum boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium tacotanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
