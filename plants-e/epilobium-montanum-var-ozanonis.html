Epilobium montanum var. ozanonis

Epilobium montanum var. ozanonis, commonly known as the Ozano's willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
