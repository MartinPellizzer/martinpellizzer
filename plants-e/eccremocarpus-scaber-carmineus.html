Eccremocarpus scaber carmineus

Eccremocarpus scaber carmineus, commonly known as the crimson velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a cover crop.
