Erigeron engelmannii var. davisii

Erigeron engelmannii var. davisii, commonly known as Engelmann's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth compared to other varieties of Erigeron engelmannii.
