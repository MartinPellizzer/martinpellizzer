Eremobium aegyptiacum subsp. diffusum

Eremobium aegyptiacum subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It typically blooms during the rainy season, adding a splash of color to the landscape.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid ecosystems.
