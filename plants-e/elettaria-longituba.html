Elettaria longituba

Elettaria longituba, commonly known as the long-stemmed cardamom, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is cultivated for its aromatic seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces elongated, three-lobed fruits containing the aromatic seeds.

The botanical characteristics of Elettaria longituba make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and the spice trade.
