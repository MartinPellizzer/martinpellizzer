Erica venustiflora subsp. glandulosa

Erica venustiflora subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular heath, is a species native to South Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
