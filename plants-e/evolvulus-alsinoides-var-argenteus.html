Evolvulus alsinoides var. argenteus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
