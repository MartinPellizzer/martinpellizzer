Eria davaensis

Eria davaensis, commonly known as the Davaeana orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria davaensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
