Echinodorus intermedius

Echinodorus intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It has heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface, followed by distinctive blue or purple berries. It is often used in aquariums and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

Echinodorus intermedius is also known for its resilience and adaptability to various water conditions.
