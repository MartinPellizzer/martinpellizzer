Eucalyptus resinifera var. grandiflora

Eucalyptus resinifera var. grandiflora, commonly known as the lemon-scented gum, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark that peels in thin strips. The plant produces aromatic, lemon-scented leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and is valued for its timber and essential oils.
