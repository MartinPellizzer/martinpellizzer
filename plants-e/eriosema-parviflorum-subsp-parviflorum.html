Eriosema parviflorum subsp. parviflorum

Eriosema parviflorum subsp.

parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered eriosema, is a species native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is often found in areas with well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various insects.
