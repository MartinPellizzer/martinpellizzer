Euphrasia subexserta

Euphrasia subexserta, commonly known as the slender dog's mercury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically features slender, upright stems covered in small, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy sepals that are longer than the petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Euphrasia subexserta is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in horticulture.
