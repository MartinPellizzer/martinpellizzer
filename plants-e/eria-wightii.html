Eria wightii

Eria wightii, commonly known as the Wight's orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

Eria wightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
