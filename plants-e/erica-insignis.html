Erica insignis

Erica insignis, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. Erica insignis is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact size and ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
