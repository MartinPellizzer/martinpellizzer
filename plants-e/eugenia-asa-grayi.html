Eugenia asa-grayi

Eugenia asa-grayi, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark blue, edible berries that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
