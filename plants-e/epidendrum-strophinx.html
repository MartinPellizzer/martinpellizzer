Epidendrum strophinx

Epidendrum strophinx, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth and storage of water and nutrients. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural cultivation.
