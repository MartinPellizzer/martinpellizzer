Euphrasia lepida

Euphrasia lepida, commonly known as the pale dog's mercury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has slender, branching stems covered with fine, hair-like structures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Euphrasia lepida is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value in traditional herbal practices.
