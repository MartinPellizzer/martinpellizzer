Eleocharis blakeana

Eleocharis blakeana, commonly known as the Blake's spike-rush, is a species of sedges native to wetland environments in eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often found in swampy areas and along the edges of water bodies. Its rhizomes help stabilize the soil, making it ecologically important in maintaining wetland habitats.

Eleocharis blakeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens and restoration projects.
