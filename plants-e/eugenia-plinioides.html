Eugenia plinioides

Eugenia plinioides, commonly known as the pink velvetwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and striking, showy flowers that range from pink to red. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves of Eugenia plinioides have also been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
