Erythraea rhodensis

Erythraea rhodensis, commonly known as the Red Rhododendron, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in late spring, adding striking visual contrast to its evergreen foliage. The plant belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known shrubs like blueberries and azaleas. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms.

Erythraea rhodensis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
