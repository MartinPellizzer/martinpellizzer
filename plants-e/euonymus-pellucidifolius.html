Euonymus pellucidifolius

Euonymus pellucidifolius, commonly known as the glassyleaf woodbine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, translucent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite and have a waxy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

Euonymus pellucidifolius is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
