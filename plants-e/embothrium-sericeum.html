Embothrium sericeum

Embothrium sericeum, commonly known as the silky embothrium, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-white, silky-hair covered leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or orange when mature, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Embothrium sericeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
