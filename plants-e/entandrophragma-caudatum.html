Entandrophragma caudatum

Entandrophragma caudatum, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to Central and West Africa.

It is characterized by its straight grain and high density, making it valuable for construction and furniture. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is resistant to termites and decay.
