Eucalyptus stellulata var. luehmanniana

Eucalyptus stellulata var. luehmanniana, commonly known as the Luehmann's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is also noted for its aromatic leaves, which have been used in traditional herbal remedies.
