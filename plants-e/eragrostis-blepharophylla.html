Eragrostis blepharophylla

Eragrostis blepharophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf bluemouth, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, which helps it thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, blue-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding visual interest to its appearance. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is noted for its ability to form dense mats, making it suitable for erosion control in certain regions.
