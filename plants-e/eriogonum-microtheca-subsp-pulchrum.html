Eriogonum microtheca subsp. pulchrum

Eriogonum microtheca subsp.

pulchrum, commonly known as the Sierra fairy duster, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as chaparral and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
