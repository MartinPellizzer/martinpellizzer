Eria hindei

Eria hindei, commonly known as the Hinde's orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a subtle fragrance, making it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria hindei is considered a rare and endangered species.
