Erianthus brevibarbis var. contortus

Erianthus brevibarbis var. contortus, commonly known as the twisted short-barbed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a distinct silver-gray sheen. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are often adorned with silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
