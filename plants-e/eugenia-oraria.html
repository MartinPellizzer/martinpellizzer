Eugenia oraria

Eugenia oraria, commonly known as the sandpaper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, sandpapery bark and elliptical, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Eugenia oraria thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance among Indigenous Australian communities.
