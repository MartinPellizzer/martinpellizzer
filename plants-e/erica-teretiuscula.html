Erica teretiuscula

Erica teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. This evergreen shrub typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its small, needle-like leaves are evergreen, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white.

Erica teretiuscula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
