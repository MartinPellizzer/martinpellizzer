Erica manifesta

Erica manifesta, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature.

Erica manifesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
