Eurybia elaeophila

Eurybia elaeophila, commonly known as the white wood aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Eurybia elaeophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
