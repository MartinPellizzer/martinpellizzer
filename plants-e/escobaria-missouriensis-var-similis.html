Escobaria missouriensis var. similis

Escobaria missouriensis var. similis, commonly known as the Missouri prickly pear, is a succulent cactus native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and wildlife. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to sandy soils.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Opuntia genus but is distinguished by its unique morphology and geographic distribution.
