Erodium chium var. pseudoaragonense

Erodium chium var. pseudoaragonense, commonly known as the desert cowpea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is an annual herb with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, oval leaves and produces slender, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of desert and semi-desert ecosystems.
