Eryngium × mohamedanii

Eryngium × mohamedanii, commonly known as the blue foxtail, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Eryngium × mohamedanii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
