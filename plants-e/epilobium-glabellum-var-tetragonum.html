Epilobium glabellum var. tetragonum

Epilobium glabellum var. tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled willowherb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, quadrangular stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, pink to purple flowers in spikes during the summer months.

This species is often found in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides.
