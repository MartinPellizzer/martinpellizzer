Eutrema japonicum var. sachalinense

Eutrema japonicum var. sachalinense, commonly known as the Sakhalin sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Japan and the Russian Far East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, where it is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. Its botanical features include a prostrate growth habit and a system of shallow roots that help it anchor in unstable substrates.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to coastal environments and its role in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
