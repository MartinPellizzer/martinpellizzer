Eulophus tenuisectus

Eulophus tenuisectus, commonly known as the slender-leafed euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Its structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic ecosystems.
