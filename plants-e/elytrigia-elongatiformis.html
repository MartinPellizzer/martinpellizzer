Elytrigia elongatiformis

Elytrigia elongatiformis, commonly known as the elongate bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like blades and a fibrous root system.

Elytrigia elongatiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings.
