Eugenia organosia

Eugenia organosia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant red berries and fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while its fruit is a small, edible drupe that is often used in jams and desserts.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
