Eurycaulis cinereus

Eurycaulis cinereus, commonly known as the silverleaf eurycaulis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from underground sources.

Eurycaulis cinereus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand prolonged drought periods.
