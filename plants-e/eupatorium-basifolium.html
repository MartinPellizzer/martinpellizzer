Eupatorium basifolium

Eupatorium basifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium basifolium is a valuable plant for attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
