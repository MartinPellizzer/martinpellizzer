Erica eburnea

Erica eburnea, commonly known as the silver heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. Erica eburnea produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
