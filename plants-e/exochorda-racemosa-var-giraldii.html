Exochorda racemosa var. giraldii

Exochorda racemosa var. giraldii, commonly known as the white thorned spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it suitable for hedging or as a specimen plant in gardens.
