Epilobium angustifolium subsp. circumvagum

Epilobium angustifolium subsp.

circumvagum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is notable for its adaptability and is often found in disturbed or transitional habitats.
