Erysimum nevadense subsp. merxmuelleri

Erysimum nevadense subsp.

merxmuelleri, commonly known as the Sierra Peninsula wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as slopes and cliffs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, known for its cruciform flowers and distinctive seed pods.
