Echinocereus dasyacanthus var. ctenoides

Echinocereus dasyacanthus var. ctenoides, commonly known as the hooked echinocereus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
