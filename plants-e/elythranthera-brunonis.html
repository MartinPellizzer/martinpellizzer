Elythranthera brunonis

Elythranthera brunonis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface.

Elythranthera brunonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses for its beauty.
