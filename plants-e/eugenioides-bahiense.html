Eugenioides bahiense

Eugenioides bahiense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but have a spicy, peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
