Erodendrum tenax

Erodendrum tenax, commonly known as the rock fig, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in coastal and rocky habitats, often growing in well-drained soils.

Erodendrum tenax is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
