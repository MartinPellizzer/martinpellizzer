Effusiella erucosa

Effusiella erucosa, commonly known as the creeping thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their fragrant, thyme-like aroma.

Effusiella erucosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
