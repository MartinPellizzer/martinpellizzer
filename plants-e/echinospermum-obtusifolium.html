Echinospermum obtusifolium

Echinospermum obtusifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf echinocone, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features soft, hairy stems and broad, ovate leaves with a waxy texture. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, producing small, hard seeds enclosed in a spiny pome.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
