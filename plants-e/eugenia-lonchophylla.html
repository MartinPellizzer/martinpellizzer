Eugenia lonchophylla

Eugenia lonchophylla, commonly known as the satin leaf or satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by edible, red berries.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
