Eugenia schlechtendaliana

Eugenia schlechtendaliana, commonly known as the golden apple or white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
