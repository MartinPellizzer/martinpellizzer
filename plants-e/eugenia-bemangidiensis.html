Eugenia bemangidiensis

Eugenia bemangidiensis, commonly known as the Bemangidi cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and red or purple berries that resemble cherries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
