Erythrophleum pubistamineum var. parvifolium

Erythrophleum pubistamineum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically small and lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
