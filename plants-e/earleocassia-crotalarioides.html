Earleocassia crotalarioides

Earleocassia crotalarioides, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance.
