Ericameria nauseosa var. hololeuca

Ericameria nauseosa var. hololeuca, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, silver-gray foliage and produces yellow-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its glandular hairs, which give it a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical structure includes opposite leaves and a branching habit that contributes to its ornamental value.
