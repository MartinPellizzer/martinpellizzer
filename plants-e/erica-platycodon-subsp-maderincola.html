Erica platycodon subsp. maderincola

Erica platycodon subsp.

maderincola, commonly known as the Mader's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
