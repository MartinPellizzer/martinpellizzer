Earleocassia bauhinioides

Earleocassia bauhinioides, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
