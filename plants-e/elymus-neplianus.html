Elymus neplianus

Elymus neplianus, commonly known as the Neplian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowering stems bear panicle-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers.

Elymus neplianus is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
