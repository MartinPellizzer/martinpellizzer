Euphorbia philippiana

Euphorbia philippiana, commonly known as the Philippine spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Euphorbia philippiana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
