Eupatorium saluberrimum

Eupatorium saluberrimum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium saluberrimum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
