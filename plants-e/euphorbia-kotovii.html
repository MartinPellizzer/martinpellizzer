Euphorbia kotovii

Euphorbia kotovii, commonly known as the Kotov's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
