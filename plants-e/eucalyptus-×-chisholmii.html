Eucalyptus × chisholmii

Eucalyptus × chisholmii, commonly known as the Chisholm's gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips. The plant produces small, globular inflorescences with white or pale blue flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a aromatic, camphor-like scent.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its therapeutic properties.
