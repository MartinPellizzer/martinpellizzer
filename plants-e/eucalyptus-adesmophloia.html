Eucalyptus adesmophloia

Eucalyptus adesmophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stringybark, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, stringy bark that peels in long strips, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry sclerophyll forests and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a greyish-green hue, and it produces small, globular flower clusters.

The tree is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
