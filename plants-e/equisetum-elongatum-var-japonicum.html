Equisetum elongatum var. japonicum

Equisetum elongatum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing strobila, which are typically found at the apex of the plant.

The species is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
