Echinospermum condylophorum

Echinospermum condylophorum, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds enclosed in a pappus, which aids in its propagation. It typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
