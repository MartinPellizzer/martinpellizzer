Echinops rubromontanus

Echinops rubromontanus, commonly known as the red mountain thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and Angola. The plant is characterized by its spiny, spherical heads of yellow flowers surrounded by reddish bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Echinops rubromontanus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
