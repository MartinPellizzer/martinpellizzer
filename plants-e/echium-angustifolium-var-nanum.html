Echium angustifolium var. nanum

Echium angustifolium var. nanum, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and succulent-like leaves. Its flowers attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths, contributing to its ecological role in nighttime ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
