Eucalyptus blakelyi var. irrorata

Eucalyptus blakelyi var. irrorata, commonly known as the silver-leafed malleeforest eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-grey foliage, which consists of narrow, linear leaves with a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically in late winter to early spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils in open woodlands and mallee regions.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
