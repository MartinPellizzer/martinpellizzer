Echinocactus lutens

Echinocactus lutens, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny shape and a dense covering of light-colored spines. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the top of the plant during the spring and summer months.

Echinocactus lutens is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal water and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
