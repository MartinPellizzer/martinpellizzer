Epidendrum caeciliae

Epidendrum caeciliae, commonly known as the Celia's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical and often have a waxy texture, aiding in water retention.

Epidendrum caeciliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, moist conditions.
