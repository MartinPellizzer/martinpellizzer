Epilobium muelleri

Epilobium muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's willowherb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and streambanks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy pink to purple flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a woody base and hairy stems, which help it thrive in its native habitat.

It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
