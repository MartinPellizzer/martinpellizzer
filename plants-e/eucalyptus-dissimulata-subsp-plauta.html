Eucalyptus dissimulata subsp. plauta

Eucalyptus dissimulata subsp.

plauta, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in dry sclerophyll forests and is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a grey-green hue with a distinctive whitish underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

The plant is valued for its aromatic oils and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
