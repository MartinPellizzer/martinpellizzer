Evolvulus villosus

Evolvulus villosus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Evolvulus villosus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.
