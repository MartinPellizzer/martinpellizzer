Eragrostis pectinacea subsp. felzinesii

Eragrostis pectinacea subsp.

felzinesii, commonly known as the velvet false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silver-green coloration.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
