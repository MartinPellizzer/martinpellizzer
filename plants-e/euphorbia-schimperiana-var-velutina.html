Euphorbia schimperiana var. velutina

Euphorbia schimperiana var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
