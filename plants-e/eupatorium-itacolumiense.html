Eupatorium itacolumiense

Eupatorium itacolumiense, commonly known as the Itacolumi feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and herbal medicine research.
