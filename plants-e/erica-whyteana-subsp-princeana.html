Erica whyteana subsp. princeana

Erica whyteana subsp.

princeana, commonly known as the Prince's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal sand dunes and is adapted to well-drained, acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is often used in coastal gardens for its ornamental value.
