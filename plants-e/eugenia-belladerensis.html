Eugenia belladerensis

Eugenia belladerensis, commonly known as the belladonna eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
