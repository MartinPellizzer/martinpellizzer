Embothrium lanceolatum

Embothrium lanceolatum, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Embothrium lanceolatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
