Eragrostis lurida subsp. contracta

Eragrostis lurida subsp.

contracta, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America. It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
