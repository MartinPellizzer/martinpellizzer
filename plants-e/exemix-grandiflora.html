Exemix grandiflora

Exemix grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora emerald vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lush, trailing foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to climb or trail.

However, it requires careful management to prevent it from becoming invasive in non-native habitats.
