Erica placentiflora

Erica placentiflora, commonly known as the placenta heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions.

Erica placentiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
