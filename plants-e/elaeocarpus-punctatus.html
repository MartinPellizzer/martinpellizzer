Elaeocarpus punctatus

Elaeocarpus punctatus, commonly known as the punctate elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a distinctive dotted pattern on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like to small tree-like, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
