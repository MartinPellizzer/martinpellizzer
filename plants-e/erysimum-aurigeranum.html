Erysimum aurigeranum

Erysimum aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden mustache, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often resembling a mustache in shape. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are basal and linear, while the stems are upright and branching.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and ease of growth.
