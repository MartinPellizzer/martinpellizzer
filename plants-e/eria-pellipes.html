Eria pellipes

Eria pellipes, commonly known as the small-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, typically blooming during the summer months. Eria pellipes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a unique species within the Orchidaceae family.
