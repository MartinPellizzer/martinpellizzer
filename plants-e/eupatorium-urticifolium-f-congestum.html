Eupatorium urticifolium f. congestum

Eupatorium urticifolium f. congestum, commonly known as the Texas feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are usually white or pale purple.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
