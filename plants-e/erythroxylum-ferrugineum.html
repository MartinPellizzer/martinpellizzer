Erythroxylum ferrugineum

Erythroxylum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-leaf erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, green leaves with a reddish-brown underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, red berries.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure.
