Endespermum

Endespermum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the sunlight. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its endosperm, which is rich in nutrients and serves as a food source for various desert animals. Endespermum thrives in extreme conditions, adapting to high temperatures and low water availability.

Its reproductive cycle is closely tied to seasonal rainfall, making it a rare and fascinating species in botanical studies.
