Eurya nitida var. rigida

Eurya nitida var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid waxleaf eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and often covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by small, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in suitable climates.
