Erythrina verna

Erythrina verna, commonly known as the early flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The flowers are typically bright red or orange, and they bloom in early spring, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It is well adapted to poor soils and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
