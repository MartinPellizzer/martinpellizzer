Ervum monanthos

Ervum monanthos, commonly known as the single-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

Ervum monanthos is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.
