Eragrostis ciliaris var. laxa

Eragrostis ciliaris var. laxa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, meadows, and grasslands. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, giving it a feathery appearance.

This variety is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
