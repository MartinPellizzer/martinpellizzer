Erythroxylum exaltatum var. ellipticum

Erythroxylum exaltatum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves contain alkaloids, including cocaine, making it significant in both botanical and pharmacological studies.

It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.
