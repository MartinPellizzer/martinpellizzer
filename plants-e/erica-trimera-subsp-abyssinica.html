Erica trimera subsp. abyssinica

Erica trimera subsp.

abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian heath, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils.

The plant features small, dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
