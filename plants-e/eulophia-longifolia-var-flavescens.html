Eulophia longifolia var. flavescens

Eulophia longifolia var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden-flowered eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often epiphytic on trees. Its floral structure includes long, slender petals and a distinctive, spurred lip.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
