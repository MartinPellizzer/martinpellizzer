Erythraea chilensis

Erythraea chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean erythraea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural reserves.
