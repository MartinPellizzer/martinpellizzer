Eugenia rufescens

Eugenia rufescens, commonly known as the red eucalyptus or rufescens guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some applications in local herbal remedies.
