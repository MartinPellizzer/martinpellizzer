Encelia sanguinea

Encelia sanguinea, commonly known as the red globe mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Encelia sanguinea is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species in desert landscapes.
