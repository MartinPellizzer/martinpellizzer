Echinops dahuricus

Echinops dahuricus, commonly known as the Russian thistle or goatweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are blue-gray and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Echinops dahuricus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
