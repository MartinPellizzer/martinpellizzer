Echinochloa holubii

Echinochloa holubii, commonly known as Holub's barnyardgrass, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can become a weed in agricultural fields.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its distinctive morphology and growth patterns.
