Excoecaria affinis

Excoecaria affinis, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by green, fleshy fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
