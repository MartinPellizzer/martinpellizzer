Eupatorium sachalinense f. aureoreticulatum

Eupatorium sachalinense f. aureoreticulatum, commonly known as the golden-veined eupatorium, is a variant of the perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow veins on its lance-shaped leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage in shaded gardens.
