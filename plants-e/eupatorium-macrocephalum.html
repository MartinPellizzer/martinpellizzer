Eupatorium macrocephalum

Eupatorium macrocephalum, commonly known as roughhead thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, showy composite flowers. The plant produces white to pale purple flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, giving it its common name.

Eupatorium macrocephalum is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodlands.
