Erica cinerascens

Erica cinerascens, commonly known as the silver heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pale pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica cinerascens is well adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
