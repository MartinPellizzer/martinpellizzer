Eragrostis lehmanniana

Eragrostis lehmanniana, commonly known as Lehmann's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in arid landscapes.
