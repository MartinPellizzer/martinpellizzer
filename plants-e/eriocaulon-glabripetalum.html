Eriocaulon glabripetalum

Eriocaulon glabripetalum, commonly known as the smooth-lip watergrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds and rivers.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
