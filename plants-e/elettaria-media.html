Elettaria media

Elettaria media, commonly known as cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, greenish-yellow rhizomes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds. The primary commercial product is the dried, aromatic seed, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Its essential oil and compounds are valued for their flavoring and medicinal properties.
