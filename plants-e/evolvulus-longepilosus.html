Evolvulus longepilosus

Evolvulus longepilosus, commonly known as the long-hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
