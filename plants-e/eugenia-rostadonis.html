Eugenia rostadonis

Eugenia rostadonis, commonly known as the Rostad's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring dense, dark green foliage with glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture, while its roots are fibrous and well-developed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
