Echinopsis paraguayensis

Echinopsis paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay star cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Echinopsis paraguayensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
