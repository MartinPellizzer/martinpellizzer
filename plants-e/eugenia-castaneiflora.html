Eugenia castaneiflora

Eugenia castaneiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits with a sweet and tart flavor. Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
