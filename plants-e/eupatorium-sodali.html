Eupatorium sodali

Eupatorium sodali, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
