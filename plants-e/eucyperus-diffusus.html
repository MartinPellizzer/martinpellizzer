Eucyperus diffusus

Eucyperus diffusus, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Southeast Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Eucyperus diffusus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small aquatic organisms.
