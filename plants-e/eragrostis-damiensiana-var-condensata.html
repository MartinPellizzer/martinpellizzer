Eragrostis damiensiana var. condensata

Eragrostis damiensiana var. condensata, commonly known as the dense false brome, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control in dry environments.
