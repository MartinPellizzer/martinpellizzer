Eufragia

Eufragia, commonly known as the "Cape jasmine," is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants are typically shrubs or small trees with woody stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The leaves are usually simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically white or yellow and are borne in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Eufragia species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their beauty.
