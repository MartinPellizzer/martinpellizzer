Erica × arendsiana

Erica × arendsiana, commonly known as the Cape heath or coastal heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and profuse flowering. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Erica × arendsiana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often used in coastal gardens due to its salt tolerance.
