Euphlebium rhodochilum

Euphlebium rhodochilum, commonly known as the red-lip fern, is a unique species within the fern family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glossy, reddish-brown rhizomes and its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the undersides of the fronds.

Euphlebium rhodochilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
