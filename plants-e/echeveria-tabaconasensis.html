Echeveria tabaconasensis

Echeveria tabaconasensis, commonly known as the Tabaconesensis Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
