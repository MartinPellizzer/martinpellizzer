Edraianthus caudatus

Edraianthus caudatus, commonly known as the trailing bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant has slender, trailing stems with small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
