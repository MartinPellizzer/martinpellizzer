Euphorbia calcicola

Euphorbia calcicola, commonly known as the limestone spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

Euphorbia calcicola is drought-tolerant and is adapted to full sun exposure.
