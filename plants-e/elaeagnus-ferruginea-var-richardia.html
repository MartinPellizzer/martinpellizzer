Elaeagnus ferruginea var. richardia

Elaeagnus ferruginea var. richardia, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or rocky habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it an important pioneer species in disturbed environments.
