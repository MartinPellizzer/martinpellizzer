Epidendrum polystachyoides

Epidendrum polystachyoides, commonly known as the Christmas orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that aids in pollination.

Epidendrum polystachyoides is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation.
