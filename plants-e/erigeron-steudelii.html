Erigeron steudelii

Erigeron steudelii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with blue to purple petals and white centers, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are opposite and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its foliage and flowers attract beneficial insects, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
