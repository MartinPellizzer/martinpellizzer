Ecbolium periplocifolium

Ecbolium periplocifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf ecbolium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies due to its role in supporting local biodiversity.

It is also of interest in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
