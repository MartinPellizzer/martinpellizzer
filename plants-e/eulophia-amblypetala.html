Eulophia amblypetala

Eulophia amblypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia amblypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
