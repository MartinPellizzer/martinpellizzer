Epicranthes hexarhopalon

Epicranthes hexarhopalon, commonly known as the six-winged orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has slender, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption from the moist, shaded environments it inhabits.

The orchid's unique floral structure and pollination mechanisms make it an important species in its ecosystem.
