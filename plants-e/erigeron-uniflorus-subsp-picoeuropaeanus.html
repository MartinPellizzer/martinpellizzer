Erigeron uniflorus subsp. picoeuropaeanus

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

picoeuropaeanus, commonly known as the European blueweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has fine, hairy leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
