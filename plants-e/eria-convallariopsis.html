Eria convallariopsis

Eria convallariopsis, commonly known as the miniature ivy orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, vine-like growth habit and produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to conserve water.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
