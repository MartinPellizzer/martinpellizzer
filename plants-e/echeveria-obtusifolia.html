Echeveria obtusifolia

Echeveria obtusifolia, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces vibrant purple or blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
