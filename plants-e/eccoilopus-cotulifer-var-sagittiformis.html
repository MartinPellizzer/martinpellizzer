Eccoilopus cotulifer var. sagittiformis

Eccoilopus cotulifer var. sagittiformis, commonly known as the arrowhead ivy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arrow-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, attractive ground cover.
