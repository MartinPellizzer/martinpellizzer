Echinocactus microspermus var. erythrantha

Echinocactus microspermus var. erythrantha, commonly known as the red-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a reddish tinge. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
