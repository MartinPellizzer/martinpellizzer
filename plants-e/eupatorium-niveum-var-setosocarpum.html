Eupatorium niveum var. setosocarpum

Eupatorium niveum var. setosocarpum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its stems are hairy and often branched, and it thrives in moist, woodland habitats.

The species is known for its medicinal uses and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
