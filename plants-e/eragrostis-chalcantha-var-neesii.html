Eragrostis chalcantha var. neesii

Eragrostis chalcantha var. neesii, commonly known as bluegrass or Nees' bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
