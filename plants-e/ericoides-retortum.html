Ericoides retortum

Ericoides retortum, commonly known as the twisted heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericoides retortum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to coastal and inland environments with mild climates.
