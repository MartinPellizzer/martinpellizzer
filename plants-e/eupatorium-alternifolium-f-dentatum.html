Eupatorium alternifolium f. dentatum

Eupatorium alternifolium f. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed eupatorium, is a cultivar of the species Eupatorium alternifolium.

It is characterized by its distinctive toothed or serrated leaf margins, which give it its specific epithet "dentatum.". The plant typically features upright, branching stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue. It blooms with small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
