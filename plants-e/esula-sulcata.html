Esula sulcata

Esula sulcata, commonly known as the African violet, is a tropical plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soil and thrives in indirect sunlight. Its leaves are usually dark green with a waxy texture, and the flowers come in a variety of colors including purple, pink, and white.

Esula sulcata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
