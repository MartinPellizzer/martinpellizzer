Eupatorium amygdalinum f. serratum

Eupatorium amygdalinum f. serratum, commonly known as the serrated false dragonhead, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. It has a creeping rhizome and can form dense mats, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
