Ericoides tenuifolium

Ericoides tenuifolium, commonly known as the slender-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters around its stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Ericoides tenuifolium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
