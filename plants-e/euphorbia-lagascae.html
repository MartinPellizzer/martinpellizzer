Euphorbia lagascae

Euphorbia lagascae, commonly known as the Canary Island spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems.

Euphorbia lagascae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
