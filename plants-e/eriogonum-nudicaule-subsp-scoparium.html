Eriogonum nudicaule subsp. scoparium

Eriogonum nudicaule subsp.

scoparium, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal leaves and erect, branched stems that bear small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achene.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
