Eriobotrya deflexa f. koshunensis

Eriobotrya deflexa f. koshunensis, commonly known as the Koshunensis loquat, is a botanical variant of the loquat species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
