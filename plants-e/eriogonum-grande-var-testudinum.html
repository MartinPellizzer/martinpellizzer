Eriogonum grande var. testudinum

Eriogonum grande var. testudinum, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
