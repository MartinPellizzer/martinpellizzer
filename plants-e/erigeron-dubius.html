Erigeron dubius

Erigeron dubius, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
