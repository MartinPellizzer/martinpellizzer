Echeveria glauca

Echeveria glauca, commonly known as Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, violet-pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria glauca is popular as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
