Ericoides solandrianum

Ericoides solandrianum, commonly known as the Solander's heath, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils in forest understories or on rocky slopes.

Ericoides solandrianum is an important component of New Zealand's native flora and is often found in conservation areas.
