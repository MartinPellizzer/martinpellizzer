Eupatorium critoniforme

Eupatorium critoniforme, commonly known as the yellow woodruff, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

Eupatorium critoniforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for various insects and birds.
