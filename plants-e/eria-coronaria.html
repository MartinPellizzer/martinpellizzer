Eria coronaria

Eria coronaria, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often used for soil stabilization due to its extensive spreading habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a common ornamental and forage plant.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
