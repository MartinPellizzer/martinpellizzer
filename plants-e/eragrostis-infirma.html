Eragrostis infirma

Eragrostis infirma, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a pointed tip. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Eragrostis infirma is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it resilient to drought and poor soil conditions.
