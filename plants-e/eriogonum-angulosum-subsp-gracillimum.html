Eriogonum angulosum subsp. gracillimum

Eriogonum angulosum subsp.

gracillimum, commonly known as the slender leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a herbaceous perennial that produces seeds in early summer.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
