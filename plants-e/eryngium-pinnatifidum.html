Eryngium pinnatifidum

Eryngium pinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has feathery, pinnately divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eryngium pinnatifidum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
