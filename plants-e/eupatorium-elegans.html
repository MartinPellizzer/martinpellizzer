Eupatorium elegans

Eupatorium elegans, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, preferring moist to wet soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
