Erythrina hypaphorus

Erythrina hypaphorus, commonly known as the flame tree or red torchwood, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.

Erythrina hypaphorus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
