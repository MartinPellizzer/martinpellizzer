Epilobium coloratum f. umbrosum

Epilobium coloratum f. umbrosum, commonly known as the shadowy willow herb, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive purple to pink flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

It is a variant of the common willow herb, known for its preference for shaded, moist environments. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
