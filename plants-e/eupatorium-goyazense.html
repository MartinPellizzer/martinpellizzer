Eupatorium goyazense

Eupatorium goyazense, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium goyazense is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
