Elephantorrhiza goetzei

Elephantorrhiza goetzei, commonly known as the African elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
