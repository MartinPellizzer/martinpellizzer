Erysimum tuteliae subsp. tuteliae

Erysimum tuteliae subsp.

tuteliae, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with basal leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers are characterized by their showy, four-petaled structure and are pollinated by bees and other insects.
