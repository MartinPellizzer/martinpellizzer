Erisma panamense

Erisma panamense, commonly known as the Panamanian erisma, is a flowering plant native to Central America, particularly Panama.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shady areas and features small, delicate flowers in shades of white and purple. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Erisma panamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
