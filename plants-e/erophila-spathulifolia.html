Erophila spathulifolia

Erophila spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved erophila, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats, showcasing adaptability to saline environments. Its leaves are broad and spatulate, contributing to its common name.

Erophila spathulifolia is often found in dune systems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils.
