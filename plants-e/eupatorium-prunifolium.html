Eupatorium prunifolium

Eupatorium prunifolium, commonly known as sweet pepperweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium prunifolium is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.
