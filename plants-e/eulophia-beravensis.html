Eulophia beravensis

Eulophia beravensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers with intricate patterns. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of its native habitat.

Eulophia beravensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
