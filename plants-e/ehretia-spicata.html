Ehretia spicata

Ehretia spicata, commonly known as the spiky ehretia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
