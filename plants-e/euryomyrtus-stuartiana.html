Euryomyrtus stuartiana

Euryomyrtus stuartiana, commonly known as Stuart's myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of Western Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and is often found in heathlands. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the winter months. It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
