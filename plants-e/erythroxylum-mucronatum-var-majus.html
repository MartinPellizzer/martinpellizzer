Erythroxylum mucronatum var. majus

Erythroxylum mucronatum var. majus, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a climbing plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, and it is often found growing in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its traditional uses in herbal medicine.
