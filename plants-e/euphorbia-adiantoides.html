Euphorbia adiantoides

Euphorbia adiantoides, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is a characteristic structure of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
