Echinocactus falconeri

Echinocactus falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or summer. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
