Eria thomsenii

Eria thomsenii, commonly known as the Tomson's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender rhizome with a few leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its flowers are usually clustered in a raceme and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eria thomsenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
