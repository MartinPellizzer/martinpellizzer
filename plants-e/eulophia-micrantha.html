Eulophia micrantha

Eulophia micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features erect, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which are typically fragrant and attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Eulophia micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
