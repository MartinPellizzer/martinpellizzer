Eucalyptus grossifolia

Eucalyptus grossifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Eucalyptus grossifolia is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its aromatic oils have been used in traditional medicine and for their antiseptic properties.
