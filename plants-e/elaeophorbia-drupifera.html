Elaeophorbia drupifera

Elaeophorbia drupifera, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, which have been used traditionally in some cultures.

Elaeophorbia drupifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential applications in medicinal research.
