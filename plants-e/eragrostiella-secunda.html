Eragrostiella secunda

Eragrostiella secunda, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Eragrostiella secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
