Eriocaulon ratnagiricum

Eriocaulon ratnagiricum, commonly known as the Ratnagiri spikegrass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring shallow, still or slow-moving waters. Its leaves are narrow and ribbon-like, often floating on the water surface or submerged.

Eriocaulon ratnagiricum plays a role in maintaining water quality and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
