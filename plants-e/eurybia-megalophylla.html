Eurybia megalophylla

Eurybia megalophylla, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has lance-shaped, alternate leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with hairy surfaces. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eurybia megalophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
