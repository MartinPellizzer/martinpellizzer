Eugenia toppingii

Eugenia toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in humid, subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically pink or white, and they bloom in clusters during the warmer months.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
