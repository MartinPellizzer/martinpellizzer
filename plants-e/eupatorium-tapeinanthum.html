Eupatorium tapeinanthum

Eupatorium tapeinanthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium tapeinanthum is valued in gardens for its attractive foliage and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
