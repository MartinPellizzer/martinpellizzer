Echinopsis grandis

Echinopsis grandis, commonly known as the giant echinopsis, is a striking cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, spiny stems that can grow up to two meters in height. The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers in shades of yellow and orange, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for collectors and desert gardeners.
