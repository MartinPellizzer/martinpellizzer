Enargea radicans

Enargea radicans, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a woody vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, often with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and can spread along the ground, allowing it to form dense mats.

Enargea radicans is often used in landscaping for its ability to cover walls, trellises, and ground surfaces effectively.
