Ericoides lactiflorum

Ericoides lactiflorum, commonly known as the milkflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or woodland environments. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, contributing to its adaptability in harsh conditions.

Ericoides lactiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
