Eragrostis linkii

Eragrostis linkii, commonly known as link's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis linkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
