Emerus tetrapterus

Emerus tetrapterus, commonly known as the four-winged sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grassy leaves and small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
