Euphorbia quadrialata

Euphorbia quadrialata, commonly known as the four-armed spurges, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, branched stems that resemble arms, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Euphorbia quadrialata is drought-tolerant and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
