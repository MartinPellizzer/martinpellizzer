Eragrostis burchellii

Eragrostis burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a common species in semi-arid regions.

Eragrostis burchellii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
