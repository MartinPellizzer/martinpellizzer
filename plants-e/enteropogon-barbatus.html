Enteropogon barbatus

Enteropogon barbatus, commonly known as the feathered grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, reddish-brown inflorescences that resemble a feathery plume. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
