Epipactis helleborine subsp. lusitanica

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southwestern Europe. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in calcareous soils. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots, aiding in pollination by specific insects.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and evolutionary significance.
