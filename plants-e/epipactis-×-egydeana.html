Epipactis × egydeana

Epipactis × egydeana, commonly known as the hybrid lady's tresses, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a natural hybrid between Epipactis palustris and Epipactis hirta, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and long, slender flower spikes. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
