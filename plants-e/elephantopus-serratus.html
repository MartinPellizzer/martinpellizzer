Elephantopus serratus

Elephantopus serratus, commonly known as the serrated elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, lobed leaves that resemble the feet of an elephant, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
