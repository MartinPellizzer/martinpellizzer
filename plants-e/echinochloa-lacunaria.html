Echinochloa lacunaria

Echinochloa lacunaria, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along waterways.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in cultivated areas, competing with crops for nutrients and sunlight.
