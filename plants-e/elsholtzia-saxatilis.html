Elsholtzia saxatilis

Elsholtzia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. It has a woody base and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Elsholtzia saxatilis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry garden settings.
