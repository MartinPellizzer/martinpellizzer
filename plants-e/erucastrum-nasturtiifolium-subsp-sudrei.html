Erucastrum nasturtiifolium subsp. sudrei

Erucastrum nasturtiifolium subsp.

sudrei, commonly known as the southern bittercress, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, white or pinkish flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

The leaves are usually alternate, lobed, and have a slightly peppery taste due to the presence of glucosinolates. This subspecies is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

It is an annual plant that blooms from spring to early summer.
