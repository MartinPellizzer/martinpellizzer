Euonymus japonicus f. pyramidatus

Euonymus japonicus f. pyramidatus, commonly known as the Japanese spindle tree, is a compact, upright shrub native to Japan.

It features dense, dark green foliage that remains lush throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an ornamental feature. It is often cultivated for its neat, pyramidal shape and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This variety is well-suited for use in rock gardens or as a foundation plant in landscaped settings.
