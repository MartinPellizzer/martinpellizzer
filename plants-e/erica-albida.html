Erica albida

Erica albida, commonly known as the white heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica albida prefers well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
