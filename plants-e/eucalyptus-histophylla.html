Eucalyptus histophylla

Eucalyptus histophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to white in color, which appear in clusters along the branches. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands and heathlands.

Eucalyptus histophylla is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
