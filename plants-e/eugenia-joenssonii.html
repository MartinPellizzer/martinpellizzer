Eugenia joenssonii

Eugenia joenssonii, commonly known as the Java guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia joenssonii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
