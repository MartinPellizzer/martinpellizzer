Elaphoglossum ovalifolium

Elaphoglossum ovalifolium, commonly known as the oval-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically arranged in a loose, upright rosette. The plant features small, spore-producing sorus clusters on the underside of its fronds, which are protected by a kidney-shaped indusium. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and shaded landscapes.
