Eragrostis intermedia var. appressa

Eragrostis intermedia var. appressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, compressed leaves and dense, tufted growth habit, which helps it conserve moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
