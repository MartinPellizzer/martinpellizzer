Erigeron caucasicus subsp. venustus

Erigeron caucasicus subsp.

venustus, commonly known as the beautiful fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The plant features a basal rosette of silvery-gray, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow disk in the center. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
