Erysimum cinerascens

Erysimum cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-gray mustard, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has small, rounded leaves with a grayish hue. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, and it produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.

Erysimum cinerascens is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
