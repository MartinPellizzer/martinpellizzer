Eurybia rugosa

Eurybia rugosa, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a hardy perennial plant native to North America.

It features sturdy, upright stems that can grow up to 2 to 3 feet tall, adorned with bright yellow ray flowers and dark green, rough-textured leaves. The plant is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a resilient choice for gardens. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eurybia rugosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
