Eleutherostemon laxus var. caudatus

Eleutherostemon laxus var. caudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed eleutherostemon, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and elongated, drooping inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
