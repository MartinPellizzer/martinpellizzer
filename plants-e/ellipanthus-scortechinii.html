Ellipanthus scortechinii

Ellipanthus scortechinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
