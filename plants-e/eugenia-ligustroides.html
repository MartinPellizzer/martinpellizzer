Eugenia ligustroides

Eugenia ligustroides, commonly known as the wild mango or Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic, and have a leathery texture with a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.
