Eugenia amblyophylla

Eugenia amblyophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clove, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Its aromatic properties make it a valuable plant in traditional medicine and perfumery.
