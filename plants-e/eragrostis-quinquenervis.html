Eragrostis quinquenervis

Eragrostis quinquenervis, commonly known as the five-veined lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of compact, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Eragrostis quinquenervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
