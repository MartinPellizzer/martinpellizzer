Erica purpurea

Erica purpurea, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters and blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Erica purpurea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
