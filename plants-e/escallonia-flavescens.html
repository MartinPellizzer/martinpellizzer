Escallonia flavescens

Escallonia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Escallonia flavescens is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
