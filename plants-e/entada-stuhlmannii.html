Entada stuhlmannii

Entada stuhlmannii, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain edible seeds. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody shell and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
