Eugenia neoverrucosa

Eugenia neoverrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a rough, granular texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
