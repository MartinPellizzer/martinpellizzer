Erinus maritimus

Erinus maritimus, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in salty, well-drained soils and is often found in dunes and coastal cliffs. Erinus maritimus is a hardy perennial that is well adapted to maritime climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners looking to add a touch of coastal charm to their landscapes.
