Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. laxiflorum

Eriophyllum confertiflorum var. laxiflorum, commonly known as the coastal goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils along cliffs and dunes. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its resilience to coastal conditions.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
