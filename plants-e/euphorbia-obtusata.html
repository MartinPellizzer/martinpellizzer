Euphorbia obtusata

Euphorbia obtusata, commonly known as the blunt euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in small, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
