Eragrostis peruviana var. macranthera

Eragrostis peruviana var. macranthera, commonly known as the Peruvian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically pink to purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
