Erythroxylum bolivianum

Erythroxylum bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that contain seeds, and it is often found in humid, tropical regions.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic values.
