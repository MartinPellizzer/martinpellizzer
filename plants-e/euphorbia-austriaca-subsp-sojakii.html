Euphorbia austriaca subsp. sojakii

Euphorbia austriaca subsp.

sojakii, commonly known as the Austrian spurge, is a perennial plant native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
