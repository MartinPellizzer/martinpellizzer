Elaeocarpus barbulatus var. kinabaluensis

Elaeocarpus barbulatus var. kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
