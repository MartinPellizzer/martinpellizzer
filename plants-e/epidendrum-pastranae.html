Epidendrum pastranae

Epidendrum pastranae, commonly known as the Pastrana's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Epidendrum pastranae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
