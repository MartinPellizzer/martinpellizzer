Entada schinziana

Entada schinziana, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or bean family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The tree can grow up to 25 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters.

The seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
