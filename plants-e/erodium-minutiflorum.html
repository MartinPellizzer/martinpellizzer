Erodium minutiflorum

Erodium minutiflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It features tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it is part of the Geraniaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar structural characteristics.
