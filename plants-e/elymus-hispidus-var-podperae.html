Elymus hispidus var. podperae

Elymus hispidus var. podperae, commonly known as the Podpera wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and stiff, hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, each containing two to three small, awned florets. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
