Eupatorium griseopubescens

Eupatorium griseopubescens, commonly known as gray-hairy boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and reaches heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, grayish-white hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
