Eragrostis condensata

Eragrostis condensata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form tussocks.

Eragrostis condensata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
