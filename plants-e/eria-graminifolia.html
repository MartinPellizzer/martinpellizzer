Eria graminifolia

Eria graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Eria graminifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
