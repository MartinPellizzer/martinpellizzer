Eriosema macrostipulatum

Eriosema macrostipulatum, commonly known as the large-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, rough texture. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often found in grassy areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
