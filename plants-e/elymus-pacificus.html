Elymus pacificus

Elymus pacificus, commonly known as Pacific wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along shorelines and dunes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate harsh coastal conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
