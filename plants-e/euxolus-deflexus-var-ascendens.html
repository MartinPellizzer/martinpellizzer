Euxolus deflexus var. ascendens

Euxolus deflexus var. ascendens, commonly known as the ascending euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a branching stem system with fleshy, ribbed leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which appear during the warmer months. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping in dry climates.
