Encholirium viride

Encholirium viride, commonly known as the green humpback cactus, is a species of cactus native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in green spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Encholirium viride is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
