Ervum cirrhosum

Ervum cirrhosum, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-bearing fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, and the seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods.

Ervum cirrhosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to improve soil fertility.
