Euphorbia ipecacuanhae var. portulacoides

Euphorbia ipecacuanhae var. portulacoides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or ozymandias, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
