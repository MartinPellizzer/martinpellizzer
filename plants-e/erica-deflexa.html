Erica deflexa

Erica deflexa, commonly known as the bent heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Erica deflexa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
