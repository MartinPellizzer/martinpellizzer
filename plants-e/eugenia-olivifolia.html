Eugenia olivifolia

Eugenia olivifolia, commonly known as the oliveleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Eugenia olivifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
