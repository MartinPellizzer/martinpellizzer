Eupatorium rhinanthaceum

Eupatorium rhinanthaceum, commonly known as purpletop, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom from late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium rhinanthaceum plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
