Eupatorium subplumosum

Eupatorium subplumosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
