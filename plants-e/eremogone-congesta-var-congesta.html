Eremogone congesta var. congesta

Eremogone congesta var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered eremogone, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows from a rhizomatous root system, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.
