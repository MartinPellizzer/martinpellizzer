Erigeron compositus var. discoideus

Erigeron compositus var. discoideus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, composite flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
