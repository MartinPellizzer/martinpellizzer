Eremophila resiliens

Eremophila resiliens, commonly known as the resilient emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience.

Eremophila resiliens is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
