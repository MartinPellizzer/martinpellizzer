Echinopsis marsoneri

Echinopsis marsoneri, commonly known as the Sonoran moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are typically pink or red, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Echinopsis marsoneri is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and striking appearance.
