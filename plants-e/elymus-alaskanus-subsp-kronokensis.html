Elymus alaskanus subsp. kronokensis

Elymus alaskanus subsp.

kronokensis, commonly known as Alaska wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

This subspecies thrives in moist to wet environments, such as meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
