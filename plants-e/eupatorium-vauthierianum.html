Eupatorium vauthierianum

Eupatorium vauthierianum, commonly known as the Vauthier's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and features showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
