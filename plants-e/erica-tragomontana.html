Erica tragomontana

Erica tragomontana, commonly known as the Montane Heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Erica tragomontana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
