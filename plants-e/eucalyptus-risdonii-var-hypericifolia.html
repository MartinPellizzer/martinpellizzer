Eucalyptus risdonii var. hypericifolia

Eucalyptus risdonii var. hypericifolia, commonly known as the Risdons bloodwood, is a rare and distinctive species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often displays a reddish or brownish hue, giving it its common name. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a greyish-green upper surface with a whitish underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by woody capsules.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and unique member of the eucalyptus family.
