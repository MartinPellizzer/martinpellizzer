Erythrostemon exostemma subsp. exostemma

Erythrostemon exostemma subsp.

exostemma, commonly known as the redstem or redstem bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
