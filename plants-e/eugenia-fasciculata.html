Eugenia fasciculata

Eugenia fasciculata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia fasciculata*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent when crushed.
