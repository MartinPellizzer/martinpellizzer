Episcia decurrens

Episcia decurrens, commonly known as the waterfall plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Episcia decurrens thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil for optimal growth.
