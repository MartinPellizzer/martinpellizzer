Eulophia guineensis var. tisserantii

Eulophia guineensis var. tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical Africa.

It features upright, linear leaves and produces dense clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence is racemose, with flowers that are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
