Echinochloa stagnina

Echinochloa stagnina, commonly known as watergrass, is a grass species that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its seeds are dispersed by water, aiding in its spread across wetland habitats.

Echinochloa stagnina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
