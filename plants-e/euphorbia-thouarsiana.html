Euphorbia thouarsiana

Euphorbia thouarsiana, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique inflorescence called a cyathium, which consists of a cup-shaped structure surrounded by modified leaves. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
