Euphorbia prokhanovii

Euphorbia prokhanovii, commonly known as the Prokhanov's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
