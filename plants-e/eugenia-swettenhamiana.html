Eugenia swettenhamiana

Eugenia swettenhamiana, commonly known as the Swettenham's clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are edible and have a unique, aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Myrtaceae family.
