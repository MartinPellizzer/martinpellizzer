Eriachne vesiculosa

Eriachne vesiculosa, commonly known as the bladder grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with small, pale blue to purple flowers that appear in summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eriachne vesiculosa is typically found in sandy or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
