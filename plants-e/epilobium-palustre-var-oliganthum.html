Epilobium palustre var. oliganthum

Epilobium palustre var. oliganthum, commonly known as bog willow herb, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in temperate regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, often reddish stems that grow from a rhizomatous base. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
