Eupatorium pichinchense

Eupatorium pichinchense, commonly known as the Peruvian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium pichinchense is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Andean medicine for various health purposes.
