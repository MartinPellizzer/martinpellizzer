Eleocharis montana var. nodulosa

Eleocharis montana var. nodulosa, commonly known as the nodular spike-rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its nodular or swollen nodes along the stem, which give it its specific epithet.
