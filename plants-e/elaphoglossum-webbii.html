Elaphoglossum webbii

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi-lobed and have a finely divided, feather-like appearance. Its sporangia are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns.

Elaphoglossum webbii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
