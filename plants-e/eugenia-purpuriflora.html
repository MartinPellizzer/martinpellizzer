Eugenia purpuriflora

Eugenia purpuriflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The flowers are followed by small, round, red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
