Eurybia brachyglossa

Eurybia brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sunflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swales. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinct central vein, and it produces small, clustered flower heads.

Eurybia brachyglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
