Eriodictyon parryi

Eriodictyon parryi, commonly known as parry's yerba santa, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic foliage and yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. Its leaves contain compounds that have been traditionally used by Indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

Eriodictyon parryi plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native range.
