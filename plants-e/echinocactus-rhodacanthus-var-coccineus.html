Echinocactus rhodacanthus var. coccineus

Echinocactus rhodacanthus var. coccineus, commonly known as the red-horned cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, red-brown spines that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
