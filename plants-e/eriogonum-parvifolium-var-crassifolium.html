Eriogonum parvifolium var. crassifolium

Eriogonum parvifolium var. crassifolium, commonly known as the thickleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
