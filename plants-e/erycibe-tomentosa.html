Erycibe tomentosa

Erycibe tomentosa, commonly known as the wooly erycibe, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Its fruit is a fleshy, spiny capsule that contains several seeds.

Erycibe tomentosa is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
