Eleocharis tenuis var. tenuis

Eleocharis tenuis var. tenuis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, densely packed spikelets that are typically borne in clusters. This variety is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has no true leaves but possesses reduced, scale-like structures that are arranged along the stem.

Its reproductive structures are primarily composed of tiny flowers that develop into spikelets, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.
