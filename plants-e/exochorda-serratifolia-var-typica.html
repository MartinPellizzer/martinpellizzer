Exochorda serratifolia var. typica

Exochorda serratifolia var. typica, commonly known as the serrated spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features arching stems with small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display and hardy nature.
