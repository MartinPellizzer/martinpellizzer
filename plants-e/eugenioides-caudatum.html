Eugenioides caudatum

Eugenioides caudatum, commonly known as the long-tailed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping inflorescences and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
