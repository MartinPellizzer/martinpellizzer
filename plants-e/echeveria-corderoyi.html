Echeveria corderoyi

Echeveria corderoyi, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a succulent native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves with a velvety texture and a striking red or purple edge. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
