Eupatorium polycladum

Eupatorium polycladum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodlands. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium polycladum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
