Echites bicolor

Echites bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow and green bi-colored leaves, which are opposite and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Echites bicolor is a perennial herb that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
