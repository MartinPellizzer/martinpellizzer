Ehrharta juncea

Ehrharta juncea, commonly known as slender dallisgrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and spikelets that are often compressed and closely arranged. The plant thrives in moist environments, such as wetlands and marshes, and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils. Its growth habit makes it a common weed in agricultural fields and along waterways.

Botanically, it is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for livestock.
