Eleocharis × choseiensis

Eleocharis × choseiensis, commonly known as the hybrid spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common component of marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a wiry texture, and it often forms dense mats that help stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions where it outcompetes native vegetation.
