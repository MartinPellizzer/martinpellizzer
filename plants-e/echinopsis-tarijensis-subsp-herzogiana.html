Echinopsis tarijensis subsp. herzogiana

Echinopsis tarijensis subsp.

herzogiana, commonly known as the Herzog's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Argentina. It is characterized by its globular, ribbed stem and a dense covering of white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces striking yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
