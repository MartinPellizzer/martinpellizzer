Elytranthe polychroa

Elytranthe polychroa, commonly known as the rainbow elly, is a small, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Elytranthe polychroa is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
