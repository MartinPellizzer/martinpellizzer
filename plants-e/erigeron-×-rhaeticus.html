Erigeron × rhaeticus

Erigeron × rhaeticus, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant is known for its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

Erigeron × rhaeticus is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
