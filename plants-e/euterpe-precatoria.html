Euterpe precatoria

Euterpe precatoria, commonly known as the red-berried palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, red fruits that are edible and often consumed by local communities. Euterpe precatoria thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and agricultural purposes.
