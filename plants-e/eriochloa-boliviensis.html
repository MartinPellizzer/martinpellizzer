Eriochloa boliviensis

Eriochloa boliviensis, commonly known as Bolivian grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Eriochloa boliviensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
