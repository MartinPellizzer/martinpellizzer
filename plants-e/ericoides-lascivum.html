Ericoides lascivum

Ericoides lascivum, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. It produces small, leathery fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Ericoides lascivum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
