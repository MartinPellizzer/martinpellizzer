Euphorbia ebracteolata f. coreana

Euphorbia ebracteolata f. coreana, commonly known as the Korean euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Korea.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
