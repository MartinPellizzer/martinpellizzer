Erysimum thyrsoideum subsp. ekicii

Erysimum thyrsoideum subsp.

ekicii, commonly known as the Turkish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Turkey. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
