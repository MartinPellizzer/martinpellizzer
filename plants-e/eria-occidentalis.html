Eria occidentalis

Eria occidentalis, commonly known as the western eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid-like flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It has strap-shaped leaves and is often found growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
