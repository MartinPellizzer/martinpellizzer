Epilobium × ceretanicum

Epilobium × ceretanicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a hybrid plant species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to purple petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Epilobium × ceretanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
