Epilobium brunnescens subsp. brunnescens

Epilobium brunnescens subsp.

brunnescens, commonly known as the brown willow-herb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as those found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshes.

The plant features erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are pink to reddish, clustered in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
