Elaeocarpus simaluensis

Elaeocarpus simaluensis, commonly known as the Maluku elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
