Eulophia perrieri

Eulophia perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and a pseudobulb system. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia perrieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
