Euosma pusilla

Euosma pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf euosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and cinnamon trees, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Euosma pusilla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
