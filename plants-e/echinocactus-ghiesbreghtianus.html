Echinocactus ghiesbreghtianus

Echinocactus ghiesbreghtianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered with dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
