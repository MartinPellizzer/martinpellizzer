Ellipanthus tomentosus subsp. kingii

Ellipanthus tomentosus subsp.

kingii, commonly known as King's woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its densely hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
