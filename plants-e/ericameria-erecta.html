Ericameria erecta

Ericameria erecta, commonly known as narrowleaf ericameria, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ericameria erecta thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
