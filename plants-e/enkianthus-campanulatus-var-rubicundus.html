Enkianthus campanulatus var. rubicundus

Enkianthus campanulatus var. rubicundus, commonly known as the red-flowered bell-enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from pink to deep red, blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
