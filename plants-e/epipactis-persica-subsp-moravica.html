Epipactis persica subsp. moravica

Epipactis persica subsp.

moravica, commonly known as the Moravian Persian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-shaped leaves and produces striking, fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive red lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.

Its subspecific classification highlights its distinct ecological and morphological traits compared to other members of the Epipactis genus.
