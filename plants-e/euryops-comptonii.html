Euryops comptonii

Euryops comptonii, commonly known as the Compton's ironbark, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance through its thick, hairy leaves. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Euryops comptonii is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
