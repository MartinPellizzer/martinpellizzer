Eriocaulon cristatum var. maackii

Eriocaulon cristatum var. maackii, commonly known as the Maack's spikegrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, and it blooms during the late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.
