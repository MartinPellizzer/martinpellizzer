Eugenia scaphephylla

Eugenia scaphephylla, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous areas with rich, well-drained soil. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are borne in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Eugenia scaphephylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
