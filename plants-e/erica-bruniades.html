Erica bruniades

Erica bruniades, commonly known as the Bruniad's heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica bruniades thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
