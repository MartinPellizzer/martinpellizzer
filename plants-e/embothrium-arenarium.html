Embothrium arenarium

Embothrium arenarium, commonly known as the sand embothrium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Embothrium arenarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
