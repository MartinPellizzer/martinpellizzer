Epiphyllum hookeri

Epiphyllum hookeri, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a fascinating epiphytic cactus native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. These flowers are ephemeral, lasting only a few hours before closing, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

Epiphyllum hookeri is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
