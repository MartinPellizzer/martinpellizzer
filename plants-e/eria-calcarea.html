Eria calcarea

Eria calcarea, commonly known as the limestone orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, erect stem and a single, showy flower that typically blooms in spring. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in shaded, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Eria calcarea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
