Elaeagnus pungens subsp. reflexa

Elaeagnus pungens subsp.

reflexa, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to western North America. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring.

The plant produces small, black berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common invasive species in certain regions.
