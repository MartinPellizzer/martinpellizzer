Euodia brachiata

Euodia brachiata, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and fragrant leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Euodia brachiata is also cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
