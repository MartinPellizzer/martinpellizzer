Euphorbia brassii

Euphorbia brassii, commonly known as the yellow-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia brassii is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.
