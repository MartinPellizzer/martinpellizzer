Edraianthus niveus

Edraianthus niveus, commonly known as the snow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving them a silvery appearance.

Edraianthus niveus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
