Euonymus occidentalis var. occidentalis

Euonymus occidentalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, with smooth or slightly toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile ornamental plant.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
