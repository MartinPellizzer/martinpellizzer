Eleocharis atropurpurea var. zanardinii

Eleocharis atropurpurea var. zanardinii, commonly known as the purple spike-rush, is a sedge species native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that often display a purplish hue. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its reproductive structures are typically small and clustered, contributing to its ability to propagate in wetland ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing aquatic habitats.
