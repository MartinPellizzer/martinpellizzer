Eucalyptus apodophylla nothosubsp. provecta

Eucalyptus apodophylla nothosubsp.

provecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a subspecies of the eucalyptus genus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance.

The flowers are usually arranged in umbels and are typically white or pale yellow, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and mallee ecosystems.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native fauna.
