Epilobium adenocaulon var. parishii

Epilobium adenocaulon var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks and moist meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
