Eucalyptus melanoxylon

Eucalyptus melanoxylon, commonly known as the messmate stringy gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark grey to black bark that peels in long, fibrous strips. The tree has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly curved and have a rough, textured surface. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale or creamy white in color.

The wood of Eucalyptus melanoxylon is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
