Eremophila glabra subsp. glabra

Eremophila glabra subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dryland gardening. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
