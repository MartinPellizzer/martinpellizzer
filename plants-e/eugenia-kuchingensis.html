Eugenia kuchingensis

Eugenia kuchingensis, commonly known as the Kuching hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Eugenia kuchingensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
