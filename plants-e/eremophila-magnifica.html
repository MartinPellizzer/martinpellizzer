Eremophila magnifica

Eremophila magnifica, commonly known as the pink ghost pipeflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to harsh climates.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
