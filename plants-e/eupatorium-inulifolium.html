Eupatorium inulifolium

Eupatorium inulifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
