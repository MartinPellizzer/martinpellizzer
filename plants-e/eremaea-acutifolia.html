Eremaea acutifolia

Eremaea acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved eremaea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically from spring to early summer. It is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.

Eremaea acutifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
