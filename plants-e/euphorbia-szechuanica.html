Euphorbia szechuanica

Euphorbia szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan spurge, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. The plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be toxic. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
