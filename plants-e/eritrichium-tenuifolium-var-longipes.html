Eritrichium tenuifolium var. longipes

Eritrichium tenuifolium var. longipes, commonly known as the slender-leafed mouse-eared cress, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stem. The leaves are narrow and elongated, often with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or alpine soils and is adapted to cool, moist environments.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
