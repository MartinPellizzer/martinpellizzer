Epicharis hyacinthodora

Epicharis hyacinthodora, commonly known as the blue hyacinth orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue and purple floral petals. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Epicharis hyacinthodora is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
