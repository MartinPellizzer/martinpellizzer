Empogona coriacea

Empogona coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink or red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
