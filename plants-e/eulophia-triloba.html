Eulophia triloba

Eulophia triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings. The plant features long, wiry stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia triloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, tropical climates.
