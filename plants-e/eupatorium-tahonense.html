Eupatorium tahonense

Eupatorium tahonense, commonly known as Tahon's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
