Epidendrum formosum

Epidendrum formosum, commonly known as the Formosa orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, branching stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum formosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
