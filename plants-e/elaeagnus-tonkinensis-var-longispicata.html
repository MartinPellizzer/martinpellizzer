Elaeagnus tonkinensis var. longispicata

Elaeagnus tonkinensis var. longispicata, commonly known as the longspine silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
