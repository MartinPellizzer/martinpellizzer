Eragrostis australiensis

Eragrostis australiensis, commonly known as the Australian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis australiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
