Electra cuneifolia

Electra cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf electra, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Electra cuneifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
