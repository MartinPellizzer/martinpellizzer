Eragrostis patentipilosa

Eragrostis patentipilosa, commonly known as the broad-haired lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, spreading stems. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis patentipilosa is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
