Euphorbia bracei

Euphorbia bracei, commonly known as the Brazilian euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Euphorbia bracei is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, making it drought-tolerant.
