Eurya glabra

Eurya glabra, commonly known as the smooth eurya, is a flowering plant species native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the autumn.

Eurya glabra is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
