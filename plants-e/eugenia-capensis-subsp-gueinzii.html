Eugenia capensis subsp. gueinzii

Eugenia capensis subsp.

gueinzii, commonly known as the Cape cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are a source of food for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
