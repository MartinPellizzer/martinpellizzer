Eryngium isauricum

Eryngium isauricum, commonly known as the Isaurian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flowers and gray-green, hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its structure resembles that of other thistle species, with bracts surrounding the flower cluster.

Eryngium isauricum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
