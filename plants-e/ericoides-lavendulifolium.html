Ericoides lavendulifolium

Ericoides lavendulifolium, commonly known as the lavenderleaf ericoides, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a distinctive lavender-colored hue, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or rocky areas. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
