Erysimum brayoides

Erysimum brayoides, commonly known as the braya mustard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial species that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are pinnately divided and bright green, with small, yellow flowers clustered in dense, terminal racemes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open meadows and along roadsides.

Erysimum brayoides has lanceolate leaves and is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many mustard species.
