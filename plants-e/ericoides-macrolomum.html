Ericoides macrolomum

Ericoides macrolomum, commonly known as the broad-leaf heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant typically has small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Ericoides macrolomum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
