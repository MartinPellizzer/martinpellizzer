Echinopsis leucantha var. brasiliensis

Echinopsis leucantha var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian moon cactus, is a cacti species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in both indoor and outdoor settings.
