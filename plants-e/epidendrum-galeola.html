Epidendrum galeola

Epidendrum galeola, commonly known as the yellow lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green flowers, which resemble a lady's slipper in appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of white or gray pubescence.

Epidendrum galeola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
