Eurya gynandra

Eurya gynandra, commonly known as the Chinese holly or the sacred holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Eurya gynandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in various regions.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
