Eugenia guayavillo

Eugenia guayavillo, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Eugenia guayavillo is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
