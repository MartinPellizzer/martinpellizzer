Euchiton forsteri

Euchiton forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's echinocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented structure. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
