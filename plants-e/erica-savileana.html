Erica savileana

Erica savileana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a species of heath plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica savileana is often used in rock gardens and as a drought-tolerant ornamental plant.
