Eupatorium mendax

Eupatorium mendax, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium mendax is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
