Eucalyptus crebra var. macrocarpa

Eucalyptus crebra var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and fire.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
