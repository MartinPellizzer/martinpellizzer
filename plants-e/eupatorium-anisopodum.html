Eupatorium anisopodum

Eupatorium anisopodum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and swamps. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium anisopodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
