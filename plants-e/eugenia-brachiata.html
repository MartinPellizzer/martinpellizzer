Eugenia brachiata

Eugenia brachiata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Eugenia brachiata is often found in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests.
