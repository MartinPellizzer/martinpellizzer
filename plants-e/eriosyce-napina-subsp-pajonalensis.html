Eriosyce napina subsp. pajonalensis

Eriosyce napina subsp.

pajonalensis, commonly known as the Pajonal cholla, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in tubercles.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its adaptations include thick, fleshy stems for water storage and spines for protection.

This subspecies is well-suited to harsh, sun-exposed environments and is often found in rocky soils.
