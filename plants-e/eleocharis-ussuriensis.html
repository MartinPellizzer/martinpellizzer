Eleocharis ussuriensis

Eleocharis ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often densely clustered, with small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis ussuriensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
