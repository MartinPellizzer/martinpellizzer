Erythroxylum ambiguum var. hymenophyllum

Erythroxylum ambiguum var. hymenophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf copaiba, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often silvery or grayish in appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its aromatic resins and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
