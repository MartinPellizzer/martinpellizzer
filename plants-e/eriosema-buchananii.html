Eriosema buchananii

Eriosema buchananii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed areas, thriving in full sun. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in dry to semi-arid environments.

Eriosema buchananii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
