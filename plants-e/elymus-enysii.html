Elymus enysii

Elymus enysii, commonly known as Enys' grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. Elymus enysii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
