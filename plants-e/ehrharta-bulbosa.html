Ehrharta bulbosa

Ehrharta bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous grass or coastal grass, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, tufted form with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments.

Ehrharta bulbosa is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
