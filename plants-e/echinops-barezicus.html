Echinops barezicus

Echinops barezicus, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Echinops barezicus is often used in xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
