Ericoides salax

Ericoides salax, commonly known as the lax ericoid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. It produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color, which bloom in late spring or early summer.

Ericoides salax is often found in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments.
