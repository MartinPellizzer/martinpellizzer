Elaphoglossum moritzianum

Elaphoglossum moritzianum, commonly known as the Moritz's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Blechnaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bright green and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in structures called sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in montane forests.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
