Erica tristis

Erica tristis, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It is often found in heathlands and coastal areas, where it thrives in harsh, sunny conditions.

Erica tristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult environments.
