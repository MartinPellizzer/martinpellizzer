Eryngium suaveolens

Eryngium suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white to gray bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs.

Eryngium suaveolens is often found in dry meadows, roadsides, and other disturbed habitats.
