Eupatorium connivens

Eupatorium connivens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium connivens is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
