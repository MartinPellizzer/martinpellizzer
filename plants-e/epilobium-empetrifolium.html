Epilobium empetrifolium

Epilobium empetrifolium, commonly known as rock spindle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant has slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
