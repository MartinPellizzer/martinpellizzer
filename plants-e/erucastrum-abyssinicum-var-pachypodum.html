Erucastrum abyssinicum var. pachypodum

Erucastrum abyssinicum var. pachypodum, commonly known as Abyssinian desert cress, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

This variety is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing remarkable resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.
