Eucalyptus sessilis

Eucalyptus sessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southern Australia, particularly in the states of South Australia and Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue. Eucalyptus sessilis is a shrub or small tree that often grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
