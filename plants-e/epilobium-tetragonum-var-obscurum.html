Epilobium tetragonum var. obscurum

Epilobium tetragonum var. obscurum, commonly known as the dark willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. The plant features square, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spike-like clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
