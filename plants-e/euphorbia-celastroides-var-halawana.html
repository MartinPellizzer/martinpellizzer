Euphorbia celastroides var. halawana

Euphorbia celastroides var. halawana, commonly known as the halawana spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching structure and fleshy, greenish-yellow stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
