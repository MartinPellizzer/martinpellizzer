Epipactis viridans f. remota

Epipactis viridans f. remota, commonly known as the green helleborine, is a rare and specialized orchid species found primarily in moist, shaded forest environments.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a unique inflorescence that bears greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant is a terrestrial orchid, typically growing from a rhizome and producing a single spike of flowers. It is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, often forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi.
