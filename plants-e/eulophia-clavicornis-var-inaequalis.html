Eulophia clavicornis var. inaequalis

Eulophia clavicornis var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal-flowered eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and pseudobulbs that bear elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces inflorescences with clustered flowers that have distinctive, spurred petals and a unique coloration pattern. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow with contrasting purple or brown markings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
