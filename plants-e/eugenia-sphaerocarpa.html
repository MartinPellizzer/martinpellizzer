Eugenia sphaerocarpa

Eugenia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the red globe guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, round, red fruits that are edible. The plant produces fragrant white flowers and has glossy, elliptical leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
