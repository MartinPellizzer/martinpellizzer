Eriocephalus umbellulatus

Eriocephalus umbellulatus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The African daisy is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
