Eupatorium illitum

Eupatorium illitum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Eupatorium illitum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
