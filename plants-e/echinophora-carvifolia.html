Echinophora carvifolia

Echinophora carvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Echinophora carvifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
