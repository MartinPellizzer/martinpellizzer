Epipactis albensis subsp. fibri

Epipactis albensis subsp.

fibri, commonly known as the fibrous helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its fibrous roots and lanceolate, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip and column, typical of orchids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in calcareous soils.
