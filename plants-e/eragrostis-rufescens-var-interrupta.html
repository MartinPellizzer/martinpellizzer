Eragrostis rufescens var. interrupta

Eragrostis rufescens var. interrupta, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, reddish-brown stems that often have a distinctive interrupted or broken appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique foliage.
