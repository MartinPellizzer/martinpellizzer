Eupatorium lobatifolium

Eupatorium lobatifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are often described as having a rough, hairy texture and are arranged oppositely along the stem.
