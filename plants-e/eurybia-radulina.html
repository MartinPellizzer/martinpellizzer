Eurybia radulina

Eurybia radulina, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver or white bracts. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. Eurybia radulina has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

It blooms from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
