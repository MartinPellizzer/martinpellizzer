Eucalyptus forresterae

Eucalyptus forresterae, commonly known as the Forrester's eucalyptus, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves contain essential oils that have medicinal properties, making it valuable in traditional and modern herbal remedies.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
