Eupatorium bahamense

Eupatorium bahamense, commonly known as Bahamian boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium bahamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
