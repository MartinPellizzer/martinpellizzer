Eulophia longicollis

Eulophia longicollis, commonly known as the long-necked eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its slender, upright pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves that can reach several centimeters in length. The plant produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. Eulophia longicollis thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique morphology and ornamental flowers make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
