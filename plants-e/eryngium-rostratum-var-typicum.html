Eryngium rostratum var. typicum

Eryngium rostratum var. typicum, commonly known as the common thistle or sea thistle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or disturbed soils along shorelines. The plant features spiny, blue-green leaves and produces purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistles. Its flowers are composed of tubular florets surrounded by bracts, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
