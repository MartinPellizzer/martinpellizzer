Erodium radicatum

Erodium radicatum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its stolons and its ability to reproduce both sexually and vegetatively.
