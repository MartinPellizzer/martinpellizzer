Encyclia ciliaris

Encyclia ciliaris, commonly known as the ear-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strap-like, linear leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive ear-like shape. The plant typically blooms in the spring and summer, with flowers ranging in color from white to pink and yellow. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Encyclia ciliaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
