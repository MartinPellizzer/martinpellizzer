Eriophyllum absinthiifolium

Eriophyllum absinthiifolium, commonly known as yellow coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant features slender, gray-green leaves with a silvery appearance, and its flowers bloom in shades of yellow to orange in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Eriophyllum absinthiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
