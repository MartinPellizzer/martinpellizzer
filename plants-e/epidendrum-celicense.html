Epidendrum celicense

Epidendrum celicense, commonly known as the Brazilian pink orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces clusters of fragrant, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical study.
