Echinopsis santiaguensis

Echinopsis santiaguensis, commonly known as the Santiago moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and large, white to pinkish flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a spiny exterior and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
