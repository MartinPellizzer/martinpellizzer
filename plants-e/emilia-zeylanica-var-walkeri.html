Emilia zeylanica var. walkeri

Emilia zeylanica var. walkeri, commonly known as the purple emilia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds enclosed in a fluffy, white pappus, which aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable.
