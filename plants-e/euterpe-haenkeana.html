Euterpe haenkeana

Euterpe haenkeana, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a species of palm tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clustered, edible fruits resembling small, sweet cherries. Euterpe haenkeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is used in local cuisine.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
