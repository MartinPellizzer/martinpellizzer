Echinocactus pentacanthus

Echinocactus pentacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom from the areoles. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and xeriscaping.
