Ervum trijugum

Ervum trijugum, commonly known as the three-lobed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Ervum trijugum is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
