Eupatorium myricifolium

Eupatorium myricifolium, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine, and the plant is also a valuable nectar source for pollinators.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
