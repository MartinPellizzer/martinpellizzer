Elaphoglossum macrophyllum var. schmalzii

Elaphoglossum macrophyllum var. schmalzii, commonly known as the Schmalzii holly fern, is a variant of the broad-leaf holly fern.

It is a perennial fern with a dense, compact growth habit, typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green fronds with a leathery texture and serrated margins. Its spore-producing structures, or sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the undersides of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
