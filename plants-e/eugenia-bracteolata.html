Eugenia bracteolata

Eugenia bracteolata, commonly known as the purple-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark purple foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage.

Eugenia bracteolata is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
