Euphorbia × peisonis

Euphorbia × peisonis, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, woody stem with a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia × peisonis is popular in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and striking appearance.
