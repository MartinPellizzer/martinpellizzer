Epidendrum myosurus

Epidendrum myosurus, commonly known as the mouse ear orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a velvety texture and produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

Epidendrum myosurus is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
