Eugenia selvana

Eugenia selvana, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Eugenia selvana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
