Esenbeckia cornuta

Esenbeckia cornuta, commonly known as the horned esenbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, horn-like fruit capsules. Esenbeckia cornuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and seasonal floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
