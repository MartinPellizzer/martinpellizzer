Englerophytum ferrugineum

Englerophytum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty englerophytum, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
