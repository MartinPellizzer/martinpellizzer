Eupatorium flexuosum

Eupatorium flexuosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and swamps.

Eupatorium flexuosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
