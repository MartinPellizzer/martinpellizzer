Eremochloa pectinata

Eremochloa pectinata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a silvery sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Eremochloa pectinata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
