Erica transsylvanica

Erica transsylvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Erica transsylvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
