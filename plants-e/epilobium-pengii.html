Epilobium pengii

Epilobium pengii, commonly known as the Pengi willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in low-light conditions.

Epilobium pengii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
