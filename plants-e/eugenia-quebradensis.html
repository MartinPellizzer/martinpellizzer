Eugenia quebradensis

Eugenia quebradensis, commonly known as the Quebradense myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and aromatic. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
