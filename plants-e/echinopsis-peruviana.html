Echinopsis peruviana

Echinopsis peruviana, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant is a succulent, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they emit a sweet fragrance to attract nocturnal pollinators.

Echinopsis peruviana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
