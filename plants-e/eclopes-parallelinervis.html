Eclopes parallelinervis

Eclopes parallelinervis, commonly known as the parallel-veined grass.

It is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves with parallel veins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small and often wind-pollinated.

Eclopes parallelinervis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
