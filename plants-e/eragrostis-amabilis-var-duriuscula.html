Eragrostis amabilis var. duriuscula

Eragrostis amabilis var. duriuscula, commonly known as the hardy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which makes it visually appealing. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
