Erigeron alpinus subsp. villarsii

Erigeron alpinus subsp.

villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It features a low-growing habit with basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow disc, blooming in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for alpine ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
