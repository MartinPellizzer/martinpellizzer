Echinopsis densispina f. eburnea

Echinopsis densispina f. eburnea, commonly known as the White-spined Cactus, is a compact, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance.

It features a smooth, pale green stem covered in white, radial spines that give it its common name. The plant produces elegant, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens due to its hardiness and unique visual appeal.
