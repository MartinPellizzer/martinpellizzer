Eriogonum cernuum subsp. rotundifolium

Eriogonum cernuum subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as the roundleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The leaves are rounded and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is an important component of local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
