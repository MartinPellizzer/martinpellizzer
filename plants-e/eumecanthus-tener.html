Eumecanthus tener

Eumecanthus tener, commonly known as the slender euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, a feature common in many desert plants.

Eumecanthus tener is adapted to survive in low-water environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
