Eryngium vaseyi var. globosum

Eryngium vaseyi var. globosum, commonly known as the globe eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white bracts. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture.

It is valued in gardens for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
