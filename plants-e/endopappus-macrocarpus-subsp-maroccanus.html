Endopappus macrocarpus subsp. maroccanus

Endopappus macrocarpus subsp.

maroccanus, commonly known as the Moroccan endopappus, is a rare and specialized plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated, hairy bracts and a distinctive inflorescence structure.

The plant exhibits adaptations to arid environments, with thick leaves and a deep root system for water retention. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in dense heads.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
