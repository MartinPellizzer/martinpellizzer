Erica rhopalantha

Erica rhopalantha, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica rhopalantha thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought tolerance and frost resistance.
