Equisetum ramosissimum var. paleaceum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. paleaceum, commonly known as the pale field horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of the stems, which are typically found near the ground level. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
