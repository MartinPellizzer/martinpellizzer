Eucalyptus calcicola subsp. calcicola

Eucalyptus calcicola subsp.

calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, fissured bark that often peels in strips, and its lance-shaped or elliptical leaves that are usually grey-green in color.

The flowers are typically white or pale blue, clustered in umbels, and are borne on rigid, often ascending spikes. This subspecies is adapted to grow in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or on rocky outcrops.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
