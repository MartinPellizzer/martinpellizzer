Echeveria goldmanii

Echeveria goldmanii, commonly known as the Gold Dust Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray with a powdery white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, which is typical of succulents. It produces showy pink or purple flowers during the spring or summer.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.
