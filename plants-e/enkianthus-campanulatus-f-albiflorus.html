Enkianthus campanulatus f. albiflorus

Enkianthus campanulatus f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered bell-Enkianthus, is a rare and striking variant of the Enkianthus genus.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, contrasting with the typical pink or red hues of the species. The plant is native to China, where it grows in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates. It features lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking backdrop to its delicate blooms.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens.
