Epipactis baumanniorum

Epipactis baumanniorum, commonly known as the Baumann's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Southern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.

The plant is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
