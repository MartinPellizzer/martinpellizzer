Eriogonum longifolium var. floridanum

Eriogonum longifolium var. floridanum, commonly known as the Florida buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal dunes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
