Echinofossulocactus corniger var. rubrospinus

Echinofossulocactus corniger var. rubrospinus, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, reddish spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, adding a vibrant contrast to its spiky appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and unique features, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
