Euphorbia pillansii var. albovirens

Euphorbia pillansii var. albovirens, commonly known as the White-green Pillans' Spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, woody stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often branching out into a shrub-like form. The leaves are small, fleshy, and typically have a pale green or bluish hue, which helps it conserve water in arid environments. It produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.

This plant is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and resilience.
