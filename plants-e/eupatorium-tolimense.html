Eupatorium tolimense

Eupatorium tolimense, commonly known as Toliman hemp or Toliman feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eupatorium tolimense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
