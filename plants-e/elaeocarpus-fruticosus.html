Elaeocarpus fruticosus

Elaeocarpus fruticosus, commonly known as the shrub elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, pale blue or white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically reddish or purplish when mature. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.

Elaeocarpus fruticosus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
