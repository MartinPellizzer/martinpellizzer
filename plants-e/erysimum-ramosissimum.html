Erysimum ramosissimum

Erysimum ramosissimum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers and hairy, branched stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Erysimum ramosissimum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
