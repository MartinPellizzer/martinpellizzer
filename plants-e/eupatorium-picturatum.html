Eupatorium picturatum

Eupatorium picturatum, commonly known as the painted eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fluffy white to pinkish flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium picturatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
