Eugenia rigida var. ovata

Eugenia rigida var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaved roughshanks, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and coastal areas.
