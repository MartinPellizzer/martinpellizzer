Eriocaulon heterochiton var. acuminatum

Eriocaulon heterochiton var. acuminatum, commonly known as the acute-leaved flat sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist soils in temperate regions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

This variant is distinguished by its acuminate (tapering to a point) leaf tips and is often found in coastal or freshwater marshes.
