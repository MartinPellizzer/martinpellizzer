Eugenia jurujubensis

Eugenia jurujubensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-preto, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black when ripe, containing a single seed.

Eugenia jurujubensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
