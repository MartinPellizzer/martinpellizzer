Echinopsis × poselgeri

Echinopsis × poselgeri, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a hybrid succulent native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of bright pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a short, thick stem with spines that provide protection from herbivores. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It requires full sun and well-draining soil to flourish.
