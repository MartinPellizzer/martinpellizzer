Epipactis purpurata subsp. rechingeri

Epipactis purpurata subsp.

rechingeri, commonly known as the Rechinger's helleborine, is a herbaceous orchid native to Central and Southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in calcareous soils.

The orchid is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is of interest to botanists due to its ecological and horticultural significance.
