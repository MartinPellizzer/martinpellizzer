Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp. parviflorus

Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp.

parviflorus, commonly known as the rusty elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
