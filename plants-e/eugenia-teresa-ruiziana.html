Eugenia teresa-ruiziana

Eugenia teresa-ruiziana, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or Mexican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
