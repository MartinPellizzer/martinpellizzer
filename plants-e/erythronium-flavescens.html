Erythronium flavescens

Erythronium flavescens, commonly known as the yellow trout lily, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features a bulbous root system and produces a single, showy flower with a yellowish-orange corona and white petals. The plant typically grows in moist, deciduous woodlands and thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and emerge early in the spring, often before the flowers.

The species is known for its early blooming habit and is often found in forest understories.
