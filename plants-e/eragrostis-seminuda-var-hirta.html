Eragrostis seminuda var. hirta

Eragrostis seminuda var. hirta, commonly known as hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its ecological significance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
