Entada glandulosa

Entada glandulosa, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces long, flat pods that contain flat, seed-like structures called arils. It is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant also plays a role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
