Eugenia chnoosepala

Eugenia chnoosepala, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that changes from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia chnoosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
