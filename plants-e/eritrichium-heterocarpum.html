Eritrichium heterocarpum

Eritrichium heterocarpum, commonly known as the purple poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are basal, hairy, and often covered in fine pubescence.

The fruit is a unique, twisted, two-lobed capsule that aids in seed dispersal.
