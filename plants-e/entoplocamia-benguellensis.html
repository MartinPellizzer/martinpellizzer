Entoplocamia benguellensis

Entoplocamia benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguella thornveld grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Entoplocamia benguellensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
