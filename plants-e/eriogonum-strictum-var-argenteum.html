Eriogonum strictum var. argenteum

Eriogonum strictum var. argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hairs, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
