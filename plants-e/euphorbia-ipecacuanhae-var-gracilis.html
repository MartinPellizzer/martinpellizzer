Euphorbia ipecacuanhae var. gracilis

Euphorbia ipecacuanhae var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that are typically green to reddish in color, often adorned with small, spiny leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures containing both male and female flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
