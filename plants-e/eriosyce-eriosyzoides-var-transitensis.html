Eriosyce eriosyzoides var. transitensis

Eriosyce eriosyzoides var. transitensis, commonly known as the Transient Eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of pale green to white spines. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
