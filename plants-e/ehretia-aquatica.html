Ehretia aquatica

Ehretia aquatica, commonly known as water hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a prominent yellow center. It thrives in moist or wet environments, often found near water bodies.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
