Ericoides medium

Ericoides medium, commonly known as the medium heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky habitats.

Ericoides medium is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is a valuable component of biodiversity-rich ecosystems.
