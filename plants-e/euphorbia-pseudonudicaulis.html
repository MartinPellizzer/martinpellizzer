Euphorbia pseudonudicaulis

Euphorbia pseudonudicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a short, thick stem with narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as an ornamental plant.
