Eugenia gomonenensis

Eugenia gomonenensis, commonly known as the Gomonene guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, pinkish-red fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
