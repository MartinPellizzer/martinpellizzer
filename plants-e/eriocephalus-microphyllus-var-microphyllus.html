Eriocephalus microphyllus var. microphyllus

Eriocephalus microphyllus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of white, woolly flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens due to its compact growth habit.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
