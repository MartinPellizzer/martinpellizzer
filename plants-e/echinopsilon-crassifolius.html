Echinopsilon crassifolius

Echinopsilon crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed echinops, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, succulent stems and large, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
