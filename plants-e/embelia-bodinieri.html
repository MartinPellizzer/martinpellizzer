Embelia bodinieri

Embelia bodinieri, commonly known as the Indian embelia or wild pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a peppery flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in both horticulture and ecological contexts.
