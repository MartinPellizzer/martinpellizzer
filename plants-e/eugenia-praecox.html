Eugenia praecox

Eugenia praecox, commonly known as the early wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red or yellow berries. Its fruits are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.

Eugenia praecox thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.
