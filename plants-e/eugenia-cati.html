Eugenia cati

Eugenia cati, commonly known as the Java apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries resembling small apples. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
