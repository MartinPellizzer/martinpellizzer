Erysimum hookeri

Erysimum hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated leaves that are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Erysimum hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its cheerful blossoms.
