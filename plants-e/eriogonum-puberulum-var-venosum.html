Eriogonum puberulum var. venosum

Eriogonum puberulum var. venosum, commonly known as the Venosum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
