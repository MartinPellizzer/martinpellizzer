Eucharis korsakoffii

Eucharis korsakoffii, commonly known as the "Flamingo Flower," is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae), characterized by its distinctive inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a colorful spathe. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are often brightly colored, with the spathe resembling a flamingo's pink feathers. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Eucharis korsakoffii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
