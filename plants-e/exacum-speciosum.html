Exacum speciosum

Exacum speciosum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often velvety to the touch, and it thrives in bright, indirect light. Exacum speciosum is a popular houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

It is typically propagated through stem cuttings or division.
