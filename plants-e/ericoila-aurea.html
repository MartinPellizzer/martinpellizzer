Ericoila aurea

Ericoila aurea, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers. Ericoila aurea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
