Eria pleiostachya

Eria pleiostachya, commonly known as the many-stemmed orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts interested in rare orchids.
