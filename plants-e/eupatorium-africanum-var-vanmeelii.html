Eupatorium africanum var. vanmeelii

Eupatorium africanum var. vanmeelii, commonly known as the African blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest margins.
