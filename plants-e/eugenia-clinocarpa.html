Eugenia clinocarpa

Eugenia clinocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid climate for optimal growth.
