Eupatorium nodiflorum

Eupatorium nodiflorum, commonly known as nodding thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium nodiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
