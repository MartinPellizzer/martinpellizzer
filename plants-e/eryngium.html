Eryngium

Eryngium, commonly known as sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. Eryngium species are often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. They are perennial herbs with upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The flowers are typically tubular and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
