Eriosema pulcherrimum

Eriosema pulcherrimum, commonly known as the velvetleaf plectranthus, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its softly hairy, oval-shaped leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Eriosema pulcherrimum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
