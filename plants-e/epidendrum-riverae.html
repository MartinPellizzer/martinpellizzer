Epidendrum riverae

Epidendrum riverae, commonly known as the River's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its clustered, pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated, fleshy, and often have a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Epidendrum riverae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
