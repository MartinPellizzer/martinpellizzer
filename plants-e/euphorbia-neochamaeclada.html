Euphorbia neochamaeclada

Euphorbia neochamaeclada, commonly known as the Neochamaeclada spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, specifically South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

Euphorbia neochamaeclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
