Erigeron alpinus var. pyrenaicus

Erigeron alpinus var. pyrenaicus, commonly known as the Pyrenean blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a central cluster of yellow disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens for its delicate appearance.
