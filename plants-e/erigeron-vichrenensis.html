Erigeron vichrenensis

Erigeron vichrenensis, commonly known as the Vichrean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white to pale blue.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
