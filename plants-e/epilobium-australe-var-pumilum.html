Epilobium australe var. pumilum

Epilobium australe var. pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf willow, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathland or woodland environments. The plant features slender, willow-like stems covered in fine, silky hairs. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native garden landscapes.
