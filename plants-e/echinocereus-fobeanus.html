Echinocereus fobeanus

Echinocereus fobeanus, commonly known as the golden hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless plant with a globular shape and typically produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features include a thick, ribbed stem that stores water and a flowering pattern that attracts pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
