Eragrostis pilosa f. imberbis

Eragrostis pilosa f. imberbis, commonly known as the hairless speargrass, is a variant of the common speargrass species.

It is a perennial grass native to regions of Africa and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its distinguishing feature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
