Eryngium campestre f. latiinvolucratum

Eryngium campestre f. latiinvolucratum, commonly known as the broad-involucrate thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by a wide involucre of bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with rough textures.
