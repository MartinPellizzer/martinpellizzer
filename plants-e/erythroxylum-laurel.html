Erythroxylum laurel

Erythroxylum laurel, commonly known as the red laurier, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant red flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that contain the psychoactive alkaloid caffeine. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a historical significance in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive member of the laurel family.
