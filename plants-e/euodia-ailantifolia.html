Euodia ailantifolia

Euodia ailantifolia, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters and red berries that are edible for birds. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Euodia ailantifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact form and year-round aesthetic appeal.
