Espeletia argentea f. argentea

Espeletia argentea f. argentea, commonly known as the silver bush or silver plant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It produces showy, cone-like flower heads that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticultural interest.
