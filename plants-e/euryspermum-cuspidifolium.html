Euryspermum cuspidifolium

Euryspermum cuspidifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved euryspermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, pointed leaves that give it its specific epithet "cuspidifolium.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
