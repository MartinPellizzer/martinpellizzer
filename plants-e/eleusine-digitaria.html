Eleusine digitaria

Eleusine digitaria, commonly known as wiregrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Eleusine digitaria is sometimes considered a weed but can also serve as forage for livestock in certain regions.
