Eriogonum ovalifolium var. anserinum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. anserinum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
