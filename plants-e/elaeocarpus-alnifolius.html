Elaeocarpus alnifolius

Elaeocarpus alnifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarp, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
