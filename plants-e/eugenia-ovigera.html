Eugenia ovigera

Eugenia ovigera, commonly known as the West Indian cherry or acerola, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, acidic berries that are rich in vitamin C and other antioxidants. Eugenia ovigera thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
