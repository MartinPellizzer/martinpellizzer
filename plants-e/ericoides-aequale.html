Ericoides aequale

Ericoides aequale, commonly known as the common eric, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

Ericoides aequale is adapted to cool, moist climates and is known for its ecological importance in supporting various insect and bird species.
