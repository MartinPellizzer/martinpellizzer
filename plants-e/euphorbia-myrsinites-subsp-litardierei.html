Euphorbia myrsinites subsp. litardierei

Euphorbia myrsinites subsp.

litardierei, commonly known as the Algerian spurge, is a succulent shrub native to North Africa. It features a compact, branched structure with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
