Epidendrum squalidum

Epidendrum squalidum, commonly known as the dirty orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers. The plant thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in moist forest floors. It has a rhizomatous growth habit and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper care.

Epidendrum squalidum is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
