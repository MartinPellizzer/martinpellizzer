Eucalyptus aggregata

Eucalyptus aggregata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Eucalyptus aggregata is well adapted to arid conditions and is an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
