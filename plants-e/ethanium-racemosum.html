Ethanium racemosum

Ethanium racemosum, commonly known as the clustered ethanium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Ethanium racemosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
