Eucalyptus rubida

Eucalyptus rubida, commonly known as the red mallet gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to southeastern Australia, particularly in New South Wales and Victoria. The tree is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a red or pinkish color underneath. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive red or pinkish hue when young.

The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.
