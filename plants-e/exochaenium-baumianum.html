Exochaenium baumianum

Exochaenium baumianum, commonly known as the Baumann's spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by water or wind.

Due to its ecological significance, it is often used in wetland restoration projects.
