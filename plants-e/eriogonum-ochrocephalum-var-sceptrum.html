Eriogonum ochrocephalum var. sceptrum

Eriogonum ochrocephalum var. sceptrum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright yellow blooms.
