Ehretia meyersii

Ehretia meyersii, commonly known as the Mexican mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by round, red berries that are a food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
