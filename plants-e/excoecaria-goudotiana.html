Excoecaria goudotiana

Excoecaria goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's croton, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with variegated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
