Erechtites argutus var. bathurstianus

Erechtites argutus var. bathurstianus, commonly known as the Bathurst thistle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in soft, grayish-green leaves that are deeply lobed and often hairy. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers arranged in a rounded cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves have a distinctive serrated margin and are often covered with fine, white hairs.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
