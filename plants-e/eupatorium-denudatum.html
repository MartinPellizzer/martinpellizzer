Eupatorium denudatum

Eupatorium denudatum, commonly known as slender boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium denudatum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
