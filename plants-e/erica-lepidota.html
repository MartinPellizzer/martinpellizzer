Erica lepidota

Erica lepidota, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it its common name. Erica lepidota produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
