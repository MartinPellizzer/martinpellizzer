Eupatorium eugenei

Eupatorium eugenei, commonly known as the Eugene's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium eugenei is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
