Ericameria viscidiflora subsp. axillaris

Ericameria viscidiflora subsp.

axillaris, commonly known as the stickyhead ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has small, oblong leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
