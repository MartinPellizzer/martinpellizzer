Elymus valdiviae

Elymus valdiviae, commonly known as the Valdivia grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in the temperate rainforests of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Elymus valdiviae is adapted to moist, cool climates and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
