Eleocharis nodulosa f. trigyna

Eleocharis nodulosa f. trigyna, commonly known as the three-styled spike rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its inflorescence consists of three distinct styles, which is a key identifying feature of this botanical form.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
