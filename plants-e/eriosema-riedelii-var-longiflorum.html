Eriosema riedelii var. longiflorum

Eriosema riedelii var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered eriosema, is a species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "eriosema," meaning "hairy.". The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
