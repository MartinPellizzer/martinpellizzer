Echeveria mondragoniana

Echeveria mondragoniana, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-gray to silver in color, with a powdery coating that gives them a distinctive appearance. It produces vibrant pink or red flowers in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
