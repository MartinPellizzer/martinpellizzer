Eupatorium foliatum var. incanum

Eupatorium foliatum var. incanum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Its common name reflects the appearance of its stiff, bone-like stems.
