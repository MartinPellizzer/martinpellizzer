Eriocoma robusta

Eriocoma robusta, commonly known as the rough ericoid, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocoma robusta is well adapted to poor, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
