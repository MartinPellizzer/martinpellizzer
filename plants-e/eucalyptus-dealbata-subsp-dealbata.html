Eucalyptus dealbata subsp. dealbata

Eucalyptus dealbata subsp.

dealbata, commonly known as the white gum, is a tall, slender tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in long strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a greyish-white underside, and they are typically narrow and leathery. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale yellow to cream in color.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open forests and woodlands.
