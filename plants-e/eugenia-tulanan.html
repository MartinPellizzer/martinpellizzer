Eugenia tulanan

Eugenia tulanan, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, segmented fruits that are typically bright red or yellow when ripe. Its botanical features include small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Eugenia tulanan is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the consumption of its sweet, aromatic fruit.
