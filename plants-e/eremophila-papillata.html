Eremophila papillata

Eremophila papillata, commonly known as the pink ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eremophila papillata is a hardy shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
