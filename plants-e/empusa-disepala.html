Empusa disepala

Empusa disepala, commonly known as the six-winged orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring six sepals that are often mistaken for petals, giving it its common name. Native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are typically small and delicate, with a complex arrangement of petals and stamens.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, Empusa disepala is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
