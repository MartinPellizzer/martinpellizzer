Elegia asperiflora

Elegia asperiflora, commonly known as the rough-flowered elegia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small, tubular, and arranged in clusters, often displaying vibrant shades of red or orange.

Elegia asperiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
