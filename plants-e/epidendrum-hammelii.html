Epidendrum hammelii

Epidendrum hammelii, commonly known as the Hamel's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal stem that produces new shoots from the nodes. The plant typically features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters. Its flowers are usually yellow or white with a distinctive red or orange lip.

Epidendrum hammelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
