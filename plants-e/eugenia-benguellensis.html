Eugenia benguellensis

Eugenia benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguella eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of southern Africa, particularly in countries like Angola and Namibia. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature, and they are often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
