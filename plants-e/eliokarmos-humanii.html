Eliokarmos humanii

Eliokarmos humanii, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to China and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible but often covered in a sticky resin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
