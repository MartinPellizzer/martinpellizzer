Ericoides calyculata

Ericoides calyculata, commonly known as the Cape mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Ericoides calyculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
