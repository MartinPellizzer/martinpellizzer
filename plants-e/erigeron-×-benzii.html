Erigeron × benzii

Erigeron × benzii, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and fluffy, daisy-like flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and pond edges. It has a fibrous root system and can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to wetland restoration projects and native plant gardens.
