Euphorbia tuberculata var. macowanii

Euphorbia tuberculata var. macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small tubercles. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
