Eupatorium compactum

Eupatorium compactum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a mild, pleasant scent when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium compactum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
