Echinochloa crus-galli var. macera

Echinochloa crus-galli var. macera, commonly known as cocklebur, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Americas. The plant has a robust stem with stiff, hairy leaves that are often lance-shaped. It produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
