Elymus uclueletensis

Elymus uclueletensis, commonly known as the Ucluelet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along riverbanks. Elymus uclueletensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its flowering heads consist of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are wind-pollinated.
