Erigeron cinerascens

Erigeron cinerascens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its flowers are composed of a white disk surrounded by blue to purple ray florets.

Erigeron cinerascens is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
