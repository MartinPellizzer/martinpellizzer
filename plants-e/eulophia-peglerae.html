Eulophia peglerae

Eulophia peglerae, commonly known as the Pegler's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, strap-like leaves and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

Eulophia peglerae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
