Eurybia macrophylla var. velutina

Eurybia macrophylla var. velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy lavender to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features broad, velvety leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
