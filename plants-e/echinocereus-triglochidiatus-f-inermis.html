Echinocereus triglochidiatus f. inermis

Echinocereus triglochidiatus f. inermis, commonly known as the naked hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the hedgehog cactus species.

It is characterized by its reduced spines, giving it a more delicate appearance compared to its spiny relatives. The plant typically grows as a solitary or clustered shrub, with cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, white to pale green areoles. It produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and bats.

This cactus is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
