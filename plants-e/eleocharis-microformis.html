Eleocharis microformis

Eleocharis microformis, commonly known as the dwarf spike-rush, is a small, grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland and marshy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered and bear tiny, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Eleocharis microformis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
