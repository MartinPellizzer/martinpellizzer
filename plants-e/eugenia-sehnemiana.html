Eugenia sehnemiana

Eugenia sehnemiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small cherry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia sehnemiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
