Elaeodendron glaucum var. macrocarpum

Elaeodendron glaucum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited blueberry, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy, blue-gray bloom. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are aggregate berries that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
