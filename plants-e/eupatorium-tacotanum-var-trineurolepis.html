Eupatorium tacotanum var. trineurolepis

Eupatorium tacotanum var. trineurolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, showcasing its adaptability to humid environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also noted for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
