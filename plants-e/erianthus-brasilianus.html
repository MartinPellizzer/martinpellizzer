Erianthus brasilianus

Erianthus brasilianus, commonly known as Brazilian silver grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces dense, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
