Erlangea prolixa

Erlangea prolixa, commonly known as the slender erlang plant, is a unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and woody stems that provide structural support.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Erlangea prolixa is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
