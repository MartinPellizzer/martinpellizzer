Euphorbia hintonii

Euphorbia hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate and covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hair.

Euphorbia hintonii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
