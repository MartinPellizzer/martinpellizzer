Eugenia alnifolia

Eugenia alnifolia, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its edible, red, elongated fruits that resemble peppers, which are often used in culinary applications. Eugenia alnifolia thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible yield.

It is also sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
