Echinosciadium arabicum

Echinosciadium arabicum, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates and is adapted to poor soil conditions. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in folk remedies for digestive issues.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that disperses seeds by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh environments.
