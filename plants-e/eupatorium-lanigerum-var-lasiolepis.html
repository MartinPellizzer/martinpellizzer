Eupatorium lanigerum var. lasiolepis

Eupatorium lanigerum var. lasiolepis, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features tall, erect stems that are covered in stiff, bristly hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles, and are usually white or pale purple, blooming in late summer to early fall.
