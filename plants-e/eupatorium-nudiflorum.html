Eupatorium nudiflorum

Eupatorium nudiflorum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white, showy flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

White boneset is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
