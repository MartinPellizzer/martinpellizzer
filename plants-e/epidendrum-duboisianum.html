Epidendrum duboisianum

Epidendrum duboisianum, commonly known as the Dubois's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum duboisianum is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
