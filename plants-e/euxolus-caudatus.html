Euxolus caudatus

Euxolus caudatus, commonly known as the long-tailed euphorbia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features distinctive, spiny stems and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its leaves are usually small and opposite, with a somewhat succulent texture.

Euxolus caudatus is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
