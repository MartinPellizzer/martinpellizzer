Eugenia sulphurata

Eugenia sulphurata, commonly known as the sulfurwood or sulfur tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its yellowish sap, which gives it its common name and is used in traditional medicine. Eugenia sulphurata thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
