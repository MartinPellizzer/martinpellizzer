Eriocaulon congense

Eriocaulon congense, commonly known as the African bulbous grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, white to pale brown flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to aquatic environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it is often found in dense clusters.

Eriocaulon congense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a decorative plant in humid climates.
