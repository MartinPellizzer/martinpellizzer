Euphorbia montenegrina

Euphorbia montenegrina, commonly known as the Montenegro spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and yellowish-green sap, which can be toxic if ingested. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in rocky or dry habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
