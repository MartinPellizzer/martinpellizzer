Evolvulus diosmoides var. subsericeus

Evolvulus diosmoides var. subsericeus, commonly known as the silky blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing vines.
