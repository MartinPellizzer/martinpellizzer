Excoecaria bicolor var. orientalis

Excoecaria bicolor var. orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, colorful foliage.

The leaves are typically green, yellow, or red, with a velvety texture and prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant contains toxic compounds, so it should be handled with care.
