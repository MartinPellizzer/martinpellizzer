Epistylium latifolium

Epistylium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf epistylium, is a colonial marine algae belonging to the order Rhodophyceae.

It forms dense, bushy colonies composed of numerous interconnected thallus fragments. The plant has a distinctive broad, flat morphology with a central axis and lateral branches. It typically grows in shallow, coastal waters where it attaches to substrates such as rocks or shells.

Its structure supports a diverse array of marine life, making it an ecologically significant species in intertidal zones.
