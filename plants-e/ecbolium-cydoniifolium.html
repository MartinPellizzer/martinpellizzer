Ecbolium cydoniifolium

Ecbolium cydoniifolium, commonly known as the horse chestnut tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces fruit that consists of spiny, woody capsules containing bright red, shiny seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to many regions around the world.

The plant is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
