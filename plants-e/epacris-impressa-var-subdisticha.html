Epacris impressa var. subdisticha

Epacris impressa var. subdisticha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, often in coastal regions. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, and it has a compact, bushy growth habit.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
