Eupatorium caaguazuense var. hirsutum

Eupatorium caaguazuense var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hirsute eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in moist, open habitats and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
