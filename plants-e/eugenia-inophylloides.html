Eugenia inophylloides

Eugenia inophylloides, commonly known as the red cedar or purple leaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that range from dark green to purple in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
