Eugenia rubescens

Eugenia rubescens, commonly known as the red eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Eugenia rubescens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm regions.
