Eragrostis vahlii

Eragrostis vahlii, commonly known as Vahl's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous minute, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and typically have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib.

Eragrostis vahlii is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
