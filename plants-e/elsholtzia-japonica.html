Elsholtzia japonica

Elsholtzia japonica, commonly known as Japanese elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often grayish-green in color. Its flowers are typically blue to purple and are borne in whorled clusters, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Elsholtzia japonica is often cultivated for its medicinal properties and as a decorative plant in moist, shady environments.
