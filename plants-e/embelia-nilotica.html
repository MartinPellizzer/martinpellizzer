Embelia nilotica

Embelia nilotica, commonly known as the African embelia or black myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, black, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves and fruits contain essential oils and are valued for their medicinal properties.

Embelia nilotica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
