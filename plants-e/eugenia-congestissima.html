Eugenia congestissima

Eugenia congestissima, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia congestissima*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
