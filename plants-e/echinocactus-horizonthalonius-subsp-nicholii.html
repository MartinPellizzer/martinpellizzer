Echinocactus horizonthalonius subsp. nicholii

Echinocactus horizonthalonius subsp.

nicholii, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, hair-like spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
