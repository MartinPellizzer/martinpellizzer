Euphrasia officinalis subsp. major

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

major, commonly known as the greater eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
