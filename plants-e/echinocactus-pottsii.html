Echinocactus pottsii

Echinocactus pottsii, commonly known as the Potts' hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on short pedicels. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
