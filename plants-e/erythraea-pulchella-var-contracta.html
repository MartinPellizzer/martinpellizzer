Erythraea pulchella var. contracta

Erythraea pulchella var. contracta, commonly known as the compact erythraea, is a rare and striking plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant red to purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
