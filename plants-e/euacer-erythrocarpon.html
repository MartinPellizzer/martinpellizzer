Euacer erythrocarpon

Euacer erythrocarpon, commonly known as the red-fruited euacer, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red berries that mature in the late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical environments, often in shaded, moist habitats. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a glossy surface that reflects light.

The flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish-white clusters, which attract various pollinators.
