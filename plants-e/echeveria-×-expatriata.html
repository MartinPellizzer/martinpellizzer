Echeveria × expatriata

Echeveria × expatriata, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, often blue-green or purple-toned leaves that are densely packed and covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
