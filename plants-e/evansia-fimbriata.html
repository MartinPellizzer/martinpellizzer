Evansia fimbriata

Evansia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed bursage, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and showy, pink to red bracts that resemble small flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its seed capsules split open to release tiny seeds.

Evansia fimbriata is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
