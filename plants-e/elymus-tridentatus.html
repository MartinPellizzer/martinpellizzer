Elymus tridentatus

Elymus tridentatus, commonly known as the three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive three-branched awns on the seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Elymus tridentatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
