Ervum megalotropis

Ervum megalotropis, commonly known as the giant yellow vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, with slender, hairy stems that twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets.

Ervum megalotropis is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
