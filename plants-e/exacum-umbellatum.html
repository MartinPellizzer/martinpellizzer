Exacum umbellatum

Exacum umbellatum, commonly known as the common ivy.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces white or pinkish blooms that appear in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Exacum umbellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
