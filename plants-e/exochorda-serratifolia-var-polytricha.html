Exochorda serratifolia var. polytricha

Exochorda serratifolia var. polytricha, commonly known as the serrated honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
