Eranthemum cuspidatum

Eranthemum cuspidatum, commonly known as the crimson torch or red torch flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features vibrant red or orange bracts that resemble torches, often with small, tubular flowers clustered within. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eranthemum cuspidatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
