Eugenia sylvana

Eugenia sylvana, commonly known as the swamp myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia sylvana is often found in wetlands and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils.
