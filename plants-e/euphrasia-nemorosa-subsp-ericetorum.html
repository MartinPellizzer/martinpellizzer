Euphrasia nemorosa subsp. ericetorum

Euphrasia nemorosa subsp.

ericetorum, commonly known as the creeping dog's-tongue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and hedgerows.

The plant features slender, trailing stems with small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs.

It is often found in association with other woodland flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
