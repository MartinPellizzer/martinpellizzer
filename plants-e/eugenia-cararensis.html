Eugenia cararensis

Eugenia cararensis, commonly known as the Carare guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia cararensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
