Eragrostis capitata

Eragrostis capitata, commonly known as speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis capitata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in some traditional practices.
