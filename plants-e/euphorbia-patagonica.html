Euphorbia patagonica

Euphorbia patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
