Eugenia egensis

Eugenia egensis, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
