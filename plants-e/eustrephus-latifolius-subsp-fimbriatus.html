Eustrephus latifolius subsp. fimbriatus

Eustrephus latifolius subsp.

fimbriatus, commonly known as the broadleaf eustrephus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive fringed or toothed edge, giving it its subspecies name "fimbriatus.".

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements, it is occasionally cultivated by gardeners interested in native or drought-tolerant plants.
