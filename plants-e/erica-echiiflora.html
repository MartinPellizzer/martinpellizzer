Erica echiiflora

Erica echiiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica echiiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
