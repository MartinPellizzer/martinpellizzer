Euphorbia arteagae

Euphorbia arteagae, commonly known as the Sierra Madre euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
