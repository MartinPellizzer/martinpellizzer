Eriocaulon dictyophyllum

Eriocaulon dictyophyllum, commonly known as the net-leaf marsh grass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the margins of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, with the lower bracts forming a distinctive net-like structure.

Eriocaulon dictyophyllum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
