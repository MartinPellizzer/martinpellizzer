Eupatorium alternifolium var. glabrum

Eupatorium alternifolium var. glabrum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, though the variety glabrum is smooth and hairless. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive serrated margin.

Small, white to pale purple flowers appear in clustered inflorescences during the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
