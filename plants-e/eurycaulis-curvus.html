Eurycaulis curvus

Eurycaulis curvus, commonly known as the curved-stemmed eurycaulis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features a fibrous root system and produces slender, branching stems that often curve downward. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pale yellow to white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
