Eragrostis leptantha

Eragrostis leptantha, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its drooping, fountain-like growth habit and fine, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a silky coating. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, narrow clusters. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

Eragrostis leptantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
