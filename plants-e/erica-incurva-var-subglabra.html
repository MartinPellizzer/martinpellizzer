Erica incurva var. subglabra

Erica incurva var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with narrow, leathery leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is well-suited to coastal or sandy environments.

Erica incurva var. subglabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
