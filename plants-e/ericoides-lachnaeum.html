Ericoides lachnaeum

Ericoides lachnaeum, commonly known as the mossy lichen, is a member of the Lecanoraceae family.

It is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungal partner and a green algal or cyanobacterial partner. This lichen typically grows on shaded, moist surfaces such as tree bark, rocks, and soil. Its morphology includes a crustose thallus with a dense, moss-like appearance.

Ericoides lachnaeum is often found in temperate and boreal regions, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in forest ecosystems.
