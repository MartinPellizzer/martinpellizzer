Encyclia candollei

Encyclia candollei, commonly known as the Candolle's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and linear to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow and green coloration. Encyclia candollei typically blooms during the rainy season, and its flowers are pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and rocky slopes.
