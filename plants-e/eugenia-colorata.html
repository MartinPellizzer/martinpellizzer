Eugenia colorata

Eugenia colorata, commonly known as the red bell apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn red when ripe, resembling small apples.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
