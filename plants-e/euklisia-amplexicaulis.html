Euklisia amplexicaulis

Euklisia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clinging vine or mistletoe vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that often wrap around supporting structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing along tree trunks or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its rapid growth and ability to climb, it is both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some areas.
