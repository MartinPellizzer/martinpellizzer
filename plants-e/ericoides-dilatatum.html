Ericoides dilatatum

Ericoides dilatatum, commonly known as the broadleaf heath, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions where the climate is cool and moist.

Ericoides dilatatum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
