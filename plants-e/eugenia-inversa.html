Eugenia inversa

Eugenia inversa, commonly known as the upside-down tree, is a unique tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive growth pattern, with branches growing downward and roots developing upward. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits. Its unusual morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
