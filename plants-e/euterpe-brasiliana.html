Euterpe brasiliana

Euterpe brasiliana, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or buriti palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, creamy pulp. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Euterpe brasiliana plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
