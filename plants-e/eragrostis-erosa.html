Eragrostis erosa

Eragrostis erosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
