Euphorbia clementei var. euclementei

Euphorbia clementei var. euclementei, commonly known as the Blue Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its striking blue-gray foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique inflorescences, rather than typical flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid climates.
