Eugenia wilsonella

Eugenia wilsonella, commonly known as Wilson's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features glossy, elliptical leaves with a dark green upper surface. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native habitat.
