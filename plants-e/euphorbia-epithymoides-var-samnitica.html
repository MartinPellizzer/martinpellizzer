Euphorbia epithymoides var. samnitica

Euphorbia epithymoides var. samnitica, commonly known as the Southern Italian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Italy.

It features succulent, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a unique, cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
