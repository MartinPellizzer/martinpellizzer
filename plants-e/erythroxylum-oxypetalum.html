Erythroxylum oxypetalum

Erythroxylum oxypetalum, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The flowers bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
