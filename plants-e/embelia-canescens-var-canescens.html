Embelia canescens var. canescens

Embelia canescens var. canescens, commonly known as the graystem embelia, is a small shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, with slender, often grayish stems that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
