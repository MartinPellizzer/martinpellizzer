Erythraea clementii

Erythraea clementii, commonly known as the Clementine erythraea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its delicate floral display.
