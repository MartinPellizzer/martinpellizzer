Eryngium caricinum

Eryngium caricinum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-green bracts. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along sea shores and coastal dunes. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium caricinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
