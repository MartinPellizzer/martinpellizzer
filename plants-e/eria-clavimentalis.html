Eria clavimentalis

Eria clavimentalis, commonly known as the club-shaped eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as tropical forests. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive club-shaped lip.

Eria clavimentalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
