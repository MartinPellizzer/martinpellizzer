Eriogonum sphaerocephalum subsp. tenue

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum subsp.

tenue, commonly known as the slender globemallow, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
