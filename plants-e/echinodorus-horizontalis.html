Echinodorus horizontalis

Echinodorus horizontalis, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, arrow-shaped leaves that float on the water surface or extend just below it. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often found in marshes and ponds. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats that provide habitat for aquatic life.

Echinodorus horizontalis is popular in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
