Epicharis sericea

Epicharis sericea, commonly known as the silky epicharis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Epicharis sericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
