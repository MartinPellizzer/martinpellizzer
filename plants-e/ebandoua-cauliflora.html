Ebandoua cauliflora

Ebandoua cauliflora, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom along the trunk and branches. The plant produces edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp and a central core containing seeds. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The botanical features of Ebandoua cauliflora make it a distinctive and valuable species in tropical horticulture.
