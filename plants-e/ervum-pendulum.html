Ervum pendulum

Ervum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that hang from the stems. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its growth habit is typically climbing or trailing, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
