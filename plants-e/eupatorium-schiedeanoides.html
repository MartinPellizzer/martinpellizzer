Eupatorium schiedeanoides

Eupatorium schiedeanoides, commonly known as the Schiede's thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium schiedeanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
