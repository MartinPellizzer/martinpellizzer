Eubotryoides grayana f. hypoleuca

Eubotryoides grayana f. hypoleuca, commonly known as the white-flowered form of the Chinese bellflower, is a variant of the species native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, contrasting with the typical blue or purple hues of the species. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its foliage is typically lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
