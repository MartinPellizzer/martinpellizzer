Eupatoriadelphus steelei

Eupatoriadelphus steelei, commonly known as steel's eupatorium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and small animals.
