Eriogonum villosissimum

Eriogonum villosissimum, commonly known as the woolly buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It has small, elliptical leaves covered in a thick layer of soft, white hairs.

Eriogonum villosissimum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in open, sunny areas.
