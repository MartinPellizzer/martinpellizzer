Elsholtzia luteola

Elsholtzia luteola, commonly known as yellow elsholtzia, is a flowering plant native to regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to golden flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a fragrant scent.

Elsholtzia luteola is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
