Eugenia eburnea

Eugenia eburnea, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are glossy and emit a pleasant aroma when crushed.

Eugenia eburnea thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.
