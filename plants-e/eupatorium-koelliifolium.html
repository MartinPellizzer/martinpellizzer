Eupatorium koelliifolium

Eupatorium koelliifolium, commonly known as the koelliifolium eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Eupatorium koelliifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
