Euphorbia issykkulensis

Euphorbia issykkulensis, commonly known as the Issykkul spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.
