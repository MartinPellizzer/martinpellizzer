Escallonia thyrsoidea

Escallonia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the silverleaf escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are evergreen and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Escallonia thyrsoidea is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
