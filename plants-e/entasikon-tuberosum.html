Entasikon tuberosum

Entasikon tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous entasikon, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which are the primary storage organs for nutrients and water. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its botanical features make it suitable for cultivation in moist, well-drained soils.

The tubers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
