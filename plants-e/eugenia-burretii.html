Eugenia burretii

Eugenia burretii, commonly known as the Burret's eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
