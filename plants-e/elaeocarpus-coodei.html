Elaeocarpus coodei

Elaeocarpus coodei, commonly known as the Coode's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
