Erigeron pulchellus

Erigeron pulchellus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves and grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides. It is often found in moist to wet soils and is a common component of wetland ecosystems.

Erigeron pulchellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
