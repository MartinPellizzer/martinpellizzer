Eichhornia venezuelensis

Eichhornia venezuelensis, commonly known as the Venezuelan water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It possesses broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces striking purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive three-lobed bract. Its roots are adapted for absorbing nutrients from the water, which can lead to rapid growth in nutrient-rich environments.

However, its invasive nature can disrupt local ecosystems if not managed properly.
