Ebenus teysmannii

Ebenus teysmannii, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has a short stem and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Ebenus teysmannii is adapted to arid conditions and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
