Euphrasia carolensis

Euphrasia carolensis, commonly known as the Carolina springbeauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features delicate, showy flowers with five petals and a distinctive yellow nectar guide. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Euphrasia carolensis plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
