Enydra integrifolia

Enydra integrifolia, commonly known as marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, often saturated soils.

Enydra integrifolia is often found in swamps, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.
