Eulophia inandensis

Eulophia inandensis, commonly known as the Andean eulophia, is a species of orchid native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its elegant, strap-like leaves and strikingly colored flowers that often feature intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer months.

Eulophia inandensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
