Elaeocarpus firdausii

Elaeocarpus firdausii, commonly known as the Firdaus's elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
