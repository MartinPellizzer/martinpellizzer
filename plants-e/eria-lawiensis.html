Eria lawiensis

Eria lawiensis, commonly known as the Law's orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Eria lawiensis typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value has made it a sought-after plant in horticulture, though it remains relatively rare in cultivation.
