Euphorbia celastroides var. ingrata

Euphorbia celastroides var. ingrata, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent shrub native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its distinctive floral structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
