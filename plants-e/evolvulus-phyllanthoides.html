Evolvulus phyllanthoides

Evolvulus phyllanthoides, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are covered in fine, white hairs.

Evolvulus phyllanthoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
