Eucalyptus perriniana

Eucalyptus perriniana, commonly known as the mountain mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.

Eucalyptus perriniana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
