Eberlanzia triticiformis var. subglobosa

Eberlanzia triticiformis var. subglobosa, commonly known as the globe-shaped false wheat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular growth habit and small, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, three-lobed husk, resembling the structure of wheat grains.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
