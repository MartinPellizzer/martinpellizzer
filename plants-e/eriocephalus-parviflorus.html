Eriocephalus parviflorus

Eriocephalus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered eriocephalus, is a small shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its high biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
