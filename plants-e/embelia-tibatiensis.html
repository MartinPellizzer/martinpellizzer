Embelia tibatiensis

Embelia tibatiensis, commonly known as the Tibetan embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Embelia tibatiensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.
