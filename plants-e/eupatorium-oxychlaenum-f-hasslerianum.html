Eupatorium oxychlaenum f. hasslerianum

Eupatorium oxychlaenum f. hasslerianum, commonly known as the Hassler's false dragonhead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and often covered with fine hairs.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.
