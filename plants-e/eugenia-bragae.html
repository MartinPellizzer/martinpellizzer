Eugenia bragae

Eugenia bragae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia brasiliensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma.

Eugenia bragae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
