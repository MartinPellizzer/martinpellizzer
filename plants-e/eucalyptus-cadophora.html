Eucalyptus cadophora

Eucalyptus cadophora, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or cream-colored flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. Eucalyptus cadophora is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
