Erica erigena

Erica erigena, commonly known as the heath plant, is a species of flowering shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and purple. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats such as heathlands and moorlands. Erica erigena is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes other heathers and related plants.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
