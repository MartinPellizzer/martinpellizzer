Encephalartos longifolius

Encephalartos longifolius, commonly known as the Natal onion fern, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fan-shaped foliage composed of long, narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, sunny environment.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
