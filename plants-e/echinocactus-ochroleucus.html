Echinocactus ochroleucus

Echinocactus ochroleucus, commonly known as the yellow-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. Its spines are relatively short and sparse, providing minimal protection but contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Echinocactus ochroleucus is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
