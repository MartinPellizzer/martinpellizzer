Eryngium uruguayense

Eryngium uruguayense, commonly known as the Uruguay thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Eryngium uruguayense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
