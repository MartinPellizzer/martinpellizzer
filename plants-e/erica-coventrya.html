Erica coventrya

Erica coventrya, commonly known as the Coventry heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica coventrya is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense thickets that provide habitat for various wildlife.

It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and requires minimal water once established.
