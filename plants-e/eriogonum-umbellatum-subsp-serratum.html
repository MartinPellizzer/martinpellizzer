Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. serratum

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

serratum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically serrated, which is reflected in its subspecies name, and are often hairy. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun.

This species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
