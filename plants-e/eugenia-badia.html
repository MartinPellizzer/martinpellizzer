Eugenia badia

Eugenia badia, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, star-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The wood of Eugenia badia is also valued for its durability and aromatic properties.
