Eugenia subalterna

Eugenia subalterna, commonly known as the lesser eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia subalterna is often found in humid, shaded environments within dense forests.
