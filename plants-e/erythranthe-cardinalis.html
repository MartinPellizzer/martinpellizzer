Erythranthe cardinalis

Erythranthe cardinalis, commonly known as Cardinal's flower, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Erythranthe cardinalis is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
