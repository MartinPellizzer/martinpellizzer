Epilobium californicum var. exaltatum

Epilobium californicum var. exaltatum, commonly known as the California willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
