Euphorbia flamandii

Euphorbia flamandii, commonly known as the Flamand's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble flowers. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
