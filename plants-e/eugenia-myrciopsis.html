Eugenia myrciopsis

Eugenia myrciopsis, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
