Eriobotrya tinctoria

Eriobotrya tinctoria, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, pinnate leaves. The plant produces white flowers in spring, followed by edible, aromatic fruits that are similar to pears. Its fruits are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of natural dyes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.
