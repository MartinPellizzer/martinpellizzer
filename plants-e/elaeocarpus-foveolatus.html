Elaeocarpus foveolatus

Elaeocarpus foveolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, woody, spherical fruits that are typically clustered in groups of three. Its bark is smooth and light brown, and the wood is valued for its fine grain and durability.

Elaeocarpus foveolatus is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
