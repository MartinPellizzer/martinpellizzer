Eatonia intermedia

Eatonia intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate eatonia, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish-orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
