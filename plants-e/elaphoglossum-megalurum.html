Elaphoglossum megalurum

Elaphoglossum megalurum, commonly known as the giant holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This fern is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in dense forests or along stream banks.

Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically arranged in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds.
