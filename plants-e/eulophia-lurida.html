Eulophia lurida

Eulophia lurida, commonly known as the pale eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant has a slender, upright stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in a spike, and are known for their subtle fragrance.

Eulophia lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
