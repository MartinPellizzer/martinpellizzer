Eupatorium simonsii

Eupatorium simonsii, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium simonsii plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
