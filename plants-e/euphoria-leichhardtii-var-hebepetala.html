Euphoria leichhardtii var. hebepetala

Euphoria leichhardtii var. hebepetala, commonly known as the pink-flowered desert everlasting, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ability to survive in extreme conditions makes it a resilient species adapted to dry, sandy soils.

The varietal name *hebepetala* refers to its petal-like appearance, which is distinct from the typical foliage of the species.
