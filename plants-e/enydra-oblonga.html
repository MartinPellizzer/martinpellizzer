Enydra oblonga

Enydra oblonga, commonly known as water spinach or kangkong, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating in water, contributing to its aquatic appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by cylindrical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist environments and is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are used in various cuisines.

Enydra oblonga is also valued for its ability to purify water and stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
