Epidendrum vesicatum var. roseum

Epidendrum vesicatum var. roseum, commonly known as the Pink Lady's Slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features showy, pinkish-red flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch that attracts pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The orchid blooms in spring and summer, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that add ornamental value to gardens.
