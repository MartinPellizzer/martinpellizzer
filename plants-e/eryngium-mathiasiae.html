Eryngium mathiasiae

Eryngium mathiasiae, commonly known as the Mathias' thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a composite flower, with tightly packed ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture.

Eryngium mathiasiae is often found in alpine environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
