Eriogonum acaule

Eriogonum acaule, commonly known as the leafless buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with the stems bearing clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open areas such as meadows and sagebrush steppe.
