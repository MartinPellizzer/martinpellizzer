Eucalyptus mimica

Eucalyptus mimica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
