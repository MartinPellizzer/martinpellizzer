Echites glaucescens

Echites glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually blue-gray in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate floral display. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Echites glaucescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
