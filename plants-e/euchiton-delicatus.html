Euchiton delicatus

Euchiton delicatus, commonly known as the delicate echinocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, globular shape and smooth, greenish-gray skin. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and endangered species.
