Erythroxylum pallidum

Erythroxylum pallidum, commonly known as the pale copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers and elliptical leaves with a smooth surface. It produces a milky latex sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Erythroxylum.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
