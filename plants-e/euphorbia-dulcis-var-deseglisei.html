Euphorbia dulcis var. deseglisei

Euphorbia dulcis var. deseglisei, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a branching, tree-like structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers, often surrounded by leaf-like structures. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, spiny structures, and it has a slow growth rate.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments.
