Euonymus mexicanus

Euonymus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican woodbine, is a woody vine native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cistaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive orange-red berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Euonymus mexicanus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
