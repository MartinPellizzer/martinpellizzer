Eragrostis secundiflora subsp. oxylepis

Eragrostis secundiflora subsp.

oxylepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

This grass is often found in open grasslands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
