Epidendrum rhodanthum

Epidendrum rhodanthum, commonly known as the red-flowered epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow or green throat. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Epidendrum rhodanthum is prized in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
