Echium confusum var. perplexans

Echium confusum var. perplexans, commonly known as the perplexing evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers that bloom in the late afternoon and evening. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

This variant is noted for its unique floral display and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
