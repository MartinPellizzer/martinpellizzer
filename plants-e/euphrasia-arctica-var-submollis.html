Euphrasia arctica var. submollis

Euphrasia arctica var. submollis, commonly known as Arctic eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Arctic and subarctic regions.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has slender, hairy stems and oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Euphrasia arctica var. submollis is often found in tundra and alpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
