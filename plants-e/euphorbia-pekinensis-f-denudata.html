Euphorbia pekinensis f. denudata

Euphorbia pekinensis f. denudata, commonly known as the naked euphorbia, is a unique variant of the pekinensis euphorbia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with stems that are often covered in a waxy, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its floral structures. It is native to China and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its distinctive appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent collections.
