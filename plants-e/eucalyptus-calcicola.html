Eucalyptus calcicola

Eucalyptus calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, usually appearing in late winter to early spring. Eucalyptus calcicola is adapted to harsh, dry conditions and is often found growing on sandy or rocky soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
