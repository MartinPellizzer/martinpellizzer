Euphorbia nutans

Euphorbia nutans, commonly known as the hanging spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, cylindrical stems that bear clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia nutans thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
