Erianthus saccharoides var. compactus

Erianthus saccharoides var. compactus, commonly known as dwarf sugarcane, is a compact, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features tall, sturdy stalks covered in dense, fibrous leaves that can reach up to several feet in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, typical of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and have a slightly rough texture, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in biofuel production due to its high biomass yield.
