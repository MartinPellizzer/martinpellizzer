Elaeocarpus subpetiolatus

Elaeocarpus subpetiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a short petiole. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
