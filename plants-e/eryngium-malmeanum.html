Eryngium malmeanum

Eryngium malmeanum, commonly known as the Malmean thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough, hairy texture.

Eryngium malmeanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
