Eupatoriastrum clavisetum

Eupatoriastrum clavisetum, commonly known as the key-armed eupatoriastrum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in dense clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
