Erysimum deflexum

Erysimum deflexum, commonly known as the bent-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are characterized by their drooping or bent appearance, giving it its common name.

Erysimum deflexum produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
