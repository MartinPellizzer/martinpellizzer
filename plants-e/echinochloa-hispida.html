Echinochloa hispida

Echinochloa hispida, commonly known as roughseed grass, is a species of annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and spikelets that are often bristly to the touch. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Echinochloa hispida is sometimes considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
