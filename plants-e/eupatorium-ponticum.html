Eupatorium ponticum

Eupatorium ponticum, commonly known as common boneset, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along waterways, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and can spread widely.

Eupatorium ponticum is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional remedies.
