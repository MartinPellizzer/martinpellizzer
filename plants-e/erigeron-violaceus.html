Erigeron violaceus

Erigeron violaceus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically grows in open fields, prairies, and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Erigeron violaceus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
