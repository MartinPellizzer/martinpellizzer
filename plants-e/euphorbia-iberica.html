Euphorbia iberica

Euphorbia iberica, commonly known as the Iberian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Euphorbia iberica is also noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
