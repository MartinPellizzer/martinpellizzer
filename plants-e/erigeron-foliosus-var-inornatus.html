Erigeron foliosus var. inornatus

Erigeron foliosus var. inornatus, commonly known as the leafy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are white to pale blue in color.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its spread in suitable environments.
