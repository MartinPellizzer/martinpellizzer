Euphrasia rostkoviana var. borealis

Euphrasia rostkoviana var. borealis, commonly known as the northern eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically features small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy petals that resemble eyes, giving it its common name. The plant has a hairy, branched stem and produces numerous tiny, glandular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its unique floral structure and adaptation to cool, temperate climates.
