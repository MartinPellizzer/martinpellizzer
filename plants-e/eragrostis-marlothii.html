Eragrostis marlothii

Eragrostis marlothii, commonly known as Marloth's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that helps it conserve water.

Eragrostis marlothii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
