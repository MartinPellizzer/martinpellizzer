Eleocharis valleculosa

Eleocharis valleculosa, commonly known as the water starwort, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and pond edges. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often white or pale green. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic habitats.

Eleocharis valleculosa is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
