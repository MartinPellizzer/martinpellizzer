Emilia bellioides

Emilia bellioides, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow to white. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Emilia bellioides is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional herbal medicine.
