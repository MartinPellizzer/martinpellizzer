Erigeron plurifolius

Erigeron plurifolius, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open areas such as fields and roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are generally hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
