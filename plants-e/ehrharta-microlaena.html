Ehrharta microlaena

Ehrharta microlaena, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to reddish spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soil, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Ehrharta microlaena is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
