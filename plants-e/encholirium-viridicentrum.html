Encholirium viridicentrum

Encholirium viridicentrum, commonly known as the green-centered grass.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup. It produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

The species is noted for its unique leaf structure and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
