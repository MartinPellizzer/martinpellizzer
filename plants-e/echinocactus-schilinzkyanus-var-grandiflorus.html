Echinocactus schilinzkyanus var. grandiflorus

Echinocactus schilinzkyanus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often borne on long, branched pedicels. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection against herbivores and reducing water loss.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
