Elymus elymoides var. hordeoides

Elymus elymoides var. hordeoides, commonly known as the jointed goatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically found in clusters along the stem, with each spikelet containing several small, awned flowers. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, fields, and along roadsides.

This species is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
