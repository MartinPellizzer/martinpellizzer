Erica infundibuliformis

Erica infundibuliformis, commonly known as the Cape bell heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Erica infundibuliformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a coastal plant due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
