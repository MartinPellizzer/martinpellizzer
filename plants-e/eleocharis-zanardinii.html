Eleocharis zanardinii

Eleocharis zanardinii, commonly known as the Zanardi's spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to certain regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms in its native wetlands.
