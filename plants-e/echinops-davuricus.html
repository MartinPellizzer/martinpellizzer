Echinops davuricus

Echinops davuricus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by the distinctive spiny bracts.

Echinops davuricus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
