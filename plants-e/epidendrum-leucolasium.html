Epidendrum leucolasium

Epidendrum leucolasium, commonly known as the White-lipped Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences and striking white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Epidendrum leucolasium is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.
