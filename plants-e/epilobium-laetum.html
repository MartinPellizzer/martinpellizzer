Epilobium laetum

Epilobium laetum, commonly known as the yellow willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Epilobium laetum is often used in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and diuretic properties.
