Euplassa legalis

Euplassa legalis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy, brown seeds in its conical flower head. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Euplassa legalis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
