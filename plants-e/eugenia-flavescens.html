Eugenia flavescens

Eugenia flavescens, commonly known as the Java apple or yellow eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia flavescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
