Eryngium gracile

Eryngium gracile, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spiky blue or purple flowers. Its structure is characterized by a central stem with bracts that form a spiny crown.

Eryngium gracile is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
