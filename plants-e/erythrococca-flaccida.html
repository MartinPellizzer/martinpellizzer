Erythrococca flaccida

Erythrococca flaccida, commonly known as the red henna plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its unique appearance and historical use in traditional practices, it is of interest in both botanical and cultural studies.
