Erythrocephalum bicolor

Erythrocephalum bicolor, commonly known as the pinkhead or redhead sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Erythrocephalum bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
