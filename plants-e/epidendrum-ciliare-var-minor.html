Epidendrum ciliare var. minor

Epidendrum ciliare var. minor, commonly known as the lesser ciliated epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
