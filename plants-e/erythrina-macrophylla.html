Erythrina macrophylla

Erythrina macrophylla, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod, and the plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant floral display.

Erythrina macrophylla is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
