Escobaria missouriensis

Escobaria missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the central United States.

It is a low-growing plant with a short stem and clusters of spiny, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Escobaria missouriensis is a drought-tolerant plant that plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
