Epidendrum baryanthum

Epidendrum baryanthum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it one of the most striking orchids in the genus. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive yellow or orange lip that attracts pollinators.
