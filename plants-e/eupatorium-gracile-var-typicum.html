Eupatorium gracile var. typicum

Eupatorium gracile var. typicum, commonly known as slender boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often branched and covered with fine, white hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.
