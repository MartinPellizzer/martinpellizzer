Erythrodes major

Erythrodes major, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range from red to pink in color, with five broad petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
