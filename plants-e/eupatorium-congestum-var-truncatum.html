Eupatorium congestum var. truncatum

Eupatorium congestum var. truncatum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in herbal remedies.
