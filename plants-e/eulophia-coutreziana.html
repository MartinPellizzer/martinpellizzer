Eulophia coutreziana

Eulophia coutreziana, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a sympodial growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a popular subject in botanical research and conservation efforts.
