Erica straussiana

Erica straussiana, commonly known as the Strauss's heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica straussiana is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
