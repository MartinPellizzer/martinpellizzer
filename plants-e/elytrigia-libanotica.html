Elytrigia libanotica

Elytrigia libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and steppes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Elytrigia libanotica is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
