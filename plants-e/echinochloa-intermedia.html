Echinochloa intermedia

Echinochloa intermedia, commonly known as rough false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Echinochloa intermedia is an annual plant with a short life cycle, typically flowering and setting seed within a single growing season.
