Erigeron lalehzaricus

Erigeron lalehzaricus, commonly known as the Persian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
