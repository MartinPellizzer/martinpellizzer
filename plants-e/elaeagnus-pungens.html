Elaeagnus pungens

Elaeagnus pungens, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil fertility through nitrogen fixation. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in dry climates.
