Euphorbia linearifolia var. nallamalayana

Euphorbia linearifolia var. nallamalayana, commonly known as the Nallamalayana spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
