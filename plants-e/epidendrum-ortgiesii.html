Epidendrum ortgiesii

Epidendrum ortgiesii, commonly known as the Orange-tinged Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, orange-pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Epidendrum ortgiesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
