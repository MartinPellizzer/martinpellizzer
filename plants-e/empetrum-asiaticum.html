Empetrum asiaticum

Empetrum asiaticum, commonly known as Asian crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cold climates of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces tiny, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in rocky, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic regions.

Its ability to survive in harsh environments makes it an important component of cold climate ecosystems.
