Eurya myrtifolia

Eurya myrtifolia, commonly known as the glossyleaf eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya myrtifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and seasonal interest.
