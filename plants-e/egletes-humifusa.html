Egletes humifusa

Egletes humifusa, commonly known as the groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough, hairy texture, and the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Egletes humifusa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
