Epilobium sertulatum

Epilobium sertulatum, commonly known as the clustered willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pairs of opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
