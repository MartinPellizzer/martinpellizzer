Eriocaulon ciliipetalum

Eriocaulon ciliipetalum, commonly known as the ciliated-petaled spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often surrounded by short, hairy bracts. It thrives in wet, boggy environments and is often found in marshes or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists studying wetland ecosystems.
