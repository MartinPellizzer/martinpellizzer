Eupatorium ayapana

Eupatorium ayapana, commonly known as Ayapana or the sacred herb, is a perennial shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Eupatorium ayapana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
