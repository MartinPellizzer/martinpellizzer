Eremurus jungei

Eremurus jungei, commonly known as the Japanese sasparilla, is a striking plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces a thick, fleshy root that is often used in traditional medicine. Eremurus jungei thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and medicinal properties make it a valued plant in both horticulture and herbal remedies.
