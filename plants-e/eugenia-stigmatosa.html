Eugenia stigmatosa

Eugenia stigmatosa, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to black as they mature.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
