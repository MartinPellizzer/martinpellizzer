Ehretia cymosa var. divaricata

Ehretia cymosa var. divaricata, commonly known as the hairy leaf mahonia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy, especially along the veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
