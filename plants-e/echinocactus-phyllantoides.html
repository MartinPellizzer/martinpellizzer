Echinocactus phyllantoides

Echinocactus phyllantoides, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny structure. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
