Eriogonum fremonti

Eriogonum fremonti, commonly known as fremont's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, often sprawling growth habit and its clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, shrublands, and along rocky slopes.

Eriogonum fremonti plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
