Erigeron acris var. manshuricus

Erigeron acris var. manshuricus, commonly known as the Siberian blueweed, is a perennial herb native to eastern Siberia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and riverbanks, preferring cool, temperate climates. It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to purple ray flowers.

Its medicinal properties have been explored for their potential anti-inflammatory and antioxidant effects.
