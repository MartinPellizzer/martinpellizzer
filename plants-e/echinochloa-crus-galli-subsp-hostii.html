Echinochloa crus-galli subsp. hostii

Echinochloa crus-galli subsp.

hostii, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its broad, flat blades and a dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and agricultural areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish spikelets.

The subspecies hostii is distinguished by its broader leaves and more robust growth compared to other forms of E. crus-galli.
