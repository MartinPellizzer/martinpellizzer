Echinodorus fluitans

Echinodorus fluitans, commonly known as the floating heart, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with the roots submerged in the water. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments and prefers moderate to high light conditions. It is often used in aquascaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.

Echinodorus fluitans is also known for its ability to absorb excess nutrients, making it beneficial for water quality in ponds and aquariums.
