Erica princeps

Erica princeps, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes various heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica princeps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
