Eria biglandulosa

Eria biglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant features prominently veined, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and typically white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences.

Eria biglandulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
