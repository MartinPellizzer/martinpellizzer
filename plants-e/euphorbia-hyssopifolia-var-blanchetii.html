Euphorbia hyssopifolia var. blanchetii

Euphorbia hyssopifolia var. blanchetii, commonly known as the Blanquet's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white or pale green sap. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish flowers surrounded by bright green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
