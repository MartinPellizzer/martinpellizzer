Eranthemum album

Eranthemum album, commonly known as the white eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eranthemum album is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
