Eriogonum ovalifolium var. monarchense

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. monarchense, commonly known as the Monarch's eriogonum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of yellow to orange flowers. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This species thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
