Elymus caninus var. behmii

Elymus caninus var. behmii, commonly known as the Bebm's goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces spikelet clusters that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

This variant is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological resilience.
