Eucalyptus leiophloia

Eucalyptus leiophloia, commonly known as the smooth-barked gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels off in thin sheets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery or grayish underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
