Euplassa

Euplassa, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that resemble small raindrops, hence its common name. Euplassa species typically grow as small trees or shrubs, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and the plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.
