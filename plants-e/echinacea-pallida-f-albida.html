Echinacea pallida f. albida

Echinacea pallida f. albida, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a cultivar of the purple coneflower species.

It is characterized by its pale white to pale pink petals and a prominent yellow central disk. This plant is native to the central United States and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet.

The white coneflower is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
