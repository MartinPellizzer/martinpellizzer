Eleocharis acutangula subsp. neotropica

Eleocharis acutangula subsp.

neotropica, commonly known as the tropical spike-rush, is a grass-like plant native to the Americas. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
