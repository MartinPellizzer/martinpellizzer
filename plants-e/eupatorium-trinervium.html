Eupatorium trinervium

Eupatorium trinervium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Eupatorium trinervium is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
