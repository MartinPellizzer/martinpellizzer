Echinocactus ottonis var. tortuosus

Echinocactus ottonis var. tortuosus, commonly known as the twisted hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise spiky appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
