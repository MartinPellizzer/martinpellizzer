Eumecanthus colimae

Eumecanthus colimae, commonly known as the Mexican redwood, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Podocarpaceae family and is characterized by its tall, evergreen tree form with a straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
