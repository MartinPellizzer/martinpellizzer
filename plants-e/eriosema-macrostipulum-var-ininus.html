Eriosema macrostipulum var. ininus

Eriosema macrostipulum var. ininus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with a distinctive glandular margin. The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and typically have five petals with a prominent midrib. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
