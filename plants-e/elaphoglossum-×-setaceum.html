Elaphoglossum × setaceum

Elaphoglossum × setaceum, commonly known as the hybrid maidenhair fern, is a fern species native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Elaphoglossum × setaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
