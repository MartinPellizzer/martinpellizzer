Eunanus fremontii

Eunanus fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eunanus fremontii is an important species in its native habitat, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological balance.
