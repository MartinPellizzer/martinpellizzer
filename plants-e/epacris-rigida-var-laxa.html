Epacris rigida var. laxa

Epacris rigida var. laxa, commonly known as the lax flowered epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands. It produces showy, pink to purple flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
