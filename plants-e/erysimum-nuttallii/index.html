Erysimum Nuttallii

Nuttall's wallflower, scientifically known as Erysimum nuttallii, is a striking member of the mustard family native to the western United States. This perennial plant is celebrated for its vibrant yellow and white flowers that bloom in late spring and early summer. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions across Nevada, Utah, and Arizona. With its delicate appearance and hardy nature, it has become a symbol of resilience in harsh landscapes. Despite its beauty, Nuttall's wallflower remains relatively obscure, making it a fascinating subject for botanists and nature enthusiasts alike.
