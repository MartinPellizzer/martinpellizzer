Eruca tanacetifolia

Eruca tanacetifolia, commonly known as wild mustard or Italian rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in Mediterranean regions, characterized by its upright stems and basal leaves that are lobed and somewhat fern-like. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by slender, elongated pods containing seeds. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery, mustard-like flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
