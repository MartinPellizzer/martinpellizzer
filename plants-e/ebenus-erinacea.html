Ebenus erinacea

Ebenus erinacea, commonly known as the hairy sandwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy structures. Ebenus erinacea thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dunes.

It is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
