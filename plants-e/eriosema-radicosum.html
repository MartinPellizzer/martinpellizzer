Eriosema radicosum

Eriosema radicosum, commonly known as the sticky root eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Eriosema radicosum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
