Erianthus tinctorius

Erianthus tinctorius, commonly known as the bluegrass or Indian grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and erect growth habit, which makes it visually striking. The plant is often found in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in full sun. Its fibrous roots contribute to soil stabilization, and it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Erianthus tinctorius is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping and its historical use in traditional crafts.
