Eulophia tenella

Eulophia tenella, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and a single, narrow leaf. The plant produces a spike of small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
