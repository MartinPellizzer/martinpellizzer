Erodium cheilanthifolium var. valentinum

Erodium cheilanthifolium var. valentinum, commonly known as Valentine's parsley, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It blooms from late winter to early spring, making it a valuable early-season nectar source for pollinators.

Its unique appearance and seasonal bloom make it a notable species in local botanical gardens and natural areas.
