Euodia parkinsonii

Euodia parkinsonii, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that contain smooth, black seeds. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Euodia parkinsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional pharmacology.
