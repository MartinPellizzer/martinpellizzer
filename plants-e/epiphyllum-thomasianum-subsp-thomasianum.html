Epiphyllum thomasianum subsp. thomasianum

Epiphyllum thomasianum subsp.

thomasianum, commonly known as the Thomasian night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico. It is a climbing plant with flat, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in spines.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for night-blooming gardens and botanical collections.
