Euphorbia saratoi

Euphorbia saratoi, commonly known as the Saratov spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
