Eugenia colnettiana

Eugenia colnettiana, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Eugenia colnettiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
