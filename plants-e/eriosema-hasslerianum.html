Eriosema hasslerianum

Eriosema hasslerianum, commonly known as the velvetleaf senna, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a pod that splits open to release small, bean-like seeds.

Eriosema hasslerianum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
