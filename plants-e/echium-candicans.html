Echium candicans

Echium candicans, commonly known as the Canary Island fireweed, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous violet to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal or rocky habitats.

Echium candicans is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
