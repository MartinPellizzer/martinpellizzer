Eryngium paniculatum var. oligodon

Eryngium paniculatum var. oligodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has spiny, blue-green foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to both wild and cultivated landscapes.
