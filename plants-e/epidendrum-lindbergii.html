Epidendrum lindbergii

Epidendrum lindbergii, commonly known as the Lindberg's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces striking, long-lasting inflorescences with vibrant, colorful flowers that can range from red to orange and yellow. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitats.
