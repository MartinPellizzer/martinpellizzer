Echites guaraniticus

Echites guaraniticus, commonly known as the Guarani thorn, is a shrub native to the Brazilian Pantanal region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Echites guaraniticus is adapted to thrive in wetland environments and is often found near water bodies.
