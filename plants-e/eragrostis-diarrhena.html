Eragrostis diarrhena

Eragrostis diarrhena, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are narrow with a prominent midvein. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-ranked spikelets.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
