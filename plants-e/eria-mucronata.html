Eria mucronata

Eria mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Eria mucronata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
