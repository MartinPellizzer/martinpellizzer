Erica umbellata var. filiformis

Erica umbellata var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in late summer, which are borne on slender, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
