Erythroxylum macrochaetum

Erythroxylum macrochaetum, commonly known as the long-hair erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

Erythroxylum macrochaetum is primarily of interest for its botanical features rather than for any commercial use.
