Eucalyptus turbinata

Eucalyptus turbinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and distinctive, often twisted branches. The plant produces small, creamy-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its bark is often flaky and can vary in color from grey to brown.

Eucalyptus turbinata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in arid and semi-arid regions.
