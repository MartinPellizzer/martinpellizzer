Epidorkis volucris

Epidorkis volucris, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Epidorkis volucris is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
