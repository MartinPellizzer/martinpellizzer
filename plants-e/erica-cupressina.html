Erica cupressina

Erica cupressina, commonly known as the evergreen heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the western Mediterranean region, including parts of Spain, Portugal, and southern France. This shrub typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its small, needle-like leaves are dark green and emit a slight fragrance, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late spring.

Erica cupressina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
