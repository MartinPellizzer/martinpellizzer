Eupatorium rhinanthaceum var. oinopolepis

Eupatorium rhinanthaceum var. oinopolepis, commonly known as the Greek feverfew, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, with a strong, aromatic scent, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white, appearing in late summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
