Euphorbia ziaratensis

Euphorbia ziaratensis, commonly known as the Ziarat spurge, is a flowering plant native to Pakistan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
