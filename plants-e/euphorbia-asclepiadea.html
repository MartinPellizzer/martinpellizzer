Euphorbia asclepiadea

Euphorbia asclepiadea, commonly known as the milkweed euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and distinctive yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Euphorbia asclepiadea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
