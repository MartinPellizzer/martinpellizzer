Erysimum alyssoides

Erysimum alyssoides, commonly known as cheeseweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, upright stems with alternate leaves. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
