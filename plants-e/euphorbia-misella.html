Euphorbia misella

Euphorbia misella, commonly known as the Cape coral tree, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, tree-like structure with a thick, woody trunk and branching stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color and have a waxy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Euphorbia misella is adapted to survive in dry environments, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
