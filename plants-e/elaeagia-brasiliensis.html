Elaeagia brasiliensis

Elaeagia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Elaeaginaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces bright purple berries that are a striking feature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. Elaeagia brasiliensis is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
