Entada polystachya var. simplicata

Entada polystachya var. simplicata, commonly known as the African tamarind or simple-stalked tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and distinctive, long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers. The tree produces woody pods containing edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in medicine and cuisine.
