Euphorbia eichleri

Euphorbia eichleri, commonly known as the Eichleri's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia eichleri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
