Epidendrum quisayanum

Epidendrum quisayanum, commonly known as the Quezon orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

Epidendrum quisayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
