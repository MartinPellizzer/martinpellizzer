Euosma serpyllifolia

Euosma serpyllifolia, commonly known as field euodia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
