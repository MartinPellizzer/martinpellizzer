Echinocactus rufispinus

Echinocactus rufispinus, commonly known as the red-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in bright red, spiny tubercles that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
