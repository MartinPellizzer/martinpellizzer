Eupatorium thyrsoideum

Eupatorium thyrsoideum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium thyrsoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
