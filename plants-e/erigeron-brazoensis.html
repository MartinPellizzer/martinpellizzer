Erigeron brazoensis

Erigeron brazoensis, commonly known as the Brazilian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, with blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open fields and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
