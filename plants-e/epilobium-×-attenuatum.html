Epilobium × attenuatum

Epilobium × attenuatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a flowering plant in the family Onagraceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, ascending stems covered with glandular hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a reddish or purplish hue.

The flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and have four petals, typically pink or red in color.
