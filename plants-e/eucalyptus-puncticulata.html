Eucalyptus puncticulata

Eucalyptus puncticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The flowers are small, usually white or pale pink, and are clustered in umbels, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
