Echinocactus spiniflorus

Echinocactus spiniflorus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical fruits that resemble small apples. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its adaptations include a deep root system for water absorption and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Echinocactus spiniflorus is well-suited for xeriscaping and is often cultivated in dry gardens due to its low maintenance requirements.
