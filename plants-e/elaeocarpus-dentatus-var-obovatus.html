Elaeocarpus dentatus var. obovatus

Elaeocarpus dentatus var. obovatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its ornamental value is due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit structure, making it a popular choice for horticultural use.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.
