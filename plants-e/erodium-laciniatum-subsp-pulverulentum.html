Erodium laciniatum subsp. pulverulentum

Erodium laciniatum subsp.

pulverulentum, commonly known as the finely divided erodium, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa. It features small, trifoliate leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate, lace-like appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecological researchers.
