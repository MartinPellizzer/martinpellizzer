Eranthemum erythrochilum

Eranthemum erythrochilum, commonly known as the red-lipped eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking red lips on the inner side of its corolla. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are tubular and often attract pollinators such as birds and insects.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
