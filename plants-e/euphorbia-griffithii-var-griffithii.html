Euphorbia griffithii var. griffithii

Euphorbia griffithii var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of India.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to greenish-white. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
