Ericoides filipendulum

Ericoides filipendulum, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late spring and are an important nectar source for pollinators.

Ericoides filipendulum is often found in rocky or forested habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
