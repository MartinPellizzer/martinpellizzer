Eugenia coalita

Eugenia coalita, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Eugenia coalita is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
