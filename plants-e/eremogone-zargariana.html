Eremogone zargariana

Eremogone zargariana, commonly known as the Zargarian's eremogone, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom during the brief rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its resilience and unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
