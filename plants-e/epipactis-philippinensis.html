Epipactis philippinensis

Epipactis philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with purple or brown streaks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Epipactis philippinensis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
