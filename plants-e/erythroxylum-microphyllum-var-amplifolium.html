Erythroxylum microphyllum var. amplifolium

Erythroxylum microphyllum var. amplifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often broad and leathery. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and montane forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
