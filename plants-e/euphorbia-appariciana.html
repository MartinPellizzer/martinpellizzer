Euphorbia appariciana

Euphorbia appariciana, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It features a unique, branched structure with fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are its most noticeable floral features. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its distinctive appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
