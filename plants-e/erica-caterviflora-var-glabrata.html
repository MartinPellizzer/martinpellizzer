Erica caterviflora var. glabrata

Erica caterviflora var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety 'glabrata' is distinguished by its smooth, hairless leaves, which set it apart from other varieties within the species.
