Erodium pallidiflorum

Erodium pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, slender leaves and produces small, dry fruits known as legumes. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
