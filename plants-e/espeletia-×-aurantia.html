Espeletia × aurantia

Espeletia × aurantia, commonly known as the yellow hawksbeard, is a hybrid plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow floral heads and fuzzy, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, contributing to its ornamental value.

Espeletia × aurantia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
