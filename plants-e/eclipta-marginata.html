Eclipta marginata

Eclipta marginata, commonly known as blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue or purple composite flower heads. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in open fields and along roadsides.

Eclipta marginata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
