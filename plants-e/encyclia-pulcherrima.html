Encyclia pulcherrima

Encyclia pulcherrima, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with a single leaf and produces striking red or orange flower spikes. The flowers are borne on branching inflorescences and are characterized by their velvety petals and sepals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
