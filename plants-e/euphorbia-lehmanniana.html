Euphorbia lehmanniana

Euphorbia lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
