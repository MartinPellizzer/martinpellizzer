Echinocereus rayonesensis

Echinocereus rayonesensis, commonly known as the Rayonesensis Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem with prominent, ribbed structures and spines that provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by insects and bats. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
