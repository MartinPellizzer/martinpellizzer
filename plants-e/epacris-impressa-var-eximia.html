Epacris impressa var. eximia

Epacris impressa var. eximia, commonly known as the silver mat flower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white, tubular flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
