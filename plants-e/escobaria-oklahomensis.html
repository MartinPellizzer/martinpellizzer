Escobaria oklahomensis

Escobaria oklahomensis, commonly known as the Oklahoma liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Escobaria oklahomensis is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
