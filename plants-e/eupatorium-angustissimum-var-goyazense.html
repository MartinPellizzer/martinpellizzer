Eupatorium angustissimum var. goyazense

Eupatorium angustissimum var. goyazense, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Asteraceae family.
