Eutrochium steelei

Eutrochium steelei, commonly known as steel's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eutrochium steelei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
