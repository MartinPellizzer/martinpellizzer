Echinopsis erigens

Echinopsis erigens, commonly known as the erect echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming during the night. Its spines are relatively short and sparse compared to other members of the Echinopsis genus.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
