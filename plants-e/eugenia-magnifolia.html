Eugenia magnifolia

Eugenia magnifolia, commonly known as the grand eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. Eugenia magnifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
