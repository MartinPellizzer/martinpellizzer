Erica areolata

Erica areolata, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, mountainous environments. Erica areolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
