Euphorbia nicaeensis var. hispanica

Euphorbia nicaeensis var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and has a slow growth rate.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care.
