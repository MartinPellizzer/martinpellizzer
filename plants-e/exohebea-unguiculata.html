Exohebea unguiculata

Exohebea unguiculata, commonly known as the broad-leaved hebe, is a perennial flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Exohebea unguiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
