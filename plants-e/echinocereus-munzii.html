Echinocereus munzii

Echinocereus munzii, commonly known as the Munz's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features a short, cylindrical stem with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its spines are usually pale to reddish in color, and the plant is well adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Echinocereus munzii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
