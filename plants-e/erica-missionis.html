Erica missionis

Erica missionis, commonly known as Mission heath, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica missionis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
