Ervum venosum

Ervum venosum, commonly known as the common vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and long, slender pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides. Its root system has symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhance soil fertility.

Ervum venosum is also valued for its use in livestock feed and as a green manure crop.
