Echinofossulocactus campylacanthus

Echinofossulocactus campylacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant features a distinctive ribbed structure with prominent areoles from which spines and flowers emerge. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions with minimal water.
