Encholirium longiflorum

Encholirium longiflorum, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a striking bromeliad native to Mexico.

It features vibrant red bracts that surround its yellow flowers, creating a fiery display. The plant is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that collect water, forming a central pool. It thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its bold, eye-catching appearance.
