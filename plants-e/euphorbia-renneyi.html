Euphorbia renneyi

Euphorbia renneyi, commonly known as the Renney's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. Euphorbia renneyi is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study and a valuable addition to xeriscapic gardens.
