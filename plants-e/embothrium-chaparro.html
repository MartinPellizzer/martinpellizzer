Embothrium chaparro

Embothrium chaparro, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in coastal and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Embothrium chaparro is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
