Euphorbia taiwaniana

Euphorbia taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan spurge, is a succulent plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Euphorbia taiwaniana is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
