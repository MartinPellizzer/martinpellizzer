Elettaria anthodioides

Elettaria anthodioides, commonly known as the Indian cardamom or karpuram, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of India and Sri Lanka.

The plant produces a distinctive, pungent fragrance due to the presence of essential oils, primarily cineole and borneol. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while the stems are often hairy.

Elettaria anthodioides is valued not only for its medicinal properties but also for its use in culinary applications.
