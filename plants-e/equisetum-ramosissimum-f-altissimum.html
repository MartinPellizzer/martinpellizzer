Equisetum ramosissimum f. altissimum

Equisetum ramosissimum f. altissimum, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features whorls of small, needle-like leaves along its stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
