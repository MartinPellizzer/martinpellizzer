Echites cajalbanicus

Echites cajalbanicus, commonly known as the Cajeput tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are extracted from its leaves and used in perfumery and traditional medicine.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
