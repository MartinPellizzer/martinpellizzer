Eugenia sclerocalyx var. cambajuvensis

Eugenia sclerocalyx var. cambajuvensis, commonly known as the Yarumal tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
