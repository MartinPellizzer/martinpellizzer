Eremophila platythamnos subsp. platythamnos

Eremophila platythamnos subsp.

platythamnos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which appear in summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resistance.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
