Eucalyptus synandra

Eucalyptus synandra, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a tall, slender tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often peeling bark. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the summer months. Eucalyptus synandra is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought.

Its essential oils contain compounds like eucalyptol, which have medicinal and aromatic uses.
