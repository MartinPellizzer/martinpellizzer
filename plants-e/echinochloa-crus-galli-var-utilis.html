Echinochloa crus-galli var. utilis

Echinochloa crus-galli var. utilis, commonly known as common barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to Africa but now widespread globally.

It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and spikelets that are often reddish in color. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as fields, wetlands, and along waterways. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its rapid growth and competitive nature in agricultural settings.

This weed is often a challenge in crop production due to its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and conditions.
