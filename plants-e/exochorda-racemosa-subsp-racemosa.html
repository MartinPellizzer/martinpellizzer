Exochorda racemosa subsp. racemosa

Exochorda racemosa subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the common snowball bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features showy, rounded clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit with grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
