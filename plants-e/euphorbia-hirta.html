Euphorbia hirta

Euphorbia hirta, commonly known as greater spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered with fine, stiff hairs, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy. It produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Euphorbia hirta is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
