Erianthus viguieri

Erianthus viguieri, commonly known as the golden beard grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, golden-colored inflorescences that resemble a beard. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green hue.

Erianthus viguieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
