Enterolobium schomburgkii

Enterolobium schomburgkii, commonly known as the "whistling bean" or "sabre bean," is a tropical leguminous tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pod-like fruits that can grow up to 1 meter in length, containing edible seeds. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its unique seed pods and nitrogen-fixing ability make it ecologically significant in its native habitats.
