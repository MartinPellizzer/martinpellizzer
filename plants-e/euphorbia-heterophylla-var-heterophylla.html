Euphorbia heterophylla var. heterophylla

Euphorbia heterophylla var. heterophylla, commonly known as the common spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its alternate, usually simple, and sometimes lobed leaves, which vary in shape depending on the environment. Its flowers are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

The plant produces a milky latex sap that can be irritant to the skin.
