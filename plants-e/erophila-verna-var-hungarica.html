Erophila verna var. hungarica

Erophila verna var. hungarica, commonly known as the Hungarian spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. It blooms in early spring, usually between March and May, and has a compact, upright growth habit.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are clustered in inflorescences at the top of the stem.
