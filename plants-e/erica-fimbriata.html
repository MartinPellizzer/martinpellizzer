Erica fimbriata

Erica fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and delicate, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Erica fimbriata is known for its fragrant blooms, which range in color from white to pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its adaptability to coastal and inland environments makes it a resilient and popular choice in horticulture.
