Elwendia chaerophylloides

Elwendia chaerophylloides, commonly known as the silverleaf elwendia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
