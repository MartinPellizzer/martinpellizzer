Eriochloa subglabra

Eriochloa subglabra, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairless or very sparsely hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
