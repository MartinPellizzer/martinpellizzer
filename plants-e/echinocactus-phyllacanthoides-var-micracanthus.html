Echinocactus phyllacanthoides var. micracanthus

Echinocactus phyllacanthoides var. micracanthus, commonly known as the narrow-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
