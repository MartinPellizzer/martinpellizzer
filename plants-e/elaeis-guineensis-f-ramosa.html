Elaeis guineensis f. ramosa

Elaeis guineensis f. ramosa, commonly known as the dwarf oil palm, is a variant of the oil palm characterized by its compact growth habit and shorter stature compared to the standard Elaeis guineensis.

It exhibits a highly branched structure with multiple stems emerging from the base, which contributes to its unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, facilitating its reproductive process. Its botanical features make it suitable for cultivation in smaller spaces, offering a viable alternative to traditional oil palm farming.

This variety is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture and conservation efforts.
