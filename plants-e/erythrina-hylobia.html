Erythrina hylobia

Erythrina hylobia, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. Erythrina hylobia is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and possesses nitrogen-fixing capabilities through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine.
