Erigeron garrettii

Erigeron garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.
