Eugenia cachoeirensis var. sessilifolia

Eugenia cachoeirensis var. sessilifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple passionfruit, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy texture and a short petiole. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by round, purple fruits with a thick, leathery rind. The fruit contains a sweet, aromatic pulp surrounding numerous small, edible seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
