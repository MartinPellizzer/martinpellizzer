Ebenidium monbrettii

Ebenidium monbrettii, commonly known as the African ebony tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Ebenaceae, which includes other species known for their hard, dark wood. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged alternately along the branches. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The tree is valued for its strong, durable wood, which has been traditionally used in crafting and construction.
