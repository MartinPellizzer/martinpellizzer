Erica daphniflora

Erica daphniflora, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly referred to as the heath or heather family. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica daphniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
