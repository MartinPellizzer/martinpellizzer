Euphorbia osyridiformis

Euphorbia osyridiformis, commonly known as the yellow milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is known for its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia osyridiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
