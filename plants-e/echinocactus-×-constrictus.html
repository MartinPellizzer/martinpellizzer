Echinocactus × constrictus

Echinocactus × constrictus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a hybrid species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, ribbed stem that becomes barrel-shaped as it matures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a striking addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
