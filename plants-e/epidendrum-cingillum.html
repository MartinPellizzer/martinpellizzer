Epidendrum cingillum

Epidendrum cingillum, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

Epidendrum cingillum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
