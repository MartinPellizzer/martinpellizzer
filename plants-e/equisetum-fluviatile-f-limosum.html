Equisetum fluviatile f. limosum

Equisetum fluviatile f. limosum, commonly known as the swamp horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, segmented stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically found near water bodies. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Paleozoic era, making it one of the oldest living plant groups.
