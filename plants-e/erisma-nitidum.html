Erisma nitidum

Erisma nitidum, commonly known as the glossy erica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, often with a slight waxiness to their surface. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to maritime climates with frequent coastal winds.

Erisma nitidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, compact foliage.
