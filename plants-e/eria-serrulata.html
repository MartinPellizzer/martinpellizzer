Eria serrulata

Eria serrulata, commonly known as the serrated eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, giving it its common name.

Eria serrulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
