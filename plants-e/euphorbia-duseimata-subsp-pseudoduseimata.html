Euphorbia duseimata subsp. pseudoduseimata

Euphorbia duseimata subsp.

pseudoduseimata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
