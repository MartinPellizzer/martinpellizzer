Eupatorium fortunei var. glandulosum

Eupatorium fortunei var. glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
