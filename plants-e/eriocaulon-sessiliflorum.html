Eriocaulon sessiliflorum

Eriocaulon sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower marsh plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized adaptations for aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with short, creeping stems and small, clustered flowers that are often subtended by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the flowers are sessile, meaning they lack stalks, giving the plant its common name.

Eriocaulon sessiliflorum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
