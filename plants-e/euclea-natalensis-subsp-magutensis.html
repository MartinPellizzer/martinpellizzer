Euclea natalensis subsp. magutensis

Euclea natalensis subsp.

magutensis, commonly known as the Natal ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for local use.
