Erigeron eatonii var. eatonii

Erigeron eatonii var. eatonii, commonly known as Eaton's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along creek banks. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland ecosystems.
