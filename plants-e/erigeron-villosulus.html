Erigeron villosulus

Erigeron villosulus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

It produces small, yellow-centered flowers that bloom in the summer months.
