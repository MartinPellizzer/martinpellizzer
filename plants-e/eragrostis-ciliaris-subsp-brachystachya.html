Eragrostis ciliaris subsp. brachystachya

Eragrostis ciliaris subsp.

brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stalked beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a hairy underside.

The inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, two-ranked spikelets. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
