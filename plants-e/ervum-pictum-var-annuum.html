Ervum pictum var. annuum

Ervum pictum var. annuum, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, bright orange to red ray flowers and green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, and it is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to gardens.
