Escobaria abdita

Escobaria abdita, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, star-shaped leaves. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a hardy species that thrives in full sun.
