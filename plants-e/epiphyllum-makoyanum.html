Epiphyllum makoyanum

Epiphyllum makoyanum, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, leaf-like stems that are adapted for storing water. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically open at night, emitting a strong fragrance. Its stems can grow up to several meters in length and often climb or trail along supports.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink and are followed by small, round fruits.
