Eryngium aquaticum var. synchaetum

Eryngium aquaticum var. synchaetum, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet or marshy habitats, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water edges.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in aquatic ecosystems.
