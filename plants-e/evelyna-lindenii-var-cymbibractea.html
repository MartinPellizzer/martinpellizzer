Evelyna lindenii var. cymbibractea

Evelyna lindenii var. cymbibractea, commonly known as the velvetleaf lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a slight sheen. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically heart-shaped and can grow up to 12 inches in length.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in humid, warm climates.
