Euphorbia pseudodidiereoides

Euphorbia pseudodidiereoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures containing both male and female flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and has a milky sap that can be toxic.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
