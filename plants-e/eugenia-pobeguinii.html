Eugenia pobeguinii

Eugenia pobeguinii, commonly known as the Pobeguin's evergreen, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia pobeguinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native habitat.
