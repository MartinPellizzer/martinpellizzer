Eragrostis desolata

Eragrostis desolata, commonly known as desert lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in poor, sandy soils, making it well adapted to harsh environments.

Eragrostis desolata is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
