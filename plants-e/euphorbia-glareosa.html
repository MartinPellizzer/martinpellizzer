Euphorbia glareosa

Euphorbia glareosa, commonly known as the shining spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
