Eragrostis interrupta var. namaquensis

Eragrostis interrupta var. namaquensis, commonly known as theNamaqua lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping and for erosion control due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
