Eupatorium prunellifolium

Eupatorium prunellifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It has opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Its showy inflorescences bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
