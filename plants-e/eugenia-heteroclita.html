Eugenia heteroclita

Eugenia heteroclita, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

Eugenia heteroclita is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
