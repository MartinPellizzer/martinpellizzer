Eriogonum umbellatum var. desereticum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. desereticum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert scrub and rocky soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a hardy and resilient species in its native habitat.
