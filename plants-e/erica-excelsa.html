Erica excelsa

Erica excelsa, commonly known as the silver tree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, silvery-green foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. Erica excelsa is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and can thrive in coastal areas.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environments.
