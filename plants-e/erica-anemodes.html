Erica anemodes

Erica anemodes, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica anemodes thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
