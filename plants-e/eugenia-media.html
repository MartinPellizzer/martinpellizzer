Eugenia media

Eugenia media, commonly known as the common guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when mature. Eugenia media thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
