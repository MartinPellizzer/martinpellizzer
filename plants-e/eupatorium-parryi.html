Eupatorium parryi

Eupatorium parryi, commonly known as Parry's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called cymes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments.

Eupatorium parryi is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
