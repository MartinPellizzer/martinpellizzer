Elaeocarpus hinau

Elaeocarpus hinau, commonly known as the Hinau hinau, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
