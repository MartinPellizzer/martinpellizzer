Eriophorum × rufescens

Eriophorum × rufescens, commonly known as the redstem sedgeland, is a hybrid plant species within the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its reddish stems and is typically found in wetland or moist soil environments. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. Its morphology resembles other sedges, with narrow, grass-like leaves and a rhizomatous growth habit.

Eriophorum × rufescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
