Eryngium oblanceolatum

Eryngium oblanceolatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pinkish flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are composed of small, tubular florets surrounded by a series of bracts, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium oblanceolatum is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
