Euphorbia lasiocaula var. pseudolucorum

Euphorbia lasiocaula var. pseudolucorum, commonly known as the silver euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery wax, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
