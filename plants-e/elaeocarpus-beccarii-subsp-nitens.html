Elaeocarpus beccarii subsp. nitens

Elaeocarpus beccarii subsp.

nitens, commonly known as the shining elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, fleshy seeds, which are dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
