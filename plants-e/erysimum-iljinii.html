Erysimum iljinii

Erysimum iljinii, commonly known as the Iljin's mustard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, often arranged in racemes, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

Erysimum iljinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
