Elaeocarpus subdenticulatus

Elaeocarpus subdenticulatus, commonly known as the toothed elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
