Echinocactus gibbosus var. leucacanthus

Echinocactus gibbosus var. leucacanthus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny tubercles that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom from the areoles located along the stem. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature in xeriscaping.
