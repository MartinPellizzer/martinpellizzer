Euonymus vaganoides

Euonymus vaganoides, commonly known as the creeping euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for ground cover or as a low hedge in landscaping.
