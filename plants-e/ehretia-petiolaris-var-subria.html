Ehretia petiolaris var. subria

Ehretia petiolaris var. subria, commonly known as the subri variety of the petioled ehretia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The wood of this species is lightweight and has been used traditionally in various crafts and construction.
