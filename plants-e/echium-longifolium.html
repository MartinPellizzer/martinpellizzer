Echium longifolium

Echium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Echium longifolium is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
