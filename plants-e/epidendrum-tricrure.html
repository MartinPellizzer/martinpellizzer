Epidendrum tricrure

Epidendrum tricrure, commonly known as the Three-ribbed Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America, particularly found in tropical regions. The plant typically grows as a sympodial herb, forming pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its inflorescence bears clusters of fragrant, showy flowers.

Epidendrum tricrure is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
