Echinocactus hypocrateriformis

Echinocactus hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stems that resemble a barrel, which are covered in a layer of white, waxy hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ability to store water, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
