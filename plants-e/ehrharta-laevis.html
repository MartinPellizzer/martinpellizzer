Ehrharta laevis

Ehrharta laevis, commonly known as silky grass or silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental grass. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

Its low maintenance requirements and attractive appearance make it suitable for landscaping and erosion control.
