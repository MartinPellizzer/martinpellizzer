Eremitis vinacea

Eremitis vinacea, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking red petals that resemble those of a spider, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its botanical characteristics include a bulbous root system and lance-shaped leaves.

Eremitis vinacea blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of showy, red flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
