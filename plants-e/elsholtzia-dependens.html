Elsholtzia dependens

Elsholtzia dependens, commonly known as the hanging elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Elsholtzia dependens is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant in high-altitude regions.
