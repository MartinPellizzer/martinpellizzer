Eulophia lanceolata

Eulophia lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaf eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia lanceolata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
