Epiblastus acuminatus

Epiblastus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-tipped epiblastus, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, pointed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, which helps reduce water loss. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences.

Due to its unique appearance and growth pattern, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation in warm, humid environments.
