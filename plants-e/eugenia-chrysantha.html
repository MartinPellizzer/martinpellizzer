Eugenia chrysantha

Eugenia chrysantha, commonly known as the golden eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for consumption. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and continuous flowering.

Eugenia chrysantha is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
