Erica lanuginosa

Erica lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant features small, pale pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, and its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica lanuginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
