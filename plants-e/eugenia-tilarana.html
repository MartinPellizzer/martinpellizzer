Eugenia tilarana

Eugenia tilarana, commonly known as the Tilarana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia tilarana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a subtle, fragrant aroma.
