Elaphoglossum squamosum

Elaphoglossum squamosum, commonly known as the scaly holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds. The plant features a creeping rhizome and produces sporangia on the undersides of its leaves. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive scaly appearance on the fronds.

Elaphoglossum squamosum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
