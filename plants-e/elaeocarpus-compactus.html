Elaeocarpus compactus

Elaeocarpus compactus, commonly known as the dwarf elaeocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to the wet tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and has dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are about 1 centimeter in diameter. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Elaeocarpus compactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
