Erucastrum erucastrum

Erucastrum erucastrum, commonly known as the common erucoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are yellow with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It produces small, spherical pods that contain several seeds.

Erucastrum erucastrum is often found in disturbed soils and is common in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
