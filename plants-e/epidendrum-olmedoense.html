Epidendrum olmedoense

Epidendrum olmedoense, commonly known as the Olmedo's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and can display a range of colors, including white, pink, and purple.

Epidendrum olmedoense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
