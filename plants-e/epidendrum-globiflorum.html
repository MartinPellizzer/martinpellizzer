Epidendrum globiflorum

Epidendrum globiflorum, commonly known as the globe-flowered orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

The flowers are often white or pale pink, and they bloom in clusters, giving the plant its distinctive appearance.
