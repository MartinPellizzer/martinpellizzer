Eclipta dentata

Eclipta dentata, commonly known as the hairy eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disc, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Eclipta dentata is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
