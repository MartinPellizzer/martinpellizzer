Eucalyptus gardneri subsp. ravensthorpensis

Eucalyptus gardneri subsp.

ravensthorpensis, commonly known as Ravensthorpe mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous beneath.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and the plant produces small, woody capsules as fruit. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is part of the diverse eucalyptus flora of the Mallee region, known for its ecological and horticultural significance.
