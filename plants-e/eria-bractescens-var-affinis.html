Eria bractescens var. affinis

Eria bractescens var. affinis, commonly known as the slender eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
