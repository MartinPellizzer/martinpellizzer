Eriosema pseudodistinctum

Eriosema pseudodistinctum, commonly known as the hairy eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in dense, globular clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for its ecological benefits.
