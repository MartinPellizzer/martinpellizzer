Eupatorium sordidum

Eupatorium sordidum, commonly known as the stinking weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

Eupatorium sordidum is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is more pronounced when the plant is crushed.
