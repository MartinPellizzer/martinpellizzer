Epilobium brunnescens subsp. beaugleholei

Epilobium brunnescens subsp.

beaugleholei, commonly known as the redstem willow, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
