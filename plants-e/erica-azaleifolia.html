Erica azaleifolia

Erica azaleifolia, commonly known as the coastal heath or narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal environments with high salinity and strong winds. Erica azaleifolia is often found in dune systems and is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions.

Its small, leathery leaves and clustered flower heads make it a distinctive feature of the South African coastal flora.
