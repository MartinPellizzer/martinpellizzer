Elaeagnus breyniastrum

Elaeagnus breyniastrum, commonly known as the African silverberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by edible berries that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Elaeagnus breyniastrum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
