Eugenia excelsa

Eugenia excelsa, commonly known as the "mountain apple" or "wild coffee," is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
