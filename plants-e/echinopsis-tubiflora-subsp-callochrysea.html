Echinopsis tubiflora subsp. callochrysea

Echinopsis tubiflora subsp.

callochrysea, commonly known as the golden echinopsis, is a cacti species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a spiny exterior, with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
