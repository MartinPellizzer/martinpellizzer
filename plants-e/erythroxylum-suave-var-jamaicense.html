Erythroxylum suave var. jamaicense

Erythroxylum suave var. jamaicense, commonly known as Jamaican red pepper, is a species native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that develop into oval, red berries containing elliptical seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
