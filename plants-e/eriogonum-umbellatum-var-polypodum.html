Eriogonum umbellatum var. polypodum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. polypodum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces white to pale pink flowers that are arranged in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments.

This variety is often found in meadows and open slopes, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
