Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp. acerina

Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp.

acerina, commonly known as the snow gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark that peels in thin strips, often revealing a white or pale green underlayer.

The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, such as alpine regions and cool temperate forests. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-gray hue, and it produces small, white or cream-colored flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
