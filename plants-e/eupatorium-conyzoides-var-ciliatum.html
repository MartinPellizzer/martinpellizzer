Eupatorium conyzoides var. ciliatum

Eupatorium conyzoides var. ciliatum, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, the Caribbean, and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are often covered in fine cilia, giving it the specific epithet "ciliatum.".

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in open forests.
