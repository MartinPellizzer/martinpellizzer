Eria dujakorum

Eria dujakorum, commonly known as the Dujakorum orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, clustered growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming during the wet season. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and surrounding substrates, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Eria dujakorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
