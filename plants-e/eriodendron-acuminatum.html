Eriodendron acuminatum

Eriodendron acuminatum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The tree has a spreading canopy with pinnate leaves and produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall in suitable conditions.
