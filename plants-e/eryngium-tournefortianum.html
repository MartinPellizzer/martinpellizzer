Eryngium tournefortianum

Eryngium tournefortianum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium tournefortianum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
