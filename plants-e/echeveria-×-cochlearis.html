Echeveria × cochlearis

Echeveria × cochlearis, commonly known as the hybrid sunset echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure, with fleshy, thick leaves that are often colored in shades of green, blue, and purple. The plant produces vibrant pink or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in rock gardens.
