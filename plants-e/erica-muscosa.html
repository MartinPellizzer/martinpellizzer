Erica muscosa

Erica muscosa, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. Erica muscosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
