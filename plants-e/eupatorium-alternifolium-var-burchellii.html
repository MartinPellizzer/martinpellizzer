Eupatorium alternifolium var. burchellii

Eupatorium alternifolium var. burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's boneset, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is named after the botanist Andrew Burchell, who contributed significantly to the study of African flora.
