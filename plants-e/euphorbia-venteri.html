Euphorbia venteri

Euphorbia venteri, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra, is a succulent plant native to Namibia and South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are often spine-tipped, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Euphorbia venteri is well adapted to desert conditions, with a slow growth rate and the ability to survive prolonged droughts.
