Eumachia inconspicua

Eumachia inconspicua, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its berries are typically red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Eumachia inconspicua is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
