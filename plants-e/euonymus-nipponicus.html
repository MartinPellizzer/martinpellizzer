Euonymus nipponicus

Euonymus nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with four petals, and it produces red, fleshy fruits that are attractive to birds.

Euonymus nipponicus thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively easy to maintain in gardens.
