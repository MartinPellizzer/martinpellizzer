Eupatorium roupalifolium

Eupatorium roupalifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, rough-textured leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
