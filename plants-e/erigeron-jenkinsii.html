Erigeron jenkinsii

Erigeron jenkinsii, commonly known as Jenkins' fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer.

Jenkins' fleabane is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.
