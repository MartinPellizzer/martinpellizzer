Eugenia warszewiczii

Eugenia warszewiczii, commonly known as the Warzewicz's eucalyptus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
