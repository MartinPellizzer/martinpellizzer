Eugenia balerensis

Eugenia balerensis, commonly known as the Baler guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, red or yellow berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
