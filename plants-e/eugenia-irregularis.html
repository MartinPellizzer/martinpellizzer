Eugenia irregularis

Eugenia irregularis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in jams and beverages.

Eugenia irregularis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
