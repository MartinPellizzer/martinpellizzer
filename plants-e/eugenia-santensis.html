Eugenia santensis

Eugenia santensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a problematic species in some regions.
