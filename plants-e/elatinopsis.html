Elatinopsis

Elatinopsis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Elatinaceae.

These plants are typically small and herbaceous, often found in sandy or coastal environments. They are characterized by their delicate, ephemeral flowers that bloom quickly and often vanish within a short period. Elatinopsis species are adapted to thrive in saline and arid conditions, showcasing unique physiological traits.

Their rapid life cycle and specialized habitats make them an interesting subject in ecological and botanical studies.
