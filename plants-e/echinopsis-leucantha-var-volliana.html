Echinopsis leucantha var. volliana

Echinopsis leucantha var. volliana, commonly known as the White-topped Cereus, is a cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces striking white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
