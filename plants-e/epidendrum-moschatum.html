Epidendrum moschatum

Epidendrum moschatum, commonly known as the chocolate orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, chocolate-brown flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It thrives in well-draining potting media and requires consistent moisture and bright, indirect light.

Epidendrum moschatum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
