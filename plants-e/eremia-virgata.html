Eremia virgata

Eremia virgata, commonly known as the desert sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, perennial shrub that thrives in sandy and rocky soils, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming during the spring and early summer. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize sandy soils, making it an important component of desert ecosystems.

Eremia virgata is well adapted to extreme environmental conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience in harsh, dry climates.
