Eriogynia pectinata

Eriogynia pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Eriogynia pectinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
