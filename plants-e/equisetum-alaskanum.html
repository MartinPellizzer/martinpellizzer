Equisetum alaskanum

Equisetum alaskanum, commonly known as Alaska horsetail, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the genus Equisetum.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a hollow, jointed stem that resembles a grass. This plant is typically found in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions, often near streams or in wetlands. It reproduces via spores rather than seeds, and its structure reflects its evolutionary history as one of the oldest groups of land plants.

The plant contains silica, which gives it a rough texture and contributes to its resistance to herbivory.
