Eugenia multicostata var. octocostata

Eugenia multicostata var. octocostata, commonly known as the eight-costate eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves that are prominently veined with eight distinct costae, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is occasionally cultivated for its fruit in horticultural settings.
