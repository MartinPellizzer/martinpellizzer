Erysimum redowskii

Erysimum redowskii, commonly known as the Redowski's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often branched.

Erysimum redowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
