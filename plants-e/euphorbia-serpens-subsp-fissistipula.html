Euphorbia serpens subsp. fissistipula

Euphorbia serpens subsp.

fissistipula, commonly known as the fissistipula spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in small, white or pale green tubercles.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, typically during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative ground cover.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
