Epilobium roseum subsp. trigonum

Epilobium roseum subsp.

trigonum, commonly known as the purple willow-herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are often reddish in color.
