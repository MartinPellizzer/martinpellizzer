Equisetum variegatum var. nelsonii

Equisetum variegatum var. nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's variable horsetail, is a variant of the variable horsetail, characterized by its distinctive variegation in foliage.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family, known for its ancient lineage dating back to the Carboniferous period. The plant features whorled, needle-like leaves and produces spore-bearing cones, typical of its primitive evolutionary structure. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
