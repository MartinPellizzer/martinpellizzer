Erigeron hybridus

Erigeron hybridus, commonly known as common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a distinctive silvery-gray appearance.
