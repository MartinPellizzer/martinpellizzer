Erysimum integrifolium

Erysimum integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and can tolerate light frost.

Erysimum integrifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
