Eriocaulon coeruleum

Eriocaulon coeruleum, commonly known as blue spikegrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in spikes. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contributes to its common name.

The plant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
