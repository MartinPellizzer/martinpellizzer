Erigeron inornatus f. subradiatus

Erigeron inornatus f. subradiatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, daisy-like blue or purple flowers. This subspecies is native to the western United States, particularly in regions with dry, rocky soils. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
