Erysimum zanonii

Erysimum zanonii, commonly known as the Andean wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Erysimum zanonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
