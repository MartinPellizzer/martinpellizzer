Eugenia coccinea

Eugenia coccinea, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental horticulture. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Eugenia coccinea thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
