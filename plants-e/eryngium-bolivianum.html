Eryngium bolivianum

Eryngium bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its foliage consists of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, almost woolly texture.

Eryngium bolivianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
