Erysimum loesellii

Erysimum loesellii, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum loesellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
