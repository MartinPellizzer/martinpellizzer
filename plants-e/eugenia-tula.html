Eugenia tula

Eugenia tula, commonly known as the Tula myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia tula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
