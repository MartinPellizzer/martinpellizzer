Eugenia altipeta

Eugenia altipeta, commonly known as the highland guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit, which is typically small and tart, is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Eugenia altipeta thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cooler, humid climates.
