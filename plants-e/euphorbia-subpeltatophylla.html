Euphorbia subpeltatophylla

Euphorbia subpeltatophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that is often branched and covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are narrow, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, helping the plant store water. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance.
