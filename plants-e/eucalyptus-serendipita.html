Eucalyptus serendipita

Eucalyptus serendipita, commonly known as the Serendipity gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white, grey, and green. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a whitish underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
