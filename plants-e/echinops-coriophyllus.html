Echinops coriophyllus

Echinops coriophyllus, commonly known as the spiked centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a distinctive feature of the genus Echinops. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Echinops coriophyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
