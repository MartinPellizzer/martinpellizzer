Euploca personata

Euploca personata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved euploca, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in swampy or seasonally flooded areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are clustered in loose spikes and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Euploca personata plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
