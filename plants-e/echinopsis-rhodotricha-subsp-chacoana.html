Echinopsis rhodotricha subsp. chacoana

Echinopsis rhodotricha subsp.

chacoana, commonly known as the Chacoan night-blooming cactus, is a species native to Argentina and Paraguay. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit.

The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers that open at night. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in cactus gardens and conservation programs.
