Ericoides gnaphalodes

Ericoides gnaphalodes, commonly known as the silverleaf ericoid, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
