Eustrephus latifolius var. brownii

Eustrephus latifolius var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's broadleaf eustrephus, is a variant of the eustrephus plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically a deep green and have a smooth, waxy surface, which helps in reducing water loss. This plant is native to specific regions in southern Africa, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage rather than its flowers.
