Eremophila oldfieldii subsp. papula

Eremophila oldfieldii subsp.

papula, commonly known as the Oldfield's emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of tubular, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it resilient in harsh environments. Its floral structure is specialized for pollination by nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
