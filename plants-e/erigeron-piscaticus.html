Erigeron piscaticus

Erigeron piscaticus, commonly known as the marsh fleabane, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, ponds, and marshes. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

Erigeron piscaticus is often found in disturbed or wetland environments and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
