Eucalyptus orbifolia

Eucalyptus orbifolia, commonly known as the orbifoliate gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orb-shaped leaves, which are typically smooth and glossy. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
