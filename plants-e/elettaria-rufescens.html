Elettaria rufescens

Elettaria rufescens, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces small, white flowers and has strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its rhizomes contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Elettaria rufescens is valued for its use in culinary and herbal applications.
