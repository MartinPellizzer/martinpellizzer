Erucaria aleppica var. latifolia

Erucaria aleppica var. latifolia, commonly known as the Aleppo pine, is a variant of the Aleppo pine species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its broad, flat needles and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces cones that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires and harsh environmental conditions.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
