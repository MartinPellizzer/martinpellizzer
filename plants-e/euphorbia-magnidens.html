Euphorbia magnidens

Euphorbia magnidens, commonly known as the giant euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, a characteristic common in many euphorbias for protection against herbivores.

Euphorbia magnidens is known for its slow growth and ability to survive in poor soil conditions.
