Eucalyptus staigeriana

Eucalyptus staigeriana, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that ranges from grey to brown in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow.

This species is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
