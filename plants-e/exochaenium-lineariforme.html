Exochaenium lineariforme

Exochaenium lineariforme, commonly known as the slender threadgrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are arranged in dense clusters along the stem.

Exochaenium lineariforme plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists studying xerophytic plants.
