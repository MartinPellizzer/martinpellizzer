Erucastrum virgatum subsp. pseudosinapis

Erucastrum virgatum subsp.

pseudosinapis, commonly known as the lesser wall rocket, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its rosette of hairy, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along paths.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and western Asia, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
