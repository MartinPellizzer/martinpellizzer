Erythrina williamsii

Erythrina williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' flame tree, is a species of legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, and it is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.

Erythrina williamsii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
