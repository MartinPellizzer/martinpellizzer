Eriogonum viscidulum

Eriogonum viscidulum, commonly known as sticky leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular, sticky leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with multiple stems and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Eriogonum viscidulum is often found in sagebrush communities and is an important plant for local pollinators.
