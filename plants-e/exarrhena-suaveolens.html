Exarrhena suaveolens

Exarrhena suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and emit a pleasant aroma. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
