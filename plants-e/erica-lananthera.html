Erica lananthera

Erica lananthera, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, white flowers that resemble snowflakes. Erica lananthera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
