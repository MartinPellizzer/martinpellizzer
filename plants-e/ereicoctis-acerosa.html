Ereicoctis acerosa

Ereicoctis acerosa, commonly known as the hard-leaved ericaceous shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, leathery leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white or pink, during the spring season. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a cool, moist climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant landscapes.
