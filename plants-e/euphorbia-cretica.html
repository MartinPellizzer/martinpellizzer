Euphorbia cretica

Euphorbia cretica, commonly known as Cretan spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a branched stem covered in short, woolly hairs and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It is often found growing in rocky soils and coastal areas.

Euphorbia cretica is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
