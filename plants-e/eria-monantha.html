Eria monantha

Eria monantha, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are specialized for absorbing nutrients from the air and decaying organic matter.

Eria monantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
