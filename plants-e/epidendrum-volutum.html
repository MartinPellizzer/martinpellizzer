Epidendrum volutum

Epidendrum volutum, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiral-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, ranging from pink to red, and are borne in dense clusters.

Epidendrum volutum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
