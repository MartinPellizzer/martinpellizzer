Epidendrum lignosum

Epidendrum lignosum, commonly known as the wood epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral displays.

Epidendrum lignosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
