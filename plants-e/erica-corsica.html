Erica corsica

Erica corsica, commonly known as the Corsican heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its low-growing shrub form, with small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. Erica corsica thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
