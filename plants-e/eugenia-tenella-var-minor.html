Eugenia tenella var. minor

Eugenia tenella var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
