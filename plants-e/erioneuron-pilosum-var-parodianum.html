Erioneuron pilosum var. parodianum

Erioneuron pilosum var. parodianum, commonly known as the Parodiana thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to South America.

It features small, aromatic, gray-green leaves covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny white or pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage.
