Eupatorium umbrosum

Eupatorium umbrosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium umbrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
