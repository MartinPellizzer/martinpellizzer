Eucalyptus suggrandis subsp. alipes

Eucalyptus suggrandis subsp.

alipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet tree, is a small to medium-sized eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are small and arranged in clusters, producing a distinctive honey-scented aroma. The bark is smooth and often peels in thin, flaky sheets, revealing a pale yellow or white underbark.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
