Echiochilon arabicum

Echiochilon arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian thyme, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with small, fragrant leaves and pinkish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to hot, dry climates. Its aromatic properties have made it valuable in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.

Echiochilon arabicum is also noted for its ability to prevent soil erosion in desert regions.
