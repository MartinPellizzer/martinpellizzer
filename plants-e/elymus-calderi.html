Elymus calderi

Elymus calderi, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Elymus calderi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
