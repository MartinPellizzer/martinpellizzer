Echinocactus setispinus var. cachetianus

Echinocactus setispinus var. cachetianus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It has spiny, white to yellowish spines that provide protection from herbivores.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well-suited for cultivation in dry, well-drained soil conditions.
