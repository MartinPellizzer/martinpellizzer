Eucalyptus obtusiflora subsp. cowcowensis

Eucalyptus obtusiflora subsp.

cowcowensis, commonly known as the Cowcowen malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped, grey-green leaves.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
