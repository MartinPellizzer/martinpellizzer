Elaeocarpus thorelii

Elaeocarpus thorelii, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, orange-red aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
