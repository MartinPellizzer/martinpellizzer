Epilobium glareosum

Epilobium glareosum, commonly known as the shining willow herb, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, especially along the veins.

The flowers are small, pink to purple, and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
