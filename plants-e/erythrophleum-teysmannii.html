Erythrophleum teysmannii

Erythrophleum teysmannii, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by flat, pod-like fruits.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
