Eupatorium viscosissimum

Eupatorium viscosissimum, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its sticky, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough, viscid texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
