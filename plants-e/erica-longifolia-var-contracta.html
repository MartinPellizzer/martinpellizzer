Erica longifolia var. contracta

Erica longifolia var. contracta, commonly known as the compact heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It features dense, needle-like foliage that is typically dark green and covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
