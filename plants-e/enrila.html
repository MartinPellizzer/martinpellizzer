Enrila

Enrila, commonly known as the wild pepper plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae, which includes other pepper plants like black pepper. The plant is characterized by its small, green, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of tiny, white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a peppercorn, is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Enrila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
