Eugenia gracillima

Eugenia gracillima, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia gracillima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of tropical and subtropical climates.
