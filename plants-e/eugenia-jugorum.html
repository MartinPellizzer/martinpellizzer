Eugenia jugorum

Eugenia jugorum, commonly known as the Java apple or *Syzygium jugorum*, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit.

The tree is also sometimes cultivated for its aromatic wood and medicinal properties.
