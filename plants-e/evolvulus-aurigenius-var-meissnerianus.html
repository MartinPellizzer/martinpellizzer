Evolvulus aurigenius var. meissnerianus

Evolvulus aurigenius var. meissnerianus, commonly known as the Meissner's blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
