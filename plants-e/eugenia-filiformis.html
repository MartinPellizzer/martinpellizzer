Eugenia filiformis

Eugenia filiformis, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible, spherical berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The botanical features of Eugenia filiformis make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
