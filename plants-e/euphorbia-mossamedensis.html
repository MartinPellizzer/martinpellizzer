Euphorbia mossamedensis

Euphorbia mossamedensis, commonly known as the Mossamedes spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia and South Africa.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Euphorbia mossamedensis is well adapted to harsh desert environments and is often found growing on rocky soils.
