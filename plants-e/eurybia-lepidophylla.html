Eurybia lepidophylla

Eurybia lepidophylla, commonly known as the resurrection plant, is a unique species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is notable for its ability to survive extreme drought conditions by entering a desiccated, dormant state. When water becomes available, the plant "resurrects," rapidly rehydrating and regaining its vibrant green color and structural form. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers that are often pale yellow or white.

This remarkable adaptability makes it a fascinating subject in the study of plant resilience and survival strategies.
