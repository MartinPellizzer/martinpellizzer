Egania appressa

Egania appressa, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, shrub-like growth form. Its leaves are narrow, often with a bluish-gray hue, and are covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
