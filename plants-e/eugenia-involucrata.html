Eugenia involucrata

Eugenia involucrata, commonly known as the velvetwood or blackwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Eugenia involucrata is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
