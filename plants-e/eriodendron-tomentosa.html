Eriodendron tomentosa

Eriodendron tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly eriodendron, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its hairy, greyish leaves and clusters of yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in savanna environments.

Eriodendron tomentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
