Excoecaria serrulata

Excoecaria serrulata, commonly known as the African hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive yellow throat. The plant has palmate leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Excoecaria serrulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
