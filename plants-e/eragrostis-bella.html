Eragrostis bella

Eragrostis bella, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking color and texture.

Eragrostis bella is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
