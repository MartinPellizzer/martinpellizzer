Epipactis latifolia var. viridans

Epipactis latifolia var. viridans, commonly known as the broadleaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces erect stems that bear clusters of flowers.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
