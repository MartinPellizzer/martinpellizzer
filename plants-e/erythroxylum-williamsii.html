Erythroxylum williamsii

Erythroxylum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains copal resin, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

Erythroxylum williamsii is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
