Echinochloa eruciformis

Echinochloa eruciformis, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its adaptability, it can become invasive in certain areas, impacting native vegetation.
