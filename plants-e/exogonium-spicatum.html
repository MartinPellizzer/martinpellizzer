Exogonium spicatum

Exogonium spicatum, commonly known as the spiky exogonium, is a rare and unique plant species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the family Exogoniaceae and is native to specific regions in South America. The plant features elongated, spiky bracts that enclose its small, clustered flowers. Its botanical significance lies in its specialized reproductive system, which involves the development of flowers outside the usual floral axis.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists studying plant evolution and adaptation.
