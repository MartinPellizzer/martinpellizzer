Eryngium elegans var. boliviense

Eryngium elegans var. boliviense, commonly known as the Bolivian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-violet flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
