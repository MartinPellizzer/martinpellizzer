Echinocactus coptonogonus

Echinocactus coptonogonus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that becomes rounded and barrel-shaped as it matures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its spines are typically white to yellow and are arranged in clusters.

Echinocactus coptonogonus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
