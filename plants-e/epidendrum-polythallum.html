Epidendrum polythallum

Epidendrum polythallum, commonly known as the white-veined epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. This plant typically grows as an epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with intricate veining patterns that give it its common name.

Epidendrum polythallum prefers warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
