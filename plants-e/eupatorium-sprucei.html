Eupatorium sprucei

Eupatorium sprucei, commonly known as Spruce's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eupatorium sprucei plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
