Eryngium carlinae var. erectum

Eryngium carlinae var. erectum, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. Its flowers are composed of small, tubular florets surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Eryngium carlinae var. erectum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
