Eremogone fendleri var. brevicaulis

Eremogone fendleri var. brevicaulis, commonly known as the short-stemmed eremogone, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with fleshy, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This variety is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant suited for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
