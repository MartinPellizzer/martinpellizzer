Eritrichium pedicellare

Eritrichium pedicellare, commonly known as the slender alpine bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Eritrichium pedicellare is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
