Eugenia vincifolia

Eugenia vincifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple leaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark purple leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Eugenia vincifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
