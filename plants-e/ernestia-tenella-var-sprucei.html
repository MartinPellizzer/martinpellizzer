Ernestia tenella var. sprucei

Ernestia tenella var. sprucei, commonly known as the Spruce's lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
