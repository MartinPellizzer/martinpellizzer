Escobaria sneedii subsp. orcuttii

Escobaria sneedii subsp.

orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's liveforever, is a succulent perennial native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it produces small, globular fruits that contain seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic landscapes.
