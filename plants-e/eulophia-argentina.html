Eulophia argentina

Eulophia argentina, commonly known as the Argentine orchid, is a tropical orchid native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Eulophia argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
