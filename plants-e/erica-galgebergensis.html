Erica galgebergensis

Erica galgebergensis, commonly known as the Galgeberg heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This species is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica galgebergensis is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region, with its flowering period typically occurring during the summer months.
