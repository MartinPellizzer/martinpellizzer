Epilobium cernuum

Epilobium cernuum, commonly known as common willowherb, is an herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium cernuum is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
