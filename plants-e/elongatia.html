Elongatia

Elongatia, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and delicate, often fragrant flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, elongatia thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plants typically have narrow leaves and produce small, clustered blooms in a variety of colors.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, elongatia is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
