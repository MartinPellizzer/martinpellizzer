Eugenia sanjuanensis

Eugenia sanjuanensis, commonly known as the San Juan holly, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
