Elaeocarpus elliffii

Elaeocarpus elliffii, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
