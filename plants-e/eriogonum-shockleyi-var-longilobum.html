Eriogonum shockleyi var. longilobum

Eriogonum shockleyi var. longilobum, commonly known as the long-lobed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with long, narrow lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This variety is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub environments.
