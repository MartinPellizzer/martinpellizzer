Erigeron acris f. politus

Erigeron acris f. politus, commonly known as the whiteleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, almost silky leaves that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to North America and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
