Erythroxylum dumosum

Erythroxylum dumosum, commonly known as the bushy copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is often found in dry to semi-deciduous forests and is adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
