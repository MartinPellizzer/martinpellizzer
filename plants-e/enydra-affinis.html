Enydra affinis

Enydra affinis, commonly known as common enydra, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive heart-shaped base. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in loose inflorescences.

Enydra affinis thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in wetlands.
