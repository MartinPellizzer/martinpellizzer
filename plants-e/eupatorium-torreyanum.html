Eupatorium torreyanum

Eupatorium torreyanum, commonly known as Torrey's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
