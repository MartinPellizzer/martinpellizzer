Erica × murrayana

Erica × murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's heath, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica × murrayana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late winter to early spring, adding color to gardens during cooler months.
