Eugenia crucigera

Eugenia crucigera, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, yellow berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Eugenia crucigera is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
