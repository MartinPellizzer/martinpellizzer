Erigeron multiradiatus

Erigeron multiradiatus, commonly known as the common fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of silvery-gray, fuzzy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erigeron multiradiatus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
