Egletes bellidiflora

Egletes bellidiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, preferring full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Egletes bellidiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
