Erucastrum nasturtiifolium f. subbipinnatifidum

Erucastrum nasturtiifolium f. subbipinnatifidum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved watercress, is a variant of the watercress species characterized by its finely divided, bipinnatifid leaves.

The plant is an aquatic or semi-aquatic herb, typically found in shallow, freshwater environments. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to other watercress varieties. The botanical features include small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, and the plant is often used in culinary applications.

It is valued for its nutritional content and traditional use in various cuisines.
