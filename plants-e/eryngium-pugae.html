Eryngium pugae

Eryngium pugae, commonly known as the Pugan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Eryngium pugae thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
