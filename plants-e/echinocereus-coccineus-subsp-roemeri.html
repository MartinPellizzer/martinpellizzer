Echinocereus coccineus subsp. roemeri

Echinocereus coccineus subsp.

roemeri, commonly known as the roemer's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in bright red, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to desert conditions make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
