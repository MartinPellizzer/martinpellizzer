Echeveria shaviana

Echeveria shaviana, commonly known as the Shaviana Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight, though it should be protected from intense midday heat.

Echeveria shaviana is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections due to its ornamental appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
