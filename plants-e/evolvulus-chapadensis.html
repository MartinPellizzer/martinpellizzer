Evolvulus chapadensis

Evolvulus chapadensis, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its rapid growth and attractive flowers make it a common ornamental plant in gardens.
