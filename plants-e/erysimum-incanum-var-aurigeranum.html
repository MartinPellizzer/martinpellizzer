Erysimum incanum var. aurigeranum

Erysimum incanum var. aurigeranum, commonly known as the golden wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics include a branched stem, simple leaves, and four-petaled flowers with a distinctive yellow coloration.
