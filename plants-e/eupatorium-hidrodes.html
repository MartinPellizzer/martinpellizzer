Eupatorium hidrodes

Eupatorium hidrodes, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eupatorium hidrodes is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
