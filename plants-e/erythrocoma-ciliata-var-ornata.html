Erythrocoma ciliata var. ornata

Erythrocoma ciliata var. ornata, commonly known as the ornate erythrocoma, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of numerous small, red to purple flowers clustered in dense, rounded spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The ornate erythrocoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
