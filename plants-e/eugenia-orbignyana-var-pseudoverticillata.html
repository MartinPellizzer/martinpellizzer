Eugenia orbignyana var. pseudoverticillata

Eugenia orbignyana var. pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces edible, red berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warm regions.
