Eugenia nana var. congesta

Eugenia nana var. congesta, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy fruits resembling small apples. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various soil types.
