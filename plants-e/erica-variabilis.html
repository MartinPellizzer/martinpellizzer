Erica variabilis

Erica variabilis, commonly known as the variable heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica variabilis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
