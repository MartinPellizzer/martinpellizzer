Echium angustifolium subsp. angustifolium

Echium angustifolium subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
