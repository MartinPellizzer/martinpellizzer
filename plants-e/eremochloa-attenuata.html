Eremochloa attenuata

Eremochloa attenuata, commonly known as the slender porcupine grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted, spiky inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it often forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Eremochloa attenuata is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
