Eriobotrya merguiensis

Eriobotrya merguiensis, commonly known as the Mergui loquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. The fruits are typically yellow or orange when mature and have a sweet, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in tropical and subtropical regions.
