Eragrostis amabilis f. varia

Eragrostis amabilis f. varia, commonly known as the variable lovegrass, is a variant of the lovegrass species native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden flowers.

Botanically, it is notable for its morphological variation, which makes it a subject of interest in plant taxonomy and ecological studies.
