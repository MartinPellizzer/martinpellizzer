Eucalyptus hypochlamydea

Eucalyptus hypochlamydea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is known for its resilience and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
