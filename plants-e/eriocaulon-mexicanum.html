Eriocaulon mexicanum

Eriocaulon mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. This plant is native to Mexico and is often found in wetland or marshy environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and ecologically significant species.
