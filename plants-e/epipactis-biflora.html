Epipactis biflora

Epipactis biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its unique floral structure, with two flowers per spike, one of which is typically sterile. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Epipactis biflora thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
