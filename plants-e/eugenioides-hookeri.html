Eugenioides hookeri

Eugenioides hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
