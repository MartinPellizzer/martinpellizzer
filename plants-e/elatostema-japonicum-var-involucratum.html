Elatostema japonicum var. involucratum

Elatostema japonicum var. involucratum, commonly known as the Japanese velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.
