Erica amabilis

Erica amabilis, commonly known as the sweet heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica amabilis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
