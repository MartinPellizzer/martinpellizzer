Eriogonum angulosum subsp. viridescens

Eriogonum angulosum subsp.

viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
