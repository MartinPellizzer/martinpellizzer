Echinocactus spinosissmus

Echinocactus spinosissmus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are densely covered with sharp, spiny bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Echinocactus spinosissmus is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
