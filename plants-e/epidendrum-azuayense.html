Epidendrum azuayense

Epidendrum azuayense, commonly known as the Azuay orchid, is a species of orchid native to Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes. The plant produces erect, branching inflorescences that bear showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a waxy surface and a prominent midrib.

Epidendrum azuayense thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests at moderate elevations.
