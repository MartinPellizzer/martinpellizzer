Elegia parviflora

Elegia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered elegia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Elegia parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific soil and climate requirements.
