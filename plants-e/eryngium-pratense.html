Eryngium pratense

Eryngium pratense, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking spiky blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by bracts that resemble a crown. The plant has a robust root system and hairy, gray-green leaves that are often mistaken for thistle. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
