Ervum acutifolium

Ervum acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several elliptical seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
