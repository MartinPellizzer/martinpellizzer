Eria salwinensis

Eria salwinensis, commonly known as the Salween eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream banks. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Eria salwinensis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in its native habitats.
