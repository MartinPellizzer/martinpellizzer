Elaeocarpus bontocensis

Elaeocarpus bontocensis, commonly known as the Bontoc elaeocarp, is a flowering plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen nature and ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, with smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often producing a distinctive fruit with a hard, woody capsule.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
