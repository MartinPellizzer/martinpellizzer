Eucharis formosa

Eucharis formosa, commonly known as the Amazonian white lily, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It features large, glossy, waxy leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that resemble lilies, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Eucharis formosa is popular in botanical gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
