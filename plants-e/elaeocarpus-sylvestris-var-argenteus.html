Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. argenteus

Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. argenteus, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, silver-veined leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, hard-shelled fruits that are typically consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
