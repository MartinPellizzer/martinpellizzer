Erica glaphyra

Erica glaphyra, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Erica glaphyra is well adapted to poor, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
