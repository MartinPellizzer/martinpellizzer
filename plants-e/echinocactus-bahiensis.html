Echinocactus bahiensis

Echinocactus bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented ribs, which provide structural support and protection against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and rock gardens.
