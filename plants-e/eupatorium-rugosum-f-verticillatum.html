Eupatorium rugosum f. verticillatum

Eupatorium rugosum f. verticillatum, commonly known as the clustered boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in stiff, hairy hairs and produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped with serrated edges and a rough texture. This plant is often found in moist meadows, stream banks, and wet woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
