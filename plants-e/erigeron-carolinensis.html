Erigeron carolinensis

Erigeron carolinensis, commonly known as Billy goatweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along trails. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white and purple.

It is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
