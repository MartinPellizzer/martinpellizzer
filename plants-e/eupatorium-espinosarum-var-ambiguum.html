Eupatorium espinosarum var. ambiguum

Eupatorium espinosarum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
