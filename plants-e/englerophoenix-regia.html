Englerophoenix regia

Englerophoenix regia, commonly known as the royal date palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, making it economically important in the region. Its resilience to arid conditions and historical significance in ancient civilizations further highlight its botanical and cultural value.

Englerophoenix regia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its fruit yield.
