Elytrigia antarctica

Elytrigia antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted, compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds, low temperatures, and nutrient-poor soils. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Elytrigia antarctica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environments.
