Euchile citrina

Euchile citrina, commonly known as the yellow euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a yellowish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a clustered inflorescence called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euchile citrina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
