Elsholtzia blanda

Elsholtzia blanda, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its leaves are often used in herbal medicine for their purported calming properties.

The species is part of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic and medicinal plants.
