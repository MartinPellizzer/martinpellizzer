Eria lactiflora

Eria lactiflora, commonly known as the milk-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive milky or waxy appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Eria lactiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
