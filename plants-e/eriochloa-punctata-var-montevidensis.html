Eriochloa punctata var. montevidensis

Eriochloa punctata var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidens grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has fibrous roots and is often found in regions with moderate to high rainfall.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various soil types and environmental conditions.
