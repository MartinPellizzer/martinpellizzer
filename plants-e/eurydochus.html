Eurydochus

Eurydochus, commonly known as the common tamarisk, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, often thorny branches and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Eurydochus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal areas and saline soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species in challenging climates.
