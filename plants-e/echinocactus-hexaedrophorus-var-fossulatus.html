Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. fossulatus

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. fossulatus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the central and southern regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, thick pedicels. Its distinctive features include prominent areoles with spines and the ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
