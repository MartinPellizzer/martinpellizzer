Eugenia earthiana

Eugenia earthiana, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia earthiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
