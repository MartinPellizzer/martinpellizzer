Erythroxylum rufum

Erythroxylum rufum, commonly known as the red-leaf copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and it is often found in dry forest and scrubland habitats.

Erythroxylum rufum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
