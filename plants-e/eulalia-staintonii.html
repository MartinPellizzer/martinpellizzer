Eulalia staintonii

Eulalia staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, native to South America.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments. The plant features slender, arching stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny seeds.

Eulalia staintonii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
