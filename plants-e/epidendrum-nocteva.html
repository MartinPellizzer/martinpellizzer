Epidendrum nocteva

Epidendrum nocteva, commonly known as the night orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, nocturnal blooming habit, with flowers opening in the evening and closing by dawn. The plant features long, slender stems and broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are typically pale green or white with a subtle fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Epidendrum nocteva is a hardy epiphytic orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments.
