Epilobium molle

Epilobium molle, commonly known as soft willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshy areas. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium molle is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
