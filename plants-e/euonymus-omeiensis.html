Euonymus omeiensis

Euonymus omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese spindletree, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Cercidiphyllaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by distinctive red, spherical fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Euonymus omeiensis is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
