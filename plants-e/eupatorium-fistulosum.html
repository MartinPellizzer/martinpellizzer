Eupatorium fistulosum

Eupatorium fistulosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium fistulosum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
