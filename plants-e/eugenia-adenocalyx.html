Eugenia adenocalyx

Eugenia adenocalyx, commonly known as the glossy apple or satin leaf, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a shiny appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are sweet and juicy. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.

Eugenia adenocalyx is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
