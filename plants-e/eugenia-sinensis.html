Eugenia sinensis

Eugenia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese guava, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.
