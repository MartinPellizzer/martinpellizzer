Erigeron pappocromus var. oblongatus

Erigeron pappocromus var. oblongatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces fluffy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
