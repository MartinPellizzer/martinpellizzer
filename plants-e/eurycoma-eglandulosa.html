Eurycoma eglandulosa

Eurycoma eglandulosa, commonly known as the greater yew or wild yew, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a red, fleshy berry that contains seeds.

Eurycoma eglandulosa is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
