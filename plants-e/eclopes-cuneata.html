Eclopes cuneata

Eclopes cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eclopes, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of North America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eclopes cuneata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
