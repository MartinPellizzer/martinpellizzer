Elaphoglossum merrillii

Elaphoglossum merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous small, linear segments. The plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
