Eragrostis bicolor

Eragrostis bicolor, commonly known as two-colored lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-toned inflorescence, with the upper portion being violet and the lower part green. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis bicolor is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
