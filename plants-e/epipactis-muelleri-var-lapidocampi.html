Epipactis muelleri var. lapidocampi

Epipactis muelleri var. lapidocampi, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to certain regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, often greenish-yellow flowers with a unique lip structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous habitats, showcasing its adaptation to nutrient-poor environments. Its morphology includes narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, erect stem.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in many areas.
