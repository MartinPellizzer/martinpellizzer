Elymus harsukhii

Elymus harsukhii, commonly known as the Harsukh grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Elymus harsukhii is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
