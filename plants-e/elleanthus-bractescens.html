Elleanthus bractescens

Elleanthus bractescens, commonly known as the bracteate elleanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen.

Elleanthus bractescens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
