Echinops serratifolius

Echinops serratifolius, commonly known as the serrated thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and spherical, spiky seed heads that are prominent in the autumn. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer. Echinops serratifolius is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
