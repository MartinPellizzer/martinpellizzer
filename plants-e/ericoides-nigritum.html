Ericoides nigritum

Ericoides nigritum, commonly known as the black eric, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific soil conditions.
