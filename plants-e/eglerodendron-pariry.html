Eglerodendron pariry

Eglerodendron pariry, commonly known as the Pariry tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense canopy of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are woody capsules containing several seeds, and the tree is valued for its durable timber and ornamental appeal.

The Pariry tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a variety of wildlife.
