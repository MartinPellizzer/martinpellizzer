Eupatorium determinatum

Eupatorium determinatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
