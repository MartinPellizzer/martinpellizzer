Epidendrum aurorae

Epidendrum aurorae, commonly known as the Aurora Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that display shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a visually appealing and sought-after ornamental plant.
