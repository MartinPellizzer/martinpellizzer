Euphrasia minima var. lapeyrousii

Euphrasia minima var. lapeyrousii, commonly known as the dwarf eyebright, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features delicate, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense umbels, often with two lips resembling a bird's eye.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
