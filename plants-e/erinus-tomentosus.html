Erinus tomentosus

Erinus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a woolly appearance.

Erinus tomentosus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
