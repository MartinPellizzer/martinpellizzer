Epidendrum rhombicapitellatum

Epidendrum rhombicapitellatum, commonly known as the rhombic-headed epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, velvety green leaves that are often adorned with silver markings. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
