Eryngium cymosum

Eryngium cymosum, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a compact, spherical cluster, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Eryngium cymosum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
