Eugenia concinna

Eugenia concinna, commonly known as the satinwood or sweetgum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Eugenia concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
