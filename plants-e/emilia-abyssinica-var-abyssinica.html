Emilia abyssinica var. abyssinica

Emilia abyssinica var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian emilia, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Emilia abyssinica var. abyssinica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in its native region.
