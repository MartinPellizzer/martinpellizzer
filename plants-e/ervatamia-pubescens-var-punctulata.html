Ervatamia pubescens var. punctulata

Ervatamia pubescens var. punctulata, commonly known as the spotted ervatamia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a rough texture and may bear small, dark spots.

This species is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
