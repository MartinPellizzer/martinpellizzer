Eria bifolia

Eria bifolia, commonly known as the two-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has two leaves, as its name suggests, which are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette formation. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Eria bifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
