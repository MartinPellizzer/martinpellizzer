Eulophia pulchella

Eulophia pulchella, commonly known as the elegant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, and the flowers are borne on lateral spikes.

Eulophia pulchella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
