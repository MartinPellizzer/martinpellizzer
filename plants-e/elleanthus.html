Elleanthus

Elleanthus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants native to southern Africa.

These plants typically feature showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. Elleanthus species are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in full sun. They have a compact growth habit and are popular in gardens for their ornamental value.

The genus includes several species, such as Elleanthus africanus and Elleanthus corymbosus, which are valued for their hardiness and beauty.
