Elytrigia ciliolata

Elytrigia ciliolata, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Elytrigia ciliolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
