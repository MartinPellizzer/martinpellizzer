Eugenia suzukii

Eugenia suzukii, commonly known as the Hawaiian apple or rough-skinned cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly aromatic scent.

Eugenia suzukii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its use in traditional Hawaiian culture and cuisine.
