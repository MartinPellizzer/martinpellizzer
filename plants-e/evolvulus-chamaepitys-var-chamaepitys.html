Evolvulus chamaepitys var. chamaepitys

Evolvulus chamaepitys var. chamaepitys, commonly known as the prostrate morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a low-growing annual vine characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively.
