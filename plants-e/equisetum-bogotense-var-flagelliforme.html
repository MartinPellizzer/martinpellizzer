Equisetum bogotense var. flagelliforme

Equisetum bogotense var. flagelliforme, commonly known as the bog whip fern, is a rare and ancient vascular plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, whip-like stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili at the tips of its branches, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. It thrives in wetlands and boggy areas, often forming dense colonies.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is considered a valuable species for ecological and botanical studies.
