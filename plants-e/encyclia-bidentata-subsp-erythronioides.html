Encyclia bidentata subsp. erythronioides

Encyclia bidentata subsp.

erythronioides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America. It is characterized by its striking red and green veined leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of tropical forests. It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that helps it store water and nutrients.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated by hobbyists and conservationists alike.
