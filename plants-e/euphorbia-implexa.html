Euphorbia implexa

Euphorbia implexa, commonly known as the Mexican devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of narrow, fleshy leaves that give it a spiky appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia implexa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
