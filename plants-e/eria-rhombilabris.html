Eria rhombilabris

Eria rhombilabris, commonly known as the rhombic orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
