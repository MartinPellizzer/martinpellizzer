Eupatorium pseudodalea

Eupatorium pseudodalea, commonly known as swamp boneset, is a perennial herb native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium pseudodalea is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
