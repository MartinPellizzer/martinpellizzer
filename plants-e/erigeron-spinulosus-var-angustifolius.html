Erigeron spinulosus var. angustifolius

Erigeron spinulosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with fine, bristly hairs along the edges.

Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
