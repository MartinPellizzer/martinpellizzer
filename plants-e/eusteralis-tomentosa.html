Eusteralis tomentosa

Eusteralis tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features soft, grayish-white hairs covering its stems and leaves, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers typically bloom in late summer to early fall, producing yellow disc flowers surrounded by white ray flowers.

Eusteralis tomentosa thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
