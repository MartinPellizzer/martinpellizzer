Eriogonum nealleyi

Eriogonum nealleyi, commonly known as Nealley's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriogonum nealleyi is an important component of its local ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
