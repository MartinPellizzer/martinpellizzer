Eurycaulis rhodocentrum

Eurycaulis rhodocentrum, commonly known as the redstem fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that are typically reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Eurycaulis rhodocentrum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and forest clearings.
