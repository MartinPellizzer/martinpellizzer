Eugenioides costaricanum

Eugenioides costaricanum, commonly known as the Costa Rican eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
