Eupatorium paucicrenatum

Eupatorium paucicrenatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for its purported fever-reducing effects.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
