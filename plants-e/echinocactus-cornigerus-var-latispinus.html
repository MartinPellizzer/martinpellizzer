Echinocactus cornigerus var. latispinus

Echinocactus cornigerus var. latispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are covered in thick, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often followed by red fruits. It is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
