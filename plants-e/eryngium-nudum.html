Eryngium nudum

Eryngium nudum, commonly known as the naked thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafless stems and blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers are composed of numerous small, tubular florets surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

Eryngium nudum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
