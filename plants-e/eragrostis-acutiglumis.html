Eragrostis acutiglumis

Eragrostis acutiglumis, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils, often forming dense clumps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize various habitats.

It is valued in some regions for forage and as a component of native plant communities.
