Eucalyptus astringens subsp. astringens

Eucalyptus astringens subsp.

astringens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions with a Mediterranean climate.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
