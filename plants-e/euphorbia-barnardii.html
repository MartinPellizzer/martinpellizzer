Euphorbia barnardii

Euphorbia barnardii, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking, pinkish-white cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Euphorbia barnardii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
