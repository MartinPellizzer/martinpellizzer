Eugenia cambucae

Eugenia cambucae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye. Eugenia cambucae thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
