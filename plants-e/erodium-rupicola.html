Erodium rupicola

Erodium rupicola, commonly known as the rock erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is a member of the Leguminosae family.
