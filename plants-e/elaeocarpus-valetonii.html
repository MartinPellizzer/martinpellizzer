Elaeocarpus valetonii

Elaeocarpus valetonii, commonly known as the Moluccan elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, adapted to the humid, tropical climate in which it thrives.

Elaeocarpus valetonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
