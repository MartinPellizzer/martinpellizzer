Ephedra saxatilis var. mairei

Ephedra saxatilis var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in desert areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional Chinese medicine.
