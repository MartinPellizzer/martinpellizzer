Epidendrum acrochordonium

Epidendrum acrochordonium, commonly known as the beard lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, hairy sepals and petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often hairy or furred.

Epidendrum acrochordonium is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
