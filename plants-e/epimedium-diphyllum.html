Epimedium diphyllum

Epimedium diphyllum, commonly known as barrenwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often divided into two lobes, giving it its scientific name "diphyllum.". The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on leafless stems. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value.

Epimedium diphyllum is also valued for its ability to improve soil quality and its historical use in traditional medicine.
