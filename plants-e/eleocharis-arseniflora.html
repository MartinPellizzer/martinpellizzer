Eleocharis arseniflora

Eleocharis arseniflora, commonly known as the arsenic grass, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is notable for its ability to hyperaccumulate arsenic, making it useful in phytoremediation efforts. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features include narrow leaves and small, greenish flowers that bloom in summer.
