Excoecaria melanosticta

Excoecaria melanosticta, commonly known as the black-stained croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that can range from bright green to deep red and orange. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
