Entada camerunensis

Entada camerunensis, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise tree, is a deciduous tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its thick, gnarled trunk and compound leaves. The tree produces yellowish-green pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Its wood is durable and has been used traditionally for various purposes.
