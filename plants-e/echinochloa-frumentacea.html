Echinochloa frumentacea

Echinochloa frumentacea, commonly known as false rice, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, white to pale green flowers. It is often found in wetland areas, rice fields, and disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its success in various environments.
