Evolvulus nummularius var. grandifolia

Evolvulus nummularius var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, succulent leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant is an annual or perennial herb with a trailing growth habit, often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, producing abundant blooms throughout the growing season.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it a popular choice for ground cover or hanging baskets.
