Erica solandra

Erica solandra, commonly known as the Christmas flower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers range in color from white to deep pink, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erica solandra is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
