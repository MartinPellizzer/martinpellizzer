Eugenia buettneriana

Eugenia buettneriana, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
