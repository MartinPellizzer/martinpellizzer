Eragrostis atrovirens

Eragrostis atrovirens, commonly known as black wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, including South Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble a cluster of wires. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Additionally, it serves as a food source for certain herbivores and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
