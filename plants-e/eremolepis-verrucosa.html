Eremolepis verrucosa

Eremolepis verrucosa, commonly known as the rough eremolepis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features basal leaves that are rough and hairy, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils.

Eremolepis verrucosa is often found in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in arid to semi-arid conditions.
