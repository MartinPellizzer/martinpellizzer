Eurynome

Eurynome, commonly known as the broadleaf tree, is a deciduous plant native to temperate regions.

It features a tall, straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown composed of large, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically green in summer and turn vibrant shades of red and yellow in autumn. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by small, edible fruits.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
