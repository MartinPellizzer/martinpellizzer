Encyclia bidentata

Encyclia bidentata, commonly known as the two-toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate floral structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive two-lobed lip, which is a key feature in its classification. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette and are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Encyclia bidentata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
