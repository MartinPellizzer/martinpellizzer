Ericameria nauseosa var. iridis

Ericameria nauseosa var. iridis, commonly known as the iridescent goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland regions. Its foliage has a distinctive bluish-gray hue, and the flowers are often tinged with purple or red, adding visual interest.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
