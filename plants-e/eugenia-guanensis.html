Eugenia guanensis

Eugenia guanensis, commonly known as the Guanabana or soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces a distinctive, spiky fruit with a green, thick rind and a soft, creamy interior. The fruit is rich in nutrients and contains compounds like acetogenins, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
