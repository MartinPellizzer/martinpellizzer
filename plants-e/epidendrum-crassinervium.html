Epidendrum crassinervium

Epidendrum crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple pseudobulbs. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves with prominent veins, and it produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
