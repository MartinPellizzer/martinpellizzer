Erigeron wahwahensis

Erigeron wahwahensis, commonly known as the Wahwah fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
