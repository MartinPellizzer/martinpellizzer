Eragrostis parvula

Eragrostis parvula, commonly known as the dwarf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Eragrostis parvula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
