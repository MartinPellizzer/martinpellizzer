Eugenia guadalupensis

Eugenia guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupan guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in limestone soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
