Eremophila jamesiorum

Eremophila jamesiorum, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its grey-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and heat resistance. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in woodland and heathland communities.

Eremophila jamesiorum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
