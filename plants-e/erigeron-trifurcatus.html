Erigeron trifurcatus

Erigeron trifurcatus, commonly known as the three-branched fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.
