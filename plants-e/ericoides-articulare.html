Ericoides articulare

Ericoides articulare, commonly known as the articulated ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands.

Ericoides articulare is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
