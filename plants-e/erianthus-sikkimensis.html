Erianthus sikkimensis

Erianthus sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim reed, is a perennial grass native to the Himalayan region of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. It produces panicles of small, pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Erianthus sikkimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
