Epilobium × pseudoaggregatum

Epilobium × pseudoaggregatum, commonly known as the purple marsh willow.

It is a flowering plant in the evening primrose family, characterized by its erect or ascending growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Epilobium × pseudoaggregatum is known for its adaptability to wet conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
