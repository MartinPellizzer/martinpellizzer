Eupatorium kupperi

Eupatorium kupperi, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and grayish-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Eupatorium kupperi is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
