Erica cinerea f. atrorubens

Erica cinerea f. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a dwarf shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and heathland environments. Its flowers range in color from pink to deep red, depending on the specific variety.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
