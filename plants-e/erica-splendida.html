Erica splendida

Erica splendida, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica splendida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
