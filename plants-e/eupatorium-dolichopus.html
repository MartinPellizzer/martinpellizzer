Eupatorium dolichopus

Eupatorium dolichopus, commonly known as purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Purple boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
