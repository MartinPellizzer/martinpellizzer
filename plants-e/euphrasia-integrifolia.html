Euphrasia integrifolia

Euphrasia integrifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved eyebright, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has oval, toothed leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Euphrasia integrifolia is often found in calcareous soils and is part of the Scrophulariaceae family.
