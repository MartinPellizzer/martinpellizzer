Eupatorium cordigerum

Eupatorium cordigerum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium cordigerum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
