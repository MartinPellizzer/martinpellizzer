Euchiton breviscapus

Euchiton breviscapus, commonly known as the short-horned bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
