Eugenia pseudodichasiantha

Eugenia pseudodichasiantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
