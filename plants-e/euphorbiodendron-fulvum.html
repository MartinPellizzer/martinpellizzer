Euphorbiodendron fulvum

Euphorbiodendron fulvum, commonly known as the golden euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically yellow or orange, and are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Euphorbiodendron fulvum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
