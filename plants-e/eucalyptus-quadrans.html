Eucalyptus quadrans

Eucalyptus quadrans, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus quadrans is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

Its essential oils, particularly cineole, have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
