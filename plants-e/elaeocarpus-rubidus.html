Elaeocarpus rubidus

Elaeocarpus rubidus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish-brown when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
