Eremophila hurteri

Eremophila hurteri, commonly known as the pink ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowering period usually occurs in spring.

Eremophila hurteri is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
