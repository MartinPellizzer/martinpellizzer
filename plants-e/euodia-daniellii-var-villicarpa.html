Euodia daniellii var. villicarpa

Euodia daniellii var. villicarpa, commonly known as the rough-fruited Chinese sandpaper tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The varietal name "villicarpa" refers to its hairy or rough fruit surface.
