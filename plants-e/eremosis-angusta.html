Eremosis angusta

Eremosis angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf eremosis, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Eremosis angusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
