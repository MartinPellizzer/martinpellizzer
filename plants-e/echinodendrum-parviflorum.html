Echinodendrum parviflorum

Echinodendrum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered echinodendrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes many species with showy flowers and often succulent characteristics. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, spiny fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

Echinodendrum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
