Eugenia ruschiana

Eugenia ruschiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and its small, dark red to black berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy texture.

Eugenia ruschiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
