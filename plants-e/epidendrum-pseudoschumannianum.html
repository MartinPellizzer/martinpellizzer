Epidendrum pseudoschumannianum

Epidendrum pseudoschumannianum, commonly known as the Schumann's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to withstand periods of drought.

The flowers are usually clustered in racemes, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, depending on the specific variety.
