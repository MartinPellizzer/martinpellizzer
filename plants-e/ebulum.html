Ebulum

Ebulum, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stems rising from the water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. Ebulum is known for its symbolic significance in various cultures, often representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.

Botanically, it belongs to the Nymphaeaceae family and is closely related to other water lilies.
