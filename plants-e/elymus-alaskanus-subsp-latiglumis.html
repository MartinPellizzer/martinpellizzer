Elymus alaskanus subsp. latiglumis

Elymus alaskanus subsp.

latiglumis, commonly known as the Alaska wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to brown, with a distinctive, bristly appearance. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
