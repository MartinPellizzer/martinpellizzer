Elymus hirsutiglumis

Elymus hirsutiglumis, commonly known as the hairy-leaved wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces spikelets that are often covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Elymus hirsutiglumis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
