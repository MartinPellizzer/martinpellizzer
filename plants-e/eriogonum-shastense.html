Eriogonum shastense

Eriogonum shastense, commonly known as Shasta buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces distinctive, three-lobed leaves that are often hairy.

Shasta buckwheat is a perennial species valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
