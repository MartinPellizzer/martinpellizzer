Epipactis pseudopurpurata

Epipactis pseudopurpurata, commonly known as the purple-veined helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a tall, unbranched stem that bears a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with distinctive purple veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

The orchid is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators through its unique floral structure.
