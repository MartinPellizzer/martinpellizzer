Eriochloa nubica

Eriochloa nubica, commonly known as the cloudgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed areas.

Eriochloa nubica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in certain ecosystems.
