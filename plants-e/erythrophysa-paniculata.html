Erythrophysa paniculata

Erythrophysa paniculata, commonly known as the red-flowered panicled hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters at the ends of branches. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Erythrophysa paniculata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
