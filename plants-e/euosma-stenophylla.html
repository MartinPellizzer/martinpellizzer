Euosma stenophylla

Euosma stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf euosma, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape.

Euosma stenophylla thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.
