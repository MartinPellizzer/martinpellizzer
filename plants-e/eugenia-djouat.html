Eugenia djouat

Eugenia djouat, commonly known as the Djouat myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Eugenia djouat thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
