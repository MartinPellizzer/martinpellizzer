Eryngium bourgatii subsp. hispanicum

Eryngium bourgatii subsp.

hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white, bristly leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its foliage is covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
