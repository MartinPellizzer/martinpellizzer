Erica comorensis

Erica comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica comorensis typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to humid, tropical climates.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
