Eleocharis tetraquetra var. wichurae

Eleocharis tetraquetra var. wichurae, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

The variety *wichurae* is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical form of *E. tetraquetra*.
