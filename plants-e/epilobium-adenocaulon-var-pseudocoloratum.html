Epilobium adenocaulon var. pseudocoloratum

Epilobium adenocaulon var. pseudocoloratum, commonly known as the purple willow or false purple willow, is a flowering plant in the evening primrose family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
