Echinopsis taquimbalensis var. wilkeae

Echinopsis taquimbalensis var. wilkeae, commonly known as the Wilke's moon cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white spines that provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
