Eremophila willsii var. integrifolia

Eremophila willsii var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
