Euphorbia neogoetzei

Euphorbia neogoetzei, commonly known as the Neogoetze's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Euphorbia neogoetzei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
