Epipactis guegelii

Epipactis guegelii, commonly known as the Guegel's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, strap-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Epipactis guegelii is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for various pollinators.
