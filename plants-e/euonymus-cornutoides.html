Euonymus cornutoides

Euonymus cornutoides, commonly known as the horned euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny buds that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds.

Euonymus cornutoides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
