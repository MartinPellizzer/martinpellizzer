Eugenia tephrogyna

Eugenia tephrogyna, commonly known as the broadleaf eucalypt, is a species native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Eugenia tephrogyna is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
