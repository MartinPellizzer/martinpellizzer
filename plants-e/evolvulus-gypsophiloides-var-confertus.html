Evolvulus gypsophiloides var. confertus

Evolvulus gypsophiloides var. confertus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It is often found growing in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental species within the Convolvulaceae family.
