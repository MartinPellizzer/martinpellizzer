Epidendrum stahlii

Epidendrum stahlii, commonly known as the Stahli's epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white to yellowish hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Epidendrum stahlii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
