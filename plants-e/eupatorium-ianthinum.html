Eupatorium ianthinum

Eupatorium ianthinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble mist, which appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium ianthinum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators.
