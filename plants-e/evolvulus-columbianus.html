Evolvulus columbianus

Evolvulus columbianus, commonly known as the Colombian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.
