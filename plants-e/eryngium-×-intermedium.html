Eryngium × intermedium

Eryngium × intermedium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from Eryngium maritimum and Eryngium yuccifolium.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal areas of Europe and North America. The plant features spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by silver-green bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Eryngium × intermedium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.
