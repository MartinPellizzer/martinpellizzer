Escallonia viscosa

Escallonia viscosa, commonly known as sticky escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is often covered in a fine, sticky resin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Escallonia viscosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
