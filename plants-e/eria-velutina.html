Eria velutina

Eria velutina, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as rainforests. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil and decaying organic matter.

Eria velutina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
