Erythrina schimpffii

Erythrina schimpffii, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a striking tropical legume native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom throughout the year, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves with three elliptical leaflets and produces distinctive, showy flowers with five petals. Its pods are long and cylindrical, containing several seeds enclosed in a fibrous membrane.

Erythrina schimpffii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
