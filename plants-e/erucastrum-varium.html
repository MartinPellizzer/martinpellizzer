Erucastrum varium

Erucastrum varium, commonly known as the variable cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and along roadsides.

Erucastrum varium is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
