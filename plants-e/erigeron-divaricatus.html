Erigeron divaricatus

Erigeron divaricatus, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, thriving in damp environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are white to pale blue, often with a yellow center.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
