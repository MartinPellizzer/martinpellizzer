Euphorbia fleckii

Euphorbia fleckii, commonly known as the streaked spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that often display a distinctive streaked or mottled pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
