Exostema acuminatum

Exostema acuminatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions.
