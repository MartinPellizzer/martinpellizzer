Euphorbia schimperiana var. buchananii

Euphorbia schimperiana var. buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are typically small and may be either present or reduced, depending on the growth stage.

Euphorbia schimperiana var. buchananii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
