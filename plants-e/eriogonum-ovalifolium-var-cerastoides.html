Eriogonum ovalifolium var. cerastoides

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. cerastoides, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often found in desert and sagebrush habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
