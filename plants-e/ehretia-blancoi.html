Ehretia blancoi

Ehretia blancoi, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
