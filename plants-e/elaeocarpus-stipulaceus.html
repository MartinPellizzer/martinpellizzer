Elaeocarpus stipulaceus

Elaeocarpus stipulaceus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
