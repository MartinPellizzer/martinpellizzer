Euphorbia heterantha

Euphorbia heterantha, commonly known as the variable spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, fleshy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
