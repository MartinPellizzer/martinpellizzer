Eucalyptus tephroclada

Eucalyptus tephroclada, commonly known as the white mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or grey. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, and its wood is hard and durable.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mallee woodlands.
