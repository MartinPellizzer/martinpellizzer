Eroteum tomentosum

Eroteum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly eroteum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to its ecological niche.
