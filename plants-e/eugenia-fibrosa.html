Eugenia fibrosa

Eugenia fibrosa, commonly known as the fibrous eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, fleshy berries.

It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
