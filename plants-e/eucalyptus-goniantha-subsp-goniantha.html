Eucalyptus goniantha subsp. goniantha

Eucalyptus goniantha subsp.

goniantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the summer months. It is commonly found in dry, sandy soils in the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

This species is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions and is often used in rehabilitation of degraded landscapes.
