Eugenia samuelssonii

Eugenia samuelssonii, commonly known as the Samuelson's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
