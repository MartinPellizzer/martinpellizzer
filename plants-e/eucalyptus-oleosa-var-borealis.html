Eucalyptus oleosa var. borealis

Eucalyptus oleosa var. borealis, commonly known as the northern eucalyptus, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to the colder regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin sheets. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a silvery underside, which helps reduce water loss in its cooler, drier habitats. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

This variant is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained soils.
