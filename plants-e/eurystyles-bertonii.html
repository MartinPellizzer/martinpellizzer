Eurystyles bertonii

Eurystyles bertonii, commonly known as the Berton's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
