Eucalyptus decipiens nothosubsp. chalara

Eucalyptus decipiens nothosubsp.

chalara, commonly known as the Chalara eucalyptus, is a hybrid subspecies of the eucalyptus genus. It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled appearance.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a grey-green hue and a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is native to specific regions of southern Australia and is often found in open woodlands or along coastal areas.
