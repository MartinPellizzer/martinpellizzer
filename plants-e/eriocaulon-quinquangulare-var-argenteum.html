Eriocaulon quinquangulare var. argenteum

Eriocaulon quinquangulare var. argenteum, commonly known as silver spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland or marshy environments, often growing in shallow water or damp soil.

Its silver-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, making it a notable species in wetland botany.
