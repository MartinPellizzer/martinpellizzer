Eriogonum glaberrimum

Eriogonum glaberrimum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, blooming in the spring.

Eriogonum glaberrimum is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions with deep root systems and minimal water requirements.
