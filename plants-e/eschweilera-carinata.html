Eschweilera carinata

Eschweilera carinata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its bark is smooth and often has a reddish or brownish hue, and it can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat.

Eschweilera carinata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
