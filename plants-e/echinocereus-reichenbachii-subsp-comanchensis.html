Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. comanchensis

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

comanchensis, commonly known as the Comanche prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has spiny areoles that support both flowers and glochids, which are fine, barbed bristles. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
