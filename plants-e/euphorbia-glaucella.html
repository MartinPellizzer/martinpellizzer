Euphorbia glaucella

Euphorbia glaucella, commonly known as the silver euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems and leaves. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap.

Euphorbia glaucella is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
