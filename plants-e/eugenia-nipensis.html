Eugenia nipensis

Eugenia nipensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Eugenia nipensis is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of Hawaii and plays a role in the local ecosystem.
