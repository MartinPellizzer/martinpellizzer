Erigeron pumilus var. intermedius

Erigeron pumilus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Intermediate Daisy, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
