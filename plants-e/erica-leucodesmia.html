Erica leucodesmia

Erica leucodesmia, commonly known as the white heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica leucodesmia is well adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
