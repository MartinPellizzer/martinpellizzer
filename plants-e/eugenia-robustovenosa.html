Eugenia robustovenosa

Eugenia robustovenosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia robustovenosa*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, red, fleshy drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
