Eriocephalus artemisiifolius

Eriocephalus artemisiifolius, commonly known as the Cape golden bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
