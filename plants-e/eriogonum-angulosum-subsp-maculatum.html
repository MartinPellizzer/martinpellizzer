Eriogonum angulosum subsp. maculatum

Eriogonum angulosum subsp.

maculatum, commonly known as the spotted leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its distinctive spotted or mottled leaves, which provide it with a unique appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This plant is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
