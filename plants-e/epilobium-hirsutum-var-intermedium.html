Epilobium hirsutum var. intermedium

Epilobium hirsutum var. intermedium, commonly known as the woolly willow or intermediate willow, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
