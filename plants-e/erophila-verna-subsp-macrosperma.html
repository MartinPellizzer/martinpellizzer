Erophila verna subsp. macrosperma

Erophila verna subsp.

macrosperma, commonly known as the spring everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
