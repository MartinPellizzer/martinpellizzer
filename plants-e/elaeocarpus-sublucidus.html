Elaeocarpus sublucidus

Elaeocarpus sublucidus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a woody, spherical drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
