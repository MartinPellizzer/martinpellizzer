Eriostemon tuberculosus var. microphyllus

Eriostemon tuberculosus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved rock heath, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are densely covered with fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers with prominent yellow stamens, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in native gardens.
