Euphrasia officinalis subvar. polyadena

Euphrasia officinalis subvar. polyadena, commonly known as the common eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense groups, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for eye and respiratory conditions.
