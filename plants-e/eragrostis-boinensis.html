Eragrostis boinensis

Eragrostis boinensis, commonly known as the broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, dense spikelets that mature into pale to brownish seeds.

Eragrostis boinensis plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
