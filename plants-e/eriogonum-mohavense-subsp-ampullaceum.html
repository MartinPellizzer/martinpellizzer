Eriogonum mohavense subsp. ampullaceum

Eriogonum mohavense subsp.

ampullaceum, commonly known as the Mohave buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
