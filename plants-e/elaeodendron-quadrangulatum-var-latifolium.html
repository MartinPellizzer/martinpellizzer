Elaeodendron quadrangulatum var. latifolium

Elaeodendron quadrangulatum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeodendron, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its quadrangular stems and broad, lanceolate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in native habitats.
