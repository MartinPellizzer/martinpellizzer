Eriosema pulchellum

Eriosema pulchellum, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety appearance, and its flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eriosema pulchellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
