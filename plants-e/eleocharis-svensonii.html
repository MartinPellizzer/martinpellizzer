Eleocharis svensonii

Eleocharis svensonii, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant typically grows in shallow water or saturated soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas make it both a pioneer species and a potential invasive in certain regions.
