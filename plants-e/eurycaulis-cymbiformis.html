Eurycaulis cymbiformis

Eurycaulis cymbiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed cymbiform, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or woodland borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
