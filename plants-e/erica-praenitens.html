Erica praenitens

Erica praenitens, commonly known as the early heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. Erica praenitens typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils in open habitats such as rocky slopes and fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
