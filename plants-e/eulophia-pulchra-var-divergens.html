Eulophia pulchra var. divergens

Eulophia pulchra var. divergens, commonly known as the Divergent Eulophia, is a striking orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its inflorescence often consists of multiple flowers arranged along a spike, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

This variety is valued for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
