Everardia spongiosa

Everardia spongiosa, commonly known as the spongey everardia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, spongy texture and distinctive leaf structure, which features a waxy surface and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural interest.
