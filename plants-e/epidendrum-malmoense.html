Epidendrum malmoense

Epidendrum malmoense, commonly known as the Malmo's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, branching stems that bear numerous linear to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive greenish or yellowish throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum malmoense is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
