Erysimum ibericum

Erysimum ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
