Eriocaulon leucogenes

Eriocaulon leucogenes, commonly known as the white-stemmed pipewort, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with whorled leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Eriocaulon leucogenes is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
