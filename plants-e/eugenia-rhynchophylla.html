Eugenia rhynchophylla

Eugenia rhynchophylla, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, bright red fruits that are highly valued for their sweet, tart flavor and medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The fruit's pericarp contains bioactive compounds, making it an important species in both culinary and pharmaceutical applications.
