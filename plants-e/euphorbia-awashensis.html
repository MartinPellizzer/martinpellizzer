Euphorbia awashensis

Euphorbia awashensis, commonly known as the Awash spurge, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia awashensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils in its native habitat.
