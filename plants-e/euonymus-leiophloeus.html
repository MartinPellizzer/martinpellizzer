Euonymus leiophloeus

Euonymus leiophloeus, commonly known as the smoothleaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Euonymus leiophloeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
