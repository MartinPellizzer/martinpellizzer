Euphorbia bergii

Euphorbia bergii, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the spurge family, Euphorbiaceae, and is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its milky sap, known as latex, contains toxic compounds and can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia bergii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
