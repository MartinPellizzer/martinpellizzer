Eremophila freelingii

Eremophila freelingii, commonly known as the Freeling's emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of bright red or pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant is adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Eremophila freelingii is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
