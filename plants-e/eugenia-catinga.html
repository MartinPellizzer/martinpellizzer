Eugenia catinga

Eugenia catinga, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or catinga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark red to black berries that are edible. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in seasonal forests.

The fruit is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
