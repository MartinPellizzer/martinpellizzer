Eugenia tamaensis

Eugenia tamaensis, commonly known as the Tama waxleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Eugenia tamaensis is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
