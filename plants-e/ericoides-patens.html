Ericoides patens

Ericoides patens, commonly known as the small heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub that typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often in heathland or moorland environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and dark green, and it is adapted to cool, humid climates.

Ericoides patens plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
