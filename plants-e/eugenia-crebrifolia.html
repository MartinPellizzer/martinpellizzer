Eugenia crebrifolia

Eugenia crebrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or small-leaved evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia crebrifolia is also valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in craftsmanship and as a source of essential oils.
