Ericoides versicolor

Ericoides versicolor, commonly known as the variable heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, depending on the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ericoides versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
