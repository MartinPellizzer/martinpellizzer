Euphorbia multifida

Euphorbia multifida, commonly known as the palmate spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like leaves that are deeply lobed and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Euphorbia multifida is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
