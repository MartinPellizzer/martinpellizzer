Eugenia inocarpa

Eugenia inocarpa, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish-purple when mature, with a thin, leathery skin. The fruit contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
