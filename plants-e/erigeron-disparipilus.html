Erigeron disparipilus

Erigeron disparipilus, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are small, daisy-like, with white ray florets surrounding a yellow disc of tubular flowers.

It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in disturbed areas.
