Ebermaiera mandioccana var. triandra

Ebermaiera mandioccana var. triandra, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its adaptability, it can also become invasive in some regions outside its native habitat.
