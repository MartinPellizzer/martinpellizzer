Eugenia lanceifolia

Eugenia lanceifolia, commonly known as the lanceleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is a small, dark purple drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
