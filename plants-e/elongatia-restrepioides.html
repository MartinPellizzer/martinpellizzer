Elongatia restrepioides

Elongatia restrepioides, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Elongatia restrepioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
