Eupatorium urubambense var. typicum

Eupatorium urubambense var. typicum, commonly known as the Andean boneset, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
