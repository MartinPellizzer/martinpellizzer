Eupatorium trinitarium var. volubile

Eupatorium trinitarium var. volubile, commonly known as the creeping boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, ascending stems that often root at the nodes, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a distinctive grayish-green color.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and streambanks.
