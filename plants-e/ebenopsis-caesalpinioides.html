Ebenopsis caesalpinioides

Ebenopsis caesalpinioides, commonly known as the yellowwood tree, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree is known for its hard, durable wood and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features include a tall, straight trunk and a rounded canopy, making it a distinctive species in its native habitat.
