Eugenia jaboticaba

Eugenia jaboticaba, commonly known as the Brazilian grapefruit or jabuticaba, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a unique, sweet-tart flavor. The plant's leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are white and fragrant, blooming in clusters, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.

The bark is rough and fissured, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
