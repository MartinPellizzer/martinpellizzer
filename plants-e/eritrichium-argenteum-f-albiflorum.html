Eritrichium argenteum f. albiflorum

Eritrichium argenteum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the silver hair vetch, is a flowering plant characterized by its silvery-white foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to the western United States. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its low, spreading growth habit. Its flowers are borne in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
