Elythrostamna convolvulacea

Elythrostamna convolvulacea, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Elythrostamna convolvulacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.
