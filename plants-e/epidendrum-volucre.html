Epidendrum volucre

Epidendrum volucre, commonly known as the velvet epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate structure. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum volucre is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
