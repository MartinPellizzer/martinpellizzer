Ehretia laxa

Ehretia laxa, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The botanical features of Ehretia laxa make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
