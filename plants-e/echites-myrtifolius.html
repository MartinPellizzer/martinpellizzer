Echites myrtifolius

Echites myrtifolius, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow and orange, which bloom during the summer months. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Echites myrtifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
