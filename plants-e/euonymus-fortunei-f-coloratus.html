Euonymus fortunei f. coloratus

Euonymus fortunei f. coloratus, commonly known as the red-leaf euonymus, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its vibrant foliage.

Its leaves are typically a striking red or bronze color, especially in the spring and early summer, before maturing to a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

This variety is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
