Eugeissona wendlandiana

Eugeissona wendlandiana, commonly known as the Wendland's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and wetland restoration projects.
