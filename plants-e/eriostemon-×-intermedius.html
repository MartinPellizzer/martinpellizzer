Eriostemon × intermedius

Eriostemon × intermedius, commonly known as the hybrid lemon verbena, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a cross between Eriostemon caeruleus and Eriostemon chrysanthemifolius, resulting in a unique combination of traits from both parents. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its aromatic foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
