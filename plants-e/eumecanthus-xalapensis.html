Eumecanthus xalapensis

Eumecanthus xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange.

Eumecanthus xalapensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
