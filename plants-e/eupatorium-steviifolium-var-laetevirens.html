Eupatorium steviifolium var. laetevirens

Eupatorium steviifolium var. laetevirens, commonly known as sweet eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
