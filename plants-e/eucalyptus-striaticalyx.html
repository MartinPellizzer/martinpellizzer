Eucalyptus striaticalyx

Eucalyptus striaticalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
