Erysimum cornutum

Erysimum cornutum, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun.

Erysimum cornutum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
