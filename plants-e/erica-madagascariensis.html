Erica madagascariensis

Erica madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar heath, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other related species. This plant is characterized by its small, dense clusters of flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, white, or purple. Erica madagascariensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
