Erythronium indicum

Erythronium indicum, commonly known as the Indian dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It features a bulbous root system and produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge, and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Erythronium indicum is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
