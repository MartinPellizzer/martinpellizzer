Echinocactus platyacanthus

Echinocactus platyacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
