Erica penicilliformis var. chrysantha

Erica penicilliformis var. chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salt and wind.
