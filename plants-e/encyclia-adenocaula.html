Encyclia adenocaula

Encyclia adenocaula, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking pink and white flowers that resemble a slipper. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while the roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Encyclia adenocaula is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
