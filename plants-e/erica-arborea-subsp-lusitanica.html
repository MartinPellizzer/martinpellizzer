Erica arborea subsp. lusitanica

Erica arborea subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
