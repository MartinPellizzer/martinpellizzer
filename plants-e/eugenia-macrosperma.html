Eugenia macrosperma

Eugenia macrosperma, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are fleshy, berry-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Eugenia macrosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
