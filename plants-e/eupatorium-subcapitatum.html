Eupatorium subcapitatum

Eupatorium subcapitatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
