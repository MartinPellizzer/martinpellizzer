Epidendrum novogranatense

Epidendrum novogranatense, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its long, pendulous stems and clusters of fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces showy, brightly colored blooms in shades of pink, red, and orange, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Epidendrum novogranatense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
