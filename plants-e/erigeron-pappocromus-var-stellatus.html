Erigeron pappocromus var. stellatus

Erigeron pappocromus var. stellatus, commonly known as the starflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with white petals and a yellow center, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
