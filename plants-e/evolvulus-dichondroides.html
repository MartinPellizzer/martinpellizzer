Evolvulus dichondroides

Evolvulus dichondroides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round berries that contain the seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and rapid growth habit.

Evolvulus dichondroides is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
