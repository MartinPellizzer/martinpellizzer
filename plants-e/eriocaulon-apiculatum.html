Eriocaulon apiculatum

Eriocaulon apiculatum, commonly known as the spike moss, is a member of the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes. The plant features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves with a dense cluster of small, spike-like inflorescences at the center. Its reproductive structures are adapted for dispersal by wind, and it plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.

Eriocaulon apiculatum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
