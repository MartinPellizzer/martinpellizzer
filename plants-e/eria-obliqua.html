Eria obliqua

Eria obliqua, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and often have a distinctive coloration, with white and green hues.

Eria obliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
