Euphorbia subcordata var. eriophylla

Euphorbia subcordata var. eriophylla, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, low-growing stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

The plant's unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
