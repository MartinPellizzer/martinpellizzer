Eupatorium hosanense

Eupatorium hosanense, commonly known as the Himalayan boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Eupatorium hosanense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic effects.
