Electra mutica var. simplicifolia

Electra mutica var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf electra, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the southern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, elongated leaves that are usually smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
