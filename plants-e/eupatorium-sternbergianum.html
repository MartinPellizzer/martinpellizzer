Eupatorium sternbergianum

Eupatorium sternbergianum, commonly known as stiffleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are stiff and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and its flowers are white to pale blue, appearing in late summer to early fall.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
