Eugenia myrsinocarpa

Eugenia myrsinocarpa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
