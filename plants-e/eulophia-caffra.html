Eulophia caffra

Eulophia caffra, commonly known as the Cape eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas or mountainous regions. The plant produces a single, erect stem with several lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Eulophia caffra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
