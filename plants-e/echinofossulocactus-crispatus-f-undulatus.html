Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. undulatus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. undulatus, commonly known as the undulating crinkled cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating rib structure that gives it a wavy appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its fleshy, cylindrical stems are covered in small spines, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
