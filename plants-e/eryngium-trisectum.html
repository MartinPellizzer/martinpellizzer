Eryngium trisectum

Eryngium trisectum, commonly known as the three-lobed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive, spiky appearance, and it often grows in dry, rocky soils.

Eryngium trisectum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
