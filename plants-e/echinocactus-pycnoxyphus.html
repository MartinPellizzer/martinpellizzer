Echinocactus pycnoxyphus

Echinocactus pycnoxyphus, commonly known as the dense-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, branched inflorescences. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
