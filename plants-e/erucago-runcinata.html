Erucago runcinata

Erucago runcinata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed erucoid, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known plants such as cabbages and mustards. The plant features slender, upright stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators.

The plant is known for its peppery scent and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
