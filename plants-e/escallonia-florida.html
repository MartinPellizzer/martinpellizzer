Escallonia florida

Escallonia florida, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the winter months, which is why it is often associated with the holiday season. Its botanical name reflects its flowering time, as "flora" refers to flowers, and "escallonia" is derived from the genus name.

Escallonia florida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
