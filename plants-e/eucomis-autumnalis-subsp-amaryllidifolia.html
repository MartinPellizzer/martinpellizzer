Eucomis autumnalis subsp. amaryllidifolia

Eucomis autumnalis subsp.

amaryllidifolia, commonly known as the silver grass or autumn eucomis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and clusters of white to pale purple flower heads that bloom in late summer to autumn.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Hyacinthaceae family.
