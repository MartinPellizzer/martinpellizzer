Erica arborea var. alpina

Erica arborea var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its evergreen leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round structure.

Erica arborea var. alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
