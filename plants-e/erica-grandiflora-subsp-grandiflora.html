Erica grandiflora subsp. grandiflora

Erica grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically blooms in spring, producing fragrant, pink to purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Erica grandiflora subsp.

grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
