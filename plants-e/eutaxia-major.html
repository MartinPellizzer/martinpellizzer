Eutaxia major

Eutaxia major, commonly known as the large-leaf eutaxia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eutaxia major is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas.
