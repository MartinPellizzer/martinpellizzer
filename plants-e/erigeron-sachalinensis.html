Erigeron sachalinensis

Erigeron sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sakhalin fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Far East, including parts of Russia, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. It produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
