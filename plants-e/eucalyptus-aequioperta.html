Eucalyptus aequioperta

Eucalyptus aequioperta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue. It is well adapted to arid environments and can withstand periods of drought.

The bark is often smooth and greyish, with some rough, flaky sections.
