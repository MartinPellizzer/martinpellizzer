Eremaea hadra

Eremaea hadra, commonly known as the coastal eremaea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and drought conditions.

Eremaea hadra is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
