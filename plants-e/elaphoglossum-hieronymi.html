Elaphoglossum hieronymi

Elaphoglossum hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi's holly fern, is a small, delicate fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that often resemble holly leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are often clustered in small, compact clusters.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species among fern enthusiasts.
