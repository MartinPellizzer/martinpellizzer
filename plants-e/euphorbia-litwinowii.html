Euphorbia litwinowii

Euphorbia litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry climates.
