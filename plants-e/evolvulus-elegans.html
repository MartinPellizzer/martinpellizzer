Evolvulus elegans

Evolvulus elegans, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
