Eleusine scindica

Eleusine scindica, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
