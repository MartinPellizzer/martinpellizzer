Erigeron alpinus var. khasianus

Erigeron alpinus var. khasianus, commonly known as the Khasi fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small white to pale blue flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral morphology.
