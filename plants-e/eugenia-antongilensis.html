Eugenia antongilensis

Eugenia antongilensis, commonly known as the Madagascar apple or Antongil's eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling apples.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
