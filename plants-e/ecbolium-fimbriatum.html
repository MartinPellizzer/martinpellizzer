Ecbolium fimbriatum

Ecbolium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed ecbolium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Ecbolium fimbriatum is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
