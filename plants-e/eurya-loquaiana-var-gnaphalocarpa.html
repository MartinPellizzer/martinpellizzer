Eurya loquaiana var. gnaphalocarpa

Eurya loquaiana var. gnaphalocarpa, commonly known as the white-flowered eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical forests.
