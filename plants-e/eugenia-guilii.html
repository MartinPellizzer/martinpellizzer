Eugenia guilii

Eugenia guilii, commonly known as the Australian blueberry or Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Eugenia guilii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
