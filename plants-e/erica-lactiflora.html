Erica lactiflora

Erica lactiflora, commonly known as the milk heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica lactiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
