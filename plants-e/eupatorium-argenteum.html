Eupatorium argenteum

Eupatorium argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The species is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
