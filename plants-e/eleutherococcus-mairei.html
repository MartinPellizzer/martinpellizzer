Eleutherococcus mairei

Eleutherococcus mairei, commonly known as Maire's eleuthero, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for its potential adaptogenic benefits.
