Eupatorium myriadenium

Eupatorium myriadenium, commonly known as the Indian thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy inflorescences consist of numerous small white to pink flowers arranged in a rounded cluster.

Eupatorium myriadenium is often found in moist habitats such as marshes and along riverbanks.
