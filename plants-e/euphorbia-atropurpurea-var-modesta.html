Euphorbia atropurpurea var. modesta

Euphorbia atropurpurea var. modesta, commonly known as the compact sunset euphorbia, is a low-growing, compact shrub native to the western United States.

It features striking, waxy, dark purple foliage that remains vibrant throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia (modified flowers) that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its foliage and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
