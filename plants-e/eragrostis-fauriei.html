Eragrostis fauriei

Eragrostis fauriei, commonly known as Faurie's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis fauriei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
