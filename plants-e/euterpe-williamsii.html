Euterpe williamsii

Euterpe williamsii, commonly known as the pink banana, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, curved leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers, which are followed by elongated, edible fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits.
