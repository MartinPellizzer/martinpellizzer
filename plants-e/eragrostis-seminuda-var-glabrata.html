Eragrostis seminuda var. glabrata

Eragrostis seminuda var. glabrata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
