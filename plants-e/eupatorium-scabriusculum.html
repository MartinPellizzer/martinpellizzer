Eupatorium scabriusculum

Eupatorium scabriusculum, commonly known as roughhead thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium scabriusculum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
