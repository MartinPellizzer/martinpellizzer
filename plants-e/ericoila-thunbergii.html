Ericoila thunbergii

Ericoila thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese heather, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ericoila thunbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its compact growth and long-lasting blooms.
