Eragrostis interrupta var. koenigii

Eragrostis interrupta var. koenigii, commonly known as koenig's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
