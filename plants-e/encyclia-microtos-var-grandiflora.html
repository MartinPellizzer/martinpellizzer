Encyclia microtos var. grandiflora

Encyclia microtos var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often grown in gardens and greenhouses.
