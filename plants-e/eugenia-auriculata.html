Eugenia auriculata

Eugenia auriculata, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in clusters. Eugenia auriculata is often found in open forests and woodlands, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
