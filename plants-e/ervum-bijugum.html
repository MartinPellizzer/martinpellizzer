Ervum bijugum

Ervum bijugum, commonly known as the twin-lobed vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Ervum bijugum is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
