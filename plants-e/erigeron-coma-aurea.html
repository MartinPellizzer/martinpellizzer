Erigeron coma-aurea

Erigeron coma-aurea, commonly known as the golden blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fluffy blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silvery-gray foliage and a compact, low-growing habit. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and drought-tolerant addition to native plant landscapes.
