Erica vestita var. alba

Erica vestita var. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a compact, mat-like structure.

Erica vestita var. alba thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous habitats.
