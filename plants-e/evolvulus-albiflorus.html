Evolvulus albiflorus

Evolvulus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Evolvulus albiflorus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
