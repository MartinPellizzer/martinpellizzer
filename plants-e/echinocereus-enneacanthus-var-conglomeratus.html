Echinocereus enneacanthus var. conglomeratus

Echinocereus enneacanthus var. conglomeratus, commonly known as the clustered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant features a compact, rounded stem with clusters of cylindrical, ribbed segments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
