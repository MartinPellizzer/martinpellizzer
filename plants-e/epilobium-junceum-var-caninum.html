Epilobium junceum var. caninum

Epilobium junceum var. caninum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in the summer months.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
