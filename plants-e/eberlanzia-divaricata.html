Eberlanzia divaricata

Eberlanzia divaricata, commonly known as the variable evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits with a tough, leathery exterior. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental use in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
