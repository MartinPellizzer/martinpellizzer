Encelia pilosiflora

Encelia pilosiflora, commonly known as the woolly globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
