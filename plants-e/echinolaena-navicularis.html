Echinolaena navicularis

Echinolaena navicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burrgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear bristly, awned flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. Native to Australia, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and habitat for small invertebrates.
