Epidendrum ochriodes

Epidendrum ochriodes, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Epidendrum ochriodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
