Erica unibracteata

Erica unibracteata, commonly known as the single-bract heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related species. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. Erica unibracteata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
