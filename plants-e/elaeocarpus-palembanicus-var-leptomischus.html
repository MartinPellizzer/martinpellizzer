Elaeocarpus palembanicus var. leptomischus

Elaeocarpus palembanicus var. leptomischus, commonly known as the slender-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and distinctive, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, clustered fruits that are often consumed by various bird species.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
