Echinopsis rubinghiana

Echinopsis rubinghiana, commonly known as the Red-ribbed Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and vibrant red spines that give it its common name. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with thick, water-storing stems.

Echinopsis rubinghiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
