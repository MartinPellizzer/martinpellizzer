Euphorbia portulacoides subsp. collina

Euphorbia portulacoides subsp.

collina, commonly known as the Sierra Madre euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves and a low-growing, cushion-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy soils at higher elevations.
