Einadia nutans var. linifolia

Einadia nutans var. linifolia, commonly known as the slender false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil health.
