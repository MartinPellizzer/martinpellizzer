Ericoides nivenia

Ericoides nivenia, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Ericoides nivenia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

It is a hardy plant that is well adapted to cool, moist climates.
