Elaeocarpus knuthii subsp. cuspidatus

Elaeocarpus knuthii subsp.

cuspidatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a subtropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface.

The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
