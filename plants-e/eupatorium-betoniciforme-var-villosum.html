Eupatorium betoniciforme var. villosum

Eupatorium betoniciforme var. villosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators including bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
