Eremopyrum bonaepartis subsp. sublanuginosum

Eremopyrum bonaepartis subsp.

sublanuginosum, commonly known as the silky eremopyrum, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in a fine, silky pubescence.

The inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, each bearing a single floret. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological traits and ecological resilience.
