Euphorbia flavicoma subsp. verrucosa

Euphorbia flavicoma subsp.

verrucosa, commonly known as the yellow-flowered euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a branched stem covered in a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it often has a spiny appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its unique morphology and ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils.
