Erigeron bonariensis f. filifolia

Erigeron bonariensis f. filifolia, commonly known as the filifoliated blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its foliage is distinctive for its silky, thread-like texture, which gives it its common name.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
