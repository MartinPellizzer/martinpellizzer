Eugenia prieurii var. tenuiramis

Eugenia prieurii var. tenuiramis, commonly known as the slender-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
