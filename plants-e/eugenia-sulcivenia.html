Eugenia sulcivenia

Eugenia sulcivenia, commonly known as the Sulci Valley guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor.

Eugenia sulcivenia is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
