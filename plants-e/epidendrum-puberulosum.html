Epidendrum puberulosum

Epidendrum puberulosum, commonly known as the hairy epidendrum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Epidendrum puberulosum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
