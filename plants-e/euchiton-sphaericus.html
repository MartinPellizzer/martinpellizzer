Euchiton sphaericus

Euchiton sphaericus, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky flower heads that resemble small globes, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while the stem is unbranched and somewhat woody at the base. Euchiton sphaericus produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
