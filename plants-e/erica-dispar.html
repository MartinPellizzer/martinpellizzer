Erica dispar

Erica dispar, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heather family (Ericaceae).

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica dispar is well adapted to nutrient-poor environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
