Eremanthus rivularis

Eremanthus rivularis, commonly known as the river eremans, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as along riverbanks and in dense forests. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lance-shaped. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually white or pale green.

Eremanthus rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
