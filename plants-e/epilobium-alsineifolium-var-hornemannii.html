Epilobium alsineifolium var. hornemannii

Epilobium alsineifolium var. hornemannii, commonly known as the Hornemann's willowherb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with hairy surfaces, and it produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and other damp habitats.
