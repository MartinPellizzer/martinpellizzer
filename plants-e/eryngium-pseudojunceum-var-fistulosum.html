Eryngium pseudojunceum var. fistulosum

Eryngium pseudojunceum var. fistulosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features gray-green, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by spiny bracts, typical of the Eryngium genus. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in its native habitat.
