Epipactis hemsleyana

Epipactis hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and bamboo thickets. It is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a sweet, vanilla-like scent.

Epipactis hemsleyana is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms, attracting specific insects like bees and wasps.
