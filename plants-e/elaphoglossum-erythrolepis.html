Elaphoglossum erythrolepis

Elaphoglossum erythrolepis, commonly known as the red-veined holly fern, is a delicate fern species native to the wet tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often tinged with red veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks, where it thrives in the understory. Its rhizomes are wiry and produce numerous short, upright sporangium-bearing shoots.

The fern's intricate structure and vibrant coloration make it a notable species in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
