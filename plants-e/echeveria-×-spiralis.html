Echeveria × spiralis

Echeveria × spiralis, commonly known as the "Coral Glow" or "Coral Beauty," is a hybrid succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green with a bluish or pinkish tint. The leaves are fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers in the spring, and it is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.
