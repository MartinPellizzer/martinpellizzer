Elionurus glaber var. villosus

Elionurus glaber var. villosus, commonly known as the woolly flaxweed, is a variant of the flaxweed species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its hairy, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This plant is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
