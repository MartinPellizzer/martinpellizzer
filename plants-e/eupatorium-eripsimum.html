Eupatorium eripsimum

Eupatorium eripsimum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks.

Eupatorium eripsimum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
