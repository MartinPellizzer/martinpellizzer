Eriodictyon californicum f. latifolium

Eriodictyon californicum f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf yerba santa, is a variant of the yerba santa plant native to California.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy, grayish-white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties, it has been historically used by indigenous peoples for various health purposes.
