Echinocactus gibbosus var. leucodictyus

Echinocactus gibbosus var. leucodictyus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, white spines that contrast sharply with its green body. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
