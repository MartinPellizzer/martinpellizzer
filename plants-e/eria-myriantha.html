Eria myriantha

Eria myriantha, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eria myriantha thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and greenhouses.
