Elaeocarpus hypadenus

Elaeocarpus hypadenus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and mountainous regions.

It has limited economic importance but is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in its native habitat.
