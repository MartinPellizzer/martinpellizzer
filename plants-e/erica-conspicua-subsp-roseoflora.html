Erica conspicua subsp. roseoflora

Erica conspicua subsp.

roseoflora, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Western Cape region.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
