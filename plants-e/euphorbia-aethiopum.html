Euphorbia aethiopum

Euphorbia aethiopum, commonly known as Ethiopian spurge, is a flowering plant native to Africa, particularly Ethiopia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows up to 1 meter tall, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating.

Euphorbia aethiopum is known for its latex-like sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
