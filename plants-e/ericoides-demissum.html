Ericoides demissum

Ericoides demissum, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats. It produces small, violet or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in mild climates.
