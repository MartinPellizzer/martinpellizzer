Erythroxylum macrophyllum var. savannarum

Erythroxylum macrophyllum var. savannarum, commonly known as the savanna erythroxylum, is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is adapted to grow in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and is often found in nutrient-poor soils.
