Eriocaulon itapevense

Eriocaulon itapevense, commonly known as the Brazilian spikerush, is a member of the family Poaceae, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid and tropical climates. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems covered in fine, hair-like structures, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its small, clustered inflorescences contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family.

Eriocaulon itapevense plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
