Eriogonum heracleoides var. minus

Eriogonum heracleoides var. minus, commonly known as the smallleaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valued plant for native landscaping.
