Eryngium aquifolium var. elongatum

Eryngium aquifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the longleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a rosette of spiny, blue-green leaves that are lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are arranged in a tall, spiky inflorescence, producing small, pale blue to purple blooms.

The plant is characterized by its unique morphology and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
