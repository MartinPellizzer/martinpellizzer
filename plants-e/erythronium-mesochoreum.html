Erythronium mesochoreum

Erythronium mesochoreum, commonly known as the Balkan dog's-tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features a distinctive, showy flower with a yellow perianth and a prominent, spurred petal. The plant has a corm-like structure that serves as its storage organ. Its foliage consists of basal, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

It typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to woodland and meadow habitats.
