Eurya myrtilloides

Eurya myrtilloides, commonly known as the blueberry eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other acid-loving plants like blueberries and rhododendrons. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and turn vibrant red in the fall.

Eurya myrtilloides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.
