Eriobotrya platyphylla

Eriobotrya platyphylla, commonly known as the flat-leaf loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, followed by yellow-orange berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
