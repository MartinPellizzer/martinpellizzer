Echinalysium articulatum

Echinalysium articulatum, commonly known as the articulated echinopsis, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated in dry gardens and rockeries.
