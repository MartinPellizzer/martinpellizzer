Eriocaulon miquelianum var. monococcon

Eriocaulon miquelianum var. monococcon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rice grass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brown, and it is often found in temperate regions.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced floral structure and is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological preferences.
