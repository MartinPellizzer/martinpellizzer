Epipactis atrorubens subsp. triploidea

Epipactis atrorubens subsp.

triploidea, commonly known as the red-stemmed helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dark red or purplish stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of greenish-yellow to brownish flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Epipactis atrorubens subsp.

triploidea is often found growing on rocky or wooded substrates, and it is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.
