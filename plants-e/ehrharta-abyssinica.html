Ehrharta abyssinica

Ehrharta abyssinica, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for marginal lands.

Ehrharta abyssinica is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
