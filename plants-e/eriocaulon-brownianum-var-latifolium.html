Eriocaulon brownianum var. latifolium

Eriocaulon brownianum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf marsh grass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are often broad at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers arranged in dense clusters near the base of the stems. It thrives in saturated soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
