Eriope parvifolia subsp. glandulosa

Eriope parvifolia subsp.

glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular eriope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, glandular-dotted leaves and clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to rock gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
