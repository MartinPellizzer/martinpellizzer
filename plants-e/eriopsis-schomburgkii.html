Eriopsis schomburgkii

Eriopsis schomburgkii, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a star-like pattern, which bloom in spring or early summer. Its leaves are usually small and hairy, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Eriopsis schomburgkii is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant landscaping.
