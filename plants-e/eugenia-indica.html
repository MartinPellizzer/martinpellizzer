Eugenia indica

Eugenia indica, commonly known as the Java apple or Indian laurel, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Eugenia indica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
