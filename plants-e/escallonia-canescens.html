Escallonia canescens

Escallonia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy blooms.

Escallonia canescens prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
