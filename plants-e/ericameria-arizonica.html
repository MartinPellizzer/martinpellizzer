Ericameria arizonica

Ericameria arizonica, commonly known as Arizona greasewood, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Ericameria arizonica is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
