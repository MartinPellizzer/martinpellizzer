Excoecaria biglandulosa var. salicifolia

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. salicifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved croton, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, broad, ovate leaves with a waxy surface.

The leaves are typically green on the upper surface and pale or white underneath, giving it a striking appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy berries. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit.
