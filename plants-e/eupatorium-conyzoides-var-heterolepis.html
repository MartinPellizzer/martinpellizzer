Eupatorium conyzoides var. heterolepis

Eupatorium conyzoides var. heterolepis, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom during the summer months. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
