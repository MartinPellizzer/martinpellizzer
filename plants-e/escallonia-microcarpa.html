Escallonia microcarpa

Escallonia microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited escallonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, red berry that matures in the fall, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Escallonia microcarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in coastal gardens.
