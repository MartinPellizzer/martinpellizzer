Elytrigia smithii var. molle

Elytrigia smithii var. molle, commonly known as the soft brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it ecologically valuable in arid environments.
