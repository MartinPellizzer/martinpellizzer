Erythrococca welwitschiana

Erythrococca welwitschiana, commonly known as the red cocoon tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking red fruits that encase its seeds. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ecological value.

The tree's name derives from the red, cocoon-like structures that protect its seeds.
