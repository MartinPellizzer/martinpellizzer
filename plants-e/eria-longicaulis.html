Eria longicaulis

Eria longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in clusters. Eria longicaulis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions in Southeast Asia.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
