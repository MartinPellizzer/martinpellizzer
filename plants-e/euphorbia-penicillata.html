Euphorbia penicillata

Euphorbia penicillata, commonly known as the pencil euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a cylindrical, branching stem that resembles a pencil, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia penicillata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
