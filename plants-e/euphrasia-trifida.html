Euphrasia trifida

Euphrasia trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Euphrasia trifida is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported eye health benefits.
