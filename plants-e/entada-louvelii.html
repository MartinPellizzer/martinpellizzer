Entada louvelii

Entada louvelii, commonly known as the Brazilian trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy that provides shade in its natural habitat.
