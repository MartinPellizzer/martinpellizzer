Epilobium glandulosum var. macounii

Epilobium glandulosum var. macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with glandular hairs, which contribute to its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and it produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
