Eugenia isabelensis

Eugenia isabelensis, commonly known as the Isabel's eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in understory layers of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
