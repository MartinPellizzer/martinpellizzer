Eugenia imbricata

Eugenia imbricata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Eugenia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
