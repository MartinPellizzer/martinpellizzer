Elephas incurva

Elephas incurva, commonly known as the curled elephant's ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often curled or twisted, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Araceae family, which includes other familiar genera such as Caladium and Dieffenbachia. Its foliage is typically a deep green, sometimes with variegation, and it thrives in moist, shady environments.

Elephas incurva is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking leaf structure and ease of care.
