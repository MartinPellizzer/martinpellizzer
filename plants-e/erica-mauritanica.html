Erica mauritanica

Erica mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan heath, is a flowering shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can thrive in harsh, coastal environments. Erica mauritanica produces small, dark blue to black berries that are typically consumed by birds.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
