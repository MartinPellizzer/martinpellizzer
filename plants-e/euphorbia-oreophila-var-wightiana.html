Euphorbia oreophila var. wightiana

Euphorbia oreophila var. wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and fleshy, often blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified floral structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
