Emilia ukambensis

Emilia ukambensis, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia ukambensis is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in sunny, well-drained soils.
