Erica × boucheana

Erica × boucheana, commonly known as the Cape heath or the African heather, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.

Erica × boucheana is also noted for its drought tolerance and adaptability to various climates.
