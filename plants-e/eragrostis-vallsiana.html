Eragrostis vallsiana

Eragrostis vallsiana, commonly known as the Valls' lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in drought-prone environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping in regions with similar climates.
