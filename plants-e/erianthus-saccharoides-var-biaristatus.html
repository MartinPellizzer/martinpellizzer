Erianthus saccharoides var. biaristatus

Erianthus saccharoides var. biaristatus, commonly known as the silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant is characterized by its dense, clump-forming growth habit and is often found in wetland or moist soil environments. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
