Eucalyptus pauciflora var. nana

Eucalyptus pauciflora var. nana, commonly known as the alpine snow gum, is a small, hardy shrub or tree native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, silver-blue foliage, and small, white or pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is adapted to cold, harsh environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and they are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging climates.
