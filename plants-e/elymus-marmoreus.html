Elymus marmoreus

Elymus marmoreus, commonly known as the marble grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish to reddish flowers.

Elymus marmoreus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
