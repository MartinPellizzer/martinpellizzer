Eriosyce clavata

Eriosyce clavata, commonly known as the Clavate Eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
