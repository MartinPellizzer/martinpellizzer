Elymus lazicus

Elymus lazicus, commonly known as the desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds, aiding in its propagation across dry, sandy soils. Elymus lazicus is often found in steppe and desert ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.

Its resilience to drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of its native habitats.
