Elaeocarpus gitingensis

Elaeocarpus gitingensis, commonly known as the Gitingen elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
