Ericoides decipiens

Ericoides decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive eric, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in temperate regions with cool, moist climates. Its unique morphology and adaptive features allow it to survive in nutrient-poor environments.

Ericoides decipiens plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insect pollinators.
