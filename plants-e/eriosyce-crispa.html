Eriosyce crispa

Eriosyce crispa, commonly known as the crinkled rat's tail cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that are covered in small, white, woolly spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its rugged appearance. Eriosyce crispa is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within its native habitat.
