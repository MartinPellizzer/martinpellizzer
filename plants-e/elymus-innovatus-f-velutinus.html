Elymus innovatus f. velutinus

Elymus innovatus f. velutinus, commonly known as the velvet foxtail, is a variant of the foxtail grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silky, and soft foliage, which gives it a distinctive velvety texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces tall, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in landscaping for its unique appearance.
