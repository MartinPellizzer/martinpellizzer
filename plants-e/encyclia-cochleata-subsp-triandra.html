Encyclia cochleata subsp. triandra

Encyclia cochleata subsp.

triandra, commonly known as the triandra orchid, is a species native to Central and South America. It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that supports its growth during dry periods.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

This orchid thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
