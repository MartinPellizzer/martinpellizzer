Euodia bracteata

Euodia bracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed frankincense tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a fragrant resin, which has been used traditionally in incense and medicinal applications. It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Euodia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
