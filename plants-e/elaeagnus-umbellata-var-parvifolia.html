Elaeagnus umbellata var. parvifolia

Elaeagnus umbellata var. parvifolia, commonly known as silverberry or sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its small, silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries that turn red in autumn. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Additionally, it has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for wildlife.
