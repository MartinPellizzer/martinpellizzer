Euphorbia beharensis var. guillemetii

Euphorbia beharensis var. guillemetii, commonly known as the Cape coral tree, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branching structure and clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of succulents, and it produces small, spiny leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
