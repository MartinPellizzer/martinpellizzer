Eleutherococcus simonii var. longipedicellatus

Eleutherococcus simonii var. longipedicellatus, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, dark green leaves that are oval in shape. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in shaded woodland environments.

This species is valued for its adaptogenic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
