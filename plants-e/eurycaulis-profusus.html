Eurycaulis profusus

Eurycaulis profusus, commonly known as the denseleaf flax, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are densely packed, giving it a lush, mat-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Eurycaulis profusus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
