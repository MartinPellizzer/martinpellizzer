Erysimum griffithii

Erysimum griffithii, commonly known as Griffith's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its compact growth habit and showy flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.
