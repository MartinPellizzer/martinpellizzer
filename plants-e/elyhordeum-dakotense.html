Elyhordeum dakotense

Elyhordeum dakotense, commonly known as the Dakota needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the central United States.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Elyhordeum dakotense plays an important role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife in its native range.
