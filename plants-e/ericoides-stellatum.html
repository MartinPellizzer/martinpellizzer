Ericoides stellatum

Ericoides stellatum, commonly known as the starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Ericoides stellatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
