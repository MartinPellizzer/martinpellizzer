Erythraea elodes

Erythraea elodes, commonly known as the redwater lily, is a water plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and broad, floating leaves. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it to the pond bottom while its stems extend toward the surface.

Erythraea elodes plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
