Eulophia porphyroglossa

Eulophia porphyroglossa, commonly known as the purple-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking purple-lip flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forests and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eulophia porphyroglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
