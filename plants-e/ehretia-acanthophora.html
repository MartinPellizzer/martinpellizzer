Ehretia acanthophora

Ehretia acanthophora, commonly known as the hairy leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
