Eucalyptus chloroclada

Eucalyptus chloroclada, commonly known as the grey box, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellow in color. Eucalyptus chloroclada is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
