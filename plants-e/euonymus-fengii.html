Euonymus fengii

Euonymus fengii, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange foliage that turns vibrant in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruits are four-lobed capsules that split open to release seeds.

Euonymus fengii thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for garden use.
