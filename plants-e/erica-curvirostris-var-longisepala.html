Erica curvirostris var. longisepala

Erica curvirostris var. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The variety is distinguished by its elongated sepals, which give it its specific epithet. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
