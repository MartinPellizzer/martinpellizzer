Encyclia parallela

Encyclia parallela, commonly known as the parallel orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a single spike of white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Encyclia parallela is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
