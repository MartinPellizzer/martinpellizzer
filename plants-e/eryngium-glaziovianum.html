Eryngium glaziovianum

Eryngium glaziovianum, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant features spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Eryngium glaziovianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
