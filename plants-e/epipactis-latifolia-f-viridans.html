Epipactis latifolia f. viridans

Epipactis latifolia f. viridans, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and its distinctive, showy greenish-white flowers with a yellow lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, humusy soil. It is a perennial species that produces erect, branching stems with numerous flowers during the summer months.

The orchid is noted for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
