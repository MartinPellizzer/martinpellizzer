Eugenia talbotii

Eugenia talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the wet tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
