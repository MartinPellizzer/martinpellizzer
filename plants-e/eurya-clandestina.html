Eurya clandestina

Eurya clandestina, commonly known as the hidden eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in temperate forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
