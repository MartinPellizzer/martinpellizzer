Erodium moschatum var. cicutarioides

Erodium moschatum var. cicutarioides, commonly known as the sweet everlasting or sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

The leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine for their purported soothing and anti-inflammatory properties.
