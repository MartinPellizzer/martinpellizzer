Erythroxylum buxifolium

Erythroxylum buxifolium, commonly known as the waxleaf coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain minute, elliptical seeds.

Erythroxylum buxifolium is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is related to the plant species used in the production of coca leaves.
