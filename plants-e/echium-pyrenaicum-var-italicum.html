Echium pyrenaicum var. italicum

Echium pyrenaicum var. italicum, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a distinctive, woolly seed head that aids in wind dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
