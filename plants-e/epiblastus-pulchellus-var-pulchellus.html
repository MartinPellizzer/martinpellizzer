Epiblastus pulchellus var. pulchellus

Epiblastus pulchellus var. pulchellus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of soft, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
