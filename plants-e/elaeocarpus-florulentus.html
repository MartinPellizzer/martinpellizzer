Elaeocarpus florulentus

Elaeocarpus florulentus, commonly known as the showy elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
