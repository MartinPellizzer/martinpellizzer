Elaphoglossum gracilipes

Elaphoglossum gracilipes, commonly known as the slender fern, is a member of the Hymenophyllum family and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it thrives in high humidity. The plant has a compact growth habit with a short, creeping rhizome that supports its small, lance-shaped leaves. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species.

Elaphoglossum gracilipes is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to add a lush, green texture to shaded garden spaces.
