Erysimum carniolicum

Erysimum carniolicum, commonly known as the Carniolan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinctive four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum carniolicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
