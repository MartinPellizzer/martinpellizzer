Elaeocarpus magnifolius

Elaeocarpus magnifolius, commonly known as the large-leaf elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
