Eria dasyphylla

Eria dasyphylla, commonly known as the hairy-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss.

Eria dasyphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
