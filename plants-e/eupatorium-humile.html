Eupatorium humile

Eupatorium humile, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium humile is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported fever-reducing and anti-inflammatory effects.
