Eriocereus × jusbertii

Eriocereus × jusbertii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to southern Texas and northeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit, with large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers typically bloom in the summer months, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

Eriocereus × jusbertii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
