Eria schildiana

Eria schildiana, commonly known as the Schild's orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the stem. Eria schildiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its elegant appearance and ability to bloom multiple times throughout the year.

It requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight to flourish.
