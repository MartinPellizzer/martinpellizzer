Eriogonum exilifolium

Eriogonum exilifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
