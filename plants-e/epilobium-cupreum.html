Epilobium cupreum

Epilobium cupreum, commonly known as copperweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Epilobium cupreum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
