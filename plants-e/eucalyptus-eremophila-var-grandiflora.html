Eucalyptus eremophila var. grandiflora

Eucalyptus eremophila var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Great Desert Gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped, grey-green leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is also valued for its use in traditional bush medicine and its role in supporting local wildlife.
