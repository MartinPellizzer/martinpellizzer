Erianthus saccharoides subsp. biaristatus

Erianthus saccharoides subsp.

biaristatus, commonly known as the tall sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high temperatures and seasonal droughts.

This subspecies is valued for its potential in bioenergy production and as a forage crop.
