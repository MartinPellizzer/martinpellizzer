Eremophila glabra subsp. carnosa

Eremophila glabra subsp.

carnosa, commonly known as the woolly emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against harsh environmental conditions.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
