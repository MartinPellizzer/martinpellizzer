Eriogonum deflexum var. insigne

Eriogonum deflexum var. insigne, commonly known as the showy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
