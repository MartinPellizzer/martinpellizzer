Eugenia haitiensis

Eugenia haitiensis, commonly known as Haitian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
