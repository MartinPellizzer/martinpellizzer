Eryngium duriaei

Eryngium duriaei, commonly known as the Cape Harebell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a dense cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eryngium duriaei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
