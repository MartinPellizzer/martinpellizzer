Erythroxylum hasslerianum

Erythroxylum hasslerianum, commonly known as the Hassler's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spherical capsules containing numerous seeds. It is known for its latex-producing capabilities, similar to other members of the Erythroxylum genus.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.
