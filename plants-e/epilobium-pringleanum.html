Epilobium pringleanum

Epilobium pringleanum, commonly known as Pringle's willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in the summer months. Its flowers are arranged in spike-like inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

Epilobium pringleanum has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
