Erica lambertiana

Erica lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

Erica lambertiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its compact growth habit and year-round visual interest.
