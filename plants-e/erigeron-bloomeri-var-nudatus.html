Erigeron bloomeri var. nudatus

Erigeron bloomeri var. nudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairless leaves and stems that bear clusters of small, blue to purple daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
