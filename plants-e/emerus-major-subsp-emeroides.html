Emerus major subsp. emeroides

Emerus major subsp.

emeroides, commonly known as the Emerald Rush, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the main species, Emerus major.
