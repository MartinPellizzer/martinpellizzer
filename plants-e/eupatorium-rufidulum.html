Eupatorium rufidulum

Eupatorium rufidulum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium rufidulum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
