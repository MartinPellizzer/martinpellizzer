Eugenia clypeolata

Eugenia clypeolata, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and have a tart, musky flavor. Eugenia clypeolata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
