Ericoides confertum

Ericoides confertum, commonly known as the dense heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericoides confertum is often found in heathlands and moorlands, where it forms dense stands.

Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems.
