Eugenia tanaensis

Eugenia tanaensis, commonly known as the Tanaensis myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are typically small, berry-like drupes that contain several seeds.

Eugenia tanaensis is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
