Euphorbia hurusawae

Euphorbia hurusawae, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Euphorbia hurusawae is known for its ability to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.
