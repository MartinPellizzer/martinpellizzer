Eriocaulon bongardii

Eriocaulon bongardii, commonly known as the Bongard's spike-moss, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground covers in suitable conditions.
