Ervum macrocarpum

Ervum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
