Elodea brandegeeae

Elodea brandegeeae, commonly known as the Florida waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a submerged, herbaceous plant with slender, branching stems that are often green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown, which are followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Elodea brandegeeae thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes.

Its presence is often indicative of good water quality and stable aquatic ecosystems.
