Eleocharis brehmeriana

Eleocharis brehmeriana, commonly known as the Brehmer's spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in southern Africa.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually green or brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance as a low-growing, herbaceous plant.

Eleocharis brehmeriana plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
