Eugenia woodburyana

Eugenia woodburyana, commonly known as the Woodbury's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other notable plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red or purple as they mature.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
