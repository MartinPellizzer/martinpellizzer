Entada tetraptera

Entada tetraptera, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, woody pods containing hard, angular seeds. Its bark is thick and often flaky, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of timber.
