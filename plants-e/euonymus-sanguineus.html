Euonymus sanguineus

Euonymus sanguineus, commonly known as the redseeded dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
