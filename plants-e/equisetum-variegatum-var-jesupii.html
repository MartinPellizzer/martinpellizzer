Equisetum variegatum var. jesupii

Equisetum variegatum var. jesupii, commonly known as the Jesup's horse tail, is a variant of the variable horsetail.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its whorled arrangement of stems and spore-bearing structures. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its stems are segmented and covered with small, scale-like leaves, and it produces spores in specialized cones called strobili.

This variety is notable for its distinctive variegated appearance, with alternating green and yellowish bands along the stem.
