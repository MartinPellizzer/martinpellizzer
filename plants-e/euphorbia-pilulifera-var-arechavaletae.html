Euphorbia pilulifera var. arechavaletae

Euphorbia pilulifera var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a low-growing, branched stem covered in small, fleshy, greenish-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in summer, which are surrounded by a modified leaf called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in arid landscapes.
