Emilia × latens

Emilia × latens, commonly known as the hybrid emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture.

Emilia × latens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
