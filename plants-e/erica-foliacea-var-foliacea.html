Erica foliacea var. foliacea

Erica foliacea var. foliacea, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid coastal environment. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.

This hardy plant is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
