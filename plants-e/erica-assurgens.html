Erica assurgens

Erica assurgens, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white, star-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sunlight, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Erica assurgens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

Its flowers bloom in winter, adding a striking visual contrast to the landscape during the colder months.
