Euphorbia geroldii

Euphorbia geroldii, commonly known as the Gerold's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in small, greenish-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia geroldii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
