Embelia xylocarpa

Embelia xylocarpa, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The botanical features of Embelia xylocarpa make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
