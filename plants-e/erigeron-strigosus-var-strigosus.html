Erigeron strigosus var. strigosus

Erigeron strigosus var. strigosus, commonly known as the rough fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
