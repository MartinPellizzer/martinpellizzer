Echinospermum ispahanicum

Echinospermum ispahanicum, commonly known as the Persian echinus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small echinoids. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique appearance makes it a striking addition to xeriscapic gardens.
