Evolvulus ericifolius var. subcapitatus

Evolvulus ericifolius var. subcapitatus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods that split open to release tiny seeds.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
