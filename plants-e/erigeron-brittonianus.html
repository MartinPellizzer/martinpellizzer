Erigeron brittonianus

Erigeron brittonianus, commonly known as Britton's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a distinctive silver-gray underside.
