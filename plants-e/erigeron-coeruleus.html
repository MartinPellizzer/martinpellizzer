Erigeron coeruleus

Erigeron coeruleus, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and produces fluffy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Erigeron coeruleus is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
