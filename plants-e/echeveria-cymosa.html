Echeveria cymosa

Echeveria cymosa, commonly known as the silver chalice, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Echeveria cymosa is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and indoor gardens.
