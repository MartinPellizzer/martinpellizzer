Eria pumila

Eria pumila, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial plant that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers. It is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a potted plant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
