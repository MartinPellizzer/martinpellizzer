Echium italicum subsp. pyrenaicum

Echium italicum subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and parts of southern France. It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. It has a basal rosette of leaves and a tall, branching stem that supports its showy inflorescence.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
