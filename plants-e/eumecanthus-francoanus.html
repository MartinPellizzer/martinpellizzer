Eumecanthus francoanus

Eumecanthus francoanus, commonly known as the Francoan orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has long, slender leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
