Erythrophleum purpurascens

Erythrophleum purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that turn a striking reddish-purple color in autumn. The tree produces yellow flowers followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical lowland forests and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
