Erythrina herbacea

Erythrina herbacea, commonly known as the coral tree or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in thorns. Erythrina herbacea is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
