Eriosyce subgibbosa subsp. vallenarensis

Eriosyce subgibbosa subsp.

vallenarensis, commonly known as the Vallenarensis Eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to Chile. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green foliage. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
