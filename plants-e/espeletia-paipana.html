Espeletia paipana

Espeletia paipana, commonly known as the Páramo espeletia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth form and dense, woolly foliage. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments at high altitudes, typically above 3,000 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Espeletia paipana plays an important ecological role in páramo ecosystems by contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
