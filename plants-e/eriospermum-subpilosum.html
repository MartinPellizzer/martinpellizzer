Eriospermum subpilosum

Eriospermum subpilosum, commonly known as the silky eriosperm, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of softly hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, terminal heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its fibrous root system is adapted to survive in arid conditions, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Eriospermum subpilosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
