Eustachya japonica

Eustachya japonica, commonly known as Japanese grass or Japanese silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, silver-white plumes during the flowering season. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value to gardens.

Eustachya japonica is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping and traditional Japanese gardens.
