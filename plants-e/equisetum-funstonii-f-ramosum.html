Equisetum funstonii f. ramosum

Equisetum funstonii f. ramosum, commonly known as the Funston's horse tail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a distinctive, segmented stem. This plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a primitive structure, retaining features from its ancient evolutionary lineage.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in ecological succession.
