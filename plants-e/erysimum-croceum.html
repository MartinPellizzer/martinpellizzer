Erysimum croceum

Erysimum croceum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in a cross shape, which is characteristic of the Brassicaceae family. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed areas.

Erysimum croceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
