Ernstamra urens

Ernstamra urens, commonly known as the fragrant nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly hairy texture.

Ernstamra urens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.
