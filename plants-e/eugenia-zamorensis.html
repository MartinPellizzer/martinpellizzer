Eugenia zamorensis

Eugenia zamorensis, commonly known as the Zamora's evergreen myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments within humid forests.

Eugenia zamorensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has limited use in traditional medicine.
