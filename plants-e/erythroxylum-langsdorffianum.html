Erythroxylum langsdorffianum

Erythroxylum langsdorffianum, commonly known as the Brazilian erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It is notable for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
