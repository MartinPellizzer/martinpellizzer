Eupatorium semiserratum

Eupatorium semiserratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with finely serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
