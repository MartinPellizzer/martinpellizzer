Epallageiton panamitense

Epallageiton panamitense, commonly known as the Panamite orchid, is a rare and endemic species found in the cloud forests of Panama.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on tree trunks or branches, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white and pale pink.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in its native region.
