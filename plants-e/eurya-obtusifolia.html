Eurya obtusifolia

Eurya obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved eurya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are oval to elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and a broad, rounded tip.

The small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.
