Eremia totta var. bartlingiana

Eremia totta var. bartlingiana, commonly known as the Bartling's eremisia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its compact, silver-gray foliage and small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens. Its succulent leaves help it store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is often found in rocky slopes and sandy soils across the southwestern United States.
