Eperua rhomboidea

Eperua rhomboidea, commonly known as the diamond eperua, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is notable for its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, and the tree is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Eperua rhomboidea is also recognized for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
