Elymus × ebingeri

Elymus × ebingeri, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid between Elymus trachycoleus and Elymus wrightii, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant has tall, slender stems and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, awned florets. Its foliage is coarse and bluish-green, often used in landscaping for erosion control.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy ornamental grass.
