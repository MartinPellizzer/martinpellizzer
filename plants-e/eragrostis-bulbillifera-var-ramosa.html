Eragrostis bulbillifera var. ramosa

Eragrostis bulbillifera var. ramosa, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with hairy surfaces, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
