Epidendrum pterocaulum

Epidendrum pterocaulum, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizome and multiple pseudobulbs. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, intricate structure that includes a spurred lip.

Epidendrum pterocaulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
