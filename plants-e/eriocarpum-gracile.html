Eriocarpum gracile

Eriocarpum gracile, commonly known as the slender eriocarp, is a small, annual herb native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Eriocarpum gracile is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.
