Echium angustifolium var. eriobotryum

Echium angustifolium var. eriobotryum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland deserts.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller, more compact flowers compared to other echium species.
