Elaeagnus kanaii

Elaeagnus kanaii, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are pollinated by insects. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Elaeagnus kanaii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
