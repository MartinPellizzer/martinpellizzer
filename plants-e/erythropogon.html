Erythropogon

Erythropogon, commonly known as purpletop, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions of the Americas, particularly in the western United States and Mexico. The plants are characterized by their tall, slender stems and showy purple or pink flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Erythropogon species are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used historically by indigenous peoples for food and traditional medicine.
