Eleocharis uniglumis var. latior

Eleocharis uniglumis var. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has leaf-like bracts that are usually shorter than the spikelets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
