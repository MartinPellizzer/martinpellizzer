Erica cygnea

Erica cygnea, commonly known as the silver evergreen heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage that displays a distinctive silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica cygnea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
