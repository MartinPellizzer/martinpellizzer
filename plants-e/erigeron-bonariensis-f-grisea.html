Erigeron bonariensis f. grisea

Erigeron bonariensis f. grisea, commonly known as the grayleaf fleabane, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It features gray-green, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
