Euphorbia serrulata var. pubescens

Euphorbia serrulata var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a branched stem covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, which give it its varietal name "pubescens.". The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, contributing to its scientific name "serrulata.".

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
