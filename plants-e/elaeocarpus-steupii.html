Elaeocarpus steupii

Elaeocarpus steupii, commonly known as the Steup's elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
