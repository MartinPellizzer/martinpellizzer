Erysimum asperum var. lompocense

Erysimum asperum var. lompocense, commonly known as Lompoc wallflower, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
