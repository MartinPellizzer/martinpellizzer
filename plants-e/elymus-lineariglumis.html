Elymus lineariglumis

Elymus lineariglumis, commonly known as the slender spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and elongated spikelets that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, fields, and meadows. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Elymus lineariglumis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
