Eriochilus collinus subsp. sericeus

Eriochilus collinus subsp.

sericeus, commonly known as the silver-haired eriochilus, is a perennial shrub native to the alpine regions of southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
