Echinops bampsianus

Echinops bampsianus, commonly known as the Bampsian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its genus name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops bampsianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
