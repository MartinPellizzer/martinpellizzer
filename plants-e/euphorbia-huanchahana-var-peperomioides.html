Euphorbia huanchahana var. peperomioides

Euphorbia huanchahana var. peperomioides, commonly known as the Huancasacha or Huanchahana, is a unique succulent native to the Andean highlands of Peru.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow or green, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are usually alternate and may have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is noted for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
