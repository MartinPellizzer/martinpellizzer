Excoecaria lissophylla

Excoecaria lissophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.
