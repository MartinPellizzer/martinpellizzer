Erica eremocharis

Erica eremocharis, commonly known as the summer heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to autumn, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Erica eremocharis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
