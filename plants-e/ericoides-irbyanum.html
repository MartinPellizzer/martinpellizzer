Ericoides irbyanum

Ericoides irbyanum, commonly known as the Irby's heath, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and heathers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or swampy areas. Its small, leathery leaves are usually dark green and glossy, and it produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white.

Ericoides irbyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, acidic environments.
