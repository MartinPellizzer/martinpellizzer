Epigenia crenata

Epigenia crenata, commonly known as the wax plant or waxleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Epigenia crenata is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with a dense, bushy appearance.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
