Elaeocarpus pseudosepicanus

Elaeocarpus pseudosepicanus, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
