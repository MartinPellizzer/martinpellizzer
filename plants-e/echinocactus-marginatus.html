Echinocactus marginatus

Echinocactus marginatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored ornamental plant in arid environments.
