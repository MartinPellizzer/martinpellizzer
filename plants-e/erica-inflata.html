Erica inflata

Erica inflata, commonly known as the inflated heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, showy flowers that bloom in spring. Erica inflata typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
