Eulophia brevisepala

Eulophia brevisepala, commonly known as the short-lobed eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the humid environment.

Eulophia brevisepala thrives in shaded, moist forest understories and is often found in tropical regions.
