Erigeron graveolens

Erigeron graveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Sweet everlasting is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in dried floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms.
