Erodium absinthoides var. elatum

Erodium absinthoides var. elatum, commonly known as the tall field pansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, spreading habit with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden use in temperate climates.
