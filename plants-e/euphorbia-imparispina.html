Euphorbia imparispina

Euphorbia imparispina, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem system with numerous spines and a distinctive, spiny crown of leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its latex contains toxic compounds, requiring caution when handling.
