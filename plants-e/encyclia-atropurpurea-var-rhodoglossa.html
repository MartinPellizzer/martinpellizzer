Encyclia atropurpurea var. rhodoglossa

Encyclia atropurpurea var. rhodoglossa, commonly known as the red-lipped orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant features long, narrow leaves and blooms during the warmer months, typically from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
