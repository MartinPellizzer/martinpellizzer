Euphorbia sahendi

Euphorbia sahendi, commonly known as the Sahel euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a thick, succulent stem adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified inflorescences, and is known for its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. Euphorbia sahendi has a spiny stem and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Its botanical features make it a resilient species well-suited to its native habitat.
