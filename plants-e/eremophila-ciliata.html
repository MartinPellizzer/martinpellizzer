Eremophila ciliata

Eremophila ciliata, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in winter and spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dry gardens.

Eremophila ciliata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting native pollinators.
