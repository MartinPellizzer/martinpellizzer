Ewaldia lobata

Ewaldia lobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Ewaldia lobata is adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access groundwater.
