Ervum biebersteinii

Ervum biebersteinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by slender, elongated pods containing small, dark seeds. It is often found in rocky or arid environments and is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
