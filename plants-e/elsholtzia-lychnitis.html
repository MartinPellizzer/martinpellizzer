Elsholtzia lychnitis

Elsholtzia lychnitis, commonly known as the flame violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and spikes of violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Elsholtzia lychnitis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
