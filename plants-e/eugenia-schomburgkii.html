Eugenia schomburgkii

Eugenia schomburgkii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Schomburgk's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Eugenia schomburgkii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
