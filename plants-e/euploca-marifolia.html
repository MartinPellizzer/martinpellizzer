Euploca marifolia

Euploca marifolia, commonly known as the coastal euploca, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Euploca marifolia is often found in dune environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens in arid and coastal regions.
