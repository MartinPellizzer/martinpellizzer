Eriogonum wrightii subsp. subscaposum

Eriogonum wrightii subsp.

subscaposum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its wiry, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing desert soils.
