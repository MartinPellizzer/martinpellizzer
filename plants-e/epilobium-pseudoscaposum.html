Epilobium pseudoscaposum

Epilobium pseudoscaposum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks and wet meadows. The plant features slender stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are elongated with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in the summer months.

Epilobium pseudoscaposum is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
