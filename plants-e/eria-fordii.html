Eria fordii

Eria fordii, commonly known as the Ford's eria, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial herb with slender, upright stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a basal rosette.

Eria fordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
