Elaeagnus macrophylla var. kotoensis

Elaeagnus macrophylla var. kotoensis, commonly known as the Kotoena silverleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a prominent midrib.
