Excoecaria venenata

Excoecaria venenata, commonly known as the Cannonball Tree, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, red or yellow flowers. The tree produces large, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single hard seed. Its bark and leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
