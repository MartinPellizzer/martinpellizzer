Eremophila prolata

Eremophila prolata, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and grey-green, adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila prolata is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
