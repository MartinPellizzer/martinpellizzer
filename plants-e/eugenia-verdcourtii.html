Eugenia verdcourtii

Eugenia verdcourtii, commonly known as the Congo guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, yellowish fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in local markets.
