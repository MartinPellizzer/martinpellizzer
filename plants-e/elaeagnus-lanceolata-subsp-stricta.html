Elaeagnus lanceolata subsp. stricta

Elaeagnus lanceolata subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf silverleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, short hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, typically in early spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
