Eriogonum kingii var. laxifolium

Eriogonum kingii var. laxifolium, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
