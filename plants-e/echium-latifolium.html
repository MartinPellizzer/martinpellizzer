Echium latifolium

Echium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Echium latifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
