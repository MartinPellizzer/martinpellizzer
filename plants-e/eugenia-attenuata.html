Eugenia attenuata

Eugenia attenuata, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing dense foliage that offers shade and habitat for various organisms.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy berry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.
