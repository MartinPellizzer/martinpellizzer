Equisetum californicum f. herbaceum

Equisetum californicum f. herbaceum, commonly known as the California horsetail, is a low-growing, herbaceous fern-like plant native to western North America.

It features a distinctive stem covered in small, scale-like structures and produces spore-bearing cones at the top. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its slender, jointed stems and whorled leaves give it a primitive, ancient appearance reminiscent of early plant life.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
