Eremophila fasciata

Eremophila fasciata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of tubular, pink to red flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub that plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Eremophila fasciata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
