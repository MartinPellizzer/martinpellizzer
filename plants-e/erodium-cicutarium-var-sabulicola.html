Erodium cicutarium var. sabulicola

Erodium cicutarium var. sabulicola, commonly known as sand erodium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of western Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in disturbed or coastal environments. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are trifoliate, with three narrow, pointed leaflets. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
