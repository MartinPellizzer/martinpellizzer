Elatine hexandra var. pedunculata

Elatine hexandra var. pedunculata, commonly known as the slender waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches.

Due to its low-growing habit and rapid growth, it is often found in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
