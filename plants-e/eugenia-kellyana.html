Eugenia kellyana

Eugenia kellyana, commonly known as Kelly's eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Eugenia kellyana is typically found in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils and salty sea air.
