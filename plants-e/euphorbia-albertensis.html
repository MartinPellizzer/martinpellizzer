Euphorbia albertensis

Euphorbia albertensis, commonly known as Albert's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to conserve water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
