Euphorbia alainii

Euphorbia alainii, commonly known as the Alain's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.

Euphorbia alainii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
