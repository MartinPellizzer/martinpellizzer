Encelia oblonga

Encelia oblonga, commonly known as the oblong leaf encelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptation to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

The flowers, which bloom in the spring, are yellow and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
