Euonymus yedoensis var. koehneanus

Euonymus yedoensis var. koehneanus, commonly known as the Japanese spindle tree or Koechlin's euonymus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal contribute to its use in both landscape design and horticultural collections.
