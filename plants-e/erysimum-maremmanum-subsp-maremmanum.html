Erysimum maremmanum subsp. maremmanum

Erysimum maremmanum subsp.

maremmanum, commonly known as the Maremma wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine plantings.
