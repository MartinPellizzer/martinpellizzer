Eugenia flava

Eugenia flava, commonly known as the golden guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which is typically yellow or orange when ripe, has a sweet and aromatic flavor. It is widely cultivated for its edible fruit and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
