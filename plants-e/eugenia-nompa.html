Eugenia nompa

Eugenia nompa, commonly known as the Nambu or Nambu tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is edible, is often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
