Elymus triticoides var. simplex

Elymus triticoides var. simplex, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and meadows. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and forage.
