Eberlanzia disarticulata

Eberlanzia disarticulata, commonly known as the brittle vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that often break easily, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

Eberlanzia disarticulata is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
