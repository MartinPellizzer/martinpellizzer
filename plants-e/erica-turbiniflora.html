Erica turbiniflora

Erica turbiniflora, commonly known as the turbiniflora heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense, needle-like leaves. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Erica turbiniflora thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
