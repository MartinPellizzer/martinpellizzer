Echinocactus parryi

Echinocactus parryi, commonly known as the Parry hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
