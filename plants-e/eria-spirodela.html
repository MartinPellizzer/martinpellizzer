Eria spirodela

Eria spirodela, commonly known as water shield, is a floating aquatic plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It has broad, arrowhead-shaped leaves that float on the water surface, with roots submerged in the water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. Its leaves are covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss.

Eria spirodela is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats.
