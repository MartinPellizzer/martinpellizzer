Endusa

Endusa, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a striking aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, slender stalks rising from the water's surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, providing a unique habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its roots anchor it in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Endusa is also noted for its symbolic significance in many cultures, often representing purity and spiritual enlightenment.
