Eupatorium patens var. rhodolaena

Eupatorium patens var. rhodolaena, commonly known as the pink eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to lavender flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

The varietal name "rhodolaena" refers to its showy, rose-like flower clusters.
