Eupatorium sericeum

Eupatorium sericeum, commonly known as silverleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale blue to white, appearing in late summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
