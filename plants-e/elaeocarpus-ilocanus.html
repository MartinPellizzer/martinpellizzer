Elaeocarpus ilocanus

Elaeocarpus ilocanus, commonly known as the Ilocano elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the mountainous regions of Luzon. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically dark red when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture.

Elaeocarpus ilocanus plays a role in local ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional practices.
