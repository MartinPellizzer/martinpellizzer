Echinofossulocactus lancifer

Echinofossulocactus lancifer, commonly known as the red-tipped barrel cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with red or purple tips. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
