Englerastrum alstonianum

Englerastrum alstonianum, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a notable feature and can cause skin irritation.

Englerastrum alstonianum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
