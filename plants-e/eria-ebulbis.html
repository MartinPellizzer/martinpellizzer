Eria ebulbis

Eria ebulbis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Eria ebulbis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
