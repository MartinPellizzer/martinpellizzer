Eupatorium areolare var. leiocarpum

Eupatorium areolare var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth fruit eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. Its medicinal properties have been studied for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
