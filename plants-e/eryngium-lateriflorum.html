Eryngium lateriflorum

Eryngium lateriflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue-purple flower heads and silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium lateriflorum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
