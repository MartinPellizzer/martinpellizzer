Eulophia aliwalensis

Eulophia aliwalensis, commonly known as the Aliwal orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to yellowish hairs. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
