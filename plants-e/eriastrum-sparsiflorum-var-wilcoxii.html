Eriastrum sparsiflorum var. wilcoxii

Eriastrum sparsiflorum var. wilcoxii, commonly known as the Wilcox dandelion, is a low-growing, herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are oblanceolate to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface and a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal head, which bloom in early spring. Its flowering stems are often branched and covered with fine, white hairs.

This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
