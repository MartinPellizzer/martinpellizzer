Erythrophleum africanum var. angustifoliatum

Erythrophleum africanum var. angustifoliatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
