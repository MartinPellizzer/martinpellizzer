Equisetum giganteum var. chilense

Equisetum giganteum var. chilense, commonly known as the Chilean horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces fertile strobila at the top of the stem, which contains spores for reproduction. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its unique appearance and ancient lineage make it a distinctive and interesting botanical specimen.
