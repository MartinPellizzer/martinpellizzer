Eryngium baldwinii

Eryngium baldwinii, commonly known as Baldwin's thistle, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray, woolly foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer, and it is often found in wetland habitats.

Eryngium baldwinii is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
