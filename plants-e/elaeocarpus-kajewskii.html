Elaeocarpus kajewskii

Elaeocarpus kajewskii, commonly known as the Kajewskii's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
