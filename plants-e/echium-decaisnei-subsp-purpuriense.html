Echium decaisnei subsp. purpuriense

Echium decaisnei subsp.

purpuriense, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its unique floral display.
