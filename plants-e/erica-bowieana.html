Erica bowieana

Erica bowieana, commonly known as Bowie's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in sandy, acidic soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Erica bowieana is known for its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
