Eulophia paradoxa var. greatrexii

Eulophia paradoxa var. greatrexii, commonly known as the Greatrex's eulophia, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a robust rhizome with dense, fleshy roots that help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with large, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
