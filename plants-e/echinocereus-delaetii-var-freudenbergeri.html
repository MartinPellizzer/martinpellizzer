Echinocereus delaetii var. freudenbergeri

Echinocereus delaetii var. freudenbergeri, commonly known as the Freudenberger's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in prominent, white to pale blue spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are typically borne at the stem apex. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh desert conditions.
