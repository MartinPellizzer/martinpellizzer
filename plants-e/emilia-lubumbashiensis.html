Emilia lubumbashiensis

Emilia lubumbashiensis, commonly known as the Lubumbashi emilia, is a flowering plant native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically features hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, adapted to its tropical and subtropical habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia lubumbashiensis is noted for its ornamental value and potential ecological significance in its native environment.
