Echites sanctae-martae

Echites sanctae-martae, commonly known as the sacred mart's echites, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
