Eriogonum argillosum

Eriogonum argillosum, commonly known as the claybark buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or clay soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a low-growing habit with woody stems that become more prominent with age.

Eriogonum argillosum is adapted to arid conditions and is a common component of chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.
