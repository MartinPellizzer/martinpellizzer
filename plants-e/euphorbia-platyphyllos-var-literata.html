Euphorbia platyphyllos var. literata

Euphorbia platyphyllos var. literata, commonly known as the "literary euphorbia," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powder. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the species is known for its ability to store water, making it drought-tolerant.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
