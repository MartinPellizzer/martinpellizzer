Eremopyrum bonaepartis

Eremopyrum bonaepartis, commonly known as the Bonaepartis sedge, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typical of the Cyperaceae family. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats. Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
