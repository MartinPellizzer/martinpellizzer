Echinopsis thelegona

Echinopsis thelegona, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pink and are pollinated by nocturnal insects.

Echinopsis thelegona is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
