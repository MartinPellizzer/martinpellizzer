Echeveria × gilva

Echeveria × gilva, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, often blue or greenish leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder called farina. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Echeveria × gilva is popular among gardeners for its ornamental value and ease of care.
