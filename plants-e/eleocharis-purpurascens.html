Eleocharis purpurascens

Eleocharis purpurascens, commonly known as purple spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence that turns a striking purple hue as it matures. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. Its slender, leaf-like stems are often mistaken for grasses, but they lack the jointed structure typical of true grasses.

Eleocharis purpurascens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
