Ephedra nevadensis var. viridis

Ephedra nevadensis var. viridis, commonly known as green ephedra, is a shrub native to the deserts of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

This variety is often found in rocky soils and is an important component of desert ecosystems.
