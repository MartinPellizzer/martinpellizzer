Erica anomala

Erica anomala, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
