Echinocactus caespitosus

Echinocactus caespitosus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that often develop a rounded, barrel-like shape. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a striking addition to both indoor and outdoor landscapes.
