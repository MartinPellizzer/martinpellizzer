Eriosyce heinrichiana var. intermedia

Eriosyce heinrichiana var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate hairy cactus, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to grayish hairs, which provide insulation and protection against intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative plant.
