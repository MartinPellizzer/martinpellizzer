Esula peploides

Esula peploides, commonly known as the silky sandwort, is a small, mat-forming flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in loose, sandy soils.

Esula peploides plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
