Erigeron hyssopifolius var. villicaulis

Erigeron hyssopifolius var. villicaulis, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a silky texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in arid landscapes.
