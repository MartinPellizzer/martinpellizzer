Ehrharta barbinodis

Ehrharta barbinodis, commonly known as the woolly fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or sandy environments.

Ehrharta barbinodis is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
