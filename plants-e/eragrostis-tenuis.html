Eragrostis tenuis

Eragrostis tenuis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in association with other grasses and forbs in ecological communities.

Eragrostis tenuis is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
