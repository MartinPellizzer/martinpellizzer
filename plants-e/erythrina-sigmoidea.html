Erythrina sigmoidea

Erythrina sigmoidea, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often giving the tree a fiery appearance. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are long and slender, containing several seeds. Erythrina sigmoidea thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye.
