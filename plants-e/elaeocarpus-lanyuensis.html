Elaeocarpus lanyuensis

Elaeocarpus lanyuensis, commonly known as the Lanyu elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the island of Lanyu, located off the southeastern coast of Taiwan. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody drupe, which contains a single seed and is an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
