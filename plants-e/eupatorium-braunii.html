Eupatorium braunii

Eupatorium braunii, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Eupatorium braunii typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
