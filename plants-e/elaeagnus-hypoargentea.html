Elaeagnus hypoargentea

Elaeagnus hypoargentea, commonly known as silverleaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that provide a distinctive appearance, especially in the autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Elaeagnus hypoargentea is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for various landscaping applications.
