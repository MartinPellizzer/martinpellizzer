Elaeocarpus dictyophlebius

Elaeocarpus dictyophlebius, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to mid-elevation forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
