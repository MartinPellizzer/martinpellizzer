Euphorbia kamerunica

Euphorbia kamerunica, commonly known as the Cameroon spurge, is a succulent plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
