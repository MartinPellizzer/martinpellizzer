Eucalyptus triantha

Eucalyptus triantha, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a grayish-green hue and a rough texture. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.
