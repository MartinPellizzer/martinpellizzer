Eryngium grande

Eryngium grande, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white, bristly leaves. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium grande is often cultivated in gardens for its architectural form and ornamental value.
