Elymus sarymsactensis

Elymus sarymsactensis, commonly known as the Sarymsaktinsk feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves, which are typical of the grass family Poaceae. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
