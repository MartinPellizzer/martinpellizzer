Erica conspicua subsp. conspicua

Erica conspicua subsp.

conspicua, commonly known as the showy heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
