Erucastrum arabicum

Erucastrum arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian cress, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with small, pale yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and can withstand periods of drought.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.
