Erica botryoides

Erica botryoides, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and small, needle-like leaves covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica botryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
