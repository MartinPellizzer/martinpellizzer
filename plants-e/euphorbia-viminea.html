Euphorbia viminea

Euphorbia viminea, commonly known as the creeping spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a low-growing, sprawling stem that often forms dense mats, with small, alternate leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower structures, rather than true flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Euphorbia viminea is also known for its ability to propagate easily through stem cuttings.
