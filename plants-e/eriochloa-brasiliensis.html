Eriochloa brasiliensis

Eriochloa brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and drooping inflorescences that give it a weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spikelet-based flowers.

Eriochloa brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in humid habitats.
