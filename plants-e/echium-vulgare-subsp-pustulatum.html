Echium vulgare subsp. pustulatum

Echium vulgare subsp.

pustulatum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and distinctive purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats. Echium vulgare subsp.

pustulatum is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
