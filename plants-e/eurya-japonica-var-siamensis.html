Eurya japonica var. siamensis

Eurya japonica var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a waxy texture, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and year-round foliage make it a popular choice for hedges and garden borders.
