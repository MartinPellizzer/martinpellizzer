Eupatorium eurybiifolium

Eupatorium eurybiifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, covered with rough, hairy texture. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a distinctive grayish-green color.

It produces small, clustered white flowers in the late summer and early fall, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
