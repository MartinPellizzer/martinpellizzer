Eria ferruginea

Eria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
