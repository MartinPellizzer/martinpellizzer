Eragrostis pilosa var. perplexa

Eragrostis pilosa var. perplexa, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver or gray. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
