Eucalyptus cullenii

Eucalyptus cullenii, commonly known as the Cullen's mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suited to harsh conditions.

Eucalyptus cullenii is also valued for its aromatic oils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
