Elaeagnus conferta var. balansae

Elaeagnus conferta var. balansae, commonly known as the Balansae silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in catkins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
