Euscaphis japonica var. lanata

Euscaphis japonica var. lanata, commonly known as the Japanese wax tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
