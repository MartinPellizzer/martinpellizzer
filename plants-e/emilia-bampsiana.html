Emilia bampsiana

Emilia bampsiana, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features finely divided, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Emilia bampsiana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
