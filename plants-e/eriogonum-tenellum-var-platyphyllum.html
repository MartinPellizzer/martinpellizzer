Eriogonum tenellum var. platyphyllum

Eriogonum tenellum var. platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive flat, broad base, and it produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. It is known for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
