Escallonia corymbosa

Escallonia corymbosa, commonly known as the scarlet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of red or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include opposite leaves and a woody stem structure.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
