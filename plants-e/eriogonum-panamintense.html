Eriogonum panamintense

Eriogonum panamintense, commonly known as the Panamint buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various desert wildlife.

Eriogonum panamintense is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in drought-tolerant gardens.
