Eugenia × hybrida

Eugenia × hybrida, commonly known as the Syzygium genus, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia × hybrida is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
