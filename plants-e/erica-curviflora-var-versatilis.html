Erica curviflora var. versatilis

Erica curviflora var. versatilis, commonly known as the variable heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
