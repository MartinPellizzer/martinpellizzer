Eupatorium tenue

Eupatorium tenue, commonly known as slender boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are pale blue to white and attract various pollinators.

Eupatorium tenue is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
