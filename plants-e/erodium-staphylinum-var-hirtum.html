Erodium staphylinum var. hirtum

Erodium staphylinum var. hirtum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, slender stems and opposite leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and dry wastelands.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
