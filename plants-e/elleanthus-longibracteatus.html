Elleanthus longibracteatus

Elleanthus longibracteatus, commonly known as the long-bract euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stem and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated bracts that surround the flower heads, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Elleanthus longibracteatus is typically drought-tolerant and requires minimal care once established.
