Eucalyptus caleyi subsp. ovendenii

Eucalyptus caleyi subsp.

ovendenii, commonly known as the Ovenden's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which attract a variety of pollinators. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
