Eugenia consolatae

Eugenia consolatae, commonly known as the golden eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained soils.

Eugenia consolatae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract native wildlife.
