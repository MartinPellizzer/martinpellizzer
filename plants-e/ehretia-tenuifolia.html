Ehretia tenuifolia

Ehretia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slenderleaf ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
