Euphorbia babensis

Euphorbia babensis, commonly known as the Babena spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It features a distinctive, spiny stem with thick, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
