Eugenia capensis var. major

Eugenia capensis var. major, commonly known as the Natal mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, round, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its hard, durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
