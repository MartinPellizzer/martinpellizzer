Eugenia paranapitanguensis

Eugenia paranapitanguensis, commonly known as the Paranapitanga eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

The fruit, which is edible, is small and resembles a berry, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
