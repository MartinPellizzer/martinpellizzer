Elephantopus crispus

Elephantopus crispus, commonly known as the curly elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
