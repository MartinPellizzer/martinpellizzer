Echeveria longissima var. aztatlensis

Echeveria longissima var. aztatlensis, commonly known as the Aztatlensis Graptopetalum, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, often with a bluish or grayish hue. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

This variety is prized for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
