Eriogonum angulosum var. maculatum

Eriogonum angulosum var. maculatum, commonly known as the spotted leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance, often in shades of white or green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments.

This species is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
