Eriachne triseta

Eriachne triseta, commonly known as the three-branched grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eriachne triseta plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
