Erechtites sulcatus

Erechtites sulcatus, commonly known as the zigzag cupweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in wetlands, ditches, and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive wavy margin.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.
