Euphorbia engleriana

Euphorbia engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
