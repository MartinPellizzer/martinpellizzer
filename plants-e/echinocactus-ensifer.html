Echinocactus ensifer

Echinocactus ensifer, commonly known as the red-ribbed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is ribbed and covered with spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
