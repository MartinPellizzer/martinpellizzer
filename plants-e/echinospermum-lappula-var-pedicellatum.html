Echinospermum lappula var. pedicellatum

Echinospermum lappula var. pedicellatum, commonly known as the hairy lappula, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, characterized by its small, cross-shaped flowers and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are hairy and lobed, while the flowers are usually pale yellow or white.

The seeds are enclosed in a hairy, triangular capsule, which aids in wind dispersal.
