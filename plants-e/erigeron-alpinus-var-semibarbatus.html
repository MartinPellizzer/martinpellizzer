Erigeron alpinus var. semibarbatus

Erigeron alpinus var. semibarbatus, commonly known as the alpine fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, dry conditions. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
