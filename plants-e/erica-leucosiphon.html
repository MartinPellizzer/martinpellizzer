Erica leucosiphon

Erica leucosiphon, commonly known as the white-siphon heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica leucosiphon is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
