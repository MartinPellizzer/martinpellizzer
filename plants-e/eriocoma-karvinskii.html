Eriocoma karvinskii

Eriocoma karvinskii, commonly known as the Karvinsky's ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal display of white to pink flowers.

Eriocoma karvinskii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh mountainous terrains.
