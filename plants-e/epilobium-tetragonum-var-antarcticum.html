Epilobium tetragonum var. antarcticum

Epilobium tetragonum var. antarcticum, commonly known as Antarctic willow, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows in alpine and subantarctic regions, characterized by its low stature and dense growth habit. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a silver underside, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low temperatures and strong winds.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
