Echinocactus glaucescens

Echinocactus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive blue-gray coloration due to a waxy coating on its stems. The plant features prominent, sharp spines and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with succulent stems that store water efficiently.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
