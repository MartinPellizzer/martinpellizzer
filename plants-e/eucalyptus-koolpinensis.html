Eucalyptus koolpinensis

Eucalyptus koolpinensis, commonly known as the Koolpin gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small and white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels.

The bark is rough and fissured, often peeling in strips, giving the tree a distinctive appearance.
