Eugenia ternatifolia

Eugenia ternatifolia, commonly known as the three-parted eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its opposite, trifoliate leaves, which give it its specific epithet "ternatifolia.". It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often followed by edible, red berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
