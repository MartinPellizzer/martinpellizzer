Erica densiflora

Erica densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. Erica densiflora produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn.

It is well adapted to acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
