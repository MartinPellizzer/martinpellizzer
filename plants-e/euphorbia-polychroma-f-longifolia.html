Euphorbia polychroma f. longifolia

Euphorbia polychroma f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf euphorbia, is a compact, low-growing succulent with a distinctive appearance.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to South Africa and is well adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
