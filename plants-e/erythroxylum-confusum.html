Erythroxylum confusum

Erythroxylum confusum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain environments.
