Elephantopus gossweileri

Elephantopus gossweileri, commonly known as the African groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and lobed leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic component of the fynbos vegetation in its native range.
