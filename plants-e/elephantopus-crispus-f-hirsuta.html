Elephantopus crispus f. hirsuta

Elephantopus crispus f. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy elephant's foot, is a variant of the elephantopus genus native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, and often fleshy leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to dry conditions with its succulent foliage. Its flowering stems produce small, yellowish flowers, which are clustered in umbels.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience in challenging climates.
