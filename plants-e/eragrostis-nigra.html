Eragrostis nigra

Eragrostis nigra, commonly known as black lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, slender leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, reddish-brown seeds. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun conditions. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
