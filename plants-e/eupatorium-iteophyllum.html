Eupatorium iteophyllum

Eupatorium iteophyllum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
