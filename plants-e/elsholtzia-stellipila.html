Elsholtzia stellipila

Elsholtzia stellipila, commonly known as the star-leaved elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used in folk remedies for various ailments.
