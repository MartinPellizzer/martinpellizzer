Echinochloa frumentacea var. violacea

Echinochloa frumentacea var. violacea, commonly known as purple foxtail, is a grass species native to warm temperate and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikelets that are typically purple to reddish in color. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ability to compete with crops make it a significant weed in many regions.
