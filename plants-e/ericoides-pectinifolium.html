Ericoides pectinifolium

Ericoides pectinifolium, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink to white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a woolly appearance.

Ericoides pectinifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
