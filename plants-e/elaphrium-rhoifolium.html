Elaphrium rhoifolium

Elaphrium rhoifolium, commonly known as the rock heath, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, usually with pink to purple petals.

Elaphrium rhoifolium is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.
