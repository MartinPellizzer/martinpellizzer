Engelhardia serrata var. serrata

Engelhardia serrata var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated engelhardia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types, making it a popular choice for garden use.
