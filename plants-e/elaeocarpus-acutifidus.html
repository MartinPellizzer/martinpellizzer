Elaeocarpus acutifidus

Elaeocarpus acutifidus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it often grows in humid, lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
