Eucalyptus paniculata var. conferta

Eucalyptus paniculata var. conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallet tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often greyish-green in color. The tree produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in a range of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
