Elegia panicoides

Elegia panicoides, commonly known as the panicled ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, slender stems that bear whorled clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Elegia panicoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
