Erica eylesii

Erica eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica eylesii typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
