Eugenia fragrans

Eugenia fragrans, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, red-brown flower buds that are harvested and dried to produce the spice clove. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are typically small, white or pink, and clustered in inflorescences.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and is cultivated for its valuable aromatic compound eugenol.
