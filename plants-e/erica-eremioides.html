Erica eremioides

Erica eremioides, commonly known as the desert heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, dry conditions. The plant features small, dense leaves that reduce water loss and are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters and blooming during the rainy season.

Erica eremioides is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
