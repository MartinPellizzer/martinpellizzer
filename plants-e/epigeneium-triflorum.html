Epigeneium triflorum

Epigeneium triflorum, commonly known as the three-flowered epigeneium, is a member of the Araliaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its slender stems and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters of three, which are typically white or pale yellow. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in humid climates.

It thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, moist soil for optimal growth.
