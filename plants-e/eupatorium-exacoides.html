Eupatorium exacoides

Eupatorium exacoides, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and marshes.

Its aromatic foliage and showy inflorescences make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
