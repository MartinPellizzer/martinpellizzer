Eria vaginifera

Eria vaginifera, commonly known as the velvet tassel orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Eria vaginifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in horticulture for its unique appearance.
