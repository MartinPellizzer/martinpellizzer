Euphorbia delphinensis

Euphorbia delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinian spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
