Euterpe egusquizae

Euterpe egusquizae, commonly known as the Guianan palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible fruits that are small and orange in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The Guianan palm plays a significant ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
