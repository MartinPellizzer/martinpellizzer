Electra mutica var. carnosifolia

Electra mutica var. carnosifolia, commonly known as the woolly mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners and botanists alike.
