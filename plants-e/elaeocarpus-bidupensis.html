Elaeocarpus bidupensis

Elaeocarpus bidupensis, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
