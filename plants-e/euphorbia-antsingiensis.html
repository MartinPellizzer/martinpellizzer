Euphorbia antsingiensis

Euphorbia antsingiensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-like structures that house its tiny, inconspicuous flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem to survive dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
