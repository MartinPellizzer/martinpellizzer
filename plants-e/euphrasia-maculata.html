Euphrasia maculata

Euphrasia maculata, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with yellow spots. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Euphrasia maculata is often found in clusters, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
