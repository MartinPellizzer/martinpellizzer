Eriocaulon decangulare

Eriocaulon decangulare, commonly known as the decagon arrowhead, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typically with three stamens and a single pistil.

Eriocaulon decangulare is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens for its ornamental value.
