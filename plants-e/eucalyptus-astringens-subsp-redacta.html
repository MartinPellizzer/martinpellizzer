Eucalyptus astringens subsp. redacta

Eucalyptus astringens subsp.

redacta, commonly known as the red gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a vibrant red color when exposed.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive blue-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale yellow.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland areas.
