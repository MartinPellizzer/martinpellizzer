Echinocereus × roetteri

Echinocereus × roetteri, commonly known as the rosette cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that form a tight rosette, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and bats. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
