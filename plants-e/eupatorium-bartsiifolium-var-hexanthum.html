Eupatorium bartsiifolium var. hexanthum

Eupatorium bartsiifolium var. hexanthum, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Asteraceae family.
