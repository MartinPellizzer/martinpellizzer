Evolvulus arbuscula var. canus

Evolvulus arbuscula var. canus, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or spread along the ground.
