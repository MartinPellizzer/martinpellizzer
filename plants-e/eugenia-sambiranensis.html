Eugenia sambiranensis

Eugenia sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Eugenia sambiranensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
