Eleocharis xyridiformis

Eleocharis xyridiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like clusters of flowers at the tips. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is adapted to moist environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
