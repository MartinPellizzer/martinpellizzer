Erythroxylum rotundifolium

Erythroxylum rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, round berries that contain a stimulant alkaloid similar to caffeine.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
