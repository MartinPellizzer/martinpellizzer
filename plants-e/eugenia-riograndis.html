Eugenia riograndis

Eugenia riograndis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a small star, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, often cultivated in warm climates for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, subtropical environment to flourish.
