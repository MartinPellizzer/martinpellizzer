Eupatorium klattii

Eupatorium klattii, commonly known as klatt's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium klattii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
