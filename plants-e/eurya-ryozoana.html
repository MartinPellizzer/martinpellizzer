Eurya ryozoana

Eurya ryozoana, commonly known as the Ryozoan evergreen holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya ryozoana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and year-round visual interest.
