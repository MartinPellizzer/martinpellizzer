Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp. glabrescens

Elaeocarpus ferrugineus subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leafed elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, smooth, and often glabrous leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland and hill forests.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and potential ecological importance in its native habitats.
