Elymus remotiflorus

Elymus remotiflorus, commonly known as the distant-flower wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces wind-pollinated flowers.

Elymus remotiflorus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
