Eragrostiella collettii

Eragrostiella collettii, commonly known as the Collett's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
