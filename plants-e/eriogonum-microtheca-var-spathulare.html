Eriogonum microtheca var. spathulare

Eriogonum microtheca var. spathulare, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and rhubarb.
