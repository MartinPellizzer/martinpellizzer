Erica alfredii

Erica alfredii, commonly known as the Alfred's heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica alfredii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
