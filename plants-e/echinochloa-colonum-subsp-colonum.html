Echinochloa colonum subsp. colonum

Echinochloa colonum subsp.

colonum, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to the Americas. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive spikelets that give it a foxtail appearance.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in panicles.

This species is an important weed in many regions and has been studied for its ecological and agricultural impacts.
