Elaeocarpus balabanii

Elaeocarpus balabanii, commonly known as the Balabanii's elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
