Elaeagnus magna var. wushanensis

Elaeagnus magna var. wushanensis, commonly known as the Wushan silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, edible fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
