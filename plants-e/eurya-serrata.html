Eurya serrata

Eurya serrata, commonly known as the serrated evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya serrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its evergreen foliage and winter interest.
