Eulophia zeyheriana

Eulophia zeyheriana, commonly known as the Zeyher's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, with its inflorescence bearing several flowers arranged in a raceme. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eulophia zeyheriana is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained, sunny conditions.
