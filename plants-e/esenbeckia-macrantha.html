Esenbeckia macrantha

Esenbeckia macrantha, commonly known as the blueberry tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody structure and produces small, blue or purple berries that are edible. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine.
