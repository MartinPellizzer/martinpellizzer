Eschweilera loretensis

Eschweilera loretensis, commonly known as the Loretense wax palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its slow growth and can reach heights of up to 15 meters in its natural habitat. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and private collections.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
