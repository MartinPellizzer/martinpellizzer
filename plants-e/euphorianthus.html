Euphorianthus

Euphorianthus, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its striking, fiery yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features palmate leaves that are often lobed, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are clustered in complex inflorescences called cyathia, which are typical of the genus.

Euphorianthus thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
