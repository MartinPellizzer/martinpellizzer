Ephedra macedonica

Ephedra macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian ephedra, is a small shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its unique, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found growing in rocky soils and on dry slopes.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has attracted interest for its potential pharmacological properties.
