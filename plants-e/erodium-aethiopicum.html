Erodium aethiopicum

Erodium aethiopicum, commonly known as sticky weed or redstem filaree, is an annual flowering plant native to Africa, Southwestern Asia, and parts of Australia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, clustered pink or red flowers. The plant has hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly serrated. It produces small, dry fruits called legumes, which contain several seeds.

Erodium aethiopicum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
