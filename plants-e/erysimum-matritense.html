Erysimum matritense

Erysimum matritense, commonly known as the Madrid wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Erysimum matritense is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
