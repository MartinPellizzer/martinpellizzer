Ecliptica platyglossa

Ecliptica platyglossa, commonly known as the broad-leaved eclipta, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Ecliptica platyglossa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
