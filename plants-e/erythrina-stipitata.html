Erythrina stipitata

Erythrina stipitata, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often thorny. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Erythrina stipitata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
