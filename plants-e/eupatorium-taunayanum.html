Eupatorium taunayanum

Eupatorium taunayanum, commonly known as Taunay's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
