Erodium glutinosum

Erodium glutinosum, commonly known as sticky eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a sticky, glandular coating. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
