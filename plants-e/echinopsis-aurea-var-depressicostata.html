Echinopsis aurea var. depressicostata

Echinopsis aurea var. depressicostata, commonly known as the golden echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in pale yellow to greenish spines. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
