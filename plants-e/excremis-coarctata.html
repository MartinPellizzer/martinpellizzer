Excremis coarctata

Excremis coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats.
