Euphorbia nervosa

Euphorbia nervosa, commonly known as the veined spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive branching structure with spiny stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has milky sap that can be toxic and is often used for medicinal purposes in traditional practices. Its leaves are usually narrow and have prominent veins, contributing to its common name.

Euphorbia nervosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
