Eremogone eastwoodiae var. adenophora

Eremogone eastwoodiae var. adenophora, commonly known as the Eastwood's eremogone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and can tolerate high levels of sunlight and drought.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
