Erodium petroselinum

Erodium petroselinum, commonly known as common purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, fleshy leaves, and small, pink or reddish flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it is part of the family Portulacaceae and is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions.
