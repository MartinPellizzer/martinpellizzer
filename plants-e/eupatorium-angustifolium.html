Eupatorium angustifolium

Eupatorium angustifolium, commonly known as American boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and opposite, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Eupatorium angustifolium is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
