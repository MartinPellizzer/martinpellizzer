Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. versicolor

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its clustered, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, woodlands, and disturbed areas. Its leaves are often gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the stems are erect and branching.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various wildlife.
