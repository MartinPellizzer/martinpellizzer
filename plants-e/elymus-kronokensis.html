Elymus kronokensis

Elymus kronokensis, commonly known as the Alaska wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist to wet environments, such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

Elymus kronokensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
