Elaeocarpus acuminatus

Elaeocarpus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
