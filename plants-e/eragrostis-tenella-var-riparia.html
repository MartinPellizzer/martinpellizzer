Eragrostis tenella var. riparia

Eragrostis tenella var. riparia, commonly known as rivergrass or riparian grass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and riparian habitats.

It typically grows in moist soils along streams, rivers, and lakeshores, thriving in environments with regular water availability. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
