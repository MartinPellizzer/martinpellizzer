Eryngium humile var. radiciflorum

Eryngium humile var. radiciflorum, commonly known as the dwarf thistle or lesser eryngium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features compact, silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, and its flowers are blue to violet, arranged in dense, spiky inflorescences. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky areas. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
