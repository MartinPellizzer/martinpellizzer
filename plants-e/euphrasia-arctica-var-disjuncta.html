Euphrasia arctica var. disjuncta

Euphrasia arctica var. disjuncta, commonly known as the Arctic eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.

Euphrasia arctica var. disjuncta is often found in tundra and mountainous areas across the northern hemisphere.
