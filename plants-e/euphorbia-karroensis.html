Euphorbia karroensis

Euphorbia karroensis, commonly known as the Karroen's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive stem structure with swollen, fleshy nodes and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain water in arid environments. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
