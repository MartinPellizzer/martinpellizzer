Erysimum coarctatum

Erysimum coarctatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers. The plant has linear, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Erysimum coarctatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
