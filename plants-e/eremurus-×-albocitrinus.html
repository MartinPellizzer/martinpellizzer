Eremurus × albocitrinus

Eremurus × albocitrinus, commonly known as the white and yellow eremurus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Eremurus przewalskii and Eremurus przewalskii var. citrinus.

It is a tall, spire-like plant that produces large, showy inflorescences composed of numerous white and yellow flowers. The plant is native to Central Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring a cold winter dormancy period for proper growth.

The flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
