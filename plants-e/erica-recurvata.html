Erica recurvata

Erica recurvata, commonly known as the bent-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, evergreen leaves that curve inward. The plant produces delicate white or pinkish flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica recurvata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
