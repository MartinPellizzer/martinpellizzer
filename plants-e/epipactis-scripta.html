Epipactis scripta

Epipactis scripta, commonly known as the slender helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a complex root system and is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base of the plant.

Epipactis scripta is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a relatively adaptable species within its native range.
