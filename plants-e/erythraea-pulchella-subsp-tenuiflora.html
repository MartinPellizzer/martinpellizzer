Erythraea pulchella subsp. tenuiflora

Erythraea pulchella subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-flowered erythraea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or arid regions.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
