Erythroxylum fimbriatum

Erythroxylum fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with fringed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
