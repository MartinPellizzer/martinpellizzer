Elaeocarpus zambalensis

Elaeocarpus zambalensis, commonly known as the Zambales elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its potential for sustainable timber use.
