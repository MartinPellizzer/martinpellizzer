Elaeagnus coreana

Elaeagnus coreana, commonly known as Korean silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and has a strong root system that helps prevent erosion. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees.

Elaeagnus coreana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
