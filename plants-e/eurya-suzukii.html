Eurya suzukii

Eurya suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Suzukii holly, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are white and clustered in small umbels, blooming in spring, followed by small, fleshy berries that are edible for birds.

Eurya suzukii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
