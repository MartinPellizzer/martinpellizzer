Eragrostis truncata

Eragrostis truncata, commonly known as rigid false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes.
