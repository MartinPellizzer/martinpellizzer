Echinofossulocactus platyceras

Echinofossulocactus platyceras, commonly known as the Mexican lion's mouth cactus, is a species native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and prominent areoles, which are specialized structures for producing spines and flowers. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny exterior. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.

Echinofossulocactus platyceras is well adapted to arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-draining soil.
