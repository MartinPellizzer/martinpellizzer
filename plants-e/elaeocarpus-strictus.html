Elaeocarpus strictus

Elaeocarpus strictus, commonly known as the slender elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
