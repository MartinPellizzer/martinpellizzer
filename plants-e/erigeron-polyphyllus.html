Erigeron polyphyllus

Erigeron polyphyllus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals surrounding a yellow central disk, blooming from late summer into early fall. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
