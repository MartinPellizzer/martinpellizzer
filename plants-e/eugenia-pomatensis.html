Eugenia pomatensis

Eugenia pomatensis, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces edible, yellow to orange fruits that are typically harvested when fully ripe. The fruit has a sweet, slightly tart flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.
