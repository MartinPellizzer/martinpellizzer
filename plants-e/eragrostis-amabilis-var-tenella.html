Eragrostis amabilis var. tenella

Eragrostis amabilis var. tenella, commonly known as the silky blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, low-growing habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
