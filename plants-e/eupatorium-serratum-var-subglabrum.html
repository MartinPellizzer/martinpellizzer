Eupatorium serratum var. subglabrum

Eupatorium serratum var. subglabrum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a branched stem that is often hairy, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

The botanical features of this plant make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
