Erica parviflora var. glabra

Erica parviflora var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are smooth and glabrous, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale pink to white flowers during the late spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica parviflora var. glabra is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
