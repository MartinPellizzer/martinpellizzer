Exocarpos latifolius

Exocarpos latifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved wax apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round, yellow or orange berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
