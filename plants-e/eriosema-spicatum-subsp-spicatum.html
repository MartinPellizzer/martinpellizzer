Eriosema spicatum subsp. spicatum

Eriosema spicatum subsp.

spicatum, commonly known as the spiked eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
