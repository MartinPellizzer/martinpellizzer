Eupatorium dasyneurum

Eupatorium dasyneurum, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and streambanks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
