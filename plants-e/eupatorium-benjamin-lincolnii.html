Eupatorium benjamin-lincolnii

Eupatorium benjamin-lincolnii, commonly known as Benjamin Lincoln's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and often grows in moist, woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical name honors Benjamin Lincoln, a notable figure in American history.
