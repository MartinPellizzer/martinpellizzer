Euodia triphylla var. pubescens

Euodia triphylla var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy three-leaf euodia, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of this plant make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
