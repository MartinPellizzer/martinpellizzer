Epilobium paniculatum var. tracyi

Epilobium paniculatum var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense, branching clusters called panicles. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Tracy's willow herb is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
