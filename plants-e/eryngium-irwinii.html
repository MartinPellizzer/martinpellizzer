Eryngium irwinii

Eryngium irwinii, commonly known as the Irwin's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pinkish purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Eryngium irwinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
