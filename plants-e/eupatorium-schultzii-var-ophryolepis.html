Eupatorium schultzii var. ophryolepis

Eupatorium schultzii var. ophryolepis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, arranged in cone-like inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
