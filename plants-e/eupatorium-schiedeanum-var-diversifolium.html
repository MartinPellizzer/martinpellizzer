Eupatorium schiedeanum var. diversifolium

Eupatorium schiedeanum var. diversifolium, commonly known as the variegated boneset, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is typically found in wetlands, stream banks, and swampy areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
