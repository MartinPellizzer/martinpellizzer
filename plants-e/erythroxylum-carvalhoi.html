Erythroxylum carvalhoi

Erythroxylum carvalhoi, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is notable for its potential medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
