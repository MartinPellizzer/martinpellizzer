Eriosyce taltalensis subsp. taltalensis

Eriosyce taltalensis subsp.

taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal cholla, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of northern Chile. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are segmented and covered in dense, white spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and saline environments, often growing on rocky soils near the coastline.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptations to extreme desert conditions, making it a resilient and unique member of the cactus family.
