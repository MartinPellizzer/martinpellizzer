Eugenia levinei

Eugenia levinei, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia levinei is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
