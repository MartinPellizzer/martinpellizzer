Elaeagnus umbellata var. nakaiana

Elaeagnus umbellata var. nakaiana, commonly known as Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery, glandular leaves that emit a faint, pleasant fragrance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and hedging purposes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
