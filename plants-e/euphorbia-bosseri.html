Euphorbia bosseri

Euphorbia bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous spiny, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its waxy leaves and compact growth habit make it well adapted to hot, dry climates.

Euphorbia bosseri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
