Elaeocarpus leucobotryus

Elaeocarpus leucobotryus, commonly known as the white-fruited elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with a dense canopy.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
