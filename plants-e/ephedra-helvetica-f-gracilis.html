Ephedra helvetica f. gracilis

Ephedra helvetica f. gracilis, commonly known as the slender ephedra, is a dioecious plant belonging to the Ephedraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, branched stems and scale-like leaves, which are typical of the genus Ephedra. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in male and female cones. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.
