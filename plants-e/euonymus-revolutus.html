Euonymus revolutus

Euonymus revolutus, commonly known as the scarlet euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red autumn foliage, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface with finely toothed edges. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter.

This plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden landscapes.
