Eriogonum abertianum var. gillespiei

Eriogonum abertianum var. gillespiei, commonly known as Gillespie's buckbrush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
