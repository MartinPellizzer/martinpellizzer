Euphorbia lactea

Euphorbia lactea, commonly known as the White Spine Cactus, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in white, waxy tubercles that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Due to its spines and toxic sap, it requires careful handling and is often grown as a decorative houseplant.
