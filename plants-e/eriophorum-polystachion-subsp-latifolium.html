Eriophorum polystachion subsp. latifolium

Eriophorum polystachion subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf tussock sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of lakes and streams. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, brownish seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
