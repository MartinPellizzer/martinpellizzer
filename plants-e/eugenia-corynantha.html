Eugenia corynantha

Eugenia corynantha, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a bright red outer shell and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a tendency to grow in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which is rich in antioxidants.
