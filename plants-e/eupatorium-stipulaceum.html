Eupatorium stipulaceum

Eupatorium stipulaceum, commonly known as the white snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and often grows in moist, wooded areas. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antipyretic and anti-inflammatory effects.

However, it is also toxic to livestock and can cause severe illness if ingested.
