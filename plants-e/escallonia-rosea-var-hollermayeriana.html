Escallonia rosea var. hollermayeriana

Escallonia rosea var. hollermayeriana, commonly known as the Hollermayer's escallonia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are ornamental but not edible. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
