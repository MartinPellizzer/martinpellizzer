Eragrostis pilosa var. lugens

Eragrostis pilosa var. lugens, commonly known as slender beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in open fields, and is adapted to drought conditions. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
