Eryngium vaseyi

Eryngium vaseyi, commonly known as the Vasey's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features spiny, blue-green foliage and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system for water retention.

Eryngium vaseyi is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
