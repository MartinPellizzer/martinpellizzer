Eupatorium alternifolium var. hieronymi

Eupatorium alternifolium var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
