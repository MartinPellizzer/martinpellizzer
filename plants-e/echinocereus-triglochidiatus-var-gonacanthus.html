Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. gonacanthus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. gonacanthus, commonly known as the blue agave, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces striking blue-green leaves with spines, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

While it is not typically grown for its flowers, it can produce yellow or white blooms under the right conditions.
