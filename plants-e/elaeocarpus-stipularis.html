Elaeocarpus stipularis

Elaeocarpus stipularis, commonly known as the stipular elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are often covered in a persistent calyx. Its leaves are typically elliptic to ovate in shape and have a leathery texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
