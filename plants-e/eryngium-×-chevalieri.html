Eryngium × chevalieri

Eryngium × chevalieri, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a thistle, with bracts that are often blue or purplish in color. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.

Eryngium × chevalieri is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
