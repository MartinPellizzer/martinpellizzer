Eupatorium hirsutum var. bartsiifolium

Eupatorium hirsutum var. bartsiifolium, commonly known as the Bartsiifolium eupatorium, is a variant of the common boneset plant.

It is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal use.
