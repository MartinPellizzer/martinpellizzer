Eugenia jasminifolia

Eugenia jasminifolia, commonly known as the jasmine glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble jasmine. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature.

Eugenia jasminifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic blooms.
