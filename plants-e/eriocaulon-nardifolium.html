Eriocaulon nardifolium

Eriocaulon nardifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized adaptations for aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon nardifolium plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
