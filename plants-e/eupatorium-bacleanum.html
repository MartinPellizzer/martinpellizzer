Eupatorium bacleanum

Eupatorium bacleanum, commonly known as Baclean thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Eupatorium bacleanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
