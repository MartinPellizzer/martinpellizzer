Eucalyptus × westonii

Eucalyptus × westonii, commonly known as the Westonia gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a prominent midrib and a leathery texture. This species produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in late winter to early spring.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climatic conditions.
