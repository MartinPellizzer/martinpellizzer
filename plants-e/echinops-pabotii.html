Echinops pabotii

Echinops pabotii, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky globe. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Echinops pabotii blooms with purple or pink flowers in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
