Euphorbia sanguinea var. setigera

Euphorbia sanguinea var. setigera, commonly known as the red milkweed tree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are surrounded by specialized leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
