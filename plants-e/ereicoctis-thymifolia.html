Ereicoctis thymifolia

Ereicoctis thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles thyme, with small, leathery leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Ereicoctis thymifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
