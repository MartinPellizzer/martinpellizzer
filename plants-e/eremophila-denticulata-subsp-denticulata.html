Eremophila denticulata subsp. denticulata

Eremophila denticulata subsp.

denticulata, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
