Eugenia ledermannii

Eugenia ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, berry-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
