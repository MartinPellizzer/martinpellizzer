Eulophia endlichiana

Eulophia endlichiana, commonly known as the Endlich's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor in the soil, while its leaves are typically broad and strap-like.

Eulophia endlichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
