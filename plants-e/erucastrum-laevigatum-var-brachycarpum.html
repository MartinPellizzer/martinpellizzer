Erucastrum laevigatum var. brachycarpum

Erucastrum laevigatum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed erucaceous, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, elongated leaves with a waxy surface, adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are short, capsule-like structures containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
