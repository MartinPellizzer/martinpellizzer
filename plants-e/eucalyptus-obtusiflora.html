Eucalyptus obtusiflora

Eucalyptus obtusiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to grey hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to cream in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Eucalyptus obtusiflora is valued for its ecological role and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
