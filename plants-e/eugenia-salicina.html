Eugenia salicina

Eugenia salicina, commonly known as the common guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically pink to red when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

Eugenia salicina is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural ingredients in perfumes and foods.
