Eremaea ebracteata var. brachyphylla

Eremaea ebracteata var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved eremaea, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, blooming during the warmer months. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive prolonged droughts.

This variety is often found in sandy soils and is a common component of shrubland communities in its native range.
