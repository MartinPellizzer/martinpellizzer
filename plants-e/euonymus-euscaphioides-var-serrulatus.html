Euonymus euscaphioides var. serrulatus

Euonymus euscaphioides var. serrulatus, commonly known as the serrated euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are finely serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage.

The species is valued for its hardiness and ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
