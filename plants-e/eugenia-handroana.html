Eugenia handroana

Eugenia handroana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Eugenia handroana is also valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and musical instruments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
