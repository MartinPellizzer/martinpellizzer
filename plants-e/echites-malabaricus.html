Echites malabaricus

Echites malabaricus, commonly known as the Malabar spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a milky sap that is toxic. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in umbels and bloom during the rainy season.

Echites malabaricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
