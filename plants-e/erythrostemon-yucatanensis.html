Erythrostemon yucatanensis

Erythrostemon yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in open, sunny habitats. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Erythrostemon yucatanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
