Evolvulus longifolius

Evolvulus longifolius, commonly known as the longleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive flowers.
