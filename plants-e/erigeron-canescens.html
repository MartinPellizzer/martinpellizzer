Erigeron canescens

Erigeron canescens, commonly known as frostweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its hairy, silver-gray foliage. It produces small white to pink flowers in the summer, which are followed by fluffy seed heads.

Erigeron canescens is often used in native plant gardens due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
