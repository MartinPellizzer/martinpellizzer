Eugenia micropoda

Eugenia micropoda, commonly known as the small-fruited myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are white or pale pink and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit, which is usually red or yellow when ripe, is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
