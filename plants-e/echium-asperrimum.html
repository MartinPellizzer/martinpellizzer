Echium asperrimum

Echium asperrimum, commonly known as the rough echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and distinctive spikes of blue or violet flowers. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand dry and rocky conditions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Echium asperrimum is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
