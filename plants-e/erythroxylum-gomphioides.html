Erythroxylum gomphioides

Erythroxylum gomphioides, commonly known as the red mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences.

Erythroxylum gomphioides is not widely cultivated for commercial purposes but is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
