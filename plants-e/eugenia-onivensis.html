Eugenia onivensis

Eugenia onivensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or onivensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia onivensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
