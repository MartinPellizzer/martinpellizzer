Eriosyce taltalensis

Eriosyce taltalensis, commonly known as the Taltal cholla, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiky structure. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand high salinity and strong coastal winds.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a notable example of desert flora in the Atacama region.
