Evolvulus holosericeus var. obtusatus

Evolvulus holosericeus var. obtusatus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, decorative vine.
