Eugenia paranapiacabensis

Eugenia paranapiacabensis, commonly known as the Paranapiacá-baçu myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that is typically red when mature.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
