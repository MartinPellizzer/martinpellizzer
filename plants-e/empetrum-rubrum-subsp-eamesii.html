Empetrum rubrum subsp. eamesii

Empetrum rubrum subsp.

eamesii, commonly known as the Eames' red crowbar, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, dark red berries that are typically eaten by birds.

The plant thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, with a distinctive reddish hue that persists throughout the year.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
