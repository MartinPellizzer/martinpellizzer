Eugenia ugni

Eugenia ugni, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. Eugenia ugni thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
