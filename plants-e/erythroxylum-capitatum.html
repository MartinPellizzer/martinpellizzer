Erythroxylum capitatum

Erythroxylum capitatum, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescence that resembles a cluster of small flowers, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are often followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

Erythroxylum capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
