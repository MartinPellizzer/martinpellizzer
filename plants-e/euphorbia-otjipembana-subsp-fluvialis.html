Euphorbia otjipembana subsp. fluvialis

Euphorbia otjipembana subsp.

fluvialis, commonly known as the river euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
