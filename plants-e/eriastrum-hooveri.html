Eriastrum hooveri

Eriastrum hooveri, commonly known as Hoover's eriole, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Eriastrum hooveri is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
