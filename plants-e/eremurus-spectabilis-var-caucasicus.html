Eremurus spectabilis var. caucasicus

Eremurus spectabilis var. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian sago palm, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, covered in a fibrous, sago-like material. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with numerous yellowish-green flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
