Eupatorium prasiifolium

Eupatorium prasiifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate blue to purple flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has feathery, grass-like leaves that are typically green and finely divided. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Eupatorium prasiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
