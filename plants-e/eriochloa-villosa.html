Eriochloa villosa

Eriochloa villosa, commonly known as hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescence, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and prairies, and is adapted to moist or saturated soils. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

Eriochloa villosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
