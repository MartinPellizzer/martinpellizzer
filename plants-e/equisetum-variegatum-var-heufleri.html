Equisetum variegatum var. heufleri

Equisetum variegatum var. heufleri, commonly known as the variegated scouring rush, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that are typically green with yellow or white variegation. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are often visible in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to wetland habitats.
