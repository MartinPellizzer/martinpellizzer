Eragrostis interrupta var. parviflora

Eragrostis interrupta var. parviflora, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in open woods, fields, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
