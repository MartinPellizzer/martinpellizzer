Eremophila microtheca

Eremophila microtheca, commonly known as the small-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of tubular, pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees.

Eremophila microtheca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
