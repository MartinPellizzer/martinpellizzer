Eleocharis acicularis var. gracilescens

Eleocharis acicularis var. gracilescens, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the Americas.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on wind for pollination.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
