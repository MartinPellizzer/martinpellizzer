Erodium pulchellum

Erodium pulchellum, commonly known as the pretty bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the fynbos biome.
