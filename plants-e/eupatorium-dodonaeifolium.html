Eupatorium dodonaeifolium

Eupatorium dodonaeifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium dodonaeifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
