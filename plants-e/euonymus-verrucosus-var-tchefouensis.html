Euonymus verrucosus var. tchefouensis

Euonymus verrucosus var. tchefouensis, commonly known as the Tchefou euonymus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Cameroon.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a dark green to almost black hue, often with a slight waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
