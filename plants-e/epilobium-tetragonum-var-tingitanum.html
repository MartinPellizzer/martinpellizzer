Epilobium tetragonum var. tingitanum

Epilobium tetragonum var. tingitanum, commonly known as the Canary Island fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.

The species is also characterized by its ability to tolerate saline conditions, making it well adapted to its native habitat.
