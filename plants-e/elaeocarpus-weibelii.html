Elaeocarpus weibelii

Elaeocarpus weibelii, commonly known as the Weibel's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
