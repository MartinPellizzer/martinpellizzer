Ericameria nauseosa var. texensis

Ericameria nauseosa var. texensis, commonly known as Texas tansy, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, disc-shaped flowers that are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Ericameria nauseosa var. texensis is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
