Erica ampulliformis

Erica ampulliformis, commonly known as the bottle-shaped heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Erica ampulliformis is a small shrub that produces small, leathery leaves and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

It is also adapted to withstand periods of drought and is often used in rockeries or as a ground cover in suitable climates.
