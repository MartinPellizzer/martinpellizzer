Endospermum philippinense

Endospermum philippinense, commonly known as the Philippine devil's ivy, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its endosperm is a key part of the seed structure, providing nourishment to the developing embryo.

It is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles.
