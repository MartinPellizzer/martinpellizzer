Echinops philiae

Echinops philiae, commonly known as the Persian Thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the distinctive, spiky buds that give it its common name. Echinops philiae is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots and hairy leaves that reduce water loss.
