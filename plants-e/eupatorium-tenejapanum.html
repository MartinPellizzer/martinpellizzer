Eupatorium tenejapanum

Eupatorium tenejapanum, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium tenejapanum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
