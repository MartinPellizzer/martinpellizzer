Eriospermum sabulosum

Eriospermum sabulosum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white trichomes. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Eriospermum sabulosum is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, dunes, and rocky slopes.
