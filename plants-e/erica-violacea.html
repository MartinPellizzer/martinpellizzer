Erica violacea

Erica violacea, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. This evergreen shrub typically grows in sandy or acidic soils and thrives in coastal areas. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually purple or pink, blooming in spring and summer.

Erica violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
