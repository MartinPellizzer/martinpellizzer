Echium maculatum

Echium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Echium maculatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
