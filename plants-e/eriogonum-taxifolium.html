Eriogonum taxifolium

Eriogonum taxifolium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers and fern-like, finely divided leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.
