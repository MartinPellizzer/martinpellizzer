Eupatorium trichotomum

Eupatorium trichotomum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a soft, grayish-white down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium trichotomum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
