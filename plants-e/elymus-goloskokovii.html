Elymus goloskokovii

Elymus goloskokovii, commonly known as the Goloskokov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters, with lemmas that are often hairy and pointed. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture.

Elymus goloskokovii is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates.
