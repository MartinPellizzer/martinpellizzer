Eulophia flavopurpurea

Eulophia flavopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and yellow, typically arranged in a spike. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia flavopurpurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
