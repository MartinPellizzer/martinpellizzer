Evolvulus commelinifolius

Evolvulus commelinifolius, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Evolvulus commelinifolius is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
