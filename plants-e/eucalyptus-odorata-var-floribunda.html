Eucalyptus odorata var. floribunda

Eucalyptus odorata var. floribunda, commonly known as the sweet gum eucalyptus, is a variant of the sweet gum tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, pale grey bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slight waxy coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, fluffy white flowers in clusters, which are highly fragrant and attract various pollinators.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and pleasant scent.
