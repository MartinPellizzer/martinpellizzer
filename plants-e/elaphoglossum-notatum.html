Elaphoglossum notatum

Elaphoglossum notatum, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive, silver-gray fronds that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum notatum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
