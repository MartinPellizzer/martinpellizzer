Echium augustini

Echium augustini, commonly known as the Augustin's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and grows as a herbaceous annual. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
