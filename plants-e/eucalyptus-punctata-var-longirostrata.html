Eucalyptus punctata var. longirostrata

Eucalyptus punctata var. longirostrata, commonly known as the long-beak gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that is often grey or white in color. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a blue-green hue and a rough upper surface. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters along the branches.

This species is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
