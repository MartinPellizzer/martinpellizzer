Eupatorium grisebachianum

Eupatorium grisebachianum, commonly known as the gray eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, grayish pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers, which appear in clusters, are usually white or pale blue and bloom during the warmer months.
