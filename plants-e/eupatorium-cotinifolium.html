Eupatorium cotinifolium

Eupatorium cotinifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators and a component of wetland ecosystems.
