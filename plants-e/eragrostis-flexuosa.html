Eragrostis flexuosa

Eragrostis flexuosa, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern and central United States.

It features slender, tufted stems that often curve and coil, giving it a distinctive, wiry appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue-gray flowers, which are characteristic of the genus Eragrostis. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eragrostis flexuosa is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
