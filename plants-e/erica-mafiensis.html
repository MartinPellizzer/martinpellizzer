Erica mafiensis

Erica mafiensis, commonly known as the Mafikeng heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Erica mafiensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
