Echinocactus wislizeni subsp. tiburonensis

Echinocactus wislizeni subsp.

tiburonensis, commonly known as the Tiburon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Baja California, Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. It has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
