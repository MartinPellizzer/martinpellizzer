Eurycles coronata

Eurycles coronata, commonly known as the crown vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often consumed by various bird species. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy appearance.

Eurycles coronata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
