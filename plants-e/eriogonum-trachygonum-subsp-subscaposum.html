Eriogonum trachygonum subsp. subscaposum

Eriogonum trachygonum subsp.

subscaposum, commonly known as the roughleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves with a rough, textured surface.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains.

Its fibrous root system helps it survive in arid environments.
