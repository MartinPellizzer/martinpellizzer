Eragrostis procerior

Eragrostis procerior, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, fluffy panicles of seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis procerior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
