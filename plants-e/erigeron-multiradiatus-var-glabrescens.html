Erigeron multiradiatus var. glabrescens

Erigeron multiradiatus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are generally smooth and covered with fine, short hairs, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced pubescence compared to other varieties of Erigeron multiradiatus.
