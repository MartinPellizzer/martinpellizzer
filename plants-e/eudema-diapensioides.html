Eudema diapensioides

Eudema diapensioides, commonly known as the creeping jenny, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wetlands.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking a ground-cover plant.
