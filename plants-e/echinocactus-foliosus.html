Echinocactus foliosus

Echinocactus foliosus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which protect it from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor collections.
