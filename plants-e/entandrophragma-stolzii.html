Entandrophragma stolzii

Entandrophragma stolzii, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its straight grain and high density, making it highly valued for its durability and workability. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth surface and a pointed tip.

Entandrophragma stolzii is often found in moist lowland forests and is known for its ecological and economic importance in the region.
