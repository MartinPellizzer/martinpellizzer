Eria rubiginosa

Eria rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a slightly rough texture.

Eria rubiginosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
