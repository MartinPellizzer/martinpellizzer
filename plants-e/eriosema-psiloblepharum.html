Eriosema psiloblepharum

Eriosema psiloblepharum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often trifoliate leaves. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
