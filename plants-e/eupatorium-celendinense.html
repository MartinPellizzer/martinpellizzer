Eupatorium celendinense

Eupatorium celendinense, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

Eupatorium celendinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
