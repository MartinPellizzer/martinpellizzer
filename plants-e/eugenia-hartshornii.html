Eugenia hartshornii

Eugenia hartshornii, commonly known as the Hartshorn's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia hartshornii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, acidic soils.
