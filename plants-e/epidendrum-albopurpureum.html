Epidendrum albopurpureum

Epidendrum albopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, often with a purple-veined pattern on the petals. Its leaves are typically dark green and broad, arranged in a spiral along the stems.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
