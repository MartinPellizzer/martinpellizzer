Echinocereus viridiflorus var. weedinii

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. weedinii, commonly known as the Weedin's greenflower cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
