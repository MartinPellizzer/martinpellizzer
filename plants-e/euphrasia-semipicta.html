Euphrasia semipicta

Euphrasia semipicta, commonly known as the painted toothwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with intricate markings. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has hairy stems and leaves. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive floral patterns, which resemble the teeth of a saw.
