Erythroxylum microphyllum var. gonoclados

Erythroxylum microphyllum var. gonoclados, commonly known as the pink leaf mallow, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often tinged with a distinctive pink or reddish hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are typically borne on slender pedicels. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
