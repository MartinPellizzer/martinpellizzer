Erigeron nemoralis

Erigeron nemoralis, commonly known as the common fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant features hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers, which appear in summer, are daisy-like with white petals and a yellow center, attracting various pollinators.

Erigeron nemoralis is known for its adaptability and can thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
