Epithecia cornuta

Epithecia cornuta, commonly known as the horned epithecia, is a species of lichen belonging to the family Parmeliaceae.

It is characterized by its foliose thallus, which is typically pale green to yellowish in color. Its reproductive structures, known as apothecia, are disc-shaped and bear ascospores.

The lichen is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its symbiotic relationship between algae and fungi.
