Etlingera latifolia

Etlingera latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant red or orange flowers that resemble torches. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often reaching several feet in length.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
