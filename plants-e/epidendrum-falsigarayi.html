Epidendrum falsigarayi

Epidendrum falsigarayi, commonly known as the False Garay Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
