Elytrigia lazica subsp. lomatolepis

Elytrigia lazica subsp.

lomatolepis, commonly known as the Caucasian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, with small, two-lobed lemma blades.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and is often found in open, disturbed habitats.
