Exostema nipense

Exostema nipense, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Exostema nipense is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
