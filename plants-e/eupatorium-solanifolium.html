Eupatorium solanifolium

Eupatorium solanifolium, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white flowers in composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium solanifolium is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
