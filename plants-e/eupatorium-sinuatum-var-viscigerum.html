Eupatorium sinuatum var. viscigerum

Eupatorium sinuatum var. viscigerum, commonly known as sticky bones, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant has a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodland edges and disturbed soils.
