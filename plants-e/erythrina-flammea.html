Erythrina flammea

Erythrina flammea, commonly known as the flame bean or fire tree, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking red flowers and compound leaves.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Indonesia. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value is high due to its vibrant floral display, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its natural habitat.
