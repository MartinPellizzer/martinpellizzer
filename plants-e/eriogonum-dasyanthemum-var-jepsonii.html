Eriogonum dasyanthemum var. jepsonii

Eriogonum dasyanthemum var. jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
