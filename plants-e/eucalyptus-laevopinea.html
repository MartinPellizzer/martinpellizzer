Eucalyptus laevopinea

Eucalyptus laevopinea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant typically grows in heathlands and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or cream-colored flowers, followed by small, woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their potential therapeutic properties.
