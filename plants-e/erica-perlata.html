Erica perlata

Erica perlata, commonly known as the silver heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and pale purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica perlata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
