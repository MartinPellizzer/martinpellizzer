Eriastrum diffusum subsp. harwoodii

Eriastrum diffusum subsp.

harwoodii, commonly known as Harwood's eriastrum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing annual or biennial species characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
