Eucalyptus redunca var. melanophloia

Eucalyptus redunca var. melanophloia, commonly known as the blackwood gum, is a medium to tall tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, fissured bark that gives it its common name. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small white or pale yellow flowers, typically in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.
