Elaeocarpus francii

Elaeocarpus francii, commonly known as the Philippine ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate or elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
