Epilobium lechleri

Epilobium lechleri, commonly known as the Andean buttercup, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with hairy stems and alternate leaves that are often lance-shaped. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Epilobium lechleri is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Andean medicine.
