Evolvulus herrerae

Evolvulus herrerae, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Evolvulus herrerae is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
