Erica situshiemalis

Erica situshiemalis, commonly known as the Natal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica situshiemalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
