Eugenia griffithii

Eugenia griffithii, commonly known as the Taiwan beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the ornamental berries.

Eugenia griffithii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
