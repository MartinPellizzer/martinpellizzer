Erysimum cheiranthus subsp. strictum

Erysimum cheiranthus subsp.

strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
