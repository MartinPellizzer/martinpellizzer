Echinopsis nigricans

Echinopsis nigricans, commonly known as the black-topped cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in spines and have a dark green to blackish coloration. The plant produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically at night. It is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions with a deep root system and thick, water-storing stems.

Echinopsis nigricans is a popular choice for succulent collectors due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
