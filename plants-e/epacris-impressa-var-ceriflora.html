Epacris impressa var. ceriflora

Epacris impressa var. ceriflora, commonly known as the waxflower, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its long-lasting blooms.
