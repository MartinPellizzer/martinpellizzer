Eupatorium dentatum

Eupatorium dentatum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with coarse teeth along the edges, giving it its common name. Its showy, clustered flowers appear in late summer and are typically white or pale blue.

Eupatorium dentatum is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
