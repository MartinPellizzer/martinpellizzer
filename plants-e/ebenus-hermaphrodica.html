Ebenus hermaphrodica

Ebenus hermaphrodica, commonly known as the common bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces both male and female reproductive structures in the same flower, making it monoecious. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
