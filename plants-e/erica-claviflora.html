Erica claviflora

Erica claviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica claviflora produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often blooming in spring and summer.

It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
