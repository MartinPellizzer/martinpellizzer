Echinocactus subporrectus

Echinocactus subporrectus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems with narrow, ribbed segments that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
