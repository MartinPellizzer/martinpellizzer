Erica forskalii

Erica forskalii, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which are usually pink or red, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Erica forskalii is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.
