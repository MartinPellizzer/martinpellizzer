Elaeocarpus myrtoides subsp. vinkii

Elaeocarpus myrtoides subsp.

vinkii, commonly known as the Vink's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
