Eupatorium yashanalense

Eupatorium yashanalense, commonly known as the Yashanalense thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of small white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
