Eulophia roempleriana

Eulophia roempleriana, commonly known as the Roempler's eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in moist, shaded habitats.

Eulophia roempleriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
