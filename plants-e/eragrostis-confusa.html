Eragrostis confusa

Eragrostis confusa, commonly known as the confused lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

Eragrostis confusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance.
