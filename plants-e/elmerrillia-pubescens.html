Elmerrillia pubescens

Elmerrillia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy elmerrillia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
