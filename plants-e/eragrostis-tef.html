Eragrostis tef

Eragrostis tef, commonly known as false barley, is a grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is a hardy annual plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny grains, which are harvested for their nutritional value. Its grains are rich in protein and are a staple food in many parts of East Africa.

Eragrostis tef is also valued for its adaptability to poor soils and its potential as a drought-resistant crop.
