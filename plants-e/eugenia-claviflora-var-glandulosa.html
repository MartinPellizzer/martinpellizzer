Eugenia claviflora var. glandulosa

Eugenia claviflora var. glandulosa, commonly known as the fragrant guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often covered in small glands, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
