Ephedra wraithiana

Ephedra wraithiana, commonly known as the ghost ephedra, is a rare and intriguing plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that resemble the skeletal structures of ancient trees. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and is adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water. Ephedra wraithiana is notable for its chemical composition, containing ephedrine, a compound historically used in traditional medicine.

However, due to its rarity and ecological sensitivity, it is often protected and requires careful conservation efforts.
