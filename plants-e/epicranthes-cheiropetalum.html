Epicranthes cheiropetalum

Epicranthes cheiropetalum, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged petals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom during the warmer months.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
