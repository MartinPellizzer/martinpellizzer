Eupatorium calyculatum

Eupatorium calyculatum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium calyculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
