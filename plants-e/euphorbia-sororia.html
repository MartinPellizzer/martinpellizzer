Euphorbia sororia

Euphorbia sororia, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Euphorbia sororia produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is surrounded by brightly colored bracts.
