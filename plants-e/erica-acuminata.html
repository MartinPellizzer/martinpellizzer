Erica acuminata

Erica acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica acuminata is part of the Ericaceae family, known for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
