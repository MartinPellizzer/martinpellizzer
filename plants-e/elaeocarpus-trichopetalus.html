Elaeocarpus trichopetalus

Elaeocarpus trichopetalus, commonly known as the hairy-petalled elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
