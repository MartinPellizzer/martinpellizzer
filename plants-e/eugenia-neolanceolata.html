Eugenia neolanceolata

Eugenia neolanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Eugenia neolanceolata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
