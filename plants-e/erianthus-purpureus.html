Erianthus purpureus

Erianthus purpureus, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to South Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. The plant produces small, fluffy inflorescences that are arranged in panicles, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which contribute to its unique texture and appearance.

Erianthus purpureus is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
