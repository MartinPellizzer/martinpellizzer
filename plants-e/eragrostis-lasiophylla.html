Eragrostis lasiophylla

Eragrostis lasiophylla, commonly known as silky grass or silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a silky appearance, and it often forms dense, low-growing clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Eragrostis lasiophylla is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
