Echinocereus viereckii

Echinocereus viereckii, commonly known as the Viereck's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has spiny, areolate structures that provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Echinocereus viereckii is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant plant.
