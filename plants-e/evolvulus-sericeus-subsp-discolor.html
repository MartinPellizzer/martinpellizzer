Evolvulus sericeus subsp. discolor

Evolvulus sericeus subsp.

discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive silver-tinged foliage. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, sunny environments.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. Its leaves are hairy and often have a waxy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and rapid growth habit.
