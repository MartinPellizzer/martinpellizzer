Eritrichium japonicum

Eritrichium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Eritrichium japonicum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable environments.
