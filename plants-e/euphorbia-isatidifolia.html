Euphorbia isatidifolia

Euphorbia isatidifolia, commonly known as the Jerusalem crassula, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant.
