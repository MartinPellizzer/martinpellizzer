Eugenia aromatica

Eugenia aromatica, commonly known as the clove tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flowers and dark, oily seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its flower buds. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often used in traditional medicine for their antiseptic properties.

Eugenia aromatica thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
