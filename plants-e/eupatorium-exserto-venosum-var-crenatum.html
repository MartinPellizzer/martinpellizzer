Eupatorium exserto-venosum var. crenatum

Eupatorium exserto-venosum var. crenatum, commonly known as the serrated boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and opposite, serrated leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
