Eupatorium apense

Eupatorium apense, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and emit a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
