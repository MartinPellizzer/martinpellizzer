Eragrostis elegantissima

Eragrostis elegantissima, commonly known as the elegant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, arching stems that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and drought-tolerant gardens.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
