Euterpe macrospadix

Euterpe macrospadix, commonly known as the giant areca palm, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces long, drooping inflorescences with small, clustered flowers, which are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a fibrous, spadix-like structure that develops from the flowers and is often used in traditional practices.

Euterpe macrospadix plays an important ecological role in its native habitats and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
