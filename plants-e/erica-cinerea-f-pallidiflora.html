Erica cinerea f. pallidiflora

Erica cinerea f. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its narrow, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. Native to southern Europe, this plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
