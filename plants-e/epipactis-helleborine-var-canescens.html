Epipactis helleborine var. canescens

Epipactis helleborine var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and a tall, erect flowering stem that produces clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
