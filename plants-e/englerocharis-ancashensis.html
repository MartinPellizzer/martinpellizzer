Englerocharis ancashensis

Englerocharis ancashensis, commonly known as the Cashan rock orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found in the high-altitude regions of the Peruvian Andes.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, small, and often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates, showcasing its adaptability to harsh mountainous environments. Its morphology includes erect, branching stems and lanceolate leaves, which help it conserve moisture in its arid habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Englerocharis ancashensis is considered a conservation concern.
