Eugenia kingii

Eugenia kingii, commonly known as the king's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
