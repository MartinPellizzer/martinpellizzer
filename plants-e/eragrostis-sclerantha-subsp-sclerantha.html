Eragrostis sclerantha subsp. sclerantha

Eragrostis sclerantha subsp.

sclerantha, commonly known as the hard-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, stiff leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous minute flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
