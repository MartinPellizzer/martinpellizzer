Eryngium humile var. pulchellum

Eryngium humile var. pulchellum, commonly known as the slender thistle or purple thistle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spiny, blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky soils. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
