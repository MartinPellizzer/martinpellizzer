Eupatorium gaudichaudianum var. macrodon

Eupatorium gaudichaudianum var. macrodon, commonly known as the large-headed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that resemble umbels. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite, lanceolate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are predominantly white or pale blue, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
