Echinocactus laticostatus

Echinocactus laticostatus, commonly known as the broad-costed cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus collections.
