Erica permutata

Erica permutata, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica permutata is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Its flowers bloom in spring and are usually pink or white, attracting various pollinators.
