Ericameria linearifolia

Ericameria linearifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf greasewood, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Ericameria linearifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
