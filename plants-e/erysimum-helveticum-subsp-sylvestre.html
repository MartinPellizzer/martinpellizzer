Erysimum helveticum subsp. sylvestre

Erysimum helveticum subsp.

sylvestre, commonly known as the Alpine wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

The plant features basal rosettes of linear, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
