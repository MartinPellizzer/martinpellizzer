Euthamia graminifolia var. major

Euthamia graminifolia var. major, commonly known as golden tickseed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it the species name "graminifolia," while the varietal name "major" refers to its larger flower heads compared to other varieties.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
