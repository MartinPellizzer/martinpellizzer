Everettia pulcherrima

Everettia pulcherrima, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The everettia pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.
