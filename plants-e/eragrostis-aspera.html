Eragrostis aspera

Eragrostis aspera, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats. Eragrostis aspera is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
