Erica strictifolia

Erica strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica strictifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
