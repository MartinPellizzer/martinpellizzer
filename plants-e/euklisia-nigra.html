Euklisia nigra

Euklisia nigra, commonly known as black euklisia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
