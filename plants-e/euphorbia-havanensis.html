Euphorbia havanensis

Euphorbia havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban spurge, is a succulent plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique floral structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
