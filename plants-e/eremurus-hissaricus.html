Eremurus hissaricus

Eremurus hissaricus, commonly known as the Persian giant flax, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow flowers. The plant produces large, fibrous seeds that are used in traditional medicine and textile production. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance.

Eremurus hissaricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
