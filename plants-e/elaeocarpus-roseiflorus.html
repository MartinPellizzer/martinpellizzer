Elaeocarpus roseiflorus

Elaeocarpus roseiflorus, commonly known as the rose-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color as they ripen. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, contributing to its overall aesthetic and ecological role in its native habitat.
