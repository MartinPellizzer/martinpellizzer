Eria lutea

Eria lutea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and slender, often pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eria lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
