Eriocaulon nantoense var. parviceps

Eriocaulon nantoense var. parviceps, commonly known as the small-headed marsh grass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
