Euphorbia paulianii

Euphorbia paulianii, commonly known as the Paulian's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
