Eurycaulis septemcostulatus

Eurycaulis septemcostulatus, commonly known as the seven-costed eurycaulis, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, multi-branched stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the short growing season in its alpine habitat. Its unique morphology and restricted distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation to extreme environments.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
