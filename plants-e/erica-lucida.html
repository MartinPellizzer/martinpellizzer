Erica lucida

Erica lucida, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue due to fine, overlapping scales on the leaves. Erica lucida produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.
