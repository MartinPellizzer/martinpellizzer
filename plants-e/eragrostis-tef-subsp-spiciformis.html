Eragrostis tef subsp. spiciformis

Eragrostis tef subsp.

spiciformis, commonly known as wild teff, is a grass species native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces tiny, three-angled seeds that are typically brown to reddish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

This subspecies is closely related to cultivated teff and is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture.
