Escallonia tortuosa

Escallonia tortuosa, commonly known as the zigzag plant, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its twisted, zigzagging stems that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are glossy, dark green, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Escallonia tortuosa is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
