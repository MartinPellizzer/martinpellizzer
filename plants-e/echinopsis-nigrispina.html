Echinopsis nigrispina

Echinopsis nigrispina, commonly known as the Black-spined Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its spines are dark in color, giving it its common name, and it is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinopsis nigrispina is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
