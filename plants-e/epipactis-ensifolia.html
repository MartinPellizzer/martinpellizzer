Epipactis ensifolia

Epipactis ensifolia, commonly known as the sword-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and tall, erect flowering spikes that bear showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is divided into two lobes, and it produces small, dark brown seeds in a capsule.

Epipactis ensifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.
