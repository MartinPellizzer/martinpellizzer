Eria pilosissima

Eria pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eria pilosissima is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
