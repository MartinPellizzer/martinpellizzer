Euphorbia enormis

Euphorbia enormis, commonly known as the giant spurge, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces a unique inflorescence called a cyathium, which contains yellowish flowers surrounded by specialized leaves. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its leaves and stems to survive in arid conditions.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling and is often grown in controlled environments.
