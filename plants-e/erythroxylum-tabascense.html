Erythroxylum tabascense

Erythroxylum tabascense, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its striking purple and red flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, and it is often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
