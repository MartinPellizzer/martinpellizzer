Eupatorium imitans

Eupatorium imitans, commonly known as false boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium imitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal medicine.
