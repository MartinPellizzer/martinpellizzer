Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp. arida

Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp.

arida, commonly known as the river red gum, is a large, deciduous tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to access groundwater. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

The tree is often found along watercourses and is an important component of riparian ecosystems.
