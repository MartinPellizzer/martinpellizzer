Eragrostis theinlwinii

Eragrostis theinlwinii, commonly known as the Burmese lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Eragrostis theinlwinii is often found in open areas such as roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.
