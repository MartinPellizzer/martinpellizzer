Echium formosum

Echium formosum, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, black seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Echium formosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinator populations.
