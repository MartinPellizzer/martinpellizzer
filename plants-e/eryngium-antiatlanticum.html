Eryngium antiatlanticum

Eryngium antiatlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic thistle, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flowers and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats. The plant has a compact growth habit and thrives in well-drained soils. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

Eryngium antiatlanticum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
