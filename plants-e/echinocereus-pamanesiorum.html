Echinocereus pamanesiorum

Echinocereus pamanesiorum, commonly known as the Piman's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of the United States and Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically white to yellowish in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
