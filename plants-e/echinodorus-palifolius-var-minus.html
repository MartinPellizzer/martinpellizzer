Echinodorus palifolius var. minus

Echinodorus palifolius var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 cm wide, giving it a distinct appearance compared to other Echinodorus species. This variety thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to colonize submerged substrates effectively.

The plant is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
