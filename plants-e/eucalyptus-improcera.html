Eucalyptus improcera

Eucalyptus improcera, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a tall, slender tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark that peels in thin strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

Eucalyptus improcera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
