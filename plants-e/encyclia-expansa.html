Encyclia expansa

Encyclia expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are borne on erect, branching inflorescences and are pollinated by insects.

Encyclia expansa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and relatively easy care requirements.
