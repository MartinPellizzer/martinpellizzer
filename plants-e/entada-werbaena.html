Entada werbaena

Entada werbaena, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The tree is noted for its dense, dark wood, which is highly valued in traditional crafts and construction. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feathery foliage that provides shade in its natural habitat.

Entada werbaena also plays a role in local ecosystems by supporting various forms of wildlife.
