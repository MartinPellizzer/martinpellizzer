Eugenia piresiana

Eugenia piresiana, commonly known as the Pires's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor, making it a valuable plant for both culinary and ornamental purposes.
