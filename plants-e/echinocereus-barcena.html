Echinocereus barcena

Echinocereus barcena, commonly known as the Barcena hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spiny areoles, typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and minimal water.

Echinocereus barcena is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and unique appearance.
