Erodium laciniatum var. hispidum

Erodium laciniatum var. hispidum, commonly known as the hairy erodium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pink or violet flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its hairy foliage and stems give it a distinctive appearance, and it often grows in disturbed soils.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
