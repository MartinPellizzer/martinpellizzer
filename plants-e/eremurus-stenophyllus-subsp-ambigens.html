Eremurus stenophyllus subsp. ambigens

Eremurus stenophyllus subsp.

ambigens, commonly known as the desert spearplant, is a perennial plant native to arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, adapting to harsh, dry environments. Its structure resembles that of a grass, but it is actually a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
