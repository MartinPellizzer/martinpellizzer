Eriocaulon albosetaceum

Eriocaulon albosetaceum, commonly known as the white-setaceous spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered in spikes. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with a fine, silky layer of hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon albosetaceum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
