Eugenia escuintlensis

Eugenia escuintlensis, commonly known as the Mexican wild guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed raw or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Eugenia escuintlensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
