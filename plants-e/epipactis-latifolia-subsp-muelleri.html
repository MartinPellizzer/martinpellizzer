Epipactis latifolia subsp. muelleri

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a complex root system and produces a single flowering stem with several blooms.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
