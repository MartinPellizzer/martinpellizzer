Echinocereus pectinatus var. castaneus

Echinocereus pectinatus var. castaneus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and bright red tips, which distinguish it from other varieties. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding vibrant color to its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
