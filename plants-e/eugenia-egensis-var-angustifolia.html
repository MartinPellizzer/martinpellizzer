Eugenia egensis var. angustifolia

Eugenia egensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evergreen myrtle, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and fragrance.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
