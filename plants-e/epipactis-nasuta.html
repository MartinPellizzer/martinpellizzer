Epipactis nasuta

Epipactis nasuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers that often have a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Epipactis nasuta is also notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, often relying on specific insects for reproduction.
