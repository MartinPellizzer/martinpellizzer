Eremophila chamaephila

Eremophila chamaephila, commonly known as the silky eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period usually occurs during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Eremophila chamaephila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native landscapes.
