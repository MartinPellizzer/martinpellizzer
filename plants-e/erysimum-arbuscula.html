Erysimum arbuscula

Erysimum arbuscula, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright yellow and arranged in racemes. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.

The species is noted for its ability to tolerate harsh conditions and is sometimes used in rock gardens.
