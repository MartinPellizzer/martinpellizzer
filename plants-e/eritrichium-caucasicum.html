Eritrichium caucasicum

Eritrichium caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Eritrichium caucasicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
