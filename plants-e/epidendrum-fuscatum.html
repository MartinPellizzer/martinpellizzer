Epidendrum fuscatum

Epidendrum fuscatum, commonly known as the black-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect sunlight.

Epidendrum fuscatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
