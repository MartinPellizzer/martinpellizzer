Eurycoma apiculata

Eurycoma apiculata, commonly known as the Malayan toothbrush, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical rainforests, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences, while its fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Eurycoma apiculata is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional medicine.
