Echinocactus microspermus

Echinocactus microspermus, commonly known as the small-seeded hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus characterized by its globular shape and thick, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
