Elsholtzia winitiana

Elsholtzia winitiana, commonly known as the Chinese hyssop, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Elsholtzia winitiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Chinese medicine.
