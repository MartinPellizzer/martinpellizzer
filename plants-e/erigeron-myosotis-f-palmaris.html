Erigeron myosotis f. palmaris

Erigeron myosotis f. palmaris, commonly known as the palmate blueweed, is a rare horticultural form of the common blueweed.

It is characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves, which are divided into five to seven lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, delicate blue to purple flowers in the summer months, similar to its parent species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often sought after by botanical enthusiasts and gardeners.
