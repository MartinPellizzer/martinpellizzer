Erodium hoefftianum subsp. hoefftianum

Erodium hoefftianum subsp.

hoefftianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Its small seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
