Eurybia corymbosa

Eurybia corymbosa, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in herbal medicine.

Eurybia corymbosa is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to partial shade.
