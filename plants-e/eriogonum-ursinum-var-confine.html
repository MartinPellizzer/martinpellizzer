Eriogonum ursinum var. confine

Eriogonum ursinum var. confine, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pale pink to white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions.
