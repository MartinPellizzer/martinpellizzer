Eugenia retinadenia

Eugenia retinadenia, commonly known as the red cedar or red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

Eugenia retinadenia is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
