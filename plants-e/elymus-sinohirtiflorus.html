Elymus sinohirtiflorus

Elymus sinohirtiflorus, commonly known as the Chinese roughgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and its flowering period occurs in the summer months.

Elymus sinohirtiflorus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
