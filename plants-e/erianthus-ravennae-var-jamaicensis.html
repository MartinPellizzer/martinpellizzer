Erianthus ravennae var. jamaicensis

Erianthus ravennae var. jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican silver grass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-white plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
