Echinomastus johnsonii var. lutescens

Echinomastus johnsonii var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden echinomastus, is a rare and striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in dense, spherical clusters. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
