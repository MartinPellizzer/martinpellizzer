Eragrostis aristiglumis

Eragrostis aristiglumis, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis aristiglumis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
