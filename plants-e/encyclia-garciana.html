Encyclia garciana

Encyclia garciana, commonly known as the Guatemalan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and dense clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in seasonal conditions.

Encyclia garciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
