Eria longispica

Eria longispica, commonly known as the long-spurred eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the elongated, spurred flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

Eria longispica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in botanical gardens for its delicate appearance.
