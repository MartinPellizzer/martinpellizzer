Euonymus europaeus f. angustifolius

Euonymus europaeus f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodbine, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
