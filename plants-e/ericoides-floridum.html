Ericoides floridum

Ericoides floridum, commonly known as the Florida heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ericoides floridum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
