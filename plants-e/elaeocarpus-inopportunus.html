Elaeocarpus inopportunus

Elaeocarpus inopportunus, commonly known as the opportunistic elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its adaptability, it can be cultivated in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient species in horticultural settings.
