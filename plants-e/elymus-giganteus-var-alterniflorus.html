Elymus giganteus var. alterniflorus

Elymus giganteus var. alterniflorus, commonly known as the giant needlegrass, is a tall perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its robust growth and distinctive, alternate-flowered inflorescence. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often adorned with bristles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable forage species.
