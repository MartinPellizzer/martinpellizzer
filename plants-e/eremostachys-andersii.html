Eremostachys andersii

Eremostachys andersii, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with gray-green foliage adapted to withstand harsh, dry conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Eremostachys andersii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid landscapes.
