Eria phaeotricha

Eria phaeotricha, commonly known as the golden beard orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while the inflorescence bears numerous small, clustered flowers.

Eria phaeotricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
