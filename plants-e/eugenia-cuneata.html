Eugenia cuneata

Eugenia cuneata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a strong, sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Eugenia cuneata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
