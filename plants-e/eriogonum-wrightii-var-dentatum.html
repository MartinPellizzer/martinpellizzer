Eriogonum wrightii var. dentatum

Eriogonum wrightii var. dentatum, commonly known as the Wright's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in soft, woolly hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
