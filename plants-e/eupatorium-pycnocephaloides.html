Eupatorium pycnocephaloides

Eupatorium pycnocephaloides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blue to purple florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its foliage is aromatic and often covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
