Eulophia barteri

Eulophia barteri, commonly known as the Barter's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical Africa.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Eulophia barteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
