Echinops quercetorum

Echinops quercetorum, commonly known as the Persian Thistle, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, which are composed of tightly packed bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in early summer, followed by the development of its distinctive seed heads.

Echinops quercetorum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
