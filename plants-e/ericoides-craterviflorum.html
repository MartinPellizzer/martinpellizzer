Ericoides craterviflorum

Ericoides craterviflorum, commonly known as the crater-violet, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
