Euphorbia pubicaulis

Euphorbia pubicaulis, commonly known as the pubescent spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, woody stem covered in a dense layer of fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its characteristic floral structures. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a similar pubescent coating.

Euphorbia pubicaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
