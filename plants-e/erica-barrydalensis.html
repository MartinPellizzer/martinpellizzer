Erica barrydalensis

Erica barrydalensis, commonly known as the Barrow's heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal areas of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Erica barrydalensis is notable for its resilience to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh maritime environments.

Its flowers, typically pink or white, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
