Eulophia hildebrandii

Eulophia hildebrandii, commonly known as the Hildebrand's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from white to pale yellow.

Eulophia hildebrandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
