Eugenia pimenta var. ovalifolia

Eugenia pimenta var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic and antiseptic properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and medicinal qualities.
