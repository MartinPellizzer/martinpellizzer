Eria bigibba

Eria bigibba, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has fleshy, strap-like leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the canopy of tropical forests.

Eria bigibba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
