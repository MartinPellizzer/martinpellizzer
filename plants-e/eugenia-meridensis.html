Eugenia meridensis

Eugenia meridensis, commonly known as the Sierra Madre myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that ripen to a deep red or purple color.

Eugenia meridensis is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
