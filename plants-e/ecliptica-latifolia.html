Ecliptica latifolia

Ecliptica latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the warm season. Ecliptica latifolia is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional medicine for its purported skin-healing benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
