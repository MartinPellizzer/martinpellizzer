Eria aporoides

Eria aporoides, commonly known as the orchid with no roots, is a unique species belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of traditional roots, instead relying on aerial structures for nutrient absorption. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its rare and specialized growth habits, it is often cultivated in controlled conditions for conservation and horticultural purposes.
