Eugenia schumanniana

Eugenia schumanniana, commonly known as the Schumacher's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in suitable climates.
