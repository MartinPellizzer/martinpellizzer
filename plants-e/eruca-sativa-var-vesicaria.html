Eruca sativa var. vesicaria

Eruca sativa var. vesicaria, commonly known as giant wallrocket, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and large, serrated leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its leaves are edible and have a pungent, mustard-like flavor, making it a valuable addition to culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, contributing to its ecological resilience.
