Epidendrum falsum

Epidendrum falsum, commonly known as the false epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features strap-like, elongated leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip.

Epidendrum falsum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
