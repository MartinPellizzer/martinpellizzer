Eleusine tristachya

Eleusine tristachya, commonly known as swamp panic, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often surrounded by bristles, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, with a smooth texture, and it thrives in wet or moist environments.

Eleusine tristachya is often found in marshy areas, along riverbanks, and in disturbed soils.
