Eremanthus scapigerus

Eremanthus scapigerus, commonly known as the scarlet eremobat, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, red-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically clustered in composite flower heads. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Eremanthus scapigerus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
