Erica obtusata

Erica obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Erica obtusata is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
