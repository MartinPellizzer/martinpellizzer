Elaeagnus matsunoana var. hypostellata

Elaeagnus matsunoana var. hypostellata, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
