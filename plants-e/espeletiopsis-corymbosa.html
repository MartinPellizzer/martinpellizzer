Espeletiopsis corymbosa

Espeletiopsis corymbosa, commonly known as the Colombian rose or the Andean rose, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Espeletiopsis corymbosa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in its native habitat.
