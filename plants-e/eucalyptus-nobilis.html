Eucalyptus nobilis

Eucalyptus nobilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically grey-green in color and have a rough, bark-like texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are usually visited by a variety of pollinators. Eucalyptus nobilis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and woodland habitats.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous Australian communities.
