Entada philippinensis

Entada philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and rough, fissured bark. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, and the fruit pods contain several seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
