Excoecaria marginata

Excoecaria marginata, commonly known as the croton tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, and orange. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds that require caution in handling.
