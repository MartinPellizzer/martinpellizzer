Elwendia wolffii

Elwendia wolffii, commonly known as the woolly elwendia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
