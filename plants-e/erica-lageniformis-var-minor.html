Erica lageniformis var. minor

Erica lageniformis var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, usually blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
