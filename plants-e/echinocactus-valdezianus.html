Echinocactus valdezianus

Echinocactus valdezianus, commonly known as the Valdezian hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically greenish-gray in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
