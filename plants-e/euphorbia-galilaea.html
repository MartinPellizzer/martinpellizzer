Euphorbia galilaea

Euphorbia galilaea, commonly known as the Galilee spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Israel and Lebanon.

It features a compact growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia galilaea is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
