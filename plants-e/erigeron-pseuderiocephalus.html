Erigeron pseuderiocephalus

Erigeron pseuderiocephalus, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in sandy or rocky soils, thriving in disturbed or open habitats.
