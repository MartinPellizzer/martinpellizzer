Eugenia cassinoides var. difformis

Eugenia cassinoides var. difformis, commonly known as the variable myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Central America. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and oval-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The fruit, which is a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
