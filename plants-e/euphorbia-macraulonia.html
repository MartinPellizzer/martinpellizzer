Euphorbia macraulonia

Euphorbia macraulonia, commonly known as the giant candleplant, is a striking succulent native to South Africa.

It features a tall, spindly stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright yellow cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
