Eragrostiella leioptera

Eragrostiella leioptera, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, sharing structural and genetic traits with other grasses.
