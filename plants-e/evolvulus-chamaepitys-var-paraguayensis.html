Evolvulus chamaepitys var. paraguayensis

Evolvulus chamaepitys var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that open in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, easy-to-grow plants.
