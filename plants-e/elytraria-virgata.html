Elytraria virgata

Elytraria virgata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sand mat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a mat-forming growth habit, characterized by its narrow, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its succulent leaves and compact form help it conserve water in harsh environments.

Elytraria virgata plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
