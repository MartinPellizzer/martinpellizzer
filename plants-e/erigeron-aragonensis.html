Erigeron aragonensis

Erigeron aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of white and pink.

Erigeron aragonensis is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.
