Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp. ochroleucum

Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and small, clustered white to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and is well adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
