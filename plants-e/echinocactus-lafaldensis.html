Echinocactus lafaldensis

Echinocactus lafaldensis, commonly known as the La Falda cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the top of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
