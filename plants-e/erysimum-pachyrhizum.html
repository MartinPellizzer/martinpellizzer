Erysimum pachyrhizum

Erysimum pachyrhizum, commonly known as the thickroot wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has thick, fleshy roots that help it survive in dry conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Erysimum pachyrhizum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
