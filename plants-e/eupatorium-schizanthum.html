Eupatorium schizanthum

Eupatorium schizanthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium schizanthum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
