Eyrythalia sulcata

Eyrythalia sulcata, commonly known as the sulcate erythra.

It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Africa, characterized by its distinctive sulcate (grooved) leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Eyrythalia sulcata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid or tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
