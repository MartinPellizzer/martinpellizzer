Erigeron petrophilus

Erigeron petrophilus, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments such as cliffs and talus slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with blue to purple ray florets. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy member of the Asteraceae family.
