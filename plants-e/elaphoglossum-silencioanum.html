Elaphoglossum silencioanum

Elaphoglossum silencioanum, commonly known as the Silent Valley huperzia, is a fern species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, producing sporangia on the underside of its leaves in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained, acidic soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
