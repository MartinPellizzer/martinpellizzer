Elaeagnus × maritima

Elaeagnus × maritima, commonly known as sea-buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and yellow-orange berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in saline and sandy soils, making it suitable for coastal restoration projects. It produces a significant amount of biomass and has a rapid growth rate, which contributes to its ecological importance.

Additionally, its berries are rich in vitamins and have been used in traditional medicine and food products.
