Erythronium umbilicatum subsp. umbilicatum

Erythronium umbilicatum subsp.

umbilicatum, commonly known as the umbilical dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces a single, showy, bell-shaped flower that is typically white or pale yellow.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is part of the Liliaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants.
