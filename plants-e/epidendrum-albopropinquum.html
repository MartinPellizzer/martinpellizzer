Epidendrum albopropinquum

Epidendrum albopropinquum, commonly known as the white-lipped epidendron, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences and flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
