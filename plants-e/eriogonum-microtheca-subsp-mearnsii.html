Eriogonum microtheca subsp. mearnsii

Eriogonum microtheca subsp.

mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped, with stems that are upright and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
