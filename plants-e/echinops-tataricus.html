Echinops tataricus

Echinops tataricus, commonly known as the Tatar thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small, fuzzy balls. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops tataricus is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
