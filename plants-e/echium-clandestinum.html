Echium clandestinum

Echium clandestinum, commonly known as the "secret echium," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium clandestinum is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
