Eriogonum grande var. timorum

Eriogonum grande var. timorum, commonly known as the Timor flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, and it is adapted to coastal conditions with salt spray tolerance.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing dune ecosystems.
