Erica grandiflora

Erica grandiflora, commonly known as the great heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.

It also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
