Eupatorium espinosarum

Eupatorium espinosarum, commonly known as the Spiny Boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Eupatorium espinosarum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
