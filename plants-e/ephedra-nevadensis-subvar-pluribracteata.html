Ephedra nevadensis subvar. pluribracteata

Ephedra nevadensis subvar. pluribracteata, commonly known as the Nevada ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers that are borne in clusters, and its seeds are enclosed in small, three-lobed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its medicinal properties and historical use by indigenous peoples.
