Echinocactus gibbosus var. platensis

Echinocactus gibbosus var. platensis, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
