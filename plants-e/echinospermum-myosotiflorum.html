Echinospermum myosotiflorum

Echinospermum myosotiflorum, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
