Echium decaisnei subsp. decaisnei

Echium decaisnei subsp.

decaisnei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments.

The plant features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience in harsh climates.
