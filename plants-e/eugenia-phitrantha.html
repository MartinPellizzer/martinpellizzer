Eugenia phitrantha

Eugenia phitrantha, commonly known as the Thai cherry or Thai guava, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, where it grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
