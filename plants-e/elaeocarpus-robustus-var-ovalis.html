Elaeocarpus robustus var. ovalis

Elaeocarpus robustus var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, and is typically found in lowland tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
