Eragrostis jeholensis

Eragrostis jeholensis, commonly known as the Jehol grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is adapted to temperate climates.

Eragrostis jeholensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
