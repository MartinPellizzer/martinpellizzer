Elaeodendron xylocarpum var. continentale

Elaeodendron xylocarpum var. continentale, commonly known as the African ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and glossy in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and savanna habitats.
