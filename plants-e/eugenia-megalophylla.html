Eugenia megalophylla

Eugenia megalophylla, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by showy, fleshy red berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, acidic flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
