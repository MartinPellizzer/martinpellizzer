Elatostema pergameneum

Elatostema pergameneum, commonly known as the parchment plant, is a small, herbaceous species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes nettle-like plants. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that resemble parchment, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
