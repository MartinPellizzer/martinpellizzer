Egena

Egena, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its velvety, heart-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a compact shrub, often with a rosette of leaves at the base. Egena thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including purple, pink, white, and blue, making it a popular ornamental plant.
