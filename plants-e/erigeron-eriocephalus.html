Erigeron eriocephalus

Erigeron eriocephalus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by white to grayish ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Erigeron eriocephalus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
