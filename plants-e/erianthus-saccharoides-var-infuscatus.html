Erianthus saccharoides var. infuscatus

Erianthus saccharoides var. infuscatus, commonly known as the dark-leaved sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its robust growth, tall stalks, and dark green to almost black foliage. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a dense, fibrous structure, making it suitable for forage and biofuel production. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in agricultural and ecological contexts.
