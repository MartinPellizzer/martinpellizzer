Euphorbia tumistyla

Euphorbia tumistyla, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Euphorbia tumistyla is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse morphological features.
