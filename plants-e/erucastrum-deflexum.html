Erucastrum deflexum

Erucastrum deflexum, commonly known as the bentleaf bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a slightly hairy surface.

Erucastrum deflexum thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
