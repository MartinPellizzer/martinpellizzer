Erigeron humilis

Erigeron humilis, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron humilis is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, making it a common sight in prairies and along roadsides.
