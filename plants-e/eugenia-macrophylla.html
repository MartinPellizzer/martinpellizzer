Eugenia macrophylla

Eugenia macrophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant is often found in rainforests and moist woodland areas, thriving in humid, subtropical climates. Its bark is smooth and peels in strips, while its wood is valued for its strength and workability.

The plant plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
