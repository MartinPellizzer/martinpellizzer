Elephantopus glaber

Elephantopus glaber, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in dry meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural fields and gardens.
