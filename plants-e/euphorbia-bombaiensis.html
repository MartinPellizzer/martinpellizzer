Euphorbia bombaiensis

Euphorbia bombaiensis, commonly known as the Bomba euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Euphorbia bombaiensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
