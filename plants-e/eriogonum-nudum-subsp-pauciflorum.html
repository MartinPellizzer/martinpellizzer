Eriogonum nudum subsp. pauciflorum

Eriogonum nudum subsp.

pauciflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It typically features slender, branched stems covered in soft, grayish foliage.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its use in native plant landscaping.
