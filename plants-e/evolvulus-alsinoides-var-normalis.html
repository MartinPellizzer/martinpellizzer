Evolvulus alsinoides var. normalis

Evolvulus alsinoides var. normalis, commonly known as common blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in gardens. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in various regions for its beauty.
