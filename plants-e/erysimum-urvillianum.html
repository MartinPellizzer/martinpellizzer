Erysimum urvillianum

Erysimum urvillianum, commonly known as the Urvill's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.
