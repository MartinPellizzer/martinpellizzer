Excoecaria pallida var. angustifolia

Excoecaria pallida var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has alternate, simple, and typically narrow leaves that are often glossy in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in warm climates.
