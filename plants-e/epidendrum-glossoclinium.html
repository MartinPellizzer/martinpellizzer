Epidendrum glossoclinium

Epidendrum glossoclinium, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers with golden-yellow petals and a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
