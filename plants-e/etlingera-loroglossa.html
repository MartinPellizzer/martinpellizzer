Etlingera loroglossa

Etlingera loroglossa, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers that resemble torches, which bloom during the wet season. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Etlingera loroglossa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its use in traditional medicine.
