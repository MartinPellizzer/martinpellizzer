Eria sciadantha

Eria sciadantha, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a slender, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a distinctive greenish-brown stripe running along the dorsal sepal and petals.

Eria sciadantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
