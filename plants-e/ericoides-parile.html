Ericoides parile

Ericoides parile, commonly known as the parile ericoid, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and parts of Georgia. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Ericoides parile is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
