Elymus amgensis

Elymus amgensis, commonly known as the Amur wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It features tufted clumps with slender, leaf-like blades and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often used for erosion control and as a forage crop.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
