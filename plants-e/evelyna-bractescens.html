Evelyna bractescens

Evelyna bractescens, commonly known as the velvet leaf plant, is a flowering species native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that range in color from deep green to bronze. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a faint fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Evelyna bractescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
