Eugenia acetosans

Eugenia acetosans, commonly known as the sour apple myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, sour-tasting berries. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
