Euterpe gracilis

Euterpe gracilis, commonly known as the slender palm or Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, gracefully curved trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clusters of edible, sweet, and fibrous fruits. Euterpe gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of acai-like fruits.

However, it is considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and overharvesting.
