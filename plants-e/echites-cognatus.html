Echites cognatus

Echites cognatus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed echites, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often with deep root systems to access groundwater.

Echites cognatus is typically found in rocky soils and is often used in traditional medicine by local communities.
