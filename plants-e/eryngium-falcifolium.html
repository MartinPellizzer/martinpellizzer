Eryngium falcifolium

Eryngium falcifolium, commonly known as the sickle-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green, spiny leaves that are narrow and sickle-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants.
