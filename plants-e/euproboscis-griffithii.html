Euproboscis griffithii

Euproboscis griffithii, commonly known as the Indian horned poppy, is a striking flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with prominent, horn-like structures on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Euproboscis griffithii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
