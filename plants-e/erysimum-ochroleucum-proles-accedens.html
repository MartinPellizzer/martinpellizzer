Erysimum ochroleucum proles accedens

Erysimum ochroleucum proles accedens, commonly known as the yellow cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a weed in some agricultural contexts.
