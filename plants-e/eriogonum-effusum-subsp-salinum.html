Eriogonum effusum subsp. salinum

Eriogonum effusum subsp.

salinum, commonly known as saltbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, succulent leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in saline soils and is often found in coastal and inland salt marshes. Eriogonum effusum subsp.

salinum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
