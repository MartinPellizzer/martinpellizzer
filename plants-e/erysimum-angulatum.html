Erysimum angulatum

Erysimum angulatum, commonly known as the angular mustard, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive angular shape, which gives it its common name.

The plant is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
