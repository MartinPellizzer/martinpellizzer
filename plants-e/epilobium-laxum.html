Epilobium laxum

Epilobium laxum, commonly known as the woolly willow or slender willow, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Epilobium laxum thrives in moist, often wet environments such as stream banks and wet meadows.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is often found in riparian habitats.
