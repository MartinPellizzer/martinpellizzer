Erica patersonioides

Erica patersonioides, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.

Erica patersonioides plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
