Echinocactus polycephalus

Echinocactus polycephalus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
