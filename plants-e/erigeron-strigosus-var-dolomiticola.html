Erigeron strigosus var. dolomiticola

Erigeron strigosus var. dolomiticola, commonly known as the Dolomitic fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in rocky, limestone-rich soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. The plant features gray-green, woolly leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil conditions.

This variety is noted for its distinctive appearance and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
