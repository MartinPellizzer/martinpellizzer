Eucalyptus famelica

Eucalyptus famelica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Eucalyptus famelica is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species that can thrive in poor soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various native animals.
