Elleanthus linifolius

Elleanthus linifolius, commonly known as the slender leaf everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant is typically found in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
