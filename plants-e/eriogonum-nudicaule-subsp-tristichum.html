Eriogonum nudicaule subsp. tristichum

Eriogonum nudicaule subsp.

tristichum, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically features wiry, sparsely branched stems with small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
