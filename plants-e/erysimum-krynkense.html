Erysimum krynkense

Erysimum krynkense, commonly known as the Krynkels wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its stems are hairy as well. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Erysimum krynkense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its cheerful blossoms.
