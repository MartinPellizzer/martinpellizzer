Euphorbia inornata

Euphorbia inornata, commonly known as the smooth spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its milky sap, a characteristic feature of many species in the genus Euphorbia. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with dense, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia. Its leaves are usually opposite and oval-shaped, with a smooth texture.

Euphorbia inornata is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.
