Echites asperus

Echites asperus, commonly known as the rough echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant is known for its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Echites asperus is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
