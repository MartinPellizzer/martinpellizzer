Eugenia plumbea

Eugenia plumbea, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by small, fleshy berries. Eugenia plumbea is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
