Erodium guttatum subsp. euguttatum

Erodium guttatum subsp.

euguttatum, commonly known as the spotted bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and other open habitats.
