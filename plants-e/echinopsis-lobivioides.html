Echinopsis lobivioides

Echinopsis lobivioides, commonly known as the Lobivia-like Echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscape designs.
