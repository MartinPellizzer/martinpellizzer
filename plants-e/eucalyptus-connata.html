Eucalyptus connata

Eucalyptus connata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are typically visited by a variety of pollinators. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for timber and fuel.

Eucalyptus connata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.
