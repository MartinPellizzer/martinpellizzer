Eupatorium consanguineum var. inaequale

Eupatorium consanguineum var. inaequale, commonly known as the unequal bloodwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive reddish-purple stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
