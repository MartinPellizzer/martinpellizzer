Evaiezoa punctata

Evaiezoa punctata, commonly known as the spotted leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often marked with distinctive white or pale green spots. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically arranged in a loose cluster. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
