Epiphyllopsis gaertneri

Epiphyllopsis gaertneri, commonly known as the mountain marsh orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a dense spike, which bloom in late summer. Epiphyllopsis gaertneri thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant species within its native habitat.
