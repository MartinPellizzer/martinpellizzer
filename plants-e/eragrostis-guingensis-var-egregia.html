Eragrostis guingensis var. egregia

Eragrostis guingensis var. egregia, commonly known as the Chinese lovegrass, is a grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is adapted to temperate climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.
