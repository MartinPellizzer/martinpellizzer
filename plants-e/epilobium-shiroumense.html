Epilobium shiroumense

Epilobium shiroumense, commonly known as the Japanese willow herb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium shiroumense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
