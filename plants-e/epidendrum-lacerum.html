Epidendrum lacerum

Epidendrum lacerum, commonly known as the Shattered Lip Orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant stems and striking, often brightly colored flowers with a distinctive, fringed lip. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often attached to trees or rocks. Its leaves are usually elongated and may have a waxy coating to help retain moisture.

Epidendrum lacerum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and relative ease of cultivation.
