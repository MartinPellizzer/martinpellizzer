Eugenia assamica

Eugenia assamica, commonly known as the Assam tea plant, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in regions of India and Nepal. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is widely cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of black tea.

The fruit of Eugenia assamica is not typically consumed by humans but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
