Elaeocarpus gambutanus

Elaeocarpus gambutanus, commonly known as the purple-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that turn a striking purple color when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by fleshy, purple fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
