Eupatorium perornatum

Eupatorium perornatum, commonly known as sweet pepper bush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters tall, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
