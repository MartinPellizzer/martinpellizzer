Eugenia oleioides

Eugenia oleioides, commonly known as the wax myrtle or Australian tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often coated with a waxy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, hard, black drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia oleioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic oils.
