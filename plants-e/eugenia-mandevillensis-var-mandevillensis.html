Eugenia mandevillensis var. mandevillensis

Eugenia mandevillensis var. mandevillensis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit, a small, red, cherry-like berry, is rich in vitamin C and other nutrients.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
