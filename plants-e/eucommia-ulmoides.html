Eucommia ulmoides

Eucommia ulmoides, commonly known as the Chinese bleeding heart tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, corky bark that peels in irregular strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by heart-shaped, red or orange fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a smooth margin.

Eucommia ulmoides is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional Chinese medicine.
