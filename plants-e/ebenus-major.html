Ebenus major

Ebenus major, commonly known as the common evening-primrose, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Ebenus major is known for its medicinal properties, with its seeds containing high levels of gamma-linolenic acid.
