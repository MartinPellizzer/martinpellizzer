Etlingera mamasarum

Etlingera mamasarum, commonly known as the Sulu torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch and is highly ornamental. It thrives in tropical climates and requires warm, humid conditions to flourish.

The leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance.
