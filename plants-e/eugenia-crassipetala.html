Eugenia crassipetala

Eugenia crassipetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia crassipetala is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
