Ervum lens var. abyssinica

Ervum lens var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian lentil, is a variant of the common lentil native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish seeds that are typically harvested when fully mature. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is an annual legume that fixes nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to dry conditions.
