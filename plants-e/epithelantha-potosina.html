Epithelantha potosina

Epithelantha potosina, commonly known as the Potosi cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil.

Epithelantha potosina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
