Erythroxylum coelophlebium

Erythroxylum coelophlebium, commonly known as the yellow coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves contain alkaloids, although it is not as well-known for its psychoactive properties as other species in the genus.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats at moderate elevations.
