Eremophila macdonnellii

Eremophila macdonnellii, commonly known as the Macdonnell Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-tolerant gardens.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
