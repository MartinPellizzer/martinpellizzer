Encholirium subsecundum

Encholirium subsecundum, commonly known as the Mexican sun cactus, is a species of cactus native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Encholirium subsecundum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
