Eleocharis flavescens var. flavescens

Eleocharis flavescens var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its yellowish inflorescence gives it its common name, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
