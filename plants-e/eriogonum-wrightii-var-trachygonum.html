Eriogonum wrightii var. trachygonum

Eriogonum wrightii var. trachygonum, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other members like sorrel and knotweed.

Eriogonum wrightii var. trachygonum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
