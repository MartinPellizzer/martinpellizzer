Evolvulus riedelii

Evolvulus riedelii, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Evolvulus riedelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
