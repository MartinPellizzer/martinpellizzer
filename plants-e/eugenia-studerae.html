Eugenia studerae

Eugenia studerae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production.

Eugenia studerae is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
