Eschscholzia quadrangularis

Eschscholzia quadrangularis, commonly known as the quadrangular poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular (four-sided) stems and showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and chaparral habitats. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
