Eugenia rheedioides

Eugenia rheedioides, commonly known as the red cedar or red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It produces small, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to others.

Eugenia rheedioides is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
