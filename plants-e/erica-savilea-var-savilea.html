Erica savilea var. savilea

Erica savilea var. savilea, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica savilea var. savilea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
