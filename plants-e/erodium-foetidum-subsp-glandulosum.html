Erodium foetidum subsp. glandulosum

Erodium foetidum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as stinkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in disturbed soils.

The subspecies is known for its strong, unpleasant odor, which is particularly noticeable when the plant is crushed.
