Elymus trachycaulus subsp. novae-angliae

Elymus trachycaulus subsp.

novae-angliae, commonly known as the New England wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense clumps.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
