Eria toxopei

Eria toxopei, commonly known as the toxic orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
