Erica laniflora

Erica laniflora, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica laniflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to coastal and sandy environments.
