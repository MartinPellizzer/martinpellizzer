Emorycactus

Emorycactus, commonly known as the Emory's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless, columnar cactus with a distinctive greenish-gray color and a ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Emorycactus is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
