Eupatorium compositifolium

Eupatorium compositifolium, commonly known as the composite-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small white or pale blue flowers.

It is typically found in moist meadows, stream banks, and wetlands, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
