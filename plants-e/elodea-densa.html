Elodea densa

Elodea densa, commonly known as waterweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, whorled leaves. The plant is typically submerged, with leaves that are narrow and dark green, often with a slightly waxy coating. Elodea densa reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, with underwater flowers that are pollinated by insects.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
