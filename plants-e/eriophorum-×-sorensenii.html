Eriophorum × sorensenii

Eriophorum × sorensenii, commonly known as the hybrid tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Eriophorum × sorensenii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
