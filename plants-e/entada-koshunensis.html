Entada koshunensis

Entada koshunensis, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive seed pods. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several hard, angular seeds. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a yellow or greenish hue.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some African communities.
