Erythronium angustatum

Erythronium angustatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf dog-tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully unfurl. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Erythronium angustatum is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
