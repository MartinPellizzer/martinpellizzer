Eragrostis megastachya var. cilianensis

Eragrostis megastachya var. cilianensis, commonly known as the Cilician lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are typically narrow and hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
