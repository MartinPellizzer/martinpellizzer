Eugenia friburgensis

Eugenia friburgensis, commonly known as the Friburg ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically dark purple when mature. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Eugenia friburgensis is also known for its aromatic wood, which is used in the production of musical instruments and furniture.
