Echinopsis cristata

Echinopsis cristata, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Echinopsis cristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in landscaping.
