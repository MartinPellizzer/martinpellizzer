Eulophia hians

Eulophia hians, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in a raceme, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Eulophia hians is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
