Eragrostis pilosa var. minor

Eragrostis pilosa var. minor, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping. It is drought-tolerant and low-maintenance, often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
