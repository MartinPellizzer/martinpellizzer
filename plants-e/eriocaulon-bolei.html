Eriocaulon bolei

Eriocaulon bolei, commonly known as the golden spikegrass, is a small perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its inflorescence is often covered with a dense layer of silky, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon bolei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
