Epidendrum melanogastropodium

Epidendrum melanogastropodium, commonly known as the black-faced epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, dark red to purple flowers with yellow lips. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical forests. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique coloration and relatively easy care requirements make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
