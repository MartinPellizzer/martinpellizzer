Eugenioides caerulescens

Eugenioides caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered eugenioid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
