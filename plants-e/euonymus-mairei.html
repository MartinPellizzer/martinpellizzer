Euonymus mairei

Euonymus mairei, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage in the autumn, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a dark green color during the growing season. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter.

This plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various soil types.
