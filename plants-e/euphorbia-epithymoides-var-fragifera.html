Euphorbia epithymoides var. fragifera

Euphorbia epithymoides var. fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and is adapted to withstand drought.

Its fragrant flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
