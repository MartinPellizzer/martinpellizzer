Erodium carvifolium subsp. castellanum

Erodium carvifolium subsp.

castellanum, commonly known as the Castellano's bedstraw, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe. It features small, pink to purple flowers and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its morphology is characterized by slender stems and a low-growing habit.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is part of the Geraniaceae family.
