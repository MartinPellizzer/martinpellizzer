Empetrum nigrum var. hermaphroditum

Empetrum nigrum var. hermaphroditum, commonly known as the common crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, dark blue berries that are typically edible and used in culinary applications. The plant has dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing year-round visual interest. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

The variety hermaphroditum is notable for its ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same plant, enhancing its reproductive success.
