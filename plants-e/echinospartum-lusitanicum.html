Echinospartum lusitanicum

Echinospartum lusitanicum, commonly known as the Portuguese thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
