Euphrasia minima subsp. minima

Euphrasia minima subsp.

minima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant features delicate, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in spikes, producing nectar that attracts various pollinators.

It is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
