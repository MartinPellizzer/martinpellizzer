Entada gigas subsp. rectocarpa

Entada gigas subsp.

rectocarpa, commonly known as the African ivory bean, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers.

The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain hard, pale seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used in various crafts and construction.
