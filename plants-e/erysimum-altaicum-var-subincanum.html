Erysimum altaicum var. subincanum

Erysimum altaicum var. subincanum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The flowers are often arranged in racemes and are a key feature for its identification and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
