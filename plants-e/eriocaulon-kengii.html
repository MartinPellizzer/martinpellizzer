Eriocaulon kengii

Eriocaulon kengii, commonly known as the Keng's spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense mats near water bodies. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and are pollinated by wind or insects.
