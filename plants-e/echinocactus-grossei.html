Echinocactus grossei

Echinocactus grossei, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to two meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments.

Echinocactus grossei is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
