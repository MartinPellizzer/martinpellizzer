Eragrostis devolvens

Eragrostis devolvens, commonly known as the curlleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that curl at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Eragrostis devolvens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
