Eriogonum glandulosum var. carneum

Eriogonum glandulosum var. carneum, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive reddish hue, contributing to its common name.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
