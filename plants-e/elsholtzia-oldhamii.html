Elsholtzia oldhamii

Elsholtzia oldhamii, commonly known as Chinese germander, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice in horticulture.
