Eugenia venusta

Eugenia venusta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Eugenia venusta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
