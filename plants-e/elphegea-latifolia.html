Elphegea latifolia

Elphegea latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf elphegea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the wet season. Elphegea latifolia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
