Erucastrum canariense

Erucastrum canariense, commonly known as Canary Island cress, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are edible and have a peppery taste, similar to that of mustard greens.
