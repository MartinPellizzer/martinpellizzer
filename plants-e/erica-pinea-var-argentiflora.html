Erica pinea var. argentiflora

Erica pinea var. argentiflora, commonly known as the silver-flowered heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
