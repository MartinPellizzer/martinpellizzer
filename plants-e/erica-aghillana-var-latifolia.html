Erica aghillana var. latifolia

Erica aghillana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native environment.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
