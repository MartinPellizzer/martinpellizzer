Eriogonum brevicaule var. desertorum

Eriogonum brevicaule var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with gray-green foliage and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for desert conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
