Eupatorium calocephalum

Eupatorium calocephalum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
