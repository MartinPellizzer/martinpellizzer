Elwendia hissarica

Elwendia hissarica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or mountainous habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

Elwendia hissarica is also noted for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
