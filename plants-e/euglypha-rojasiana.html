Euglypha rojasiana

Euglypha rojasiana, commonly known as the Rojasian Euglypha, is a species of testate amoeba found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical shell composed of silica, which provides structural support and protection. The shell exhibits intricate patterns and is often adorned with delicate spines or ribs. This species plays a role in nutrient cycling within its aquatic habitat.

Due to its specific environmental requirements, it is considered an indicator species for certain water quality conditions.
