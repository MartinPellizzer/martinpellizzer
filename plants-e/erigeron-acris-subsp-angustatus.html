Erigeron acris subsp. angustatus

Erigeron acris subsp.

angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, blue to purple flower heads arranged in clusters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure.

This subspecies is often distinguished by its slender leaves and compact growth habit.
