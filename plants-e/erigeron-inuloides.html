Erigeron inuloides

Erigeron inuloides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
