Euonymus bungeanus var. mongolicus

Euonymus bungeanus var. mongolicus, commonly known as the Mongolian euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to northeastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and slightly pubescent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
