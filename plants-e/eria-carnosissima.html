Eria carnosissima

Eria carnosissima, commonly known as the fleshy orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to tropical and subtropical environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
