Elaeagnus pungens var. megaphylla

Elaeagnus pungens var. megaphylla, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, fuzzy leaves that provide a striking visual contrast to its dark green stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value and ecological benefits make it a popular choice for landscaping and restoration projects.
