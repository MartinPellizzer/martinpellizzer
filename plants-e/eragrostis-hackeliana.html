Eragrostis hackeliana

Eragrostis hackeliana, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
