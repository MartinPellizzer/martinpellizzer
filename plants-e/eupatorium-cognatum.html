Eupatorium cognatum

Eupatorium cognatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
