Erica umbellata var. major

Erica umbellata var. major, commonly known as the greater heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Erica umbellata var. major is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
