Eugenia citrifolia

Eugenia citrifolia, commonly known as the Surinam cherry or acerola, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and emits a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are white or pale pink, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high nutritional value.
