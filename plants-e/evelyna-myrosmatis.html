Evelyna myrosmatis

Evelyna myrosmatis, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically dark blue or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The essential oils from its leaves and flowers are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.
