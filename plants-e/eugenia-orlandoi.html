Eugenia orlandoi

Eugenia orlandoi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy dark green surface and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Eugenia orlandoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
