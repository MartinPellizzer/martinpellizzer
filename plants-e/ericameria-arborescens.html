Ericameria arborescens

Ericameria arborescens, commonly known as silverleaf erica, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it reflect sunlight in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ericameria arborescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native pollinators.
