Echinops kerneri

Echinops kerneri, commonly known as the Persian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky blue or purple spherical flower heads that resemble small combs. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, seed-filled capsules.

Echinops kerneri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
