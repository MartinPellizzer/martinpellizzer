Emurtia punicifolia

Emurtia punicifolia, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and high in vitamin C. Emurtia punicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citrus compounds.
