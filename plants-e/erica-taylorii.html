Erica taylorii

Erica taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees known for their adaptations to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica taylorii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
