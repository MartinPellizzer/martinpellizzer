Epilobium ovatum

Epilobium ovatum, commonly known as narrowleaf willow-herb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. The plant has erect, branching stems covered with fine, sticky hairs. Its leaves are narrow, ovate, and often have a slightly wavy margin.

The flowers are small, pink to red, and clustered in dense, showy spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
