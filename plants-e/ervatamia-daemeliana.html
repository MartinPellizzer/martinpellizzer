Ervatamia daemeliana

Ervatamia daemeliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eryngo, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Ervatamia daemeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
