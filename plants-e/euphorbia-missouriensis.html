Euphorbia missouriensis

Euphorbia missouriensis, commonly known as Missouri spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features upright stems covered in small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Missouri spurge thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in dry, disturbed soils.
