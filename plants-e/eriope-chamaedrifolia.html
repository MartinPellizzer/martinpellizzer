Eriope chamaedrifolia

Eriope chamaedrifolia, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eriope chamaedrifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
