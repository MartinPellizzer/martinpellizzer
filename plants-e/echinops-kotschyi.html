Echinops kotschyi

Echinops kotschyi, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Echinops kotschyi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
