Ephedra villarsii

Ephedra villarsii, commonly known as the desert jointfir, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Ephedra villarsii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
