Eupatorium foeniculaceum

Eupatorium foeniculaceum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems with finely divided, fern-like leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. Its showy, pale blue to purple flower clusters bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium foeniculaceum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
