Eriogonum nudum var. auriculatum

Eriogonum nudum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive auriculate (ear-like) base. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
