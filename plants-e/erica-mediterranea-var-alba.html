Erica mediterranea var. alba

Erica mediterranea var. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically evergreen, providing year-round structure in its native habitat. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and rocky environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
