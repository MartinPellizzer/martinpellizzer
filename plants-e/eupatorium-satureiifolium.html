Eupatorium satureiifolium

Eupatorium satureiifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
