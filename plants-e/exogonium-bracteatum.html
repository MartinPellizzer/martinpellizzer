Exogonium bracteatum

Exogonium bracteatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Exogonium bracteatum is often found in disturbed habitats and can become invasive in certain regions.
