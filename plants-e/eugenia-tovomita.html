Eugenia tovomita

Eugenia tovomita, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
