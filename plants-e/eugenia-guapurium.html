Eugenia guapurium

Eugenia guapurium, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guapurum, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
