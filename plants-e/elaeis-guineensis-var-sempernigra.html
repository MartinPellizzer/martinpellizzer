Elaeis guineensis var. sempernigra

Elaeis guineensis var. sempernigra, commonly known as the black oil palm, is a variant of the oil palm characterized by its dark-colored fruits.

It is native to western Africa and is cultivated for its high oil yield. The plant produces large, clustered fruits that contain a significant amount of oil-rich pulp. Its botanical features include tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves.

This variety is valued in both agricultural and industrial applications due to its economic importance.
