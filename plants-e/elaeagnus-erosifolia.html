Elaeagnus erosifolia

Elaeagnus erosifolia, commonly known as the sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia, including China, India, and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that give way to edible, orange-red berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for challenging soils.

Elaeagnus erosifolia is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, which improves soil fertility.
