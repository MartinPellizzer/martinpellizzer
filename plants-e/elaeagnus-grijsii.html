Elaeagnus grijsii

Elaeagnus grijsii, commonly known as the Griesii silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its tolerance to drought conditions. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Elaeagnus grijsii is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
