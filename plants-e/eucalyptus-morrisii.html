Eucalyptus morrisii

Eucalyptus morrisii, commonly known as the Morissey's mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, flaky bark and small, rounded leaves that are often grey-green in color. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Eucalyptus morrisii is well adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.
