Esenbeckia pumila

Esenbeckia pumila, commonly known as the silky esenbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Its leaves are characterized by their soft, silken texture, which gives it its common name.

Esenbeckia pumila is often cultivated for its unique foliage and ability to tolerate saline conditions.
