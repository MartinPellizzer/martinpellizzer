Ericoides sulcatum

Ericoides sulcatum, commonly known as the sulcate ericoid, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention in nutrient-poor soils. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Ericoides sulcatum is known for its ability to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for small organisms.
