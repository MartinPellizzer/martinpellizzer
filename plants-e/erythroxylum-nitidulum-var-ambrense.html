Erythroxylum nitidulum var. ambrense

Erythroxylum nitidulum var. ambrense, commonly known as the Ambrense copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the Erythroxylaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variety is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
