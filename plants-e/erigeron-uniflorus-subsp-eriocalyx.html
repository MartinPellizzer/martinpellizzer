Erigeron uniflorus subsp. eriocalyx

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

eriocalyx, commonly known as the narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

This subspecies is distinguished by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and a more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
