Elaeocarpus retusus

Elaeocarpus retusus, commonly known as the rough-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
