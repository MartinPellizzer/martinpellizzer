Edemias aegyptiaca

Edemias aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features erect, branched stems covered in a milky sap that can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Edemias aegyptiaca is often found in dry, rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
