Ericameria martirensis

Ericameria martirensis, commonly known as the Santa Barbara burrobrush, is a shrub native to southern California and northern Baja California.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and yellowish flower heads. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Ericameria martirensis is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
