Echinocactus polyacanthus

Echinocactus polyacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
