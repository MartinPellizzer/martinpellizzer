Eria mafuluensis

Eria mafuluensis, commonly known as the Mafulu orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly in the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
