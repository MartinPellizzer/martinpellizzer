Epilobium deminutum

Epilobium deminutum, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside ditches. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium deminutum is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to thrive in environments with high moisture levels.
