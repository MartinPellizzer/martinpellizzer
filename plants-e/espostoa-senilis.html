Espostoa senilis

Espostoa senilis, commonly known as the Senile Euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower clusters typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
