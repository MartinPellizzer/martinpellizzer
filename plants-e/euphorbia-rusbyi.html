Euphorbia rusbyi

Euphorbia rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a crown of fleshy, green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia rusbyi is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
