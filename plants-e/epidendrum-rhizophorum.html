Epidendrum rhizophorum

Epidendrum rhizophorum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple stems and leaves. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, with bright red or orange petals and a distinctive yellow lip.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
