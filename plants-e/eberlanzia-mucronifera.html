Eberlanzia mucronifera

Eberlanzia mucronifera, commonly known as the narrow-lobed hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming during the dry season. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Eberlanzia mucronifera is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to warm, humid climates.
