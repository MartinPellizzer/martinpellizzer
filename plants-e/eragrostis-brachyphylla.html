Eragrostis brachyphylla

Eragrostis brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
