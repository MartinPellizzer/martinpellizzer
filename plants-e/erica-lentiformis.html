Erica lentiformis

Erica lentiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This shrub typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in open, sunny habitats. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Erica lentiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
