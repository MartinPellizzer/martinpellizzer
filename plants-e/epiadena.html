Epiadena

Epiadena, commonly known as the false hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plants typically have showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. They are shrubs or small trees with rough, often hairy stems and alternate leaves.

Epiadena species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
