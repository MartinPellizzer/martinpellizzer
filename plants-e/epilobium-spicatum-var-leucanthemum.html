Epilobium spicatum var. leucanthemum

Epilobium spicatum var. leucanthemum, commonly known as white-flowered willowherb, is a flowering plant in the evening primrose family.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variant is distinguished by its more pronounced white flower color compared to the typical variety.
