Euphorbia rumelica

Euphorbia rumelica, commonly known as the Rumelian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, branching stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Euphorbia rumelica is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
