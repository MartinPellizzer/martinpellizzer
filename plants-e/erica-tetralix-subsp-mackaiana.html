Erica tetralix subsp. mackaiana

Erica tetralix subsp.

mackaiana, commonly known as the Mackaian heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
