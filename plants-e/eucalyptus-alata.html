Eucalyptus alata

Eucalyptus alata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eucalyptus, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink. Eucalyptus alata is often found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

It is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
