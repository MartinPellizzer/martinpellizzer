Euphrasia nemorosa var. curta

Euphrasia nemorosa var. curta, commonly known as the short-leaved eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy on the underside. It is known for its medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies for eye and respiratory ailments.

The variety 'curta' is distinguished by its shorter stems and leaves compared to the typical form of Euphrasia nemorosa.
