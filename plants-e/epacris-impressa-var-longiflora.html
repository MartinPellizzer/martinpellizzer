Epacris impressa var. longiflora

Epacris impressa var. longiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and tubular, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in heathlands or forested areas. It produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in spring and summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
