Erythrina merrilliana

Erythrina merrilliana, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often attracting birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in a hard, fibrous pod and are typically dispersed by animals.
