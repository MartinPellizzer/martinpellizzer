Eryngium americanum

Eryngium americanum, commonly known as sea holly, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
