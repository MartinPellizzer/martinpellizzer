Eremitis parviflora

Eremitis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered eremite, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, this plant is well adapted to dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in xeriscaping.
