Eleocharis intermedia

Eleocharis intermedia, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense colonies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Eleocharis intermedia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
