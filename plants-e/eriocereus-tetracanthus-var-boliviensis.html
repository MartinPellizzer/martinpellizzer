Eriocereus tetracanthus var. boliviensis

Eriocereus tetracanthus var. boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian night blooming cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and nocturnal flowering habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers, which open at night, are often pale pink or white, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native ecosystem.
