Eriosyce napina var. duripulpa

Eriosyce napina var. duripulpa, commonly known as the rock cactus or rock rose, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
