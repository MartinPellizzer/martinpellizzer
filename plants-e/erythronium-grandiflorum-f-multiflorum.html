Erythronium grandiflorum f. multiflorum

Erythronium grandiflorum f. multiflorum, commonly known as the multiflorous dog's tooth violet, is a variant of the common dog's tooth violet.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow. Its flowers typically bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, early-season blooms.
