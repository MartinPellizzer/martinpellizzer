Espeletia oswaldiana

Espeletia oswaldiana, commonly known as the Oswaldo's espeletia, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of Colombia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its rosette-shaped growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh habitat.

Espeletia oswaldiana plays an important ecological role in its native ecosystem, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
