Eugenia litseifolia

Eugenia litseifolia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
