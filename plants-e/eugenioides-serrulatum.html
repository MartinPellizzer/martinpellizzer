Eugenioides serrulatum

Eugenioides serrulatum, commonly known as the serrated eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
