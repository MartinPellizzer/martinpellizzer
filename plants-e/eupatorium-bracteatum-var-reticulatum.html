Eupatorium bracteatum var. reticulatum

Eupatorium bracteatum var. reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern, which is a key identifying trait. Its showy, pinkish to purple flower heads appear in late summer and attract various pollinators.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
