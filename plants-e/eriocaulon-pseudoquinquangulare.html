Eriocaulon pseudoquinquangulare

Eriocaulon pseudoquinquangulare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved papyrus, is a grass-like plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, white flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia, and thrives in moist, shady environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
