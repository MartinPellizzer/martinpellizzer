Epipactis helleborine f. obtusa

Epipactis helleborine f. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and a striking, hooded flower with a yellow-green lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex root system and produces a single, showy inflorescence with multiple flowers.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
