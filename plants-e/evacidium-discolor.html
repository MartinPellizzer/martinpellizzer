Evacidium discolor

Evacidium discolor, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Evacidium discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
