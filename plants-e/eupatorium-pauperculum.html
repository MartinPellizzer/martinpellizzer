Eupatorium pauperculum

Eupatorium pauperculum, commonly known as poorweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in heads.

Eupatorium pauperculum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
