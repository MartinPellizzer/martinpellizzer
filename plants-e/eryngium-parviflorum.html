Eryngium parviflorum

Eryngium parviflorum, commonly known as the smallflower thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant features spiny, blue-green leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eryngium parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
