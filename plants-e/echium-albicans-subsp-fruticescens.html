Echium albicans subsp. fruticescens

Echium albicans subsp.

fruticescens, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a shrub-like species that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
