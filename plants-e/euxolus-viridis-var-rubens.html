Euxolus viridis var. rubens

Euxolus viridis var. rubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed greenweed, is a variant of the greenweed plant characterized by its distinct red stems and green leaves.

It is a low-growing perennial herb typically found in moist, temperate regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellow in color. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique appearance.

This variant is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in soil stabilization.
