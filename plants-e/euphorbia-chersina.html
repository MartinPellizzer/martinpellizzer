Euphorbia chersina

Euphorbia chersina, commonly known as the Armenian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a cylindrical, branched stem covered in a thick layer of white, waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia chersina is adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
