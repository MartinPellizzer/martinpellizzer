Eriocaulon leucomelas

Eriocaulon leucomelas, commonly known as the white-stemmed rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a dense rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
