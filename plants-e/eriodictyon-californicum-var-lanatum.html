Eriodictyon californicum var. lanatum

Eriodictyon californicum var. lanatum, commonly known as California yerba buena, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It features gray-green, fuzzy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

This species is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by Indigenous peoples for its purported health benefits.
