Erica ochroleuca

Erica ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in spring. Erica ochroleuca thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
