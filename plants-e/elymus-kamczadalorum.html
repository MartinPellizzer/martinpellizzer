Elymus kamczadalorum

Elymus kamczadalorum, commonly known as the Kamchatka wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of the Russian Far East and northeastern China.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor nutrient availability. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it blooms in late summer.

Elymus kamczadalorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in marginal habitats.
