Erythraea ramosissima var. albiflora

Erythraea ramosissima var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered erythraea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
