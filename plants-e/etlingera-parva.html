Etlingera parva

Etlingera parva, commonly known as the dwarf torch ginger, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, bright red or orange flowers that resemble torches. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Etlingera parva is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
