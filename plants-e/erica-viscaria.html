Erica viscaria

Erica viscaria, commonly known as the sticky heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine, sticky resin, which gives it its common name.

Erica viscaria is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
