Eriophyllum pedatum

Eriophyllum pedatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldfields, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has thread-like leaves that are typically less than an inch long, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eriophyllum pedatum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
