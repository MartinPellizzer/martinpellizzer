Epiphyllum oxypetalum var. purpusii

Epiphyllum oxypetalum var. purpusii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that open at night and last only a few hours. These flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, sweet scent.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic nighttime blooms.
