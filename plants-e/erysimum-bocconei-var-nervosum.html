Erysimum bocconei var. nervosum

Erysimum bocconei var. nervosum, commonly known as the Sierra foothill erinaceum, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
