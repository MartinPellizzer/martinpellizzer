Eleocharis kuoi

Eleocharis kuoi, commonly known as the dwarf spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant typically found in wetland environments such as marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often submerged or floating. Eleocharis kuoi is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland biodiversity.
