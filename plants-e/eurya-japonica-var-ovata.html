Eurya japonica var. ovata

Eurya japonica var. ovata, commonly known as Japanese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and often covered in fine, waxy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Eurya japonica var. ovata is often used in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
