Erythrina polysperma

Erythrina polysperma, commonly known as the flame tree or red torch tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying a vibrant coloration that attracts pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are typically woody with a smooth, sometimes slightly hairy surface. Erythrina polysperma produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and urban landscapes.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
