Erica articulata

Erica articulata, commonly known as the articulated heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

Erica articulata is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
