Eriogonum parvifolium subsp. typicum

Eriogonum parvifolium subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the small-leaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pale yellow to white flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, oval seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
