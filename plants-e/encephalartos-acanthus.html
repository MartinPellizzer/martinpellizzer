Encephalartos acanthus

Encephalartos acanthus, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fan-shaped leaves that are bright green and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Encephalartos acanthus thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular ornamental plant in botanical gardens and private collections.
