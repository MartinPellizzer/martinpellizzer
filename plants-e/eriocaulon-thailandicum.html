Eriocaulon thailandicum

Eriocaulon thailandicum, commonly known as the Thai marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in its habitat.

Eriocaulon thailandicum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
