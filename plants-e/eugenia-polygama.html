Eugenia polygama

Eugenia polygama, commonly known as the African star gooseberry, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

Eugenia polygama is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
