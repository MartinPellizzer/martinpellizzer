Eria wildiana

Eria wildiana, commonly known as the wild orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its roots are often tuberous, and the leaves are elongated with a waxy surface.

Eria wildiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
