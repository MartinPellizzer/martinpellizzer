Eupatorium chaparense

Eupatorium chaparense, commonly known as Chaparral eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium chaparense is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional healing purposes.
