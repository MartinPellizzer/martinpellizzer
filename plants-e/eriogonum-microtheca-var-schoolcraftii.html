Eriogonum microtheca var. schoolcraftii

Eriogonum microtheca var. schoolcraftii, commonly known as the Schoolcraft's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub with gray-green foliage and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.
