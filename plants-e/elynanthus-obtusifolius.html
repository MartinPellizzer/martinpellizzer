Elynanthus obtusifolius

Elynanthus obtusifolius, commonly known as the blunt-leaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves with rounded leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of many legumes. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Elynanthus obtusifolius plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for wildlife.
