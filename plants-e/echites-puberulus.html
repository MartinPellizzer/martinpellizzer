Echites puberulus

Echites puberulus, commonly known as the hairy echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features slender stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, which give it its common name. Its small, yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Echites puberulus is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
