Erythrodes simplex

Erythrodes simplex, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark red or reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and requires a humid climate with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are small and greenish-white.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of its native ecosystems.
