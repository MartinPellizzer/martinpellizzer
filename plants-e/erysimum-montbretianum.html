Erysimum montbretianum

Erysimum montbretianum, commonly known as the Montbretian wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its flowers are bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
