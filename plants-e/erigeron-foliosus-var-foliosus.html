Erigeron foliosus var. foliosus

Erigeron foliosus var. foliosus, commonly known as the leafy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
