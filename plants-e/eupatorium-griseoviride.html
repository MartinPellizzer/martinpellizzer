Eupatorium griseoviride

Eupatorium griseoviride, commonly known as gray-green eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in wetland areas, such as marshes and swampy woodlands, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, silvery down.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
