Elaeocarpus villosiusculus

Elaeocarpus villosiusculus, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often with a reddish or brownish hue.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
