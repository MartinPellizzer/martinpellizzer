Echinospermum collinum

Echinospermum collinum, commonly known as the hill echinosperm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a rounded shape, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, growing in a basal rosette.

Echinospermum collinum is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
