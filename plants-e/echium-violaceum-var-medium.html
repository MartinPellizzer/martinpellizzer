Echium violaceum var. medium

Echium violaceum var. medium, commonly known as the medium blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
