Ericinella hillburttii

Ericinella hillburttii, commonly known as the Hillburtt's ericinella, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant typically features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Ericinella hillburttii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
