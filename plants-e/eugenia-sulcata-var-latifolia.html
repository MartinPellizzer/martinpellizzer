Eugenia sulcata var. latifolia

Eugenia sulcata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic fruits resembling small, round berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits in tropical gardens.
