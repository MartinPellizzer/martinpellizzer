Embelia tenuis

Embelia tenuis, commonly known as slender embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Embelia tenuis is often found in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in nutrient-poor soils.
