Erodium lucidum f. caerulescens

Erodium lucidum f. caerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered erodium, is a variant of the annual herb native to southern California.

It features delicate, blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely toothed, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to ecological restoration projects.
