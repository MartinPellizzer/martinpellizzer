Erianthus strictus

Erianthus strictus, commonly known as the narrow grass or tall grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. Erianthus strictus is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common component of grassland ecosystems.

Its fibrous structure and rapid growth make it both a useful forage plant and a potential invasive species in certain environments.
