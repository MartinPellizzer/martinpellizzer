Erysimum pallasii var. bracteosum

Erysimum pallasii var. bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often with distinctive bracts beneath the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to dry, well-drained environments.
