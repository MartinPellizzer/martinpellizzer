Ervum pubescens

Ervum pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves, which consist of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Ervum pubescens plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
