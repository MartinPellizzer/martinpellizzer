Epidendrum lowilliamsii

Epidendrum lowilliamsii, commonly known as the Lowilliams' epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within forests. The plant features elongated, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its inflorescence is usually terminal, with flowers arranged in a spike.

Epidendrum lowilliamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
