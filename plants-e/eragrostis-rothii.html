Eragrostis rothii

Eragrostis rothii, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a valuable component of native plant communities.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
