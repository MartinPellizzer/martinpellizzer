Erysimum lucae

Erysimum lucae, commonly known as the Lucae's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and the plant often thrives in rocky or coastal environments.

Erysimum lucae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
