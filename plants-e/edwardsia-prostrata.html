Edwardsia prostrata

Edwardsia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate edwardsia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant grows close to the ground, forming a dense mat of foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Edwardsia prostrata thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.
