Ervum meyeri

Ervum meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and disturbed soils. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Ervum meyeri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
