Euphorbia pseudoglobosa subsp. nesemannii

Euphorbia pseudoglobosa subsp.

nesemannii, commonly known as the Neuwied euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and a distinctive, globular shape that resembles a small ball.

The plant produces a milky sap, which is common in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
