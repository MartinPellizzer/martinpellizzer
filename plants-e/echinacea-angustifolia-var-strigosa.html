Echinacea angustifolia var. strigosa

Echinacea angustifolia var. strigosa, commonly known as narrowleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a striking, spiky purple flower head. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in supporting immune health.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
