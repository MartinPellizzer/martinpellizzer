Euryops linearis

Euryops linearis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Its linear, lance-shaped leaves are a distinguishing feature, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Euryops linearis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
