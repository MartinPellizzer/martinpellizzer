Episcia aurea

Episcia aurea, commonly known as the golden wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. Episcia aurea typically grows as a trailing or upright plant, producing small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
