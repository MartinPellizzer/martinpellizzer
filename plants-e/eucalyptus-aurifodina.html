Eucalyptus aurifodina

Eucalyptus aurifodina, commonly known as golden ash, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, ash-gray bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. Its growth habit is typically as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Eucalyptus aurifodina is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
