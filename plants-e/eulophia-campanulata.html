Eulophia campanulata

Eulophia campanulata, commonly known as the bell orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia campanulata thrives in warm, moist conditions and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
