Erythrina mairei

Erythrina mairei, commonly known as the flame tree or Maire's coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptical leaflets, and its seed pods are long, woody, and segmented. Erythrina mairei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
