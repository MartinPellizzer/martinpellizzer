Eupatorium drepanophyllum

Eupatorium drepanophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium drepanophyllum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
