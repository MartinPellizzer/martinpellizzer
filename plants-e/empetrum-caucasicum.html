Empetrum caucasicum

Empetrum caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, dark blue to black berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. The plant has compact, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, cushion-like formation. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

Empetrum caucasicum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small animals and insects.
