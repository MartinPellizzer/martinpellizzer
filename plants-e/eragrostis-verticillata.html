Eragrostis verticillata

Eragrostis verticillata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous narrow, pale green to brownish spikelets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Eragrostis verticillata is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and pastures, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability.
