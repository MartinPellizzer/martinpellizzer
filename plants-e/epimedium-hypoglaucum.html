Epimedium hypoglaucum

Epimedium hypoglaucum, commonly known as the barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodland environments and features attractive, shield-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose inflorescences. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in partial shade.

Epimedium hypoglaucum is also noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
