Erythrina lysistemon

Erythrina lysistemon, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical legume belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often exudes a reddish sap. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its vibrant flowers and rapid growth.

The seeds are enclosed in flat, woody pods and can remain viable for several years.
