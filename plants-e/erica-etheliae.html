Erica etheliae

Erica etheliae, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a species of shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region. Erica etheliae is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
