Erysimum delphinense

Erysimum delphinense, commonly known as the purple heart or sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and bright purple or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features pinnately divided, fern-like leaves and thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the shore. It is often found in maritime climates and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Erysimum delphinense is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
