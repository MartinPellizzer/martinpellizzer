Ericoides laetum

Ericoides laetum, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its aromatic, slightly sweet scent.

Ericoides laetum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
