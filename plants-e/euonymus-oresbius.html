Euonymus oresbius

Euonymus oresbius, commonly known as the red-berried euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by distinctive red berries that persist into winter. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant fall foliage and showy fruits.

Euonymus oresbius is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
