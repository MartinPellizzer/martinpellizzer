Elytrigia dasystachya

Elytrigia dasystachya, commonly known as the broad-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles that are broad and open, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers, typical of the Poaceae family, and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to a variety of soil types, making it a resilient species.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
