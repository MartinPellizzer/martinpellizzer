Euphorbia leuconeura

Euphorbia leuconeura, commonly known as the white-veined spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are often tinged with green or reddish hues. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have prominent white veins that give it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
