Eupatorium xylorrhizum

Eupatorium xylorrhizum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves and stems, and it often grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
