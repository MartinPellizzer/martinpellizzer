Eleocharis palustris f. italica

Eleocharis palustris f. italica, commonly known as the Italian spike-rush, is a wetland plant characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall resemblance to other members of the Cyperaceae family.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
