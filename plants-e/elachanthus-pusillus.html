Elachanthus pusillus

Elachanthus pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf elachanthus, is a small flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the family Elachanthaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, often blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as rocky slopes and grasslands, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white hair, which helps reduce water loss.

Elachanthus pusillus is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens due to its unique appearance.
