Eschscholzia rigida

Eschscholzia rigida, commonly known as the rigid poppy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive, saucer-shaped structure. The plant has slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, commonly found in dry, rocky habitats.

The rigid poppy is known for its hardy nature and ability to bloom in late summer and early fall.
