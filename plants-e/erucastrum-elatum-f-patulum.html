Erucastrum elatum f. patulum

Erucastrum elatum f. patulum, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological biodiversity.
