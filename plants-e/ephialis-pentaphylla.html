Ephialis pentaphylla

Ephialis pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed ephialis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink or white, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ephialis pentaphylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
