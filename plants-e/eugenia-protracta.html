Eugenia protracta

Eugenia protracta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in subtropical regions. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Eugenia protracta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
