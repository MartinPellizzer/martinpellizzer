Etlingera rosea

Etlingera rosea, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble torches, which are modified leaves that surround the small, yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
