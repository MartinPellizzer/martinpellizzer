Echites secundiflorus

Echites secundiflorus, commonly known as the slender ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Echites secundiflorus is also noted for its ability to thrive with minimal water, showcasing its resilience in extreme conditions.
