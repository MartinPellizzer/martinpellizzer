Erythronium utahense

Erythronium utahense, commonly known as Utah dogbane, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features a short, rhizomatous rootstock and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Erythronium utahense is adapted to withstand drought and temperature extremes, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
