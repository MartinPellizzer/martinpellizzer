Eremopyrum sibiricum var. desertorum

Eremopyrum sibiricum var. desertorum, commonly known as the desert feathergrass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry steppe and desert environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in degraded landscapes.
