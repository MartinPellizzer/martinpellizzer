Euphorbia schickendantzii

Euphorbia schickendantzii, commonly known as Schickendantz's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often the only visible part of the inflorescence. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to propagate through both seeds and offsets.

Euphorbia schickendantzii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
