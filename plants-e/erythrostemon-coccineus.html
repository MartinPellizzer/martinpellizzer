Erythrostemon coccineus

Erythrostemon coccineus, commonly known as the scarlet lily or red lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Erythrostemon coccineus is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to arid environments.
