Erythroseris somalensis

Erythroseris somalensis, commonly known as the Somali bloodroot, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive red-orange flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has a short, hairy stem and narrow, linear leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
