Epilobium parviflorum var. lusitanicum

Epilobium parviflorum var. lusitanicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
