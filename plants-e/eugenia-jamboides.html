Eugenia jamboides

Eugenia jamboides, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
