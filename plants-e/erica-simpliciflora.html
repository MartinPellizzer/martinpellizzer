Erica simpliciflora

Erica simpliciflora, commonly known as the simple-flowered heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. It is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense mats in its natural habitat.

Erica simpliciflora is well adapted to nutrient-poor environments and requires minimal water once established.
