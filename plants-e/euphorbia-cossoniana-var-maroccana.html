Euphorbia cossoniana var. maroccana

Euphorbia cossoniana var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan spurge, is a succulent plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and a distinctive crown of yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
