Eupatorium oxyhedrum

Eupatorium oxyhedrum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Eupatorium oxyhedrum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
