Eriosoriopsis

Eriosoriopsis, commonly known as a type of rust fungus, is a genus of parasitic fungi in the family Eriosoriaceae.

These fungi primarily infect plants, causing rust diseases characterized by orange or reddish pustules on the leaves. Eriosoriopsis species are known for their complex life cycles, often involving multiple host plants. They produce spores in structures called uredinia, which can spread through wind or water.

The genus is of particular interest in plant pathology due to its impact on agricultural crops and natural ecosystems.
