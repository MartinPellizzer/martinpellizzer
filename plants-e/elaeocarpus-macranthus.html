Elaeocarpus macranthus

Elaeocarpus macranthus, commonly known as the large-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are large, woody capsules that split open to release small, dark seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
