Erodium guttatum f. subacaule

Erodium guttatum f. subacaule, commonly known as the slender erodium, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, erect stems and small, ovate leaves with glandular dots that secrete a sticky substance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
