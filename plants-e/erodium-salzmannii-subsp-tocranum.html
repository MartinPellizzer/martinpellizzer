Erodium salzmannii subsp. tocranum

Erodium salzmannii subsp.

tocranum, commonly known as the Cretan catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Greece and Turkey. It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
