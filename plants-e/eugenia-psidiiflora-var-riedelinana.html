Eugenia psidiiflora var. riedelinana

Eugenia psidiiflora var. riedelinana, commonly known as the Riedelina eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
