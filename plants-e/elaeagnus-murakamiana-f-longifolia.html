Elaeagnus murakamiana f. longifolia

Elaeagnus murakamiana f. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf silverberry, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically elongated and have a leathery texture, while the flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
