Eria ferruginea var. assamica

Eria ferruginea var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam iron orchid, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, metallic-green leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on tree trunks or rocks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural range.
