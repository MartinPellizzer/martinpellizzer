Escallonia harrisii

Escallonia harrisii, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to saline and coastal conditions, making it a hardy species for seaside gardens. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Escallonia harrisii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging coastal environments.
