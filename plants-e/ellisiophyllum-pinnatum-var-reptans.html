Ellisiophyllum pinnatum var. reptans

Ellisiophyllum pinnatum var. reptans, commonly known as the creeping fern, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive fronds and growth habit.

It is a perennial plant that thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a creeping rhizome system, allowing it to spread horizontally and form dense mats. Its fronds are pinnately divided, with a delicate, feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to humid conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
