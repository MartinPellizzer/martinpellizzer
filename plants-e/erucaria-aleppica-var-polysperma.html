Erucaria aleppica var. polysperma

Erucaria aleppica var. polysperma, commonly known as the Aleppo thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in late spring. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and a flowering period that supports pollinators.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
