Echinocactus joossensianus

Echinocactus joossensianus, commonly known as the Joossensian cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a subtle bluish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
