Erythraea maritima var. occidentalis

Erythraea maritima var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sea holly, is a perennial plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, hairy leaves and striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and strong winds. It plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for maritime gardens.
