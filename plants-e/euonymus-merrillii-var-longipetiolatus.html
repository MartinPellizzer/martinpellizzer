Euonymus merrillii var. longipetiolatus

Euonymus merrillii var. longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic value in landscaped settings.
