Elaeocarpus renae

Elaeocarpus renae, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The tree produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, persistent outer layer, which is typical of the genus Elaeocarpus.

This species is often found in humid, shaded environments within mountainous regions.
