Eucalyptus × rubricaulis

Eucalyptus × rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed gum, is a hybrid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 8 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The flowers are usually pale green to yellow, and the plant is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and medicinal significance.
