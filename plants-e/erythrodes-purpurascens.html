Erythrodes purpurascens

Erythrodes purpurascens, commonly known as the red-flowered erythrodes, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and purple flowers, which are clustered in showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are slender and somewhat woody.

Erythrodes purpurascens is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
