Eupatorium quadriflorum

Eupatorium quadriflorum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

Eupatorium quadriflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
