Epidendrum scabrum

Epidendrum scabrum, commonly known as the rough epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a rough, scaly texture, giving it its common name.

Epidendrum scabrum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
