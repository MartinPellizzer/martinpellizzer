Eulophia pardalina

Eulophia pardalina, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eulophia pardalina is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
