Eupatorium sinclairii

Eupatorium sinclairii, commonly known as Sinclair's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open woodlands and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Sinclair's boneset is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
