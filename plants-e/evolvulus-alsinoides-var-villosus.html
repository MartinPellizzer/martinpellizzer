Evolvulus alsinoides var. villosus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. villosus, commonly known as hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
