Erica cymosa subsp. cymosa

Erica cymosa subsp.

cymosa, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It is a low-growing shrub with evergreen leaves that are narrow and leathery. Erica cymosa subsp.

cymosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
