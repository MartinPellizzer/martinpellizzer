Ehrharta tricostata

Ehrharta tricostata, commonly known as common lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Ehrharta tricostata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
