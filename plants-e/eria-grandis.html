Eria grandis

Eria grandis, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Eria grandis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
