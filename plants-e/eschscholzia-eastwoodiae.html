Eschscholzia eastwoodiae

Eschscholzia eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
