Eucalyptus cyathiformis

Eucalyptus cyathiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and small, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are usually pale green or yellow, which later open into white or cream flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in heathlands or open woodlands.

Eucalyptus cyathiformis is adapted to withstand drought and is sometimes used in reforestation projects due to its resilience.
