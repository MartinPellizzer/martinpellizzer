Erica victoria

Erica victoria, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.

Erica victoria is also known for its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
