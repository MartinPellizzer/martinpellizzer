Euonymus japonicus

Euonymus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese spindle tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan and Korea.

It features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a landscape plant.
