Eugenia lucidula

Eugenia lucidula, commonly known as the shining eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

Eugenia lucidula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
