Epipremnum mooreense

Epipremnum mooreense, commonly known as the Moore's ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a glossy texture. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative foliage plant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually hidden within a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Epipremnum mooreense is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
