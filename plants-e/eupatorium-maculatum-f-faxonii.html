Eupatorium maculatum f. faxonii

Eupatorium maculatum f. faxonii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a cultivar of the Eupatorium genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
