Erodium laciniatum var. bovei

Erodium laciniatum var. bovei, commonly known as the Bove's narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often finely toothed. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or purple flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of Mediterranean and temperate climates.
