Ericoides claviflorum

Ericoides claviflorum, commonly known as the clubflower heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in early spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides claviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
