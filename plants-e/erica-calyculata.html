Erica calyculata

Erica calyculata, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and summer. Erica calyculata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
