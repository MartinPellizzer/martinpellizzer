Enneapogon elegans

Enneapogon elegans, commonly known as the elegant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Enneapogon elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
