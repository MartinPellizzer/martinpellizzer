Erigeron uralensis

Erigeron uralensis, commonly known as the Siberian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the colder regions of Eurasia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
