Eupatorium oxylepis subsp. paraguariense

Eupatorium oxylepis subsp.

paraguariense, commonly known as Paraguayan boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in humid and tropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical inflorescences.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
