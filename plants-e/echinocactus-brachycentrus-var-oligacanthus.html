Echinocactus brachycentrus var. oligacanthus

Echinocactus brachycentrus var. oligacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a compact, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and is characterized by its dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
