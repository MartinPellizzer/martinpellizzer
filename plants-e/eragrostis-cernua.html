Eragrostis cernua

Eragrostis cernua, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense ground cover.
