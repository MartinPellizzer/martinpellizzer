Erica coccinea

Erica coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant red blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica coccinea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
