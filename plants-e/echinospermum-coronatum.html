Echinospermum coronatum

Echinospermum coronatum, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that resemble a crown, hence its common name. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it produces small, spiny fruits called cypselae. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Echinospermum coronatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
