Erigeron laetevirens

Erigeron laetevirens, commonly known as the cheerful blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its blue to lavender flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
