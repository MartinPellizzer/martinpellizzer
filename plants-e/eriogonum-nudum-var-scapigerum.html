Eriogonum nudum var. scapigerum

Eriogonum nudum var. scapigerum, commonly known as the scapigerous buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually leafless, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

This variety is part of the large genus Eriogonum, which includes many species of flowering plants commonly found in western North America.
