Eugenia chacteana

Eugenia chacteana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
