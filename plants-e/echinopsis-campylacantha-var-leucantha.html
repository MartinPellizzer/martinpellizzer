Echinopsis campylacantha var. leucantha

Echinopsis campylacantha var. leucantha, commonly known as the white-topped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale spines, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
