Eremophila glabra var. viridiflora

Eremophila glabra var. viridiflora, commonly known as the greenflower eremophila, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually greenish-yellow, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in native gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
