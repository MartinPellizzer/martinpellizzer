Eriosema tephrosioides var. tephrosioides

Eriosema tephrosioides var. tephrosioides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers that are often followed by distinctive, spiny fruit pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
