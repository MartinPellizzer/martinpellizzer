Echinospermum omphaloides

Echinospermum omphaloides, commonly known as the star daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-centered flower heads that resemble miniature sunflowers. The plant produces achenes, which are small, dry fruits with a single seed, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are typically alternate, hairy, and have a distinctive lobed shape.

Echinospermum omphaloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
