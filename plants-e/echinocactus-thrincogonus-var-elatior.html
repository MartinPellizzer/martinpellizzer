Echinocactus thrincogonus var. elatior

Echinocactus thrincogonus var. elatior, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing at the top of the plant during the summer months.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
