Eleutherococcus sieboldianus f. variegatus

Eleutherococcus sieboldianus f. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated Siberian ginseng, is a rare and ornamental variant of the Eleutherococcus genus.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, with white and green patterns on its leaves. Native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, this plant thrives in cool, shaded environments. It is valued for its aesthetic appeal and has been cultivated for its unique leaf coloration.

Despite its beauty, it is relatively uncommon in horticultural trade due to its specific growing requirements.
