Elaeocarpus chrysophyllus

Elaeocarpus chrysophyllus, commonly known as the golden-leaf elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
