Ericameria pinifolia

Ericameria pinifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance and helps it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ericameria pinifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species well-suited for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
