Eleocharis monticola var. laeviseta

Eleocharis monticola var. laeviseta, commonly known as the silky spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale brown flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife.
