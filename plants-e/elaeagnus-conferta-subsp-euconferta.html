Elaeagnus conferta subsp. euconferta

Elaeagnus conferta subsp.

euconferta, commonly known as silver leaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
