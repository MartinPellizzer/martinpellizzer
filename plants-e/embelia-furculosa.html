Embelia furculosa

Embelia furculosa, commonly known as the white-flowered embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
