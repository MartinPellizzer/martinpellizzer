Eupatorium betonicum

Eupatorium betonicum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium betonicum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
