Equisetum arvense f. ramulosum

Equisetum arvense f. ramulosum, commonly known as the creeping or field horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. This fern is typically found in moist, disturbed soils, often along roadsides or in wetlands. Its reproductive structures, called strobila, are cone-like and contain spores.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
