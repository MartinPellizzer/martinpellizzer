Epilobium canum subsp. garrettii

Epilobium canum subsp.

garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's willowherb, is a perennial herb native to North America. It typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks.

The plant has slender, ascending stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late summer.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
