Eurya nitida f. roseaflora

Eurya nitida f. roseaflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. The flowers are typically pink or white, adding ornamental value to the plant. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely on the stems.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
