Elaeagnus spadicea

Elaeagnus spadicea, commonly known as the silver leaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus spadicea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
