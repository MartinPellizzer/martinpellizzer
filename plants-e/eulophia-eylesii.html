Eulophia eylesii

Eulophia eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia eylesii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
