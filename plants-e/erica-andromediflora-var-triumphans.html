Erica andromediflora var. triumphans

Erica andromediflora var. triumphans, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardiness and attractive appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
