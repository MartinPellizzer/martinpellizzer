Erysimum meyerianum

Erysimum meyerianum, commonly known as the Meyer's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and often branched. Its flowers are bright yellow and bloom in early spring, producing a showy display.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
