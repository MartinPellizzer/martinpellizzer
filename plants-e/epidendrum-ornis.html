Epidendrum ornis

Epidendrum ornis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant has long, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that help it absorb moisture from the air. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Epidendrum ornis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in many regions for its aesthetic appeal.
