Eugenia myrtoides

Eugenia myrtoides, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Following flowering, it produces small, round, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
