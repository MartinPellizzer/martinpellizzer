Euphorbia × wimmeriana

Euphorbia × wimmeriana, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia × wimmeriana is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
