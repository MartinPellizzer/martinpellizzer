Eucalyptus aspratilis

Eucalyptus aspratilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional and modern medicinal applications.
