Eugenioides versicolor

Eugenioides versicolor, commonly known as the variable eugenia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pink, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenioides versicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
