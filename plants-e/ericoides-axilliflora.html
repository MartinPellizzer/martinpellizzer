Ericoides axilliflora

Ericoides axilliflora, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, providing a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Ericoides axilliflora is an important component of coastal heathland ecosystems, supporting a variety of native wildlife.
