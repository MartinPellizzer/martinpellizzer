Eremophila spuria

Eremophila spuria, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, often arranged in spikes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Eremophila spuria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
