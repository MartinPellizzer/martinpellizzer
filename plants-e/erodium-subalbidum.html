Erodium subalbidum

Erodium subalbidum, commonly known as the pale weed or silverweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and grasslands.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
