Eragrostis parviglumis

Eragrostis parviglumis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It is an annual or biennial herb that thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in arid environments.
