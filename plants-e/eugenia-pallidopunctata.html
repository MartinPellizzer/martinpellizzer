Eugenia pallidopunctata

Eugenia pallidopunctata, commonly known as the pale-spotted eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, pale dots. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
