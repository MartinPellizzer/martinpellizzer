Ericoides speciosum

Ericoides speciosum, commonly known as the showy eric, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value to gardens.

Ericoides speciosum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed areas.
