Encephalartos plumosus

Encephalartos plumosus, commonly known as the silver rosette cycad, is a species of cycad native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosette of glossy, bluish-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish male cones and large, spiky female cones, typical of cycads. It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
