Erythrophysa alata

Erythrophysa alata, commonly known as the winged acacia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, compound leaves that are often winged or flattened, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to dry climates.

The species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
