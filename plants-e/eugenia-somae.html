Eugenia somae

Eugenia somae, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
