Echinocereus canus

Echinocereus canus, commonly known as the white-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Echinocereus canus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
