Ericoides pulchellum

Ericoides pulchellum, commonly known as the showy erica, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in the spring.

Ericoides pulchellum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
