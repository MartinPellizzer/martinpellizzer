Elaeagnus schnabeliana

Elaeagnus schnabeliana, commonly known as the Schnebel's silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or open woodlands.

Elaeagnus schnabeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
