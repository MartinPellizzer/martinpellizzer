Eriosema parviflorum

Eriosema parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are trifoliate, with hairy surfaces that provide a distinctive tactile texture.

Eriosema parviflorum is often found in grassy areas and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
