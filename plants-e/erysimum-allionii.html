Erysimum allionii

Erysimum allionii, commonly known as the Allion's wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erysimum allionii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
