Euonymus oxyphyllus var. kuenbuergia

Euonymus oxyphyllus var. kuenbuergia, commonly known as the Kuenburg's euonymus, is a shrub native to China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is relatively low maintenance and suitable for use in gardens or as a decorative plant in containers.
