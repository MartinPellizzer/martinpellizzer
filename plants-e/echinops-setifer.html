Echinops setifer

Echinops setifer, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a goat's beard, hence its common name. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Echinops setifer is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
