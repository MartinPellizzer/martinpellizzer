Epidendrum moronense

Epidendrum moronense, commonly known as the orange-flowered orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of bright orange or red flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Epidendrum moronense is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
