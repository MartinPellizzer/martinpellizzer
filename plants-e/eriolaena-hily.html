Eriolaena hily

Eriolaena hily, commonly known as the silky hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

The silky hibiscus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
