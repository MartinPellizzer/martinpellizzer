Euphorbia cynanchoides

Euphorbia cynanchoides, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features a branching stem system with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, reducing water loss and deterring herbivores.

Euphorbia cynanchoides is adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
