Eupatorium subtriplinerve

Eupatorium subtriplinerve, commonly known as the three-veined eupatorium, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that have three prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in paniculate inflorescences, and typically bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and woodland edges.
