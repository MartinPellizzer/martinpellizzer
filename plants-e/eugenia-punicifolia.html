Eugenia punicifolia

Eugenia punicifolia, commonly known as the sour orange or punicifolia myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically sour and acidic, often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Eugenia punicifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its aromatic properties.
