Epipactis cretica

Epipactis cretica, commonly known as the Crete orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Crete and other islands.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color. Epipactis cretica typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.

It is a relatively rare and specialized species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
