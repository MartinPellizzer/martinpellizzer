Eulalia ciliata

Eulalia ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or silky needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with fine, silky hairs along the edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Eulalia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
