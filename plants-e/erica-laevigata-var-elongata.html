Erica laevigata var. elongata

Erica laevigata var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
