Elaeagnus multiflora var. arakiana

Elaeagnus multiflora var. arakiana, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
