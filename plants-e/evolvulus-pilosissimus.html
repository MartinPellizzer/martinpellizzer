Evolvulus pilosissimus

Evolvulus pilosissimus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardens and trellises.
