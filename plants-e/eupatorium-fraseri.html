Eupatorium fraseri

Eupatorium fraseri, commonly known as Frasier's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium fraseri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
