Eucalyptus jutsonii subsp. jutsonii

Eucalyptus jutsonii subsp.

jutsonii, commonly known as the Jutson's mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to western Australia. It typically grows in sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features smooth, often fissured bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, silky hair. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role and is often used in rehabilitation projects due to its resilience and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.
