Eugenia stirpiflora

Eugenia stirpiflora, commonly known as the swamp myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in wet, swampy areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Eugenia stirpiflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
