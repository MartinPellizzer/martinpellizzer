Echium amoenum

Echium amoenum, commonly known as the blueweed or the common thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium amoenum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
