Echinops neumayeri

Echinops neumayeri, commonly known as the Neumayer's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny bracts. The plant is known for its tall, upright growth habit and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Echinops neumayeri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
