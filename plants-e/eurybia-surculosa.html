Eurybia surculosa

Eurybia surculosa, commonly known as the showy golden aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and produces numerous small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head.

Eurybia surculosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
