Epacris microphylla var. rhombifolia

Epacris microphylla var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
