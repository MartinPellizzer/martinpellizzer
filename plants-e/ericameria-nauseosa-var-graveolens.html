Ericameria nauseosa var. graveolens

Ericameria nauseosa var. graveolens, commonly known as bitterleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that emit a strong, often disagreeable odor. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves and stems contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional and modern herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
