Ericoides elegans

Ericoides elegans, commonly known as the elegant eric, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands or rocky areas.

Ericoides elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
