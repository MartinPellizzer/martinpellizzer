Erythrodes lacteola

Erythrodes lacteola, commonly known as the milky milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that are covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are elongated, opposite, and have a distinctive milky sap when crushed. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and typically range in color from pale yellow to orange.

The plant is part of the milkweed family (Asclepiadaceae) and plays a role in supporting certain pollinators.
