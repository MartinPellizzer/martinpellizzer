Ericoides pubigerum

Ericoides pubigerum, commonly known as the hairy eric, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its hairy leaves and compact growth habit make it a distinctive species within the genus Ericoides.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
