Eupatorium ivifolium var. foliosum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. foliosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants for wet areas.
