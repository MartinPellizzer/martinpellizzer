Elaeocarpus kirtonii

Elaeocarpus kirtonii, commonly known as the Kirton's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
