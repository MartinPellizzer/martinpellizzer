Erythrodes selbyana

Erythrodes selbyana, commonly known as the Selby's red-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking red veins on its leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple markings, and it blooms during the rainy season.
