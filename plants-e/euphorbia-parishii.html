Euphorbia parishii

Euphorbia parishii, commonly known as the Paraguay euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often spine-tipped, adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures typical of the genus.

Euphorbia parishii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry conditions.
