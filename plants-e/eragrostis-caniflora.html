Eragrostis caniflora

Eragrostis caniflora, commonly known as the sharpleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often sharply pointed. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Eragrostis caniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
