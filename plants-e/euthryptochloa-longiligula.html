Euthryptochloa longiligula

Euthryptochloa longiligula, commonly known as the slender-leaf grass, is a species of grass native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, elongated leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Due to its ornamental and ecological value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
