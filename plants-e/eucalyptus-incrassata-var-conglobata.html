Eucalyptus incrassata var. conglobata

Eucalyptus incrassata var. conglobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are often yellow or cream in hue. It is a hardy shrub or small tree that thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
