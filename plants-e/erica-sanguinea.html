Erica sanguinea

Erica sanguinea, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Erica sanguinea typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant red or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
