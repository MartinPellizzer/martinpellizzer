Eugenia caipora

Eugenia caipora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or caipora, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Eugenia caipora thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a significant plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
