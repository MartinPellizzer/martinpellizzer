Eragrostis benthamii

Eragrostis benthamii, commonly known as bentham's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, pale green to brown flowers. It is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eragrostis benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
