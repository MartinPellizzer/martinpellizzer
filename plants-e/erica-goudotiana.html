Erica goudotiana

Erica goudotiana, commonly known as the Goudot's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense mats on coastal cliffs and mountain slopes.

Erica goudotiana is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to coastal salt spray.
