Eupatorium houstonianum

Eupatorium houstonianum, commonly known as Texas feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in fine, hairy tissue. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called cymes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
