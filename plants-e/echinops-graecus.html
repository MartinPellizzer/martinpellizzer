Echinops graecus

Echinops graecus, commonly known as the Greek thistle or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops graecus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
