Elaeocarpus commutatus

Elaeocarpus commutatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a drupe, often containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, colorful aril.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic features.
