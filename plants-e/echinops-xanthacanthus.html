Echinops xanthacanthus

Echinops xanthacanthus, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that turn yellow with age. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of the distinctive echinoid (spiky) fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Echinops xanthacanthus is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
