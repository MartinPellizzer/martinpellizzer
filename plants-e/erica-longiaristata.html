Erica longiaristata

Erica longiaristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and its flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

Erica longiaristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
