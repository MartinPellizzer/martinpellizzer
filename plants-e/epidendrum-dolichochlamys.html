Epidendrum dolichochlamys

Epidendrum dolichochlamys, commonly known as the long-lipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with flowers that have a distinctive elongated lip, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum dolichochlamys is often cultivated for its vibrant colors and adaptability to various growing conditions.
