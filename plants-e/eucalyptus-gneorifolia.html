Eucalyptus gneorifolia

Eucalyptus gneorifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited to arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
