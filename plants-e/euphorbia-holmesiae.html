Euphorbia holmesiae

Euphorbia holmesiae, commonly known as Holmes' spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic members. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable species in botanical studies.
