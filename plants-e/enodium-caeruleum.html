Enodium caeruleum

Enodium caeruleum, commonly known as blue thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has silvery-gray leaves covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
