Euphorbia lipskyi

Euphorbia lipskyi, commonly known as the Lipsky's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be toxic. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Euphorbia lipskyi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
