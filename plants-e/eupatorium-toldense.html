Eupatorium toldense

Eupatorium toldense, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Eupatorium toldense is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
