Eryngium marocanum

Eryngium marocanum, commonly known as the Moroccan thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features spiny, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eryngium marocanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
