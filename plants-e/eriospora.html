Eriospora

Eriospora, commonly known as the "fuzzy spore" plant, is a genus of fungi that primarily produces spores in a unique, powdery form.

These spores are often covered in a fine, cotton-like coating, which aids in their dispersal by wind. The genus is most commonly associated with plant pathogens, particularly those that affect crops such as beans and tomatoes. Eriospora species are characterized by their small, dark, and often clustered spores.

Botanically, they are classified as ascomycetes, a group of fungi that produce spores within sac-like structures called ascus.
