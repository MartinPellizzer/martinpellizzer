Epidendrum armeniacum

Epidendrum armeniacum, commonly known as the Armenian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas and thrives in cool, humid environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Epidendrum armeniacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
