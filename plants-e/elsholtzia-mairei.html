Elsholtzia mairei

Elsholtzia mairei, commonly known as Maire's elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often aromatic and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
