Eremurus velutinus

Eremurus velutinus, commonly known as the velvet eremurus, is a striking member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence composed of numerous yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces large, fibrous roots and has a distinctive, hairy texture on its leaves and stems, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Eremurus velutinus is typically dormant during the winter and produces its showy blooms in the early summer.
