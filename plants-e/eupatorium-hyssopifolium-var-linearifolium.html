Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. linearifolium

Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. linearifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has upright stems that are often branched. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple. It is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
