Elaeocarpus seringii

Elaeocarpus seringii, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
