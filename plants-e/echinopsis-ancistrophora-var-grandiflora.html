Echinopsis ancistrophora var. grandiflora

Echinopsis ancistrophora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant night bloomer, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Its botanical features make it a striking and unique addition to desert gardens.
