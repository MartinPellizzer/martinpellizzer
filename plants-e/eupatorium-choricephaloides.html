Eupatorium choricephaloides

Eupatorium choricephaloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Eupatorium choricephaloides is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
