Euphorbia × gusuleacii

Euphorbia × gusuleacii, commonly known as the hybrid spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rocky landscapes.
