Erythrina humeana

Erythrina humeana, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in the early rainy season. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is typically smooth with a reddish or brownish hue. Erythrina humeana is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and birds.
