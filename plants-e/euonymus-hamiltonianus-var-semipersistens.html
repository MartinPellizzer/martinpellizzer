Euonymus hamiltonianus var. semipersistens

Euonymus hamiltonianus var. semipersistens, commonly known as the evergreen euonymus, is a shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that remain green throughout the year, providing year-round foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This variety is particularly valued in landscaping for its dense growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
