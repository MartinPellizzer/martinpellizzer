Eritrichium strictum var. thomsonii

Eritrichium strictum var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's strict eritrichium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate or are slightly ascending. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
