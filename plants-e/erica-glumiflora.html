Erica glumiflora

Erica glumiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica glumiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
