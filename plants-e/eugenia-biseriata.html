Eugenia biseriata

Eugenia biseriata, commonly known as the two-row eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Eugenia biseriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
