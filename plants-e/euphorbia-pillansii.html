Euphorbia pillansii

Euphorbia pillansii, commonly known as the Pillans' spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny leaves, which help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia pillansii is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, allowing it to thrive in regions with low rainfall.
