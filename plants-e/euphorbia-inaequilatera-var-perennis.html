Euphorbia inaequilatera var. perennis

Euphorbia inaequilatera var. perennis, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care. Its leaves are usually thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, and it produces a unique type of flower called a cyathium.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably under suitable conditions.
