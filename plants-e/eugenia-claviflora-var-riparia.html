Eugenia claviflora var. riparia

Eugenia claviflora var. riparia, commonly known as the river guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet, and aromatic fruits. Its fruit is typically yellow or orange when ripe and is often used in culinary applications.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
