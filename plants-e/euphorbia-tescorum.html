Euphorbia tescorum

Euphorbia tescorum, commonly known as the Italian spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe.

It features a low-growing, branched stem covered in short, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Euphorbia tescorum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
