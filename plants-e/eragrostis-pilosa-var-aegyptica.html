Eragrostis pilosa var. aegyptica

Eragrostis pilosa var. aegyptica, commonly known as Egyptian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered in dense, tufted arrangements. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
