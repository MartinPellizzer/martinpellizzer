Elaeodendron subrotundum

Elaeodendron subrotundum, commonly known as the round-leaved elaeodendron, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, rounded leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a common feature in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
