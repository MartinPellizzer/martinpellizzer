Erigeron scopulinus

Erigeron scopulinus, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron scopulinus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native range.
