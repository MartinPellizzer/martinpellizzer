Epidendrum neolehmannia

Epidendrum neolehmannia, commonly known as the Lemon-scented Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive lemon scent. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
