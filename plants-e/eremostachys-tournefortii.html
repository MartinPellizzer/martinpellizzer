Eremostachys tournefortii

Eremostachys tournefortii, commonly known as the desert harebell, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
