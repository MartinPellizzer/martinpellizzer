Eschscholzia ptarmicoides

Eschscholzia ptarmicoides, commonly known as the Pacific Coast poppy, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on long, slender stems and are often clustered at the tips.

Eschscholzia ptarmicoides is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
