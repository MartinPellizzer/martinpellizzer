Eucalyptus glandulosa

Eucalyptus glandulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, glandular dots. The plant typically grows in shrubland and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its aromatic oils.

Eucalyptus glandulosa is an important component of the Australian flora and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
