Euphorbia holochlorina

Euphorbia holochlorina, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Euphorbia holochlorina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
