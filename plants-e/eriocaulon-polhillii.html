Eriocaulon polhillii

Eriocaulon polhillii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya and Tanzania. This plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white flowers. It typically grows in wet, marshy habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value in moist garden environments.
