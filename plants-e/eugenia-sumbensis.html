Eugenia sumbensis

Eugenia sumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia sumbensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in moist, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, edible fruits resembling cherries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance in its native habitat.
