Eugenia arthroopoda var. elatipes

Eugenia arthroopoda var. elatipes, commonly known as the slender waxleaf eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
