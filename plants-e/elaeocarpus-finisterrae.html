Elaeocarpus finisterrae

Elaeocarpus finisterrae, commonly known as the Socotra ebony, is a unique plant native to the island of Socotra.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its distinctive, elongated leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
