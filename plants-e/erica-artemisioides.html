Erica artemisioides

Erica artemisioides, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with a silvery-blue hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica artemisioides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
