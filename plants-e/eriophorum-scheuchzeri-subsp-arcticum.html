Eriophorum scheuchzeri subsp. arcticum

Eriophorum scheuchzeri subsp.

arcticum, commonly known as Arctic cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white flower heads that appear in late summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms mats or cushions to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in cold, nutrient-poor habitats.

Its presence is often an indicator of tundra or subarctic ecosystems.
