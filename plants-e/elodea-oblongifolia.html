Elodea oblongifolia

Elodea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in the Americas.

It has long, slender stems that often branch and are covered with small, oval-shaped leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, greenish flowers that are typically found in clusters near the water surface. Elodea oblongifolia is known for its rapid growth and is often used in aquariums and ponds for water oxygenation.

Its leaves are often used in scientific studies due to their suitability for microscopic observation.
