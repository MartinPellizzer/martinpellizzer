Eugenia claviflora var. oblongifolia

Eugenia claviflora var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or oblong-leaved guava, is a species native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications in some regions.
