Eremophila gracillima

Eremophila gracillima, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited for dry environments. It produces small, edible seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eremophila gracillima is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
