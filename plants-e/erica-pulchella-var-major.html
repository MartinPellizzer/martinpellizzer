Erica pulchella var. major

Erica pulchella var. major, commonly known as the greater heather, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers in spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica pulchella var. major is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
