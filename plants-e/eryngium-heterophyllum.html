Eryngium heterophyllum

Eryngium heterophyllum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-purple flower heads that resemble those of thistles. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eryngium heterophyllum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
