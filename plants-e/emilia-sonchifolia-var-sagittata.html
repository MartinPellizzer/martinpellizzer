Emilia sonchifolia var. sagittata

Emilia sonchifolia var. sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf emilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrowhead-shaped leaves and yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The leaves are hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, while the flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
