Erica riparia

Erica riparia, commonly known as the river heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. Erica riparia is well adapted to wet environments and can tolerate periodic flooding.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective plant for stabilizing riverbanks and preventing erosion.
