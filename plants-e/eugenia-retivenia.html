Eugenia retivenia

Eugenia retivenia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or candeia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red or purple fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber and has traditional uses in folk medicine.
