Euphrasia atropurpurea

Euphrasia atropurpurea, commonly known as purple-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, delicate flowers with distinctive purple eyespots. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
