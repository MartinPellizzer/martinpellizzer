Elymus violaceus

Elymus violaceus, commonly known as purple wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, tufted growth habit, often forming clumps that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces panicles of small, violet-tinged florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a rough texture that aids in reducing water loss in arid environments.

Elymus violaceus is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in open meadows and along rocky slopes.
