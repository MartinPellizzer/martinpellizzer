Elsholtzia lampradena

Elsholtzia lampradena, commonly known as the golden elsholtzia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Elsholtzia lampradena is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
