Eriope macrostachya var. platanthera

Eriope macrostachya var. platanthera, commonly known as the broad-leaved eriope, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that can hold water, a feature common in bromeliads. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with vibrant red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hairy structures that aid in water retention and provide some protection against herbivores.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
