Elleanthus caveroi

Elleanthus caveroi, commonly known as the Cavernous Bluebell, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to the mountainous regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and evergreen, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of moisture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
