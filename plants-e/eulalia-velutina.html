Eulalia velutina

Eulalia velutina, commonly known as the velvetgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety texture on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

Eulalia velutina plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
