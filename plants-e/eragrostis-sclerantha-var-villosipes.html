Eragrostis sclerantha var. villosipes

Eragrostis sclerantha var. villosipes, commonly known as the hairy eragrostis, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, wiry foliage with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of bracteate, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
