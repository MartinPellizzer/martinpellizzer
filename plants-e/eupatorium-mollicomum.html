Eupatorium mollicomum

Eupatorium mollicomum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, soft hairs.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
