Euphorbia portulacoides var. glaucescens

Euphorbia portulacoides var. glaucescens, commonly known as the bluish euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, low-growing form with fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental foliage make it a desirable addition to arid landscape designs.
