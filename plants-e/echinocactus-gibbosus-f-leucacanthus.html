Echinocactus gibbosus f. leucacanthus

Echinocactus gibbosus f. leucacanthus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the standard echinocactus.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white spines, which distinguish it from the more typical green spines of the parent species. The plant features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure, typical of many cacti in the Cactaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists.
