Eriobotrya luzoniensis

Eriobotrya luzoniensis, commonly known as the Luzon loquat, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by yellow-orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit is edible, though it is not as widely cultivated as other loquat species.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.
