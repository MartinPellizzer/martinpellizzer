Echinops aucheri f. myriocephalus

Echinops aucheri f. myriocephalus, commonly known as the Persian globemallow, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of blue-gray bracts that resemble small globes, giving it a unique and eye-catching appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in the summer, which are borne on tall, spiny stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and architectural form make it a valuable addition to xeriscapic gardens.
