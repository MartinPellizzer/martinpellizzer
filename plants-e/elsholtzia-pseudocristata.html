Elsholtzia pseudocristata

Elsholtzia pseudocristata, commonly known as the Chinese germander, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically purple or pink and bloom in summer.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a ornamental plant due to its fragrant foliage.
