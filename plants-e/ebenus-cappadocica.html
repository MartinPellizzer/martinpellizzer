Ebenus cappadocica

Ebenus cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian thornvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including parts of Turkey. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Ebenus cappadocica is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
