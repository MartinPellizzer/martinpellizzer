Euphorbia articulata var. bindloensis

Euphorbia articulata var. bindloensis, commonly known as the Bindloens euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its segmented, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
