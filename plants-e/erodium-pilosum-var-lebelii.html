Erodium pilosum var. lebelii

Erodium pilosum var. lebelii, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as roadsides and meadows.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
