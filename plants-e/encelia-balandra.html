Encelia balandra

Encelia balandra, commonly known as the coastal tar plant, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, silver-green foliage. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats. It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for pollinators.

Encelia balandra is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
