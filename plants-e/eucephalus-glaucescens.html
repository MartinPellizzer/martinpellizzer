Eucephalus glaucescens

Eucephalus glaucescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray inflorescences that resemble mist, blooming in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Eucephalus glaucescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
