Encyclia rhombilabia

Encyclia rhombilabia, commonly known as the rhombic orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its rhombic-shaped pseudobulbs and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia rhombilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
