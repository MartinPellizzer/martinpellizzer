Escobaria vivipara var. arizonica

Escobaria vivipara var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona livefoot, is a succulent perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces offspring through vivipary, a process where seeds germinate on the parent plant before dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
