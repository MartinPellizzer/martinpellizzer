Eugenia moraviana

Eugenia moraviana, commonly known as the Moravian evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia moraviana is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
