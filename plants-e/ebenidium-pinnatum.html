Ebenidium pinnatum

Ebenidium pinnatum, commonly known as the featherleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant has delicate, fern-like leaves that are arranged in a pinnate pattern, giving it its common name. It produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often overlooked due to their inconspicuous nature.

Ebenidium pinnatum plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for certain insects and birds.
