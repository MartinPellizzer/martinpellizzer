Eupatorium parviflorum

Eupatorium parviflorum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

Eupatorium parviflorum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported therapeutic benefits.
