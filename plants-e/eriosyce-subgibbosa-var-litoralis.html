Eriosyce subgibbosa var. litoralis

Eriosyce subgibbosa var. litoralis, commonly known as the coastal eriosyce, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and coastal environments.

Its unique morphology and ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
