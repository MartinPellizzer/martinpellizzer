Euryops pectinatus subsp. pectinatus

Euryops pectinatus subsp.

pectinatus, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces a distinctive, hollow stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
