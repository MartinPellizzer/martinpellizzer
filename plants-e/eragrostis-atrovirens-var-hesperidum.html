Eragrostis atrovirens var. hesperidum

Eragrostis atrovirens var. hesperidum, commonly known as the desert panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and degraded landscapes.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
