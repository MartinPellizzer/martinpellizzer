Eupatorium platyphyllum

Eupatorium platyphyllum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and prairies.

Eupatorium platyphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
