Ecballium purgans

Ecballium purgans, commonly known as the horse-herb or blistering cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a trailing annual plant characterized by its hairy, lobed leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a spiny, elongated capsule that turns from green to yellow as it matures. The plant contains irritant compounds that can cause blistering on the skin upon contact.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
