Eugenia gudilla

Eugenia gudilla, commonly known as the Gudilla cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are glossy and aromatic, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia gudilla thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
