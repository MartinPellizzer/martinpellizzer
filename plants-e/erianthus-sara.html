Erianthus sara

Erianthus sara, commonly known as Sara's reed, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments with cool temperatures.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
