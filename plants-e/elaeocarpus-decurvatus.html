Elaeocarpus decurvatus

Elaeocarpus decurvatus, commonly known as the curved-leaf elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often curve at the tips. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
