Eupatorium kavanayense

Eupatorium kavanayense, commonly known as the Kavanayen feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium kavanayense produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
