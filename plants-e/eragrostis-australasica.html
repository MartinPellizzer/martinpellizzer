Eragrostis australasica

Eragrostis australasica, commonly known as the Australian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters.

Eragrostis australasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
