Eulophia rostrata

Eulophia rostrata, commonly known as the rostrate eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia rostrata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
