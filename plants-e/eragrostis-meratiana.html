Eragrostis meratiana

Eragrostis meratiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

Eragrostis meratiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
