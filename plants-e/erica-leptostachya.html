Erica leptostachya

Erica leptostachya, commonly known as the narrow-staminal heath, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in whorls. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in late winter to early spring.

Erica leptostachya is well adapted to harsh, nutrient-poor environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
