Eucalyptus angophoroides

Eucalyptus angophoroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malletwood, is a tall tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pink or cream in color. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

Eucalyptus angophoroides plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
