Eryngium hemisphaericum f. elongatum

Eryngium hemisphaericum f. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated thistle, is a variant of the hemispherical thistle native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and blue to purple bracts. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
