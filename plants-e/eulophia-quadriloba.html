Eulophia quadriloba

Eulophia quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in summer, producing pink or white petals with a distinctive four-lobed lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Eulophia quadriloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
