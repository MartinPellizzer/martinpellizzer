Elymus ramosus

Elymus ramosus, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, often silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple.

Elymus ramosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
