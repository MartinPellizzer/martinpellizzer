Eugenia cochinchinensis

Eugenia cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese apple or star anise tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, star-shaped flowers and dark purple to black berries. The plant is valued for its aromatic leaves and fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its leaves are often used as a spice in Vietnamese cuisine, while the fruit is a source of flavoring in food and beverages.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic value.
