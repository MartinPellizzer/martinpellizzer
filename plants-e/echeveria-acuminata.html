Echeveria acuminata

Echeveria acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of vibrant purple or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its color and shape.

Due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
