Epipactis purpurata var. pollinensis

Epipactis purpurata var. pollinensis, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and showy, purple-tinged flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects, with a lip that serves as a landing platform.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
