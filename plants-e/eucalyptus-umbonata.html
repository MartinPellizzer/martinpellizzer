Eucalyptus umbonata

Eucalyptus umbonata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in dry, open forests and woodlands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white tomentum. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually pale yellow or cream in color.

The wood of Eucalyptus umbonata is strong and durable, making it valuable for timber and other industrial uses.
