Equisetum limosum f. linnaeanum

Equisetum limosum f. linnaeanum, commonly known as the Linnaean horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. The plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils and thrives in wetland environments. It reproduces via spores and has a unique, ancient appearance reminiscent of prehistoric plant life.

This fern-like plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
