Eucalyptus × balanopelex

Eucalyptus × balanopelex, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. Its foliage is typically aromatic and contains high levels of essential oils, contributing to its medicinal and industrial uses.

Eucalyptus × balanopelex is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian vegetation.
