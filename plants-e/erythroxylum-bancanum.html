Erythroxylum bancanum

Erythroxylum bancanum, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.

Erythroxylum bancanum is also noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
