Eupatorium adenanthum

Eupatorium adenanthum, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium adenanthum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
