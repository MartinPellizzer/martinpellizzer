Erica velatiflora

Erica velatiflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Erica velatiflora prefers well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic features.
