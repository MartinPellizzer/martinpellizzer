Eupatorium jamesonii

Eupatorium jamesonii, commonly known as Jameson's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
