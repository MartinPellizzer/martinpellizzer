Eurya glandulosa var. oblongifolia

Eurya glandulosa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features lanceolate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal color changes.
