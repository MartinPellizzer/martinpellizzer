Erigeron eucephaloides

Erigeron eucephaloides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue to purple ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers, making it visually striking. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its foliage is covered in fine, silvery hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
