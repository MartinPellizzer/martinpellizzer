Euphorbia dalmatica f. isophylla

Euphorbia dalmatica f. isophylla, commonly known as the Dalmatian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and fleshy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a unique, colorful bract. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
