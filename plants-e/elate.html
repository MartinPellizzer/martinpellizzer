Elate

Elate, commonly known as the elate plant, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant is native to certain regions of South America and thrives in moist, temperate climates. Elate is often found in wetlands and along the edges of streams, where it forms dense mats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a habitat for various invertebrates and its potential ecological value in wetland restoration projects.
