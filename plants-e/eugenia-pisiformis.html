Eugenia pisiformis

Eugenia pisiformis, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tart flavor. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Eugenia pisiformis thrives in warm, humid climates and is well-suited for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
