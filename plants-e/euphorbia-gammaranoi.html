Euphorbia gammaranoi

Euphorbia gammaranoi, commonly known as the Gammaran euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
