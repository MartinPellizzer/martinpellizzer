Ericoides odoratum

Ericoides odoratum, commonly known as the sweet-scented heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreens and shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a fragrant aroma. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a dense, compact appearance.

Ericoides odoratum thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests or high-altitude habitats.
