Epithema ceylanicum

Epithema ceylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon thorn apple, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its toxicity, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
