Ethulia burundiensis

Ethulia burundiensis, commonly known as the Burundi ethulia, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively in the mountainous regions of Burundi.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, typically growing in shaded forest understories. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the specific ecological conditions of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Ethulia burundiensis is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
