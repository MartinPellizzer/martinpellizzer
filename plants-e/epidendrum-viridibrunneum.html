Epidendrum viridibrunneum

Epidendrum viridibrunneum, commonly known as the Green-Brown Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from greenish-brown to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. It produces numerous pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

Epidendrum viridibrunneum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
