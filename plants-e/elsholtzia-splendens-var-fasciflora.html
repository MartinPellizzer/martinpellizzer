Elsholtzia splendens var. fasciflora

Elsholtzia splendens var. fasciflora, commonly known as the spotted skullcap, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features slender, aromatic stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or pale green stripe down the middle, giving it its varietal name.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.
