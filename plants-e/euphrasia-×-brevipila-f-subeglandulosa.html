Euphrasia × brevipila f. subeglandulosa

Euphrasia × brevipila f. subeglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular-fruited eyebright, is a hybrid species within the Scrophulariaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish to reddish flowers and glandular structures on its stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
