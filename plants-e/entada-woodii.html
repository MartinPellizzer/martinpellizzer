Entada woodii

Entada woodii, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and smooth, gray bark. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are dispersed by wind.

The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally for construction and tool-making.
