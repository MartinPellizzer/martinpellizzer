Epipactis purpurata subsp. pollinensis

Epipactis purpurata subsp.

pollinensis, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect flowering stem with several showy, lip-like petals. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is known for its ability to thrive in acidic soils.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and is often found in regions with a temperate climate.
