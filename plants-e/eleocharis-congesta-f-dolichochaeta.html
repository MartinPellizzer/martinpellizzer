Eleocharis congesta f. dolichochaeta

Eleocharis congesta f. dolichochaeta, commonly known as the long-hair sedge, is a variant of the sedge species Eleocharis congesta.

It is characterized by its elongated, hair-like inflorescences, which distinguish it from other members of the genus. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with the stems often emerging from the water's surface.

The botanical features of this fern-like plant make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife and contributing to habitat diversity.
