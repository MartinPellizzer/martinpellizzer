Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. centrispinus

Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. centrispinus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense, yellowish-green spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
