Eriogonum nudum var. oblongifolium

Eriogonum nudum var. oblongifolium, commonly known as the oblongleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically oblong and hairy, with a grayish-green appearance. This species thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
