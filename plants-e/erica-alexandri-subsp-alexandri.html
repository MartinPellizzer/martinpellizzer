Erica alexandri subsp. alexandri

Erica alexandri subsp.

alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with woody stems and leathery leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
