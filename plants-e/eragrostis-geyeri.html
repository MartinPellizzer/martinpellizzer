Eragrostis geyeri

Eragrostis geyeri, commonly known as the Geyeri grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Botanically, it is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive with minimal water.
