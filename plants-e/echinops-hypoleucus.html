Echinops hypoleucus

Echinops hypoleucus, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are often mistaken for small tumbleweeds. The plant has silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Echinops hypoleucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seed heads.
