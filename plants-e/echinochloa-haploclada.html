Echinochloa haploclada

Echinochloa haploclada, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields, making it a common weed in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is closely related to other grasses such as rice and corn.
