Ethanium mexicanum

Ethanium mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Ethanium mexicanum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
