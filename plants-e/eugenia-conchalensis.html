Eugenia conchalensis

Eugenia conchalensis, commonly known as the shell-berry or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
