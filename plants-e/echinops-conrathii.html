Echinops conrathii

Echinops conrathii, commonly known as the Conrath's thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky ball, which are a key feature of the genus Echinops. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of the distinctive seed heads. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Echinops conrathii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
