Equisetum × occidentale

Equisetum × occidentale, commonly known as the western horsetail, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from different species of horsetails.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems and whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It reproduces via spores and has a distinctive appearance with a tall, spiky form.

Equisetum × occidentale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
