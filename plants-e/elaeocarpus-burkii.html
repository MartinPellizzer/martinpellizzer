Elaeocarpus burkii

Elaeocarpus burkii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a specimen tree in suitable climates.
