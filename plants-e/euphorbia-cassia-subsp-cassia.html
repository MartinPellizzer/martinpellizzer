Euphorbia cassia subsp. cassia

Euphorbia cassia subsp.

cassia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear in late summer.

The plant has smooth, often slightly hairy, green leaves that are alternate and elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. The sap of Euphorbia cassia subsp.

cassia is milky and can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
