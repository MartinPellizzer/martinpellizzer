Eria iridifolia

Eria iridifolia, commonly known as the blue orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Eria iridifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in greenhouses and gardens worldwide.
