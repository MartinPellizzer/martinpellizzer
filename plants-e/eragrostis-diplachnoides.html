Eragrostis diplachnoides

Eragrostis diplachnoides, commonly known as the two-awned lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive inflorescence with two awns on each lemma. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of florets.

Eragrostis diplachnoides is often used in erosion control due to its rapid establishment and drought tolerance.
