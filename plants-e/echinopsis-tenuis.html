Echinopsis tenuis

Echinopsis tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the evening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Echinopsis tenuis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
