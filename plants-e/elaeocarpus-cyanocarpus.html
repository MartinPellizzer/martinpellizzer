Elaeocarpus cyanocarpus

Elaeocarpus cyanocarpus, commonly known as the blue-fruit elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and produces small, blue-black berries that are visually striking. The plant belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in various woodworking applications.
