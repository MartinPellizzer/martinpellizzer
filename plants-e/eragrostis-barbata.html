Eragrostis barbata

Eragrostis barbata, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
