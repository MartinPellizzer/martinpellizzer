Ericoides polygaliflorum

Ericoides polygaliflorum, commonly known as the purple-leafed ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, adding ornamental value to gardens.

The species is valued for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in woodland landscapes.
