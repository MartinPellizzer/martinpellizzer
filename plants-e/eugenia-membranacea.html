Eugenia membranacea

Eugenia membranacea, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Its leaves are opposite and elliptical, with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Eugenia membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
