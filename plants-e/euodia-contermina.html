Euodia contermina

Euodia contermina, commonly known as the Malabar kino tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

Euodia contermina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fragrant foliage.
