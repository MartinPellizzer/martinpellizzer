Eugenia brasiliensis var. leucocarpa

Eugenia brasiliensis var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruit guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round, white or pale yellow berry that is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its fruit in certain regions.
