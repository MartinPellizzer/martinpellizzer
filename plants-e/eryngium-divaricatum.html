Eryngium divaricatum

Eryngium divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native plant species.
