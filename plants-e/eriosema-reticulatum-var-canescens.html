Eriosema reticulatum var. canescens

Eriosema reticulatum var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy nettle bean, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its seeds are small and dry, often dispersed by wind or animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
