Eriogonum villiflorum var. tumulosum

Eriogonum villiflorum var. tumulosum, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing loose soil in its native habitat.
