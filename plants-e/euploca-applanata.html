Euploca applanata

Euploca applanata, commonly known as the flatleaf euploca, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are broad and flat, giving it its common name, and its flowers are small and clustered in a head-like structure.

Euploca applanata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
