Eupatorium hidalgense

Eupatorium hidalgense, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium hidalgense is also known for its use in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
