Eriosema albogriseum subsp. huillense

Eriosema albogriseum subsp.

huillense, commonly known as the Huillense eriosema, is a species native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The flowers are typically small and white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
