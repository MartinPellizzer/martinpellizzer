Eugenia carthagenensis var. myrtifolia

Eugenia carthagenensis var. myrtifolia, commonly known as the Myrtleleaf Umbrella Tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, umbrella-like canopy of glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications in some regions.
