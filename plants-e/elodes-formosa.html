Elodes formosa

Elodes formosa, commonly known as the waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are typically green or reddish in color, and it lacks true leaves, instead having small, thread-like structures called filaments. The plant reproduces through vegetative propagation, with new shoots forming at the nodes along the stem. It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing oxygen and habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Elodes formosa is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
