Euphorbia × navae

Euphorbia × navae, commonly known as the hybrid spurges, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in Mediterranean climates.
