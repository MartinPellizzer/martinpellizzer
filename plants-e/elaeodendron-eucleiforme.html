Elaeodendron eucleiforme

Elaeodendron eucleiforme, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown wood with a pleasant scent. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark purple berries.

Due to its ornamental value and wood quality, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
