Eupatorium roupalifolium var. subintegrifolium

Eupatorium roupalifolium var. subintegrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
