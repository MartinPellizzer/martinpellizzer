Eupatorium lebrijense

Eupatorium lebrijense, commonly known as the Lebrijen's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Eupatorium lebrijense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
