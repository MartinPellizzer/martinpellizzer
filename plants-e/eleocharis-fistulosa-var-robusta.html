Eleocharis fistulosa var. robusta

Eleocharis fistulosa var. robusta, commonly known as the robust spikerush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and covered with small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion, while its dense growth provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.

This species is also valued for its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and poor soil conditions.
