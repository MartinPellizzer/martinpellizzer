Eugenia umbellata

Eugenia umbellata, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Eugenia umbellata is well adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
