Eriocoelum paniculatum

Eriocoelum paniculatum, commonly known as the golden star sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble panicles. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often yellow or golden in color, contributing to its common name.

Eriocoelum paniculatum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
