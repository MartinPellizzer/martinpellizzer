Eugenia stipitata subsp. stipitata

Eugenia stipitata subsp.

stipitata, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a species native to South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red or black when mature.

The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
