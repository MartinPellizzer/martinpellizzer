Elymus thoroldianus subsp. laxiusculus

Elymus thoroldianus subsp.

laxiusculus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often lie flat on the ground, forming a dense mat. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered with fine hairs, while its spikelets are clustered in panicles.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
