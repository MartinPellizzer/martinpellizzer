Eranthemum variabile var. integrifolium

Eranthemum variabile var. integrifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often lance-shaped leaves that are typically smooth-edged. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
