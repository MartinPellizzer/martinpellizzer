Eupatorium patens var. typicum

Eupatorium patens var. typicum, commonly known as common boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and streambanks. The plant features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive white vein pattern.

The small, white to pale blue flowers are clustered in flat-topped umbels and bloom in late summer.
