Englerina woodfordioides var. subintegridisca

Englerina woodfordioides var. subintegridisca, commonly known as the Woolly Englerina, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and aesthetic appeal.
