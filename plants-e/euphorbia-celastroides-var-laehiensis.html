Euphorbia celastroides var. laehiensis

Euphorbia celastroides var. laehiensis, commonly known as the Laehien euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, clustered stems that are often covered in a waxy, greenish-blue coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
