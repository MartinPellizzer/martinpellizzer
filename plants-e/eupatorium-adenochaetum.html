Eupatorium adenochaetum

Eupatorium adenochaetum, commonly known as the hairy false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying shades of white and purple, and it produces small, dry fruits known as achenes.

Eupatorium adenochaetum is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
