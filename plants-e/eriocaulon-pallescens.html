Eriocaulon pallescens

Eriocaulon pallescens, commonly known as the pale eriocaulon, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are inconspicuous, usually clustered in dense spikes, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Eriocaulon pallescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
