Elaterium cordatum

Elaterium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf elaterium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Elaterium cordatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
