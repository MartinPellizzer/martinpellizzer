Eriogonum oblongifolium var. minus

Eriogonum oblongifolium var. minus, commonly known as the small-leafed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and knotweed.

This variety is often found in California and Oregon, where it contributes to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
