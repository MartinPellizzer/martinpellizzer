Erigeron atticus var. atticus

Erigeron atticus var. atticus, commonly known as the plains fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and prairies, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its foliage is lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically white or pale yellow.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
