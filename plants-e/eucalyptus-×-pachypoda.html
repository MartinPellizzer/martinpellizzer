Eucalyptus × pachypoda

Eucalyptus × pachypoda, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green leaves that emit a strong menthol-like scent when crushed. The tree typically grows to a height of 5–15 meters, featuring smooth, peeling bark and small, round to oval-shaped leaves. It produces globular clusters of white flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
