Elephantopus spicatus var. roseus

Elephantopus spicatus var. roseus, commonly known as the pink elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and striking pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact, cushion-like growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
