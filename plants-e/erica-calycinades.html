Erica calycinades

Erica calycinades, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a species of shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica calycinades is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.
