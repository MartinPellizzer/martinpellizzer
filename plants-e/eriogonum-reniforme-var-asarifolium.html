Eriogonum reniforme var. asarifolium

Eriogonum reniforme var. asarifolium, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are reniform (kidney-shaped) and its lanceolate, asarifolium-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy component of desert ecosystems.
