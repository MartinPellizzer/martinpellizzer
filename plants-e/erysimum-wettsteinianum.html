Erysimum wettsteinianum

Erysimum wettsteinianum, commonly known as the Wettstein's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum wettsteinianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
