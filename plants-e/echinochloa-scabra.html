Echinochloa scabra

Echinochloa scabra, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Echinochloa scabra is often found in wetland ecosystems and is an important component of aquatic and riparian plant communities.
