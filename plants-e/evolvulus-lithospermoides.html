Evolvulus lithospermoides

Evolvulus lithospermoides, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and rocky regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, violet to blue flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional herbal medicine.
