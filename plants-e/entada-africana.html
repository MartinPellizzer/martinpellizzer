Entada africana

Entada africana, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces hard, spiny pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and roots have been utilized for their medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
