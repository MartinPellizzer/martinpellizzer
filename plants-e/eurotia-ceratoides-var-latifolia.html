Eurotia ceratoides var. latifolia

Eurotia ceratoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf euonym, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are broader than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by distinctive orange-red capsules. Its fruiting bodies split open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Euonymus genus.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
