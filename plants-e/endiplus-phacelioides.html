Endiplus phacelioides

Endiplus phacelioides, commonly known as the desert phacelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to drought conditions, with a shallow root system and rapid life cycle.
