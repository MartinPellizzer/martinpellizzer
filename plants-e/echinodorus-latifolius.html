Echinodorus latifolius

Echinodorus latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a dark green coloration and a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge above the water surface, followed by distinctive blue or purple berries. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in aquariums and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to oxygenate water.

Echinodorus latifolius is also known for its tolerance to a wide range of water conditions, making it a hardy choice for aquatic gardens.
