Erigeron montevidensis var. dunensis

Erigeron montevidensis var. dunensis, commonly known as the Montevideo fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This variant is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits compared to other varieties of Erigeron montevidensis.
