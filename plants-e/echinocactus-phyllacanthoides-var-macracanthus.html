Echinocactus phyllacanthoides var. macracanthus

Echinocactus phyllacanthoides var. macracanthus, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and xerophytic gardeners.
