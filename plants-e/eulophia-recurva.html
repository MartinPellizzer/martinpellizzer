Eulophia recurva

Eulophia recurva, commonly known as the recurved eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically features long, slender leaves and has a distinctive inflorescence with multiple flowers clustered at the end of the stem. Eulophia recurva thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural collections and ecological studies.
