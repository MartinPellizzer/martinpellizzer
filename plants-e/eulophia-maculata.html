Eulophia maculata

Eulophia maculata, commonly known as the spotted eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with brightly colored, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the pseudobulb.

Eulophia maculata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty in horticulture.
