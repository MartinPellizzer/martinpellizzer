Eryngium brunardii

Eryngium brunardii, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles, with bracts that are often blue or purple in color. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium brunardii is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
