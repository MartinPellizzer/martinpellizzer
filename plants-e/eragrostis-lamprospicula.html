Eragrostis lamprospicula

Eragrostis lamprospicula, commonly known as the silver-haired lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, silvery-green leaves that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with a pale blue or greenish hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
