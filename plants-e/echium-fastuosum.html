Echium fastuosum

Echium fastuosum, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Echium fastuosum is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
