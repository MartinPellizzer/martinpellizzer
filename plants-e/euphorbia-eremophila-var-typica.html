Euphorbia eremophila var. typica

Euphorbia eremophila var. typica, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its growth habit is often low and spreading, forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
