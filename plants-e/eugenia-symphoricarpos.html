Eugenia symphoricarpos

Eugenia symphoricarpos, commonly known as the roughleaf myrtle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia symphoricarpos is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
