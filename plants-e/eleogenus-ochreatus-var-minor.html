Eleogenus ochreatus var. minor

Eleogenus ochreatus var. minor, commonly known as the lesser ochre spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
