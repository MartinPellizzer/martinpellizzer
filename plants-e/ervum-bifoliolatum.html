Ervum bifoliolatum

Ervum bifoliolatum, commonly known as the two-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically consist of two leaflets, giving it its specific epithet "bifoliolatum.". The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated for soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
