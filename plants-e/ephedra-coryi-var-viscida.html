Ephedra coryi var. viscida

Ephedra coryi var. viscida, commonly known as Chinese ephedra, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It is characterized by its green, scale-like leaves and distinctive, elongated, woody stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties, it is also subject to regulation in many countries.
