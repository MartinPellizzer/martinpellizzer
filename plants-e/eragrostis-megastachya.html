Eragrostis megastachya

Eragrostis megastachya, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis megastachya is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its role in soil stabilization.
