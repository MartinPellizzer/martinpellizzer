Echinocactus joadii

Echinocactus joadii, commonly known as the Joadii cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance featuring dense, spiny areoles and a ribbed stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its thick, water-storing stems help it survive in arid environments, and it is adapted to full sun exposure.

Echinocactus joadii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
