Escallonia hypsophila

Escallonia hypsophila, commonly known as the alpine escallonia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, adapting to harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round visual interest.

Escallonia hypsophila is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in cooler climates.
