Escallonia vaccinioides

Escallonia vaccinioides, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals.

Escallonia vaccinioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal gardens.
