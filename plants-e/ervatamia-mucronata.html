Ervatamia mucronata

Ervatamia mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf eryngo, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces a distinctive, aromatic odor.

Ervatamia mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
