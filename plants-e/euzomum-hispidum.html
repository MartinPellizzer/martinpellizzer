Euzomum hispidum

Euzomum hispidum, commonly known as the rough euzomum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
