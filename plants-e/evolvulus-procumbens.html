Evolvulus procumbens

Evolvulus procumbens, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Americas.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Evolvulus procumbens is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.
