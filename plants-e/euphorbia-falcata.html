Euphorbia falcata

Euphorbia falcata, commonly known as the sickle euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, branching structure and fleshy, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often more showy than the actual blooms. Its leaves are typically narrow and pointed, resembling a sickle shape, giving the plant its common name.

Euphorbia falcata is adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
