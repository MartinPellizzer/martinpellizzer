Erodium absinthoides subsp. armenum

Erodium absinthoides subsp.

armenum, commonly known as Armenian erodium, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
