Eupatorium nutans

Eupatorium nutans, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, oval fruits called cypsela. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and marshes.

Eupatorium nutans is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported fever-reducing effects.
