Eryngium eriophorum var. vegetius

Eryngium eriophorum var. vegetius, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its unique appearance makes it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
