Epithymum arvense

Epithymum arvense, commonly known as field thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant produces tiny, pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its aromatic properties and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental and practical gardening.
