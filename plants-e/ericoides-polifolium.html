Ericoides polifolium

Ericoides polifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Ericoides polifolium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

It is an important component of the flora in temperate regions, contributing to biodiversity and ecological stability.
