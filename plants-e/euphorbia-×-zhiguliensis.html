Euphorbia × zhiguliensis

Euphorbia × zhiguliensis, commonly known as the Zhiguliensis spurge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spine-tipped leaves and a distinctive cyathium, which is a cup-shaped inflorescence typical of the genus. The plant is native to China and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered within the cyathium, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
