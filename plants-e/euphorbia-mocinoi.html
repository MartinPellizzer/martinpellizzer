Euphorbia mocinoi

Euphorbia mocinoi, commonly known as the Mocino's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia mocinoi is also known for its ability to store water in its thick stems, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
