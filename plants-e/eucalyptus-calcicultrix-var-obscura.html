Eucalyptus calcicultrix var. obscura

Eucalyptus calcicultrix var. obscura, commonly known as the dark form of the calcicultrix eucalyptus, is a variant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its dark grey to black bark, which provides a striking visual contrast against the surrounding landscape. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a bluish-green hue and a rough texture. This variant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater.

Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are usually white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding insects and birds.
