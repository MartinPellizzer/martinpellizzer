Eugenia valdevenosa

Eugenia valdevenosa, commonly known as the Chilean guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia valdevenosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
