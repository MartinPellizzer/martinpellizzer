Elodea planchonii

Elodea planchonii, commonly known as the African waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, which can be either submerged or emergent depending on water conditions. Elodea planchonii is often found in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
