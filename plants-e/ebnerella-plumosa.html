Ebnerella plumosa

Ebnerella plumosa, commonly known as the featherleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, temperate environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery thallus that gives it its common name. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. Its structure consists of a layered cortex, medulla, and upper cortex, typical of foliose lichens.

Ebnerella plumosa is often found on decaying wood, contributing to the decomposition process in forest ecosystems.
