Epidendrum hyalinilabrum

Epidendrum hyalinilabrum, commonly known as the hyalinilabrum orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant features long, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Epidendrum hyalinilabrum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
