Eupatorium magistri

Eupatorium magistri, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium magistri is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
