Eugenia pamatensis

Eugenia pamatensis, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon Basin, particularly in Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and potential ecological benefits.
