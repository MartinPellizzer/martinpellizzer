Eragrostis braunii

Eragrostis braunii, commonly known as brown lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis braunii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
