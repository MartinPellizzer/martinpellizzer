Echinodorus gracilis

Echinodorus gracilis, commonly known as the slender swordplant, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. This plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often used in aquariums for its ornamental value and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life. Echinodorus gracilis prefers moderate to high light conditions and can tolerate a range of water temperatures.

It is also known for its ability to help maintain water quality by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
