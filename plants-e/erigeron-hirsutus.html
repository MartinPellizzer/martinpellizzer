Erigeron hirsutus

Erigeron hirsutus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils.

Erigeron hirsutus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
