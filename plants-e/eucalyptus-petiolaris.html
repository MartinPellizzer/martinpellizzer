Eucalyptus petiolaris

Eucalyptus petiolaris, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in summer. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Eucalyptus petiolaris is a hardy shrub or small tree that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
