Echeveria gracilis

Echeveria gracilis, commonly known as the silver chalice, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of narrow, blue-gray leaves that give it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The plant produces upright, branching stems with clusters of small, pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its compact size and elegant form make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
