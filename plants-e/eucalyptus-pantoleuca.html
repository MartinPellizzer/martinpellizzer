Eucalyptus pantoleuca

Eucalyptus pantoleuca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in mallee woodlands.

The tree is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal uses.
