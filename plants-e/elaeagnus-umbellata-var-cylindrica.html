Elaeagnus umbellata var. cylindrica

Elaeagnus umbellata var. cylindrica, commonly known as silverthorn or sandthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used for erosion control. It produces small, edible berries that are consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
