Eupatorium nitidulum

Eupatorium nitidulum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features erect, branched stems covered with fine, white hairs, and it produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Eupatorium nitidulum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants.
