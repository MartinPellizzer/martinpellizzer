Euphorbia kansui

Euphorbia kansui, commonly known as Chinese euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and unique floral structures called cyathia. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves known as bracts. It is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Euphorbia kansui is also known for its medicinal properties, though its sap can be toxic.
