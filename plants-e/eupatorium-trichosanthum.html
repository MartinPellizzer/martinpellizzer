Eupatorium trichosanthum

Eupatorium trichosanthum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
