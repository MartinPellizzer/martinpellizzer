Euphrasia officinalis var. alpestris

Euphrasia officinalis var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine eyebright, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy tissue, and produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Euphrasia officinalis var. alpestris is valued for its traditional medicinal uses, particularly for its potential eye health benefits.
