Elaeocarpus glabripetalus var. teres

Elaeocarpus glabripetalus var. teres, commonly known as the round-fruited elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its fruit is notable for its rounded shape and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
