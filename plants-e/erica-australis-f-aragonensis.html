Erica australis f. aragonensis

Erica australis f. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon heath, is a subspecies of the common heath plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica australis f. aragonensis plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
