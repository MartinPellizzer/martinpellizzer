Eupatorium corsicum

Eupatorium corsicum, commonly known as the Corsican eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Eupatorium corsicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
