Elymus uralensis subsp. viridiglumis

Elymus uralensis subsp.

viridiglumis, commonly known as the green awn wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are often green or reddish in color. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands.

Its fibrous roots make it an important plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.
