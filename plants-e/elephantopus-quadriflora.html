Elephantopus quadriflora

Elephantopus quadriflora, commonly known as four-flowered elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a swollen, tuberous base that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
