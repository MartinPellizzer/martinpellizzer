Eupatorium sexangulare

Eupatorium sexangulare, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium sexangulare is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
