Epidendrum luteum

Epidendrum luteum, commonly known as the Yellow Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Epidendrum luteum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
