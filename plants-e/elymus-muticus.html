Elymus muticus

Elymus muticus, commonly known as the silent grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and stream banks.

Elymus muticus is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
