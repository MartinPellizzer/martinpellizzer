Echinocereus blanckii

Echinocereus blanckii, commonly known as the Blanck's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a rounded, segmented stem that often forms dense clusters. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits. Echinocereus blanckii thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
