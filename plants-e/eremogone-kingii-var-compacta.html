Eremogone kingii var. compacta

Eremogone kingii var. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf eremogone, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant has wiry, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
