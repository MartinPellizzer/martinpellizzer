Eugenia brachyandra

Eugenia brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clove, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible, aromatic berries that are often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a humid, subtropical climate.

Eugenia brachyandra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
