Erica lasiocephala

Erica lasiocephala, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves covered in a fine, woolly coating. Erica lasiocephala produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
