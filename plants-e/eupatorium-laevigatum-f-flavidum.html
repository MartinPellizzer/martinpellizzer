Eupatorium laevigatum f. flavidum

Eupatorium laevigatum f. flavidum, commonly known as the yellow false dragonhead, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
