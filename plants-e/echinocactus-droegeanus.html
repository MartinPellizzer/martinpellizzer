Echinocactus droegeanus

Echinocactus droegeanus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stem during the warmer months. Its succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
