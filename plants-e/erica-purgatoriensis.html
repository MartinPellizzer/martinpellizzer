Erica purgatoriensis

Erica purgatoriensis, commonly known as the Purgatory heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the island of Madeira.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica purgatoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in challenging environmental conditions.
