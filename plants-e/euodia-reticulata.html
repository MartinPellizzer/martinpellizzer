Euodia reticulata

Euodia reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese yellow wood or the Chinese quince, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellowish flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and dry. Euodia reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
