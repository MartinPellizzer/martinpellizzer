Encyclia doeringii

Encyclia doeringii, commonly known as the Doering's pineapple orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers resembling those of the pineapple. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soil and bright, indirect light. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Encyclia doeringii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
