Entada pursaetha

Entada pursaetha, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a tropical tree belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate, and its distinctive pod-like fruits that contain multiple seeds. The plant is native to Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its bark and seeds have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is often found in savanna and forested areas.
