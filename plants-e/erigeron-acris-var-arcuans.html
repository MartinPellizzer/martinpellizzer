Erigeron acris var. arcuans

Erigeron acris var. arcuans, commonly known as the curlyleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in open, dry areas such as meadows and prairies, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are usually curly and gray-green, while the flowers are small, blue to purple, and appear in clusters.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
