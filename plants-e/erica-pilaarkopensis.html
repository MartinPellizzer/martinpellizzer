Erica pilaarkopensis

Erica pilaarkopensis, commonly known as the Pilaarkop heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the summer months. Erica pilaarkopensis is well adapted to acidic and nutrient-poor environments, typical of fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small animals.
