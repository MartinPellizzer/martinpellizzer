Eugenia chnoosepala var. elliptica

Eugenia chnoosepala var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
