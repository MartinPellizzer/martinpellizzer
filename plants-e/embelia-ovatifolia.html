Embelia ovatifolia

Embelia ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
