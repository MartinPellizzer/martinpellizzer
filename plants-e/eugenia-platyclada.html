Eugenia platyclada

Eugenia platyclada, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

Eugenia platyclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
