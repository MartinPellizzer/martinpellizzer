Epidendrum nanopsis

Epidendrum nanopsis, commonly known as the miniature orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a compact, compact growth habit with short, fleshy pseudobulbs and narrow, dark green leaves. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually appear in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Epidendrum nanopsis is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
