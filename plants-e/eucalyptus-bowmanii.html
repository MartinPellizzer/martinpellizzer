Eucalyptus bowmanii

Eucalyptus bowmanii, commonly known as the bowman's gum, is a tall, slender eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in long strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its essential oils, particularly eucalyptol, make it valuable in medicinal and aromatic applications.

The species is adapted to dry, inland environments and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
