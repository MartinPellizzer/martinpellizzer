Echiochilon verrucosum

Echiochilon verrucosum, commonly known as the woolly false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a branched stem that often becomes woody with age, and its leaves are usually alternate and ovate. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Echiochilon verrucosum is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions.
