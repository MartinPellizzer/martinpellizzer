Eupatorium pseudodalea var. macrodontum

Eupatorium pseudodalea var. macrodontum, commonly known as the large-flowered boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits called cypsela, which are typically hairy and wind-dispersed. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
