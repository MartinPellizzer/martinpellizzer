Erigeron ramosus subsp. septentrionalis

Erigeron ramosus subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and it has a sprawling growth habit with numerous branches. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
