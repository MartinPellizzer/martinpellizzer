Elaeodendron melanocarpum

Elaeodendron melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited elaeodendron, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by dark purple to black fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruiting characteristics, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
