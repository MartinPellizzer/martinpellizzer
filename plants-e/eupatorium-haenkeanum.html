Eupatorium haenkeanum

Eupatorium haenkeanum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is typically found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
