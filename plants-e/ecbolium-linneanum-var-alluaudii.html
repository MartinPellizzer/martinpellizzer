Ecbolium linneanum var. alluaudii

Ecbolium linneanum var. alluaudii, commonly known as the Alluaud's sandmat, is a low-growing, perennial plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a sprawling, mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, succulent leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its root system is extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
