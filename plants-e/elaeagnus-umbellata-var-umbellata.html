Elaeagnus umbellata var. umbellata

Elaeagnus umbellata var. umbellata, commonly known as silver berry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
