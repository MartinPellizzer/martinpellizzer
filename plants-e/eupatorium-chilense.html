Eupatorium chilense

Eupatorium chilense, commonly known as Chilean boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium chilense is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in South American herbal medicine.
