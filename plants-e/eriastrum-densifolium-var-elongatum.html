Eriastrum densifolium var. elongatum

Eriastrum densifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the elongated denseleaf erysimum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and pungent odor. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, mat-like growth habit. Its leaves are dense, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The flowers are usually yellow or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.
