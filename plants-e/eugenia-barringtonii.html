Eugenia barringtonii

Eugenia barringtonii, commonly known as the Barrington's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
