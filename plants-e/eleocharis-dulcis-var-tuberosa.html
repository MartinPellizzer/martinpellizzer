Eleocharis dulcis var. tuberosa

Eleocharis dulcis var. tuberosa, commonly known as water chestnut, is a aquatic plant characterized by its swollen, starchy underground stems called tubers.

It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and shallow water bodies, often forming dense vegetation. The plant has slender, leafless stems that float on the water surface, with small, spike-like inflorescences. Its tubers are the economically important part, used as a vegetable in various cuisines.

The plant plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for aquatic life.
