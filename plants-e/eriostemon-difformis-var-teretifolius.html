Eriostemon difformis var. teretifolius

Eriostemon difformis var. teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eriostemon, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland habitats.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an interesting subject for botanical study and ornamental gardening.
