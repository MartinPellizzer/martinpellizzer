Elaeocarpus stapfianus

Elaeocarpus stapfianus, commonly known as the Malayan elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
