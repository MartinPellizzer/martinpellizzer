Erysimum ochroleucum subsp. ascendens

Erysimum ochroleucum subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as the ascending whitlowgrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.
