Euphrasia aequalis

Euphrasia aequalis, commonly known as the equal-eyed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Euphrasia aequalis is a perennial herb that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil.
