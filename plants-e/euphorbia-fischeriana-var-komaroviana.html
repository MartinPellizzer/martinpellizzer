Euphorbia fischeriana var. komaroviana

Euphorbia fischeriana var. komaroviana, commonly known as the Komarov's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
