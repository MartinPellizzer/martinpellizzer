Erigeron purpuratus

Erigeron purpuratus, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and showy purple to pink petals. Its conical flower head is surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
