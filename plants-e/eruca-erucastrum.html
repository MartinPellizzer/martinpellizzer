Eruca erucastrum

Eruca erucastrum, commonly known as wild garlic or rampion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and oval with a waxy surface, and it produces small, yellow, four-petaled flowers. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, and it is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eruca erucastrum is valued for its edible leaves and is sometimes used in culinary and medicinal applications.
