Eriosema youngii

Eriosema youngii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Eriosema youngii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland areas.
