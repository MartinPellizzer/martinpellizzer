Elaphoglossum ellipticum

Elaphoglossum ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically 20 to 40 centimeters long. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with fertile fronds producing spores on the underside. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes bipinnate leaves with a distinctive, finely divided appearance.
