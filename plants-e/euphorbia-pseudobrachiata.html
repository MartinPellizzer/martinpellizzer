Euphorbia pseudobrachiata

Euphorbia pseudobrachiata, commonly known as the spiked euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures called cyathia.

Euphorbia pseudobrachiata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
