Eurybia spinulosa

Eurybia spinulosa, commonly known as the spiked sunflower, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers with a distinctive spiky appearance. It is often found in wetlands and is adapted to tolerate saturated soil conditions.

Eurybia spinulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
