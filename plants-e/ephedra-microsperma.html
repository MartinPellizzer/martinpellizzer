Ephedra microsperma

Ephedra microsperma, commonly known as the small-seeded ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by cone-like structures containing minute seeds. Ephedra microsperma is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
