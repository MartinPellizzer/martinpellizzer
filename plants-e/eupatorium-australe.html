Eupatorium australe

Eupatorium australe, commonly known as Australian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Eupatorium australe is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
