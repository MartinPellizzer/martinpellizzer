Erigeron myosotis subsp. fuegiae

Erigeron myosotis subsp.

fuegiae, commonly known as the Falkland Islands blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Falkland Islands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal grasslands and sandy soils, thriving in the temperate climate of its native habitat. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
