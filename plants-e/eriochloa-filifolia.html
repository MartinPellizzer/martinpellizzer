Eriochloa filifolia

Eriochloa filifolia, commonly known as silky beardgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides and fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriochloa filifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor soil conditions.
