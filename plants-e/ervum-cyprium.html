Ervum cyprium

Ervum cyprium, commonly known as Cyprus bean, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is well adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
