Elaeagnus multiflora f. angustata

Elaeagnus multiflora f. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf silverberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, silvery-gray leaves with a distinctive glandular surface. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in erosion control and wildlife habitat.
