Epidendrum birostratum

Epidendrum birostratum, commonly known as the two-beaked epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has long, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum birostratum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
