Eugenia deltoidea

Eugenia deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
