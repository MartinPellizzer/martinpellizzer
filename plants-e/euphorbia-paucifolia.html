Euphorbia paucifolia

Euphorbia paucifolia, commonly known as the silver rose or snowball euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded shape with pale green, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
