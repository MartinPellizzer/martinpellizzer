Eugenia incanescens var. elliptica

Eugenia incanescens var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, aromatic fruits. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and a preference for warm, humid climates.

It is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit, which has a sweet, citrusy flavor.
