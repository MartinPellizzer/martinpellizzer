Erythrina pallida

Erythrina pallida, commonly known as the pale coral tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and its bark is often smooth with a reddish hue.

Erythrina pallida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
