Eugenia rigidifolia var. phyllyreifolia

Eugenia rigidifolia var. phyllyreifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eucalypt, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a pale, often hairy underside. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens for its foliage and seasonal flowering.
