Erysimum sylvestre subsp. linariifolium

Erysimum sylvestre subsp.

linariifolium, commonly known as the slender wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture, and stems that are erect and branched.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
