Eugenia xystophylla

Eugenia xystophylla, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, woody fruits. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine.
