Eryngium bourgatii var. viridescens

Eryngium bourgatii var. viridescens, commonly known as the green sea holly, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features spiky, blue to purple flowers surrounded by silver-green bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, typically from late summer into autumn.
