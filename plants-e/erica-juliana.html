Erica juliana

Erica juliana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Erica juliana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
