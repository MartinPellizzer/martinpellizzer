Eugenia oligandra

Eugenia oligandra, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and on limestone soils.

Eugenia oligandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
