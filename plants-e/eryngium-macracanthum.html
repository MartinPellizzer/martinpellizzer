Eryngium macracanthum

Eryngium macracanthum, commonly known as the spiked eryngium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Eryngium macracanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
