Erigeron watsonii

Erigeron watsonii, commonly known as Watson's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Watson's fleabane is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments with its hairy leaves and deep root system.
