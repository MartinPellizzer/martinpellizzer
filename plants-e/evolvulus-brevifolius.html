Evolvulus brevifolius

Evolvulus brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a trailing vine that often grows along the ground or on supports, producing numerous small, edible berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to spread quickly in suitable conditions.
