Eupatorium spinaciifolium

Eupatorium spinaciifolium, commonly known as spinach-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and serrated, spinach-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium spinaciifolium is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
