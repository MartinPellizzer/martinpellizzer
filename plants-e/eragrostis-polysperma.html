Eragrostis polysperma

Eragrostis polysperma, commonly known as the common weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis polysperma is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to different soil conditions.
