Elytrigia intermedia subsp. varnensis

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

varnensis, commonly known as Varnensian intermediate grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its ecological adaptability and potential for use in restoration projects.
