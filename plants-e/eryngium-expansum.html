Eryngium expansum

Eryngium expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to violet flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium expansum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
