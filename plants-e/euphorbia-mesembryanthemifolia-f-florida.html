Euphorbia mesembryanthemifolia f. florida

Euphorbia mesembryanthemifolia f. florida, commonly known as the Florida milkweed, is a succulent plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically gray-green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a hardy, low-maintenance ornamental plant.
