Erigeron breweri var. typicus

Erigeron breweri var. typicus, commonly known as the Breweri's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to violet ray florets and white disk florets, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This species is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
