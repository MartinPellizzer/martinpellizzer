Euploca prostrata

Euploca prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate euploca, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically forms dense mats on the ground, with small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
