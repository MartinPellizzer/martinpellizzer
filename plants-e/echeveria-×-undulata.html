Echeveria × undulata

Echeveria × undulata, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, thick leaves that are often colored in shades of blue, green, and purple. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid climates.

Echeveria × undulata is popular among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
