Echinodorus ridleyi

Echinodorus ridleyi, commonly known as the red-leaf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking red to purple leaves and its ability to thrive in slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has long, slender stems that often root at the base, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps it float and resist water currents.

Echinodorus ridleyi is popular in aquariums for its ornamental value and its role in maintaining water quality.
