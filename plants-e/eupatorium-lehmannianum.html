Eupatorium lehmannianum

Eupatorium lehmannianum, commonly known as Lehmann's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade to full sun. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium lehmannianum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
