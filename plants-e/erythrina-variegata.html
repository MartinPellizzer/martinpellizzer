Erythrina variegata

Erythrina variegata, commonly known as the flame tree or variegated coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red, flame-like flowers that appear in clusters, often giving the plant a dramatic and showy appearance. The leaves are pinnate, with multiple leaflets that are typically a mix of green and white or yellow, contributing to its variegated look. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Erythrina variegata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
