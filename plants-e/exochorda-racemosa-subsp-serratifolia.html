Exochorda racemosa subsp. serratifolia

Exochorda racemosa subsp.

serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It features showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, which give it its subspecies name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
