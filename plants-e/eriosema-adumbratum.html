Eriosema adumbratum

Eriosema adumbratum, commonly known as the dark-leaved eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its leaves are often dark green and have a velvety texture due to the dense covering of fine hairs.

Eriosema adumbratum is adapted to grow in semi-arid environments and is often found in grassland and rocky habitats.
