Etlingera grandiligulata

Etlingera grandiligulata, commonly known as the giant torch ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its large, showy inflorescences that resemble torches, with bright red bracts and intricate floral structures. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
