Erica nivenia var. longiflora

Erica nivenia var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

Erica nivenia var. longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
