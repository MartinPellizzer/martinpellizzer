Engomegoma gordonii

Engomegoma gordonii, commonly known as the Gordon's engomegoma, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture.

Engomegoma gordonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
