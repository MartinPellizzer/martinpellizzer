Elymus strigatus

Elymus strigatus, commonly known as spikegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like arrangement of flowering heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture.

Elymus strigatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
