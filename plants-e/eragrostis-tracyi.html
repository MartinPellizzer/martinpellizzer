Eragrostis tracyi

Eragrostis tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that turns reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Tracy's lovegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
