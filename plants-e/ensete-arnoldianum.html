Ensete arnoldianum

Ensete arnoldianum, commonly known as the African elephant tree, is a large, evergreen monocot native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Musaceae family, which also includes bananas and plantains. The plant is characterized by its massive, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 6 meters. Its stem is thick and fibrous, containing a high amount of cellulose, making it valuable for industrial use.

Ensete arnoldianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some African communities.
