Emilia zambiensis

Emilia zambiensis, commonly known as the Zambian emilia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, blooming in summer, and it is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Emilia zambiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
