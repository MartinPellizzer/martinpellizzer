Eleocharis palustris var. nebrodensis

Eleocharis palustris var. nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebroden sedge, is a variant of the common spike-rush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its flowering structures are composed of small, densely packed spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
