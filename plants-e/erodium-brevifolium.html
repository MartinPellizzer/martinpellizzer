Erodium brevifolium

Erodium brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features tiny, clustered pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, ovate leaves with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
