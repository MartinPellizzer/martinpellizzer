Euphorbia polycarpa

Euphorbia polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems for survival.

Euphorbia polycarpa is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
