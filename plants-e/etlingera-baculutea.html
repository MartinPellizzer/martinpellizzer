Etlingera baculutea

Etlingera baculutea, commonly known as the slender torch ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, red-orange bracts that enclose clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a vibrant green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
