Epilobium dodonaei subsp. fleischeri

Epilobium dodonaei subsp.

fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant features slender, branching stems and lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
