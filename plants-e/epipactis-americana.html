Epipactis americana

Epipactis americana, commonly known as the American helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soils. Epipactis americana is known for its ability to form dense colonies and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.

Its botanical features make it an important component of eastern forest ecosystems.
