Eugenia delpechiana

Eugenia delpechiana, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
