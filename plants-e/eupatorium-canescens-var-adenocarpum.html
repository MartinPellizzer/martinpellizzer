Eupatorium canescens var. adenocarpum

Eupatorium canescens var. adenocarpum, commonly known as the silverleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its showy white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
