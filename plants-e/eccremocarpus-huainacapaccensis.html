Eccremocarpus huainacapaccensis

Eccremocarpus huainacapaccensis, commonly known as the Huainacapaccensis gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive, showy flowers with a velvety texture and a fragrance reminiscent of vanilla. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, aiding in its propagation in the wild.
