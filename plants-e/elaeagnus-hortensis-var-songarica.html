Elaeagnus hortensis var. songarica

Elaeagnus hortensis var. songarica, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
