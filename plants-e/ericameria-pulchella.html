Ericameria pulchella

Ericameria pulchella, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic, silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. Ericameria pulchella is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and produces small, cylindrical flower heads.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
