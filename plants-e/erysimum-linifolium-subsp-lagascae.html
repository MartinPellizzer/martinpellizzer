Erysimum linifolium subsp. lagascae

Erysimum linifolium subsp.

lagascae, commonly known as the Sierra wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant features erect stems with lance-shaped, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
