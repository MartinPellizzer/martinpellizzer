Elytropus ptarmica

Elytropus ptarmica, commonly known as the lesser centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, blue to violet flowers clustered in dense heads, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Elytropus ptarmica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
