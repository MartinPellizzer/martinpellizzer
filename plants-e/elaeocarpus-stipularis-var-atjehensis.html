Elaeocarpus stipularis var. atjehensis

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. atjehensis, commonly known as the Atjeh elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
