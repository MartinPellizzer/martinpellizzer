Echinocactus spegazzinii

Echinocactus spegazzinii, commonly known as the Spegazzini's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent spines that provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal watering, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collectors.
