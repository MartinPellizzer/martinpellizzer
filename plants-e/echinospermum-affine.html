Echinospermum affine

Echinospermum affine, commonly known as the rough sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers and hairy, rough-textured stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various environments.
