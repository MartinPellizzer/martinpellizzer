Eugenia magniflora

Eugenia magniflora, commonly known as the magnolia cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Eugenia magniflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
