Euphorbia portulacoides subsp. nahuelhuapina

Euphorbia portulacoides subsp.

nahuelhuapina, commonly known as the Nahuelhuapi euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Andes of Argentina. It is characterized by its dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes.
