Eriosema cordifolium var. longibracteatum

Eriosema cordifolium var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers that are borne on slender pedicels. Its habitat typically includes moist, well-drained soils in woodland or grassland areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
