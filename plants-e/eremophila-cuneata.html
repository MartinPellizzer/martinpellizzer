Eremophila cuneata

Eremophila cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Eremophila cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
