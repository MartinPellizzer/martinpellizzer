Elaphoglossum lindbergii var. truncatum

Elaphoglossum lindbergii var. truncatum, commonly known as the truncated holly fern, is a fern species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered spore sacs on the undersides of its leaves, a feature common among ferns. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique leaf morphology and ornamental appearance make it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and collections.
