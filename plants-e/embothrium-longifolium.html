Embothrium longifolium

Embothrium longifolium, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. These flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Embothrium longifolium is valued for its ecological role and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
