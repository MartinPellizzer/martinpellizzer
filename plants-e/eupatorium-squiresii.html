Eupatorium squiresii

Eupatorium squiresii, commonly known as the Florida thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Eupatorium squiresii is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
