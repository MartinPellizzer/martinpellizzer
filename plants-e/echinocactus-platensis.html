Echinocactus platensis

Echinocactus platensis, commonly known as the fish spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces large, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its spiny, cylindrical stems are adapted for protection against herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Echinocactus platensis is also valued for its edible fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a unique flavor.
