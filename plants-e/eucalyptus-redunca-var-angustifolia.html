Eucalyptus redunca var. angustifolia

Eucalyptus redunca var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color and covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white or cream flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or heathlands.

This species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
