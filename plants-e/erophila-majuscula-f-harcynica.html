Erophila majuscula f. harcynica

Erophila majuscula f. harcynica, commonly known as the Harcynica form of the greater water plantain, is a botanical variant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant features lance-shaped, broad leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Alismataceae family.

This form is noted for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic ecosystems and as a subject of botanical study.
