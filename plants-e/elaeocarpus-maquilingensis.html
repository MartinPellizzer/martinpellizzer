Elaeocarpus maquilingensis

Elaeocarpus maquilingensis, commonly known as the Maquiling elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the mountainous regions of Luzon. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
