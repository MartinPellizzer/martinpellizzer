Eupatorium psiadiifolium

Eupatorium psiadiifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Eupatorium psiadiifolium is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
