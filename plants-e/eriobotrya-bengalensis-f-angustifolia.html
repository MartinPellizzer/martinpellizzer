Eriobotrya bengalensis f. angustifolia

Eriobotrya bengalensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf loquat, is a variant of the loquat species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its elliptical, narrow leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is typically yellow or orange, with a juicy, sweet pulp and a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
