Erica aristata var. lowii

Erica aristata var. lowii, commonly known as the Low's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
