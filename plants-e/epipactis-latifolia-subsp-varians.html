Epipactis latifolia subsp. varians

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

varians, commonly known as the broadleaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces erect, branching inflorescences with numerous small, spurred flowers.

The subspecies varians is distinguished by its slightly different leaf shape and flower structure compared to the typical form of E. latifolia.
