Erigeron acris subsp. politus

Erigeron acris subsp.

politus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its finely divided, silvery-gray leaves and daisy-like blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It is an annual or biennial species that thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a common sight in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
