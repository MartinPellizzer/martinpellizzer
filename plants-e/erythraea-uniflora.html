Erythraea uniflora

Erythraea uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower erythraea, is a rare flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in late summer, and its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited to hot, dry climates. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Erythraea uniflora is considered threatened in its natural range.
