Eugenia neibensis

Eugenia neibensis, commonly known as the Neib's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
