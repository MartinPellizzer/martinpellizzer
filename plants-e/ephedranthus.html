Ephedranthus

Ephedranthus, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with its deep root system and succulent leaves. Ephedranthus typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in desert scrublands.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
