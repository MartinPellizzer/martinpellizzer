Ericameria albida

Ericameria albida, commonly known as white ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ericameria albida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
