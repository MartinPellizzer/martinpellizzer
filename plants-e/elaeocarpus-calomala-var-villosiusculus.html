Elaeocarpus calomala var. villosiusculus

Elaeocarpus calomala var. villosiusculus, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarpus, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a slightly hairy texture on the underside, giving it its varietal name. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
