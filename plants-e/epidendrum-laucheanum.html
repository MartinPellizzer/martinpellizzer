Epidendrum laucheanum

Epidendrum laucheanum, commonly known as the Laucheanum orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing multiple stems that arise from a rhizome. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and display a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink, with a distinctive lip.

Epidendrum laucheanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
