Eriospermum tuberculatum

Eriospermum tuberculatum, commonly known as the woolly eriosperm, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or xeriscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
