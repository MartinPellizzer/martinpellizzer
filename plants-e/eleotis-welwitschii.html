Eleotis welwitschii

Eleotis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia mirabilis, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is a gymnosperm, belonging to the family Welwitschiaceae, and is characterized by its two large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to three meters in length. The plant has a thick, woody trunk and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Eleotis welwitschii is also notable for its ability to survive in extremely arid conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and roots.
