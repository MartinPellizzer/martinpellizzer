Eucalyptus eremicola

Eucalyptus eremicola, commonly known as the desert eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which appear in clusters during the warmer months. Eucalyptus eremicola is adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions, with deep root systems and a capacity to shed leaves during drought.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
