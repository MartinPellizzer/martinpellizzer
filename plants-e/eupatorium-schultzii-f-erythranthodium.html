Eupatorium schultzii f. erythranthodium

Eupatorium schultzii f. erythranthodium, commonly known as the red-flowered false boneset, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to red in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
