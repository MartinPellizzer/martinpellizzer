Epidendrum lanioides

Epidendrum lanioides, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are known for their vibrant colors, ranging from yellow to orange and red.

Epidendrum lanioides is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
