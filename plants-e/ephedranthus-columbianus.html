Ephedranthus columbianus

Ephedranthus columbianus, commonly known as the Colombian ephedra, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Colombia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae, which includes species known for their medicinal and pharmacological properties. The plant is characterized by its small, green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its potential medicinal uses, it has attracted interest from botanists and researchers alike.
