Ehrharta pilosa

Ehrharta pilosa, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in sandy or well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Ehrharta pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
