Eupatorium conyzoides var. tunariense

Eupatorium conyzoides var. tunariense, commonly known as the Tunariense boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Bolivia, Paraguay, and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks, and it can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

This species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain insect species.
