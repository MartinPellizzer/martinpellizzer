Echinochloa aristifera

Echinochloa aristifera, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas.
