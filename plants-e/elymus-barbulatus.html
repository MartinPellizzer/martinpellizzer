Elymus barbulatus

Elymus barbulatus, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Elymus barbulatus is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
