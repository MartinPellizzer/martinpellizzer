Euphorbia teheranica

Euphorbia teheranica, commonly known as the Persian spurge, is a succulent plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that serve as a means of attracting pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
