Eriocaulon suishaense

Eriocaulon suishaense, commonly known as the Suisha marsh plant, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern China.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which includes many aquatic and semi-aquatic species. The plant is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in marshy or swampy areas.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
