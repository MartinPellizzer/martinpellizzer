Eupatorium magdalenae

Eupatorium magdalenae, commonly known as Magdalena's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium magdalenae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
