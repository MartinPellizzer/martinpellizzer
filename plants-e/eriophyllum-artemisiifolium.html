Eriophyllum artemisiifolium

Eriophyllum artemisiifolium, commonly known as brittlestem goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriophyllum artemisiifolium is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.
