Euryops anthemoides subsp. anthemoides

Euryops anthemoides subsp.

anthemoides, commonly known as the coastal daisy, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South Africa. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and dense foliage.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late winter to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and adapted to withstand coastal salt spray and strong winds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in coastal landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
