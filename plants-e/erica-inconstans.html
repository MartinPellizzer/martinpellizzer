Erica inconstans

Erica inconstans, commonly known as the variable heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Erica inconstans is known for its adaptability to varying environmental conditions, which contributes to its wide distribution.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and early autumn.
