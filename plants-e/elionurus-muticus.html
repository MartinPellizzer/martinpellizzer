Elionurus muticus

Elionurus muticus, commonly known as the silent grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Elionurus muticus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem, supporting various insect and bird species.
