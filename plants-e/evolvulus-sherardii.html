Evolvulus sherardii

Evolvulus sherardii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
