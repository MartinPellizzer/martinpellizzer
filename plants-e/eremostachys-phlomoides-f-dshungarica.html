Eremostachys phlomoides f. dshungarica

Eremostachys phlomoides f. dshungarica, commonly known as the Siberian tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold regions of Siberia and Mongolia.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems topped with clusters of small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, arctic or alpine environments.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
