Eugenia maroana

Eugenia maroana, commonly known as the Maroona apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to New Guinea.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, apple-like berries. These berries are typically yellow or orange when ripe and are used in traditional cuisines for their sweet and tart flavor.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in subtropical regions.
