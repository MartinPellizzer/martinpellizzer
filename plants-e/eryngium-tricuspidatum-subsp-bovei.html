Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp. bovei

Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp.

bovei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a thistle, surrounded by bracts.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
