Echinochloa notabilis

Echinochloa notabilis, commonly known as the purple seed grass, is an annual grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it both a weed and a potential forage species.
