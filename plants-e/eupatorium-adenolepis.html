Eupatorium adenolepis

Eupatorium adenolepis, commonly known as the Hawaiian ironwood, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in coastal and lowland habitats, often growing in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
