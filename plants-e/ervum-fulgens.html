Ervum fulgens

Ervum fulgens, commonly known as the golden bean or the sunflower bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional cuisines. Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants.

Its leaves are trifoliate, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil.
