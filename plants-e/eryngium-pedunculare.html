Eryngium pedunculare

Eryngium pedunculare, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical, spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

Eryngium pedunculare is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
