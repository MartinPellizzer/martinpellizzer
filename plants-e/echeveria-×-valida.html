Echeveria × valida

Echeveria × valida, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
