Eriocaulon incanum

Eriocaulon incanum, commonly known as the white-headed spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to white flowers. Its inflorescence is distinctive, with the female flowers being more prominent and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Eriocaulon incanum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
