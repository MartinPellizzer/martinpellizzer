Eria decipiens

Eria decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to mimic the appearance of other plants, a trait that may aid in its pollination strategy. Its flowers typically bloom in clusters and are often greenish or pale yellow in color.

Eria decipiens is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
