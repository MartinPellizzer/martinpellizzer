Erucastrum varium f. campestre

Erucastrum varium f. campestre, commonly known as the field erucoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes and its hairy, erect stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slight pubescence.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and is often found in temperate regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
