Encyclia boothiana var. erythronioides

Encyclia boothiana var. erythronioides, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves with a deep green color and prominent red veins. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a yellow lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
