Echinochloa hirta

Echinochloa hirta, commonly known as hairy barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown spikelets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural fields.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a challenging species to control in crops.
