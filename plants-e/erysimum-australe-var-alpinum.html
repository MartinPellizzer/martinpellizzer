Erysimum australe var. alpinum

Erysimum australe var. alpinum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the alpine regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing adaptations to cold and harsh environments. Its bright yellow or orange flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
