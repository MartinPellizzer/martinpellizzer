Eryngium nudicaule var. napuliferum

Eryngium nudicaule var. napuliferum, commonly known as the Naples thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, white flowers in the summer months.
