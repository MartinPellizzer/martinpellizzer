Eugenia mimus

Eugenia mimus, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that contain seeds.

Eugenia mimus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
