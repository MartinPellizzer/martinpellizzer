Ericoides strigosum

Ericoides strigosum, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths, heathers, and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its flowers, typically pink or white, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Ericoides strigosum is an important species for ecological stability, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
