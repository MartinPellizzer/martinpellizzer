Eremopoa glareosa

Eremopoa glareosa, commonly known as the shining eremopoa, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to violet flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils.

Eremopoa glareosa is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
