Ecastaphyllum richardi

Ecastaphyllum richardi, commonly known as the red-tipped pine or red-tipped pine tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and its distinctive reddish-brown bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are typically dark green with a reddish tinge at the base, providing a striking visual contrast. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain the plant's seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
