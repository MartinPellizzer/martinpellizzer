Echinospermum borbonicum

Echinospermum borbonicum, commonly known as the Madagascar thistle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has gray-green, hairy leaves. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite heads, and it produces seeds enclosed in a hardened, spiky shell.

Echinospermum borbonicum is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems.
