Euphorbia heteropodum var. formosa

Euphorbia heteropodum var. formosa, commonly known as the Christmas star, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing, succulent plant native to South Africa. The plant features a rosette of fleshy, spiny leaves that are typically green with a reddish tinge. It produces small, yellowish flower clusters called cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This species is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
