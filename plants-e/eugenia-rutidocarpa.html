Eugenia rutidocarpa

Eugenia rutidocarpa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
