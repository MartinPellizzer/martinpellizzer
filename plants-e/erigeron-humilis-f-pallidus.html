Erigeron humilis f. pallidus

Erigeron humilis f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale daisy, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender stems and small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support pollinators.
