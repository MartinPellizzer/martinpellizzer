Ervatamia flabelliformis

Ervatamia flabelliformis, commonly known as the fanleaf ervatamia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a fan-like pattern, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
