Erythrodes pumila

Erythrodes pumila, commonly known as the dwarf redwood, is a small shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are small, leathery, and dark green, while its flowers are bell-shaped and usually pink or red in color.

Erythrodes pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
