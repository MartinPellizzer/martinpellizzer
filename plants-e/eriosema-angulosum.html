Eriosema angulosum

Eriosema angulosum, commonly known as the angular eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive angular appearance, contributing to its common name. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Eriosema angulosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
