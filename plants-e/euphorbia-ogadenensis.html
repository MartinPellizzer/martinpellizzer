Euphorbia ogadenensis

Euphorbia ogadenensis, commonly known as the Ogaden spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water, adapting it to arid environments. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with similar medicinal and ornamental uses.

Euphorbia ogadenensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
