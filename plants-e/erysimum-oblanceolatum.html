Erysimum oblanceolatum

Erysimum oblanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum oblanceolatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
