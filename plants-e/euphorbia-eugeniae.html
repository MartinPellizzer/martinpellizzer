Euphorbia eugeniae

Euphorbia eugeniae, commonly known as the Eugenia's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collectors.
