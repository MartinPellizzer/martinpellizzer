Ericoides sicifolium

Ericoides sicifolium, commonly known as the leafless heath, is a unique plant species characterized by its lack of true leaves.

Instead, it possesses modified stems called phylloclades that perform photosynthesis. This plant is typically found in nutrient-poor, acidic soils, often in heathland or mountainous regions. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs adapted to harsh environments.

Its botanical features reflect its specialized adaptation to low-nutrient conditions, making it an important component of certain ecosystems.
