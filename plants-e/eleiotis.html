Eleiotis

Eleiotis, commonly known as the wild thyme, is a small, perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features creeping stems that form dense mats, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal and culinary uses.

Eleiotis is valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
