Escobaria tuberculosa subsp. varicolor

Escobaria tuberculosa subsp.

varicolor, commonly known as the variegated stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States. It features fleshy, blue-green leaves with distinctive yellow or white markings.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its compact growth habit and striking foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
