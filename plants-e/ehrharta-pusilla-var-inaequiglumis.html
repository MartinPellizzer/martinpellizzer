Ehrharta pusilla var. inaequiglumis

Ehrharta pusilla var. inaequiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved buffalo grass, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive bluish-gray hue, and its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for ornamental or low-maintenance landscaping.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, and its growth habit is typically clump-forming with a dense, mat-like appearance.
