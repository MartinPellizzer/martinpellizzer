Epidendrum platystomoides

Epidendrum platystomoides, commonly known as the broad-mouthed epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate to high humidity to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse environments.
