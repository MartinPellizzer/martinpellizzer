Erysimum amurense f. flavum

Erysimum amurense f. flavum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically borne in clusters on long, branched stems. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves that are hairy and grayish-green in color. Native to Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
