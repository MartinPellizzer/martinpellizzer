Euphorbia gerardiana var. dentata

Euphorbia gerardiana var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
