Empetrum nigrum subsp. asiaticum

Empetrum nigrum subsp.

asiaticum, commonly known as the Asian crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia. It features small, dark blue berries that are typically covered in a white, waxy coating.

The plant has dense, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is closely related to the common crowberry and shares similar ecological and botanical characteristics.
