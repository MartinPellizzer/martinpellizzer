Eupatorium roraimense

Eupatorium roraimense, commonly known as Roraima boneset, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments and thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are typically opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eupatorium roraimense is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies for various ailments.
