Eryngium plantaginifolium

Eryngium plantaginifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles, which are surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
