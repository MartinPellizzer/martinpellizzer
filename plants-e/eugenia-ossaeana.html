Eugenia ossaeana

Eugenia ossaeana, commonly known as the Ossaeana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, globose berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia ossaeana is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
