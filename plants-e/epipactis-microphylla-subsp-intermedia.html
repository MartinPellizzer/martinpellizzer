Epipactis microphylla subsp. intermedia

Epipactis microphylla subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the small-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its relatively small, elliptical leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial herb that produces a single spike of flowers, often with a sweet, honey-like scent.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
