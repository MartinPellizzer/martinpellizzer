Eucnemia

Eucnemia, commonly known as the Australian eucalyptus, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their aromatic oils and distinctive, often rough, bark. Many species of Eucnemia are evergreen trees or shrubs, with lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers. They play a significant role in Australian ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.

Some species are also valued for their timber and essential oils used in aromatherapy and medicine.
