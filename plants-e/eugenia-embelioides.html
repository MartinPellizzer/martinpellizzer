Eugenia embelioides

Eugenia embelioides, commonly known as the fragrant wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia embelioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
