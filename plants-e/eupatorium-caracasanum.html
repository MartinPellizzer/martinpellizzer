Eupatorium caracasanum

Eupatorium caracasanum, commonly known as the Caribbean boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium caracasanum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
