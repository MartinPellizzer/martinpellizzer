Euphorbia crispata

Euphorbia crispata, commonly known as the zigzag spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Euphorbia crispata is also known for its ability to tolerate full sun and well-drained soil.
