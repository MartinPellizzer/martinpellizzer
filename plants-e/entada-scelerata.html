Entada scelerata

Entada scelerata, commonly known as the African locust bean tree, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, bipinnate leaves and long, cylindrical pods that contain hard, kidney-shaped seeds. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and has a distinctive, straight trunk with a smooth, greyish bark. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and are arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agroforestry systems.
