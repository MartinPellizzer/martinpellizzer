Erythroxylum vaginatum

Erythroxylum vaginatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf mallow, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Botanically, it is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological features.
