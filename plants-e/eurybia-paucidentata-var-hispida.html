Eurybia paucidentata var. hispida

Eurybia paucidentata var. hispida, commonly known as the hispid sunflower, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into achene fruits.

The variety hispida is distinguished by its dense, bristly hairs, which provide protection against herbivores and environmental stress.
