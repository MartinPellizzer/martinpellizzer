Eugenia springiana

Eugenia springiana, commonly known as the spring eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Eugenia springiana is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
