Echinocereus cinerascens subsp. septentrionalis

Echinocereus cinerascens subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically blue-green in color and covered with spines.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Its structure and growth patterns make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
