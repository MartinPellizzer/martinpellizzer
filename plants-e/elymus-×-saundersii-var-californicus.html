Elymus × saundersii var. californicus

Elymus × saundersii var. californicus, commonly known as California reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fluffy panicles that produce abundant seeds. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes, coastal bluffs, and disturbed areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is well adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
