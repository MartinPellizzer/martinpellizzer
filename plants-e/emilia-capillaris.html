Emilia capillaris

Emilia capillaris, commonly known as common emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces small, delicate yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pinkish hue. Emilia capillaris is known for its feathery, finely divided leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and wildflower mixes.
