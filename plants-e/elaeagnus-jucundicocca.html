Elaeagnus jucundicocca

Elaeagnus jucundicocca, commonly known as the sweet acanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for hedging and landscape design.
