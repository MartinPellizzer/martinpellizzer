Echinofossulocactus anfractuosus

Echinofossulocactus anfractuosus, commonly known as the Mexican rock cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and dry environments, with a deep root system for water retention.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
