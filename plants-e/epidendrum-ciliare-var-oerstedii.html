Epidendrum ciliare var. oerstedii

Epidendrum ciliare var. oerstedii, commonly known as the Oersted's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, hairy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine, silky hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
