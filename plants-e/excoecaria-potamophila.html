Excoecaria potamophila

Excoecaria potamophila, commonly known as the river croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near rivers and streams.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
