Erythrodes dolichostachya

Erythrodes dolichostachya, commonly known as the long-stalked redflower plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking red floral bracts. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth surface with a reddish tinge.

The inflorescence consists of elongated spikes bearing small, tubular flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
