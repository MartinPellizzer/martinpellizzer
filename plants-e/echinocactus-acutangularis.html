Echinocactus acutangularis

Echinocactus acutangularis, commonly known as the Angular Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, angular tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
