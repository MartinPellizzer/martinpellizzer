Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp. acuta

Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf river red gum, is a variant of the river red gum species native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow. It is well adapted to riparian environments and is often found along riverbanks and in wetlands.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.
