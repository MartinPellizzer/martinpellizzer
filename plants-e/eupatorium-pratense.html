Eupatorium pratense

Eupatorium pratense, commonly known as field eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and clustered in flat-topped inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.
