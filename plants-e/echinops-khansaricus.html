Echinops khansaricus

Echinops khansaricus, commonly known as the Persian globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble a globe, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Echinops khansaricus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
