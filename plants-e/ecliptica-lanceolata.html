Ecliptica lanceolata

Ecliptica lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikes. It is known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
