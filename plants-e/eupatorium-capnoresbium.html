Eupatorium capnoresbium

Eupatorium capnoresbium, commonly known as the ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium capnoresbium is known for its fragrant blooms and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
