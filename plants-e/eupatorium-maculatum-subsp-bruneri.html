Eupatorium maculatum subsp. bruneri

Eupatorium maculatum subsp.

bruneri, commonly known as spotted Joe-pye weed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it is known for its showy, fluffy flower heads. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded areas and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
