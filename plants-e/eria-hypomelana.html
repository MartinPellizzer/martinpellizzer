Eria hypomelana

Eria hypomelana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Eria hypomelana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
