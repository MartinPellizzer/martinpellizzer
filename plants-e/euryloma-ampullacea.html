Euryloma ampullacea

Euryloma ampullacea, commonly known as the glassy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are often blue or violet in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Euryloma ampullacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and attractive blooms.
