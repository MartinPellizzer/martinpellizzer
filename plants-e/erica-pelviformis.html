Erica pelviformis

Erica pelviformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica pelviformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
