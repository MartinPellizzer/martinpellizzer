Eugenia glazioviana

Eugenia glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are edible.

Eugenia glazioviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
