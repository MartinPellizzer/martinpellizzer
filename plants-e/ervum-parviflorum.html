Ervum parviflorum

Ervum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered vetch, is a perennial legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Ervum parviflorum is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in soil enrichment.
