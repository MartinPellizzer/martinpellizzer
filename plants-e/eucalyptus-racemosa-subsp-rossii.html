Eucalyptus racemosa subsp. rossii

Eucalyptus racemosa subsp.

rossii, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and its distinctive white or pale pink flowers that appear in racemes.

The bark is often smooth and peels in thin layers, revealing a mottled or flaky appearance. This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and near-coastal regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its essential oils used in medicinal and aromatic applications.
