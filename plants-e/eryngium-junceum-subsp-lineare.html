Eryngium junceum subsp. lineare

Eryngium junceum subsp.

lineare, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical structure includes a stout, branching stem and a basal rosette of leaves.
