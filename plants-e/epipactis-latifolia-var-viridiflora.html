Epipactis latifolia var. viridiflora

Epipactis latifolia var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered broad-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

This orchid is known for its ability to form mycorrhizal associations with fungi, aiding in nutrient uptake.
