Ericoila decemfida

Ericoila decemfida, commonly known as the ten-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, leathery leaves are arranged in whorls of ten, giving it its scientific name.

Ericoila decemfida produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
