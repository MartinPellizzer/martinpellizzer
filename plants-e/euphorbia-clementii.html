Euphorbia clementii

Euphorbia clementii, commonly known as the Christ's Thorn or Crown of Thorns, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and yellowish-green sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and can survive with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
