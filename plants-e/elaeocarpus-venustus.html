Elaeocarpus venustus

Elaeocarpus venustus, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
