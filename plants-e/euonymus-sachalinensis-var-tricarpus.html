Euonymus sachalinensis var. tricarpus

Euonymus sachalinensis var. tricarpus, commonly known as the Sachalin euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by distinctive three-lobed, red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
