Elsholtzia ciliata

Elsholtzia ciliata, commonly known as Chinese germander, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of East Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features slender, aromatic stems and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink or purple. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Elsholtzia ciliata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits and is also used in ornamental gardening.
