Erythraea maritima var. erubescens

Erythraea maritima var. erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered sea erythraea, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and sandy environments.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is often found in dune habitats and is well-suited to withstand wind and salt spray.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
