Eriosorus flexuosus

Eriosorus flexuosus, commonly known as the flexible fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, arching fronds that often curl and twist, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces numerous small, lance-shaped leaves.

Eriosorus flexuosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
