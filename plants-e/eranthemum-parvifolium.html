Eranthemum parvifolium

Eranthemum parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaf eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically arranged in whorls along the stems. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Eranthemum parvifolium thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
