Eragrostis multiflora f. violacea

Eragrostis multiflora f. violacea, commonly known as violet-flowered lovegrass, is a variant of the lovegrass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and its striking violet to purple flower heads, which distinguish it from the typical green-flowered form. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
