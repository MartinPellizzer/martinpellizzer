Eryngium yuccifolium var. yuccifolium

Eryngium yuccifolium var. yuccifolium, commonly known as the yucca-leaved eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence and narrow, sword-like leaves that resemble those of yucca plants. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to arid landscape designs.
