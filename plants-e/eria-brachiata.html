Eria brachiata

Eria brachiata, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Eria brachiata is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail in garden settings.
