Enneapogon lophotrichus

Enneapogon lophotrichus, commonly known as the tufted lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Enneapogon lophotrichus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
