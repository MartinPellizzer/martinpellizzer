Ewyckia latifolia

Ewyckia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ewyckia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom during the warm season. Ewyckia latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
