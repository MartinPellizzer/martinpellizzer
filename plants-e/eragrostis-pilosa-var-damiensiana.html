Eragrostis pilosa var. damiensiana

Eragrostis pilosa var. damiensiana, commonly known as Damien's hairy grama, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, cylindrical seed heads. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in poor soils. It grows in open grasslands and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for restoration projects.
