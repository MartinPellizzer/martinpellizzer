Erigeron primuloides

Erigeron primuloides, commonly known as the blueweed or primrose weed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage is generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
