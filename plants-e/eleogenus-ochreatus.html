Eleogenus ochreatus

Eleogenus ochreatus, commonly known as the golden tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically narrow and needle-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Eleogenus ochreatus is also noted for its role in stabilizing sandy soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
