Echinocereus viridiflorus var. chloranthus

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. chloranthus, commonly known as the green-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically green or blue-green in color, with spines that are often whitish or yellowish. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is notable for its unique coloration and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in xeriscaping.
