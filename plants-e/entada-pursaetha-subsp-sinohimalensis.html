Entada pursaetha subsp. sinohimalensis

Entada pursaetha subsp.

sinohimalensis, commonly known as the Himalayan drumstick tree, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Asia. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pods that resemble drumsticks.

The plant produces yellow flowers and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

The tree is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in various cultures.
