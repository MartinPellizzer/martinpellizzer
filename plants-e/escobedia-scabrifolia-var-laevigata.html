Escobedia scabrifolia var. laevigata

Escobedia scabrifolia var. laevigata, commonly known as the smoothleaf escobedia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and fleshy, often spiny stems that store water. The leaves are typically small, thick, and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by distinctive, cylindrical fruits.

This plant is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often used in rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
