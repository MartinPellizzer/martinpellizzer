Erythrina enneandra

Erythrina enneandra, commonly known as the Crimson Flame Tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five pairs of elliptical leaflets. Its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod that splits open when mature.

Erythrina enneandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
