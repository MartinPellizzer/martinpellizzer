Erianthus hookeri

Erianthus hookeri, commonly known as the golden grass or hooker's grass, is a species of tall perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its stiff, upright growth habit and its distinctive golden-green foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. Erianthus hookeri is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along creek banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
