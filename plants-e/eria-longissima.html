Eria longissima

Eria longissima, commonly known as the long-stemmed orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a delicate, fragrant scent.

Eria longissima is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
