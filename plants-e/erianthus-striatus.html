Erianthus striatus

Erianthus striatus, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are lined with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale green or yellowish flowers in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Erianthus striatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
