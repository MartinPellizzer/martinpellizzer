Escallonia modesta

Escallonia modesta, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Escallonia modesta is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its ease of cultivation.
