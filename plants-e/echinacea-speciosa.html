Echinacea speciosa

Echinacea speciosa, commonly known as the coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central cone. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. Its root system is fibrous and contributes to its medicinal properties.

Echinacea speciosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.
