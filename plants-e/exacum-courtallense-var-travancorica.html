Exacum courtallense var. travancorica

Exacum courtallense var. travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
