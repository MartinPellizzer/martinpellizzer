Ericameria nauseosa var. ceruminosa

Ericameria nauseosa var. ceruminosa, commonly known as the yellow bush sage, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and grayish-green foliage, which are covered in a fine, woolly layer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal sage scrub habitats. Its leaves are aromatic and contain volatile oils, contributing to its medicinal and ecological significance.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
