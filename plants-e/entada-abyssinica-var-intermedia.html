Entada abyssinica var. intermedia

Entada abyssinica var. intermedia, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and distinctive, horn-like seed pods. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seeds are encased in thick, woody pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in local crafts and as a source of fiber.
