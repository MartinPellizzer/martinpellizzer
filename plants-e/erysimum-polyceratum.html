Erysimum polyceratum

Erysimum polyceratum, commonly known as the many-seedling wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces numerous small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds, contributing to its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erysimum polyceratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
