Eugenia ogoouensis

Eugenia ogoouensis, commonly known as the Cameroon apple or African snowball, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, white, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
