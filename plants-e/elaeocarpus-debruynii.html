Elaeocarpus debruynii

Elaeocarpus debruynii, commonly known as the De Bruyn's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive blue-black berries. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional construction and crafts.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various wildlife species.
