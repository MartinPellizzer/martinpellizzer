Echinochloa hispidula

Echinochloa hispidula, commonly known as rough beardgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Echinochloa hispidula is an annual plant that plays a role in soil stabilization and serves as a forage resource for livestock.
