Eritrichium rupestre subsp. sajanense

Eritrichium rupestre subsp.

sajanense, commonly known as the Sajansky alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically features slender stems with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
