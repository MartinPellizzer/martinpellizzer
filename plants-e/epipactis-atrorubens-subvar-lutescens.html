Epipactis atrorubens subvar. lutescens

Epipactis atrorubens subvar. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-sheath helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green leaves and a tall, slender flowering stem that bears clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is adapted to thrive in acidic soils. Its flowers are pollinated by bees and other insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
