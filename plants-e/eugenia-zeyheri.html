Eugenia zeyheri

Eugenia zeyheri, commonly known as the wild guava or sour guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are typically sour and used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, tropical climates.

Eugenia zeyheri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
