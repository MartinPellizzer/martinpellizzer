Eriococcus gracilis

Eriococcus gracilis, commonly known as the slender eriococcus, is a species of scale insect belonging to the family Eriococcidae.

It is characterized by its small, soft body covered in a waxy, cotton-like coating. This plant parasite typically infests a variety of host plants, including ornamental shrubs and fruit trees. The colonies often form dense, felt-like masses on the stems and leaves of the host.

Due to its ability to secrete honeydew, it can lead to the growth of sooty mold, affecting the plant's health and appearance.
