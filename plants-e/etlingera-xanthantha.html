Etlingera xanthantha

Etlingera xanthantha, commonly known as the yellow torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking orange-red bracts that resemble torches, which are actually modified leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a fragrant scent that attracts pollinators.

Etlingera xanthantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
