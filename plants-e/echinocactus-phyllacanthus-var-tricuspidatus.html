Echinocactus phyllacanthus var. tricuspidatus

Echinocactus phyllacanthus var. tricuspidatus, commonly known as the tricuspidate hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by spherical, red fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and collections.
