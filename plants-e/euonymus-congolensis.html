Euonymus congolensis

Euonymus congolensis, commonly known as the African burning bush, is a shrub native to central and western Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking, colorful foliage that changes with the seasons. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are a food source for birds and small mammals.

This plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness in various climates.
