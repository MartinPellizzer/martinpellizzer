Escobrittonia

Escobrittonia, commonly known as the "Christmas cactus," is a unique succulent plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the genus Schlumbergera and is characterized by its segmented, leaf-like stems that form a branching structure. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically during the holiday season. Escobrittonia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ability to bloom reliably makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
