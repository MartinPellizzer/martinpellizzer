Eriophorum angustifolium f. scammanianum

Eriophorum angustifolium f. scammanianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cottongrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and dense, white to pale pink inflorescences that resemble cotton. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic habitats such as bogs, fens, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight sheen, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in northern regions and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
