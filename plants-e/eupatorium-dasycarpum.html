Eupatorium dasycarpum

Eupatorium dasycarpum, commonly known as roughfruit eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, rough, dry fruits that are covered in stiff hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium dasycarpum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
