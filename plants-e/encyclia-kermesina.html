Encyclia kermesina

Encyclia kermesina, commonly known as the Kermesina orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow to reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Encyclia kermesina has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its leaves are usually fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern.

It prefers shady, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
