Elaeocarpus branderhorstii

Elaeocarpus branderhorstii, commonly known as the Brandehorst's elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in white or pale pink. Its fruits are woody, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
