Evolvulus

Evolvulus, commonly known as morning glory, is a genus of flowering plants in the Convolvulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive spiral-shaped flowers and heart-shaped leaves. They are native to the Americas but have been introduced to many other regions around the world. Evolvulus species are often cultivated for their ornamental value and can be found in gardens and wild areas.

Some species are also used in traditional medicine.
