Echinocactus recurvus var. solenacanthus

Echinocactus recurvus var. solenacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and xeriscaping.
