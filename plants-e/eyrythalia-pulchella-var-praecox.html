Eyrythalia pulchella var. praecox

Eyrythalia pulchella var. praecox, commonly known as early redflower erythalia, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and slender, branching stems. The plant typically blooms in early spring, making it a notable species in seasonal flora. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological study.
