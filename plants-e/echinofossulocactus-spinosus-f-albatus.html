Echinofossulocactus spinosus f. albatus

Echinofossulocactus spinosus f. albatus, commonly known as the white-furred echinofossulocactus, is a rare and striking cactus species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, white, woolly covering that covers the entire plant, giving it a unique and almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental value. Its slow growth rate and compact size make it suitable for cultivation in controlled environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a challenging plant for collectors and botanists alike.
