Epilobium roseum f. umbrosum

Epilobium roseum f. umbrosum, commonly known as the shade-loving willowherb, is a variant of the common willowherb.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in loose clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer. This form is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments, often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically valuable plant in woodland ecosystems.
