Eriogonum proliferum subsp. anserinum

Eriogonum proliferum subsp.

anserinum, commonly known as the Sierra sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and mountain slopes.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
