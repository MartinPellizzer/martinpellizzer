Erica dominans

Erica dominans, commonly known as the common heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica dominans is known for its ability to form dense thickets, making it a valuable plant in stabilizing soils in coastal and sandy environments.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and early autumn.
