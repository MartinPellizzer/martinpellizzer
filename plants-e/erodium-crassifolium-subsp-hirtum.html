Erodium crassifolium subsp. hirtum

Erodium crassifolium subsp.

hirtum, commonly known as hairy erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small annual herb with slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gardens.

The hairy texture of its leaves and stems is a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.
