Euphorbia szovitsii var. szovitsii

Euphorbia szovitsii var. szovitsii, commonly known as the Szovits's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the summer months, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a powdery coating, and it has a low maintenance requirement, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a potted plant.

Euphorbia szovitsii var. szovitsii is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
