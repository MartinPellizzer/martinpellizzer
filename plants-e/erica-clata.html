Erica clata

Erica clata, commonly known as the small heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in late summer and are borne in clusters at the tips of the branches.

Erica clata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
