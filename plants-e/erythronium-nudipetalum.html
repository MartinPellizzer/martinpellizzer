Erythronium nudipetalum

Erythronium nudipetalum, commonly known as the naked-petaled dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, rhizomatous root system and is often found in moist, shaded woodland habitats. Its leaves are lanceolate and emit a mild, somewhat unpleasant odor when crushed.

Erythronium nudipetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
