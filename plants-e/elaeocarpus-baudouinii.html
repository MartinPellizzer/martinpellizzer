Elaeocarpus baudouinii

Elaeocarpus baudouinii, commonly known as the Baudouin's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
