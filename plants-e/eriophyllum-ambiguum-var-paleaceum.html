Eriophyllum ambiguum var. paleaceum

Eriophyllum ambiguum var. paleaceum, commonly known as the paleleaf goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its low-growing, shrub-like structure. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal and inland scrub environments.
