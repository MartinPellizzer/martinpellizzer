Erythrina ulei

Erythrina ulei, commonly known as the scarlet coral tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its pods are long, narrow, and contain several seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and public spaces.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
