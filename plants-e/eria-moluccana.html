Eria moluccana

Eria moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are often fragrant. Eria moluccana thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

It is a popular choice for botanical gardens due to its unique appearance and ornamental value.
