Echinochloa coarctata var. ferax

Echinochloa coarctata var. ferax, commonly known as broadleaf signalgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the warm temperate and tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, flat leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. It has a fibrous root system and is drought-tolerant, allowing it to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and weed management practices.
