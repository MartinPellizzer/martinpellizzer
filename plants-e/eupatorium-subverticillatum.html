Eupatorium subverticillatum

Eupatorium subverticillatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically found in moist, woodland habitats and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value. It has a tall, upright growth habit and can reach heights of up to 6 feet.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly fuzzy texture, adding to its distinctive appearance.
