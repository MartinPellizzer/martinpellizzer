Epidendrum lambeauanum

Epidendrum lambeauanum, commonly known as the Lambeau's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces showy, long-lasting flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the warm season.

Its pseudobulbs and linear leaves are adapted to its tropical climate, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates.
