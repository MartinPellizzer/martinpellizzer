Ericameria nauseosa var. oreophila

Ericameria nauseosa var. oreophila, commonly known as the mountain goldenweed, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and aromatic foliage, which contain compounds responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a dense covering of fine hairs.

Ericameria nauseosa var. oreophila plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
