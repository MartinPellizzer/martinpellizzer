Erythroxylum novogranatense

Erythroxylum novogranatense, commonly known as the red evergreen or Colombian rose, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by red, fleshy berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
