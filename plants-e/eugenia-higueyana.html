Eugenia higueyana

Eugenia higueyana, commonly known as the Higuey hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and limestone soils. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or white in color. Its fruit is a small, round berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia higueyana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
