Epilobium × sarmentosum

Epilobium × sarmentosum, commonly known as willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often prostrate stems that produce numerous small, pink to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in spikes.

Epilobium × sarmentosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
