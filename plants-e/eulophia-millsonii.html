Eulophia millsonii

Eulophia millsonii, commonly known as the Millson's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces a series of leaves and flower spikes. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to yellow and pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
