Eugenia bumelioides

Eugenia bumelioides, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
