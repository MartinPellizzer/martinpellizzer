Elytrigia juncea subsp. bessarabica

Elytrigia juncea subsp.

bessarabica, commonly known as the Bessarabian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Eastern Europe and parts of Russia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are often hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
