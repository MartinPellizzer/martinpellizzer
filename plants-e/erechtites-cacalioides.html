Erechtites cacalioides

Erechtites cacalioides, commonly known as the golden coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a prominent, spiky central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often found in moist, open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
