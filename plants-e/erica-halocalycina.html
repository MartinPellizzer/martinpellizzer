Erica halocalycina

Erica halocalycina, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

Erica halocalycina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
