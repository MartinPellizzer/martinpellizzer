Eutacta cunninghamii

Eutacta cunninghamii, commonly known as the grey box, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to south-eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its greyish, fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the autumn season. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Eutacta cunninghamii is an important component of the local flora and provides habitat for various native fauna.
