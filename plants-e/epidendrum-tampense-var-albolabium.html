Epidendrum tampense var. albolabium

Epidendrum tampense var. albolabium, commonly known as the White-Lipped Tampense Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

This variety is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
