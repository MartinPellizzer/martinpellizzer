Echinochloa crus-galli var. kasaharae

Echinochloa crus-galli var. kasaharae, commonly known as Japanese barnyardgrass, is a variant of the common barnyardgrass species.

It is an annual grassy weed native to Asia, particularly Japan, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelets that form dense clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a robust root system and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a persistent weed in cultivated areas.

Its rapid growth and high seed production contribute to its invasive potential in different ecosystems.
