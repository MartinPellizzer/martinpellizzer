Eragrostis brasiliensis

Eragrostis brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian bluestem, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It typically grows in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink. It is well adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate poor soil quality.

Eragrostis brasiliensis is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
