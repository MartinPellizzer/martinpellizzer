Enterolobium cyclocarpum

Enterolobium cyclocarpum, commonly known as the "squirrel pea" or "bottle tree," is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bottle-shaped woody stems and compound leaves with a feathery appearance. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers followed by woody, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
