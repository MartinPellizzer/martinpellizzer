Erysimum ochroleucum var. longisiliquum

Erysimum ochroleucum var. longisiliquum, commonly known as the long silique wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

The variety longisiliquum is distinguished by its elongated, slender pods that contain the seeds.
