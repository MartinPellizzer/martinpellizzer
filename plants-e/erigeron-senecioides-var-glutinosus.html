Erigeron senecioides var. glutinosus

Erigeron senecioides var. glutinosus, commonly known as sticky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is notable for its sticky, glandular hairs that cover its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive texture. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and stream banks.

Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
