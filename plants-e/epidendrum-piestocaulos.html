Epidendrum piestocaulos

Epidendrum piestocaulos, commonly known as the pink-veined Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with pink or red veins. The plant produces pendulous flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum piestocaulos is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
