Eucalyptus haemastoma var. inophloia

Eucalyptus haemastoma var. inophloia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in groups of three. Its bark is often flaky or peeling, and it is adapted to dry, sandy soils.

This species is part of the Eucalyptus genus, known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in Australian ecosystems.
