Echium confusum var. bracteatum

Echium confusum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of purple to blue flowers. The plant produces distinctive bracts that resemble leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
