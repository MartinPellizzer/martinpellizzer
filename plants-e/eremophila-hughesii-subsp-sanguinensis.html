Eremophila hughesii subsp. sanguinensis

Eremophila hughesii subsp.

sanguinensis, commonly known as the red-flowered emu bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native habitats.
