Erigeron ruderalis

Erigeron ruderalis, commonly known as the rough blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and waste areas, showing a strong adaptability to harsh environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture and aiding in water conservation.
