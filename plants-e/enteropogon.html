Enteropogon

Enteropogon, commonly known as the spear grass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The flowers are typically arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Many species of Enteropogon are found in warm climates, often growing in grasslands or along coastal areas.

These plants are sometimes used in traditional medicine and have ecological importance as a food source for various wildlife.
