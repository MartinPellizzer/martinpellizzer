Erica forsteri

Erica forsteri, commonly known as the Forster's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in spring and summer. Erica forsteri is well adapted to acidic and nutrient-poor soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
