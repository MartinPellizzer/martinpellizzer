Eucalyptus paniculata

Eucalyptus paniculata, commonly known as the white ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
