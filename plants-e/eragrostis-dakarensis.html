Eragrostis dakarensis

Eragrostis dakarensis, commonly known as the Kori Kori, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis dakarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some traditional practices.
