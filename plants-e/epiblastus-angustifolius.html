Epiblastus angustifolius

Epiblastus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epiblastus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually white or pale green, and bloom in the spring.

Epiblastus angustifolius is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora.
