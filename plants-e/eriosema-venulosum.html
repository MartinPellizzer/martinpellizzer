Eriosema venulosum

Eriosema venulosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a soft, hairy texture on the leaves and stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Eriosema venulosum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
