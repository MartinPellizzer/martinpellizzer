Euphorbia arguta var. dasycarpa

Euphorbia arguta var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering of fine hairs on its stems and leaves, which helps to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, making it well adapted to survive in drought conditions.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
