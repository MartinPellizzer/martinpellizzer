Euphorbia erythroclada

Euphorbia erythroclada, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that often have a reddish or purple hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia erythroclada is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, which helps it survive in harsh conditions.
