Eremophila aureivisca

Eremophila aureivisca, commonly known as the golden eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its drought-resistant traits. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Eremophila aureivisca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
