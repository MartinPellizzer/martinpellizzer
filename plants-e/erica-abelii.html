Erica abelii

Erica abelii, commonly known as the Heath or Erica, is a flowering plant native to the western and southern regions of the United States.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing fragrant white, pink, or purple flowers. Erica abelii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value contribute to its widespread cultivation in horticulture.
