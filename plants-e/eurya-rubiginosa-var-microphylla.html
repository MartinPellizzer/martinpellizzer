Eurya rubiginosa var. microphylla

Eurya rubiginosa var. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are small and elliptical, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
