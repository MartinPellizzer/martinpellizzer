Erica benthamiana

Erica benthamiana, commonly known as the bentham's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Erica benthamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
