Echinocereus monacanthus

Echinocereus monacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny areoles and is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valued addition to desert landscapes.
