Elaeagnus infundibularis

Elaeagnus infundibularis, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible for birds and some animals. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and arid conditions, making it a hardy ornamental species. It produces fragrant flowers in early spring, which are often followed by the development of its distinctive, pear-shaped berries.

Elaeagnus infundibularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
