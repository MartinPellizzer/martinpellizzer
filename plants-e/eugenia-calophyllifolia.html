Eugenia calophyllifolia

Eugenia calophyllifolia, commonly known as the satinwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It is valued for its hard, dense wood, which is used in furniture and carving.

The species also has medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
