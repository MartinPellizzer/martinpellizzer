Eugenia toledinensis

Eugenia toledinensis, commonly known as the Toledinensis Eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

Its fruits are typically small, red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
