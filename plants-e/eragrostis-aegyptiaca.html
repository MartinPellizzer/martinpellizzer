Eragrostis aegyptiaca

Eragrostis aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
