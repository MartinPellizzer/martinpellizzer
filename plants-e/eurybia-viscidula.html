Eurybia viscidula

Eurybia viscidula, commonly known as sticky eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Eurybia viscidula is considered a hardy perennial, often used in ornamental gardening for its distinctive appearance.
