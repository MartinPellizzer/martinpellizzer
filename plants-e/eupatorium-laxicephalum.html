Eupatorium laxicephalum

Eupatorium laxicephalum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium laxicephalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
