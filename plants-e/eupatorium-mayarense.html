Eupatorium mayarense

Eupatorium mayarense, commonly known as Mayan feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its medicinal properties have been utilized by indigenous communities for various health purposes.

Eupatorium mayarense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
