Eucalyptus melanophitra

Eucalyptus melanophitra, commonly known as the black gum tree, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, fissured bark that is typically dark grey to black in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy underside. The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
