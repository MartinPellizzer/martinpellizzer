Eucalyptus pulchella

Eucalyptus pulchella, commonly known as the white gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale yellow to cream in color.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and forest habitats.
