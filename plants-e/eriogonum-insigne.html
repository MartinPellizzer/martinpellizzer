Eriogonum insigne

Eriogonum insigne, commonly known as the showy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of showy, pale yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and rocky soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, oval seeds in its fruiting structures.

Eriogonum insigne is an important plant for pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
