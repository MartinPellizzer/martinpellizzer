Eria ramulosa

Eria ramulosa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Eria ramulosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded, humid environments.
