Eupatorium maculatum var. foliosum

Eupatorium maculatum var. foliosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

This plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
