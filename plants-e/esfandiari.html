Esfandiari

Esfandiari, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercially grown.
