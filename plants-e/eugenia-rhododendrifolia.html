Eugenia rhododendrifolia

Eugenia rhododendrifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The fruit, which is edible, is small and berry-like, containing several seeds.
