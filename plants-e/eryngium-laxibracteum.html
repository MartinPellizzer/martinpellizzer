Eryngium laxibracteum

Eryngium laxibracteum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny, bract-covered stems. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, spiky inflorescence.

Eryngium laxibracteum plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.
