Euphorbia geldorensis

Euphorbia geldorensis, commonly known as the Gelder's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with upright, spiny leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Euphorbia geldorensis is also known for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
