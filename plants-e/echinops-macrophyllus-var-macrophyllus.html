Echinops macrophyllus var. macrophyllus

Echinops macrophyllus var. macrophyllus, commonly known as the giant globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, spiny, globular flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its unique morphology includes a thick, woody stem that becomes more prominent with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
