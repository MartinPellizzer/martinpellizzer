Eugenia gigantea

Eugenia gigantea, commonly known as the giant satinwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It can grow up to 40 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading crown and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in woodworking.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
