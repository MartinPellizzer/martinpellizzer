Ericoides bruniifolium

Ericoides bruniifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine silvery-white wax. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure and strong winds.

Ericoides bruniifolium is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
