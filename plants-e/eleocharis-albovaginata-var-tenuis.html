Eleocharis albovaginata var. tenuis

Eleocharis albovaginata var. tenuis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually short and narrow, and the inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
