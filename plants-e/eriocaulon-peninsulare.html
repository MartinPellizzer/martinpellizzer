Eriocaulon peninsulare

Eriocaulon peninsulare, commonly known as the Florida marsh grass, is a small, perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to saturated soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering spikes are usually short and inconspicuous, producing small, greenish flowers.

Eriocaulon peninsulare plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
