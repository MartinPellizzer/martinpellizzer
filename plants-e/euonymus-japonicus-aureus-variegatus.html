Euonymus japonicus aureus-variegatus

Euonymus japonicus aureus-variegatus, commonly known as the golden variegated spindle tree, is a shrub native to Japan.

It features striking foliage with a variegated pattern of yellow and green on its leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for hedges or container gardening.
