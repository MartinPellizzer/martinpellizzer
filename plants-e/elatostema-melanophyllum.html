Elatostema melanophyllum

Elatostema melanophyllum, commonly known as the black-leaved dewplant, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, dark green to blackish leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or greenish flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Elatostema melanophyllum is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant once established.
