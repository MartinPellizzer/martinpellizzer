Eurya pseudomyrtillus

Eurya pseudomyrtillus, commonly known as the glossy eurya, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly found in regions of China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eurya pseudomyrtillus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering.
