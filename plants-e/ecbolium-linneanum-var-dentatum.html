Ecbolium linneanum var. dentatum

Ecbolium linneanum var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed ecbolium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with serrated or toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported tonic properties.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to moist, well-drained soils and partial shade to full sun conditions.
