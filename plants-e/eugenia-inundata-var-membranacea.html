Eugenia inundata var. membranacea

Eugenia inundata var. membranacea, commonly known as the swamp guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in riverine forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland ecosystems.
