Euonymus alatus var. ellipticus

Euonymus alatus var. ellipticus, commonly known as the winged euonymus, is a variant of the native Chinese plant Euonymus alatus.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and elliptical-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
