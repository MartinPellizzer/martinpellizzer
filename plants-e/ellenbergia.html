Ellenbergia

Ellenbergia, commonly known as the "wild bean" or "chickpea," is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, often yellow or white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plants are typically shrubs or small trees, native to tropical and subtropical regions. Ellenbergia species are known for their ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, which enriches the soil.

They are also valued for their edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
