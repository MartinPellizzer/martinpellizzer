Erythronium grandiflorum var. smithii

Erythronium grandiflorum var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's crown imperial, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to orange-red, with a distinctive crown-like structure formed by the outer petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming display.
