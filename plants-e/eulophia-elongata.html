Eulophia elongata

Eulophia elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces numerous small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike, which are typical of the Orchidaceae family. Eulophia elongata thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing member of the orchid genus.
