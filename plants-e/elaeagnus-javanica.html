Elaeagnus javanica

Elaeagnus javanica, commonly known as the Java silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or disturbed habitats.

Elaeagnus javanica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
