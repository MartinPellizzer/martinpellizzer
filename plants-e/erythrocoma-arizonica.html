Erythrocoma arizonica

Erythrocoma arizonica, commonly known as the Arizona bloodroot, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a short, hairy stem and palmate leaves with three to five lobes. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Erythrocoma arizonica is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky areas and desert scrub habitats.
