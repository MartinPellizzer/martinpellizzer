Eulalia hydrophila var. filiformis

Eulalia hydrophila var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush grass, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland or riparian habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique, colorful inflorescences that range from red to orange.
