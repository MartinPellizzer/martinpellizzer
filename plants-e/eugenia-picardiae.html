Eugenia picardiae

Eugenia picardiae, commonly known as the Picard's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia picardiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
