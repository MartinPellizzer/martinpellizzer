Elaeagnus laetevirens

Elaeagnus laetevirens, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Elaeagnus laetevirens is also valued for its hardiness and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
