Erysimum boryanum

Erysimum boryanum, commonly known as the Boryanum wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has serrated leaves and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other flowering plants such as cabbages and mustards.

Erysimum boryanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
