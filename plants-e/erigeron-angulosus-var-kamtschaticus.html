Erigeron angulosus var. kamtschaticus

Erigeron angulosus var. kamtschaticus, commonly known as Kamtschatic mountainblue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variant is distinguished by its angular stem and smaller flower heads compared to other Erigeron species.
