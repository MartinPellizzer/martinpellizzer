Erysimum lilacinum

Erysimum lilacinum, commonly known as the lilac flowering kale, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It produces striking violet to lilac-colored flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and often covered in a fuzzy layer, while its stem is hairy and upright. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically beneficial in garden settings.
