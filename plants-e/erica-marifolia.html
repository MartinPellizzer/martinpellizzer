Erica marifolia

Erica marifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica marifolia is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late winter to early spring.
