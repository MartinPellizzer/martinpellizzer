Euonymus sclerocarpus

Euonymus sclerocarpus, commonly known as the hard-seeded woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits that contain hard, black seeds. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem with corky bark and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
