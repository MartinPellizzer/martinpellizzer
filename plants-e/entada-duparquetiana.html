Entada duparquetiana

Entada duparquetiana, commonly known as the African bignay, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
