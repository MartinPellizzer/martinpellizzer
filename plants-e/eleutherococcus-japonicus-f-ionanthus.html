Eleutherococcus japonicus f. ionanthus

Eleutherococcus japonicus f. ionanthus, commonly known as the Japanese prickly ash, is a compact shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in early summer, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

This variety is often used in gardens for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
