Eugenia lilloana

Eugenia lilloana, commonly known as the Lillo's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia lilloana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
