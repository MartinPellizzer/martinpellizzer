Erythrina corallifera

Erythrina corallifera, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fluffy flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is often covered in a rough, fissured texture. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina corallifera is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
