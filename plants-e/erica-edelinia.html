Erica edelinia

Erica edelinia, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica edelinia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
