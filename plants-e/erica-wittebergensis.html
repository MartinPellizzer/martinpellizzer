Erica wittebergensis

Erica wittebergensis, commonly known as the Witteberg heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica wittebergensis is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
