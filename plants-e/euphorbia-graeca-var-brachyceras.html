Euphorbia graeca var. brachyceras

Euphorbia graeca var. brachyceras, commonly known as the Greek spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and xeriscapes.
