Echinocereus rigidispinus

Echinocereus rigidispinus, commonly known as the rigid spine cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with dense, spiny stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
