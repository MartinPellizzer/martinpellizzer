Eurybia oligantha

Eurybia oligantha, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. The plant features wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are usually blue to violet in color and bloom in late summer.

Eurybia oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
