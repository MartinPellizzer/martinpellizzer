Eucalyptus × petrophila

Eucalyptus × petrophila, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and peels off in thin layers, revealing a mottled pattern beneath.

Eucalyptus × petrophila is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils.
