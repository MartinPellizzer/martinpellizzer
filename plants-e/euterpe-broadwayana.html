Euterpe broadwayana

Euterpe broadwayana, commonly known as the broad-leafed palm, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling small, yellowish drupes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
