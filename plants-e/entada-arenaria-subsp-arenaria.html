Entada arenaria subsp. arenaria

Entada arenaria subsp.

arenaria, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey-barked trunk and bipinnate leaves.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers followed by long, pod-like fruits containing flat, bean-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
