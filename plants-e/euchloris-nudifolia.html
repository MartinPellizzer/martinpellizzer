Euchloris nudifolia

Euchloris nudifolia, commonly known as the naked-stemmed greenbrier, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a woody vine characterized by its slender, often hairless stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. Its botanical features include a distinctive lack of stipules and the presence of stipulate leaves in some varieties.

Euchloris nudifolia is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
