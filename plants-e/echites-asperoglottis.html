Echites asperoglottis

Echites asperoglottis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed echites, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
