Ehretia umbellulata

Ehretia umbellulata, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
