Eulophia graminea

Eulophia graminea, commonly known as the grass orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and compact inflorescence that bears small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, narrow labellum.

Eulophia graminea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.
