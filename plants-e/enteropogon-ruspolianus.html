Enteropogon ruspolianus

Enteropogon ruspolianus, commonly known as the purple false rice, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces small, three-lobed seeds that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
