Eucalyptus aromatica

Eucalyptus aromatica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are typically released during the cooler months. Its essential oils, particularly cineole, are valued for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Eucalyptus aromatica is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
