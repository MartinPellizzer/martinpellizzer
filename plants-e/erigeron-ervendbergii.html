Erigeron ervendbergii

Erigeron ervendbergii, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to drought conditions, showcasing a low, spreading growth habit. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.
