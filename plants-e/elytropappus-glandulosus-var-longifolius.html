Elytropappus glandulosus var. longifolius

Elytropappus glandulosus var. longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved glandular euphorbia, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features a woody stem with numerous branching stems that are often covered in glandular hairs. The leaves are elongated, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, greenish-yellow inflorescences, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
