Epidendrum jimenezii

Epidendrum jimenezii, commonly known as the Jimenez's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its roots are often found in the soil, and it thrives in well-drained, organic-rich substrates.

Epidendrum jimenezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
