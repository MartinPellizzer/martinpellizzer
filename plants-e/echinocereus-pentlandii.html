Echinocereus pentlandii

Echinocereus pentlandii, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its segmented stems and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate water during its growing season. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems allows it to survive in arid environments.
