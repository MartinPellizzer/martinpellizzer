Etlingera purpurea

Etlingera purpurea, commonly known as the purple torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking purple bracts that surround a cluster of small, fragrant flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Etlingera purpurea make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and floral arrangements.
