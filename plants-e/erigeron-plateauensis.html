Erigeron plateauensis

Erigeron plateauensis, commonly known as the Plateau fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, adapting to the harsh environmental conditions of its habitat. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Erigeron plateauensis is known for its resilience and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.
