Eragrostis excelsa

Eragrostis excelsa, commonly known as tall lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for erosion control and as a forage plant.
