Engelhardia subsimplicifolia

Engelhardia subsimplicifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed engelhardia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
