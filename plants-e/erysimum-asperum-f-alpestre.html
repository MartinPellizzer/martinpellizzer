Erysimum asperum f. alpestre

Erysimum asperum f. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, often growing in alpine or rocky habitats.

This frost-tolerant species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in mountain ecosystems.
