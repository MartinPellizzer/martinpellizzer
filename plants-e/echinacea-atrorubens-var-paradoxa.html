Echinacea atrorubens var. paradoxa

Echinacea atrorubens var. paradoxa, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to reddish-pink petals and a prominent, spiky yellow-orange central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to dry soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
