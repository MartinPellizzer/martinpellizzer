Eugenia montevidensis

Eugenia montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevideo guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.
