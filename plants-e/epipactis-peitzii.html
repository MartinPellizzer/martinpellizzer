Epipactis peitzii

Epipactis peitzii, commonly known as the Peitz's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a purple or brown lip, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Epipactis peitzii is a perennial herb that grows from a rhizome and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
