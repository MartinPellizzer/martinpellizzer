Euphorbia gracilipes

Euphorbia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a cylindrical stem that is often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. Its leaves are narrow and often reduced to scales, which help minimize water loss.

Euphorbia gracilipes is popular in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
