Erica florida

Erica florida, commonly known as the flowering heath, is a species of shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica florida is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
