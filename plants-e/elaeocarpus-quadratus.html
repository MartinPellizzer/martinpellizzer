Elaeocarpus quadratus

Elaeocarpus quadratus, commonly known as the four-angled elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with four distinct angles. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
