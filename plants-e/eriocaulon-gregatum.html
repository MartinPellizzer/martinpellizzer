Eriocaulon gregatum

Eriocaulon gregatum, commonly known as the clustered spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface, while its flowers are tiny and clustered.

Eriocaulon gregatum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
