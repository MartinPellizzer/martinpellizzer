Erica lasciva

Erica lasciva, commonly known as the lascivious heath, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica lasciva is evergreen, with dense, leathery foliage that thrives in acidic, well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form compact, attractive shrubs.
