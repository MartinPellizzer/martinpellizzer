Eschweilera fendleriana

Eschweilera fendleriana, commonly known as the Fendler's zygocactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Eschweilera fendleriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in low-maintenance conditions.
