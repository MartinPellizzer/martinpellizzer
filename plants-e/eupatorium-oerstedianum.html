Eupatorium oerstedianum

Eupatorium oerstedianum, commonly known as Oersted's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its blooming period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.
