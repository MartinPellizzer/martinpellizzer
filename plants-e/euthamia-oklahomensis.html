Euthamia oklahomensis

Euthamia oklahomensis, commonly known as Oklahoma goldenrod, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Oklahoma goldenrod is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
