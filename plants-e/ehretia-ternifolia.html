Ehretia ternifolia

Ehretia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf mahogany, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
