Eulophia lujaeana

Eulophia lujaeana, commonly known as the Lujan's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree bark in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, while its inflorescence bears a cluster of showy, colorful blooms.

Eulophia lujaeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
