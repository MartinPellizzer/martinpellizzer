Eritrichium foliosum

Eritrichium foliosum, commonly known as the leafy eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in alpine environments. Its leaves are basal and alternate, with a distinctive lobed shape, and it produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

Eritrichium foliosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
