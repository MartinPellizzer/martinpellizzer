Epidendrum trulliforme

Epidendrum trulliforme, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, white or greenish flowers. The plant has strap-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. It is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

The species is known for its unique, spider-like appearance, which gives it its common name.
