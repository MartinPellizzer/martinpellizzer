Erysimum duriaei subsp. gorbeanum

Erysimum duriaei subsp.

gorbeanum, commonly known as the Gorbeanum wallflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its yellow or orange flowers. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
