Erica maderensis

Erica maderensis, commonly known as the Iberian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. This shrub typically grows in rocky, acidic soils and is adapted to harsh, mountainous environments. Erica maderensis produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming in spring.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
