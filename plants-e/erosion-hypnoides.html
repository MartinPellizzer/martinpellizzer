Erosion hypnoides

Erosion hypnoides, commonly known as the creeping sedum, is a low-growing succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It has fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically in the summer months. Its extensive root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable for land stabilization.

Erosion hypnoides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and hardiness.
