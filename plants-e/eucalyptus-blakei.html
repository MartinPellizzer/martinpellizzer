Eucalyptus blakei

Eucalyptus blakei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from green to red or yellow, depending on the season. Eucalyptus blakei is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
