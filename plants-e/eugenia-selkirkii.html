Eugenia selkirkii

Eugenia selkirkii, commonly known as the Selkirk myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that is often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia selkirkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
