Elaeagnus armata

Elaeagnus armata, commonly known as the silver leaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and pinnately compound leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest edges or disturbed areas.

Elaeagnus armata is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil restoration efforts.
