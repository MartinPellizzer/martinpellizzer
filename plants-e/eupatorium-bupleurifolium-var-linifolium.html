Eupatorium bupleurifolium var. linifolium

Eupatorium bupleurifolium var. linifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often branched and covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called cymes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges, and are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
