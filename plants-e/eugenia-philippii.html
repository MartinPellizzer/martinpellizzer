Eugenia philippii

Eugenia philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's Eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
