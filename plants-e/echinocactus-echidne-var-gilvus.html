Echinocactus echidne var. gilvus

Echinocactus echidne var. gilvus, commonly known as the golden hedgehog cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white or yellow flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, with a thick, fleshy stem that stores water efficiently. Its vibrant yellow flowers bloom in the spring, adding a burst of color to its desert habitat.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
