Echinopsis tapecuana

Echinopsis tapecuana, commonly known as the "Tapecuana cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of northern Argentina.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed structure and spiny areoles. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, opening at night and attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
