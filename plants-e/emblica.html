Emblica

Emblica, commonly known as Indian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish-green berries. The plant is valued for its high vitamin C content and is widely used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while its flowers are unisexual and greenish-yellow.

The fruit develops in clusters and is often harvested when fully ripe for culinary and medicinal purposes.
