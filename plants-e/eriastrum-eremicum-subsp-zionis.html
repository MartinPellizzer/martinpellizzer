Eriastrum eremicum subsp. zionis

Eriastrum eremicum subsp.

zionis, commonly known as Zion's eriastrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a low-growing annual herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and sandy soils, often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Its growth habit and floral structure are adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native range.
