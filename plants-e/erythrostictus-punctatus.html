Erythrostictus punctatus

Erythrostictus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are often pink or red with yellow markings, contributing to its ornamental value.
