Echinocactus poliocentrus

Echinocactus poliocentrus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
