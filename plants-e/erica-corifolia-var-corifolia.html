Erica corifolia var. corifolia

Erica corifolia var. corifolia, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
