Eclipta prostrata var. zippeliana

Eclipta prostrata var. zippeliana, commonly known as Zippel's ironweed, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces achenes, which are dry, one-seeded fruits that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a rough texture and can have a slight pubescence.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is adaptable to various environmental conditions.
