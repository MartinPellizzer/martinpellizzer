Erica rugata

Erica rugata, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in a fine, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica rugata is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
