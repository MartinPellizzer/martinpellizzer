Encholirium patens

Encholirium patens, commonly known as the Mexican candleplant, is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a central cup to collect water. The plant produces striking, red-orange flowers that emerge from the leaf rosette, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Encholirium patens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
