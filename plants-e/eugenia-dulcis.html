Eugenia dulcis

Eugenia dulcis, commonly known as the Java cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure.

Eugenia dulcis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in garden settings.
