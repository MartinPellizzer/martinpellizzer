Eugenia sieberi

Eugenia sieberi, commonly known as the Sierra Madre myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and rocky slopes. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy dark green surface and a fragrant, waxy texture. Its flowers are white or pale pink, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are followed by small, fleshy berries.

Eugenia sieberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
