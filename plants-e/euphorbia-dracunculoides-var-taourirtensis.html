Euphorbia dracunculoides var. taourirtensis

Euphorbia dracunculoides var. taourirtensis, commonly known as the Taourirt Desert Spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its growth habit is low and sprawling, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its resilience and unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
