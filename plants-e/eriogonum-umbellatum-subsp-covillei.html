Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. covillei

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

covillei, commonly known as Coville's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, sagebrush steppe, and disturbed areas. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Coville's buckwheat is an important nectar source for pollinators and is adapted to arid environments.
