Eurybia ilicifolia

Eurybia ilicifolia, commonly known as sweetweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, purple-tinged flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eurybia ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various pollinators.
