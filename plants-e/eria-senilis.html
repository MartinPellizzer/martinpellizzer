Eria senilis

Eria senilis, commonly known as the silver lace fern, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that are light green with a silvery sheen, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, with creeping stems that can produce new shoots and roots. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, finger-like segments, contributing to its intricate foliage.

Eria senilis thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
