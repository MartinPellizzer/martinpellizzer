Edithea floribunda subsp. leuconeura

Edithea floribunda subsp.

leuconeura, commonly known as the white-flowered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale floral coloration and specific habitat preferences.
