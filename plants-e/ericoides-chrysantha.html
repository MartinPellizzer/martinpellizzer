Ericoides chrysantha

Ericoides chrysantha, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andean regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreen shrubs and heaths. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to grow in nutrient-poor soils.

Ericoides chrysantha is often found in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
