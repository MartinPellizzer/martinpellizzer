Euphorbia mahafalensis

Euphorbia mahafalensis, commonly known as the Mahafaly spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
