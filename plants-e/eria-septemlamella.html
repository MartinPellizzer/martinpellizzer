Eria septemlamella

Eria septemlamella, commonly known as the seven-ribbed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
