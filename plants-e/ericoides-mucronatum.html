Ericoides mucronatum

Ericoides mucronatum, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Ericoides mucronatum is adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
