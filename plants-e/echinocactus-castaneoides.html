Echinocactus castaneoides

Echinocactus castaneoides, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white flowers. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The golden barrel cactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
