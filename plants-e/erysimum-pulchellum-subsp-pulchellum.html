Erysimum pulchellum subsp. pulchellum

Erysimum pulchellum subsp.

pulchellum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features basal and alternate leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape, often with a hairy surface.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in racemes, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and compact growth habit.
