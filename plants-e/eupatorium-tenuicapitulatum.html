Eupatorium tenuicapitulatum

Eupatorium tenuicapitulatum, commonly known as slender-headed eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters and bloom in the summer months.

Eupatorium tenuicapitulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
