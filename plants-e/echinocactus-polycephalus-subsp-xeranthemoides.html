Echinocactus polycephalus subsp. xeranthemoides

Echinocactus polycephalus subsp.

xeranthemoides, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
