Erigeron subtrinervis subsp. conspicuus

Erigeron subtrinervis subsp.

conspicuus, commonly known as the showy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features narrow, blue to violet ray flowers and is distinguished by its prominent, showy appearance.

Its botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a slender, branched stem that supports the flowering heads.
