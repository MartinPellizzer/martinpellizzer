Esenbeckia acapulcensis

Esenbeckia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Mexican candlewood, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in open areas with well-drained soil.
