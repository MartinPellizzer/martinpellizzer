Eriogonum multiceps subsp. typicum

Eriogonum multiceps subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows.

The plant produces showy, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
