Evolvulus glomeratus var. obtusus

Evolvulus glomeratus var. obtusus, commonly known as the bluntleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and have a rounded base, distinguishing it from other varieties of Evolvulus. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This annual plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
