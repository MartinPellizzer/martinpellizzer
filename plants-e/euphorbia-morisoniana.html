Euphorbia morisoniana

Euphorbia morisoniana, commonly known as the Morison's spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and may be lance-shaped or ovate, while its flowers are clustered in a structure called a cyathium.

Euphorbia morisoniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
