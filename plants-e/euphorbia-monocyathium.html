Euphorbia monocyathium

Euphorbia monocyathium, commonly known as the single-cyst flower or the one-cyst euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that consist of a single cup-like structure containing both male and female flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
