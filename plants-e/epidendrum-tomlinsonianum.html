Epidendrum tomlinsonianum

Epidendrum tomlinsonianum, commonly known as the Tomlinson's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

It requires bright, indirect light and consistent humidity to thrive, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
