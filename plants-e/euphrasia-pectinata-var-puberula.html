Euphrasia pectinata var. puberula

Euphrasia pectinata var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent toothworts, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered with a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
