Euphorbia brasiliensis var. lorentzii

Euphorbia brasiliensis var. lorentzii, commonly known as the Lorentz's euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to Brazil.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
