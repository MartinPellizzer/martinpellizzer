Eugenia nosibensis

Eugenia nosibensis, commonly known as the Koa tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces edible berries that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
