Encholirium harleyi

Encholirium harleyi, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Encholirium harleyi is adapted to arid environments and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
