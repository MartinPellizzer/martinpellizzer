Erxlebia fusiformis

Erxlebia fusiformis, commonly known as the fusiform eriochloa, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive fusiform (spindle-shaped) seed heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense, cylindrical spikelets.

Erxlebia fusiformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience to various environmental conditions.
