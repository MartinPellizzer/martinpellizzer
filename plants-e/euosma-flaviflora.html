Euosma flaviflora

Euosma flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered euosma, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive, spicy scent. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Euosma flaviflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
