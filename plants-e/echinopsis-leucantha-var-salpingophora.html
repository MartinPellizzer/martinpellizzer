Echinopsis leucantha var. salpingophora

Echinopsis leucantha var. salpingophora, commonly known as the White-topped Moon cactus, is a cactus species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems and produces white to pale pink flowers during the cooler months. The plant has a dense, spiny covering that helps protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
