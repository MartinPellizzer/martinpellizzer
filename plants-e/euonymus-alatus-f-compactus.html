Euonymus alatus f. compactus

Euonymus alatus f. compactus, commonly known as the winged euonymus or burning bush, is a compact, shrub-like form of the species.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and distinctive, opposite, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries that persist into winter. Its foliage turns vibrant red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, suitable for garden borders or as a hedge.
