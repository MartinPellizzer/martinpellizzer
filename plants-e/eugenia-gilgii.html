Eugenia gilgii

Eugenia gilgii, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. Eugenia gilgii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
