Elaeosticta glaucescens

Elaeosticta glaucescens, commonly known as the bluebottle or bluebottle plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds.

Elaeosticta glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
