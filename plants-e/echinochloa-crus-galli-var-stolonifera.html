Echinochloa crus-galli var. stolonifera

Echinochloa crus-galli var. stolonifera, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a widespread annual grass species native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with long, narrow leaves and spikelets that form dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and wetlands. Its stolons allow it to spread vegetatively, making it a competitive weed in various ecosystems.

It produces small, pale green to brown flowers and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
