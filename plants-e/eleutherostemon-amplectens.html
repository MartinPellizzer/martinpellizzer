Eleutherostemon amplectens

Eleutherostemon amplectens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. Its flowering period occurs in the spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes, forming dense mats.

The species is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
