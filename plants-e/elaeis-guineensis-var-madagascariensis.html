Elaeis guineensis var. madagascariensis

Elaeis guineensis var. madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar oil palm, is a variant of the oil palm native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces fruits that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soils. It is cultivated for its oil, which is used in food and industrial products.

However, its cultivation is limited due to its specific environmental requirements and lower yield compared to other oil palm varieties.
