Euryops polytrichoides

Euryops polytrichoides, commonly known as the golden bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flower heads that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
