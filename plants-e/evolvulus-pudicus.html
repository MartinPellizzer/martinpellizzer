Evolvulus pudicus

Evolvulus pudicus, commonly known as the humble morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, pale blue or white flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or ground cover. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is known for its rapid growth habit.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
