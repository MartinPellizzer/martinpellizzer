Eragrostis humidicola

Eragrostis humidicola, commonly known as swamp lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist to saturated soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eragrostis humidicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
