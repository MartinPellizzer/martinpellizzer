Erica brachycrossa

Erica brachycrossa, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense foliage of small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica brachycrossa is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and hardy nature.
