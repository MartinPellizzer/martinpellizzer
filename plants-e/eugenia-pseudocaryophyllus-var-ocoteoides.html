Eugenia pseudocaryophyllus var. ocoteoides

Eugenia pseudocaryophyllus var. ocoteoides, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or ocotea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
