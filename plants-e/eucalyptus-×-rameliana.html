Eucalyptus × rameliana

Eucalyptus × rameliana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

Eucalyptus × rameliana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
