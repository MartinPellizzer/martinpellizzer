Eryngium caeruleum

Eryngium caeruleum, commonly known as blue thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with rough textures and a distinctive, spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Eryngium caeruleum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
