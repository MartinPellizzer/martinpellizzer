Echites sessilis

Echites sessilis, commonly known as the sessile echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually with five petals.

Echites sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
