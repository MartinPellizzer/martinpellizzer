Echinodorus lanceolatus

Echinodorus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge above the water surface. Echinodorus lanceolatus thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments.

It is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental value and ability to improve water quality.
