Eugenia gracilis

Eugenia gracilis, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers, which are often followed by small, edible berries.

Eugenia gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
