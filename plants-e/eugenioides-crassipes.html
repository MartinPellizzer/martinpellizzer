Eugenioides crassipes

Eugenioides crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
