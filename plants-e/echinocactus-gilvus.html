Echinocactus gilvus

Echinocactus gilvus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny trunk and a dense cluster of spines at the top. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and desert gardens.
