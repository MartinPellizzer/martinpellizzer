Eriocephalus longifolius

Eriocephalus longifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
