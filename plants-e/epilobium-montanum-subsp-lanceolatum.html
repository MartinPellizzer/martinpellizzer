Epilobium montanum subsp. lanceolatum

Epilobium montanum subsp.

lanceolatum, commonly known as the mountain willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are usually pink or purple, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
