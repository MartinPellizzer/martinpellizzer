Elymus woroschilowii

Elymus woroschilowii, commonly known as the Woroschilow's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, feathery plumes that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Elymus woroschilowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
