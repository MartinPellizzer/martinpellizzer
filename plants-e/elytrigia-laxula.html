Elytrigia laxula

Elytrigia laxula, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, two-lobed spikelets.

Elytrigia laxula is valued for its role in ecological restoration and as a forage plant for livestock.
