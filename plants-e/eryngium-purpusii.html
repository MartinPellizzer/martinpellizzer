Eryngium purpusii

Eryngium purpusii, commonly known as the blue eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bristly, gray-green bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eryngium purpusii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
