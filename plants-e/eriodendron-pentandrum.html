Eriodendron pentandrum

Eriodendron pentandrum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flat, elongated seeds, and it is valued for its hard, dark wood. Eriodendron pentandrum thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and riverine areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts.
