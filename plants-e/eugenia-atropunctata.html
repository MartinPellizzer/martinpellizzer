Eugenia atropunctata

Eugenia atropunctata, commonly known as the black dot eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, dark purple berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia atropunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
