Epipactis leptochila var. collina

Epipactis leptochila var. collina, commonly known as the mountain helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and pale green to yellowish flowers with distinctive lip structures. This variety typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
