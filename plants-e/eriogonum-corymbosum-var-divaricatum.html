Eriogonum corymbosum var. divaricatum

Eriogonum corymbosum var. divaricatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky slopes and dry meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, and it produces edible seeds that are consumed by various wildlife species.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
