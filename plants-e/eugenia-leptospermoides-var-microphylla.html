Eugenia leptospermoides var. microphylla

Eugenia leptospermoides var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a species native to Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height. The plant has lance-shaped, glossy, dark green leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.
