Eleocharis pallens

Eleocharis pallens, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and slender, silver-gray inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland areas.

Eleocharis pallens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
