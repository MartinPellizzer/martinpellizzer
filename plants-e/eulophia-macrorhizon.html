Eulophia macrorhizon

Eulophia macrorhizon, commonly known as the large-rodent orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red lip. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia macrorhizon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
