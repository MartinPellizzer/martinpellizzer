Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp. camaldulensis

Eucalyptus camaldulensis subsp.

camaldulensis, commonly known as the river red gum, is a tall, deciduous tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that ranges from grey to red-brown in color.

The tree produces small, spherical flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green hue when fresh.

This species is well adapted to riparian zones and is known for its ability to tolerate periodic flooding and saline soils.
