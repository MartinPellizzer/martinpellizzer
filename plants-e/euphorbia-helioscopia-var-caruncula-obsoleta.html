Euphorbia helioscopia var. caruncula-obsoleta

Euphorbia helioscopia var. caruncula-obsoleta, commonly known as the sunspotted spurge, is a variant of the common spurge.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

This variant is distinguished by its unique sunspots and reduced structures, making it a notable ornamental and ecological plant.
