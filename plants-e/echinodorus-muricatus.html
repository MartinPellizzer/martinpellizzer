Echinodorus muricatus

Echinodorus muricatus, commonly known as the spiked arrowhead, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arrowhead-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, spiky protrusions. The plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds. It produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, adding to its ornamental value.

Echinodorus muricatus is popular in aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
