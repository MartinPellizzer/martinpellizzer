Echinops gordjaginii

Echinops gordjaginii, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops gordjaginii is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
