Echinocactus cachensis

Echinocactus cachensis, commonly known as the Cachanita, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservatories.
