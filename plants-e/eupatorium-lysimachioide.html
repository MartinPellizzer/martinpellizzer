Eupatorium lysimachioide

Eupatorium lysimachioide, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
