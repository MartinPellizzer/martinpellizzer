Eragrostis fallax

Eragrostis fallax, commonly known as silverleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. It produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in summer, which are clustered in dense spikes.

Eragrostis fallax is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
