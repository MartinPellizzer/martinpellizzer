Ephielis patrisiana

Ephielis patrisiana, commonly known as the Patrisian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ephielis patrisiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
