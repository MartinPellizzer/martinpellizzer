Eugenia ngoyensis

Eugenia ngoyensis, commonly known as the Ngoyi guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The botanical characteristics of Eugenia ngoyensis make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential agricultural purposes.
