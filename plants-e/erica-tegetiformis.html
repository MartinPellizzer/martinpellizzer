Erica tegetiformis

Erica tegetiformis, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica tegetiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in eroded areas.
