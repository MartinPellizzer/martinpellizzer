Eragrostis nigricans

Eragrostis nigricans, commonly known as blackgrass or black lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can dominate wetland ecosystems.

Eragrostis nigricans plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insect and bird species.
