Elaeocarpus limitaneoides

Elaeocarpus limitaneoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has limited use in traditional medicine.
