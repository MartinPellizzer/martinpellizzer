Euphorbia esuloides

Euphorbia esuloides, commonly known as the Desert Candelabra, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually small and scale-like, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Euphorbia esuloides is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
