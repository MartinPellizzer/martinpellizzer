Eugenia nicaraguensis

Eugenia nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
