Euryops lateriflorus

Euryops lateriflorus, commonly known as the coastal dandelion, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive yellow center and white petals. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Euryops lateriflorus is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong coastal winds, making it a hardy and resilient species.
