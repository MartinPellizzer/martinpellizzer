Erigeron acris var. debilis

Erigeron acris var. debilis, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It typically grows in open areas such as meadows and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often used in native plant gardens.
