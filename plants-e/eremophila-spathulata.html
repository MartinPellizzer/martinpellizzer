Eremophila spathulata

Eremophila spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience.

Eremophila spathulata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
