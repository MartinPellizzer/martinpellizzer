Echinocactus decaisneanus

Echinocactus decaisneanus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardens.
