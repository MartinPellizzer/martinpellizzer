Eugenia selangorensis

Eugenia selangorensis, commonly known as the Malacca guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
