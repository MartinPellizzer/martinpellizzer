Eremophila verrucosa subsp. verrucosa

Eremophila verrucosa subsp.

verrucosa, commonly known as the rough eremophila, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer seasons. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
