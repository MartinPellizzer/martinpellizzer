Eriospermum parvulum

Eriospermum parvulum, commonly known as the small-flowered eriosperm, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive seed capsule that is covered in fine, hairy structures, aiding in seed dispersal. It is often found in open, sunny habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
