Eragrostis papposa

Eragrostis papposa, commonly known as silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
