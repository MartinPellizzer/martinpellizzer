Eupetalum petalodes

Eupetalum petalodes, commonly known as the garden cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink or white petals that resemble those of roses. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has dark green, serrated leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
