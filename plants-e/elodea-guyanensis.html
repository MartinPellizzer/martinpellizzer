Elodea guyanensis

Elodea guyanensis, commonly known as the Guyana waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are often submerged, though they may emerge just below the water surface. Elodea guyanensis is often found in slow-moving streams, ponds, and lakes, where it plays a role in aquatic habitats by providing shelter for small organisms.

Its rapid growth makes it an important species in both ecological studies and water quality assessments.
