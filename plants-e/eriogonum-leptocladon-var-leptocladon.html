Eriogonum leptocladon var. leptocladon

Eriogonum leptocladon var. leptocladon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
