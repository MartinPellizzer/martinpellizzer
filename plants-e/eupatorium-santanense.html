Eupatorium santanense

Eupatorium santanense, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fluffy flower heads. The plant typically produces white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Eupatorium santanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
