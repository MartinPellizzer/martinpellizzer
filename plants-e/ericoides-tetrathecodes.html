Ericoides tetrathecodes

Ericoides tetrathecodes, commonly known as the four-theoded moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and dark green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered capsules for seed dispersal, typical of many moss species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of nutrient cycling in woodland ecosystems.
