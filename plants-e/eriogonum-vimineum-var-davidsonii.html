Eriogonum vimineum var. davidsonii

Eriogonum vimineum var. davidsonii, commonly known as the Davidson's buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features slender, wiry stems that often trail along the ground, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
