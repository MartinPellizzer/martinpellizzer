Eurybia gunniana var. salicina

Eurybia gunniana var. salicina, commonly known as the silverwood sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy foliage and yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
