Ericoides lawsonium

Ericoides lawsonium, commonly known as the Lawson's willow, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, zigzagging branches and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces catkins, which are cylindrical clusters of flowers, typical of willow species. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Ericoides lawsonium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in streamside habitats.
