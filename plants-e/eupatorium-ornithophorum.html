Eupatorium ornithophorum

Eupatorium ornithophorum, commonly known as the purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium ornithophorum is also known for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
