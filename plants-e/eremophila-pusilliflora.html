Eremophila pusilliflora

Eremophila pusilliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and fire resilience.

Eremophila pusilliflora plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
