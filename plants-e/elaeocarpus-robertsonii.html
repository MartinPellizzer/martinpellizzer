Elaeocarpus robertsonii

Elaeocarpus robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
