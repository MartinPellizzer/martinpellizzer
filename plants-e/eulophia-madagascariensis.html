Eulophia madagascariensis

Eulophia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and several fleshy, linear leaves. The plant produces erect, branched inflorescences bearing small, pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in forest undergrowth.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
