Eriogonum heermannii var. sulcatum

Eriogonum heermannii var. sulcatum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy, serrated leaves that are adapted to arid environments, and it produces distinctive, three-lobed bracts beneath its flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
