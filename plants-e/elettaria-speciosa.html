Elettaria speciosa

Elettaria speciosa, commonly known as the fragrant ginger or wild ginger, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

Elettaria speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal uses in various cultures.
