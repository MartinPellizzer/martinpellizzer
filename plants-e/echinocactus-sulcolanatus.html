Echinocactus sulcolanatus

Echinocactus sulcolanatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy tissue. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
