Eleocharis filiculmis var. douradae

Eleocharis filiculmis var. douradae, commonly known as golden spike rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often display a golden hue, especially in sunlight. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond edges.

Its dense growth habit helps stabilize soil and provides habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
