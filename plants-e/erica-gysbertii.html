Erica gysbertii

Erica gysbertii, commonly known as the Gysbert's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. It produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica gysbertii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
