Epilobium findlayi var. pubescens

Epilobium findlayi var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive pubescent appearance. Its showy, pink to red flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
