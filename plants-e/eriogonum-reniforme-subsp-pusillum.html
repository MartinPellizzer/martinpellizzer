Eriogonum reniforme subsp. pusillum

Eriogonum reniforme subsp.

pusillum, commonly known as the smallleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and chaparral habitats. It has a low, spreading growth habit and produces small, nut-like fruits.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid environments and is an important component of local ecosystems.
