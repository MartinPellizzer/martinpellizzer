Erica zwartbergensis

Erica zwartbergensis, commonly known as the Zwartberg heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica zwartbergensis is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
