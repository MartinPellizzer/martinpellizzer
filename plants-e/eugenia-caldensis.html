Eugenia caldensis

Eugenia caldensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *candeia*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Eugenia caldensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal regions and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical areas.
