Erigeron pulchellus var. brauniae

Erigeron pulchellus var. brauniae, commonly known as Braun's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are blue to purple in color and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It has a slender, branched stem and is often found in prairie and woodland ecosystems.
