Echinocactus polyocentrus

Echinocactus polyocentrus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
