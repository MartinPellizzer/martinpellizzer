Eriogonum umbellatum var. ellipticum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are elliptic in shape, often with a hairy texture, and the plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

This variety is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
