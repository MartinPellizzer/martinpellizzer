Eugenia chiquimulana

Eugenia chiquimulana, commonly known as the Chiquimulilla myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and dark, glossy leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments at moderate elevations.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
