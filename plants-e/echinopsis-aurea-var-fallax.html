Echinopsis aurea var. fallax

Echinopsis aurea var. fallax, commonly known as the Golden Torch Cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, golden-yellow spines. The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically pale yellow or orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
