Elytrigia farcta

Elytrigia farcta, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

Elytrigia farcta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological habitats.
