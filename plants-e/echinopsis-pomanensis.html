Echinopsis pomanensis

Echinopsis pomanensis, commonly known as the Pomanensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it conserve water in its natural habitat. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the evening. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Echinopsis pomanensis is a popular choice for collectors due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
