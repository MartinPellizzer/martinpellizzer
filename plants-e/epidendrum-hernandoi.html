Epidendrum hernandoi

Epidendrum hernandoi, commonly known as the Hernando's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, greenish-gray foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a controlled environment.
