Eriocaulon rouxianum

Eriocaulon rouxianum, commonly known as the Roux's spike-rush, is a member of the Poales order and the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.
