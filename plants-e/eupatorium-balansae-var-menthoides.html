Eupatorium balansae var. menthoides

Eupatorium balansae var. menthoides, commonly known as the Menthol-like Eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic, mint-scented leaves. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
