Erica gracilipes

Erica gracilipes, commonly known as the slender heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pale pink to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
