Eria cylindrostachya

Eria cylindrostachya, commonly known as the cylindrical-stalked eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and linear leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, typically blooming during the warmer months. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
