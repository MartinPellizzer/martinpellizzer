Erysimum ceretanum

Erysimum ceretanum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to alpine meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in cold climates.

Erysimum ceretanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
