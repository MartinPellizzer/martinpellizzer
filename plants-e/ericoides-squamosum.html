Ericoides squamosum

Ericoides squamosum, commonly known as the scaly ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, scaly hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Ericoides squamosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
