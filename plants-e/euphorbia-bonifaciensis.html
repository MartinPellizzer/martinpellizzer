Euphorbia bonifaciensis

Euphorbia bonifaciensis, commonly known as the Bonifacius spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically small and may be reduced or absent in some varieties, giving it a unique appearance.

Euphorbia bonifaciensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
