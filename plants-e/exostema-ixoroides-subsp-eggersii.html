Exostema ixoroides subsp. eggersii

Exostema ixoroides subsp.

eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' exostema, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that consist of numerous small, showy flowers clustered in dense spikes.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually opposite, simple, and lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
