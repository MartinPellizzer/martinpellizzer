Euphorbia wallichii subsp. duclouxii

Euphorbia wallichii subsp.

duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by fleshy, green bracts.

The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It is often found in rocky habitats at elevations ranging from 2,000 to 4,000 meters.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
