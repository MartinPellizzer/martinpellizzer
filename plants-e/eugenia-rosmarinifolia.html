Eugenia rosmarinifolia

Eugenia rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a slight resinous scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia rosmarinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
