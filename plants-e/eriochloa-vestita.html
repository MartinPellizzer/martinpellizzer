Eriochloa vestita

Eriochloa vestita, commonly known as the hairy bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Eriochloa vestita plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
