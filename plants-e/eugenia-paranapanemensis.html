Eugenia paranapanemensis

Eugenia paranapanemensis, commonly known as the Paranapanema guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
