Echinacea purpurea var. serotina

Echinacea purpurea var. serotina, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, pink to purple cone-shaped flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant has hairy, green leaves with a rough texture and a sturdy, upright stem. It is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
