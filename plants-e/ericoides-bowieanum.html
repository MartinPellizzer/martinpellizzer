Ericoides bowieanum

Ericoides bowieanum, commonly known as the Bowie's ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
