Euphorbia virgata f. esulifolia

Euphorbia virgata f. esulifolia, commonly known as the silver euphorbia, is a succulent plant characterized by its unique, branched structure and pale, almost silvery foliage.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Euphorbia. Its fleshy stems and leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for dry conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
