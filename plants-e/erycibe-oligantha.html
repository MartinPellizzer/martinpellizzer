Erycibe oligantha

Erycibe oligantha, commonly known as the purple passion vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, violet flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Its rapid growth habit makes it an effective ground cover in shaded environments.

The botanical features of Erycibe oligantha make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
