Eupatorium solidaginifolium

Eupatorium solidaginifolium, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is part of the Asteraceae family. It is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

However, it is toxic to livestock and can cause illness if ingested.
