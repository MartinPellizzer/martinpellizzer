Eupatorium cassinifolium

Eupatorium cassinifolium, commonly known as the fragrant aster, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. The plant produces showy, clustered purple to pink flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native garden landscapes.
