Eriochloa acuminata

Eriochloa acuminata, commonly known as rough bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its coarse, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

Eriochloa acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native landscapes.
