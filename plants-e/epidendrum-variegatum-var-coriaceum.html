Epidendrum variegatum var. coriaceum

Epidendrum variegatum var. coriaceum, commonly known as the Coriaceum Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a distinctive texture.

Its flowers are typically borne in racemes and display a range of colors, from white to pink and violet, depending on the cultivar. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
