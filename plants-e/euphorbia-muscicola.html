Euphorbia muscicola

Euphorbia muscicola, commonly known as the musk spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ability to store water in its stems make it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
