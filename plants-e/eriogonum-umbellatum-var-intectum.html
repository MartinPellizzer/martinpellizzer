Eriogonum umbellatum var. intectum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. intectum, commonly known as the Townsend's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect stems and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, and the plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is adapted to arid environments.
