Eugenia vaga

Eugenia vaga, commonly known as the slender waxtree, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia vaga is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal fruit.
