Eupatorium meyeri

Eupatorium meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and wetlands. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
