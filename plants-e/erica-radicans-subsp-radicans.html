Erica radicans subsp. radicans

Erica radicans subsp.

radicans, commonly known as the trailing heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green, with a slightly waxy texture.

The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its compact form and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to coastal and Mediterranean climates.
