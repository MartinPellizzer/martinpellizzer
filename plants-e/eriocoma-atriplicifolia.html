Eriocoma atriplicifolia

Eriocoma atriplicifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf eriocoma, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray, fuzzy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
