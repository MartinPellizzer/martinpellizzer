Eryngium luzulifolium

Eryngium luzulifolium, commonly known as blue eryngo, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble thistle-like structures. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Eryngium luzulifolium is typically perennial, with a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
