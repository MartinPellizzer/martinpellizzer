Epilobium × hungaricum

Epilobium × hungaricum, commonly known as Hungarian willow herb, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of pink or red flowers. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
