Erysimum handel-mazzettii

Erysimum handel-mazzettii, commonly known as the Italian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring.

Erysimum handel-mazzettii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
