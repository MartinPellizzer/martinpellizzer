Erlangea chevalieri

Erlangea chevalieri, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a distinctive star-shaped appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by specific species of bees and wasps.

Due to its unique beauty and rarity, Erlangea chevalieri is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
