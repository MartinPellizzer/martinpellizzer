Eriocaulon benedictum

Eriocaulon benedictum, commonly known as the sacred water plant, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, preferring muddy or sandy substrates.

Due to its cultural significance in some regions, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or ritualistic purposes.
