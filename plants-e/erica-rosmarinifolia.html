Erica rosmarinifolia

Erica rosmarinifolia, commonly known as the rosemary heath, is a flowering shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant features small, evergreen leaves that are needle-like and aromatic, similar to rosemary. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring.

Erica rosmarinifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
