Eupatorium concinnitas

Eupatorium concinnitas, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium concinnitas is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
