Erigeron alpinus var. myosotifolius

Erigeron alpinus var. myosotifolius, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft, fuzzy appearance. This variety is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
