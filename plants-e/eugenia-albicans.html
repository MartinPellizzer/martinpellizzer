Eugenia albicans

Eugenia albicans, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically white or yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in the perfume industry for its essential oils.
