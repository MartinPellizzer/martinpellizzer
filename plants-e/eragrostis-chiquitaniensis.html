Eragrostis chiquitaniensis

Eragrostis chiquitaniensis, commonly known as the Chiquitania grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly found in Bolivia and Paraguay.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and savannas, often in areas with poor soil conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains of wheat-like seeds. It is adapted to withstand drought and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the regional flora and a valuable resource for local ecosystems.
