Erysimastrum substrigosum f. longistylis

Erysimastrum substrigosum f. longistylis, commonly known as the long-styled erysimastrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to brownish flower heads. The distinguishing feature of this form is its elongated, slender styles, which are more pronounced than in the typical variety.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
