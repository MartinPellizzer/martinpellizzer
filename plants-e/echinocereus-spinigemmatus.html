Echinocereus spinigemmatus

Echinocereus spinigemmatus, commonly known as the prickly pear cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial cactus that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the spring and attracting pollinators such as bats and birds.

The plant is also known for its edible fruits, which are often harvested and used in local cuisines.
