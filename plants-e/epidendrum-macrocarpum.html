Epidendrum macrocarpum

Epidendrum macrocarpum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length, providing a striking visual appearance. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.

Epidendrum macrocarpum is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
