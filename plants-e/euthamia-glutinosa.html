Euthamia glutinosa

Euthamia glutinosa, commonly known as sticky goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and sticky leaves. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and streambanks. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Euthamia glutinosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
