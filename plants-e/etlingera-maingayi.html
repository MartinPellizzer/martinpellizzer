Etlingera maingayi

Etlingera maingayi, commonly known as the Malayan torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking, red-orange bract that resembles a torch, which is often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
