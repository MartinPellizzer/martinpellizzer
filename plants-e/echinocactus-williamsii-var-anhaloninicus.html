Echinocactus williamsii var. anhaloninicus

Echinocactus williamsii var. anhaloninicus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and prominent, spiny ribs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
