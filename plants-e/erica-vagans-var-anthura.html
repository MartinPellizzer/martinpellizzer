Erica vagans var. anthura

Erica vagans var. anthura, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
