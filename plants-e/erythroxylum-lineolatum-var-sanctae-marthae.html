Erythroxylum lineolatum var. sanctae-marthae

Erythroxylum lineolatum var. sanctae-marthae, commonly known as the Santa Marta coca, is a perennial shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, reddish-brown berries that contain seeds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Due to its association with the coca plant, it has cultural and historical significance in the Andes.
