Epidendrum chrysomyristicum

Epidendrum chrysomyristicum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant blooms during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
