Erica scoparia subsp. platycodon

Erica scoparia subsp.

platycodon, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of narrow, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
