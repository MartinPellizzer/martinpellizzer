Erigeron pseudoseravschanicus f. glabrescens

Erigeron pseudoseravschanicus f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves that become smoother with age, giving it its specific epithet "glabrescens.". The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
