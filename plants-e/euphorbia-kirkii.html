Euphorbia kirkii

Euphorbia kirkii, commonly known as the Kirk's spurge, is a succulent plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Euphorbia kirkii is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
