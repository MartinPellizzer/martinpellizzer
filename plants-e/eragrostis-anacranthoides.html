Eragrostis anacranthoides

Eragrostis anacranthoides, commonly known as narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

Eragrostis anacranthoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
