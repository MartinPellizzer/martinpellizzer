Erythroxylum macrocalyx

Erythroxylum macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white to pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It grows as an upright shrub, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

The plant is notable for its potential medicinal properties and its historical use by indigenous communities.
