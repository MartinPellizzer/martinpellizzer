Eulophia ugandae

Eulophia ugandae, commonly known as the Uganda eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its roots are fibrous, and it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Eulophia ugandae is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
