Epidendrum crucifolium

Epidendrum crucifolium, commonly known as the cross-leaved orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its striking, cross-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to attach to tree trunks or branches.

Epidendrum crucifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
