Eragrostis thunbergiana var. atrata

Eragrostis thunbergiana var. atrata, commonly known as blackgrass or black panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
