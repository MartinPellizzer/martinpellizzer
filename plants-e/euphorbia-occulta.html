Euphorbia occulta

Euphorbia occulta, commonly known as the hidden spurge, is a low-growing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions, preferring well-drained soil.

Euphorbia occulta is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance.
