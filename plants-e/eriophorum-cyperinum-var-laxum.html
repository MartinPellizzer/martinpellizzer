Eriophorum cyperinum var. laxum

Eriophorum cyperinum var. laxum, commonly known as the soft tussock sedge, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as bogs, marshes, and wet meadows, often forming dense vegetation.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small mammals.
