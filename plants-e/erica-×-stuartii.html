Erica × stuartii

Erica × stuartii, commonly known as the Stuart heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica × stuartii is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
