Euodia micrococca var. pubescens

Euodia micrococca var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing before the leaves. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
