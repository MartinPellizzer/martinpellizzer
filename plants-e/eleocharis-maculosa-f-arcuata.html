Eleocharis maculosa f. arcuata

Eleocharis maculosa f. arcuata, commonly known as the speckled spike-rush, is a variant of the speckled rush, characterized by its distinctive, curved inflorescence.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments, often found in marshes and shallow water areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
