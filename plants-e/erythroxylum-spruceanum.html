Erythroxylum spruceanum

Erythroxylum spruceanum, commonly known as the Andean holly, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in humid and cool climates. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

Erythroxylum spruceanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
