Eremogone congesta var. charlestonensis

Eremogone congesta var. charlestonensis, commonly known as the Charleston emerald, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bright green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes and disturbed areas.

Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a notable species within its native range.
