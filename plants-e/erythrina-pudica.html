Erythrina pudica

Erythrina pudica, commonly known as the coral tree or flame of the forest, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Erythrina pudica is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
