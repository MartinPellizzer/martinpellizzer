Eutrochium maculatum var. maculatum

Eutrochium maculatum var. maculatum, commonly known as spotted Joe-pye weed, is a tall, herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features showy, pinkish-white flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats like wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped with distinctive red or purple spots, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
