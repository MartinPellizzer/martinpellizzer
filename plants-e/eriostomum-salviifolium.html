Eriostomum salviifolium

Eriostomum salviifolium, commonly known as the slenderleaf eriostomum, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Eriostomum salviifolium is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
