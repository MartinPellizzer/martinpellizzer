Eugenia lugubris

Eugenia lugubris, commonly known as the sad eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Following flowering, it develops small, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

This species is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests.
