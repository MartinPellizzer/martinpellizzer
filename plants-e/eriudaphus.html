Eriudaphus

Eriudaphus, commonly known as the golden spire, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to three meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater during dry periods.

Eriudaphus is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its striking appearance.
