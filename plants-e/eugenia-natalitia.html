Eugenia natalitia

Eugenia natalitia, commonly known as Natal myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia natalitia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils in full sun.
