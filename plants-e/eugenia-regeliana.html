Eugenia regeliana

Eugenia regeliana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish when mature, containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
