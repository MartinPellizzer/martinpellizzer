Eleocharis multicaulis var. vivipara

Eleocharis multicaulis var. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its reproductive strategy involves vivipary, where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant.

This unique trait allows for rapid colonization of suitable environments, contributing to its ecological significance in aquatic ecosystems.
