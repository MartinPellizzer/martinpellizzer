Epidendrum johannis

Epidendrum johannis, commonly known as the John's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often have a waxy texture to retain water.

Epidendrum johannis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
