Erica unica

Erica unica, commonly known as the unique heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica unica is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh environmental conditions.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.
