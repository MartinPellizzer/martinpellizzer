Eriospermum porphyrium var. pallidum

Eriospermum porphyrium var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its unique floral structure and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
