Encephalartos ferox subsp. emersus

Encephalartos ferox subsp.

emersus, commonly known as the Natal knobbed cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its robust, stout trunk and a crown of stiff, dark green leaves that are often adorned with prominent leaflets.

The plant produces cycad-like cones, with male and female cones typically occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
