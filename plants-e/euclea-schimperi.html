Euclea schimperi

Euclea schimperi, commonly known as the African wild pear, is a small deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
