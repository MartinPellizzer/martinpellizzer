Eriogonum parvifolium subsp. paynei

Eriogonum parvifolium subsp.

paynei, commonly known as Payne's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller leaves and more compact growth form compared to other Eriogonum species.
