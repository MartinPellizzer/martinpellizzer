Eugenia bunchosiifolia

Eugenia bunchosiifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which resembles a star, is typically yellow or red when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
