Epipactis atrorubens var. media

Epipactis atrorubens var. media, commonly known as the red-veined helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and striking, showy flowers with dark red veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to green with a distinctive dark red or purple lip.

It is a perennial herb that reproduces through rhizomes and is known for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
