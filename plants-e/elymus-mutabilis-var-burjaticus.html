Elymus mutabilis var. burjaticus

Elymus mutabilis var. burjaticus, commonly known as the Burjat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
