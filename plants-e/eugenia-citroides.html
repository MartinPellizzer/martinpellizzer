Eugenia citroides

Eugenia citroides, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are sour and not typically eaten by humans. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Additionally, it has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
