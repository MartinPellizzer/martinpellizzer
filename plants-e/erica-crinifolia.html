Erica crinifolia

Erica crinifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its evergreen foliage is dense and leathery, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue that gives it its common name.

Erica crinifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a coastal plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
