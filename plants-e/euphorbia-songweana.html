Euphorbia songweana

Euphorbia songweana, commonly known as the Songwe spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, woody stems and distinctive, milky sap. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a rough texture.

Euphorbia songweana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
