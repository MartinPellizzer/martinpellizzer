Erica discolor

Erica discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery or greyish coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the spring and early summer.

Erica discolor thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
