Erica congesta

Erica congesta, commonly known as the Cape heath or coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly referred to as the heath or heather family. This evergreen shrub typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its small, dense clusters of flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in spring and summer.

Erica congesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
