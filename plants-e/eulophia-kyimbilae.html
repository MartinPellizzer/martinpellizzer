Eulophia kyimbilae

Eulophia kyimbilae, commonly known as the Kyimbili orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the rainforests of Papua New Guinea.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces striking, showy inflorescences with numerous fragrant, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the humid conditions of its native habitat. Its roots are specialized for nutrient absorption, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for successful germination.

Eulophia kyimbilae is a relatively rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
