Euphrasia latifolia var. inundata

Euphrasia latifolia var. inundata, commonly known as the broad-leaved eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties, particularly for eye conditions.
