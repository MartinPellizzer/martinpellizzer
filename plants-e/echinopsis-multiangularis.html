Echinopsis multiangularis

Echinopsis multiangularis, commonly known as the angular moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and bright, showy flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a unique structure with a central stem and a cluster of small, rounded tubercles. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
