Euphorbia ferdinandi var. saxosiplaniticola

Euphorbia ferdinandi var. saxosiplaniticola, commonly known as the rock-plant euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
