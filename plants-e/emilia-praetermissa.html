Emilia praetermissa

Emilia praetermissa, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Emilia praetermissa is known for its delicate, branching structure and its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas where it can spread readily.
