Eupatorium valverdeanum

Eupatorium valverdeanum, commonly known as Valverde's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Eupatorium valverdeanum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
