Erica sieberiana

Erica sieberiana, commonly known as the Sieber's heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica sieberiana typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
