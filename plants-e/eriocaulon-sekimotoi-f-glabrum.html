Eriocaulon sekimotoi f. glabrum

Eriocaulon sekimotoi f. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous marsh ricegrass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, glabrous surface, which sets it apart from other forms of the species.
