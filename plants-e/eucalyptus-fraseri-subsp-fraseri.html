Eucalyptus fraseri subsp. fraseri

Eucalyptus fraseri subsp.

fraseri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a grey-green color and a rough texture. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
