Erythroxylum cuneifolium

Erythroxylum cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain fine, white, powdery seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and floral display.
