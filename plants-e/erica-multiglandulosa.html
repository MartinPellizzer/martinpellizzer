Erica multiglandulosa

Erica multiglandulosa, commonly known as the many-gland heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica multiglandulosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
