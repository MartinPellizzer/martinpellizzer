Eriogonum leptophyllum var. vallis-rubrum

Eriogonum leptophyllum var. vallis-rubrum, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
