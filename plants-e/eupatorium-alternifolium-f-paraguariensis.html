Eupatorium alternifolium f. paraguariensis

Eupatorium alternifolium f. paraguariensis, commonly known as yerba mate, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include a woody stem and aromatic foliage, which contribute to its traditional use in herbal teas.

The species is notable for its role in the preparation of a popular caffeinated beverage.
