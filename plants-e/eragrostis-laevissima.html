Eragrostis laevissima

Eragrostis laevissima, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless leaves and slender, upright stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Eragrostis laevissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
