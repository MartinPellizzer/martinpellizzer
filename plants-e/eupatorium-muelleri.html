Eupatorium muelleri

Eupatorium muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's boneset, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the spring and summer months.

Mueller's boneset is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
