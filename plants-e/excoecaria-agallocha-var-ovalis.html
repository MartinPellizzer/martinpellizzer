Excoecaria agallocha var. ovalis

Excoecaria agallocha var. ovalis, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen tree that thrives in saline environments, often found in mangrove forests along tidal shores. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in brackish water conditions. Its fruit, which resembles an apple, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
