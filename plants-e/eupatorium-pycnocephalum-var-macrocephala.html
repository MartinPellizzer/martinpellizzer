Eupatorium pycnocephalum var. macrocephala

Eupatorium pycnocephalum var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense, rounded heads. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its showy inflorescences make it a valuable addition to wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
