Eupatorium pauperatum

Eupatorium pauperatum, commonly known as poorwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads, often appearing in late summer.

Eupatorium pauperatum is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
