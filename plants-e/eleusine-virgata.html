Eleusine virgata

Eleusine virgata, commonly known as slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a grass-like species that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its roots are often rhizomatous, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

Eleusine virgata is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
