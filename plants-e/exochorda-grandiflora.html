Exochorda grandiflora

Exochorda grandiflora, commonly known as the snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring, creating a striking visual display. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Exochorda grandiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
