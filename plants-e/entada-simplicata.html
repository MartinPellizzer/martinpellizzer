Entada simplicata

Entada simplicata, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain hard, angular seeds. Its bark is thick and ridged, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
