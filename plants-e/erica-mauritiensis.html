Erica mauritiensis

Erica mauritiensis, commonly known as the Natal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica mauritiensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy and attractive foliage.
