Etlingera polyantha

Etlingera polyantha, commonly known as the white torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, pale yellow to white flowers that resemble torches, often arranged in clusters. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Etlingera polyantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
