Eryngium pandanifolium var. pandanifolium

Eryngium pandanifolium var. pandanifolium, commonly known as the pandan-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny bracts that surround the small, blue-violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often mistaken for pandan leaves due to their similar appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
