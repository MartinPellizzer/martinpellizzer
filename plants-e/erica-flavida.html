Erica flavida

Erica flavida, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. Erica flavida typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
