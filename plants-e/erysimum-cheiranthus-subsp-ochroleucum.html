Erysimum cheiranthus subsp. ochroleucum

Erysimum cheiranthus subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of pinnately lobed, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with four petals.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow flowers compared to the more vibrant yellow of the main species.
