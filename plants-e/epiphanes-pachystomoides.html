Epiphanes pachystomoides

Epiphanes pachystomoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.

Its life cycle includes a period of dormancy during dry seasons, making it resilient to environmental stress.
