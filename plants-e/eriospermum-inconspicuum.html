Eriospermum inconspicuum

Eriospermum inconspicuum, commonly known as the inconspicuous eriosperm, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, woolly pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

Eriospermum inconspicuum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
