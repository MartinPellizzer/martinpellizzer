Elaeoselinum foetidum var. brachylobum

Elaeoselinum foetidum var. brachylobum, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its small, green leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruits are small berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
