Eugenioides henschelii

Eugenioides henschelii, commonly known as the Henschel's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
