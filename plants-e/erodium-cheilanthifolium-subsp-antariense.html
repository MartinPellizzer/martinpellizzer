Erodium cheilanthifolium subsp. antariense

Erodium cheilanthifolium subsp.

antariense, commonly known as the Antarctic erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern South America. It is a small annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces pink to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to the extreme conditions of its native habitat.
