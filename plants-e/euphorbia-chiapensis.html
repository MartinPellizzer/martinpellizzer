Euphorbia chiapensis

Euphorbia chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapas spurge, is a succulent plant native to the humid forests of southern Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green花朵 that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
