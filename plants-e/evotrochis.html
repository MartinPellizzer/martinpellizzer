Evotrochis

Evotrochis, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, white, night-blooming flowers that emit a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, while its stems are often hairy and succulent.

Evotrochis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
