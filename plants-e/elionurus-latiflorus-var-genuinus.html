Elionurus latiflorus var. genuinus

Elionurus latiflorus var. genuinus, commonly known as the broadflower elionurus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
