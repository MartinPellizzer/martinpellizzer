Elymus × vancouverensis var. californicus

Elymus × vancouverensis var. californicus, commonly known as the California wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often reach heights of 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, with lemmas that are often hairy and pointed. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

This grass is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.
