Erica umbrosa

Erica umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist conditions. It is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, such as under forest canopies.

Erica umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
