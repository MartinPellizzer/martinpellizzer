Elymus barystachyus

Elymus barystachyus, commonly known as the giant needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the primary reproductive structures of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Elymus barystachyus plays a significant role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
