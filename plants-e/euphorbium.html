Euphorbium

Euphorbium, commonly known as the spurge, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

These plants are characterized by their milky sap, which can be toxic and cause skin irritation. They typically have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. Euphorbium species are native to various regions, including Africa, the Americas, and Asia, and many are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

They play a role in both ecological systems and have been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
