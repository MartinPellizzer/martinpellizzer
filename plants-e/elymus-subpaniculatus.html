Elymus subpaniculatus

Elymus subpaniculatus, commonly known as the Panicled wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Elymus subpaniculatus is valued for its ecological role in preventing erosion and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
