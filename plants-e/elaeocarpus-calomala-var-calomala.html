Elaeocarpus calomala var. calomala

Elaeocarpus calomala var. calomala, commonly known as the Philippine ebony, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its timber and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
