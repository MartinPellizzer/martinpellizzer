Eupatorium caldense

Eupatorium caldense, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded areas. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium caldense is valued in gardens for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
