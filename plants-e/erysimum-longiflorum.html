Erysimum longiflorum

Erysimum longiflorum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately divided, gray-green leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, often around Christmas time, hence its common name. These flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum longiflorum is commonly cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
