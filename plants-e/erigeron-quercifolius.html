Erigeron quercifolius

Erigeron quercifolius, commonly known as the oak-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak tree, giving it its common name. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale blue composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Erigeron quercifolius is a member of the Asteraceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
