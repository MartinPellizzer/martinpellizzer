Eugenia myrobalana

Eugenia myrobalana, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a sour taste. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
