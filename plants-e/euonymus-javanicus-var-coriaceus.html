Euonymus javanicus var. coriaceus

Euonymus javanicus var. coriaceus, commonly known as the rough euonymus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, dark green, leathery leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The varietal form coriaceus is distinguished by its thick, textured foliage compared to other varieties of Euonymus.
