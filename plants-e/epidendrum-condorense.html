Epidendrum condorense

Epidendrum condorense, commonly known as the Condor orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained soil. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.

Epidendrum condorense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
