Eriachne ciliata

Eriachne ciliata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including swamps, marshes, and the margins of water bodies. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a distinctive silky texture along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets.

Eriachne ciliata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
