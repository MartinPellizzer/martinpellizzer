Erythrina corallodendron

Erythrina corallodendron, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, the Americas, and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant has compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its pods are elongated and often covered in a hairy texture. Erythrina corallodendron is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and its tolerance to drought conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
