Eucalyptus kochii subsp. plenissima

Eucalyptus kochii subsp.

plenissima, commonly known as the white mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often white or grey bark that peels in thin strips.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a grey-green upper surface and a white underside. This subspecies produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale green to cream in color.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
