Emblica grandis

Emblica grandis, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. Its botanical features include a smooth, glossy skin and a juicy, sour pulp containing numerous seeds.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
