Ehretia scrobiculata

Ehretia scrobiculata, commonly known as the rough-leaved ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, elliptical leaves that are often rough to the touch. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
