Erythroxylum nitidulum subsp. monticola

Erythroxylum nitidulum subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the Andean beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers and produces bright red berries that are visually striking.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
