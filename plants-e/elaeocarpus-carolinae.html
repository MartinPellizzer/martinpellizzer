Elaeocarpus carolinae

Elaeocarpus carolinae, commonly known as the Carolina elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. The plant belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive leaves and showy flowers. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
