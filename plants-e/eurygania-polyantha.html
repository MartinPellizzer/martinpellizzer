Eurygania polyantha

Eurygania polyantha, commonly known as the red-flowered beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that develop into bright red berries. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The berries are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds.
