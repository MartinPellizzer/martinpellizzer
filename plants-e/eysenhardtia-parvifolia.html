Eysenhardtia parvifolia

Eysenhardtia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered eysenhardtia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its botanical features include woody stems, hairy foliage, and a compact growth habit.

It is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
