Eleutherine guatemalensis

Eleutherine guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan yellow iris, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a distinctive purple stripe. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
