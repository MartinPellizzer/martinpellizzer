Epidendrum mancum

Epidendrum mancum, commonly known as the Purple-veined Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are borne on branching inflorescences and are known for their strong, spicy fragrance.

Epidendrum mancum is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
