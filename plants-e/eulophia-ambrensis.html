Eulophia ambrensis

Eulophia ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrean orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically produces a single, slender stem that bears a few lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Eulophia ambrensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
