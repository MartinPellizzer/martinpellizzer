Eugenia sphenophylla var. tristis

Eugenia sphenophylla var. tristis, commonly known as the slender sourwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, a small, reddish-purple drupe, develops in the summer and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
