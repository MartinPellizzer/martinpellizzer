Eupatorium ballotifolium

Eupatorium ballotifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough texture and often have a distinctive white or grayish underside.

It is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
