Escobedia curialis

Escobedia curialis, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Escobedia curialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
