Eugenia teysmannii

Eugenia teysmannii, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its fruit is a small, round, fleshy berry that is typically red or yellow when mature.

Eugenia teysmannii is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
