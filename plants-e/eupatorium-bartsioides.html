Eupatorium bartsioides

Eupatorium bartsioides, commonly known as the Madagascar boneset, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, dry fruits called cypsela. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
