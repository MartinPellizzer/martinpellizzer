Euodia daniellii var. hupehensis

Euodia daniellii var. hupehensis, commonly known as Chinese frankincense or hupeh frankincense, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, woody bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers followed by dark blue or black berries. Its bark is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
