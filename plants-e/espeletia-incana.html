Espeletia incana

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment. Its structure is often mistaken for a tree due to its upright, branching form and woody base.

Espeletia incana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
