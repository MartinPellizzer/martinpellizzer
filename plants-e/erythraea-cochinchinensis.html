Erythraea cochinchinensis

Erythraea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese erythrina, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces elongated pods that contain seeds.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
