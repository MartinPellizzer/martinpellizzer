Erica anthina

Erica anthina, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica anthina thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
