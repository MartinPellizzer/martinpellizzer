Euphorbia mazarronensis

Euphorbia mazarronensis, commonly known as the Mazarron spurge, is a succulent shrub native to southern Spain.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Euphorbia mazarronensis is often found in rocky soils and coastal areas.
