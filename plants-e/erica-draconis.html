Erica draconis

Erica draconis, commonly known as dragon heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica draconis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or heathland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
