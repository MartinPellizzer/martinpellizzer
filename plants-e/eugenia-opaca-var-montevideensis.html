Eugenia opaca var. montevideensis

Eugenia opaca var. montevideensis, commonly known as the Montevideo guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas with well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
