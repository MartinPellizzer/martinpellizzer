Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp. mauritanicum

Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp.

mauritanicum, commonly known as the Mauritania thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structure.
