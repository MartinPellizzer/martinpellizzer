Eragrostis interrupta var. diarrhena

Eragrostis interrupta var. diarrhena, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Hairy fingergrass is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
