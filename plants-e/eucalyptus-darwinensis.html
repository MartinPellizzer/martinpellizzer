Eucalyptus darwinensis

Eucalyptus darwinensis, commonly known as Darwin's eucalyptus, is a tall, slender tree native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of grey and white. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in open woodlands and riverine areas.
