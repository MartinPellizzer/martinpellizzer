Eugenia dicrossa var. latifolia

Eugenia dicrossa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a small, berry-like drupe that turns red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
