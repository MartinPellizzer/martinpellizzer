Erigeron karvinskianus var. mucronatus

Erigeron karvinskianus var. mucronatus, commonly known as the Mexican Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in dry conditions.
