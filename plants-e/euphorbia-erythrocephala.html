Euphorbia erythrocephala

Euphorbia erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
