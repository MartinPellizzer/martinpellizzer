Eriogonum effusum var. corymbosum

Eriogonum effusum var. corymbosum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular-hairy foliage and clusters of small, pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, including slopes and meadows. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators.

The species is well adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
