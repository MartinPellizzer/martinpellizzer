Eugenia rosmarinifolia var. longifolia

Eugenia rosmarinifolia var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved clove tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant and have a slightly woody texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pinkish flowers that are followed by dark purple or black berries. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cloves.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
