Epiphyllum phyllanthus var. pittieri

Epiphyllum phyllanthus var. pittieri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular epiphytic cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its segmented, thornless stems that often branch out into vibrant, showy flowers. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its blooming season typically occurs in late autumn to winter, producing colorful blossoms in shades of red, pink, and white.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a favored houseplant among gardeners.
