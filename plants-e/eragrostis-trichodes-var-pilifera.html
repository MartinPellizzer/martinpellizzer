Eragrostis trichodes var. pilifera

Eragrostis trichodes var. pilifera, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to mesic habitats, such as prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
