Euptilia cretica

Euptilia cretica, commonly known as the Cretan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Euptilia cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
