Equisetum × trachyodon f. anglicum

Equisetum × trachyodon f. anglicum, commonly known as the English horse-tail, is a hybrid fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a central, unbranched stem. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It possesses a unique reproductive structure called a strobilus, which contains sporangia for spore production.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era, and its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
