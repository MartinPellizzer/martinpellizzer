Euphrasia officinalis subsp. nemorosa

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

nemorosa, commonly known as wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale violet flower cluster at the tip of each stem. Its flowers are often yellow or orange, with five petals and a spurred nectar gland.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
