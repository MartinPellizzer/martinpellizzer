Ecbolium latifolium

Ecbolium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf ecbolium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ecbolium latifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
