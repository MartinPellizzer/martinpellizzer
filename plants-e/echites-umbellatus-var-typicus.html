Echites umbellatus var. typicus

Echites umbellatus var. typicus, commonly known as the umbrella leaf orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral structure.
