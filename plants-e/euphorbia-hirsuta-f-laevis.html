Euphorbia hirsuta f. laevis

Euphorbia hirsuta f. laevis, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant characterized by its smooth, pale green stems and thick, fleshy leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

The plant contains toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation, so caution is advised when handling it.
