Evolvulus alsinoides var. erectus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. erectus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a decorative and sometimes invasive species in certain areas.
