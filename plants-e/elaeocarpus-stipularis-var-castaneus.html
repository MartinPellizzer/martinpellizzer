Elaeocarpus stipularis var. castaneus

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. castaneus, commonly known as the black ironwood, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark, dense wood and elliptical leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are small and white, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces globose, woody fruits that are typically reddish or black when mature.

The species is valued for its hardiness and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
