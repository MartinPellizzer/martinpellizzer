Epidendrum paytense

Epidendrum paytense, commonly known as the Payten's epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid conditions.

Epidendrum paytense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
