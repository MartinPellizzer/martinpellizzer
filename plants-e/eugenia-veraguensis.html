Eugenia veraguensis

Eugenia veraguensis, commonly known as the Guanabana or soursop, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces a large, spiky fruit with a green, thick rind and a white, fibrous pulp containing numerous black seeds. The fruit is rich in nutrients and has a unique, sweet-and-sour flavor.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional remedies.
