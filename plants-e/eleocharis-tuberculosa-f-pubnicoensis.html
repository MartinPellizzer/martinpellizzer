Eleocharis tuberculosa f. pubnicoensis

Eleocharis tuberculosa f. pubnicoensis, commonly known as the pubescent sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to varying water levels and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
