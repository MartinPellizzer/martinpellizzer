Encopa tenuifolia

Encopa tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique floral structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.

Encopa tenuifolia is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
