Evolvulus alsinoides f. viridis

Evolvulus alsinoides f. viridis, commonly known as the green morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The foliage and flowers are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
