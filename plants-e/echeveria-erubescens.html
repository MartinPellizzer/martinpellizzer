Echeveria erubescens

Echeveria erubescens, commonly known as the "Blushing Echeveria," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that turn pink or red when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Echeveria erubescens is popular in rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.
