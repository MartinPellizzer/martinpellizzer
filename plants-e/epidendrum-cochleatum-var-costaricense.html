Epidendrum cochleatum var. costaricense

Epidendrum cochleatum var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive, almost spiral-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a sweet fragrance that attracts pollinators.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique morphology and ornamental appeal.
