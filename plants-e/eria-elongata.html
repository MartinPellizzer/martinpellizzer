Eria elongata

Eria elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, often with a faintly colored lip. Eria elongata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation in warm climates.
