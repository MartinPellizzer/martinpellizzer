Euodia pasteuriana

Euodia pasteuriana, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. After flowering, it produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
