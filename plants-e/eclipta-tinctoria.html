Eclipta tinctoria

Eclipta tinctoria, commonly known as Mexican sunflower or yellow ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flower heads, which are composed of numerous small ray florets surrounding a central disk. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often opposite in arrangement. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
