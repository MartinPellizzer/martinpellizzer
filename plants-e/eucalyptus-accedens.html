Eucalyptus accedens

Eucalyptus accedens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and distinctive floral spikes that appear in winter. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and salt.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal uses.
