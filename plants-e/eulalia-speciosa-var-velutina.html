Eulalia speciosa var. velutina

Eulalia speciosa var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silky, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a unique and textured foliage plant.
