Echium salmanticum

Echium salmanticum, commonly known as the Salmanticum thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
