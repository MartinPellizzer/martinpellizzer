Eragrostis guingensis

Eragrostis guingensis, commonly known as Guing's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
