Euphorbia vaticana

Euphorbia vaticana, commonly known as the sacred euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has a thick, succulent appearance, adapted to thrive in arid environments. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Euphorbia vaticana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
