Ericoides azoricum

Ericoides azoricum, commonly known as the Azorean heath, is a flowering plant native to the Azores.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Ericoides azoricum plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
