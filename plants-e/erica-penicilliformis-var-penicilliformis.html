Erica penicilliformis var. penicilliformis

Erica penicilliformis var. penicilliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen habit with dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica penicilliformis var. penicilliformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
