Eria kingii

Eria kingii, commonly known as the King's eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eria kingii is prized by horticulturists for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
