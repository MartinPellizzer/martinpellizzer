Eryngium campestre var. megacephalum

Eryngium campestre var. megacephalum, commonly known as the large-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spiny, spherical flower heads that are typically blue to purple in color, surrounded by a collar of bracts. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, yellow flowers in the center of the inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
