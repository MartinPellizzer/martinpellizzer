Eupatorium viridiflorum

Eupatorium viridiflorum, commonly known as greenflower thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers, which are greenish-white in color, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

Eupatorium viridiflorum is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
