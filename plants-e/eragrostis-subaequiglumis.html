Eragrostis subaequiglumis

Eragrostis subaequiglumis, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, often forming dense stands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
