Epacris delicatissima

Epacris delicatissima, commonly known as the slender epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and woodland areas, preferring well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Epacris delicatissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to garden environments.
