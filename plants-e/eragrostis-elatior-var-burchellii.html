Eragrostis elatior var. burchellii

Eragrostis elatior var. burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Burchell's lovegrass is often used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
