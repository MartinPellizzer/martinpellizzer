Epacris rigida

Epacris rigida, commonly known as the rigid epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its erect, woody stems and clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.

Epacris rigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal environments.
