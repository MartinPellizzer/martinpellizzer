Erica hyssopifolia

Erica hyssopifolia, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in areas with high sunlight exposure. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erica hyssopifolia is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
