Eugenia mangasiana

Eugenia mangasiana, commonly known as the Mexican myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia mangasiana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and is also valued for its aromatic properties.
