Epidendrum loefgrenii

Epidendrum loefgrenii, commonly known as the Loefgren's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Epidendrum loefgrenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
