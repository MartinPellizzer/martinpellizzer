Elymus barbicallus

Elymus barbicallus, commonly known as the bearded wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, grass-like foliage with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Elymus barbicallus is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
