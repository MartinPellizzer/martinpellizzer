Epipactis viridans var. lancifolia

Epipactis viridans var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and greenish-yellow flowers. The plant has a complex root system and produces a single spike of flowers with showy, spurred petals. It is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
