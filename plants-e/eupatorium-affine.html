Eupatorium affine

Eupatorium affine, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Boneset is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
