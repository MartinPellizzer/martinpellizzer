Elaeocarpus oxyadenius

Elaeocarpus oxyadenius, commonly known as the white-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces fleshy, reddish-purple fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture-making.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting diverse wildlife in its native habitats.
