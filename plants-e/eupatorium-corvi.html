Eupatorium corvi

Eupatorium corvi, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium corvi is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine.
