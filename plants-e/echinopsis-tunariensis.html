Echinopsis tunariensis

Echinopsis tunariensis, commonly known as the Tunariensis Cactus, is a species native to South America, specifically found in parts of Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stem and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, ribbed structure.

Its flowers bloom during the cooler months, often at night, and are pollinated by nocturnal insects and bats.
