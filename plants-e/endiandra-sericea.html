Endiandra sericea

Endiandra sericea, commonly known as the silky endiandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
