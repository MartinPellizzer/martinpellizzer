Epidendrum chimborazoense

Epidendrum chimborazoense, commonly known as the Chimborazo Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom in dense clusters along the stem. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
