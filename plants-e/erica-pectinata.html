Erica pectinata

Erica pectinata, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica pectinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
