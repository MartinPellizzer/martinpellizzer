Eschscholzia straminea

Eschscholzia straminea, commonly known as the yellow poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to California.

It features bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its natural spread across its native habitat.
