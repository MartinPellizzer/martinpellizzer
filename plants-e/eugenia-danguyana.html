Eugenia danguyana

Eugenia danguyana, commonly known as the Danguy's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are generally small, round, and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.
