Erodium malacoides var. garamantum

Erodium malacoides var. garamantum, commonly known as the Garamantian redstem, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, reddish stems and small, pink to violet flowers. The plant features alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive glandular texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
