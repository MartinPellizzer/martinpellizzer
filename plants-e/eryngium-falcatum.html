Eryngium falcatum

Eryngium falcatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple or pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eryngium falcatum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
