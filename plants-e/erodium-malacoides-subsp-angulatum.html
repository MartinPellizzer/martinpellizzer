Erodium malacoides subsp. angulatum

Erodium malacoides subsp.

angulatum, commonly known as the angular erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small annual herb with slender stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that have a distinctive angular appearance.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new habitats.
