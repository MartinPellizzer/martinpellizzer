Eulophia chrysea

Eulophia chrysea, commonly known as the golden eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers are often clustered in inflorescences and can bloom multiple times a year.

Eulophia chrysea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
