Ensete superbum

Ensete superbum, commonly known as the African banana tree, is a large herbaceous plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its tall, clustered stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces a pseudostem that eventually splits to reveal a cluster of edible, starchy fruits. Ensete superbum is adapted to grow in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its nutritional value and agricultural significance.

Its cultivation plays a vital role in the subsistence farming practices of local communities.
