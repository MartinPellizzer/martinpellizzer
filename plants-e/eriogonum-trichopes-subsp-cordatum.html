Eriogonum trichopes subsp. cordatum

Eriogonum trichopes subsp.

cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more rounded leaf shape compared to other forms of Eriogonum trichopes.
