Ericoides sagittatum

Ericoides sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf ericoides, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in montane or cloud forest environments. Its leaves are narrow and arrow-shaped, and it produces small, usually white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
