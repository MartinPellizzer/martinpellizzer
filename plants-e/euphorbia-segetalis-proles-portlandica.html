Euphorbia segetalis proles portlandica

Euphorbia segetalis proles portlandica, commonly known as the Portland spurge, is a variant of the common spurge.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and milky sap. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rocky landscapes.
