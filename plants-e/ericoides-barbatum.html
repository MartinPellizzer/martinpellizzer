Ericoides barbatum

Ericoides barbatum, commonly known as the hairy eric, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ericoides barbatum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
