Erodium cicutarium var. pimpinellifolium

Erodium cicutarium var. pimpinellifolium, commonly known as the common sticky weed, is a flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and sticky, glandular hairs on its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides. It has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This variety is native to Europe and has been naturalized in many parts of the world.
