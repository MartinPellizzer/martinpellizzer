Endodeca reticulata

Endodeca reticulata, commonly known as the net-veined fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate, lacy fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Asia. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping stems that produce clusters of small, delicate fronds. Its leaves are typically divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a distinctive reticulate pattern.

Endodeca reticulata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
