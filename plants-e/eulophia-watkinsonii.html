Eulophia watkinsonii

Eulophia watkinsonii, commonly known as the Watson's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, erect, lance-shaped leaf. The plant produces a raceme of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular species among orchid enthusiasts and botanists.
