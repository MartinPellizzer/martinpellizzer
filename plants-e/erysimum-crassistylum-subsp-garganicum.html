Erysimum crassistylum subsp. garganicum

Erysimum crassistylum subsp.

garganicum, commonly known as the Alaskan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the northwestern United States and southern Canada. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
