Euteline germanica

Euteline germanica, commonly known as the German gentian, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Euteline germanica has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
