Eriogonum effusum var. rosmarinoides

Eriogonum effusum var. rosmarinoides, commonly known as Rosmarinus-like Buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a mint-like fragrance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in its native habitats.
