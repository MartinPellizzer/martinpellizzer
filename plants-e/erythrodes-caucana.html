Erythrodes caucana

Erythrodes caucana, commonly known as the Caucasus redwood, is a rare and endangered species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Due to habitat loss and climate change, it faces significant threats to its survival.

Conservation efforts are underway to protect and propagate this unique botanical species.
