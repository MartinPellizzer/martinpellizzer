Echinocactus coptonogonus var. major

Echinocactus coptonogonus var. major, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a globular, ribbed stem with prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its vibrant, radial spines are often a mix of red, yellow, and brown, adding to its ornamental appeal.

The variety is particularly noted for its unique coloration and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
