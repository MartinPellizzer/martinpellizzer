Eugenia stolonifera

Eugenia stolonifera, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark red or black color.

Eugenia stolonifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in saline environments.
