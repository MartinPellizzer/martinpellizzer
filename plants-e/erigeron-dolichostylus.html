Erigeron dolichostylus

Erigeron dolichostylus, commonly known as the long-styled blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The long, slender style of its flowers is a distinguishing feature, contributing to its scientific name.
