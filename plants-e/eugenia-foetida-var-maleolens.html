Eugenia foetida var. maleolens

Eugenia foetida var. maleolens, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is known for its strong, pleasant aroma, which is released when the leaves are crushed.
