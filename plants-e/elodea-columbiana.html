Elodea columbiana

Elodea columbiana, commonly known as Western waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, round fruits. Elodea columbiana thrives in freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and slow-moving streams.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
