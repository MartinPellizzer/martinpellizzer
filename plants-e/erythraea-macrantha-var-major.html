Erythraea macrantha var. major

Erythraea macrantha var. major, commonly known as the greater redroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features a robust, branched root system that is reddish in color, giving the plant its common name. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
