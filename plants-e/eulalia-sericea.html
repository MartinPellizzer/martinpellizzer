Eulalia sericea

Eulalia sericea, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades and fluffy, feathery seed heads that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and low-maintenance gardens. Its foliage is covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive silvery sheen.

Eulalia sericea is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators like birds and insects.
