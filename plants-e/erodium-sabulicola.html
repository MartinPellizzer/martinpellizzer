Erodium sabulicola

Erodium sabulicola, commonly known as sand erodium, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered pink or purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Erodium sabulicola is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to the dry, coastal climate of its native habitat.
