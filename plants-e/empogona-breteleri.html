Empogona breteleri

Empogona breteleri, commonly known as the African bitter apple, is a small, deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by fleshy, yellow or orange berries. These berries are edible when ripe but are known to be quite bitter and have a strong, unpleasant taste.

Empogona breteleri is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
