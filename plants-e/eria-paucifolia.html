Eria paucifolia

Eria paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
