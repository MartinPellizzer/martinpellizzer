Eulophia excavata

Eulophia excavata, commonly known as the hollow-leaf eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces showy, greenish-white to yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, often with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a waxy surface, and the plant thrives in shaded, moist habitats.

Eulophia excavata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
