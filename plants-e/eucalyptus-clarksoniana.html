Eucalyptus clarksoniana

Eucalyptus clarksoniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stringybark, is a tall, slender eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and smooth, flaky bark that peels in long strips. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or cream in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

Eucalyptus clarksoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in traditional medicine.
