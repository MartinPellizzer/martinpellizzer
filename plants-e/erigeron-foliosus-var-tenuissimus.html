Erigeron foliosus var. tenuissimus

Erigeron foliosus var. tenuissimus, commonly known as narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
