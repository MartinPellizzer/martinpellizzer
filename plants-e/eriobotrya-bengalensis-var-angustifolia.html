Eriobotrya bengalensis var. angustifolia

Eriobotrya bengalensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf loquat, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit yield.

The varietal form, angustifolia, distinguishes it by its narrower leaf structure compared to other varieties.
