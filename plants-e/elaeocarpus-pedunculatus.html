Elaeocarpus pedunculatus

Elaeocarpus pedunculatus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or orange when ripe, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
