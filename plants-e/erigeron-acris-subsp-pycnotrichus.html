Erigeron acris subsp. pycnotrichus

Erigeron acris subsp.

pycnotrichus, commonly known as the thick-hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves with dense, stiff hairs. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

It is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows.
