Epacris impressa var. rhodantha

Epacris impressa var. rhodantha, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
