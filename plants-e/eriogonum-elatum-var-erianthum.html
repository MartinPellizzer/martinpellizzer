Eriogonum elatum var. erianthum

Eriogonum elatum var. erianthum, commonly known as the stickyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky resin, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
