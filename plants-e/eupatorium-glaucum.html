Eupatorium glaucum

Eupatorium glaucum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure.

Eupatorium glaucum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in wetland restoration projects.
