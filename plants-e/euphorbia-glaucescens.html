Euphorbia glaucescens

Euphorbia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
