Eschweilera carrii

Eschweilera carrii, commonly known as the Carrii's ceiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and can grow up to 40 meters tall. The tree produces showy, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for numerous species.
