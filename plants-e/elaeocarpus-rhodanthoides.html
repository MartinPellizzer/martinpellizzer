Elaeocarpus rhodanthoides

Elaeocarpus rhodanthoides, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and striking red flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically green when mature but turn red upon ripening. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
