Euodia obtusifolia var. cuneifolia

Euodia obtusifolia var. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by small, woody fruits containing aromatic resinous seeds. It is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The varietal form, cuneifolia, distinguishes it by its narrower leaf shape compared to the typical variety.
