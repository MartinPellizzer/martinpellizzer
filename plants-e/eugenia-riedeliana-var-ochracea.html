Eugenia riedeliana var. ochracea

Eugenia riedeliana var. ochracea, commonly known as the yellow myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and seasonal flowering, making it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.
