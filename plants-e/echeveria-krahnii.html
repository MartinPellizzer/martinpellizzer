Echeveria krahnii

Echeveria krahnii, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps to reduce water loss. It produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months.

This species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
