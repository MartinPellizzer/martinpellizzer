Eragrostis limbata

Eragrostis limbata, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct, often hairy texture. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color.

Eragrostis limbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
