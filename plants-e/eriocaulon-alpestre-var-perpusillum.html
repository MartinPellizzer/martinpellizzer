Eriocaulon alpestre var. perpusillum

Eriocaulon alpestre var. perpusillum, commonly known as the alpine spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountain stream banks. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its cold, arid environment.

The species is noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions, making it a characteristic component of alpine flora.
