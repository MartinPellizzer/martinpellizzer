Eucalyptus viminalis var. macrocarpa

Eucalyptus viminalis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the river gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white, pink, and grey. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The tree is well adapted to wetter environments and is often found along riverbanks and in swampy areas.
