Euonymus angustifolius

Euonymus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Euonymus angustifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural ecosystems.
