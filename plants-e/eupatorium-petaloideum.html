Eupatorium petaloideum

Eupatorium petaloideum, commonly known as the pink coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that resemble coneflowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium petaloideum is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
