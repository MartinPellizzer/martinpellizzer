Echium italicum subsp. biebersteinii

Echium italicum subsp.

biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra Madre evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
