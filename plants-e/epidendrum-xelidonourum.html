Epidendrum xelidonourum

Epidendrum xelidonourum, commonly known as the Xelidonia orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
