Echinospermum subdecumbens

Echinospermum subdecumbens, commonly known as the creeping echinospermum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with a sprawling habit, often forming dense mats on the ground. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are linear to oblanceolate, with hairy surfaces, and the stems are often covered in fine pubescence.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
