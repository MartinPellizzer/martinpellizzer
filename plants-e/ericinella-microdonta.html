Ericinella microdonta

Ericinella microdonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ericinella, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant typically features small, oval-shaped leaves that are densely packed along its stems. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark berries as fruit.

Ericinella microdonta thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.
