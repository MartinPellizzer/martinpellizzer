Ethesia

Ethesia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to the Americas. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, yellowish flowers that are arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Ethesia species are often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
