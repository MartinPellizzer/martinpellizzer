Eulophia milanjiana

Eulophia milanjiana, commonly known as the Milanjian's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which often display vibrant colors, bloom in clusters and are pollinated by various insects.

Eulophia milanjiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
