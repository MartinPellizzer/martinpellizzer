Euphorbia lakshminarasimhanii

Euphorbia lakshminarasimhanii, commonly known as the Lakshminarasimhan euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Indian state of Tamil Nadu.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble small cup-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
