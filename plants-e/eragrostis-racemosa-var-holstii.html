Eragrostis racemosa var. holstii

Eragrostis racemosa var. holstii, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped flowers.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
