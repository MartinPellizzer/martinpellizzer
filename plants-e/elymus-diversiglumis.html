Elymus diversiglumis

Elymus diversiglumis, commonly known as the variable brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles that are often loosely arranged. The plant produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture.

Elymus diversiglumis is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils.
