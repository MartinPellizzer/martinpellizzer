Eulophia adenoglossa

Eulophia adenoglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, spurred flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eulophia adenoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
