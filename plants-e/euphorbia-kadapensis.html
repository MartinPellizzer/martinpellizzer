Euphorbia kadapensis

Euphorbia kadapensis, commonly known as Kadapensis Spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, providing additional protection against intense sunlight and drought.

Euphorbia kadapensis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
