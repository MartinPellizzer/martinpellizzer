Elaeocarpus brachypodus

Elaeocarpus brachypodus, commonly known as the short-stalked elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The tree produces woody, spherical fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
