Ericoides walkeria

Ericoides walkeria, commonly known as the Walkerville heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the southern regions of Africa.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal dunes and sandy habitats. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are usually arranged in a compact, bushy form. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming during the cooler months.

Ericoides walkeria is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils in coastal environments.
