Euphorbia ankaranae

Euphorbia ankaranae, commonly known as the Ankarana euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to greenish-white. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique defensive mechanisms.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
