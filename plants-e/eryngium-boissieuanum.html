Eryngium boissieuanum

Eryngium boissieuanum, commonly known as the Boissieu eryngo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble a thistle. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Eryngium boissieuanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
