Erica confusa

Erica confusa, commonly known as the confusion heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Erica confusa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
