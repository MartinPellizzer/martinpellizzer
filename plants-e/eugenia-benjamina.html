Eugenia benjamina

Eugenia benjamina, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
