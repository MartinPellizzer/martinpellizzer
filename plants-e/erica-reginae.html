Erica reginae

Erica reginae, commonly known as the queen's heath, is a species of shrub native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica reginae thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
