Erica silvatica

Erica silvatica, commonly known as the silver heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family.

It is native to the western and southern regions of Europe, particularly in areas with acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Erica silvatica thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and heathlands.

It plays an important role in supporting local biodiversity and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
