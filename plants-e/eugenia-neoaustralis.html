Eugenia neoaustralis

Eugenia neoaustralis, commonly known as the New South Wales guava, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a humid, subtropical climate.

Eugenia neoaustralis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
