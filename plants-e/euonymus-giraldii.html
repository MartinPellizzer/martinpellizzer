Euonymus giraldii

Euonymus giraldii, commonly known as the Italian spindle tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive orange-red berries that persist through winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Euonymus giraldii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide seasonal interest in gardens.
