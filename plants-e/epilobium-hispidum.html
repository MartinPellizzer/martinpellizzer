Epilobium hispidum

Epilobium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems covered in glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive hairy appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium hispidum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
