Eupatorium purpureum f. decolor

Eupatorium purpureum f. decolor, commonly known as the white boneset, is a variant of the boneset plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple of the species. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along streams, thriving in moist, sunny environments. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
