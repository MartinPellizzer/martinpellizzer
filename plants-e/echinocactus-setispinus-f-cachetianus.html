Echinocactus setispinus f. cachetianus

Echinocactus setispinus f. cachetianus, commonly known as the Cachetianus Echinocactus, is a unique variant of the standard Echinocactus setispinus.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny ribs and a compact, globular shape. The plant features a deep green body with a slight bluish tint, and its spines are typically reddish-brown. It is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This succulent is prized for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in cactus gardens.
