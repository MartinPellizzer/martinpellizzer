Embelia grossularia

Embelia grossularia, commonly known as the Indian embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include opposite leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive aromatic scent.

Embelia grossularia is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
