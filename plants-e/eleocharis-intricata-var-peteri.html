Eleocharis intricata var. peteri

Eleocharis intricata var. peteri, commonly known as the Peter's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are usually unbranched, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
