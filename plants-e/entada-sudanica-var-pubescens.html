Entada sudanica var. pubescens

Entada sudanica var. pubescens, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a tropical climbing legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces distinctive, spirally arranged pods that contain hard, angular seeds. Its flowers are small and yellowish, typical of leguminous plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in various African cultures.
