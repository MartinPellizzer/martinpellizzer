Elleanthus aureus

Elleanthus aureus, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and vibrant color.

Elleanthus aureus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
