Echinocactus uncinatus var. wrightii

Echinocactus uncinatus var. wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's hedgehog cactus, is a slow-growing, spine-tipped cactus native to the deserts of Texas and northern Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, white to yellow spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with succulent stems that store water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
