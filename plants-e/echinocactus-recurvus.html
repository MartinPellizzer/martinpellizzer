Echinocactus recurvus

Echinocactus recurvus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny appearance and a dense covering of bright yellow spines. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor cultivation.
