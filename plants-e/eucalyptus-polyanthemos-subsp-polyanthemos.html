Eucalyptus polyanthemos subsp. polyanthemos

Eucalyptus polyanthemos subsp.

polyanthemos, commonly known as the honey gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically during spring and summer.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
