Escobaria vivipara var. bisbeeana

Escobaria vivipara var. bisbeeana, commonly known as the Bisbee livefoot, is a succulent plant native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems that often produce offsets, giving it a livefoot appearance. The plant produces white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
