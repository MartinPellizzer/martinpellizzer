Eugenia sachetiae

Eugenia sachetiae, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of pink to white. The plant produces small, round, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful flowers.

Eugenia sachetiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
