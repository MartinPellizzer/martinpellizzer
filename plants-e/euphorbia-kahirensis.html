Euphorbia kahirensis

Euphorbia kahirensis, commonly known as the Kahirensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, further reducing water loss.

Euphorbia kahirensis is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xerophytic gardens.
