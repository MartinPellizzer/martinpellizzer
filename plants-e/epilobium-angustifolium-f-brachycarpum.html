Epilobium angustifolium f. brachycarpum

Epilobium angustifolium f. brachycarpum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb.

It is a variant of the narrow-leaved willowherb, characterized by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to the typical form. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers. It is typically found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadsides, ditches, and wet meadows.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
