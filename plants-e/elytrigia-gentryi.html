Elytrigia gentryi

Elytrigia gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
