Eupatorium subglabratum

Eupatorium subglabratum, commonly known as the smooth eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshy areas. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium subglabratum is also known for its aromatic foliage, which can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
