Euterpe pubigera

Euterpe pubigera, commonly known as the red-veined bamboo, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered stems and elongated, lanceolate leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, sweet fruits similar to those of the açaí palm. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural cultivation.
