Erycibe paniculata var. subspicata

Erycibe paniculata var. subspicata, commonly known as the red spider lily, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

The species is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance in certain regions.
