Eriobotrya fragrans var. fragrans

Eriobotrya fragrans var. fragrans, commonly known as the loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet-tart flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a fragrant aroma. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The loquat is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
