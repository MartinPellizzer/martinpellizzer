Eugenia rufa

Eugenia rufa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Eugenia rufa thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
