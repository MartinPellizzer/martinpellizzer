Eupatorium erodiifolium var. obtusiloba

Eupatorium erodiifolium var. obtusiloba, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy on both surfaces. Its small, clustered white flowers appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and ornamental appeal.
