Erica dicranifolia

Erica dicranifolia, commonly known as the tufted heath, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa, particularly found in the fynbos biome, which is known for its rich biodiversity. The plant is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form with dense, needle-like leaves. Erica dicranifolia produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
