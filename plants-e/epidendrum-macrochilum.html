Epidendrum macrochilum

Epidendrum macrochilum, commonly known as the large-lipped epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, featuring a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and slightly hairy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

It requires well-draining soil and consistent moisture to flourish.
