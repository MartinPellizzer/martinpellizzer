Eriocaulon pilosum

Eriocaulon pilosum, commonly known as the woolly ricegrass, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and has small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon pilosum is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique texture and adaptability to moist conditions.
