Eragrostis leonina

Eragrostis leonina, commonly known as lemon grass or lemon grass panicum, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. It has been used traditionally for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to different climates and soil types.
