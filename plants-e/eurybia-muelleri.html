Eurybia muelleri

Eurybia muelleri, commonly known as Mueller's goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Eurybia muelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic features.
