Eragrostis reflexa

Eragrostis reflexa, commonly known as silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation across open habitats.

Eragrostis reflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
