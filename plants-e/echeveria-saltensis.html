Echeveria saltensis

Echeveria saltensis, commonly known as the Salt Lake echeveria, is a succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its compact size and striking foliage make it a desirable ornamental plant for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
