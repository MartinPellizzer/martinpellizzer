Evolvulus echioides var. longepilosus

Evolvulus echioides var. longepilosus, commonly known as the long-haired morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems, which give it its varietal name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative ground cover.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it suitable for various ornamental purposes.
