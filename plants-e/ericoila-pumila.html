Ericoila pumila

Ericoila pumila, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom in spring and early summer. Ericoila pumila thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
