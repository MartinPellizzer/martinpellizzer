Erigeron elongatiformis

Erigeron elongatiformis, commonly known as the long-stemmed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. Its slender stems and white to pale blue flowers bloom from late summer to early fall.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
