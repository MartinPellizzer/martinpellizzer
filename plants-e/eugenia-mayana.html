Eugenia mayana

Eugenia mayana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp.

Eugenia mayana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
