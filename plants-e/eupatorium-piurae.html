Eupatorium piurae

Eupatorium piurae, commonly known as the Piura eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium piurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
