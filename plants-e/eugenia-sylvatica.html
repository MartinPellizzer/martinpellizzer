Eugenia sylvatica

Eugenia sylvatica, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers and dark red, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The fruit, which ripens in late summer, is rich in vitamin C and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
