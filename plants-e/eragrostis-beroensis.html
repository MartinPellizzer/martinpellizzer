Eragrostis beroensis

Eragrostis beroensis, commonly known as Bero's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

It is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
