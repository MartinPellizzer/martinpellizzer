Eupatorium intermedium

Eupatorium intermedium, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, clustered white or pale blue flowers.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
