Euonymus schensianus

Euonymus schensianus, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red stems that become more pronounced in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has alternate, simple, and elliptic-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds.

Euonymus schensianus thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.
