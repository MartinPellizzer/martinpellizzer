Eupatorium constanzae

Eupatorium constanzae, commonly known as the Constantza's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium constanzae is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
