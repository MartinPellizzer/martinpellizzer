Euterpe kalbreyeri

Euterpe kalbreyeri, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and tall, feather-like leaves that form a dense crown. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into elongated, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
