Emilia ukingensis

Emilia ukingensis, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture. Emilia ukingensis produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
