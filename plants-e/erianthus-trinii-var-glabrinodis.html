Erianthus trinii var. glabrinodis

Erianthus trinii var. glabrinodis, commonly known as the smooth-node bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is noted for its smooth, hairless nodes, which distinguish it from other related species.
