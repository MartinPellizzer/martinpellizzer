Echeveria edulis

Echeveria edulis, commonly known as the edible echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or yellow during the spring. Its leaves contain a gel-like substance that is used in traditional Mexican cuisine.

Due to its unique appearance and culinary uses, it is both ornamental and edible.
