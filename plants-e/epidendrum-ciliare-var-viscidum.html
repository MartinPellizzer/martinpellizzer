Epidendrum ciliare var. viscidum

Epidendrum ciliare var. viscidum, commonly known as the sticky leaf epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are covered in a sticky substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
