Elaeocarpus coumbouiensis

Elaeocarpus coumbouiensis, commonly known as the Madagascar elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces fruit that is typically a woody capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
