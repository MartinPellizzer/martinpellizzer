Epipactis latifolia f. violacea

Epipactis latifolia f. violacea, commonly known as the violet orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and its distinctive violet or purple flowers, which are often tinged with pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are highly specialized, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

This orchid is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens due to its unique appearance and ecological significance.
