Epidendrum durum var. parviflorum

Epidendrum durum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and long, pendant inflorescences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relative ease of care.
