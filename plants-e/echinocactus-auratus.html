Echinocactus auratus

Echinocactus auratus, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and distinctive golden spines that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
