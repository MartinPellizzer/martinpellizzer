Epidendrum muricatisepalum

Epidendrum muricatisepalum, commonly known as the spiked sepal epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its flowers are usually violet to purple in color, with distinctive, spiky sepals that give it its common name.

Epidendrum muricatisepalum is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
