Eupatorium macropodum

Eupatorium macropodum, commonly known as the large-leaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium macropodum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
