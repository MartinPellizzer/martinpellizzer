Eria convallarioides

Eria convallarioides, commonly known as the creeping bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically blue or violet in color, with a distinctive hood-like structure. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add a touch of elegance to shaded landscapes.
