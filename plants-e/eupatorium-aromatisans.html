Eupatorium aromatisans

Eupatorium aromatisans, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its fragrant quality. Its showy, white to pale blue flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium aromatisans is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
