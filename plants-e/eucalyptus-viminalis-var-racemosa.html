Eucalyptus viminalis var. racemosa

Eucalyptus viminalis var. racemosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the mallees, which are shrubs or small trees native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually pink or white, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
