Echeveria spicata

Echeveria spicata, commonly known as the spiked echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear clusters of purple or pink flowers during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is popular among gardeners and succulent enthusiasts.
