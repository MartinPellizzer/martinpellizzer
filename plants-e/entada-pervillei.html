Entada pervillei

Entada pervillei, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted pods that contain hard, angular seeds. The plant produces large, palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
