Eucalyptus × conjuncta

Eucalyptus × conjuncta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which open to reveal creamy white flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Eucalyptus × conjuncta is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
