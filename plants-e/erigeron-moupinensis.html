Erigeron moupinensis

Erigeron moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin fleur-de-lis, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late summer. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.
