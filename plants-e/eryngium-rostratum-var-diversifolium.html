Eryngium rostratum var. diversifolium

Eryngium rostratum var. diversifolium, commonly known as the purple thistle or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by pale blue bracts. The plant has basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and its stems are typically hairy and erect. It thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate salt spray and wind exposure.
