Euphrasia nemorosa

Euphrasia nemorosa, commonly known as the wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive pattern of veins and produces clusters of delicate yellow flowers. It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family, which includes parasitic and hemiparasitic species.
