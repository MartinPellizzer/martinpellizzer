Elymus dasystachys f. angustiformis

Elymus dasystachys f. angustiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid environments. It has fine, wiry stems and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
