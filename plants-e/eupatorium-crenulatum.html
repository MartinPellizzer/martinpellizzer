Eupatorium crenulatum

Eupatorium crenulatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.

The flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
