Eriosyce laui

Eriosyce laui, commonly known as the Laui's hairy cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Eriosyce laui is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
