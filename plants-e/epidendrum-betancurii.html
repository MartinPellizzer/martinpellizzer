Epidendrum betancurii

Epidendrum betancurii, commonly known as the Betancurii orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence.

Epidendrum betancurii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
