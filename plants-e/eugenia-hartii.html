Eugenia hartii

Eugenia hartii, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, reddish-purple rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The fruit is not only valued for its flavor but also for its medicinal properties.
