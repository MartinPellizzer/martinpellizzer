Echinopsis clavata

Echinopsis clavata, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with its spiny structure. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Echinopsis clavata requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
