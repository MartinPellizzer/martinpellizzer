Elaeocarpus seramicus

Elaeocarpus seramicus, commonly known as the Sulu satinash, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is typically found in lowland and montane forest areas. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink.

Its fruits are woody capsules that contain several seeds, and the plant is often valued for its ornamental appearance and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
