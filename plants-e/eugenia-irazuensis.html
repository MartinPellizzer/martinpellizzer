Eugenia irazuensis

Eugenia irazuensis, commonly known as the Irazú myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Costa Rica, specifically the Irazú Volcano region, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eugenia irazuensis is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
