Eriogonum sphaerocephalum

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum, commonly known as the globe globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, woolly flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. Its flowers are pale yellow to greenish in color and are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences.

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
