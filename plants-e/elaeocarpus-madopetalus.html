Elaeocarpus madopetalus

Elaeocarpus madopetalus, commonly known as the Madu Petai tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
