Erica calycina var. periplociflora

Erica calycina var. periplociflora, commonly known as the coastal heath or coastal Erica, is a dwarf shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
