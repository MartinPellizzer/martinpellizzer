Eugenia cerina

Eugenia cerina, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring. The plant is known for its edible, sweet-tart berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and showy floral display.

Eugenia cerina is also valued in horticulture for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
