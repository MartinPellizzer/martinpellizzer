Eulalia quadrinervis

Eulalia quadrinervis, commonly known as the four-veined grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves with a distinct four-veined structure. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
