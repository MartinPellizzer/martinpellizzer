Euphorbia perplexa

Euphorbia perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
