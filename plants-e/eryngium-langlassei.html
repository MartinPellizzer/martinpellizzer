Eryngium langlassei

Eryngium langlassei, commonly known as the Langlasse's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by white bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a striking addition to gardens, though it is often cultivated for its ornamental value rather than its edibility.

Eryngium langlassei is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
