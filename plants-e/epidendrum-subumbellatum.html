Epidendrum subumbellatum

Epidendrum subumbellatum, commonly known as the Umbrella Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its inflorescence features a spicate structure with numerous small, clustered flowers, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Epidendrum subumbellatum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
