Erigeron confinis

Erigeron confinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.
