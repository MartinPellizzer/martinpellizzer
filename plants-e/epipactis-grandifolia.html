Epipactis grandifolia

Epipactis grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Epipactis grandifolia is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments.
