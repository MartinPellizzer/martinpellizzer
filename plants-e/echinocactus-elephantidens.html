Echinocactus elephantidens

Echinocactus elephantidens, commonly known as the elephant's tooth.

It is a species of cactus native to Mexico, characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
