Euphorbia corollata var. angustifolia

Euphorbia corollata var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures that contain the flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has smooth, ribbed stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its small yellowish-green flowers are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.
