Epilobium squamatum

Epilobium squamatum, commonly known as the silverleaf willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, woolly leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and in wet meadows.

Epilobium squamatum is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ornamental value and potential ecological benefits.
