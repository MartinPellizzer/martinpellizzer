Eupatorium pedale var. dissitiflorum

Eupatorium pedale var. dissitiflorum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its flowering display.
