Erica punctata

Erica punctata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica punctata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
