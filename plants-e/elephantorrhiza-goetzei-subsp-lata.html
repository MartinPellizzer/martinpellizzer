Elephantorrhiza goetzei subsp. lata

Elephantorrhiza goetzei subsp.

lata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elephant's garlic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces tall, slender flowering stems with small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance through its deep root system. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its narrower leaves compared to other subspecies of the same species.
