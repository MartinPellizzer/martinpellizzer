Eriospermum karrooense

Eriospermum karrooense, commonly known as the Karoo eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its unique, spiky seed heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are often hairy and succulent, aiding in water retention.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family.
