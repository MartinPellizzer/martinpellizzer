Eulophia cooperi

Eulophia cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically features strap-like leaves and can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia cooperi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both greenhouse and outdoor settings.
