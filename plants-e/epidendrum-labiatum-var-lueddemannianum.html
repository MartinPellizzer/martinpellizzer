Epidendrum labiatum var. lueddemannianum

Epidendrum labiatum var. lueddemannianum, commonly known as the Lueddemann's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are relatively small but numerous, often blooming in late spring or early summer.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
