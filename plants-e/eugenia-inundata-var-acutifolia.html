Eugenia inundata var. acutifolia

Eugenia inundata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the wetlands of northern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, narrow elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pink to red berries. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
