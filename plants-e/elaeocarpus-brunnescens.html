Elaeocarpus brunnescens

Elaeocarpus brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-fruit elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, brownish fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
