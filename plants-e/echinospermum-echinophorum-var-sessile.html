Echinospermum echinophorum var. sessile

Echinospermum echinophorum var. sessile, commonly known as the sessile echinosperm, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It has adapted to harsh environments through its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
