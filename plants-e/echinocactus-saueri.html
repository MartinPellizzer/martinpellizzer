Echinocactus saueri

Echinocactus saueri, commonly known as the Sauer's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping projects.
