Eriocaulon senile

Eriocaulon senile, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
