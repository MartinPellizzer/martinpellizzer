Ehrharta calycina var. versicolor

Ehrharta calycina var. versicolor, commonly known as the variable lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and rocky conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to pink, giving it its varietal name "versicolor.". It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Ehrharta calycina var. versicolor is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in arid environments.
