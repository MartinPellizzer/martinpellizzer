Elaeis guineensis var. repanda

Elaeis guineensis var. repanda, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is widely used in the production of palm oil, which has both culinary and industrial applications.

The variety 'repanda' is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology and is often cultivated for its high oil yield.
