Elettariopsis fusiformis

Elettariopsis fusiformis, commonly known as the slender elettariopsis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae, commonly known as the orchid family, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and narrow, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
