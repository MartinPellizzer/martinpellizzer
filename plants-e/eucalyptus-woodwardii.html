Eucalyptus woodwardii

Eucalyptus woodwardii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in umbels.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
