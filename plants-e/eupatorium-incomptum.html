Eupatorium incomptum

Eupatorium incomptum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium incomptum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
