Eulophia squalida var. neoguineensis

Eulophia squalida var. neoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of New Guinea.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit with elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces striking, showy inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
