Eryngium foetidum f. comosum

Eryngium foetidum f. comosum, commonly known as horseweed or bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes pungent foliage.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb in some cultures.
