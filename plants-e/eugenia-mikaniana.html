Eugenia mikaniana

Eugenia mikaniana, commonly known as the Java tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
