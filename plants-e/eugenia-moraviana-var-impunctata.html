Eugenia moraviana var. impunctata

Eugenia moraviana var. impunctata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
