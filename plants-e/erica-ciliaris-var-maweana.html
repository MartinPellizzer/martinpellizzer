Erica ciliaris var. maweana

Erica ciliaris var. maweana, commonly known as the Maweana heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand coastal conditions.
