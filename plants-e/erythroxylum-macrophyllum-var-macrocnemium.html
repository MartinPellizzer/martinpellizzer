Erythroxylum macrophyllum var. macrocnemium

Erythroxylum macrophyllum var. macrocnemium, commonly known as the large-leafed coca, is a plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cultural practices. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing elliptical seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
