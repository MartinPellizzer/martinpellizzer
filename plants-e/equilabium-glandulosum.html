Equilabium glandulosum

Equilabium glandulosum, commonly known as the hairy labellum orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy labellum, which is a modified petal that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

The species is notable for its intricate floral structure, which plays a key role in its pollination strategy.
