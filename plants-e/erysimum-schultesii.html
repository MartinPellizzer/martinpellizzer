Erysimum schultesii

Erysimum schultesii, commonly known as Schultes' wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants known for their cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Erysimum schultesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
