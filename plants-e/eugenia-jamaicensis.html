Eugenia jamaicensis

Eugenia jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

The fruit, which is similar to a guava, is often used in culinary applications and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.
