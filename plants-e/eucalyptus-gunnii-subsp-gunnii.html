Eucalyptus gunnii subsp. gunnii

Eucalyptus gunnii subsp.

gunnii, commonly known as the Tasmanian gum, is a tall, slender tree native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that ranges from white to grey in color.

The leaves are lance-shaped, glossy green, and have a aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellowish-white.

This subspecies is well adapted to the cool, moist climate of its native habitat.
