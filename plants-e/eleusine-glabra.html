Eleusine glabra

Eleusine glabra, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are clustered in panicles, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Eleusine glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.
