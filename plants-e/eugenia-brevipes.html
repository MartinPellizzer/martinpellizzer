Eugenia brevipes

Eugenia brevipes, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eugenia brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in commercial agriculture.
