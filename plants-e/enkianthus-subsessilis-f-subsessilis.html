Enkianthus subsessilis f. subsessilis

Enkianthus subsessilis f. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and sessile. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by small, leathery berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
