Eubotryoides grayana f. psilocarpa

Eubotryoides grayana f. psilocarpa, commonly known as the psilocarpic form of the genus Eubotryoides, is a variant of the original species characterized by its unique fungal symbiosis.

This form exhibits distinct morphological features, including a more pronounced cap and a lighter-colored stem compared to the typical variety. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is notable for its association with specific mycorrhizal fungi, which enhance its nutrient uptake and survival in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its unique biological traits, it is of interest in both botanical research and ecological studies.
