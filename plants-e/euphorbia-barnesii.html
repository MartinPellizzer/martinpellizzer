Euphorbia barnesii

Euphorbia barnesii, commonly known as the Barnes' spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded stem with dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and rockeries.
