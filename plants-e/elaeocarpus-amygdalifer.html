Elaeocarpus amygdalifer

Elaeocarpus amygdalifer, commonly known as the Java apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
