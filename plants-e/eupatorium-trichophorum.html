Eupatorium trichophorum

Eupatorium trichophorum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, fertile soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine, silky hairs.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported fever-reducing and anti-inflammatory properties.
