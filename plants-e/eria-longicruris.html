Eria longicruris

Eria longicruris, commonly known as the long-legged eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil.

Eria longicruris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens.
