Euphrasia cyclophylla

Euphrasia cyclophylla, commonly known as the cyclophyll bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with toothed margins. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with five lobes and a spurred lower lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Euphrasia cyclophylla is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
