Eryngium sarcophyllum

Eryngium sarcophyllum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Eryngium sarcophyllum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
