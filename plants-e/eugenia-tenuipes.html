Eugenia tenuipes

Eugenia tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
