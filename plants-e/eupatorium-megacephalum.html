Eupatorium megacephalum

Eupatorium megacephalum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
