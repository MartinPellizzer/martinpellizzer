Eleogiton beccarii

Eleogiton beccarii, commonly known as the Beccari's elephant tree, is a rare and unique plant native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.

Its botanical study contributes to the understanding of tropical plant diversity and ecological adaptation.
