Ellipeia costata

Ellipeia costata, commonly known as the serrated leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, serrated leaf margins. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a loose cluster. Ellipeia costata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
