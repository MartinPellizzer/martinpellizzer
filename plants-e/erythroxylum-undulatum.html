Erythroxylum undulatum

Erythroxylum undulatum, commonly known as the wavy-leaved copaiba, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, wavy-edged leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

However, it is not related to the coca plant and does not contain cocaine.
