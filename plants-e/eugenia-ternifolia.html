Eugenia ternifolia

Eugenia ternifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
