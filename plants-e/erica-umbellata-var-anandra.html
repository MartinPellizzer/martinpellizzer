Erica umbellata var. anandra

Erica umbellata var. anandra, commonly known as the Cape snowdrop, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
