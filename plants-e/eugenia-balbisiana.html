Eugenia balbisiana

Eugenia balbisiana, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruit, which is small and often sour, is used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in tropical climates.
