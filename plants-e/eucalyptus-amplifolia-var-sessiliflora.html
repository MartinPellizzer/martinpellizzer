Eucalyptus amplifolia var. sessiliflora

Eucalyptus amplifolia var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping and for erosion control in suitable climates.
