Erysimum witmannii subsp. transsilvanicum

Erysimum witmannii subsp.

transsilvanicum, commonly known as the Transylvanian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils along mountain slopes.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are basal, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
