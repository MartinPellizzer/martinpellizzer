Euxenia mitiqui var. subintegerrima

Euxenia mitiqui var. subintegerrima, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
