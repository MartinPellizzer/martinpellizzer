Euphorbia schlechteri

Euphorbia schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
