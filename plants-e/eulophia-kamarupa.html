Eulophia kamarupa

Eulophia kamarupa, commonly known as the Kamarupa orchid, is a species native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is a terrestrial orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae, characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers with intricate patterns, often blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
