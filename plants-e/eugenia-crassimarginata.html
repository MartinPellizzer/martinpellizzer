Eugenia crassimarginata

Eugenia crassimarginata, commonly known as the broad-margin eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
