Echinocactus ingens f. helophorus

Echinocactus ingens f. helophorus, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, spiny, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 feet in height. The plant features a dense covering of white to pale green spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring and adding a dramatic visual contrast to the plant's rugged structure.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is a challenging plant to cultivate outside its native environment.
