Echinocereus fendleri var. kuenzleri

Echinocereus fendleri var. kuenzleri, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, woolly flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem covered in spines and produces small, round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
