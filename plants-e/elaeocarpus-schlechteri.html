Elaeocarpus schlechteri

Elaeocarpus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
