Eupatorium platylepis

Eupatorium platylepis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

Eupatorium platylepis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
