Epidendrum phoeniceum

Epidendrum phoeniceum, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red flowers that resemble flames, which are borne on pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
