Euphorbia celastroides var. haupuana

Euphorbia celastroides var. haupuana, commonly known as the Hawaiian euphorbia, is a shrub native to the island of Hawaii.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is adapted to the unique climate of Hawaii.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
