Eriogonum vimineum var. commixtum

Eriogonum vimineum var. commixtum, commonly known as the mixedleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue with a fuzzy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
