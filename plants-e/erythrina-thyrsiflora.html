Erythrina thyrsiflora

Erythrina thyrsiflora, commonly known as the flame tree or red torchwood, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking crimson flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to regions of South and Central America. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces long, clustered inflorescences of vivid red or orange flowers. Its seeds are encased in flat, pod-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Erythrina thyrsiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also serve as a nitrogen-fixing plant in agricultural settings.
