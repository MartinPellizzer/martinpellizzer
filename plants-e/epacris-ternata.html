Epacris ternata

Epacris ternata, commonly known as the common epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and woodlands.

Epacris ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscaping.
