Eremium erianthum

Eremium erianthum, commonly known as the woolly eremium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid or semi-arid gardens.
