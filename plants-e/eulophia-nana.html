Eulophia nana

Eulophia nana, commonly known as the dwarf eulophia, is a small orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color, often with a distinctive dark marking. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia nana is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
