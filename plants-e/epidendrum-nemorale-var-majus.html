Epidendrum nemorale var. majus

Epidendrum nemorale var. majus, commonly known as the Mexican tree orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and produces large, showy flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows on trees or shrubs, forming a symbiotic relationship with its host. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in clusters, making it a striking addition to gardens and a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
