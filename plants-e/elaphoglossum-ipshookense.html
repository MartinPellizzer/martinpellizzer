Elaphoglossum ipshookense

Elaphoglossum ipshookense, commonly known as the Ipshook's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely divided structure, typical of many ferns in the genus Elaphoglossum. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in circinate patterns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories across its native range.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
