Elsholtzia oppositifolia

Elsholtzia oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaf elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue to purple and appear in clusters during the summer months.

Elsholtzia oppositifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
