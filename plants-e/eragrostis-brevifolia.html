Eragrostis brevifolia

Eragrostis brevifolia, commonly known as dwarf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
