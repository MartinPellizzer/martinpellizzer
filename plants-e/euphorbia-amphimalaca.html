Euphorbia amphimalaca

Euphorbia amphimalaca, commonly known as the Malacca spurge, is a succulent plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique type of inflorescence called a cyathium, which contains both male and female flowers. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
