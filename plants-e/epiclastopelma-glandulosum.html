Epiclastopelma glandulosum

Epiclastopelma glandulosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is herbaceous, with a sprawling or semi-woody stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.
