Eugenia kuranda

Eugenia kuranda, commonly known as Kuranda cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red or pink fruits that resemble cherries. The leaves are oval-shaped, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities.
