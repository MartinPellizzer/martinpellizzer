Eupatorium adenospermum var. pleianthum

Eupatorium adenospermum var. pleianthum, commonly known as the Chinese eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating wind dispersal. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.
