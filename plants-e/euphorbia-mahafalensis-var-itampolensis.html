Euphorbia mahafalensis var. itampolensis

Euphorbia mahafalensis var. itampolensis, commonly known as the Mahafaly spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturalists alike.
