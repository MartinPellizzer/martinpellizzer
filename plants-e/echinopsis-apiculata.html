Echinopsis apiculata

Echinopsis apiculata, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Moon," is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in white spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
