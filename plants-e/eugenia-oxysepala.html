Eugenia oxysepala

Eugenia oxysepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical rainforests.
