Eugenia domingensis var. membranacea

Eugenia domingensis var. membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered with a fine, membranous layer, giving it its specific epithet. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry with a thin, edible skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits in suitable climates.
