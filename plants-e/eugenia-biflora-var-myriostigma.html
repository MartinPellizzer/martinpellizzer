Eugenia biflora var. myriostigma

Eugenia biflora var. myriostigma, commonly known as the myriostigma eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant features simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape, with a glossy, dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, white to pale pink, and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
