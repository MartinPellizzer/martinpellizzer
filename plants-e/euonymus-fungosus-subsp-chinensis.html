Euonymus fungosus subsp. chinensis

Euonymus fungosus subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its vibrant red stems that become more pronounced in the fall, providing ornamental value.

The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various soil types.
