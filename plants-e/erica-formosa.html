Erica formosa

Erica formosa, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. Erica formosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
