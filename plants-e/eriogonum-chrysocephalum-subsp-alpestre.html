Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp. alpestre

Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp.

alpestre, commonly known as the alpine goldenbush, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and yellowish-green foliage.

The plant produces showy, yellow flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
