Eucalyptus goniocalyx subsp. goniocalyx

Eucalyptus goniocalyx subsp.

goniocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal creamy-white or pinkish flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
