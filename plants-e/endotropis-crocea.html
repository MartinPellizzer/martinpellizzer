Endotropis crocea

Endotropis crocea, commonly known as the golden sand cress, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the tips of the stems. This plant is known for its rapid growth cycle and ability to thrive in arid environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
