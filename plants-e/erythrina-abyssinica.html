Erythrina abyssinica

Erythrina abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its distinctive red seed pods split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Erythrina abyssinica also has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.
