Evolvulus pterocaulon

Evolvulus pterocaulon, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Evolvulus pterocaulon is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
