Eugenia bajaverapazana

Eugenia bajaverapazana, commonly known as the Baja Verapaz guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the highlands of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
