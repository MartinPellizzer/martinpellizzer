Epilobium brevifolium subsp. pannosum

Epilobium brevifolium subsp.

pannosum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant in the evening primrose family. It is characterized by its glandular-hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in wet meadows and along water edges.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
