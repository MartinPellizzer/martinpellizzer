Eucalyptus punctata var. didyma

Eucalyptus punctata var. didyma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The flowers are usually small and arranged in clusters, producing a distinctive yellow-green color. This plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
