Erycina gnomus

Erycina gnomus, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to the Andes Mountains.

It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant features a compact growth habit with a single, short stem and a few narrow leaves. Its flowers are small, often white or pale green, and are clustered in a spike-like arrangement.

Erycina gnomus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
