Erythroxylum tikalense

Erythroxylum tikalense, commonly known as the Tikal tobacco, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Guatemala, Belize, and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with simple, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces elliptical, reddish-brown capsules that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine and its association with the cultivation of psychoactive compounds, it holds cultural and botanical significance in the region.
