Eucalyptus chippendalei

Eucalyptus chippendalei, commonly known as Chippendale's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

Eucalyptus chippendalei is a valuable component of its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
