Eleocharis sachalinensis

Eleocharis sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes.

Eleocharis sachalinensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
