Ecteinanthus ecklonianus

Ecteinanthus ecklonianus, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its resilience and ability to withstand drought conditions.
