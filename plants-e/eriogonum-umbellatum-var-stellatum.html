Eriogonum umbellatum var. stellatum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. stellatum, commonly known as the star flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, star-shaped inflorescences composed of numerous small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
