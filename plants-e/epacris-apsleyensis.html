Epacris apsleyensis

Epacris apsleyensis, commonly known as the Apsley's epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in heathlands and open forests. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions and is often found in the southeastern regions of Australia.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological significance.
