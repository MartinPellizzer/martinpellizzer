Excoecaria cochinchinensis var. viridis

Excoecaria cochinchinensis var. viridis, commonly known as the green croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
