Eschweilera macrophylla

Eschweilera macrophylla, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, showcasing a deep green color with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in panicles, followed by edible, starchy tubers that resemble those of the arrowroot plant. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Eschweilera macrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
