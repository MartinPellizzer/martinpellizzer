Echinopsis rauschii var. grandiflora

Echinopsis rauschii var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant night bloomer, is a striking cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant thrives in arid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
