Eclipta humilis

Eclipta humilis, commonly known as field ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eclipta humilis is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common weed in agricultural and urban areas.
