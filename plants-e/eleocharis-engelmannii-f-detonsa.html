Eleocharis engelmannii f. detonsa

Eleocharis engelmannii f. detonsa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a distinct, pointed tip.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
