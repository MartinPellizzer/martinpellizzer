Erythrospermum ampullaceum

Erythrospermum ampullaceum, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant display.
