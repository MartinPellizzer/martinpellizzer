Echinocactus acuatus var. corynodes

Echinocactus acuatus var. corynodes, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar shape and dense, spiny appearance, with green to blue-green stems that develop a reddish tinge when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
