Eupatorium purpurascens

Eupatorium purpurascens, commonly known as purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, showy purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps, wet meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
