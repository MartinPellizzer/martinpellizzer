Eugenia cantuana

Eugenia cantuana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceriman* in some regions, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.

The bark and leaves contain compounds with potential pharmacological uses.
