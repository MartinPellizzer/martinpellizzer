Eugenia altoalegre

Eugenia altoalegre, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to well-drained soils in humid climates.
