Ezeria

Ezeria, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting blue or purple throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Ezeria has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
