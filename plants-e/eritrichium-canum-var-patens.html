Eritrichium canum var. patens

Eritrichium canum var. patens, commonly known as the hairy beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique floral structure and adaptive features make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
