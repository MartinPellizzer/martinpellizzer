Eragrostis patens var. pilosa

Eragrostis patens var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, hair-covered inflorescences that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique visual characteristics.
