Eucalyptus celastroides subsp. virella

Eucalyptus celastroides subsp.

virella, commonly known as the green-leaved malleecarp, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, often glaucous leaves that range from green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its bark is usually smooth and peels in strips, revealing a pale or reddish inner layer.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
