Eriosema shirense var. longibracteatum

Eriosema shirense var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract eriosema, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, glandular texture. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a range of colors from white to purple.

This variant is noted for its unique bract structures that extend beyond the flower clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.
