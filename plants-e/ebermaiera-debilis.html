Ebermaiera debilis

Ebermaiera debilis, commonly known as the weak ebermaiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features delicate, narrow leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Ebermaiera debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
