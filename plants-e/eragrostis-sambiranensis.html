Eragrostis sambiranensis

Eragrostis sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambira grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense clusters.

Eragrostis sambiranensis is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to the island's specific ecological conditions.
