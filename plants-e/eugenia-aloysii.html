Eugenia aloysii

Eugenia aloysii, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural purposes.
