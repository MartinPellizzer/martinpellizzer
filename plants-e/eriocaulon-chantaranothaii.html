Eriocaulon chantaranothaii

Eriocaulon chantaranothaii, commonly known as the Thai marsh plant, is a member of the Poales order and belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and tiny, clustered inflorescences. Native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, it thrives in wetland and marsh environments. The plant has a short, stolonic stem that often forms dense mats on the ground.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important species for ecological studies in its native region.
