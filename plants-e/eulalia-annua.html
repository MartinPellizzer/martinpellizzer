Eulalia annua

Eulalia annua, commonly known as the annual bulrush, is a herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical flowering stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clumps at the base.

Eulalia annua thrives in moist or aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
