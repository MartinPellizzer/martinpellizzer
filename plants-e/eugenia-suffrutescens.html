Eugenia suffrutescens

Eugenia suffrutescens, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Eugenia suffrutescens is well adapted to humid, warm climates and thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.
