Erucastrum arabicum var. hararense

Erucastrum arabicum var. hararense, commonly known as the Harar mustard, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family. Its seeds are oval and contain essential oils, contributing to its aromatic properties.

This variety is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes in its native region.
