Eugenia ibitensis

Eugenia ibitensis, commonly known as the Ibiti tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
