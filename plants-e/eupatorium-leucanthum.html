Eupatorium leucanthum

Eupatorium leucanthum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

White boneset is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
