Eupatorium amethystinum

Eupatorium amethystinum, commonly known as purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant's name reflects its historical use in treating fever and its striking, amethyst-colored blooms.
