Eugenia poiretii

Eugenia poiretii, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are often aromatic when crushed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, which is small and fleshy, is edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
