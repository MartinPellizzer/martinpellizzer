Erythraea venusta var. genuina

Erythraea venusta var. genuina, commonly known as the red-flowered erythraea, is a striking member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in spring, producing clusters of five petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its visual appeal.
