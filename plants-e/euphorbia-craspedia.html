Euphorbia craspedia

Euphorbia craspedia, commonly known as the red globular euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
