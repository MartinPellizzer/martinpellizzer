Euphorbia sudanica

Euphorbia sudanica, commonly known as the Sudanese spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Sahelian regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its thick, succulent stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its fleshy tissues.

Euphorbia sudanica is also known for its latex, which contains toxic compounds and is used in traditional medicine.
