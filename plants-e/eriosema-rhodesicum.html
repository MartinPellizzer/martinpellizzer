Eriosema rhodesicum

Eriosema rhodesicum, commonly known as the Rhodes velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and a fibrous root system.

Eriosema rhodesicum is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
