Erigeron allisonii

Erigeron allisonii, commonly known as Allison's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
