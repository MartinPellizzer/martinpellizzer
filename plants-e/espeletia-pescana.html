Espeletia pescana

Espeletia pescana, commonly known as the Pescan mountain heather, is a perennial shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit with silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, dry climates.

Espeletia pescana is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing the soil and supporting local biodiversity.
