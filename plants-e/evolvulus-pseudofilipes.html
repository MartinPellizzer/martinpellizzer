Evolvulus pseudofilipes

Evolvulus pseudofilipes, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to Central and South America, it thrives in warm, sunny environments with well-drained soil.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add vibrant color to landscapes.
