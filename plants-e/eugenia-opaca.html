Eugenia opaca

Eugenia opaca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. Eugenia opaca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and wetland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
