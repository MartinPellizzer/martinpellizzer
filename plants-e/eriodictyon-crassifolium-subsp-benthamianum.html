Eriodictyon crassifolium subsp. benthamianum

Eriodictyon crassifolium subsp.

benthamianum, commonly known as California licorice, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant contains compounds similar to licorice root, giving it a sweet, spicy scent. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
