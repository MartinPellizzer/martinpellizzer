Epidendrum portillae

Epidendrum portillae, commonly known as the Portilla's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its pseudobulbs are short and cylindrical, with broad, strap-like leaves that are dark green and glossy.

Epidendrum portillae blooms during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value to both greenhouse and garden settings.
