Euphorbia ariensis var. villicaulis

Euphorbia ariensis var. villicaulis, commonly known as the hair-frond euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems and clustered, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow or green, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
