Eperua decandra

Eperua decandra, commonly known as the red eperua, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red flowers and compound leaves. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a dense canopy and smooth, dark bark. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is hard and durable.
