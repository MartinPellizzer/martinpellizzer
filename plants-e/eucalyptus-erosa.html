Eucalyptus erosa

Eucalyptus erosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and dry environments. The plant is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white bloom. Eucalyptus erosa produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is well adapted to harsh, arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
