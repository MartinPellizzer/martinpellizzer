Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp. moluccensis

Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp.

moluccensis, commonly known as the Moluccan elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pale pink to white flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
