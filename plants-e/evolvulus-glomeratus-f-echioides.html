Evolvulus glomeratus f. echioides

Evolvulus glomeratus f. echioides, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that contain seeds. It is native to regions of South America and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
