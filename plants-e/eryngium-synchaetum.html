Eryngium synchaetum

Eryngium synchaetum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eryngium synchaetum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
