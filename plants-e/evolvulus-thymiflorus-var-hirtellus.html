Evolvulus thymiflorus var. hirtellus

Evolvulus thymiflorus var. hirtellus, commonly known as the hairy thymeleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and delicate, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
