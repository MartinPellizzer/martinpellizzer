Eugenia modesta

Eugenia modesta, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and herbal practices.
