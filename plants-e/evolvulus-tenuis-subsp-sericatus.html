Evolvulus tenuis subsp. sericatus

Evolvulus tenuis subsp.

sericatus, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs.

The species produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
