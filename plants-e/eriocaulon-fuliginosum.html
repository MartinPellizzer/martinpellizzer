Eriocaulon fuliginosum

Eriocaulon fuliginosum, commonly known as the sooty ricegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a dark green or bluish-gray appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are borne on short stalks. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in wetlands or marshy habitats.

Eriocaulon fuliginosum is adapted to moist environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
