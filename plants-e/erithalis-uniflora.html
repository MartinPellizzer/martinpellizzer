Erithalis uniflora

Erithalis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower erithalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring or early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Erithalis uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
