Echinocactus setispinus var. mierensis

Echinocactus setispinus var. mierensis, commonly known as the Mierensis cactus, is a rare and striking species native to the Chihuahua Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is typically green with a bluish tinge, and it produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant is well adapted to arid conditions, with spines that provide shade and reduce water loss. It is often found growing in rocky soils and is prized by collectors for its unique appearance and resilience.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
