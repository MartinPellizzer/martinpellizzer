Elynanthus aphyllus

Elynanthus aphyllus, commonly known as the leafless water clover, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its absence of true leaves, with only small, scale-like structures present. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in early summer. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
