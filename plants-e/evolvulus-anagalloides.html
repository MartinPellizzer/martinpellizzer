Evolvulus anagalloides

Evolvulus anagalloides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, five-petaled blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. It is native to the Americas and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Evolvulus anagalloides is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
