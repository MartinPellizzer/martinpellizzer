Eugenia aborensis

Eugenia aborensis, commonly known as the mountain guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at higher elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and unique flavor, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
