Erica gerhardii

Erica gerhardii, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica gerhardii is known for its aromatic leaves and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
