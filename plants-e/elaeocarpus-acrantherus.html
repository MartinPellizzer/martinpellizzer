Elaeocarpus acrantherus

Elaeocarpus acrantherus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are red when mature.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
