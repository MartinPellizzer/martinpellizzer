Euphorbia pubescens subvar. integrifolia

Euphorbia pubescens subvar. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and may be present during the growing season before shedding in winter.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
