Epilobium miyabei

Epilobium miyabei, commonly known as the Japanese willow herb, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and meadows.

Epilobium miyabei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
