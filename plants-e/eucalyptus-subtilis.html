Eucalyptus subtilis

Eucalyptus subtilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. This plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

Eucalyptus subtilis is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
