Erianthus pedicellaris

Erianthus pedicellaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in prairies, open woodlands, and disturbed sites, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are usually blue to purple in color. It is well adapted to full sun and is often found in dry to mesic soil conditions.

Erianthus pedicellaris plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
