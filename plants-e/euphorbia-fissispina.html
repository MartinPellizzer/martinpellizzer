Euphorbia fissispina

Euphorbia fissispina, commonly known as the fissured spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Euphorbia fissispina is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
