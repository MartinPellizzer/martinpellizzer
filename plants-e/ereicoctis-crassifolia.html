Ereicoctis crassifolia

Ereicoctis crassifolia, commonly known as the thickleaf ericaceous plant, is a shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with dense, glandular hairs, and it is often found in moist, acidic soils.

Ereicoctis crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
