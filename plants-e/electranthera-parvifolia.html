Electranthera parvifolia

Electranthera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved waterplantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters, adding a delicate appearance to its aquatic habitat.

It plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
