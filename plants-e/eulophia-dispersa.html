Eulophia dispersa

Eulophia dispersa, commonly known as the scattered eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in racemes, and are characterized by their elongated, narrow petals.

Eulophia dispersa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
