Eriogonum ochrocephalum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum ochrocephalum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, typically growing in dry, rocky habitats.

The plant produces small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
