Eucalyptus argillacea

Eucalyptus argillacea, commonly known as the argillaceous eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically cream or pale yellow in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sandy or rocky habitats.

Eucalyptus argillacea is valued for its aromatic oils and is used in traditional medicine and horticulture.
