Erica pyxidiflora

Erica pyxidiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes various shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica pyxidiflora thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
