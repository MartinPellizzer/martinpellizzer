Epipactis helleborine subsp. molochina

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

molochina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for calcareous soils.
