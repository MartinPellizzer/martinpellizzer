Euodia tsaratananensis

Euodia tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a dark blue drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
