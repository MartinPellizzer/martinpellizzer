Eupatorium amambayense

Eupatorium amambayense, commonly known as the Amambay feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks and in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a pale blue or white color, which attracts various pollinators.
