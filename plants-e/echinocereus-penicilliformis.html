Echinocereus penicilliformis

Echinocereus penicilliformis, commonly known as the penicilliform cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
