Elytranthe platyphylla

Elytranthe platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf elytranthe, is a small, evergreen herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
