Erythroxylum pervillei

Erythroxylum pervillei, commonly known as the Perville's erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces elliptical, red-brown fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its morphological features and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
