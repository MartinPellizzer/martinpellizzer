Euphrasia rostkoviana subsp. pseudomontana

Euphrasia rostkoviana subsp.

pseudomontana, commonly known as the Alpine eyebright, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, often on rocky slopes and meadows.

The plant features slender stems that are usually hairy, with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are also covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, resembling those of other eyebright species.

It is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for eye and respiratory conditions.
