Eugenia Leptalea

Lemon myrtle, scientifically known as Eugenia leptalea, is a unique and aromatic plant native to the rainforests of eastern Australia. Its leaves emit a distinctively sharp, citrusy scent reminiscent of lemon, which sets it apart from other myrtle species. This evergreen shrub is prized for its culinary and medicinal uses, often incorporated into teas, sauces, and traditional remedies. Its vibrant green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers add both beauty and fragrance to gardens. Despite its popularity, Lemon myrtle remains relatively obscure compared to more widely cultivated citrus plants.
