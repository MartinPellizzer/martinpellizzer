Eugenia havanensis

Eugenia havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia havanensis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its edible fruit and historical use in traditional medicine.
