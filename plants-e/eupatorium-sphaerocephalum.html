Eupatorium sphaerocephalum

Eupatorium sphaerocephalum, commonly known as globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spherical blue or purple flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
