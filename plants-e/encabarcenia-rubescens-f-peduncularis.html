Encabarcenia rubescens f. peduncularis

Encabarcenia rubescens f. peduncularis, commonly known as the red-fruited pedunculate encabarcenia, is a rare and distinctive variant of the encabarcenia genus.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange fruits that contrast with its green foliage. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in dense inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and unique coloration, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
