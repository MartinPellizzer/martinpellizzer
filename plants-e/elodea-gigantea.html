Elodea gigantea

Elodea gigantea, commonly known as giant waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are typically found near the water's surface. Its leaves are narrow and arranged alternately along the stem, often forming dense underwater vegetation.

Elodea gigantea plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
