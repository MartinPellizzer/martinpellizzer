Eupatorium arechavaletae

Eupatorium arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Eupatorium arechavaletae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
