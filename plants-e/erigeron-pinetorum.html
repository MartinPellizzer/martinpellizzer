Erigeron pinetorum

Erigeron pinetorum, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erigeron pinetorum is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
