Epilobium × neogradiense

Epilobium × neogradiense, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium × neogradiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for various pollinators.
