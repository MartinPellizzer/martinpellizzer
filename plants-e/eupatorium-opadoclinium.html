Eupatorium opadoclinium

Eupatorium opadoclinium, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its blooming period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
