Eriogonum microtheca subsp. confertiflorum

Eriogonum microtheca subsp.

confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its clustered, pale yellow to cream-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its dense inflorescences and is often found in association with other native flora in its ecosystem.
