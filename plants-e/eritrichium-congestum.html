Eritrichium congestum

Eritrichium congestum, commonly known as the clustered eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, delicate flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eritrichium congestum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
