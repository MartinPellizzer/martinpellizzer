Eupatorium militare

Eupatorium militare, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive serrated edge.

Eupatorium militare is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
