Erythrina secundiflora

Erythrina secundiflora, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often giving the plant a fiery appearance. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
