Euphorbia riedeliana

Euphorbia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
