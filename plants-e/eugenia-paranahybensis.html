Eugenia paranahybensis

Eugenia paranahybensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *mangaba*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing a single hard seed. Eugenia paranahybensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
