Equisetum hyemale var. japonicum

Equisetum hyemale var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green to brown in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are often referred to as strobila. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
