Euonymus latifolius var. sachalinensis

Euonymus latifolius var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin boxwood, is a shrub native to the Korean Peninsula and southern Japan.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical, dark green leaves that have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, inflated seed capsules that persist into winter. It is often cultivated as a decorative hedge or ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and year-round aesthetic appeal.

The variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
