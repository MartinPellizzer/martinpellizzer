Elymus elymoides var. californicus

Elymus elymoides var. californicus, commonly known as California wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, meadows, and forest edges. The plant has tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are clustered in a loose, open arrangement.

This variety is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
