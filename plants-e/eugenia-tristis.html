Eugenia tristis

Eugenia tristis, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
