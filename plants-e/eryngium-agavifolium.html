Eryngium agavifolium

Eryngium agavifolium, commonly known as the agave-leaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage that resembles the leaves of agave plants. The plant produces striking purple or pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
