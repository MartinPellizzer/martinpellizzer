Eriogonum kennedyi subsp. gracilipes

Eriogonum kennedyi subsp.

gracilipes, commonly known as the slender leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, often hairy leaves and small, clustered white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in local ecosystems.
