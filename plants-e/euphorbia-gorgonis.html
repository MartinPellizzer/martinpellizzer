Euphorbia gorgonis

Euphorbia gorgonis, commonly known as the Gorgon's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by bracts, which are often the only visible part of the inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic sap, handling the plant requires caution.
