Eugenia handroi

Eugenia handroi, commonly known as the Java guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia handroi is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
