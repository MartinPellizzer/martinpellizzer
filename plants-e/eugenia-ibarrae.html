Eugenia ibarrae

Eugenia ibarrae, commonly known as the Ibarra's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, yellowish-red fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
