Echites bignoniiflorus

Echites bignoniiflorus, commonly known as the yellow trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb, often used as a vine in gardens and landscapes.
