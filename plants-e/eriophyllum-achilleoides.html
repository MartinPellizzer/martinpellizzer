Eriophyllum achilleoides

Eriophyllum achilleoides, commonly known as the common woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eriophyllum achilleoides is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to attract pollinators.
