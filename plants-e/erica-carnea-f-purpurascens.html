Erica carnea f. purpurascens

Erica carnea f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This ornamental plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
