Eugenia sobraliana

Eugenia sobraliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or sobralian cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
