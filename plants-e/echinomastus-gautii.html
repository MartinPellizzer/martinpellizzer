Echinomastus gautii

Echinomastus gautii, commonly known as the Gautier's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, a typical feature of the spurge family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
