Eucalyptus myriadena

Eucalyptus myriadena, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin layers, and it is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus myriadena is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
