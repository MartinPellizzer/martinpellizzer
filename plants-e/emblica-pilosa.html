Emblica pilosa

Emblica pilosa, commonly known as the hairy Indian gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Emblica pilosa is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine.
