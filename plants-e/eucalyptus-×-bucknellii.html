Eucalyptus × bucknellii

Eucalyptus × bucknellii, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus palustris and Eucalyptus robusta.

It is a tall, slender tree that typically grows to heights of 10–20 meters, featuring smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a fragrant, peppermint-like aroma. It produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This species is native to eastern Australia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
