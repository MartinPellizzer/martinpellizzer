Eria nummularia

Eria nummularia, commonly known as the silver coin plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, coin-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typically during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
