Espeletia standleyana subsp. ampla

Espeletia standleyana subsp.

ampla, commonly known as the "Páramo sunflower," is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the páramo ecosystems of Colombia and Ecuador. It is characterized by its rosette growth form, with dense, woolly foliage that helps it survive in high-altitude, cold environments.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of montane regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a notable species in the Andean flora.
