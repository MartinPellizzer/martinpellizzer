Euphorbia kischenensis

Euphorbia kischenensis, commonly known as the Kischen spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
