Ericoila royleana

Ericoila royleana, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude alpine environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand cold and harsh climatic conditions.

Ericoila royleana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
