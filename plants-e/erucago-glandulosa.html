Erucago glandulosa

Erucago glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky geranium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erucago glandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
