Encelia sanguinea var. palmeri

Encelia sanguinea var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's encelia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and high temperatures. Its yellow to orange flowers are borne on slender, branched stems, and the leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs.

This variety is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
