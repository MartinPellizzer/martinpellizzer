Eulophia mackinnonii

Eulophia mackinnonii, commonly known as the Mackinnon's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, meaning it grows in clusters of pseudobulbs. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophia mackinnonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
