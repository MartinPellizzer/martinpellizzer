Euterpe brachyspatha

Euterpe brachyspatha, commonly known as the short-leaf palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its compact size and distinctive, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are used in traditional basket weaving due to their durability and flexibility.

Euterpe brachyspatha plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
