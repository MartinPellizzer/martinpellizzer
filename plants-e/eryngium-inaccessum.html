Eryngium inaccessum

Eryngium inaccessum, commonly known as the inaccessible thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and scrublands. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium inaccessum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
