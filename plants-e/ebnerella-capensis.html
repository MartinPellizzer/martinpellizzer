Ebnerella capensis

Ebnerella capensis, commonly known as the Cape candelabra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its branched, tree-like structure with fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to scales, a feature common in many succulents adapted to minimize water loss.

Ebnerella capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
