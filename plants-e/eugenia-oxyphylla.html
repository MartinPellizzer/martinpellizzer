Eugenia oxyphylla

Eugenia oxyphylla, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a distinct aromatic scent.

Eugenia oxyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
