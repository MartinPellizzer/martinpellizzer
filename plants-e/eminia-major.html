Eminia major

Eminia major, commonly known as the giant eminia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a waxy texture to reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
