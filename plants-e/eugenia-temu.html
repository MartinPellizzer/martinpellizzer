Eugenia temu

Eugenia temu, commonly known as the Java apple or Java pear, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruits are commonly used in traditional medicine and as a source of flavoring in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
