Echites fragrans

Echites fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, while the roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption.

The species is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
