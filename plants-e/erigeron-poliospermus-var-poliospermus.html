Erigeron poliospermus var. poliospermus

Erigeron poliospermus var. poliospermus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and marshes, and it thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
