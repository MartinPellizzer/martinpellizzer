Echidiocarya californica var. ursina

Echidiocarya californica var. ursina, commonly known as the California bear grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It features tall, stiff culms that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
