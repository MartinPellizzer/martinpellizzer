Erysimum rupicola

Erysimum rupicola, commonly known as the rock erysimum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped, while its flowers are bright yellow with four petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Erysimum rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping.
