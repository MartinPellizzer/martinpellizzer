Eccremocarpus mutisianus

Eccremocarpus mutisianus, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Fabaceae family.
