Eucalyptus × studleyensis

Eucalyptus × studleyensis, commonly known as the Studleyensis gum tree, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue, and they are adapted to reduce water loss in arid conditions. This species produces small, globular flower buds that typically open to reveal white or cream-colored flowers.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and landscapes for its unique appearance and resilience.
