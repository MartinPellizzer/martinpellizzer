Eulalia trispicata

Eulalia trispicata, commonly known as the three-bristled grass, is a species of grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Argentina, and Paraguay. The plant is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and its distinctive inflorescence with three bristles on each spikelet. Eulalia trispicata typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecosystems where it is found.
