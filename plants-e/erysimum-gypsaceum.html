Erysimum gypsaceum

Erysimum gypsaceum, commonly known as the gypsum mustard, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

Erysimum gypsaceum is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
