Eupatorium soleirolii

Eupatorium soleirolii, commonly known as the velvetleaf false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in some areas.
