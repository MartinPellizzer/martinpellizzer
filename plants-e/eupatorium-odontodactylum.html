Eupatorium odontodactylum

Eupatorium odontodactylum, commonly known as the toothed eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it often grows in moist, woodland habitats. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower structure.

Its common name reflects the tooth-like appearance of its foliage.
