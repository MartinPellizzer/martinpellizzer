Eugenia velloziana

Eugenia velloziana, commonly known as the Velloziana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
