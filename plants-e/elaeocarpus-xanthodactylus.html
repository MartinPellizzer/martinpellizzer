Elaeocarpus xanthodactylus

Elaeocarpus xanthodactylus, commonly known as the yellow-fingered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish fingers on the fruit's surface. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a smooth texture, while its flowers are small and clustered.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its role in forest regeneration.
