Eria brownei

Eria brownei, commonly known as the Brown's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clustered, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent lips, typically blooming during the rainy season. Eria brownei thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining substrates for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
