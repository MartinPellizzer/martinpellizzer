Elaeocarpus tonganus

Elaeocarpus tonganus, commonly known as the Tongan hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including Tonga and Samoa.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
