Erythronium grandiflorum f. giganteum

Erythronium grandiflorum f. giganteum, commonly known as the giant white trout lily, is a striking variation of the white trout lily.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow markings, resembling a trout's tail. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is native to the northeastern United States. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that emerge in early spring.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
