Euphorbia × martini

Euphorbia × martini, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
