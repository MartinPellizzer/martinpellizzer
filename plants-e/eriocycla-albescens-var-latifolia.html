Eriocycla albescens var. latifolia

Eriocycla albescens var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eriocycla, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a tendency to spread via rhizomes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic foliage and delicate blooms.
