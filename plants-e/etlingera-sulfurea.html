Etlingera sulfurea

Etlingera sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch and is highly ornamental. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Etlingera sulfurea is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
