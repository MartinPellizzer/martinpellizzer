Eremophila divaricata subsp. callewatta

Eremophila divaricata subsp.

callewatta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands and sandplains.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
