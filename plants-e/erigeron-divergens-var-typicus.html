Erigeron divergens var. typicus

Erigeron divergens var. typicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.
