Eria fractiflexa

Eria fractiflexa, commonly known as the flexed eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by hobbyists and conservationists.
