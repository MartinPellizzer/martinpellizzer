Eugenia jequitinhonhensis

Eugenia jequitinhonhensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *jequitinhonha* tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
