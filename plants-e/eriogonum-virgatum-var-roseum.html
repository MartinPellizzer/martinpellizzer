Eriogonum virgatum var. roseum

Eriogonum virgatum var. roseum, commonly known as the roseleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and showy, rose-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
