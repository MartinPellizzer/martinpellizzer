Eulophia ochreata

Eulophia ochreata, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a greenish throat and a distinctive, elongated lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its overall compact appearance.

Eulophia ochreata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
