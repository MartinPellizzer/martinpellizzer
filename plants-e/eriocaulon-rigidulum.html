Eriocaulon rigidulum

Eriocaulon rigidulum, commonly known as the rigid spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats across tropical and subtropical regions.

Eriocaulon rigidulum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
