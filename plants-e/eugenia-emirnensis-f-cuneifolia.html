Eugenia emirnensis f. cuneifolia

Eugenia emirnensis f. cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
