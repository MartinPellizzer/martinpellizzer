Eupatorium amygdalinum f. heterophyllum

Eupatorium amygdalinum f. heterophyllum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of violet to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fluffy seeds in its composite flower heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
