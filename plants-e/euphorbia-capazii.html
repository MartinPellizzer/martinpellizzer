Euphorbia capazii

Euphorbia capazii, commonly known as the Capazii spurges, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts, giving it a striking appearance. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Euphorbia capazii is typically found in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
