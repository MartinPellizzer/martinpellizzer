Epidendrum ochricolor

Epidendrum ochricolor, commonly known as the golden-tinged epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive orange or reddish hue at the base. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Epidendrum ochricolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
