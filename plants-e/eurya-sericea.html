Eurya sericea

Eurya sericea, commonly known as silver eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a fine silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Eurya sericea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and seasonal color changes.
