Euphorbia mazandaranica

Euphorbia mazandaranica, commonly known as the Mazandaran spurge, is a perennial herb native to the forests of Iran.

It features a distinctive stem with a smooth, greenish-gray surface and produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. The plant has milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and interesting species within the Euphorbiaceae family.
