Erigeron pulchellus var. townsendii

Erigeron pulchellus var. townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
