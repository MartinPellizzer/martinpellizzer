Eplingiella fruticosa

Eplingiella fruticosa, commonly known as the desert broom, is a shrub native to arid regions of North America.

It features small, narrow leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Eplingiella fruticosa plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
