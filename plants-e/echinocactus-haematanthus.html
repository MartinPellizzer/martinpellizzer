Echinocactus haematanthus

Echinocactus haematanthus, commonly known as the red-hair cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and bright red spines that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
