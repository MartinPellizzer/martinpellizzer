Ervum chilense

Ervum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean vetch, is a leguminous plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish-pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ervum chilense is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
