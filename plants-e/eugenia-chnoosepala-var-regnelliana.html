Eugenia chnoosepala var. regnelliana

Eugenia chnoosepala var. regnelliana, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple, aromatic fruits that are encased in a thick, segmented rind. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and the edible, sweet-tart fruit it produces.
