Echinopsis skottsbergii

Echinopsis skottsbergii, commonly known as the Skottsberg's cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its spines provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration.

Echinopsis skottsbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
