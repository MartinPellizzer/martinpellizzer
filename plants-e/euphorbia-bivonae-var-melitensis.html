Euphorbia bivonae var. melitensis

Euphorbia bivonae var. melitensis, commonly known as the Maltese spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
