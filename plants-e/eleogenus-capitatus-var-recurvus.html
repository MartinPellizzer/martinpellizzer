Eleogenus capitatus var. recurvus

Eleogenus capitatus var. recurvus, commonly known as the curly-headed elephant tree, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and fleshy, cylindrical stems that often curl and twist. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. Its thick, waxy leaves help it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its distinctive appearance and adaptability to dry conditions.
