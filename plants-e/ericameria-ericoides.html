Ericameria ericoides

Ericameria ericoides, commonly known as silverleaf ericoides, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Ericameria ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
