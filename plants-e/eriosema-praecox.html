Eriosema praecox

Eriosema praecox, commonly known as the early eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "eriosema," derived from the Greek words for "hairy" and "seed.". Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in spring.

It is often found in grassland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
