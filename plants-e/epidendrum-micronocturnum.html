Epidendrum micronocturnum

Epidendrum micronocturnum, commonly known as the night-blooming Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America. The plant produces fragrant, star-shaped flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive, velvety texture.
