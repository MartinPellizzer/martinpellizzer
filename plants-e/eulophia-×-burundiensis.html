Eulophia × burundiensis

Eulophia × burundiensis, commonly known as the Burundi orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of Burundi.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, adapted to the cool, humid conditions of its native habitat.

Eulophia × burundiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
