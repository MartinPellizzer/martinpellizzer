Eryngium alismifolium

Eryngium alismifolium, commonly known as the water dropwort, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist soils and is often found near ponds, marshes, and streams. Its striking appearance makes it a notable species in ecological restoration projects.

Eryngium alismifolium plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland biodiversity.
