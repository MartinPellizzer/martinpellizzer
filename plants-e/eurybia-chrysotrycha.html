Eurybia chrysotrycha

Eurybia chrysotrycha, commonly known as the golden wood-sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Eurybia chrysotrycha is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
