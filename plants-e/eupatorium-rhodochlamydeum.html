Eupatorium rhodochlamydeum

Eupatorium rhodochlamydeum, commonly known as the redstem goldenweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, yellow to orange inflorescences consist of small disc flowers surrounded by ray florets.

Eupatorium rhodochlamydeum typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.
