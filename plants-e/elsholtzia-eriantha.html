Elsholtzia eriantha

Elsholtzia eriantha, commonly known as the woolly elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Elsholtzia eriantha is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
