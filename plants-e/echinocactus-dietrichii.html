Echinocactus dietrichii

Echinocactus dietrichii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

The spines, which are modified leaves, provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration.
