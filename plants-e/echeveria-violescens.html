Echeveria violescens

Echeveria violescens, commonly known as the Violet Leaf Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically bluish-gray with a subtle violet hue. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
