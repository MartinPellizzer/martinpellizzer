Eriocephalus capitellatus

Eriocephalus capitellatus, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually small, elliptical, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

The Cape snowbush is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
