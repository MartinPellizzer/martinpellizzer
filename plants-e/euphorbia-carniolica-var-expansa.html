Euphorbia carniolica var. expansa

Euphorbia carniolica var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
