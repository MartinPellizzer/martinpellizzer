Erythrophysopsis aesculina

Erythrophysopsis aesculina, commonly known as the red-fruit maple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves that turn vibrant red in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and naturalized areas.

Due to its unique foliage and ecological benefits, it is appreciated in horticulture for its aesthetic and environmental contributions.
