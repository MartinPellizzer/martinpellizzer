Erica pycnantha

Erica pycnantha, commonly known as the snowflake heath, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are a food source for various birds and animals. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Erica pycnantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
