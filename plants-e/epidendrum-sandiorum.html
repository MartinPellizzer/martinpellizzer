Epidendrum sandiorum

Epidendrum sandiorum, commonly known as the Sandiorum orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that often feature a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are long-lasting and attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.

The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive in cultivation.
