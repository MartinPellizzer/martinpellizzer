Eugenia sublaeta

Eugenia sublaeta, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, yellowish drupe, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

Eugenia sublaeta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
