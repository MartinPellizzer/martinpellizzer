Eucalyptus pluricaulis subsp. porphyrea

Eucalyptus pluricaulis subsp.

porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyrea malleable, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous beneath.

The flowers are usually arranged in umbels and are typically white or pale pink in color. This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
