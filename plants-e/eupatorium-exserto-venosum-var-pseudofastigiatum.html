Eupatorium exserto-venosum var. pseudofastigiatum

Eupatorium exserto-venosum var. pseudofastigiatum, commonly known as the slender eupatorium, is a variant of the eupatorium genus native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and finely divided, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
