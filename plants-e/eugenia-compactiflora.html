Eugenia compactiflora

Eugenia compactiflora, commonly known as the dwarf guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. The plant has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a landscape plant or in containers.
