Echium strictum subsp. strictum

Echium strictum subsp.

strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flax or blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces striking spikes of blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
