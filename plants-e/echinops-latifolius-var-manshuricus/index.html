Echinops Latifolius Var. Manshuricus

Manshurian thistle, scientifically known as Echinops latifolius var. manshuricus, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to the cold climates of Eastern Europe and Siberia. This plant is characterized by its spiny, spherical heads and silvery-green leaves that reflect the harsh winter conditions it thrives in. Despite its prickly appearance, the thistle plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals. Its resilience and adaptability make it a notable species in its native regions. However, its introduction to new areas has raised concerns about its potential to become invasive.
