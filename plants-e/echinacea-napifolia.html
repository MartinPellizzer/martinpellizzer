Echinacea napifolia

Echinacea napifolia, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its robust, upright stems and striking purple-pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a distinctive, spiky central disk surrounded by showy petals. Echinacea napifolia is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its immune-boosting potential.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens and herbal cultivation.
