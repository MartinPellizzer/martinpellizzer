Eremophila hygrophana

Eremophila hygrophana, commonly known as the swamp eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as swampy areas and creeklines. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in regions with a subtropical or temperate climate.

Eremophila hygrophana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
