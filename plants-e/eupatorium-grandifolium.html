Eupatorium grandifolium

Eupatorium grandifolium, commonly known as great blue lupine, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy blue to purple flower heads. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands, and is known for its ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.

The genus Eupatorium is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants.
