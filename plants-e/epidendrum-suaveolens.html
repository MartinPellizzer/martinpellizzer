Epidendrum suaveolens

Epidendrum suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, drooping inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its pleasant aroma.
