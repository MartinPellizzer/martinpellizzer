Echeveria trianthina

Echeveria trianthina, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green with a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its compact form.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
