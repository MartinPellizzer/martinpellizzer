Eragrostis ciliaris var. compta

Eragrostis ciliaris var. compta, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silver-gray sheen on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
