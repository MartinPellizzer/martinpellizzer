Epacris campanulata var. alba

Epacris campanulata var. alba, commonly known as the white bell heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained acidic soils. It is a shrub that can reach up to 1 meter in height, with narrow, linear leaves.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
