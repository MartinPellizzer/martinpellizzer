Erythrina santamartensis

Erythrina santamartensis, commonly known as the Santa Marta coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to northern South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often covered in thorns. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Additionally, its seeds are encased in a hard, woody pod that splits open when mature, releasing the seeds for propagation.
