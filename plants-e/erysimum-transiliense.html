Erysimum transiliense

Erysimum transiliense, commonly known as the Transylvanian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erysimum transiliense is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.
