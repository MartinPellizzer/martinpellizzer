Erysimum aurosicum

Erysimum aurosicum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustards. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Erysimum aurosicum is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ease of cultivation.
