Erica junonia

Erica junonia, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica junonia is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for coastal gardens and landscaping.
