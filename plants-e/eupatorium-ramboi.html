Eupatorium ramboi

Eupatorium ramboi, commonly known as the white eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its showy, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium ramboi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
