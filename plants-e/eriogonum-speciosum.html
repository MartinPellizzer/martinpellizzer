Eriogonum speciosum

Eriogonum speciosum, commonly known as the showy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife species.

Eriogonum speciosum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant landscaping.
