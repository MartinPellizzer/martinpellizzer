Eclipta ciliata

Eclipta ciliata, commonly known as slender false dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer, followed by fluffy, white seeds dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as fields and roadsides.

Eclipta ciliata is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
