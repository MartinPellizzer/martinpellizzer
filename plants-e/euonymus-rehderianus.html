Euonymus rehderianus

Euonymus rehderianus, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruits are four-lobed capsules that split open to release seeds.

Euonymus rehderianus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden environments.
