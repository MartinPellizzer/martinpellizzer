Epacris rubrograndiflora

Epacris rubrograndiflora, commonly known as the red-stemmed epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking red or orange-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, evergreen leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Epacris rubrograndiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
