Eugenia steyermarkii

Eugenia steyermarkii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or West Indian cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Eugenia steyermarkii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
