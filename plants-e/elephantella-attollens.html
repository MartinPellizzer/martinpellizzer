Elephantella attollens

Elephantella attollens, commonly known as the giant sea anemone, is a marine cnidarian found in tropical and subtropical waters.

It possesses a large, umbrella-shaped body called a polyp, which contains numerous tentacles used for capturing prey. The plant's structure is composed of a central gastrovascular cavity surrounded by a ring of tentacles. It thrives in shallow coastal areas and is often found attached to coral or rocky substrates.

Its vibrant colors and unique morphology make it a notable species in marine ecosystems.
