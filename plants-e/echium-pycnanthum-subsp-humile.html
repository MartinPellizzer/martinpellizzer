Echium pycnanthum subsp. humile

Echium pycnanthum subsp.

humile, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered with a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The species is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
