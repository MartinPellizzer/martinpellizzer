Echinopsis haematantha var. jasimanensis

Echinopsis haematantha var. jasimanensis, commonly known as the Jasimanensis Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and is covered in spines that vary in color from yellow to reddish-brown. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a hardy plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
