Ericoides polytrichofolium

Ericoides polytrichofolium, commonly known as the hairy leaf ericoid, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and northern Baja California.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides polytrichofolium thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
