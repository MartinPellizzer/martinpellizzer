Eucalyptus eremophila var. pterocarpa

Eucalyptus eremophila var. pterocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, exhibiting features such as reduced leaf size and thick cuticles to minimize water loss. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces woody fruits that contain seeds.

This variant plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
