Euphrasia rostkoviana subsp. grandiflora

Euphrasia rostkoviana subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered bedstraw, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces small, oval-shaped seeds in its fruiting structures. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

This subspecies is often found in calcareous soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
