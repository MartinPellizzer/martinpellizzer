Eugenia teffensis var. subcordata

Eugenia teffensis var. subcordata, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a shrub native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. The leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
