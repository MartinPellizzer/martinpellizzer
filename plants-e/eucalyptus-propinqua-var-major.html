Eucalyptus propinqua var. major

Eucalyptus propinqua var. major, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale blue.

This species is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid environments.
