Erythrochiton incomparabilis

Erythrochiton incomparabilis, commonly known as the crimson flame orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are borne on long, slender inflorescences. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in shaded understory conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouse settings.
