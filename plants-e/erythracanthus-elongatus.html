Erythracanthus elongatus

Erythracanthus elongatus, commonly known as the long-stalked erythracanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface.
