Eupatorium rhytidodes

Eupatorium rhytidodes, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium rhytidodes is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
