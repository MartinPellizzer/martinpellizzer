Eriosyce napina

Eriosyce napina, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy foliage. Eriosyce napina is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, arid conditions with minimal rainfall.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
