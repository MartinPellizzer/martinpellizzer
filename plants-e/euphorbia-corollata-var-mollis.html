Euphorbia corollata var. mollis

Euphorbia corollata var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is a defining feature of the genus Euphorbia.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
