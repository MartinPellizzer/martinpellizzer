Emilia lateritica

Emilia lateritica, commonly known as the golden balsam, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers with four petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia lateritica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
