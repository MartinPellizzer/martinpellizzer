Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. neomexicanus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. neomexicanus, commonly known as the New Mexico hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its adaptations include reduced leaf surfaces and a deep root system to conserve water.
