Ericoides fulgidum

Ericoides fulgidum, commonly known as the golden erica, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Ericoides fulgidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
