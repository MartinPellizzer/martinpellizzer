Echinostachys rosea

Echinostachys rosea, commonly known as the pink ponytail palm, is a striking plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a type of cycad, characterized by its thick, fleshy trunk and a crown of feathery, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that eventually develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and arid landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often used in ornamental gardening.
