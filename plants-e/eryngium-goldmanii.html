Eryngium goldmanii

Eryngium goldmanii, commonly known as the golden thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and striking purple-pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
