Enckea orthostachya

Enckea orthostachya, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood with a rich, reddish-brown color and a fine, uniform texture. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically appearing in yellow or greenish-yellow hues.

The tree is valued for its high-quality timber, which has been historically used in the production of musical instruments and fine furniture.
