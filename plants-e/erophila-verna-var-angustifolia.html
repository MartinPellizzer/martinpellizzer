Erophila verna var. angustifolia

Erophila verna var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, angular stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
