Erythroxylum durum

Erythroxylum durum, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant features opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and typically have five petals.

The fruit is a small, round capsule that contains several seeds.
