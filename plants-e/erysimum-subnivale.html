Erysimum subnivale

Erysimum subnivale, commonly known as the snowdrop erysimum, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cold, moist conditions. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, often before the snow has fully melted.

Erysimum subnivale is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
