Eugenia molliana

Eugenia molliana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon basin and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and edible, red, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
