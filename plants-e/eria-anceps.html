Eria anceps

Eria anceps, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered blooms in various colors.

Eria anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
