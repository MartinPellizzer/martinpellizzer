Eriogonum flexum

Eriogonum flexum, commonly known as the bentleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that often curve downward, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Eriogonum flexum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
