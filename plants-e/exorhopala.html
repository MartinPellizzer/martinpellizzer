Exorhopala

Exorhopala, commonly known as the golden sunflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a robust, branching stem that can grow up to two meters in height. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface to reduce water loss.

Exorhopala is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments, making it a resilient and visually appealing botanical specimen.
