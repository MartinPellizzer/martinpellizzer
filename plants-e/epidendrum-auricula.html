Epidendrum auricula

Epidendrum auricula, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, pendulous flower spikes with bright yellow or orange flowers that resemble small ears, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Epidendrum auricula is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
