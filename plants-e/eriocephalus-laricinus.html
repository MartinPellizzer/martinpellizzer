Eriocephalus laricinus

Eriocephalus laricinus, commonly known as the silverleaf eriocephalus, is a South African plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eriocephalus laricinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
