Eucalyptus aquilina

Eucalyptus aquilina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often zigzagging branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in umbels, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Eucalyptus aquilina plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for various pollinators and shelter for small fauna.
