Eriosyce heinrichiana

Eriosyce heinrichiana, commonly known as the Heinrich's bulbous cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its globular or cylindrical shape and is covered in white, woolly spines that provide insulation and protection. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
