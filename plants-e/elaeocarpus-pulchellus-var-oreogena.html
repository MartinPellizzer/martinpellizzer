Elaeocarpus pulchellus var. oreogena

Elaeocarpus pulchellus var. oreogena, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant species native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white to pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to cooler, mountainous climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitats.
