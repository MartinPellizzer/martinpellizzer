Erica dianthera

Erica dianthera, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing form and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white. Erica dianthera thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
