Excoecaria biglandulosa f. obovata

Excoecaria biglandulosa f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
