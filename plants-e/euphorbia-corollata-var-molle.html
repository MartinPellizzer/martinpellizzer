Euphorbia corollata var. molle

Euphorbia corollata var. molle, commonly known as soft-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features smooth, succulent stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
