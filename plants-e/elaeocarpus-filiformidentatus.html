Elaeocarpus filiformidentatus

Elaeocarpus filiformidentatus, commonly known as the slender-toothed elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with finely serrated edges. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological importance in its native habitats.
