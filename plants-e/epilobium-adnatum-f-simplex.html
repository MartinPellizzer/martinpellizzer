Epilobium adnatum f. simplex

Epilobium adnatum f. simplex, commonly known as the simple willow herb, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features simple, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a slender stem and small, oval-shaped fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
