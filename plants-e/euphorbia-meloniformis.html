Euphorbia meloniformis

Euphorbia meloniformis, commonly known as the melon-shaped euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that resemble a melon, and it produces small, yellowish cyathia as its flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its leaves are often sparse, giving it a unique, spiny appearance.

Euphorbia meloniformis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
