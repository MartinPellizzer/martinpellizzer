Eragrostis solida

Eragrostis solida, commonly known as rigid lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Eragrostis solida is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to thrive in poor soils.
