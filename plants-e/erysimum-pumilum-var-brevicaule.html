Erysimum pumilum var. brevicaule

Erysimum pumilum var. brevicaule, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in mild climates.
