Emiliomarcelia

Emiliomarcelia, commonly known as the golden spire plant, is a unique species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged spirally along the stem. Emiliomarcelia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
