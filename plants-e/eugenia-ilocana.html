Eugenia ilocana

Eugenia ilocana, commonly known as the Ilocano guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The fruits are edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is well adapted to the humid conditions of its native habitat.
