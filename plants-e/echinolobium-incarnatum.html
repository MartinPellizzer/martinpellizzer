Echinolobium incarnatum

Echinolobium incarnatum, commonly known as the red globeflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
