Euphorbia bottae

Euphorbia bottae, commonly known as the bottle cactus, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a shallow root system adapted for drought conditions.

Euphorbia bottae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
