Echinocactus tricolor

Echinocactus tricolor, commonly known as the rainbow cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful spines that come in shades of red, yellow, and green, giving it its common name. The plant has a globular shape with thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Echinocactus tricolor is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
