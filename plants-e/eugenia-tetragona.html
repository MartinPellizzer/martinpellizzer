Eugenia tetragona

Eugenia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical leaves with a four-angled stem. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit, which is edible, is typically small and berry-like, containing several seeds.
