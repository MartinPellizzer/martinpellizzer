Erica racemosa

Erica racemosa, commonly known as the heath or the common heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreens adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink, white, or purple, during the summer months. Erica racemosa is well-suited to coastal and sandy environments due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
