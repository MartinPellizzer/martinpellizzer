Eriosema tephrosioides var. angustifoliolatum

Eriosema tephrosioides var. angustifoliolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets that have a slightly serrated edge. Small, pale yellow flowers are borne in clusters along the stems, and the plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

It is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
