Eugenia belemitana

Eugenia belemitana, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
