Escallonia cuneifolia

Escallonia cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf escallonia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Escallonia cuneifolia is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, making it a low-maintenance garden plant.
