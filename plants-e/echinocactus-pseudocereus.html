Echinocactus pseudocereus

Echinocactus pseudocereus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
