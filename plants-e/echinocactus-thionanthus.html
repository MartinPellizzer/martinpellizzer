Echinocactus thionanthus

Echinocactus thionanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, which is covered in prominent, yellowish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
