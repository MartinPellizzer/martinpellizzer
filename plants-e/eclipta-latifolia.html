Eclipta latifolia

Eclipta latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Eclipta latifolia is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
