Erythrococca mitis

Erythrococca mitis, commonly known as the red cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

However, it is not commonly cultivated for commercial purposes.
