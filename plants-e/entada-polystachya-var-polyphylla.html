Entada polystachya var. polyphylla

Entada polystachya var. polyphylla, commonly known as the African flame bean, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, compound leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental and agricultural species in its native range.

The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
