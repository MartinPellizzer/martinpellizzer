Erica coccinea var. willdenowii

Erica coccinea var. willdenowii, commonly known as the Willdenow's heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are evergreen and often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
