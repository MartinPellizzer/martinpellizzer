Elaeagnus takeshitae

Elaeagnus takeshitae, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has a distinctive fragrance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Elaeagnus takeshitae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
