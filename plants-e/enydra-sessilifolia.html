Enydra sessilifolia

Enydra sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessileleaf enydra, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are attached directly to the stem without stalks. The plant produces tiny, white to greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in swamps and along the edges of ponds.

Enydra sessilifolia is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to the ecological balance through its role in nutrient cycling and habitat provision.
