Euphorbia calyptrata var. involucrata

Euphorbia calyptrata var. involucrata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Natal milkweed, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, spiny stems and produces unique, cup-shaped flower clusters known as cyathia. The plant has yellowish-green bracts that surround the central flower, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves and stems.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
