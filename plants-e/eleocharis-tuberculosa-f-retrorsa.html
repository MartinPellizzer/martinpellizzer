Eleocharis tuberculosa f. retrorsa

Eleocharis tuberculosa f. retrorsa, commonly known as the backward-pointing sedge, is a variant of the tubercled sedge.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often curved backward, giving it its distinctive appearance.

This form is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
