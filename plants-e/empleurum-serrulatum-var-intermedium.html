Empleurum serrulatum var. intermedium

Empleurum serrulatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate sawleaf fern, is a variant of the sawleaf fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins, which give it its common name. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive, narrow, and elongated shape. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of fern morphology and ecological adaptation.
