Eriogonum pondii var. gentryi

Eriogonum pondii var. gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a fuzzy texture.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed structures and ecological importance in arid environments.
