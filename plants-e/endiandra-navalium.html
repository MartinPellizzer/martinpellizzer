Endiandra navalium

Endiandra navalium, commonly known as the coastal endiandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, thriving in sandy soils and saline environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
