Epidendrum tricallosum

Epidendrum tricallosum, commonly known as the Three-lobed Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features lanceolate, dark green leaves with a waxy surface, and its flowers are usually pink or white with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum tricallosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
