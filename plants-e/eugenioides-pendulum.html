Eugenioides pendulum

Eugenioides pendulum, commonly known as the weeping myrtle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and emit a mild, pleasant scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Eugenioides pendulum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and aromatic foliage.
