Echinops gedrosiacus var. glaber

Echinops gedrosiacus var. glaber, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Echinops gedrosiacus var. glaber is well adapted to dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
