Elaeocarpus auricomus

Elaeocarpus auricomus, commonly known as the golden ear tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, brown seeds.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
