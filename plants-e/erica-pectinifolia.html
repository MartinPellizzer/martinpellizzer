Erica pectinifolia

Erica pectinifolia, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica pectinifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
