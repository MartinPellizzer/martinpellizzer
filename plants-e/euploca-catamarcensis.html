Euploca catamarcensis

Euploca catamarcensis, commonly known as the Catamarca grass, is a flowering plant native to Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to botanists and ecological researchers.
