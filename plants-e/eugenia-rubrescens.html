Eugenia rubrescens

Eugenia rubrescens, commonly known as the red-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green to reddish leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant bears edible, pinkish-red berries that are often used in jams and preserves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia rubrescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
