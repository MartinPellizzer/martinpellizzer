Epidendrum isaucapitellatum

Epidendrum isaucapitellatum, commonly known as the yellow-tipped epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees in its natural habitat.

It requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
