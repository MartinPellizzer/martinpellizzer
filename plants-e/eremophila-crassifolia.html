Eremophila crassifolia

Eremophila crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Eremophila crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
