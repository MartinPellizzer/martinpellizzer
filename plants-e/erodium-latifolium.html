Erodium latifolium

Erodium latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are broad and heart-shaped. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its hairy stems and stipulate leaves, which are common features in the Geraniaceae family.
