Eupatorium rhodopappum

Eupatorium rhodopappum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces showy purple to pink flower heads in the summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This species is often found in moist woodlands and prairies, thriving in partial to full sunlight.
