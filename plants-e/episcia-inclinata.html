Episcia inclinata

Episcia inclinata, commonly known as the hanging heartleaf, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. Episcia inclinata produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.
