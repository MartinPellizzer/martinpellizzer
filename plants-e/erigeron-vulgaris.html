Erigeron vulgaris

Erigeron vulgaris, commonly known as common fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 cm. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

Erigeron vulgaris is often used in herbal medicine and is also a source of nectar for pollinators.
