Elionurus pretoriensis

Elionurus pretoriensis, commonly known as the Pretoria grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Elionurus pretoriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
