Euphorbia cassioides

Euphorbia cassioides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a woody stem with smooth, greenish-gray bark and produces small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic and is used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia cassioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
