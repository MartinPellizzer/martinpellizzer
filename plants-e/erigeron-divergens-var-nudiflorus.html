Erigeron divergens var. nudiflorus

Erigeron divergens var. nudiflorus, commonly known as the nakedflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that contribute to its distribution in arid environments.
