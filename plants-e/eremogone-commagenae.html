Eremogone commagenae

Eremogone commagenae, commonly known as the mountain hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

Eremogone commagenae plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its mountainous habitat.
