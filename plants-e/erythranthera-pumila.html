Erythranthera pumila

Erythranthera pumila, commonly known as the dwarf red sage, is a low-growing herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
