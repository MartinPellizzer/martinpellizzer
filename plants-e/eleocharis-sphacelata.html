Eleocharis sphacelata

Eleocharis sphacelata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bulrush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its presence is often an indicator of healthy, undisturbed wetland habitats.
