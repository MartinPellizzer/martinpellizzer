Erythroxylum goyazensis

Erythroxylum goyazensis, commonly known as the Goiás Erythroxylum, is a shrub native to Brazil, particularly found in the state of Goiás.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by berries that contain the stimulant compound cathinone.
