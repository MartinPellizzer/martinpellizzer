Ecastaphyllum

Ecastaphyllum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern shores of the Atlantic Ocean. The plant is characterized by its blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. Its succulent leaves are adapted to tolerate salty air and wind.

Ecastaphyllum is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
