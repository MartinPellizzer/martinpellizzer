Eremosis solorzanoana

Eremosis solorzanoana, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eremosis solorzanoana is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
