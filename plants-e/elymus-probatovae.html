Elymus probatovae

Elymus probatovae, commonly known as the Siberian wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its role in pollination and seed dispersal. Elymus probatovae thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and disturbed sites.

Its adaptability and resilience make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
