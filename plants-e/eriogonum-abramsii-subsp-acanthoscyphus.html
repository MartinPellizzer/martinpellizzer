Eriogonum abramsii subsp. acanthoscyphus

Eriogonum abramsii subsp.

acanthoscyphus, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
