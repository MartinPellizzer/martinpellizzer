Erianthemum heterochromum

Erianthemum heterochromum, commonly known as the velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Erianthemum heterochromum is known for its adaptability and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
