Eupatorium urticifolium var. nanum

Eupatorium urticifolium var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf boneset, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture and produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its foliage is often covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough, almost bristly appearance.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
