Eria magnicallosa

Eria magnicallosa, commonly known as the large-flowered eria, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The orchid's inflorescence consists of multiple flowers with intricate, colorful petals and sepals.
