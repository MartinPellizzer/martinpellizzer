Erigeron aureus

Erigeron aureus, commonly known as the golden fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, with bright yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Erigeron aureus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
