Echeveria laxa var. nevadensis

Echeveria laxa var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.
