Eucalyptus × rivularis

Eucalyptus × rivularis, commonly known as the river gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark that peels in thin layers. The plant typically grows as a tall tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels, and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
