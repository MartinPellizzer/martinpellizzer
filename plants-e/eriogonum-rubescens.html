Eriogonum rubescens

Eriogonum rubescens, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum rubescens is an important plant for pollinators and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
