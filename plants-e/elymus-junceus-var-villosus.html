Elymus junceus var. villosus

Elymus junceus var. villosus, commonly known as hairy jointgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of loose, spike-like clusters of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
