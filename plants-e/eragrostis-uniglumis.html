Eragrostis uniglumis

Eragrostis uniglumis, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and rapid growth, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage plant.
