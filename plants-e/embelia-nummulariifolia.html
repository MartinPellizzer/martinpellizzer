Embelia nummulariifolia

Embelia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf embelia, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in diameter. The plant produces small, white, four-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange berries. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
