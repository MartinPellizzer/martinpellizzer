Euodia philippinensis

Euodia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine lauraceae, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in lowland to montane forests and is often found in secondary growth areas. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, with a distinct scent when crushed.

Euodia philippinensis is also valued for its wood, which is durable and used in local crafts and construction.
