Elaeocarpus kostermansii

Elaeocarpus kostermansii, commonly known as the Koster's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
