Elaeocarpus argentellus

Elaeocarpus argentellus, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a smooth, waxy surface.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
