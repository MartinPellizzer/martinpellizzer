Eugenia tingui

Eugenia tingui, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

Eugenia tingui is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for landscaping purposes.
