Eriobotrya fragrans var. furfuracea

Eriobotrya fragrans var. furfuracea, commonly known as the rough-lobe loquat, is a variant of the loquat tree native to China.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy texture on the leaves and stems, distinguishing it from the more commonly cultivated varieties. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet-sour flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown for its ornamental value as well as its fruit yield.
