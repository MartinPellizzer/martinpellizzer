Echinocactus longihamatus f. sinuatus

Echinocactus longihamatus f. sinuatus, commonly known as the Sinuatus Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the long-armed hedgehog cactus.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant's leaves are reduced to spines, and its stems are ribbed, which helps it store water in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

This cultivar is prized for its distinctive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
