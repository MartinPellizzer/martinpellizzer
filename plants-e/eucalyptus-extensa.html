Eucalyptus extensa

Eucalyptus extensa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or tree form, often growing in dry, sandy soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. Its flowers are small and white or pale blue, appearing in clusters during the winter months.

Eucalyptus extensa is adapted to arid environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
