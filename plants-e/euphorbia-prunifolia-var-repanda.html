Euphorbia prunifolia var. repanda

Euphorbia prunifolia var. repanda, commonly known as the smoothleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features pale green, fleshy, and often ribbed stems that are covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically small, oval, and arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
