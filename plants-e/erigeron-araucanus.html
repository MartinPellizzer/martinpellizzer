Erigeron araucanus

Erigeron araucanus, commonly known as the Chilean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.
