Euploca microsalsoloides

Euploca microsalsoloides, commonly known as the dwarf saltbush, is a halophytic shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, succulent leaves that help reduce water loss in saline environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Euploca microsalsoloides plays an important role in stabilizing soils in coastal and saline habitats.
