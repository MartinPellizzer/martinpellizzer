Evolvulus corumbaensis

Evolvulus corumbaensis, commonly known as the Corumba morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. It has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Evolvulus corumbaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
