Eremochloa eriopoda

Eremochloa eriopoda, commonly known as the silky grass or silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a clumping growth habit and is well adapted to drought conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eremochloa eriopoda is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
