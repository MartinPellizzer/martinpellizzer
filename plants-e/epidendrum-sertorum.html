Epidendrum sertorum

Epidendrum sertorum, commonly known as the clustered epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a strong, sweet fragrance. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum sertorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
