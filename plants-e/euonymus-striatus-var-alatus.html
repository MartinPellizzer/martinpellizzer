Euonymus striatus var. alatus

Euonymus striatus var. alatus, commonly known as the winged euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems, which are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. Its leaves are elliptical, with a smooth margin and a slightly toothed base.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
