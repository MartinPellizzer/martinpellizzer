Eucalyptus redunca var. elata

Eucalyptus redunca var. elata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine greyish bloom. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which appear in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as mallee and heathlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
