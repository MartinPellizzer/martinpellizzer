Eugenia rivulorum

Eugenia rivulorum, commonly known as the river eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, sweet-smelling berries. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional remedies.
