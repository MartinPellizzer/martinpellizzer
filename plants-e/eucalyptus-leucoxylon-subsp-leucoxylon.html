Eucalyptus leucoxylon subsp. leucoxylon

Eucalyptus leucoxylon subsp.

leucoxylon, commonly known as the white gum, is a medium to large tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, white to pale grey bark that peels in thin, papery layers.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a grey-green or silver-white upper surface. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, and it is known for its aromatic oils.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, fertile areas.
