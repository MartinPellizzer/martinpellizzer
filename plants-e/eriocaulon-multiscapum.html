Eriocaulon multiscapum

Eriocaulon multiscapum, commonly known as the many-stemmed spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland and marshy environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, often stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
