Eugenia tisserantii

Eugenia tisserantii, commonly known as the Tisserant's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
