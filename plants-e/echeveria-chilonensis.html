Echeveria chilonensis

Echeveria chilonensis, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically green with red or purple hues. The plant produces showy clusters of violet or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight.

Echeveria chilonensis is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
