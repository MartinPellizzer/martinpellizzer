Enemion raddeanum

Enemion raddeanum, commonly known as the white geranium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Enemion raddeanum is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
