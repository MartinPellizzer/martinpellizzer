Encyclia chloroleuca

Encyclia chloroleuca, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a short, creeping rhizome and produces compact, pseudobulbous stems with a single leaf. The plant's flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive yellowish-green lip that has prominent veins. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Encyclia chloroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
