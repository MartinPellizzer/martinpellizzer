Epilobium adenocaulon var. americanum

Epilobium adenocaulon var. americanum, commonly known as American willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

The plant is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open habitats.
