Eclipta lanceolata

Eclipta lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a bushy growth habit with hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped, often with serrated edges, and its flowers are yellow with dark centers.

Eclipta lanceolata is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
