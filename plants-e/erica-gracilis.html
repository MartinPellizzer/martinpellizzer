Erica gracilis

Erica gracilis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, usually during the spring and summer months. Erica gracilis is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, leathery leaves.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
