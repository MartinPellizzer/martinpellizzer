Echinopsis pelecyrhachis var. lobivioides

Echinopsis pelecyrhachis var. lobivioides, commonly known as the Lobel's moon cactus, is a succulent belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile. The plant features a short, thick stem with a single, large, globular body that is often covered in white or yellow spines. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in the evening.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.
