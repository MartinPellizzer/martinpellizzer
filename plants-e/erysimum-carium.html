Erysimum carium

Erysimum carium, commonly known as the Brazilian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, and it produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Erysimum carium is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
