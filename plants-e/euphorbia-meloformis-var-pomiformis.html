Euphorbia meloformis var. pomiformis

Euphorbia meloformis var. pomiformis, commonly known as the globe-shaped euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, rounded, and fleshy stems that resemble a globe, which are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and striking appearance make it a desirable addition to arid landscapes.
