Erysimum portugalense

Erysimum portugalense, commonly known as the Portuguese wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with distinct four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Erysimum portugalense is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
