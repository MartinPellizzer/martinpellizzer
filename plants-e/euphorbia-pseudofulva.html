Euphorbia pseudofulva

Euphorbia pseudofulva, commonly known as the yellow spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, green, ribbed stems and yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by orange-red cyathia, the specialized flower structure of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
