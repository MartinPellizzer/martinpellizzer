Echinodorus aschersonianus var. nulliglandulosus

Echinodorus aschersonianus var. nulliglandulosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge above the water surface. It thrives in slow to moderately flowing freshwater environments with moderate to high light levels.

This variety is valued in aquariums for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
