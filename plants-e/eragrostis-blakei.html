Eragrostis blakei

Eragrostis blakei, commonly known as Blake's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in low-nutrient soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

Botanically, it is classified within the Poaceae family, which includes other grasses.
