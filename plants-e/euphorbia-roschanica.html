Euphorbia roschanica

Euphorbia roschanica, commonly known as the Roschan's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. Its foliage consists of alternate, simple leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia roschanica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils at higher elevations.
