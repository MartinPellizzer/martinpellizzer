Epimedium latisepalum

Epimedium latisepalum, commonly known as the broad-sepal epimedium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with leathery, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are often hidden beneath its foliage. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Epimedium latisepalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate shade, contributing to its use in horticulture.
