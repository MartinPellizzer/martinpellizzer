Echinocactus linkii

Echinocactus linkii, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a cylindrical shape with ribbed stems that help it store water, adapting to its arid environment. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Echinocactus linkii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
