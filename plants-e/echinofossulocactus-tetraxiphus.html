Echinofossulocactus tetraxiphus

Echinofossulocactus tetraxiphus, commonly known as the four-spined hedgehog cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently in arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species within the cactus family.
