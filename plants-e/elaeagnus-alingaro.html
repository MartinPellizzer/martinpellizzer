Elaeagnus alingaro

Elaeagnus alingaro, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, oblong leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Elaeagnus alingaro is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
