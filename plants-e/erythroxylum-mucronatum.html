Erythroxylum mucronatum

Erythroxylum mucronatum, commonly known as the Amazonian coca, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elliptical, reddish-brown fruits. Its leaves contain alkaloids, including cocaine, which have been traditionally used by indigenous communities.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
