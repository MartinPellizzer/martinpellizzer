Eucalyptus eugenioides var. nana

Eucalyptus eugenioides var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf mallees, is a small, multi-stemmed shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and smooth, often greyish bark. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a fragrant aroma.

This variety is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
