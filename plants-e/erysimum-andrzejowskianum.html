Erysimum andrzejowskianum

Erysimum andrzejowskianum, commonly known as the Andrzejowski's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Erysimum andrzejowskianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
