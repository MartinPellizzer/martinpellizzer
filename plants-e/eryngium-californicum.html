Eryngium californicum

Eryngium californicum, commonly known as the California sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eryngium californicum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
