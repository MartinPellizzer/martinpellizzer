Erica regia var. williana

Erica regia var. williana, commonly known as the Williana heath, is a dwarf shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
