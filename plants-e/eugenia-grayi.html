Eugenia grayi

Eugenia grayi, commonly known as the gray eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, particularly in the state of Santa Catarina. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It produces small, round fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife.

Eugenia grayi is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
