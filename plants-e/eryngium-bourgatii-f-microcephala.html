Eryngium bourgatii f. microcephala

Eryngium bourgatii f. microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf thistle or miniature sea holly, is a compact, mat-forming perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by white, bristly bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grayish-green leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value lies in its unique foliage and delicate, short-lived flowers.
