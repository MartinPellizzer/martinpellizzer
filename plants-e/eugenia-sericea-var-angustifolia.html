Eugenia sericea var. angustifolia

Eugenia sericea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
