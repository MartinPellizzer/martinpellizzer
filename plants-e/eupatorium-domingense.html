Eupatorium domingense

Eupatorium domingense, commonly known as the Dominican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a serrated edge.

The flowers, which appear in late summer, are small and clustered, forming a rounded inflorescence.
