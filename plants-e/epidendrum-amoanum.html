Epidendrum amoanum

Epidendrum amoanum, commonly known as the Amoan orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Puerto Rico.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive hooded lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Epidendrum amoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
