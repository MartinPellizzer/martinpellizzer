Eugenia rara

Eugenia rara, commonly known as the rare myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Due to its limited natural habitat, Eugenia rara is considered a rare and endangered species.
