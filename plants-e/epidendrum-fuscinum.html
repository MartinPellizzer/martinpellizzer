Epidendrum fuscinum

Epidendrum fuscinum, commonly known as the Yellow Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in racemes, often with a contrasting green or red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
