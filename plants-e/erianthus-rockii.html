Erianthus rockii

Erianthus rockii, commonly known as the rock grass, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Erianthus rockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
