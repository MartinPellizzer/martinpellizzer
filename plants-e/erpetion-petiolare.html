Erpetion petiolare

Erpetion petiolare, commonly known as the leafy spurge, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in late summer. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it an invasive species in some regions. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh conditions.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to control.
