Eucalyptus cometae-vallis

Eucalyptus cometae-vallis, commonly known as the Cometae-vallis eucalypt, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, often tall and slender form, with smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin layers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in white or cream colors, and are pollinated by insects and birds.
