Erica petiveriana

Erica petiveriana, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica petiveriana is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.

Its flowering season usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
