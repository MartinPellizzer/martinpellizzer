Eugenia dasyantha

Eugenia dasyantha, commonly known as the rough-leaved guava, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some use in traditional medicine.
