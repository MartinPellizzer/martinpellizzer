Encyclia sisyrinchiifolia

Encyclia sisyrinchiifolia, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its overall aesthetic.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are known for their vibrant color and subtle fragrance.
