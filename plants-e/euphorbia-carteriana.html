Euphorbia carteriana

Euphorbia carteriana, commonly known as Carter's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes.
