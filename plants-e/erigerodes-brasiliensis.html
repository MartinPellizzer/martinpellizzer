Erigerodes brasiliensis

Erigerodes brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian geranium, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant typically features lobed leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in shades of red, pink, and white, adding ornamental value to landscapes.
