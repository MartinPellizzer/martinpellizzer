Eclipta parviflora

Eclipta parviflora, commonly known as false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Eclipta parviflora is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
