Echites hirsutus var. angustifolius

Echites hirsutus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy species suitable for xerophytic gardens.
