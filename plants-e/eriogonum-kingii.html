Eriogonum kingii

Eriogonum kingii, commonly known as the King's flax or King's globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy leaves and produces showy, yellowish to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

Eriogonum kingii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
