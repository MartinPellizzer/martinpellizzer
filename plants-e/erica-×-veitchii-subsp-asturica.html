Erica × veitchii subsp. asturica

Erica × veitchii subsp.

asturica, commonly known as the Asturian heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, characterized by its needle-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically produces pink to reddish-purple blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
