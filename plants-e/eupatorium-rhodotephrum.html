Eupatorium rhodotephrum

Eupatorium rhodotephrum, commonly known as the redroot eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its showy, pinkish-white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
