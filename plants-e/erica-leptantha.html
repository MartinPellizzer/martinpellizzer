Erica leptantha

Erica leptantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica leptantha typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Cape region of South Africa.
