Ericoides teucriifolium

Ericoides teucriifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Ericoides teucriifolium is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
