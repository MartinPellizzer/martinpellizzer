Eugenia subdisticha

Eugenia subdisticha, commonly known as the silverleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
