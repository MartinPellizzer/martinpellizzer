Erisma parvifolium var. pallidiflorum

Erisma parvifolium var. pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower erisma, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features slender, often prostrate stems that produce small, clustered flowers with pale pink to white petals. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically covered in a fine layer of white or grayish pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
