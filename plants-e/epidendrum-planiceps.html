Epidendrum planiceps

Epidendrum planiceps, commonly known as the flat-headed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive flat, elongated shape.

Epidendrum planiceps is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
