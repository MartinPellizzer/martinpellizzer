Eclipta prostrata f. aureoreticulata

Eclipta prostrata f. aureoreticulata, commonly known as the golden nettle, is a variant of the common eclipta plant.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate habit and hairy stems. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a distinctive yellowish netting on the underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by fluffy, pale brown seeds.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
