Eumachia cephalantha

Eumachia cephalantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Eumachia cephalantha is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
