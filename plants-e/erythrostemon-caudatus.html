Erythrostemon caudatus

Erythrostemon caudatus, commonly known as the trailing bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It features trailing stems that can grow up to several feet long, often cascading over rock formations or slopes. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive hooded shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
