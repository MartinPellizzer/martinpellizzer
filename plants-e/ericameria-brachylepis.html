Ericameria brachylepis

Ericameria brachylepis, commonly known as silverbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, silvery foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Ericameria brachylepis is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

Its aromatic leaves and hardy nature make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
