Erophila adriatica

Erophila adriatica, commonly known as Adriatic eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disc.

Adriatic eryngo is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
