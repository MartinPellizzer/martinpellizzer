Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. megacephalum

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum var. megacephalum, commonly known as the giant globemallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has palmate leaves with a hairy surface and produces spherical seed pods that split open when mature.

This variety is distinguished by its larger flower heads compared to other varieties within the Eriogonum genus.
