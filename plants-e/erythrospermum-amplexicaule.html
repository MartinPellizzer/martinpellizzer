Erythrospermum amplexicaule

Erythrospermum amplexicaule, commonly known as the red-flowered rock heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and opposite, ovate leaves that often wrap around the stems. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erythrospermum amplexicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
