Elaphoglossum gardnerianum

Elaphoglossum gardnerianum, commonly known as Gardner's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a finely divided appearance. The plant produces sporangia in clusters on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or along stream banks.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant foliage.
