Elaeagnus argentea subsp. pauciflora

Elaeagnus argentea subsp.

pauciflora, commonly known as silverthorn, is a shrub native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are small, yellowish berries that ripen in late summer. Elaeagnus argentea subsp.

pauciflora is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
