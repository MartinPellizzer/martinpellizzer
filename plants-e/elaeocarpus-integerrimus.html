Elaeocarpus integerrimus

Elaeocarpus integerrimus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
