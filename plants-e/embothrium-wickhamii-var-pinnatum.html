Embothrium wickhamii var. pinnatum

Embothrium wickhamii var. pinnatum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has compound leaves with a pinnate structure, which is a distinguishing feature of this variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
