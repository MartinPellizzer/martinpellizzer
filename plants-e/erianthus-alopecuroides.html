Erianthus alopecuroides

Erianthus alopecuroides, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and can reach heights of up to 3 feet.

Erianthus alopecuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
