Elytrophorus articulatus

Elytrophorus articulatus, commonly known as the common marsh orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to wetland and marshy habitats in the northern hemisphere. The plant has a slender, erect stem with a single spike of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette at the base.

Elytrophorus articulatus is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and its unique floral structure.
