Epiphyllum ackermannii

Epiphyllum ackermannii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing or trailing cactus that originates from Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers, which open at night, are large, showy, and typically white or pale pink in color.

Epiphyllum ackermannii is adapted to grow on other plants or rocks, making it an epiphytic species.
