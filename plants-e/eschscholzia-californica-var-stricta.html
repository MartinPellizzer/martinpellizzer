Eschscholzia californica var. stricta

Eschscholzia californica var. stricta, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers typically bloom from spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
