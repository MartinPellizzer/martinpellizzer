Equisetum variegatum f. virgatum

Equisetum variegatum f. virgatum, commonly known as the variegated horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, a feature typical of its ancient lineage. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique appearance and historical significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
