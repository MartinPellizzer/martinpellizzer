Eugenia fourcadei

Eugenia fourcadei, commonly known as the Fourcade's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. After flowering, it produces small, round, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
