Eupatorium purpureum f. anomalum

Eupatorium purpureum f. anomalum, commonly known as the purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a variant of the common boneset, characterized by its striking purple flowers and distinctive, often branched stems. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic leaves. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Its foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
