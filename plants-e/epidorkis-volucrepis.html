Epidorkis volucrepis

Epidorkis volucrepis, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to tropical regions, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are often tuberous, aiding in water storage and nutrient absorption.

The plant's unique morphology makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
