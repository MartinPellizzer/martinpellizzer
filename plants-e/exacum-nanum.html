Exacum nanum

Exacum nanum, commonly known as the dwarf African violet, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it an attractive addition to both gardens and containers.
