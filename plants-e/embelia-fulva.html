Embelia fulva

Embelia fulva, commonly known as the red sanders or red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and small, elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

The wood of Embelia fulva is prized for its durability and is used in carving and furniture making.
