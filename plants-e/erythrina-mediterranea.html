Erythrina mediterranea

Erythrina mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces legumes as its fruit, which contain flat, seed-like structures. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina mediterranea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
