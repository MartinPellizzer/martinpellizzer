Eragrostis neomexicana

Eragrostis neomexicana, commonly known as New Mexico lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its foliage is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Eragrostis neomexicana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
