Emilia abyssinica

Emilia abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia abyssinica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
