Eberlanzia sedoides

Eberlanzia sedoides, commonly known as the Brazilian flame tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Eberlanzia sedoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
