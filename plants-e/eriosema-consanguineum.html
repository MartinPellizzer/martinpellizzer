Eriosema consanguineum

Eriosema consanguineum, commonly known as the sticky false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture, and its flowers are usually yellow or orange with a distinctive tubular shape. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Eriosema consanguineum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
