Eschenbachia maxima

Eschenbachia maxima, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a large aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, providing shade and habitat for various aquatic life. The plant has long, thin stems that anchor it to the substrate at the bottom of water bodies. Its flowers are typically white or pink and bloom above the water surface, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eschenbachia maxima plays an important role in maintaining water quality by absorbing nutrients and reducing algae growth.
