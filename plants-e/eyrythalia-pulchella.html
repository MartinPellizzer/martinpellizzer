Eyrythalia pulchella

Eyrythalia pulchella, commonly known as the red-bellied beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and bright yellow stamens, which make it a visually appealing species. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elliptical with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Eyrythalia pulchella is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
