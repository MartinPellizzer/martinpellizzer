Echinocereus polyacanthus subsp. huitcholensis

Echinocereus polyacanthus subsp.

huitcholensis, commonly known as the Huichol cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, sharp spines.

The plant produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
