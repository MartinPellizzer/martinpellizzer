Eriocaulon roseum

Eriocaulon roseum, commonly known as the pink spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It features slender, upright stems covered in dense, pinkish to reddish hairs, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Eriocaulon roseum is often used in ornamental wetland gardens due to its unique coloration and ecological benefits.
