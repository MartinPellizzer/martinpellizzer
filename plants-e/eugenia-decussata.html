Eugenia decussata

Eugenia decussata, commonly known as the Java apple or white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

Eugenia decussata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
