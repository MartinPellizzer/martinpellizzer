Epilobium antipodum

Epilobium antipodum, commonly known as New Zealand willow, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, often woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring. Epilobium antipodum thrives in moist, shady environments such as streambanks and forest margins.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native insects and birds.
