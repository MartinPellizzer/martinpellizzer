Epiphyllum truncatum var. cruentum

Epiphyllum truncatum var. cruentum, commonly known as the Crimson Trumpet Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with long, segmented stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically red or pink, often with a distinctive crimson hue. These flowers bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

The plant is adapted to grow on other plants or trees, making it an epiphytic species that thrives in humid, shaded environments.
