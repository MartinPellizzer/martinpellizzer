Echinocereus bakeri

Echinocereus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a short, spiny shrub that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white to yellow spines. Its flowers are usually pink to purple and bloom in the spring.

Echinocereus bakeri is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy plant for desert conditions.
