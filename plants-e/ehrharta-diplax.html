Ehrharta diplax

Ehrharta diplax, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, soft-textured blades that give it a velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along waterways. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Ehrharta diplax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
