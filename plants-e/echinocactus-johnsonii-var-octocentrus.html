Echinocactus johnsonii var. octocentrus

Echinocactus johnsonii var. octocentrus, commonly known as the Johnson's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent spines, which are typically light to dark brown in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It is a slow-growing, cold-hardy cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
