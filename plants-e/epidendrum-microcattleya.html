Epidendrum microcattleya

Epidendrum microcattleya, commonly known as the miniature cattleya orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces blooms in shades of pink, white, and purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
