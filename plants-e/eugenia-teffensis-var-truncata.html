Eugenia teffensis var. truncata

Eugenia teffensis var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
