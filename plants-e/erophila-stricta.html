Erophila stricta

Erophila stricta, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered purple to pink flowers in the summer. Its stems are usually rigid and can become woody with age.

Erophila stricta is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
