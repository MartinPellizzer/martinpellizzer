Eugenia decorticans

Eugenia decorticans, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and showy fruit, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
