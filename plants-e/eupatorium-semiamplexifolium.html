Eupatorium semiamplexifolium

Eupatorium semiamplexifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady woodlands and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.
