Esula juncoides

Esula juncoides, commonly known as the slender rush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Juncaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Esula juncoides is typically perennial and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
