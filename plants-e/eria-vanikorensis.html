Eria vanikorensis

Eria vanikorensis, commonly known as the Vanikoro orchid, is a rare and endemic species native to the island of Vanikoro in the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structure and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical rainforest environment. Its flowers are small but striking, often displaying a mix of white, yellow, and green hues.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat vulnerability, Eria vanikorensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
