Excoecaria guineensis

Excoecaria guineensis, commonly known as the African poisonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often reddish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain toxic seeds.

Its latex is known to be irritant and toxic to humans and animals, making it a plant of caution in its native habitat.
