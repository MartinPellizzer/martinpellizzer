Elettaria cardamomum var. major

Elettaria cardamomum var. major, commonly known as large cardamom, is a member of the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, herbaceous stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, dark reddish-brown pods containing aromatic seeds. These seeds are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their strong, spicy aroma.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
