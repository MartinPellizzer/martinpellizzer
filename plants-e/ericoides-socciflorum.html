Ericoides socciflorum

Ericoides socciflorum, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its flowers, which are typically pink or white, bloom in late spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides socciflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
