Euphoria foveolata

Euphoria foveolata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in early to mid-summer. Euphoria foveolata thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
