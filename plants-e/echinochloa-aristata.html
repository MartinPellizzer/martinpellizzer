Echinochloa aristata

Echinochloa aristata, commonly known as speargrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive pointed tip. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
