Eriosyce riparia

Eriosyce riparia, commonly known as the Desert Rose Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the stem tips. Eriosyce riparia is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water in its thick stems make it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
