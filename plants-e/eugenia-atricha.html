Eugenia atricha

Eugenia atricha, commonly known as the black eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and desserts. Eugenia atricha thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
