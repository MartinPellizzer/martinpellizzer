Erianthus giganteus

Erianthus giganteus, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of the Americas.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a fibrous, hollow stem. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist environments and is often found near rivers, lakes, and marshes.

Its robust growth makes it both an important ecological species and a potential invasive plant in non-native regions.
