Erythraea venusta

Erythraea venusta, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Erythraea venusta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
