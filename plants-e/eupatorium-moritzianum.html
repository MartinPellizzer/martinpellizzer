Eupatorium moritzianum

Eupatorium moritzianum, commonly known as Moritz's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eupatorium moritzianum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
