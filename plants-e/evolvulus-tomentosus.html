Evolvulus tomentosus

Evolvulus tomentosus, commonly known as the wooly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant features hairy, fuzzy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Evolvulus tomentosus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.
