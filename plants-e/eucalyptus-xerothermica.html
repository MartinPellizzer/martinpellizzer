Eucalyptus xerothermica

Eucalyptus xerothermica, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is a tall, fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that turns reddish-brown with age. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside.

The flowers are typically white or cream, and the plant produces small, woody capsules as fruit.
