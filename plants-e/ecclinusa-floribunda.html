Ecclinusa floribunda

Ecclinusa floribunda, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from yellow to orange in color, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are erect and often branched. Ecclinusa floribunda thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and can be propagated through seeds or division.
