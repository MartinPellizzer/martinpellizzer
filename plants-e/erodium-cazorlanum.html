Erodium cazorlanum

Erodium cazorlanum, commonly known as the California bird's-beak, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces distinctive, narrow, winged fruits that resemble a bird's beak, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Erodium cazorlanum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
