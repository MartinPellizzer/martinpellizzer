Eragrostis diandra

Eragrostis diandra, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name, and it typically grows in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis diandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
