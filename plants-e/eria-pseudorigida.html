Eria pseudorigida

Eria pseudorigida, commonly known as the rigid eria, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and the presence of long, slender pseudobulbs. The plant produces delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Eria pseudorigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
