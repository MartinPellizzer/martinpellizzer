Euonymus japonicus var. acutus

Euonymus japonicus var. acutus, commonly known as Japanese spindlewood, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, spherical fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and seasonal color changes in garden landscapes.
