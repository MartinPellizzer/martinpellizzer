Echium pomponium var. tangerinum

Echium pomponium var. tangerinum, commonly known as the orange thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to orange. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and a short, fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
