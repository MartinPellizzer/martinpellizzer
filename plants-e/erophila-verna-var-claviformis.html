Erophila verna var. claviformis

Erophila verna var. claviformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with succulent, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, adapted to arid or rocky environments. It blooms in early spring, producing white or pale pink flowers that attract pollinators.

This variety is characterized by its distinctive, club-shaped inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet.
