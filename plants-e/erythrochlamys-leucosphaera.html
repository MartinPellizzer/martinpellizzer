Erythrochlamys leucosphaera

Erythrochlamys leucosphaera, commonly known as the white globe bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, globe-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to botanical gardens.
