Eriobotrya grandiflora

Eriobotrya grandiflora, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces large, sweet, yellowish-white fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated edge.

Eriobotrya grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in both horticulture and culinary practices.
