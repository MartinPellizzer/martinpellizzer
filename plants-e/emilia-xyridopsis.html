Emilia xyridopsis

Emilia xyridopsis, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. The plant has a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Emilia xyridopsis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
