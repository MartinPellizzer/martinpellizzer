Echeveria brauntonii

Echeveria brauntonii, commonly known as the Braunton's echeveria, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
