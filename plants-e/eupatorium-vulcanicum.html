Eupatorium vulcanicum

Eupatorium vulcanicum, commonly known as the volcanic eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to volcanic regions in Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically blooms during the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium vulcanicum is adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in areas with high volcanic activity.
