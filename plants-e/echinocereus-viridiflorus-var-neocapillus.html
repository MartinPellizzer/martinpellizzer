Echinocereus viridiflorus var. neocapillus

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. neocapillus, commonly known as the greenflower hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
