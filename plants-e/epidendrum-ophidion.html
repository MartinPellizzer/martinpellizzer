Epidendrum ophidion

Epidendrum ophidion, commonly known as the snake orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically pale green to yellowish with a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a snake's head.

Epidendrum ophidion thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
