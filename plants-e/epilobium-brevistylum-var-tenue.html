Epilobium brevistylum var. tenue

Epilobium brevistylum var. tenue, commonly known as the narrowleaf willow herb, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
