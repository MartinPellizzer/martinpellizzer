Eucalyptus willisii subsp. falciformis

Eucalyptus willisii subsp.

falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hairs.

The flowers are typically white or cream and are arranged in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is part of the eucalyptus group known for its ecological importance in supporting native wildlife and stabilizing soil in its habitat.
