Euphorbia amygdaloides subsp. semiperfoliata

Euphorbia amygdaloides subsp.

semiperfoliata, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the northern United States and southern Canada. It features succulent, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its foliage and unique floral structure make it an attractive addition to native plant gardens.
