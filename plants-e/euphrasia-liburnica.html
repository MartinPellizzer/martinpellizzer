Euphrasia liburnica

Euphrasia liburnica, commonly known as the Balkan eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Europe and parts of Turkey.

It typically grows in grassy areas, woodland edges, and along riverbanks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers with two lobes, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its medicinal use in traditional herbal remedies.

Euphrasia liburnica is valued for its potential therapeutic properties, particularly in treating eye conditions and respiratory ailments.
