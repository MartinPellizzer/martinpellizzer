Eriobotrya serrata

Eriobotrya serrata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental white flowers and edible, sweet fruits. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and fruit production.

Its fruit, resembling a pear, is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
