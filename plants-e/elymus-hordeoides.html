Elymus hordeoides

Elymus hordeoides, commonly known as the barleygrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Elymus hordeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
