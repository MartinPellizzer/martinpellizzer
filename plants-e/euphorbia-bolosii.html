Euphorbia bolosii

Euphorbia bolosii, commonly known as the Bolosii spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, cylindrical stem covered in a thick layer of white, waxy bark that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in shades of yellow or green, which are surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called bracts. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
