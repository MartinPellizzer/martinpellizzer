Eucalyptus populifolia

Eucalyptus populifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Its bark is usually smooth and peels off in strips, revealing a mottled pattern beneath.

Eucalyptus populifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in regions with low rainfall.
