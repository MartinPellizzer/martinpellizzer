Eugenia capuli var. macroterantha

Eugenia capuli var. macroterantha, commonly known as the large-fruited eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits resembling small grapes. These fruits are edible, with a sweet and aromatic flavor, often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.
