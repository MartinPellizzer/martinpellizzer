Euphorbia griseola subsp. griseola

Euphorbia griseola subsp.

griseola, commonly known as the gray euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a distinctive, branching stem with a grayish-green color and small, rounded leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its slow growth and unique appearance make it a desirable ornamental plant.
