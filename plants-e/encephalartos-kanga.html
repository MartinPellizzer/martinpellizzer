Encephalartos kanga

Encephalartos kanga, commonly known as the Kanga cycad, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant with a thick, rugged trunk and a crown of feather-like leaves. The plant produces cone-like structures, with male and female cones on separate plants, typical of cycads. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
