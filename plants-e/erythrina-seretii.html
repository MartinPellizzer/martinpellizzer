Erythrina seretii

Erythrina seretii, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its pods contain several seeds surrounded by a fibrous pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
