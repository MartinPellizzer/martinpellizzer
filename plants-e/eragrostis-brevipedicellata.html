Eragrostis brevipedicellata

Eragrostis brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
