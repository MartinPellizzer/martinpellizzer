Elaeagnus hortensis subsp. songarica

Elaeagnus hortensis subsp.

songarica, commonly known as silver berry or Chinese silver bush, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia. It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible fruits that ripen in late summer.

The plant has a strong, aromatic scent and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry, rocky landscapes.

This subspecies is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in arid environments.
