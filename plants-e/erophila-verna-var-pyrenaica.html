Erophila verna var. pyrenaica

Erophila verna var. pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
