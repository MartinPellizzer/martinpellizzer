Epidendrum joaquin-ortizii

Epidendrum joaquin-ortizii, commonly known as the Joaquin-Ortiz Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and elongated, strap-like leaves. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
