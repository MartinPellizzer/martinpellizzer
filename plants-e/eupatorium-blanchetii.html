Eupatorium blanchetii

Eupatorium blanchetii, commonly known as the white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are white to pale blue and are arranged in clusters.

Eupatorium blanchetii is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
