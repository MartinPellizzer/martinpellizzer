Euonymus cornutus var. typicus

Euonymus cornutus var. typicus, commonly known as the horned euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-like buds on young shoots, which give it its common name. The plant has alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.
