Eugenia correae

Eugenia correae, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in winter, followed by edible, dark blue berries. It is often grown as an ornamental tree due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Eugenia correae is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
