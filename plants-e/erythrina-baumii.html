Erythrina baumii

Erythrina baumii, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, and its stems are often woody with thorn-like structures. Erythrina baumii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
