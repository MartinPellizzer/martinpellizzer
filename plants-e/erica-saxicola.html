Erica saxicola

Erica saxicola, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. Erica saxicola is well adapted to harsh environments, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
