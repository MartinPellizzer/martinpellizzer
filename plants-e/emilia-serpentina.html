Emilia serpentina

Emilia serpentina, commonly known as the "serpent's root," is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and clusters of small, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous tiny seeds. Emilia serpentina is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
