Eugenia cajalbanica

Eugenia cajalbanica, commonly known as the pink hibiscus mealybug plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, pinkish flowers and waxy, mealybug-like appearance. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are typically thick and glossy, with a slight waxy coating that helps it retain moisture.

Eugenia cajalbanica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
