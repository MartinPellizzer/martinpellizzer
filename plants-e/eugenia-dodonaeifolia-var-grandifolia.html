Eugenia dodonaeifolia var. grandifolia

Eugenia dodonaeifolia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-fruited myrtle, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth.
