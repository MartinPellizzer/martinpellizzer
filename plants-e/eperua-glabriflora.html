Eperua glabriflora

Eperua glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered eperua, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of northern Australia and parts of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
