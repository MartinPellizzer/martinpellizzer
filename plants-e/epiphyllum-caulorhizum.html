Epiphyllum caulorhizum

Epiphyllum caulorhizum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to Central America.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, and it produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

It is prized for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
