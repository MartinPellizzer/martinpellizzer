Eucalyptus laophila

Eucalyptus laophila, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and mallee regions. It is known for its aromatic foliage, which contains essential oils used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

Eucalyptus laophila plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
