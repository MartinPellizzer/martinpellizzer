Eriogonum hemipterum var. hemipterum

Eriogonum hemipterum var. hemipterum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, often pale yellow to greenish flowers. Its root system is adapted to survive in dry conditions, and it is drought-tolerant.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert wildlife.
