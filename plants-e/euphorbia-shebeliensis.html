Euphorbia shebeliensis

Euphorbia shebeliensis, commonly known as the Shebelien's spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often reduced to small, scale-like structures. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble flowers but are actually clusters of modified leaves and flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its stems and having a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
