Eugenia lateriflora

Eugenia lateriflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Eugenia lateriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
