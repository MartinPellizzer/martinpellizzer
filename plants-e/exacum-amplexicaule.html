Exacum amplexicaule

Exacum amplexicaule, commonly known as the bushy pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and the plant is well-suited for container gardening or as a ground cover.

Exacum amplexicaule is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
