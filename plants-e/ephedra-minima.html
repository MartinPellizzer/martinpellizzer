Ephedra minima

Ephedra minima, commonly known as the small ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Ephedra minima contains alkaloids, including ephedrine, which has historically been used in traditional medicine.

Its structure and chemical composition make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
