Equisetum palustre f. fallax

Equisetum palustre f. fallax, commonly known as the marsh horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. This plant thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in damp soils along stream banks. It belongs to the family Equisetaceae and is known for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.

The plant lacks true leaves and instead has sterile and fertile stems that produce spores.
