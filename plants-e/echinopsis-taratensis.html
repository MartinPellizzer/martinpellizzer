Echinopsis taratensis

Echinopsis taratensis, commonly known as the Taratensis cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Andes Mountains of southern Peru.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its spines provide protection from herbivores and help shade the stem from intense sunlight.

Echinopsis taratensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
