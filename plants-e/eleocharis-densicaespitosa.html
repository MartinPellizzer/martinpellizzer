Eleocharis densicaespitosa

Eleocharis densicaespitosa, commonly known as the dense spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of its flowering stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a wiry texture, and it lacks true leaves in the traditional sense.

Eleocharis densicaespitosa plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
