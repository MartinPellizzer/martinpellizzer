Entada gigas subsp. umbonata

Entada gigas subsp.

umbonata, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The tree produces long, pod-like fruits containing hard, chestnut-like seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable botanical species.
