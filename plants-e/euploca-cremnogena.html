Euploca cremnogena

Euploca cremnogena, commonly known as the coastal euploca, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale blue flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing on rocky shores and sandy soils. Euploca cremnogena is adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
