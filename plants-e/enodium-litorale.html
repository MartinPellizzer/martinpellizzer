Enodium litorale

Enodium litorale, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the blooms. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are upright and sturdy.

Enodium litorale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
