Eragrostis pilosa var. glabra

Eragrostis pilosa var. glabra, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless blades that are often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to green flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its dense, attractive foliage.
