Eria aporina

Eria aporina, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Due to its habitat specificity and vulnerability to environmental changes, Eria aporina is considered a conservation concern.
