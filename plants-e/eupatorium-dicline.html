Eupatorium dicline

Eupatorium dicline, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded areas.

However, it is toxic to livestock and can cause severe illness if ingested.
