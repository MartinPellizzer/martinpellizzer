Euphorbia incurva

Euphorbia incurva, commonly known as the zigzag spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted stems that give it its common name. The plant has small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Euphorbia incurva is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
