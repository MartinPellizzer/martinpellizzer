Euphorbia mahafalensis var. xanthadenia

Euphorbia mahafalensis var. xanthadenia, commonly known as the Mahafaly euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and unique floral structures. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid or semi-arid conditions, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in dry gardens.
