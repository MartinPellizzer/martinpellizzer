Elymus glabriflorus var. australis

Elymus glabriflorus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Australia.

It features slender, tufted stems with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing sandy or rocky soils.
