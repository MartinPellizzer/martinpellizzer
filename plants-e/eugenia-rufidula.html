Eugenia rufidula

Eugenia rufidula, commonly known as the red-fruited eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is notable for its edible red berries, which are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Eugenia rufidula thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
