Eremophila caerulea

Eremophila caerulea, commonly known as the blue eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower spikes. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eremophila caerulea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
