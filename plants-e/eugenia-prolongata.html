Eugenia prolongata

Eugenia prolongata, commonly known as the long-fruited eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which is a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Eugenia prolongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
