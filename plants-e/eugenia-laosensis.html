Eugenia laosensis

Eugenia laosensis, commonly known as the Laos guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Laos and Vietnam.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when ripe. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
