Epilobium × knafii

Epilobium × knafii, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, often branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, riverbanks, and along woodland edges.

Epilobium × knafii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators and wildlife.
