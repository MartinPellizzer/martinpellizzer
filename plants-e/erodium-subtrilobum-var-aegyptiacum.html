Erodium subtrilobum var. aegyptiacum

Erodium subtrilobum var. aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian stormweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in arid environments.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
