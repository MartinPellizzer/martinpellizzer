Eupatorium metense

Eupatorium metense, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Eupatorium metense is often found in shaded to partially sunny environments and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
