Eugeniopsis ovata

Eugeniopsis ovata, commonly known as the oval eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
