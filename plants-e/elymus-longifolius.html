Elymus longifolius

Elymus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Elymus longifolius is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
