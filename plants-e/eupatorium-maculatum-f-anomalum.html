Eupatorium maculatum f. anomalum

Eupatorium maculatum f. anomalum, commonly known as the purple stem Joe-Pye weed, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and showy, fluffy purple-pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
