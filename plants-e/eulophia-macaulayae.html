Eulophia macaulayae

Eulophia macaulayae, commonly known as the Macaulay's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the wet tropical forests of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Eulophia macaulayae is a rare and endemic species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
