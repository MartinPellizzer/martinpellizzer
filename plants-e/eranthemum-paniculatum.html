Eranthemum paniculatum

Eranthemum paniculatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and drought tolerance.
