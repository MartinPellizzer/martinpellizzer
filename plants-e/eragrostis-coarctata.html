Eragrostis coarctata

Eragrostis coarctata, commonly known as narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis coarctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
