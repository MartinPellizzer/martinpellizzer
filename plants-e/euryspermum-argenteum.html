Euryspermum argenteum

Euryspermum argenteum, commonly known as silverleaf fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its drought tolerance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and erosion control.
