Evolvulus discolor

Evolvulus discolor, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Evolvulus discolor is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering vines and herbs.
