Eupatorium lanigerum var. aureoviride

Eupatorium lanigerum var. aureoviride, commonly known as golden green eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
