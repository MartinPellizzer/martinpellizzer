Elaeocarpus kasiensis

Elaeocarpus kasiensis, commonly known as the Kasi elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
