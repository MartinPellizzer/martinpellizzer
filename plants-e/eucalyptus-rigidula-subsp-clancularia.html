Eucalyptus rigidula subsp. clancularia

Eucalyptus rigidula subsp.

clancularia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet gum, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually pale or creamy white in color. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

It is well adapted to the dry, sandy soils of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and inland regions.
