Erythroxylum fiscalense

Erythroxylum fiscalense, commonly known as the Fiscal's erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
