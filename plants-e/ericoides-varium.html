Ericoides varium

Ericoides varium, commonly known as the Variable Heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. This plant is typically found in acidic, well-drained soils in woodland or heathland environments. Ericoides varium thrives in cool, moist climates and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense, compact form.
