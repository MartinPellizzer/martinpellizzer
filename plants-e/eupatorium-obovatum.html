Eupatorium obovatum

Eupatorium obovatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium obovatum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
