Echeveria eastwoodiae

Echeveria eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant violet or purple flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
