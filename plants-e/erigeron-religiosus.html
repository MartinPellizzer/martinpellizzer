Erigeron religiosus

Erigeron religiosus, commonly known as the sacred fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, featuring white to pale blue petals and yellow centers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Erigeron religiosus is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
