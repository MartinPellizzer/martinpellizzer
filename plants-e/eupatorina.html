Eupatorina

Eupatorina, commonly known as false blueweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Eupatorina is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
