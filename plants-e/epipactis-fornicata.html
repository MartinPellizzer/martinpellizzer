Epipactis fornicata

Epipactis fornicata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Epipactis fornicata is often found in association with other orchids and is a valuable component of diverse forest ecosystems.
