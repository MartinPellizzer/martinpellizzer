Eriogonum affine

Eriogonum affine, commonly known as the stickyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Eriogonum affine is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
