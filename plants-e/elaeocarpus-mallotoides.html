Elaeocarpus mallotoides

Elaeocarpus mallotoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by various bird species.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.
