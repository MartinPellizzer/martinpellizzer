Episcia resioides

Episcia resioides, commonly known as the velvet plant, is a tropical species native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Episcia resioides is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
