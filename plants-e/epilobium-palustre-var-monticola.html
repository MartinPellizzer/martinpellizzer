Epilobium palustre var. monticola

Epilobium palustre var. monticola, commonly known as mountain willow or swamp willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and mountainous areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glandular underside and produces showy, pink to red flowers in terminal clusters. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in cool, humid environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
