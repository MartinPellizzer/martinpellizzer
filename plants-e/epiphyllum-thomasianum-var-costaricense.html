Epiphyllum thomasianum var. costaricense

Epiphyllum thomasianum var. costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican moon cactus, is a hybrid epiphytic cactus native to Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that resemble leaves, which are often brightly colored with vibrant pink, red, or purple hues. The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, opening in the evening. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires indirect sunlight to prevent sunburn.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
