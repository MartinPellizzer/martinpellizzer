Erica woodii

Erica woodii, commonly known as the woodland heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink.

Erica woodii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.
