Ericameria parishii var. peninsularis

Ericameria parishii var. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular goldenbush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California and the Baja California Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with moderate rainfall. It is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for local pollinators.
