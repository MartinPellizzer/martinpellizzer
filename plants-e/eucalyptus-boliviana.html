Eucalyptus boliviana

Eucalyptus boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian gum tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that bloom in shades of white, pink, or red. It is well adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Eucalyptus boliviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
