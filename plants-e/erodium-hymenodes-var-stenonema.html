Erodium hymenodes var. stenonema

Erodium hymenodes var. stenonema, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its flowers are borne on elongated pedicels and are adapted for wind pollination.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and is characterized by its delicate, herbaceous appearance.
