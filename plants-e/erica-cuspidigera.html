Erica cuspidigera

Erica cuspidigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This evergreen shrub typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
