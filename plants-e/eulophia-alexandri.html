Eulophia alexandri

Eulophia alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulbous structure with a single leaf and a raceme of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eulophia alexandri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
