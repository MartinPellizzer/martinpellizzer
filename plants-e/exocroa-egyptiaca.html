Exocroa egyptiaca

Exocroa egyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant.

Exocroa egyptiaca is also known for its latex-like sap, which can be irritating to the skin.
