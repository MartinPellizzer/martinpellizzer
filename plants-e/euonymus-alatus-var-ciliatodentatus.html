Euonymus alatus var. ciliatodentatus

Euonymus alatus var. ciliatodentatus, commonly known as the silky sandpaper plant, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and hairy veins. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by red, inflated fruits that split open to release seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
