Echinodorus paniculatus var. dubius

Echinodorus paniculatus var. dubius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that emerge above the water surface. It thrives in slow to moderate flowing freshwater environments with good light exposure.

Echinodorus paniculatus var. dubius is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
