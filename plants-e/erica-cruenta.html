Erica cruenta

Erica cruenta, commonly known as the crimson heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces dense clusters of bright red flowers during the summer months, attracting various pollinators. Its evergreen foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that provide year-round structure.

Erica cruenta is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic conditions.
