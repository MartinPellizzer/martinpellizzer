Eragrostis floridana

Eragrostis floridana, commonly known as Florida lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in open sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and disturbed areas. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in coastal environments.
