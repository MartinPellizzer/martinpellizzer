Erica × serratifolia var. subnuda

Erica × serratifolia var. subnuda, commonly known as the naked-leaved heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are often glabrous or nearly so. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Erica × serratifolia var. subnuda is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers.
