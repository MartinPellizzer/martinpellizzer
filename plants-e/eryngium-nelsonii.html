Eryngium nelsonii

Eryngium nelsonii, commonly known as the blueweed or blue thistle, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flower heads are composed of small, tubular florets surrounded by a series of spiny bracts.

Eryngium nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
