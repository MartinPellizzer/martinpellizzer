Empogona macrophylla

Empogona macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leafed elder, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by black, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and riverbanks.
