Echinopsis formosa var. albispina

Echinopsis formosa var. albispina, commonly known as the White-spined Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny tubercles. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens and collections.
