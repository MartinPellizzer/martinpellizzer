Echidiocarya ursina

Echidiocarya ursina, commonly known as the bear's ear plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, rounded leaves that resemble bear ears, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
