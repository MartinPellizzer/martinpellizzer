Erythrocephalum plantaginifolium

Erythrocephalum plantaginifolium, commonly known as the redhead or redhead daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and heathlands.

Erythrocephalum plantaginifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
