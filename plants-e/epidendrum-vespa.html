Epidendrum vespa

Epidendrum vespa, commonly known as the wasp orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces pendulous flower spikes that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers resembling wasps, hence its common name. Its flowers are typically yellow with green and brown markings, and they bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Epidendrum vespa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
