Euphorbia eleanoriae

Euphorbia eleanoriae, commonly known as the Eleanoria's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, branching stem covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, typical of many euphorbias.

Euphorbia eleanoriae is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
