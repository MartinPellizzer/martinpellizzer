Euphorbia ventosica

Euphorbia ventosica, commonly known as the windplant, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures, rather than traditional flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Euphorbia ventosica is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
