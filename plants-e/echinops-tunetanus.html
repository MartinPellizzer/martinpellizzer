Echinops tunetanus

Echinops tunetanus, commonly known as the Tunisian thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Echinops tunetanus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
