Eupatorium perfoliatum f. purpureum

Eupatorium perfoliatum f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, typically found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
