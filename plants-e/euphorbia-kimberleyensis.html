Euphorbia kimberleyensis

Euphorbia kimberleyensis, commonly known as Kimberley spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
