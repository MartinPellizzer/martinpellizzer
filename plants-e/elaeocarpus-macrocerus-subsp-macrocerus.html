Elaeocarpus macrocerus subsp. macrocerus

Elaeocarpus macrocerus subsp.

macrocerus, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The fruit is a woody, fleshy drupe with a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
