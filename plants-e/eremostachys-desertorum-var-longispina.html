Eremostachys desertorum var. longispina

Eremostachys desertorum var. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined desert eryngo, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and sparsely branched stems that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish to greenish flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme heat and drought conditions.

This species is often found in rocky soils and along dry slopes, contributing to the ecological diversity of desert ecosystems.
