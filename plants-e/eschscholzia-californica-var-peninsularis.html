Eschscholzia californica var. peninsularis

Eschscholzia californica var. peninsularis, commonly known as the Peninsular poppy, is a subspecies native to the Peninsular Ranges of southern California and Baja California.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its striking red to orange flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also recognized for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
