Eugenia geraensis

Eugenia geraensis, commonly known as the Geraensian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
