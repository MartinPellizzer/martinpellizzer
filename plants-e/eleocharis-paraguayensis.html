Eleocharis paraguayensis

Eleocharis paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. It is adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Eleocharis paraguayensis is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its purported health benefits.
