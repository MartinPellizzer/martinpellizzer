Escobaria fobei

Escobaria fobei, commonly known as the Fobe's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water in its native desert environment. Escobaria fobei produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or early summer.

It is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
