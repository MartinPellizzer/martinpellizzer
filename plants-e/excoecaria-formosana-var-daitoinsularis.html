Excoecaria formosana var. daitoinsularis

Excoecaria formosana var. daitoinsularis, commonly known as the Daito Island croton, is a shrub native to Japan.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with reddish or yellowish veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity should be noted.
