Erythronium longifolium

Erythronium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved dog's mercury, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features distinctive, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or purple hues. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a prominent green throat. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in woodland ecosystems.
