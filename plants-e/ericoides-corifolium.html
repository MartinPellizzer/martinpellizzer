Ericoides corifolium

Ericoides corifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas. It is adapted to cool, moist climates and can thrive in shaded environments.

Ericoides corifolium plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
