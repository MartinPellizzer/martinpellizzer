Erica euryphylla

Erica euryphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Erica euryphylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
