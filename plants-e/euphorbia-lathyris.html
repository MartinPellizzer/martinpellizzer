Euphorbia lathyris

Euphorbia lathyris, commonly known as the common spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Euphorbia lathyris is an annual or biennial plant that thrives in temperate climates.
