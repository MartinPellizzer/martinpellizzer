Eugenia erythrocarpa

Eugenia erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales, where it grows in subtropical rainforests and moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that ripens in late summer, attracting various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
