Eupatorium camachense

Eupatorium camachense, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Eupatorium camachense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
