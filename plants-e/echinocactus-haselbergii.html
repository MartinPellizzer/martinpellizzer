Echinocactus haselbergii

Echinocactus haselbergii, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange spines and a globular, rounded shape. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
