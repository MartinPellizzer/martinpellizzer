Eragrostis neesii var. lindmanii

Eragrostis neesii var. lindmanii, commonly known as Lindman's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
