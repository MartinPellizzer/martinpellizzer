Eulophia baginsensis

Eulophia baginsensis, commonly known as the Bagins' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces a single spike bearing several pale yellow to greenish-white flowers with intricate patterns. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, often with a waxy texture.

Eulophia baginsensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
