Erica glauca var. glauca

Erica glauca var. glauca, commonly known as heath or dwarf heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in spring and early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is well adapted to coastal climates with salt spray.

Erica glauca var. glauca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
