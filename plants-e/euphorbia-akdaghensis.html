Euphorbia akdaghensis

Euphorbia akdaghensis, commonly known as the Akdagh euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Euphorbia akdaghensis is well adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
