Esenbeckia febrifuga var. fruticosa

Esenbeckia febrifuga var. fruticosa, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the spring and summer. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used for its fever-reducing effects.
