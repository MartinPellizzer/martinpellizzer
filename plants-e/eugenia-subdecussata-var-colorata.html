Eugenia subdecussata var. colorata

Eugenia subdecussata var. colorata, commonly known as the variegated myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its compact growth habit and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
