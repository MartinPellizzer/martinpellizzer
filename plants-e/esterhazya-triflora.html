Esterhazya triflora

Esterhazya triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered esterhazya, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters of three, giving it its scientific name. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens despite its limited distribution in the wild.
