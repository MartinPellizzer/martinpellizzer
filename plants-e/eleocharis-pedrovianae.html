Eleocharis pedrovianae

Eleocharis pedrovianae, commonly known as the Pedrovan's spike rush, is a species of sedge native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with high humidity. Its leaves are narrow and cylindrical, often with a slight sheen, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis pedrovianae plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
