Euphorbia victoriensis

Euphorbia victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
