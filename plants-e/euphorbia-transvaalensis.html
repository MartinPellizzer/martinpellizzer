Euphorbia transvaalensis

Euphorbia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a crown of fleshy, spiny leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia transvaalensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
