Eugenia dumicola

Eugenia dumicola, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or dwarf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia dumicola thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
