Eugenia tenella var. spathulata

Eugenia tenella var. spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed guava, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features small, elliptical to ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is often cultivated in gardens and tropical landscapes.
