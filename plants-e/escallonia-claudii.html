Escallonia claudii

Escallonia claudii, commonly known as the Claudius's escallonia, is a shrub native to South America, specifically Chile.

It belongs to the family Escalloniaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds but generally unpalatable to humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant flowers and attractive, glossy leaves.
