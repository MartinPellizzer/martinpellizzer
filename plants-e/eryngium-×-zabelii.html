Eryngium × zabelii

Eryngium × zabelii, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spiny bracts that surround the small yellow flowers. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

Eryngium × zabelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
