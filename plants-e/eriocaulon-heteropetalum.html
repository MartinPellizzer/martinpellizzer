Eriocaulon heteropetalum

Eriocaulon heteropetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a member of the Poales order and the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of green or brown, and are pollinated by wind.
