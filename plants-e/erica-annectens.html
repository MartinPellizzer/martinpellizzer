Erica annectens

Erica annectens, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica annectens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and year-round visual interest.
