Endocoma peruviana

Endocoma peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian endocoma, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti, and is characterized by its succulent stems and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
