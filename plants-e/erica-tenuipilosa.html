Erica tenuipilosa

Erica tenuipilosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Erica tenuipilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
