Echinops abazariae

Echinops abazariae, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Echinops abazariae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and provides food for local wildlife.
