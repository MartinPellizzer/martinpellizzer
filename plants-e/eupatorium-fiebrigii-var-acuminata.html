Eupatorium fiebrigii var. acuminata

Eupatorium fiebrigii var. acuminata, commonly known as feverfew, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a serrated edge, with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
