Ehretia dichotoma

Ehretia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched euphorbia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
