Eriosorus schwackeanus

Eriosorus schwackeanus, commonly known as the Schwacke's fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that are often covered in fine, hair-like scales. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a complex arrangement of pinnae and pinnules that contribute to its ornamental appearance.

Eriosorus schwackeanus is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
