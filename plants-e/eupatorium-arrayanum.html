Eupatorium arrayanum

Eupatorium arrayanum, commonly known as Indian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as riverbanks and marshy areas. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their antipyretic and analgesic properties.

Eupatorium arrayanum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
