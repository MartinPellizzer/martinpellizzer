Euclea undulata var. myrtina

Euclea undulata var. myrtina, commonly known as the red-stemmed wild pear, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or purple stems, which are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
