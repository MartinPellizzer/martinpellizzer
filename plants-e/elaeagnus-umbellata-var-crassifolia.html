Elaeagnus umbellata var. crassifolia

Elaeagnus umbellata var. crassifolia, commonly known as sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with 5 to 9 pairs of elliptic leaflets, which are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in racemes, followed by edible, red-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate foliage and fruit.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
