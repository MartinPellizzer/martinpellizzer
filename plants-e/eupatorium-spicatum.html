Eupatorium spicatum

Eupatorium spicatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that are covered in fine hairs and bear clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often dotted with small glands. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
