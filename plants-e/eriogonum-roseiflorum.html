Eriogonum roseiflorum

Eriogonum roseiflorum, commonly known as the roseflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum roseiflorum is often found in California and is an important component of local ecosystems.
