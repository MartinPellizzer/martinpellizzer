Eugenia jambosa

Eugenia jambosa, commonly known as the Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. Eugenia jambosa thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
