Eupatorium asperulaceum

Eupatorium asperulaceum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its sturdy, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
