Eremogone congesta var. prolifera

Eremogone congesta var. prolifera, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and large, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, demonstrating high drought tolerance. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

This variety is known for its ability to produce multiple shoots, contributing to its widespread distribution in desert environments.
