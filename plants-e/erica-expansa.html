Erica expansa

Erica expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf heath, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and early summer. Erica expansa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and as a landscape plant.
