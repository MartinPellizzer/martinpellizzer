Erigeron × trachselii

Erigeron × trachselii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family, characterized by its daisy-like flowers and silvery-blue foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, featuring showy white to pale blue ray flowers surrounding yellow disk flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
