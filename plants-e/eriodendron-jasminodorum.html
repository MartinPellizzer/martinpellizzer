Eriodendron jasminodorum

Eriodendron jasminodorum, commonly known as the jasmine wood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that resemble jasmine, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.
