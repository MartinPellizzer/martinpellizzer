Erica pygmaea

Erica pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many species of heathers and related plants. The plant features dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Erica pygmaea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
