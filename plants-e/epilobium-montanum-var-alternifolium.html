Epilobium montanum var. alternifolium

Epilobium montanum var. alternifolium, commonly known as the mountain willow or alpine willow, is a herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate leaves that are typically elliptic to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are pink to reddish, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
