Enneapogon persicus

Enneapogon persicus, commonly known as Persian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

Enneapogon persicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for livestock.
