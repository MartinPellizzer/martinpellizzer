Elaeagnus pungens f. aurea

Elaeagnus pungens f. aurea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, edible berries that are dark red to black in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and hardy nature.

Elaeagnus pungens f. aurea is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to drought.
