Ericameria parryi var. salmonensis

Ericameria parryi var. salmonensis, commonly known as the salmonbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellow to golden-yellow inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of whitish hair.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
