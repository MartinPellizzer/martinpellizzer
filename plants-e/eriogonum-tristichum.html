Eriogonum tristichum

Eriogonum tristichum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum tristichum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
