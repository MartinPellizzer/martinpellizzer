Eugenia pseudoclaviflora

Eugenia pseudoclaviflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia pseudoclaviflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid forest environments.
