Evolvulus sericeus subsp. lanuginosus

Evolvulus sericeus subsp.

lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a notable species within the Convolvulaceae family.
