Epidendrum bryophilum

Epidendrum bryophilum, commonly known as the mossy epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees in humid environments. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of moss-like structures. Its flowers are typically small, fragrant, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Epidendrum bryophilum is valued for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in diverse tropical habitats.
