Erythrodes bifalcis

Erythrodes bifalcis, commonly known as the red-veined twinleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have a striking red venation pattern against a green background. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
