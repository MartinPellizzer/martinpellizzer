Erica schelpeorum

Erica schelpeorum, commonly known as the Schelpe's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica schelpeorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
