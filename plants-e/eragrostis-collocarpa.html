Eragrostis collocarpa

Eragrostis collocarpa, commonly known as the clustered false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tufted growth habit.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
