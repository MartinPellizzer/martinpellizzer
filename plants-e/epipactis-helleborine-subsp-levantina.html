Epipactis helleborine subsp. levantina

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

levantina, commonly known as the Levantine helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a showy, greenish-yellow flower spike that bears numerous small, spurred flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces a single, erect stem that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively long flowering period, which extends from spring to early summer.
