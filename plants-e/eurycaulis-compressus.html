Eurycaulis compressus

Eurycaulis compressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compressed, cylindrical stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Eurycaulis compressus is notable for its ability to absorb nutrients through its leaves, a feature common among members of the Bladderwort family.
