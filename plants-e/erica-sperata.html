Erica sperata

Erica sperata, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, creating a delicate, snow-like appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica sperata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
