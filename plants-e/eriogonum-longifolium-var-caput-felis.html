Eriogonum longifolium var. caput-felis

Eriogonum longifolium var. caput-felis, commonly known as the cat's foot buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, woolly leaves and a flowering stem that produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and coastal scrub habitats. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed structures called achenes.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
