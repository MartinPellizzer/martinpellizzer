Eriogonum depressum

Eriogonum depressum, commonly known as the low flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Eriogonum depressum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
