Epidendrum woytkowskianum

Epidendrum woytkowskianum, commonly known as the Woytkowski's epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with vibrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Epidendrum woytkowskianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
