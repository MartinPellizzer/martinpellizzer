Erica porteri

Erica porteri, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions. Erica porteri is often used in ornamental gardening for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
