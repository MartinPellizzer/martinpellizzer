Encyclia trachypus

Encyclia trachypus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia trachypus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
