Elsholtzia ianthina

Elsholtzia ianthina, commonly known as blue-flowered elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Elsholtzia ianthina is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions.
