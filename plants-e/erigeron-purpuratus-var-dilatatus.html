Erigeron purpuratus var. dilatatus

Erigeron purpuratus var. dilatatus, commonly known as the Purple Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features blue to purple ray flowers surrounding a yellow disk, with hairy, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
