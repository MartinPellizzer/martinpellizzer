Eustachys argentina

Eustachys argentina, commonly known as the Argentine grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Eustachys argentina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
