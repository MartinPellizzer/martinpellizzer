Evolvulus altissimus

Evolvulus altissimus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners and ecological restoration projects.
