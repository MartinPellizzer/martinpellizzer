Echinocereus albiflorus

Echinocereus albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, typically growing up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
