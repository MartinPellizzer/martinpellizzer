Emilia pseudactis var. minor

Emilia pseudactis var. minor, commonly known as the lesser emilia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
