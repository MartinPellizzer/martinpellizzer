Eulophiopsis concolor

Eulophiopsis concolor, commonly known as the smooth-surfaced fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, lance-shaped fronds that are typically leathery in texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. Eulophiopsis concolor thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on tree trunks or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
