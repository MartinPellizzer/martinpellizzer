Elaeagnus multiflora subsp. yoshinoi

Elaeagnus multiflora subsp.

yoshinoi, commonly known as Yoshino elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan. It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in catkins. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it useful for erosion control and as a hedge.
