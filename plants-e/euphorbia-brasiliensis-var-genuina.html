Euphorbia brasiliensis var. genuina

Euphorbia brasiliensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features thick, ribbed stems and produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures. The plant has a dense, spiny foliage that helps it retain water in arid conditions. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a striking addition to xeriscaped gardens.
