Eragrostis lehmannii

Eragrostis lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eragrostis lehmannii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
