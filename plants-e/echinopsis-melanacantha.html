Echinopsis melanacantha

Echinopsis melanacantha, commonly known as the black spiny cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinopsis melanacantha is a popular choice for collectors due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
