Eupatorium breviflorum

Eupatorium breviflorum, commonly known as narrowflower thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic odor.

Eupatorium breviflorum is often found in open areas such as meadows and along stream banks.
