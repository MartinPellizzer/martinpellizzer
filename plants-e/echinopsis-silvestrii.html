Echinopsis silvestrii

Echinopsis silvestrii, commonly known as the Silvestri's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and a dense covering of white spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
