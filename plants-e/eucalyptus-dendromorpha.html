Eucalyptus dendromorpha

Eucalyptus dendromorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often forming dense shrubs or low trees.

Eucalyptus dendromorpha is well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat, with mechanisms to conserve water and withstand drought.
