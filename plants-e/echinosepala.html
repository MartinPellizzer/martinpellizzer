Echinosepala

Echinosepala, commonly known as the spiny-sepal orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiny sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and often have a fragrant scent, which helps attract pollinators.

Echinosepala is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
