Erythrina amazonica

Erythrina amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
