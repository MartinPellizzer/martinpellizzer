Escobaria strobiliformis

Escobaria strobiliformis, commonly known as the crested sandmat, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its desert environment. Escobaria strobiliformis is well adapted to arid conditions, storing water in its fleshy leaves and stems.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
