Erysimum sylvestre subsp. cheiranthus

Erysimum sylvestre subsp.

cheiranthus, commonly known as the woodland wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring.

The flowers are typically arranged in racemes and have four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, often forming dense colonies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
