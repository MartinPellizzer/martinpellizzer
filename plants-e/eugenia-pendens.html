Eugenia pendens

Eugenia pendens, commonly known as the hanging eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences, which consist of small, fragrant white flowers. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blooms.

Eugenia pendens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
