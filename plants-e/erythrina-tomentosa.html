Erythrina tomentosa

Erythrina tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet bean or flame acacia, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive red, flame-like flowers.

It is native to regions of Central and South America, as well as parts of Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are used in various traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
