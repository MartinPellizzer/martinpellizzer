Euphorbia epicyparissias var. wahlbergii

Euphorbia epicyparissias var. wahlbergii, commonly known as the Wahlberg's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Euphorbia epicyparissias var. wahlbergii is drought-tolerant and requires minimal care, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
