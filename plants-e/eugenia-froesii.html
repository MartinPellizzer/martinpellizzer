Eugenia froesii

Eugenia froesii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Eugenia froesii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
