Ebenus pinnata

Ebenus pinnata, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

The genus Ebenus is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology and its association with specific ecological habitats.
