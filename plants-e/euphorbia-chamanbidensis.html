Euphorbia chamanbidensis

Euphorbia chamanbidensis, commonly known as the Chamanbid Desert Spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive, milky sap, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are usually narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Euphorbia chamanbidensis is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, desert-like conditions.
