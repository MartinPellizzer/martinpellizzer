Eugenia strigosa

Eugenia strigosa, commonly known as the rough eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
