Erythroxylum deciduum var. opacum

Erythroxylum deciduum var. opacum, commonly known as the opaque leaf copal tree, is a species native to parts of South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Bolivia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often covered with a waxy coating, giving them a dull or opaque appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
