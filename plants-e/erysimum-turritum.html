Erysimum turritum

Erysimum turritum, commonly known as the turbaned wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are hairy and slightly lobed, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Erysimum turritum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.
