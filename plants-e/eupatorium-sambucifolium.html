Eupatorium sambucifolium

Eupatorium sambucifolium, commonly known as the sweet fern or false elderberry, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, appearing in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The genus Eupatorium is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and the species is notable for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
