Eugenia tenuifolia

Eugenia tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which are followed by small, round berries. These berries are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Eugenia tenuifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
