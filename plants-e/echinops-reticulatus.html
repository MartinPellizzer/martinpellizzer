Echinops reticulatus

Echinops reticulatus, commonly known as the Persian crownflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, which are covered in dense, white hairs. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of its distinctive seed heads. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Echinops reticulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
