Eurya japonica var. nitida

Eurya japonica var. nitida, commonly known as Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and densely packed, giving it a neat, compact appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its year-round foliage and winter interest.

Eurya japonica var. nitida thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.
