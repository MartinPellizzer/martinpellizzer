Epilobium gillotii

Epilobium gillotii, commonly known as Gillot's willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and coastal areas. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, textured surface. It produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
