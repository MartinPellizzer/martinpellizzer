Exacum rosulatum

Exacum rosulatum, commonly known as the Rosulate African Violet, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are typically velvety and dark green, often with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
