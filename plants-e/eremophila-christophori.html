Eremophila christophori

Eremophila christophori, commonly known as the Christmas bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and clusters of tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn and winter months, producing vibrant red or pink flowers.

Eremophila christophori is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
