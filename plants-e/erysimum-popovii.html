Erysimum popovii

Erysimum popovii, commonly known as the Popov's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or arid environments. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Erysimum popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
