Etlingera heliconiifolia subsp. leucocheilos

Etlingera heliconiifolia subsp.

leucocheilos, commonly known as the white-lipped ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle pinkish hue.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green, providing a lush backdrop to its showy blooms. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
