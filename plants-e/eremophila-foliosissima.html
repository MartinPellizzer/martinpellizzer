Eremophila foliosissima

Eremophila foliosissima, commonly known as the Woolly Everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, pink or purple flower spikes. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry climates. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eremophila foliosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and landscaping.
