Erythrina folkersii

Erythrina folkersii, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its woody structure supports its role as a decorative and ornamental species. Erythrina folkersii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and public spaces.

Its rapid growth and ability to tolerate urban environments make it a popular choice for landscaping.
