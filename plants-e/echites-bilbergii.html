Echites bilbergii

Echites bilbergii, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its milky sap, which is toxic, is a characteristic feature of the genus Echites.

The plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
