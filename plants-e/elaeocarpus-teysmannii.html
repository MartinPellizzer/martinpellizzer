Elaeocarpus teysmannii

Elaeocarpus teysmannii, commonly known as the Bornean elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitats.
