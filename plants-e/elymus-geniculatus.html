Elymus geniculatus

Elymus geniculatus, commonly known as the bentgrass or nodding goatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often nodding inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Elymus geniculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
