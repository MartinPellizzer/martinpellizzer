Eleocharis flaccida var. capitata

Eleocharis flaccida var. capitata, commonly known as the clustered spikerush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that bears numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, three-lobed berries.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
