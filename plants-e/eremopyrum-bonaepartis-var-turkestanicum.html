Eremopyrum bonaepartis var. turkestanicum

Eremopyrum bonaepartis var. turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan wild wheat, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and dry grasslands.

Its seeds are adapted for wind dispersal, contributing to its widespread distribution across the region.
