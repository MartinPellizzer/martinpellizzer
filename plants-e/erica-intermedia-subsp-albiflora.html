Erica intermedia subsp. albiflora

Erica intermedia subsp.

albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
