Eriopexis subpetiolata

Eriopexis subpetiolata, commonly known as the velvet leaf, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that are often borne on short pedicels. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
