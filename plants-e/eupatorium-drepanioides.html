Eupatorium drepanioides

Eupatorium drepanioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called cymes. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Eupatorium drepanioides is also known for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
