Ehretia alba

Ehretia alba, commonly known as white flame tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
