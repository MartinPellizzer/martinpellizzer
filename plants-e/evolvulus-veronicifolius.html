Evolvulus veronicifolius

Evolvulus veronicifolius, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stems. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
