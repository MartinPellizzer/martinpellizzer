Eriachne benthamii

Eriachne benthamii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands.

Eriachne benthamii is an important component of native vegetation, contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
