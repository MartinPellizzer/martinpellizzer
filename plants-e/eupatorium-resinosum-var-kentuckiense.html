Eupatorium resinosum var. kentuckiense

Eupatorium resinosum var. kentuckiense, commonly known as Kentucky boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and produces a sticky resin, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
