Eriocephalus xerophilus

Eriocephalus xerophilus, commonly known as the Cape goldcup, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent leaves that help store water. It produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.

Eriocephalus xerophilus is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
