Euphorbia desmondii

Euphorbia desmondii, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, reducing water loss.

Euphorbia desmondii is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
