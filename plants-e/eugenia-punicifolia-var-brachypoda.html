Eugenia punicifolia var. brachypoda

Eugenia punicifolia var. brachypoda, commonly known as the dwarf pomegranate, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits similar to pomegranates, though they are typically smaller and less acidic. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruits in its native habitat.
