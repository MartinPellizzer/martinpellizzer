Erigeron hyssopifolius

Erigeron hyssopifolius, commonly known as blueweed or narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
