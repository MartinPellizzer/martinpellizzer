Eragrostis inconstans var. rufescens

Eragrostis inconstans var. rufescens, commonly known as redstem bulrush, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant is characterized by its reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
