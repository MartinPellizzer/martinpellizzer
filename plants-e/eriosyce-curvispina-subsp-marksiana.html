Eriosyce curvispina subsp. marksiana

Eriosyce curvispina subsp.

marksiana, commonly known as the Marks' hairy cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to Argentina. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that provide insulation and protection against intense sunlight and moisture loss.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its fuzzy appearance. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
