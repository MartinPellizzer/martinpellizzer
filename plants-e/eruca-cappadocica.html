Eruca cappadocica

Eruca cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian rocket, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like broccoli and mustard. The plant features basal leaves that are elongated and hairy, with a peppery, mustard-like flavor. It produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by round, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Eruca cappadocica is often cultivated as a leafy green vegetable and is valued for its nutritional content and culinary uses.
