Epacris squarrosa

Epacris squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly epacris, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of its branches. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris squarrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
