Eriope anamariae

Eriope anamariae, commonly known as the Anamariae woolly vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, glandular stems. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriope anamariae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
