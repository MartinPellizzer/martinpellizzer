Euphorbia atimovatae

Euphorbia atimovatae, commonly known as the Atimov's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
