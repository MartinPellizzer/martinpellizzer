Eragrostis wilmsii

Eragrostis wilmsii, commonly known as Wilms' lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are often described as resembling a brush or a broom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis wilmsii is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
