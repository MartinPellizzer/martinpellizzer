Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp. maleevii

Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp.

maleevii, commonly known as the Corsican spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
