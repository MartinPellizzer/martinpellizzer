Elsholtzia serotina

Elsholtzia serotina, commonly known as late-flowering elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.

Elsholtzia serotina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
