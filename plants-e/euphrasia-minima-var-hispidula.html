Euphrasia minima var. hispidula

Euphrasia minima var. hispidula, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, showy eyespots. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its common name.
