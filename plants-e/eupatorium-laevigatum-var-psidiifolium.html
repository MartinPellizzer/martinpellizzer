Eupatorium laevigatum var. psidiifolium

Eupatorium laevigatum var. psidiifolium, commonly known as the silky eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
