Erythroxylum caffrum

Erythroxylum caffrum, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting yellow center. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Erythroxylum caffrum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
