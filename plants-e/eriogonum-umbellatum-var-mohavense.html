Eriogonum umbellatum var. mohavense

Eriogonum umbellatum var. mohavense, commonly known as the Mohave buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clustered, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It has slender, branching stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
