Euphorbion myrsinitum

Euphorbion myrsinitum, commonly known as the common euphorbia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and distinctive cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is a common feature among members of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is typically found in a variety of habitats, including gardens, waste areas, and disturbed soils.

Euphorbion myrsinitum is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
