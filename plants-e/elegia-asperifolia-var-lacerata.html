Elegia asperifolia var. lacerata

Elegia asperifolia var. lacerata, commonly known as the roughleaf elegia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. The leaves are often deeply lobed or toothed, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
