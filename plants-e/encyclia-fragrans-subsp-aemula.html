Encyclia fragrans subsp. aemula

Encyclia fragrans subsp.

aemula, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, intricate pattern.

The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
