Eupatorium zacuapanum

Eupatorium zacuapanum, commonly known as the Zacuapan thoroughwort, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium zacuapanum is often used in horticulture for its visual appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
