Embelia impressa var. serrata

Embelia impressa var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated embelia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
