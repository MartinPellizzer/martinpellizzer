Eupatorium alternifolium f. nitidum

Eupatorium alternifolium f. nitidum, commonly known as the glossy eupatorium, is a variant of the common eupatorium, characterized by its smooth, shiny foliage.

It is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States, often found in moist woodlands and along streambanks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
