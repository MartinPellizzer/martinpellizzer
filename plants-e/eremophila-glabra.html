Eremophila glabra

Eremophila glabra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pink or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions of southern Australia.

Eremophila glabra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native garden landscaping.
