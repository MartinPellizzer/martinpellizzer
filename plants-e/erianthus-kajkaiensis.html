Erianthus kajkaiensis

Erianthus kajkaiensis, commonly known as the Kajkai reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves that can reach several meters in height. The plant thrives in moist to aquatic environments, often found in marshes and along riverbanks. Its flowering inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Erianthus kajkaiensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
