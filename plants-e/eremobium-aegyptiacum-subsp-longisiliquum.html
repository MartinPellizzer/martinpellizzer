Eremobium aegyptiacum subsp. longisiliquum

Eremobium aegyptiacum subsp.

longisiliquum, commonly known as the long silique eremobium, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods called silique, which contain several seeds. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
