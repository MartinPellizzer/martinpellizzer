Epidendrum saaronicum

Epidendrum saaronicum, commonly known as the Saronic Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are often a mix of white and yellow hues, with a strong, sweet fragrance.

Epidendrum saaronicum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under proper conditions.
