Eria lanuginosa

Eria lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The woolly eria is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
