Euphorbia eriophylla

Euphorbia eriophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight and reduces water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
