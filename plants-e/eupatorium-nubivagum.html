Eupatorium nubivagum

Eupatorium nubivagum, commonly known as the cloud wandering eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, humid climates. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium nubivagum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
