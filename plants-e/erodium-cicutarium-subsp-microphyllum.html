Erodium cicutarium subsp. microphyllum

Erodium cicutarium subsp.

microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved field pansy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, dry fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is characterized by its smaller leaves compared to other subspecies of Erodium cicutarium.
