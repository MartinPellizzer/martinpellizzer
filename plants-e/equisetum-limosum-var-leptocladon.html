Equisetum limosum var. leptocladon

Equisetum limosum var. leptocladon, commonly known as the slender-stemmed field horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically found in moist, nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Its ancient lineage and unique morphology make it a fascinating subject in the study of early vascular plants.
