Eupatorium flavidulum

Eupatorium flavidulum, commonly known as the yellow thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
