Emblica pisiformis

Emblica pisiformis, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to yellow, fleshy fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
