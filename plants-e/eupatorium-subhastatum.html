Eupatorium subhastatum

Eupatorium subhastatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Eupatorium subhastatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
