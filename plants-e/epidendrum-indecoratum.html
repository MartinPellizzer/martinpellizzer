Epidendrum indecoratum

Epidendrum indecoratum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Forest, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often in clusters. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining potting media.
