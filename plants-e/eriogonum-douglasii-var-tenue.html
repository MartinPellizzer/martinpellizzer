Eriogonum douglasii var. tenue

Eriogonum douglasii var. tenue, commonly known as the slender leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It is a perennial herb that produces seeds in its second year, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important species for local biodiversity and habitat stability.
