Erigeron aureus var. acutifolius

Erigeron aureus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the sharp-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped, serrated leaves. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
