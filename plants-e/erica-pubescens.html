Erica pubescens

Erica pubescens, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form with hairy foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica pubescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
