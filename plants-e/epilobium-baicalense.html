Epilobium baicalense

Epilobium baicalense, commonly known as Baikal willowherb, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Epilobium baicalense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
