Eugenia kunthiana var. opaca

Eugenia kunthiana var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, aromatic fruits that are typically small and yellow when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in subtropical regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
