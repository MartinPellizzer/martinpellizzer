Erica thalictriflora

Erica thalictriflora, commonly known as the silver Erica, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica thalictriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
