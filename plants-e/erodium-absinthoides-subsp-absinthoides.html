Erodium absinthoides subsp. absinthoides

Erodium absinthoides subsp.

absinthoides, commonly known as narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it identifiable by its narrow leaves and distinctive inflorescence structure.
