Epilobium latifolium subsp. leucanthum

Epilobium latifolium subsp.

leucanthum, commonly known as white-flowered willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides. It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.

The subspecies leucanthum is distinguished by its lighter floral coloration compared to other subspecies of Epilobium latifolium.
