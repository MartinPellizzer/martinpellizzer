Eupatorium ruae

Eupatorium ruae, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist woodland areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium ruae is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
