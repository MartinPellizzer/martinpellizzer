Euphorbia subfalcata

Euphorbia subfalcata, commonly known as the sickle euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features distinctive, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Euphorbia subfalcata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
