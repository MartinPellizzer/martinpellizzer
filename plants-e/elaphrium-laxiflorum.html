Elaphrium laxiflorum

Elaphrium laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered elaphrium, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and often hairy foliage. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in loose inflorescences, typical of the genus Elaphrium.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or rocky habitats.
