Eugenia lamprophylla

Eugenia lamprophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
