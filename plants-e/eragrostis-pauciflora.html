Eragrostis pauciflora

Eragrostis pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny florets. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Eragrostis pauciflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal in dryland landscapes.
