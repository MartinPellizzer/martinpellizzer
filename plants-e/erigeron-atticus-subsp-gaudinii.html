Erigeron atticus subsp. gaudinii

Erigeron atticus subsp.

gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces blue to purple flowers in late summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological traits.
