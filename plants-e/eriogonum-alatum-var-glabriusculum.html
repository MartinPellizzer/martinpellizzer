Eriogonum alatum var. glabriusculum

Eriogonum alatum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the hairyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is a perennial species that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
