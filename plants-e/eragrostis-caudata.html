Eragrostis caudata

Eragrostis caudata, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and tufted inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and have a fine texture, while the flowers are small and arranged in dense clusters.

Eragrostis caudata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
