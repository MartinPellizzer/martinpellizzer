Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. polyacanthus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. polyacanthus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in the winter, typically around Christmas, hence its common name. The plant has a compact, branching structure with short, ribbed stems covered in spines. Its leaves are modified into spines, and the flowers come in a range of colors, including red, orange, and yellow.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
