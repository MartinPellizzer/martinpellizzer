Erigeron asper var. subinteger

Erigeron asper var. subinteger, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies.

Its fibrous root system allows it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.
