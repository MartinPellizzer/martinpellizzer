Eugenia parviflora

Eugenia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters and has glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
