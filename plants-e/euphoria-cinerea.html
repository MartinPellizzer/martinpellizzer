Euphoria cinerea

Euphoria cinerea, commonly known as the gray euphoria, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
