Epidendrum glossoceras

Epidendrum glossoceras, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Epidendrum glossoceras is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
