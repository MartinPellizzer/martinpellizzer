Eremophila youngii subsp. youngii

Eremophila youngii subsp.

youngii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from pale yellow to red.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots and a compact growth form that helps it conserve water. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and shrublands. Eremophila youngii subsp.

youngii is an important component of local ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
