Eranthemum tricolor

Eranthemum tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color ivy or three-color ivy, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, tri-colored flowers that typically feature red, yellow, and white petals. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and garden displays. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eranthemum tricolor thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring regular watering to maintain its lush appearance.
