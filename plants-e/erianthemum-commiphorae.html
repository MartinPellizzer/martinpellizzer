Erianthemum commiphorae

Erianthemum commiphorae, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that resemble dandelion flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the sunflower family. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Erianthemum commiphorae is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
