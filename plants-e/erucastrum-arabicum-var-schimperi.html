Erucastrum arabicum var. schimperi

Erucastrum arabicum var. schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian mustard, is a flowering plant native to the highland regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant produces small, round seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
