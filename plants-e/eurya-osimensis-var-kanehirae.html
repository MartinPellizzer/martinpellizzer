Eurya osimensis var. kanehirae

Eurya osimensis var. kanehirae, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its evergreen foliage and seasonal flowering.
