Eucalyptus platypus var. nutans

Eucalyptus platypus var. nutans, commonly known as the slender grey gum, is a medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a whitish underside. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers that bloom during spring and summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
