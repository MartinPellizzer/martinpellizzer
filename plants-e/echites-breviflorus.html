Echites breviflorus

Echites breviflorus, commonly known as the short-flowered echites, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to reduce water loss in dry environments.

Echites breviflorus is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to xerophytic conditions.
