Exochaenium clavatum

Exochaenium clavatum, commonly known as the clustered beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Exochaenium clavatum is valued for its role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
