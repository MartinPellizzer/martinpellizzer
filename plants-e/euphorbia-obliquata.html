Euphorbia obliquata

Euphorbia obliquata, commonly known as the zigzag spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Euphorbia obliquata is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often spiny structures.
