Erigeron fragilis

Erigeron fragilis, commonly known as brittle blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It features slender stems and small, delicate blue or purple flowers.

Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
