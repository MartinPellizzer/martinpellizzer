Eria rufinula

Eria rufinula, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
