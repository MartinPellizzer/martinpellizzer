Evolvulus grandiflorus

Evolvulus grandiflorus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Evolvulus grandiflorus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and easy care requirements.
