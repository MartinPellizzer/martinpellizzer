Eupatorium robinsonianum

Eupatorium robinsonianum, commonly known as the Florida boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its showy white to pale blue flowers appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
