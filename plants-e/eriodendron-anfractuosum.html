Eriodendron anfractuosum

Eriodendron anfractuosum, commonly known as the African yellowwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, elliptical leaves. Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is considered endangered in some regions.
