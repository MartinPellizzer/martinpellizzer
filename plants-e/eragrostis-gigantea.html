Eragrostis gigantea

Eragrostis gigantea, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to golden flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Eragrostis gigantea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
