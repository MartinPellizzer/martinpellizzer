Euphorbia corollata var. joorii

Euphorbia corollata var. joorii, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy stems that are often tinged with a pink or reddish hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.
