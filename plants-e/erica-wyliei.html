Erica wyliei

Erica wyliei, commonly known as the Wylie's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. This species is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica wyliei thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
