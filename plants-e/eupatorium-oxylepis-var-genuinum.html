Eupatorium oxylepis var. genuinum

Eupatorium oxylepis var. genuinum, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters called capitula. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used traditionally in herbal remedies.
