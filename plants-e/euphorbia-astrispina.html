Euphorbia astrispina

Euphorbia astrispina, commonly known as the star-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny, lobed leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Euphorbia astrispina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
