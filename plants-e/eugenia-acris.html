Eugenia acris

Eugenia acris, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, elliptical leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, dark red berries.

Eugenia acris is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
