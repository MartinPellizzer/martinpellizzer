Elymus ciliaris subsp. japonicus

Elymus ciliaris subsp.

japonicus, commonly known as Japanese basket grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with fine hairs along the edges.

The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
