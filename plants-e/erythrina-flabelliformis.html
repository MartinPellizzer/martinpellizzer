Erythrina flabelliformis

Erythrina flabelliformis, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, feather-like leaves and vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and is adaptable to various soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The species is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.
