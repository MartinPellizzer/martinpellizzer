Erythronium japonicum var. leucanthum

Erythronium japonicum var. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered Japanese dog's tooth violet, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Japan.

It features showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April. The plant has heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that emerge from a bulb and are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and are known for their delicate, nodding appearance.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
