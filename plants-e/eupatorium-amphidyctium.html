Eupatorium amphidyctium

Eupatorium amphidyctium, commonly known as the swamp eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in marshy areas, often forming dense stands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, panicle-like inflorescence consists of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium amphidyctium plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
