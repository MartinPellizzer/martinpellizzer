Eucalyptus conglobata subsp. conglobata

Eucalyptus conglobata subsp.

conglobata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are usually arranged in clusters and are white to pale blue in hue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
