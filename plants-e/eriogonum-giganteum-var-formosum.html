Eriogonum giganteum var. formosum

Eriogonum giganteum var. formosum, commonly known as the Sierra Giant Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 3 meters.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
