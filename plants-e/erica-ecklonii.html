Erica ecklonii

Erica ecklonii, commonly known as the Cape heath or silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica ecklonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal environments.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
