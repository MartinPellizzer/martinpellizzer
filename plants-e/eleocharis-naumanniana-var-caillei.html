Eleocharis naumanniana var. caillei

Eleocharis naumanniana var. caillei, commonly known as the Caille's spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are clustered in dense spikes, and it produces tiny, dry fruits that are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
