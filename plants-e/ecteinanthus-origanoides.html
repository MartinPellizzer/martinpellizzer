Ecteinanthus origanoides

Ecteinanthus origanoides, commonly known as the creeping chamomile, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, hairy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
