Eleocharis nupeensis

Eleocharis nupeensis, commonly known as the Nigerian spikerush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant thrives in muddy or aquatic habitats and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis nupeensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
