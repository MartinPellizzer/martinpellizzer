Eulophia dichroma

Eulophia dichroma, commonly known as the dark-veined eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and often have a dark green color with prominent veins.

The flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive dark marking on the lip, contributing to its ornamental value.
