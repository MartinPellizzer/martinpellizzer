Echinopsis kratochviliana

Echinopsis kratochviliana, commonly known as the Kratochvil's moon cactus, is a cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a striking white to pale blue coloration. The plant produces delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, adding to its ornamental appeal. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
