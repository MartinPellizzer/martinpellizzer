Eriogonum vimineum var. porphyreticum

Eriogonum vimineum var. porphyreticum, commonly known as the purpleleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of showy, violet to purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
