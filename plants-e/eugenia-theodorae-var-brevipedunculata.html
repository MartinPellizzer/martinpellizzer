Eugenia theodorae var. brevipedunculata

Eugenia theodorae var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the dwarf guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
