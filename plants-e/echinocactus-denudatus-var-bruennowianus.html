Echinocactus denudatus var. bruennowianus

Echinocactus denudatus var. bruennowianus, commonly known as the Mexican peacock cactus, is a compact, columnar cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a rounded, ribbed stem with prominent, sharp spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for desert gardens.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
