Erica canaliculata

Erica canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in areas with high humidity and acidic conditions. Erica canaliculata is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to harsh environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
