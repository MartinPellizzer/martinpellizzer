Eragrostis habrantha

Eragrostis habrantha, commonly known as slender bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the United States, particularly found in the southeastern region.

It typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant has narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it useful for erosion control.

Eragrostis habrantha is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
