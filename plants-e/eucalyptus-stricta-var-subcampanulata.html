Eucalyptus stricta var. subcampanulata

Eucalyptus stricta var. subcampanulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the winter and spring seasons. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in strips, revealing a reddish or brownish underbark.

This variety is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
