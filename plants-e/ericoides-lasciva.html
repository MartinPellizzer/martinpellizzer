Ericoides lasciva

Ericoides lasciva, commonly known as the naked lady, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, upright stem with clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, appearing after the flowers have faded.

Ericoides lasciva is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
