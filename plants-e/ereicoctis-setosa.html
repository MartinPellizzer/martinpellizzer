Ereicoctis setosa

Ereicoctis setosa, commonly known as the hairy false heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, contributing to its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and woodland edges.

Ereicoctis setosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
