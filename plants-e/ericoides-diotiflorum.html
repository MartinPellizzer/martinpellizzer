Ericoides diotiflorum

Ericoides diotiflorum, commonly known as the two-flowered heath, is a small evergreen shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

Ericoides diotiflorum is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
