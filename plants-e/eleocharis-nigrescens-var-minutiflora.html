Eleocharis nigrescens var. minutiflora

Eleocharis nigrescens var. minutiflora, commonly known as the black-stemmed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tiny, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its flowers are usually green or brown, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
