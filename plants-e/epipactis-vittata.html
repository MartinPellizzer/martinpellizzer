Epipactis vittata

Epipactis vittata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet or purple spots on its leaves and flowers, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a rosette of leaves.

The flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a lip that is often marked with purple or brown patterns.
