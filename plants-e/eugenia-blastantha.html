Eugenia blastantha

Eugenia blastantha, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, sour-sweet berries that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
