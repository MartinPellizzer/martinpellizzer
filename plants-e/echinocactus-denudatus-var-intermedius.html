Echinocactus denudatus var. intermedius

Echinocactus denudatus var. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which are characteristic of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its structure is adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in low-maintenance gardens.
