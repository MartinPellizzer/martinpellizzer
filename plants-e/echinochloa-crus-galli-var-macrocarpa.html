Echinochloa crus-galli var. macrocarpa

Echinochloa crus-galli var. macrocarpa, commonly known as cocklebur, is an annual grassy weed characterized by its rough, bristly leaves and distinctive spiny burs.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and wetlands. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, prickly fruits. Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a competitive weed in various ecosystems.

The variety macrocarpa is distinguished by its larger seeds compared to other forms of Echinochloa crus-galli.
