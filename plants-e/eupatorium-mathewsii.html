Eupatorium mathewsii

Eupatorium mathewsii, commonly known as Mathews' boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eupatorium mathewsii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
