Erodium cicutarium var. jacquinianum

Erodium cicutarium var. jacquinianum, commonly known as the Jacquin's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

The species is characterized by its slender stems and small, bead-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.
