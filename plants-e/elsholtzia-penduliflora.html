Elsholtzia penduliflora

Elsholtzia penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging-flower elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and typically have a pale purple to white coloration.

Elsholtzia penduliflora is often found in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
