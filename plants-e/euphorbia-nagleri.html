Euphorbia nagleri

Euphorbia nagleri, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in shades of yellow or green. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Euphorbia nagleri is known for its ability to thrive in harsh desert environments with minimal water.
