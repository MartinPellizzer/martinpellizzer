Eugenia calleryana

Eugenia calleryana, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but has become an invasive species in many regions.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types contribute to its widespread distribution.
