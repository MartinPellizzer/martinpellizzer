Erigeron eatonii subsp. villosus

Erigeron eatonii subsp.

villosus, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is often found in California and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
