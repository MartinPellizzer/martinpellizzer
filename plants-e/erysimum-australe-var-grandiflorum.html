Erysimum australe var. grandiflorum

Erysimum australe var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Australian wallaby's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, lobed leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, cruciform flowers. The plant is part of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in local ecosystems.
