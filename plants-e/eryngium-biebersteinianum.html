Eryngium biebersteinianum

Eryngium biebersteinianum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, bract-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native range.

The plant is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
