Escallonia adscendens

Escallonia adscendens, commonly known as the ascending escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

Escallonia adscendens is also valued for its fragrant leaves and adaptability to various garden conditions.
