Eriobotrya acuminatissima

Eriobotrya acuminatissima, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by yellow, sweet-smelling fruits.

The fruit is typically harvested in the fall and is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses.
