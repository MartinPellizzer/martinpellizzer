Eupatorium pichinchense f. glandulare

Eupatorium pichinchense f. glandulare, commonly known as the glandular eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant features glandular hairs on its leaves and stems, which give it its specific epithet "glandulare.". It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
