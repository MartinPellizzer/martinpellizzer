Euphorbia longipedunculata

Euphorbia longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers atop long, slender peduncles. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia longipedunculata is also known for its ability to store water in its stems, which helps it survive in dry conditions.
