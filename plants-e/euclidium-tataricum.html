Euclidium tataricum

Euclidium tataricum, commonly known as the Tatarian euclidium, is a perennial herb native to the steppes of Eastern Europe and Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, branching stems with small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in dry, sandy soils. It is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including drought and cold temperatures.

The species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
