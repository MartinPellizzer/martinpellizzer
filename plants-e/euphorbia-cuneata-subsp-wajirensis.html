Euphorbia cuneata subsp. wajirensis

Euphorbia cuneata subsp.

wajirensis, commonly known as the Wajirensis spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Africa. It is a succulent shrub characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. It has a shallow root system and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh conditions.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
