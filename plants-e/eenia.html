Eenia

Eenia, commonly known as the "false holly," is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, spiny leaves that resemble those of holly, though they are not related botanically. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical classification is Euonymus europaeus, and it belongs to the family Celastraceae.
