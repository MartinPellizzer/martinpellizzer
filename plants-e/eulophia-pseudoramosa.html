Eulophia pseudoramosa

Eulophia pseudoramosa, commonly known as the false branched orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in undergrowth. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Eulophia pseudoramosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
