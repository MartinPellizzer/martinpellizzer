Eugenia lacistema

Eugenia lacistema, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and seasonal floral displays.
