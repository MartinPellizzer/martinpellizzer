Euphorbia alaica

Euphorbia alaica, commonly known as the Crimean spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Crimean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.

Euphorbia alaica is often found in open areas with well-drained soil, making it a resilient plant in its native environment.
