Erianthus saccharoides subvar. villosus

Erianthus saccharoides subvar. villosus, commonly known as the hairy sugarcane grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and hairy leaves, which give it its subspecific name. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies.

Due to its high biomass and potential for bioenergy production, it has attracted interest for sustainable agricultural applications.
