Excoecaria biglandulosa var. montevidensis

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often lobed or notched, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
