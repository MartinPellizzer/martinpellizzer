Euphrasia pectinata subsp. albanica

Euphrasia pectinata subsp.

albanica, commonly known as the Albanian eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive pectinate margin, giving it its specific epithet "pectinata.".

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
