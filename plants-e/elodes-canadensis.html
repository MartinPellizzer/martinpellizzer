Elodes canadensis

Elodes canadensis, commonly known as Canadian waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has long, branching stems that are typically green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged. The plant is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, elongated leaves. It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as lakes, ponds, and streams.

Elodes canadensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
