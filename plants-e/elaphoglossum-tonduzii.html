Elaphoglossum tonduzii

Elaphoglossum tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduz's holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, evergreen fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically less than 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces spore-bearing soruses that are clustered on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in many fern species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect light.
