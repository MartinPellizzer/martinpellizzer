Entada parrana

Entada parrana, commonly known as the African tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is valued for its hard, heavy wood and its ability to thrive in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and attract various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in a thick, fibrous pod and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
