Eucalyptus celastroides

Eucalyptus celastroides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey to white bark, which peels off in thin, fibrous strips. The plant produces small, creamy-white flowers in clusters, typically blooming between September and November. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight blue-green hue.

Eucalyptus celastroides is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
