Erythronium dens-canis var. roseum

Erythronium dens-canis var. roseum, commonly known as the rose-flowered dog's tooth violet, is a variant of the dog's tooth violet species.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States. The plant features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers. Its botanical name reflects its dense, canine-like leaves and rose-colored blooms.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
