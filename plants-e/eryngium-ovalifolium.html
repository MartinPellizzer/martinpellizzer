Eryngium ovalifolium

Eryngium ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Eryngium ovalifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
