Erodium romanum

Erodium romanum, commonly known as common stork's bill, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a distinctive, two-lobed capsule that resembles a stork's bill, giving it its common name.

Erodium romanum is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
