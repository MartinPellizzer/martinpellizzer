Eugenia lacustris

Eugenia lacustris, commonly known as the lake guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a dark red or black color.

The leaves are ovate, with a glossy surface and a strong, aromatic scent.
