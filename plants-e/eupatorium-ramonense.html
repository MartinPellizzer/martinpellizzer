Eupatorium ramonense

Eupatorium ramonense, commonly known as the Ramon's thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and somewhat woody.

Eupatorium ramonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
