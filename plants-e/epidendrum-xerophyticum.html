Epidendrum xerophyticum

Epidendrum xerophyticum, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a unique species adapted to arid environments.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its ability to survive in low-water conditions. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store moisture, and its flowers are typically small and clustered. It thrives in regions with high temperatures and minimal rainfall, such as deserts and rocky areas.

Its specialized morphology allows it to conserve water and withstand extreme environmental conditions.
