Erica steinbergiana

Erica steinbergiana, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica steinbergiana is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and can tolerate drought conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
