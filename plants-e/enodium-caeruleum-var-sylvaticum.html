Enodium caeruleum var. sylvaticum

Enodium caeruleum var. sylvaticum, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-white down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
