Eugenia angkae

Eugenia angkae, commonly known as the Angkae myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Eugenia angkae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
