Eugenia dittocrepis

Eugenia dittocrepis, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, reddish-purple drupe that is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia dittocrepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
