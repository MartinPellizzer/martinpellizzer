Eragrostis fragilis

Eragrostis fragilis, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant typically thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce light green to brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Eragrostis fragilis is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and rapid growth.
