Echium tuberculatum var. densiflorum

Echium tuberculatum var. densiflorum, commonly known as the dense-flowered thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, blue to violet flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that remain on the plant after flowering, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
