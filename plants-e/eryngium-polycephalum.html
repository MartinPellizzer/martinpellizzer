Eryngium polycephalum

Eryngium polycephalum, commonly known as the many-headed thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, spiny foliage and produces spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small umbrellas. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its unique morphology makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and other thistle species.
