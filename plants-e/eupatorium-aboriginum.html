Eupatorium aboriginum

Eupatorium aboriginum, commonly known as the Australian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eupatorium aboriginum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used by Indigenous Australians for various traditional purposes.
