Euphorbia capuronii

Euphorbia capuronii, commonly known as the Capuron's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a branching stem system with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences containing both male and female flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
