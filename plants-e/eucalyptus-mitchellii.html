Eucalyptus mitchellii

Eucalyptus mitchellii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or small tree form, with smooth, often peeling bark. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal white or pale pink flowers.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils.
