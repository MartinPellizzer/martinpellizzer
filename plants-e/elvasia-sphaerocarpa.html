Elvasia sphaerocarpa

Elvasia sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-fig, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small figs. Its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in minimal water make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Elvasia sphaerocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appearance and low maintenance requirements.
