Echinopsis eyriesii var. glaucescens

Echinopsis eyriesii var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Blue Ghost Cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and blue-gray spines that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
