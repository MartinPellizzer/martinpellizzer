Eremophila canaliculata

Eremophila canaliculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila canaliculata is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
