Echinops griffithianus

Echinops griffithianus, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spiny bracts that surround the small yellow flowers. The plant produces a tall, sparsely branched stem with narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Echinops griffithianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
