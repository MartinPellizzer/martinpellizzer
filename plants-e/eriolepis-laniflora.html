Eriolepis laniflora

Eriolepis laniflora, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in the spring. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique texture, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
