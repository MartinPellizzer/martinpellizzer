Eragrostis interrupta var. diplachnoides

Eragrostis interrupta var. diplachnoides, commonly known as the silky false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and open woodlands.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of native plant communities.
