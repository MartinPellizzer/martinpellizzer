Epipactis collaris

Epipactis collaris, commonly known as the collar orchid, is a terrestrial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive white or greenish collar-like marking near the base.

Epipactis collaris is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
