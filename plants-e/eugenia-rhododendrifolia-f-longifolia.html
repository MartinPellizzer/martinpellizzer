Eugenia rhododendrifolia f. longifolia

Eugenia rhododendrifolia f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved eugenia, is a flowering plant species native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in spring. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in tropical botany and horticulture.

The variety f. longifolia is distinguished by its notably longer leaves compared to the typical form of Eugenia rhododendrifolia.
