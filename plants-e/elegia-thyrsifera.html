Elegia thyrsifera

Elegia thyrsifera, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces long, winding tendrils that help it climb. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental species in horticulture.
