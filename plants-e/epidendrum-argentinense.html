Epidendrum argentinense

Epidendrum argentinense, commonly known as the Argentine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces aerial pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and come in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and pink.

Epidendrum argentinense is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
