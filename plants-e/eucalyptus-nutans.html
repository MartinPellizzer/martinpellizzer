Eucalyptus nutans

Eucalyptus nutans, commonly known as the weeping eucalyptus, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its bark is smooth and peels in thin layers, revealing a mottled appearance.

Eucalyptus nutans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a range of soil types and climates.
