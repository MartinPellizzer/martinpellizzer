Echinodorus berteroi var. patagonicus

Echinodorus berteroi var. patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian swordplant, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant is often cultivated in ponds and water gardens for its ornamental value and ability to improve water quality. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with moderate to high light exposure.

Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense underwater foliage.
