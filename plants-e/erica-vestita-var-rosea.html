Erica vestita var. rosea

Erica vestita var. rosea, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts to harsh climatic conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
