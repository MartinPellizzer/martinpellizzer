Eranthemum verbenaceum

Eranthemum verbenaceum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf geranium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its showy flowers, typically in shades of pink or purple, bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eranthemum verbenaceum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight.
