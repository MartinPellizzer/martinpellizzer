Erica bakeri

Erica bakeri, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica bakeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
