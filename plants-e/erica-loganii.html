Erica loganii

Erica loganii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Erica loganii is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
