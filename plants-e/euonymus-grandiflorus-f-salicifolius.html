Euonymus grandiflorus f. salicifolius

Euonymus grandiflorus f. salicifolius, commonly known as the willow-leafed woodbine, is a variant of the woodbine plant characterized by its distinctive willow-like leaves.

It is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant features small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. Its foliage provides ornamental value throughout the growing season, and it is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving the plant a graceful, delicate appearance.
