Elymus ciliaris var. amurensis

Elymus ciliaris var. amurensis, commonly known as Amur wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, tufted culms with fine, thread-like leaves that are often covered in silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in ecological restoration projects.
