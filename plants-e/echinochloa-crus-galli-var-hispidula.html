Echinochloa crus-galli var. hispidula

Echinochloa crus-galli var. hispidula, commonly known as rough barnyardgrass, is a highly invasive annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and stems, which are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a major weed in many parts of the world.
