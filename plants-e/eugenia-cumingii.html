Eugenia cumingii

Eugenia cumingii, commonly known as the Java apple or cempedak, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces large, round fruits with a thick, fibrous rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible yield.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
