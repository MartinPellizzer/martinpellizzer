Erycibe rheedii

Erycibe rheedii, commonly known as the red-veined hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to deep red. The plant features palmate leaves with prominent veins, and its stems are often covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
