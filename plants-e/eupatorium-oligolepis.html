Eupatorium oligolepis

Eupatorium oligolepis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Eupatorium oligolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
