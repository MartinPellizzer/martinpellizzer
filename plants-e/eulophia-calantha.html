Eulophia calantha

Eulophia calantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eulophia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white or grayish hairs. The plant produces a single flower spike bearing several showy, fragrant orchid flowers with a distinctive coloration. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
