Encyclia semiaperta

Encyclia semiaperta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinctive yellow lip.

Encyclia semiaperta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
