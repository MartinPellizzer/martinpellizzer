Eurya acuminata var. wallichiana

Eurya acuminata var. wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

The species is valued in horticulture for its compact growth and year-round aesthetic appeal.
