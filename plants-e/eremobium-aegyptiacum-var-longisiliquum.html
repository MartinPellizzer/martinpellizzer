Eremobium aegyptiacum var. longisiliquum

Eremobium aegyptiacum var. longisiliquum, commonly known as the Egyptian sand lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces striking, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers are often clustered in dense spikes, and the plant has a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapic gardens.
