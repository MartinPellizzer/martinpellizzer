Eriophyllum multiflorum

Eriophyllum multiflorum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It features bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.
