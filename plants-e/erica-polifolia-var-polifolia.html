Erica polifolia var. polifolia

Erica polifolia var. polifolia, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica polifolia var. polifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
