Echinocactus schottii

Echinocactus schottii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and golden-green color, with a spiny exterior that provides protection against herbivores. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are typically yellow or orange and bloom at the top of the stem.

Echinocactus schottii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent collections.
