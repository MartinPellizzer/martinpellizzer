Eucalyptus urnigera var. elongata

Eucalyptus urnigera var. elongata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers that typically bloom in winter. The plant is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions. It has a low, spreading growth habit and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
