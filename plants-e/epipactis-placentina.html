Epipactis placentina

Epipactis placentina, commonly known as the Placentina helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower that often has a dark red or purple lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial species that produces a single flower per stem, often in late spring or early summer.

Epipactis placentina is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
