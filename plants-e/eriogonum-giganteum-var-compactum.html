Eriogonum giganteum var. compactum

Eriogonum giganteum var. compactum, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
