Eulophia gusukumae

Eulophia gusukumae, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features a slender, erect stem with several leaves and produces flowers in clusters. Its flowers are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
