Erigeron kumaunensis

Erigeron kumaunensis, commonly known as the Kumaun fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of India.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky areas at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its delicate white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also studied for its potential ecological and medicinal applications.
