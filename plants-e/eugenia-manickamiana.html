Eugenia manickamiana

Eugenia manickamiana, commonly known as the Malabar apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Ayurvedic practices.
