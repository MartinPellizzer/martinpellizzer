Epipactis latifolia f. albiflora

Epipactis latifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and distinctive white flowers with a yellow-green lip. This plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dark brown capsules as its fruit.

It is native to parts of Europe and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
