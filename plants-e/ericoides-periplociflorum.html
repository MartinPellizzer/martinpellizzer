Ericoides periplociflorum

Ericoides periplociflorum, commonly known as the Periplociflorum ericoides, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or heathlands. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and seasonal blooms.
