Echinopsis bridgesii subsp. yungasensis

Echinopsis bridgesii subsp.

yungasensis, commonly known as the Yungas echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to the high-altitude regions of Bolivia and Argentina. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
