Eriogonum vimineum var. eriocladon

Eriogonum vimineum var. eriocladon, commonly known as the woolly buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has a mat-forming growth habit, with slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes.

The leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
