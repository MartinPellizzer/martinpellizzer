Elaeocarpus degenerianus

Elaeocarpus degenerianus, commonly known as the Degener's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
