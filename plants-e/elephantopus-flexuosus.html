Elephantopus flexuosus

Elephantopus flexuosus, commonly known as the curly elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy, and often hairy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its thick leaves.

The species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
