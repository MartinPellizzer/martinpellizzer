Eryngium campestre var. parvifolium

Eryngium campestre var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, known as bloom. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological value make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
