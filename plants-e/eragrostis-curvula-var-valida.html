Eragrostis curvula var. valida

Eragrostis curvula var. valida, commonly known as silverleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray leaves and compact growth habit, making it visually distinct. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
