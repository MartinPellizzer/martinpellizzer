Erigeron coronarius var. coronarius

Erigeron coronarius var. coronarius, commonly known as the crownweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as meadows and roadsides, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually blue to purple in color.

It is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
