Epilobium × marshallianum

Epilobium × marshallianum, commonly known as marsh thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features upright stems that are often covered in glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape with toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its capsules are ribbed and contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Epilobium × marshallianum is adapted to moist soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
