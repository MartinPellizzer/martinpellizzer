Epilobium obscurum subsp. virgatum

Epilobium obscurum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the purple willow-herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae). It is characterized by its upright, herbaceous growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside ditches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually reddish or purplish in color.

The species is native to North America and is often found in the eastern and central regions of the continent.
