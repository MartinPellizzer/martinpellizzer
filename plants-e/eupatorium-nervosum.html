Eupatorium nervosum

Eupatorium nervosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features opposite, rough-hairy leaves with prominent veins, giving it its common name. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, clustered white to pale purple flowers.

It is often found in the eastern United States and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
