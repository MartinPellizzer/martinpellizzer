Eupatorium isolepis

Eupatorium isolepis, commonly known as marsh boneset, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Eupatorium isolepis plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
