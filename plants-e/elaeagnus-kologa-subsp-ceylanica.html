Elaeagnus kologa subsp. ceylanica

Elaeagnus kologa subsp.

ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon silver bush, is a deciduous shrub native to Sri Lanka. It features silvery-white to pale yellow foliage, which is characteristic of the Elaeagnus genus.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a leathery texture and a distinctive silvery underside.

This species is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
