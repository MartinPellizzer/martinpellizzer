Eragrostis triflora

Eragrostis triflora, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

Eragrostis triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
