Eragrostis blepharolepis

Eragrostis blepharolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis blepharolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
