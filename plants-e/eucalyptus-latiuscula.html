Eucalyptus latiuscula

Eucalyptus latiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Eucalyptus latiuscula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardiness and visual interest.
