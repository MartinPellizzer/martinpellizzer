Echinops ceratophorus

Echinops ceratophorus, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble horns, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Echinops ceratophorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
