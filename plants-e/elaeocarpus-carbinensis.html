Elaeocarpus carbinensis

Elaeocarpus carbinensis, commonly known as the New Zealand hakea, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Elaeocarpus carbinensis is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.
