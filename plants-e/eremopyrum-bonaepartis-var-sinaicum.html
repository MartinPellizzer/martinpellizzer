Eremopyrum bonaepartis var. sinaicum

Eremopyrum bonaepartis var. sinaicum, commonly known as the Sinaitic eryngle, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spiky flower heads that are typically blue to purple in color, which bloom in early summer. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can tolerate high levels of salinity.

This variety is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological resilience.
