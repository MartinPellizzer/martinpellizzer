Euonymus chloranthoides

Euonymus chloranthoides, commonly known as the yellow-leaf euonymus, is a shrub native to parts of East Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often yellowish-green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Due to its unique leaf color and adaptability, it is often used in landscape design for its visual interest.
