Eugenia lisboae

Eugenia lisboae, commonly known as the Lisbon guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy surface.

Eugenia lisboae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
