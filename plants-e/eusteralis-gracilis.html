Eusteralis gracilis

Eusteralis gracilis, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient uptake.

Eusteralis gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
