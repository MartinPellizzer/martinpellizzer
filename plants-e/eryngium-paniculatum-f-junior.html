Eryngium paniculatum f. junior

Eryngium paniculatum f. junior, commonly known as the lesser thimbleweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in prairies, wetlands, and along stream banks. It produces a compact, paniculate inflorescence that becomes more showy as the season progresses.

The species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
