Erythroxylum umbu

Erythroxylum umbu, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or umbu, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The fruit's pulp is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor, often used in traditional foods and beverages.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some medicinal uses in local cultures.
