Encelia halimifolia

Encelia halimifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf bush.

It is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
