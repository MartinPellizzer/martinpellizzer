Evolvulus piurensis

Evolvulus piurensis, commonly known as the Peruvian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Evolvulus piurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
