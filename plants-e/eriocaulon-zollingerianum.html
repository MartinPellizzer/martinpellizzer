Eriocaulon zollingerianum

Eriocaulon zollingerianum, commonly known as the Zollinger's bulbous grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. The plant features compact, rosette-like growth with dense, fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-bearing panicle, which produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
