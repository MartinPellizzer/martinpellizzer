Eucalyptus globulosus

Eucalyptus globulosus, commonly known as the globulus gum tree, is a tall, evergreen tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often displays a striking silver or white color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed. It produces small, spherical flower buds that turn into woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is well-known for its essential oils, which are widely used in medicinal and aromatic applications.
