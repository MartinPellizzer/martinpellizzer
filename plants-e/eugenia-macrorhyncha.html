Eugenia macrorhyncha

Eugenia macrorhyncha, commonly known as the large-fruited eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia macrorhyncha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
