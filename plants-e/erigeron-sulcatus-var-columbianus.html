Erigeron sulcatus var. columbianus

Erigeron sulcatus var. columbianus, commonly known as the Columbia blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads.

This variety is often found in the western United States, particularly in Idaho and Montana.
