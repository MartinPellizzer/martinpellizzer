Eruca vesicaria subsp. sativa

Eruca vesicaria subsp.

sativa, commonly known as garden cress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features small, bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves that are often used in culinary applications.

The plant thrives in cool climates and is often grown in gardens for its peppery flavor. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables.

Its seeds are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
