Erica labialis

Erica labialis, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica labialis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
