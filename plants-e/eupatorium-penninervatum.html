Eupatorium penninervatum

Eupatorium penninervatum, commonly known as slenderleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinct central vein, and it produces small, white to pale blue flowers in late summer.

The plant is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
