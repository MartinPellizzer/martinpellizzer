Enarthrocarpus uncatus

Enarthrocarpus uncatus, commonly known as the hairy tamarind, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its distinctive hairy appearance, particularly on its stems and young leaves. It produces small, yellow flowers and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
