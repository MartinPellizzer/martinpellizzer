Erysimum preslianum

Erysimum preslianum, commonly known as the Preslia's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum preslianum is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture for its vibrant floral display.
