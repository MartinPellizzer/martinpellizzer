Erysimastrum lazistanicum

Erysimastrum lazistanicum, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Erysimastrum lazistanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
