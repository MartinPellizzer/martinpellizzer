Elaphoglossum leucolepis subsp. nanolepis

Elaphoglossum leucolepis subsp.

nanolepis, commonly known as the small-leaved holly fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

The plant features a creeping rhizome with small, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a leathery texture.

This subspecies is noted for its compact size and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
