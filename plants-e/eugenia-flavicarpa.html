Eugenia flavicarpa

Eugenia flavicarpa, commonly known as the golden apple or yellow guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The fruit is a smooth, yellow or orange berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor and a high content of vitamin C. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
