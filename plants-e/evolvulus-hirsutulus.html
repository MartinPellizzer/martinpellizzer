Evolvulus hirsutulus

Evolvulus hirsutulus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that open in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils.

Evolvulus hirsutulus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing vines.
