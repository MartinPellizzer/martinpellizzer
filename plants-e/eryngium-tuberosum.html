Eryngium tuberosum

Eryngium tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous eryngium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble a sea urchin, surrounded by bristly leaves. The plant produces tuberous roots that store nutrients, aiding in its survival in various soil conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eryngium tuberosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
