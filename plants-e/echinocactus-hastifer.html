Echinocactus hastifer

Echinocactus hastifer, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines, giving it a distinctive, spiky appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The plant's structure helps it store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh, dry environment.
