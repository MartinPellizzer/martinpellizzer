Echinopsis churinensis

Echinopsis churinensis, commonly known as the Churin's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a columnar cactus with a cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Echinopsis churinensis is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
