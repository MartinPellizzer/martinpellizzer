Eremophila arguta

Eremophila arguta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eremophila arguta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
