Erica longifolia var. breviflora

Erica longifolia var. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Europe.

It features dense, wiry foliage with needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
