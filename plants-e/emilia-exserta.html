Emilia exserta

Emilia exserta, commonly known as the blueweed or blue bonnet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branching stems and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features alternate, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Emilia exserta is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and dry conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
