Eritrichium scouleri

Eritrichium scouleri, commonly known as the Cascade bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eritrichium scouleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
