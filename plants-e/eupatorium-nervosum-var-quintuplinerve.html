Eupatorium nervosum var. quintuplinerve

Eupatorium nervosum var. quintuplinerve, commonly known as the five-veined boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late summer into early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have prominent, distinct veins that give it its scientific name.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
