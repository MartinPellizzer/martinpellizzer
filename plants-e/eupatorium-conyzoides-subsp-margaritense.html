Eupatorium conyzoides subsp. margaritense

Eupatorium conyzoides subsp.

margaritense, commonly known as the margaritense eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture. This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
