Echidiocarya californica var. fulvescens

Echidiocarya californica var. fulvescens, commonly known as the California goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense, sprawling growth with small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
