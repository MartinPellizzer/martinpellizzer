Elaeocarpus crassus

Elaeocarpus crassus, commonly known as the thick-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. Elaeocarpus crassus is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its hardy nature and ornamental qualities.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
