Erythroxylum campestre var. magnifolium

Erythroxylum campestre var. magnifolium, commonly known as the large-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal properties.
