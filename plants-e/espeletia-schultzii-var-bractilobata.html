Espeletia schultzii var. bractilobata

Espeletia schultzii var. bractilobata, commonly known as the Santa Marta espeletia, is a striking plant native to the high Andes of Colombia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and a central rosette of tightly packed leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
