Eugenia pseudotetraptera

Eugenia pseudotetraptera, commonly known as the West African cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic use in local markets.
