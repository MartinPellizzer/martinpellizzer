Epidendrum sympetalostele

Epidendrum sympetalostele, commonly known as the sympetalostele orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, and pink. Its roots are adapted for aerial attachment to trees, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
