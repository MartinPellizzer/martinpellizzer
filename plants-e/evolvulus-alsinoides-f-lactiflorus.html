Evolvulus alsinoides f. lactiflorus

Evolvulus alsinoides f. lactiflorus, commonly known as the milky morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive milky sap and showy blue or violet flowers.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is native to tropical regions of the Americas. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
