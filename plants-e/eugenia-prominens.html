Eugenia prominens

Eugenia prominens, commonly known as the showy wax apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a tart flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia prominens is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
