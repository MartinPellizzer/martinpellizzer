Erica ciliata

Erica ciliata, commonly known as the coastal heath or silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features dense, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Erica ciliata produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
