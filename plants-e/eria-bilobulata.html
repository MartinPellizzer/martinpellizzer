Eria bilobulata

Eria bilobulata, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
