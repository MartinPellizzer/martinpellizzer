Eulophia baumiana

Eulophia baumiana, commonly known as the Baum's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and emits a fragrant, showy inflorescence with numerous flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia baumiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
