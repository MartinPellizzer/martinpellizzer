Erysimum pallasii f. humile

Erysimum pallasii f. humile, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically features dense, compact growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or sandy soils.
