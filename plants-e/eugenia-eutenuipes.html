Eugenia eutenuipes

Eugenia eutenuipes, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in tropical ecosystems.
