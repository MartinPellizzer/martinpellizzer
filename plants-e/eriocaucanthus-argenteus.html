Eriocaucanthus argenteus

Eriocaucanthus argenteus, commonly known as the silver leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its stems are thick and branched, often growing in a shrub-like form.

Eriocaucanthus argenteus is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal watering once established.
