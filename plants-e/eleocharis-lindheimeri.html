Eleocharis lindheimeri

Eleocharis lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's spike rush, is a species of sedges native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are typically borne in dense clusters. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms. Its flowering structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Eleocharis lindheimeri is an important component of the ecological diversity in its native habitats.
