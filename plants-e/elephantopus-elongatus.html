Elephantopus elongatus

Elephantopus elongatus, commonly known as the slender elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that resembles an elephant's foot, adapted for storing water in arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
