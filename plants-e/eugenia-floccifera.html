Eugenia floccifera

Eugenia floccifera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant's leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit, which is a small, round drupe, is edible and has a sweet, sour flavor.
