Eugenioides phaeocladon

Eugenioides phaeocladon, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has dark, dense foliage and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its bark is thick and deeply fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and usually glossy, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
