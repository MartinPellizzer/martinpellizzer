Embothrium umbellatum

Embothrium umbellatum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a leathery texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Embothrium umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
