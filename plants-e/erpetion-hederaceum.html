Erpetion hederaceum

Erpetion hederaceum, commonly known as the common groundsel, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
