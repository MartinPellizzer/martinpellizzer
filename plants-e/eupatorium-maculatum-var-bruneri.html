Eupatorium maculatum var. bruneri

Eupatorium maculatum var. bruneri, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often reddish-brown in color, giving it its varietal name "bruneri.". The plant produces showy, clustered purple to pink flowers at the top of the stems, which bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
