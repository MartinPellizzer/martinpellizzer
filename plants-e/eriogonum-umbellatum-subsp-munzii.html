Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. munzii

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

munzii, commonly known as the Munz's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, triangular seeds.

This subspecies is often found in sagebrush steppe and is an important component of its local ecosystem.
