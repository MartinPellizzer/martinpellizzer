Eucosia brachiorrhynchos

Eucosia brachiorrhynchos, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eucosia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clustered, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its growth habit is typically low and sprawling, with branches that often root at the nodes.

Eucosia brachiorrhynchos plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
