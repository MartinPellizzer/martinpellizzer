Erigeron glabratus var. angustifolius

Erigeron glabratus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils, often forming dense colonies. It has linear to narrowly elliptic leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers from late summer to early fall.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
