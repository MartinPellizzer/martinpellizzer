Erysimum cheiranthoides f. paniculatum

Erysimum cheiranthoides f. paniculatum, commonly known as the paniculate wallflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, branched inflorescences.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in temperate climates.
