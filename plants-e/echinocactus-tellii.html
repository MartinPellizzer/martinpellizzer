Echinocactus tellii

Echinocactus tellii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and a spiny, segmented appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne at the top of the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a striking addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
