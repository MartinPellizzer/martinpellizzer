Evolvulus laetus

Evolvulus laetus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and slender.

The blue morning glory is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
