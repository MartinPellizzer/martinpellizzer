Eucalyptus lucens

Eucalyptus lucens, commonly known as the silver-leafed eucalyptus, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale blue.

It is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its aromatic oils and ecological importance.
