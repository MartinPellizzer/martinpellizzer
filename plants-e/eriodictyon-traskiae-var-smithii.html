Eriodictyon traskiae var. smithii

Eriodictyon traskiae var. smithii, commonly known as California yerba santa, is a shrub native to the coastal ranges of California.

It is characterized by its gray-green, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats. Its leaves contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
