Epidendrum cristobalense

Epidendrum cristobalense, commonly known as the Cristobalense Vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are typically broad and fleshy, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Epidendrum cristobalense is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
