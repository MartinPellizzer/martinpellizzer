Eleocharis uniglumis var. pallida

Eleocharis uniglumis var. pallida, commonly known as the pale spikerush, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pale spikelets that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes, swamps, or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike of tiny, pale brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The pale spikerush plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
