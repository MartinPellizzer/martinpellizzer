Eragrostis pulchra

Eragrostis pulchra, commonly known as the beautiful lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks. It is drought-tolerant and has a clumping growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient choice for gardens and restoration projects.
