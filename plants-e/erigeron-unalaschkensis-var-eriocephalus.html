Erigeron unalaschkensis var. eriocephalus

Erigeron unalaschkensis var. eriocephalus, commonly known as the Alaskan Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as tundra, rocky slopes, and gravelly soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

The species is adapted to cold climates and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
