Euphorbia tannensis subsp. eremophila

Euphorbia tannensis subsp.

eremophila, commonly known as the desert euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its structure and morphology reflect its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a symbol of endurance in challenging landscapes.
