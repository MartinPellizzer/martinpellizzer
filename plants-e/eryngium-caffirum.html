Eryngium caffirum

Eryngium caffirum, commonly known as the African thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into spherical seed heads.

Eryngium caffirum is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
