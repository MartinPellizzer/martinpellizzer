Erica viridiflora subsp. viridiflora

Erica viridiflora subsp.

viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs.

The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to greenish-yellow. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica viridiflora subsp.

viridiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
