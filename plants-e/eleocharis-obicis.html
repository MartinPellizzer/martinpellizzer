Eleocharis obicis

Eleocharis obicis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spike-rush, is a species of sedge found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical spikelets that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

Eleocharis obicis plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
