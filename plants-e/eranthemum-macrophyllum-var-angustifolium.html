Eranthemum macrophyllum var. angustifolium

Eranthemum macrophyllum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eranthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
