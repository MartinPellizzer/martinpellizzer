Erythroxylum campestre var. ovatifolium

Erythroxylum campestre var. ovatifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leafed copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ovate leaves that are typically hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, woody fruits containing seeds. It is often found in dry to moist forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.

The species is also noted for its role in local ecosystems and its potential for sustainable harvesting.
