Entada tamarindifolia

Entada tamarindifolia, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The tree produces large, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds surrounded by a fibrous, edible pulp. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction, as well as for its medicinal properties.
