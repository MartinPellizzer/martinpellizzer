Elaeocarpus vieillardii var. vieillardii

Elaeocarpus vieillardii var. vieillardii, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a globose, segmented berry that turns from green to dark purple when mature, with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
