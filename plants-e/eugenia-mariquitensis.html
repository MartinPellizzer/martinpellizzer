Eugenia mariquitensis

Eugenia mariquitensis, commonly known as the Mariquit mango, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. The fruit is a small, yellow-orange drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp and a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
