Eriocaulon gracile

Eriocaulon gracile, commonly known as slender thread-plant, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that often float on the water surface. The plant typically grows in shallow wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in dense clusters, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon gracile is often used in ornamental water gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of cultivation.
