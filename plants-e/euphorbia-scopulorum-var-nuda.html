Euphorbia scopulorum var. nuda

Euphorbia scopulorum var. nuda, commonly known as the naked rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a thick cuticle to reduce water loss. Its leaves are typically reduced or absent, giving it a more shrub-like appearance.

It is often found growing on sunny, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions.
