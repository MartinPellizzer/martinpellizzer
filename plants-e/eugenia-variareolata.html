Eugenia variareolata

Eugenia variareolata, commonly known as the spotted eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves with distinctive white or pale spots. Its fragrant white flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia variareolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in traditional remedies.
