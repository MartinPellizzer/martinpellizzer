Ekebergia welwitschii

Ekebergia welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia, is a unique plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia and Angola.

It is characterized by its two long, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 4 meters in length and persist for decades. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater in the arid environment. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Due to its slow growth and ecological significance, it is considered a living fossil and a symbol of the Namib Desert's harsh yet beautiful landscape.
