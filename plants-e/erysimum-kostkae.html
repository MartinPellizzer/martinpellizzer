Erysimum kostkae

Erysimum kostkae, commonly known as the Kostka's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum kostkae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
