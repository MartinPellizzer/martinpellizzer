Euphorbia lamarckii var. pseudodendroides

Euphorbia lamarckii var. pseudodendroides, commonly known as the "false tree euphorbia," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem that resembles a tree, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
