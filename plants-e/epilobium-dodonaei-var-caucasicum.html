Epilobium dodonaei var. caucasicum

Epilobium dodonaei var. caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian willow, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It has a fibrous root system and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
