Euphorbia esula var. mosana

Euphorbia esula var. mosana, commonly known as Canadian spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, somewhat woody stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a distinctive waxy texture.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and meadows.
