Eremurus pubescens

Eremurus pubescens, commonly known as the woolly candleplant, is a striking perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, composed of numerous yellow to orange flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and a thick, fleshy stem covered in soft, hairy tissue. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eremurus pubescens is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
