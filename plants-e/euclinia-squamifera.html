Euclinia squamifera

Euclinia squamifera, commonly known as the silverleaf euclinia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing tiny seeds.

Euclinia squamifera is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
