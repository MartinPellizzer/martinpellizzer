Epilobium ciliatum subsp. glandulosum

Epilobium ciliatum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which are often covered in small, sticky secretions.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
