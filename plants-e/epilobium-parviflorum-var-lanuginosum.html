Epilobium parviflorum var. lanuginosum

Epilobium parviflorum var. lanuginosum, commonly known as the downy willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, roadsides, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs, which give it its common name. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
