Eugenia ruscifolia

Eugenia ruscifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
