Eupatorium grandiceps

Eupatorium grandiceps, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands.

Eupatorium grandiceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
