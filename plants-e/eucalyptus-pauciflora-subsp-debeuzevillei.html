Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp. debeuzevillei

Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp.

debeuzevillei, commonly known as the alpine eucalypt, is a hardy shrub or small tree native to the alpine regions of Tasmania. It is characterized by its grey-blue, narrow leaves and small, white flowers that appear in winter.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It has a slow growth rate and is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
