Erigeron breviscapus var. leucanthus

Erigeron breviscapus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the whitehead fleabane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features a basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
