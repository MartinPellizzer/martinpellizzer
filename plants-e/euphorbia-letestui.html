Euphorbia letestui

Euphorbia letestui, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soil.

Euphorbia letestui is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
