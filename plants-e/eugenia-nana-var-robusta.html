Eugenia nana var. robusta

Eugenia nana var. robusta, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears small, round fruits that turn from green to red or yellow when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm climates.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
