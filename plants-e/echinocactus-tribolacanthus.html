Echinocactus tribolacanthus

Echinocactus tribolacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, storing water in its succulent stems.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
