Evolvulus falcatus

Evolvulus falcatus, commonly known as the sickleleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The flowers bloom in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
