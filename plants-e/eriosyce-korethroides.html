Eriosyce korethroides

Eriosyce korethroides, commonly known as the silver leaf cactus, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray spines and a compact, globular shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert gardens.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
