Eugenia gardneri

Eugenia gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
