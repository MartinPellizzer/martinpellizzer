Echium orientale

Echium orientale, commonly known as the oriental viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Echium orientale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
