Elaeocarpus fuscoides

Elaeocarpus fuscoides, commonly known as the black elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dark purple or black when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
