Eugenia mangifolia

Eugenia mangifolia, commonly known as the mangifolia or the Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, round, yellowish fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor.

Eugenia mangifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
