Eriocaulon siamense

Eriocaulon siamense, commonly known as the Siam hairgrass, is a small aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Eriocaulon siamense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
