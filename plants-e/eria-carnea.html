Eria carnea

Eria carnea, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and glossy, while its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Eria carnea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
