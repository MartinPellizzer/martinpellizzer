Epidendrum aguaricoense

Epidendrum aguaricoense, commonly known as the Aguarico orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to the Amazon rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences. The plant produces showy, colorful flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
