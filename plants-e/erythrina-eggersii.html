Erythrina eggersii

Erythrina eggersii, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern and eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which bloom in dense clusters and are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of dye. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its fruit is a pod that splits open to release its seeds.

Erythrina eggersii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
