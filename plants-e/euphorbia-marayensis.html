Euphorbia marayensis

Euphorbia marayensis, commonly known as the Maray Mountain euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
