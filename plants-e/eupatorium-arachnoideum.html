Eupatorium arachnoideum

Eupatorium arachnoideum, commonly known as the spiderhead eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, grayish-green foliage and stems that are often covered in fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
