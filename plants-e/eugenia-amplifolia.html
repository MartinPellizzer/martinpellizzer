Eugenia amplifolia

Eugenia amplifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
