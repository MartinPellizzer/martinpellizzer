Echinospermum pachypodum

Echinospermum pachypodum, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed capsules that are covered in spines, giving it its scientific name. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water storage.

The plant contains latex, which can be toxic if ingested.
