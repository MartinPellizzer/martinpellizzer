Euonymus atropurpureus var. oblongifolius

Euonymus atropurpureus var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the red-veined elderberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves with prominent red veins that become more pronounced in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking visual interest throughout the year.
