Epilobium × brachiatum

Epilobium × brachiatum, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as stream banks, wet meadows, and marshes. The plant has slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. Its flowers are pink to reddish-purple, clustered in spikes, and bloom from June to August.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is often found in disturbed or riparian areas.
