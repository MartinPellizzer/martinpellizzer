Erysimum nervosum

Erysimum nervosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive vein pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
