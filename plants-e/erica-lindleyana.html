Erica lindleyana

Erica lindleyana, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces delicate, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage consists of small, leathery leaves that are dense and provide a lush green appearance.

Erica lindleyana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
