Euphorbia nagleri var. baliensis

Euphorbia nagleri var. baliensis, commonly known as the Bali euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the island of Bali in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to the tropical climate of Bali.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
