Exocarpos sandwicensis

Exocarpos sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian hibiscus or *Hibiscus sandwicensis*, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its fruit is a small, round, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

The species is culturally significant to Native Hawaiians and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
