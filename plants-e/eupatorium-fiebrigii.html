Eupatorium fiebrigii

Eupatorium fiebrigii, commonly known as feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eupatorium fiebrigii is known for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in treating headaches and inflammatory conditions.
