Eupatorium zunilanum

Eupatorium zunilanum, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowering period occurs in the summer months.

Eupatorium zunilanum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
