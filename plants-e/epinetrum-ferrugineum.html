Epinetrum ferrugineum

Epinetrum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty epineme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a rusty brown or reddish hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Epinetrum ferrugineum is often found in rocky or limestone areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
