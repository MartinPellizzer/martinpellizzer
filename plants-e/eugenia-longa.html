Eugenia longa

Eugenia longa, commonly known as the longleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Eugenia longa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for medicinal purposes.
