Eriogonum annuum f. roseum

Eriogonum annuum f. roseum, commonly known as the pink-tipped buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, rounded flowers that are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The foliage is usually gray-green, and the plant is known for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
