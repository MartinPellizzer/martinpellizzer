Epacris villosa

Epacris villosa, commonly known as the woolly epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
