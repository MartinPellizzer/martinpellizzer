Erigeron flagellaris var. typicus

Erigeron flagellaris var. typicus, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
