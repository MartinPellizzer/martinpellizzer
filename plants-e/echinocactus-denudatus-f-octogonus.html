Echinocactus denudatus f. octogonus

Echinocactus denudatus f. octogonus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to Mexico.

It features a dense covering of white, woolly spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
