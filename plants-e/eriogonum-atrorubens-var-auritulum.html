Eriogonum atrorubens var. auritulum

Eriogonum atrorubens var. auritulum, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is distinguished by its larger, more showy flowers compared to other varieties of the same species.
