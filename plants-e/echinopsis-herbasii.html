Echinopsis herbasii

Echinopsis herbasii, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white to pink spines and a globular, often brightly colored body. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stem. It typically blooms with yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
