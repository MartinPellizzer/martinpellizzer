Eugenia inaequiloba

Eugenia inaequiloba, commonly known as the unequal myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as a bloom. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pink and are followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types under suitable climatic conditions.
