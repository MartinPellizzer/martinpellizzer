Euploca arenitensis

Euploca arenitensis, commonly known as the sand euploca, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune ecosystems.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
