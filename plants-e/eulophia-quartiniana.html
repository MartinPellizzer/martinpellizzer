Eulophia quartiniana

Eulophia quartiniana, commonly known as the Quartin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich substrates. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Eulophia quartiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
