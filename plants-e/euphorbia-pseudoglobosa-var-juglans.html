Euphorbia pseudoglobosa var. juglans

Euphorbia pseudoglobosa var. juglans, commonly known as the black euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
