Echium cinereum

Echium cinereum, commonly known as silverweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces tall spikes of blue or purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It has a taproot system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Echium cinereum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
