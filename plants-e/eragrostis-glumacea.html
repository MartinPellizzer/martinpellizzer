Eragrostis glumacea

Eragrostis glumacea, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis glumacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
