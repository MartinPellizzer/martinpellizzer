Evolvulus holosericeus var. incomtus

Evolvulus holosericeus var. incomtus, commonly known as the silky blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its ability to climb and spread across surfaces, making it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
