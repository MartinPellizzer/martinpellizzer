Eragrostis perennis

Eragrostis perennis, commonly known as perennial lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often produce dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically blooms in late summer, producing pale green to brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage crop.
