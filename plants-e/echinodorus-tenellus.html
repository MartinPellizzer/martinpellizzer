Echinodorus tenellus

Echinodorus tenellus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy surface. This plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often used in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance. Echinodorus tenellus prefers moderate to high light conditions and can grow both submerged and emersed.

It is also known for its ability to help oxygenate water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
