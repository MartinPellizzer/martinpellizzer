Eulophia rigidifolia

Eulophia rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid leaf eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Madagascar.

It is characterized by its upright, cane-like stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are often densely packed. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Eulophia rigidifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
