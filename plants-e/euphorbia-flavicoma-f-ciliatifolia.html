Euphorbia flavicoma f. ciliatifolia

Euphorbia flavicoma f. ciliatifolia, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and fleshy leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. The plant features small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. Its leaves are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it the "ciliatifolia" designation, meaning "fringed leaves.".

This species is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.
