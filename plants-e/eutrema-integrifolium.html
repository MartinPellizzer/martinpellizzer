Eutrema integrifolium

Eutrema integrifolium, commonly known as the sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, blue-green leaves that are smooth and entire. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in salt marshes and dunes.

Eutrema integrifolium is an important species for coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
