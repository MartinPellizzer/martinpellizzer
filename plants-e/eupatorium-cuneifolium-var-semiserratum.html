Eupatorium cuneifolium var. semiserratum

Eupatorium cuneifolium var. semiserratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that are often branched and covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are lance-shaped, with a narrow base and finely toothed margins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
