Epidendrum adenocaulon

Epidendrum adenocaulon, commonly known as the velvet epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

Epidendrum adenocaulon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
