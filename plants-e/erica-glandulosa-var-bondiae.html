Erica glandulosa var. bondiae

Erica glandulosa var. bondiae, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to white, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica glandulosa var. bondiae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
