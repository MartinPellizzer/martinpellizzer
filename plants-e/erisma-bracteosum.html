Erisma bracteosum

Erisma bracteosum, commonly known as the bracted erisma, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are linear and slightly hairy, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Erisma bracteosum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
