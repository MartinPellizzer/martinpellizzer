Echinocactus trollietii

Echinocactus trollietii, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a rounded, ribbed stem with prominent spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique morphology make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
