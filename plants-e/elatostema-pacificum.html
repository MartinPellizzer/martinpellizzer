Elatostema pacificum

Elatostema pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features tiny, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, round fruits.

Elatostema pacificum plays a minor but important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
