Eurybia lyallii

Eurybia lyallii, commonly known as the Lyall's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flower heads and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal and alpine regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces numerous small, composite flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eurybia lyallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
