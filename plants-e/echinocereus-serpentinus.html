Echinocereus serpentinus

Echinocereus serpentinus, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble leaves, and it produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant prefers well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid environments, though it benefits from occasional watering during the growing season.

The Christmas cactus is also known for its ability to bloom around the holiday season, hence its common name.
