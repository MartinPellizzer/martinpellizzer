Epiphyllum speciosum

Epiphyllum speciosum, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, flat, and often segmented stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. Its leaves are usually short and rounded, and it thrives in well-draining soil with ample sunlight.

Epiphyllum speciosum is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique blooming pattern.
