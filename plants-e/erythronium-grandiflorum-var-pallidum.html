Erythronium grandiflorum var. pallidum

Erythronium grandiflorum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pale trout lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive white to pale green coloration and produces showy, nodding flowers in shades of yellow to white. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soil. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and dormancy, allowing it to survive in temperate climates.

The pale trout lily is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
