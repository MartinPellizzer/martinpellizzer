Eleutherococcus lasiogyne var. ferrugineus

Eleutherococcus lasiogyne var. ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty eleutherococcus, is a variant of the eleutherococcus plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that often have a reddish or rusty-colored underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variant is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
