Echinofossulocactus violaciflorus

Echinofossulocactus violaciflorus, commonly known as the violet-flowered echinofossulocactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its columnar stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its growth habit is typically solitary or in small clusters, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
