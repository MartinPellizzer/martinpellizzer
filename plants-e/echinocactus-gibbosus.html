Echinocactus gibbosus

Echinocactus gibbosus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a striking addition to arid gardens.
