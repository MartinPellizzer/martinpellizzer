Echeveria laxa var. setchellii

Echeveria laxa var. setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
