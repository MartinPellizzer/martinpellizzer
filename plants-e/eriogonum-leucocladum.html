Eriogonum leucocladum

Eriogonum leucocladum, commonly known as the whiteleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, white to grayish foliage and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Eriogonum leucocladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
