Endodeca dodecandra

Endodeca dodecandra, commonly known as the twelve-angled tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, multi-angled stems and broad, palmate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Endodeca dodecandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
