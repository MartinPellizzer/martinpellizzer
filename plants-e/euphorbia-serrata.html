Euphorbia serrata

Euphorbia serrata, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves with serrated edges, which help reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia serrata is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
