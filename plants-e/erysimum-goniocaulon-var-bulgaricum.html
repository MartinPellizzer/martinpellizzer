Erysimum goniocaulon var. bulgaricum

Erysimum goniocaulon var. bulgaricum, commonly known as the Balkan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has a compact, herbaceous growth habit with linear leaves and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species in its native habitat.
