Eugenia oreophila

Eugenia oreophila, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are typically edible and used in local cuisines.

Eugenia oreophila thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
