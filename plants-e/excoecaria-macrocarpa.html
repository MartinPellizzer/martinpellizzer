Excoecaria macrocarpa

Excoecaria macrocarpa, commonly known as the Natal hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
