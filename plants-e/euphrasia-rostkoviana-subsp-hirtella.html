Euphrasia rostkoviana subsp. hirtella

Euphrasia rostkoviana subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. Its small, yellowish-orange flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, distinguishing it from other members of the genus Euphrasia.
