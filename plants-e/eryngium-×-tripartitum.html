Eryngium × tripartitum

Eryngium × tripartitum, commonly known as the thistle eryngo, is a hybrid plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters and bloom from late summer to autumn.

Eryngium × tripartitum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
