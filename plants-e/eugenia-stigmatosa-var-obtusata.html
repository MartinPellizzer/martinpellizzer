Eugenia stigmatosa var. obtusata

Eugenia stigmatosa var. obtusata, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically broad and pointed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
