Echinocactus ensifer var. pallidus

Echinocactus ensifer var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
