Erythraea ramosissima var. simplex

Erythraea ramosissima var. simplex, commonly known as the simple erythraea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources, which contributes to its survival in dry conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in low-water landscapes.
