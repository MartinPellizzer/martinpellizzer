Epidendrum jasminosmum

Epidendrum jasminosmum, commonly known as the jasmine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and large, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically yellow or white with a distinctive, fragrant scent reminiscent of jasmine. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique aroma.
