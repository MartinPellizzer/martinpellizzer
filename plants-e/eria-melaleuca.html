Eria melaleuca

Eria melaleuca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf melaleuca, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its slender, often drooping stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences. Eria melaleuca is typically found in coastal and sandy soils, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
