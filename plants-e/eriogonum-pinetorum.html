Eriogonum pinetorum

Eriogonum pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass or mountain flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open coniferous forests and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a basal rosette of leaves.

Eriogonum pinetorum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
