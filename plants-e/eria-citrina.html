Eria citrina

Eria citrina, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually elongated and covered with a fine, waxy coating.

Eria citrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
