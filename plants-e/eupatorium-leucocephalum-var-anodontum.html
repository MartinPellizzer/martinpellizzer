Eupatorium leucocephalum var. anodontum

Eupatorium leucocephalum var. anodontum, commonly known as the whitehead panicled aster, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale blue flower heads that resemble small, rounded umbels. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
