Ermania villosa

Ermania villosa, commonly known as the hairy ermania, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early to mid-summer.

Ermania villosa is known for its aromatic leaves, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies.
