Eucalyptus crucis

Eucalyptus crucis, commonly known as the cross-shaped eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a silver-green underlayer, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus crucis is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species within the eucalyptus genus.
