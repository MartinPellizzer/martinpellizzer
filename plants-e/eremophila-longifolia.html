Eremophila longifolia

Eremophila longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila longifolia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
