Erica hibbertia

Erica hibbertia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica hibbertia produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
