Eriodesmia capitata

Eriodesmia capitata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact cluster at the top of the stem.

The leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
