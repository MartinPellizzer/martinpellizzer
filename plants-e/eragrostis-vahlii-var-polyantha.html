Eragrostis vahlii var. polyantha

Eragrostis vahlii var. polyantha, commonly known as the polyantha lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue, and the inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dryland environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid regions.
