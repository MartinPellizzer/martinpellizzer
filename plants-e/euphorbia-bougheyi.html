Euphorbia bougheyi

Euphorbia bougheyi, commonly known as the Boughey's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
