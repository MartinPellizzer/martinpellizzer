Encyclia powellii

Encyclia powellii, commonly known as Powell's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, spider-like flower structure. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, while its flowers are small, greenish-white with purple markings.

Encyclia powellii is known for its unique morphology and is often found in open woodlands and chaparral habitats.
