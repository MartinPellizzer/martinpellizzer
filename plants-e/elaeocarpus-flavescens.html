Elaeocarpus flavescens

Elaeocarpus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature.

Elaeocarpus flavescens is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
