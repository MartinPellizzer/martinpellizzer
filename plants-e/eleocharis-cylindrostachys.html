Eleocharis cylindrostachys

Eleocharis cylindrostachys, commonly known as the cylindrical spike rush, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikes that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
