Erica lycopodiastrum

Erica lycopodiastrum, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Erica lycopodiastrum typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers that attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
