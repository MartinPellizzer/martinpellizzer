Eupatorium odoratum f. squarrosum

Eupatorium odoratum f. squarrosum, commonly known as the roughhead purple coneflower, is a variant of the purple coneflower species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and showy purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. It has a tall, erect stem and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
