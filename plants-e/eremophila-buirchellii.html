Eremophila buirchellii

Eremophila buirchellii, commonly known as the Buirchell's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that help it access groundwater.

Eremophila buirchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
