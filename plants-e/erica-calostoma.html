Erica calostoma

Erica calostoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica calostoma thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
