Eugenia cereja

Eugenia cereja, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Eugenia cereja thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
