Epidendrum fritzimegalotylosum

Epidendrum fritzimegalotylosum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter, making it a striking addition to any garden or collection. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the dry seasons.

The orchid thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
