Eupatorium tashiroi

Eupatorium tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's boneset, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting various pollinators.
