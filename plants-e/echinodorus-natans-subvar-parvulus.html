Echinodorus natans subvar. parvulus

Echinodorus natans subvar. parvulus, commonly known as the dwarf waterweed, is a compact aquatic plant native to South America.

It features slender, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, often forming dense mats. The plant is characterized by its small, pale green leaves and is well adapted to slow-moving or still freshwater environments. It thrives in nutrient-rich substrates and requires moderate to high lighting for optimal growth.

Echinodorus natans subvar. parvulus is popular in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
