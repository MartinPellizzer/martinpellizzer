Efulensia clematoides

Efulensia clematoides, commonly known as the silverleaf clematis, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Violaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped, silver-veined leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
