Enchosanthera anomala

Enchosanthera anomala, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and resilient addition to native plant gardens.
