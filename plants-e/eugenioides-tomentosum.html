Eugenioides tomentosum

Eugenioides tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive, fluffy appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Eugenioides tomentosum is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
