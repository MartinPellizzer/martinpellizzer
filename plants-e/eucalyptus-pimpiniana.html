Eucalyptus pimpiniana

Eucalyptus pimpiniana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pink or pale yellow, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus pimpiniana is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various traditional and commercial uses.
