Escallonia rubra

Escallonia rubra, commonly known as the red escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not typically consumed by humans. Escallonia rubra thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant flowers make it an attractive addition to coastal and temperate climates.
