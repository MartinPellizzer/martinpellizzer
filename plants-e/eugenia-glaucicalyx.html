Eugenia glaucicalyx

Eugenia glaucicalyx, commonly known as the silverleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Eugenia glaucicalyx is relatively low maintenance and adapts well to a variety of soil types.
