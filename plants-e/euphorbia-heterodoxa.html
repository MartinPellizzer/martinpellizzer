Euphorbia heterodoxa

Euphorbia heterodoxa, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia heterodoxa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
