Eragrostis multiflora var. pooides

Eragrostis multiflora var. pooides, commonly known as the slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, fine-textured leaves and compact, cone-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its adaptability and drought tolerance.
