Eriocaulon nadjae

Eriocaulon nadjae, commonly known as the Nadja's spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in tropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized adaptations for water environments. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems with whorled leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically forming dense spikes at the tips of the stems.

Eriocaulon nadjae plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
