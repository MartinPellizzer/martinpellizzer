Euphorbia × tsimbazazae

Euphorbia × tsimbazazae, commonly known as the Namib Desert euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the hyperarid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in extreme drought conditions. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to high temperatures and intense sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
