Euphorbia francoisii

Euphorbia francoisii, commonly known as the Francois' spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
