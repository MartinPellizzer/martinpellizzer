Eriosyce subgibbosa subsp. wagenknechtii

Eriosyce subgibbosa subsp.

wagenknechtii, commonly known as the Wagenknecht's areca, is a species of cactus native to the dry regions of southern Peru. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months, typically from late winter to early spring. Its unique morphology and habitat make it an interesting subject for botanical study, particularly in arid ecosystems.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions, which contributes to its survival in its native range.
