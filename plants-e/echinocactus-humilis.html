Echinocactus humilis

Echinocactus humilis, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is a small, globular cactus with a short, thick stem and a crown of spiny, ribbed segments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for succulent collections and landscaping.
