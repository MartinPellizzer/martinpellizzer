Eugenia analamerensis

Eugenia analamerensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-para, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, particularly in Brazil, Peru, and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
