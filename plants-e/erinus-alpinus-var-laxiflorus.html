Erinus alpinus var. laxiflorus

Erinus alpinus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the alpine erinus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features dense clusters of small, fluffy, pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, upright stems covered in fine, silvery hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and linear, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
