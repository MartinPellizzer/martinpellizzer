Elaeocarpus amboinensis

Elaeocarpus amboinensis, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by distinctive purple or black berries. The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a thick, fleshy aril, which is the edible part.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
