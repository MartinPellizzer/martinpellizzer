Efossus heteracanthus

Efossus heteracanthus, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night. The plant features heart-shaped, velvety leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
