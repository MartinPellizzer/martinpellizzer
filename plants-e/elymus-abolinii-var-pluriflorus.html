Elymus abolinii var. pluriflorus

Elymus abolinii var. pluriflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces multiple inflorescences along its stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescences consist of spikelets that bear small, spikelet-like flowers, contributing to its ornamental value in some horticultural settings.
