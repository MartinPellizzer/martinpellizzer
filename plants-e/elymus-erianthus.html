Elymus erianthus

Elymus erianthus, commonly known as the silvergrass or bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, silver-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically blooming in late summer. Elymus erianthus is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
