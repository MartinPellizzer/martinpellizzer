Equilabium dissectum

Equilabium dissectum, commonly known as the dissected bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to August. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are finely dissected, giving it a feathery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
