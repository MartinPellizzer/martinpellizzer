Eugenia gomesiana

Eugenia gomesiana, commonly known as the Gomesian myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
