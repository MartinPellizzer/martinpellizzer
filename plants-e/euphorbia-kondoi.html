Euphorbia kondoi

Euphorbia kondoi, commonly known as the Kondoi spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its bark and sap contain toxic compounds, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Euphorbia kondoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
