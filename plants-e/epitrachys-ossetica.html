Epitrachys ossetica

Epitrachys ossetica, commonly known as the Ossetian sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are usually narrow and fleshy. It is adapted to arid environments and can tolerate extreme temperature fluctuations.

Epitrachys ossetica is often found in mountainous areas and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
