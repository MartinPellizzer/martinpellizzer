Equisetum variegatum var. virgatum

Equisetum variegatum var. virgatum, commonly known as the variable horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its hollow, jointed stems and whorled arrangement of leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are covered in small, scale-like leaves and produce spore-bearing cones at the tips.

This variety is notable for its distinct variegation in stem color, which can range from green to reddish-brown.
