Erythroxylum lancifolium

Erythroxylum lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythroxylum, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and therapeutic purposes.
