Erysimum humile subsp. gorbeanum

Erysimum humile subsp.

gorbeanum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
