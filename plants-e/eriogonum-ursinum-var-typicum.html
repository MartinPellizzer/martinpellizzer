Eriogonum ursinum var. typicum

Eriogonum ursinum var. typicum, commonly known as the bear's breeches or bear's tail, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and showy, yellowish-white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and woodland areas. It produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
