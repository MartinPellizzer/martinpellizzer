Ericoides gilvum

Ericoides gilvum, commonly known as the golden heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are typically bright green or yellowish in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in late summer. Ericoides gilvum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense ground cover in suitable habitats.
