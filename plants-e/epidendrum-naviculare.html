Epidendrum naviculare

Epidendrum naviculare, commonly known as the canoe orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near rivers or in forest understories. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink in color. Its leaves are broad and ovate, and the orchid is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types as long as they remain consistently moist.

Epidendrum naviculare is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
