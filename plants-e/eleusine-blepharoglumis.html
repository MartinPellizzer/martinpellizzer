Eleusine blepharoglumis

Eleusine blepharoglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eleusine, is a species of grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed or open habitats. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and narrow, linear leaves with a prominent midrib.

It is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value or used in ecological restoration projects.
