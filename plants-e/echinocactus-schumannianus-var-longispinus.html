Echinocactus schumannianus var. longispinus

Echinocactus schumannianus var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
