Epidendrum annabellae

Epidendrum annabellae, commonly known as the Annabell's epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are elongated and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

This orchid thrives in humid, tropical environments with bright, indirect light.
