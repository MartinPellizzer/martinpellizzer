Eria perpusilla

Eria perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. The plant produces delicate, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to conserve water in its natural habitat.

Eria perpusilla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
