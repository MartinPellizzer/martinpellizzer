Escallonia myrtilloides var. patens

Escallonia myrtilloides var. patens, commonly known as the spreading blueberry bush, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces fragrant white flowers in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
