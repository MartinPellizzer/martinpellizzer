Erica galioides

Erica galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces delicate white or pink flowers during the summer months. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is well-suited to coastal areas.

Erica galioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
