Erysimum chazarjurti

Erysimum chazarjurti, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to high altitudes. It produces small, rounded leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Erysimum chazarjurti is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
