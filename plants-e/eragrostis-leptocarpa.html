Eragrostis leptocarpa

Eragrostis leptocarpa, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, erect blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils, roadsides, and prairies. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny grains, which are a food source for various wildlife.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
