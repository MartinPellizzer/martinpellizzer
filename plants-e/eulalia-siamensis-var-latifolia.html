Eulalia siamensis var. latifolia

Eulalia siamensis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flax lily, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, with a bluish-green coloration that adds visual interest to gardens. The plant produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in summer, typically in shades of pink or purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
