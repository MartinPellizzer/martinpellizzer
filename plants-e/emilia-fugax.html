Emilia fugax

Emilia fugax, commonly known as the annual blueweed, is an herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Emilia fugax has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It blooms from late spring to early autumn, producing copious amounts of nectar that attract various pollinators.
