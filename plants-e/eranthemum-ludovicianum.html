Eranthemum ludovicianum

Eranthemum ludovicianum, commonly known as the redroot eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wetlands. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically pink or red and bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
