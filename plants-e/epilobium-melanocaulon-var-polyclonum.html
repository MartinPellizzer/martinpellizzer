Epilobium melanocaulon var. polyclonum

Epilobium melanocaulon var. polyclonum, commonly known as the dark-stemmed willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as along stream banks and in wet meadows. The plant features slender, often reddish stems that are covered with fine, glandular hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and are pollinated primarily by insects.
