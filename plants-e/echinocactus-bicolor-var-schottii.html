Echinocactus bicolor var. schottii

Echinocactus bicolor var. schottii, commonly known as the Schott's golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
