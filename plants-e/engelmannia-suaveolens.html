Engelmannia suaveolens

Engelmannia suaveolens, commonly known as sweetleaf or sweetbriar, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Engelmannia suaveolens is often found in woodlands and along streambanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
