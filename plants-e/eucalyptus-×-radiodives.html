Eucalyptus × radiodives

Eucalyptus × radiodives, commonly known as the silver dollar gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to south-western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant produces small, urn-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
