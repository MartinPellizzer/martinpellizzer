Erythroxylum burmanicum

Erythroxylum burmanicum, commonly known as Burman's copal, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is adapted to thrive in warm, moist environments.

The plant has historical significance in traditional medicine and has been used for various therapeutic purposes by indigenous communities.
