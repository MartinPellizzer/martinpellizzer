Echinocereus longisetus

Echinocereus longisetus, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which are typically long and straight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are borne at the areoles on the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Echinocereus longisetus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
