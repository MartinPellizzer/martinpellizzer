Eupatorium leonardii

Eupatorium leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
