Endosiphon primuloides var. hirsuta

Endosiphon primuloides var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy endosiphon, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its leaves are generally ovate with a rough texture, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink in color.
