Echinocactus dietrichianus

Echinocactus dietrichianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent, ribbed structure, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Echinocactus dietrichianus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
