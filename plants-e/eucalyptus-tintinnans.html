Eucalyptus tintinnans

Eucalyptus tintinnans, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pink or red, which are clustered in groups of three. Eucalyptus tintinnans is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
