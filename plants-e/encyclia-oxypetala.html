Encyclia oxypetala

Encyclia oxypetala, commonly known as the sharp-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces striking, fragrant flowers with elongated petals. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, and its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, violet or purple flowers. Encyclia oxypetala prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
