Elaeocarpus gadgilii

Elaeocarpus gadgilii, commonly known as the Gadgil's elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds. This species is typically found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical and subtropical forests.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
