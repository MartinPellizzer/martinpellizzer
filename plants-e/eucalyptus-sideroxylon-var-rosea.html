Eucalyptus sideroxylon var. rosea

Eucalyptus sideroxylon var. rosea, commonly known as the rose gum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a grey-green hue. This species is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
