Eriogonum concinnum

Eriogonum concinnum, commonly known as the pink curlcup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is characterized by its soft, gray-green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. Eriogonum concinnum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, well-drained conditions.
