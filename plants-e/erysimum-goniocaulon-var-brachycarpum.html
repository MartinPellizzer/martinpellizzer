Erysimum goniocaulon var. brachycarpum

Erysimum goniocaulon var. brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The variety brachycarpum is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Erysimum goniocaulon.
