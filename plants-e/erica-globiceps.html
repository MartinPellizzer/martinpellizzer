Erica globiceps

Erica globiceps, commonly known as the globular heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming in late summer. Erica globiceps is well adapted to sandy soils and can tolerate salt spray, making it a hardy coastal plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
