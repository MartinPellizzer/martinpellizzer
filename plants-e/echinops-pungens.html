Echinops pungens

Echinops pungens, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Echinops pungens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
