Eryngium hemisphaericum var. abbreviatum

Eryngium hemisphaericum var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compact, spiky appearance with blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

The species is notable for its unique floral structure and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
