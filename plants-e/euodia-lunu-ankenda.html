Euodia lunu-ankenda

Euodia lunu-ankenda, commonly known as the Sri Lankan tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as citrus and rue. The tree is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, elongated fruits. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fibrous, yellowish pulp, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the biodiversity of Sri Lanka's tropical ecosystems.
