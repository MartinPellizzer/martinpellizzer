Eremophea spinosa

Eremophea spinosa, commonly known as the spiny eremophea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny foliage and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

Eremophea spinosa is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
