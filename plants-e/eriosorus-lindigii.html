Eriosorus lindigii

Eriosorus lindigii, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of fern native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Eriosorus lindigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
