Embothrium tinctorium

Embothrium tinctorium, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Embothrium tinctorium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional dyeing practices.
