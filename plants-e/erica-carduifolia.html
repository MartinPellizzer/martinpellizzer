Erica carduifolia

Erica carduifolia, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in winter, adding a delicate touch to its evergreen foliage. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica carduifolia is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
