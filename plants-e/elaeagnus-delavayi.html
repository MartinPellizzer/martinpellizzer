Elaeagnus delavayi

Elaeagnus delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Elaeagnus delavayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
