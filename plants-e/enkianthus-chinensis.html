Enkianthus chinensis

Enkianthus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese lantern tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble lanterns, which appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Enkianthus chinensis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
