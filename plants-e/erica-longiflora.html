Erica longiflora

Erica longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Erica longiflora is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique type of shrubland ecosystem.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.
