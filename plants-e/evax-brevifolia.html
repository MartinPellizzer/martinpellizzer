Evax brevifolia

Evax brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved evax, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stem. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, which helps to reduce water loss in its native arid environment.

Evax brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
