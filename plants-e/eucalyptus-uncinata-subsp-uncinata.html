Eucalyptus uncinata subsp. uncinata

Eucalyptus uncinata subsp.

uncinata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of grey or white felt-like hairs.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually white or pale blue in color. The plant produces small, woody capsules that contain numerous seeds.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
