Eupatorium kalenbornianum

Eupatorium kalenbornianum, commonly known as Kalenborn's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for its purported health benefits.

The plant's name reflects its historical use in treating ailments, particularly those related to the skeletal system.
