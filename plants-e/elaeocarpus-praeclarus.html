Elaeocarpus praeclarus

Elaeocarpus praeclarus, commonly known as the golden elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elliptical leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
