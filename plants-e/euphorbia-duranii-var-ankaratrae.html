Euphorbia duranii var. ankaratrae

Euphorbia duranii var. ankaratrae, commonly known as the Ankaratra euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces a milky sap that is toxic.

This variety is part of the Euphorbiaceae family and is valued for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
