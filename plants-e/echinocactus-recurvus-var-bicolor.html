Echinocactus recurvus var. bicolor

Echinocactus recurvus var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that form a dense, globular shape. The plant features a distinctive coloration pattern, with a greenish-blue body and yellowish or reddish spines. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

This cactus is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a slow growth rate.
