Euphorbia meloformis

Euphorbia meloformis, commonly known as the melon-spur euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with paired, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia meloformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
