Epilobium ramosum

Epilobium ramosum, commonly known as willow herb, is a flowering plant in the family Onagraceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. The plant features upright stems that are often reddish in color and are covered with fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a serrated margin.

The showy pink to red flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom from late spring to early summer.
