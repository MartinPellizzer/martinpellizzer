Eugenia capensis subsp. gracilipes

Eugenia capensis subsp.

gracilipes, commonly known as the slender-stemmed bushwillow, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually hairless.

The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that release seeds when mature.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
