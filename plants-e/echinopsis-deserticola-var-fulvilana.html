Echinopsis deserticola var. fulvilana

Echinopsis deserticola var. fulvilana, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines and a central stem that often produces yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water requirements. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise muted appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry environments.
