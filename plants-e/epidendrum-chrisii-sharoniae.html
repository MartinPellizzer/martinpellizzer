Epidendrum chrisii-sharoniae

Epidendrum chrisii-sharoniae, commonly known as the Sharonae Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
