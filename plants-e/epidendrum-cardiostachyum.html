Epidendrum cardiostachyum

Epidendrum cardiostachyum, commonly known as the heart-shaped spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
