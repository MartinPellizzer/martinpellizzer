Elymus trachycaulus subsp. donianus

Elymus trachycaulus subsp.

donianus, commonly known as the Donian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, needle-like leaves that give it its common name.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated for erosion control and as a forage plant.
