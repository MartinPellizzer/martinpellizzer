Eugenia augustana

Eugenia augustana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its fragrant, edible berries that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and wetlands.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a problematic species.
