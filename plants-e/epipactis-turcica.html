Epipactis turcica

Epipactis turcica, commonly known as the Turkish helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and meadows. It produces a single, erect stem that bears a single flower, which is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Epipactis turcica is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
