Eupatorium theifolium var. typicum

Eupatorium theifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the common boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-blue color. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological studies.
