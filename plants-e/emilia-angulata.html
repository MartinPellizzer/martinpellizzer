Emilia angulata

Emilia angulata, commonly known as the angular emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in a cluster, often surrounded by bracts. Emilia angulata is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its leaves are usually angular and covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name.
