Eragrostis capillaris var. frankii

Eragrostis capillaris var. frankii, commonly known as Frank's slendergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
