Eupatorium benjamin-lincolnii var. vernicosum

Eupatorium benjamin-lincolnii var. vernicosum, commonly known as the Benjamin Lincoln's eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and contributes to ecological diversity in its habitat.
