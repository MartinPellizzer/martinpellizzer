Eupatorium baccharoides

Eupatorium baccharoides, commonly known as saltmarsh fleabane, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It thrives in brackish or saline environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant has aromatic, glandular leaves and produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its growth habit is typically herbaceous, with a sprawling or upright form depending on the habitat.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
