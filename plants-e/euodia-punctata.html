Euodia punctata

Euodia punctata, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces a fragrant resin that has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.

Euodia punctata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic qualities.
