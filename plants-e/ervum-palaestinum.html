Ervum palaestinum

Ervum palaestinum, commonly known as the Palestinian vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow pea-like flowers. The plant produces slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is often found in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Ervum palaestinum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
