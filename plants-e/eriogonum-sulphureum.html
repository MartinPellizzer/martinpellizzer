Eriogonum sulphureum

Eriogonum sulphureum, commonly known as the sulphur flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

The sulphur flax plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators.
