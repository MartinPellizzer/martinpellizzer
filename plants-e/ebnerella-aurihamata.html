Ebnerella aurihamata

Ebnerella aurihamata, commonly known as the golden-hamate orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange inflorescences, which are adorned with intricate, hamate-shaped petals and sepals. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded forest understories, where it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its unique floral structure and coloration make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
