Eugenia emarginata

Eugenia emarginata, commonly known as the margined eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit, a small, leathery drupe, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

Eugenia emarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
