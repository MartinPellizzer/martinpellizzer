Eucalyptus × kirtoniana

Eucalyptus × kirtoniana, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and smooth, peeling bark. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and urban environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is widely cultivated for landscaping and essential oil production.
