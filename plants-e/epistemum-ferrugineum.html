Epistemum ferrugineum

Epistemum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty epistemum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a distinctive rust-colored hue on the petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, hairy texture.

Epistemum ferrugineum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
