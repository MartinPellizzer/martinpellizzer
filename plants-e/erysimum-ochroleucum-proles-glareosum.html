Erysimum ochroleucum proles glareosum

Erysimum ochroleucum proles glareosum, commonly known as the yellow cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in temperate regions of Europe. It has small, siliquous fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
