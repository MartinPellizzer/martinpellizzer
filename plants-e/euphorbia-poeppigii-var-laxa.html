Euphorbia poeppigii var. laxa

Euphorbia poeppigii var. laxa, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent shrub native to Mexico.

It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant has a milky latex sap that can be irritating to the skin. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its appeal among succulent enthusiasts.
