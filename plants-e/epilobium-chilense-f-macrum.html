Epilobium chilense f. macrum

Epilobium chilense f. macrum, commonly known as the Chilean willow herb, is a botanical variant of the species Epilobium chilense, native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
