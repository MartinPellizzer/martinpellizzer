Elionurus tripsacoides var. sericeus

Elionurus tripsacoides var. sericeus, commonly known as the silky grass or silky panicum, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically arranged in a panicle-like structure. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
