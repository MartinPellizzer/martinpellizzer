Evolvulus agrestis

Evolvulus agrestis, commonly known as the common blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a trailing annual vine that produces small, blue or violet flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both useful and invasive in certain regions.
