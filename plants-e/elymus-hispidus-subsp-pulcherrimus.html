Elymus hispidus subsp. pulcherrimus

Elymus hispidus subsp.

pulcherrimus, commonly known as the showy wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces panicles of pale to golden florets that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
