Espeletia elongata

Espeletia elongata, commonly known as the "Cape of Good Hope" or "Puya raimondii," is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures. Its flowers, which bloom in shades of yellow and orange, are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators.

Espeletia elongata is also notable for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
