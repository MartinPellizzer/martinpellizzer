Eupatorium argentinum

Eupatorium argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine boneset, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called cymes. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Eupatorium argentinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
