Eriosema squarrosum var. acuminatum

Eriosema squarrosum var. acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-pointed eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems covered in fine, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its unique botanical profile.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
