Elaphoglossum rudolphi

Elaphoglossum rudolphi, commonly known as the Rudolphi's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive pattern of pinnae and pinnules. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, arranged in a unique, linear pattern.

Elaphoglossum rudolphi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
