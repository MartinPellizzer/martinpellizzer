Elodea kochii

Elodea kochii, commonly known as waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, dark green leaves. The plant is typically submerged in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Elodea kochii reproduces through both sexual and asexual means, often spreading via stolons or by releasing floating seeds.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
