Eupatorium bangii

Eupatorium bangii, commonly known as the Java feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a distinctive aromatic scent.

Eupatorium bangii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
