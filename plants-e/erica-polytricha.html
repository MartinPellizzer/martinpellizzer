Erica polytricha

Erica polytricha, commonly known as the heath plant, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica polytricha is well adapted to nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Its resilience to drought and frost makes it a hardy ornamental plant in suitable climates.
