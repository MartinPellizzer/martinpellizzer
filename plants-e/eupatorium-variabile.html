Eupatorium variabile

Eupatorium variabile, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium variabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
