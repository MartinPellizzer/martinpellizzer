Eulophia exaltata var. sarasinorum

Eulophia exaltata var. sarasinorum, commonly known as the Sarasin's spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, often branched stem and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces inflorescences of showy, fragrant flowers with three sepals and three petals, typically in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
