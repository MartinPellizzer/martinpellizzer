Eulophia grantii

Eulophia grantii, commonly known as the Grant's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Eulophia grantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
