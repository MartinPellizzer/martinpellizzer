Erigeron obliquus

Erigeron obliquus, commonly known as hoary frostweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are covered in fine white hairs, giving it a frost-like appearance, and its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
