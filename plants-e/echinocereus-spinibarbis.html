Echinocereus spinibarbis

Echinocereus spinibarbis, commonly known as the hooked hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, backward-pointing spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, typically at the base of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
