Eurybia × herveyi

Eurybia × herveyi, commonly known as the Hervey's golden groundsel, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and is native to the eastern United States. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces numerous small florets in a single flower head.

Eurybia × herveyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
