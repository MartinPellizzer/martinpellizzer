Euphorbia ambroseae var. ambroseae

Euphorbia ambroseae var. ambroseae, commonly known as the Ambrose's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with distinctive, fleshy leaves that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
