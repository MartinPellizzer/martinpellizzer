Eucalyptus hypostomatica

Eucalyptus hypostomatica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions.

This species is often found in shrublands and is an important part of the local flora in its native habitat.
