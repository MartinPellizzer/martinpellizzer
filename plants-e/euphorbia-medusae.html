Euphorbia medusae

Euphorbia medusae, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, tree-like structure with thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, giving it a unique and rugged appearance.

Euphorbia medusae is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for arid landscapes.
