Echinocereus engelmannii subsp. variegatus

Echinocereus engelmannii subsp.

variegatus, commonly known as the variegated hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing, spiny cactus with a distinctive appearance featuring bright green to blue-green stems covered in white or pale blue spines.

The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique coloration and resilience make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
