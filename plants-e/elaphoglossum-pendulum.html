Elaphoglossum pendulum

Elaphoglossum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging holly fern, is a delicate fern species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance, often growing in shaded, moist environments. The plant's spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically arranged along the margins of the leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique pendulous growth habit and intricate leaf structure make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
