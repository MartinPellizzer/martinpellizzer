Echium medium

Echium medium, commonly known as the common thimbleweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces striking blue to violet flowers in late summer. The plant features a straight, branching stem covered in fine hairs and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium medium is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun.
