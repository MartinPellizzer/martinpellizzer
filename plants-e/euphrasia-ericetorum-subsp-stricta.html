Euphrasia ericetorum subsp. stricta

Euphrasia ericetorum subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eyebright, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodlands and heathlands.

The plant features small, yellow-orange flowers with prominent yellow spots, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported eye-health benefits.
