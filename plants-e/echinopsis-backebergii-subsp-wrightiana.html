Echinopsis backebergii subsp. wrightiana

Echinopsis backebergii subsp.

wrightiana, commonly known as the Wright's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to South Africa. It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stems and is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the cooler months, adding ornamental value. It requires minimal water and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
