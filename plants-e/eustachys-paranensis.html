Eustachys paranensis

Eustachys paranensis, commonly known as the Parana grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

Eustachys paranensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
