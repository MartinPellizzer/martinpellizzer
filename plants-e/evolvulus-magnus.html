Evolvulus magnus

Evolvulus magnus, commonly known as the giant blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the late afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and vine-like habit make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, climbing plants.
