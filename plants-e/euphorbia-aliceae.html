Euphorbia aliceae

Euphorbia aliceae, commonly known as Alice's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often spiny structures. The plant features a short, thick stem with clustered leaves that are typically green and fleshy. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is a defining feature of the genus Euphorbia.

It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy ornamental plant.
