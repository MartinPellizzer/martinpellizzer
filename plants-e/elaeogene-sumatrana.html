Elaeogene sumatrana

Elaeogene sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Sumatra.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain hard seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable to extinction.
