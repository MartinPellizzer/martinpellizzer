Epiphyllum thomasianum

Epiphyllum thomasianum, commonly known as the Thomasian night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus native to Mexico and is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are green and photosynthetic. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. These flowers are followed by small, red berries that contain the seeds.

Epiphyllum thomasianum is often grown as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
