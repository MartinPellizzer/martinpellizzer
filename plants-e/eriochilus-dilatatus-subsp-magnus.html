Eriochilus dilatatus subsp. magnus

Eriochilus dilatatus subsp.

magnus, commonly known as the large-flowered eriochilus, is a perennial herb native to the southern regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to the dry, Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
