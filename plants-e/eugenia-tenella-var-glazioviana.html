Eugenia tenella var. glazioviana

Eugenia tenella var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's roughleaf eugenia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom during the wet season. These flowers are followed by small, round, edible fruits that are typically red or orange in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
