Epidendrum beatricis-vasqueziae

Epidendrum beatricis-vasqueziae, commonly known as the Beatrice-Vasquez orchid, is a species of orchid native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous flowers with a unique combination of colors, often featuring a mix of white, purple, and pink hues. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are typically fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts due to its beauty and rarity.
