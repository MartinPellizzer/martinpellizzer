Eupatorium holguinense

Eupatorium holguinense, commonly known as the Cuban boneset, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium holguinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
