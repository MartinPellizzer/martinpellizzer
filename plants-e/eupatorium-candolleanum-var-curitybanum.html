Eupatorium candolleanum var. curitybanum

Eupatorium candolleanum var. curitybanum, commonly known as the Curitiba eupatorium, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
