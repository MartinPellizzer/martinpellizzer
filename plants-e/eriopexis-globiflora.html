Eriopexis globiflora

Eriopexis globiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, globe-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
