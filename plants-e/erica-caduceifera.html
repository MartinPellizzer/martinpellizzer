Erica caduceifera

Erica caduceifera, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica caduceifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, compact foliage.
