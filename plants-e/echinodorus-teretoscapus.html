Echinodorus teretoscapus

Echinodorus teretoscapus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread rapidly in aquatic environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color.

Echinodorus teretoscapus is often used in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to oxygenate water.
