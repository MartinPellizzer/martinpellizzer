Echinolobium pallidum

Echinolobium pallidum, commonly known as the pale globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, globe-shaped flower heads composed of numerous small, pale yellow to white petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually basal, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.

Echinolobium pallidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
