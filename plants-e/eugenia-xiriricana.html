Eugenia xiriricana

Eugenia xiriricana, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, bright red berries. Eugenia xiriricana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and landscapes.
