Echinops araxinus

Echinops araxinus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its flowers are small and blue to purple in color, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Echinops araxinus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
