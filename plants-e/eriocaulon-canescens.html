Eriocaulon canescens

Eriocaulon canescens, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that are covered in a silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often near the base of the plant.

Eriocaulon canescens is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
