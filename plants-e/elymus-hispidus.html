Elymus hispidus

Elymus hispidus, commonly known as the rough false oat, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant has rough, hairy leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals.

Elymus hispidus is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species.
