Embothrium minus

Embothrium minus, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile and western Argentina.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often accompanied by a strong, spicy aroma. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery down. Its fruit is a small, red, berry-like structure that ripens in late summer.

Embothrium minus is well adapted to coastal climates, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
