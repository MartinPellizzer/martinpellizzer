Evolvulus elegans var. confertifolius

Evolvulus elegans var. confertifolius, commonly known as the thickleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous annual vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, ovate leaves that are often arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in the morning. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
