Eurya rostrata

Eurya rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. Eurya rostrata thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in shaded garden settings.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
