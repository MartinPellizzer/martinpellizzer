Euphorbia cuneata subsp. spinescens

Euphorbia cuneata subsp.

spinescens, commonly known as the spiny euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, spiny stems.

The plant produces yellowish-green花朵, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

Due to its spines and drought tolerance, it is well adapted to harsh, dry climates.
