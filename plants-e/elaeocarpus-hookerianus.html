Elaeocarpus hookerianus

Elaeocarpus hookerianus, commonly known as the Hooker's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
