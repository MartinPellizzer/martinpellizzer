Eupatorium urticifolium var. verticillatum

Eupatorium urticifolium var. verticillatum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences.

The species is often found in wetlands and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
