Eugenia pluriflora var. opaca

Eugenia pluriflora var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca guava, is a species native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, sweet fruits with a distinctive flavor. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, providing dense foliage.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
