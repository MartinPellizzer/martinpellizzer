Erica steinbergiana var. abbreviata

Erica steinbergiana var. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a compact evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, dense clusters of needle-like leaves and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires full sun to partial shade. It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its compact size and ornamental value.

Its foliage remains evergreen, providing year-round interest in the garden.
