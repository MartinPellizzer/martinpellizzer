Echinops kasakorum

Echinops kasakorum, commonly known as the Kaskarum thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical crown of bracts and its yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppes and rocky soils. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.

Echinops kasakorum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual appeal.
