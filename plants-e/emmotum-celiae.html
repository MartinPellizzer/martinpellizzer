Emmotum celiae

Emmotum celiae, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Emmotum celiae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
