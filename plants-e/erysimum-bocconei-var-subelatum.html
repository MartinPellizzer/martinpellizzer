Erysimum bocconei var. subelatum

Erysimum bocconei var. subelatum, commonly known as the San Benito wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to California.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
