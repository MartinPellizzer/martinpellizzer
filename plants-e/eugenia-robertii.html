Eugenia robertii

Eugenia robertii, commonly known as the Robert's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, though they are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.

Eugenia robertii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
