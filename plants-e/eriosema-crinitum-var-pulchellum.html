Eriosema crinitum var. pulchellum

Eriosema crinitum var. pulchellum, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is noted for its ornamental value due to its attractive, velvety appearance.
