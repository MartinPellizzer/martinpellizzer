Epidendrum vareschii

Epidendrum vareschii, commonly known as the Vareschii Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Epidendrum vareschii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
