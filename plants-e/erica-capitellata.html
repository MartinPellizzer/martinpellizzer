Erica capitellata

Erica capitellata, commonly known as the heather bell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica capitellata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
