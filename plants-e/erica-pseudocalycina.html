Erica pseudocalycina

Erica pseudocalycina, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica pseudocalycina is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
