Ephedra procera var. chrysocarpa

Ephedra procera var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange to red fruits. Its stems contain ephedrine, a compound historically used in traditional medicine.

This species is adapted to harsh environments and is often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.
