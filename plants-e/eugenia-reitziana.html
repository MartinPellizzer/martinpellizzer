Eugenia reitziana

Eugenia reitziana, commonly known as the reitziana myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in perfumery.
