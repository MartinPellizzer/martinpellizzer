Erodium filicinum

Erodium filicinum, commonly known as the water crowfoot, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating on the water surface. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in shallow water and moist soils, making it a common sight in ponds and streams.

Erodium filicinum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
