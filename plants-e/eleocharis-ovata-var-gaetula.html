Eleocharis ovata var. gaetula

Eleocharis ovata var. gaetula, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland saline environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
