Erodium neuradifolium var. aegyptiacum

Erodium neuradifolium var. aegyptiacum, commonly known as Egyptian false flax, is a flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves and pink or purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, legume-like pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
