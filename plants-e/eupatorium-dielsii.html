Eupatorium dielsii

Eupatorium dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium dielsii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
