Eulophia saundersiana

Eulophia saundersiana, commonly known as the Saunders' eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia saundersiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
