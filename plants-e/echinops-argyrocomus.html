Echinops argyrocomus

Echinops argyrocomus, commonly known as the silver-leaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and silvery-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are followed by the distinctive, spiky seed heads.

Echinops argyrocomus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
