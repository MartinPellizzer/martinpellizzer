Eupatorium orgyale

Eupatorium orgyale, commonly known as the sacred weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium orgyale is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine and ritual practices.
