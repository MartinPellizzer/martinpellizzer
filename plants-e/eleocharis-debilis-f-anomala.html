Eleocharis debilis f. anomala

Eleocharis debilis f. anomala, commonly known as the slender spike rush, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and wetland biodiversity.
