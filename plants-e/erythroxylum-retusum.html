Erythroxylum retusum

Erythroxylum retusum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its woody structure and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and open areas.

The fruit of Erythroxylum retusum is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.
