Eriocapitella elegans

Eriocapitella elegans, commonly known as the velvethead or velvetcap, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are small, clustered, and often pale in color. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its ornamental value due to its compact size and attractive foliage.

Eriocapitella elegans is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
