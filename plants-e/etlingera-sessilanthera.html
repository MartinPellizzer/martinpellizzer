Etlingera sessilanthera

Etlingera sessilanthera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved torch ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, sword-shaped leaves and striking red or orange bract-like flowers that resemble torches. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix enclosed by a showy, petal-like bract, which is a common feature among ginger family plants.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
