Elaeocarpus moratii

Elaeocarpus moratii, commonly known as the Moratii's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and showy, white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, woody fruits that are typically clustered in spherical heads, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
