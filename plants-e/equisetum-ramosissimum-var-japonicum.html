Equisetum ramosissimum var. japonicum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are typically found in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.
