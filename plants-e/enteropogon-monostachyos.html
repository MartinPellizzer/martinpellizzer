Enteropogon monostachyos

Enteropogon monostachyos, commonly known as the purple false rice, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and small, pale purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
