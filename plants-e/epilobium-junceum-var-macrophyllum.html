Epilobium junceum var. macrophyllum

Epilobium junceum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy pink to reddish-purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
