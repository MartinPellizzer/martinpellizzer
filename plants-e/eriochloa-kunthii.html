Eriochloa kunthii

Eriochloa kunthii, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures on its leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eriochloa kunthii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
