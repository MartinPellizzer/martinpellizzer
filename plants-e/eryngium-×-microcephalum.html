Eryngium × microcephalum

Eryngium × microcephalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by rigid, silver-gray bracts. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to the eastern and central United States, it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils.

Eryngium × microcephalum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
