Eucalyptus myriadena subsp. parviflora

Eucalyptus myriadena subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the slender-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the winter months. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
