Euphorbia flindersica

Euphorbia flindersica, commonly known as the Flinders Island tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, evergreen foliage composed of opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny seed capsules. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and succulent-like properties.

Euphorbia flindersica is often found in coastal and inland regions of South Australia and the Flinders Ranges.
