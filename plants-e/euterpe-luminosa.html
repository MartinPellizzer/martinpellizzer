Euterpe luminosa

Euterpe luminosa, commonly known as the luminous bamboo, is a species of palm native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its unique name is derived from its bioluminescent properties, which are observed in its flower buds at night.

This rare trait makes it a subject of scientific interest and ecological importance.
