Eupatorium sordescens

Eupatorium sordescens, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, clustered in heads, and bloom in late summer to early fall.
