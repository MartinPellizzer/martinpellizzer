Epidendrum medinae

Epidendrum medinae, commonly known as the Medina Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

Epidendrum medinae is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
