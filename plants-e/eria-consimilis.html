Eria consimilis

Eria consimilis, commonly known as the similar orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, epiphytic plant that grows on trees and rocks in tropical rainforests. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale green flowers. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
