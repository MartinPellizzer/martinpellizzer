Elymus elymoides subsp. elymoides

Elymus elymoides subsp.

elymoides, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, sandy soils, and disturbed areas.

The plant has narrow, blue-green leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. It is well adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
