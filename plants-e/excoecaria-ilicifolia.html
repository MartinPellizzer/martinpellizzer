Excoecaria ilicifolia

Excoecaria ilicifolia, commonly known as the Christmasberry, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are edible when ripe.

The plant is adapted to coastal and tropical environments, often found in sandy soils and along shorelines.
