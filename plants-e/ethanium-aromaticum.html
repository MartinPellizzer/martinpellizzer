Ethanium aromaticum

Ethanium aromaticum, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, aromatic white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their soothing properties. Ethanium aromaticum prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its essential oils are valued for their calming effects and are often used in aromatherapy.
