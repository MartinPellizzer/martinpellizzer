Eragrostis decumbens

Eragrostis decumbens, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its unique, cascading appearance.
