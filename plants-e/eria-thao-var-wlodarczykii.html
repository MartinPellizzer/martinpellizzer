Eria thao var. wlodarczykii

Eria thao var. wlodarczykii, commonly known as the Wlodarczyk's ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and unique, translucent greenish-white flowers that resemble ghostly apparitions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the rainy season, typically in the summer months. It thrives in shaded, humid environments, often growing on tree bark or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
