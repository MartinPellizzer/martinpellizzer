Euphorbia denisiana var. ankarensis

Euphorbia denisiana var. ankarensis, commonly known as the Ankarensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to eastern Africa.

It features a caudex, which is a thickened stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flowers typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a slow growth rate.

This species is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in succulent collections.
