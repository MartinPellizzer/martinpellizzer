Euonymus angulatus

Euonymus angulatus, commonly known as the angular euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by distinctive red, berry-like fruits that persist into winter. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Euonymus angulatus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
