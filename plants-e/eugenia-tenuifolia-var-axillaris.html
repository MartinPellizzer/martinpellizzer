Eugenia tenuifolia var. axillaris

Eugenia tenuifolia var. axillaris, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple guava, is a compact evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries resembling pineapples in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for container gardening.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet fruit flavor.
