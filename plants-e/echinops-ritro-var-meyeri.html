Echinops ritro var. meyeri

Echinops ritro var. meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are typically blue to purple in color. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.

Echinops ritro var. meyeri is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
