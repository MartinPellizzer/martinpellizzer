Echinospermum occidentale

Echinospermum occidentale, commonly known as the western echinosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow to orange in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
