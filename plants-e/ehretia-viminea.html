Ehretia viminea

Ehretia viminea, commonly known as the silverleaf ehretia, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique foliage and compact growth habit, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
