Elaeocarpus vaccinioides

Elaeocarpus vaccinioides, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, and it produces dark blue, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
