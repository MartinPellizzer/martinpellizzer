Epidendrum gualaquicense

Epidendrum gualaquicense, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum gualaquicense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
