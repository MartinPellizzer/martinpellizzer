Eugenioides glaucescens

Eugenioides glaucescens, commonly known as the silver eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish-white waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. It is typically shrub-like in growth habit and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Eugenioides glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
