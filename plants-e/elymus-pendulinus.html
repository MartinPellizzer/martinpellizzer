Elymus pendulinus

Elymus pendulinus, commonly known as the hanging grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Elymus pendulinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
