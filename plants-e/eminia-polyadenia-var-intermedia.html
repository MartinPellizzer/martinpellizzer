Eminia polyadenia var. intermedia

Eminia polyadenia var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in coastal areas where it thrives in sandy soils. It produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in traditional medicine.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to saline environments and its role in coastal ecosystems.
