Eriogonum cithariforme var. agninum

Eriogonum cithariforme var. agninum, commonly known as the silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
