Eucalyptus parviflora

Eucalyptus parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often giving it a delicate appearance. It is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

Eucalyptus parviflora is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
