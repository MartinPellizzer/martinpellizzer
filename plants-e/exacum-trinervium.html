Exacum trinervium

Exacum trinervium, commonly known as the three-veined morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. Exacum trinervium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
