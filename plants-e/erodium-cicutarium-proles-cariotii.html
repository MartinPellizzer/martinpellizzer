Erodium cicutarium proles cariotii

Erodium cicutarium proles cariotii, commonly known as the hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 30 cm tall, with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom from spring to summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine hairs, and its fruits are small, dry capsules containing several seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.
