Eritrichium pseudostrictum

Eritrichium pseudostrictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf mouse-eared cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white.

Eritrichium pseudostrictum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
