Erysimum ruscinonense

Erysimum ruscinonense, commonly known as the Balkan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Europe.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it often forms dense clusters along the ground.

Erysimum ruscinonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
