Elytrigia aegilopoides

Elytrigia aegilopoides, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and roadside environments. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Elytrigia aegilopoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
