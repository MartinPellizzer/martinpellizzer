Erica spissifolia

Erica spissifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved heath, is a compact evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a distinctive, bushy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica spissifolia is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
