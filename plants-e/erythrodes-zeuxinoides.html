Erythrodes zeuxinoides

Erythrodes zeuxinoides, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant red petals and intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a unique color contrast between the petals and sepals.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
