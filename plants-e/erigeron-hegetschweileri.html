Erigeron hegetschweileri

Erigeron hegetschweileri, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its silvery foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
