Echinospermum canum

Echinospermum canum, commonly known as the spiny sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss. Its fruit is a dry capsule containing several small, hard seeds.

Echinospermum canum is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
