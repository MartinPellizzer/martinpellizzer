Epidendrum palmeri

Epidendrum palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its terrestrial growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with a distinctive spurred shape. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the arid environments it inhabits.

Epidendrum palmeri is often found in rocky soils and is known for its resilience in harsh, dry conditions.
