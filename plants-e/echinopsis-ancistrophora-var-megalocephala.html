Echinopsis ancistrophora var. megalocephala

Echinopsis ancistrophora var. megalocephala, commonly known as the giant white star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant has a short, cylindrical stem with spiny areoles, and its leaves are typically reduced to spines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming pattern, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
