Emilia subscaposa

Emilia subscaposa, commonly known as the blueweed or bluecup, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly rough texture. Emilia subscaposa thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological gardens.
