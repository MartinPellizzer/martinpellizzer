Eragrostis pooides var. suaveolens

Eragrostis pooides var. suaveolens, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often produce a pleasant, aromatic scent. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.
