Euphorbia ocellata var. typica

Euphorbia ocellata var. typica, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, palmate leaves with a distinctive white or yellowish veining pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are its unique flower-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and ability to thrive with minimal care.
