Epilobium × intermedium

Epilobium × intermedium, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces showy pink or purple flowers in clusters at the ends of the stems, typically blooming from June to August. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture.

Epilobium × intermedium is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in wet meadows, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
