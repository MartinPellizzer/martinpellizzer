Eurybia gunniana var. scaberula

Eurybia gunniana var. scaberula, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It features a rosette of basal leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
