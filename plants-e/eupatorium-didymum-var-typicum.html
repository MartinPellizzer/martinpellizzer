Eupatorium didymum var. typicum

Eupatorium didymum var. typicum, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. It is known for its aromatic foliage and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

However, it is toxic to livestock and can cause serious illness if ingested.
