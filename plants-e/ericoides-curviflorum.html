Ericoides curviflorum

Ericoides curviflorum, commonly known as the curlflower heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features compact, leathery leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Ericoides curviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
