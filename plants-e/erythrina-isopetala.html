Erythrina isopetala

Erythrina isopetala, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical legume native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like birds and bees. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, and its seed pods are elongated and woody. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.

The plant also has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
