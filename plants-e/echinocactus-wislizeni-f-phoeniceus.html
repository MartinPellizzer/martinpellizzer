Echinocactus wislizeni f. phoeniceus

Echinocactus wislizeni f. phoeniceus, commonly known as the Queen of the Night cactus, is a unique variant of the fishhook cactus.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like stem and the presence of long, white spines. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces striking, fragrant night-blooming flowers that are typically pink or purple.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
