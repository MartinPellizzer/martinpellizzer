Epilobium lamyi

Epilobium lamyi, commonly known as the sticky willow or redstem sedgeland, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, often sticky stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in riparian zones and wetlands.

Epilobium lamyi plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
