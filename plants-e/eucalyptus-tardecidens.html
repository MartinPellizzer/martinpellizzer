Eucalyptus tardecidens

Eucalyptus tardecidens, commonly known as the late-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are usually white or pale yellow, which open to reveal creamy-white flowers. Its growth habit is shrub-like or tree-like, depending on environmental conditions, and it thrives in sandy or loamy soils.

Eucalyptus tardecidens is well adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
