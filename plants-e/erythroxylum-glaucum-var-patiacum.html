Erythroxylum glaucum var. patiacum

Erythroxylum glaucum var. patiacum, commonly known as the Patiaca copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its latex, which has been used in traditional medicine and for producing chewing gum.
