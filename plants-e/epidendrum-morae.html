Epidendrum morae

Epidendrum morae, commonly known as the Morae's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces large, showy flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum morae is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
