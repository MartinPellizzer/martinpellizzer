Eupatorium geranifolium

Eupatorium geranifolium, commonly known as the sweet pepper bush, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium geranifolium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
