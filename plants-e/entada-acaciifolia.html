Entada acaciifolia

Entada acaciifolia, commonly known as the African honeycomb tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive honeycomb-like bark that peels off in layers. The tree produces long, flat pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
