Echinocereus poselgeri

Echinocereus poselgeri, commonly known as the Poselger's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spineless cactus with a globular shape and typically produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for arid environments, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and collections.
