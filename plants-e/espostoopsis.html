Espostoopsis

Espostoopsis, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical name, Espostoopsis, reflects its tendency to display its flowers prominently.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and greenhouses.
