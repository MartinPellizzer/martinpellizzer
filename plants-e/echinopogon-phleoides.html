Echinopogon phleoides

Echinopogon phleoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tufted grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown bracts. It is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Echinopogon phleoides is adapted to survive in harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and rapid growth cycles.
