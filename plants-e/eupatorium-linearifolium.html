Eupatorium linearifolium

Eupatorium linearifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Eupatorium linearifolium is often found in wetlands and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
