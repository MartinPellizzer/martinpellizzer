Epipactis phyllanthes var. cambrensis

Epipactis phyllanthes var. cambrensis, commonly known as the Welsh marsh helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to western Europe, particularly found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent purple markings. The plant is perennial, producing upright flowering stems that bear several flowers arranged in a loose spiral. It has a complex life cycle involving mycorrhizal associations with fungi, which are essential for its nutrient uptake.

This orchid is of conservation interest due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity.
