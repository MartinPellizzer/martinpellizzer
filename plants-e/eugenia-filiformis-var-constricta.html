Eugenia filiformis var. constricta

Eugenia filiformis var. constricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fruit, which is small and berry-like, is typically consumed by various bird species.
