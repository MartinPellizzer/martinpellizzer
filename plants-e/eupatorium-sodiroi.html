Eupatorium sodiroi

Eupatorium sodiroi, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, arranged in clusters, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eupatorium sodiroi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
