Echinocactus ritteri

Echinocactus ritteri, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that bear yellow bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its thick, water-storing stems help it survive in dry environments.

Echinocactus ritteri is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
