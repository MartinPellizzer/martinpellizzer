Eragrostis purshii

Eragrostis purshii, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often have a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis purshii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
