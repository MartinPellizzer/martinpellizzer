Eugenia macedoi

Eugenia macedoi, commonly known as the Macdoei tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia macedoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in traditional medicine.
