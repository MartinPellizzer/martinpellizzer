Ehrharta panicea var. cuspidata

Ehrharta panicea var. cuspidata, commonly known as the panicled lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces dense, panicled inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop.

Its ornamental value is also appreciated in gardens and landscapes.
