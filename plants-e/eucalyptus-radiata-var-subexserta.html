Eucalyptus radiata var. subexserta

Eucalyptus radiata var. subexserta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale or cream in color.

This variant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and semi-arid regions.
