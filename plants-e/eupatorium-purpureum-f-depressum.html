Eupatorium purpureum f. depressum

Eupatorium purpureum f. depressum, commonly known as the purple stem false dragonhead, is a variant of the purple stem eupatorium.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States. The plant features erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically bloom in late summer.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
