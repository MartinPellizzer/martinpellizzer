Euphorbia falcata var. vernalis

Euphorbia falcata var. vernalis, commonly known as the spring euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features distinctive, falcate (sickle-shaped) leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage and ability to withstand drought conditions.
