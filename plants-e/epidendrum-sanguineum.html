Epidendrum sanguineum

Epidendrum sanguineum, commonly known as the blood orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting green or white throat. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around its stem. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum sanguineum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant color and relatively easy care requirements.
