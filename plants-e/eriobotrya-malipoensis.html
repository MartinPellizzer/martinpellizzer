Eriobotrya malipoensis

Eriobotrya malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo loquat, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its deciduous leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,000 and 2,500 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
