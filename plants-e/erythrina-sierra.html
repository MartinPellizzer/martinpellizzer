Erythrina sierra

Erythrina sierra, commonly known as the Sierra coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its bright red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and bloom during the dry season. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets, and the plant produces pods that contain flat, fibrous seeds.
