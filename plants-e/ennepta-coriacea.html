Ennepta coriacea

Ennepta coriacea, commonly known as the roughleaf spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, thriving in full sun.

Ennepta coriacea is a hardy species that is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
