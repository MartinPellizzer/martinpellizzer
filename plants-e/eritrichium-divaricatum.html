Eritrichium divaricatum

Eritrichium divaricatum, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are usually basal, with a hairy texture, and it has a fibrous root system.

Eritrichium divaricatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
