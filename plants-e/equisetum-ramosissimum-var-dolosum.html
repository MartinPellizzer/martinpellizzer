Equisetum ramosissimum var. dolosum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. dolosum, commonly known as the slender horse tail, is a variety of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, spore-bearing cones and its distinctive, hollow, segmented stems. This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in wetlands or along streams. Its stems are typically green and lack leaves, with the spore-producing structures forming at the nodes.

The plant is notable for its ancient lineage, belonging to one of the oldest groups of vascular plants.
