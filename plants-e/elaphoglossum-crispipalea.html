Elaphoglossum crispipalea

Elaphoglossum crispipalea, commonly known as the crinkled holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, crinkled fronds that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum crispipalea is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing the adaptability of ferns to diverse ecological niches.
