Eragrostis macropoda

Eragrostis macropoda, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, narrow leaves that can reach lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis macropoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its robust growth habit.
