Euphorbia anderssonii

Euphorbia anderssonii, commonly known as the Andersson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Euphorbia anderssonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
