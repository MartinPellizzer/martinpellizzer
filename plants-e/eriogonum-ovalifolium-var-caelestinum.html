Eriogonum ovalifolium var. caelestinum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. caelestinum, commonly known as the blue flax or sky flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas. Its leaves are oval-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
