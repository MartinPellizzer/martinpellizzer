Erysimum bisaccatum

Erysimum bisaccatum, commonly known as the two-poded wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive two-lobed seed pods. It typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Erysimum bisaccatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
