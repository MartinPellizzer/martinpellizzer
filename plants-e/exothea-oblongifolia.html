Exothea oblongifolia

Exothea oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved exothea, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
