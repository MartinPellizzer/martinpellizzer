Eremolimon drepanostachyum

Eremolimon drepanostachyum, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a broom, hence its scientific name. The plant produces showy, often purple or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. It has adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with deep root systems and thick, waxy leaves.

Eremolimon drepanostachyum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
