Epidendrum silvanum

Epidendrum silvanum, commonly known as the silver orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on trees or rocks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name.

Epidendrum silvanum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
