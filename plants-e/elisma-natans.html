Elisma natans

Elisma natans, commonly known as the floating water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. The plant has long, slender stems that float on the water surface, with small, oval leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are pale yellow and bloom in early spring, contributing to its role in wetland ecosystems.

Elisma natans plays a vital role in stabilizing water bodies and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
