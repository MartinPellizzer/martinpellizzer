Elymus zagricus

Elymus zagricus, commonly known as the Zagreb grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Elymus zagricus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
