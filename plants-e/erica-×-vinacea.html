Erica × vinacea

Erica × vinacea, commonly known as the common heath, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Erica × vinacea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
