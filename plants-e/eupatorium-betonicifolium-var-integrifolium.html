Eupatorium betonicifolium var. integrifolium

Eupatorium betonicifolium var. integrifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, pale blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually hairy and have a distinctive blue-green color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
