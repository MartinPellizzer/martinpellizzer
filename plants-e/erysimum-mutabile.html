Erysimum mutabile

Erysimum mutabile, commonly known as the variable wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Erysimum mutabile is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
