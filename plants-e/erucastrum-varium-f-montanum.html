Erucastrum varium f. montanum

Erucastrum varium f. montanum, commonly known as the mountain erucoid, is a variant of the erucoid plant characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy foliage.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in rocky, alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions of Europe. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are elongated and slightly lobed, with a distinctive peppery aroma when crushed.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
