Eryngium giganteum

Eryngium giganteum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems and large, purple-blue flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces small, white to blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eryngium giganteum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
