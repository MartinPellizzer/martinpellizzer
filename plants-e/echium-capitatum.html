Echium capitatum

Echium capitatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium capitatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
