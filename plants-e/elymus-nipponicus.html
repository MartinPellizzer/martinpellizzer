Elymus nipponicus

Elymus nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of spikelets, which are often hairy and bristly.

Elymus nipponicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its graceful appearance.
