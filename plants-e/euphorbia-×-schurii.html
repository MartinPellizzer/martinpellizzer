Euphorbia × schurii

Euphorbia × schurii, commonly known as the Schur's spurge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, often fleshy stems and leaves that can vary in shape and color depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is native to regions of southern Africa and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Euphorbia × schurii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
