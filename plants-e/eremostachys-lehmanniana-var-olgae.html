Eremostachys lehmanniana var. olgae

Eremostachys lehmanniana var. olgae, commonly known as the Lehmann's eremostachys, is a low-growing perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to store water. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
