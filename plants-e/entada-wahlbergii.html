Entada wahlbergii

Entada wahlbergii, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves. The tree produces long, cylindrical pods that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it is valued for its timber and traditional uses.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in various soil types and its role in local ecosystems.
