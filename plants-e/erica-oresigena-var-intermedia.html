Erica oresigena var. intermedia

Erica oresigena var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, producing fragrant white or pink blooms.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
