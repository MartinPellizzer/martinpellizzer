Ericoides verticillatum

Ericoides verticillatum, commonly known as the spiral moss, is a species of moss that belongs to the family Pottiaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and its ability to thrive in moist, shaded environments. The plant features closely arranged, spirally twisted stems that give it its common name. Its leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a smooth, glossy surface.

Ericoides verticillatum is often found growing on rocky substrates or in forest floors, contributing to soil stabilization and moisture retention.
