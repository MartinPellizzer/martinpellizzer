Echinochloa crus-galli subsp. utilis

Echinochloa crus-galli subsp.

utilis, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a widely distributed annual grass species native to the Old World. It is characterized by its flat, narrow leaves and spikelets that are typically 3 to 6 mm long.

The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers in dense clusters, and it has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various soil types. It is often found in cultivated fields and disturbed areas, where it can become a significant weed.

Its rapid growth and high seed production make it a challenging species to control in agricultural settings.
