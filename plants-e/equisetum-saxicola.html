Equisetum saxicola

Equisetum saxicola, commonly known as the rock hawthorn, is a species of horsetail that thrives in calcareous soils.

It is characterized by its slender, jointed stems and whorls of small, needle-like leaves. This plant is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, particularly in mountainous regions. It reproduces via spores and is typically found in moist, well-drained environments.

Equisetum saxicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native range.
