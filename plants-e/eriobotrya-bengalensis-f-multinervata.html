Eriobotrya bengalensis f. multinervata

Eriobotrya bengalensis f. multinervata, commonly known as the Bengal loquat, is a variant of the loquat species native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, orange-yellow fruits in the summer. The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it a popular plant in horticulture and agriculture.
