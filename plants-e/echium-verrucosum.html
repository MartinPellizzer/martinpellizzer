Echium verrucosum

Echium verrucosum, commonly known as the rough thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or grayish down. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Echium verrucosum is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
