Eriophorum cyperinum f. condensatum

Eriophorum cyperinum f. condensatum, commonly known as the dense cottongrass, is a variant of the common cottongrass.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically thrives in moist, acidic environments such as peat bogs and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like and often covered with fine, silky hairs.

The inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that give the plant its distinctive, cotton-like appearance.
