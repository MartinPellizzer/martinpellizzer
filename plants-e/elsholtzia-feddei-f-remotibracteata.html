Elsholtzia feddei f. remotibracteata

Elsholtzia feddei f. remotibracteata, commonly known as the remote bract elsholtzia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, arranged in whorled clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
