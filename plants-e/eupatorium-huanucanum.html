Eupatorium huanucanum

Eupatorium huanucanum, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium huanucanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
