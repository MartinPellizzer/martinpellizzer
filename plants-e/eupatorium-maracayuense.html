Eupatorium maracayuense

Eupatorium maracayuense, commonly known as the Maracayu shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
