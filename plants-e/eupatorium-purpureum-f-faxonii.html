Eupatorium purpureum f. faxonii

Eupatorium purpureum f. faxonii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
