Eugenia expansa

Eugenia expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. Eugenia expansa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
