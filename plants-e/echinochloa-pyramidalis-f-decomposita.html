Echinochloa pyramidalis f. decomposita

Echinochloa pyramidalis f. decomposita, commonly known as the decomposed pyramid grass, is a variant of the pyramid grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a grassy plant characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its botanical features include a compact growth habit and the ability to form dense stands.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
