Eugenia fruticosa

Eugenia fruticosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
