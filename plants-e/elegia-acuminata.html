Elegia acuminata

Elegia acuminata, commonly known as the slender water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Elegia acuminata is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in muddy or silty substrates.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality through oxygenation.
