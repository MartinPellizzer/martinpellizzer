Ehrharta varicosa

Ehrharta varicosa, commonly known as variable lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Ehrharta varicosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
