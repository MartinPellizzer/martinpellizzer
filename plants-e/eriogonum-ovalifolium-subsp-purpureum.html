Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp. purpureum

Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp.

purpureum, commonly known as the purpleleaf filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The leaves are oval-shaped and often have a bluish or purplish hue, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

This subspecies is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
