Euphorbia pilosula

Euphorbia pilosula, commonly known as the wooly spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or dry soils. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Euphorbia pilosula is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
