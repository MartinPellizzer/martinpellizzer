Eugenia karsteniana var. albicans

Eugenia karsteniana var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
