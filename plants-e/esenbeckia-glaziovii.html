Esenbeckia glaziovii

Esenbeckia glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's esenbeckia, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known outside of its native range.
