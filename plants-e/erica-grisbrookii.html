Erica grisbrookii

Erica grisbrookii, commonly known as the Grey Heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Erica grisbrookii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
