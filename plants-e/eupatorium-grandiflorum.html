Eupatorium grandiflorum

Eupatorium grandiflorum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its showy purple flowers bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium grandiflorum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
