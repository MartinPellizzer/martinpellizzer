Eupatorium

Eupatorium, commonly known as boneset, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, often white or purple flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and have a rough, hairy texture. Eupatorium species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Many species are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
