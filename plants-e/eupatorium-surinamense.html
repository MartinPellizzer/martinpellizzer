Eupatorium surinamense

Eupatorium surinamense, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The genus Eupatorium is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.
