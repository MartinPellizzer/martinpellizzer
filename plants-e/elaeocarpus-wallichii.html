Elaeocarpus wallichii

Elaeocarpus wallichii, commonly known as the Kashmiri elaeocarpus, is a deciduous tree native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
