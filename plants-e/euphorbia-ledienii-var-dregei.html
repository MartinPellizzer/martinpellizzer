Euphorbia ledienii var. dregei

Euphorbia ledienii var. dregei, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a branched stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, making it well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
