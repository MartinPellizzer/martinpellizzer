Erythroxylum subcordatum

Erythroxylum subcordatum, commonly known as the pink velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically pink or reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in hue. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, winged seeds.

Erythroxylum subcordatum is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is part of the Erythroxylaceae family.
