Eucalyptus × taeniola

Eucalyptus × taeniola, commonly known as the hybrid gum tree, is a species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus robusta.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that can range from grey to white in color. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are grey-green in appearance and often have a slightly hairy texture. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is commonly found in coastal and riverine regions of eastern Australia, where it thrives in a variety of soil types.
