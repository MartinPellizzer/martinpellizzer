Elaeagnus lanpingensis

Elaeagnus lanpingensis, commonly known as the Lanping silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is tolerant of drought and poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus lanpingensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
