Eugenia minuscula

Eugenia minuscula, commonly known as the small eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its compact size and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine.
