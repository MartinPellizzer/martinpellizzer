Echium coeleste

Echium coeleste, commonly known as the blueweed or celestial echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking tall spikes of blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, which is known for its hairy stems and showy flowers.

Echium coeleste is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
