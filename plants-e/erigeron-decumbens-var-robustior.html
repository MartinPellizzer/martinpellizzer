Erigeron decumbens var. robustior

Erigeron decumbens var. robustior, commonly known as the robust daisy or robust blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and features blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white ray flowers surrounding a central disk. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

Its botanical name reflects its growth form and robust appearance, making it a notable species in ecological studies.
