Eriocaulon siamense var. macrophyllum

Eriocaulon siamense var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the giant Siam sedge, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length, with a dense, fibrous root system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and marshes.

Its large leaves and robust growth make it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
