Erythranthe lutea var. lutea

Erythranthe lutea var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. The plant features striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
