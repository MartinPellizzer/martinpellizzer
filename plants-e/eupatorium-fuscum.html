Eupatorium fuscum

Eupatorium fuscum, commonly known as black eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale purple, blooming in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium fuscum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
