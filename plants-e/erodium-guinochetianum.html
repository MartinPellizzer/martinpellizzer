Erodium guinochetianum

Erodium guinochetianum, commonly known as the Guinochet's filaree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are oval-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are often prostrate or ascending.

The species is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
