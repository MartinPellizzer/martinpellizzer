Equisetum × ascendens

Equisetum × ascendens, commonly known as the climbing horsetail, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Equisetum arvense and Equisetum hyemale.

It is a member of the Equisetaceae family, which includes ancient vascular plants often referred to as "living fossils.". The plant is notable for its tall, slender stems that can climb and twine around other vegetation, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches.

Equisetum × ascendens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.
