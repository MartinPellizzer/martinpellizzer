Egassea laurifolia

Egassea laurifolia, commonly known as the fragrant laurel, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle aromatic scent. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish or orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Its foliage and berries are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
