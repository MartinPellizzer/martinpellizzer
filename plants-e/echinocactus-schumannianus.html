Echinocactus schumannianus

Echinocactus schumannianus, commonly known as the Schumann's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
