Eriochilus autumnalis

Eriochilus autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn chile, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense, silver-gray foliage that provides excellent ground cover. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Eriochilus autumnalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
