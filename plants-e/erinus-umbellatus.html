Erinus umbellatus

Erinus umbellatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erinus umbellatus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
