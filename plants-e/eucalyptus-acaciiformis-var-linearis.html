Eucalyptus acaciiformis var. linearis

Eucalyptus acaciiformis var. linearis, commonly known as the linear eucalypt, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture.
