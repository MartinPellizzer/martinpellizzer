Eupatorium laevigatum var. submembranacea

Eupatorium laevigatum var. submembranacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that are often branched and can grow up to 1.5 meters tall. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy coating on the underside.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows.
