Eugenia axillaris

Eugenia axillaris, commonly known as the slender waxleaf, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Eugenia axillaris is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
