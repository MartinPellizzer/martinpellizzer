Euphorbia bevilaniensis

Euphorbia bevilaniensis, commonly known as the Bevilani's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves and distinctive yellow sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and plant enthusiasts.
