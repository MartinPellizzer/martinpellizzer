Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp. nicaeensis

Euphorbia nicaeensis subsp.

nicaeensis, commonly known as the Nicaea spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late spring, which are surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
