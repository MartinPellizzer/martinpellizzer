Euterpe espiritosantensis

Euterpe espiritosantensis, commonly known as the Espirito Santo palm, is a species of palm native to Brazil, specifically the state of Espirito Santo.

It is a medium-sized tree with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by edible fruits resembling small cherries. The fruit is a key component of the local ecosystem, providing food for various wildlife species.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species.
