Eupatorium santacruzense

Eupatorium santacruzense, commonly known as Santa Cruz eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
