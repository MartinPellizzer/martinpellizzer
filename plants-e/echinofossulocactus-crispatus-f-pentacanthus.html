Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. pentacanthus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. pentacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican golden cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in thick, waxy, greenish-gray spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a bold contrast to its spiny structure. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after specimen in horticultural collections.

Due to its limited natural range and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a delicate and challenging plant to cultivate.
