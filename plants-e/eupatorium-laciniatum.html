Eupatorium laciniatum

Eupatorium laciniatum, commonly known as cutleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium laciniatum is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
