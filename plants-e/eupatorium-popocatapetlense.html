Eupatorium popocatapetlense

Eupatorium popocatapetlense, commonly known as the Popocatapetl eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
