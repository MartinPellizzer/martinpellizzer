Echeveria atropurpurea

Echeveria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple emperor, is a species of flowering plant in the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically bluish-gray with a purple or reddish tinge. The plant produces vibrant violet or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its thick, fleshy leaves, making it drought-tolerant.

Echeveria atropurpurea is popular as a houseplant and in rock gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
