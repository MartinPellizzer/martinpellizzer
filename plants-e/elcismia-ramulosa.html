Elcismia ramulosa

Elcismia ramulosa, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement and possess a waxy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Elcismia ramulosa is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it forms a unique ecological relationship with its host.
