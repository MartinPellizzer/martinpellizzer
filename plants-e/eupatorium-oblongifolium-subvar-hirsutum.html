Eupatorium oblongifolium subvar. hirsutum

Eupatorium oblongifolium subvar. hirsutum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems covered in stiff, hairy textures, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are elongated, heart-shaped, and often have a rough, hairy surface.

It is typically found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and meadows.
