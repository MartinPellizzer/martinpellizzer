Eremostachys speciosa var. schugnanica

Eremostachys speciosa var. schugnanica, commonly known as the Schugnanica eremostachys, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and dry landscapes.
