Eriobotrya ochracea

Eriobotrya ochracea, commonly known as the yellow loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange to yellow fruits resembling small apricots. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is commonly used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
