Eupatorium concinnum

Eupatorium concinnum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy inflorescences.
