Echeveria pringlei

Echeveria pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating called farina. The plant produces striking violet to purple flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
