Eupatorium artemisiifolium

Eupatorium artemisiifolium, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Boneset is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
