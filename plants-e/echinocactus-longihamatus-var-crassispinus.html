Echinocactus longihamatus var. crassispinus

Echinocactus longihamatus var. crassispinus, commonly known as the thick-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiky structure. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
