Eugenia angustifolia var. latifolia

Eugenia angustifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf guava, is a variant of the guava species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broader, lance-shaped leaves compared to the typical variety, which contributes to its distinct appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, aromatic fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm climate with moderate rainfall.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
