Erodium malacoides subsp. eumalacoides

Erodium malacoides subsp.

eumalacoides, commonly known as the redstem bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often reddish stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
