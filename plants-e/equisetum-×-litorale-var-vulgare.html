Equisetum × litorale var. vulgare

Equisetum × litorale var. vulgare, commonly known as sea clubmoss, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Equisetum litorale and Equisetum vulgare.

It is a spore-bearing plant belonging to the order Equisetales, which includes ancient vascular plants. The plant typically grows in moist, coastal environments, such as sandy shores and dunes, where it thrives in saline conditions. Its morphology includes whorled stems and small, scale-like leaves, typical of the genus Equisetum.

It plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and is an indicator species for maritime environments.
