Eritrichium turkestanicum

Eritrichium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its low-growing habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
