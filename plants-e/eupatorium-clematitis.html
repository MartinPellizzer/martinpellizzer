Eupatorium clematitis

Eupatorium clematitis, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
