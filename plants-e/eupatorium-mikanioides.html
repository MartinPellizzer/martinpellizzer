Eupatorium mikanioides

Eupatorium mikanioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces tiny, white to blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slight hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
