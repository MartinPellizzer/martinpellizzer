Eremophila densifolia subsp. pubiflora

Eremophila densifolia subsp.

pubiflora, commonly known as the woolly eremophila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It features dense, grey-green foliage that is covered in fine, woolly hairs, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
