Eurya arisanensis

Eurya arisanensis, commonly known as the Taiwan holly, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia, particularly Taiwan and parts of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an attractive feature. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at moderate elevations.

Eurya arisanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
