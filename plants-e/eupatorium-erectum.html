Eupatorium erectum

Eupatorium erectum, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges. Boneset blooms in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
