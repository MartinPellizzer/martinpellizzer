Eugenia yatuae

Eugenia yatuae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Yatua, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Eugenia yatuae thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value and culinary uses make it a notable plant in both horticulture and gastronomy.
