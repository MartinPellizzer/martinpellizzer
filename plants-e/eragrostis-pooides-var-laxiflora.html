Eragrostis pooides var. laxiflora

Eragrostis pooides var. laxiflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
