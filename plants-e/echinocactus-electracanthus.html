Echinocactus electracanthus

Echinocactus electracanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
