Elyhordeum elymoides

Elyhordeum elymoides, commonly known as the prairie grass or western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Elyhordeum elymoides is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
