Eugenia javanica var. parviflora

Eugenia javanica var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The fruit, though not commonly consumed, is small and contains a single seed.
