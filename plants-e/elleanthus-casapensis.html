Elleanthus casapensis

Elleanthus casapensis, commonly known as the Casapense daisy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

Elleanthus casapensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and hardy nature.
