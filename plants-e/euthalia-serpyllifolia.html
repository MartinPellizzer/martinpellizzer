Euthalia serpyllifolia

Euthalia serpyllifolia, commonly known as the common sandwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats. It is characterized by its simple, unbranched stems and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Euthalia serpyllifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local insect populations.
