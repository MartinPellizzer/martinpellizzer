Elatostema eximium

Elatostema eximium, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Elatostema eximium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
