Ebingeria

Ebingeria, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a thick, purple rind and a sweet, segmented white flesh. Its botanical features include woody stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its edible fruit.
