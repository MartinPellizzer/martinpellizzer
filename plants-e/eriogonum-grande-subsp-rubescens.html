Eriogonum grande subsp. rubescens

Eriogonum grande subsp.

rubescens, commonly known as the redstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and showy, clustered purple flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems turn reddish-brown with age.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
