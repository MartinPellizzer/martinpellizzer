Eugenia obtusiflora

Eugenia obtusiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often fragrant. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. Eugenia obtusiflora thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
