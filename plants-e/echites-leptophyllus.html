Echites leptophyllus

Echites leptophyllus, commonly known as the slender-leafed echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role.

Echites leptophyllus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
