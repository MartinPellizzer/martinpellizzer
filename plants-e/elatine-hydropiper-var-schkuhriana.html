Elatine hydropiper var. schkuhriana

Elatine hydropiper var. schkuhriana, commonly known as the water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, wet habitats such as ditches, marshes, and the edges of ponds. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually with four petals.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often found in temperate regions.
