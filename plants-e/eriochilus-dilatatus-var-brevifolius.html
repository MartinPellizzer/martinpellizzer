Eriochilus dilatatus var. brevifolius

Eriochilus dilatatus var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Australia.

It features tufted, upright growth with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing white to pale blue flowers. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

This variety is often found in coastal and heathland environments.
