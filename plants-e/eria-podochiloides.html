Eria podochiloides

Eria podochiloides, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid tropical environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warm seasons.
