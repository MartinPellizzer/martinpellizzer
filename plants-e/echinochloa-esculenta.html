Echinochloa esculenta

Echinochloa esculenta, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its flat, ribbed leaves and spike-like inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant is often considered a weed in agricultural fields due to its rapid growth and competitive nature. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats, including rice paddies and pastures.

While it has some historical use as a food source, it is primarily known for its invasive potential in cultivated areas.
