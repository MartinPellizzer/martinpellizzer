Eriocephalus sericeus

Eriocephalus sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it adapts well to dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
