Erica hippurus

Erica hippurus, commonly known as the purple heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Erica hippurus thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
