Euphorbia bertemariae

Euphorbia bertemariae, commonly known as the Bertemaria's spurge, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces striking, yellow-orange cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It blooms during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted appearance.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
