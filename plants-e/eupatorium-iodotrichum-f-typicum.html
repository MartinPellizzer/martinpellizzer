Eupatorium iodotrichum f. typicum

Eupatorium iodotrichum f. typicum, commonly known as the eastern mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its foliage is often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental qualities.
