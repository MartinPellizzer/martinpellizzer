Eugenia buxifolia

Eugenia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, red berries.

Eugenia buxifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal.
