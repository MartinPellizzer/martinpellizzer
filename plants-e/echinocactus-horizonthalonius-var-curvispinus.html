Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. curvispinus

Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. curvispinus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny appearance. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
