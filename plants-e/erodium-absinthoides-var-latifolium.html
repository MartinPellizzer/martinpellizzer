Erodium absinthoides var. latifolium

Erodium absinthoides var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf field eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, pale blue or purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
