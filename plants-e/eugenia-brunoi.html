Eugenia brunoi

Eugenia brunoi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe. Eugenia brunoi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its leaves are evergreen, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
