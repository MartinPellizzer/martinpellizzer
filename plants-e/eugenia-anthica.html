Eugenia anthica

Eugenia anthica, commonly known as the pink guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a tart flavor, often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia anthica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage.
