Eremophila ternata

Eremophila ternata, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its succulent stems and reduced leaf surface. It produces nectar-rich flowers that attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.

Eremophila ternata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
