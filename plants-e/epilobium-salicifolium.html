Epilobium salicifolium

Epilobium salicifolium, commonly known as willow-leaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. Its flowers are showy, with four pink to purple petals that open in the evening.

It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, dark seeds.
