Erica cremnophila

Erica cremnophila, commonly known as the cliff heath, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica cremnophila thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found growing on rocky slopes and cliffs.

It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
