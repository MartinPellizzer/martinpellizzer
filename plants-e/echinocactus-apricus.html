Echinocactus apricus

Echinocactus apricus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Echinocactus apricus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
