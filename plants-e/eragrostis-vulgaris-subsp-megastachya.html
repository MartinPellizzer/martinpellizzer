Eragrostis vulgaris subsp. megastachya

Eragrostis vulgaris subsp.

megastachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage, which can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or pale brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it a useful plant for erosion control.
