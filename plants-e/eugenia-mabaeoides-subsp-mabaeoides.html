Eugenia mabaeoides subsp. mabaeoides

Eugenia mabaeoides subsp.

mabaeoides, commonly known as the Mabae's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, warm climates.

The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit, a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
