Eugenia atronervia

Eugenia atronervia, commonly known as the black-veined eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves with prominent, dark veins. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
