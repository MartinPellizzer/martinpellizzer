Erodium oxyrhinchum var. obtusilobum

Erodium oxyrhinchum var. obtusilobum, commonly known as the broad-leaved bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
