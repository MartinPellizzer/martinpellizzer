Excoecaria marginata var. longifolia

Excoecaria marginata var. longifolia, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a yellow throat. The plant has a woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic to some animals.
