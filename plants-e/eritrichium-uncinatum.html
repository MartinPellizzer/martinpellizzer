Eritrichium uncinatum

Eritrichium uncinatum, commonly known as hooked eriogonum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is typically found in rocky or sandy soils, often in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. It has a short growing season and is adapted to cold climates, blooming early in the year.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
