Eupatorium arizonicum

Eupatorium arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features tall, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in umbels, and range in color from white to pale pink.

Eupatorium arizonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
