Erigeron hyemalis

Erigeron hyemalis, commonly known as frostweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Frostweed is known for its ability to withstand cold temperatures, making it a resilient species in temperate climates.
