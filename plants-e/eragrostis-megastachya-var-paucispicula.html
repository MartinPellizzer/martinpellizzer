Eragrostis megastachya var. paucispicula

Eragrostis megastachya var. paucispicula, commonly known as the slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The inflorescence consists of elongated spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with the flowers being small and wind-pollinated. This plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, and it is often found in regions with temperate to subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it a useful species for ecological studies and potentially for erosion control in suitable environments.
