Eugenia corozalensis

Eugenia corozalensis, commonly known as the Corozal guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, fleshy berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has potential medicinal properties.
