Eucalyptus calycogona subsp. spaffordii

Eucalyptus calycogona subsp.

spaffordii, commonly known as the Spafford's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of South Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months of spring and autumn. It is well adapted to dry conditions and has a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations and limited distribution.
