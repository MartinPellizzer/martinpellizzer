Eugenia alpestris

Eugenia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine guava, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America and is typically found in high-altitude regions. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit, when it matures, is a small, berry-like structure that can be edible.
