Erica plukenetii var. bicarinata

Erica plukenetii var. bicarinata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and profuse flowering. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine hairs.

Erica plukenetii var. bicarinata is well adapted to poor, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
