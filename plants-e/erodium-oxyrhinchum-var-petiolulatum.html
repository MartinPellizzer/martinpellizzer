Erodium oxyrhinchum var. petiolulatum

Erodium oxyrhinchum var. petiolulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with hairy stems and small, pale pink to white flowers. The leaves are narrow and often have a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is known for its ability to colonize disturbed sites and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
