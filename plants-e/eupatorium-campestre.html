Eupatorium campestre

Eupatorium campestre, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
