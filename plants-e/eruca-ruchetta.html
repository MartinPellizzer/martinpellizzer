Eruca ruchetta

Eruca ruchetta, commonly known as the Italian rocket or rucola, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, bright green leaves that are slightly curled and have a peppery flavor. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil.

Eruca ruchetta is widely cultivated for its edible leaves and is a popular ingredient in salads and gourmet cooking.
