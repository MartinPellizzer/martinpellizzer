Epidendrum guaridense

Epidendrum guaridense, commonly known as the Guaridense Vanda, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces aerial roots and thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. It is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
