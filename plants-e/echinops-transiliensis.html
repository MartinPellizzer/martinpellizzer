Echinops transiliensis

Echinops transiliensis, commonly known as the Transylvanian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian region of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its spherical shape and silver-green foliage make it a distinctive and ornamental species.

Echinops transiliensis produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
