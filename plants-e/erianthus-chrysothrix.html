Erianthus chrysothrix

Erianthus chrysothrix, commonly known as the golden thread grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a golden hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

Erianthus chrysothrix is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
