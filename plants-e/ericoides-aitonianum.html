Ericoides aitonianum

Ericoides aitonianum, commonly known as the Aiton's heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer. Ericoides aitonianum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
