Eupatorium polanthum

Eupatorium polanthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium polanthum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
