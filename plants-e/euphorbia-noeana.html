Euphorbia noeana

Euphorbia noeana, commonly known as the Sicilian spurge, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be toxic. Euphorbia noeana typically grows in rocky or dry soils, adapting well to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and drought tolerance make it a distinctive member of its genus.
