Erythraea brachiata

Erythraea brachiata, commonly known as the red-stemmed milkweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Erythraea brachiata is known for its milky sap, which is a characteristic feature of the milkweed genus.
