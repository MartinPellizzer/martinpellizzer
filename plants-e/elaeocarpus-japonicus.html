Elaeocarpus japonicus

Elaeocarpus japonicus, commonly known as the Japanese elaeocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn red or yellow when ripe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.
