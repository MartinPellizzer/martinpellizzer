Eucalyptus socialis

Eucalyptus socialis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed structure and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically appearing in spring. Eucalyptus socialis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance and a deep root system.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
