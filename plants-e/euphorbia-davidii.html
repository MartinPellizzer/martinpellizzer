Euphorbia davidii

Euphorbia davidii, commonly known as the David's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its thick, fleshy stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Euphorbia davidii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
