Erythroxylum impressum

Erythroxylum impressum, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

The plant is also notable for its historical use in traditional medicine.
