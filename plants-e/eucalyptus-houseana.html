Eucalyptus houseana

Eucalyptus houseana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Eucalyptus houseana is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
