Echinocereus fitchii subsp. armatus

Echinocereus fitchii subsp.

armatus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, requiring minimal water.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.
