Eriosoriopsis okamotoi

Eriosoriopsis okamotoi, commonly known as the Okamoto's rust fungus, is a species of rust fungus that primarily affects plants in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its unique rust-like pustules that develop on the leaves of its host plants. The fungus produces asexual spores in the form of urediniospores, which are dispersed by wind to infect new hosts. It thrives in moist and temperate environments, often causing significant damage to the foliage of its host plants.

Due to its specific host range and limited geographic distribution, it is of interest to botanists and plant pathologists.
