Eugenia villamilii

Eugenia villamilii, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, similar to guavas. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Eugenia villamilii is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruits, making it a significant plant in both horticulture and local cuisine.
