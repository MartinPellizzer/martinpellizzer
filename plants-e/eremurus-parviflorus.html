Eremurus parviflorus

Eremurus parviflorus, commonly known as the dwarf Spearflower, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a spear, composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to survive in dry, sandy soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a striking visual display.

Eremurus parviflorus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
