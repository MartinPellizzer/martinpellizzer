Eupatorium dejectum

Eupatorium dejectum, commonly known as the drooping boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium dejectum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
