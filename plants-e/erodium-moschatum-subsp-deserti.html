Erodium moschatum subsp. deserti

Erodium moschatum subsp.

deserti, commonly known as desert bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, pink to reddish-purple flowers.

The plant has hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of desert and coastal sage scrub habitats.
