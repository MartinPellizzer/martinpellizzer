Encabarcenia aurea

Encabarcenia aurea, commonly known as the golden encabarcenia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Encabarcenia aurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
