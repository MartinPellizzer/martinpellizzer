Eragrostis racemosa

Eragrostis racemosa, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, branched inflorescences that appear in late summer. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and a rhizomatous growth habit, which help it spread and establish in various environments.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
