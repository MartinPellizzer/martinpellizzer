Eria angustifolia

Eria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant typically grows in clusters and has a fibrous root system.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
