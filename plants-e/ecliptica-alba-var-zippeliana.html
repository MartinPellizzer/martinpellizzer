Ecliptica alba var. zippeliana

Ecliptica alba var. zippeliana, commonly known as white eclipta, is a variant of the eclipta genus native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a branching stem that supports its foliage.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
