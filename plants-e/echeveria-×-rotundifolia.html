Echeveria × rotundifolia

Echeveria × rotundifolia, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a hybrid succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
