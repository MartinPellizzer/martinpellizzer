Eryngium aquifolium

Eryngium aquifolium, commonly known as the water thistle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has silvery, spoon-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eryngium aquifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various healing purposes.
