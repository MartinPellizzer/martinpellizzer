Eremopoa songarica

Eremopoa songarica, commonly known as the Songaric grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Eremopoa songarica is often found in grasslands and sandy soils, contributing to the ecological stability of its habitat.
