Eutrema heterophyllum

Eutrema heterophyllum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and spiky, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower cluster. The plant is adapted to saline and coastal environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions. Its growth habit is typically low and compact, with leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

Eutrema heterophyllum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as cabbage and mustard.
