Euphorbia medicaginea var. oblongifolia

Euphorbia medicaginea var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, oblong leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a dense, fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its toxic sap, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
