Elaphoglossum bakeri

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely divided, feather-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sorus.

Elaphoglossum bakeri is native to regions of Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates.
