Eremophila virgata

Eremophila virgata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy pink or red flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila virgata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and resilience.
