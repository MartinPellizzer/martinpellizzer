Eugenia johnsonii

Eugenia johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. Eugenia johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to the Myrtaceae family.
