Eragrostis pulchella

Eragrostis pulchella, commonly known as the purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and produces delicate, pinkish-purple flower spikes in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats. Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for native landscaping.

Eragrostis pulchella plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
