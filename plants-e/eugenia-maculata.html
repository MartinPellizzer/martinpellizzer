Eugenia maculata

Eugenia maculata, commonly known as the spotted eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often spotted with red or purple markings. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The berries are used in traditional medicine and can be consumed fresh or processed into jams and beverages.
