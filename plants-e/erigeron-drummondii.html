Erigeron drummondii

Erigeron drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Erigeron drummondii is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to arid environments.
