Escallonia carmelita

Escallonia carmelita, commonly known as the Carmel escallonia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in perfumery. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Escallonia carmelita is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
