Echinocactus knippelianus

Echinocactus knippelianus, commonly known as the Knippel's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
