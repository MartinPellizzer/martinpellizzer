Euphorbia francoisii var. rakatozafyi

Euphorbia francoisii var. rakatozafyi, commonly known as the Rakatozafy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
