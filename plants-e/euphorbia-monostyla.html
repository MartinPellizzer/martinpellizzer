Euphorbia monostyla

Euphorbia monostyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Euphorbia monostyla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
