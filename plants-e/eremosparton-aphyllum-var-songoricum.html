Eremosparton aphyllum var. songoricum

Eremosparton aphyllum var. songoricum, commonly known as the Songar Desert saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and small, clustered leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are adapted to attract pollinators in its dry habitat. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in its native ecosystems.
