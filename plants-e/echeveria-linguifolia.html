Echeveria linguifolia

Echeveria linguifolia, commonly known as the "tongue-shaped echeveria," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with a subtle pinkish hue. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy pink or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its compact growth habit and striking appearance make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
