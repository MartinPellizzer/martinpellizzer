Eremophila arachnoides subsp. arachnoides

Eremophila arachnoides subsp.

arachnoides, commonly known as the sandplain emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and produces clusters of pink to red tubular flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of native pollinators, including bees and birds.
