Eugenia cumingiana

Eugenia cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, which are edible and used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
