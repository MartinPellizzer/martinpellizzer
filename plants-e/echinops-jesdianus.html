Echinops jesdianus

Echinops jesdianus, commonly known as the Jesdian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of its distinctive, prickly fruits. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Echinops jesdianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
