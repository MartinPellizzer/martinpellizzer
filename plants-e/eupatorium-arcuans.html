Eupatorium arcuans

Eupatorium arcuans, commonly known as curlyleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming from July to September. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium arcuans is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
