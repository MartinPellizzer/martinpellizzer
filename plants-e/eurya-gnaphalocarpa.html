Eurya gnaphalocarpa

Eurya gnaphalocarpa, commonly known as the woolly alder or silverleaf eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Eurya gnaphalocarpa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
