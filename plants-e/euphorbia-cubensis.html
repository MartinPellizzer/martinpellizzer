Euphorbia cubensis

Euphorbia cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban spurge, is a succulent plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique, capsule-like fruit that contains seeds surrounded by a sticky, milky sap. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
