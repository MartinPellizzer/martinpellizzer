Erica dentata

Erica dentata, commonly known as the saw-tooth heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are narrow and have serrated edges, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica dentata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
