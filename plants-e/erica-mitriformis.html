Erica mitriformis

Erica mitriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Erica mitriformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, evergreen shrubs.
