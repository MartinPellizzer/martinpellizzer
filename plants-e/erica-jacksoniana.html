Erica jacksoniana

Erica jacksoniana, commonly known as Jackson's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica jacksoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
