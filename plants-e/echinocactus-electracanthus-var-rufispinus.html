Echinocactus electracanthus var. rufispinus

Echinocactus electracanthus var. rufispinus, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spine-tipped cactus native to Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, reddish-brown spines that provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
