Echinops polyceras

Echinops polyceras, commonly known as the many-armed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in bristly, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by the development of its distinctive, spiky fruits. Echinops polyceras is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid climates.
