Euphrasia ramosissima var. dinarica

Euphrasia ramosissima var. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky slopes.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
