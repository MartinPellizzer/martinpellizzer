Ebnerella glochidiata

Ebnerella glochidiata, commonly known as the needlepod burr, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is known for its ability to spread rapidly through seed dispersal by animals and wind.
