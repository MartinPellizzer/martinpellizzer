Erodium tordylioides subsp. mouretii

Erodium tordylioides subsp.

mouretii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The plant has small, opposite leaves that are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple and are borne in clusters, giving it a delicate appearance.

It is a member of the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing habit and aromatic foliage.
