Eria anonoensis

Eria anonoensis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, while its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Eria anonoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
