Erythrina vespertilio var. typica

Erythrina vespertilio var. typica, commonly known as the evening swallowbird or red torch tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its striking, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and ability to thrive in warm climates.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
