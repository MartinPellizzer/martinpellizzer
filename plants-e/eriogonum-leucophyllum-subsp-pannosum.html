Eriogonum leucophyllum subsp. pannosum

Eriogonum leucophyllum subsp.

pannosum, commonly known as the whiteleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial herb that produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds.

This species is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
