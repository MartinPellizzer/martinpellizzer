Eucalyptus percostata

Eucalyptus percostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually small and white or pink, arranged in clusters along the branches.

Eucalyptus percostata is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
