Erica nigricans

Erica nigricans, commonly known as the black heath, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica nigricans thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and birds.
