Eremostachys nuda var. septentrionalis

Eremostachys nuda var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern naked eremostachys, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with succulent leaves that store water and reduce transpiration. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is noted for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
