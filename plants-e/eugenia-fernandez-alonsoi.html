Eugenia fernandez-alonsoi

Eugenia fernandez-alonsoi, commonly known as the Alfonso's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in regions of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
