Ericoides hirtiflorum

Ericoides hirtiflorum, commonly known as the hairy-flowered ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands or rocky areas. It is adapted to cool, moist climates and is often found in regions with high humidity.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves provides protection against environmental stressors.
