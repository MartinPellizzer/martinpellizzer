Erica superba

Erica superba, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Erica superba thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
