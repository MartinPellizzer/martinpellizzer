Echinopsis densispina var. kreuzingeri

Echinopsis densispina var. kreuzingeri, commonly known as the Kreuzinger's moon cactus, is a striking cactus species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny ribs and a compact, globular shape that gives it a moon-like appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a decorative indoor plant.
