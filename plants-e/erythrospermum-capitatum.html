Erythrospermum capitatum

Erythrospermum capitatum, commonly known as the red-flowered hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by birds and insects, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
