Erodium allotrichum

Erodium allotrichum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a small annual herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its slender stems and small, pink or white flowers. The plant has alternate, narrow leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Erodium allotrichum is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
