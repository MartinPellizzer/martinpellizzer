Epidendrum cuzcoense

Epidendrum cuzcoense, commonly known as the Cuzco orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often white or pale pink, with a distinct lip that is usually darker in color.

Epidendrum cuzcoense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
