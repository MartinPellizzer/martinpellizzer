Elaeocarpus floridanus

Elaeocarpus floridanus, commonly known as the Florida elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal hammocks and wetlands.

The Florida elaeocarp is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
