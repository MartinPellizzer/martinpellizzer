Eriosema pauciflorum var. pauciflorum

Eriosema pauciflorum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the woolly eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of soft, hairy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple, typically arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in some botanical gardens and private collections.
