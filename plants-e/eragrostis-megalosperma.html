Eragrostis megalosperma

Eragrostis megalosperma, commonly known as giant false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
