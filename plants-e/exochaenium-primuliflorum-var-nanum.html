Exochaenium primuliflorum var. nanum

Exochaenium primuliflorum var. nanum, commonly known as dwarf primrose grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in dry, open habitats. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
