Elephantopus pratensis subsp. arenarius

Elephantopus pratensis subsp.

arenarius, commonly known as the sand elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive component of its native ecosystem.
