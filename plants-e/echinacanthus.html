Echinacanthus

Echinacanthus, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent. Echinacanthus thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
