Eupatorium vernale

Eupatorium vernale, commonly known as common boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium vernale is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
