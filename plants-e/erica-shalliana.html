Erica shalliana

Erica shalliana, commonly known as the Cape heath or silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Erica shalliana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact shrubs.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators.
