Eugenia mindorensis

Eugenia mindorensis, commonly known as Mindanao eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
