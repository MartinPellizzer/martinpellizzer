Encyclia odoratissima

Encyclia odoratissima, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticulturists.
