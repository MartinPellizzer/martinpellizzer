Erythronium oregonum

Erythronium oregonum, commonly known as the Oregon trillium, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its three broad, waxy leaves and a single, showy white or pinkish flower with six petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is a perennial herb that blooms in early spring and plays an important role in its native ecosystem.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
