Erica pageana

Erica pageana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica pageana is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
