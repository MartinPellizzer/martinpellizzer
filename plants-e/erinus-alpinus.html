Erinus alpinus

Erinus alpinus, commonly known as the alpine eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Erinus alpinus is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in low-maintenance, ornamental plants.
