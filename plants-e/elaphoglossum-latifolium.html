Elaphoglossum latifolium

Elaphoglossum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf harts-tongue fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, bi- or tri-pinnate fronds that are typically broad and gracefully arching. The plant produces small, clustered spore sacs on the underside of its fronds, which are essential for its reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests and rocky areas.

Elaphoglossum latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
