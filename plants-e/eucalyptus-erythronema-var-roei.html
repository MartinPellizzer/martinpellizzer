Eucalyptus erythronema var. roei

Eucalyptus erythronema var. roei, commonly known as the red-stemmed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

This variant is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
