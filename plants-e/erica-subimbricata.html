Erica subimbricata

Erica subimbricata, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica subimbricata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
