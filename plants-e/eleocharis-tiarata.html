Eleocharis tiarata

Eleocharis tiarata, commonly known as the tiarata sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to New Zealand.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its small, spike-like inflorescence consists of numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Eleocharis tiarata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
