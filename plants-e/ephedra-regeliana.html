Ephedra regeliana

Ephedra regeliana, commonly known as the desert tea or jointed clubmoss, is a shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains the alkaloid ephedrine, which has been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological significance, it is a subject of both scientific study and conservation interest.
