Echium humile var. pycnanthum

Echium humile var. pycnanthum, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically reaches a height of less than 10 inches, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
