Eugenia mosenii

Eugenia mosenii, commonly known as the Moseni guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the humid lowland forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
