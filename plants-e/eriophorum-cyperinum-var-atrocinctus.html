Eriophorum cyperinum var. atrocinctus

Eriophorum cyperinum var. atrocinctus, commonly known as the black-stemmed cottongrass, is a variant of the cottongrass genus native to northern regions.

It is characterized by its dark, hairy stems and fluffy, white seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing peat soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
