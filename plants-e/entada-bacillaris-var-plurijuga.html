Entada bacillaris var. plurijuga

Entada bacillaris var. plurijuga, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, fibrous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
