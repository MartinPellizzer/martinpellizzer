Eucalyptus × castrensis

Eucalyptus × castrensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. Its bark is often flaky and peels away in thin layers, revealing a smooth, pale underbark.

Eucalyptus × castrensis is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and resilient species in its native range.
