Eupatorium lanigerum var. crassipes

Eupatorium lanigerum var. crassipes, commonly known as roughhead thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features sturdy, hairy stems and large, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
