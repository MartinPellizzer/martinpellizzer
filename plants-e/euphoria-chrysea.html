Euphoria chrysea

Euphoria chrysea, commonly known as the golden euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its environment. The plant has a succulent stem and produces small, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
