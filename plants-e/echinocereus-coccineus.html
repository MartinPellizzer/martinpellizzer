Echinocereus coccineus

Echinocereus coccineus, commonly known as the red torch cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, reddish-brown spines. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and ability to withstand arid conditions make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
