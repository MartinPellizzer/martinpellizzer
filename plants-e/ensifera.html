Ensifera

Ensifera, commonly known as the grasshopper plant, is a group of flowering plants belonging to the order Lamiales.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and showy, tubular flowers that often attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. The genus includes several species, such as *Verbena* and *Scrophularia*, which are widely cultivated for their ornamental value. These plants typically thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making them popular in gardens and landscapes.

Their botanical significance also lies in their role in traditional medicine and ecological systems.
