Evansia gracilipes

Evansia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a succulent species characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces a milky sap that is toxic.

Evansia gracilipes is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
