Eriochloa eggersii

Eriochloa eggersii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it its common name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriochloa eggersii plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
