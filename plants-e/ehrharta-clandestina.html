Ehrharta clandestina

Ehrharta clandestina, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ehrharta clandestina plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes cultivated for erosion control.
