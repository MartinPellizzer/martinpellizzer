Erica torosa

Erica torosa, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer. Erica torosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native environment.
