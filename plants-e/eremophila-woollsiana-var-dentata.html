Eremophila woollsiana var. dentata

Eremophila woollsiana var. dentata, commonly known as the Woolly Emu Bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It features wiry, grey-green stems and lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically during the spring and summer seasons. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for low-water gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
