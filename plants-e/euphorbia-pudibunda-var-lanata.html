Euphorbia pudibunda var. lanata

Euphorbia pudibunda var. lanata, commonly known as the woolly euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
