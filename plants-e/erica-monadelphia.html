Erica monadelphia

Erica monadelphia, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. Erica monadelphia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
