Echium pycnanthum var. mogadorense

Echium pycnanthum var. mogadorense, commonly known as the Canary Island thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Canary Islands.

It features a robust, branching stem covered in dense, spiny leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
