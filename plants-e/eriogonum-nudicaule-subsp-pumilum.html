Eriogonum nudicaule subsp. pumilum

Eriogonum nudicaule subsp.

pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf leaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
