Erica vanheurckii

Erica vanheurckii, commonly known as the Vanheurck's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Erica vanheurckii produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
