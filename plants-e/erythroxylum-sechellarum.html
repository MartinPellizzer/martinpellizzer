Erythroxylum sechellarum

Erythroxylum sechellarum, commonly known as the Seychelles erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to the Seychelles Islands.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest undergrowth. It is notable for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.

However, it is not widely cultivated and remains relatively rare in the wild.
