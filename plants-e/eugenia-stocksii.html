Eugenia stocksii

Eugenia stocksii, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Following flowering, it produces small, fleshy berries that are red when mature.

Eugenia stocksii is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
