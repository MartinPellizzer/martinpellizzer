Epidendrum vasquezii

Epidendrum vasquezii, commonly known as the Vasquez's orchid, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its sympodial growth habit and elongated pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum vasquezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
