Erica approximata

Erica approximata, commonly known as the common heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in sandy, acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica approximata is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
