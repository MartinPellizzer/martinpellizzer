Euphorbia moratii var. bemarahensis

Euphorbia moratii var. bemarahensis, commonly known as the Bemarahensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and modified leaves that serve as spines. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
