Erica tiariflora

Erica tiariflora, commonly known as the Cape heath or yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica tiariflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and resilience in harsh environments.
