Erythrina coralloides

Erythrina coralloides, commonly known as the coral tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red, flame-like flowers.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions such as Madagascar and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant features palmately compound leaves with three leaflets, and its distinctive coral-colored inflorescences are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers.

Erythrina coralloides is also notable for its woody, branching structure and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils with ample sunlight.
