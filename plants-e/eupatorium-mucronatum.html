Eupatorium mucronatum

Eupatorium mucronatum, commonly known as the narrow-tipped thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, white to pale blue, and appear in clusters, blooming from late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium mucronatum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
