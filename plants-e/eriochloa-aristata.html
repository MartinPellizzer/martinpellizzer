Eriochloa aristata

Eriochloa aristata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to purple flowers. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Eriochloa aristata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
