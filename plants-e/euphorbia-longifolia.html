Euphorbia longifolia

Euphorbia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has spiny leaves and produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Euphorbia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
