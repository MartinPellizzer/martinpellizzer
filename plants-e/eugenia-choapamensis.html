Eugenia choapamensis

Eugenia choapamensis, commonly known as the Choapamensis Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and understudied species in botanical research.
