Erysimum × suffruticosum

Erysimum × suffruticosum, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a perennial plant that exhibits characteristics of both Erysimum and its parent species.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, are borne in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum × suffruticosum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
