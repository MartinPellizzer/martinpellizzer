Eriophyllum staechadifolium

Eriophyllum staechadifolium, commonly known as the sticky goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving it a sticky texture. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriophyllum staechadifolium is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.
