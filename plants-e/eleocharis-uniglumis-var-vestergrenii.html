Eleocharis uniglumis var. vestergrenii

Eleocharis uniglumis var. vestergrenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its leaves are narrow and erect, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various invertebrates.

The species is notable for its adaptability to fluctuating water levels and its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
