Eulychnia acida var. procumbens

Eulychnia acida var. procumbens, commonly known as the sour fig or sour orange, is a variant of the Eulychnia acida species native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, sour-tasting berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its drought tolerance.
