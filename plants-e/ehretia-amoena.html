Ehretia amoena

Ehretia amoena, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy green leaves and striking clusters of white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing three seeds each. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
