Eriogonum latifolium

Eriogonum latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Eriogonum latifolium is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and its tolerance to drought conditions.
