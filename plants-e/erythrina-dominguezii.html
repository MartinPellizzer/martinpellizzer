Erythrina dominguezii

Erythrina dominguezii, commonly known as the Mexican coral tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

Erythrina dominguezii is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
