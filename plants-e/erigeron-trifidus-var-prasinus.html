Erigeron trifidus var. prasinus

Erigeron trifidus var. prasinus, commonly known as the greenleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and prairies. Its leaves are deeply divided and often have a bluish-green hue, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
