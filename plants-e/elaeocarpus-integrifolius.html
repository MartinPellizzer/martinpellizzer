Elaeocarpus integrifolius

Elaeocarpus integrifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically entire and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several hard seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
