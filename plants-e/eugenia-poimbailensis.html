Eugenia poimbailensis

Eugenia poimbailensis, commonly known as the Poimbail's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a strong aromatic scent.

Eugenia poimbailensis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
