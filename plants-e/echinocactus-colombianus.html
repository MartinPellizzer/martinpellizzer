Echinocactus colombianus

Echinocactus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a slow-growing cactus characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by insects and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
