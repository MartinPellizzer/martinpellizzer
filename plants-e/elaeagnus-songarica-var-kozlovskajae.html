Elaeagnus songarica var. kozlovskajae

Elaeagnus songarica var. kozlovskajae, commonly known as the Kozlovskaya silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climates.
