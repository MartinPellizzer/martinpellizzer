Eupatoriadelphus maculatus

Eupatoriadelphus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features tall, upright stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing pink to purple disc florets surrounded by white ray florets.

Eupatoriadelphus maculatus is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
