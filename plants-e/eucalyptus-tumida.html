Eucalyptus tumida

Eucalyptus tumida, commonly known as the woolly gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or yellow-green in color. Eucalyptus tumida is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is well adapted to the dry and sunny conditions of its native habitat.
