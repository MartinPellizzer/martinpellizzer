Eurybia imbricata

Eurybia imbricata, commonly known as the white wood-sorrel, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Eurybia imbricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
