Euphorbia brasiliensis var. blanchetii

Euphorbia brasiliensis var. blanchetii, commonly known as the Brazilian spurge, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
