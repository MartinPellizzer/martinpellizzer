Euphrasia anglica

Euphrasia anglica, commonly known as the common eyebright, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant features upright, hairy stems with opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in whorls along the stem, with prominent, showy glands.

Euphrasia anglica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
