Eulophia bella

Eulophia bella, commonly known as the golden eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces striking, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Eulophia bella is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
