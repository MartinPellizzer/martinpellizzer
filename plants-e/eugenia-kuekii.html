Eugenia kuekii

Eugenia kuekii, commonly known as the kumquat tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, orange-colored fruits. The leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, with a leathery texture. The fruit, which is edible, contains a segmented, segmented pulp and a membranous peel that can be eaten as well.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
