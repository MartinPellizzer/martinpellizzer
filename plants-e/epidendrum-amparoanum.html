Epidendrum amparoanum

Epidendrum amparoanum, commonly known as the Amparo orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendant-like inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Epidendrum amparoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
