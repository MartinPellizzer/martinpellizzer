Erica aragonensis

Erica aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon heath, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous regions, and is adapted to dry, acidic conditions. Erica aragonensis produces pale pink to white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
