Erymophyllum ramosum subsp. ramosum

Erymophyllum ramosum subsp.

ramosum, commonly known as the coastal goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California. It is a perennial herb with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a low, sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with salt spray and strong winds.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
