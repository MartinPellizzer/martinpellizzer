Elaeis melanococca

Elaeis melanococca, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its large, feather-like leaves and a robust, unbranched trunk. The plant produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds, which are harvested for palm oil production. Its botanical features make it an important agricultural species in the region.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils.
