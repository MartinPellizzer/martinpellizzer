Erica pavettiflora

Erica pavettiflora, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica pavettiflora is also appreciated for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
