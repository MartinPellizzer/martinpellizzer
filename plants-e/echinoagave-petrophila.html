Echinoagave petrophila

Echinoagave petrophila, commonly known as the rock agave, is a succulent plant native to the rocky regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence with small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of succulents.
