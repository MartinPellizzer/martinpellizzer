Eria carnicolor

Eria carnicolor, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, pinkish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as tropical forests. Its leaves are broad and often have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
