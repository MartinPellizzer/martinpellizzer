Euterpe caatinga

Euterpe caatinga, commonly known as the caatinga palm, is a species native to the semi-arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
