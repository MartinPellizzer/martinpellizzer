Euphorbia hispida

Euphorbia hispida, commonly known as the wooly spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Euphorbia hispida is drought-tolerant and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
