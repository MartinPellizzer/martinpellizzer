Echinochloa crus-galli f. zelayensis

Echinochloa crus-galli f. zelayensis, commonly known as the Zelaya panicgrass, is a variant of the common barnyard grass.

It is a grass species native to the Americas, characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a competitive weed in various ecosystems.
