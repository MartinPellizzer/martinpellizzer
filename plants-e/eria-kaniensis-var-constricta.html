Eria kaniensis var. constricta

Eria kaniensis var. constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
