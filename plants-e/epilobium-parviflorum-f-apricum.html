Epilobium parviflorum f. apricum

Epilobium parviflorum f. apricum, commonly known as the apricot-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The flowers are small, typically pink or purple, and are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches. This plant is native to Europe and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
