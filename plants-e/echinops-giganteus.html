Echinops giganteus

Echinops giganteus, commonly known as the giant thimbleweed, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and spherical, blue-gray flower heads that resemble a globe. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Echinops giganteus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
