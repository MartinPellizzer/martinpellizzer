Elaeocarpus rufovestitus

Elaeocarpus rufovestitus, commonly known as the red-barked elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and potential ecological roles.

The species is also noted for its slow growth and long lifespan, making it a significant component of tropical forest ecosystems.
