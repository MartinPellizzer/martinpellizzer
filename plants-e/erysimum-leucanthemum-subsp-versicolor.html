Erysimum leucanthemum subsp. versicolor

Erysimum leucanthemum subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the variable wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, four-petaled flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
