Erica bosseri

Erica bosseri, commonly known as the Bosser's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Erica bosseri typically blooms in spring, producing pink or white flowers that attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
