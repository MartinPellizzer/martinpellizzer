Elymus beijingensis

Elymus beijingensis, commonly known as Beijing wild rye, is a perennial grass native to northern China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and disturbed areas.

Elymus beijingensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
