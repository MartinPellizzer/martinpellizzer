Eugenia excoriata

Eugenia excoriata, commonly known as the African wild plum, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a whitish, waxy bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species, including birds and small mammals.

Eugenia excoriata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
