Eleocharis palustris var. calva

Eleocharis palustris var. calva, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland habitats.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
