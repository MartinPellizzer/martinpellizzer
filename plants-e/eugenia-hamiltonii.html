Eugenia hamiltonii

Eugenia hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Eugenia hamiltonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
