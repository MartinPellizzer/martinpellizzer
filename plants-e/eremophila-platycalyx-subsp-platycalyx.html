Eremophila platycalyx subsp. platycalyx

Eremophila platycalyx subsp.

platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaved emu plant, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and a preference for well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky areas and open woodlands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
