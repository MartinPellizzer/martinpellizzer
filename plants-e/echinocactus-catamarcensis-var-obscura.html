Echinocactus catamarcensis var. obscura

Echinocactus catamarcensis var. obscura, commonly known as the Catamarca obscura cactus, is a rare and striking species native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which are borne on elongated pedicels. Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and requires careful cultivation.
