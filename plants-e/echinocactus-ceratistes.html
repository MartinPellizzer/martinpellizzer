Echinocactus ceratistes

Echinocactus ceratistes, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise spiky appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

This cactus is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant.
