Elaeocarpus costatus

Elaeocarpus costatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a distinct midrib. The tree produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its foliage and bark are often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Elaeocarpus costatus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is commonly found in lowland forests.
