Euodia elaeodendroides

Euodia elaeodendroides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically orange to red in color. Its hard, durable wood is highly valued for its strength and resistance to decay.

Euodia elaeodendroides thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
