Eriogonum ovalifolium var. typicum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its fibrous roots help it survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
