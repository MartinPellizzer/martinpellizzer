Elephantopsis biflora

Elephantopsis biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers during the cooler months. Its unique appearance, resembling a large, rounded bulb, has earned it the common name.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking visual appeal and low maintenance requirements.
