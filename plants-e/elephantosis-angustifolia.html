Elephantosis angustifolia

Elephantosis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf elephant tree, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it grows in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, angular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by elongated, pod-like fruits.

It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
