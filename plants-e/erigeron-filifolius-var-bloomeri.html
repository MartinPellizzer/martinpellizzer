Erigeron filifolius var. bloomeri

Erigeron filifolius var. bloomeri, commonly known as the Bloomer's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and clusters of small, sky-blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It is an annual or biennial species, often found in disturbed soils and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
