Eriophorum alpicola

Eriophorum alpicola, commonly known as the alpine tussock sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eriophorum alpicola plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its harsh, mountainous habitat.
