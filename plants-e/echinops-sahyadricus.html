Echinops sahyadricus

Echinops sahyadricus, commonly known as the Sahyadri thimpu, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are prominent during the dry season. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. Its blue to purple flowers appear in clusters and are visited by various pollinators.

Echinops sahyadricus plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for small animals and insects.
