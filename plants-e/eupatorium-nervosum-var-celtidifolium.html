Eupatorium nervosum var. celtidifolium

Eupatorium nervosum var. celtidifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in composite heads, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
