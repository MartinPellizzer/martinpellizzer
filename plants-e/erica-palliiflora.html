Erica palliiflora

Erica palliiflora, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica palliiflora is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
