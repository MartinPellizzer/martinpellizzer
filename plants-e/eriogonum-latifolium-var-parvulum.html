Eriogonum latifolium var. parvulum

Eriogonum latifolium var. parvulum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white or pink flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil.
