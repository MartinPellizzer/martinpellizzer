Echinopsis hamatispina

Echinopsis hamatispina, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, spiny tubercles. The plant produces pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in cactus gardens and collections.
