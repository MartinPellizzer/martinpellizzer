Eupatorium lallavei

Eupatorium lallavei, commonly known as the Caribbean feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in white or pale blue hues.

Eupatorium lallavei is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
