Eriocaulon bryoides

Eriocaulon bryoides, commonly known as the moss-like ricegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and tiny, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and swampy areas. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts.

Eriocaulon bryoides plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
