Euodia tahitensis var. brachiata

Euodia tahitensis var. brachiata, commonly known as Tahitian laurier or Tahitian cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces yellowish-orange fruits that contain aromatic seeds, which are used in traditional Polynesian medicine and as a spice. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal forests and shaded areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanic gardens and ecological studies.
