Eupatorium huantae

Eupatorium huantae, commonly known as the Huantae boneset, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium huantae is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
