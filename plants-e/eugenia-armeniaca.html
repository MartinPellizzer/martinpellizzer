Eugenia armeniaca

Eugenia armeniaca, commonly known as the Armenian cherry or the cherry laurel, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region and parts of Turkey, growing in temperate climates. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, cherry-like berries. Its leaves are glossy and evergreen, and the tree can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
