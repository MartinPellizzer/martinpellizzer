Eugenioides parviflorum

Eugenioides parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered eugenioid, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fragrant aroma, which is a common trait among members of the Myrtaceae family. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
