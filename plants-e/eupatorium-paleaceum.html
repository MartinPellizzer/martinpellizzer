Eupatorium paleaceum

Eupatorium paleaceum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, rich soils. Its showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
