Euphorbia abyssinica var. erythraeae

Euphorbia abyssinica var. erythraeae, commonly known as the Red-veined Spurge, is a succulent plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and striking red veins that run through its green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
