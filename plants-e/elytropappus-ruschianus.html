Elytropappus ruschianus

Elytropappus ruschianus, commonly known as the golden aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense cluster of disk flowers, which are typically tubular in shape. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Elytropappus ruschianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
