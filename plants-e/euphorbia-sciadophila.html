Euphorbia sciadophila

Euphorbia sciadophila, commonly known as the shadow euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia sciadophila is also known for its ability to tolerate high temperatures and poor soil conditions.
