Echinocereus rischeri

Echinocereus rischeri, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments with minimal water.

Echinocereus rischeri is well adapted to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
