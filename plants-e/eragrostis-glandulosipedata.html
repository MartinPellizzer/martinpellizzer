Eragrostis glandulosipedata

Eragrostis glandulosipedata, commonly known as the glandular-stemmed lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis glandulosipedata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
