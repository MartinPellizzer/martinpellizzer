Elaphoglossum taylorianum

Elaphoglossum taylorianum, commonly known as Taylor's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests.

Elaphoglossum taylorianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
