Erica pearsoniana

Erica pearsoniana, commonly known as the Pearson's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Erica pearsoniana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
