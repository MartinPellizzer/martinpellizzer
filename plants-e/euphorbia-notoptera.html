Euphorbia notoptera

Euphorbia notoptera, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, woody stem with a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble petals. Its leaves are typically narrow and may be shed during periods of drought.

Euphorbia notoptera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience to arid conditions.
