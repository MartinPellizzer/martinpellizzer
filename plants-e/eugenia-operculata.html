Eugenia operculata

Eugenia operculata, commonly known as the operculated guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy appearance, often with a slight pubescence.

Eugenia operculata is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
