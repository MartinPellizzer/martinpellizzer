Eupatorium origanoides

Eupatorium origanoides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium origanoides is a valuable plant for pollinators, particularly butterflies and bees.
