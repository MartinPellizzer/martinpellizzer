Eragrostis pubiculmis

Eragrostis pubiculmis, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Eragrostis pubiculmis is often used in restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
