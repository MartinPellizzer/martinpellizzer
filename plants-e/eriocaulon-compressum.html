Eriocaulon compressum

Eriocaulon compressum, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often compressed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in dense clusters, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon compressum plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
