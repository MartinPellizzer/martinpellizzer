Eria hawkesii

Eria hawkesii, commonly known as the Hawkes' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome and multiple stems that arise from the base. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, and green, with intricate patterns on the petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, organic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
