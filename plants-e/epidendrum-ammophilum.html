Epidendrum ammophilum

Epidendrum ammophilum, commonly known as the sandhill epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in open, sandy or rocky habitats. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum ammophilum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
