Elaeagnus umbellata var. coreana

Elaeagnus umbellata var. coreana, commonly known as Korean silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
