Elaeocarpus batui

Elaeocarpus batui, commonly known as the Batu elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
