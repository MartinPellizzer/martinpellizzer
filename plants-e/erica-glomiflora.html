Erica glomiflora

Erica glomiflora, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica glomiflora is well adapted to dry conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal or Mediterranean climates.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
