Eryngium longipetiolatum

Eryngium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features striking blue to purple spiny flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a distinct central vein. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its unique appearance and ecological benefits make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
