Eriogonum pratense

Eriogonum pratense, commonly known as the meadow flax, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, spikelike clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in open, grassy areas and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Eriogonum pratense plays a role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
