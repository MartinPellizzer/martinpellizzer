Eugenia viridescens

Eugenia viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and edible, often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
