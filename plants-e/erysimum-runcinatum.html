Erysimum runcinatum

Erysimum runcinatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum runcinatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
