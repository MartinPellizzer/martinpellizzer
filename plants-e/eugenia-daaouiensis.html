Eugenia daaouiensis

Eugenia daaouiensis, commonly known as the Moroccan myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Morocco.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
