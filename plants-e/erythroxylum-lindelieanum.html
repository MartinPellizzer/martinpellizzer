Erythroxylum lindelieanum

Erythroxylum lindelieanum, commonly known as the Lindelieanum Erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a waxy texture. It produces small, red berries that contain the seeds, and it is often found in humid, tropical environments.

Due to its botanical features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists studying plant diversity in the Andean region.
