Euxolus scleranthoides

Euxolus scleranthoides, commonly known as the woolly bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of eastern Australia.

It is a small herbaceous plant characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in freshwater environments. It has a unique ability to absorb nutrients from water through its specialized leaves.

Euxolus scleranthoides plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to the biodiversity of wetland areas.
