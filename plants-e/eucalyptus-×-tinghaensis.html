Eucalyptus × tinghaensis

Eucalyptus × tinghaensis, commonly known as the Tingha gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a blue-green hue, while the flowers are typically white or pale yellow. This species is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

It is valued for its timber and has also been used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
