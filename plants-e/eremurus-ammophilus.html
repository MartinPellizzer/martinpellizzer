Eremurus ammophilus

Eremurus ammophilus, commonly known as the desert candle, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow to orange-red flowers. The plant has a robust, fibrous root system that allows it to thrive in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, reducing water loss in harsh environments.

Eremurus ammophilus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
