Echinocereus polyacanthus var. pacificus

Echinocereus polyacanthus var. pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a columnar cactus with a spiny, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to arid and coastal climates.
