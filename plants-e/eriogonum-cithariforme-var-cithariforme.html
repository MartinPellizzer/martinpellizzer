Eriogonum cithariforme var. cithariforme

Eriogonum cithariforme var. cithariforme, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance in its native range.
