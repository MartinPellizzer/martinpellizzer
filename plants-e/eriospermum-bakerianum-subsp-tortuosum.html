Eriospermum bakerianum subsp. tortuosum

Eriospermum bakerianum subsp.

tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted eriosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, and often hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually linear and may have a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
