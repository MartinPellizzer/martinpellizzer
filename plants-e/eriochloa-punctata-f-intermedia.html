Eriochloa punctata f. intermedia

Eriochloa punctata f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate punctate grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or clumps. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered with small, white to pale green tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in marginal conditions.
