Erysimum capitatum var. purshii

Erysimum capitatum var. purshii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features include a short, hairy stem and small, pinnately divided leaves.
