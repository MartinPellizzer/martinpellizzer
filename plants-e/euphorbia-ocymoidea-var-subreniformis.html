Euphorbia ocymoidea var. subreniformis

Euphorbia ocymoidea var. subreniformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of North America.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, narrow leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
