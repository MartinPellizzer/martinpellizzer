Ervum lunatum

Ervum lunatum, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The moonflower vine is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
