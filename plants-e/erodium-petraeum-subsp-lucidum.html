Erodium petraeum subsp. lucidum

Erodium petraeum subsp.

lucidum, commonly known as the glossy bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to rocky and arid environments. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance.

The flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive spur at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
