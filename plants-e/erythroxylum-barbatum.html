Erythroxylum barbatum

Erythroxylum barbatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or woolly balsa, is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, tropical environments and is adapted to thrive in shaded undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
