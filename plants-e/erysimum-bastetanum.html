Erysimum bastetanum

Erysimum bastetanum, commonly known as the Canary Island wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh, sunny conditions of its native habitat.
