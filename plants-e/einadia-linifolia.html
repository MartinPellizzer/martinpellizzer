Einadia linifolia

Einadia linifolia, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage that helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine, silky hairs that contribute to its common name.

Einadia linifolia is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in conditions with limited water availability.
