Echinopsis pasacana

Echinopsis pasacana, commonly known as the Pasacana cactus, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a dense covering of white spines and produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
