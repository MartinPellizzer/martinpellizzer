Eucharidium grandiflorum var. album

Eucharidium grandiflorum var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered eucharidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and produces showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
