Erysimum grandiflorum

Erysimum grandiflorum, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.

Erysimum grandiflorum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
