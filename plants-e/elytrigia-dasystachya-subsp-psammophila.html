Elytrigia dasystachya subsp. psammophila

Elytrigia dasystachya subsp.

psammophila, commonly known as the sand fescue, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. It is well adapted to sandy and gravelly soils, making it a common species in dry, disturbed habitats.

Its ability to thrive in harsh conditions makes it an important component of ecological restoration projects.
