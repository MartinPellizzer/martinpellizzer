Eugenia kanarensis

Eugenia kanarensis, commonly known as the Malabar plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically purple or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
