Eriocaulon rockianum

Eriocaulon rockianum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that often form dense, mat-like growths in wetland environments. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds, marshes, and along stream edges. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spike-like inflorescences.

Eriocaulon rockianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
