Eucalyptus brandiana

Eucalyptus brandiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus brandiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its ecological significance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
