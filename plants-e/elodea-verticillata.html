Elodea verticillata

Elodea verticillata, commonly known as water smartweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has whorled leaves that are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces small, green flowers that emerge above the water surface in late summer.

Elodea verticillata plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.
