Erica trimera subsp. meruensis

Erica trimera subsp.

meruensis, commonly known as the Meru heath, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants.

The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in montane forests and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
