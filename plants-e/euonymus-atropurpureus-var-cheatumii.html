Euonymus atropurpureus var. cheatumii

Euonymus atropurpureus var. cheatumii, commonly known as the purpleleaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lobed leaves that turn a vibrant red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red, four-lobed fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a hedge plant.
