Elaeagnus lipoensis

Elaeagnus lipoensis, commonly known as the Lipoensis silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible fruits that ripen in the autumn. The plant has pinnately compound leaves and produces yellowish flowers in the spring. It is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

Elaeagnus lipoensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
