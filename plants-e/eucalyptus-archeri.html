Eucalyptus archeri

Eucalyptus archeri, commonly known as the mountain eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal creamy-white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in cooler, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eucalyptus archeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
