Elatostema surculosum var. subincisum

Elatostema surculosum var. subincisum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false shamrock, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for woodland gardens.
