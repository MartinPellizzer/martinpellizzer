Echium candicans var. noronhae

Echium candicans var. noronhae, commonly known as the Cape Verde evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Verde Islands.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its stems are erect and often branched, while its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
