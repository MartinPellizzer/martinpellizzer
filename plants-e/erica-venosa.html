Erica venosa

Erica venosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
