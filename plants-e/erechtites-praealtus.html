Erechtites praealtus

Erechtites praealtus, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and pond edges.

Its seed heads are distinctive, featuring a crown-like structure that aids in wind dispersal.
