Eucalyptus nova-anglica

Eucalyptus nova-anglica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other eucalyptus species.

Eucalyptus nova-anglica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
