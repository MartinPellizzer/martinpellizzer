Erythronium sajanense var. rubinum

Erythronium sajanense var. rubinum, commonly known as the Siberian dog-tooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces a single, showy bell-shaped flower with a distinctive purple-pink hue. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its blooming period typically occurs in late spring, adding a striking visual element to woodland gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
