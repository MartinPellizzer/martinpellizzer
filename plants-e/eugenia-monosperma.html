Eugenia monosperma

Eugenia monosperma, commonly known as the solitary seed eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a deep red color, containing a single seed, which gives the species its name.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
