Eryngium phyteumatos

Eryngium phyteumatos, commonly known as the spiked eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
