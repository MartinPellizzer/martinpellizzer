Eriogonum baileyi var. tomentosum

Eriogonum baileyi var. tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
