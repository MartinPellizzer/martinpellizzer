Eysenhardtia byei

Eysenhardtia byei, commonly known as the Mexican ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

Eysenhardtia byei is valued for its hardiness and ornamental appeal in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
