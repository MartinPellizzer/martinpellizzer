Episcia erythropus

Episcia erythropus, commonly known as the red-leaved wax plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or orange foliage, which contrasts strikingly with its dark green stems. It produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds.

Episcia erythropus is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
