Eugenia diaphana

Eugenia diaphana, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is typically smooth and may peel in thin layers, and it can grow as a tree or shrub depending on environmental conditions.

Eugenia diaphana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
