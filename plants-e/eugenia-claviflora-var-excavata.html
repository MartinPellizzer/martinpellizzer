Eugenia claviflora var. excavata

Eugenia claviflora var. excavata, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but have a strong, musky flavor. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
