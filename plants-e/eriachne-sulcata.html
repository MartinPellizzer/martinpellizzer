Eriachne sulcata

Eriachne sulcata, commonly known as the channel grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands.

Eriachne sulcata plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
