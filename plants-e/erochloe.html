Erochloe

Erochloe, commonly known as the bluegrass grass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical leaves. The inflorescence consists of a spikelet with two to three small, spikelet-like flowers. Erochloe species are typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

They play an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
