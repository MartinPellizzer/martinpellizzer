Eugenia cavalcanteana

Eugenia cavalcanteana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia cavalcanteana*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
