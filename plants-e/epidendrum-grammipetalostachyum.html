Epidendrum grammipetalostachyum

Epidendrum grammipetalostachyum, commonly known as the Grammitis-leaved Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and elongated, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a waxy coating that helps retain moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to a range of growing conditions.
