Euphrasia imbricans

Euphrasia imbricans, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a velvety texture.

Euphrasia imbricans is also noted for its medicinal uses, with traditional applications in treating eye conditions and respiratory ailments.
