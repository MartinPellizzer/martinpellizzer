Echinacea purpurea var. laevigata

Echinacea purpurea var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and large, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent yellow central disk. The plant produces a distinctive, spiny seed head that persists through the winter, providing food for birds and insects. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its medicinal properties, including immune-boosting effects, have contributed to its widespread cultivation and use in herbal remedies.
