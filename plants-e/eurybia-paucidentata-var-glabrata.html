Eurybia paucidentata var. glabrata

Eurybia paucidentata var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth yarrow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves that are smooth and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in flat-topped clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological and ornamental purposes.
