Erigeron compactus var. consimilis

Erigeron compactus var. consimilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features a compact, bushy growth habit with small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often used in native plant gardens.
