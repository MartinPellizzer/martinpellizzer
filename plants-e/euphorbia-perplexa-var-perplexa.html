Euphorbia perplexa var. perplexa

Euphorbia perplexa var. perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a thick, cylindrical stem covered in a layer of fine, white hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its fleshy stem for survival during dry periods.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping in regions with limited water availability.
