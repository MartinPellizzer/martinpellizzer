Eriogonum pendulum

Eriogonum pendulum, commonly known as the hanging buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Eriogonum pendulum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
