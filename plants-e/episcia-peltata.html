Episcia peltata

Episcia peltata, commonly known as the velvet leaf.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and silver. It produces small, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which bloom in clusters.

Episcia peltata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
