Epiphyllum cooperi

Epiphyllum cooperi, commonly known as the night bloomer cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing, succulent plant that produces large, showy flowers at night. The plant has flat, leaf-like stems that are often used for propagation. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Its flowers are typically pink or white and emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.
