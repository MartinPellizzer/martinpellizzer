Erigeron breviscapus var. tibeticus

Erigeron breviscapus var. tibeticus, commonly known as the Tibetan fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
