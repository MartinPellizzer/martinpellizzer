Echinocactus chrysacanthus

Echinocactus chrysacanthus, commonly known as the golden cholla, is a spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with yellowish-green skin and produces yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and ability to thrive in harsh conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
