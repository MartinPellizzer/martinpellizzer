Eriosyce napina subsp. aerocarpa

Eriosyce napina subsp.

aerocarpa, commonly known as the skyflower, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom during the dry season, typically in summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a dense root system adapted to arid conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
