Echeandia vaginata

Echeandia vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Echeandia vaginata is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
