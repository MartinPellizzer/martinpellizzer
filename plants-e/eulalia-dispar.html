Eulalia dispar

Eulalia dispar, commonly known as the slender grass or dwarf pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, feathery flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, forming panicles at the top of the stems. Eulalia dispar thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its graceful appearance.
