Eugenia ramonae

Eugenia ramonae, commonly known as the Ramon's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Eugenia ramonae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
