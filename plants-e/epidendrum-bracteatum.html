Epidendrum bracteatum

Epidendrum bracteatum, commonly known as the Orchid with a Velvet Leaf, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

Epidendrum bracteatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
