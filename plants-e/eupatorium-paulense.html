Eupatorium paulense

Eupatorium paulense, commonly known as Brazilian boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, humid conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium paulense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential medicinal properties.
