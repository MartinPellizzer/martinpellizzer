Eugenia haberi

Eugenia haberi, commonly known as the Haber's Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, smooth, reddish-brown berries that are edible but not widely cultivated.

Eugenia haberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
