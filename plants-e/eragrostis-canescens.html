Eragrostis canescens

Eragrostis canescens, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
