Eupatorium stuebelii

Eupatorium stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium stuebelii is known for its aromatic foliage and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
