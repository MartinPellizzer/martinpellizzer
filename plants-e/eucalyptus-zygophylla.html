Eucalyptus zygophylla

Eucalyptus zygophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. This species is part of the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and medicinal significance. Eucalyptus zygophylla typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
