Eugenia pusilana

Eugenia pusilana, commonly known as the small eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia pusilana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
