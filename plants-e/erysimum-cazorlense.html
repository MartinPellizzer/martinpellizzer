Erysimum cazorlense

Erysimum cazorlense, commonly known as the Cazorla wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other well-known flowering plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. It has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain seeds.
