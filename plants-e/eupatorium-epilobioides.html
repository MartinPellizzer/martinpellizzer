Eupatorium epilobioides

Eupatorium epilobioides, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in moist woodlands.

Eupatorium epilobioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
