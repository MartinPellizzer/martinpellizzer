Eremopappus

Eremopappus, commonly known as desert dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States and northern Mexico. The plants typically have upright stems covered in fine hairs, and their leaves are usually narrow and linear. Eremopappus species produce small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads that aid in wind dispersal.

These plants are adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and rapid growth cycles.
