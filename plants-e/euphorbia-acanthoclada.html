Euphorbia acanthoclada

Euphorbia acanthoclada, commonly known as the spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in spines or bristles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
