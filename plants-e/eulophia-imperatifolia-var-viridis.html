Eulophia imperatifolia var. viridis

Eulophia imperatifolia var. viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, clump-forming growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces striking, greenish-white flowers that are often fragrant and appear in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
