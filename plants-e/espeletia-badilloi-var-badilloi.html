Espeletia badilloi var. badilloi

Espeletia badilloi var. badilloi, commonly known as the Santa Rosa mountain heather, is a species native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, compact rosettes of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces striking pink to purple flower clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
