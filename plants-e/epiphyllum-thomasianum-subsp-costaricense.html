Epiphyllum thomasianum subsp. costaricense

Epiphyllum thomasianum subsp.

costaricense, commonly known as the Costa Rican epiphyllum, is a climbing cactus native to Central America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that resemble leaves.

The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often opening at night. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
