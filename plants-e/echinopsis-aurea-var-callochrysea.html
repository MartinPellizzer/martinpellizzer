Echinopsis aurea var. callochrysea

Echinopsis aurea var. callochrysea, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are typically tubular with a distinctive golden hue, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.
