Eugenia melanadenia var. melanadenia

Eugenia melanadenia var. melanadenia, commonly known as the black-adenia eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elliptical leaves with a glossy dark green surface and prominent veins. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, round berries that are dark purple when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
