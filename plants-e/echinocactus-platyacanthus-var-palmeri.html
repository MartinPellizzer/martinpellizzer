Echinocactus platyacanthus var. palmeri

Echinocactus platyacanthus var. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and conservation programs.
