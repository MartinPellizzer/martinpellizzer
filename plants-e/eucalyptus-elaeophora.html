Eucalyptus elaeophora

Eucalyptus elaeophora, commonly known as the white box, is an evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark that peels in thin strips, and its lance-shaped, grey-green leaves. The plant produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Eucalyptus elaeophora plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
