Ephedra distachya f. caspia

Ephedra distachya f. caspia, commonly known as the Caspian ephedra, is a variant of the ephedra plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typically pollinated by wind. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

This species is often found in regions such as Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.
