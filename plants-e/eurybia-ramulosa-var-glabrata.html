Eurybia ramulosa var. glabrata

Eurybia ramulosa var. glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous goldenrod, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils in open habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. The plant features upright, branching stems that are usually hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabrata.". Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy underside, while the flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in panicles.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
