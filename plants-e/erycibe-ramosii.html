Erycibe ramosii

Erycibe ramosii, commonly known as the hairy red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Erycibe ramosii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant blooms.
