Eriocoma scribneri

Eriocoma scribneri, commonly known as the Scribner's ericoid, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, and is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
