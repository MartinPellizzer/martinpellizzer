Elaeocarpus hainanensis var. brachyphyllus

Elaeocarpus hainanensis var. brachyphyllus, commonly known as the Hainan elaeocarpus, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
