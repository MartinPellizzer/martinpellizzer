Echinopsis mamillosa subsp. silvatica

Echinopsis mamillosa subsp.

silvatica, commonly known as the Silvatic Echinopsis, is a succulent cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season.

The plant has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
