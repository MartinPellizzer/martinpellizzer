Elleanthus ensatus

Elleanthus ensatus, commonly known as the bearded reed, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found along the edges of lakes and rivers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Elleanthus ensatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
