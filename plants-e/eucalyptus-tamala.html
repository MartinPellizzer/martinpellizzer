Eucalyptus tamala

Eucalyptus tamala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue.

Eucalyptus tamala is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
