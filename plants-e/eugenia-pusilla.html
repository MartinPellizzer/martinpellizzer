Eugenia pusilla

Eugenia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fragrance.

Eugenia pusilla is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions, making it a resilient choice for landscaping.
