Enkianthus deflexus var. variegatus

Enkianthus deflexus var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow tones on its lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in late spring, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
