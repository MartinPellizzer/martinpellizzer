Erica inflaticalyx

Erica inflaticalyx, commonly known as the inflated calyx heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are typically small, leathery, and arranged in a compact cluster.

The inflated calyx, from which the species gets its name, is a distinctive feature that sets it apart from other Erica species.
