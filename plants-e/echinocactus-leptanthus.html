Echinocactus leptanthus

Echinocactus leptanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in low-rainfall conditions.

Echinocactus leptanthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
