Eria flava

Eria flava, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded forest environments. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, while the flowers are usually yellow or orange in color.

Eria flava is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
