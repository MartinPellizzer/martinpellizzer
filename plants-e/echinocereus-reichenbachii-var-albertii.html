Echinocereus reichenbachii var. albertii

Echinocereus reichenbachii var. albertii, commonly known as the Albert's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spine-tipped cactus with a distinctive appearance featuring ribbed stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun exposure. It is drought-tolerant and well-suited for arid climates, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a notable addition to desert gardens.
