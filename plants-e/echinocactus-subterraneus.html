Echinocactus subterraneus

Echinocactus subterraneus, commonly known as the Pincushion Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus with a distinctive spiny, globular shape that resembles a pincushion. The plant features a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Echinocactus subterraneus produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiky structure.
