Eupatorium heteroclinium

Eupatorium heteroclinium, commonly known as the purpletop eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, lavender to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinct, slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium heteroclinium is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
