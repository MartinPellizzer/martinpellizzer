Erica cunoniensis

Erica cunoniensis, commonly known as the Cunoni heath, is a flowering plant native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the warmer months. Erica cunoniensis is well adapted to acidic, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining biodiversity in its native environment.
