Elaeocarpus sallehianus

Elaeocarpus sallehianus, commonly known as the Sallehian elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically dispersed by birds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
