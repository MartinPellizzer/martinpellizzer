Eragrostis venustula

Eragrostis venustula, commonly known as the velvet lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense tufts or clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
