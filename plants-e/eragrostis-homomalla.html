Eragrostis homomalla

Eragrostis homomalla, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in association with other grasses and forbs in diverse ecological communities.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems.
