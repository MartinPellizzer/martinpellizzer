Elymus wiegandii f. calvescens

Elymus wiegandii f. calvescens, commonly known as the Wiegand's barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, awned grains. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and tough, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent when crushed.

This grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
