Evolvulus gypsophiloides var. brevifolius

Evolvulus gypsophiloides var. brevifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is part of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
