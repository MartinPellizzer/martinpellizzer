Eugenia woodii

Eugenia woodii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or the white cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and smooth, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. Eugenia woodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for gardens in temperate climates.
