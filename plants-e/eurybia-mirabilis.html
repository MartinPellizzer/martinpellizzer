Eurybia mirabilis

Eurybia mirabilis, commonly known as the showy golden aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and open woodlands, reaching heights of up to 2 feet. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy as well.

Eurybia mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
