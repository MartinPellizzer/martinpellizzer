Elaeis guineensis var. gracilinux

Elaeis guineensis var. gracilinux, commonly known as the slender African oil palm, is a variant of the oil palm native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its slender trunk and smaller leaves compared to the standard oil palm. The plant produces fruit clusters that contain oil-rich kernels, making it valuable for agricultural and industrial uses. It thrives in tropical climates with high rainfall and well-drained soil.

Due to its genetic distinctiveness, it is of interest for breeding programs aimed at improving oil yield and resilience.
