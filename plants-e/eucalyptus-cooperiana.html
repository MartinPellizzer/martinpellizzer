Eucalyptus cooperiana

Eucalyptus cooperiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. It produces woody, cylindrical spikes that are often used in traditional Aboriginal practices.

Eucalyptus cooperiana is well adapted to harsh environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
