Eulophia angornensis

Eulophia angornensis, commonly known as the Angorn's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its erect, slender pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to rocky or forested habitats.

Eulophia angornensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
