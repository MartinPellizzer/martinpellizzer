Eupatorium burchellii

Eupatorium burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's boneset, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it often grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic properties.

Eupatorium burchellii is also valued for its medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
