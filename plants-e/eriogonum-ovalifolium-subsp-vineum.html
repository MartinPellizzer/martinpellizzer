Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp. vineum

Eriogonum ovalifolium subsp.

vineum, commonly known as the vine-mountain buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with hairy surfaces and serrated margins. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
