Erysimum inconspicuum var. syrticola

Erysimum inconspicuum var. syrticola, commonly known as the Desert Mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This species is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
