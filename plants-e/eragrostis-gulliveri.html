Eragrostis gulliveri

Eragrostis gulliveri, commonly known as Gulliver's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a blue-gray sheen. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
