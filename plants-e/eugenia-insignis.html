Eugenia insignis

Eugenia insignis, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red or yellow berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit yield.

The bark and leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
