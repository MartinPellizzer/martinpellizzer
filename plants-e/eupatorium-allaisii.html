Eupatorium allaisii

Eupatorium allaisii, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, branched stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

Eupatorium allaisii is known for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
