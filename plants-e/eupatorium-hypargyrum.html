Eupatorium hypargyrum

Eupatorium hypargyrum, commonly known as the silver leaf eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silvery underside, which helps in identifying the species.

Eupatorium hypargyrum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
