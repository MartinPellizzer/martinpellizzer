Elaeocarpus rarotongensis

Elaeocarpus rarotongensis, commonly known as the Cook Islands hibiscus, is a small evergreen tree native to the Cook Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native habitat.
