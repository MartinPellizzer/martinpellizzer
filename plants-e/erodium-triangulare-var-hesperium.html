Erodium triangulare var. hesperium

Erodium triangulare var. hesperium, commonly known as the California bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the coastal and inland valleys of California.
