Eulophia divergens

Eulophia divergens, commonly known as the spreading eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers with yellow petals and a distinctive purple lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia divergens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
