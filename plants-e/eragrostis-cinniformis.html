Eragrostis cinniformis

Eragrostis cinniformis, commonly known as the arrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive arrow-shaped seed heads. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and can thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to both wet and dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
