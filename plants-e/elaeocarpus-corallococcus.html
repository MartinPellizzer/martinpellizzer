Elaeocarpus corallococcus

Elaeocarpus corallococcus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
