Echinocactus grahlianus

Echinocactus grahlianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiky appearance. It is a slow-growing succulent that stores water in its fleshy stem, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
