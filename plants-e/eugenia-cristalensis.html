Eugenia cristalensis

Eugenia cristalensis, commonly known as the Crystal Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma when crushed.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
