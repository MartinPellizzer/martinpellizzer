Eranthemum parviflorum

Eranthemum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flower eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or red in color. The flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by bees and other pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
