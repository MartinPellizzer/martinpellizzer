Eragrostis peregrina

Eragrostis peregrina, commonly known as the wandering grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in regions with a Mediterranean climate. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that helps it conserve water.

Eragrostis peregrina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
