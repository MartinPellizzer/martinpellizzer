Elaeocarpus grandiflorus

Elaeocarpus grandiflorus, commonly known as the large-flowered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain a single, edible seed, often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitats.
