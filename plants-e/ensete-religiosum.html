Ensete religiosum

Ensete religiosum, commonly known as the sacred bamboo, is a large, ornamental plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in landscaping due to its dramatic appearance. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Ensete religiosum is also valued for its cultural significance in some regions.
