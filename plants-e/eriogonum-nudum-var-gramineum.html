Eriogonum nudum var. gramineum

Eriogonum nudum var. gramineum, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
