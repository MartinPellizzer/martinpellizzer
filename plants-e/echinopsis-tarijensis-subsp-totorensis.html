Echinopsis tarijensis subsp. totorensis

Echinopsis tarijensis subsp.

totorensis, commonly known as the Totorenos cactus, is a species native to Bolivia and northern Argentina. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles.

The plant produces white to pale pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by cactus enthusiasts.
