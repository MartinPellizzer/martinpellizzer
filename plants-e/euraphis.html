Euraphis

Euraphis, commonly known as the aphid genus, is a group of small, soft-bodied insects belonging to the family Aphididae.

These plants are not plants themselves but rather pests that feed on the sap of various plants, including crops and ornamentals. They are often found in colonies on the underside of leaves, stems, and shoots. Euraphis species are known for their ability to reproduce rapidly and cause damage through the secretion of honeydew, which can lead to sooty mold growth.

Their presence can significantly impact plant health and yield, making them a concern for agricultural and horticultural practices.
