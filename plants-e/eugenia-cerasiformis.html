Eugenia cerasiformis

Eugenia cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry apple tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that resemble cherries, hence its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia cerasiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
