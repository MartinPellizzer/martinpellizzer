Eugenia capensis subsp. albanensis

Eugenia capensis subsp.

albanensis, commonly known as the Albany eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves.

The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral displays.
