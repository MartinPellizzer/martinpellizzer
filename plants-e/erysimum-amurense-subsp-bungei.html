Erysimum amurense subsp. bungei

Erysimum amurense subsp.

bungei, commonly known as the Siberian Wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found growing in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
