Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. acifer

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. acifer, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a short, cylindrical stem covered in dense, blue-gray spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces striking clusters of white to pale purple flowers during the spring and early summer. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, with the main photosynthetic function carried out by the stem.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive drought conditions.
