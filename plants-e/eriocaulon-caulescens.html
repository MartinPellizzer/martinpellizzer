Eriocaulon caulescens

Eriocaulon caulescens, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, greenish flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

Eriocaulon caulescens plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
