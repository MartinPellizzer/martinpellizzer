Espeletia tapirophila

Espeletia tapirophila, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada snowbush, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is adapted to the harsh conditions of mountainous regions. Its rosette growth pattern helps it retain moisture and withstand strong winds.

Espeletia tapirophila plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
