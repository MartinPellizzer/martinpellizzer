Eupatorium ceanothifolium

Eupatorium ceanothifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
