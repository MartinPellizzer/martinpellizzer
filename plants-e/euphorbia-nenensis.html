Euphorbia nenensis

Euphorbia nenensis, commonly known as the Neneng euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the island of Nenen in the Indonesian archipelago.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows as a low-lying shrub with thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with a cyathium structure that includes a single female flower surrounded by several male flowers.

Euphorbia nenensis is adapted to arid environments, showcasing features that help it conserve water and survive in harsh conditions.
