Elleanthus virgatus

Elleanthus virgatus, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps near water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality in aquatic ecosystems.

Elleanthus virgatus is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
