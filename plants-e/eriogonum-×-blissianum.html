Eriogonum × blissianum

Eriogonum × blissianum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eriogonum fasciculatum and Eriogonum hookeri. The plant features showy, bright orange or red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The California poppy is also the state flower of California and is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
