Echinocactus denudatus var. typica

Echinocactus denudatus var. typica, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is covered in dense, white bristles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a succulent, meaning it stores water in its stems and is adapted to survive in dry environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
