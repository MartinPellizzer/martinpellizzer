Elaphoglossum tambillense

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries such as Colombia and Ecuador. The plant features delicate, bipinnate fronds with a distinctive, upright growth habit. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the undersides of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum tambillense is often found in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid and nutrient-rich environments.
