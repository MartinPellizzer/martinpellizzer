Eugenia striatula

Eugenia striatula, commonly known as the streaked eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive striped appearance. Its fragrant white flowers are clustered in spikes and bloom during the wet season.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red color, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
