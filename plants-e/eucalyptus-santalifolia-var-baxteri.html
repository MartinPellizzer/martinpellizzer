Eucalyptus santalifolia var. baxteri

Eucalyptus santalifolia var. baxteri, commonly known as the red gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that ranges from grey to red-brown in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with prominent veins. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodland habitats.
