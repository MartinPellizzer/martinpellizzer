Elaeagnus yoshinoi

Elaeagnus yoshinoi, commonly known as the Yoshino buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and lance-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Elaeagnus yoshinoi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
