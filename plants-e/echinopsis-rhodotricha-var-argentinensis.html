Echinopsis rhodotricha var. argentinensis

Echinopsis rhodotricha var. argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentinian red spurge cactus, is a species native to Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stem and is often found in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, red fruits. It is adapted to survive in drought conditions with a deep root system and thick, fleshy stems for water storage.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
