Escallonia resinosa

Escallonia resinosa, commonly known as the red-flowered escallonia, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and clusters of vibrant red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a resinous sap, which gives it its scientific name and contributes to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Escallonia resinosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
