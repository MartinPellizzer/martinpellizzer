Erythronium albidum var. mesochoreum

Erythronium albidum var. mesochoreum, commonly known as the white trout lily, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically white to pale green in color. The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing a bell-shaped flower with white petals and a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, loamy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
