Epidendropsis flexuosissima

Epidendropsis flexuosissima, commonly known as the flexuous epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without being parasitic. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with showy, often fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a spiral pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
