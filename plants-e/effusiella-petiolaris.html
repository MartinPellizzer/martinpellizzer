Effusiella petiolaris

Effusiella petiolaris, commonly known as the slender petioled fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Pteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping in appearance. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus, which are clustered on the underside of the fronds. Effusiella petiolaris thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological studies.
