Elaeagnus × hisauchii

Elaeagnus × hisauchii, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves, which are covered in fine, powdery hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Elaeagnus × hisauchii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
