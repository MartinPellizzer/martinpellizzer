Eusteralis stellata var. roxburghiana

Eusteralis stellata var. roxburghiana, commonly known as the star-shaped bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, star-like blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
