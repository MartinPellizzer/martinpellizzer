Eupatorium atriplicifolium

Eupatorium atriplicifolium, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves that resemble those of the hogweed genus. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Black cohosh is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for various health purposes.
