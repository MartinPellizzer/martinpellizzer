Eulophia paradoxa

Eulophia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that are typically borne on tall, erect stems. The plant has a pseudobulb structure, which helps it store water and nutrients, adapting it to its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eulophia paradoxa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
