Elsholtzia communis

Elsholtzia communis, commonly known as Chinese germander, is a perennial herb native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Elsholtzia communis is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
