Ekmanochloa subaphylla

Ekmanochloa subaphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a species of grass native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and compact inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include narrow, parallel-veined leaves and small, spike-like flower clusters.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
