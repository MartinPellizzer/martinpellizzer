Eugenia basilaris

Eugenia basilaris, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, pinkish-red fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia basilaris is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its resistance to pests and diseases.
