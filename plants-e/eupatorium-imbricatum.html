Eupatorium imbricatum

Eupatorium imbricatum, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features tall, erect stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a serrated edge.

Boneset is often found in moist habitats such as meadows, stream banks, and woodlands.
