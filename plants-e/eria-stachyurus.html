Eria stachyurus

Eria stachyurus, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It features showy, pendulous clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate floral display and seasonal foliage changes.
