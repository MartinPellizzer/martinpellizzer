Erigeron benghalensis

Erigeron benghalensis, commonly known as the Bengal fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, with ray florets that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
