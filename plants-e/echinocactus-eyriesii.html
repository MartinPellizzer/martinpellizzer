Echinocactus eyriesii

Echinocactus eyriesii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that bear white or yellowish glochids. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
