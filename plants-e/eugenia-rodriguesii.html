Eugenia rodriguesii

Eugenia rodriguesii, commonly known as the Rodrigues guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Rodrigues in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
