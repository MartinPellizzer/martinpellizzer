Eriogonum wrightii var. taxifolium

Eriogonum wrightii var. taxifolium, commonly known as the Townsend's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other plants with similar leaf structures. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

The plant is often found in coastal and inland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
