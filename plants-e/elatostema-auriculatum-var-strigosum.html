Elatostema auriculatum var. strigosum

Elatostema auriculatum var. strigosum, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as undergrowth in tropical forests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces berries that are eaten by various wildlife.

This species is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
