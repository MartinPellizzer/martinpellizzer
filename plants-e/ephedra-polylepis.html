Ephedra polylepis

Ephedra polylepis, commonly known as the Andean ephedra, is a shrub native to the high Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves, which are adapted to the harsh, high-altitude environment. The plant produces yellow-green flowers and red-brown fruits, which contain seeds dispersed by wind. Ephedra polylepis is a member of the Ephedraceae family, which includes plants known for their medicinal properties.

Its growth is typically found in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.
