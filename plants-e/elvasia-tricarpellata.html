Elvasia tricarpellata

Elvasia tricarpellata, commonly known as the three-ovary elvasia, is a rare flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, pale green flowers with a distinctive three-ovary structure in its fruit. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a dry, Mediterranean climate to flourish. Its unique reproductive features make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes.
