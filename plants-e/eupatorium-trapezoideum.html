Eupatorium trapezoideum

Eupatorium trapezoideum, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
