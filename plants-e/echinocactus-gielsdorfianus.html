Echinocactus gielsdorfianus

Echinocactus gielsdorfianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem that is covered in spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
