Eugenia psidioides

Eugenia psidioides, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia psidioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
