Erigeron grandiflorus var. lanatus

Erigeron grandiflorus var. lanatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features silver-blue foliage that contrasts beautifully with its daisy-like white to pale blue flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces showy composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and naturalized landscapes.
