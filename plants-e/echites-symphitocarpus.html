Echites symphitocarpus

Echites symphitocarpus, commonly known as the rock fig, is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its woody, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are dark purple when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The rock fig is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
