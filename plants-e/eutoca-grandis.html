Eutoca grandis

Eutoca grandis, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, alternate, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a faintly aromatic scent.

Eutoca grandis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
