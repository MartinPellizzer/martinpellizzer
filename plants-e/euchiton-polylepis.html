Euchiton polylepis

Euchiton polylepis, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvet tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings due to its adaptability.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes, though its botanical properties are not extensively studied.
