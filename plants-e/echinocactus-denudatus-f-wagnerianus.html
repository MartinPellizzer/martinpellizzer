Echinocactus denudatus f. wagnerianus

Echinocactus denudatus f. wagnerianus, commonly known as the Wagner's naked echinocactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
