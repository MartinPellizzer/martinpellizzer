Epidendrum grisebachianum

Epidendrum grisebachianum, commonly known as the Grisebach's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
