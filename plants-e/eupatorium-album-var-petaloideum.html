Eupatorium album var. petaloideum

Eupatorium album var. petaloideum, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture. This plant thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
