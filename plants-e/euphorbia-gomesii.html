Euphorbia gomesii

Euphorbia gomesii, commonly known as the Gomes' spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
