Eulophia warneckeana

Eulophia warneckeana, commonly known as the Warnecke's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eulophia warneckeana is valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
