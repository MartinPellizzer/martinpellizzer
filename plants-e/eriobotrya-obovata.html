Eriobotrya obovata

Eriobotrya obovata, commonly known as the Japanese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces pome fruits, which are typically sweet and edible, often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and agricultural significance.
