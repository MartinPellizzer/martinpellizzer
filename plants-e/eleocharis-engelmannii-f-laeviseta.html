Eleocharis engelmannii f. laeviseta

Eleocharis engelmannii f. laeviseta, commonly known as the smooth spike-rush, is a sedgy plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are often hairless or very softly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
