Euphorbia socotrana

Euphorbia socotrana, commonly known as Socotra candelabra, is a unique succulent native to the Socotra Archipelago.

It is characterized by its distinctive branched stem structure that resembles a candle, hence its common name. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to store water in its arid habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often surrounded by colorful bracts that attract pollinators.

Euphorbia socotrana is well-suited to dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a resilient plant in its native environment.
