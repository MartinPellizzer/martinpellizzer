Eugenia frondosa

Eugenia frondosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pink hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia frondosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
