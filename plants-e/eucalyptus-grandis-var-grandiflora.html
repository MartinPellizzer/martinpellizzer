Eucalyptus grandis var. grandiflora

Eucalyptus grandis var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Sydney blue gum, is a tall evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a bluish-gray color underneath. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green hue.

This species is valued for its timber, which is strong and durable, and for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
