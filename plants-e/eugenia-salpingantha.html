Eugenia salpingantha

Eugenia salpingantha, commonly known as the narrow-fruit eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that matures to a reddish color.

Eugenia salpingantha is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
