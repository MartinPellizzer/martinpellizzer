Eyrythalia rotata

Eyrythalia rotata, commonly known as the red-stemmed tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, pinkish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive and deep, allowing it to thrive in dry, saline soils.

Eyrythalia rotata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
