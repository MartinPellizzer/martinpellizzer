Echinocactus violaciflorus

Echinocactus violaciflorus, commonly known as the violet-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with white to yellowish spines. The plant produces striking violet or purple flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
