Echinocereus engelmannii subsp. engelmannii

Echinocereus engelmannii subsp.

engelmannii, commonly known as the Engelmann's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a solitary stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall.

The plant features prominent spines and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It is well-suited for xeriscaping and requires minimal water once established.
