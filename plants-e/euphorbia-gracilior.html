Euphorbia gracilior

Euphorbia gracilior, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters called cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia gracilior is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
