Elaeocarpus graeffei

Elaeocarpus graeffei, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, durable wood and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, black fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and horticultural collections.
