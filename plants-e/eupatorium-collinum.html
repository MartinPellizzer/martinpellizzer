Eupatorium collinum

Eupatorium collinum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium collinum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
