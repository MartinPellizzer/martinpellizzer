Erigeron peregrinus var. thompsonii

Erigeron peregrinus var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's wandering thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. Its flowers are composite, consisting of a central disk surrounded by pale blue to purple ray florets.

This variety is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its distinctive, showy inflorescences.
