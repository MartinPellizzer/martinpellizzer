Euphorbia filicaulis

Euphorbia filicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with alternate, narrow, and fleshy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a unique structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia filicaulis is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.
