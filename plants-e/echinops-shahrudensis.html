Echinops shahrudensis

Echinops shahrudensis, commonly known as the Shahrud thistle, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptation to dry climates. It produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Echinops shahrudensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
