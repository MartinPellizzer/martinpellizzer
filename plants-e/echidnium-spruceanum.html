Echidnium spruceanum

Echidnium spruceanum, commonly known as the Spruce-leaved Echidnium, is a rare and unusual plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, needle-like leaves that resemble those of coniferous trees, despite being a flowering plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
