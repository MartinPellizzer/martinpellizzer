Ebnerella jaliscana

Ebnerella jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
