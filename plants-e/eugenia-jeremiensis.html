Eugenia jeremiensis

Eugenia jeremiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Jeremi's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, red, edible drupe, is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber and medicinal properties.
