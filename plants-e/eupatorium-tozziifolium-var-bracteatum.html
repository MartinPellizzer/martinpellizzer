Eupatorium tozziifolium var. bracteatum

Eupatorium tozziifolium var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
