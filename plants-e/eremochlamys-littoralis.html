Eremochlamys littoralis

Eremochlamys littoralis, commonly known as the coastal thistle, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, herbaceous stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to saline and windy coastal environments.

Eremochlamys littoralis plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
