Eurya guatemalensis

Eurya guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan waxleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that remains green throughout the year. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
