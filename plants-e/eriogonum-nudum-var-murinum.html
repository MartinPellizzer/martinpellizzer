Eriogonum nudum var. murinum

Eriogonum nudum var. murinum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has simple, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eriogonum nudum var. murinum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
