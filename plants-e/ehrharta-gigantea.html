Ehrharta gigantea

Ehrharta gigantea, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Ehrharta gigantea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
