Eugenia stipitata

Eugenia stipitata, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. The fruit has a firm, wax-like skin and a sweet, tart flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
