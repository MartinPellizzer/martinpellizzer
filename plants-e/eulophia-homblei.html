Eulophia homblei

Eulophia homblei, commonly known as the Hombli orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features a short, stocky rhizome with a few leaves and produces inflorescences with clustered, showy blooms. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with intricate patterns, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Eulophia homblei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
