Eucalyptus australiana var. nitida

Eucalyptus australiana var. nitida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding insects and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in dry, open habitats such as mallee woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
