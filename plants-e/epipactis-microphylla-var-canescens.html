Epipactis microphylla var. canescens

Epipactis microphylla var. canescens, commonly known as the woolly helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive, hooded labellum. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually arranged in a dense spike and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The variety canescens is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, giving it a woolly appearance.
