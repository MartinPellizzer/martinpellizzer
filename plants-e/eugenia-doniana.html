Eugenia doniana

Eugenia doniana, commonly known as the African wild plum, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish fruits resembling plums. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Eugenia doniana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
