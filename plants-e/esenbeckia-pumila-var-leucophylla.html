Esenbeckia pumila var. leucophylla

Esenbeckia pumila var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf esenbeckia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are typically white or pale green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
