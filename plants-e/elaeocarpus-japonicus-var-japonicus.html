Elaeocarpus japonicus var. japonicus

Elaeocarpus japonicus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese elaeocarpus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits that are often referred to as "star fruits" due to their five-lobed appearance. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
