Eupatorium sieberianum

Eupatorium sieberianum, commonly known as Sieber's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and swampy areas, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties, historically used by indigenous peoples for its purported fever-reducing and anti-inflammatory effects.
