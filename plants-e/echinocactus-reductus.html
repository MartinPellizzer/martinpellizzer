Echinocactus reductus

Echinocactus reductus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, ribbed structure, which helps it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.

The species is adapted to full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to thrive.
