Erica nigrimontana

Erica nigrimontana, commonly known as the Black Mountain heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica nigrimontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
