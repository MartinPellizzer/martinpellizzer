Eupatoriastrum nelsonii

Eupatoriastrum nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its upright, branched stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, with a rough texture.

Eupatoriastrum nelsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
