Eugenia obovata

Eugenia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically round and fleshy, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia obovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in home gardens.
