Ehretia diffusa

Ehretia diffusa, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed.

Ehretia diffusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
