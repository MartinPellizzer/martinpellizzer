Eulophia biloba

Eulophia biloba, commonly known as the two-lobed eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and long, slender leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive two-lobed lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia biloba is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
