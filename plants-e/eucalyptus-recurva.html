Eucalyptus recurva

Eucalyptus recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed tree form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or yellow-green in color. Its bark is often flaky and varies in color from grey to brown.

Eucalyptus recurva is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.
