Eupatorium planellasianum

Eupatorium planellasianum, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, producing small, clustered white or pale purple flowers.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.
