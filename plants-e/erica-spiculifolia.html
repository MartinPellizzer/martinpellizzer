Erica spiculifolia

Erica spiculifolia, commonly known as the spike heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Erica spiculifolia typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
