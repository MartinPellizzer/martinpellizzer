Erigeron chionophilus var. sericeus

Erigeron chionophilus var. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
