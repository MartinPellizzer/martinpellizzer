Erodium cicutarium var. albidum

Erodium cicutarium var. albidum, commonly known as white field bean, is an annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in various habitats.
