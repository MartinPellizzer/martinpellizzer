Echinocactus salpigophorus

Echinocactus salpigophorus, commonly known as the red-tipped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its adaptations include succulent stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments.

Echinocactus salpigophorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
