Euphorbia buchtormensis var. alatavica

Euphorbia buchtormensis var. alatavica, commonly known as the Alatavica euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, typically in shades of yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This species is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant in its native habitat.
