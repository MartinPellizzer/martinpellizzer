Etlingera mucronata

Etlingera mucronata, commonly known as the spike ginger, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly serrated edge.

Etlingera mucronata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
