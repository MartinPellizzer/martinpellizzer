Echinospermum concavum

Echinospermum concavum, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
