Erigeron petrophilus var. viscidulus

Erigeron petrophilus var. viscidulus, commonly known as the sticky blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purplish flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a sticky texture. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The variety viscidulus is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage compared to other varieties of Erigeron petrophilus.
