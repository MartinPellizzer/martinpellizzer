Elythranthera × intermedia

Elythranthera × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a cross between Elythranthera acuminata and Elythranthera pusilla.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia, typically found in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinct lip petal. Its botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and rhizomatous roots, which help it thrive in its natural habitat.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to cultivated environments.
