Enydra sessilis

Enydra sessilis, commonly known as the sessile enydra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Enydra sessilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
