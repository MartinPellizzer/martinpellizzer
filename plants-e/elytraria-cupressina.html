Elytraria cupressina

Elytraria cupressina, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cypress, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact, conical shape. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are inconspicuous, and it relies on wind for pollination.

Elytraria cupressina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
