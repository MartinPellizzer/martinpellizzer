Eschweilera fracta

Eschweilera fracta, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its tonal qualities in musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Eschweilera fracta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
