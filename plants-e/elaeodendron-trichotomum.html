Elaeodendron trichotomum

Elaeodendron trichotomum, commonly known as the three-branched elaeodendron, is a shrub native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit with multiple branching stems that give it a bushy appearance. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a striking contrast to its delicate white flowers.

Elaeodendron trichotomum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
