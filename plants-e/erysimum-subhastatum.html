Erysimum subhastatum

Erysimum subhastatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum subhastatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
