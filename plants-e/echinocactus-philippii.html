Echinocactus philippii

Echinocactus philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscaping.
