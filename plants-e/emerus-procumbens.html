Emerus procumbens

Emerus procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate emerus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on the ground. The plant features small, green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
