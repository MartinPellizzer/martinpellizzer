Eupatorium petasites

Eupatorium petasites, commonly known as the giant cow-wheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
