Eriogonum wrightii subsp. typicum

Eriogonum wrightii subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the Wright's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
