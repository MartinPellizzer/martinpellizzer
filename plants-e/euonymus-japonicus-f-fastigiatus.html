Euonymus japonicus f. fastigiatus

Euonymus japonicus f. fastigiatus, commonly known as the Japanese spindle tree, is a compact, shrub-like plant with a unique upright growth habit.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are a notable ornamental feature. It is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and winter interest.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens or as a specimen plant in garden settings.
