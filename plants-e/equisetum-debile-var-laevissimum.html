Equisetum debile var. laevissimum

Equisetum debile var. laevissimum, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a diminutive, herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem that is typically green or yellow-green in color. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, less hairy appearance compared to other horsetail species. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, damp environments such as woodlands or stream banks.

The plant reproduces via spores and is a remnant of ancient plant life, showcasing evolutionary adaptations from its prehistoric ancestors.
