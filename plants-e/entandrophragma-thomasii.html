Entandrophragma thomasii

Entandrophragma thomasii, commonly known as African teak, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its straight, durable wood that is highly valued for its strength and workability. The plant has bipinnate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by leguminous pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its economic importance, it is also subject to conservation concerns.
