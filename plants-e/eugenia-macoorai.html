Eugenia macoorai

Eugenia macoorai, commonly known as the Macoorai apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a slight aromatic scent.

Eugenia macoorai is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
