Erythroxylum badium

Erythroxylum badium, commonly known as the Badia shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It grows as a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The plant is notable for its potential medicinal uses and its role in local ecosystems.
