Elaeocarpus tjerengii

Elaeocarpus tjerengii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.

However, it is relatively rare and faces threats from habitat loss and overharvesting.
