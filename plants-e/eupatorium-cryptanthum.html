Eupatorium cryptanthum

Eupatorium cryptanthum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium cryptanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
