Euphorbia transsilvanica

Euphorbia transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It features a woody stem with a distinctive greenish-gray bark and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It is adapted to rocky and dry environments, often growing in alpine or subalpine regions.

Euphorbia transsilvanica is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
