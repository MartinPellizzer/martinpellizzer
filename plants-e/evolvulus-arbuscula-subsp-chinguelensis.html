Evolvulus arbuscula subsp. chinguelensis

Evolvulus arbuscula subsp.

chinguelensis, commonly known as the Chinguelensis morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Its flowers bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
