Eupatorium celtidifolium var. typicum

Eupatorium celtidifolium var. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and meadows.

This plant is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and fever-reducing properties.
