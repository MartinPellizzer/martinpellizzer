Euphorbia ambroseae

Euphorbia ambroseae, commonly known as Ambrose's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-yellow, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse array of succulent and shrub species.

Euphorbia ambroseae is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited rainfall.
