Ernestia goudotii

Ernestia goudotii, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with a star-like pattern, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. The plant produces small, greenish leaves that are arranged in a rosette formation, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Ernestia goudotii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and vibrant floral display.

It requires well-drained soil and moderate watering to flourish.
