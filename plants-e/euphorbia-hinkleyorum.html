Euphorbia hinkleyorum

Euphorbia hinkleyorum, commonly known as Hinkley's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water and withstand prolonged drought. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Euphorbia hinkleyorum is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and should be handled with care.
