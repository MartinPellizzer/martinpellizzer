Erythrina hazomboay

Erythrina hazomboay, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
