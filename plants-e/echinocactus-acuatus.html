Echinocactus acuatus

Echinocactus acuatus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus with a cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent collections.
