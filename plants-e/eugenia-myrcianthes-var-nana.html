Eugenia myrcianthes var. nana

Eugenia myrcianthes var. nana, commonly known as dwarf myrtle, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant leaves and produces small, white flowers that are often followed by edible, dark purple berries. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental berries make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The variety 'nana' is distinguished by its dwarf growth habit, making it suitable for container cultivation and small garden spaces.
