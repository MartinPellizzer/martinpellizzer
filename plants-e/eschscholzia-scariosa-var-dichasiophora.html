Eschscholzia scariosa var. dichasiophora

Eschscholzia scariosa var. dichasiophora, commonly known as the desert poppy, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open grasslands.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
