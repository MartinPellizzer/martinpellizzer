Erythrina insularis

Erythrina insularis, commonly known as the island coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are divided into three to five leaflets, and its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Erythrina insularis produces distinctive pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
