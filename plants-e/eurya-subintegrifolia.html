Eurya subintegrifolia

Eurya subintegrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaf eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with smooth margins and a glossy surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds.

This plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
