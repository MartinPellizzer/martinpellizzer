Eurycaulis multiflorus

Eurycaulis multiflorus, commonly known as the multi-flowered eurycaulis, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces vibrant, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eurycaulis multiflorus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
