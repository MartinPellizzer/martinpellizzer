Epidendrum ciliipetalum

Epidendrum ciliipetalum, commonly known as the ciliated lip orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers with intricate petal structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help in water retention.

The flowers bloom in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
