Eria consanguinea

Eria consanguinea, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Eria consanguinea is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in traditional medicine.
