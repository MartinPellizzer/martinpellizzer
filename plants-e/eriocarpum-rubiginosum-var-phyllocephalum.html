Eriocarpum rubiginosum var. phyllocephalum

Eriocarpum rubiginosum var. phyllocephalum, commonly known as the red-flowered eriocephalum, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves with a distinctive velvety texture. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
