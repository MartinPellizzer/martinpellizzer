Erigeron orientalis

Erigeron orientalis, commonly known as the oriental fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, with white to pale blue ray florets surrounding a central cluster of yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in regions with temperate climates.

Erigeron orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like blooms.
