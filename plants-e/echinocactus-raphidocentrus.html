Echinocactus raphidocentrus

Echinocactus raphidocentrus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, ribbed stem that is covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor plant collections.
