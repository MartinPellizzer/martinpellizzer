Echinopsis acanthura

Echinopsis acanthura, commonly known as the White Pincushion Cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that form a dense, spherical shape. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Echinopsis acanthura is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
