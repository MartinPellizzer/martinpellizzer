Euphorbia neorugosa

Euphorbia neorugosa, commonly known as the rugose spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy environments.

Euphorbia neorugosa is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
