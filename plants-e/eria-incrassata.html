Eria incrassata

Eria incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Eria incrassata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many subtropical and tropical gardens.
