Eria rhomboidalis

Eria rhomboidalis, commonly known as the rhomboid orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria rhomboidalis is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
