Eragrostis echinochloidea

Eragrostis echinochloidea, commonly known as hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often appear in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and have a bluish-green color, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eragrostis echinochloidea is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
