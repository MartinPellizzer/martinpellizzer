Echinocactus dumelianus

Echinocactus dumelianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sunlight, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements have made it a favored ornamental plant.

However, it is important to note that it can be toxic if ingested, so care should be taken when handling it.
