Ericoides pusilla

Ericoides pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, and it produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. Ericoides pusilla thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
