Erinus alpinus var. typicus

Erinus alpinus var. typicus, commonly known as the alpine Erinus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and profuse display of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wool, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to bloom reliably in cooler climates.
