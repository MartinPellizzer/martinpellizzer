Eulophia dipodiiformis

Eulophia dipodiiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers have a distinct, intricate structure typical of the Orchidaceae family.

Eulophia dipodiiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
