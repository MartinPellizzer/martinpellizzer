Erysimum clausioides

Erysimum clausioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum clausioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
