Eriophorum russeolum f. leucothrix

Eriophorum russeolum f. leucothrix, commonly known as the white-topped cottongrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, white or pale pink inflorescences that contrast with its grass-like foliage. This plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments and is often found in northern regions. The inflorescence is composed of numerous small, fluffy flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in moist habitats and is a common indicator plant for peatlands.
