Erica macilenta

Erica macilenta, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica macilenta is evergreen, with dense foliage that provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
