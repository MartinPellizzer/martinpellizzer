Eupatorium solidaginoides var. armourii

Eupatorium solidaginoides var. armourii, commonly known as the Armstrong's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small, white to pale blue composite flowers.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
