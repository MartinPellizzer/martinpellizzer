Euphorbia pediculifera var. inornata

Euphorbia pediculifera var. inornata, commonly known as the unadorned spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, branching stem structure with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
