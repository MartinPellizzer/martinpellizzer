Evolvulus argyreus

Evolvulus argyreus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a herbaceous annual with hairy stems and opposite leaves that are heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used as an ornamental in gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a popular choice for containers and hanging baskets.
