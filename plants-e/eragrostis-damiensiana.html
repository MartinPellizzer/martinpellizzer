Eragrostis damiensiana

Eragrostis damiensiana, commonly known as the Damien's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and supporting local biodiversity.
