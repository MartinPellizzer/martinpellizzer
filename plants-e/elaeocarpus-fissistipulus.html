Elaeocarpus fissistipulus

Elaeocarpus fissistipulus, commonly known as the fissistipule elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
