Edmondia spectabilis

Edmondia spectabilis, commonly known as the showy edmondia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features distinctive, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Edmondia spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
