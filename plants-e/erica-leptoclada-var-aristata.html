Erica leptoclada var. aristata

Erica leptoclada var. aristata, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

This variety is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
