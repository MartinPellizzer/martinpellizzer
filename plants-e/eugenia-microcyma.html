Eugenia microcyma

Eugenia microcyma, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or black when mature, which are edible but not widely consumed. Eugenia microcyma thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and fragrance.
