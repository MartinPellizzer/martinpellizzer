Euryops discoideus

Euryops discoideus, commonly known as the yellow daisy or African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Euryops discoideus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant appearance and drought tolerance.
