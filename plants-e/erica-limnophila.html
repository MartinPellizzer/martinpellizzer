Erica limnophila

Erica limnophila, commonly known as the marsh heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in wetland and swampy environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with dense foliage of small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters and blooming during the summer months.

Erica limnophila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
