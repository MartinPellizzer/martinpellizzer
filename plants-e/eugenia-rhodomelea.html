Eugenia rhodomelea

Eugenia rhodomelea, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers. The fruit is a small, smooth, reddish drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
