Eragrostis uniolae

Eragrostis uniolae, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its botanical features include narrow, linear leaves and a fibrous root system.

Eragrostis uniolae is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and withstand drought conditions.
