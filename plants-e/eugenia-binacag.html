Eugenia binacag

Eugenia binacag, commonly known as the Philippine guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, pinkish-red fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas but have a distinct flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The botanical features of Eugenia binacag make it a valuable species for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
