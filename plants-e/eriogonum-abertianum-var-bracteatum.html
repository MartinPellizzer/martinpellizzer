Eriogonum abertianum var. bracteatum

Eriogonum abertianum var. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs. It has hairy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
