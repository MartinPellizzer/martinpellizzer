Etlingera grandiflora

Etlingera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, red-orange inflorescence that resembles a torch, with showy bracts and small, fragrant flowers. Its leaves are broad, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture.

Etlingera grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
