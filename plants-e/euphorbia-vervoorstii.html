Euphorbia vervoorstii

Euphorbia vervoorstii, commonly known as the Vervorst's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss.

Euphorbia vervoorstii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
