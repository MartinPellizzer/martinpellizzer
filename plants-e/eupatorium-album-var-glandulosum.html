Eupatorium album var. glandulosum

Eupatorium album var. glandulosum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine, glandular hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

This plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
