Eriospermum pusillum

Eriospermum pusillum, commonly known as the small eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is a low-growing perennial with small, hairy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
