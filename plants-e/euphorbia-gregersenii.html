Euphorbia gregersenii

Euphorbia gregersenii, commonly known as the Grevillea euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
