Eupatorium paraguariense var. integrifolia

Eupatorium paraguariense var. integrifolia, commonly known as the Paraguayan boneset, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale blue, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
