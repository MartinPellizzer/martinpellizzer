Echinophora cryptantha

Echinophora cryptantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has a compact, upright growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters.

Echinophora cryptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
