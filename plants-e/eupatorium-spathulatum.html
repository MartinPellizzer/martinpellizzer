Eupatorium spathulatum

Eupatorium spathulatum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features tall, hairy stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often rough to the touch. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically white or pale blue in color.

It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
