Euphorbia insulae-europae

Euphorbia insulae-europae, commonly known as the European island spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is native to specific regions of Europe and is adapted to rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
