Euphorbia jasiewiczii

Euphorbia jasiewiczii, commonly known as the Polish spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Poland and parts of Ukraine.

It features a short, thick stem with paired, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners in arid climates.
