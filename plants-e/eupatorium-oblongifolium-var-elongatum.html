Eupatorium oblongifolium var. elongatum

Eupatorium oblongifolium var. elongatum, commonly known as the longleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
