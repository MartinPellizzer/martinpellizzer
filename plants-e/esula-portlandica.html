Esula portlandica

Esula portlandica, commonly known as the Portland bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern England.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is often found in coastal dunes and cliffs.

Esula portlandica is a rare species, listed as endangered due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
