Eugenia mestrealvarensis

Eugenia mestrealvarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-do-brejo*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.

Eugenia mestrealvarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
