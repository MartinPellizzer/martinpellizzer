Eragrostis rubens var. effusa

Eragrostis rubens var. effusa, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish bracts. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
