Eugenia bonplandiana

Eugenia bonplandiana, commonly known as the bonplandian holly, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while the fruits are small, berry-like, and often red when mature.

Eugenia bonplandiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
