Erica parilis var. parilis

Erica parilis var. parilis, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Erica parilis var. parilis is well adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
