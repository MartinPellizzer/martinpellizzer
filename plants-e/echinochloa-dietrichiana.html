Echinochloa dietrichiana

Echinochloa dietrichiana, commonly known as the dwarf barnyard grass, is a species of annual grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, contributing to weed problems in crops.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a challenging weed to control in various ecosystems.
