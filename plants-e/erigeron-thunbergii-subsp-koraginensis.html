Erigeron thunbergii subsp. koraginensis

Erigeron thunbergii subsp.

koraginensis, commonly known as the Korean blueweed, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flower heads and silvery-gray leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
