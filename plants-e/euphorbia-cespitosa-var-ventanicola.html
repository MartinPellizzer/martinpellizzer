Euphorbia cespitosa var. ventanicola

Euphorbia cespitosa var. ventanicola, commonly known as the prairie spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form with dense clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairie and disturbed habitats.

Euphorbia cespitosa var. ventanicola is adapted to dry conditions and is considered a valuable native plant for ecological restoration projects.
