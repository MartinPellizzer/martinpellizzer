Eranthemum eldorado

Eranthemum eldorado, commonly known as the golden flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warm seasons. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
