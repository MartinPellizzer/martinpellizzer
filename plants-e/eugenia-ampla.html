Eugenia ampla

Eugenia ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Eugenia ampla thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
