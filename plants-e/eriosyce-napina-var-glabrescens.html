Eriosyce napina var. glabrescens

Eriosyce napina var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular body covered in dense, white, silky hairs that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in cactus collections.
