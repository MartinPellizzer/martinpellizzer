Eupatorium mesoreopolum

Eupatorium mesoreopolum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its stems are often hairy.

It serves as a valuable nectar source for pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
