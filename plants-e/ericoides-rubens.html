Ericoides rubens

Ericoides rubens, commonly known as the red heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and small, leathery leaves that are typically reddish or dark green in color. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericoides rubens thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to contribute to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
