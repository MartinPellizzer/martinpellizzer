Eupatorium iresinoides var. breviflorum

Eupatorium iresinoides var. breviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, branched stems covered with fine hairs and is characterized by its small, clustered white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges.

This plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
