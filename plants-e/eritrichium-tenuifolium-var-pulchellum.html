Eritrichium tenuifolium var. pulchellum

Eritrichium tenuifolium var. pulchellum, commonly known as the slenderleaf alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It has lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
