Ebnerella fraileana

Ebnerella fraileana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed piper.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Piperaceae, native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often narrow and glossy. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Ebnerella fraileana is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
