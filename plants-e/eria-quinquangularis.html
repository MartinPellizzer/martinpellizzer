Eria quinquangularis

Eria quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a slender, erect stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria quinquangularis is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
