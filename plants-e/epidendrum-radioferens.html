Epidendrum radioferens

Epidendrum radioferens, commonly known as the radiate epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows epiphytically on trees, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are often pollinated by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
