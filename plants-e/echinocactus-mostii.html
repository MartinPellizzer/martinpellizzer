Echinocactus mostii

Echinocactus mostii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant features a thick, segmented stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, blooming in the spring and attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

Echinocactus mostii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
