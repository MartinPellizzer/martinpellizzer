Earleocassia arida

Earleocassia arida, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, or red. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its fibrous roots enable it to survive in poor soil conditions and withstand long periods of drought.

Earleocassia arida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging climates.
