Erica speciosa

Erica speciosa, commonly known as the showy heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica speciosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and birds.
