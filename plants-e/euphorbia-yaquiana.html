Euphorbia yaquiana

Euphorbia yaquiana, commonly known as the Yaquilla spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features succulent stems and leaves, which help it conserve water in its native habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia yaquiana is also noted for its ability to produce a milky latex when damaged, a characteristic common among many species in the genus.
