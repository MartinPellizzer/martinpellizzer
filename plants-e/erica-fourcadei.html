Erica fourcadei

Erica fourcadei, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica fourcadei is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

It is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to coastal and Mediterranean climates.
