Eucalyptus apothalassica

Eucalyptus apothalassica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are usually yellow or cream, which develop into woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the harsh, arid conditions of its native habitat.

Eucalyptus apothalassica is an important component of the local flora and plays a role in supporting native wildlife.
