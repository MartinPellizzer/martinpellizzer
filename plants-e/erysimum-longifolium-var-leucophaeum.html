Erysimum longifolium var. leucophaeum

Erysimum longifolium var. leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-flowered wallflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing on rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
