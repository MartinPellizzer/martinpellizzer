Eupatorium formosanum

Eupatorium formosanum, commonly known as the Taiwan thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium formosanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
