Eupatorium verae-crucis

Eupatorium verae-crucis, commonly known as the Crossroads Hemp, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Eupatorium verae-crucis is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
