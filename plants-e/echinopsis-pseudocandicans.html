Echinopsis pseudocandicans

Echinopsis pseudocandicans, commonly known as the golden echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical or columnar shape and is covered in dense, white to yellow spines. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
