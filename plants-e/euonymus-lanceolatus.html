Euonymus lanceolatus

Euonymus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often used in landscaping for its dense, compact growth habit and ornamental value.

Euonymus lanceolatus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
