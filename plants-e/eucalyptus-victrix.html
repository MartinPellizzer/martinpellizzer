Eucalyptus victrix

Eucalyptus victrix, commonly known as the victoria gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a striking mottled pattern of white, pink, and grey. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a rough upper surface. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

The tree is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
