Erigeron candidissimus

Erigeron candidissimus, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in summer, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
