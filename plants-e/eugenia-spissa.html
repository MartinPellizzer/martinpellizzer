Eugenia spissa

Eugenia spissa, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in small, stiff hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

The fruit is a small, round berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.
