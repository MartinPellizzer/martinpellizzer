Eremophila mirabilis

Eremophila mirabilis, commonly known as the red mistflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in sandy soils.

Eremophila mirabilis plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly pollinators like birds and insects.
