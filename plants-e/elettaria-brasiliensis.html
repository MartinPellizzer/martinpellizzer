Elettaria brasiliensis

Elettaria brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing essential oils that contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

Elettaria brasiliensis is valued for its culinary and traditional medicinal uses in South America.
