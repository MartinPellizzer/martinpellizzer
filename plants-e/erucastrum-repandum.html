Erucastrum repandum

Erucastrum repandum, commonly known as the creeping rocket, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pale green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to spread along the ground, forming dense mats. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Erucastrum repandum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.
