Eremophila lanceolata

Eremophila lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for coastal and sandy soils. It produces bright red or pink flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila lanceolata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
