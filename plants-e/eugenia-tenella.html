Eugenia tenella

Eugenia tenella, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia tenella is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
