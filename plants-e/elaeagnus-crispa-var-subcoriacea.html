Elaeagnus crispa var. subcoriacea

Elaeagnus crispa var. subcoriacea, commonly known as silverberry, is a shrub native to Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its hardy nature and potential use in erosion control.
