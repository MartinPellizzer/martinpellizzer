Erica flexistyla

Erica flexistyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica flexistyla is adapted to coastal and mountainous regions, where it withstands strong winds and variable moisture conditions.
