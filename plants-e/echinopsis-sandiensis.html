Echinopsis sandiensis

Echinopsis sandiensis, commonly known as the sandhill cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Echinopsis sandiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
