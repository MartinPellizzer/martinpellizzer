Eugenia pubescens

Eugenia pubescens, commonly known as the velvetleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries.

Eugenia pubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
