Elachanthemum polycephalum

Elachanthemum polycephalum, commonly known as the many-headed sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit, with woolly, grayish-green leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
