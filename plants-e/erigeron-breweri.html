Erigeron breweri

Erigeron breweri, commonly known as the Whorled Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron breweri is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss and limited distribution.
