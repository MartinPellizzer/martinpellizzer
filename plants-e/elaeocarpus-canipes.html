Elaeocarpus canipes

Elaeocarpus canipes, commonly known as the red sanders or red sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape, and its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
