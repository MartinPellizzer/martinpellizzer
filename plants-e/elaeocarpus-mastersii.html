Elaeocarpus mastersii

Elaeocarpus mastersii, commonly known as the New Guinea elaeocarpus, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and nearby islands. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
