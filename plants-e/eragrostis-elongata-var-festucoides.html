Eragrostis elongata var. festucoides

Eragrostis elongata var. festucoides, commonly known as narrowleaf bearded grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and prairies, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture along the leaf sheaths. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, bract-like structures that bear the flowering heads.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
