Elaeagnus oldhamii

Elaeagnus oldhamii, commonly known as silver berry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and fragrant, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Elaeagnus oldhamii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
