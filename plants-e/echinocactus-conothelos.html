Echinocactus conothelos

Echinocactus conothelos, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
