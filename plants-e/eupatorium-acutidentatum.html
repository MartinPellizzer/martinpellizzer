Eupatorium acutidentatum

Eupatorium acutidentatum, commonly known as the sharp-toothed thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features tall, slender stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant often thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium acutidentatum is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators.
