Elaeocarpus habbemensis subsp. schoddei

Elaeocarpus habbemensis subsp.

schoddei, commonly known as the Habbemans Elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to specific regions of Australia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
