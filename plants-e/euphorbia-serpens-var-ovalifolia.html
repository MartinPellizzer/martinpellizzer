Euphorbia serpens var. ovalifolia

Euphorbia serpens var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the "snake's head" or "lizard's tail," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
