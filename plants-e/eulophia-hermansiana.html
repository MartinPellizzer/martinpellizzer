Eulophia hermansiana

Eulophia hermansiana, commonly known as the Hermann's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white petals. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eulophia hermansiana is often found in open grasslands and forest margins, where it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.
