Elaeocarpus forbesii subsp. forbesii

Elaeocarpus forbesii subsp.

forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
