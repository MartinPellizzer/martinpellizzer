Erythroxylum caatingae

Erythroxylum caatingae, commonly known as the Caatinga erythroxylum, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family, which includes other plants known for their psychoactive properties. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are capsules that contain tiny, hard seeds.

This species is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of the Caatinga biome.
