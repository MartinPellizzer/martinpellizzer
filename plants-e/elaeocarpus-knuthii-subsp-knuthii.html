Elaeocarpus knuthii subsp. knuthii

Elaeocarpus knuthii subsp.

knuthii, commonly known as the Knuth's elaeocarp, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland and hill forest environments.

The plant is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
