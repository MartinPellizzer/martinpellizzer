Ereicoctis conferta

Ereicoctis conferta, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
