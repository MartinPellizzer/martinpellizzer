Eupatorium phlogifolium

Eupatorium phlogifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.
