Erigeron galeottii

Erigeron galeottii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. Erigeron galeottii is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
