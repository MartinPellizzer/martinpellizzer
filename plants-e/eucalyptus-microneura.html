Eucalyptus microneura

Eucalyptus microneura, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the winter months. Its bark is usually smooth and peels in thin, fibrous strips, and it is adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Eucalyptus microneura plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
