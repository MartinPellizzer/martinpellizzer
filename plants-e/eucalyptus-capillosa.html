Eucalyptus capillosa

Eucalyptus capillosa, commonly known as the slender-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and small, rounded fruit capsules. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eucalyptus capillosa is often found in shrublands and is part of the diverse flora of the Australian desert ecosystems.
