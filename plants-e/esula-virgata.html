Esula virgata

Esula virgata, commonly known as the red-flowered lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers with bright red petals. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem, contributing to its elegant appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
