Eugenia suavis

Eugenia suavis, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. Its foliage is evergreen, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

Eugenia suavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
