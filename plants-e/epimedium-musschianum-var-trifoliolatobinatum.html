Epimedium musschianum var. trifoliolatobinatum

Epimedium musschianum var. trifoliolatobinatum, commonly known as the golden flame bar sinister, is a low-growing perennial plant native to China.

It features striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and unique foliage structure.
