Eugenia scortechinii var. cuneata

Eugenia scortechinii var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a fragrant aroma. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
