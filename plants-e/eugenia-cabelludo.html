Eugenia cabelludo

Eugenia cabelludo, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe, with a thick, segmented rind. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and small, leathery fruit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in warm climates.
