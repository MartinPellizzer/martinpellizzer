Eryngium zanonii

Eryngium zanonii, commonly known as the Andean thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in cool, temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs during the spring and early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium zanonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
