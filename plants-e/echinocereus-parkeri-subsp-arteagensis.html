Echinocereus parkeri subsp. arteagensis

Echinocereus parkeri subsp.

arteagensis, commonly known as the Arteaga's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and desert gardens.
