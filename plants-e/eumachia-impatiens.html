Eumachia impatiens

Eumachia impatiens, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and abandoned fields.

Eumachia impatiens is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
