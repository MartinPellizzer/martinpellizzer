Euphorbia matabelensis

Euphorbia matabelensis, commonly known as the Matabelena spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
