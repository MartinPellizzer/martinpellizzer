Eremophila pterocarpa subsp. acicularis

Eremophila pterocarpa subsp.

acicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of violet or purple flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
