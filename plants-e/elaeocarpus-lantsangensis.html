Elaeocarpus lantsangensis

Elaeocarpus lantsangensis, commonly known as the Lantsang elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically clustered together, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
