Encyclia boothiana subsp. favoris

Encyclia boothiana subsp.

favoris, commonly known as the favored encyclia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Caribbean. It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering structure.

The plant produces linear, dark green leaves and bears fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
