Eugenia argutata

Eugenia argutata, commonly known as the rough eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. These berries are a food source for various wildlife and have been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
