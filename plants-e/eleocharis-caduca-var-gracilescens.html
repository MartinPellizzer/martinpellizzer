Eleocharis caduca var. gracilescens

Eleocharis caduca var. gracilescens, commonly known as slender threadgrass, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, and the flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
