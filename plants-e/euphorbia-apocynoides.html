Euphorbia apocynoides

Euphorbia apocynoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Euphorbia apocynoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
