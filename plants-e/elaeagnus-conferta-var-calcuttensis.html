Elaeagnus conferta var. calcuttensis

Elaeagnus conferta var. calcuttensis, commonly known as the Indian silver bush, is a shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its fruits are small, red drupes that ripen in late summer.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant plant.
