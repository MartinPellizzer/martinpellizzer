Epidendrum glumaceum

Epidendrum glumaceum, commonly known as the golden spike epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive, spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum glumaceum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
