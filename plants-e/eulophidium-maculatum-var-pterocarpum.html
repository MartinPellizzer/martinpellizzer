Eulophidium maculatum var. pterocarpum

Eulophidium maculatum var. pterocarpum, commonly known as the spotted leaf euphorbia, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spotted leaves and yellowish-green stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of green and yellow.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
