Eugenia antiseptica

Eugenia antiseptica, commonly known as the antiseptic eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's name reflects its historical use in treating infections due to its antimicrobial properties.
