Erica ebracteata

Erica ebracteata, commonly known as the naked-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica ebracteata typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Western Cape region of South Africa.
