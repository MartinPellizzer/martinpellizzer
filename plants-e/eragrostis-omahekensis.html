Eragrostis omahekensis

Eragrostis omahekensis, commonly known as the Omahekense grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodland areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping or for erosion control in suitable environments.
