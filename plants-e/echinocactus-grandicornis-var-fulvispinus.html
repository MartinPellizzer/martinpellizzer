Echinocactus grandicornis var. fulvispinus

Echinocactus grandicornis var. fulvispinus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are adorned with prominent, backward-curving spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which emerge from the areoles along the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
