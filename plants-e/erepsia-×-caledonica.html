Erepsia × caledonica

Erepsia × caledonica, commonly known as the Caledonian cross-erepsia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the crossbreeding of two species within the Erepsia genus.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Scotland, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often found growing in shaded, rocky habitats, and its growth is slow, making it a rare and delicate species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Erepsia × caledonica is considered a vulnerable plant in its native range.
