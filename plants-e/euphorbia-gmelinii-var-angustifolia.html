Euphorbia gmelinii var. angustifolia

Euphorbia gmelinii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered with a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and linear, adapted to minimize transpiration in harsh environments.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is well-suited for xerophytic landscapes.
