Eucalyptus occidentalis var. spathulata

Eucalyptus occidentalis var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed malleeflower, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands or shrublands. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The species is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
