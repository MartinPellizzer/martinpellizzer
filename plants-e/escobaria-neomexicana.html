Escobaria neomexicana

Escobaria neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit. The plant features small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. It produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Escobaria neomexicana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
