Eragrostis arabica

Eragrostis arabica, commonly known as Arabian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green to brown in color. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for restoration projects in degraded landscapes. It plays a role in preventing soil erosion and provides forage for livestock in certain regions.

However, it is not widely cultivated for agricultural purposes due to its low nutritional value for grazing animals.
