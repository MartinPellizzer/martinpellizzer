Euphrasia minima

Euphrasia minima, commonly known as the lesser eyebright, is a small flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky soils, preferring temperate climates. The plant has delicate, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent white markings. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

Euphrasia minima is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.
