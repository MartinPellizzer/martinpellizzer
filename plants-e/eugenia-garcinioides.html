Eugenia garcinioides

Eugenia garcinioides, commonly known as the red torchwood or red eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. After flowering, it produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in various regions.
