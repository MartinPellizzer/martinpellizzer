Eleutherococcus japonicus var. variegata

Eleutherococcus japonicus var. variegata, commonly known as Japanese prickly ash or variegated eleuthero, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white or yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
