Erigeron pulchellus var. tolsteadii

Erigeron pulchellus var. tolsteadii, commonly known as Tolstoy's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a grayish-green color that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.
