Elettaria walang

Elettaria walang, commonly known as wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are followed by elongated, finger-like fruits containing aromatic seeds. The seeds, often referred to as "wild cardamom," are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and economic purposes.
