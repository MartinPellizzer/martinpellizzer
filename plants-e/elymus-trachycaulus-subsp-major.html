Elymus trachycaulus subsp. major

Elymus trachycaulus subsp.

major, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, blue-green foliage.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically clustered in panicles, with lemmas that are often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration projects and forage production.
