Elymus longiglumis

Elymus longiglumis, commonly known as the longawn wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets at the ends of its stems, which are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Elymus longiglumis is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for livestock.
