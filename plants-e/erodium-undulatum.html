Erodium undulatum

Erodium undulatum, commonly known as the wavy bedstraw, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and along trails.

Its botanical characteristics make it an adaptable and resilient species within its native habitat.
