Ericoides hirsutum

Ericoides hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy eric, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine, hairy textures, providing a unique appearance.

Ericoides hirsutum is well adapted to sandy, acidic soils and can thrive in coastal environments with high salt exposure.
