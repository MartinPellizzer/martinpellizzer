Euphorbia gracilicaulis

Euphorbia gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree with a slender, branching stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and can vary in shape depending on the environment.

Euphorbia gracilicaulis is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
