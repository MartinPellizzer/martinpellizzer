Echinocactus multiflorus f. parisiensis

Echinocactus multiflorus f. parisiensis, commonly known as the Parisiensis Cactus, is a unique variant of the Echinocactus genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
