Echinocactus gibbosus var. celsianus

Echinocactus gibbosus var. celsianus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles covered with white, fluffy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
