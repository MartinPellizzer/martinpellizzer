Elaphrium collinum

Elaphrium collinum, commonly known as the mountain elaphrium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in loose panicles and are usually pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Elaphrium collinum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
