Eucalyptus colossea

Eucalyptus colossea, commonly known as the white box gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators.

The species is also known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
