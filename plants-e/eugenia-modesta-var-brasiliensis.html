Eugenia modesta var. brasiliensis

Eugenia modesta var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerbera odollam*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
