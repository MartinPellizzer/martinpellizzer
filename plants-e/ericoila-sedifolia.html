Ericoila sedifolia

Ericoila sedifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, leathery berries that are edible for some bird species.

Ericoila sedifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense ground covers.
