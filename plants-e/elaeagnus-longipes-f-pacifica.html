Elaeagnus longipes f. pacifica

Elaeagnus longipes f. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific silverberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, glandular hairs that cover the stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
