Eupatorium trinervatum

Eupatorium trinervatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
