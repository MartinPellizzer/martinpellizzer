Entada paranaguana

Entada paranaguana, commonly known as the "Brazil nut tree," is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and compound leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. The tree produces yellowish-green flowers and distinctive pod fruits that contain the edible Brazil nuts. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife and its economic importance as a source of Brazil nuts.
