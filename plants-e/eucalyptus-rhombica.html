Eucalyptus rhombica

Eucalyptus rhombica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that are often tinged with red or pink. Eucalyptus rhombica is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height.

It plays an important role in the ecosystems of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
