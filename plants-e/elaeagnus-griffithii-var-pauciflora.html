Elaeagnus griffithii var. pauciflora

Elaeagnus griffithii var. pauciflora, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeagnus, is a shrub or small tree native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or pale green, appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration.
