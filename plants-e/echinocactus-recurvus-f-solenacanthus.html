Echinocactus recurvus f. solenacanthus

Echinocactus recurvus f. solenacanthus, commonly known as the Solenacanthus cactus, is a unique variant of the Echinocactus genus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spiny fruits. Its spines are shorter and more sparse compared to other Echinocactus species, giving it a distinct appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and arid landscapes.
