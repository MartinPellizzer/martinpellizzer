Echium longifolium var. maroccanum

Echium longifolium var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue or violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
