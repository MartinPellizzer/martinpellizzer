Echinocereus viridiflorus var. russanthus

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. russanthus, commonly known as the green-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow to green flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
