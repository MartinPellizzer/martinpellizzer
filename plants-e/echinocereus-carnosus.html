Echinocereus carnosus

Echinocereus carnosus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

Its structure and appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
