Eucalyptus alligatrix subsp. limaensis

Eucalyptus alligatrix subsp.

limaensis, commonly known as the Lima eucalyptus, is a subshrub or small tree native to coastal regions of central Chile. It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators. This species is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
