Euphorbia moratii var. antsingiensis

Euphorbia moratii var. antsingiensis, commonly known as the Antsingi euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a short, thick stem with a crown of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
