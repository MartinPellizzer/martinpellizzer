Eupatorium amphidyctium var. morungavense

Eupatorium amphidyctium var. morungavense, commonly known as the Morung Valley eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern regions of India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
