Eryngium triquetrum subsp. triquetrum

Eryngium triquetrum subsp.

triquetrum, commonly known as the three-angled thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
