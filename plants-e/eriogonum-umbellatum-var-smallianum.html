Eriogonum umbellatum var. smallianum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. smallianum, commonly known as the smallleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
