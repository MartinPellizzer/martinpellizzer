Euonymus yedoensis f. calocarpus

Euonymus yedoensis f. calocarpus, commonly known as the Japanese spindletree, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in autumn. Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is relatively low maintenance.
