Eremopoa persica subsp. multiradiata

Eremopoa persica subsp.

multiradiata, commonly known as the Persian sand grass, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Iran. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
