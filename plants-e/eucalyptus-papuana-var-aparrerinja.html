Eucalyptus papuana var. aparrerinja

Eucalyptus papuana var. aparrerinja, commonly known as the Aparrerinja gum, is a distinctive species native to the rainforests of north-eastern Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, pale grey to white bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. This variant is notable for its unique floral structure, with flowers arranged in umbels and typically displaying pink or pale yellow petals.

The plant is adapted to humid, shaded environments and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
