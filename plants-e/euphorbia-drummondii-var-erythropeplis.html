Euphorbia drummondii var. erythropeplis

Euphorbia drummondii var. erythropeplis, commonly known as the red-veined milkweed, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are typically green with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by vibrant red bracts, making it visually striking. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens in regions with hot, dry climates.
