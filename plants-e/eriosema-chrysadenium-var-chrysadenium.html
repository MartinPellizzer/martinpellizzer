Eriosema chrysadenium var. chrysadenium

Eriosema chrysadenium var. chrysadenium, commonly known as the golden sandveld eriosema, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features erect, branched stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white to pale yellow hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or golden in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a velvety texture.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in sandy soils.
