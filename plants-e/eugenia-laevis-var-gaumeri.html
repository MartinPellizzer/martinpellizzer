Eugenia laevis var. gaumeri

Eugenia laevis var. gaumeri, commonly known as the Mexican wax tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
