Erigeron philippii var. elatior

Erigeron philippii var. elatior, commonly known as the tall blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
