Echinocactus peninsulae

Echinocactus peninsulae, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Baja California Peninsula.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy fruits that resemble apples, which are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation programs.
