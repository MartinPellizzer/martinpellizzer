Eugenia avicenniae

Eugenia avicenniae, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a small evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, typically found in mangrove forests. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a fleshy, red or orange berry containing several seeds.

Eugenia avicenniae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
