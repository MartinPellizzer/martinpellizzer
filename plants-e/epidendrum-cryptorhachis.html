Epidendrum cryptorhachis

Epidendrum cryptorhachis, commonly known as the hidden spine orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is relatively easy to cultivate with proper humidity and drainage.

Epidendrum cryptorhachis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
