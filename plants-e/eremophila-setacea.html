Eremophila setacea

Eremophila setacea, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eremophila setacea is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant in dry environments.
