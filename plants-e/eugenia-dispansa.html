Eugenia dispansa

Eugenia dispansa, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia dispansa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.
