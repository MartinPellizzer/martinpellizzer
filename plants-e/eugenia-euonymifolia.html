Eugenia euonymifolia

Eugenia euonymifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically silver-white on the underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique foliage and seasonal blooms make it a desirable plant for botanical gardens and private collections.
