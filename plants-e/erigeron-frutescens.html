Erigeron frutescens

Erigeron frutescens, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.
