Elaeocarpus nitidus var. wrayi

Elaeocarpus nitidus var. wrayi, commonly known as the glossy elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating.

This variant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
