Euphorbia gymnocalycioides

Euphorbia gymnocalycioides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbia genus. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Euphorbia gymnocalycioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
