Eulophia fractiflexa

Eulophia fractiflexa, commonly known as the flexed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia fractiflexa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
