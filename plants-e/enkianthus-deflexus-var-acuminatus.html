Enkianthus deflexus var. acuminatus

Enkianthus deflexus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the Acuminated Enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in early spring. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
