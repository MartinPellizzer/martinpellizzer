Enceliopsis argophylla var. grandiflora

Enceliopsis argophylla var. grandiflora, commonly known as the giant milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and lobed, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is part of the Asteraceae family and plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
