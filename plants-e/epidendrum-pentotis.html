Epidendrum pentotis

Epidendrum pentotis, commonly known as the purple-flowered epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its striking, showy flowers. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous pale purple to pinkish flowers, which are borne on pendulous stems. Epidendrum pentotis prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its foliage is typically dark green and glossy, adding to its ornamental appeal.
