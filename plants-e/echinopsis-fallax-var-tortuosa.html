Echinopsis fallax var. tortuosa

Echinopsis fallax var. tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted echinopsis, is a succulent cactus native to South America.

It features a short, cylindrical stem with a distinctive spiral pattern of spines, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens and collections.
