Eriogonum niveum subsp. decumbens

Eriogonum niveum subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the white flax or snowflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers.

The plant typically thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies decumbens is distinguished by its prostrate growth habit and dense, woolly leaves.
