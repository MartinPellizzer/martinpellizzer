Echinocereus baileyi var. brunispinus

Echinocereus baileyi var. brunispinus, commonly known as the black spines cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and dark, spiny structures that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh desert conditions.
