Eupatorium semistriatum

Eupatorium semistriatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium semistriatum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
