Eugenia quilombola

Eugenia quilombola, commonly known as the quilombola guava, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a pleasant scent.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.
