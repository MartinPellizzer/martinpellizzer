Eritrichium incanum var. maackii

Eritrichium incanum var. maackii, commonly known as the Maack's hoary vetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or shrubland habitats. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
