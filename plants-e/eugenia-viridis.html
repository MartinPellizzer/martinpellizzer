Eugenia viridis

Eugenia viridis, commonly known as the green eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
