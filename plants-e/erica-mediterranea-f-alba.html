Erica mediterranea f. alba

Erica mediterranea f. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

Erica mediterranea f. alba is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it contributes to the local biodiversity and landscape.
