Eriocaulon nipponicum

Eriocaulon nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese marsh grass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny white or pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.

Eriocaulon nipponicum is an important species for wetland conservation due to its ecological role in maintaining habitat for various organisms.
