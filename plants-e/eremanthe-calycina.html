Eremanthe calycina

Eremanthe calycina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sweetleaf, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures containing numerous tiny seeds.

Eremanthe calycina is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
