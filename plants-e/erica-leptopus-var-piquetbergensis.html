Erica leptopus var. piquetbergensis

Erica leptopus var. piquetbergensis, commonly known as the Piquetberg heath, is a dwarf shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to pale red, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
