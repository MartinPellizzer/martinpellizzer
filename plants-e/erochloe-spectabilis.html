Erochloe spectabilis

Erochloe spectabilis, commonly known as the showy bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue-green foliage and spike-like inflorescence that blooms in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish hue.

Erochloe spectabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
