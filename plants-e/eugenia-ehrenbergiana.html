Eugenia ehrenbergiana

Eugenia ehrenbergiana, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, pinkish-red fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
