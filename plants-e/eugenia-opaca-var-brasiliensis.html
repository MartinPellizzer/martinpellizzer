Eugenia opaca var. brasiliensis

Eugenia opaca var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja-brasileira*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and for its fruit, which is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
