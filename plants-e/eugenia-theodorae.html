Eugenia theodorae

Eugenia theodorae, commonly known as the Theodore's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia theodorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
