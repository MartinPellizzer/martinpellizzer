Eupatorium mallotum var. typicum

Eupatorium mallotum var. typicum, commonly known as the broadleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.
