Eucalyptus caerulescens

Eucalyptus caerulescens, commonly known as the blue mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often displays a striking blue or greyish hue. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a greyish-blue coloration that gives it its common name.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry climates.
