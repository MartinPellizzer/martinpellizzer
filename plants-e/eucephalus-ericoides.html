Eucephalus ericoides

Eucephalus ericoides, commonly known as coast sages, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. It is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Eucephalus ericoides plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
