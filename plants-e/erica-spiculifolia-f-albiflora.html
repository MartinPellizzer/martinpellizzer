Erica spiculifolia f. albiflora

Erica spiculifolia f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage and showy white flowers. Native to the coastal regions of South Africa, it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. The flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically arranged in dense spikes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
