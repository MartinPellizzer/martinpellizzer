Erica pulchella

Erica pulchella, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in spring. Erica pulchella is evergreen, with leathery, needle-like leaves that help it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic conditions.
