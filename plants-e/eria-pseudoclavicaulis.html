Eria pseudoclavicaulis

Eria pseudoclavicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-clubbed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that often hang from trees, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Eria pseudoclavicaulis is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
