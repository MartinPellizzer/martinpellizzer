Eryngium pristis var. mitigatum

Eryngium pristis var. mitigatum, commonly known as the lesser thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a basal rosette structure.

Eryngium pristis var. mitigatum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
