Elaeagnus matsunoana

Elaeagnus matsunoana, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and some animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

Elaeagnus matsunoana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
