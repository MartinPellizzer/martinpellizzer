Erythroxylum kanga

Erythroxylum kanga, commonly known as the red leaf mallow, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding visual interest to its appearance. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Erythroxylum kanga is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens due to its striking foliage.
