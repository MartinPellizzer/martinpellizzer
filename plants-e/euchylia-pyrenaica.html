Euchylia pyrenaica

Euchylia pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage, and it produces showy blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Euchylia pyrenaica is valued in horticulture for its resilience and aesthetic beauty in garden settings.
