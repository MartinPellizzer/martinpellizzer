Euphorbia bravoana

Euphorbia bravoana, commonly known as Bravo's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and often spiny structures. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is a defining feature of the genus Euphorbia.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions, making it resilient in harsh climates.
