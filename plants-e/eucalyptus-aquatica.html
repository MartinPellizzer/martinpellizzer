Eucalyptus aquatica

Eucalyptus aquatica, commonly known as the water malleeflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. Eucalyptus aquatica is often found in wetland areas and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
