Eriochloa macclounii

Eriochloa macclounii, commonly known as Gulf grass or Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green florets. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and swamp edges, and is adapted to fluctuating water levels.

Eriochloa macclounii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
