Euphorbia fieldii

Euphorbia fieldii, commonly known as the Field's spurge, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to greenish-white. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry fields.

Euphorbia fieldii is adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperatures, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
