Ehretia braunii

Ehretia braunii, commonly known as the Brahmaputra holly, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical gardens.
