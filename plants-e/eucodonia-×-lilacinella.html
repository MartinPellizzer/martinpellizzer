Eucodonia × lilacinella

Eucodonia × lilacinella, commonly known as the lilac-leafed eucalyptus, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from its parent species.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that often display a distinctive lilac or pale blue hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale blue, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates with mild winters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
