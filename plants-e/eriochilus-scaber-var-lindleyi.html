Eriochilus scaber var. lindleyi

Eriochilus scaber var. lindleyi, commonly known as the rough leaved eriochilus, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, pink or white flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom during the cooler months.

This variant is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
