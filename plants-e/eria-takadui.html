Eria takadui

Eria takadui, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and leathery, while its flowers come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Eria takadui is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
