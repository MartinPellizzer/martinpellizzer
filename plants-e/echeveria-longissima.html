Echeveria longissima

Echeveria longissima, commonly known as the "Mexican Firecracker," is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring and summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant coloration.

Echeveria longissima is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
