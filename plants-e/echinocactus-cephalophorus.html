Echinocactus cephalophorus

Echinocactus cephalophorus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stem during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and rockeries.
