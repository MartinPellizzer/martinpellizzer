Eugenia kashotoensis

Eugenia kashotoensis, commonly known as the Kashoto hibiscus, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania and Mozambique. The plant is characterized by its showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe.

Eugenia kashotoensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories.
