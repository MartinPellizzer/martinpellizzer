Echinacea pallida var. angustifolia

Echinacea pallida var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, pinkish-purple flower heads with a prominent central disk, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
