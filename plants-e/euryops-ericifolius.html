Euryops ericifolius

Euryops ericifolius, commonly known as the woolly bush, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice in garden settings.
