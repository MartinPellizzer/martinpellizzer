Erigeron pulcherrimus

Erigeron pulcherrimus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, featuring white petals with a yellow center and finely divided, silvery-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing numerous small flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erigeron pulcherrimus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
