Eugenia ploumensis

Eugenia ploumensis, commonly known as the Pouloumense myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia ploumensis is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
