Echinofossulocactus macracanthus

Echinofossulocactus macracanthus, commonly known as the giant fissurated cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its fleshy stems store water, making it well adapted to survive in dry environments.

The cactus also has spines that provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss through transpiration.
