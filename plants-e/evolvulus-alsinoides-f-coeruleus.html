Evolvulus alsinoides f. coeruleus

Evolvulus alsinoides f. coeruleus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. The plant has hairy, alternate leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens for its vibrant, fleeting blooms.
