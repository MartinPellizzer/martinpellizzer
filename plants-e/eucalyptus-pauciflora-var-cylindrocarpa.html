Eucalyptus pauciflora var. cylindrocarpa

Eucalyptus pauciflora var. cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cylindrical flowers that are typically white or pale blue, which appear in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cooler, moist environments and is well adapted to high-altitude conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
