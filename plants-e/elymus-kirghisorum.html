Elymus kirghisorum

Elymus kirghisorum, commonly known as Kirghiz wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

Elymus kirghisorum is valued for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
