Erigeron macdonaldii

Erigeron macdonaldii, commonly known as Macdonald's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, silver-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron macdonaldii is often found in desertscrub and chaparral ecosystems, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
