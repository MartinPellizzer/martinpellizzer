Eugenia attenuatifolia

Eugenia attenuatifolia, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, smooth, reddish drupe that ripens in the dry season.
