Ericoides burchellii

Ericoides burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's ericoid, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

Ericoides burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
