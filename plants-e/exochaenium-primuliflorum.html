Exochaenium primuliflorum

Exochaenium primuliflorum, commonly known as the primrose false brome, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
