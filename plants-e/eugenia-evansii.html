Eugenia evansii

Eugenia evansii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia evansii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
