Erigeron hyssopifolius var. typicus

Erigeron hyssopifolius var. typicus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple composite flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
