Eulophia antunesii

Eulophia antunesii, commonly known as the Antunes' orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stem and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single inflorescence with several blooms.

Eulophia antunesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
