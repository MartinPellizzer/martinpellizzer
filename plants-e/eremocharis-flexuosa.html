Eremocharis flexuosa

Eremocharis flexuosa, commonly known as the twisted spider orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like inflorescence with elongated, recurved flowers that display a mix of white, green, and purple hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Eremocharis flexuosa is considered a protected species in many parts of its native range.
