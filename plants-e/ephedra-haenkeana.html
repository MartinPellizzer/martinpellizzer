Ephedra haenkeana

Ephedra haenkeana, commonly known as the Desert Tea, is a member of the Ephedraceae family and is native to arid regions of North America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Ephedra haenkeana is notable for containing ephedrine, a compound with historical use in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptation to extreme climates.
