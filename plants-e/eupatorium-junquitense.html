Eupatorium junquitense

Eupatorium junquitense, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
