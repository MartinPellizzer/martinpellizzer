Eriogonum aliquantum

Eriogonum aliquantum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color, and it thrives in well-drained soils in sunny, open habitats. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eriogonum aliquantum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
