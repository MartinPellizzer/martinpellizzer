Euphorbia cespitosa subsp. typica

Euphorbia cespitosa subsp.

typica, commonly known as the creeping spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America. It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by bracts.

The plant has thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
