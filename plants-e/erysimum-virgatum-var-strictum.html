Erysimum virgatum var. strictum

Erysimum virgatum var. strictum, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
