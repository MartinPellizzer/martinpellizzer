Eupatorium pandurifolium

Eupatorium pandurifolium, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads that persist through the winter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium pandurifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
