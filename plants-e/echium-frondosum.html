Echium frondosum

Echium frondosum, commonly known as the fronded thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium frondosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
