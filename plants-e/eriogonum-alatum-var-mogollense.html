Eriogonum alatum var. mogollense

Eriogonum alatum var. mogollense, commonly known as the Mojave buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky areas. It has a low, spreading growth habit and is well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
