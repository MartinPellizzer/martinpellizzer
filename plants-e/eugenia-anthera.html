Eugenia anthera

Eugenia anthera, commonly known as the red anthera myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and initially green, turning red when mature.

Eugenia anthera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
