Erysimum confertum

Erysimum confertum, commonly known as the clustered wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its ornamental value.

Erysimum confertum is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
