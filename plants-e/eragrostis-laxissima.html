Eragrostis laxissima

Eragrostis laxissima, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Eragrostis laxissima is often used in ornamental gardening for its elegant appearance and drought tolerance.
