Elaeocarpus floribundus

Elaeocarpus floribundus, commonly known as the clustered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces round, woody fruits that are typically green when mature and turn dark when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface.

Elaeocarpus floribundus is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
