Ephialis simplicifolia

Ephialis simplicifolia, commonly known as the simpleleaf ephialis, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, greenish-white flowers that bloom in summer. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Ephialis simplicifolia is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
