Eugenia penangiana

Eugenia penangiana, commonly known as the Penang guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
