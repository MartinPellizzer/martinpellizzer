Erigeron coronarius

Erigeron coronarius, commonly known as the crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display of white petals with a yellow center.
