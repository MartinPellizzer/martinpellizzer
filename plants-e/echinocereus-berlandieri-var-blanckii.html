Echinocereus berlandieri var. blanckii

Echinocereus berlandieri var. blanckii, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often spiny and covered with a whitish coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh desert conditions.
