Elaeocarpus valetonii var. longibarbatus

Elaeocarpus valetonii var. longibarbatus, commonly known as the long-beaked elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
