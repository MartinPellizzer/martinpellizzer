Eugenia ramoniana

Eugenia ramoniana, commonly known as the ramon tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The ramon tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
