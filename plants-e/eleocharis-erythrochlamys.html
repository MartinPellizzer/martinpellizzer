Eleocharis erythrochlamys

Eleocharis erythrochlamys, commonly known as the red-chalmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often reddish in color, especially at the base, and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor aquatic conditions.

Eleocharis erythrochlamys plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
