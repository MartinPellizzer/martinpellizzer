Eutrema pseudocordifolium

Eutrema pseudocordifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sea rocket, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

Eutrema pseudocordifolium is typically found in salt marshes and dunes, where it plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
