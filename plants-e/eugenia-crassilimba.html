Eugenia crassilimba

Eugenia crassilimba, commonly known as the broad-leafed myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are thick and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Eugenia crassilimba is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions for its aromatic properties.
