Erysimum metlesicsii

Erysimum metlesicsii, commonly known as the desert wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy leaves and produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

Erysimum metlesicsii is often found in desert scrub and creosote bush communities.
