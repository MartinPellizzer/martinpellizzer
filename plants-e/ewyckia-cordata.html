Ewyckia cordata

Ewyckia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which features dark green, ovate leaves with a distinctive heart-shaped base. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and adaptability to various climates contribute to its widespread cultivation.
