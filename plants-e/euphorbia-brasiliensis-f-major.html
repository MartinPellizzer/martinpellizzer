Euphorbia brasiliensis f. major

Euphorbia brasiliensis f. major, commonly known as the Brazilian euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
