Eritrichium pugae

Eritrichium pugae, commonly known as the Pugan bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features small, delicate blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Eritrichium pugae is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
