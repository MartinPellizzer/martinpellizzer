Eucalyptus dealbata subsp. aperticola

Eucalyptus dealbata subsp.

aperticola, commonly known as the white ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped, pale green leaves that are often covered in a fine white wax.

The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are clustered in umbels, typically appearing in spring. The tree produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, brown seeds.

It is often found in open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
