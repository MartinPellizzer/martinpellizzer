Erythroxylum divaricatum

Erythroxylum divaricatum, commonly known as the red-veined or red-vein shrub, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oblong, woody capsules containing seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
