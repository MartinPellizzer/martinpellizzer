Emilia cryptantha

Emilia cryptantha, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in a cluster, often mistaken for a single flower. Emilia cryptantha has hairy, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

It is known for its ability to thrive in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
