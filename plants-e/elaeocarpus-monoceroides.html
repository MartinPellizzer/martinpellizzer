Elaeocarpus monoceroides

Elaeocarpus monoceroides, commonly known as the single-horned elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
