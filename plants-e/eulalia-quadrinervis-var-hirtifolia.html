Eulalia quadrinervis var. hirtifolia

Eulalia quadrinervis var. hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
