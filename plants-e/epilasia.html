Epilasia

Epilasia, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of ray florets and disk florets. Epilasia species typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped or egg-shaped, often with a hairy texture. They are native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and are often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides.

The genus includes several species, such as Epilasia hirsuta and Epilasia palustris, which are known for their medicinal and ecological significance.
