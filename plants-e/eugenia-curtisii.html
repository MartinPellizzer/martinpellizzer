Eugenia curtisii

Eugenia curtisii, commonly known as the Kurisii cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, cherry-like fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
