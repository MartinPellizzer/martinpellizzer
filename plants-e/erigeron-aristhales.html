Erigeron aristhales

Erigeron aristhales, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of silvery-blue, fuzzy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems that bear numerous small, fluffy flower heads. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Erigeron aristhales is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
