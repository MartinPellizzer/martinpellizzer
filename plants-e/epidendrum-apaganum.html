Epidendrum apaganum

Epidendrum apaganum, commonly known as the purple-tinged epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences, with petals that range in color from white to pink and sometimes with a subtle purple hue.

Epidendrum apaganum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
