Ephedra intermedia

Ephedra intermedia, commonly known as the middle ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Ephedra intermedia contains ephedrine, a compound with historical medicinal uses.

However, it is also classified as a controlled substance due to its potential for abuse and health risks.
