Ellisia acuta

Ellisia acuta, commonly known as the sharpleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and palmate leaves with sharply pointed lobes. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Ellisia acuta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
