Eucalyptus tereticornis var. linearis

Eucalyptus tereticornis var. linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leaf eucalyptus.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant is native to southeastern Australia and thrives in dry, sandy soils. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which attract a variety of pollinators.

This variant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
