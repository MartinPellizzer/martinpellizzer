Evolvulus macroblepharis

Evolvulus macroblepharis, commonly known as the large-eyed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes morning glories and other flowering vines. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Evolvulus macroblepharis is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid environments.
