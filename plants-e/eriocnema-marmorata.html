Eriocnema marmorata

Eriocnema marmorata, commonly known as the marble-stemmed vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive marbled patterns on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
