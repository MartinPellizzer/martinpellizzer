Eugenia vacana

Eugenia vacana, commonly known as the cherry tomato or hibiscus cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, bright red berries that resemble cherries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a fragrant scent, while its flowers are white or pink and often have a strong aroma.

The fruit is edible, often used in culinary applications, and is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
