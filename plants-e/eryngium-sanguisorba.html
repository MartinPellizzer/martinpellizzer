Eryngium sanguisorba

Eryngium sanguisorba, commonly known as bloodroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a distinctive, rough, thistle-like appearance with bracts that are often tinged with red. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged aesthetic.

Eryngium sanguisorba is often found in dry meadows, woodlands, and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.
