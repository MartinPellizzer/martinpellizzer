Echinocactus setispinus var. martelii

Echinocactus setispinus var. martelii, commonly known as the Martel's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in xeriscapes and succulent gardens.
