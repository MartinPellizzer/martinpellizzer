Eucalyptus binacag

Eucalyptus binacag, commonly known as the Binacag gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to the Philippines.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 30 meters, with smooth, peeling bark that is often grey or white in color. The leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and typically have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and white or pale pink, clustered in umbels, and are followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber and has traditional uses in local medicine.
