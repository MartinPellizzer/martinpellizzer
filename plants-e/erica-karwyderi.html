Erica karwyderi

Erica karwyderi, commonly known as the Karwyder's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. Erica karwyderi produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
