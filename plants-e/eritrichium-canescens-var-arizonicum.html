Eritrichium canescens var. arizonicum

Eritrichium canescens var. arizonicum, commonly known as Arizona hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a low, spreading growth habit and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in dry regions.
