Eriogonum microtheca subsp. laxiflorum

Eriogonum microtheca subsp.

laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats such as grasslands and slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapting to arid conditions.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.
