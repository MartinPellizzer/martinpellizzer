Eria caricifolia

Eria caricifolia, commonly known as the water lily of the genus Eria, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle that helps repel water, a feature common among aquatic plants.

Eria caricifolia plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
