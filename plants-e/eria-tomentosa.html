Eria tomentosa

Eria tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
