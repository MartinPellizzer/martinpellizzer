Eupatorium shimadae

Eupatorium shimadae, commonly known as Shimada's boneset, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and streamside areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium shimadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
