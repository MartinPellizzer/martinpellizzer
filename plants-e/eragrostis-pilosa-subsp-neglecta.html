Eragrostis pilosa subsp. neglecta

Eragrostis pilosa subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and a distinctive weeping or drooping growth habit.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
