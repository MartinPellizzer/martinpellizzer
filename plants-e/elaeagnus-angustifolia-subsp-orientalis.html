Elaeagnus angustifolia subsp. orientalis

Elaeagnus angustifolia subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, red-orange berries. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
