Eritrichium brevipes

Eritrichium brevipes, commonly known as the short-footed eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy stems and small, delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense mats. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eritrichium brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.
