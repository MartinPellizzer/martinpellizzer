Eriocephala schumanniana subsp. nigrispina

Eriocephala schumanniana subsp.

nigrispina, commonly known as the black-spined eriocephala, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates. Its subspecies name, *nigrispina*, refers to the dark, prominent spines that cover its stems.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
