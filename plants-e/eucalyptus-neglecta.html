Eucalyptus neglecta

Eucalyptus neglecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the winter and spring seasons. Eucalyptus neglecta is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and heathland ecosystems.

Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, making it a valuable plant in both ecological and aromatic contexts.
