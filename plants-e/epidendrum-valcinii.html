Epidendrum valcinii

Epidendrum valcinii, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually dark green and elliptical in shape, and it has a sympodial growth pattern.

Epidendrum valcinii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
