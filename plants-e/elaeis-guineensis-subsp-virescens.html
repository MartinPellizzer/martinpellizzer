Elaeis guineensis subsp. virescens

Elaeis guineensis subsp.

virescens, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a species native to West Africa. It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces fruit-bearing clusters called inflorescences.

The plant is well adapted to tropical climates and requires high temperatures and abundant rainfall. Its fruits contain oil-rich seeds that are widely used in food and industrial products.

The subspecies is notable for its resilience and economic importance in the palm oil industry.
