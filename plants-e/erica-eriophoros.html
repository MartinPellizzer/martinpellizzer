Erica eriophoros

Erica eriophoros, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica eriophoros is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high salinity and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
