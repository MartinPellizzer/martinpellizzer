Excoecaria biglandulosa var. grandifolia

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
