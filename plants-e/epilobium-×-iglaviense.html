Epilobium × iglaviense

Epilobium × iglaviense, commonly known as the velvetleaf willow herb, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Epilobium × iglaviense is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes, making it a persistent presence in its native habitats.
