Enteropogon dolichostachyus

Enteropogon dolichostachyus, commonly known as the long-stalked false witchgrass, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that are often arranged in elongated clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs in the late summer to early autumn, and it is characterized by its pale to brownish spikelets.

This species is often found in temperate regions and is of interest in ecological and agricultural studies.
