Elatostema oblongifolium var. magnistipulum

Elatostema oblongifolium var. magnistipulum, commonly known as the broad-leaved elatostema, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a short, rhizomatous stem that produces numerous small, upright, leafy shoots. The leaves are oblong to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and they are often clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in spring.

This species is well adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream margins.
