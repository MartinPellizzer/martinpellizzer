Eupatorium siltepecanum

Eupatorium siltepecanum, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium siltepecanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
