Erythrina corallodendron var. connata

Erythrina corallodendron var. connata, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It produces elongated pods containing flattened, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
