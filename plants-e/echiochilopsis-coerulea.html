Echiochilopsis coerulea

Echiochilopsis coerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue petals and a white center that resembles an eye, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth from the base.

The flowers are often fragrant and bloom during the warmer months of the year.
