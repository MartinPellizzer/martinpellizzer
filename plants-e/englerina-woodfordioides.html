Englerina woodfordioides

Englerina woodfordioides, commonly known as the New Zealand woodfordia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.
