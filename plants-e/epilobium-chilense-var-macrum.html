Epilobium chilense var. macrum

Epilobium chilense var. macrum, commonly known as the Chilean willow herb, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
