Eriogonum ternatum

Eriogonum ternatum, commonly known as the three-toothed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that are divided into three lobes, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and coastal dunes.

Eriogonum ternatum is an important plant for pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
