Echinus claoxyloides var. ficifolia

Echinus claoxyloides var. ficifolia, commonly known as the fig-leaf echinus, is a variant of the echinus plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture, resembling the leaves of the common fig tree. It is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
