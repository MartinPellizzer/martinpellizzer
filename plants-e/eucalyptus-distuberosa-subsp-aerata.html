Eucalyptus distuberosa subsp. aerata

Eucalyptus distuberosa subsp.

aerata, commonly known as the grey box, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that is often grey or brown in color.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and usually have a grey-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland communities.
