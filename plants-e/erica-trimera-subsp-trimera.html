Erica trimera subsp. trimera

Erica trimera subsp.

trimera, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as part of native plant landscapes.
