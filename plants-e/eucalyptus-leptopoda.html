Eucalyptus leptopoda

Eucalyptus leptopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The flowers are usually pale blue or white, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators. This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in low-nutrient soils and extreme temperatures.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.
