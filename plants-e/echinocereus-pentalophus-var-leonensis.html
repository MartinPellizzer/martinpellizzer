Echinocereus pentalophus var. leonensis

Echinocereus pentalophus var. leonensis, commonly known as the Leonensis Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny tubercles, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and distinctive, spiny appearance.
