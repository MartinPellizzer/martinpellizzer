Eleocharis papillosa

Eleocharis papillosa, commonly known as the silky spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with fine, silky hairs that give it its common name.

Eleocharis papillosa is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
