Ephedra yangthangensis

Ephedra yangthangensis, commonly known as the Tibetan ephedra, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ephedraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet, China, and is adapted to cold, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. It produces greenish-yellow flowers and is known for its medicinal properties.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been used in traditional medicine and is also of interest in modern pharmacological research.
