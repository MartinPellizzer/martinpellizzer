Eugenia marambaiensis

Eugenia marambaiensis, commonly known as the Marambai guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are typically elliptical with a smooth texture and a dark green color.
