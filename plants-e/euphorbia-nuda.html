Euphorbia nuda

Euphorbia nuda, commonly known as the naked euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by a crown of bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems for survival.

Euphorbia nuda is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
