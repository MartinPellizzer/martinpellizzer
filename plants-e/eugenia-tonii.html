Eugenia tonii

Eugenia tonii, commonly known as the Malabar cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. Eugenia tonii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical climates.
