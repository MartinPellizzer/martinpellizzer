Elaeocarpus sarcanthus

Elaeocarpus sarcanthus, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark-colored wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 20 meters, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental and commercial value, it is sometimes cultivated for timber and landscape purposes.
