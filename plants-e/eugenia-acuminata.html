Eugenia acuminata

Eugenia acuminata, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a round, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
