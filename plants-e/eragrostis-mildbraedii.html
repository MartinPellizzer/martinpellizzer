Eragrostis mildbraedii

Eragrostis mildbraedii, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis mildbraedii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in regions with limited rainfall.
