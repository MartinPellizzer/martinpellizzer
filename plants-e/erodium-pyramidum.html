Erodium pyramidum

Erodium pyramidum, commonly known as the mountain bidens, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes plants known for their distinctive leaf structures and sometimes edible species.
