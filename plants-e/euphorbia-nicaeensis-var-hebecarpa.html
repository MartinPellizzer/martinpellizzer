Euphorbia nicaeensis var. hebecarpa

Euphorbia nicaeensis var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the Canary Island spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is characterized by its ability to store water in its stems, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
