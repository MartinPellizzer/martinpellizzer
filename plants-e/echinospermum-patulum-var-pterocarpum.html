Echinospermum patulum var. pterocarpum

Echinospermum patulum var. pterocarpum, commonly known as the hairy pterocarpum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by distinctive, hairy fruits. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
