Excoecaria integrifolia

Excoecaria integrifolia, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring a rough, often fissured bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive red calyx.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or coastal areas.
