Euphrasia minima subsp. masclansii

Euphrasia minima subsp.

masclansii, commonly known as the small mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky crevices. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is part of the Orobanchaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
