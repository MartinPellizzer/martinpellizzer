Excoecaria eglandulosa

Excoecaria eglandulosa, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by orange-red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in rubber production.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
