Eria imbricata

Eria imbricata, commonly known as the crested fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping leaf structure that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant orchid-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Eria imbricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.

It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
