Eurya acuminata var. suzukii

Eurya acuminata var. suzukii, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Suzukii holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and China, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is suitable for hedges, container planting, and rock gardens.
