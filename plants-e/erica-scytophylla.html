Erica scytophylla

Erica scytophylla, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica scytophylla is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Its flowers bloom in winter, providing color during the colder months.
