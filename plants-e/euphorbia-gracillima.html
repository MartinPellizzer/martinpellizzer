Euphorbia gracillima

Euphorbia gracillima, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branching stem with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by a modified leaf called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia gracillima is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
