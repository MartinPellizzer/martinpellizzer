Eriogonum racemosum var. cordifolium

Eriogonum racemosum var. cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are heart-shaped, hence the specific epithet "cordifolium," and are often covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is an important plant for pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.
