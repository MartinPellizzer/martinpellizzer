Echium vulnerans

Echium vulnerans, commonly known as the wounded healer, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking spikes of blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Echium vulnerans is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
