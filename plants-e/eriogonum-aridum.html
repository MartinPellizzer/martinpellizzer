Eriogonum aridum

Eriogonum aridum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs to reduce water loss.

Eriogonum aridum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
