Eugenia queretaroana

Eugenia queretaroana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia queretaroana is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
