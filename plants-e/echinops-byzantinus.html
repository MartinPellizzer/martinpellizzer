Echinops byzantinus

Echinops byzantinus, commonly known as the Byzantine thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, which are a striking feature of the plant. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by the development of its distinctive bristly cones. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Echinops byzantinus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
