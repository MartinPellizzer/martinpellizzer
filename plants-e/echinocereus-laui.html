Echinocereus laui

Echinocereus laui, commonly known as the Laui's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and spines that are typically brown or yellowish in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Echinocereus laui is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
