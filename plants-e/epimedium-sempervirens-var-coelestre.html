Epimedium sempervirens var. coelestre

Epimedium sempervirens var. coelestre, commonly known as the "Chinese barrenwort," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are typically bronze to reddish in color, especially in the spring. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in cooler climates.
