Epidendrum coccineum

Epidendrum coccineum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features striking red or orange flowers that bloom along its stems, often in clusters. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new growth at the base. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two rows, providing structural support for its aerial roots.

Epidendrum coccineum thrives in humid, well-lit environments and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant appearance.
