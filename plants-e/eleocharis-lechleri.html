Eleocharis lechleri

Eleocharis lechleri, commonly known as the "Amazonian bulrush," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of South America, particularly the Amazon Basin.

It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that grow close to the ground, with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes are known for their medicinal properties, used by indigenous communities for their anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.

Eleocharis lechleri plays a vital ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
