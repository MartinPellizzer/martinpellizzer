Eupatorium vattuonei

Eupatorium vattuonei, commonly known as the Italian boneset, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic properties. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in paniculate inflorescences.

Eupatorium vattuonei is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory and digestive ailments.
