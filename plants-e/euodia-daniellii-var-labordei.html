Euodia daniellii var. labordei

Euodia daniellii var. labordei, commonly known as the laborde's wax tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.
