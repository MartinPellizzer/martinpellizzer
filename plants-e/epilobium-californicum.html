Epilobium californicum

Epilobium californicum, commonly known as California willow or evergreen willow, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in stream banks, meadows, and woodland edges.

Epilobium californicum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
