Eupatorium atrorubens

Eupatorium atrorubens, commonly known as blackroot eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain the seeds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium atrorubens is often found in moist, wooded areas and is part of the Asteraceae family.
