Eupatorium vauthierianum var. glabriusculum

Eupatorium vauthierianum var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the smoothleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are generally smooth and lance-shaped, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
