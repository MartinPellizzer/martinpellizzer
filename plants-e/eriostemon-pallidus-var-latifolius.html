Eriostemon pallidus var. latifolius

Eriostemon pallidus var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf eriostemon, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal and inland gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its popularity in horticultural settings.
