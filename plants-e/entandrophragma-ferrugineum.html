Entandrophragma ferrugineum

Entandrophragma ferrugineum, commonly known as the redheart tree, is a tropical hardwood species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, reddish-brown heartwood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has a straight grain and is often used in furniture and construction due to its strength and resistance to decay. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland rainforests.

The tree's botanical features include compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that are followed by woody pods containing seeds.
