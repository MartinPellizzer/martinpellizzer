Epidendrum ochrochlorum

Epidendrum ochrochlorum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy coating. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a contrasting green throat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum ochrochlorum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
