Eulophia exigua

Eulophia exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eulophia, is a species of orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, linear leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinct lip that is often darker in color. This orchid thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
