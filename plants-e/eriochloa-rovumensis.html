Eriochloa rovumensis

Eriochloa rovumensis, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy texture.

Eriochloa rovumensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
