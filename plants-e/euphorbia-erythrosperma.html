Euphorbia erythrosperma

Euphorbia erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-seeded spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

The bright red seeds it produces are a striking feature, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.
