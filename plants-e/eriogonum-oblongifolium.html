Eriogonum oblongifolium

Eriogonum oblongifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
