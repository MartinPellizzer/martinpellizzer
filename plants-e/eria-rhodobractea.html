Eria rhodobractea

Eria rhodobractea, commonly known as the red-bracted erythrin, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that enclose the inflorescence. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination.

Eria rhodobractea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
