Erica paludosa

Erica paludosa, commonly known as bog heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the wet, acidic environments of northern Europe and North America.

It thrives in peat bogs and heathlands, where it forms dense mats that stabilize the soil and prevent erosion. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to retain moisture in nutrient-poor conditions.

Erica paludosa plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing food and shelter for various insect and bird species.
