Erigeron zosonius

Erigeron zosonius, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron zosonius is often found in moist meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks, thriving in partially shaded to sunny conditions.
