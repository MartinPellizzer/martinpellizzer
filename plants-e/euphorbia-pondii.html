Euphorbia pondii

Euphorbia pondii, commonly known as the Pond's spurge, is a succulent plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stem and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often spiny members.

Euphorbia pondii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
