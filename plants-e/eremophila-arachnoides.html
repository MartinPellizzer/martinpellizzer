Eremophila arachnoides

Eremophila arachnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Eremophila arachnoides is a hardy species that thrives in full sun and is commonly used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
