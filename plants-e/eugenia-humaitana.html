Eugenia humaitana

Eugenia humaitana, commonly known as the Amazonian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. Eugenia humaitana thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in the understory of dense rainforest canopies.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
