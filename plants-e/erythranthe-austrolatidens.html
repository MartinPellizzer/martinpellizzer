Erythranthe austrolatidens

Erythranthe austrolatidens, commonly known as the southern wallflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and basal rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Erythranthe austrolatidens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
