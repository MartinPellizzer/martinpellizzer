Eucalyptus largeana

Eucalyptus largeana, commonly known as the large-leaved malleeflower, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as mallee woodlands and heathlands. It has a woody structure with smooth bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, glandular surface.

Eucalyptus largeana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
