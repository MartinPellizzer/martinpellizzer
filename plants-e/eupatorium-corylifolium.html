Eupatorium corylifolium

Eupatorium corylifolium, commonly known as Chinese boneset, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves and stems contain compounds that have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

Eupatorium corylifolium is often found in moist, shady areas such as forests and stream banks.
