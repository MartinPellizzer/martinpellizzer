Eria vulpina

Eria vulpina, commonly known as the foxglove orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a small, terrestrial herb with a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that help it store water. The plant produces striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, featuring intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of foxgloves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
