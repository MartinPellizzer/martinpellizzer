Erica thomae

Erica thomae, commonly known as the Thomson's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape province, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica thomae is a shrub that can grow up to 1 meter tall, with dense, evergreen foliage.

It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
