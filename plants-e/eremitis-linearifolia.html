Eremitis linearifolia

Eremitis linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eremitis, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the cooler months, often in spring or early summer. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Eremitis linearifolia is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
