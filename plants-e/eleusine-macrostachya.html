Eleusine macrostachya

Eleusine macrostachya, commonly known as lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green or reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

Eleusine macrostachya is often used in erosion control due to its rapid growth and ability to stabilize soil.
