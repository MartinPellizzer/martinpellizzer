Eugenia aherniana

Eugenia aherniana, commonly known as the Ahern's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia aherniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native region.
