Exarrhena albiflora

Exarrhena albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
