Eremurus libanoticus

Eremurus libanoticus, commonly known as the Lebanese sasa, is a perennial plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a candle or torch. The plant produces showy, often yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Eremurus libanoticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
