Erysimum asperum var. parviflorum

Erysimum asperum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
