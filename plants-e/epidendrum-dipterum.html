Epidendrum dipterum

Epidendrum dipterum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Forest, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on trees or rocks in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum dipterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
