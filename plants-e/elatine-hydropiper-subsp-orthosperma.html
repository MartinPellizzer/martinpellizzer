Elatine hydropiper subsp. orthosperma

Elatine hydropiper subsp.

orthosperma, commonly known as water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams.

The plant has small, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces tiny, greenish flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and its preference for temperate climates.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
