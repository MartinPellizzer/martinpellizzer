Euterpe longepetiolata

Euterpe longepetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in branched clusters, which are followed by elongated, slender fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as thatch material due to their durability.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
