Eriophorum transiens

Eriophorum transiens, commonly known as the transitional tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. Eriophorum transiens is often found in shallow water or moist soil, contributing to the ecological stability of its environment.
