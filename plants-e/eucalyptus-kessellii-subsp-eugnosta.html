Eucalyptus kessellii subsp.

eugnosta, commonly known as the Eucalyptus kessellii subsp. eugnosta, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in areas with sandy or rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and aromatic. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, woody capsules as fruit.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
