Eupatorium pacacanum

Eupatorium pacacanum, commonly known as the Pacific eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest region of North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, clustered inflorescences produce small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium pacacanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
