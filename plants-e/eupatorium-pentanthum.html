Eupatorium pentanthum

Eupatorium pentanthum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium pentanthum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
