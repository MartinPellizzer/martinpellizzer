Echites membranaceus

Echites membranaceus, commonly known as the paperleaf echites, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
