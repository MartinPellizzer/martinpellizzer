Eremurus chloranthus

Eremurus chloranthus, commonly known as the yellow torchflower, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the sunflower family, Asteraceae. The plant is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellow ray florets surrounded by a dense cluster of disk flowers. Eremurus chloranthus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its dramatic, torch-like appearance.
