Eremophila jucunda

Eremophila jucunda, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a deep root system for water absorption. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eremophila jucunda is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
