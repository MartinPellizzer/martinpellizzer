Ericoides petiolatum

Ericoides petiolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stems. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky coastal habitats.

Ericoides petiolatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
