Erica parilis

Erica parilis, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica parilis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense ground cover.
