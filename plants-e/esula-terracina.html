Esula terracina

Esula terracina, commonly known as the terracina thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves, which are adapted to thrive in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a rough, spherical capsule that contains several seeds.

Esula terracina is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
