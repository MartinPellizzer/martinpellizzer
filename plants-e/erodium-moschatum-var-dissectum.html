Erodium moschatum var. dissectum

Erodium moschatum var. dissectum, commonly known as the dissected bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and modest blooms.
