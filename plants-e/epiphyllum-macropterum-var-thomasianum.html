Epiphyllum macropterum var. thomasianum

Epiphyllum macropterum var. thomasianum, commonly known as Thomas's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming habit.
