Ervum stipulaceum

Ervum stipulaceum, commonly known as the stipulate vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a leguminous species, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its compound leaves with stipules. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Ervum stipulaceum is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.
