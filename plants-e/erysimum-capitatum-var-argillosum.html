Erysimum capitatum var. argillosum

Erysimum capitatum var. argillosum, commonly known as the clay erysimum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is adapted to full sun and has a low water requirement, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators in native gardens.
