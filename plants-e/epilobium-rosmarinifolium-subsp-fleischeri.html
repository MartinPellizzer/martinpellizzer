Epilobium rosmarinifolium subsp. fleischeri

Epilobium rosmarinifolium subsp.

fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's willowherb, is a flowering plant species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and lanceolate, often hairy leaves that resemble those of rosemary.

The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed sites.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
