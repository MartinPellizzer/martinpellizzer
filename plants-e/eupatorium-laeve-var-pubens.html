Eupatorium laeve var. pubens

Eupatorium laeve var. pubens, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant features erect, hairy stems that are often reddish-brown in color. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface.

It produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
