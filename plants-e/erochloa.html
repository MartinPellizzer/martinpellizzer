Erochloa

Erochloa, commonly known as false brome, is a genus of grasses native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

These plants typically have slender, erect stems and produce small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Erochloa species are often found in grasslands, open woodlands, and disturbed soils.

They play a role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
