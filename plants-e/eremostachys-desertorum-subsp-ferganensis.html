Eremostachys desertorum subsp. ferganensis

Eremostachys desertorum subsp.

ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana eremostachys, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves adapted to conserve water.

The plant produces small, pale green to brown flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its resilience, it plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
