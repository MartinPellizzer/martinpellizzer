Elymus × vancouverensis

Elymus × vancouverensis, commonly known as Vancouver needlegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, needle-like leaves, which are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas, and is often found in coastal regions. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer.

Elymus × vancouverensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
