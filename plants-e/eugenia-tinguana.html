Eugenia tinguana

Eugenia tinguana, commonly known as the Tinguana myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine. Eugenia tinguana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of its native region.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
