Erica rhodantha

Erica rhodantha, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. Erica rhodantha thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
