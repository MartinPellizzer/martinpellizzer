Erlangea eylesii

Erlangea eylesii, commonly known as the Eyles' erlang, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its growth habit is upright, with a tendency to form dense clumps, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Erlangea eylesii is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
