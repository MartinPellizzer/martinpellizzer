Epidendrum madsenii

Epidendrum madsenii, commonly known as the Madsen's epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in a spiral arrangement along the inflorescence. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Epidendrum madsenii is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
