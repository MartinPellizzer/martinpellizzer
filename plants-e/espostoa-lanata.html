Espostoa lanata

Espostoa lanata, commonly known as the woolly espostoa, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which provide a distinctive appearance and aid in water retention. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
