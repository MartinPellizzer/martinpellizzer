Eriogonum flavum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum flavum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
