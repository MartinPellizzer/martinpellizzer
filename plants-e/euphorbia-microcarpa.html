Euphorbia microcarpa

Euphorbia microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Euphorbia microcarpa is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
