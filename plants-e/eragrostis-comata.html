Eragrostis comata

Eragrostis comata, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to form dense turf, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
