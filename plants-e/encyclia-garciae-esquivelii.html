Encyclia garciae-esquivelii

Encyclia garciae-esquivelii, commonly known as the Esquivel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces erect, hairy pseudobulbs that bear linear, lanceolate leaves. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-yellow, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
