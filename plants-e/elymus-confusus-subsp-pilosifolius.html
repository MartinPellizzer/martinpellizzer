Elymus confusus subsp. pilosifolius

Elymus confusus subsp.

pilosifolius, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form compact clumps.

The leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential in ecological restoration projects.
