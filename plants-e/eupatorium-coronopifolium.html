Eupatorium coronopifolium

Eupatorium coronopifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a distinctive white or silver underside.

Eupatorium coronopifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
