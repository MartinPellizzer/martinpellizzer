Eupatorium purpureum var. ternifolium

Eupatorium purpureum var. ternifolium, commonly known as the three-leaf purple stem, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with purple or reddish hues. The leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and typically have three lobes, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer.

This plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and roadsides.
