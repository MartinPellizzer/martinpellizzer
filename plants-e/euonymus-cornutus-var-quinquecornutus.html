Euonymus cornutus var. quinquecornutus

Euonymus cornutus var. quinquecornutus, commonly known as the five-angled euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, five-angled stems that are often covered in a whitish, waxy coating. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated margins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
