Eragrostis chloromelas

Eragrostis chloromelas, commonly known as blue-green lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and narrow, erect stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are often rolled into a cylinder, a feature common in many grasses.

Eragrostis chloromelas is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
