Eleocharis pellucida

Eleocharis pellucida, commonly known as the glassworts, is a species of sedge that is characterized by its translucent, grass-like leaves.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water areas, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in its habitat.

Eleocharis pellucida is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and ability to stabilize soil.
