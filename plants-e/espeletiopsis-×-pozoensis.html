Espeletiopsis × pozoensis

Espeletiopsis × pozoensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a rare and unique plant species that results from the crossbreeding of two different orchid species.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
