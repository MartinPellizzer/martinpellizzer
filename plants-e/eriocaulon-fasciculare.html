Eriocaulon fasciculare

Eriocaulon fasciculare, commonly known as the clustered spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, fasciculate (bundled) growth of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams.

Eriocaulon fasciculare is a perennial species that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its wetland habitats.
