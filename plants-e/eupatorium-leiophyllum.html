Eupatorium leiophyllum

Eupatorium leiophyllum, commonly known as smoothleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and wet meadows, thriving in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
