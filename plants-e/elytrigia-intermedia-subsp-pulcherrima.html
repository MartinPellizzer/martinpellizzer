Elytrigia intermedia subsp. pulcherrima

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

pulcherrima, commonly known as the beautiful intermediate grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-lipped flowers, which are usually pink to purple in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
