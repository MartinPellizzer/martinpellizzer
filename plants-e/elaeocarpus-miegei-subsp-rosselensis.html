Elaeocarpus miegei subsp. rosselensis

Elaeocarpus miegei subsp.

rosselensis, commonly known as the Rossel's elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Caledonia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white to pink flowers.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds. This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often found in humid, tropical forest environments.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
