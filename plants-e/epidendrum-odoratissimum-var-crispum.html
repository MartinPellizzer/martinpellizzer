Epidendrum odoratissimum var. crispum

Epidendrum odoratissimum var. crispum, commonly known as the fragrant epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its ability to produce fragrant, showy flowers in a variety of colors, including pink, red, and white. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves that are often slightly curled or crinkled, giving it the "crispum" designation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and pleasant fragrance.
