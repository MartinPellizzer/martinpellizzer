Elaeocarpus harunii

Elaeocarpus harunii, commonly known as the Harun's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, colorful aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
