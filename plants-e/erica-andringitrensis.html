Erica andringitrensis

Erica andringitrensis, commonly known as the Andringitra heath, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This species is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and is adapted to the humid, mountainous regions of the Andringitra Massif.

Erica andringitrensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
