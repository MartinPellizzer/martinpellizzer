Endiandra crassifolia

Endiandra crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved endiandra, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is often found in humid, shaded environments. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in traditional crafts and construction.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting various forms of wildlife.
