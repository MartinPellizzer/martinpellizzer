Erica lowryensis

Erica lowryensis, commonly known as Lowry's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, range in color from white to pink and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica lowryensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic beauty.
