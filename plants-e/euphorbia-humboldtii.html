Euphorbia humboldtii

Euphorbia humboldtii, commonly known as the desert candle, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like stems that are covered in a thick layer of white, waxy resin. The plant produces small, greenish flowers surrounded by vibrant yellow bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is adapted to store water in its fleshy stems.

Euphorbia humboldtii is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
