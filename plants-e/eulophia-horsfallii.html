Eulophia horsfallii

Eulophia horsfallii, commonly known as the Horsfall's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green to brownish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Eulophia horsfallii is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
