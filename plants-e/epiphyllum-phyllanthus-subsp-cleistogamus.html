Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp. cleistogamus

Epiphyllum phyllanthus subsp.

cleistogamus, commonly known as the ghost cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems that can grow up to several meters in length.

The plant produces showy, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which open only at night. Its subspecies status reflects subtle differences in morphology and flowering behavior compared to the main species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
