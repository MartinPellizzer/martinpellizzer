Erodium brachycarpum

Erodium brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrow-fruit bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It has small, trifoliate leaves and produces tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in moist environments. Its seeds are enclosed in small, capsule-like fruits that split open to release the seeds.

Erodium brachycarpum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
