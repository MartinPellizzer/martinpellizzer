Ecastaphyllum brownei

Ecastaphyllum brownei, commonly known as the Brown's sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline, adapting to saline and wind-exposed environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ecastaphyllum brownei is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
