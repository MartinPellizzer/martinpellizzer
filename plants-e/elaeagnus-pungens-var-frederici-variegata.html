Elaeagnus pungens var. frederici-variegata

Elaeagnus pungens var. frederici-variegata, commonly known as the silver fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white, fringed flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves. The plant has compound leaves with a silvery underside, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
