Eupatorium areolare

Eupatorium areolare, commonly known as the areolar eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many familiar flowering plants.
