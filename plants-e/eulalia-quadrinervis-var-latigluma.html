Eulalia quadrinervis var. latigluma

Eulalia quadrinervis var. latigluma, commonly known as the broad-leafed pampas grass, is a variant of the pampas grass species native to South America.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and dense, feathery plumes that appear in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often broader than the standard variety, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it suitable for erosion control and as a windbreak in arid or semi-arid regions.
