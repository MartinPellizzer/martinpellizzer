Epipactis fascicularis

Epipactis fascicularis, commonly known as the clustered helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and streambanks. It has a rhizomatous root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Epipactis fascicularis is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.
