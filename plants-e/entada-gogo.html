Entada gogo

Entada gogo, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The tree produces large, pod-like fruits that contain hard, chestnut-like seeds. Its bark is thick and often fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
