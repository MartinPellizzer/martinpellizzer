Eupatorium coriaceum

Eupatorium coriaceum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded habitats, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium coriaceum is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
