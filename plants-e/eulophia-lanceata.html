Eulophia lanceata

Eulophia lanceata, commonly known as the lance-leaf eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and multiple pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces long, linear leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, often fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip.

Eulophia lanceata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
