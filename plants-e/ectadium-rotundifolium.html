Ectadium rotundifolium

Ectadium rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed ectadium, is a species of flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
