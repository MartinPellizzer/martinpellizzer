Enneapogon dipsaceus

Enneapogon dipsaceus, commonly known as the slender grass or desert grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
