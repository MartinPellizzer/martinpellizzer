Echinocactus knuthianus

Echinocactus knuthianus, commonly known as the Knuth's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed, cylindrical stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Echinocactus knuthianus is well adapted to desert environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
