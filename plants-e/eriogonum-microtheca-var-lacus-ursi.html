Eriogonum microtheca var. lacus-ursi

Eriogonum microtheca var. lacus-ursi, commonly known as the Sierra Basin buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces oval, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
