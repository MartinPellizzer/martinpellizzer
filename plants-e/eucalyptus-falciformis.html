Eucalyptus falciformis

Eucalyptus falciformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, bottle-shaped flowers that are typically pale or greenish in color. The plant is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
