Eupatorium cotacachense

Eupatorium cotacachense, commonly known as the Andean thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium cotacachense is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
