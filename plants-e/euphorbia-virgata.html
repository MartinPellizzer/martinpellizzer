Euphorbia virgata

Euphorbia virgata, commonly known as the silver euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with its gray-green, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia virgata is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick stems, aiding in its survival in dry conditions.
