Euphorbia spurca

Euphorbia spurca, commonly known as the spurge or blistering spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the genus Euphorbia. Its latex contains toxic compounds that can cause skin irritation.

Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats.
