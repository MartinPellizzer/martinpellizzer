Erica microdonta

Erica microdonta, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant features dense, compact growth with tiny, needle-like leaves that are typically bright green. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica microdonta is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
