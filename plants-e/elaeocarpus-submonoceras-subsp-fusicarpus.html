Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp. fusicarpus

Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp.

fusicarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruit elaeocarp, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit is a globose or ovoid capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
