Epilobium palustre var. simplex

Epilobium palustre var. simplex, commonly known as bog willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along streambanks. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered with short, stiff hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a hairy surface, and it produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
