Eremophila maculata var. brevifolia

Eremophila maculata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a small shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
