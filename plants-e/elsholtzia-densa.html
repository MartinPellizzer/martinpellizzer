Elsholtzia densa

Elsholtzia densa, commonly known as Chinese lavender, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow leaves, and clusters of small, fragrant purple or pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its aromatic properties make it both ornamental and potentially useful in traditional medicine.
