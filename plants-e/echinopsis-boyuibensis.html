Echinopsis boyuibensis

Echinopsis boyuibensis, commonly known as the Boyuibens Thorned Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globular stems that store water for survival in harsh environments. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert conditions, with reduced leaves and thick, fleshy stems.

It is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
