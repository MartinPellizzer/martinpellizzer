Eupatorium jaegerianum

Eupatorium jaegerianum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
