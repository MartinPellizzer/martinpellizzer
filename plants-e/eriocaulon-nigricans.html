Eriocaulon nigricans

Eriocaulon nigricans, commonly known as black spikegrass, is a species of aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and rivers. Its leaves are narrow and often submerged, while its flowering spikes emerge above the water surface.

Eriocaulon nigricans plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water filtration.
