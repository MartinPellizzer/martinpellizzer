Eugenia glandulosopunctata

Eugenia glandulosopunctata, commonly known as the punctate guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glandular-punctate leaves and fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are typically edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
