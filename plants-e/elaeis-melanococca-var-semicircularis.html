Elaeis melanococca var. semicircularis

Elaeis melanococca var. semicircularis, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruits that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils. Its cultivation is economically significant due to the high yield of palm oil.

However, it is also associated with environmental concerns due to deforestation and habitat loss.
