Eugenia biflora var. virgultosa

Eugenia biflora var. virgultosa, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for landscaping in coastal areas.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
