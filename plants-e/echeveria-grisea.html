Echeveria grisea

Echeveria grisea, commonly known as the Gray Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria grisea is a popular choice for rock gardens and as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
