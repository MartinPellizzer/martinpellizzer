Erica pseudovestita

Erica pseudovestita, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in winter and spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica pseudovestita is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
