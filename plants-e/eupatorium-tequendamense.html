Eupatorium tequendamense

Eupatorium tequendamense, commonly known as the West Indian thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest understories. The plant features tall, hairy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Eupatorium tequendamense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
