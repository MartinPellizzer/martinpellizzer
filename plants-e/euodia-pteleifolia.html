Euodia pteleifolia

Euodia pteleifolia, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces small, yellowish-green fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
