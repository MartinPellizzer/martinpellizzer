Eryngium filiforme var. latifolium

Eryngium filiforme var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, spiny stems and broad, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a spherical cluster, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage and flowers are often used in native gardens for their ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The species is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.
