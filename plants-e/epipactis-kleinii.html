Epipactis kleinii

Epipactis kleinii, commonly known as the Klein's helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, hooded lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Epipactis kleinii is often found in association with other orchids and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance.
