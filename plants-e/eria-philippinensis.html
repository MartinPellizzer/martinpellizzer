Eria philippinensis

Eria philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they attach to trees without parasitizing them.

Eria philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
