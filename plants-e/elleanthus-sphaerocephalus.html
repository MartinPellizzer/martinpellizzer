Elleanthus sphaerocephalus

Elleanthus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe-headed elleanthus, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, spherical inflorescence that gives it its common name. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
