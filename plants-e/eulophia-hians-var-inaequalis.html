Eulophia hians var. inaequalis

Eulophia hians var. inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a rhizomatous growth habit and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, white to pale green flowers. The leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, often with a waxy coating. This species is characterized by its unique floral structure and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
