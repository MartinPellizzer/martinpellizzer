Erica globuliflora

Erica globuliflora, commonly known as the globular heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees known for their ability to thrive in acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica globuliflora is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
