Ericoides cephalotes

Ericoides cephalotes, commonly known as the purple flame orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking purple and pink floral hues, which are particularly vivid in full sun. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the dry season, attracting various pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to minimize water loss in its arid habitat.

Ericoides cephalotes is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
