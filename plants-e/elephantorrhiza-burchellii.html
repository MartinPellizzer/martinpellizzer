Elephantorrhiza burchellii

Elephantorrhiza burchellii, commonly known as the elephant's tail, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in coastal and inland regions. The plant features upright stems with clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
