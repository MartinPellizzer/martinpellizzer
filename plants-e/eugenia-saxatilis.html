Eugenia saxatilis

Eugenia saxatilis, commonly known as the rock myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows in rocky or coastal habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Eugenia saxatilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
