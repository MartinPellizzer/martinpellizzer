Eria australiensis

Eria australiensis, commonly known as the Australian eria, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy soils. It produces pale green to white flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

Eria australiensis is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
