Eugenia cambemba

Eugenia cambemba, commonly known as the African cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Eugenia cambemba thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.
