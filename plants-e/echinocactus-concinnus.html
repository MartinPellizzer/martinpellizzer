Echinocactus concinnus

Echinocactus concinnus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features cylindrical stems covered in dense, pale green spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles in the spring.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
