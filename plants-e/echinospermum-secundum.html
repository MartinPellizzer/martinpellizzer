Echinospermum secundum

Echinospermum secundum, commonly known as the hairy sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called achenes, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
