Eupatorium grossedentatum

Eupatorium grossedentatum, commonly known as the clustered boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
