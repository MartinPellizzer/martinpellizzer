Ephedra distachya subsp. helvetica

Ephedra distachya subsp.

helvetica, commonly known as the Swiss ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the Alps and parts of Central Europe. It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and the presence of specialized stems that store water, a feature common in arid environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers, which are typically unisexual and arranged in clusters. Its stems contain alkaloids, such as ephedrine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
