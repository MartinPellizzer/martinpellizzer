Euphorbia famatamboay subsp. itampolensis

Euphorbia famatamboay subsp.

itampolensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves.

The plant produces unique cyathium inflorescences, which are specialized flower clusters typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
