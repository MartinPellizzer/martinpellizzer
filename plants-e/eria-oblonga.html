Eria oblonga

Eria oblonga, commonly known as the broad-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria oblonga is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
