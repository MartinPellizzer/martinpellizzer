Eriogonum nudum var. regirivum

Eriogonum nudum var. regirivum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow to greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture in harsh, dry conditions.
