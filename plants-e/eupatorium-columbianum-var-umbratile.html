Eupatorium columbianum var. umbratile

Eupatorium columbianum var. umbratile, commonly known as the shade-loving boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to low-light conditions and wet soils.
