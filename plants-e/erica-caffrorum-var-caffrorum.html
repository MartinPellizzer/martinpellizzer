Erica caffrorum var. caffrorum

Erica caffrorum var. caffrorum, commonly known as the Caffrorum heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
