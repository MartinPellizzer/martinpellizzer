Erodium absinthoides f. pilosiuscula

Erodium absinthoides f. pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy dwarf morning glory, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the early morning. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant collections.
