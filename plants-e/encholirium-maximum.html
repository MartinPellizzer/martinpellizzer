Encholirium maximum

Encholirium maximum, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its massive, rosette-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in height, forming a large water-holding structure. The plant belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is adapted to thrive in high-altitude, cloud forest environments. Its flowers, which are typically violet or purple, bloom infrequently and are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.
