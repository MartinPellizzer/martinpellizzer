Erysimum pusillum subsp. atticum

Erysimum pusillum subsp.

atticum, commonly known as the Greek wallflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May.

The plant has glabrous, lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a popular choice in horticulture.
