Eugenia taipingensis

Eugenia taipingensis, commonly known as the Taiping guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruit, which is typically small and yellowish, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia taipingensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.
