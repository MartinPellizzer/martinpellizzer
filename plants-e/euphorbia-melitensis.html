Euphorbia melitensis

Euphorbia melitensis, commonly known as the Maltese spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
