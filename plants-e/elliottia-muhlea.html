Elliottia muhlea

Elliottia muhlea, commonly known as muhlenbergia, is a species of grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. Elliottia muhlea is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
