Eurybia quercifolia

Eurybia quercifolia, commonly known as oakleaf aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that resemble the shape of an oak leaf. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers in the fall, typically in shades of purple or pink. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Eurybia quercifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
