Eriocaulon parvitepalum

Eriocaulon parvitepalum, commonly known as the small-leaf bulbous grass, is a member of the Eriocaulaceae family, characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structures.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically forms dense clumps with short, rhizomatous stems. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets that are often enclosed in bracts.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, shady environments.
