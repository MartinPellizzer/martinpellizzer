Eupatorium amygdalinum var. heterophyllum

Eupatorium amygdalinum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the heterophyllous false indigo, is a variant of the false indigo plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves varying in shape and size depending on their position on the plant. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to six feet. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late summer, and are typically blue or purple in color.

The species is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
