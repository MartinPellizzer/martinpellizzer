Epidendrum leonii

Epidendrum leonii, commonly known as the Leon's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid, characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, colorful inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapting to its humid, shaded habitat.

Epidendrum leonii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
