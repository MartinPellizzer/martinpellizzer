Eriogonum saxicola

Eriogonum saxicola, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to local biodiversity.

Eriogonum saxicola is adapted to harsh conditions, showcasing resilience in arid and high-altitude regions.
