Echinopsis oxygona f. brasiliensis

Echinopsis oxygona f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brasiliensis Cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white, spiny areoles, and it produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months. This plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stem to survive dry conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.

Its ornamental value and relatively easy care make it a favored plant among cactus enthusiasts.
