Erica plukenetii var. brachysepala

Erica plukenetii var. brachysepala, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, needle-like leaves that are densely packed, giving it a dense, mat-like appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
