Eucalyptus olivina

Eucalyptus olivina, commonly known as the olive gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Eucalyptus olivina is a hardy tree that thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.

Its aromatic oils have been used in traditional medicine and for various industrial purposes.
