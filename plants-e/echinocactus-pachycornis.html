Echinocactus pachycornis

Echinocactus pachycornis, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior, which provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
