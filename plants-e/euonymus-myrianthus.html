Euonymus myrianthus

Euonymus myrianthus, commonly known as the coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that appear in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Euonymus myrianthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
