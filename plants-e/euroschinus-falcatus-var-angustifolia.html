Euroschinus falcatus var. angustifolia

Euroschinus falcatus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features make it suitable for ornamental and agroforestry purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
