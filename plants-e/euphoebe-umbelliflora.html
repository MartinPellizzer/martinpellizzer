Euphoebe umbelliflora

Euphoebe umbelliflora, commonly known as the umbrella flower, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of small white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Euphoebe umbelliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
