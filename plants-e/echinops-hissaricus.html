Echinops hissaricus

Echinops hissaricus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops hissaricus is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
