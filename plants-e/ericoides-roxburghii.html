Ericoides roxburghii

Ericoides roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan heath, is a shrub native to the alpine regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers, typically pink or white, bloom in late summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides roxburghii is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and supporting biodiversity.
