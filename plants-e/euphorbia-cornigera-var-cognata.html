Euphorbia cornigera var. cognata

Euphorbia cornigera var. cognata, commonly known as the Cornish euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the British Isles.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with fleshy, green stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces striking yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to coastal and rocky environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and salt resistance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in mild climates.
