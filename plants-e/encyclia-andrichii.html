Encyclia andrichii

Encyclia andrichii, commonly known as the Andrich's spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, thread-like petals. The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Encyclia andrichii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
