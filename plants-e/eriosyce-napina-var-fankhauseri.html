Eriosyce napina var. fankhauseri

Eriosyce napina var. fankhauseri, commonly known as the Fankhauser's rock cactus, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of pale green to white spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
