Eritrichium oppositifolium

Eritrichium oppositifolium, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces delicate blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. It is adapted to cold climates and has a short growing season.

Eritrichium oppositifolium is an important component of alpine ecosystems and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
