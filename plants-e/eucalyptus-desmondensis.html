Eucalyptus desmondensis

Eucalyptus desmondensis, commonly known as the Desmonds River gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the northern regions of Queensland, Australia, where it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. The tree is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pink, attracting various pollinators.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and ecological benefits.
