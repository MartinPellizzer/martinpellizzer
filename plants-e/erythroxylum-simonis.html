Erythroxylum simonis

Erythroxylum simonis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian or Peruvian copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, including Ecuador, Peru, and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various purposes, including medicinal and industrial applications.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in the study of tropical flora and its ecological role in Andean ecosystems.
