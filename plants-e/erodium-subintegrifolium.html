Erodium subintegrifolium

Erodium subintegrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring, typically from May to July. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are finely toothed along the margins. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
