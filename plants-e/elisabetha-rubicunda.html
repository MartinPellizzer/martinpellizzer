Elisabetha rubicunda

Elisabetha rubicunda, commonly known as the crimson elisabetha, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.
