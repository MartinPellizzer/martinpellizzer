Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. stellatum

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

stellatum, commonly known as star flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescences that resemble stars, giving it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in coastal and inland valleys, contributing to local biodiversity and ecological stability.
