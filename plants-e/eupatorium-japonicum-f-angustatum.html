Eupatorium japonicum f. angustatum

Eupatorium japonicum f. angustatum, commonly known as narrowleaf Japanese eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its foliage and flowers attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
