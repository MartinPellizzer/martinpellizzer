Eirmocephala brachiata

Eirmocephala brachiata, commonly known as the branched headless cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that branch out from the base, forming a compact, low-growing plant. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers during the summer months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its thick stems to survive in arid conditions.

Eirmocephala brachiata is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun.
