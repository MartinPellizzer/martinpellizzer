Eremaea fimbriata var. brevifolia

Eremaea fimbriata var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eremaea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow, fimbriate (fringed) leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its structure and foliage make it a valuable component of local ecosystems and a subject of botanical study.
