Euphorbia hirtella var. longifolia

Euphorbia hirtella var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive milky sap that is toxic to humans and animals. The plant features a low-growing, spreading habit with simple, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in cyathia, which are modified leaf-like structures that contain both male and female flowers.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
