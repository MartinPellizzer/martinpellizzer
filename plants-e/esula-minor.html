Esula minor

Esula minor, commonly known as the lesser esula, is a small herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a light green color. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often display shades of white or pale blue.

Esula minor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
