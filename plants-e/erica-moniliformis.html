Erica moniliformis

Erica moniliformis, commonly known as the Heath or Heathling, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica moniliformis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense thickets.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
