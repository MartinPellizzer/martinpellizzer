Eupatorium tweedieanum

Eupatorium tweedieanum, commonly known as the Tweedie's thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The showy white to pale blue flowers appear in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
