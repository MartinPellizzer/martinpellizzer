Ebermeyera

Ebermeyera, commonly known as the "crown of the Andes," is a genus of flowering plants native to the Andean regions of South America.

Ebermeyera species are typically herbaceous, with narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, brightly colored flowers that bloom in spring. They are often found in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

Due to their ornamental value and ecological significance, some species are cultivated in botanical gardens and are of interest to horticulturists.
