Eucalyptus gittinsii

Eucalyptus gittinsii, commonly known as the Gittins' eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the northern regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that ranges from grey to white in color. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and typically have a dull green upper surface with a lighter underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale green or cream in color.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.
