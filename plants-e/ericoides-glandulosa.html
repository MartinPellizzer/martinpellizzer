Ericoides glandulosa

Ericoides glandulosa, commonly known as the sticky leaf ericoid, is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, glandular-dotted leaves.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Ericoides glandulosa is known for its ability to form dense thickets, which can stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important component of certain ecosystems, particularly in the Pacific Northwest.
