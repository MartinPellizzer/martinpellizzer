Echinops khuzistanicus

Echinops khuzistanicus, commonly known as the Khuzistan thistle, is a flowering plant native to western Iran.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, hence its genus name "Echinops.". The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry climates. It produces small blue or purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Echinops khuzistanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
