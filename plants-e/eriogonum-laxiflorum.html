Eriogonum laxiflorum

Eriogonum laxiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eriogonum laxiflorum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in ecological restoration projects.
