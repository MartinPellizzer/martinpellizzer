Ericoides umbelliflorum

Ericoides umbelliflorum, commonly known as the umbrellaflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and bears small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are typically dark green, leathery, and densely arranged along the stems.

Ericoides umbelliflorum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
