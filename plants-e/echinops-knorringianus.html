Echinops knorringianus

Echinops knorringianus, commonly known as the Knorring's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of the distinctive, prickly seed heads. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in steppe and desert habitats.

Echinops knorringianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
