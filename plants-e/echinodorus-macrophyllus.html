Echinodorus macrophyllus

Echinodorus macrophyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a striking aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often with a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant typically produces small, white to pale purple flowers that emerge above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

Echinodorus macrophyllus is popular in aquariums and ponds for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
