Echium tinctorium

Echium tinctorium, commonly known as the common thornapple, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has spiny stems and leaves with a rough, hairy texture. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas due to its adaptability to poor soils.

Echium tinctorium is also notable for its historical use in dye production and as a source of medicinal compounds.
