Euphorbia xylophylloides subsp. crassa

Euphorbia xylophylloides subsp.

crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It has a slow growth rate and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.

Its foliage and structure make it well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
