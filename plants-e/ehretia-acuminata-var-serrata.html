Ehretia acuminata var. serrata

Ehretia acuminata var. serrata, commonly known as the serrate ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are serrated along the edges, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
