Eriogonum thymoides var. pallens

Eriogonum thymoides var. pallens, commonly known as the whiteleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
