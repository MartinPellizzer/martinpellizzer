Elaeocarpus jackii

Elaeocarpus jackii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white or pale pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

Its growth habit and ecological role make it an important species in tropical ecosystems.
