Eugenia tecta

Eugenia tecta, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are frequently used in culinary applications, particularly in Southeast Asian cuisines.

Eugenia tecta thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
