Escallonia hirta

Escallonia hirta, commonly known as the hairy escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to red flowers in the spring and summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal climates.

Escallonia hirta is also valued for its resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
