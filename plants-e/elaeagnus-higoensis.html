Elaeagnus higoensis

Elaeagnus higoensis, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its tolerance to coastal conditions. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Elaeagnus higoensis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
