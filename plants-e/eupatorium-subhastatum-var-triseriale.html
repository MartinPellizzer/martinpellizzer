Eupatorium subhastatum var. triseriale

Eupatorium subhastatum var. triseriale, commonly known as the three-flowered eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
