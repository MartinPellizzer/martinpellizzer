Erodium cicutarium f. pentalobum

Erodium cicutarium f. pentalobum, commonly known as the five-lobed common bedstraw, is a variant of the common bedstraw, characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves.

It is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Geraniaceae family, typically found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
