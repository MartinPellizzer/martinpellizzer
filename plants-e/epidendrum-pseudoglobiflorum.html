Epidendrum pseudoglobiflorum

Epidendrum pseudoglobiflorum, commonly known as the globeflower orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically has thick, fleshy leaves that help it store water in its natural habitat. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and floral display.
