Echinocactus melanocarpus

Echinocactus melanocarpus, commonly known as the black fruit cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by dark purple fruits. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
