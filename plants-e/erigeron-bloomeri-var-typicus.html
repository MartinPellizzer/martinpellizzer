Erigeron bloomeri var. typicus

Erigeron bloomeri var. typicus, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
