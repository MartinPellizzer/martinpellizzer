Erica amphigena

Erica amphigena, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This evergreen shrub typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, leathery seeds.

Erica amphigena is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
