Eulophia diffusiflora

Eulophia diffusiflora, commonly known as the spreading eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, featuring a rhizome that produces multiple shoots. The plant has linear to lanceolate leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green to white flowers with prominent, spurred sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural collections.
