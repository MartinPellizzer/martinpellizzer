Erysimum elbrusense

Erysimum elbrusense, commonly known as the Elbrus mustard, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus Mountains.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Erysimum elbrusense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
