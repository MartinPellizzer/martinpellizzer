Erigeron mesatlanticus

Erigeron mesatlanticus, commonly known as the Atlantic blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and dunes, showcasing its adaptability to coastal environments. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its fibrous roots help stabilize dune ecosystems, contributing to coastal habitat preservation.
