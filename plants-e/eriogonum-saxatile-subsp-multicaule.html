Eriogonum saxatile subsp. multicaule

Eriogonum saxatile subsp.

multicaule, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
