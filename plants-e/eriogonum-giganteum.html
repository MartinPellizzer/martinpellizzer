Eriogonum giganteum

Eriogonum giganteum, commonly known as the giant buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and can reach heights of up to six feet.

Eriogonum giganteum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
