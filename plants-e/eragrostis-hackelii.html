Eragrostis hackelii

Eragrostis hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-gray hue that helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eragrostis hackelii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
