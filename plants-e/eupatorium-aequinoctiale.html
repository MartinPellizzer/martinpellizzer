Eupatorium aequinoctiale

Eupatorium aequinoctiale, commonly known as the equinox plant, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium aequinoctiale is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
