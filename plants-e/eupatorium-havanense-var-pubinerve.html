Eupatorium havanense var. pubinerve

Eupatorium havanense var. pubinerve, commonly known as the Havana feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, hairy veins, giving it its varietal name "pubinerve.". The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive rough texture.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
