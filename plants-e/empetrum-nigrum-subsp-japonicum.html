Empetrum nigrum subsp. japonicum

Empetrum nigrum subsp.

japonicum, commonly known as Japanese crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia. It features small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant has compact, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like structure. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
