Elleanthus caricoides

Elleanthus caricoides, commonly known as the water chestnut, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its stout, edible tubers that grow underground and are often harvested for food. The plant typically thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as marshes and ponds. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Elleanthus caricoides is also known for its ability to form dense stands, which can impact local ecosystems.
