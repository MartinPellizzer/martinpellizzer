Erianthus japonicus

Erianthus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to East Asia.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it well-suited for stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
