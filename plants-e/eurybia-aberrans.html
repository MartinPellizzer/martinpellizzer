Eurybia aberrans

Eurybia aberrans, commonly known as the purple ray flover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to pink ray florets and green, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in full to partial sunlight. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eurybia aberrans plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
