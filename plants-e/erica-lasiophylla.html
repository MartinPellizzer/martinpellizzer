Erica lasiophylla

Erica lasiophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in nutrient-poor, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of white wax, giving it a silvery appearance.

Erica lasiophylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
