Eugenia domingensis

Eugenia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ecological studies.
