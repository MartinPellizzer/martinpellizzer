Eriosema fusiforme

Eriosema fusiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, and it produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds.

Eriosema fusiforme is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
