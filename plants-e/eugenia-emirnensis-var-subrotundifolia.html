Eugenia emirnensis var. subrotundifolia

Eugenia emirnensis var. subrotundifolia, commonly known as the Persian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aromatic foliage and floral display.
