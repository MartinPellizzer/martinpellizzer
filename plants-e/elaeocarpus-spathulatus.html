Elaeocarpus spathulatus

Elaeocarpus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain one or two seeds. It is typically found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
