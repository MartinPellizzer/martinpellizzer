Elephantopus plurisetus

Elephantopus plurisetus, commonly known as the hairy elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched, and hairy stems that resemble an elephant's foot. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Elephantopus plurisetus is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
