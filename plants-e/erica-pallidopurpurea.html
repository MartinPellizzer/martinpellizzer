Erica pallidopurpurea

Erica pallidopurpurea, commonly known as the pale purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale purple to white, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils.

Erica pallidopurpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and rocky environments.
