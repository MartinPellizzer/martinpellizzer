Elaeagnus thwaitesii

Elaeagnus thwaitesii, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, inconspicuous flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

Elaeagnus thwaitesii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
