Entandrophragma angolense var. dolichocarpum

Entandrophragma angolense var. dolichocarpum, commonly known as the long-seeded African shea tree, is a species native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds. Its bark and wood are valued for their medicinal and economic uses, including the production of oils and timber.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems and traditional practices across its native range.
