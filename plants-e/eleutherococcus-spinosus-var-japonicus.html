Eleutherococcus spinosus var. japonicus

Eleutherococcus spinosus var. japonicus, commonly known as Japanese prickly eleuthero, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
