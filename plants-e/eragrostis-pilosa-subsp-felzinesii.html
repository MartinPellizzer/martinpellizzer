Eragrostis pilosa subsp. felzinesii

Eragrostis pilosa subsp.

felzinesii, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It features slender, wiry stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family Poaceae.

The subspecies is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
