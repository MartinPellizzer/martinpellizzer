Eurya hasseltii

Eurya hasseltii, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage that remains throughout the year. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. Eurya hasseltii thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
