Enicostema verticillatum

Enicostema verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation.

Enicostema verticillatum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
