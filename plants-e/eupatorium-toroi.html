Eupatorium toroi

Eupatorium toroi, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Eupatorium toroi is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local pollinators.
