Eriophyllum lanatum var. monoense

Eriophyllum lanatum var. monoense, commonly known as the narrowleaf common sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
