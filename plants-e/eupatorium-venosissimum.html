Eupatorium venosissimum

Eupatorium venosissimum, commonly known as the veiny thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that are often covered in fine, vein-like markings. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough texture and prominent veins.

Eupatorium venosissimum is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows.
