Eremophila pinnatifida

Eremophila pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy pink to red flowers. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting well to dry conditions. It produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals.

Eremophila pinnatifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
