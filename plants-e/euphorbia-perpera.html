Euphorbia perpera

Euphorbia perpera, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, branching stem covered in small, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, which are actually modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Euphorbia perpera is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
