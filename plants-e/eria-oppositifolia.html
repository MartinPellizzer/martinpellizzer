Eria oppositifolia

Eria oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its opposite arrangement of leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
