Eupatorium makinoi var. oppositifolium

Eupatorium makinoi var. oppositifolium, commonly known as Japanese blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has opposite leaves that are usually ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
