Eriogonum baileyi var. porphyreticum

Eriogonum baileyi var. porphyreticum, commonly known as the Porphyritic Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive purple to reddish bracts that surround the small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and cliffs, where it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are often lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
