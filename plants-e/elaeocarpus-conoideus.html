Elaeocarpus conoideus

Elaeocarpus conoideus, commonly known as the cone-shaped elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
