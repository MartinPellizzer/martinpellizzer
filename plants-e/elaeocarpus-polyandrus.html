Elaeocarpus polyandrus

Elaeocarpus polyandrus, commonly known as the multi-stamened elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Elaeocarpus polyandrus is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental potential due to its attractive floral display.
