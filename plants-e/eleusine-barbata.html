Eleusine barbata

Eleusine barbata, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hairy bracts. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eleusine barbata is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
