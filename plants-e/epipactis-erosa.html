Epipactis erosa

Epipactis erosa, commonly known as the slender helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Epipactis erosa is known for its ability to thrive in shaded conditions, making it a common sight in temperate forests.
