Elodes virginica

Elodes virginica, commonly known as the American waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, green flowers that are typically found near the water's surface. Elodes virginica plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

It is often found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
