Ebenus plumosa

Ebenus plumosa, commonly known as the silvery scabious, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, pale blue or purple flowers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Ebenus plumosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators.
