Erica mutabilis

Erica mutabilis, commonly known as the variable heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white, pink, or purple flowers that change color as they age, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica mutabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
