Erica acutifolia

Erica acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. Erica acutifolia is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate drought conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
