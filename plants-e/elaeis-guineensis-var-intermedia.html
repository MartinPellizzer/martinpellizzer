Elaeis guineensis var. intermedia

Elaeis guineensis var. intermedia, commonly known as the African oil palm hybrid, is a variant of the oil palm tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces fruiting bunches that contain oil-rich nuts, making it valuable for agricultural and industrial purposes. It is often used in breeding programs to improve oil yield and disease resistance in oil palm cultivation.

This variety is also adapted to tropical climates with high temperatures and rainfall.
