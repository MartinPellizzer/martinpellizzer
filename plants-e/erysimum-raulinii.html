Erysimum raulinii

Erysimum raulinii, commonly known as the Raulin's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
