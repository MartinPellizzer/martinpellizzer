Eugenia sphenoides

Eugenia sphenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit is a small, edible drupe that is often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia sphenoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
