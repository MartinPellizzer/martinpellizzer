Eupatorium lilacinum

Eupatorium lilacinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium lilacinum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators.
