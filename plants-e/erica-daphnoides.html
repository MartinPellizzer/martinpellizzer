Erica daphnoides

Erica daphnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica daphnoides thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a ground cover.
