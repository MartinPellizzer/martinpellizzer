Eriostemon lamprophyllus

Eriostemon lamprophyllus, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and woodland habitats.

The golden wattle is an important species in Australian flora and is the national floral emblem of Australia.
