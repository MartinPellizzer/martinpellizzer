Eupatorium pallidum

Eupatorium pallidum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium pallidum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
