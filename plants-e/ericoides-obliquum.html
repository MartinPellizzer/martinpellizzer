Ericoides obliquum

Ericoides obliquum, commonly known as the slant-leaved heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and typically pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring.

Ericoides obliquum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests and high-altitude habitats.
