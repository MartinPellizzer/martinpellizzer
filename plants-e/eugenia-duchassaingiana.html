Eugenia duchassaingiana

Eugenia duchassaingiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in jams and beverages. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
