Epidorkis gilpiniae

Epidorkis gilpiniae, commonly known as the Gilpin's epidermis, is a rare and specialized plant species characterized by its unique epidermal structures.

It exhibits a peculiar adaptation where its outer layer, or epidermis, forms a protective, waxy covering that aids in water retention in arid environments. The plant's morphology includes a short, compact stem with reduced leaves, which contributes to its survival in harsh conditions. Its reproductive structures are highly modified, with flowers that are often hidden beneath the epidermal layer.

This plant is of particular interest to botanists due to its unusual developmental features and ecological niche.
