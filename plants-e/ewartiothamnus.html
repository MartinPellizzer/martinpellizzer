Ewartiothamnus

Ewartiothamnus, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that reduce water loss through transpiration, a key adaptation to its desert habitat. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.

Ewartiothamnus is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun and exhibiting resilience to drought conditions.
