Echinopsis caineana

Echinopsis caineana, commonly known as the Caine's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems and a dense covering of white spines. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with thick, water-storing stems.

Due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and botanical gardens.
