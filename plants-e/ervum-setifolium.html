Ervum setifolium

Ervum setifolium, commonly known as the slender vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in racemes, followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
