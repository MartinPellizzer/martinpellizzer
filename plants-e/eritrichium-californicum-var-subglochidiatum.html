Eritrichium californicum var. subglochidiatum

Eritrichium californicum var. subglochidiatum, commonly known as the California mouse-eared cress, is a low-growing annual herb native to the western United States.

It features small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, four-petaled white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The species is characterized by its distinctive seed pods that are inflated and often have a hooked or spiny appearance.
