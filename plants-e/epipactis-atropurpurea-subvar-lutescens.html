Epipactis atropurpurea subvar. lutescens

Epipactis atropurpurea subvar. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a yellowish-green lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces a distinctive, fleshy root system and has a complex pollination mechanism involving specific insects.

This orchid is listed as vulnerable in some regions due to habitat loss and over-collection.
