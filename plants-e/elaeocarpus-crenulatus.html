Elaeocarpus crenulatus

Elaeocarpus crenulatus, commonly known as the serrated elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white flowers. The fruit is a woody drupe, typically containing a single seed, and is often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
