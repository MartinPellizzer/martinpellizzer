Eriogonum cernuum var. purpurascens

Eriogonum cernuum var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple flax or sticky flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a low, spreading growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
