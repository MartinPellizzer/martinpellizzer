Eremophila prostrata

Eremophila prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate eremophila, is a low-growing shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Eremophila prostrata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
