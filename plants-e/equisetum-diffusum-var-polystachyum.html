Equisetum diffusum var. polystachyum

Equisetum diffusum var. polystachyum, commonly known as the variable horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typically found in clusters. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it one of the oldest living plant groups.
