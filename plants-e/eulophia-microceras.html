Eulophia microceras

Eulophia microceras, commonly known as the small-capped eulophia, is a member of the orchid family Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are small and usually greenish or yellowish, with a distinctive, narrow spur.

The leaves are linear and arranged in two ranks, contributing to its overall compact appearance.
