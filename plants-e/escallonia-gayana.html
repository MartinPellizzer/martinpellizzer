Escallonia gayana

Escallonia gayana, commonly known as the New Zealand glorybower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are usually pink or red in color. Escallonia gayana thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaped areas.
