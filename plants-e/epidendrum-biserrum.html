Epidendrum biserrum

Epidendrum biserrum, commonly known as the serrated epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence.

Epidendrum biserrum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
