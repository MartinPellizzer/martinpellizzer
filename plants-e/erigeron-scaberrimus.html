Erigeron scaberrimus

Erigeron scaberrimus, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are covered in a rough, sandpapery texture, which gives it its common name.

Erigeron scaberrimus produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
