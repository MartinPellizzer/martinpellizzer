Eugenia racemoides

Eugenia racemoides, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in shaded, moist habitats.

Its ornamental value and cultural significance make it a notable species in its native range.
