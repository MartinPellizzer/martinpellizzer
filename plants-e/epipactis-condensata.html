Epipactis condensata

Epipactis condensata, commonly known as the dense epipactis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single or few leaves, and it produces a spike of small, greenish-yellow flowers. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, and it relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered in many regions and is protected in several countries.
