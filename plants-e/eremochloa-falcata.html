Eremochloa falcata

Eremochloa falcata, commonly known as the sicklegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in summer, which are arranged in spikelets. Eremochloa falcata is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

It is considered a drought-tolerant species, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid regions.
