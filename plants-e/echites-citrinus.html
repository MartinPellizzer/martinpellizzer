Echites citrinus

Echites citrinus, commonly known as the yellow corymbia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale green to yellowish bark. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Echites citrinus is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
