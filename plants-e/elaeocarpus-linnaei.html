Elaeocarpus linnaei

Elaeocarpus linnaei, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and attract various bird species.

It thrives in humid tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.
