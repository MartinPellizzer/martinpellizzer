Enantia atrocyanescens

Enantia atrocyanescens, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a striking perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are opposite on the stems, and its root system is fibrous and adapted to arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and has been cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
