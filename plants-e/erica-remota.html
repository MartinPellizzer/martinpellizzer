Erica remota

Erica remota, commonly known as the remote heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Erica remota produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor environments.
