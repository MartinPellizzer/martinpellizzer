Eria griffithii

Eria griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a slender, upright stem that bears a cluster of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically arranged in a spike. Eria griffithii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
