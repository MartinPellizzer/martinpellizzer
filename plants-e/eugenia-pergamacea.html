Eugenia pergamacea

Eugenia pergamacea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pergamaceae, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
