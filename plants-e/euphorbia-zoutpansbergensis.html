Euphorbia zoutpansbergensis

Euphorbia zoutpansbergensis, commonly known as the Zoutpansberg spurge, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers during the warmer months. It has a spiny appearance with milky sap that can cause skin irritation.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in arid conditions make it a notable species in its native habitat.
