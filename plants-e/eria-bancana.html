Eria bancana

Eria bancana, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Eria bancana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
