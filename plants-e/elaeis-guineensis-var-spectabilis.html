Elaeis guineensis var. spectabilis

Elaeis guineensis var. spectabilis, commonly known as the ornamental oil palm, is a variant of the oil palm characterized by its striking floral display.

It is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that are often used in horticultural displays rather than for oil production. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a tall, slender trunk.

While it shares genetic similarities with the common oil palm, it is primarily cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than commercial oil yield.
