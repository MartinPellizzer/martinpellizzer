Euterpe oleracea

Euterpe oleracea, commonly known as the açaí palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly the Amazon basin.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple fruits that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. These fruits are clustered in panicles and are typically harvested when fully mature.

The açaí palm plays an important ecological role in its native habitat and is also economically significant for local communities.
