Endolepis

Endolepis, commonly known as the African star grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to various regions in Africa and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often blue-green colored blades. The plants typically have a basal rosette structure with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy. Endolepis species are often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and along riverbanks.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in traditional medicine.
