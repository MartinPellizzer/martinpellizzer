Echium verecundum

Echium verecundum, commonly known as the blushweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
