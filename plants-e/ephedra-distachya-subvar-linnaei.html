Ephedra distachya subvar. linnaei

Ephedra distachya subvar. linnaei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its green to yellowish stems, which are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain the characteristic alkaloids, including ephedrine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

This subspecies is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
