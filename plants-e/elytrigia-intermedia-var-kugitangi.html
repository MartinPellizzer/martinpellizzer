Elytrigia intermedia var. kugitangi

Elytrigia intermedia var. kugitangi, commonly known as the Kugitangi grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in native forests and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
