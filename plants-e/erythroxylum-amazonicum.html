Erythroxylum amazonicum

Erythroxylum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian copal tree, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and montane regions of South America.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal properties, it plays a significant role in the biodiversity of the Amazon.
