Erica onusta

Erica onusta, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica onusta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
