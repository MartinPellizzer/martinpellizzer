Eriogonum crocatum

Eriogonum crocatum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its soft, gray-green foliage and showy, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eriogonum crocatum is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
