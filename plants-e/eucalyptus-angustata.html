Eucalyptus angustata

Eucalyptus angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically small and white or pale blue, arranged in clusters along the branches. It prefers sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is known for its aromatic oils, which have various uses in traditional and modern applications.
