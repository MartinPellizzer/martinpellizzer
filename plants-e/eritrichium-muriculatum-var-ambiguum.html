Eritrichium muriculatum var. ambiguum

Eritrichium muriculatum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its stems are usually unbranched and can grow up to 15 centimeters in height.

This species is often found in open, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.
