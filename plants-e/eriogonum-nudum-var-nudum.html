Eriogonum nudum var. nudum

Eriogonum nudum var. nudum, commonly known as the nakedleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its simple, often hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
