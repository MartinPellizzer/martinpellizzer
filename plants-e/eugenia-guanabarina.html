Eugenia guanabarina

Eugenia guanabarina, commonly known as the Brazilian guanabara myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical climates.

The fruit, which is edible, develops into a small, fleshy berry and is often used in local cuisines.
