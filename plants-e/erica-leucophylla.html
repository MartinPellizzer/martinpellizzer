Erica leucophylla

Erica leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica leucophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
