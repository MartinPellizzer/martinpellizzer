Erica sicifolia

Erica sicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Erica sicifolia typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it demonstrates remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.
