Epidendrum tetracuniculatum

Epidendrum tetracuniculatum, commonly known as the four-ribbed epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Epidendrum tetracuniculatum is relatively easy to care for and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
