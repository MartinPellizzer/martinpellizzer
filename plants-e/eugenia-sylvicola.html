Eugenia sylvicola

Eugenia sylvicola, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain up to 20 edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. These seeds are rich in protein, healthy fats, and essential nutrients.

Eugenia sylvicola plays a vital ecological role in the rainforest, providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
