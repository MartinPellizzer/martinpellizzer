Elaeocarpus corymbifer

Elaeocarpus corymbifer, commonly known as the clustered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, spherical inflorescences that bear small, white flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is often surrounded by a fleshy, edible aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use.
