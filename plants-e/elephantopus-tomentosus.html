Elephantopus tomentosus

Elephantopus tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, hairy, bulbous base that resembles an elephant's foot, which stores water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.
