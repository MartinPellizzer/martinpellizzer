Elaeocarpus lagunensis var. lagunensis

Elaeocarpus lagunensis var. lagunensis, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in local communities.
