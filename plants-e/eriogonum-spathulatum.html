Eriogonum spathulatum

Eriogonum spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

Eriogonum spathulatum is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
