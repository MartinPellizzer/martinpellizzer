Eupatorium rufum

Eupatorium rufum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are rough and hairy, giving it its common name, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eupatorium rufum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
