Echeveria secunda var. glauca

Echeveria secunda var. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating. The leaves are arranged in a compact, symmetrical pattern, giving the plant a neat and ornamental appearance. It produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its foliage.

This variety is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.
