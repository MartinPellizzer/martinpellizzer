Eugenia matanzensis

Eugenia matanzensis, commonly known as the Matanzas guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The fruit is valued for its aromatic flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
