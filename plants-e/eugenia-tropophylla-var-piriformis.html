Eugenia tropophylla var. piriformis

Eugenia tropophylla var. piriformis, commonly known as the broad-leaved satinash, is a tropical rainforest tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and estuarine regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
