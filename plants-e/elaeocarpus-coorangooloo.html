Elaeocarpus coorangooloo

Elaeocarpus coorangooloo, commonly known as the Coorangooloo tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree's fruit is a woody, spherical capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

The plant has cultural significance to the Yuku-Baja people and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
