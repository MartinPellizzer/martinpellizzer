Eria merapiensis

Eria merapiensis, commonly known as the Merapi orchid, is a species native to the Indonesian island of Java.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near the base of trees or on rocky slopes. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and yellow. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique inflorescence structure.

Due to habitat loss, Eria merapiensis is considered a rare and endangered species.
