Eleocharis argentina

Eleocharis argentina, commonly known as the Argentine spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. This plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and the edges of ponds. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Eleocharis argentina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
