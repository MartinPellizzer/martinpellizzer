Eritrichium longipes

Eritrichium longipes, commonly known as the longipes biscuitroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with hairy, slender stems and small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple or pink. The plant's leaves are alternate, narrow, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Eritrichium longipes plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
