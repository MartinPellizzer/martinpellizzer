Elleanthus tovarensis

Elleanthus tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the island of Tova in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is known for its long-lasting blooms, which remain intact for extended periods, making it popular in floral arrangements. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Elleanthus tovarensis thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
