Erythrina pisamo

Erythrina pisamo, commonly known as the red mangrove or red flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, and its bark is often reddish in color. It is a fast-growing tree that thrives in coastal areas and can tolerate saline conditions.

Erythrina pisamo is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
