Euphorbia canariensis var. spiralis

Euphorbia canariensis var. spiralis, commonly known as the spiral euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of stems, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its striking form, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and rockeries.
