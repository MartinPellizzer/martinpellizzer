Eremochloa horneri

Eremochloa horneri, commonly known as the silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, exhibiting drought tolerance once established. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Eremochloa horneri is also valued for its ability to prevent soil erosion and its low maintenance requirements.
