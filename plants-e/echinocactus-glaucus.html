Echinocactus glaucus

Echinocactus glaucus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Echinocactus glaucus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
