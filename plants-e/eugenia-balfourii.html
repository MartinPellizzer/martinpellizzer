Eugenia balfourii

Eugenia balfourii, commonly known as the Balfour's eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and typically turn red when mature, containing several seeds.

Eugenia balfourii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
