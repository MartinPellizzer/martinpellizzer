Euryangium sumbul

Euryangium sumbul, commonly known as the sacred lotus of Indonesia, is a water-lily species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water, providing habitat for aquatic life. Its roots grow in muddy substrates, and it thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

The plant is often associated with cultural and religious symbolism in local traditions.
