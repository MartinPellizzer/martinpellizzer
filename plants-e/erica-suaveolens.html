Erica suaveolens

Erica suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Erica suaveolens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Its aromatic foliage and delicate blooms make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
