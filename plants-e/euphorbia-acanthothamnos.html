Euphorbia acanthothamnos

Euphorbia acanthothamnos, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and branched stems covered in a thick layer of white, waxy substance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. Its leaves are typically small, oval, and often covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia acanthothamnos is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant for xeriscaping.
