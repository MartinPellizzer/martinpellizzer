Elaeis nigrescens

Elaeis nigrescens, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a species native to western and central Africa.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces fruit-bearing clusters called inflorescences, which contain the valuable palm oil. It thrives in tropical climates with high rainfall and requires well-drained, fertile soils.

Due to its economic importance, it is widely cultivated for its oil, which is used in food, cosmetics, and industrial products.
