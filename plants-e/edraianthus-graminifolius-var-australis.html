Edraianthus graminifolius var. australis

Edraianthus graminifolius var. australis, commonly known as the coastal flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils. It is well adapted to salty air and can tolerate light frost.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
