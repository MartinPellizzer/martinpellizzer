Equisetum telmateia var. polystachyum

Equisetum telmateia var. polystachyum, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems covered in small, scale-like structures, and it lacks true leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stem, which are distinctive in shape and arrangement. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich environments such as wetlands and stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with unique botanical features.
