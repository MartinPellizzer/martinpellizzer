Eupatorium alternifolium var. deltoideum

Eupatorium alternifolium var. deltoideum, commonly known as the deltoid eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, deltoid-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
