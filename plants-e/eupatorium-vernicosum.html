Eupatorium vernicosum

Eupatorium vernicosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium vernicosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
