Eugenia maestrensis

Eugenia maestrensis, commonly known as the Colombian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
