Enydra paludosa

Enydra paludosa, commonly known as swampweed, is a perennial herb native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in marshy areas, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. The plant has slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are adapted for wind pollination.

Enydra paludosa plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
