Eugenia gladiata

Eugenia gladiata, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia gladiata thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
