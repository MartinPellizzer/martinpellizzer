Epilobium cinerascens

Epilobium cinerascens, commonly known as silverweed, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a soft, fuzzy texture.

The flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in spikes, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
