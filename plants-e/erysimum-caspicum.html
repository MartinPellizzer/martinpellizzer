Erysimum caspicum

Erysimum caspicum, commonly known as the Caspian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Erysimum caspicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
