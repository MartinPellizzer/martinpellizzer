Erica muscicapa

Erica muscicapa, commonly known as the sweet heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica muscicapa is known for its aromatic leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
