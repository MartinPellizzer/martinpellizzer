Elytrigia intermedia subsp. afghanica

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

afghanica, commonly known as Afghan intermediate grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in dry, rocky, or semi-arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually glabrous. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lipped florets.

It is valued for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
