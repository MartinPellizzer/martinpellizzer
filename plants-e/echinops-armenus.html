Echinops armenus

Echinops armenus, commonly known as the Armenian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountains of Armenia and adjacent regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive leaf structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are adapted to arid environments, and it produces spherical, purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. Echinops armenus is often found in rocky soils and high-altitude areas, where it thrives in full sun.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
