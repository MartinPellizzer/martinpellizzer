Erythroxylum macrocnemium

Erythroxylum macrocnemium, commonly known as the large-leafed copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It produces a milky latex that has been traditionally used in folk medicine.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
