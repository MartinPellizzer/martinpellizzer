Erica leptopus

Erica leptopus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreens adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils.

Erica leptopus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
