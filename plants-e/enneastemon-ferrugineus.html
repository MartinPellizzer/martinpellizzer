Enneastemon ferrugineus

Enneastemon ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty leaved aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a reddish or rust-colored hue, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports a variety of pollinators, making it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
