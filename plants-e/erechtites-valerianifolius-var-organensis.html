Erechtites valerianifolius var. organensis

Erechtites valerianifolius var. organensis, commonly known as the organensis ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, sturdy stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces dense clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
