Eupatorium luxii

Eupatorium luxii, commonly known as the Silverleaf Bluet, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a striking appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Eupatorium luxii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
