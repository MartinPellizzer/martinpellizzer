Excoecaria poilanei

Excoecaria poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a striking evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with a glossy green surface and a distinctive reddish-brown underside. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves contain toxic compounds, making it important to handle with care.
