Echeveria imbricatissima

Echeveria imbricatissima, commonly known as the Mexican snowflake, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a compact rosette of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy pink to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
