Euodia anisodora

Euodia anisodora, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or sweet woodruff, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

Euodia anisodora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
