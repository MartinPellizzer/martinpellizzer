Eugenia cerasiflora

Eugenia cerasiflora, commonly known as the cherry apple tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant features simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, appearing in clusters during the spring.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that resembles a cherry, hence its common name.
