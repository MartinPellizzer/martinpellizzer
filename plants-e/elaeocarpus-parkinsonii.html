Elaeocarpus parkinsonii

Elaeocarpus parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often reddish or yellowish when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
