Eupatorium prionobium

Eupatorium prionobium, commonly known as the narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, clustered white to pale purple flowers.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
