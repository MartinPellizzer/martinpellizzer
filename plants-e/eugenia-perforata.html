Eugenia perforata

Eugenia perforata, commonly known as the perforate eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with distinctive perforations along the margins. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red color, often containing several seeds.
