Epipactis reflexa

Epipactis reflexa, commonly known as the reflexed helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive, reflexed lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated primarily by bees.

Epipactis reflexa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
