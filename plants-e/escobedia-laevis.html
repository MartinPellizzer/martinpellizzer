Escobedia laevis

Escobedia laevis, commonly known as the smooth escobedia, is a member of the family Escobidiaceae and is characterized by its unique, cushion-like growth form.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a dense, woolly covering of white or grayish hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its harsh habitat. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Escobedia laevis is adapted to survive in extreme conditions, making it a resilient and specialized species in its native range.
