Epidendrum mimeticum

Epidendrum mimeticum, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often mimic the appearance of other plants or insects. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that includes a prominent lip, which plays a role in pollination.

Epidendrum mimeticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
