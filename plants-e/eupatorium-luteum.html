Eupatorium luteum

Eupatorium luteum, commonly known as golden boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, often minty scent. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional herbal medicine.

Eupatorium luteum thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.
