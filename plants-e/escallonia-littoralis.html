Escallonia littoralis

Escallonia littoralis, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a textured appearance.

Escallonia littoralis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh coastal environments.
