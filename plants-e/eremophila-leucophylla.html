Eremophila leucophylla

Eremophila leucophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is part of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila leucophylla is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and visual appeal.
