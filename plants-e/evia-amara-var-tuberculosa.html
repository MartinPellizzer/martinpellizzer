Evia amara var. tuberculosa

Evia amara var. tuberculosa, commonly known as the bitterleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough, serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

The leaves are known for their bitter taste and have been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
