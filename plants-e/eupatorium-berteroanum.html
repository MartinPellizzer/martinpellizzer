Eupatorium berteroanum

Eupatorium berteroanum, commonly known as the Bertero's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems with opposite leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white to pale blue, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium berteroanum is often found in moist, open habitats such as forests and disturbed areas.
