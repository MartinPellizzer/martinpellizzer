Echeveria gloriosa

Echeveria gloriosa, commonly known as the gloriosa echeveria, is a species of flowering plant in the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and orange during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish.

Echeveria gloriosa is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
