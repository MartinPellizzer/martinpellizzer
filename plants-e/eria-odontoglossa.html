Eria odontoglossa

Eria odontoglossa, commonly known as the toothed lip orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tooth-like structures on the lip of its flowers, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

Eria odontoglossa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
