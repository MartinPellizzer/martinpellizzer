Embelia subcordata

Embelia subcordata, commonly known as the Indian embelia or false black pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
