Euclea racemosa subsp. sinuata

Euclea racemosa subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as the mountain euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are elliptical, with a distinctive sinuous margin, giving the subspecies its name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
