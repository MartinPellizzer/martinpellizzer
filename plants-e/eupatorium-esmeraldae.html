Eupatorium esmeraldae

Eupatorium esmeraldae, commonly known as Emerald Beauty, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in native gardens for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
