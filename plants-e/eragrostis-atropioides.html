Eragrostis atropioides

Eragrostis atropioides, commonly known as blackhead grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Eragrostis atropioides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
