Echinocactus wislizeni var. albus

Echinocactus wislizeni var. albus, commonly known as the white hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on erect, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
