Eugenia cribrata

Eugenia cribrata, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are often red when mature. Eugenia cribrata is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

It is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in coastal areas.
