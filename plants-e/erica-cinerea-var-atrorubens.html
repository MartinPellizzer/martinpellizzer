Erica cinerea var. atrorubens

Erica cinerea var. atrorubens, commonly known as the dark red heath, is a variant of the common heath plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
