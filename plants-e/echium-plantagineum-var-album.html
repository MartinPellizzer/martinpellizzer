Echium plantagineum var. album

Echium plantagineum var. album, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
