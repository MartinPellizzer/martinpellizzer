Ericoides dianthifolium

Ericoides dianthifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its leaves are leathery and covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, which helps reduce water loss in its natural habitat.

Ericoides dianthifolium is often found in woodlands and rocky areas, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
