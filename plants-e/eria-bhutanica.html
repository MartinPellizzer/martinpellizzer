Eria bhutanica

Eria bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region, particularly Bhutan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, small, and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Eria bhutanica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Bhutan.
