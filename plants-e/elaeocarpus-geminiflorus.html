Elaeocarpus geminiflorus

Elaeocarpus geminiflorus, commonly known as the twin-flowered elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive two-flowered inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
