Epidendrum megalopentadactylum

Epidendrum megalopentadactylum, commonly known as the giant five-finger orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces striking clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with prominent, showy sepals and petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining potting mix.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
