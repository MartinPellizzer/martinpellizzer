Emerus exasperatus

Emerus exasperatus, commonly known as the spiny spurgrass, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive spiny structures along its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Emerus exasperatus is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and can tolerate high temperatures and limited rainfall.
