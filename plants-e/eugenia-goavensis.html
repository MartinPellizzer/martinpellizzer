Eugenia goavensis

Eugenia goavensis, commonly known as the Madagascar guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, orange-red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, while its bark is smooth and grayish.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
