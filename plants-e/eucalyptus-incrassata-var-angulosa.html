Eucalyptus incrassata var. angulosa

Eucalyptus incrassata var. angulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, angular leaves and typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree, often with a gnarled appearance due to its growth habits. It produces small, spherical flower buds that open to reveal white or pale pink flowers.

The species is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.
