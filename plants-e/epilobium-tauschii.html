Epilobium tauschii

Epilobium tauschii, commonly known as Tausch's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and clustered in spikes, blooming in the summer months.

Epilobium tauschii is part of the Onagraceae family, which includes other willow herb species.
