Eupatorium spectabile

Eupatorium spectabile, commonly known as showy eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafed stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, lavender to pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage is typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a bluish-green hue.

Eupatorium spectabile is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
