Eupatorium auriculatum

Eupatorium auriculatum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Eupatorium auriculatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
