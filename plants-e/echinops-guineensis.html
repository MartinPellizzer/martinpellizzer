Echinops guineensis

Echinops guineensis, commonly known as the African thistle, is a flowering plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown, which are a key feature of the echinops genus. The plant typically grows in dry, arid environments and is well adapted to withstand harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the summer months.

Echinops guineensis is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
