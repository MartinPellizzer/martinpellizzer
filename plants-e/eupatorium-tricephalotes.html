Eupatorium tricephalotes

Eupatorium tricephalotes, commonly known as the three-headed eupatorium, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence that appears to have three heads, giving it its common name. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense, spherical heads, and are typically white or pale blue in color.

Eupatorium tricephalotes is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as coastal areas and disturbed soils.
