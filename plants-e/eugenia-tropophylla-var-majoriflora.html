Eugenia tropophylla var. majoriflora

Eugenia tropophylla var. majoriflora, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that can reach up to 20 cm in length. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers, which are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rainforest areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
