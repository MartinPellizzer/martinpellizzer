Epilobium pyrricholophum var. anuoleucholophum

Epilobium pyrricholophum var. anuoleucholophum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as those found in wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and are pollinated by insects.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
