Elytranthe lonicerioides

Elytranthe lonicerioides, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are typically oval to elliptic in shape, with a hairy surface and toothed margins.

Elytranthe lonicerioides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.
