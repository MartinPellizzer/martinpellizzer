Eriogonum effusum var. durum

Eriogonum effusum var. durum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy clusters of yellow to orange flowers. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access underground water.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its habitat.
