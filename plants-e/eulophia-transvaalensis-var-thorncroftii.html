Eulophia transvaalensis var. thorncroftii

Eulophia transvaalensis var. thorncroftii, commonly known as the Transvaal orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to South Africa.

It features erect, hairy stems that bear broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
