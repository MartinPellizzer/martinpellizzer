Eupatorium chapalense var. salicifolium

Eupatorium chapalense var. salicifolium, commonly known as the willow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
