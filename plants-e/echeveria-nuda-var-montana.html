Echeveria nuda var. montana

Echeveria nuda var. montana, commonly known as the Mountain Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a compact rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy texture. The leaves are arranged in a tight spiral, giving the plant a distinctive, almost geometric appearance. It produces tall, branching inflorescences with clusters of small, star-shaped flowers, often in pink or purple hues.

This variety is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
