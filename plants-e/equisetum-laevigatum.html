Equisetum laevigatum

Equisetum laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are typically found in moist, shaded environments. Equisetum laevigatum is known for its ability to thrive in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of silica has contributed to its ecological and cultural significance.
