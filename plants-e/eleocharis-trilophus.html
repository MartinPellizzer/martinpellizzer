Eleocharis trilophus

Eleocharis trilophus, commonly known as the three-ridged spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is characterized by the presence of three distinct ridges on its stem.

Eleocharis trilophus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
