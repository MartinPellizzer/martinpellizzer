Elaeagia maguirei var. pubens

Elaeagia maguirei var. pubens, commonly known as the hairy broadleaf elaeagnus, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, pubescent hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a rough texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
