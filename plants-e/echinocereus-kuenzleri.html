Echinocereus kuenzleri

Echinocereus kuenzleri, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that bear clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish. It is a popular ornamental plant, especially during the holiday season due to its flowering period around Christmas.

Echinocereus kuenzleri is also known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.
