Euthamia minor

Euthamia minor, commonly known as white coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features narrow, grass-like leaves. Its yellow disk flowers are surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Euthamia minor thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.
