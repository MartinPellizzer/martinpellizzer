Eragrostis hasskarlii

Eragrostis hasskarlii, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis hasskarlii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
