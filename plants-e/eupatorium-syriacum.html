Eupatorium syriacum

Eupatorium syriacum, commonly known as Syrian boneset, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium syriacum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
