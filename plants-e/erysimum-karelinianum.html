Erysimum karelinianum

Erysimum karelinianum, commonly known as the Karelin's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually pinnately divided, and the flowers can range in color from yellow to orange.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained conditions.
