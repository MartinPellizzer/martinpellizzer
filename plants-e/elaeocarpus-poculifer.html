Elaeocarpus poculifer

Elaeocarpus poculifer, commonly known as the helmetwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage and distinctive, helmet-shaped fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive appearance. Its flowers are small and white, often clustered in dense inflorescences, while the fruit is a hard, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
