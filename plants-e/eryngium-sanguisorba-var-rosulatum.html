Eryngium sanguisorba var. rosulatum

Eryngium sanguisorba var. rosulatum, commonly known as the Rosulate Thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its rosette of blue-gray, hairy leaves and its striking spiky, purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in woodland or marshy areas. Its foliage and flowers are often used in native plant gardens for their ornamental value.

It is also valued for its ability to support local pollinators and its role in ecological restoration projects.
