Epilobium hirsutum var. villosissimum

Epilobium hirsutum var. villosissimum, commonly known as the woolly willow or hairy willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
