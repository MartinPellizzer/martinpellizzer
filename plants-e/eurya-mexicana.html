Eurya mexicana

Eurya mexicana, commonly known as Mexican evergreen holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Eurya mexicana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
