Eryngium cylindricum

Eryngium cylindricum, commonly known as the cylindrical thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking, spiky blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant has a compact, upright growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in a cylindrical shape, giving it its common name.

Eryngium cylindricum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
