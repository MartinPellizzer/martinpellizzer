Erica perspicuoides

Erica perspicuoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica perspicuoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
