Escallonia megapotamica var. spiraeifolia

Escallonia megapotamica var. spiraeifolia, commonly known as the Chilean spiraea, is a compact evergreen shrub native to South America.

It features dense, dark green foliage that provides a lush backdrop for its showy clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and year-round structure.
