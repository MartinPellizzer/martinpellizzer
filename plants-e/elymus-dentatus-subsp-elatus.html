Elymus dentatus subsp. elatus

Elymus dentatus subsp.

elatus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, greenish to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along streambanks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
