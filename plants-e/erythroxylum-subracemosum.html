Erythroxylum subracemosum

Erythroxylum subracemosum, commonly known as the redstem copaiba, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, fleshy berries containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in humid, tropical environments and is adapted to grow in shaded understory conditions within forests.
