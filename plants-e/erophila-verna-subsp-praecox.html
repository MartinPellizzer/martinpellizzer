Erophila verna subsp. praecox

Erophila verna subsp.

praecox, commonly known as early yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks.

The plant produces small, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom early in the spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The subspecies is distinguished by its earlier flowering time compared to other members of the species.
