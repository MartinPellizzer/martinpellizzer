Eleutherococcus higoensis

Eleutherococcus higoensis, commonly known as Japanese aralia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically divided into multiple pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
