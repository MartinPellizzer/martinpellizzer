Erigeron bloomeri var. bloomeri

Erigeron bloomeri var. bloomeri, commonly known as the bloomer bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-gray, often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
