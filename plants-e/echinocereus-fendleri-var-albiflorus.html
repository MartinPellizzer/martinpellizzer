Echinocereus fendleri var. albiflorus

Echinocereus fendleri var. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and white, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its adaptations include spines for protection and a shallow root system for efficient water absorption.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
