Eulophia filifolia

Eulophia filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaved eulophia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of multiple small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme.

Eulophia filifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
