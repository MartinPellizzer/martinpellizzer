Echites quinquangularis

Echites quinquangularis, commonly known as the five-angle grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense spikes at the tips of the stems.

It is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
