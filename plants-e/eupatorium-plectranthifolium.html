Eupatorium plectranthifolium

Eupatorium plectranthifolium, commonly known as the Florida boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used by Native American tribes for its purported health benefits.
