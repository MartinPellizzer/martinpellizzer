Eurycaulis derryi

Eurycaulis derryi, commonly known as the Derry's bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant found in freshwater habitats.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap small aquatic organisms, aiding in its nutrient uptake. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. It thrives in still or slow-moving waters with abundant sunlight.

Its specialized adaptations make it an important part of aquatic ecosystems.
