Elaeagnus paraguayensis

Elaeagnus paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay silverleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often borne in clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The fruit is a small, edible drupe that is consumed by various wildlife species.
