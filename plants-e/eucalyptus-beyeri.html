Eucalyptus beyeri

Eucalyptus beyeri, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrubs with smooth, flaky bark. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside, and the flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and exhibiting drought tolerance.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
