Erigeron uniflorus subsp. glabratus

Erigeron uniflorus subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually smooth and lance-shaped, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.
