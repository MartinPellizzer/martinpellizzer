Eritrichium hispidum

Eritrichium hispidum, commonly known as the hairy eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name and help in reducing water loss in arid environments. It thrives in rocky or well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Eritrichium hispidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
