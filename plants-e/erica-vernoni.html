Erica vernoni

Erica vernoni, commonly known as the summer heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. Erica vernoni thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
