Epidendrum guillermoi

Epidendrum guillermoi, commonly known as the Guillermoi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often adorned with numerous lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive yellow throat, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
