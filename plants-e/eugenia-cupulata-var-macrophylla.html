Eugenia cupulata var. macrophylla

Eugenia cupulata var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
