Eriogonum deflexum subsp. watsonii

Eriogonum deflexum subsp.

watsonii, commonly known as Watson's buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features basal leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive crinkled appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense spikes, and are usually pale green to reddish in color.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
