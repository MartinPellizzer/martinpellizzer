Eremanthus curumbensis

Eremanthus curumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant is known for its hard, dark wood, which is highly valued in the production of musical instruments. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in humid forests.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species.
