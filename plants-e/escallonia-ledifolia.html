Escallonia ledifolia

Escallonia ledifolia, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. Escallonia ledifolia is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil types, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It also attracts birds and beneficial insects due to its nectar-rich flowers and edible berries.
