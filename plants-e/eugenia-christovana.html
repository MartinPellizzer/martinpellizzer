Eugenia christovana

Eugenia christovana, commonly known as the Java apple or Java cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, hence its common name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
