Elaphoglossum burchellii

Elaphoglossum burchellii, commonly known as the Burchell's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, pinnate fronds that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in a distinctive pattern along the margins of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum burchellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
