Echinocereus dasyacanthus

Echinocereus dasyacanthus, commonly known as the hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive spiny appearance, featuring dense, white to pale blue spines that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique aesthetic, it is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
