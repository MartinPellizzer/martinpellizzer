Esula pseudocyparissias

Esula pseudocyparissias, commonly known as the false cypress.

It is a member of the Cupressaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and coniferous appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with scale-like leaves arranged in dense clusters. It is native to parts of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it suitable for rock gardens and as a foundation plant in landscaping.
