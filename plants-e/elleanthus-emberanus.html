Elleanthus emberanus

Elleanthus emberanus, commonly known as the Ember Bush, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Elleanthus emberanus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in its natural habitat.
