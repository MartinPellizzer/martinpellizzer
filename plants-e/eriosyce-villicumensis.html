Eriosyce villicumensis

Eriosyce villicumensis, commonly known as the Villicum cholla, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture from the sandy soils of its arid habitat.

Eriosyce villicumensis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
