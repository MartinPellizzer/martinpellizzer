Echinopsis Arachnacantha Var. Sulphurea

Golden hedgehog cactus, scientifically known as Echinopsis arachnacantha var. sulphurea, is a striking member of the cactus family native to the arid regions of South America. Its name comes from its spiny, hedgehog-like appearance and the golden hue of its stems, which shimmer under the sun. This plant is prized for its unique beauty and ability to thrive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water. It produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom at night, adding a magical touch to its already eye-catching form. Due to its resilience and ornamental value, it has become a popular choice for collectors and gardeners in dry climates.
