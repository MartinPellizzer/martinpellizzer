Eurybia floribunda

Eurybia floribunda, commonly known as the showy goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eurybia floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
