Eragrostis tridentata

Eragrostis tridentata, commonly known as three-awn grass, is a perennial grass species native to the warmer regions of North America.

It features slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear three awns on each lemma, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and prairies. It is drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its fine, grass-like foliage and unique floral structure make it a distinctive component of native plant communities.
