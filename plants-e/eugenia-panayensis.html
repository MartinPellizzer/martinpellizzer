Eugenia panayensis

Eugenia panayensis, commonly known as the Panay guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

The species is of interest for its potential horticultural and ecological value in tropical regions.
