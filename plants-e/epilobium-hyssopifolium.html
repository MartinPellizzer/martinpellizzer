Epilobium hyssopifolium

Epilobium hyssopifolium, commonly known as woolly willow or blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along riverbanks. The plant features upright stems covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink to purple in color.

Epilobium hyssopifolium is known for its adaptability to wet environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
