Erica crossota

Erica crossota, commonly known as the Cross's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica crossota typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
