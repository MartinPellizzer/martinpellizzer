It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Elaphoglossum gomezianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
