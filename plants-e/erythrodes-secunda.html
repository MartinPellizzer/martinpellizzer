Erythrodes secunda

Erythrodes secunda, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within forest understories. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive spurred shape. Its botanical classification places it within the Orchidaceae family, highlighting its ornamental value.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
