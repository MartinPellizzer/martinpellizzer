Eryngium moritzii

Eryngium moritzii, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and striking purple to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Eryngium moritzii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or open habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native environment.
