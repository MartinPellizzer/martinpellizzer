Epilobium tetragonum var. ampelusii

Epilobium tetragonum var. ampelusii, commonly known as the creeping willow or yellow willow, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its prostrate or semi-upright growth habit and is often found in moist, calcareous soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are typically yellow or reddish, arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This variant is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
