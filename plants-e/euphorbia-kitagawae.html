Euphorbia kitagawae

Euphorbia kitagawae, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry conditions.

Euphorbia kitagawae is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
