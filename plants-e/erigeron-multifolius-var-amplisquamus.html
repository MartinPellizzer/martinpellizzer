Erigeron multifolius var. amplisquamus

Erigeron multifolius var. amplisquamus, commonly known as the broad-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are broadly ovate to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces yellow-centered flower heads with white ray florets.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
