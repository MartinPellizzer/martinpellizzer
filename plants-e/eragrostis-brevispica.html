Eragrostis brevispica

Eragrostis brevispica, commonly known as the short-awned lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear short awns, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis brevispica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
