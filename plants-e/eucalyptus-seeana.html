Eucalyptus seeana

Eucalyptus seeana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with smooth, peeling bark. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically ranging from 5 to 15 centimeters in length. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing the resilience of eucalyptus species in harsh conditions.
