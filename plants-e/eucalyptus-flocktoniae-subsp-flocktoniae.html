Eucalyptus flocktoniae subsp. flocktoniae

Eucalyptus flocktoniae subsp.

flocktoniae, commonly known as the Flockton's mallees, is a small to medium-sized eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The species produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing ribbed seeds. It is adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils and low rainfall conditions.

This subspecies is of particular interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
