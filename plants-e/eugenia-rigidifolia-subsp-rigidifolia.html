Eugenia rigidifolia subsp. rigidifolia

Eugenia rigidifolia subsp.

rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaved eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its rigid, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by small, round berries that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and mangrove ecosystems.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
