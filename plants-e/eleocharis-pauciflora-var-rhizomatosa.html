Eleocharis pauciflora var. rhizomatosa

Eleocharis pauciflora var. rhizomatosa, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typically found at the tips of its stems. Its rhizomes allow it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

The leaves are narrow and cylindrical, adapted for underwater or saturated soil conditions.
