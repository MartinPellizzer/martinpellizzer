Eugenia maximiliana

Eugenia maximiliana, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fragrant foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural use.

The fruit, though not typically consumed by humans, is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
