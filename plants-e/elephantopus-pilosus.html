Elephantopus pilosus

Elephantopus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
