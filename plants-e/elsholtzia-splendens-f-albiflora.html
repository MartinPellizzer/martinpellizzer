Elsholtzia splendens f. albiflora

Elsholtzia splendens f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered elsholtzia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinguishing feature is its clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and pleasant scent.
