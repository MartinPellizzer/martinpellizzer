Euryops abrotanifolius

Euryops abrotanifolius, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, gray-green leaves. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in winter, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
