Epilobium peruvianum

Epilobium peruvianum, commonly known as the Peruvian willow or Peruvian fireweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Epilobium peruvianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
