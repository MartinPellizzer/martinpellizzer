Eriophyllum harfordii

Eriophyllum harfordii, commonly known as Harford's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriophyllum harfordii is often used in native plant gardens due to its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
