Erigeron peregrinus f. membranaceus

Erigeron peregrinus f. membranaceus, commonly known as the membranous fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
