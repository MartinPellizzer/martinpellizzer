Erythrocoma campanulata

Erythrocoma campanulata, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to deep red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Erythrocoma campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
