Euphorbia namuskluftensis

Euphorbia namuskluftensis, commonly known as the Namuskluft euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a unique inflorescence called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among cactus and succulent enthusiasts.
