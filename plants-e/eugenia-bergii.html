Eugenia bergii

Eugenia bergii, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, white or pale blue berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Eugenia bergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
