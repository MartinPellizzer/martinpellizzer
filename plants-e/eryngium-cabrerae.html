Eryngium cabrerae

Eryngium cabrerae, commonly known as the Canary Islands thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts, which give it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged look.

Eryngium cabrerae is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
