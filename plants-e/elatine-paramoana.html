Elatine paramoana

Elatine paramoana, commonly known as the Andean waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Elatinaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often submerged leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is adapted to cold, temperate climates. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist environments make it an important component of alpine wetlands.

Elatine paramoana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
