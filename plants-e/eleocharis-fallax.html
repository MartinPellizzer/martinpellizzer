Eleocharis fallax

Eleocharis fallax, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help prevent erosion.

Eleocharis fallax is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
