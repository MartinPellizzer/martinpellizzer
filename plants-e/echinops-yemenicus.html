Echinops yemenicus

Echinops yemenicus, commonly known as the Yemen thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough texture, and it produces small yellow flowers in the summer.

Echinops yemenicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
