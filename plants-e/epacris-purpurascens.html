Epacris purpurascens

Epacris purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open woodlands and heathlands. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to purple, bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Epacris purpurascens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
