Eugenia contracta

Eugenia contracta, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits resembling small apples. The fruits have a fragrant aroma and a tart, acidic flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
