Epidendrum ornithoglossum

Epidendrum ornithoglossum, commonly known as the bird's tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, elongated flowers that resemble a bird's tongue, typically in shades of yellow and green. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum ornithoglossum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
