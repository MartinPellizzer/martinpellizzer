Eriogonum effusum subsp. simpsonii

Eriogonum effusum subsp.

simpsonii, commonly known as Simpson's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green appearance.

Simpson's buckwheat plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
