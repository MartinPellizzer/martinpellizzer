Erysimum graecum

Erysimum graecum, commonly known as the Greek wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Erysimum graecum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
