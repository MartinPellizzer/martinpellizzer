Euodia triphylla var. cambodiana

Euodia triphylla var. cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange or red in color. Its wood is highly valued for its fine grain and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional carpentry.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
