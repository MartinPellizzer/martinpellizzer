Eremophila regia

Eremophila regia, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, which helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

Eremophila regia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
