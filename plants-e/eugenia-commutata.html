Eugenia commutata

Eugenia commutata, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which is typically round and green to red in color, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
