Emilia lopollensis

Emilia lopollensis, commonly known as the Lopollan emilia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant is known for its vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Emilia lopollensis typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool and moist conditions.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh climate of its native habitat.
