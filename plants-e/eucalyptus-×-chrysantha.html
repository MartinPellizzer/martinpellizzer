Eucalyptus × chrysantha

Eucalyptus × chrysantha, commonly known as the golden gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It is a medium-sized tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, often found in coastal and dry sclerophyll forests.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
