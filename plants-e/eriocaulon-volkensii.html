Eriocaulon volkensii

Eriocaulon volkensii, commonly known as the African bulbous grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is often found in marshy habitats along rivers and lakes. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, which can help stabilize soil in wetland environments.

Eriocaulon volkensii is also valued for its ornamental appeal in aquatic gardens and water features.
