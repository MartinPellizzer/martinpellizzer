Eriophyllum lanatum var. grandiflorum

Eriophyllum lanatum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features soft, woolly gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to arid and coastal environments.
