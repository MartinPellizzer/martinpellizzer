Elaeagnus utilis

Elaeagnus utilis, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible berries that are rich in nutrients. Elaeagnus utilis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in soil rehabilitation.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in various climates.
