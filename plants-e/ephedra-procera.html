Ephedra procera

Ephedra procera, commonly known as the tall ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Ephedra procera is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Its chemical composition includes ephedrine, which has historically been used in traditional medicine.
