Eupatorium huambutiense

Eupatorium huambutiense, commonly known as the Huambuti feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Eupatorium huambutiense is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its traditional uses in local medicine.
