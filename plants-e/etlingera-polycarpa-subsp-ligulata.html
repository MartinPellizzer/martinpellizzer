Etlingera polycarpa subsp. ligulata

Etlingera polycarpa subsp.

ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking red bracts that enclose small, yellow flowers, which are often used in floral arrangements.

The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
