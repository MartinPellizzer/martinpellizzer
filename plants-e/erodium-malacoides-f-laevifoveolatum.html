Erodium malacoides f. laevifoveolatum

Erodium malacoides f. laevifoveolatum, commonly known as the smooth-crested bedstraw, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Leguminosae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or violet. The leaves are simple and opposite, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is distinguished by its smooth, shallowly furrowed calyx, which gives it its specific epithet.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils in full sun.
