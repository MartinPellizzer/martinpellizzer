Erythropalla

Erythropalla, commonly known as the red-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Erythropalla thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique coloration and role in local ecosystems.
