Euphorbia djimilensis

Euphorbia djimilensis, commonly known as the Djimilensis Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, further adapting it to arid conditions.

Euphorbia djimilensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
