Equisetum ramosissimum var. orientale

Equisetum ramosissimum var. orientale, commonly known as the oriental horse tail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are typically found near the soil surface. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

This ancient plant has remained relatively unchanged for millions of years, showcasing its evolutionary resilience.
