Eritrichium difforme

Eritrichium difforme, commonly known as the purple mountain fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eritrichium difforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
