Erysimum × cheiri var. corinthium

Erysimum × cheiri var. corinthium, commonly known as the winter flowering sweet rocket, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces heart-shaped, lobed leaves that are hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide early color in the garden.
