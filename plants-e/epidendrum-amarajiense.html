Epidendrum amarajiense

Epidendrum amarajiense, commonly known as the Amarajiense Orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and unique botanical features.
