Eugenia leptosperma

Eugenia leptosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eucalypt, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain small, dark seeds.

Eugenia leptosperma is often found in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils.
