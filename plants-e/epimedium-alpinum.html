Epimedium alpinum

Epimedium alpinum, commonly known as barrenwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Epimedium alpinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
