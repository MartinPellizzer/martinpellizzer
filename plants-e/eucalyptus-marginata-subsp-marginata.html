Eucalyptus marginata subsp. marginata

Eucalyptus marginata subsp.

marginata, commonly known as the slender gum, is a tall tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark that peels in thin strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and they are arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. This subspecies is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.

It is also valued for its timber and its use in traditional Indigenous practices.
