Erica imbecilla

Erica imbecilla, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant features dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica imbecilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
