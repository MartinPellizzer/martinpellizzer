Eutoca sericea

Eutoca sericea, commonly known as the silky eutoca, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
