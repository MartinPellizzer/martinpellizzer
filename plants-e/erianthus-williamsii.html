Erianthus williamsii

Erianthus williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' elephant grass, is a tall perennial grass native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous foliage and can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Erianthus williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
