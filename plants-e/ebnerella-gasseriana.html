Ebnerella gasseriana

Ebnerella gasseriana, commonly known as the Gasser's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Ebnerella gasseriana plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
