Euphorbia antisyphilitica

Euphorbia antisyphilitica, commonly known as the syphilis euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among members of its family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
