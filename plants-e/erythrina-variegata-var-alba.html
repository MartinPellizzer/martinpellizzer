Erythrina variegata var. alba

Erythrina variegata var. alba, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in well-drained soils.
