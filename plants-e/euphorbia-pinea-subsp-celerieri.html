Euphorbia pinea subsp. celerieri

Euphorbia pinea subsp.

celerieri, commonly known as the fast-growing euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a branched stem system with cylindrical, green, and sometimes reddish stems that are covered in a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rapid growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid environments.
