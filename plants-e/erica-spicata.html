Erica spicata

Erica spicata, commonly known as heather spike, is a flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant features small, dense clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its evergreen foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine, white waxy coating.

Erica spicata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
