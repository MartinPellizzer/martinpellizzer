Epilobium adnatum f. rodriguezii

Epilobium adnatum f. rodriguezii, commonly known as the Rodriguez's willowherb, is a variant of the willowherb species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically pink to red, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
