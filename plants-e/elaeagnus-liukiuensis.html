Elaeagnus liukiuensis

Elaeagnus liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu silverberry, is a shrub native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Elaeagnus liukiuensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
