Erigeron lepidopodus

Erigeron lepidopodus, commonly known as the blueweed or blueweed aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and dry meadows. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
