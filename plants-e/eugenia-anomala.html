Eugenia anomala

Eugenia anomala, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is typically yellow or red when mature and contains a single seed.

Eugenia anomala is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
