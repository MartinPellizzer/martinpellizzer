Eriophorum russeolum f. subalbidum

Eriophorum russeolum f. subalbidum, commonly known as the white tussock sedge, is a variant of the common tussock sedge.

It is characterized by its pale or white inflorescence compared to the typical greenish or brownish flowers of the species. This plant is typically found in moist, acidic soils in temperate regions, often in bogs or wet meadows. The leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
