Enterolobium blanchetii

Enterolobium blanchetii, commonly known as the yellow poinciana, is a tropical leguminous tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fern-like, bipinnate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Enterolobium blanchetii has a deep root system that helps stabilize soil and can tolerate a variety of soil types.
