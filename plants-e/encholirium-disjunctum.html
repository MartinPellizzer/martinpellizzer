Encholirium disjunctum

Encholirium disjunctum, commonly known as the Mexican firecracker cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the highlands of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and vibrant orange to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and bats.

Encholirium disjunctum is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
