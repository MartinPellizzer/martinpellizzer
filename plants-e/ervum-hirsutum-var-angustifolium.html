Ervum hirsutum var. angustifolium

Ervum hirsutum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a variant of the common cowpea species.

It is a legume characterized by its slender, angular pods and small, elongated leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and thrives in warm climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces edible seeds that are used in various cuisines.

The narrow leaves and compact growth habit make it distinct from other cowpea varieties.
