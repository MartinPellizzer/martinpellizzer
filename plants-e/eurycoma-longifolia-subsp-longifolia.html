Eurycoma longifolia subsp. longifolia

Eurycoma longifolia subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the greater yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often found in open woodland and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
