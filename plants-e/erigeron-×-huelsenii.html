Erigeron × huelsenii

Erigeron × huelsenii, commonly known as the Blueweed or Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a hybrid species that exhibits a combination of traits from its parent species, Erigeron acerifolius and Erigeron karvinskianus. The plant is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Erigeron × huelsenii is typically found in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands and streambanks.
