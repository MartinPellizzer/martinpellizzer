Erysimum crassistylum

Erysimum crassistylum, commonly known as the broad-leaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum crassistylum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
