Eriosoriopsis cinnamomea

Eriosoriopsis cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon leaf rust fungus, is a plant pathogen that primarily affects the leaves of certain species of the genus Cinnamomum.

It belongs to the order Pucciniales and is characterized by its rust-like symptoms, which include orange to reddish pustules on the undersides of leaves. The fungus produces asexual spores called aeciospores, which are responsible for spreading the infection between host plants. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its presence can significantly reduce the health and yield of cinnamon trees, making it an important concern for agricultural management.
