Erythrina moori

Erythrina moori, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense panicles and bloom during the warmer months. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in thorns. Native to regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils.

Erythrina moori is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plants.
