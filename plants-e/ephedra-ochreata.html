Ephedra ochreata

Ephedra ochreata, commonly known as the yellow ephedra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is also known as a hallucinogenic plant in some cultures.
