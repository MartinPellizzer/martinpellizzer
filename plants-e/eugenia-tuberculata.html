Eugenia tuberculata

Eugenia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-leaved guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its edible fruits and use in traditional medicine.
