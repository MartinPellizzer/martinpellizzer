Eucalyptus insignis

Eucalyptus insignis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and woodland habitats.

Eucalyptus insignis is a valuable component of native ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
