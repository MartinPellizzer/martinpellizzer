Eugenia macancheana

Eugenia macancheana, commonly known as the Macancha guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. Its fruit, similar to a guava, is typically small and edible, with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

Eugenia macancheana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in local communities.
