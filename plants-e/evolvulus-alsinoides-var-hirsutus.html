Evolvulus alsinoides var. hirsutus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. hirsutus, commonly known as hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of five-petaled blue or purple flowers that open in the morning, giving it its common name. Its hairy stems and leaves are characteristic of the varietal form, hirsutus.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
