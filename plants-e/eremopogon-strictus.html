Eremopogon strictus

Eremopogon strictus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed false rye, is a grass-like plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Eremopogon strictus produces small, spore-bearing structures called spathes, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

Its morphology is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
