Eryngium castrense var. castrense

Eryngium castrense var. castrense, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which attract various pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in gardens and natural habitats.
