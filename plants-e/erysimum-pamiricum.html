Erysimum pamiricum

Erysimum pamiricum, commonly known as the Pamir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum pamiricum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
