Euphorbia annulata

Euphorbia annulata, commonly known as the ringed spurge, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ringed stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with latex-producing properties. Euphorbia annulata typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is an evergreen shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter.
