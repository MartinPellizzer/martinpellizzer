Eryngium rostratum var. paludosum

Eryngium rostratum var. paludosum, commonly known as swamp thistle, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of North America.

It features a robust, branching stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, often with a reddish hue. The plant produces spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by white to pale blue bracts. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of wetlands.
