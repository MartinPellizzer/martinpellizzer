Eugenia baramensis

Eugenia baramensis, commonly known as the Baram hill ebony, is a small evergreen tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant has simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries.

The species is often found in lowland rainforests and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
