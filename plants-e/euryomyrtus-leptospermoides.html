Euryomyrtus leptospermoides

Euryomyrtus leptospermoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mugga ironbark, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical and subtropical regions of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant has smooth, greyish bark that often exfoliates, revealing a mottled appearance. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture and a prominent midrib.

The flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value.
