Epipremnum amplissimum

Epipremnum amplissimum, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically bright green with a yellow or variegated pattern. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its rapid growth rate, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, and it can reach lengths of several meters in suitable environments.

Epipremnum amplissimum is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, contributing to its popularity as a houseplant.
