Eriogonum jamesii var. undulatum

Eriogonum jamesii var. undulatum, commonly known as the crested buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, or undulating leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats.

This species is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
