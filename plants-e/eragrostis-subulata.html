Eragrostis subulata

Eragrostis subulata, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a ornamental plant.
