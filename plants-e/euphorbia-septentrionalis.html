Euphorbia septentrionalis

Euphorbia septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern spurge, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Euphorbia septentrionalis is a hardy plant that is relatively drought-tolerant and can survive in a variety of environmental conditions.
