Erysimum australe var. ramosum

Erysimum australe var. ramosum, commonly known as the Australian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers, which are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
