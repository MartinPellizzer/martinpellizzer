Erythranthe lagunensis

Erythranthe lagunensis, commonly known as the salt marsh flowering plant, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of South America.

It thrives in saline and brackish environments, often found in salt marshes and tidal flats. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to retain water in arid conditions.

Erythranthe lagunensis plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
