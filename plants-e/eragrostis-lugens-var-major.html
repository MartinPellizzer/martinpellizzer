Eragrostis lugens var. major

Eragrostis lugens var. major, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

This grass is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
