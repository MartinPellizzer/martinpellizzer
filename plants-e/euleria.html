Euleria

Euleria, commonly known as the "devil's backbone," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, often red or orange, tubular flowers that are arranged in dense spikes. The leaves are typically opposite and lance-shaped, with a rough texture. Euleria species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.
