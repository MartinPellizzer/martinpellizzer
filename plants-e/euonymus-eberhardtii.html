Euonymus eberhardtii

Euonymus eberhardtii, commonly known as the red-eared spurge, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and oval, dark green leaves with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
