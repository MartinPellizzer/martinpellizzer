Elettaria pallida

Elettaria pallida, commonly known as the pale cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and produces small, pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes, which are the underground stems, are the primary part used for its aromatic properties.

The essential oils extracted from Elettaria pallida are valued for their fragrance and potential medicinal uses.
