Ericoides mackayi

Ericoides mackayi, commonly known as the Mackay's ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the spring. Ericoides mackayi thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
