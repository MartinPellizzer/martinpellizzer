Erysimum hieraciifolium var. cheiranthus

Erysimum hieraciifolium var. cheiranthus, commonly known as the cheiranthus wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are basal, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
