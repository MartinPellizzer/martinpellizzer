Exacum punctatum

Exacum punctatum, commonly known as the spotted pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with slender stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy surface, and the flowers often have a distinctive spotted pattern on their petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun, making it a versatile choice for gardens and landscapes.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
