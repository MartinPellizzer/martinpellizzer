Elymus albicans var. griffithsii

Elymus albicans var. griffithsii, commonly known as Griffiths' wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to whitish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and as a forage crop.
