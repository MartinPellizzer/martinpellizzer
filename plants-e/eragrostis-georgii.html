Eragrostis georgii

Eragrostis georgii, commonly known as George's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
