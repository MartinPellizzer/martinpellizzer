Eragrostis lindmanii

Eragrostis lindmanii, commonly known as Lindman's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert and semi-desert environments. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant.

Lindman's lovegrass is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
