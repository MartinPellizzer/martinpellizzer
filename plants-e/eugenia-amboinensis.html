Eugenia amboinensis

Eugenia amboinensis, commonly known as the Java apple or amboina cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and traditional medicine.
