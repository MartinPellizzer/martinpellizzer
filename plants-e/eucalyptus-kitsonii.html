Eucalyptus kitsonii

Eucalyptus kitsonii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which are typically pollinated by insects. Eucalyptus kitsonii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its reduced leaf surface area.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
