Eucalyptus calophylla var. hawkeyi

Eucalyptus calophylla var. hawkeyi, commonly known as the Hawkeye malleable, is a small to medium-sized eucalyptus tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often greyish bark that peels in thin, papery strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and usually have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels, and attract a variety of pollinators.

This variety is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional and contemporary woodworking.
