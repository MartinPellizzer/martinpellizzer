Epipactis xiphophylla

Epipactis xiphophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip, which is often adorned with purple or brown markings. Epipactis xiphophylla is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
