Euphorbia disclusa

Euphorbia disclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified floral structures. It has a milky sap that can be toxic and is a characteristic feature of the Euphorbia genus.

Euphorbia disclusa is often used in rock gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
