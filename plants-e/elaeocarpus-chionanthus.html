Elaeocarpus chionanthus

Elaeocarpus chionanthus, commonly known as the snow-flower elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shady environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
