Eriogonum marifolium var. marifolium

Eriogonum marifolium var. marifolium, commonly known as the Sierra snowbells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

It is well adapted to dry environments and plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
