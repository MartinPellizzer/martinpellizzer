Erodium malacoides var. chium

Erodium malacoides var. chium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
