Evolvulus alsinoides var. decumbens

Evolvulus alsinoides var. decumbens, commonly known as creeping morning glory, is a low-growing annual herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate or decumbent stems that sprawl along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, gardens, and along roadsides.
