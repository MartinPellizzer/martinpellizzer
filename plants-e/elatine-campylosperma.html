Elatine campylosperma

Elatine campylosperma, commonly known as the slender waterwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces tiny, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is often found in muddy or sandy substrates.

Elatine campylosperma plays a role in stabilizing aquatic environments and providing habitat for microorganisms.
