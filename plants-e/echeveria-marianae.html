Echeveria marianae

Echeveria marianae, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette of thick, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powdery bloom. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its compact size and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
