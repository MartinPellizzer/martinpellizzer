Epilobium × hugueninii

Epilobium × hugueninii, commonly known as the hybrid willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along streambanks and roadsides.

Epilobium × hugueninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
