Erica albertyniae

Erica albertyniae, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica albertyniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
