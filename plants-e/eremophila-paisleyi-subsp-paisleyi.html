Eremophila paisleyi subsp. paisleyi

Eremophila paisleyi subsp.

paisleyi, commonly known as the Paisley eremophila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and aromatic foliage.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in drought-tolerant gardens.
