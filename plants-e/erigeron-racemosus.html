Erigeron racemosus

Erigeron racemosus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are generally linear to lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.
