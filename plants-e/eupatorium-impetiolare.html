Eupatorium impetiolare

Eupatorium impetiolare, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and its role in supporting local pollinators.
