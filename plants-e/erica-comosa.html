Erica comosa

Erica comosa, commonly known as the tufted heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months.

Erica comosa is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
