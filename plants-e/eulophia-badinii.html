Eulophia badinii

Eulophia badinii, commonly known as the Badini's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia badinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
