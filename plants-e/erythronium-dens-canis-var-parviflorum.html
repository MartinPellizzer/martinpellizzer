Erythronium dens-canis var. parviflorum

Erythronium dens-canis var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered dog's-tooth violet, is a member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, particularly in California and Oregon. The plant features a rosette of narrow, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
