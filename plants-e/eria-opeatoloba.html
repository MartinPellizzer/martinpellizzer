Eria opeatoloba

Eria opeatoloba, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors. The plant produces striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eria opeatoloba is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
