Eritrichium haplostachyum

Eritrichium haplostachyum, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers and hairy, basal leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. It has a short growing season and is often found in mountainous areas of Europe, Asia, and North America.

Due to its habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
