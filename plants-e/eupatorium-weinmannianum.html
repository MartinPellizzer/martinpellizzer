Eupatorium weinmannianum

Eupatorium weinmannianum, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom from summer to autumn.

Eupatorium weinmannianum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
