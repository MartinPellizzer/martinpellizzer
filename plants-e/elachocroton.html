Elachocroton

Elachocroton, commonly known as the croton plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, variegated foliage, which can display shades of green, yellow, red, and orange. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are often thick and waxy, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits.

Elachocroton is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
