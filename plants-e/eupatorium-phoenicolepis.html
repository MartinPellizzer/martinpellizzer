Eupatorium phoenicolepis

Eupatorium phoenicolepis, commonly known as the flameweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its showy, pink to red flower heads bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium phoenicolepis is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
