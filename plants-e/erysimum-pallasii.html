Erysimum pallasii

Erysimum pallasii, commonly known as Pallas's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are characterized by their four petals and a distinctive yellow color.

Erysimum pallasii is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
