Echinopsis cordobensis

Echinopsis cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoban prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, fleshy, green to yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its flowers are typically pale yellow or orange, and it produces edible fruits similar to other prickly pear cacti.

Echinopsis cordobensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
