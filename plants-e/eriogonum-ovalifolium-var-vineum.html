Eriogonum ovalifolium var. vineum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. vineum, commonly known as the vine-like buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and the presence of hairy stems and leaves. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip, and they are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green to yellowish in color, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
