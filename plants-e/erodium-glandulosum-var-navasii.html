Erodium glandulosum var. navasii

Erodium glandulosum var. navasii, commonly known as Navas' sticky erodia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems covered in glandular hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear glandular structures, contributing to its sticky texture. The flowers are small, pink to purple, and borne in clusters at the leaf axils.

This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
