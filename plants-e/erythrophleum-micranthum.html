Erythrophleum micranthum

Erythrophleum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its durable timber and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
