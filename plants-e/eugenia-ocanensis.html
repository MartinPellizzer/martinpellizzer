Eugenia ocanensis

Eugenia ocanensis, commonly known as the Ocanensis Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, smooth, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
