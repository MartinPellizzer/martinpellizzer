Excoecaria eglandulata

Excoecaria eglandulata, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its historical use in the production of natural rubber.
