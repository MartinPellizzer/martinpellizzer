Eupatorium pintobambense

Eupatorium pintobambense, commonly known as the Brazilian thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the late spring to early summer.

Eupatorium pintobambense is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
