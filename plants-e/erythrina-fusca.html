Erythrina fusca

Erythrina fusca, commonly known as the red torchwood or scarlet coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and southern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its bark is often covered in a thick, rough layer. Erythrina fusca is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
