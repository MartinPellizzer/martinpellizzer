Eupatorium longipetiolatum var. arbusculare

Eupatorium longipetiolatum var. arbusculare, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, creeping stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its aromatic foliage and is used in traditional herbal medicine.
