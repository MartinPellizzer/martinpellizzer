Euphorbia pseudoserpyllifolia f. typica

Euphorbia pseudoserpyllifolia f. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
