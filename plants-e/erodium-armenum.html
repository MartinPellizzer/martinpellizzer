Erodium armenum

Erodium armenum, commonly known as Armenian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
