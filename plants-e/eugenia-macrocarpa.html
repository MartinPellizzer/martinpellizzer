Eugenia macrocarpa

Eugenia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited clove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
