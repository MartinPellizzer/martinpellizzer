Erione jasminodora

Erione jasminodora, commonly known as the jasmine-like orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling jasmine in scent and appearance. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Erione jasminodora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories where it receives dappled sunlight.
