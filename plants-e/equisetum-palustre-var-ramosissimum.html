Equisetum palustre var. ramosissimum

Equisetum palustre var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the swamp horsehair fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a central, unbranched stem. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments, such as bogs and along the edges of ponds. It produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stem, which are distinctive and contribute to its reproductive strategy.

This variety is notable for its robust growth and adaptability to moist, nutrient-poor soils.
