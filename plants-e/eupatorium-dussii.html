Eupatorium dussii

Eupatorium dussii, commonly known as the narrowleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive grayish-green color with hairy surfaces.

Eupatorium dussii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
