Eucalyptus daphnoides

Eucalyptus daphnoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves and clusters of small white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands and heathlands. It produces small, woody fruits known as capsules, which contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Eucalyptus daphnoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
