Euphorbia neohumbertii var. aureoviridiflora

Euphorbia neohumbertii var. aureoviridiflora, commonly known as the golden-green spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, ribbed stems that are typically green with a subtle yellowish or golden hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
