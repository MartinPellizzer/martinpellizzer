Eulophia pentheri

Eulophia pentheri, commonly known as the purple-tinged eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically feature a purple or pinkish hue. The plant produces inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant orchid flowers arranged in a raceme. Eulophia pentheri thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical significance lies in its ornamental value and its role in the biodiversity of tropical ecosystems.
