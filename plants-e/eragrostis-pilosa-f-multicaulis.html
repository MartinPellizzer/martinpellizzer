Eragrostis pilosa f. multicaulis

Eragrostis pilosa f. multicaulis, commonly known as hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, often silver-tomentose foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color.

The botanical features of this grass make it adaptable to arid environments and suitable for use in erosion control and forage.
