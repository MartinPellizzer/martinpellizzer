Eupatorium tamboense

Eupatorium tamboense, commonly known as African feverfew, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Eupatorium tamboense is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional African medicine.
