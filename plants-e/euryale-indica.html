Euryale indica

Euryale indica, commonly known as the water caltrop, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, floating fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and marshes. Its leaves are arrowhead-shaped and submerged, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The seeds are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
