Elaeocarpus transultus

Elaeocarpus transultus, commonly known as the white-fruited elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, blue-black fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
