Elaeocarpus calomala

Elaeocarpus calomala, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a hard, purple-black drupe that contains a single seed, and it is prized for its aromatic, edible flesh. This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
