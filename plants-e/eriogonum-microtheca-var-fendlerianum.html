Eriogonum microtheca var. fendlerianum

Eriogonum microtheca var. fendlerianum, commonly known as the Fendler's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant features slender, branching stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert habitats.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
