Eupatorium jahnii

Eupatorium jahnii, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow to white inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist soils, often forming dense stands in swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium jahnii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
