Eriospermum somalense

Eriospermum somalense, commonly known as the Somali eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Somalia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

Eriospermum somalense is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.
