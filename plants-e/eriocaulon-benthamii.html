Eriocaulon benthamii

Eriocaulon benthamii, commonly known as the bentham's pipewort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon benthamii is often found in still or slow-moving water, where it plays a role in stabilizing sediment and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
