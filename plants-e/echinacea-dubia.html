Echinacea dubia

Echinacea dubia, commonly known as the western purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features rough, hairy leaves and produces a distinctive, spiky yellow center in its flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to native gardens.

Echinacea dubia is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
