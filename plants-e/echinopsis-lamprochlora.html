Echinopsis lamprochlora

Echinopsis lamprochlora, commonly known as the golden echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the surface after rare rainfall. Its spines are short and often covered in a whitish bloom, which helps to reduce water loss.

Echinopsis lamprochlora is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
