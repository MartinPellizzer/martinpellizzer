Eugenia lineatifolia

Eugenia lineatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruit, which is small and berry-like, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia lineatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
