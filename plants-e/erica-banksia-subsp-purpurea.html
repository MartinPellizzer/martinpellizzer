Erica banksia subsp. purpurea

Erica banksia subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the purple banksia, is a flowering plant native to Western Australia. It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from white to pink and purple.

The plant typically grows in heathland and sandy soils, thriving in full sun and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces woody cones that persist on the plant for several years.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
