Eriope silvatica

Eriope silvatica, commonly known as the silverleaf eriope, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Eriope silvatica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
