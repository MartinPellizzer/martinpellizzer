Eremostachys speciosa var. bipinnatifida

Eremostachys speciosa var. bipinnatifida, commonly known as the twin-leaf eremostachys, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and gray-green foliage that is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers in dense clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in dry climates.
