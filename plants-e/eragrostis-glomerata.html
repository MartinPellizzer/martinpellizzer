Eragrostis glomerata

Eragrostis glomerata, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to the central and western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and compact, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to bluish in color. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a common component of native grasslands.

Blue grama plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
