Erythrina vogelii

Erythrina vogelii, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. Erythrina vogelii is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules, enhancing soil fertility.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance.
