Elymus pubinodis

Elymus pubinodis, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems that can reach heights of up to 1 meter. The leaves are narrow, rough in texture, and have a bluish-green color, with prominent veins running along their length. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.
