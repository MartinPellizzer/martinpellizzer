Epidendrum dixiorum

Epidendrum dixiorum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum dixiorum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
