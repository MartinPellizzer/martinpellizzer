Erica × macnabiana

Erica × macnabiana, commonly known as the Macnab heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and profuse flowering. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica × macnabiana is often used in coastal gardens due to its tolerance of salty air and drought conditions.
