Eulophia cordylinophylla

Eulophia cordylinophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and large, broad, ovate leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
