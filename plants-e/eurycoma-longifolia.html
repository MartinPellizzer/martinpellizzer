Eurycoma longifolia

Eurycoma longifolia, commonly known as longleaf wild betel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stem, simple leaves, and small yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, elliptical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Eurycoma longifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
