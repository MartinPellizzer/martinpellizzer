Ericoides eriopus

Ericoides eriopus, commonly known as the rock heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Ericoides eriopus thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

It is an important component of heathland ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
