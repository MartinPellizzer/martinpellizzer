Encyclia perplexa

Encyclia perplexa, commonly known as the perplexing orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration, often in shades of yellow and green. Its pseudobulbs are short and stout, supporting the foliage and flowering structures.

Encyclia perplexa is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for both cultivation and botanical study.
