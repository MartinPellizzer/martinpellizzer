Eugenia ciliaris

Eugenia ciliaris, commonly known as the satinwood or Queensland satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, edible berries that are typically dark purple to black in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and cabinetry.

Eugenia ciliaris thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.
