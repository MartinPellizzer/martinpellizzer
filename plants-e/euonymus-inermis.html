Euonymus inermis

Euonymus inermis, commonly known as the smooth euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Euonymus inermis is relatively low maintenance and tolerates a variety of soil conditions.
