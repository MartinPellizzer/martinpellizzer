Euphorbia pinkavana

Euphorbia pinkavana, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a pinkish or reddish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
