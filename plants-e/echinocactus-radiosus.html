Echinocactus radiosus

Echinocactus radiosus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Echinocactus radiosus is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
