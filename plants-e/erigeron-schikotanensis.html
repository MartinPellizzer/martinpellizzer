Erigeron schikotanensis

Erigeron schikotanensis, commonly known as the Japanese fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Erigeron schikotanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
