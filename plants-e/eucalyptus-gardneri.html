Eucalyptus gardneri

Eucalyptus gardneri, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
