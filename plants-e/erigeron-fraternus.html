Erigeron fraternus

Erigeron fraternus, commonly known as fraternus blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the central and southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Erigeron fraternus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in water-saturated soils.
