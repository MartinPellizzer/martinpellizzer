Eperua jenmanii subsp. sandwithii

Eperua jenmanii subsp.

sandwithii, commonly known as the Sandwith's eperua, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, and it plays a role in local ecosystems as a habitat and food source.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some areas.
