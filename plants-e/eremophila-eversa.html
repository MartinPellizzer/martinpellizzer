Eremophila eversa

Eremophila eversa, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Eremophila eversa is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low water requirements.
