Eatonia pensylvanica var. longiflora

Eatonia pensylvanica var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered Pennsylvania eatonia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This variety is often found in moist, woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
