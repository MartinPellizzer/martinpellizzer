Eryngium bungei

Eryngium bungei, commonly known as the Bunge's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central Asia.

It features spiny, blue-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces purple to blue flower heads that resemble thistles, with bracts that are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Eryngium bungei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
