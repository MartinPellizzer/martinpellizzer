Eremophila oldfieldii var. angustifolia

Eremophila oldfieldii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
