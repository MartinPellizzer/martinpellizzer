Euphorbia syncameronii

Euphorbia syncameronii, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
