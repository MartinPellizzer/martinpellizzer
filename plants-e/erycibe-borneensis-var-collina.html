Erycibe borneensis var. collina

Erycibe borneensis var. collina, commonly known as the mountain erycibe, is a climbing vine native to the highlands of Borneo.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces fleshy, red or orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The species thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.
