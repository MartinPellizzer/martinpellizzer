Euphrasia collina subsp. trichocalycina

Euphrasia collina subsp.

trichocalycina, commonly known as the mountain eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, oval-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in dense, glandular-hairy inflorescences. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Scrophulariaceae family.
