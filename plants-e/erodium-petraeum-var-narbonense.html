Erodium petraeum var. narbonense

Erodium petraeum var. narbonense, commonly known as the Narrow-leaved Bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This variety is closely related to other species in the Geraniaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
