Evolvulus sericeus var. averyi

Evolvulus sericeus var. averyi, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray foliage and trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in xeriscapic gardens.
