Elaphoglossum fuliginosum, commonly known as the sooty holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its fronds are bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, serrated pinnae.

Elaphoglossum fuliginosum is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
