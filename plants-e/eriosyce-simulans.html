Eriosyce simulans

Eriosyce simulans, commonly known as the "Cushion Eriosyce," is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit, forming a compact cushion or mat-like structure. The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly hairs that help reduce water loss in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters at the stem tips.

Eriosyce simulans is well adapted to rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.
