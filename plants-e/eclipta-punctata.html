Eclipta punctata

Eclipta punctata, commonly known as the punctate false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and thrives in full sun. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in wetlands and along roadsides.

Eclipta punctata is sometimes considered a weed but has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
