Evolvulus rariflorus

Evolvulus rariflorus, commonly known as the rareflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
