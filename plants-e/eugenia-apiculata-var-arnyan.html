Eugenia apiculata var. arnyan

Eugenia apiculata var. arnyan, commonly known as the Arnyan cherry, is a shrub or small tree native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests.

Its ornamental value and unique fruit make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
