Erica crateriformis

Erica crateriformis, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Erica crateriformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
