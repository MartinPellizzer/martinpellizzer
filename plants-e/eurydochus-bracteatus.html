Eurydochus bracteatus

Eurydochus bracteatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, fertile soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

Eurydochus bracteatus is considered an invasive species in some areas due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
