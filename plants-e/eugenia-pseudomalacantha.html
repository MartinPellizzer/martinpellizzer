Eugenia pseudomalacantha

Eugenia pseudomalacantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, narrow elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
