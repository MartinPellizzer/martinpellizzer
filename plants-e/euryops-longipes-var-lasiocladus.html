Euryops longipes var. lasiocladus

Euryops longipes var. lasiocladus, commonly known as the roughhead dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "lasiocladus.". The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seed heads. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
