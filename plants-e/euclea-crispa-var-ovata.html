Euclea crispa var. ovata

Euclea crispa var. ovata, commonly known as the oval-leaf euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
