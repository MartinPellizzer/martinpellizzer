Excoecaria bicolor

Excoecaria bicolor, commonly known as the crocodile tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its bark contains a toxic sap that has been historically used for various purposes.

The botanical features of Excoecaria bicolor make it both ecologically significant and potentially hazardous.
