Euphorbia serpens var. imbricata

Euphorbia serpens var. imbricata, commonly known as the African ice plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features a prostrate growth habit with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable ornamental plant.
