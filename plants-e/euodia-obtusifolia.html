Euodia obtusifolia

Euodia obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leafed euodia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn yellow or red when ripe. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Euodia obtusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
