Epidendrum ophrydis

Epidendrum ophrydis, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with fleshy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which are typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Epidendrum ophrydis is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
