Erysimum pachycarpum subsp. cachemiricum

Erysimum pachycarpum subsp.

cachemiricum, commonly known as the Kashmir mustard, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has pinnately lobed leaves and produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
