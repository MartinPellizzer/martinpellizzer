Eugenia grayumii

Eugenia grayumii, commonly known as the gray's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia grayumii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.
