Erigeron × parviflorus

Erigeron × parviflorus, commonly known as the blueweed or common blueweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed soils.
