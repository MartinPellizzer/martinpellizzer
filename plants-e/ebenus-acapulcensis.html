Ebenus acapulcensis

Ebenus acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats. Its foliage is aromatic and has been used traditionally in local herbal remedies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
