Erythraea vulgaris

Erythraea vulgaris, commonly known as the common erythraea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
