Eupatorium purpureum var. angustifolium

Eupatorium purpureum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
