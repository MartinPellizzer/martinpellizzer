Erythrodes viridiflora

Erythrodes viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered erythrodes, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant typically grows as an erect shrub with lance-shaped leaves that have a slightly hairy texture. Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and adaptability to humid, shaded environments.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
