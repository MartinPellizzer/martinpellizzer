Eulalia collina

Eulalia collina, commonly known as the mountain grass or slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fluffy, feathery seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation across rocky and arid environments. Eulalia collina thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude grasslands and open slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the flora in its native habitat.
