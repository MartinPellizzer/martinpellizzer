Erodium bipinnatum var. petroselinum

Erodium bipinnatum var. petroselinum, commonly known as broadleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lobed or pinnately divided. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish to white flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and along fences.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and urban environments.
