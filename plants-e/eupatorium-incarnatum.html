Eupatorium incarnatum

Eupatorium incarnatum, commonly known as purple boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-purple flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has hairy, often reddish stems. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
