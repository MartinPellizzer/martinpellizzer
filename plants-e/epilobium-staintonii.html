Epilobium staintonii

Epilobium staintonii, commonly known as Stainton's willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in damp, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with five pink to purple petals, and are arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Epilobium staintonii is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local insect populations.
