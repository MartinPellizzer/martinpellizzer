Epiphyllum hookeri subsp. columbiense

Epiphyllum hookeri subsp.

columbiense, commonly known as the Mexican night bloomer, is a climbing cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are often green or reddish in color.

The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
