Elaeocarpus sericoloides var. sericoloides

Elaeocarpus sericoloides var. sericoloides, commonly known as the red-eyed tree.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, red-veined flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange to reddish in color, containing a single seed. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
