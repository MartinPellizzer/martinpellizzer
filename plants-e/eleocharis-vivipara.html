Eleocharis vivipara

Eleocharis vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous spike-rush, is a species of sedges native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant is notable for its vivipary, a reproductive strategy where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. Its rhizomes form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in marshy areas.

Eleocharis vivipara plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
