Epacris crassifolia subsp. macroflora

Epacris crassifolia subsp.

macroflora, commonly known as the large-flowered epacris, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native pollinators.
