Eria lactea

Eria lactea, commonly known as the white milk orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in racemes, giving it a delicate appearance.

Eria lactea is typically grown as a houseplant due to its preference for warm, humid conditions.
