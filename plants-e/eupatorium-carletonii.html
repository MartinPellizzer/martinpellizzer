Eupatorium carletonii

Eupatorium carletonii, commonly known as Carleton's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its historical use in herbal medicine, though it is now considered rare and protected in many regions.
