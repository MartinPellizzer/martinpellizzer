Eryngium proliferum

Eryngium proliferum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust root system and can grow up to 1 meter tall, with narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas.

Eryngium proliferum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
