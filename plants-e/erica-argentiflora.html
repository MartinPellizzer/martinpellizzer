Erica argentiflora

Erica argentiflora, commonly known as silverleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring. Erica argentiflora thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
