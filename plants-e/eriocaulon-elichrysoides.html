Eriocaulon elichrysoides

Eriocaulon elichrysoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a member of the Poales order and the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial plant typically found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its ecological importance lies in its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
