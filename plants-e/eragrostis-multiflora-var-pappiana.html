Eragrostis multiflora var. pappiana

Eragrostis multiflora var. pappiana, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The variety pappiana is notable for its hairy, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation and adaptability to various environments.
