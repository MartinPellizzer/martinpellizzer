Eulalia praemorsa

Eulalia praemorsa, commonly known as the death's-head hawkmoth, is a striking plant characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, tubular flowers.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas. The flowers are typically pale green to white, with a faintly colored lip that serves as a landing platform for pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique, moth-pollinated flowers.
