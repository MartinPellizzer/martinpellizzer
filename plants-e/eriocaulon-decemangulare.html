Eriocaulon decemangulare, commonly known as the many-toothed spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. Its inflorescence consists of numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Eriocaulon decemangulare is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
