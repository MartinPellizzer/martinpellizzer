Eugenia boliviensis

Eugenia boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish or purple when ripe. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, often with a slight waxy coating.

Eugenia boliviensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits in suitable climates.
