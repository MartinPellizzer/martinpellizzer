Eucalyptus radiata subsp. sejuncta

Eucalyptus radiata subsp.

sejuncta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to south-eastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous blue-green in color.

The flowers are arranged in globular clusters and are usually pale yellow to cream, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It prefers sandy or loamy soils in open woodland or heathland environments.

This subspecies is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
