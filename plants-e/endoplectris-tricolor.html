Endoplectris tricolor

Endoplectris tricolor, commonly known as the three-colored grasshopper orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia and is characterized by its unique, grass-like foliage and strikingly colorful flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its flowers, which can be yellow, red, or purple, are often clustered in inflorescences and attract various pollinators.

Endoplectris tricolor is a perennial herb that exhibits a distinctive growth habit with a short, stocky stem and elongated leaves.
