Eupatorium vincifolium

Eupatorium vincifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic leaves. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium vincifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
