Eulophia barbata

Eulophia barbata, commonly known as the bearded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive beard-like structure on the lip. Eulophia barbata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
