Eupatorium brevipetiolatum

Eupatorium brevipetiolatum, commonly known as narrow-leaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and have a rough texture, often with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
