Eupatorium perrinianum

Eupatorium perrinianum, commonly known as the Florida ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands and moist woodlands. The plant features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles.

The plant produces white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
