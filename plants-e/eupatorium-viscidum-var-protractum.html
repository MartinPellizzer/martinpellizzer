Eupatorium viscidum var. protractum

Eupatorium viscidum var. protractum, commonly known as the sticky eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often sticky to the touch. The plant produces small, clustered white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
