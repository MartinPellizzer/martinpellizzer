Eupatorium axillare

Eupatorium axillare, commonly known as the creeping eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
