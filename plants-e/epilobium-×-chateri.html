Epilobium × chateri

Epilobium × chateri, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Epilobium palustre and Epilobium hirsutum. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. It features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces showy pink or red flowers in late summer.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important plant in ecological studies and habitat restoration projects.
