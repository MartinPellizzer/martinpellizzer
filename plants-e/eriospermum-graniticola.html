Eriospermum graniticola

Eriospermum graniticola, commonly known as the granitic eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a distinctive, often hairy appearance. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces a unique seed structure known as an eriosperm, which is covered in fine, hair-like projections.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its specialized adaptations to harsh, rocky habitats.
