Emilia mucronata

Emilia mucronata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a compact, bushy habit with hairy stems and narrow leaves. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their attractive appearance.

Emilia mucronata is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its resilience to drought conditions.
