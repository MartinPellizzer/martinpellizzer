Erythraea barrelieri

Erythraea barrelieri, commonly known as the Madagascar star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a yellowish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is popular in local markets.
