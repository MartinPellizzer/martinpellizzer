Epidendrum ilense

Epidendrum ilense, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, with showy, fragrant blooms in shades of white and pink.

Epidendrum ilense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
