Epimedium × lilacinum

Epimedium × lilacinum, commonly known as the Japanese barberry, is a deciduous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, glossy, dark green foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to white, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from both its foliage and its delicate floral display.
