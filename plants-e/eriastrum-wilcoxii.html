Eriastrum wilcoxii

Eriastrum wilcoxii, commonly known as Wilcox's eriocephalus, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native range.
