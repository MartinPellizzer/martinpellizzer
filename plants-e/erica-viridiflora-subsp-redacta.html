Erica viridiflora subsp. redacta

Erica viridiflora subsp.

redacta, commonly known as the red-haired heath, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the western Cape of South Africa. It is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage that ranges from green to reddish-brown, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
