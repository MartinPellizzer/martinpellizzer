Erica kougabergensis var. recurvifolia

Erica kougabergensis var. recurvifolia, commonly known as the Kougaberg heath, is a South African plant species belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its leaves are leathery and recurved, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to harsh, semi-arid environments and is an important component of fynbos vegetation.
