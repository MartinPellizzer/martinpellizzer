Eriogonum wrightii var. nodosum

Eriogonum wrightii var. nodosum, commonly known as the nodding buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, nodding clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the stems are often branched.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
