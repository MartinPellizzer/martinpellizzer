Epidendrum guislainae

Epidendrum guislainae, commonly known as the Guislain's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil and regular watering.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
