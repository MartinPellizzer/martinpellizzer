Euphorbia serpillifolia

Euphorbia serpillifolia, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves and yellowish-green sap, which can be toxic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Euphorbia serpillifolia is drought-tolerant and often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
