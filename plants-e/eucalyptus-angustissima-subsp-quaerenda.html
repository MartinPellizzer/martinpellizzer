Eucalyptus angustissima subsp. quaerenda

Eucalyptus angustissima subsp.

quaerenda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters, which are usually white or pale blue, and are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils and supporting local wildlife.
