Etlingera versteegii

Etlingera versteegii, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Etlingera versteegii is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a cut flower.
