Eria dasystachys

Eria dasystachys, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in length, typically in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria dasystachys thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
