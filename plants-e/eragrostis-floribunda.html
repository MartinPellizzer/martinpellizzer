Eragrostis floribunda

Eragrostis floribunda, commonly known as the flowering bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and its ability to form dense, upright clumps. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Eragrostis floribunda is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
