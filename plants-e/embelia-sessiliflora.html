Embelia sessiliflora

Embelia sessiliflora, commonly known as the sessile-flower embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, making it visually distinct.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
