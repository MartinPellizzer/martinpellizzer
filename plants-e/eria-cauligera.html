Eria cauligera

Eria cauligera, commonly known as the aerial orchid, is a unique epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ability to grow on other plants without parasitizing them, often found in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, creeping stem with numerous aerial roots that help it anchor to its host. Its flowers, which are typically small and fragrant, bloom in clusters and are often white or pale yellow.

Eria cauligera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to certain indoor growing conditions.
