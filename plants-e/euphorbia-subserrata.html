Euphorbia subserrata

Euphorbia subserrata, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia subserrata is also noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
