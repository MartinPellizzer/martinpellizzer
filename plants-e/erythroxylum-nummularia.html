Erythroxylum nummularia

Erythroxylum nummularia, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, coin-shaped leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erythroxylum nummularia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
