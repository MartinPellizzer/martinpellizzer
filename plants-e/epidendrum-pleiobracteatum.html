Epidendrum pleiobracteatum

Epidendrum pleiobracteatum, commonly known as the showy epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

The species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
