Eugenia denigrata

Eugenia denigrata, commonly known as the black eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black fruit, which is somewhat similar in appearance to the common guava. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while the flowers are small and white or pale pink.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests.
