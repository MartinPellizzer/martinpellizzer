Erigeron spiculosus var. umbrosus

Erigeron spiculosus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
