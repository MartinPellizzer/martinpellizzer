Equisetum variegatum f. geminatum

Equisetum variegatum f. geminatum, commonly known as the variegated scouring rush, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is characterized by its distinctive, segmented stems that are often green with yellow or brown markings. The plant produces spore-bearing structures called strobili at the top of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
