Erythroxylum zehntneri

Erythroxylum zehntneri, commonly known as the Zehntner's coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is closely related to the coca plant, Erythroxylum coca, and shares similar ecological and botanical features.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential pharmacological properties.
