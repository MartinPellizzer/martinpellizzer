Erythroxylum havanense var. continentis

Erythroxylum havanense var. continentis, commonly known as the Cuban or West Indian copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various purposes, including traditional medicine and as a source of copal. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
