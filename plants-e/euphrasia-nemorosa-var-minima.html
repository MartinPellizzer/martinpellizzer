Euphrasia nemorosa var. minima

Euphrasia nemorosa var. minima, commonly known as the lesser eyebright, is a small perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodland areas and has delicate, hairy stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its characteristic appearance. This plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.

Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle floral display to its natural habitat.
