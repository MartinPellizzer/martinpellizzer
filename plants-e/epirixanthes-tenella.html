Epirixanthes tenella

Epirixanthes tenella, commonly known as the dwarf ice plant, is a small, succulent plant native to South America.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink and purple during the warmer months. It is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
