Eragrostis spectabilis

Eragrostis spectabilis, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive blue-green foliage that turns golden in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dryland environments.

Blue grama is an important species in native grasslands and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
