Eryngium unifultum

Eryngium unifultum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Eryngium unifultum plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
