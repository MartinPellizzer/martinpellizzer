Echinopsis kellyana

Echinopsis kellyana, commonly known as Kelly's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant white or pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Echinopsis kellyana is often cultivated for its striking appearance and is relatively easy to care for in a home garden setting.
