Euphorbia milii

Euphorbia milii, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a succulent shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its leaves are typically small, opposite, and covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Euphorbia milii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
