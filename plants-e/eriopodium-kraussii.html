Eriopodium kraussii

Eriopodium kraussii, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid native habitat. It produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Eriopodium kraussii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
