Echium vulgare subsp. schifferi

Echium vulgare subsp.

schifferi, commonly known as the Alpine evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat woody.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
