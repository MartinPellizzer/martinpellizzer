Excoecaria sebifera

Excoecaria sebifera, commonly known as the rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is the source of natural rubber. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appearance.
