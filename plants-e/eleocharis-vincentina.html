Eleocharis vincentina

Eleocharis vincentina, commonly known as Vincent's spikerush, is a species of sedge native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, wind-pollinated flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces tiny, dark brown seeds.

Eleocharis vincentina plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
