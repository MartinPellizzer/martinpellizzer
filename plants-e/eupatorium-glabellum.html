Eupatorium glabellum

Eupatorium glabellum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported cooling and anti-inflammatory properties.
