Echeveria halbingeri

Echeveria halbingeri, commonly known as the Halbinger Echeveria, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray to silver-green in color, often with a powdery coating called farina. The plant produces vibrant purple-pink flowers on tall, branching stems during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its compact shape and coloration.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
