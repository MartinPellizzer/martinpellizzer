Epilobium duclouxii

Epilobium duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, often near streams or in wet meadows. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually square in cross-section.

Epilobium duclouxii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
