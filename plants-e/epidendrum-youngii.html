Epidendrum youngii

Epidendrum youngii, commonly known as the Young's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and violet. The plant typically has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum youngii is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
