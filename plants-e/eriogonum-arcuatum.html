Eriogonum arcuatum

Eriogonum arcuatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum arcuatum is an important part of local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
