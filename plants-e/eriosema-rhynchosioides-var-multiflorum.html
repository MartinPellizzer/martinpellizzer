Eriosema rhynchosioides var. multiflorum

Eriosema rhynchosioides var. multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered eriosema, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
