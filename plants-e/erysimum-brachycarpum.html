Erysimum brachycarpum

Erysimum brachycarpum, commonly known as the narrowpod wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in small, glandular hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
