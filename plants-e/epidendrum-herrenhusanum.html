Epidendrum herrenhusanum

Epidendrum herrenhusanum, commonly known as the Herrenhusen's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with erect or pendant stems that can produce multiple flowering spikes. The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes, with showy, often fragrant blooms in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

This species is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
