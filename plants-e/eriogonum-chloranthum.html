Eriogonum chloranthum

Eriogonum chloranthum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Eriogonum chloranthum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native range.
