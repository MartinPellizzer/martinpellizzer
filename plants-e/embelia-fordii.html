Embelia fordii

Embelia fordii, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The berries are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
