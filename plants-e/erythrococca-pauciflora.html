Erythrococca pauciflora

Erythrococca pauciflora, commonly known as the red cocculus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, which are borne on slender stems. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate in shape. Erythrococca pauciflora is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the genus Erythrococca within the family Cucurbitaceae.
