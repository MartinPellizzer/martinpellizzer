Eragrostis villamontana

Eragrostis villamontana, commonly known as the Argentine wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to golden florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
