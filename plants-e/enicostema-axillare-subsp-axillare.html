Enicostema axillare subsp. axillare

Enicostema axillare subsp.

axillare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false dandelion, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, reaching a height of up to 50 centimeters.

The plant features linear, often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and yellow, clustered in dense heads, and it produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal.

It is characterized by its fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed soils.
