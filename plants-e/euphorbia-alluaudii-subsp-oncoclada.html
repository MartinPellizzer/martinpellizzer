Euphorbia alluaudii subsp. oncoclada

Euphorbia alluaudii subsp.

oncoclada, commonly known as the Oncoclada euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and spiny, paddle-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a distinctive cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
