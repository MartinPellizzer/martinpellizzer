Einsteinia sericantha

Einsteinia sericantha, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, mat-like growth habit with grayish-green, fleshy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its succulent leaves and deep root system allow it to store water and thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
