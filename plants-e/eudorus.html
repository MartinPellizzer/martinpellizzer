Eudorus

Eudorus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Eudorus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and delicate appearance.
