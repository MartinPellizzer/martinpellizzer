Eugenia zeylanica

Eugenia zeylanica, commonly known as the Java apple or Java strawberry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia zeylanica is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which are used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
