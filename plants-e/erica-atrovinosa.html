Erica atrovinosa

Erica atrovinosa, commonly known as the black heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, dark purple flowers that bloom in spring. Erica atrovinosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
