Erica vestiflua

Erica vestiflua, commonly known as the white heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica vestiflua thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
