Eclipta filicaulis

Eclipta filicaulis, commonly known as the slender false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

Eclipta filicaulis is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
