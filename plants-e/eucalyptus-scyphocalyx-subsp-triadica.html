Eucalyptus scyphocalyx subsp. triadica

Eucalyptus scyphocalyx subsp.

triadica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale yellow to cream in color. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to dry, inland environments. Eucalyptus scyphocalyx subsp.

triadica is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its resilience and ornamental qualities.
