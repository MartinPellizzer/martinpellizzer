Euterpe

Euterpe, commonly known as the açaí palm, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly the Amazon and Orinoco river basins.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are rich in antioxidants and nutrients. The açaí fruit is harvested from the infrutescent clusters that develop at the end of the plant's leafy stems.

Due to its ecological and nutritional significance, Euterpe plays an important role in both local ecosystems and global markets.
