Echinops ritro subsp. ritro

Echinops ritro subsp.

ritro, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, giving it its genus name "Echinops.".

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
