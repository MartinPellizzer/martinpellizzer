Ericoides viridipurpureum

Ericoides viridipurpureum, commonly known as the purple-green heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features dense clusters of small, leathery leaves that are typically a mix of green and purple hues. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands or rocky slopes.

Ericoides viridipurpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
