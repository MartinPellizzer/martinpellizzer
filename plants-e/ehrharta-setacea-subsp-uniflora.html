Ehrharta setacea subsp. uniflora

Ehrharta setacea subsp.

uniflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor soil conditions.
