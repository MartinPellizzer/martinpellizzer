Eucalyptus cyanoclada

Eucalyptus cyanoclada, commonly known as the blue gum, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale blue-gray bark that peels in thin, flaky layers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The tree is known for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and for their antiseptic properties.
