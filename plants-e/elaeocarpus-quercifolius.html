Elaeocarpus quercifolius

Elaeocarpus quercifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of an oak, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and furniture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
