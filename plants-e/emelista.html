Emelista

Emelista, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cup-shaped seed pods that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Emelista is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
