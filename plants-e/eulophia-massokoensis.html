Eulophia massokoensis

Eulophia massokoensis, commonly known as the Massoko orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of eastern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its upright, grass-like growth habit. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to the specific climatic conditions of its native habitat.

Eulophia massokoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its region.
