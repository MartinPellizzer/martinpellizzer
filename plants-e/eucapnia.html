Eucapnia

Eucapnia, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Eucapnia contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to both humans and animals.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and medicinal studies.
