Ellisia microcalyx

Ellisia microcalyx, commonly known as the dwarf lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red at the base. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its delicate appearance and early spring blooming make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
