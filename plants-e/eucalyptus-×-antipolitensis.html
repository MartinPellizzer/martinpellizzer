Eucalyptus × antipolitensis

Eucalyptus × antipolitensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and peels off in strips, revealing a mottled pattern of white and brown.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodland areas.
