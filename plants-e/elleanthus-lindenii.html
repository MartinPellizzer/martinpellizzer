Elleanthus lindenii

Elleanthus lindenii, commonly known as the Lindens' grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in dense clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

Elleanthus lindenii is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
