Eugenia libanensis

Eugenia libanensis, commonly known as the Lebanon eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
