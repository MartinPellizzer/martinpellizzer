Erophila minutissima

Erophila minutissima, commonly known as the minute erophila, is a small, annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its tiny, clustered flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, pale green to reddish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it is known for its rapid growth and short life cycle.

Due to its small size and limited distribution, it is not widely cultivated but is of interest in botanical studies.
