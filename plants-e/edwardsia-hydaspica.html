Edwardsia hydaspica

Edwardsia hydaspica, commonly known as the Himalayan water plant, is a rare aquatic plant found in the cold mountain streams of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and small white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in clear, fast-flowing water and requires a cool, temperate climate to flourish. Its root system is adapted to anchor in rocky substrates, allowing it to withstand strong currents.

Edwardsia hydaspica plays a vital role in maintaining the ecological balance of its alpine habitat.
