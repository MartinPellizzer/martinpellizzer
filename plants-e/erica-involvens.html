Erica involvens

Erica involvens, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Erica involvens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
