Eugenia subobliqua

Eugenia subobliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible berries.

Eugenia subobliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
