Endespermum zeylanicum

Endespermum zeylanicum, commonly known as Indian breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a fruit that is fibrous and edible, often used in local cuisines. Its seeds are encased in a thick, starchy pulp, which is a key feature of its botanical structure.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
