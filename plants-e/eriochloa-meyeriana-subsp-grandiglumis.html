Eriochloa meyeriana subsp. grandiglumis

Eriochloa meyeriana subsp.

grandiglumis, commonly known as the large glume eriochloa, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets with long, slender glumes.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
