Eupatorium reflexum

Eupatorium reflexum, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clustered umbels, which bloom from late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in herbal medicine, though it can be toxic to livestock.
