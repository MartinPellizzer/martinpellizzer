Eupatorium rhinanthaceum var. latisquamulosum

Eupatorium rhinanthaceum var. latisquamulosum, commonly known as the broad-scale eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
