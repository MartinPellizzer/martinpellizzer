Eriogonum spathulatum var. kayeae

Eriogonum spathulatum var. kayeae, commonly known as the Kaye's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a low, spreading growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped or spatulate, with serrated edges. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland dunes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
