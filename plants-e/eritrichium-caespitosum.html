Eritrichium caespitosum

Eritrichium caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, grass-like foliage that forms compact tufts. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
