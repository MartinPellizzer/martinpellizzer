Embothrium rurale

Embothrium rurale, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to central and southern Chile.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright red, lantern-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant is adapted to the temperate climate of its native region and is often found in coastal and valley areas.
