Ephedra monosperma

Ephedra monosperma, commonly known as the single-seeded ephedra, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ephedraceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of China, Mongolia, and Russia. The plant is characterized by its jointed, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing a single seed.

Ephedra monosperma is adapted to survive in harsh environments, with a slow growth rate and the ability to store water in its stem tissues.
