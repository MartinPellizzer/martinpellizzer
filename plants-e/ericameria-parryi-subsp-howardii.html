Ericameria parryi subsp. howardii

Ericameria parryi subsp.

howardii, commonly known as the desert gumweed, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open habitats. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

The species is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.
