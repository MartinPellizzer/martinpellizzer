Epidendrum ternatum

Epidendrum ternatum, commonly known as the Three-leaf Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Epidendrum ternatum is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its low maintenance requirements and attractive appearance.
