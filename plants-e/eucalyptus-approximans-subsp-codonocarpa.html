Eucalyptus approximans subsp. codonocarpa

Eucalyptus approximans subsp.

codonocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and smooth, often fissured bark.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The subspecies is noted for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
