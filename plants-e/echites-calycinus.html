Echites calycinus

Echites calycinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes make it an invasive species in many areas.
