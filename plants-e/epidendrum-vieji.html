Epidendrum vieji

Epidendrum vieji, commonly known as the old man orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its blooming period occurs during the warmer months, often in spring or early summer.

Epidendrum vieji is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
