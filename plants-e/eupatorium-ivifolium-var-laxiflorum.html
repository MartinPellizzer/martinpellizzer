Eupatorium ivifolium var. laxiflorum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. laxiflorum, commonly known as swamp milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features showy, pink to purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically reddish and hairy. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal historical uses.
