Epipactis latifolia var. thomsonii

Epipactis latifolia var. thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's broad-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent lips. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex root system and relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.
