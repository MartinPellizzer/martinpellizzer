Eriogonum ternatum var. congdonii

Eriogonum ternatum var. congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered blooms of white to pale pink petals. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, winged fruits called achenes.
