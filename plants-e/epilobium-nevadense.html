Epilobium nevadense

Epilobium nevadense, commonly known as Sierra buckweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are generally narrow and hairy, adapted to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Sierra buckweed plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.
