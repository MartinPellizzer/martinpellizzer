Emilia zairensis

Emilia zairensis, commonly known as the Zaire emilia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, daisy-like blooms with bright yellow disc florets surrounded by white ray florets. Emilia zairensis typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and seeds for birds and small mammals.
