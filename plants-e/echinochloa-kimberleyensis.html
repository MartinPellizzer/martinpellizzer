Echinochloa kimberleyensis

Echinochloa kimberleyensis, commonly known as Kimberley grass, is a perennial grass species native to northern Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and riparian habitats, often found in swampy areas and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
