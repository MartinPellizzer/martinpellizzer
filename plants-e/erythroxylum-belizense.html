Erythroxylum belizense

Erythroxylum belizense, commonly known as the Belize pepper plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central America, particularly Belize, Guatemala, and southern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, elliptical leaves, and clusters of small, bright red berries. The berries contain a mild stimulant, similar to caffeine, and have been used traditionally for their medicinal properties.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and has cultural significance in the regions where it grows.
