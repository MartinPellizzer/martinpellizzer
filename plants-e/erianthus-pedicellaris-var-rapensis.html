Erianthus pedicellaris var. rapensis

Erianthus pedicellaris var. rapensis, commonly known as the African finger grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening and for erosion control.
