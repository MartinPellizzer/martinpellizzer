Elephantopus scaber var. coeruleus

Elephantopus scaber var. coeruleus, commonly known as blue elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the warmer months.
