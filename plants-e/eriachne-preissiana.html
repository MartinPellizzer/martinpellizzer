Eriachne preissiana

Eriachne preissiana, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water retention.

The species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
