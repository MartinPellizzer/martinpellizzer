Euonymus japonicus var. carrierei

Euonymus japonicus var. carrierei, commonly known as Japanese spurge, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in late spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are attractive to birds. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.

Its foliage is also valued for its ability to withstand urban conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
