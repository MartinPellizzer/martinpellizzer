Embothrium cinereum

Embothrium cinereum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
