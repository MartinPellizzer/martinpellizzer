Eremochloa truncata

Eremochloa truncata, commonly known as the African grass or red grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish coloration, especially in its inflorescence, which gives it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

Eremochloa truncata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique color and drought tolerance.
