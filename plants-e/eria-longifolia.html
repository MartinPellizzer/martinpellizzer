Eria longifolia

Eria longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that is often brightly colored.

Eria longifolia thrives in humid environments and is often found growing in shaded forest undergrowth.
