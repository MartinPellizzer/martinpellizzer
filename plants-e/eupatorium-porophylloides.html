Eupatorium porophylloides

Eupatorium porophylloides, commonly known as the purplestem thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
