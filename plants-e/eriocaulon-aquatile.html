Eriocaulon aquatile

Eriocaulon aquatile, commonly known as the aquatic eriocaulon, is a small, herbaceous plant that thrives in wetland and marsh environments.

It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spike-like inflorescences. Its roots are fibrous and help stabilize the soil in shallow water habitats.

Eriocaulon aquatile plays a role in water filtration and provides habitat for small aquatic organisms.
