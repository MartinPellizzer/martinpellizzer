Evolvulus mucronatus

Evolvulus mucronatus, commonly known as the small-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a trailing vine that produces small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use, though it can be invasive in some regions.
