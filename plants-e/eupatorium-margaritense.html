Eupatorium margaritense

Eupatorium margaritense, commonly known as the margaritense blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium margaritense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
