Eupatorium endytum

Eupatorium endytum, commonly known as New Zealand feverfew, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded areas of forests. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eupatorium endytum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
