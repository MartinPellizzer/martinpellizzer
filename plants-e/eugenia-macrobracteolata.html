Eugenia macrobracteolata

Eugenia macrobracteolata, commonly known as the large bract eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant's ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
