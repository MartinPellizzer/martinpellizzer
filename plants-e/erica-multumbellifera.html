Erica multumbellifera

Erica multumbellifera, commonly known as the many-bracted heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica multumbellifera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
