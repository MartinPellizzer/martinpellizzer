Eupatorium ellipticum

Eupatorium ellipticum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are elliptic in shape, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and are usually hairy underneath.

Eupatorium ellipticum is often found in moist to wet soils in woodlands and along stream banks.
