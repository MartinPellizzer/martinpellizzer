Echinopsis cristata var. purpurea

Echinopsis cristata var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple crown cactus, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that often form a crown-like shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the warmer months, typically in the evening. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The varietal name 'purpurea' refers to the distinctive purple coloration of its flowers and stems.
