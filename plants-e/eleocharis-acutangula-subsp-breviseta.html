Eleocharis acutangula subsp. breviseta

Eleocharis acutangula subsp.

breviseta, commonly known as the narrow-seta spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water or saturated soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of dense clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
