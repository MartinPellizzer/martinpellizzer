Elymus maroccanus

Elymus maroccanus, commonly known as Moroccan wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Elymus maroccanus is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
