Escallonia illinita var. eu-illinita

Escallonia illinita var. eu-illinita, commonly known as the European escallonia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn bronze in the fall, and it produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers in late spring. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden conditions.

While it is not widely cultivated, it holds ecological significance in its native habitat.
