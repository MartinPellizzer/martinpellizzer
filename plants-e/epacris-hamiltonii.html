Epacris hamiltonii

Epacris hamiltonii, commonly known as the Hamilton's epacris, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris hamiltonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
