Eupatorium coelestinum

Eupatorium coelestinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which appear in late summer to early fall, are blue to purple in color and produce a mist-like appearance.

Eupatorium coelestinum is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
