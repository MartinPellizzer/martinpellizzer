Elodea linearis

Elodea linearis, commonly known as waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by small, oval-shaped fruits. Elodea linearis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to water organisms.

It is also widely used in scientific research for studying plant cell structure and function.
