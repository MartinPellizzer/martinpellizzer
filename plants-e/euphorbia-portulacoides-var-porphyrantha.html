Euphorbia portulacoides var. porphyrantha

Euphorbia portulacoides var. porphyrantha, commonly known as the Porphyrantha euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and unique floral structure.
