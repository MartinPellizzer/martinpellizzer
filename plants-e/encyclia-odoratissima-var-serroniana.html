Encyclia odoratissima var. serroniana

Encyclia odoratissima var. serroniana, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, supporting its growth in varied environmental conditions. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming during the dry season. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water conservation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
