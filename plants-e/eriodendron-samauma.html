Eriodendron samauma

Eriodendron samauma, commonly known as the Malabar kino, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark that exudes a reddish sap when cut. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated pods.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
