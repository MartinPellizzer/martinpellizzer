Erica perplexa

Erica perplexa, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate appearance to its evergreen foliage. Erica perplexa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
