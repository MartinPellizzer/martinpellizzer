Erica coventryana

Erica coventryana, commonly known as the Coventry heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica coventryana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
