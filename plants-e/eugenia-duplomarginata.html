Eugenia duplomarginata

Eugenia duplomarginata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cherry myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
