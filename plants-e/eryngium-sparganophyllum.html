Eryngium sparganophyllum

Eryngium sparganophyllum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Eryngium sparganophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
