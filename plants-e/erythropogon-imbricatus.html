Erythropogon imbricatus

Erythropogon imbricatus, commonly known as the red grass or red panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive reddish tinge in the foliage, especially during the flowering season. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant.
