Equisetum alpestre

Equisetum alpestre, commonly known as the alpine horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. This species thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils, often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its reproductive structures include spore-bearing cones at the base of the stem.

The plant is known for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
