Eriocaulon supinum

Eriocaulon supinum, commonly known as the flat mossy grass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short, leafless stalks. It thrives in wet, shady environments such as marshes and swampy areas.

Eriocaulon supinum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
