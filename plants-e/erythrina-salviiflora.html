Erythrina salviiflora

Erythrina salviiflora, commonly known as the flame tree or red tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and vibrant red flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its ornamental value is due to its showy blooms and distinctive foliage, making it a popular choice in horticulture.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
