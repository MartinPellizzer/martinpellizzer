Ervatamia balansae

Ervatamia balansae, commonly known as the Balansae sandmat, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its succulent leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
