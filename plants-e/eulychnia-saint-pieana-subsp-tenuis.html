Eulychnia saint-pieana subsp. tenuis

Eulychnia saint-pieana subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as the slender eulychnia, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and sparse, needle-like spines.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its succulent stem.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh, dry environments and requires minimal water and well-drained soil.
