Echinopsis pelecyrhachis

Echinopsis pelecyrhachis, commonly known as the yellow star cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, ribbed stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water. Its flowers are typically nocturnal, opening in the evening and closing by dawn.

Echinopsis pelecyrhachis is popular among cactus enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
