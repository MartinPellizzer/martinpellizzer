Eria lobata

Eria lobata, commonly known as the waxy leaf orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the undergrowth of tropical forests. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Eria lobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
