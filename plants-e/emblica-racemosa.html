Emblica racemosa

Emblica racemosa, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are alternately arranged and have serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into bright orange, fleshy fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins.

It is widely cultivated for its medicinal and nutritional value in traditional systems of medicine.
