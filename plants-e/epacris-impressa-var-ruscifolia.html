Epacris impressa var. ruscifolia

Epacris impressa var. ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
