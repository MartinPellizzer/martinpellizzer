Esula characias

Esula characias, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Esula characias is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
