Eragrostis jerichoensis

Eragrostis jerichoensis, commonly known as Jericho grass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid conditions and can tolerate poor soil conditions.

Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, contributing to its ecological importance in local ecosystems.
