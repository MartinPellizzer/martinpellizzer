Echeveria weinbergii

Echeveria weinbergii, commonly known as the Weinbergii Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a powdery coating known as farina. The plant produces vibrant violet or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its compact form.

Due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
