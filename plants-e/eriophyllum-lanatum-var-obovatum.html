Eriophyllum lanatum var. obovatum

Eriophyllum lanatum var. obovatum, commonly known as the Woolly Sunflower, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features dense, woolly silver-gray foliage that provides a striking visual contrast to its bright yellow flower heads. The plant produces small, composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
