Epidendrum odoratissimum

Epidendrum odoratissimum, commonly known as the fragrant epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinctive red or orange lip, and they emit a sweet, spicy fragrance.

Epidendrum odoratissimum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique scent.
