Epipactis latifolia subsp. violacea

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

violacea, commonly known as the violet helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and striking violet or purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often clustered in a spike, and it has a complex root system adapted for nutrient absorption.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
