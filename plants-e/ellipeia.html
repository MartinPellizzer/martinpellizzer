Ellipeia

Ellipeia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Ellipeia grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
