Elaeocarpus sericopetalus

Elaeocarpus sericopetalus, commonly known as the silky-petaled elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, silky-haired petals. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
