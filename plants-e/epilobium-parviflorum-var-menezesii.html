Epilobium parviflorum var. menezesii

Epilobium parviflorum var. menezesii, commonly known as the Menezes' willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
