Erica follicularis var. inflata

Erica follicularis var. inflata, commonly known as the inflated heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflated, leathery leaves are a distinguishing feature, and the flowers bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It is often cultivated for its hardiness and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
