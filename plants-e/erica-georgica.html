Erica georgica

Erica georgica, commonly known as the Georgian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica georgica thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
