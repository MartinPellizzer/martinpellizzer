Euphorbia linguiformis

Euphorbia linguiformis, commonly known as the tongue-shaped spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branching stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia linguiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
