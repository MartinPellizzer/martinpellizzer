Eupatorium pinnatisectum

Eupatorium pinnatisectum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Eupatorium pinnatisectum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
