Erica kingaensis subsp. leleupiana

Erica kingaensis subsp.

leleupiana, commonly known as the Leleupia Erica, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa. It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
