Euploca nexosa

Euploca nexosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Euploca nexosa is adapted to thrive in moist, shaded environments, such as along stream banks and in wetlands.

Its growth habit and appearance make it a distinctive component of its native wetland ecosystems.
