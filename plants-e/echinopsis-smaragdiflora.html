Echinopsis smaragdiflora

Echinopsis smaragdiflora, commonly known as the Emerald Column Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its smooth, green stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived but highly attractive to pollinators. It has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice for experienced cactus growers.
