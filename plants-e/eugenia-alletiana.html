Eugenia alletiana

Eugenia alletiana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed.

The tree is valued for its hard, aromatic wood, which has been traditionally used in furniture and carving.
