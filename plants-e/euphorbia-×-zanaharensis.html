Euphorbia × zanaharensis

Euphorbia × zanaharensis, commonly known as the Zanzibar tree, is a hybrid species native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts, which are often the showy part visible to the eye. It thrives in coastal climates with well-drained soil and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
