Epidendrum dolichostachyum

Epidendrum dolichostachyum, commonly known as the long-stemmed epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
