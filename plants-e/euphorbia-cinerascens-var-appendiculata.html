Euphorbia cinerascens var. appendiculata

Euphorbia cinerascens var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem with numerous cylindrical, leafless stems that are covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
