Echinocactus staplesiae

Echinocactus staplesiae, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, making it highly drought-resistant.

Echinocactus staplesiae is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
