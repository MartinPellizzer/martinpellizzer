Erigeron caulinifolius

Erigeron caulinifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
