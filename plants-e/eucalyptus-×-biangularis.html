Eucalyptus × biangularis

Eucalyptus × biangularis, commonly known as the angular eucalyptus, is a hybrid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is a medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

Eucalyptus × biangularis is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is commonly used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
