Epidendrum rocalderianum

Epidendrum rocalderianum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and in well-drained soils, adapting to arid and semi-arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Epidendrum rocalderianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique floral display.
