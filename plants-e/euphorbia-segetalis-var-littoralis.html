Euphorbia segetalis var. littoralis

Euphorbia segetalis var. littoralis, commonly known as the coastal spurge, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by yellowish bracts. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to saline and windy environments. It has a shallow root system, allowing it to stabilize sandy or coastal soils.

Its botanical features make it a hardy, drought-resistant plant suitable for coastal landscaping.
