Encyclia recurvata

Encyclia recurvata, commonly known as the recurved orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its compact, terrestrial growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, and it has a short, thick rhizome.

Encyclia recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
