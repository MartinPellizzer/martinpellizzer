Euphorbia celastroides var. typica

Euphorbia celastroides var. typica, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, often spiny stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified floral structures characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
