Echium rosulatum subsp. campestre

Echium rosulatum subsp.

campestre, commonly known as the field fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

The flowers are arranged in a compact head and are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
