Erica condensata var. condensata

Erica condensata var. condensata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Erica condensata var. condensata is well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover in coastal or Mediterranean climates.
