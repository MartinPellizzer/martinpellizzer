Eriosema yerbalium f. longifolium

Eriosema yerbalium f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved eriosema, is a variant of the species Eriosema yerbalium, which belongs to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant typically produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, adding to its ornamental value. It is native to South Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
