Eriosema montanum var. brevipedunculatum

Eriosema montanum var. brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the mountain eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, upright stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are softly hairy on both surfaces. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, making it resilient in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is often found in grassland and mountainous regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
