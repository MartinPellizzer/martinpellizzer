Eryngium caerulescens

Eryngium caerulescens, commonly known as blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are composed of many small, tubular petals that surround a central disk.

Eryngium caerulescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
