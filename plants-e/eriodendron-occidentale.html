Eriodendron occidentale

Eriodendron occidentale, commonly known as the western eriodendron, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations and its resilience to arid conditions.
