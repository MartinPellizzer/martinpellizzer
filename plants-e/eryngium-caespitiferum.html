Eryngium caespitiferum

Eryngium caespitiferum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bristly, silver-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss.

Eryngium caespitiferum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun.
