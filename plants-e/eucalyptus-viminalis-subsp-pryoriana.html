Eucalyptus viminalis subsp. pryoriana

Eucalyptus viminalis subsp.

pryoriana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a subshrub or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom.

The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging landscapes.
