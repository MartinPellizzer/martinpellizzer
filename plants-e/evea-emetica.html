Evea emetica

Evea emetica, commonly known as the vomiting vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, palmate-lobed foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its roots and leaves contain toxic compounds, which have historically been used in traditional medicine.
