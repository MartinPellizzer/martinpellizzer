Echinops polycephalus

Echinops polycephalus, commonly known as the Persian Spear, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown of thorns, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers in early summer, which are followed by the distinctive spiky structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Echinops polycephalus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
