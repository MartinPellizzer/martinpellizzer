Eugenia robinsoniana

Eugenia robinsoniana, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and its ability to thrive in saline environments. Its small, fragrant white flowers are followed by edible, dark purple berries.

Eugenia robinsoniana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
