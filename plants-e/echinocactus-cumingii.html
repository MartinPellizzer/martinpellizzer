Echinocactus cumingii

Echinocactus cumingii, commonly known as the Golden Barrel Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny, cylindrical stem that resembles a barrel. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. Its thick, waxy skin helps it store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
