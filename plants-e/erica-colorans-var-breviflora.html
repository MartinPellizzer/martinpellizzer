Erica colorans var. breviflora

Erica colorans var. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf heather, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features small, dense clusters of bright pink to magenta flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically a deep green, providing a lush backdrop for its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
