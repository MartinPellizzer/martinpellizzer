Erica gnaphalodes

Erica gnaphalodes, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its small, needle-like leaves covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Erica gnaphalodes produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
