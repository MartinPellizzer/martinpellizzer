Enteropogon monostachyos subsp. africanus

Enteropogon monostachyos subsp.

africanus, commonly known as the African false rice, is a grass species native to sub-Saharan Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces inflorescences that consist of a single spikelet, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found near rivers and lakes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and ecosystem.
