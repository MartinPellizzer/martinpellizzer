Euphorbia tamaulipasana

Euphorbia tamaulipasana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northeastern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its thick, fleshy stems. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, further reducing water loss.

Euphorbia tamaulipasana is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
