Eugenioides urceolare

Eugenioides urceolare, commonly known as the urn-shaped eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, urn-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
