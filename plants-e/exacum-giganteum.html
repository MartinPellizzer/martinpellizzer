Exacum giganteum

Exacum giganteum, commonly known as the giant wax plant, is a striking flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant produces long, slender stems that can grow up to several feet in height, making it a dramatic addition to gardens. Its foliage consists of broad, waxy leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture.

Exacum giganteum thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
