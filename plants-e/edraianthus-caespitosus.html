Edraianthus caespitosus

Edraianthus caespitosus, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate blooms and drought tolerance.
