Ericoides spicata

Ericoides spicata, commonly known as the spike heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form with dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or woodland areas.

Ericoides spicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
