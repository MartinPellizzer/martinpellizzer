Eugenia farneyi

Eugenia farneyi, commonly known as the Farney's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters and features glossy, elliptical leaves with a dark green hue. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its botanical characteristics make it a unique species within the Myrtaceae family.

It is often found in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
