Echinocactus intertextus

Echinocactus intertextus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
