Euphorbia × angustifrons

Euphorbia × angustifrons, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, narrow leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia. The plant is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can tolerate drought conditions due to its water-storing stems.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to livestock and humans if ingested.
