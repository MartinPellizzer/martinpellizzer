Eupatorium macropus

Eupatorium macropus, commonly known as the large-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Eupatorium macropus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
