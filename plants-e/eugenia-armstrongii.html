Eugenia armstrongii

Eugenia armstrongii, commonly known as the Armstrong's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and produces small, edible fruits that are similar to guavas. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.
