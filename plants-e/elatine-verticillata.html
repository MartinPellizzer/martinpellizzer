Elatine verticillata

Elatine verticillata, commonly known as water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant that thrives in wetlands, ditches, and the edges of ponds.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating or submerged leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces tiny, white flowers that are typically clustered in a spire-like structure, which helps in attracting pollinators. Elatine verticillata is a member of the Elatinaceae family, known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed water bodies.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
