Erica dracomontana

Erica dracomontana, commonly known as the dragon's mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its floral display, which can range from white to pink or red, occurs primarily during the summer months.

Erica dracomontana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
