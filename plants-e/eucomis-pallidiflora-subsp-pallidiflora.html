Eucomis pallidiflora subsp. pallidiflora

Eucomis pallidiflora subsp.

pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered bulbous plant, is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family. It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence composed of white to pale pink flowers.

The plant features a bulbous base with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
