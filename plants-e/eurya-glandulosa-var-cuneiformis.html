Eurya glandulosa var. cuneiformis

Eurya glandulosa var. cuneiformis, commonly known as the cuneiform waxleaf eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, dark red drupe that matures in late summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
