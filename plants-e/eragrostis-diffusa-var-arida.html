Eragrostis diffusa var. arida

Eragrostis diffusa var. arida, commonly known as desert lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its wiry, erect growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in restoration projects in dry environments.
