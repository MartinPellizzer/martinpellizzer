Echinops armatus var. papillosus

Echinops armatus var. papillosus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical heads composed of tightly packed bracts. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are sturdy and upright.

This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
