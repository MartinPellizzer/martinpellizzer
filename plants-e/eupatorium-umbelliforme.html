Eupatorium umbelliforme

Eupatorium umbelliforme, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white, umbellate flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
