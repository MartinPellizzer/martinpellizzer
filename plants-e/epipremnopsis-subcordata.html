Epipremnopsis subcordata

Epipremnopsis subcordata, commonly known as the silver-veined plectranthus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite and have a velvety texture, while the flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

It prefers warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
