Enterolobium contortisiliquum

Enterolobium contortisiliquum, commonly known as the "Bottle tree" or "Cup tree," is a tropical legume native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, bottle-shaped trunk and dense, feathery foliage. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its nitrogen-fixing ability makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
