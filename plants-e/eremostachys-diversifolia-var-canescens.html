Eremostachys diversifolia var. canescens

Eremostachys diversifolia var. canescens, commonly known as the hairy eremostachys, is a member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
