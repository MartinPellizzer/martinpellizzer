Eugenia psidioidea

Eugenia psidioidea, commonly known as the red-fruit eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Eugenia psidioidea is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
