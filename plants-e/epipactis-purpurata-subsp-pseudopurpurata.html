Epipactis purpurata subsp. pseudopurpurata

Epipactis purpurata subsp.

pseudopurpurata, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are often violet or purple in color, with a distinctive hooded structure. It has a complex life cycle involving a symbiotic relationship with specific mycorrhizal fungi.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
