Eryngium woodii

Eryngium woodii, commonly known as the "ghost flower," is a rare and endangered plant species native to the deserts of Jordan.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue-green foliage and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom during the spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with deep roots that allow it to survive in extreme drought conditions. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is critically endangered and protected by conservation efforts.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of scientific interest and botanical study.
