Eucalyptus effusa

Eucalyptus effusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Eucalyptus effusa is often found in dry, sandy soils in regions with a Mediterranean climate.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
