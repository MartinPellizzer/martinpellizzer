Euphorbia brittingeri

Euphorbia brittingeri, commonly known as the Brittinger's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures rather than true flowers. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Euphorbia brittingeri is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant.
