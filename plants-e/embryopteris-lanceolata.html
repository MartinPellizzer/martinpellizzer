Embryopteris lanceolata

It is characterized by its small, floating leaves that are typically divided into four lobes, resembling a cloverleaf shape. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are located on the underside of the leaves, facilitating spore dispersal.

Embryopteris lanceolata plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
