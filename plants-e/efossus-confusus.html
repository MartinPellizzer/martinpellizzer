Efossus confusus

Efossus confusus, commonly known as the confused efossus, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its humid, shaded habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming during the early morning hours. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant adaptation.

Due to habitat destruction, Efossus confusus is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation reserves.
