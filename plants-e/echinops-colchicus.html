Echinops colchicus

Echinops colchicus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, hence its genus name "Echinops.". The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are blue to purple in color and appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops colchicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
