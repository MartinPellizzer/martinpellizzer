Eupatorium venosum

Eupatorium venosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium venosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
