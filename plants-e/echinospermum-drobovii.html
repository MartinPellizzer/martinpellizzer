Echinospermum drobovii

Echinospermum drobovii, commonly known as the roughleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, hairy stems and large, showy yellow flowers. The plant produces composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets, typical of the sunflower family. Its leaves are usually rough and serrated, contributing to its common name.

Echinospermum drobovii is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
