Erigeron andicola f. humilior

Erigeron andicola f. humilior, commonly known as the Andean fleabane, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically features narrow, hairy leaves and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
