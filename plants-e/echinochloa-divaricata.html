Echinochloa divaricata

Echinochloa divaricata, commonly known as slender barnyard grass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
