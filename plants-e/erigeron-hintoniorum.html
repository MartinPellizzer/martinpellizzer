Erigeron hintoniorum

Erigeron hintoniorum, commonly known as Hinton's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features slender stems with opposite leaves that are usually hairy and lance-shaped.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing white to pale yellow petals and yellow central disk flowers.
