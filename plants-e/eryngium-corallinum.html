Eryngium corallinum

Eryngium corallinum, commonly known as the coral eryngium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and produces a compact, globular inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eryngium corallinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
