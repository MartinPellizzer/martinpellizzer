Eugenia merrillii

Eugenia merrillii, commonly known as the Hainan guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly the Hainan Island, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
