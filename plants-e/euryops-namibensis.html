Euryops namibensis

Euryops namibensis, commonly known as the Namib Desert daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season. The plant has adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions with deep root systems and waxy leaves that reduce water loss. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is considered a valuable species for studying plant resilience in harsh environments.
