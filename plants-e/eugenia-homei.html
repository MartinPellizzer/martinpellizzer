Eugenia homei

Eugenia homei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia homei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
