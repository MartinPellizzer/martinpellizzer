Epidendrum capitellatum

Epidendrum capitellatum, commonly known as the Cape Cod orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky areas. Its roots are fibrous and often form a compact mat near the soil surface.

Epidendrum capitellatum is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
