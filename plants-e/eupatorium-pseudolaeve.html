Eupatorium pseudolaeve

Eupatorium pseudolaeve, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. Its flowers are small and blue to purple in color, arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Eupatorium pseudolaeve thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.
