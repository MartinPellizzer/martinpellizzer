Echinocactus armatissimus

Echinocactus armatissimus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a cylindrical, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring or early summer.

The cactus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
