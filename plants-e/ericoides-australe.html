Ericoides australe

Ericoides australe, commonly known as the Southern Ericoid, is a small shrub native to the southern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heaths, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Ericoides australe is adapted to coastal and sandy environments, making it a resilient species in harsh, sun-exposed conditions.
