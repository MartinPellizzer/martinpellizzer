Escallonia rubra var. pubescens

Escallonia rubra var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Escallonia rubra var. pubescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
