Eriogonum strictum subsp. proliferum

Eriogonum strictum subsp.

proliferum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a shrub or subshrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically has gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are usually pink to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
