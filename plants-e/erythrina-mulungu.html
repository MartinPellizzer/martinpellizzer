Erythrina mulungu

Erythrina mulungu, commonly known as mulungu, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. Its bark and leaves contain alkaloids, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Erythrina mulungu is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
