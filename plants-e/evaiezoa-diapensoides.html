Evaiezoa diapensoides

Evaiezoa diapensoides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive silver sheen on the underside. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
