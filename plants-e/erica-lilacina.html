Erica lilacina

Erica lilacina, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Erica lilacina typically blooms in spring, displaying shades of pink to purple.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
