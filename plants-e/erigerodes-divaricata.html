Erigerodes divaricata

Erigerodes divaricata, commonly known as the variable erigeron, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
