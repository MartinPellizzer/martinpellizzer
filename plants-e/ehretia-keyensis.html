Ehretia keyensis

Ehretia keyensis, commonly known as the Key Island hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Florida Keys.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and mangrove swamps, thriving in saline and humid conditions. The plant features showy, pentagonal flowers with five petals, often in shades of pink or white. Its leaves are ovate and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ehretia keyensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.
