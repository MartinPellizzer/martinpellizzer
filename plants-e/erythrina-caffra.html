Erythrina caffra

Erythrina caffra, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange floral clusters that appear at the ends of its branches. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves and produces long, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species.

Erythrina caffra is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which benefits soil fertility.
