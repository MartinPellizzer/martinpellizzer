Eugenia zeylanica var. laxiflora

Eugenia zeylanica var. laxiflora, commonly known as the large-leaved clove or Indian clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a reddish or purple tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their essential oils.
