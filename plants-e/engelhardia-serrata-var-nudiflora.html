Engelhardia serrata var. nudiflora

Engelhardia serrata var. nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered engelhardia, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and compact growth habit.
