Elaeocarpus rigidus

Elaeocarpus rigidus, commonly known as the rigid elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that are typically covered in a thick, leathery skin. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
