Escallonia illinita

Escallonia illinita, commonly known as the Illinita bush, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Escalloniaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Escallonia illinita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
