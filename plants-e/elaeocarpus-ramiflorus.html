Elaeocarpus ramiflorus

Elaeocarpus ramiflorus, commonly known as the ramiflorous elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
