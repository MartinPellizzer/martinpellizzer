Elymus panormitanus subsp. sirouanus

Elymus panormitanus subsp.

sirouanus, commonly known as the Sirouan reed, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along riverbanks and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
