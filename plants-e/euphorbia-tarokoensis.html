Euphorbia tarokoensis

Euphorbia tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that serve as a means of attracting pollinators. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the subtropical climate of its native region.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
