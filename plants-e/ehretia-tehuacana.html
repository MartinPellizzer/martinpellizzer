Ehretia tehuacana

Ehretia tehuacana, commonly known as the Mexican ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and landscapes.
