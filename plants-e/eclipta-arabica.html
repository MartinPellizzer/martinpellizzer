Eclipta arabica

Eclipta arabica, commonly known as false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow, dandelion-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and grows up to 1 meter in height. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Eclipta arabica is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
