Eriogonum trachygonum subsp. taxifolium

Eriogonum trachygonum subsp.

taxifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb that produces seeds in late summer, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and a valuable component of native plant communities.
