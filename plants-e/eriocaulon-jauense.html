Eriocaulon jauense

Eriocaulon jauense, commonly known as the Jauense spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its reproductive structures are often hidden beneath the leaves, making it less conspicuous than other rush species.

Eriocaulon jauense plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
