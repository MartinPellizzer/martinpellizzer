Encyclia argentinensis

Encyclia argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine everlasting, is a terrestrial orchid native to South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, typically blooming in spring. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil, allowing it to thrive in humid environments.

Encyclia argentinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
