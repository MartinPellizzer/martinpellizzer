Echinochloa utilis

Echinochloa utilis, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a widespread annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its coarse, serrated leaves and spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the plant. This plant thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and wetlands. Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a significant weed in many crops.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
