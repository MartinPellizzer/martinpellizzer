Eugenia subcorymbosa

Eugenia subcorymbosa, commonly known as the small-fruited guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible, aromatic fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
