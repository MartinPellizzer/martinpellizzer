Eragrostis lugens var. flaccida

Eragrostis lugens var. flaccida, commonly known as slender false wheatgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its fibrous roots make it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control in degraded soils.

It is often used in restoration ecology due to its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
