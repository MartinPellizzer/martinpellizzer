Elymus praeruptus

Elymus praeruptus, commonly known as Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in the summer months. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Elymus praeruptus is often used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop in its native regions.
