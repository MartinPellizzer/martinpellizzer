Ericoila capitata

Ericoila capitata, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. Ericoila capitata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
