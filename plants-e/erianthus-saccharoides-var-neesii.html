Erianthus saccharoides var. neesii

Erianthus saccharoides var. neesii, commonly known as Nees' sugarcane grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its robust growth and high biomass production. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. It is often cultivated for its potential in biofuel production and as a forage crop.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a valuable species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
