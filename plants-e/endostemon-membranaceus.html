Endostemon membranaceus

Endostemon membranaceus, commonly known as the paperflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery bracts that resemble petals, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it blooms during the cooler months.

Endostemon membranaceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
