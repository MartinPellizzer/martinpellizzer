Eugenia microcarpos var. mini

Eugenia microcarpos var. mini, commonly known as the dwarf pineapple guava, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its fruit is a small, edible berry with a sweet and tangy flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and mild fruit.
