Epidendrum pterocarpum

Epidendrum pterocarpum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, linear leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and red. Its root system is adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Epidendrum pterocarpum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
