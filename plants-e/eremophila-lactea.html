Eremophila lactea

Eremophila lactea, commonly known as the milk bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically shrub-like, with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila lactea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
