Epilobium tiarogynum

Epilobium tiarogynum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a sprawling growth habit with a mat-forming structure, often covering the ground densely. The plant has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late summer.

Epilobium tiarogynum is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.
