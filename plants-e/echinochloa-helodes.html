Echinochloa helodes

Echinochloa helodes, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often mistaken for wheat or other grasses, making it a common weed in agricultural fields. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it can become a significant pest in cereal crops.
