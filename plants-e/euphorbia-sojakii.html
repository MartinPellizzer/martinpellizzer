Euphorbia sojakii

Euphorbia sojakii, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or green. The plant has a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it retain water in dry environments. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, which further minimize water loss.

Euphorbia sojakii is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
