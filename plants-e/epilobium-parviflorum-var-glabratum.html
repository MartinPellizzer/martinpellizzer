Epilobium parviflorum var. glabratum

Epilobium parviflorum var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth willow herb, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
