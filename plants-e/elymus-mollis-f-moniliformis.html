Elymus mollis f. moniliformis

Elymus mollis f. moniliformis, commonly known as the silvergrass or soft wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with silky, hair-like bristles. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
