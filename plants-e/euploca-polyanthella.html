Euploca polyanthella

Euploca polyanthella, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or violet in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euploca polyanthella thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
