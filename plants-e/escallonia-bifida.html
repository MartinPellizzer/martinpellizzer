Escallonia bifida

Escallonia bifida, commonly known as the splitleaf escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Escallonia bifida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
