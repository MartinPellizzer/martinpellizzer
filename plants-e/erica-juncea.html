Erica juncea

Erica juncea, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. Erica juncea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, mat-like growth.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
