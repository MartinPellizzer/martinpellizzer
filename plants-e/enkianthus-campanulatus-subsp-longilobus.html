Enkianthus campanulatus subsp. longilobus

Enkianthus campanulatus subsp.

longilobus, commonly known as the long-lipped enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
