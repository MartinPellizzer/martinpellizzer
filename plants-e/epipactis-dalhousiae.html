Epipactis dalhousiae

Epipactis dalhousiae, commonly known as the Dalhousie helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has a complex life cycle involving mycorrhizal associations with fungi to aid in nutrient uptake.

Epipactis dalhousiae is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
