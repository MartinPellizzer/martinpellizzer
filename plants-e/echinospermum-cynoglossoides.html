Echinospermum cynoglossoides

Echinospermum cynoglossoides, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet flowers. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a schizocarp, splitting into two one-seeded parts when mature.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
