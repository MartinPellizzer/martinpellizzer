Elleanthus brasiliensis

Elleanthus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian grass or Brazilian silver grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, silver-gray blades. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is often found in disturbed soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of grasses.

Elleanthus brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appearance.
