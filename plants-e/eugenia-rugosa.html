Eugenia rugosa

Eugenia rugosa, commonly known as the rough eugenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant has dark green, rough-textured leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia rugosa is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
