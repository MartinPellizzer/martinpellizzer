Epistephium sclerophyllum var. album

Epistephium sclerophyllum var. album, commonly known as the white-leaved stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are usually pale yellow or white, adding a delicate contrast to its foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a notable species in the Crassulaceae family.
