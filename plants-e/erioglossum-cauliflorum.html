Erioglossum cauliflorum

Erioglossum cauliflorum, commonly known as the dwarf waxplant, is a unique species native to South Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant is notable for its distinctive wax-like leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense, rounded inflorescences.

Erioglossum cauliflorum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
