Eriogonum effusum var. shandsii

Eriogonum effusum var. shandsii, commonly known as Shands' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, clustered pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
