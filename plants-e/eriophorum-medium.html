Eriophorum medium

Eriophorum medium, commonly known as cotton grass, is a low-growing perennial herb native to northern regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the ends of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs, marshes, and tundra. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

Eriophorum medium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
