Erigeron alcicornutus

Erigeron alcicornutus, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered in a fine layer of silver-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It blooms in late spring to early summer, producing small, daisy-like flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
