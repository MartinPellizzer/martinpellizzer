Erythroxylum amplifolium f. lancifolium

Erythroxylum amplifolium f. lancifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved erythroxylum, is a variant of the erythroxylum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by elliptical, red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in horticultural settings.
