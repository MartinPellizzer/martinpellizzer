Eugenia wrayi

Eugenia wrayi, commonly known as the African evergreen or African eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its glossy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year.

The fruit is small, edible, and often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
