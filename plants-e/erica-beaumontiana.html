Erica beaumontiana

Erica beaumontiana, commonly known as the Beaufort heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Erica beaumontiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
