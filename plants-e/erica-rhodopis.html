Erica rhodopis

Erica rhodopis, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica rhodopis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
