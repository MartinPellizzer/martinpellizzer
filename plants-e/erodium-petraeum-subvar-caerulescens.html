Erodium petraeum subvar. caerulescens

Erodium petraeum subvar. caerulescens, commonly known as the bluestem bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, often blue-tinged stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
