Epilobium hirsutum f. alboroseum

Epilobium hirsutum f. alboroseum, commonly known as the white-flowered willow herb, is a variant of the common willow herb.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the showy white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
