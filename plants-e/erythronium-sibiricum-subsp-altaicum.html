Erythronium sibiricum subsp. altaicum

Erythronium sibiricum subsp.

altaicum, commonly known as the Siberian dog-tooth violet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features a rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces showy, nodding flowers with six petal-like segments that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forested areas or on rocky slopes.

Its blooming period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable wildflower in its native habitat.
