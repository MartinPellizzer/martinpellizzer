Eupatorium lindleyanum var. eglandulosum

Eupatorium lindleyanum var. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a glandular texture. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
