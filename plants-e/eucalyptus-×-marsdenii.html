Eucalyptus × marsdenii

Eucalyptus × marsdenii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed tree or shrub form, with smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and brown. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue, and they are covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. This species produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it a resilient plant in various Australian ecosystems.
