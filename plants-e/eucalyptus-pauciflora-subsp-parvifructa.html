Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp. parvifructa

Eucalyptus pauciflora subsp.

parvifructa, commonly known as the snow gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically white or cream, followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
