Echeveria bracteolata

Echeveria bracteolata, commonly known as the silver echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically silver-gray in color, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a favored ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
