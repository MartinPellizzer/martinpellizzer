Erythroxylum alemquerense

Erythroxylum alemquerense, commonly known as the Alemquer enchanter's nightshade, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and thrives in shaded environments. Its botanical features include showy inflorescences and a woody structure, making it a distinctive member of its family.

It is also noted for its potential ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
