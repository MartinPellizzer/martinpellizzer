Elaeagnus umbellata var. borealis

Elaeagnus umbellata var. borealis, commonly known as the northern silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in late summer. The plant is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in poor soils, making it a hardy ornamental species. Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological significance in disturbed habitats.

The shrub also provides food and shelter for various wildlife species.
