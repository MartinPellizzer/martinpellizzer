Eriogonum effusum var. leptophyllum

Eriogonum effusum var. leptophyllum, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and it has slender, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
