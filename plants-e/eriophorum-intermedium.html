Eriophorum intermedium

Eriophorum intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the tips of its flowering stems. The plant typically thrives in wet, acidic environments such as bogs, marshes, and peatlands. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriophorum intermedium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
