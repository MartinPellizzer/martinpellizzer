Elaeocarpus sphaerocarpus

Elaeocarpus sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe fruit or round-fruit elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically blue or black when mature. The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are white or pale pink and are borne in clusters, adding to its visual interest.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
