Embothrium sericeum var. major

Embothrium sericeum var. major, commonly known as the silky embothrium, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, silver-green leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in coastal areas with well-drained soil. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens, though it requires protection from frost.

The species is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
