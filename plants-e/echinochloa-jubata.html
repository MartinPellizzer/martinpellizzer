Echinochloa jubata

Echinochloa jubata, commonly known as the hairy barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered at the nodes, and its inflorescence is typically a panicle. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields.

Echinochloa jubata is considered a weed in many crops but also has some traditional uses in certain cultures.
