Evelyna xanthocoma

Evelyna xanthocoma, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. Evelyna xanthocoma thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
