Eriogonum latifolium subsp. saxicola

Eriogonum latifolium subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife.
