Escallonia cordobensis

Escallonia cordobensis, commonly known as the Cordoba Escallonia, is a shrub native to Argentina.

It belongs to the family Escalloniaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, while its flowers range from pink to red, blooming in spring.

Escallonia cordobensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
