Eclipta prostrata var. undulata

Eclipta prostrata var. undulata, commonly known as the zigzag ironweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to the Americas.

It features slender, prostrate stems that often curl and twist, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late spring to early fall. Its leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
