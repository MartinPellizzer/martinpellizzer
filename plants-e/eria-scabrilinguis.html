Eria scabrilinguis

Eria scabrilinguis, commonly known as the rough-leaved eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
