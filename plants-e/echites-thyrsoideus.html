Echites thyrsoideus

Echites thyrsoideus, commonly known as the tropical milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces a milky sap that is toxic to some animals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and restoration projects.

Its nectar-rich flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
