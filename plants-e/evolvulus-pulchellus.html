Evolvulus pulchellus

Evolvulus pulchellus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with toothed edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Its rapid growth and prolific blooming make it a common weed in gardens and along stream banks.
