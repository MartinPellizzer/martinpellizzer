Erigeron bellidiastrum var. typicus

Erigeron bellidiastrum var. typicus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy, blue to purple ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It produces small, yellow disk flowers at the center of each bloom, which attract various pollinators.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
