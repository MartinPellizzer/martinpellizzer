Erigeron spinulosus var. umbrosus

Erigeron spinulosus var. umbrosus, commonly known as the shadowy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, spinulose hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The flowers are usually pale blue to white, with ray florets that bloom in late summer to early fall.
