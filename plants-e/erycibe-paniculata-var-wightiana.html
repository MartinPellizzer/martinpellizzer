Erycibe paniculata var. wightiana

Erycibe paniculata var. wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pink to red. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and thrives in humid, warm climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking blooms.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
