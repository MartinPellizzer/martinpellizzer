Echinops mersinensis

Echinops mersinensis, commonly known as the Mersin thistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, globular heads of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces a distinctive, spiny fruit that disperses its seeds by wind.

Echinops mersinensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
