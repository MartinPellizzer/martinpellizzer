Eupatorium sinaloense

Eupatorium sinaloense, commonly known as Sinaloan boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are often aromatic and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium sinaloense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
