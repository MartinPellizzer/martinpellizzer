Encyclia porrecta

Encyclia porrecta, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, strap-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and hammocks. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Encyclia porrecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
