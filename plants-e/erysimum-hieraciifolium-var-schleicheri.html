Erysimum hieraciifolium var. schleicheri

Erysimum hieraciifolium var. schleicheri, commonly known as the Alpine Wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. This variety is particularly adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine environments.

It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
