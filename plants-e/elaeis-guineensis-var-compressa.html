Elaeis guineensis var. compressa

Elaeis guineensis var. compressa, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a variant of the oil palm species native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, tall stature and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces fruit-bearing clusters called "hands," which contain oil-rich nuts. Its botanical features include a straight, unbranched stem and compound leaves with a waxy surface.

This variety is highly valued for its economic importance in the production of palm oil.
