Ebermaiera fastigiata

Ebermaiera fastigiata, commonly known as the upright evergreen shrub, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, cylindrical form. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for those seeking a low-maintenance, visually striking plant.
