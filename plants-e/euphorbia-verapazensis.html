Euphorbia verapazensis

Euphorbia verapazensis, commonly known as the Verapaz euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
