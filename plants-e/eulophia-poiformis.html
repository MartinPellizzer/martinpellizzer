Eulophia poiformis

Eulophia poiformis, commonly known as the broad-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, often hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia poiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
