Erysimum persepolitanum

Erysimum persepolitanum, commonly known as the Persian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

Erysimum persepolitanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
