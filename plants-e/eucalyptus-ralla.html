Eucalyptus ralla

Eucalyptus ralla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Eucalyptus ralla is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It is a valuable plant for biodiversity, providing nectar for native pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
