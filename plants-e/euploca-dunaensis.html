Euploca dunaensis

Euploca dunaensis, commonly known as the Danube euploca, is a perennial herb native to the Danube River floodplains in Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is adapted to periodic flooding. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender, creeping stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are pale blue to lavender and appear in clusters, blooming from June to August.

Euploca dunaensis plays a role in stabilizing riverbanks and is of interest in ecological restoration projects.
