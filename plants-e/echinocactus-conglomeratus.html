Echinocactus conglomeratus

Echinocactus conglomeratus, commonly known as the Mexican horse chestnut, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to small spines, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Echinocactus conglomeratus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
