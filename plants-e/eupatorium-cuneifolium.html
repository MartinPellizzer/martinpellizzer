Eupatorium cuneifolium

Eupatorium cuneifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and wet meadows. It has a tall, erect stem with opposite leaves and is often found in areas with rich, organic soil.

Eupatorium cuneifolium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
