Eryngium pseudojunceum var. bracteosum

Eryngium pseudojunceum var. bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate eryngium, is a flowering plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and distinctive, thistle-like flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie or open woodland habitats. Its flowers are usually violet to blue in color and bloom in the summer months.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
