Eugenia cuspidifolia

Eugenia cuspidifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Eugenia cuspidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in its native regions.
