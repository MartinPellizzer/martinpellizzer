Ekebergia benguelensis

Ekebergia benguelensis, commonly known as the Benguela acacia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
