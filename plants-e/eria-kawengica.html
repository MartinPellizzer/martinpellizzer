Eria kawengica

Eria kawengica, commonly known as the Kawengica orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in clusters and are often adorned with intricate patterns.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, Eria kawengica is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
