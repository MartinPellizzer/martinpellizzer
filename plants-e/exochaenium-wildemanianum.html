Exochaenium wildemanianum

Exochaenium wildemanianum, commonly known as the wildemanian grass, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the warmer months, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its resilience and ecological role, it is often found in native grasslands and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
