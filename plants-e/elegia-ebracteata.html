Elegia ebracteata

Elegia ebracteata, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that are adapted for trapping and digesting small aquatic organisms. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshes and ponds.

Elegia ebracteata plays a unique role in its ecosystem by contributing to nutrient cycling through its predation of microorganisms.
