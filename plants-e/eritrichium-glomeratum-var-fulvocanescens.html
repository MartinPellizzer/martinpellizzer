Eritrichium glomeratum var. fulvocanescens

Eritrichium glomeratum var. fulvocanescens, commonly known as the golden hoary vetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a mat-forming growth habit.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration in its native range.
