Erica pellucida

Erica pellucida, commonly known as the silver heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its profuse display of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. Erica pellucida thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
