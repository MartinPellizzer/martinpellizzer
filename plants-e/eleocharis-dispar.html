Eleocharis dispar

Eleocharis dispar, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of sedges belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib.

Eleocharis dispar plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
