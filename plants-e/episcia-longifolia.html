Episcia longifolia

Episcia longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved waxy plant, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom during the warm seasons. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Episcia longifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
