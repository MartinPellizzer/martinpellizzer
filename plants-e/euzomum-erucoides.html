Euzomum erucoides

Euzomum erucoides, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its purple-black berries that contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are simple and ovate, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Despite its toxicity, it has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
