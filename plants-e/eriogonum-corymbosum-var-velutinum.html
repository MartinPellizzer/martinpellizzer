Eriogonum corymbosum var. velutinum

Eriogonum corymbosum var. velutinum, commonly known as the woolly flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has a compact growth habit and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
