Eupatorium ignoratum

Eupatorium ignoratum, commonly known as wild feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a strong, bitter scent.

The small white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the summer months.
