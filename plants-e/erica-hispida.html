Erica hispida

Erica hispida, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, wiry foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica hispida thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and can tolerate drought conditions.
