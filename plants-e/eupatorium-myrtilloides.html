Eupatorium myrtilloides

Eupatorium myrtilloides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and wetlands, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery-gray underside.

Eupatorium myrtilloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
