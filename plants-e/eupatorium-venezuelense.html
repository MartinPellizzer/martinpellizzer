Eupatorium venezuelense

Eupatorium venezuelense, commonly known as the Venezuelan boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, fertile soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium venezuelense is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
