Epidendrum colonense

Epidendrum colonense, commonly known as the Colombian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and fleshy, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Epidendrum colonense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
