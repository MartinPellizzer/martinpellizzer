Erica coccinea subsp. uniflora

Erica coccinea subsp.

uniflora, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The subspecies uniflora is distinguished by its single flower per stem, setting it apart from other Erica species.
