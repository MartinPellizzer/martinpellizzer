Encyclia zaslawskiana

Encyclia zaslawskiana, commonly known as the Zaslawskian orchid, is a species native to Central America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branching stems and linear, lanceolate leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Encyclia zaslawskiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
