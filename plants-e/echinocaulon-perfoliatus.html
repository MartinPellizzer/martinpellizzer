Echinocaulon perfoliatus

Echinocaulon perfoliatus, commonly known as the perfoliate echinocaulon, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that are often perforated or have a perfoliate structure, meaning they pass through the stems of adjacent leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Echinocaulon perfoliatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
