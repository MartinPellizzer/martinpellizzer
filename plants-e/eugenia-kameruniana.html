Eugenia kameruniana

Eugenia kameruniana, commonly known as the Cameroon ebony, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy appearance, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

The tree is also noted for its ecological importance in supporting biodiversity within its native habitat.
