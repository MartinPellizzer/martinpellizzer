Eugenia lotoides

Eugenia lotoides, commonly known as the small-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia lotoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
