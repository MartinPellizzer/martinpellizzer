Echinocactus exsculptus var. dichroacanthus

Echinocactus exsculptus var. dichroacanthus, commonly known as the two-colored hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which are typically adorned with both white and yellow bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts and botanists.
