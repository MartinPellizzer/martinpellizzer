Erodium leucanthum

Erodium leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Erodium leucanthum is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
