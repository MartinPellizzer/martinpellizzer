Eugenia subsessiliflora

Eugenia subsessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible but often have a sharp, acidic taste. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.

Botanically, it belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove.
