Equisetum trachyodon

Equisetum trachyodon, commonly known as the scaly horse-hair fern, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the order Equisetales.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. The plant is often found in moist, calcareous soils in temperate regions. It reproduces through spores rather than seeds, and its structure reflects its ancient lineage.

Equisetum trachyodon is considered a living fossil due to its minimal morphological changes over millions of years.
