Erigeron compositus var. trifidus

Erigeron compositus var. trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers bloom in summer, producing a showy display of white or pale yellow petals.
