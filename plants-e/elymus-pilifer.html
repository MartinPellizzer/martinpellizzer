Elymus pilifer

Elymus pilifer, commonly known as the hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields, meadows, and along roadsides.

Elymus pilifer is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
