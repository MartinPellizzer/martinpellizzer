Eurycaulis platycaulos

Eurycaulis platycaulos, commonly known as the flat-stemmed eurycaulis, is a unique plant species characterized by its broad, flat stems that often lie horizontally along the ground.

It typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as wet meadows and riverbanks, where it can access adequate water for its growth. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss.

Eurycaulis platycaulos is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
