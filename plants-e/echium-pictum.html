Echium pictum

Echium pictum, commonly known as the painted evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in the late afternoon and evening. The plant has slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are often tinged with purple. Its flowers are tubular and attract various pollinators, including moths and bees.

Echium pictum is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
