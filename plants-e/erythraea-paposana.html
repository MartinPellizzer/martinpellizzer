Erythraea paposana

Erythraea paposana, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
