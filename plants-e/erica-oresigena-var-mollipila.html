Erica oresigena var. mollipila

Erica oresigena var. mollipila, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Erica oresigena var. mollipila is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
