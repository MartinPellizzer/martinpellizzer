Eugenia leucomyrtillus

Eugenia leucomyrtillus, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian culture.
