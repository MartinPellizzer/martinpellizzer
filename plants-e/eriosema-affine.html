Eriosema affine

Eriosema affine, commonly known as the roughleaf eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in grassland and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Eriosema affine is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
