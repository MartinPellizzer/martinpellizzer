Eulophia leucantha

Eulophia leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Eulophia leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
