Euodia obtusifolia subsp. gigas

Euodia obtusifolia subsp.

gigas, commonly known as the giant tallow tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
