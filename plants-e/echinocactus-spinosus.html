Echinocactus spinosus

Echinocactus spinosus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, sharp spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique morphology and adaptability, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
