Eurya subcordata

Eurya subcordata, commonly known as the broadleaf eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

Eurya subcordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
