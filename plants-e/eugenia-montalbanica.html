Eugenia montalbanica

Eugenia montalbanica, commonly known as the Montalban guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens and agricultural settings.
