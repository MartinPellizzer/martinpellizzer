Elaeocarpus brachyphyllus

Elaeocarpus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
