Euphorbia oncoclada

Euphorbia oncoclada, commonly known as the giant spurge or African milk bush, is a striking succulent native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
