Echium incanum

Echium incanum, commonly known as the silverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and striking spikes of blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It is an annual or biennial species, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
