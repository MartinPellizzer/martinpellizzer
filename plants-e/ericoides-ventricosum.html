Ericoides ventricosum

Ericoides ventricosum, commonly known as the bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant characterized by its inflated, bladder-like structures used for capturing small aquatic organisms.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and ditches. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to its aquatic habitat. Its unique trapping mechanism involves specialized bladders that create a vacuum to suck in prey.

Ericoides ventricosum plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by controlling insect populations and providing habitat for microorganisms.
