Eupatorium alternifolium f. subintegrum

Eupatorium alternifolium f. subintegrum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a variant of the boneset plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, alternate leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It has a tall, herbaceous stem with a distinct, hollow appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
