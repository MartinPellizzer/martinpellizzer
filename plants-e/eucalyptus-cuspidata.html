Eucalyptus cuspidata

Eucalyptus cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The tree typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are typically white or pink, clustered in umbels, and attract various pollinators.

The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.
