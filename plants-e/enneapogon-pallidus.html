Enneapogon pallidus

Enneapogon pallidus, commonly known as the pale false lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to drought conditions and thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Enneapogon pallidus is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
