Elaeocarpus roseoalbus

Elaeocarpus roseoalbus, commonly known as the pink-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking pink or white flowers, which are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in lowland evergreen forests.
