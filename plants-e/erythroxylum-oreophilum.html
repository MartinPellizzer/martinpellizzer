Erythroxylum oreophilum

Erythroxylum oreophilum, commonly known as the Andean coca, is a shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, orange-red berries containing numerous seeds. It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is closely related to the coca species used in traditional medicine and cultural practices.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and ethnobotanical contexts.
