Erysimum pannonicum subsp. wahlenbergii

Erysimum pannonicum subsp.

wahlenbergii, commonly known as the Wahlenberg's wallflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features basal and cauline leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
