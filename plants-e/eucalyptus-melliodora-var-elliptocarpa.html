Eucalyptus melliodora var. elliptocarpa

Eucalyptus melliodora var. elliptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honey gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The tree produces creamy-white flowers arranged in clusters, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its bark is typically smooth and greyish-white, peeling in strips as the tree matures.

This variety is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
