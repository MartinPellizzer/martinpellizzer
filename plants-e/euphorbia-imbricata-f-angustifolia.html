Euphorbia imbricata f. angustifolia

Euphorbia imbricata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with a distinctive crown of narrow, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
