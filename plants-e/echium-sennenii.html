Echium sennenii

Echium sennenii, commonly known as the Canary Island viper's bugloss, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial herb with striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium sennenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
