Eyrythalia gebleri

Eyrythalia gebleri, commonly known as the red-veined fern, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that display a striking contrast of deep green and vivid red veins. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique vascular system contributes to its distinctive coloration and structural form.

Due to habitat loss, Eyrythalia gebleri is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
