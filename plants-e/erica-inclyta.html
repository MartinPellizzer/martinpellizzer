Erica inclyta

Erica inclyta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica inclyta typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
