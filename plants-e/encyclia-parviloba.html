Encyclia parviloba

Encyclia parviloba, commonly known as the small-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit. The plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and green, often with intricate patterns. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted for climbing and photosynthesis in shaded environments.

Encyclia parviloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
