Eugenia grandis

Eugenia grandis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
