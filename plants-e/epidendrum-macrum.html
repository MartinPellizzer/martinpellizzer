Epidendrum macrum

Epidendrum macrum, commonly known as the Macrum's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its inflorescence is often branched, with numerous blooms that can last for several months.

Epidendrum macrum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in greenhouses and gardens.
