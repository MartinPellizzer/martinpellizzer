Euphorbia triangolensis

Euphorbia triangolensis, commonly known as the triangular euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped stems, which are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia triangolensis is also known for its ability to store water in its thick stems, aiding in its survival in harsh conditions.
