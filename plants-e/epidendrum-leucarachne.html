Epidendrum leucarachne

Epidendrum leucarachne, commonly known as the white-veined epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated pseudobulbs and long, arching inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white flowers with distinctive red veins. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Epidendrum leucarachne is a member of the Orchidaceae family, which includes many other epiphytic and terrestrial orchid species.
