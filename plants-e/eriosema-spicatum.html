Eriosema spicatum

Eriosema spicatum, commonly known as the hairy beggar's-ticks, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.

Eriosema spicatum is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
