Erythroxylum campinense

Erythroxylum campinense, commonly known as the Colombian coffee plant, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a shrub or small tree belonging to the Erythroxylaceae family, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elliptical, greenish-brown fruits containing two seeds.

It is notable for its role in the cultivation of coffee, as it is the source of the coffee bean used in traditional Colombian coffee production.
