Eriosyce aurata var. aurata

Eriosyce aurata var. aurata, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical stems that are covered in white to pale yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires ample sunlight, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
