Exacum appendiculatum

Exacum appendiculatum, commonly known as the dwarf African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features fleshy, dark green leaves with a velvety texture, and its flowers are borne on short pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Exacum appendiculatum is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ease of care.
