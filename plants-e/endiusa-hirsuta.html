Endiusa hirsuta

Endiusa hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy endiusa, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. It is native to regions in Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Endiusa hirsuta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
