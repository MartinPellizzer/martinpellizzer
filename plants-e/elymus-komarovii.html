Elymus komarovii

Elymus komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three bracts. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a prominent midrib.

Elymus komarovii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
