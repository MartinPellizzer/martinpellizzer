Eugenia dimorpha var. oeidocarpa

Eugenia dimorpha var. oeidocarpa, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its dark purple to black berries, which are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
