Evelyna robusta

Evelyna robusta, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically a deep green with a yellowish underside. The plant produces spores in specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Evelyna robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
