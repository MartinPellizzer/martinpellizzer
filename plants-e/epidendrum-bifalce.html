Epidendrum bifalce

Epidendrum bifalce, commonly known as the two-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in the orchid family.
