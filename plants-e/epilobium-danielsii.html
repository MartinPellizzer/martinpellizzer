Epilobium danielsii

Epilobium danielsii, commonly known as Daniel's willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are showy, with pink to red petals and yellow stamens, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium danielsii is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous insects.
