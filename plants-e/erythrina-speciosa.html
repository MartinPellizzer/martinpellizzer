Erythrina speciosa

Erythrina speciosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its distinctive appearance makes it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Erythrina speciosa also plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its association with symbiotic root nodules.
