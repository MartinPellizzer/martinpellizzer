Erysimum talijevii

Erysimum talijevii, commonly known as the Balkan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum talijevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.
