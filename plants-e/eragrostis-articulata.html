Eragrostis articulata

Eragrostis articulata, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis articulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
