Erica newdigateae

Erica newdigateae, commonly known as the Newdigate heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family Ericaceae.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically occurring in regions of South Africa and Lesotho. This evergreen shrub typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, acidic environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer.

Erica newdigateae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
