Elaeocarpus eximius

Elaeocarpus eximius, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery wax. The tree produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
