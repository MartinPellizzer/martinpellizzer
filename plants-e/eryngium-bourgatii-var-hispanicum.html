Eryngium bourgatii var. hispanicum

Eryngium bourgatii var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It features spiny, blue-green foliage and produces striking purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistle-like blooms. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
