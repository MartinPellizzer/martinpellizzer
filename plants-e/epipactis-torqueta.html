Epipactis torqueta

Epipactis torqueta, commonly known as the twisted helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive, twisted lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in acidic soils. It produces a small, dark brown capsule as its fruit, containing several minute seeds.

Epipactis torqueta is a perennial herb that blooms from June to August, depending on the region.
