Echium fruticosum

Echium fruticosum, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Echium fruticosum is well adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a hardy species found in coastal habitats.
