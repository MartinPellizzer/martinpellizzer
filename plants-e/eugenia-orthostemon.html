Eugenia orthostemon

Eugenia orthostemon, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, segmented fruits that are dark red when mature.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ornamental horticulture and local ecosystems.
