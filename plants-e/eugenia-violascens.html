Eugenia violascens

Eugenia violascens, commonly known as the purple eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically purple or red when mature, which are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Eugenia violascens thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
