Eria porteri

Eria porteri, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety purple or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense, velvety coating.

Eria porteri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique appearance.
