Euphorbia chaculana

Euphorbia chaculana, commonly known as the Chaculana spurge, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which help reduce water loss through transpiration. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in a structure called a cyathium, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia chaculana is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
