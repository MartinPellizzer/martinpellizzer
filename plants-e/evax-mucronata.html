Evax mucronata

Evax mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, narrow leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows close to the ground, forming dense mats that help stabilize sandy soils. Its ability to thrive in harsh environments makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The seeds are often dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution in suitable habitats.
