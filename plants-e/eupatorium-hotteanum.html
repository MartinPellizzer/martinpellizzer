Eupatorium hotteanum

Eupatorium hotteanum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branched stems that are hairy and can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.
