Eugenia decidua

Eugenia decidua, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters, featuring glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Eugenia decidua thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a versatile ornamental plant.
