Erythronium obtusiflorum

Erythronium obtusiflorum, commonly known as the blunt-flowered dog's tooth violet, is a member of the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western North America, including parts of the United States and Canada. The plant features a basal rosette of waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish throat.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and meadows.
