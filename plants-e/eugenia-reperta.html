Eugenia reperta

Eugenia reperta, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that turn red when mature.

Eugenia reperta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
