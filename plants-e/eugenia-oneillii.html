Eugenia oneillii

Eugenia oneillii, commonly known as the O'Neill's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia, particularly Queensland. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia oneillii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
