Eupatorium extensum

Eupatorium extensum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall, creating a mist-like appearance. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium extensum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
