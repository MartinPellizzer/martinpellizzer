Echinocactus pilosus var. stainesii

Echinocactus pilosus var. stainesii, commonly known as the Stain's Hedgehog Cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, spiny stems and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. Its unique appearance makes it a sought-after specimen among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and over-collection.
