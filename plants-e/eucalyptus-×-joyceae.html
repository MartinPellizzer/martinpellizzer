Eucalyptus × joyceae

Eucalyptus × joyceae, commonly known as the hybrid eucalyptus, is a species resulting from the cross between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus robusta.

It is a tall, fast-growing tree with smooth, peeling bark that reveals a range of colors, from grey to cream. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive aromatic scent. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, attracting various pollinators.

This species is widely cultivated for its timber and as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
