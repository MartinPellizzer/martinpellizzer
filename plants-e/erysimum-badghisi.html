Erysimum badghisi

Erysimum badghisi, commonly known as the Badghis wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs.
