Elaphoglossum atrosquamatum

Elaphoglossum atrosquamatum, commonly known as the black-squamate holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant produces sporangia that are clustered in sori, which are covered by a protective indusium. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within its ecological niche.
