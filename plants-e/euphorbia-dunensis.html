Euphorbia dunensis

Euphorbia dunensis, commonly known as the Dun Valley spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in a powdery white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
