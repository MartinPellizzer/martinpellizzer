Erythrina melanacantha subsp. melanacantha

Erythrina melanacantha subsp.

melanacantha, commonly known as the black erythrina, is a species of legume native to parts of Africa. It is characterized by its striking red flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typical of the Erythrina genus.

The plant produces distinctive, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing abilities, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and ecological purposes.
