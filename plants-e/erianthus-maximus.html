Erianthus maximus

Erianthus maximus, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, robust grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its stiff, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. This plant thrives in moist environments, often found near water bodies such as rivers and lakes. Its fibrous structure makes it valuable for erosion control and as a source of natural fiber.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated.
