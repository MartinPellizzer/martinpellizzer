Elaeocarpus polyanthus

Elaeocarpus polyanthus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
