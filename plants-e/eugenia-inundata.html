Eugenia inundata

Eugenia inundata, commonly known as the water myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the wetlands of northern South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its moist environment. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Its root system is adapted to waterlogged soils, making it an important species in floodplain ecosystems.

Eugenia inundata plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
