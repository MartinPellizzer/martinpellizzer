Erica curtophylla

Erica curtophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring. Erica curtophylla thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
