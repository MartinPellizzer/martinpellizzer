Euphorbia remyi var. pteropoda

Euphorbia remyi var. pteropoda, commonly known as the winged euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching stems that are often adorned with small, wing-like structures. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
