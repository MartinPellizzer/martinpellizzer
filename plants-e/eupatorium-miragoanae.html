Eupatorium miragoanae

Eupatorium miragoanae, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally for its purported health benefits.
