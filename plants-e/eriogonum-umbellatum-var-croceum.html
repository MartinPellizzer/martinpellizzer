Eriogonum umbellatum var. croceum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. croceum, commonly known as yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

It produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
