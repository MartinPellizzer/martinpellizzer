Erica hybrida

Erica hybrida, commonly known as the heather, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of western Asia, and is known for its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica hybrida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging environments.
