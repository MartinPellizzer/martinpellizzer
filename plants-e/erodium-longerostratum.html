Erodium longerostratum

Erodium longerostratum, commonly known as the long-beaked bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and rocky areas.

The seeds are tiny and dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
