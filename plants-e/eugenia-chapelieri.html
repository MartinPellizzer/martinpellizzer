Eugenia chapelieri

Eugenia chapelieri, commonly known as the golden apple or golden guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright orange, fleshy fruits resembling apples. The fruit has a sweet and tangy flavor, often used in culinary applications. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
