Eulophia leontoglossa

Eulophia leontoglossa, commonly known as the lion's tongue orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowering stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a distinctive lip that resembles a tongue. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Eulophia leontoglossa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
