Echinocystis echinata

Echinocystis echinata, commonly known as the prickly cucumber, is a vining plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into green, spiny fruits resembling small cucumbers. Its botanical features include a robust root system and the ability to climb or trail along the ground.

Echinocystis echinata is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woods.
