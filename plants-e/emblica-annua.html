Emblica annua

Emblica annua, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its small, sour-tasting berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces yellowish flowers in clusters. Its fruit develops from green to dark orange as it matures.

The botanical features of Emblica annua make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and modern nutrition.
