Eragrostis laeviglumis

Eragrostis laeviglumis, commonly known as smoothawn, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
