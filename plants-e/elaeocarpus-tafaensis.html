Elaeocarpus tafaensis

Elaeocarpus tafaensis, commonly known as the Tafa sand apple, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, round, yellow fruits with a sweet and aromatic pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane forests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
