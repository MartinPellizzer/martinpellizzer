Emilia parnassiifolia

Emilia parnassiifolia, commonly known as the Parnassian emilia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia parnassiifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
