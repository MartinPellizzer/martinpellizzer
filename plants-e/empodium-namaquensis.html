Empodium namaquensis

Empodium namaquensis, commonly known as the Namakwa buttercup, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that form a dense basal cluster. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single, terminal inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Empodium namaquensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
