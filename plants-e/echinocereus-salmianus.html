Echinocereus salmianus

Echinocereus salmianus, commonly known as the Salmi's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a ribbed stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny areoles and produces edible fruits known as tunas, which are often consumed raw or cooked. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
