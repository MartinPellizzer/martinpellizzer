Euphorbia pergracilis

Euphorbia pergracilis, commonly known as the slender spurge, is a low-growing, succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, green, fleshy leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are its specialized flower-like structures, often mistaken for flowers. It has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a characteristic common among many euphorbias.

Euphorbia pergracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
