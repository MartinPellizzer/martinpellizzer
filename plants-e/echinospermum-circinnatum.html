Echinospermum circinnatum

Echinospermum circinnatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. Its fruit is a spiny capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds.

Echinospermum circinnatum is often found in disturbed soils and thrives in full sun.
