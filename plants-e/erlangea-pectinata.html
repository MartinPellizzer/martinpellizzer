Erlangea pectinata

Erlangea pectinata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Erlangea pectinata is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
