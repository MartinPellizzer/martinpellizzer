Eupatorium dryadeum

Eupatorium dryadeum, commonly known as the dryad's eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is often found in swampy or wetland habitats, where it thrives in partial to full shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
