Echites vellozianus

Echites vellozianus, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields. It has a climbing habit and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Echites vellozianus is valued for its ornamental appeal but can also be considered a weed in some environments.
