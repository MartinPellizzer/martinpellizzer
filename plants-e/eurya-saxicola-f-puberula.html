Eurya saxicola f. puberula

Eurya saxicola f. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by small, orange-red berries. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
