Elachyptera festiva

Elachyptera festiva, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that appear in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets that turn red in the fall. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
