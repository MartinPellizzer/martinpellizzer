Elymus zagricus var. lorestanicus

Elymus zagricus var. lorestanicus, commonly known as the Lorestan wild rye, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are arranged in a loose, open structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
