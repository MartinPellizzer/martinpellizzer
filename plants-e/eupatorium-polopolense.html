Eupatorium polopolense

Eupatorium polopolense, commonly known as the white snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are white to pale purple and are arranged in dense clusters.

Eupatorium polopolense is also known for its historical association with the spread of milkweed pests due to its role as a host plant.
