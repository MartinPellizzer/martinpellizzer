Eulalia leptostachys

Eulalia leptostachys, commonly known as the slender spike grass, is a perennial grass native to the wetlands and marshes of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal and freshwater wetland habitats.

Eulalia leptostachys plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
