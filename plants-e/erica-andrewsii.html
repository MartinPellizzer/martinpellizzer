Erica andrewsii

Erica andrewsii, commonly known as the Andrew's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it grows in coastal fynbos vegetation. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Erica andrewsii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
