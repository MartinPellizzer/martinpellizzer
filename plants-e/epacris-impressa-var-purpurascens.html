Epacris impressa var. purpurascens

Epacris impressa var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
