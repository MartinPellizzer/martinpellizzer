Erodium chamaedryoides f. roseum

Erodium chamaedryoides f. roseum, commonly known as the pink deadnettle, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Lamiaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves that are hairy and oval in shape. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers with two lips, typical of the mint family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
