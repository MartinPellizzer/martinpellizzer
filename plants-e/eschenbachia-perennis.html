Eschenbachia perennis

Eschenbachia perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long-lasting blooms, which can persist for several months under suitable conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, often resembling miniature roses. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its enduring beauty.
