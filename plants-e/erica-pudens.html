Erica pudens

Erica pudens, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the summer months.

Erica pudens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
