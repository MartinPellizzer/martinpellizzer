Erysimum pallens

Erysimum pallens, commonly known as the pale wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in grayish-white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, four-petaled yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white wax, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Erysimum pallens is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
