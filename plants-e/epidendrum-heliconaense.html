Epidendrum heliconaense

Epidendrum heliconaense, commonly known as the Heliconia Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features pendulous inflorescences with brightly colored, velvety flowers that resemble heliconias, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
