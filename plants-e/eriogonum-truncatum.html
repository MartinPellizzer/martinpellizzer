Eriogonum truncatum

Eriogonum truncatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eriogonum truncatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
