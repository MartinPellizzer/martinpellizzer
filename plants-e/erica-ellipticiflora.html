Erica ellipticiflora

Erica ellipticiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of delicate pink or white flowers. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments.

Erica ellipticiflora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
