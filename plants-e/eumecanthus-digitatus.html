Eumecanthus digitatus

Eumecanthus digitatus, commonly known as the digitate euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, woody stem with a dense cluster of fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves at the top. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Eumecanthus digitatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
