Edwardia laurifolia

Edwardia laurifolia, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or gold, arranged in clusters that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Edwardia laurifolia is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
