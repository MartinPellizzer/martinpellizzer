Eriocaulon hanningtonii

Eriocaulon hanningtonii, commonly known as the Hannington's spike-moss, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes form a dense network that helps stabilize the substrate and support its growth.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
