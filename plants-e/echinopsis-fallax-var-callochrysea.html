Echinopsis fallax var. callochrysea

Echinopsis fallax var. callochrysea, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, requiring minimal water. Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green, ribbed structure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
