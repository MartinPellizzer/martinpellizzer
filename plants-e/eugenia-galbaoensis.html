Eugenia galbaoensis

Eugenia galbaoensis, commonly known as the Galbao's evergreen myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that contain a single seed, and they are often used in local traditional medicine.

Eugenia galbaoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
