Eucalyptus suffulgens

Eucalyptus suffulgens, commonly known as the silver-leafed gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in woodland and heathland environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy surface.

Eucalyptus suffulgens produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators.
