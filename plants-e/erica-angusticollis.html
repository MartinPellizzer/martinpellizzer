Erica angusticollis

Erica angusticollis, commonly known as narrow-necked heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This species is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pink or white and bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica angusticollis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
