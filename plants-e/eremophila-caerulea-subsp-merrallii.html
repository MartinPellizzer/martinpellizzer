Eremophila caerulea subsp. merrallii

Eremophila caerulea subsp.

merrallii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

This subspecies is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
