Enneapogon decipiens

Enneapogon decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and grassland ecosystems.

Due to its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
