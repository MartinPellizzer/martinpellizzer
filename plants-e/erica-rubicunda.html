Erica rubicunda

Erica rubicunda, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica rubicunda thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
