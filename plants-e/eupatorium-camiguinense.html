Eupatorium camiguinense

Eupatorium camiguinense, commonly known as Camiguin blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests, and is often found in the island of Camiguin. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Eupatorium camiguinense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
