Euphorbia cassia subsp. rigoi

Euphorbia cassia subsp.

rigoi, commonly known as the golden spurges, is a shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant has smooth, green, often hairy stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also noted for its ability to produce a milky latex when injured, a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family.
