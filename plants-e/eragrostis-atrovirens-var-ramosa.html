Eragrostis atrovirens var. ramosa

Eragrostis atrovirens var. ramosa, commonly known as the black oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
