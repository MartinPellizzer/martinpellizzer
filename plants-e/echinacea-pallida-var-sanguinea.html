Echinacea pallida var. sanguinea

Echinacea pallida var. sanguinea, commonly known as the pale coneflower or roseate coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central United States.

It features showy, pink to rose-red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
