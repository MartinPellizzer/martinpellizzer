Elatine verticillaris

Elatine verticillaris, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments.

It has whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 inch long, and its tiny, star-shaped flowers are usually pink or white. The plant is known for its ability to spread rapidly through vegetative reproduction, forming dense mats on the water surface. It is often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing water ecosystems.

Elatine verticillaris is also valued for its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
