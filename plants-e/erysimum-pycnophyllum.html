Erysimum pycnophyllum

Erysimum pycnophyllum, commonly known as the dense-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with four-petaled flowers.

Erysimum pycnophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
