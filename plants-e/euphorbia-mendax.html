Euphorbia mendax

Euphorbia mendax, commonly known as the false spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem covered in soft, pale greenish-gray spines. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
