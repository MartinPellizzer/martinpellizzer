Elatine aestivosa

Elatine aestivosa, commonly known as summer waterwort, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has tiny, opposite leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Elatine aestivosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed wetlands, making it an indicator of nutrient-rich water conditions.
