Erigeron concinnus var. condensatus

Erigeron concinnus var. condensatus, commonly known as the condensed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
