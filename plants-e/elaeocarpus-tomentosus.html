Elaeocarpus tomentosus

Elaeocarpus tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
