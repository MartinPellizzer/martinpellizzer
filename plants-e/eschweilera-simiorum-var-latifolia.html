Eschweilera simiorum var. latifolia

Eschweilera simiorum var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often fragrant, followed by ornamental red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense forests.

Due to its striking foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
