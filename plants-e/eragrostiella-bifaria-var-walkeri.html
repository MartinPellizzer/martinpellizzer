Eragrostiella bifaria var. walkeri

Eragrostiella bifaria var. walkeri, commonly known as the narrowleaf bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spikelet-based flowers that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
