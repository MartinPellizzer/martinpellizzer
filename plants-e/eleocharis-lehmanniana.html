Eleocharis lehmanniana

Eleocharis lehmanniana, commonly known as the Lehmann's spike rush, is a species of sedge native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help in preventing erosion.

Eleocharis lehmanniana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and environmental benefits.
