Euphorbia esculenta var. cyparissioides

Euphorbia esculenta var. cyparissioides, commonly known as the cypress spurge, is a succulent perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features a distinctive, tree-like appearance with a thick, woody stem that resembles a cypress tree. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by colorful bracts. Its leaves are fleshy and alternate along the stem, and it has a milky sap that can be toxic.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
