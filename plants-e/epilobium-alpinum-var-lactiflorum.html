Epilobium alpinum var. lactiflorum

Epilobium alpinum var. lactiflorum, commonly known as the alpine willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb with erect, branched stems that often bear a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in a soft, woolly texture.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous environments.
