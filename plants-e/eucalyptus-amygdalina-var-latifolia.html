Eucalyptus amygdalina var. latifolia

Eucalyptus amygdalina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleeflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the spring and summer seasons. It is typically found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tough conditions.
