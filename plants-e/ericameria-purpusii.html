Ericameria purpusii

Ericameria purpusii, commonly known as the Purpus' goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, woody fruits called cypselae, which are dispersed by wind.

Ericameria purpusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
