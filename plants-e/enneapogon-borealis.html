Enneapogon borealis

Enneapogon borealis, commonly known as the northern dropseed, is a grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, dunes, and sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Enneapogon borealis is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient plant in its native range.
