Erodium cygnorum subsp. cygnorum

Erodium cygnorum subsp.

cygnorum, commonly known as the water plantain or water dock, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and ditches.

The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are small, pink or purple, and arranged in clusters. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
