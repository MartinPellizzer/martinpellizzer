Equisetum debile var. gracile

Equisetum debile var. gracile, commonly known as slender horsetail, is a diminutive variety of the horsetail plant.

It is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its slender, jointed stems covered in small, scale-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are essential for reproduction.

Due to its delicate appearance and ecological significance, it is often found in nutrient-rich soils and plays a role in maintaining wetland ecosystems.
