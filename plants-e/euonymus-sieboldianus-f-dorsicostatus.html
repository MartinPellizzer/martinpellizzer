Euonymus sieboldianus f. dorsicostatus

Euonymus sieboldianus f. dorsicostatus, commonly known as the dwarf euonymus, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a distinctive raised midrib. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its foliage remains dense and attractive throughout the year, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.
