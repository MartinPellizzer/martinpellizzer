Eriogonum atrorubens var. pseudociliatum

Eriogonum atrorubens var. pseudociliatum, commonly known as the dark red buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of small, dark red to purple flowers. The leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grasslands.

This variety is distinguished by its darker coloration compared to other forms of Eriogonum atrorubens.
