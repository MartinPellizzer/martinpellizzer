Eriophorum cyperinum var. pelium

Eriophorum cyperinum var. pelium, commonly known as the purple tussock sedge, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering spikes are often tinged with purple or pink hues.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
