Elaeocarpus amygdalinus

Elaeocarpus amygdalinus, commonly known as the black ebony tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark purple, fleshy fruits that contain smooth, shiny seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest regions.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
