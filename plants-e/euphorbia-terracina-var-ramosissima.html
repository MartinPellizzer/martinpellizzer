Euphorbia terracina var. ramosissima

Euphorbia terracina var. ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It thrives in dry, sunny conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires caution when handling.
