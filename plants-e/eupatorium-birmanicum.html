Eupatorium birmanicum

Eupatorium birmanicum, commonly known as the Burmese thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium birmanicum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
