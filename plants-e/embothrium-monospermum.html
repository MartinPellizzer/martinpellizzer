Embothrium monospermum

Embothrium monospermum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and can tolerate salt spray, making it well adapted to its native habitat.

Embothrium monospermum is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
