Epilobium montanum subsp. hypericifolium

Epilobium montanum subsp.

hypericifolium, commonly known as the alpine willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
