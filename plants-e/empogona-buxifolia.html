Empogona buxifolia

Empogona buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in spring and summer. Empogona buxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of growing conditions.
