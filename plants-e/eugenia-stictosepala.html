Eugenia stictosepala

Eugenia stictosepala, commonly known as the stictosepala myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia stictosepala is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
