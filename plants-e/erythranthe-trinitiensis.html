Erythranthe trinitiensis

Erythranthe trinitiensis, commonly known as the Trinidad morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Erythranthe trinitiensis is often found in disturbed habitats, including roadsides and open fields.
