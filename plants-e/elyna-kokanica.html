Elyna kokanica

Elyna kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica lily, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Europe.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Elyna kokanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
