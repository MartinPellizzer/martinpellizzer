Eria xanthotricha

Eria xanthotricha, commonly known as the golden-haired eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name.

Eria xanthotricha is often grown as a decorative orchid due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
