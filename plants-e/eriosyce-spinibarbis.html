Eriosyce spinibarbis

Eriosyce spinibarbis, commonly known as the spiny-bearded rock cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered with dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. Eriosyce spinibarbis is adapted to arid and rocky environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus collections.
