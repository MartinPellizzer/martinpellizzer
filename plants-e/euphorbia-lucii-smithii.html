Euphorbia lucii-smithii

Euphorbia lucii-smithii, commonly known as Smith's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to form a dense, rounded shrub.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptation to harsh environments, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
