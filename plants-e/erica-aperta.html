Erica aperta

Erica aperta, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Erica aperta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
