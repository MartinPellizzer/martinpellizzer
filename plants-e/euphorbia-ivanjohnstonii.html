Euphorbia ivanjohnstonii

Euphorbia ivanjohnstonii, commonly known as the Ivan Johnston euphorbia, is a striking succulent native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, waxy leaves that help it store water in its native environment. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by colorful bracts that attract pollinators. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular specimen plant among succulent enthusiasts.
