Ericoides obtusata

Ericoides obtusata, commonly known as the bluntleaf ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically leathery and oval in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or forest understory environments.

Ericoides obtusata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
