Embothrium coccineum

Embothrium coccineum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils.

Embothrium coccineum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
