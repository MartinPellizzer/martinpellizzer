Eucephalus glaucophyllus

Eucephalus glaucophyllus, commonly known as the bluebottle bush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are actually clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant has silvery-gray foliage that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eucephalus glaucophyllus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
