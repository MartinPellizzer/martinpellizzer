Epipactis latifolia lusus rosea

Epipactis latifolia lusus rosea, commonly known as the roseate broad-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and distinctive, pale pink to white flowers with a spurred lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in rich, humus-filled soil. It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
