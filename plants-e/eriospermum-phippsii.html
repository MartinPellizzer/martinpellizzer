Eriospermum phippsii

Eriospermum phippsii, commonly known as the Phipps' eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring. It is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a notable species in its native range.
