Echinocactus geissei

Echinocactus geissei, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and a dense covering of spines that provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for both ornamental and educational purposes.
