Eragrostis amabilis var. effusa

Eragrostis amabilis var. effusa, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and prairies, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its narrow leaves are often covered in a fine layer of hairs, giving it a silky appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and drought tolerance.
