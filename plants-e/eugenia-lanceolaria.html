Eugenia lanceolaria

Eugenia lanceolaria, commonly known as the wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature, containing a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Eugenia lanceolaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cuisines and medicines.
