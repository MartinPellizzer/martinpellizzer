Erigeron scotteri

Erigeron scotteri, commonly known as the Scotch eryngo, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is also valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
