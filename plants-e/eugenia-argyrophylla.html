Eugenia argyrophylla

Eugenia argyrophylla, commonly known as silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia, where it thrives in sandy soils and humid climates. The plant is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense, overlapping scales on the leaves. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
