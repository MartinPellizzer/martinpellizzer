Eucalyptus megasepala

Eucalyptus megasepala, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleefowl eucalypt, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and its distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically white or pale green. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, revealing a mottled appearance.

Eucalyptus megasepala is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian vegetation.
