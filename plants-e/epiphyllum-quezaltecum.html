Epiphyllum quezaltecum

Epiphyllum quezaltecum, commonly known as the Zapatetl night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with flat, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are usually reduced to spines, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

It is often cultivated for its striking floral display and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
