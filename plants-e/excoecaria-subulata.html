Excoecaria subulata

Excoecaria subulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pointed leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are followed by small, round fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
