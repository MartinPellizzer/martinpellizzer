Empetrum sibiricum var. japonicum

Empetrum sibiricum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese stonecrop, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces tiny, white or pinkish flowers in clusters during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
