Erigeron ramosus

Erigeron ramosus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces small, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Erigeron ramosus is often found in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides.
