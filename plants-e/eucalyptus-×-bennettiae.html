Eucalyptus × bennettiae

Eucalyptus × bennettiae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf honey gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often pendulous branches and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by woody capsules containing viable seeds. Eucalyptus × bennettiae is a hardy, fast-growing tree that thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in reforestation and erosion control.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
