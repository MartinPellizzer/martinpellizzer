Epilobium dolichopodum

Epilobium dolichopodum, commonly known as long-footed willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as stream banks and meadows. The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

Epilobium dolichopodum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
