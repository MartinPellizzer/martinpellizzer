Elymus fibrosus subsp. subfibrosus

Elymus fibrosus subsp.

subfibrosus, commonly known as the fibrous wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open areas, and is often found in prairies and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage plant for wildlife.
