Eucalyptus curtisii

Eucalyptus curtisii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which appear during the winter months. Eucalyptus curtisii is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, making it valuable for both ecological and potential medicinal uses.
