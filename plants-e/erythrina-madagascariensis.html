Erythrina madagascariensis

Erythrina madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar flame tree, is a striking deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree.
