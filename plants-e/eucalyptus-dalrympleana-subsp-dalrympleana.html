Eucalyptus dalrympleana subsp. dalrympleana

Eucalyptus dalrympleana subsp.

dalrympleana, commonly known as the Dalrymple's mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and grey-green foliage.

The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive honey-scented nectar that attracts various pollinators. This subspecies is adapted to harsh, dry environments and has a slow growth rate.

It plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat, providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
