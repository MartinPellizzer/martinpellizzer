Eunanus angustifolius

Eunanus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eunanus, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It features slender, upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Eunanus angustifolius thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
