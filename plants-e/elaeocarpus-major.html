Elaeocarpus major

Elaeocarpus major, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal properties.
