Erica obtecta

Erica obtecta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica obtecta produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often blooming from late spring to early summer.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
