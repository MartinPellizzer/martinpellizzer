Eriosema triformum

Eriosema triformum, commonly known as the three-lobed eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with three oval or heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often with a distinctive hairy texture. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Eriosema triformum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
