Eulophidium ambongense

Eulophidium ambongense, commonly known as the Ambongense grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland ecosystems.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
