Elatine hydropiper f. orthosperma

Elatine hydropiper f. orthosperma, commonly known as water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatine genus in the family Elatineaceae.

It typically grows in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in clusters, and it produces tiny, elongated seeds that are dispersed by water.

It is a hardy species that can tolerate a range of water conditions and is often found in temperate regions.
