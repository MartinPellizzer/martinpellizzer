Elatostema goudotianum var. goudotianum

Elatostema goudotianum var. goudotianum, commonly known as the Goudot's prostrate buttercup, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a prostrate or ascending growth habit with thin, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
