Eumachia goodenoughiensis

Eumachia goodenoughiensis, commonly known as the Goodenough's eumachia, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its foliage is often used in traditional Aboriginal practices for its medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
