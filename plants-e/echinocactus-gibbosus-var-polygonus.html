Echinocactus gibbosus var. polygonus

Echinocactus gibbosus var. polygonus, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green with a yellowish or reddish tinge. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its spines are usually short and cylindrical, giving it a unique appearance.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
