Eritrichium intermedium

Eritrichium intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soils. Its flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, are often pale yellow or white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eritrichium intermedium is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
