Endymion campanulatus var. albus

Endymion campanulatus var. albus, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, dark blue berries after flowering.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
