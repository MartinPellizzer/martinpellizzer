Euphorbia mohammerensis

Euphorbia mohammerensis, commonly known as the Mohammer's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which store water to help it survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in hot, dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
