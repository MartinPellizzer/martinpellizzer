Eupatorium purpureum var. bruneri

Eupatorium purpureum var. bruneri, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-purple flower clusters that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture and emits a mild, aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This species is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
