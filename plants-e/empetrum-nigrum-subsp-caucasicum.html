Empetrum nigrum subsp. caucasicum

Empetrum nigrum subsp.

caucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian crowberry, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, dark green, leathery leaves.

The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and its ecological significance in its native range.
