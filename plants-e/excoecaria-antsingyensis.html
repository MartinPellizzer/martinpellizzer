Excoecaria antsingyensis

Excoecaria antsingyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and striking, brightly colored flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in moist forest understories.
