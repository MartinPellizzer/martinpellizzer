Erica altevivens

Erica altevivens, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica altevivens thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

It is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
