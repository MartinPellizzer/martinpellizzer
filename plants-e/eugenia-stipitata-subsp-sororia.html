Eugenia stipitata subsp. sororia

Eugenia stipitata subsp.

sororia, commonly known as the red-berried guava, is a tropical shrub native to South America. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible. The berries are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal regions and lowland forests.
