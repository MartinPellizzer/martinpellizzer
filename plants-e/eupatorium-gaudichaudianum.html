Eupatorium gaudichaudianum

Eupatorium gaudichaudianum, commonly known as the Hawaiian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Hawaii.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
