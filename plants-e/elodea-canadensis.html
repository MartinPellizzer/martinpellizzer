Elodea canadensis

Elodea canadensis, commonly known as Canadian waterweed, is a freshwater plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, ribbon-like leaves. The plant is typically submerged, though it can also be found in shallow waters with some parts exposed. Elodea canadensis reproduces through vegetative propagation, with nodes on its stems capable of producing new shoots.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
