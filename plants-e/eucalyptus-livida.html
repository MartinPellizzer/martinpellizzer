Eucalyptus livida

Eucalyptus livida, commonly known as the silver-leafed gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name, and its distinctive greyish-white bark that peels in thin strips. The plant typically grows in dry, open forests and woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Eucalyptus livida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some uses in traditional medicine.
