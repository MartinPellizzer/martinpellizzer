Echinodorus natans

Echinodorus natans, commonly known as the floating heart, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water surface. The plant has long, slender stems that can extend several inches below the water. It thrives in shallow, still waters with moderate light exposure.

Echinodorus natans is often used in ponds and aquariums for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
