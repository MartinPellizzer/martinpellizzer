Epipactis helleborine var. rubiginosa

Epipactis helleborine var. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stained helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a single, showy flower with a distinctive greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The varietal name "rubiginosa" refers to the reddish or brownish markings on its leaves and flower.
