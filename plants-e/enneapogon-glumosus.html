Enneapogon glumosus

Enneapogon glumosus, commonly known as the spikegrass, is a species of grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale brown to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a bluish-green color.

Enneapogon glumosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
