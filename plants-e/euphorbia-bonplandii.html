Euphorbia bonplandii

Euphorbia bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonpland's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
