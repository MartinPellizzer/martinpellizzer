Echinopsis limensis

Echinopsis limensis, commonly known as the Limen's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stems and dense clusters of white or pale blue flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well-suited for desert conditions.

Echinopsis limensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
