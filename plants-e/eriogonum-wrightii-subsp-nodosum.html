Eriogonum wrightii subsp. nodosum

Eriogonum wrightii subsp.

nodosum, commonly known as the nodding buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
