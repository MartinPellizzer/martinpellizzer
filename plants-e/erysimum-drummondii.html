Erysimum drummondii

Erysimum drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate and bright yellow flowers with four petals. It is well adapted to drought conditions and can thrive in full sun.

Erysimum drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
