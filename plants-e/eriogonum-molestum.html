Eriogonum molestum

Eriogonum molestum, commonly known as the stickyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-like growth habit and hairy, often sticky leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Eriogonum molestum is a hardy species that plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
