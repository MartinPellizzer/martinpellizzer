Erythrina picta

Erythrina picta, commonly known as the painted flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. Its showy inflorescences are not only ornamental but also attract a variety of pollinators.

Erythrina picta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant due to its striking appearance.
