Epidendrum pucunoense

Epidendrum pucunoense, commonly known as the Pucuno orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments within the cloud forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

The botanical significance of Epidendrum pucunoense lies in its unique adaptations to its ecological niche and its role in the biodiversity of its native habitat.
