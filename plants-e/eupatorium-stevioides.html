Eupatorium stevioides

Eupatorium stevioides, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

Eupatorium stevioides is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
