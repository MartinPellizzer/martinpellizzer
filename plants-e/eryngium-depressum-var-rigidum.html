Eryngium depressum var. rigidum

Eryngium depressum var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of gray-green, spiny leaves that are typically stiff and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are surrounded by bracts that resemble additional leaves. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a distinctive component of its native ecosystem.
