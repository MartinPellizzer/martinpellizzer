Echinocactus wrightii

Echinocactus wrightii, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive crown of spiny, cylindrical stems that resemble a hat. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Echinocactus wrightii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and hardy nature.
