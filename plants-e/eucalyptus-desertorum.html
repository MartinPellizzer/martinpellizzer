Eucalyptus desertorum

Eucalyptus desertorum, commonly known as the desert eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming during the cooler months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to access water from deeper soil layers.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
