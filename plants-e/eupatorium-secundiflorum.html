Eupatorium secundiflorum

Eupatorium secundiflorum, commonly known as slender false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
