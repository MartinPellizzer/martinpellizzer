Eucalyptus loxophleba var. fruticosa

Eucalyptus loxophleba var. fruticosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system for water conservation. It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The varietal form, fruticosa, is distinguished by its more shrub-like growth habit compared to the typical tree form.
