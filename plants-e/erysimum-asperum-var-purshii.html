Erysimum asperum var. purshii

Erysimum asperum var. purshii, commonly known as the Pursh's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
