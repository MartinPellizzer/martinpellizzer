Eriospermum lavranosii

Eriospermum lavranosii, commonly known as the Lavranos' eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriospermum lavranosii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
