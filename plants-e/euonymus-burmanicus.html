Euonymus burmanicus

Euonymus burmanicus, commonly known as Burman's spindletree, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cercidiphyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, followed by orange-red berries that are ornamental. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its attractive foliage and berries, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
