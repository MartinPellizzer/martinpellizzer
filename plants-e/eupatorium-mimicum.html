Eupatorium mimicum

Eupatorium mimicum, commonly known as the mimic eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with hairy stems and serrated leaves. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually purple or pink and are arranged in clustered inflorescences.

Eupatorium mimicum is often found in moist habitats such as swamps and wet meadows.
