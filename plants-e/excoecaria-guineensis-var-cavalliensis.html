Excoecaria guineensis var. cavalliensis

Excoecaria guineensis var. cavalliensis, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers, followed by bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
