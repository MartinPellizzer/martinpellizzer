Eriogonum purpureum

Eriogonum purpureum, commonly known as the purple flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eriogonum purpureum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
