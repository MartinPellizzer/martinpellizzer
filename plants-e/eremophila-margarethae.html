Eremophila margarethae

Eremophila margarethae, commonly known as the silver-leaf eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila margarethae is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
