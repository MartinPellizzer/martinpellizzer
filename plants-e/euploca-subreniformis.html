Euploca subreniformis

Euploca subreniformis, commonly known as the broadleaf euploca, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are broad and reniform, meaning kidney-shaped, and its flowers are small and clustered in heads.

Euploca subreniformis plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is often found in conservation areas due to its ecological significance.
