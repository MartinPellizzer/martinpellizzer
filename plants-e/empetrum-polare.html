Empetrum polare

Empetrum polare, commonly known as snowberry or polar heath, is a hardy shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant thrives in cold, nutrient-poor soils and is adapted to extreme environmental conditions. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, providing year-round cover in harsh climates.

Empetrum polare plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife in its native habitats.
