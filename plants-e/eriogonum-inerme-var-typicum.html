Eriogonum inerme var. typicum

Eriogonum inerme var. typicum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. Its fibrous roots help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh desert conditions.
