Echinopsis calochlora

Echinopsis calochlora, commonly known as the golden echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from white to yellow, often blooming at night. It is a slow-growing, columnar cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
