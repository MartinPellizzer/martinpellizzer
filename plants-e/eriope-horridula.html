Eriope horridula

Eriope horridula, commonly known as the hairy eriope, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, and they bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Eriope horridula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
