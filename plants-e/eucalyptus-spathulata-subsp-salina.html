Eucalyptus spathulata subsp. salina

Eucalyptus spathulata subsp.

salina, commonly known as the coastal mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically pale blue to white in color. It thrives in coastal regions and is adapted to sandy, saline soils. Eucalyptus spathulata subsp.

salina is a hardy species that plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
