Evolvulus fuscus var. acutifolius

Evolvulus fuscus var. acutifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It is native to Central and South America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This species is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and rapid growth habit.
