Engelhardia villosa

Engelhardia villosa, commonly known as the woolly engelhardia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
