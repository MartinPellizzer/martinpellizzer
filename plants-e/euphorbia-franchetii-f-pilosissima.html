Euphorbia franchetii f. pilosissima

Euphorbia franchetii f. pilosissima, commonly known as the hairy-fringed spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
