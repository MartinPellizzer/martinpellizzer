Eriogonum ovalifolium var. williamsiae

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. williamsiae, commonly known as the Williams' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces showy, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are oval-shaped and typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
