Euphorbia claytonioides

Euphorbia claytonioides, commonly known as the Cape Tribulation euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and has a distinctive appearance with its branching structure and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well adapted to its coastal habitat.

Euphorbia claytonioides is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
