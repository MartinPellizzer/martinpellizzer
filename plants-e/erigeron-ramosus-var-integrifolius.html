Erigeron ramosus var. integrifolius

Erigeron ramosus var. integrifolius, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are entire, meaning they lack teeth or lobes, and are covered in fine hairs.

The showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
