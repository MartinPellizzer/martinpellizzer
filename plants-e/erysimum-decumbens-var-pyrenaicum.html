Erysimum decumbens var. pyrenaicum

Erysimum decumbens var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It features basal rosettes of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
