Eugenia petrinensis

Eugenia petrinensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Natal tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Eugenia petrinensis is also valued for its timber and has cultural significance in some regions.
