Eupatorium glaberrimum var. michelianum

Eupatorium glaberrimum var. michelianum, commonly known as Michelia's rough eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped, toothed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
