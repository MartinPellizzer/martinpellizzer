Echinocactus pflanzii

Echinocactus pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stem that is typically ribbed and spiny, adapted for water storage and protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are capable of storing significant amounts of water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Echinocactus pflanzii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
