Eulophia venosa var. papuana

Eulophia venosa var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan eulophia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and nearby islands.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish-white in color. The plant has erect, leafy stems and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually long and strap-like, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
