Erysimum curvifolium

Erysimum curvifolium, commonly known as the bent-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are elongated and often curved, giving it its common name.

Erysimum curvifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers.
