Elymus bakeri

Elymus bakeri, commonly known as Baker's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. The plant has slender, grass-like blades with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicine.

Elymus bakeri plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
