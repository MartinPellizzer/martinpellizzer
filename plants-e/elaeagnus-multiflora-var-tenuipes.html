Elaeagnus multiflora var. tenuipes

Elaeagnus multiflora var. tenuipes, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It is adapted to grow in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

The fruit is a small, dark reddish-brown drupe that ripens in late summer.
