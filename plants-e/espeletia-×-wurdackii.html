Espeletia × wurdackii

Espeletia × wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's espeletia, is a hybrid plant native to the cloud forests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its native environment.

Espeletia × wurdackii is often found in association with other high-altitude flora, contributing to the unique biodiversity of its habitat.
