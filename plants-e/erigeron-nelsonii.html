Erigeron nelsonii

Erigeron nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features slender stems and narrow, gray-green leaves.

Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
