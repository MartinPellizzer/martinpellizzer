Eragrostis mossulensis

Eragrostis mossulensis, commonly known as the Mesopotamian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
