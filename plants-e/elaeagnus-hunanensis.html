Elaeagnus hunanensis

Elaeagnus hunanensis, commonly known as the Hunan silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has a spreading habit and produces fragrant flowers in the spring. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus hunanensis is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
