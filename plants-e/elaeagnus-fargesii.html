Elaeagnus fargesii

Elaeagnus fargesii, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and fragrant, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese cuisine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Elaeagnus fargesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
