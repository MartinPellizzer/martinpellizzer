Eupatorium vitalbae

Eupatorium vitalbae, commonly known as the lesser boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium vitalbae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antispasmodic and anti-inflammatory effects.
