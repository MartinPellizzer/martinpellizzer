Eleocharis quadrangulata var. typica

Eleocharis quadrangulata var. typica, commonly known as the four-angled sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are typically four-angled, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture.

Eleocharis quadrangulata var. typica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
