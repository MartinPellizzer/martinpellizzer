Epidendrum godseffianum

Epidendrum godseffianum, commonly known as the Godseff's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing a series of pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features long, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, arranged in a raceme, and are known for their delicate, fragrant appearance.

Epidendrum godseffianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively ease of care in suitable growing conditions.
