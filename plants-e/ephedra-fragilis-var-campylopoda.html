Ephedra fragilis var. campylopoda

Ephedra fragilis var. campylopoda, commonly known as the slender ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

This species is often found in rocky habitats and is considered a valuable plant for ecological restoration in desert ecosystems.
