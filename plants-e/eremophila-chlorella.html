Eremophila chlorella

Eremophila chlorella, commonly known as the yellow emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and heat resistance. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eremophila chlorella is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
