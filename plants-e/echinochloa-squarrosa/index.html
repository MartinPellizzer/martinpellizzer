Echinochloa Squarrosa

Echinochloa squarrosa is a common grass species found in various habitats across the globe. It is often seen growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides due to its adaptability. This plant is known for its distinctive appearance, featuring hairy leaves and spiky seed heads. It plays an important role in ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil. Despite its invasive tendencies in some regions, it remains a significant part of many natural landscapes.
