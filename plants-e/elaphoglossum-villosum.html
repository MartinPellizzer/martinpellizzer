Elaphoglossum villosum

Elaphoglossum villosum, commonly known as the hairy maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can thrive in cool, humid conditions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Elaphoglossum villosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
