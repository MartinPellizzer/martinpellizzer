Eleocharis mucronulata

Eleocharis mucronulata, commonly known as the spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical inflorescence.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, triangular stems that are often covered in short, stiff hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in spike-like structures, with the female flowers at the base and the male flowers above.

Eleocharis mucronulata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
