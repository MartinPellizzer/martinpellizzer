Elaeocarpus humblotii

Elaeocarpus humblotii, commonly known as the Madagascar hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary rainforests.

Due to its restricted distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
