Eupatorium conyzoides var. tambillense

Eupatorium conyzoides var. tambillense, commonly known as tambillense blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it often grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its striking floral display.
