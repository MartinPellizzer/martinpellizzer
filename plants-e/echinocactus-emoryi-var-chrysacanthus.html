Echinocactus emoryi var. chrysacanthus

Echinocactus emoryi var. chrysacanthus, commonly known as the golden cholla, is a distinctive cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that branch out in a bushy formation. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its arid habitat. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss in hot, dry environments.

The golden cholla is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant in its native range.
