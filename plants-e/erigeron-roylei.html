Erigeron roylei

Erigeron roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its blue to violet flower heads are often adorned with white or pale blue ray florets, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Erigeron roylei is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
