Elaeocarpus longipetiolatus

Elaeocarpus longipetiolatus, commonly known as the long-petioled elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with long petioles. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
