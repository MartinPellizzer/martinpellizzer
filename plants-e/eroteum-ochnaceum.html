Eroteum ochnaceum

Eroteum ochnaceum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Ochnaceae and is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with five petals and prominent stamens.

Eroteum ochnaceum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
