Eritrichium ovalifolium

Eritrichium ovalifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf alpine fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and oval-shaped, often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Eritrichium ovalifolium is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine tundra and meadows.
