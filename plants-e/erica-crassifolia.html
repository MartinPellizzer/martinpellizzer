Erica crassifolia

Erica crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica crassifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
