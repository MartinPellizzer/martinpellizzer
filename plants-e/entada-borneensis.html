Entada borneensis

Entada borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean flame tree, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody stem with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its fruit is a long, cylindrical pod that splits open to release its seeds.

The Bornean flame tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of timber.
