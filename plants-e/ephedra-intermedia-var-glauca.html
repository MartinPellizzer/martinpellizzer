Ephedra intermedia var. glauca

Ephedra intermedia var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-green ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive blue-green appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically pollinated by wind. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

This species is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
