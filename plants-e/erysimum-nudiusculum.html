Erysimum nudiusculum

Erysimum nudiusculum, commonly known as the naked-stemed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are smooth and often hairless. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals and are borne in clusters at the base of the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
