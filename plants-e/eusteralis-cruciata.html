Eusteralis cruciata

Eusteralis cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a cross-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eusteralis cruciata is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its role in supporting pollinator populations.
