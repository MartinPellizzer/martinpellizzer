Erica canescens var. micranthera

Erica canescens var. micranthera, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
