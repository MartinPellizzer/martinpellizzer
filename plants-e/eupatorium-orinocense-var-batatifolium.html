Eupatorium orinocense var. batatifolium

Eupatorium orinocense var. batatifolium, commonly known as the batatifolium feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, large, and often fuzzy leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
