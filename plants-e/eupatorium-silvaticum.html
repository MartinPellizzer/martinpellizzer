Eupatorium silvaticum

Eupatorium silvaticum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fuzzy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and thickets, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
