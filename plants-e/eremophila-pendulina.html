Eremophila pendulina

Eremophila pendulina, commonly known as the hanging eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching structure. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila pendulina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
