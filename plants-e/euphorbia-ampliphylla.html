Euphorbia ampliphylla

Euphorbia ampliphylla, commonly known as the giant leaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, and often lobed leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves and stems.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
