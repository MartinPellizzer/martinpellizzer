Eriochloa ekmanii

Eriochloa ekmanii, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Eriochloa ekmanii is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to low-nutrient soils.
