Eugenia megacarpa

Eugenia megacarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that develop into large, yellow or orange fruits with a thick, aromatic skin. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
