Euphrasia curta subsp. coerulea

Euphrasia curta subsp.

coerulea, commonly known as the blue-eyed toadflax, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to blue, with prominent yellow markings.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
