Eugenia umbellulifera

Eugenia umbellulifera, commonly known as the tropical myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
