Eurya japonica var. uniflora

Eurya japonica var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower evergreen holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that matures in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
