Eragrostis rojasii

Eragrostis rojasii, commonly known as the Rosas' lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, and is adapted to low-nutrient soils.

Eragrostis rojasii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
