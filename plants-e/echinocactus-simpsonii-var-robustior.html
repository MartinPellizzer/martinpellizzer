Echinocactus simpsonii var. robustior

Echinocactus simpsonii var. robustior, commonly known as the Simpson's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and prominent spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
