Epimedium pinnatum f. integrifolium

Epimedium pinnatum f. integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leaved barberry, is a cultivar of the barberry plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and uninterrupted.

It is a deciduous shrub native to China, typically growing in woodland or rocky habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. Its foliage provides ornamental value throughout the growing season, and it is often cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in partial shade.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
