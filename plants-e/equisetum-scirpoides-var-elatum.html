Equisetum scirpoides var. elatum

Equisetum scirpoides var. elatum, commonly known as the tall swamp horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the base of the stems, which are used for reproduction. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
