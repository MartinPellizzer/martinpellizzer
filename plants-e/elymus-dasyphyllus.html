Elymus dasyphyllus

Elymus dasyphyllus, commonly known as the rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicle-like inflorescences with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils.

Elymus dasyphyllus is often found in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites.
