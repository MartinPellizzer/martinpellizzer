Eucalyptus conferruminata subsp. conferruminata

Eucalyptus conferruminata subsp.

conferruminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to western Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are usually arranged in clusters and are typically white or pale yellow, attracting a variety of pollinators. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
