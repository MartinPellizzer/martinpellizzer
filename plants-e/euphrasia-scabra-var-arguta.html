Euphrasia scabra var. arguta

Euphrasia scabra var. arguta, commonly known as the rough eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it its common name.

Euphrasia scabra var. arguta is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
