Erythronium purpurascens var. uniflorum

Erythronium purpurascens var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered dog's-tooth violet, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces a single, showy, bell-shaped flower that is typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive purple tinge. The plant features heart-shaped, bright green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
