Eupatorium squarrosoramosum

Eupatorium squarrosoramosum, commonly known as the roughside eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium squarrosoramosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
