Erodium moschatum var. scissifolium

Erodium moschatum var. scissifolium, commonly known as the fragrant bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers and trifoliate leaves that are often cut or frayed, giving it its scientific name "scissifolium.". The plant is an annual herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
