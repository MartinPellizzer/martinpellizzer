Echinops turczaninowii

Echinops turczaninowii, commonly known as the Persian globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble a globe, which are a key feature of the genus Echinops. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by the distinctive, woody, spiky fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Echinops turczaninowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
