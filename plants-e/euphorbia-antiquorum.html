Euphorbia antiquorum

Euphorbia antiquorum, commonly known as the ancient spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous branching stems that produce small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has spiny leaves and produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique and resilient member of the Euphorbiaceae family.
