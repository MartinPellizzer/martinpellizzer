Erica laeta

Erica laeta, commonly known as the silver heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax, giving it a distinctive appearance. Erica laeta typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It produces delicate white or pale pink flowers in the spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.
