Erica westphalingia

Erica westphalingia, commonly known as the Westphalian heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in spring. Erica westphalingia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
