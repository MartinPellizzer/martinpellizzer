Equisetum ramosissimum

Equisetum ramosissimum, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a primitive vascular plant that belongs to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its jointed, segmented stems and its ability to produce both spores and seeds, making it a unique example of a living fossil. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it possesses whorls of branches that give it a distinctive appearance.

Equisetum ramosissimum is known for its rapid growth and its role in ecological succession in disturbed habitats.
