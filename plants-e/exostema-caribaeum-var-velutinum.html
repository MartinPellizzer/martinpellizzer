Exostema caribaeum var. velutinum

Exostema caribaeum var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvetleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure includes a hairy stem and alternate leaves with a distinctive pubescent surface.
