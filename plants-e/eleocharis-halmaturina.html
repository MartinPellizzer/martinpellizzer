Eleocharis halmaturina

Eleocharis halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often in dry, open habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to poor soil conditions.

Eleocharis halmaturina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
