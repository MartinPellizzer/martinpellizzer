Eugenia laterifolia

Eugenia laterifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Eugenia laterifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical climates.
