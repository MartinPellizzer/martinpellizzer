Erysimum riphaeanum

Erysimum riphaeanum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Erysimum riphaeanum is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
