Eugenia botequimensis

Eugenia botequimensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or botequim tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in local traditional medicine.

The plant's ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species within its native habitat.
