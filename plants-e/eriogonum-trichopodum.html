Eriogonum trichopodum

Eriogonum trichopodum, commonly known as Townsend's milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, thread-like leaflets, and it produces legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.

Eriogonum trichopodum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing food for various wildlife species.
