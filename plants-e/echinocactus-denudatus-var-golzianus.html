Echinocactus denudatus var. golzianus

Echinocactus denudatus var. golzianus, commonly known as the Golzian hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem covered in thick, spiny areoles, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and desert gardeners.
