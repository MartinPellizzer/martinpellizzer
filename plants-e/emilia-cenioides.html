Emilia cenioides

Emilia cenioides, commonly known as the golden weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields or along roadsides.

Emilia cenioides is known for its ability to spread rapidly and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
