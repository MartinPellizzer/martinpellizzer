Euodia trichopetala

Euodia trichopetala, commonly known as the three-lobed euodia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color as they ripen. Euodia trichopetala is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
