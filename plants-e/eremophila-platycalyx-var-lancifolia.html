Eremophila platycalyx var. lancifolia

Eremophila platycalyx var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikes of tubular, violet or purple flowers, which are typically borne in late summer to autumn. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid landscapes.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
