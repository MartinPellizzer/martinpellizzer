Eleocharis juncoides

Eleocharis juncoides, commonly known as the common spike-rush, is a species of sedges native to wetland environments across much of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and contribute to the ecological health of wetland ecosystems.

Eleocharis juncoides is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate a range of water conditions.
