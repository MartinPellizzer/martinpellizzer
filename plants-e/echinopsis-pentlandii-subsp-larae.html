Echinopsis pentlandii subsp. larae

Echinopsis pentlandii subsp.

larae, commonly known as the Lara's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
