Eurybia cardiophylla

Eurybia cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped, which gives the plant its common name, and they are often covered in fine, white hairs. This species thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
