Euphorbia octogona

Euphorbia octogona, commonly known as the eight-lobed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a crown of modified leaves called bracts. It is well adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Euphorbia octogona is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
