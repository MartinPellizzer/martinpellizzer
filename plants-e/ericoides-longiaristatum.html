Ericoides longiaristatum

Ericoides longiaristatum, commonly known as the slender heath shrub, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy surface. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the tips of the branches. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salinity and strong winds.

Ericoides longiaristatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize sandy soils in coastal environments.
