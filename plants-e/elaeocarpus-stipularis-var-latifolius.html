Elaeocarpus stipularis var. latifolius

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
