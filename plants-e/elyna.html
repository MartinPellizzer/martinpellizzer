Elyna

Elyna, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Elyna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.

Its flowers emit a sweet, intoxicating scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.
