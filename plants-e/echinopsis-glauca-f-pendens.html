Echinopsis glauca f. pendens

Echinopsis glauca f. pendens, commonly known as the pendant echinopsis, is a unique variant of the echinopsis genus native to South America.

This plant is characterized by its drooping, cylindrical stems that bear clusters of white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for succulent gardens. The plant's stems are covered in white, waxy tubercles that help reduce water loss.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.
