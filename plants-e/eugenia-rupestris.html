Eugenia rupestris

Eugenia rupestris, commonly known as the rock apple or wild guava, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which is usually yellow or red, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Eugenia rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
