Erythrina × orba

Erythrina × orba, commonly known as the coral tree, is a hybrid species belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its striking bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina × orba also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which helps improve soil fertility.
