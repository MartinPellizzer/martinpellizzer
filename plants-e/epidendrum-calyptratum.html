Epidendrum calyptratum

Epidendrum calyptratum, commonly known as the Christmas Orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, arching stems that bear clusters of pendulous flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers that are typically 3-5 cm in diameter, with a distinctive calyptra covering the reproductive structures. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Epidendrum calyptratum is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
