Echinopsis scheeri

Echinopsis scheeri, commonly known as the Scheeri's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, specifically Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and a crown of spiny, white to pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments with its thick, fleshy stems that store water.

Echinopsis scheeri is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
