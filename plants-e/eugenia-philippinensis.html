Eugenia philippinensis

Eugenia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Eugenia philippinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.
