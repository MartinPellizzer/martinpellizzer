Etlingera facifera

Etlingera facifera, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble torches, which are modified leaves that protect the small, yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
