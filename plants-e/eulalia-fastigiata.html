Eulalia fastigiata

Eulalia fastigiata, commonly known as the narrowleaf pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features tall, stiff, and upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces fluffy, white plumes during the summer months, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical structure is characterized by its dense growth habit and distinctive, showy inflorescences.
