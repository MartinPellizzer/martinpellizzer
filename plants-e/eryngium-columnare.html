Eryngium columnare

Eryngium columnare, commonly known as the spiked eryngium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, spiky flower heads that are typically blue to purple in color, surrounded by stiff, bristly leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough, hairy texture.

Eryngium columnare is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
