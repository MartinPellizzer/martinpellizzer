Elaeocarpus hainanensis

Elaeocarpus hainanensis, commonly known as the Hainan elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to China's Hainan Island.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pale pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a rough, woody husk. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
