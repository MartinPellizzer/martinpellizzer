Erythrina rubrinervia

Erythrina rubrinervia, commonly known as the red-veined coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often arranged in dense, branching clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, each with prominent red veins that give it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina rubrinervia is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
