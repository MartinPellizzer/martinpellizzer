Epidendrum callista

Epidendrum callista, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Epidendrum callista is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
