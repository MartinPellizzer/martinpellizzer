Echinops tricholepis

Echinops tricholepis, commonly known as the thorned globe thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads. The plant features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the distinctive spiky fruit heads.

Echinops tricholepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
