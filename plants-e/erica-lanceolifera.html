Erica lanceolifera

Erica lanceolifera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in sandy or well-drained soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Erica lanceolifera is adapted to harsh environments, with its small leaves reducing water loss and enabling survival in arid conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
