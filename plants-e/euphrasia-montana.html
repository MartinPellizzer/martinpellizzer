Euphrasia montana

Euphrasia montana, commonly known as the mountain eyebright, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, ovate leaves that have a velvety texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in whorls along the stems, and they bloom from late spring to early summer.

Euphrasia montana is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for eye and respiratory conditions.
