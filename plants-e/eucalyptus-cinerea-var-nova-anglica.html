Eucalyptus cinerea var. nova-anglica

Eucalyptus cinerea var. nova-anglica, commonly known as the silver dollar eucalyptus, is a variant of the narrow-leaf mallee.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, typically flowering during the cooler months. It is native to the southern regions of Australia and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its unique foliage and resilience.
