Eugenia jambosoides

Eugenia jambosoides, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
