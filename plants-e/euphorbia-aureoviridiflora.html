Euphorbia aureoviridiflora

Euphorbia aureoviridiflora, commonly known as the golden-green spurge, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a subtle yellowish or golden hue. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
