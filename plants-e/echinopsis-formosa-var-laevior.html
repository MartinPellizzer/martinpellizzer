Echinopsis formosa var. laevior

Echinopsis formosa var. laevior, commonly known as the Laevior Moon Cactus, is a compact cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a swollen, globular stem with a distinctive white or pale green coloration, often adorned with spines that are shorter and more sparse compared to other Echinopsis species. The plant produces striking yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It is well-suited for cultivation in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor collections.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance make it a favored ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
