Eleusine lima

Eleusine lima, commonly known as finger millet, is a small-grained annual grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted inflorescence and small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant produces edible grains that are rich in calcium and iron, making it a nutritious staple in many regions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is well adapted to poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important crop for both food security and ecological resilience.
