Elytraria

Elytraria, commonly known as the golden orchid or elytraria grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with narrow leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The flowers are typically greenish or yellowish, with a distinctive structure that includes a bract-like sheath enclosing the spikelet. Elytraria species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.

These plants are sometimes cultivated for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
