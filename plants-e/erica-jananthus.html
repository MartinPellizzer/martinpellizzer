Erica jananthus

Erica jananthus, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica jananthus thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
