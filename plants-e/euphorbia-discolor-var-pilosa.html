Euphorbia discolor var. pilosa

Euphorbia discolor var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are usually small, fleshy, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
