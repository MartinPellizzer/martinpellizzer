Emilia negellensis

Emilia negellensis, commonly known as the Ethiopian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. Emilia negellensis thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
