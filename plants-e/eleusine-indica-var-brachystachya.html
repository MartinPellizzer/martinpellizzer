Eleusine indica var. brachystachya

Eleusine indica var. brachystachya, commonly known as slender false brome, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or tufts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and soil stabilization efforts.
