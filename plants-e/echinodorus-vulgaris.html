Echinodorus vulgaris

Echinodorus vulgaris, commonly known as the broad-leaf waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length. This plant thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes. Echinodorus vulgaris is a popular choice for aquascaping due to its attractive foliage and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.

It also plays a role in water filtration and oxygenation in aquatic ecosystems.
