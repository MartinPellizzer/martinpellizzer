Elaeocarpus rumphii

Elaeocarpus rumphii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically blue or black when mature. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
