Eugenia adenodes

Eugenia adenodes, commonly known as the red apple tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional medicine.
