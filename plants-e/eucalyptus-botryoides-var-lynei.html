Eucalyptus botryoides var. lynei

Eucalyptus botryoides var. lynei, commonly known as the Lyne's honey gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are highly aromatic. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
