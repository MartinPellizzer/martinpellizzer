Eriochloa purpurascens

Eriochloa purpurascens, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces dense, purple-tinged inflorescences that are often mistaken for true brome grasses. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils. It is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along rocky outcrops.

Eriochloa purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
