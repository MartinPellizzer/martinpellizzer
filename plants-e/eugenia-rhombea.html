Eugenia rhombea

Eugenia rhombea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
