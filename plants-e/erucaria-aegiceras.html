Erucaria aegiceras

Erucaria aegiceras, commonly known as the mangrove shrub, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline and brackish water environments.

It is native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia and the Indian subcontinent. The plant has a distinctive appearance with elongated, leathery leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays a crucial role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features reflect its ability to thrive in challenging, waterlogged conditions.
