Eupatorium flavisetum var. typicum

Eupatorium flavisetum var. typicum, commonly known as the yellow woodruff, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellowish to whitish inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
