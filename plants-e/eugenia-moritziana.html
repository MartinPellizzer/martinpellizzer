Eugenia moritziana

Eugenia moritziana, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are yellow when ripe and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Eugenia moritziana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
