Eleutherococcus setulosus

Eleutherococcus setulosus, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Russia, Korea, and northeastern China.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its adaptogenic properties, which are believed to enhance physical and mental performance.
