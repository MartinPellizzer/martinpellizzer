Eugenia sticheromischa

Eugenia sticheromischa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red or yellow berries that are rich in vitamin C and often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
