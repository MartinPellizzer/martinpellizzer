It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely toothed margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum barnebyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
