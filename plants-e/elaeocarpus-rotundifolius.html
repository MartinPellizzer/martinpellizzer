Elaeocarpus rotundifolius

Elaeocarpus rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. Its foliage and fruit structure make it a valuable species for ornamental and ecological purposes.

Elaeocarpus rotundifolius is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to humid, shaded environments.
