Echinopogon asper

Echinopogon asper, commonly known as the rough spikegrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Echinopogon asper is typically found in dry, open habitats such as sandy soils and disturbed areas.

It is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
