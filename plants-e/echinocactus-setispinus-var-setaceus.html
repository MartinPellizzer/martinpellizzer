Echinocactus setispinus var. setaceus

Echinocactus setispinus var. setaceus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, often covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on long, slender stems. It is well adapted to dry climates, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem to survive periods of drought.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
