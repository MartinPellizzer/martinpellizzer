Echinopsis trichosa

Echinopsis trichosa, commonly known as the White Cactus or White Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Echinopsis trichosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
