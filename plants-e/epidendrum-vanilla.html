Epidendrum vanilla

Epidendrum vanilla, commonly known as the vanilla orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Americas, particularly Central and South America, and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are often used in the extraction of vanilla flavoring. Its roots are typically found in the bark of trees, where it absorbs moisture and nutrients from the air.

The plant's botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a unique reproductive system involving specialized pollination by specific bees.
