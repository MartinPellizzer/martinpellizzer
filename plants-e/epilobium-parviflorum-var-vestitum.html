Epilobium parviflorum var. vestitum

Epilobium parviflorum var. vestitum, commonly known as the woolly willow or velvet willow, is a herbaceous plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest understories. It produces small, pink to reddish flowers in late spring to early summer.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and habitat restoration.
