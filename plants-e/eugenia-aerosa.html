Eugenia aerosa

Eugenia aerosa, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically appearing in spring. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin layers, and it has a reputation for being a hardy, fast-growing tree.

Eugenia aerosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
