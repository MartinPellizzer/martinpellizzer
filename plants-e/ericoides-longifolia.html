Ericoides longifolia

Ericoides longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved ericoid, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late summer. Ericoides longifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
