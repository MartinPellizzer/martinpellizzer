Erodium cicutarium var. psilorhynchum

Erodium cicutarium var. psilorhynchum, commonly known as the narrowleaf bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in some regions.
