Elaeocarpus quadrilobus

Elaeocarpus quadrilobus, commonly known as the four-lobed elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
