Echinocereus acifer var. tenuispinus

Echinocereus acifer var. tenuispinus, commonly known as the slender-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sparse, pale green to blue-gray spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer, which are typically borne at the stem tips. It is a succulent, adapted to arid environments with thick, water-storing stems.

This variety is distinguished by its shorter, more delicate spines compared to other Echinocereus species.
