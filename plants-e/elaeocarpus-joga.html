Elaeocarpus joga

Elaeocarpus joga, commonly known as the Joga elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or purple in color. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a leathery texture, while the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

This species is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
