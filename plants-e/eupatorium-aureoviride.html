Eupatorium aureoviride

Eupatorium aureoviride, commonly known as golden-green eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Eupatorium aureoviride is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique leaf color and ability to attract pollinators.
