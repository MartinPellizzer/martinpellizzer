Eustoma russellianum var. gracile

Eustoma russellianum var. gracile, commonly known as the slender blue mistflower, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, often in clusters atop slender stems. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
