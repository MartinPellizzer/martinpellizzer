Erysimum erucastrifolium

Erysimum erucastrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinct serrated edge. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
