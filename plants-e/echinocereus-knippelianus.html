Echinocereus knippelianus

Echinocereus knippelianus, commonly known as the knippelianus cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Echinocereus knippelianus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
