Epicampes macrotis

Epicampes macrotis, commonly known as the large-flowered lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

The flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, making it a notable wildflower in its native range.
