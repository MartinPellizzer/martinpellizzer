Eulophia caricifolia var. antennisepala

Eulophia caricifolia var. antennisepala, commonly known as the Antennae-petaled Eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with elongated petals that resemble antennae, giving it its distinctive name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
