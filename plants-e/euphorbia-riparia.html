Euphorbia riparia

Euphorbia riparia, commonly known as the river spurge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures. It thrives in moist, sandy soils along riverbanks and wetlands.

Euphorbia riparia is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance.
