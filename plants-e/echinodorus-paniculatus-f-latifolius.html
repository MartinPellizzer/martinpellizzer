Echinodorus paniculatus f. latifolius

Echinodorus paniculatus f. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a variant of the waterweed genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 20 cm in length, providing a striking visual in aquatic environments. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical freshwater habitats, often found in slow-moving rivers and ponds. It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to oxygenate water.

Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to its aquatic setting.
