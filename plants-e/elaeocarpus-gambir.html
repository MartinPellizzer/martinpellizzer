Elaeocarpus gambir

Elaeocarpus gambir, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has cultural significance in some regions.
