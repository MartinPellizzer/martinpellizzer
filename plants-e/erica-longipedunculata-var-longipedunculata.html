Erica longipedunculata var. longipedunculata

Erica longipedunculata var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stalked heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its floral display, which ranges from white to pink, occurs during the summer months, making it a notable ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
