Ecbolium acuminatum

Ecbolium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed ecbolium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 60 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ecbolium acuminatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
