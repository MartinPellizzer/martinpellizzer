Erica venusta

Erica venusta, commonly known as the velvet heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

Erica venusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
