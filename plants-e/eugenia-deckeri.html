Eugenia deckeri

Eugenia deckeri, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

Eugenia deckeri is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
