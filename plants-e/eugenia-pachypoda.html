Eugenia pachypoda

Eugenia pachypoda, commonly known as the broad-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish or yellowish when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
