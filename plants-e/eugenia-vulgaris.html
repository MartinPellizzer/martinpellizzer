Eugenia vulgaris

Eugenia vulgaris, commonly known as the common guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in vitamin C and other nutrients. Its fruit is widely used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Eugenia vulgaris thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
