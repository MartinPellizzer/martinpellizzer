Epifagus virginiana f. atropurpurea

Epifagus virginiana f. atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple-leafed witch hazel, is a rare and striking variant of the witch hazel species.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its yellow flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy texture.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in late winter or early spring, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.
