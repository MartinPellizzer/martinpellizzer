Eriocaulon maronderanum

Eriocaulon maronderanum, commonly known as the Marondera rush, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Zimbabwe and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
