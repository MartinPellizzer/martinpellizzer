Echinopsis haematantha var. elongata

Echinopsis haematantha var. elongata, commonly known as the long-spined moon cactus, is a succulent belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina, and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
