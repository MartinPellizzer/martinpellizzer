Eulophia scottii

Eulophia scottii, commonly known as the Scott's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a distinctive lip, and it blooms during the wet season.

Eulophia scottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
