Eriogonum angulosum subsp. bidentatum

Eriogonum angulosum subsp.

bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant has finely toothed leaves with a distinctive two-toothed appearance at the leaf tips, which is a key identifying feature. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
