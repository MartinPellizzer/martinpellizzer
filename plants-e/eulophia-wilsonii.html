Eulophia wilsonii

Eulophia wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's eulophia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow blooms that are often adorned with distinctive markings. Eulophia wilsonii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for collectors and horticulturists.
