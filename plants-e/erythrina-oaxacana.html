Erythrina oaxacana

Erythrina oaxacana, commonly known as the Oaxacan coral tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vivid red, flame-like flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and public spaces.

Additionally, it plays a role in local ecosystems and has been used in traditional medicine.
