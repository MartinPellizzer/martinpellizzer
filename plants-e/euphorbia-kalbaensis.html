Euphorbia kalbaensis

Euphorbia kalbaensis, commonly known as the Kalba spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, spine-tipped leaves and produces a unique, yellowish-green sap that is toxic. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other spurge species.

Euphorbia kalbaensis is often found in rocky soils and can thrive with minimal water, making it a resilient plant in desert ecosystems.
