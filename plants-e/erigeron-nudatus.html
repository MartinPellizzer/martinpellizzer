Erigeron nudatus

Erigeron nudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually hairless and alternate, with a distinctive silvery-gray appearance.

It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.
