Euphorbia polycarpa var. intermixta

Euphorbia polycarpa var. intermixta, commonly known as the variegated milk bush, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to both indoor and outdoor collections.
