Euphorbia falsa

Euphorbia falsa, commonly known as false spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its milky sap and often spiny structures. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with erect, branched stems that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia.
