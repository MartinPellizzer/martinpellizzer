Echinocactus meonacanthus

Echinocactus meonacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is typically covered in dense, white spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, branched pedicels. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and xeriscaping.
