Espeletia × guascensis

Espeletia × guascensis, commonly known as the Escobilla de Guasca, is a hybrid plant native to the Andean highlands of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted rosette of silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in the spring. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
