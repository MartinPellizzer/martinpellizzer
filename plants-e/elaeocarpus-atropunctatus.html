Elaeocarpus atropunctatus

Elaeocarpus atropunctatus, commonly known as the black-punctured elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
