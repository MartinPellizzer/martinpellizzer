Evolvulus elegans var. strictus

Evolvulus elegans var. strictus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, upright stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in some regions.
