Eugenia rigidifolia subsp. tsugifolia

Eugenia rigidifolia subsp.

tsugifolia, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family. It is native to Japan and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers.

The leaves are glossy, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture, which contributes to its ornamental value. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its dense foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
