Eutropia obovata

Eutropia obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and dark green, broad-ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Eutropia obovata is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

It is used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
