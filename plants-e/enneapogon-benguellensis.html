Enneapogon benguellensis

Enneapogon benguellensis, commonly known as the Benguella grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
