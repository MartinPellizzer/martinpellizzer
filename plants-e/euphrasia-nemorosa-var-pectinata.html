Euphrasia nemorosa var. pectinata

Euphrasia nemorosa var. pectinata, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is often found in moist, calcareous soils and is part of the Scrophulariaceae family.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
