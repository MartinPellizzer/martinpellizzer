Eupatorium pycnocephalum

Eupatorium pycnocephalum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves and a compact, rounded growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium pycnocephalum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
