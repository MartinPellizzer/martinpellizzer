Elaeocarpus montanus

Elaeocarpus montanus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded forest environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
