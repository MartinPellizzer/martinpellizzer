Eleocharis atropurpurea

Eleocharis atropurpurea, commonly known as purple spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive purple-tinted inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense mats that help stabilize shoreline habitats. Its small, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis atropurpurea plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
