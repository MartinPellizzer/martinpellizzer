Echeveria bifurcata

Echeveria bifurcata, commonly known as the Split Leaf Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often split or lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the spring or summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts.
