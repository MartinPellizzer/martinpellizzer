Erigeron nevadensis var. pygmaeus

Erigeron nevadensis var. pygmaeus, commonly known as the Sierra daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white to pale pink petals and yellow centers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountain meadows and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
