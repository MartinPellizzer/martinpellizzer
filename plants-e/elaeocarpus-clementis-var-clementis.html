Elaeocarpus clementis var. clementis

Elaeocarpus clementis var. clementis, commonly known as the Clement's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
