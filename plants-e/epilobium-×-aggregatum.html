Epilobium × aggregatum

Epilobium × aggregatum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a hybrid species within the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

Epilobium × aggregatum is known for its ability to spread vegetatively through stolons, making it a common and sometimes invasive species in various habitats.
