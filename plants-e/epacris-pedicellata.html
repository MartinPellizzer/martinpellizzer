Epacris pedicellata

Epacris pedicellata, commonly known as the pink epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink to red in color and are arranged in dense spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
