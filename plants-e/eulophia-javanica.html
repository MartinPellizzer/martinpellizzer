Eulophia javanica

Eulophia javanica, commonly known as the Java eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia javanica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is occasionally cultivated for horticultural purposes.
