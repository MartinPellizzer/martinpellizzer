Eupatorium baccharoides var. typicum

Eupatorium baccharoides var. typicum, commonly known as coastal eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or saline environments, such as marshes and dunes. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
