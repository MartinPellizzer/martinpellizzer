Ervum tenoreanum

Ervum tenoreanum, commonly known as the tenorean bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, similar to other beans. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged symmetrically.

Ervum tenoreanum is often grown for both its ornamental value and its potential as a food source.
