Erigeron alpinus var. ramosus

Erigeron alpinus var. ramosus, commonly known as alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, dry environments. It features a compact, low-growing structure with hairy stems and blue to purple ray florets.

Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh, high-altitude environment.
