Erythrophleum gabunense

Erythrophleum gabunense, commonly known as the Gabon legume or African ironwood, is a large evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The tree has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces showy, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

Its fruit is a legume pod containing several flat, kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source.
