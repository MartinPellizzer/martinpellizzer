Ectrosia agrostoides

Ectrosia agrostoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ectrosia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access moisture.

Ectrosia agrostoides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
