Ecuadoria intagana

Ecuadoria intagana, commonly known as the Ecuadorian starflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped petals that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of pubescence.

Ecuadoria intagana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.
