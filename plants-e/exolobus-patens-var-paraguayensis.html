Exolobus patens var. paraguayensis

Exolobus patens var. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
