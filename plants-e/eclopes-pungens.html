Eclopes pungens

Eclopes pungens, commonly known as the spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers during the warm season, which are clustered in a cup-like structure. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its sharp spines, it requires careful handling to avoid injury.
