Euphorbia mauritanica

Euphorbia mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania spurge, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia. It has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation.

Euphorbia mauritanica is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued as an ornamental plant.
