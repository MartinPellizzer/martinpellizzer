Eria monophylla

Eria monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf orchid, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its single, elongated leaf that serves as both a photosynthetic organ and a water-storing structure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Eria monophylla is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
