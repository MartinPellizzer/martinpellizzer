Echium hypertropicum var. nudum

Echium hypertropicum var. nudum, commonly known as the naked echium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to a Mediterranean climate.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
