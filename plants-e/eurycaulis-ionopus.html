Eurycaulis ionopus

Eurycaulis ionopus, commonly known as the golden spire, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to three meters in height, adorned with clusters of bright yellow flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardens.
