Eugenia libens

Eugenia libens, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. The fruits are typically harvested when they are fully ripe, and they are valued for their unique flavor and medicinal properties.

Eugenia libens thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
