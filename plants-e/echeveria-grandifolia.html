Echeveria grandifolia

Echeveria grandifolia, commonly known as the Grandifolia Echeveria, is a species of flowering plant in the Crassulaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure composed of thick, fleshy, and often blue-green colored leaves. The plant produces showy, violet to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria grandifolia is a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
