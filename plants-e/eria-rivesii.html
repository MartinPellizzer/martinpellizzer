Eria rivesii

Eria rivesii, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its roots are epiphytic, meaning they attach to trees rather than growing in soil.

Eria rivesii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
