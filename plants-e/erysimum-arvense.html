Erysimum arvense

Erysimum arvense, commonly known as field honesty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is an annual or biennial herb that grows up to 60 cm tall, featuring upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are arranged in racemes. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.
