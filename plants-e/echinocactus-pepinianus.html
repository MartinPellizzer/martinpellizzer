Echinocactus pepinianus

Echinocactus pepinianus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, apple-like fruits. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and collections.
