Eryngium pallescens

Eryngium pallescens, commonly known as the pale thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. Eryngium pallescens thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and desert regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
