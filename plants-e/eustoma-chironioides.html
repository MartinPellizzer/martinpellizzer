Eustoma chironioides

Eustoma chironioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Eustoma chironioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
