Elatine hydropiper var. gussonei

Elatine hydropiper var. gussonei, commonly known as the water pepper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces tiny white or pink flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils and has a distinctive peppery scent when crushed.

This variant is often found in temperate regions and is known for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
