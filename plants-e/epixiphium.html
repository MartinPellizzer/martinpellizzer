Epixiphium

Epixiphium, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its lack of roots and its ability to absorb nutrients through its leaves rather than through soil. The plant typically grows on the branches of trees in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are usually white or pale in color, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a ghostly figure.

Due to its specific habitat requirements and limited distribution, Epixiphium is considered a conservation concern.
