Euonymus ficoides

Euonymus ficoides, commonly known as the Indian currant or red currant bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and often have a reddish or bronze hue, especially in the fall. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and have a tart flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

The botanical characteristics of Euonymus ficoides make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
