Euphorbia hexagonoides

Euphorbia hexagonoides, commonly known as the hexagonalea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are often ribbed and can store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Euphorbia hexagonoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
