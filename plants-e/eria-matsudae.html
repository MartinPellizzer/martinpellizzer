Eria matsudae

Eria matsudae, commonly known as the Matsudae's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eria matsudae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
