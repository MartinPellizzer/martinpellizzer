Evolvulus pseudoincanus

Evolvulus pseudoincanus, commonly known as the Andean morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Evolvulus pseudoincanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
