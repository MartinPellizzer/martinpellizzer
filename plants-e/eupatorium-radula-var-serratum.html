Eupatorium radula var. serratum

Eupatorium radula var. serratum, commonly known as the serrated blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, blue to purple flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
