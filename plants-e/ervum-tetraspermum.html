Ervum tetraspermum

Ervum tetraspermum, commonly known as the four-seeded vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing four seeds each.
