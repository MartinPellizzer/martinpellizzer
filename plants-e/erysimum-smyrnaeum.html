Erysimum smyrnaeum

Erysimum smyrnaeum, commonly known as the Smyrna wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. Its flowers are bright yellow, often with a darker central stripe, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
