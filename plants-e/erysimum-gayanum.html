Erysimum gayanum

Erysimum gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Erysimum gayanum is typically found growing in rocky or disturbed soils in open habitats.
