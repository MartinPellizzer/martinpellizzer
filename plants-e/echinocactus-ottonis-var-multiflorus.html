Echinocactus ottonis var. multiflorus

Echinocactus ottonis var. multiflorus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny ribs that protect it from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
