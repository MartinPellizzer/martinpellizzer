Eupatorium kingii

Eupatorium kingii, commonly known as King's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium kingii is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
