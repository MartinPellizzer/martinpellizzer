Eranthis sibirica var. uncinata

Eranthis sibirica var. uncinata, commonly known as the Siberian bentwillow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features showy, yellow flowers that bloom early in the spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. Its stems are erect and bear the characteristic yellow flowers in clusters.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in cool, moist environments.
