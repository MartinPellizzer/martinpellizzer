Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. rostratus

Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. rostratus, commonly known as the rostratus grass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina and Uruguay.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
