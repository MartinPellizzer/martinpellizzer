Eupatorium chaseae

Eupatorium chaseae, commonly known as Chase's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

Eupatorium chaseae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally by Native American tribes.
