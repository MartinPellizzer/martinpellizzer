Eria dischorensis

Eria dischorensis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination.

Eria dischorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
