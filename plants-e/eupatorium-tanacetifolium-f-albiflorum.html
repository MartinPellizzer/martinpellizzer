Eupatorium tanacetifolium f. albiflorum

Eupatorium tanacetifolium f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower false geranium, is a horticultural form of the common boneset.

It is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers, which contrast with its dark green, finely toothed leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is native to the eastern United States and is often found in moist, woodland habitats.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
