Ervum kioshanicum

Ervum kioshanicum, commonly known as the Kioshan pea, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of its genus. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Ervum kioshanicum is often cultivated for both its ornamental value and its edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.
