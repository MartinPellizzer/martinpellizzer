Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. armatus

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

armatus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a short, cylindrical cactus with a ribbed stem and typically grows to a height of about 10 inches.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, and it is known for its spiny, areolate stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

This subspecies is particularly adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.
