Euphorbia medicaginea

Euphorbia medicaginea, commonly known as the medicinal euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a stem that is often branched and covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically fleshy and may have a distinctive pattern or coloration.

Euphorbia medicaginea is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.
