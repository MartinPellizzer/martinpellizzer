Eremanthus stellatus var. gardnerianus

Eremanthus stellatus var. gardnerianus, commonly known as the Gardner's eremanthus, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare and requires careful cultivation.
