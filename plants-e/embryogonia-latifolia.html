Embryogonia latifolia

Embryogonia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf embryogonia, is a flowering plant species characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
