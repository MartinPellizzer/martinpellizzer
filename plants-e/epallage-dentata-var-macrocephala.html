Epallage dentata var. macrocephala

Epallage dentata var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed epallage, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive, elongated inflorescences.

The plant features finely toothed leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink, depending on the cultivar. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
