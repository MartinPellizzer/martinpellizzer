Echinocereus spinosissimus

Echinocereus spinosissimus, commonly known as the spiny hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
