Echinocactus sessiliflorus

Echinocactus sessiliflorus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, backward-pointing spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom at the top of the stem, typically in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
