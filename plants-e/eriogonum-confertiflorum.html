Eriogonum confertiflorum

Eriogonum confertiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Eriogonum confertiflorum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
