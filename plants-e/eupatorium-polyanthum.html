Eupatorium polyanthum

Eupatorium polyanthum, commonly known as the Chinese feverfew, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs and lance-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads.
