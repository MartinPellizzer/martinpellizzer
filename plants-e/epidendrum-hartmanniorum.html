Epidendrum hartmanniorum

Epidendrum hartmanniorum, commonly known as the Hartmann's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, often brightly colored flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are usually elongated and strap-like, adapted to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

Epidendrum hartmanniorum is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
