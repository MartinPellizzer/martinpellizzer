Elaeagnus pungens var. aurea

Elaeagnus pungens var. aurea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that emit a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.

This variety is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil improvement.
