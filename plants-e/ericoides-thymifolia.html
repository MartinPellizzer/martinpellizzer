Ericoides thymifolia

Ericoides thymifolia, commonly known as the sweetroot or sweet fern, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Ericoides thymifolia is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
