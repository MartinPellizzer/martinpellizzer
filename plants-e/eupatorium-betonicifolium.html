Eupatorium betonicifolium

Eupatorium betonicifolium, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
