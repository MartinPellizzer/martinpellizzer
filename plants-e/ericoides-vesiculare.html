Ericoides vesiculare

Ericoides vesiculare, commonly known as the bladderleaf ericoid, is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its compact growth habit and waxy, leathery leaves.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is typically found in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Ericoides vesiculare is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
