Erythranthe unimaculata

Erythranthe unimaculata, commonly known as the one-spotted erythranthe, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its basal rosette of elliptic to ovate leaves. The flowers are typically pink to purple, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a trait common in the mustard family. It produces small, round, green pods that contain several seeds.

This plant is native to the western United States and thrives in dry, sunny habitats.
