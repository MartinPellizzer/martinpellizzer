Eugenia comitanensis

Eugenia comitanensis, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or wild cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are elliptical and have a glossy appearance, while its wood is valued for its durability and aromatic properties.

Eugenia comitanensis plays an important role in both ecological and cultural contexts in its native habitat.
