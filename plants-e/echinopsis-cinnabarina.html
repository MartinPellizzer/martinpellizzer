Echinopsis cinnabarina

Echinopsis cinnabarina, commonly known as the red echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in clusters of bright red spines. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It is a drought-tolerant species, adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
