Eria paleata

Eria paleata, commonly known as the pale spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when injured, which is a common trait among members of its family. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eria paleata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
