Eriophorum angustifolium var. congestum

Eriophorum angustifolium var. congestum, commonly known as narrow-leaved cottongrass, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the end of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
