Eucalyptus leptophylla var. densa

Eucalyptus leptophylla var. densa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant typically grows in a shrub or small tree form, with smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips. It produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This variety is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.
