Echinocactus ottonis f. paraguayensis

Echinocactus ottonis f. paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguayensis Echinocactus, is a succulent cactus native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a slight bluish tinge. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
