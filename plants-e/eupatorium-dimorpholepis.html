Eupatorium dimorpholepis

Eupatorium dimorpholepis, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are typically hairy and branched, with leaves that are lance-shaped and have serrated edges.

Eupatorium dimorpholepis is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, thriving in partial to full shade.
