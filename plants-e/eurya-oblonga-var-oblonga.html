Eurya oblonga var. oblonga

Eurya oblonga var. oblonga, commonly known as the broadleaf eurya, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
