Eremurus persicus subsp. sikesarus

Eremurus persicus subsp.

sikesarus, commonly known as the Persian Spear, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous narrow, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
