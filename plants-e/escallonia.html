Escallonia

Escallonia, commonly known as the evergreen hibiscus, is a genus of flowering plants in the mallow family, Malvaceae.

It consists of approximately 40 species, primarily native to southern Africa, South America, and Australia. These plants are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and white. Escallonia species are typically shrubs or small trees, often with glossy, dark green leaves.

They are popular in horticulture for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
