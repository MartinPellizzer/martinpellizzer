Eugenia beccarii

Eugenia beccarii, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia beccarii thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and edible fruits make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
