Enneapogon filifolius

Enneapogon filifolius, commonly known as the thread-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, thread-like leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Enneapogon filifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
