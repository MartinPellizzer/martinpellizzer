Elaphoglossum ernestii

Elaphoglossum ernestii, commonly known as the Ernest's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori covered by indusia. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
