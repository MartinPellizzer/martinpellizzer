Eccremocarpus scaber var. carmineus

Eccremocarpus scaber var. carmineus, commonly known as the crimson burr vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, burr-like fruits that encase the seeds. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine, rough texture.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils.
