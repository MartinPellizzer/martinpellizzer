Eugenia linearifolia var. oppositifolia

Eugenia linearifolia var. oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, bright red berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Myrtaceae family.
