Eriospermum spirale

Eriospermum spirale, commonly known as the spiral eriosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine, white to grayish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spiral pattern, which is a distinctive feature. Its flowering period usually occurs during the summer months, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Eriospermum spirale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
