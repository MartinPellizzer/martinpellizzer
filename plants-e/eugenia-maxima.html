Eugenia maxima

Eugenia maxima, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or great myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
