Echinochloa crus-galli var. mitis

Echinochloa crus-galli var. mitis, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green, bristly inflorescence that resembles a fox's tail. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as fields, roadsides, and waste areas, and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and have a prominent midrib, while the stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

This variant is closely related to other Echinochloa species and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
