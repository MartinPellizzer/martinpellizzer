Elaeocarpus pentadactylus

Elaeocarpus pentadactylus, commonly known as the five-finger elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves with five prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
