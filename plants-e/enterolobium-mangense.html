Enterolobium mangense

Enterolobium mangense, commonly known as the Brazilian wisteria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a woody legume that produces long, pendulous clusters of violet to purple flowers. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and pods that contain edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its nitrogen-fixing ability, it also plays a role in soil enrichment.
