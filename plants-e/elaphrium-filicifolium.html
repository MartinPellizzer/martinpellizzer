Elaphrium filicifolium

Elaphrium filicifolium, commonly known as the fern-like elaphrium, is a member of the Elaphriaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and its leaves are compound, with a feathery appearance. Elaphrium filicifolium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
