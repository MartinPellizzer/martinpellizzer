Euphorbia waldsteinii

Euphorbia waldsteinii, commonly known as the Waldstein's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, upright stem covered in small, green, spiny leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flower clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
