Echinocactus winkleri

Echinocactus winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are densely covered with bright green, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. Its adaptations to drought include a thick, waxy cuticle and the ability to store water in its stems.

Echinocactus winkleri is often cultivated as a decorative cactus due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
