Euphorbia punctulata

Euphorbia punctulata, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, upright stems covered in small, white spots. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Euphorbia punctulata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
