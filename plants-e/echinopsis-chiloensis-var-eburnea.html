Echinopsis chiloensis var. eburnea

Echinopsis chiloensis var. eburnea, commonly known as the White Echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and white, spiky flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a dense covering of white hairs, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to many succulent collections.
