Euphorbia ephedroides subsp. imminuta

Euphorbia ephedroides subsp.

imminuta, commonly known as the dwarf euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of Central Asia. It features small, fleshy leaves and produces unique, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water requirements. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its botanical characteristics include a thick, woody stem and a tendency to form dense colonies.
