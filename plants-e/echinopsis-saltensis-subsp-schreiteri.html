Echinopsis saltensis subsp. schreiteri

Echinopsis saltensis subsp.

schreiteri, commonly known as the Schreiter's moon cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile. It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of white to pale blue spines.

The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing species that requires minimal water and thrives in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
