Evolvulus elegans var. elegans

Evolvulus elegans var. elegans, commonly known as the "Mexican morning glory," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a vine that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, sunny climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb or trail along supports.

The flowers are typically open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
