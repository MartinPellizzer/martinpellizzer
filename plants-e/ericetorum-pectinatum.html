Ericetorum pectinatum

Ericetorum pectinatum, commonly known as the sticky heath, is a low-growing shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, sticky resin. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky habitats.

Ericetorum pectinatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
