Eriosyce crispa var. totoralensis

Eriosyce crispa var. totoralensis, commonly known as the Totora cholla, is a cactus native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a shrub-like structure. The plant has small, pale green cladodes that are covered with a whitish, powdery coating. It produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, round fruits.

This species is adapted to withstand extreme drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
