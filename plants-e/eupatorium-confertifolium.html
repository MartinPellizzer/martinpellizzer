Eupatorium confertifolium

Eupatorium confertifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and woodland edges. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Eupatorium confertifolium is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
