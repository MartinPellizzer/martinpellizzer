Eucalyptus acroleuca

Eucalyptus acroleuca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

Eucalyptus acroleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
