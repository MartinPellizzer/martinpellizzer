Eremophila elachantha

Eremophila elachantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil and low rainfall environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for native pollinators.

Eremophila elachantha is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
