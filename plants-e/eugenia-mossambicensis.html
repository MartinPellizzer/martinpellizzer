Eugenia mossambicensis

Eugenia mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique guava, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
