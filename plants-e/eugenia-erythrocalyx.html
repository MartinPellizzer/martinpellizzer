Eugenia erythrocalyx

Eugenia erythrocalyx, commonly known as the red calyx eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and showy floral display.

Eugenia erythrocalyx is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to warm, humid climates.
