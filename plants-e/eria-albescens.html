Eria albescens

Eria albescens, commonly known as the white orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its elegant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eria albescens is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a potted plant.
