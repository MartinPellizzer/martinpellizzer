Elymus glaucus var. hirsutus

Elymus glaucus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often hairy, giving it its varietal name.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
