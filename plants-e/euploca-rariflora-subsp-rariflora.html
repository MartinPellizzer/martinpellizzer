Euploca rariflora subsp. rariflora

Euploca rariflora subsp.

rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered euploca, is a flowering plant native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink.

The leaves are elliptical and glossy, with a smooth texture that helps it thrive in shaded environments. This subspecies is notable for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss.
