Euonymus japonicus var. pyramidatus

Euonymus japonicus var. pyramidatus, commonly known as the Japanese spindletree, is a compact, upright shrub native to Japan.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are a striking feature of its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its dense growth habit makes it ideal for use as a hedge or foundation plant.
