Eulophia petersii

Eulophia petersii, commonly known as the Peters' eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features long, narrow leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchids. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia petersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
