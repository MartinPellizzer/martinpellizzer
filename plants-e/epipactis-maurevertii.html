Epipactis maurevertii

Epipactis maurevertii, commonly known as the Maurevert's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and forest undergrowth. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces erect, leafy stems with a single, showy, greenish-yellow to brownish flower. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns, aiding in pollination by specific insect species.

Epipactis maurevertii is known for its low-growing habit and is often found in association with other orchid species in its native habitats.
