Eugenia piloesis

Eugenia piloesis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Pitanga*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aroma, while its flowers are small and white or pink.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine due to its nutritional and medicinal properties.
