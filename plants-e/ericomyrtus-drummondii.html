Ericomyrtus drummondii

Ericomyrtus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's heath, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, dense foliage and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Ericomyrtus drummondii is often found in coastal and inland heathlands, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
