Engelhardia colebrookeana

Engelhardia colebrookeana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. The plant has opposite, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish underside. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain flat, winged seeds.

Engelhardia colebrookeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
