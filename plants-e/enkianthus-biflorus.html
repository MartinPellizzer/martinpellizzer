Enkianthus biflorus

Enkianthus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Enkianthus biflorus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
