Eupatorium dispar

Eupatorium dispar, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.

Eupatorium dispar is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported fever-reducing properties.
