Eleocharis recurvata

Eleocharis recurvata, commonly known as the curled spike rush, is a species of sedges native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that often curve downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically found in the upper part of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist soils, often in marshes or along the edges of ponds.

Eleocharis recurvata plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
