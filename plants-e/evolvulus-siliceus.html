Evolvulus siliceus

Evolvulus siliceus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a quick-growing vine.
