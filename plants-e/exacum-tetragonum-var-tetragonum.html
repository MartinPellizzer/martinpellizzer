Exacum tetragonum var. tetragonum

Exacum tetragonum var. tetragonum, commonly known as the four-angled African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mound-like growth habit and features showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are oval-shaped with a distinctive four-angled stem, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of care.
