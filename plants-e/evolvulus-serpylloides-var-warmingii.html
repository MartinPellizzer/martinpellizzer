Evolvulus serpylloides var. warmingii

Evolvulus serpylloides var. warmingii, commonly known as the purple morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Central America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and produces striking violet to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
