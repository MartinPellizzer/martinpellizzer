Ericoides pallidopurpureum

Ericoides pallidopurpureum, commonly known as the pale purple heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale purple flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Ericoides pallidopurpureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, hardy ground cover in suitable conditions.
