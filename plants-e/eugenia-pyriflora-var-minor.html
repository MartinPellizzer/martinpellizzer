Eugenia pyriflora var. minor

Eugenia pyriflora var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters, featuring glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
