Echinocactus leopoldii

Echinocactus leopoldii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is covered in prominent, radial spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscapic gardens.
