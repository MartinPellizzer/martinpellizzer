Eragrostis hobdyi

Eragrostis hobdyi, commonly known as Hobdy's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or red. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in grasslands, disturbed sites, and along roadsides.

Eragrostis hobdyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
