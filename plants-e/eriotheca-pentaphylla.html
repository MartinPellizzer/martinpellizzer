Eriotheca pentaphylla

Eriotheca pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed eriotheca, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular and hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
