Eriocaucanthus cinereus

Eriocaucanthus cinereus, commonly known as the gray-leaved hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects.
