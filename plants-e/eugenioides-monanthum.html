Eugenioides monanthum

Eugenioides monanthum, commonly known as the single-flowered eugenia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenioides monanthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
