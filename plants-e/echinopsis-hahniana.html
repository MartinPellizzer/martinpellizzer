Echinopsis hahniana

Echinopsis hahniana, commonly known as the "Moon Cactus," is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a globular shape with areoles that bear spines and flowers. It is drought-tolerant and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and rock gardens.
