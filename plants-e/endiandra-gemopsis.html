Endiandra gemopsis

Endiandra gemopsis, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a member of the Dipterocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elongated, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces woody, aromatic fruits.

Endiandra gemopsis is valued for its timber, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of essential oils.
