Euphorbia geminata

Euphorbia geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and opposite leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in inflorescences called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Euphorbia geminata is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
