Eustylis purpurea

Eustylis purpurea, commonly known as the purple-stemmed everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple stems and clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
