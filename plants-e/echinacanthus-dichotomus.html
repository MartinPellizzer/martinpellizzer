Echinacanthus dichotomus

Echinacanthus dichotomus, commonly known as the forked spurge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Echinacanthus dichotomus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
