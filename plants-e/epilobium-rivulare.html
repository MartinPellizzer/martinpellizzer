Epilobium rivulare

Epilobium rivulare, commonly known as water thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or damp environments such as stream banks and marshes. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink or purple. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
