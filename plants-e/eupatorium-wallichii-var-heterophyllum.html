Eupatorium wallichii var. heterophyllum

Eupatorium wallichii var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the Himalayan boneset, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically opposite and have a serrated edge, with some variation in shape among different populations. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
