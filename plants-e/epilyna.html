Epilyna

Epilyna, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of a single species, Epilyna aculeata, which is native to parts of southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow and clustered in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family.

Epilyna is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique floral structure.
