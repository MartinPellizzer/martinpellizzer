Epidendrum alejandroi

Epidendrum alejandroi, commonly known as Alejandro's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It features erect, hairy pseudobulbs that bear lanceolate leaves with a dark green coloration. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
