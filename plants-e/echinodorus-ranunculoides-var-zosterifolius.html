Echinodorus ranunculoides var. zosterifolius

Echinodorus ranunculoides var. zosterifolius, commonly known as the velvet leaf waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which can help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic life.

It is popular in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.
