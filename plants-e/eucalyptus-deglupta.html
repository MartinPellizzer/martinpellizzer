Eucalyptus deglupta

Eucalyptus deglupta, commonly known as the rainbow eucalyptus, is a tropical tree native to the forests of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines.

It is distinguished by its striking, multicolored bark that peels away in strips, revealing layers of green, orange, red, and purple. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall and has lance-shaped leaves that are typically light green. It produces small, globular flower clusters and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.

The plant is also valued for its timber and has some medicinal uses.
