Erigeron subcanescens

Erigeron subcanescens, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its blue-gray foliage and daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are composed of white ray florets surrounding a yellow disk, making it a popular choice for native gardens.
