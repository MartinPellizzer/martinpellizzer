Echinodorus parviflorus

Echinodorus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered arrowhead, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It features broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often used in aquascaping due to its ornamental value.

Echinodorus parviflorus is also known for its ability to improve water quality by absorbing nutrients and providing habitat for aquatic life.
