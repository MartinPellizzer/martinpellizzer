Eupatorium neurolepis

Eupatorium neurolepis, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Eupatorium neurolepis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
