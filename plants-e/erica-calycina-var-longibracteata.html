Erica calycina var. longibracteata

Erica calycina var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its distinctive feature is the elongated bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it a unique appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
