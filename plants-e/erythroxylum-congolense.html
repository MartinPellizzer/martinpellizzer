Erythroxylum congolense

Erythroxylum congolense, commonly known as the Congo coca, is a shrub native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its potential medicinal properties, it has attracted interest for research and conservation efforts.
