Erica scaettae

Erica scaettae, commonly known as the Sicilian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Erica scaettae typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
