Ericoides turbiniflorum

Ericoides turbiniflorum, commonly known as the Turbiniflorous Heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or moorland environments.

Ericoides turbiniflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
