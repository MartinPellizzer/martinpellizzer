Echeveria lilacina

Echeveria lilacina, commonly known as the lilac echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically lavender to pink in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
