Eucalyptus pulverulenta var. ovatifolia

Eucalyptus pulverulenta var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in spherical or cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as mallee woodlands.

Its foliage and floral structure make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and horticultural use.
