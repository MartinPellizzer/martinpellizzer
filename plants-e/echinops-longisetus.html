Echinops longisetus

Echinops longisetus, commonly known as the long-hair thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in long, bristly hairs. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a rough, hairy texture.

Echinops longisetus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
