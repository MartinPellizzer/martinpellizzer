Eugenia petenensis

Eugenia petenensis, commonly known as the Maya plum or wild plum, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, purple-black fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
