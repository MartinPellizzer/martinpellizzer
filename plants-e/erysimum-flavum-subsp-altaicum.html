Erysimum flavum subsp. altaicum

Erysimum flavum subsp.

altaicum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are usually basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience in harsh conditions.
