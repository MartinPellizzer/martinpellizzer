Erica trichophylla

Erica trichophylla, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring.

Erica trichophylla thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
