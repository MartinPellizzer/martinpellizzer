Elaeocarpus subisensis

Elaeocarpus subisensis, commonly known as the Subis ebony, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and has a broad, spreading canopy with lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
