Epilobium rosmarinifolium

Epilobium rosmarinifolium, commonly known as rosemary bush or woolly willow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, fuzzy leaves and fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine due to their high content of volatile oils.

Epilobium rosmarinifolium is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
