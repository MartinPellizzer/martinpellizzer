Erica globiceps subsp. consors

Erica globiceps subsp.

consors, commonly known as the Cape heath or yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. It is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
