Eugenia terpnophylla var. keralensis

Eugenia terpnophylla var. keralensis, commonly known as Keralan myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that is edible and often used in local traditional medicine.
