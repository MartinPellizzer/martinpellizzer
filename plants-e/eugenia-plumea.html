Eugenia plumea

Eugenia plumea, commonly known as the silky pear, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits resembling small pears. Eugenia plumea thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
