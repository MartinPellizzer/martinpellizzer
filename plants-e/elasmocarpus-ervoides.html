Elasmocarpus ervoides

Elasmocarpus ervoides, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland areas.

Elasmocarpus ervoides is an important species in Australian flora and is also the floral emblem of South Australia.
