Eugenia cerina var. montana

Eugenia cerina var. montana, commonly known as the mountain cherry or mountain guava, is a variant of the common guava.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Andes region of South America. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which is typically smaller than the common guava, has a sweet and tangy flavor.

The leaves are glossy and dark green, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.
