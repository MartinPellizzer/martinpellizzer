Eriocoma pinetorum

Eriocoma pinetorum, commonly known as the pine haircup, is a small annual plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is typically found in dry, disturbed soils in open habitats such as roadsides and meadows.

Eriocoma pinetorum is well adapted to arid environments and is often seen in the western United States.
