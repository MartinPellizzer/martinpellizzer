Erica sicula subsp. libanotica

Erica sicula subsp.

libanotica, commonly known as the Lebanese heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.

The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
