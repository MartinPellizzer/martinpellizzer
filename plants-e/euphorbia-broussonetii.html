Euphorbia broussonetii

Euphorbia broussonetii, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by a unique, colorful bract. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine.

Euphorbia broussonetii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
