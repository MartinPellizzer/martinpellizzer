Eriophyllum pedunculatum

Eriophyllum pedunculatum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange or red flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and disturbed soils. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

The California poppy is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
