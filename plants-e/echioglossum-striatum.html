Echioglossum striatum

Echioglossum striatum, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a distinctive zigzagging growth pattern in its stems. Its leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stem, and it produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

Echioglossum striatum is often found in wetland habitats and is considered a rare and endangered species in many areas.
