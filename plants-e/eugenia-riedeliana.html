Eugenia riedeliana

Eugenia riedeliana, commonly known as the Riedel's evergreen eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to reddish when mature.

Eugenia riedeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
