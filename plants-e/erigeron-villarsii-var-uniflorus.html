Erigeron villarsii var. uniflorus

Erigeron villarsii var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, narrow leaves. Its flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in desert scrub and grassland environments.
