Eucalyptus mcquoidii

Eucalyptus mcquoidii, commonly known as the McQuoid's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, aromatic leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin strips, a common feature among eucalyptus species.

Eucalyptus mcquoidii is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
