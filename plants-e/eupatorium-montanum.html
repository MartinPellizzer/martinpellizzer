Eupatorium montanum

Eupatorium montanum, commonly known as mountain boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium montanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
