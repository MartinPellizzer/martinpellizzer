Euphrasia papyracea

Euphrasia papyracea, commonly known as the paperleaf eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

The plant is part of the Scrophulariaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
