Eugenia nana

Eugenia nana, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 1 meter, featuring compact, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Eugenia nana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
