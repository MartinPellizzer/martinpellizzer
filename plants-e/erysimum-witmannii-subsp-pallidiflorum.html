Erysimum witmannii subsp. pallidiflorum

Erysimum witmannii subsp.

pallidiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and serrated leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in early spring. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

This plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, where it adapts well to drought conditions.
