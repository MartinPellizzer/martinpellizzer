Eriosorus glaberrimus

Eriosorus glaberrimus, commonly known as the glabrous shield fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and small, leathery fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of trichomes. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as under forest canopies or in rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are usually located on the underside of the fronds, and the spores are released through the indusium.

The botanical features of Eriosorus glaberrimus make it an interesting subject for studies in fern morphology and ecology.
