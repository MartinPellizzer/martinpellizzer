Eragrostis tenella var. purpurea

Eragrostis tenella var. purpurea, commonly known as purple lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and distinctive purple tinge on its inflorescence. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its small, wind-dispersed seeds contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed areas.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
