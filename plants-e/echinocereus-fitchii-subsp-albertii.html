Echinocereus fitchii subsp. albertii

Echinocereus fitchii subsp.

albertii, commonly known as the Albert's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often featuring a bluish-green coloration.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
