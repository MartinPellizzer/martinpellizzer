Euploca racemosa

Euploca racemosa, commonly known as the clustered everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Euploca racemosa is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
