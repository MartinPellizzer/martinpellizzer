Eryngium arvense

Eryngium arvense, commonly known as field thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green leaves and purple or pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often considered a weed. Its flowers are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a spherical shape, and it produces achenes as its seeds.

Eryngium arvense is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
