Euphorbia brunellii

Euphorbia brunellii, commonly known as the red sand boa, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are typically small and rounded, often with a slightly waxy texture.

Euphorbia brunellii is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
