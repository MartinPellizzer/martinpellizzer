Elymus nakaii

Elymus nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Elymus nakaii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
