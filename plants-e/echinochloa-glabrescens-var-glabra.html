Echinochloa glabrescens var. glabra

Echinochloa glabrescens var. glabra, commonly known as smooth false brome, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth to the touch. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
