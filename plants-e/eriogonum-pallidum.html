Eriogonum pallidum

Eriogonum pallidum, commonly known as the pale globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has hairy, glandular leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eriogonum pallidum is often found in open woodlands and is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae.
