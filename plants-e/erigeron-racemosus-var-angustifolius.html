Erigeron racemosus var. angustifolius

Erigeron racemosus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrowleaf blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces a tall, branched stem with numerous small, blue to purple flower heads. The flowers are typically arranged in a raceme, consisting of composite flower heads made up of numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is relatively drought-tolerant.
