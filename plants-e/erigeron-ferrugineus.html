Erigeron ferrugineus

Erigeron ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty fleabane, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
