Epidendrum widgrenii

Epidendrum widgrenii, commonly known as the Widgren's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, featuring pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Epidendrum widgrenii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm, humid conditions.
