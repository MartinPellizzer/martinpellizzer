Eria rubropunctata

Eria rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and are typically pink or red with distinctive dark spots.

Eria rubropunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
