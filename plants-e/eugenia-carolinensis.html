Eugenia carolinensis

Eugenia carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina allspice, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round, dark purple fruits that are edible and have a spicy, aromatic flavor. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
