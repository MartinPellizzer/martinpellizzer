Echinocactus platensis var. guerkeanus

Echinocactus platensis var. guerkeanus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, contains edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
