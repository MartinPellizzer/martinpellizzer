Eupatorium guadalupense var. gardneri

Eupatorium guadalupense var. gardneri, commonly known as Gardner's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

This variant is often distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological characteristics.
