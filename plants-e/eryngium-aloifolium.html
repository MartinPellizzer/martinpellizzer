Eryngium aloifolium

Eryngium aloifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Eryngium aloifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
