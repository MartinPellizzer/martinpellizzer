Euphorbia eendornensis

Euphorbia eendornensis, commonly known as the Eendorn euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
