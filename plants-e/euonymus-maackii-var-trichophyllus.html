Euonymus maackii var. trichophyllus

Euonymus maackii var. trichophyllus, commonly known as the glossy euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often used in landscaping, though it can be invasive in some regions.
