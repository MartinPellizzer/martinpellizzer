Eremogone caricifolia

Eremogone caricifolia, commonly known as the desert fig, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which are often hidden among the foliage. Its fruit, resembling a small fig, is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

Eremogone caricifolia is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in poor soils.
