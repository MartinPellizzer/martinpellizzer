Eupatorium gracile

Eupatorium gracile, commonly known as slender boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in wetland ecosystems and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.

The species is also used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
