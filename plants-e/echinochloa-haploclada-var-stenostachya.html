Echinochloa haploclada var. stenostachya

Echinochloa haploclada var. stenostachya, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas and disturbed sites.
