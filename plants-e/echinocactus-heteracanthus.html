Echinocactus heteracanthus

Echinocactus heteracanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. Its spines are modified leaves that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

Echinocactus heteracanthus is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and well-draining soil to flourish.
