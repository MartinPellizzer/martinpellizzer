Echinopsis krahn-juckeri

Echinopsis krahn-juckeri, commonly known as the Krahn-Jucker's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a dense covering of spines that provide protection against herbivores and extreme weather conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
