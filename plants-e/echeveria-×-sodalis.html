Echeveria × sodalis

Echeveria × sodalis, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves. The leaves are typically blue-green or grayish in color, often with a powdery coating called farina. It produces clusters of vibrant pink or red flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and low maintenance requirements.
