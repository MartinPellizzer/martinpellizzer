Eucalyptus urnigera

Eucalyptus urnigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it often forms dense thickets in dry, sandy soils.

Eucalyptus urnigera is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
