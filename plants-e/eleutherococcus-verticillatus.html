Eleutherococcus verticillatus

Eleutherococcus verticillatus, commonly known as the clustered ginseng or Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, dark green leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in early summer, followed by red berries that are rich in antioxidants. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a hardy plant suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its adaptability and medicinal properties, it is often cultivated for its potential health benefits.
