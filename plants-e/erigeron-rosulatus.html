Erigeron rosulatus

Erigeron rosulatus, commonly known as the rosette fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, blue-gray leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is an annual or biennial species, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Erigeron rosulatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
