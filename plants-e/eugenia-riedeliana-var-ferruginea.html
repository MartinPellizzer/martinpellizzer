Eugenia riedeliana var. ferruginea

Eugenia riedeliana var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and typically red or orange when ripe, attracting various wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
