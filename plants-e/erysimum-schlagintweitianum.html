Erysimum schlagintweitianum

Erysimum schlagintweitianum, commonly known as the Schlagintweit's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal areas. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Erysimum schlagintweitianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
