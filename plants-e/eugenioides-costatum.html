Eugenioides costatum

Eugenioides costatum, commonly known as the serrated eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are dark purple when ripe. Eugenioides costatum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
