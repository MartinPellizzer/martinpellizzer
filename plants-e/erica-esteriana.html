Erica esteriana

Erica esteriana, commonly known as the Esterian heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family, Ericaceae.

It is native to the island of Madeira, where it grows in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica esteriana is a low-growing shrub, often forming dense mats that contribute to the unique landscape of its native habitat.

It is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
