Erioxantha

Erioxantha, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that are often bright red or orange, resembling a poker or torch. The plants are native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, with some species found in North Africa and western Asia. Erioxantha species are typically perennial, producing showy flowers from late spring to early autumn.

They are often cultivated in gardens for their striking appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
