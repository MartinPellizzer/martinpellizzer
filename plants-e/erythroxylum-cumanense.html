Erythroxylum cumanense

Erythroxylum cumanense, commonly known as the Cumanense Erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family, which includes other plants known for their psychoactive properties. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in humid, tropical environments.
