Eulophia jumelleana

Eulophia jumelleana, commonly known as the Jumellean orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a complex root system and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eulophia jumelleana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitats.
