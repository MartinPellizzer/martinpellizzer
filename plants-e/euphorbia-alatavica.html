Euphorbia alatavica

Euphorbia alatavica, commonly known as the Armenian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
