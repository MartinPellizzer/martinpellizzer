Eria fragrans

Eria fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

The orchid is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its pleasant aroma.
