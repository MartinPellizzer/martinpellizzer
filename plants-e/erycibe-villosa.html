Erycibe villosa

Erycibe villosa, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Erycibe villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
