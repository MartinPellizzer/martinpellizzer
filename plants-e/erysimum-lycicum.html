Erysimum lycicum

Erysimum lycicum, commonly known as the Armenian wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, and its stems are often hairy as well. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Erysimum lycicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
