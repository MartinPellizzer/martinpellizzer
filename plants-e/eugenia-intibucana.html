Eugenia intibucana

Eugenia intibucana, commonly known as the Honduran guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible fruits. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The fruit, which is similar to guava, is valued for its flavor and nutritional content.
