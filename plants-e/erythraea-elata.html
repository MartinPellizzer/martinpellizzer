Erythraea elata

Erythraea elata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dense, aromatic foliage. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich reddish color, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts and furniture.

However, due to overharvesting and habitat loss, it is now considered an endangered species in many parts of its range.
