Erica primulina

Erica primulina, commonly known as the spring heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica primulina blooms in spring, producing clusters of white or pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
