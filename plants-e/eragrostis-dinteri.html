Eragrostis dinteri

Eragrostis dinteri, commonly known as Dinter's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers.

Eragrostis dinteri is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to prevent soil erosion.
