Eugenia pyriformis f. ponhi

Eugenia pyriformis f. ponhi, commonly known as the Ponche guayabilla, is a horticultural form of a species native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
