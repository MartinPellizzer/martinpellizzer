Echinocereus knippelianus subsp. kaplani

Echinocereus knippelianus subsp.

kaplani, commonly known as the Kaplani's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a short, cylindrical cactus with a ribbed stem and spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it an interesting subject for botanical study and cultivation.
