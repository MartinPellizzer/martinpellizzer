Echites barbatus

Echites barbatus, commonly known as the hairy devil's ivy, is a perennial climbing plant native to South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive hairy texture on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in racemes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Echites barbatus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
