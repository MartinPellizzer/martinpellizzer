Eurybia alpina

Eurybia alpina, commonly known as alpine eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, with a rough texture and a distinctive blue-green color.

Eurybia alpina blooms in late summer, producing small white or pale purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
