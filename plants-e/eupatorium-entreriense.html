Eupatorium entreriense

Eupatorium entreriense, commonly known as the Caribbean blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Eupatorium entreriense produces small, purple to blue flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
