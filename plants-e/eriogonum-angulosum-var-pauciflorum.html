Eriogonum angulosum var. pauciflorum

Eriogonum angulosum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It features narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid environments with poor soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
