Eucalyptus oleosa subsp. ampliata

Eucalyptus oleosa subsp.

ampliata, commonly known as the broad-leaved malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, often hairy leaves that are typically broad and flat.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its bark is often flaky or fibrous, and it is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils.

This subspecies is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
