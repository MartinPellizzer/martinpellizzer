Eriogonum pilicaule

Eriogonum pilicaule, commonly known as the hairyleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a perennial species that produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriogonum pilicaule plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
