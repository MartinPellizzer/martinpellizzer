Evolvulus maximiliani

Evolvulus maximiliani, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant color and ability to thrive in warm climates. It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible seeds.

The species belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
