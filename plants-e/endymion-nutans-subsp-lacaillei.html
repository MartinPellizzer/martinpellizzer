Endymion nutans subsp. lacaillei

Endymion nutans subsp.

lacaillei, commonly known as the nodding endymion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
