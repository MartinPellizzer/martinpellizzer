Eupatorium havanense var. typicum

Eupatorium havanense var. typicum, commonly known as the Cuban blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
