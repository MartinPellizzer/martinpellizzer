Equisetum ramosissimum var. muelleri

Equisetum ramosissimum var. muelleri, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial fern-like plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems and whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves.

It belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is native to regions of Australia, particularly in the southern and western parts of the country. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It reproduces through spores and is known for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Paleozoic era.

This variety is notable for its distinctive appearance and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
