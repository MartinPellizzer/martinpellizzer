Eugenia sarasinii

Eugenia sarasinii, commonly known as the Sarasin's guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, aromatic fruits that are similar in appearance to guavas. Eugenia sarasinii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential culinary uses.
