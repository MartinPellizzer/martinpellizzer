Erechthites erechthitoides

Erechthites erechthitoides, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent yellow central disk. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
