Euphorbia spruceana

Euphorbia spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce Spurge, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia spruceana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
