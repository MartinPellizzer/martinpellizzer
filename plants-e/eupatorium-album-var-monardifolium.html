Eupatorium album var. monardifolium

Eupatorium album var. monardifolium, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, typically found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important plant in ecological landscapes.
