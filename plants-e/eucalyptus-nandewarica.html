Eucalyptus nandewarica

Eucalyptus nandewarica, commonly known as the Nandewar mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Nandewar region of New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, typically in summer, which attract a variety of pollinators. Its wood is dense and hard, making it valuable for local crafts and fuel.

The species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is part of the diverse eucalyptus flora of the region.
