Euphorbia obtusifolia var. wildpretii

Euphorbia obtusifolia var. wildpretii, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, well-drained soils.

This variety is also known for its resilience to drought and minimal care requirements.
