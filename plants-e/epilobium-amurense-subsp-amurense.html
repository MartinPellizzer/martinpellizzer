Epilobium amurense subsp. amurense

Epilobium amurense subsp.

amurense, commonly known as the Amur rosebay willowherb, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows.

The plant features upright stems covered in glandular hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural use.
