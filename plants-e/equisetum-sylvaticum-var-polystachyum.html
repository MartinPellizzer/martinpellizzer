Equisetum sylvaticum var. polystachyum

Equisetum sylvaticum var. polystachyum, commonly known as the giant horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, jointed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features whorled leaves that are reduced to small, scale-like structures, with fertile stems bearing spore-producing cones. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.
