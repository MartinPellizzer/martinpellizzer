Erodium malacoides subsp. crassifolium

Erodium malacoides subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and oval, succulent leaves.

The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
