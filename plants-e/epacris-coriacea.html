Epacris coriacea

Epacris coriacea, commonly known as the rough leaved epacris, is an evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in coastal and heathland environments. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, are pollinated by birds and insects.

Epacris coriacea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
