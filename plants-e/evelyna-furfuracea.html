Evelyna furfuracea

Evelyna furfuracea, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant characterized by its soft, fuzzy appearance due to the dense covering of fine hairs on its leaves.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions, where it has adapted to thrive in harsh, dry conditions. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, and they may change color with seasonal variations. Evelyna furfuracea is often found in rocky soils and is known for its ability to store water in its leaves.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
