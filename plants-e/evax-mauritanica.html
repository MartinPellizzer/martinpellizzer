Evax mauritanica

Evax mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritania thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with its deep root system and water-storing tissues.

Evax mauritanica is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
