Echinops ellenbeckii

Echinops ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops ellenbeckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
