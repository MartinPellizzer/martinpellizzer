Erica andevalensis

Erica andevalensis, commonly known as the Andalucian heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. Erica andevalensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
