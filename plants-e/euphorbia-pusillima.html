Euphorbia pusillima

Euphorbia pusillima, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a low-growing succulent native to the western United States.

It features small, fleshy leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be irritant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for arid landscape designs.
