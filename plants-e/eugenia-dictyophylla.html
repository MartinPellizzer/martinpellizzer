Eugenia dictyophylla

Eugenia dictyophylla, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The fruit, which is a small, round berry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Eugenia dictyophylla is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
