Euphorbia leucochlamys

Euphorbia leucochlamys, commonly known as the white-veined spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous branching stems that bear small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its leaves and stems.

Euphorbia leucochlamys is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
