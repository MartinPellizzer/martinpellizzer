Echinochloa ramosa

Echinochloa ramosa, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, narrow leaves, and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is often found in agricultural fields and disturbed areas. It is an annual plant that produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its rapid growth and competitive nature make it a common weed in many cultivated environments.
