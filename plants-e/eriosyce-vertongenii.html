Eriosyce vertongenii

Eriosyce vertongenii, commonly known as the Vertongen's cholla, is a species of cactus native to Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of cylindrical stems that form a compact, branching structure. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by pale green fruits. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Eriosyce vertongenii is well-suited to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy plant in desert landscapes.
