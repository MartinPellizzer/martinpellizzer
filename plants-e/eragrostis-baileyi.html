Eragrostis baileyi

Eragrostis baileyi, commonly known as Bailey's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, grass-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects in dry regions.
