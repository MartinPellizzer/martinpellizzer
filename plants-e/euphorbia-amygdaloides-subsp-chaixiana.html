Euphorbia amygdaloides subsp. chaixiana

Euphorbia amygdaloides subsp.

chaixiana, commonly known as the Chaix's spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are typically green or reddish in color, often covered with a waxy cuticle.

The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping.
