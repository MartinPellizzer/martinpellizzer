Eriogonum nudum

Eriogonum nudum, commonly known as the naked buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum nudum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
