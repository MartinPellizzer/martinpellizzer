Eugenioides coriaceum

Eugenioides coriaceum, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
