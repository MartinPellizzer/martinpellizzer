Elatine hydropiper var. submersa

Elatine hydropiper var. submersa, commonly known as water pepper, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, thriving in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has tiny, thread-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged or floating. It is characterized by its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels and is often found in nutrient-rich soils.

This species plays a role in stabilizing aquatic environments and providing habitat for microorganisms.
