Elaeocarpus pittosporoides

Elaeocarpus pittosporoides, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
