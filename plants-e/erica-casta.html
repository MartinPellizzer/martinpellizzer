Erica casta

Erica casta, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heath and heather plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica casta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
