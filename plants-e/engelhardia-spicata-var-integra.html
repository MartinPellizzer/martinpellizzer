Engelhardia spicata var. integra

Engelhardia spicata var. integra, commonly known as the silky hakea, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that appear in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in spires. It is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in coastal and sandy soils.

The variety integra is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different leaf morphology compared to other varieties of Engelhardia spicata.
