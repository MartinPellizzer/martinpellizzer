Erigeron multicaulis

Erigeron multicaulis, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Erigeron multicaulis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
