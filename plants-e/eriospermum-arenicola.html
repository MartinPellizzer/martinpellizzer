Eriospermum arenicola

Eriospermum arenicola, commonly known as the sand eriosperm, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and is adapted to sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. It is often found in coastal areas and is well-suited to dry, sunny conditions.

Eriospermum arenicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in harsh environments.
