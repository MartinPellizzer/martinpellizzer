Encyclia pentotis

Encyclia pentotis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to yellowish pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with dark red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Encyclia pentotis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
