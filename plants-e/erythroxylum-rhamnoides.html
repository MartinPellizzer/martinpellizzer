Erythroxylum rhamnoides

Erythroxylum rhamnoides, commonly known as the red rhamnus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, reddish seeds.

Erythroxylum rhamnoides is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, shaded environments.
