Euryloma curvifolia

Euryloma curvifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaved euryloma, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that curve inward, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
