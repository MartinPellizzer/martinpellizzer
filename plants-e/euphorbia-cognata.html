Euphorbia cognata

Euphorbia cognata, commonly known as the showy spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures called cyathia. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. It produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Euphorbia cognata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
