Embelia ruminata

Embelia ruminata, commonly known as the Indian embelia or wild pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red, berry-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and emit a peppery scent when crushed.

The fruit is used in traditional medicine for its purported digestive and antimicrobial properties.
