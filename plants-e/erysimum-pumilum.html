Erysimum pumilum

Erysimum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to thrive in rocky or gravelly soils. It is often found in sunny, well-drained habitats such as coastal cliffs and dry meadows.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and border plantings.
