Eriocaulon geoffreyi

Eriocaulon geoffreyi, commonly known as the Geoffrey's spike-rush, is a member of the Poales order and the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and narrow, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive sheathing base.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.
