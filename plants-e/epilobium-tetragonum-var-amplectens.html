Epilobium tetragonum var. amplectens

Epilobium tetragonum var. amplectens, commonly known as sticky willow or redstem dogwood, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its square stems, which are often covered in glandular hairs that produce a sticky substance. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins. The flowers are typically pink to red, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats including meadows and woodland edges.
