Echinopsis cinnabarina var. cheroniana

Echinopsis cinnabarina var. cheroniana, commonly known as the Red Echinopsis, is a striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and vibrant red or orange spines, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses as a decorative and resilient plant.
