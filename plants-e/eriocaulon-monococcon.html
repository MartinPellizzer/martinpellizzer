Eriocaulon monococcon

Eriocaulon monococcon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and dense inflorescence of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a reddish tint, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eriocaulon monococcon plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
