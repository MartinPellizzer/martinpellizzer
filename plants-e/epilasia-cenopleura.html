Epilasia cenopleura

Epilasia cenopleura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epilasia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun conditions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epilasia cenopleura is known for its hardy nature and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
