Eriocaulon recurvifolium

Eriocaulon recurvifolium, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a zigzag pattern, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

Eriocaulon recurvifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
