Euphorbia trichadenia var. gibbsiae

Euphorbia trichadenia var. gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with cylindrical, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by red bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and striking floral display.
