Eriosema scioanum subsp. scioanum

Eriosema scioanum subsp.

scioanum, commonly known as the Cape ironwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, leathery foliage.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and is often used in ornamental gardening.
