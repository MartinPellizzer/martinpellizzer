Erysimum pieninicum

Erysimum pieninicum, commonly known as the Pienin Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pieniny Mountains in Slovakia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Erysimum pieninicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local biodiversity.
