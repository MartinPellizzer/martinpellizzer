Eurybiopsis hookeri var. incana

Eurybiopsis hookeri var. incana, commonly known as the woolly eurybiopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique texture and color.
