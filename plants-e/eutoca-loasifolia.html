Eutoca loasifolia

Eutoca loasifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Eutoca loasifolia is also known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
