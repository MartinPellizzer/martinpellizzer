Eucalyptus × oviformis

Eucalyptus × oviformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. Its bark is often flaky or peeling, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus × oviformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
