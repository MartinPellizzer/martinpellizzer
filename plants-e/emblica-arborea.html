Emblica arborea

Emblica arborea, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and yellowish-orange fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains various bioactive compounds, making it valuable in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

The plant also plays an important role in ecological systems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
