Eugenia phillyreoides

Eugenia phillyreoides, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in coastal and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its large, broad, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. After flowering, it produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
