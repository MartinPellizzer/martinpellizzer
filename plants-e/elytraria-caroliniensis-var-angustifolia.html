Elytraria caroliniensis var. angustifolia

Elytraria caroliniensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf elytraria, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly referred to as grasses, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth surface, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is distinguished by its narrower leaf blades compared to other varieties of Elytraria caroliniensis.
