Erysimum insubricum

Erysimum insubricum, commonly known as the Alpine Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a characteristic feature of the Brassicaceae family.

It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
