Eremodraba intricatissima

Eremodraba intricatissima, commonly known as the intricate sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to harsh desert conditions with thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss. Its intricate floral structure and unique growth habit make it an interesting subject for botanical study.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing sandy soils.
