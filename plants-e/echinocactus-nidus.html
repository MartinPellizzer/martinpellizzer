Echinocactus nidus

Echinocactus nidus, commonly known as the "nest cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular, spiny shape and is typically covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the top of the plant. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
