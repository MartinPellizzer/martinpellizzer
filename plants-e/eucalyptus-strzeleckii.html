Eucalyptus strzeleckii

Eucalyptus strzeleckii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stringy gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically yellow or cream in color. Its bark is fibrous and peels in long strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eucalyptus strzeleckii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.
