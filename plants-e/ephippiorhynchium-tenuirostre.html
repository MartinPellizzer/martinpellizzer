Ephippiorhynchium tenuirostre

Ephippiorhynchium tenuirostre, commonly known as the slender-beaked euphorbia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a distinctive capsule fruit that contains seeds enclosed in a fleshy, yellowish structure called an epipetalum. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
