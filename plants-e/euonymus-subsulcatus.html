Euonymus subsulcatus

Euonymus subsulcatus, commonly known as the smooth euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Euonymus subsulcatus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
