Elaphoglossum elegans

Elaphoglossum elegans, commonly known as the elegant holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or drooping in appearance. Native to the temperate regions of the Americas, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. Its spores are produced in clusters on the underside of its fertile fronds, a feature typical of many ferns.

The plant's intricate leaf structure and seasonal growth patterns make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
