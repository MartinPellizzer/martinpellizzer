Ericoides hirtum

Ericoides hirtum, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or coastal regions. Its flowers are typically white or pink and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides hirtum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
