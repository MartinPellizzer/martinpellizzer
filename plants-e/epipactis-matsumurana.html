Epipactis matsumurana

Epipactis matsumurana, commonly known as the Matsumura's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves that form a compact basal cluster. The plant produces a single, upright flowering stem bearing several showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent lip petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions.

Epipactis matsumurana is notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
