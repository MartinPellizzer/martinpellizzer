Eupatorium vauthierianum var. glandulosum

Eupatorium vauthierianum var. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine, glandular hairs. This species belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads.

It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.
