Epidendrum guillemianum

Epidendrum guillemianum, commonly known as the Guillemian's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its erect, pseudobulbous stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum guillemianum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
