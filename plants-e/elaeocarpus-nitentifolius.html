Elaeocarpus nitentifolius

Elaeocarpus nitentifolius, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that resemble apples. The fruit contains a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
