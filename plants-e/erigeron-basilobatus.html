Erigeron basilobatus

Erigeron basilobatus, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a distinctive brown central disk. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including prairies and open woodlands. It is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
