Eupatorium organense var. maximum

Eupatorium organense var. maximum, commonly known as the greater boneset, is a tall herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, rich soils. The plant features erect, branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, adorned with opposite, serrated leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers.

This species is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for respiratory ailments.
