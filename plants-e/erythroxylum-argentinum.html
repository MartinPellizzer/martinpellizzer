Erythroxylum argentinum

Erythroxylum argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and neighboring regions. The plant is characterized by its woody stems, opposite leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, oval berries that contain seeds.

Erythroxylum argentinum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.
