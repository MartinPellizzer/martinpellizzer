Epilobium latifolium subsp. speciosum

Epilobium latifolium subsp.

speciosum, commonly known as the showy willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along streambanks and wet meadows. It is an annual or biennial species, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Its flowers are arranged in dense, terminal spikes, making it a striking addition to wetland habitats.
