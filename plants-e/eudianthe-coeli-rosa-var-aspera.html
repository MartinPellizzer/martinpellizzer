Eudianthe coeli-rosa var. aspera

Eudianthe coeli-rosa var. aspera, commonly known as the rough skyflower, is a variant of the Eudianthe coeli-rosa species, characterized by its distinctive rough-textured foliage.

This plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is native to the Mediterranean region. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with a central yellow disc, and bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

It is also noted for its resilience to drought and mild frost, enhancing its ornamental value in gardens.
