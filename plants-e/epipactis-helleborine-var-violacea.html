Epipactis helleborine var. violacea

Epipactis helleborine var. violacea, commonly known as the violet helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
