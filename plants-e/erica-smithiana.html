Erica smithiana

Erica smithiana, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a hardy evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica smithiana thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
