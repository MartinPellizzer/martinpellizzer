Eucalyptus leucophloia

Eucalyptus leucophloia, commonly known as the white gum, is a tall, slender eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to pale grey bark that peels in thin, fibrous sheets. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue, and they are typically narrow and pointed. The flowers are white or pale yellow, clustered in groups of seven, and are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
