Elaeagnus oldhamii var. nakaii

Elaeagnus oldhamii var. nakaii, commonly known as the Nakai silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which results from dense, fine hairs covering the leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible, red berries that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
