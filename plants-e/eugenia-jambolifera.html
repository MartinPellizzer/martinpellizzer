Eugenia jambolifera

Eugenia jambolifera, commonly known as the Java apple or jambul, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but have a tart, sour flavor. Its botanical features include a smooth, greyish bark and a tendency to grow in tropical and subtropical climates.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of traditional medicinal uses.
