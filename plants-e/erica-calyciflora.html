Erica calyciflora

Erica calyciflora, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica calyciflora is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
