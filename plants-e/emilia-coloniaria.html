Emilia coloniaria

Emilia coloniaria, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its erect stem and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Emilia coloniaria is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used in herbal medicine.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
