Eryngium ferrisiae

Eryngium ferrisiae, commonly known as the Ferris eryngo, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and upright.

Eryngium ferrisiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
