Eupatorium dibollii

Eupatorium dibollii, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, acidic soils. It produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.

Eupatorium dibollii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
