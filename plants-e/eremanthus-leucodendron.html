Eremanthus leucodendron

Eremanthus leucodendron, commonly known as the white-leaf eremanthus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Eremanthus leucodendron is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.
