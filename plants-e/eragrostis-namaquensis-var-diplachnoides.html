Eragrostis namaquensis var. diplachnoides

Eragrostis namaquensis var. diplachnoides, commonly known as the Namibian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is noted for its resilience to drought and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions.
