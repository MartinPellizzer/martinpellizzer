Epidendrum nora-mesae

Epidendrum nora-mesae, commonly known as the Nora-Mesae Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
