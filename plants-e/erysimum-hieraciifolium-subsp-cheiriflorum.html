Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp. cheiriflorum

Erysimum hieraciifolium subsp.

cheiriflorum, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
