Excoecaria oblongifolia

Excoecaria oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oblong in shape and have a distinctive greenish-blue hue. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.
