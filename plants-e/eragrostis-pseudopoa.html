Eragrostis pseudopoa

Eragrostis pseudopoa, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, it is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
