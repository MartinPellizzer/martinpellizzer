Epilobium × opacum

Epilobium × opacum, commonly known as the woolly willow, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, soft hairs, which give it a woolly appearance. The plant produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found along stream banks and in wet meadows.

Epilobium × opacum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
