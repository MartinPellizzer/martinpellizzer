Eucalyptus leptoloma

Eucalyptus leptoloma, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus leptoloma is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
