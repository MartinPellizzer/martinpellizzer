Eugenia verruculosa

Eugenia verruculosa, commonly known as the rough eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves with a rough, waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia verruculosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
