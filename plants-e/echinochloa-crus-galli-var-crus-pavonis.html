Echinochloa crus-galli var. crus-pavonis

Echinochloa crus-galli var. crus-pavonis, commonly known as cocklebur, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is a perennial weed characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and distinctive spiny seed casings. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system.

The variety crus-pavonis is distinguished by its larger, more robust growth compared to other varieties of Echinochloa.
