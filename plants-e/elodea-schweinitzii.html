Elodea schweinitzii

Elodea schweinitzii, commonly known as Schweinitz's waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a submerged, herbaceous plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, whorled leaves that are typically dark green in color. Elodea schweinitzii produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown, and it is often used in aquariums due to its ability to oxygenate water.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
