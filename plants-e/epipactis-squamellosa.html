Epipactis squamellosa

Epipactis squamellosa, commonly known as the wrinkled helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, wrinkled leaves and pale greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive, wrinkled lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It is a perennial herb that produces a single, showy flower per stem.

Epipactis squamellosa is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insects for reproduction.
