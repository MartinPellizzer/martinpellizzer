Eragrostis silveana

Eragrostis silveana, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-gray foliage and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. This plant is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites, where it forms dense clumps. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Eragrostis silveana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
