Erythroxylum affine

Erythroxylum affine, commonly known as the narrowleaf Erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces berries that contain the alkaloid pseudoephedrine.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been studied for potential pharmaceutical applications.
