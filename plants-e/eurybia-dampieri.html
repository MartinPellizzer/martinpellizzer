Eurybia dampieri

Eurybia dampieri, commonly known as the coastal bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the northeastern United States and eastern Canada.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and tidal flats. It has a low-growing habit with basal leaves that are often hairy and lance-shaped.

Eurybia dampieri is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal ecosystems.
