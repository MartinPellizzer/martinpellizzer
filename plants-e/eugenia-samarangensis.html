Eugenia samarangensis

Eugenia samarangensis, commonly known as the Samarang guava, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
