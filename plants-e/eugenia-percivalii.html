Eugenia percivalii

Eugenia percivalii, commonly known as the Percival's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. After flowering, it produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
