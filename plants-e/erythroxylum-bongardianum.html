Erythroxylum bongardianum

Erythroxylum bongardianum, commonly known as the Bongardianum coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. It produces a stimulant alkaloid called cocaine, which is found in its leaves.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is classified as a controlled substance in many countries.
