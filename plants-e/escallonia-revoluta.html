Escallonia revoluta

Escallonia revoluta, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern and eastern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers in spring, which are followed by bright red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Escallonia revoluta is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
