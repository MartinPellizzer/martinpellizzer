Euphorbia squamigera var. carthaginiensis

Euphorbia squamigera var. carthaginiensis, commonly known as the Carthaginian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a distinctive, swollen stem that often branches into a shrub-like form. The leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
