Eragrostis ekmanii

Eragrostis ekmanii, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
