Eupatorium organense

Eupatorium organense, commonly known as the Cuban thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, often displaying shades of white, pink, or purple.

Eupatorium organense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
