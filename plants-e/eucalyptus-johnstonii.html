Eucalyptus johnstonii

Eucalyptus johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale pink flowers, which typically bloom in winter. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

Eucalyptus johnstonii is a hardy species that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
