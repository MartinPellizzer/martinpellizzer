Eremophila exilifolia

Eremophila exilifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf eremophila, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring a compact growth habit and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss. It blooms in winter or early spring, producing pink to red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila exilifolia is often used in native gardens for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
