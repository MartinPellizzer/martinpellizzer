Eupatorium adenophorum

Eupatorium adenophorum, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has finely hairy leaves and is often found in moist, disturbed areas such as roadsides and wetlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eupatorium adenophorum is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
