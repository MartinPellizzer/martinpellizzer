Eriopsis mesae

Eriopsis mesae, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its slender stems and small, blue-gray leaves help it conserve water in harsh environments.

Eriopsis mesae plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
