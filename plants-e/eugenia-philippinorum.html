Eugenia philippinorum

Eugenia philippinorum, commonly known as the Philippine myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas within its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
