Erythroxylum coca var. coca

Erythroxylum coca var. coca, commonly known as coca, is a shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing stimulant alkaloids, primarily cocaine. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, temperate climates.

Its leaves have been traditionally used for their medicinal and stimulant properties by indigenous communities.
