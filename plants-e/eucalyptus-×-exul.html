Eucalyptus × exul

Eucalyptus × exul, commonly known as the hybrid eucalyptus, is a naturally occurring cross between two parent species, typically Eucalyptus robusta and Eucalyptus camaldulensis.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy on top and grayish underneath. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is native to Australia and thrives in a variety of soil types, preferring well-drained conditions.

Eucalyptus × exul is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to adapt to different environmental conditions.
