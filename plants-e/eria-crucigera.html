Eria crucigera

Eria crucigera, commonly known as the cross-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cross-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and often pale green or white, with a delicate fragrance.

Eria crucigera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
