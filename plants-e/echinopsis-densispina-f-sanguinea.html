Echinopsis densispina f. sanguinea

Echinopsis densispina f. sanguinea, commonly known as the blood red moon cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Its unique coloration and nocturnal blooming habits make it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
