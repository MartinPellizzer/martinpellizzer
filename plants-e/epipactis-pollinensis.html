Epipactis pollinensis

Epipactis pollinensis, commonly known as the Pollin's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and Southern Europe.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces erect flowering stems with showy, greenish-yellow flowers that have a distinct lip. Epipactis pollinensis is characterized by its intricate floral structure, which is adapted for pollination by specific insect species.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in calcareous soils.
