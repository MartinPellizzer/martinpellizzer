Elaeocarpus shunningensis

Elaeocarpus shunningensis, commonly known as the Shunning Island elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the island of Shunning in the South China Sea.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit is a woody, capsule-like structure containing several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. This species is notable for its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity and its role in traditional medicine.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
