Euterpe manaele

Euterpe manaele, commonly known as the Manaele palm, is a species of palm native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, upright trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers, which are pollinated by wind and insects. Its fruits are small, round, and typically contain a single seed, making it an important species for both ecological and potential horticultural use.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to the nutrient-poor soils of the rainforest understory.
