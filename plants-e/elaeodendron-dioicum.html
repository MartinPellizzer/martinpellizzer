Elaeodendron dioicum

Elaeodendron dioicum, commonly known as the yellow-leafed elaeodendron, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often yellowish leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds.

Elaeodendron dioicum is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
