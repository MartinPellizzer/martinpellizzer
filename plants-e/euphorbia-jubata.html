Euphorbia jubata

Euphorbia jubata, commonly known as the silver euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem covered in dense, white to silver hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia jubata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and resilience.
