Echinocactus viridescens var. cylindraceus

Echinocactus viridescens var. cylindraceus, commonly known as the cylindrical green echinocactus, is a succulent cactus native to Mexico.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, covered in bright green, cylindrical ribs. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its foliage. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.

Its unique shape and vibrant color make it a distinctive addition to any collection of cacti.
