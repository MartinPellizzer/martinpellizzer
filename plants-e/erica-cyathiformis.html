Erica cyathiformis

Erica cyathiformis, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica cyathiformis is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its flowers bloom in spring, adding a delicate pink or white color to the landscape.
