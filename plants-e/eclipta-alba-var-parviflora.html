Eclipta alba var. parviflora

Eclipta alba var. parviflora, commonly known as white daisy or parviflora false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and alternate leaves that are usually lanceolate or ovate in shape. Its inflorescence consists of both ray and disc florets, which contribute to its ornamental appearance.

The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
