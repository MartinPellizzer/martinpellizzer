Erigeron talyschensis

Erigeron talyschensis, commonly known as the Talyschense Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its delicate, daisy-like appearance. It has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers bloom in the summer months.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
