Echinopsis aurea subsp. shaferi

Echinopsis aurea subsp.

shaferi, commonly known as the Golden Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white to yellow spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
