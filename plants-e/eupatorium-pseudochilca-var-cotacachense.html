Eupatorium pseudochilca var. cotacachense

Eupatorium pseudochilca var. cotacachense, commonly known as the Andean feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in cool, mountainous climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
