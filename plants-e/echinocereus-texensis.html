Echinocereus texensis

Echinocereus texensis, commonly known as the Texas hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny plant that typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 inches. The plant features cylindrical stems covered in white to yellow spines, with clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its structure and appearance are characteristic of the Cactaceae family, showcasing typical adaptations for desert survival.
