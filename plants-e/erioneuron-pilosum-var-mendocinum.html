Erioneuron pilosum var. mendocinum

Erioneuron pilosum var. mendocinum, commonly known as the Mendocino hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to California.

It features fine, hair-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of short, soft hairs. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like clusters of pale green to blue-green flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
