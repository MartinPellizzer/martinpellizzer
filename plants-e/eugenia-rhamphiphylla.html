Eugenia rhamphiphylla

Eugenia rhamphiphylla, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Eugenia rhamphiphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
