Epidendrum trulla

Epidendrum trulla, commonly known as the Trulla Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous plant that typically grows as a pseudobulbous species, featuring upright, branching stems with fleshy leaves. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often arranged in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

Epidendrum trulla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
