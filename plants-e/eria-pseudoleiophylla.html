Eria pseudoleiophylla

Eria pseudoleiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its root system is specialized for absorbing nutrients from the surrounding soil, which is typical for many orchid species.

Eria pseudoleiophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
