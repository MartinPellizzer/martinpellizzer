Erigeron austinae

Erigeron austinae, commonly known as the Austin blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Erigeron austinae is often found in grasslands and along rocky slopes, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
