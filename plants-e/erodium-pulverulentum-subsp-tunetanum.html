Erodium pulverulentum subsp. tunetanum

Erodium pulverulentum subsp.

tunetanum, commonly known as the Tunisian erodium, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa. It belongs to the family Leguminosae, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

The plant typically grows as a low-growing annual herb with hairy, lance-shaped leaves and small, pink or violet flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in pods that split open to disperse the seeds, a common reproductive strategy in the family.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
