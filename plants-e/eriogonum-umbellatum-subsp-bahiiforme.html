Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. bahiiforme

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

bahiiforme, commonly known as the coastal buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California and Oregon. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
