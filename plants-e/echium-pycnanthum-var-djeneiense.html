Echium pycnanthum var. djeneiense

Echium pycnanthum var. djeneiense, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, spindly stems and dense clusters of blue to purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Its fibrous stems and leaves are often used in traditional practices by local communities.
