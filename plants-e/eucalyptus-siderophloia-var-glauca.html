Eucalyptus siderophloia var. glauca

Eucalyptus siderophloia var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous ironbark, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that ranges from grey to pale brown, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish or whitish underside. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are produced in summer. This variety is known for its hard, dense wood and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and rocky areas.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
