Excoecaria biglandulosa var. serrata

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. serrata, commonly known as the serrated croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are deeply lobed and have a distinctive serrated edge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been used in traditional medicine.
