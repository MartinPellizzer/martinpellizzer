Enslenia volubilis

Enslenia volubilis, commonly known as the climbing moonflower, is a vigorous vine native to tropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb various structures. Its flowers emit a sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Botanically, it belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its tuberous roots.
