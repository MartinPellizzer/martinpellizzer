Euchiton gymnocephalus

Euchiton gymnocephalus, commonly known as the naked-headed flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the cooler months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure.
