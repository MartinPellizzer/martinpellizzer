Erythroxylum urbanii

Erythroxylum urbanii, commonly known as the urbanii copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. The tree is known for its ability to produce copal, a resinous substance used in traditional medicine and incense.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has cultural significance in certain indigenous communities.
