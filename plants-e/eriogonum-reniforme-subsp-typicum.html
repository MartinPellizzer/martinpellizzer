Eriogonum reniforme subsp. typicum

Eriogonum reniforme subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky slopes. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and has a deep root system for water absorption.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local pollinators and its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
