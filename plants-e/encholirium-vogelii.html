Encholirium vogelii

Encholirium vogelii, commonly known as the Vogel's torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of vibrant orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has spiny, ribbed stems that help it retain water in its harsh environment. Its flowers are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem, adding to its striking appearance.

Encholirium vogelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
