Eucrosia mirabilis

Eucrosia mirabilis, commonly known as the golden torch lily, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble torches. The plant produces tall, erect stems with large, showy blooms that open in the early morning and close by midday. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

Eucrosia mirabilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, sun-loving display.
