Encyclia × osmentii

Encyclia × osmentii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Encyclia fraterna and Encyclia tatei.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant typically features elongated pseudobulbs covered with a fibrous sheath, and it produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers with a distinctive coloration. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Encyclia × osmentii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
