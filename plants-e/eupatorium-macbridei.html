Eupatorium macbridei

Eupatorium macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic foliage. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
