Epilobium alpestre

Epilobium alpestre, commonly known as alpine willow or mountain willow, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, often at high altitudes. The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium alpestre is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a habitat for various invertebrates.
