Epilobium virgatum subsp. brachyathum

Epilobium virgatum subsp.

brachyathum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils in meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its showy pink to reddish-purple flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the stems during late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
