Erinus fragrans

Erinus fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant erinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Primulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil. Its fragrant blooms, which range in color from white to pink, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erinus fragrans is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
