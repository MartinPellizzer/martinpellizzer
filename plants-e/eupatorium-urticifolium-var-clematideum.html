Eupatorium urticifolium var. clematideum

Eupatorium urticifolium var. clematideum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, spiky purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, stinging hairs, which can cause irritation upon contact.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in native gardens.
