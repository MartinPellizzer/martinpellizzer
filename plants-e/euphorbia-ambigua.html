Euphorbia ambigua

Euphorbia ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its stout, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it potentially hazardous to handle. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Euphorbia ambigua is often found in desert and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
