Eugenia nigricans

Eugenia nigricans, commonly known as the black eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

Eugenia nigricans thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
