Eryngium humile var. latipes

Eryngium humile var. latipes, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features spiny, blue-green foliage and produces small, thistle-like flowers that range from white to purple. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
