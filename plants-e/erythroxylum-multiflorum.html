Erythroxylum multiflorum

Erythroxylum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers and its elliptical, dark green leaves. It produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain seeds.

This species is often grown for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
