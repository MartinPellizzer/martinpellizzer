Erodendrum cynariflorum

Erodendrum cynariflorum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or red petals and yellow center, forming a distinctive crown-like shape. The plant is an annual herb with slender, branching stems and finely divided leaves. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in both horticulture and ecological studies.
