Euphorbia fimbriata var. laxa

Euphorbia fimbriata var. laxa, commonly known as the soft-spined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that is often branched and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Its leaves are typically narrow and may be either opposite or alternate in arrangement.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
