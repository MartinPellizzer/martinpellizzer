Epilobium fischerianum

Epilobium fischerianum, commonly known as Fischer's willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges. The plant features erect, branching stems covered with glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are pink to reddish, with five petals, and are arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.
