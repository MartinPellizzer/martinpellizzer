Elaeagnus crispa

Elaeagnus crispa, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Elaeagnus crispa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
