Euphorbia Falcata Var. Genuina

Euphorbia falcata var. genuina is a striking member of the spurge family known for its unique and dramatic appearance. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in arid and rocky environments where it adapts to harsh conditions with remarkable resilience. Its most distinctive feature is its falcate, or sickle-shaped, leaves that curve dramatically toward the base. Despite its beauty, it is considered a toxic plant due to the presence of milky sap that can cause skin irritation. This plant is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts for its architectural form and low maintenance requirements.
