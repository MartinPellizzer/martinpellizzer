Euodia incisifolia

Euodia incisifolia, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood or incised euodia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. Euodia incisifolia is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Its growth habit and ornamental features make it a popular choice for horticultural use in tropical and subtropical regions.
