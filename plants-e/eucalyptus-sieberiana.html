Eucalyptus sieberiana

Eucalyptus sieberiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that are typically pale green to yellow in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Eucalyptus sieberiana plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
