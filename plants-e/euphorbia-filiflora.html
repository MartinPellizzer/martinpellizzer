Euphorbia filiflora

Euphorbia filiflora, commonly known as the threadleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with narrow, thread-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
