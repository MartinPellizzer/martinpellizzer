Erianthus mishmeensis

Erianthus mishmeensis, commonly known as the Mishme grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Erianthus mishmeensis plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest for its potential in ecological restoration projects.
