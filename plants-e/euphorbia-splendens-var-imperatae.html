Euphorbia splendens var. imperatae

Euphorbia splendens var. imperatae, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in the winter season. The plant has a branching structure with spiny, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. It requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light to thrive.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular ornamental plant.
