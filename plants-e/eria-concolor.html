Eria concolor

Eria concolor, commonly known as the white leaching orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria concolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
