Eugenia chacoensis

Eugenia chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Paraguay and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and small, edible berries that turn red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.
