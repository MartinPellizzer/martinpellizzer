Echinocactus longihamatus var. insignis

Echinocactus longihamatus var. insignis, commonly known as the Mexican golden barrel cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that becomes more rounded with age, often displaying a golden or yellowish hue. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. Its spines are typically yellow to orange, providing both protection and a distinctive appearance.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
