Eucalyptus oxymitra

Eucalyptus oxymitra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stringy gum, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, stringy branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, creamy-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Eucalyptus oxymitra is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its aromatic oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
