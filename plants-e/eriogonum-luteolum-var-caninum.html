Eriogonum luteolum var. caninum

Eriogonum luteolum var. caninum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and open woodlands. Its leaves are often hairy and lance-shaped, with a grayish-green color.

The species is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes mallows and hibiscus.
