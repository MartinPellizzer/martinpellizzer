Euphorbia hypericifolia var. maculata

Euphorbia hypericifolia var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted spurge, is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, greenish-gray leaves with distinctive red or purple spots.

It is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
