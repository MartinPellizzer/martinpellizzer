Elaeocarpus hylobroma

Elaeocarpus hylobroma, commonly known as the red-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
