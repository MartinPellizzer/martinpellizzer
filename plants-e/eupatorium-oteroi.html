Eupatorium oteroi

Eupatorium oteroi, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.

The genus Eupatorium includes many species with similar medicinal uses and ecological roles in their native habitats.
