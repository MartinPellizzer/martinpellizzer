Eleocharis jacobsiana

Eleocharis jacobsiana, commonly known as the Jacobs' spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in short, bristly hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact growth pattern.

Eleocharis jacobsiana plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
