Eria hutchinsoniana

Eria hutchinsoniana, commonly known as the Hutchinson's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its intricate and often showy blooms. The plant typically features slender, erect stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
