Eugenia emirnensis var. submaritima

Eugenia emirnensis var. submaritima, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are adapted to withstand saline conditions. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, which bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains numerous seeds.

This species is often used in coastal landscaping due to its resilience and ornamental value.
