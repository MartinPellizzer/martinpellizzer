Erucastrum procumbens

Erucastrum procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate cress, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, green, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces tiny, yellow flowers in clusters. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its life cycle is annual, and it is known for its peppery flavor, making it a useful plant in culinary applications.

Erucastrum procumbens is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
