Eleocharis litoralis

Eleocharis litoralis, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a flowering plant species native to coastal and wetland areas.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces spikelets that bear tiny flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. Eleocharis litoralis thrives in brackish or saline environments, making it an important component of salt marsh ecosystems.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
