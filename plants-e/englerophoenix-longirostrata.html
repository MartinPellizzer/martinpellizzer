Englerophoenix longirostrata

Englerophoenix longirostrata, commonly known as the long-beaked date palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

This plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The inflorescences are clustered at the base of the trunk, and the plant produces small, yellowish flowers that eventually develop into edible dates.

Englerophoenix longirostrata is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
