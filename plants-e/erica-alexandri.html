Erica alexandri

Erica alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. Erica alexandri typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing shrubs.
