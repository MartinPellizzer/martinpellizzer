Epidendrum centradenia

Epidendrum centradenia, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with erect, cane-like stems that bear numerous fleshy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers that are highly fragrant. It thrives in humid, well-lit environments with good air circulation and moderate watering.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and conservation purposes.
