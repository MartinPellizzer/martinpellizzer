Eriocaulon talbotii

Eriocaulon talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's flat sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in shallow water or in saturated soil, contributing to the ecological balance of its habitat.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
