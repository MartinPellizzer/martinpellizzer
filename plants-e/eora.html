Eora

Eora, commonly known as the Australian wattle, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Australia and includes over 1,200 species, many of which are shrubs or small trees. The plant is characterized by its feathery, yellow flowers and compound leaves, which are often bipinnate. Eora species are well adapted to the diverse climates of Australia, from coastal regions to arid inland areas.

They play an important role in soil stabilization and provide habitat for native wildlife.
