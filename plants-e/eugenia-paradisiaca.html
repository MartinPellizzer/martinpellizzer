Eugenia paradisiaca

Eugenia paradisiaca, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a glossy surface, while its bark is smooth and reddish in color.

Eugenia paradisiaca thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and riverine forests.
