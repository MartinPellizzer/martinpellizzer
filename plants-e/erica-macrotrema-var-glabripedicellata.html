Erica macrotrema var. glabripedicellata

Erica macrotrema var. glabripedicellata, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed heath, is a shrub native to the Cape Region of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its flowers are usually pale pink to white, clustered in dense inflorescences, and bloom during the summer months.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in coastal gardens due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
