Emiliella biensis

Emiliella biensis, commonly known as the biensian orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The plant has narrow, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Emiliella biensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
