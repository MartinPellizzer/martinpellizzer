Erica toringbergensis

Erica toringbergensis, commonly known as the Toringberg heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica toringbergensis is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
