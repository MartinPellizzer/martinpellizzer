Erica quadrisulcata

Erica quadrisulcata, commonly known as the four-ribbed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom in spring.

Erica quadrisulcata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in acidic soil conditions.
