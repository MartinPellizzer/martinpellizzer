Eupatorium lindleyanum f. eglandulosum

Eupatorium lindleyanum f. eglandulosum, commonly known as the glandular eupatorium, is a variant of the species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swamps. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

The glandular features of its leaves are a distinguishing trait that sets it apart from other eupatorium species.
