Elymus pseudoagropyrum

Elymus pseudoagropyrum, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are arranged in clusters, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Elymus pseudoagropyrum is valued for its ecological importance and is used in restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
