Eryngium grossii

Eryngium grossii, commonly known as the spiked eryngo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems are tall and branched, supporting the showy inflorescence.

Eryngium grossii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
