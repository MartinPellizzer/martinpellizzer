Eryngium tricuspidatum var. subintegrum

Eryngium tricuspidatum var. subintegrum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking blue to purple spiny flower heads that resemble thistles, surrounded by finely divided, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet, with a compact, upright habit. Its foliage is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
