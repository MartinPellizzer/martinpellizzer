Eugenia yasuniana

Eugenia yasuniana, commonly known as the Yasuni myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and cloves, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are dark green and leathery, while its flowers are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
