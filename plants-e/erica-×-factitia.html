Erica × factitia

Erica × factitia, commonly known as the common heath, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica × factitia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
