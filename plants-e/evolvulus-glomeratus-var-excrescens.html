Evolvulus glomeratus var. excrescens

Evolvulus glomeratus var. excrescens, commonly known as the clustered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous annual with prostrate or creeping growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of blue, purple, or white, typically opening in the morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
