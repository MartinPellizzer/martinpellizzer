Eperua praesagata

Eperua praesagata, commonly known as the sacred eperua, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods that contain hard, woody seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves have been utilized by indigenous communities for their purported healing properties.

Eperua praesagata is also valued for its role in local ecosystems and cultural significance.
