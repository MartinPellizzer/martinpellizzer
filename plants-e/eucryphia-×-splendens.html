Eucryphia × splendens

Eucryphia × splendens, commonly known as the golden candle bush, is a hybrid shrub native to New Zealand.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. Its growth habit is upright and spreading, making it suitable for hedges or as a specimen plant in gardens.

The bark is smooth and peels in thin layers, adding to its ornamental appeal.
