Eremopyrum bonaepartis subsp. hirsutum

Eremopyrum bonaepartis subsp.

hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy eremopyrum, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy leaves and inflorescences that are typically spike-like.

The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, which contribute to its ability to colonize disturbed or sandy soils. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in xerophytic ecosystems.
