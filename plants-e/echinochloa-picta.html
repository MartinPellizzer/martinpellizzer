Echinochloa picta

Echinochloa picta, commonly known as the painted spikerush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with a mix of green and reddish hues. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Echinochloa picta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
