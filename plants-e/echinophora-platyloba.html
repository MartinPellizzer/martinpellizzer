Echinophora platyloba

Echinophora platyloba, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, upright stem with spiny leaves that are typically broad and lobed. The plant produces purple to pink flowers in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, slightly sticky texture.

Echinophora platyloba is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
