Eriobotrya oblongifolia

Eriobotrya oblongifolia, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to pears but are typically less commonly consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Eriobotrya oblongifolia is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
