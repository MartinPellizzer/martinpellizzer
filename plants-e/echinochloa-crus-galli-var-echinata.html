Echinochloa crus-galli var. echinata

Echinochloa crus-galli var. echinata, commonly known as cocklebur, is a species of annual grass that is native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and distinctive, spiny burs that encase its seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides, fields, and waste areas. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
