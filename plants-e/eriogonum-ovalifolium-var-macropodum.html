Eriogonum ovalifolium var. macropodum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. macropodum, commonly known as the large-footed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is well adapted to arid conditions and has a deep root system to access water.

The species is part of the Polygonaceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
