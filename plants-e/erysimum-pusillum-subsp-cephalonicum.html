Erysimum pusillum subsp. cephalonicum

Erysimum pusillum subsp.

cephalonicum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions.

The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow, borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and bloom in early spring.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
