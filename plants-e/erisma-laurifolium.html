Erisma laurifolium

Erisma laurifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf erisma, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, often with a silver or grayish hue, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Erisma laurifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.
