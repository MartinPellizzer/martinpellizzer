Eclipta philippinensis

Eclipta philippinensis, commonly known as Philippine false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dandelion-like appearance with yellow flowers and lobed leaves. The plant is often found in open areas, grasslands, and disturbed soils across its native range. It has small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are typically short-lived, opening in the morning and closing by midday.
