Erica macropus

Erica macropus, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and inland regions. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

Erica macropus is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
