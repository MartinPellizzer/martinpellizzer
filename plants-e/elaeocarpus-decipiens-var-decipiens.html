Elaeocarpus decipiens var. decipiens

Elaeocarpus decipiens var. decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical forests. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that is often used in traditional medicine and has a unique, aromatic scent.
