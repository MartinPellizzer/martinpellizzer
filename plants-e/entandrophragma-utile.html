Entandrophragma utile

Entandrophragma utile, commonly known as African teak, is a tropical hardwood tree native to West and Central Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, durable wood with a fine, even grain, making it highly valued for furniture and construction. The tree grows to a height of up to 40 meters and has a straight trunk with a cylindrical shape. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic, while the flowers are small and greenish-yellow.

The plant is also known for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
