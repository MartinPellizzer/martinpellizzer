Erythroxylum glaucum

Erythroxylum glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-green bark and elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, woody fruits containing seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
