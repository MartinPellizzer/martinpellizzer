Erysimum sinuatum

Erysimum sinuatum, commonly known as the candytuft, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and its clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are lobed and often have a wavy edge, while the upper leaves are narrower. It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Erysimum sinuatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
