Erica copiosa

Erica copiosa, commonly known as the common heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. Erica copiosa typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
