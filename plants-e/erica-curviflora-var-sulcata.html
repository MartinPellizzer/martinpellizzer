Erica curviflora var. sulcata

Erica curviflora var. sulcata, commonly known as the serrated heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica curviflora var. sulcata is often used in rock gardens and as a coastal plant due to its tolerance of salty air and drought conditions.
