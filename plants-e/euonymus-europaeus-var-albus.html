Euonymus europaeus var. albus

Euonymus europaeus var. albus, commonly known as the white euonymus, is a variant of the European spindle tree.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a white or pale underside. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and appear in clusters, followed by red, inflated fruits that split open to reveal seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
