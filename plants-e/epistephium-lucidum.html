Epistephium lucidum

Epistephium lucidum, commonly known as the shining epistephium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers that are typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
