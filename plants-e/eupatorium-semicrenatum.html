Eupatorium semicrenatum

Eupatorium semicrenatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are white to pale blue and attract various pollinators.

The leaves are narrow and serrated, often with a slightly hairy texture.
