Eupatorium laevigatum var. genuinum

Eupatorium laevigatum var. genuinum, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
