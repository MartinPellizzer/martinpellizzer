Erica fusiformis

Erica fusiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Erica fusiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to support local wildlife.
