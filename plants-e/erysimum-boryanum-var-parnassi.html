Erysimum boryanum var. parnassi

Erysimum boryanum var. parnassi, commonly known as the Parnassian wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compact, low-growing stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
