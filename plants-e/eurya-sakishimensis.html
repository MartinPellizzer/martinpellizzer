Eurya sakishimensis

Eurya sakishimensis, commonly known as the Japanese holly or Japanese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that remain throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eurya sakishimensis is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
