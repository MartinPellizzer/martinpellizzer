Erythrolaena conspicua

Erythrolaena conspicua, commonly known as the red-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that contains small, hard seeds.
