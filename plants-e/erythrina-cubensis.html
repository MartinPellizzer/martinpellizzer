Erythrina cubensis

Erythrina cubensis, commonly known as the Cuban coral tree, is a tropical leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. Erythrina cubensis is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.
