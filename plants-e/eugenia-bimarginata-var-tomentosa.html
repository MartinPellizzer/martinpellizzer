Eugenia bimarginata var. tomentosa

Eugenia bimarginata var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy wax apple, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Its leaves are elliptical with a hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
