Eupatorium suratense var. typicum

Eupatorium suratense var. typicum, commonly known as Indian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to India and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antipyretic properties.
