Espeletia paltonioides

Espeletia paltonioides, commonly known as the Andean starflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems. Its structure includes a rosette of basal leaves that protect the central flowering stalk.

Espeletia paltonioides plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
