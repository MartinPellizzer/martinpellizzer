Eritrichium clandestinum var. decumbens

Eritrichium clandestinum var. decumbens, commonly known as the "secret eric" or "lucky plant," is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has a mat-forming habit with creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
