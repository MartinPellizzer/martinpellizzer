Eurybia ramosissima

Eurybia ramosissima, commonly known as New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woodlands and prairies. Its foliage is usually basal, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and dark green.

Eurybia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
