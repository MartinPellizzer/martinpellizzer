Eriogonum bidentatum

Eriogonum bidentatum, commonly known as the two-toothed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves with two distinct teeth on each leaf margin, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eriogonum bidentatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a member of the Polygonaceae family.
