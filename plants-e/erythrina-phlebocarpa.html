Erythrina phlebocarpa

Erythrina phlebocarpa, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Erythrina phlebocarpa thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
