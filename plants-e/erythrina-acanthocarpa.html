Erythrina acanthocarpa

Erythrina acanthocarpa, commonly known as the red torch tree, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in thorns. It produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Erythrina acanthocarpa is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
