Epimedium circinnatocucullatum

Epimedium circinnatocucullatum, commonly known as the golden shield fern, is a deciduous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, shield-shaped leaves that are often yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on short, erect pedicels. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and hardy nature.
