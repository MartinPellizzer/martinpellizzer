Eriocaulon megapotamicum

Eriocaulon megapotamicum, commonly known as the Amazonian spike-rush, is a grass-like plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in shallow, nutrient-rich waters and is often found in marshy areas and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in spikes and are often wind-pollinated.

Eriocaulon megapotamicum plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic invertebrates and contributing to the wetland ecosystem.
