Erica hibernica

Erica hibernica, commonly known as the Irish heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western and northern regions of Ireland.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that remain green throughout the year.

Erica hibernica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
