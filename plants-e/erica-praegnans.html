Erica praegnans

Erica praegnans, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica praegnans thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
