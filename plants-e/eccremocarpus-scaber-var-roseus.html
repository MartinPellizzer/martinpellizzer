Eccremocarpus scaber var. roseus

Eccremocarpus scaber var. roseus, commonly known as the rose-flowered glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, rose-colored flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces distinctive, woody fruits that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique appearance and vibrant color make it a standout addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
