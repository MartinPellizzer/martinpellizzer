Erythrina orophila

Erythrina orophila, commonly known as the rock firetree, is a species of legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which bloom in dense clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and thorny. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Erythrina orophila plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
