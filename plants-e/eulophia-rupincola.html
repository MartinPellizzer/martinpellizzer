Eulophia rupincola

Eulophia rupincola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant typically grows on rocky substrates and thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

Eulophia rupincola is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
