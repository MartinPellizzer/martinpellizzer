Eryngium × rocheri

Eryngium × rocheri, commonly known as the hybrid sea holly, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble the structure of true holly. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage consists of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eryngium × rocheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
