Echium flavum subsp. saetabense

Echium flavum subsp.

saetabense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
