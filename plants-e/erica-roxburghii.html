Erica roxburghii

Erica roxburghii, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub is characterized by its dense, compact growth and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
