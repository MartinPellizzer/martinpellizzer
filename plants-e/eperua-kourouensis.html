Eperua kourouensis

Eperua kourouensis, commonly known as the Kourou Eperua, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon Basin, specifically in French Guiana.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, tropical forests, where it thrives in the understory layer.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.
