Euryops speciosissimus

Euryops speciosissimus, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with grey-green, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
