Eupatorium symphyopappus

Eupatorium symphyopappus, commonly known as the clustered thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
