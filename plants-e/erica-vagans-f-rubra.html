Erica vagans f. rubra

Erica vagans f. rubra, commonly known as the red heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, brightly colored flowers. The plant typically displays vibrant red or pink blooms, which appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

Erica vagans f. rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
