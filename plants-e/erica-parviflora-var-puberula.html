Erica parviflora var. puberula

Erica parviflora var. puberula, commonly known as the silky heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silky pubescence. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and pale red, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This hardy species is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
