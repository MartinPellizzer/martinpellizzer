Epidendrum pallidiflorum

Epidendrum pallidiflorum, commonly known as the pale-flowered epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features long, linear leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow or greenish-white flowers with a distinctive lip. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, blooming during the warmer months.

Epidendrum pallidiflorum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
