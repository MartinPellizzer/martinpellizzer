Eriocycla ghafooriana

Eriocycla ghafooriana, commonly known as the Kashmiri hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a prominent yellow or orange throat. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its leaves are ovate and hairy, providing a textured appearance, while the stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
