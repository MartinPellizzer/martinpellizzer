Erodium chrysanthum

Erodium chrysanthum, commonly known as common stormweed, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typically hairy and serrated. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a shallow root system and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Erodium chrysanthum is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
