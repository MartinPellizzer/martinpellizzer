Erysimum helveticum var. commatum

Erysimum helveticum var. commatum, commonly known as the Swiss wallflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in natural habitats.
