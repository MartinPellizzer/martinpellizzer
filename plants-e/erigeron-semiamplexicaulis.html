Erigeron semiamplexicaulis

Erigeron semiamplexicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, alternate leaves that are often slightly clasping at the base. Its flowers bloom in shades of white to pale yellow, typically from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
