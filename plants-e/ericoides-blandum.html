Ericoides blandum

Ericoides blandum, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure and strong winds. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are small, leathery, and densely packed, forming a mat-like structure that helps retain moisture.

Ericoides blandum is a key component of coastal heath ecosystems and is often found in association with other Ericaceae species.
