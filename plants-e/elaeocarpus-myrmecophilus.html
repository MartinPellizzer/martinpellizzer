Elaeocarpus myrmecophilus

Elaeocarpus myrmecophilus, commonly known as the ant-loving elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its unique relationship with ants, as its fruits are specifically adapted to be dispersed by these insects. Its bark is thick and fissured, and it produces fleshy, red berries that attract various wildlife.

This species is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
