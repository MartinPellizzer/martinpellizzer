Euphorbia gayi

Euphorbia gayi, commonly known as the Gay's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces distinctive yellowish-green flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
