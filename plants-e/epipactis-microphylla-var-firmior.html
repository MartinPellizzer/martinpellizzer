Epipactis microphylla var. firmior

Epipactis microphylla var. firmior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and a single, pale green to yellowish flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually short-lived and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect species for reproduction.
