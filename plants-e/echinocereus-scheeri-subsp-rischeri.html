Echinocereus scheeri subsp. rischeri

Echinocereus scheeri subsp.

rischeri, commonly known as the Rischer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to desert conditions, with succulent tissues for water storage.
