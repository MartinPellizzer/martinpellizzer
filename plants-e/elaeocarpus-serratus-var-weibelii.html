Elaeocarpus serratus var. weibelii

Elaeocarpus serratus var. weibelii, commonly known as the Weibel's holly.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
