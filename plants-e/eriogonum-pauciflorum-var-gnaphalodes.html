Eriogonum pauciflorum var. gnaphalodes

Eriogonum pauciflorum var. gnaphalodes, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, pale green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
