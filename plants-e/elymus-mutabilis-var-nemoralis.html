Elymus mutabilis var. nemoralis

Elymus mutabilis var. nemoralis, commonly known as the wood grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear spikelets arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

The inflorescence is usually a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
