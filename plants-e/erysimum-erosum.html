Erysimum erosum

Erysimum erosum, commonly known as the hoary erysimum, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arid environments. Erysimum erosum is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
