Euonymus melananthus

Euonymus melananthus, commonly known as black euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn a striking red or purple in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red, spherical berries that persist into winter. Euonymus melananthus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
