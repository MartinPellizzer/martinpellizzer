Epiphyllum macrocarpum

Epiphyllum macrocarpum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is native to Central America and has a stem that can grow up to several meters in length. Its leaves are modified into spiny structures, and it thrives in warm, humid environments.

The flowers are typically white or pale pink and emit a fragrant scent to attract nocturnal pollinators.
