Eupatorium leptophlebium

Eupatorium leptophlebium, commonly known as narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinct veining pattern, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
