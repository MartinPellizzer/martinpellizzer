Echites crassipes

Echites crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf water lily, is a semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has broad, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to aquatic ecosystems. The plant produces showy, white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor it in shallow water, while its rhizomes spread horizontally.

Echites crassipes plays a vital role in water filtration and habitat creation for various aquatic organisms.
