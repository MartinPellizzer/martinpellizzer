Elatine hydropiper f. intermedia

Elatine hydropiper f. intermedia, commonly known as water pepper, is a member of the Elatinaceae family and is characterized by its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habit.

It typically grows in moist soils or shallow water, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other wet habitats. The plant has tiny, oval-shaped leaves that are usually arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with four petals, and it produces small, bladder-like fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
