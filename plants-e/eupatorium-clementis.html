Eupatorium clementis

Eupatorium clementis, commonly known as the white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as swamps, marshes, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium clementis is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
