Erigeron basalticus

Erigeron basalticus, commonly known as the basalt aster, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, daisy-like white or blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its scientific name reflects its association with basaltic rock formations.

Erigeron basalticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
