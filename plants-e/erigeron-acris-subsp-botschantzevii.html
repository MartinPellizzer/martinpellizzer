Erigeron acris subsp. botschantzevii

Erigeron acris subsp.

botschantzevii, commonly known as the Botchantzev's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and flower structure compared to the typical E. acris. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its blooming period typically occurs from late spring to early summer.
