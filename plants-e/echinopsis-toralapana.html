Echinopsis toralapana

Echinopsis toralapana, commonly known as the Toralapana cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Bolivia.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick stem and produces clusters of white or yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water, storing moisture in its thick, fleshy stems. Its spines are typically white or pale in color, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.

Echinopsis toralapana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and resilience.
