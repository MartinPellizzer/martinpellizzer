Eucalyptus leptophylla var. leptorrhyncha

Eucalyptus leptophylla var. leptorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, cylindrical flower buds. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, forming dense shrubs or small trees.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
