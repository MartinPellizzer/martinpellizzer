Elaeocarpus homalioides

Elaeocarpus homalioides, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as mountain forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
