Eremophila hughesii subsp. hughesii

Eremophila hughesii subsp.

hughesii, commonly known as the Hughes' emu bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pale blue or purple flowers during spring. It is well-suited for drought-tolerant landscaping and is often used in revegetation projects.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
