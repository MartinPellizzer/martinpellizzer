Ephaiola odorata

Ephaiola odorata, commonly known as the sweet-scented wood betony, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. It is often cultivated for its fragrant leaves and ornamental value.

The essential oils extracted from its leaves are used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.
