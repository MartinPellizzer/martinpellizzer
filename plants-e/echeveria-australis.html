Echeveria australis

Echeveria australis, commonly known as the Australian echeveria, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming structure, with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces vibrant clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Echeveria australis is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-draining soil.
