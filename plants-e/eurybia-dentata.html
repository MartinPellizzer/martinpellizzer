Eurybia dentata

Eurybia dentata, commonly known as the Eastern Aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features alternate, toothed leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eurybia dentata produces showy purple to pink flowers in the late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
