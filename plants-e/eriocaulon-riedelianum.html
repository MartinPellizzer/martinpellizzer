The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense covering of minute, hair-like structures that give it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon riedelianum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
