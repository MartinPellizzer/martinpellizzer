Eugenia subverticillaris

Eugenia subverticillaris, commonly known as the mountain guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
