Eriocaulon caesium

Eriocaulon caesium, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems covered in fine, silver-white hairs. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a soft, velvety texture.

Eriocaulon caesium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
