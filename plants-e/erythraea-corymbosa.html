Erythraea corymbosa

Erythraea corymbosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown heartwood and is part of the Rutaceae family. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in corymbs, giving it its scientific name. Its leaves are typically simple and alternate, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

The tree is valued for its durable wood, which has been historically used in furniture and musical instruments.
