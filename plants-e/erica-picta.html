Erica picta

Erica picta, commonly known as the painted heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, brightly colored flowers, typically pink or red. Erica picta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a hardy, evergreen shrub that is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.
