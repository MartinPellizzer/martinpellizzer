Eucalyptus suggrandis

Eucalyptus suggrandis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Eucalyptus suggrandis is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various native animals.
