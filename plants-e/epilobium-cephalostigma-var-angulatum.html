Epilobium cephalostigma var. angulatum

Epilobium cephalostigma var. angulatum, commonly known as the angular willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers. Its flowers are borne on terminal spikes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
