Erica linderi

Erica linderi, commonly known as the Linder's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica linderi typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in rocky or mountainous regions, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of its native habitat.
