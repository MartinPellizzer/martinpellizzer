Erythroxylum schliebenii

Erythroxylum schliebenii, commonly known as Schlieben's copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ethnobotanical studies.
