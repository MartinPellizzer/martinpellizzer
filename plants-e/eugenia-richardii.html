Eugenia richardii

Eugenia richardii, commonly known as the Richard's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, is edible and has a tart flavor.

Eugenia richardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
