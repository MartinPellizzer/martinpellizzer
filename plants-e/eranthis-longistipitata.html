Eranthis longistipitata

Eranthis longistipitata, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a basal rosette of broad, hairy leaves that are typically lanceolate in shape. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive elongated floral tube. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
