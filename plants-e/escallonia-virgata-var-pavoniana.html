Escallonia virgata var. pavoniana

Escallonia virgata var. pavoniana, commonly known as the pavoniana escallonia, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are oval in shape and emit a subtle, pleasant fragrance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its dense growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and butterflies.
