Embothrium acanthifolium

Embothrium acanthifolium, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile and western Argentina.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has compound leaves with spiny margins, giving it a distinctive and somewhat prickly appearance. It thrives in coastal climates with well-drained soils and is often found growing near the shoreline.

Embothrium acanthifolium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
