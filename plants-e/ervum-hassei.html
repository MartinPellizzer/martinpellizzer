Ervum hassei

Ervum hassei, commonly known as the white vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun.

Ervum hassei is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
