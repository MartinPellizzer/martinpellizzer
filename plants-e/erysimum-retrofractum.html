Erysimum retrofractum

Erysimum retrofractum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to orange. The plant has linear to lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Erysimum retrofractum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
