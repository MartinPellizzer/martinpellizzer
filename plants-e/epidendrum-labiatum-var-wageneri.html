Epidendrum labiatum var. wageneri

Epidendrum labiatum var. wageneri, commonly known as the Wagener's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple pseudobulbs. The plant features lanceolate, dark green leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
