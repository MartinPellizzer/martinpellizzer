Echium proditorum

Echium proditorum, commonly known as the desert evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to blue-purple flowers that open in the late afternoon and remain open through the night. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
