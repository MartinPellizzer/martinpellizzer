Eugenia foetida var. rhombea

Eugenia foetida var. rhombea, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, dark red berries that are edible and have a sweet, peppery flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
