Eruca sativa

Eruca sativa, commonly known as rocket or arugula, is a leafy green plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like broccoli and cabbage. The plant has serrated, lobed leaves that are typically pale green to reddish in color. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods.

Eruca sativa is valued for its peppery flavor and is often used in salads, soups, and as a garnish.
