Eriosyce aspillagae

Eriosyce aspillagae, commonly known as the Desert Rose Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its gray-green body. It is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
