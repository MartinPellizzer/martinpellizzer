Eucalyptus robertsonii nothosubsp. hemisphaerica

Eucalyptus robertsonii nothosubsp.

hemisphaerica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a subspecies of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, smooth bark.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
