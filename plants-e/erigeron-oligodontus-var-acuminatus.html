Erigeron oligodontus var. acuminatus

Erigeron oligodontus var. acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and narrow, linear leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
