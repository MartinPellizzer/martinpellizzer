Eupatorium chabrense

Eupatorium chabrense, commonly known as the Chabrense boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium chabrense is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
