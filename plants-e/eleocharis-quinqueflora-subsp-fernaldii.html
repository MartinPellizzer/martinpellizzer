Eleocharis quinqueflora subsp. fernaldii

Eleocharis quinqueflora subsp.

fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's spike rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color.

The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
