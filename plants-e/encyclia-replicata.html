Encyclia replicata

Encyclia replicata, commonly known as the Mexican sun orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in seasonal climates. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding environment.

Encyclia replicata prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
