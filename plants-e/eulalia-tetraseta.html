Eulalia tetraseta

Eulalia tetraseta, commonly known as the four-branched grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Eulalia tetraseta is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
