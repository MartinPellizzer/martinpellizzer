Euphorbia cylindrifolia

Euphorbia cylindrifolia, commonly known as the cylindrical spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or green. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to store water in its stems.

Euphorbia cylindrifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
