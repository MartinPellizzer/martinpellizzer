Eucalyptus odorata var. calcicultrix

Eucalyptus odorata var. calcicultrix, commonly known as the coastal mallee, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically green or yellow-green in color. It is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or semi-arid regions.

This variety is known for its aromatic oils and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
