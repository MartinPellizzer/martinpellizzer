Euphorbia pulchella

Euphorbia pulchella, commonly known as the yellow spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that features a rosette of fleshy, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to wetland environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Euphorbia pulchella is notable for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
