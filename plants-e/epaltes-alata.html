Epaltes alata

Epaltes alata, commonly known as the winged spurge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, wing-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by green, fleshy capsules. Epaltes alata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

It is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
