Euphorbia zonosperma

Euphorbia zonosperma, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, green, globe-shaped fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a cyathium, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a tangy, sweet flavor.
