Eriostemon oblongifolius

Eriostemon oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eriostemon, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to the local biodiversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
