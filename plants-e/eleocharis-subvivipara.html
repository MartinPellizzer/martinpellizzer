Eleocharis subvivipara

Eleocharis subvivipara, commonly known as the viviparous sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is notable for its ability to produce viviparous seeds, which germinate while still attached to the parent plant.
