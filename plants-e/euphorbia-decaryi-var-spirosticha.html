Euphorbia decaryi var. spirosticha

Euphorbia decaryi var. spirosticha, commonly known as the spiral euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces striking yellow-orange cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by succulent enthusiasts.
