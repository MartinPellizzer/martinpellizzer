Eupatorium martii

Eupatorium martii, commonly known as wild beebalm, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic properties.

Eupatorium martii is also a valuable nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
