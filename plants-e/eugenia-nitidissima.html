Eugenia nitidissima

Eugenia nitidissima, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia nitidissima is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
