Eriostemon × pulchellus

Eriostemon × pulchellus, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Eriostemon heterophyllus and Eriostemon pulchellus, showcasing a blend of characteristics from both parents. The plant is notable for its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and is well adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
