Eriosyce curvispina subsp. choapensis

Eriosyce curvispina subsp.

choapensis, commonly known as the Choapan Desert Cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
