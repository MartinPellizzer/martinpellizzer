Eragrostis laetevirens

Eragrostis laetevirens, commonly known as blue grass or sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades that are often a striking blue-green color, especially when young. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Eragrostis laetevirens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
