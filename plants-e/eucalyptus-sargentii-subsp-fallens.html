Eucalyptus sargentii subsp. fallens

Eucalyptus sargentii subsp.

fallens, commonly known as the Sargent's honey gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color.

The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are borne in clustered inflorescences, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and is commonly found in open forests and woodlands.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
