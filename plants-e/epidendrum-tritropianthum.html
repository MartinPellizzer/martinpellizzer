Epidendrum tritropianthum

Epidendrum tritropianthum, commonly known as the showy epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is an epiphytic orchid, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures without being parasitic.

Epidendrum tritropianthum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
