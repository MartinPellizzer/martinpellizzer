Ericameria teretifolia

Ericameria teretifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is adapted to arid environments with low moisture availability.

Ericameria teretifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
