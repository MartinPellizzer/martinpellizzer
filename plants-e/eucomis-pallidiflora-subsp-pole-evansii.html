Eucomis pallidiflora subsp. pole-evansii

Eucomis pallidiflora subsp.

pole-evansii, commonly known as the pale-flowered pineapple lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant is typically found in grassland habitats and thrives in well-drained soils. It is a perennial herb that is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different floral morphology compared to the main species.
