Erythroxylum strobilaceum

Erythroxylum strobilaceum, commonly known as the Mexican pepper plant, is a shrub native to Central America and northern South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its foliage rather than its fruits.
