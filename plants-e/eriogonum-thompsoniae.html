Eriogonum thompsoniae

Eriogonum thompsoniae, commonly known as Thompson's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Thompson's buckwheat is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
