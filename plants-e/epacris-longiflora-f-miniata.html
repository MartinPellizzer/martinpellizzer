Epacris longiflora f. miniata

Epacris longiflora f. miniata, commonly known as the miniature epacris, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink to red flowers during the summer months, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry forests.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
