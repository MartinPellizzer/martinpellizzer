Euphorbia hypericifolia var. bracteolaris

Euphorbia hypericifolia var. bracteolaris, commonly known as the bracteolaris spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with prominent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
