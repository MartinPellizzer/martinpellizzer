Echium creticum subsp. coincyanum

Echium creticum subsp.

coincyanum, commonly known as the Cretan viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in Greece and Turkey. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vivid floral display.
