Echites brevipedunculatus

Echites brevipedunculatus, commonly known as the short-stemmed echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Echites brevipedunculatus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
