Ervum volubile

Ervum volubile, commonly known as the climbing bean or wild vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with slender, hairy stems that often twine around supports. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Ervum volubile is often found in disturbed soils and can be a common weed in agricultural fields.
