Echinocactus campylacanthus

Echinocactus campylacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny exterior. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
