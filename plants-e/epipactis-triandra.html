Epipactis triandra

Epipactis triandra, commonly known as the yellow helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and produces tall, erect flowering stems with clusters of yellowish-green to brownish flowers. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Epipactis triandra is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often associated with specific fungal associations for nutrient uptake.
