Elaeocarpus dolichostylus subsp. collinus

Elaeocarpus dolichostylus subsp.

collinus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to northern Australia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical to ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top.

The plant produces small, white flowers followed by distinctive, woody, spherical fruits that are often used by native fauna. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
