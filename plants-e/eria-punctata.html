Eria punctata

Eria punctata, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Eria punctata is known for its unique spotted pattern on its petals, which distinguishes it from other orchid species.
