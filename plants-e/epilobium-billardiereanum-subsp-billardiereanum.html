Epilobium billardiereanum subsp. billardiereanum

Epilobium billardiereanum subsp.

billardiereanum, commonly known as the pink willow, is a herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and wetlands.

The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a reddish tinge. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
