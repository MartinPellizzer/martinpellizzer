Echinops commutatus f. sectatus

Echinops commutatus f. sectatus, commonly known as the spiny globe thistle, is a cultivated form of the species Echinops commutatus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe, characterized by its spiny, spherical flower heads. The plant features silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, bristly hairs. It produces yellow flowers in late summer, which develop into distinctive, spiky seed heads.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
