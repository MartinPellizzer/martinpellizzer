Erythronium × pagodiforme

Erythronium × pagodiforme, commonly known as the pagoda lily, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Colchicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, nodding, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant produces a rosette of heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in a waxy coating. Native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its striking floral display and is relatively easy to care for in garden settings.
