Eschweilera jarana var. latifolia

Eschweilera jarana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its dispersal in the forest canopy.

This species plays a significant role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat due to its structural complexity and support for various fauna.
