Eruca arvensis

Eruca arvensis, commonly known as field rocket, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields.

Eruca arvensis is known for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in salads or as a culinary herb.
