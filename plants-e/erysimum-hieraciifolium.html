Erysimum hieraciifolium

Erysimum hieraciifolium, commonly known as the mouse-ear mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It has heart-shaped or kidney-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum hieraciifolium is an annual plant that blooms from spring to early summer.
