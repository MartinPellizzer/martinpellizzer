Emmotum apogon

Emmotum apogon, commonly known as the swamp verbena, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems and small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in swampy areas and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Emmotum apogon plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
