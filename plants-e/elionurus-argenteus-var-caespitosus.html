Elionurus argenteus var. caespitosus

Elionurus argenteus var. caespitosus, commonly known as the silver-haired grass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its silver-gray foliage.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has a tufted growth habit with narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a compact panicle with spikelets arranged in pairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
