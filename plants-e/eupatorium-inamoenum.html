Eupatorium inamoenum

Eupatorium inamoenum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a fuzzy texture.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
