Eriocaulon humboldtii

Eriocaulon humboldtii, commonly known as the Humboldt's bulbous rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon humboldtii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
