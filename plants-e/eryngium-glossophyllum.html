Eryngium glossophyllum

Eryngium glossophyllum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Eryngium glossophyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
