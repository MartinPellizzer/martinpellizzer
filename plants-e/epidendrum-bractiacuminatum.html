Epidendrum bractiacuminatum

Epidendrum bractiacuminatum, commonly known as the yellow-veined epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, often displaying vibrant yellow and green hues.

Epidendrum bractiacuminatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
