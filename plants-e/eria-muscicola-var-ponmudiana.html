Eria muscicola var. ponmudiana

Eria muscicola var. ponmudiana, commonly known as the Ponmudian orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-yellow blossoms that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, organic-rich substrates for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
