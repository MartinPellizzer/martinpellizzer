Euphorbia nicaeensis var. aurasiaca

Euphorbia nicaeensis var. aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasiaca Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and as a houseplant.
