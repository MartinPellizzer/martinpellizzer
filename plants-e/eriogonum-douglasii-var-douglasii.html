Eriogonum douglasii var. douglasii

Eriogonum douglasii var. douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
