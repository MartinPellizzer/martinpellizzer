Erigeron × wilczekii

Erigeron × wilczekii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and easy maintenance.

Erigeron × wilczekii prefers full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.
