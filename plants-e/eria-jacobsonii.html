Eria jacobsonii

Eria jacobsonii, commonly known as the Jacobson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria jacobsonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
