Ericinella passerinoides

Ericinella passerinoides, commonly known as the common ericinella, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to parts of Europe and North Africa.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky slopes. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are dark green and slightly glossy. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or reddish in color, which bloom in early spring.

Ericinella passerinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
