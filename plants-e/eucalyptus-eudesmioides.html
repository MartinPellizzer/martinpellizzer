Eucalyptus eudesmioides

Eucalyptus eudesmioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee and dry forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
