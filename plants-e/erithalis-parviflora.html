Erithalis parviflora

Erithalis parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered erithalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream sides. The plant features small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
