Euphorbia pugniformis

Euphorbia pugniformis, commonly known as the war club euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and a distinctive, club-like shape that resembles a war club, hence its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia pugniformis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
