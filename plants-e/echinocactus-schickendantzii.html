Echinocactus schickendantzii

Echinocactus schickendantzii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles covered with white, woolly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking contrast to its green body. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor collections.
