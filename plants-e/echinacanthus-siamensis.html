Echinacanthus siamensis

Echinacanthus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Orchidaceae and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
