Epacris alpina

Epacris alpina, commonly known as the alpine epacris, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, often in rocky or heathy habitats. The plant produces spikes of tubular, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It has narrow, linear leaves and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Epacris alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
