Euphorbia salicifolia f. pseudosalicifolia

Euphorbia salicifolia f. pseudosalicifolia, commonly known as the False Willowleaf Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are covered in a thick, waxy cuticle, which helps it retain moisture in arid conditions. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue, resembling the leaves of willow trees. It produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
