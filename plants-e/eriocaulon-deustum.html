Eriocaulon deustum

Eriocaulon deustum, commonly known as the swamp lily, is a small aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or wetlands, with its rhizomes anchoring it to the substrate. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. It is often found in marshy areas and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its botanical structure supports its role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
