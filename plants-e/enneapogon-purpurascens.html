Enneapogon purpurascens

Enneapogon purpurascens, commonly known as the red-stemmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and savannas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Enneapogon purpurascens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
