Elaeagia pastoensis

Elaeagia pastoensis, commonly known as the Pasto beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the family Elaeaginaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and small mammals.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
