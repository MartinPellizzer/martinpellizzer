Erisma costatum var. gracilipes

Erisma costatum var. gracilipes, commonly known as the slender erisma, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant collections.
