Erythrophleum ivorense

Erythrophleum ivorense, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The tree produces woody pods that contain flat, smooth seeds. It is valued for its hard, heavy wood used in furniture and carving.

The plant also plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various wildlife species.
