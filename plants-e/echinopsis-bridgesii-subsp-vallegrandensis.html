Echinopsis bridgesii subsp. vallegrandensis

Echinopsis bridgesii subsp.

vallegrandensis, commonly known as the Valle Grande cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the arid regions of Argentina. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and a dense covering of white, spiny bristles.

The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and conservation efforts.
