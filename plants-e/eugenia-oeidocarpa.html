Eugenia oeidocarpa

Eugenia oeidocarpa, commonly known as the purple-leafed guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark purple leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is small, round, and typically green when mature, though it may turn red or yellow depending on the variety.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in culinary applications.
