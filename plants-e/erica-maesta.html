Erica maesta

Erica maesta, commonly known as the alpine heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Pyrenees and the Alps.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.

Erica maesta is an important component of alpine ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
