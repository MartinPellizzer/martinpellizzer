Erica grandiflora subsp. perfoliosa

Erica grandiflora subsp.

perfoliosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a border plant. Erica grandiflora subsp.

perfoliosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
