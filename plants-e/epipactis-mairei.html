Epipactis mairei

Epipactis mairei, commonly known as Maire's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears a single, showy flower. The orchid produces a distinctive, hooded flower with a lip that is often marked with purple or brown spots. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in shaded forest understories.

Epipactis mairei is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
