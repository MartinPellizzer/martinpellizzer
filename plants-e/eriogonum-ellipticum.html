Eriogonum ellipticum

Eriogonum ellipticum, commonly known as the elliptic buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

Eriogonum ellipticum plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
