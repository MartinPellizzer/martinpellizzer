Eugenia rigidifolia var. genuina

Eugenia rigidifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species native to the humid forests of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers bloom in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
