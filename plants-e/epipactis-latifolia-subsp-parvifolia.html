Epipactis latifolia subsp. parvifolia

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, spurred flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is known for its relatively small flowers compared to other members of the Epipactis genus.
