Eriosema procumbens

Eriosema procumbens, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features sprawling stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. Small, yellow flowers appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.
