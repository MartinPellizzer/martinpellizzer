Eurycoma longifolia var. cochinchinensis

Eurycoma longifolia var. cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Malayan kuching or wild betel, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks. Its roots are valued in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The varietal name *cochinchinensis* refers to its origin in regions such as Vietnam and Cambodia.
