Euodia impellucida var. macrococca

Euodia impellucida var. macrococca, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often elliptic-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries that contain several seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value but can become invasive in certain regions.
