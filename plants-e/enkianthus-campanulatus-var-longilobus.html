Enkianthus campanulatus var. longilobus

Enkianthus campanulatus var. longilobus, commonly known as the long-lobed bellflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
