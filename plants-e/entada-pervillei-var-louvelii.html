Entada pervillei var. louvelii

Entada pervillei var. louvelii, commonly known as the African horned bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped pods. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

The seeds, encased in its elongated pods, are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
