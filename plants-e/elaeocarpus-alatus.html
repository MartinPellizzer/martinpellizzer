Elaeocarpus alatus

Elaeocarpus alatus, commonly known as the winged elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged seeds, which are adapted for wind dispersal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The tree is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
