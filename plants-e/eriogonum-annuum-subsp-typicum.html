Eriogonum annuum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum annuum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the annual buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, disturbed soils. It is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is typically found in open habitats like grasslands, roadsides, and disturbed areas.
