Erica turrigera

Erica turrigera, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica turrigera typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
