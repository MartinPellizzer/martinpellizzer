Eumorphia dregeana

Eumorphia dregeana, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and vibrant red bracts that resemble poker chips. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense spikes, which are often surrounded by the showy red foliage. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Eumorphia dregeana is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
