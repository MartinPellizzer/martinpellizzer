Eranthemum grandiflorum

Eranthemum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are typically pink or red, with five petals and prominent stamens, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
