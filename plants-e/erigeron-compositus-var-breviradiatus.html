Erigeron compositus var. breviradiatus

Erigeron compositus var. breviradiatus, commonly known as the short-rayed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually gray-green and hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
