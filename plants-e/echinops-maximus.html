Echinops maximus

Echinops maximus, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its large, spiny, spherical heads composed of numerous bracts, which give it a unique and eye-catching appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Echinops maximus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
