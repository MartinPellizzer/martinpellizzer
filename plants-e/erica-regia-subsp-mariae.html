Erica regia subsp. mariae

Erica regia subsp.

mariae, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a shrub native to the fynbos vegetation of South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage of small, leathery leaves.

The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
