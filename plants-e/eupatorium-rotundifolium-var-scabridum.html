Eupatorium rotundifolium var. scabridum

Eupatorium rotundifolium var. scabridum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are often hairy and covered in rough, scabrous texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are round or kidney-shaped with serrated edges, and they are covered in fine, stiff hairs.

It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
