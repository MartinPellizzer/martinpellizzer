Eugenia intumescens

Eugenia intumescens, commonly known as the swamp guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
