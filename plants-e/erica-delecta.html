Erica delecta

Erica delecta, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heather family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Erica delecta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
