Epipactis helleborine var. latifolia

Epipactis helleborine var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent lip structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and possesses a complex system of mycorrhizal associations.

Its morphology and reproductive strategies reflect adaptations to its specific ecological niche.
