Elaphoglossum tantalinum

Elaphoglossum tantalinum, commonly known as the Tantalin fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, floating fronds that are often divided into small, delicate segments. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum tantalinum plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
