Eleocharis intermedia var. habereri

Eleocharis intermedia var. habereri, commonly known as the Habereri spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and shallow water bodies. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are often reddish or brownish in color. Its slender, grass-like stems are usually erect, and it has narrow, linear leaves.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
