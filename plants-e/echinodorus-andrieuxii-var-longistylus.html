Echinodorus andrieuxii var. longistylus

Echinodorus andrieuxii var. longistylus, commonly known as the long-styled echinodorus, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically bright green and slightly wavy in appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water surface, often in clusters. It thrives in slow to moderate water currents and requires moderate to high light levels for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in aquariums due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various water conditions.
