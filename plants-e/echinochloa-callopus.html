Echinochloa callopus

Echinochloa callopus, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a loose, open panicle.

Echinochloa callopus plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
