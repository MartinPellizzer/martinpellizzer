Epidendrum cirrhochiloides

Epidendrum cirrhochiloides, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with a reddish or purple lip. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Epidendrum cirrhochiloides is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
