Elymus elongatus subsp. turcicus

Elymus elongatus subsp.

turcicus, commonly known as the Turkish needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, needle-like leaves, which are typically green and slightly hairy.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Elymus elongatus subsp.

turcicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insect species.
