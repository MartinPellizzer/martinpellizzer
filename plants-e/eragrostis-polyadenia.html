Eragrostis polyadenia

Eragrostis polyadenia, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
