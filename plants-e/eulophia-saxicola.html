Eulophia saxicola

Eulophia saxicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pink and purple.

Eulophia saxicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
