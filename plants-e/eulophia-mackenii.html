Eulophia mackenii

Eulophia mackenii, commonly known as the Mackenzie's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white, with a distinctive lip that is often marked with darker pigments.

Eulophia mackenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
