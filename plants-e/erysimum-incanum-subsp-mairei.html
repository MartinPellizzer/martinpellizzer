Erysimum incanum subsp. mairei

Erysimum incanum subsp.

mairei, commonly known as the Maire's wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving the plant a silvery appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
