Eugenia effusa

Eugenia effusa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or Brazilian sandalwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, sweet scent and is used in the production of incense and perfumes.

The plant also produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
