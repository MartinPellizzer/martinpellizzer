Eryngium billardierei var. kotschyi

Eryngium billardierei var. kotschyi, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and heathlands.

This variety is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth compared to other Eryngium species.
