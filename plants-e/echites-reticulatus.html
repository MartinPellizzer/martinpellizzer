Echites reticulatus

Echites reticulatus, commonly known as the nettleleaf echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to its more prominent foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
