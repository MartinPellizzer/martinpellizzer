Erlangea brachycalyx

Erlangea brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx erlangia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or white in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is commonly found in temperate forest regions.
