Eucalyptus × lamprocarpa

Eucalyptus × lamprocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a hybrid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, multi-stemmed shrub or tree form with rough, fibrous bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray coloration. It produces small, globular flower clusters in shades of white or pale yellow, typically during the winter months.

This plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
