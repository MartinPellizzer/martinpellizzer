Eupatorium hintonii

Eupatorium hintonii, commonly known as Hinton's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its stiff, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

Eupatorium hintonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, supporting various pollinators.
