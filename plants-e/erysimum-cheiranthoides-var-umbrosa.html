Erysimum cheiranthoides var. umbrosa

Erysimum cheiranthoides var. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in shaded woodland areas. The plant features basal rosettes of dark green, hairy leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in spring. Its flowers are typically four-petaled and have a slightly fragrant aroma.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to bloom in low-light conditions.
