Elaeocarpus samari

Elaeocarpus samari, commonly known as the Samari elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange to red in color, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
