Eugenia grandis var. lepidocarpa

Eugenia grandis var. lepidocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or lepidocarpa eugenia, is a species native to Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit is used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
