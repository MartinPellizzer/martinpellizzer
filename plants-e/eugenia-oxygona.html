Eugenia oxygona

Eugenia oxygona, commonly known as the Australian white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Eugenia oxygona is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
