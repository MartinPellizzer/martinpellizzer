Eulophia albobrunnea

Eulophia albobrunnea, commonly known as the white-brown eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, upright stem with a few broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces pendulous, greenish-white flowers with yellow and brown markings, typical of the genus Eulophia. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soil conditions.
