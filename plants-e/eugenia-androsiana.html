Eugenia androsiana

Eugenia androsiana, commonly known as the Andros Island hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
