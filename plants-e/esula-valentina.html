Esula valentina

Esula valentina, commonly known as the Valentine's rose, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink and white petals, which bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces fragrant, clustered flowers. Esula valentina thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and floral arrangements.
