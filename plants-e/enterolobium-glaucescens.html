Enterolobium glaucescens

Enterolobium glaucescens, commonly known as the yellow glory pea, is a tropical legume native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with compound leaves and produces showy yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits that encase its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing ability, it is sometimes cultivated for landscaping and agricultural purposes.
