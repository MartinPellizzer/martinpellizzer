Eugenia tekuensis

Eugenia tekuensis, commonly known as the West African myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, hard drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia tekuensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
