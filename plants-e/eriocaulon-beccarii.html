Eriocaulon beccarii

Eriocaulon beccarii, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikegrass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability to waterlogged conditions, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
