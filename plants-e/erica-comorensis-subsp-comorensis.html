Erica comorensis subsp. comorensis

Erica comorensis subsp.

comorensis, commonly known as the Comorian heath, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros archipelago. It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils.

The plant produces small, dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the rainy season. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to the humid tropical climate. Erica comorensis subsp.

comorensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for local wildlife.
