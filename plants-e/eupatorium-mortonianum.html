Eupatorium mortonianum

Eupatorium mortonianum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in umbels.

It is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
