Erodium gruinum f. cyrenaicum

Erodium gruinum f. cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Cyprus catchfly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pinkish or reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its seeds are small and dry, typically dispersed by wind or through animal activity.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.
