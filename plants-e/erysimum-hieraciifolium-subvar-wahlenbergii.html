Erysimum hieraciifolium subvar. wahlenbergii

Erysimum hieraciifolium subvar. wahlenbergii, commonly known as the Wahlenberg's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Scandinavia.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that bear clusters of bright yellow flowers. The leaves are pinnately divided, with small, lance-shaped leaflets that give it a delicate, fern-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
