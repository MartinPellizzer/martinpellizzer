Eupatorium hecatanthum

Eupatorium hecatanthum, commonly known as the great blue lobelia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium hecatanthum thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.
