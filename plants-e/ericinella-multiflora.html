Ericinella multiflora

Ericinella multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered ericinella, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its hardy nature in coastal environments.

Ericinella multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
