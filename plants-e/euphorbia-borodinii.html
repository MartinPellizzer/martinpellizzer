Euphorbia borodinii

Euphorbia borodinii, commonly known as the Borodin's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a distinctive, branched stem that often develops a reddish hue, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its thick, fleshy leaves are adapted to store water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Euphorbia borodinii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
