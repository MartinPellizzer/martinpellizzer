Eccoilopus cotulifer subsp. densiflorus

Eccoilopus cotulifer subsp.

densiflorus, commonly known as the dense-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, and its basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it its subspecies name "densiflorus.".

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
