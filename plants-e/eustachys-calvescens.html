Eustachys calvescens

Eustachys calvescens, commonly known as the coastal panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments due to its tolerance for salt and wind.

Eustachys calvescens plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
