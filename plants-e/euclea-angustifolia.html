Euclea angustifolia

Euclea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Euclea angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
