Elymus puberulus

Elymus puberulus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it its common name, and it typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces tall, slender flowering stems with spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Elymus puberulus is also known to thrive in a variety of soil types and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
