Eupatorium apiculatum

Eupatorium apiculatum, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium apiculatum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
