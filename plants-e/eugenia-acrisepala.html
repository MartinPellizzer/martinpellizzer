Eugenia acrisepala

Eugenia acrisepala, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release small, shiny seeds.

Eugenia acrisepala is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
