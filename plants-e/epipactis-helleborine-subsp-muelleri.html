Epipactis helleborine subsp. muelleri

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a tall, erect flowering stem that bears a single, showy greenish-yellow flower.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial species that produces a pseudobulb and has a complex system of roots.

The orchid is notable for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its unique floral structure.
