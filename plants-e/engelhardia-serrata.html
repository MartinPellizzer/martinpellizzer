Engelhardia serrata

Engelhardia serrata, commonly known as the serrated engelhardia, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody capsules containing its seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
