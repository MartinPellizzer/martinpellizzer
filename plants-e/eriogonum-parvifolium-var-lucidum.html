Eriogonum parvifolium var. lucidum

Eriogonum parvifolium var. lucidum, commonly known as the golden foothills buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a shiny, waxy surface.

This variety is part of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and knotweed.
