Escobaria vivipara var. kaibabensis

Escobaria vivipara var. kaibabensis, commonly known as the Kaibab liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. It reproduces vegetatively through offsets and can also produce live seeds, a trait that contributes to its survival in harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
