Elaeagnus glabra subsp. oxyphylla

Elaeagnus glabra subsp.

oxyphylla, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and Australia. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, dark berries that are edible but not particularly palatable to humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny environments. Elaeagnus glabra subsp.

oxyphylla is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological contexts.
