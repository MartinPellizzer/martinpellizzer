Eryngium radiciflorum

Eryngium radiciflorum, commonly known as the rootflower thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and robust, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Eryngium radiciflorum is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.
