Eugenia minima

Eugenia minima, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 meter and features dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a preference for well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia minima is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
