Eugenia sigillata

Eugenia sigillata, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia sigillata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also valued for its essential oils.
