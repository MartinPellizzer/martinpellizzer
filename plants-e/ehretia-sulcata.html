Ehretia sulcata

Ehretia sulcata, commonly known as the roughleaf ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, raised dots, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
