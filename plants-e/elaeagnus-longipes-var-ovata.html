Elaeagnus longipes var. ovata

Elaeagnus longipes var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate silverberry, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery, finely hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible berries that ripen in the fall. It is adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy species suitable for arid environments.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
