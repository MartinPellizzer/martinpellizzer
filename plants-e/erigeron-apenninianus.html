Erigeron apenninianus

Erigeron apenninianus, commonly known as the Apennine fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly the Apennine Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
