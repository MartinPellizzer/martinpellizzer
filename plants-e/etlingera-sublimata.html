Etlingera sublimata

Etlingera sublimata, commonly known as the flame ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a torch, with bright red and orange flowers. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a glossy texture, adding to its ornamental value.

Etlingera sublimata is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
