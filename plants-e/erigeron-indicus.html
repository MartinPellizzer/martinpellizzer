Erigeron indicus

Erigeron indicus, commonly known as the Indian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the summer.

Erigeron indicus is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
