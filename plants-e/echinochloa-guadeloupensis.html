Echinochloa guadeloupensis

Echinochloa guadeloupensis, commonly known as Guadeloupe panicgrass, is a species of grass native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland and coastal areas, thriving in moist, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
