Eucalyptus sparsicoma

Eucalyptus sparsicoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a member of the Myrtaceae family native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its gnarled, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and fire resilience.

Eucalyptus sparsicoma plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
