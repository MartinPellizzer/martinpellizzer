Eryngium aquaticum

Eryngium aquaticum, commonly known as water thistle, is a perennial aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It thrives in wetland environments, including ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features erect, spiny stems and basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in a spherical shape, and bloom from late summer to autumn.

Eryngium aquaticum plays an important role in supporting aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and waterfowl.
