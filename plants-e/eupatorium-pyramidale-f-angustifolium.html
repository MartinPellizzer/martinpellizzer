Eupatorium pyramidale f. angustifolium

Eupatorium pyramidale f. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The leaves are narrow and linear, often with a hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
