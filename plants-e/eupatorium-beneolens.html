Eupatorium beneolens

Eupatorium beneolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a sweet, honey-like scent when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Eupatorium beneolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
