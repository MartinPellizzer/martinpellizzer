Eriosyce chilensis var. albidiflora

Eriosyce chilensis var. albidiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to Chile.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense, spiny stems that help it retain water in arid environments. The plant produces white flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
