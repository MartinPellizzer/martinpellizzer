Euphorbia repetita

Euphorbia repetita, commonly known as the clustered spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Euphorbia repetita is also noted for its ability to propagate through both seeds and stem cuttings.
