Echinops gedrosiacus var. gedrosiacus

Echinops gedrosiacus var. gedrosiacus, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog, which give it its genus name "Echinops.". The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
