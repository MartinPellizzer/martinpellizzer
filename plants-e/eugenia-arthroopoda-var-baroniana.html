Eugenia arthroopoda var. baroniana

Eugenia arthroopoda var. baroniana, commonly known as the Baroni's guava, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, yellowish berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruit.
