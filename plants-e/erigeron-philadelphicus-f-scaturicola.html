Erigeron philadelphicus f. scaturicola

Erigeron philadelphicus f. scaturicola, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. Its flowers are usually blue to violet in color, with white ray florets, and bloom in late summer to early fall.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
