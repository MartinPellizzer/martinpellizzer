Euphorbia leucoloma

Euphorbia leucoloma, commonly known as the white-spotted spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with distinctive white spots and a milky sap that is toxic. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
