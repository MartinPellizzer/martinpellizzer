Epacris longiflora

Epacris longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple and are borne in dense spikes, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
