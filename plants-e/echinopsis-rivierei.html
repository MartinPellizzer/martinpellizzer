Echinopsis rivierei

Echinopsis rivierei, commonly known as the Riviere's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stems and is typically found in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces striking white or pink flowers during the warmer months. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
