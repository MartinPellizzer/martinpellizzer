Eutrochium dubium

Eutrochium dubium, commonly known as rough horsemint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Eutrochium dubium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it of interest in traditional herbal practices.
