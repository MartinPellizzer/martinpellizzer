Eupatorium mancoanum

Eupatorium mancoanum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium mancoanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
