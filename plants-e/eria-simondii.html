Eria simondii

Eria simondii, commonly known as the Indian orchid or Simond's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its roots are fibrous and often grow in the shade of tropical forests.

Eria simondii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
