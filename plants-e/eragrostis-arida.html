Eragrostis arida

Eragrostis arida, commonly known as the desert lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Eragrostis arida is often found in open woodlands, grasslands, and along sandy or rocky soils.
