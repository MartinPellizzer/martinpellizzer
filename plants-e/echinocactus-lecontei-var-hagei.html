Echinocactus lecontei var. hagei

Echinocactus lecontei var. hagei, commonly known as the Hagei Hedgehog Cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It features a globular shape with a ribbed stem and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It has shallow roots that allow it to thrive in rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
