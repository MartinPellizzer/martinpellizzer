Eupatorium subvenosum

Eupatorium subvenosum, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded or partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium subvenosum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
