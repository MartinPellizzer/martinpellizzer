Eleocharis brittonii

Eleocharis brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown in color. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or saturated soils.

Eleocharis brittonii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
