Echeveria × calva

Echeveria × calva, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray or green in color. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its compact form.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
