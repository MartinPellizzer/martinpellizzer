Eucalyptus viminalis var. baeuerlenii

Eucalyptus viminalis var. baeuerlenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leaved mallees species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which appear during the warmer months. Its bark is often flaky and peels off in thin layers, revealing a pale, smooth underbark.

This variant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
