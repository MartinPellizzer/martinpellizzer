Euploca calcicola

Euploca calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola euploca, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glandular leaves adapted to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy plains.
