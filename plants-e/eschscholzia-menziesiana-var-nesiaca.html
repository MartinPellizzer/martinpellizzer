Eschscholzia menziesiana var. nesiaca

Eschscholzia menziesiana var. nesiaca, commonly known as the Nez Perce poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange petals and a distinctive, narrow leaf shape. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous small, elliptical seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and historical significance to indigenous communities.
