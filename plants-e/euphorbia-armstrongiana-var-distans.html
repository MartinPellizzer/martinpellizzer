Euphorbia armstrongiana var. distans

Euphorbia armstrongiana var. distans, commonly known as the Armstrong's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem system with thick, fleshy stems that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the specialized flower-like structures of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

It is drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
