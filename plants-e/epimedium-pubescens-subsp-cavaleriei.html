Epimedium pubescens subsp. cavaleriei

Epimedium pubescens subsp.

cavaleriei, commonly known as the Chinese barrenwort, is a perennial herb native to China. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are clustered in upright inflorescences. Its leaves are typically leathery, ovate, and have a distinctive pubescent texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to woodland gardens.
