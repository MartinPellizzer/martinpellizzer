Entada rheedei

Entada rheedei, commonly known as the African tamarind or elephant tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous, edible pulp. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a broad, umbrella-like canopy. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction, as well as for its medicinal properties.
