Eupatorium pellucidum

Eupatorium pellucidum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, translucent hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium pellucidum is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
