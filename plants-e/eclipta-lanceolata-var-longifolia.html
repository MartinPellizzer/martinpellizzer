Eclipta lanceolata var. longifolia

Eclipta lanceolata var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish to white composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, and it often grows in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
