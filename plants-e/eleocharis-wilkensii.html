Eleocharis wilkensii

Eleocharis wilkensii, commonly known as the Wilkens' spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences and is typically found in wetland habitats. The plant has slender, leaf-like stems that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments, such as marshes and pond margins.

Eleocharis wilkensii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
