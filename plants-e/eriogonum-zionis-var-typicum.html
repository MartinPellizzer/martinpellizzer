Eriogonum zionis var. typicum

Eriogonum zionis var. typicum, commonly known as the Zion's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It has small, lance-shaped leaves and is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native range.
