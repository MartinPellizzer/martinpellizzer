Erythroxylum borneense

Erythroxylum borneense, commonly known as Bornean copal, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It is often found in lowland and hill forest environments, thriving in humid and shaded conditions.

Due to its ecological significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
