Eugenia alata

Eugenia alata, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Eugenia alata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
