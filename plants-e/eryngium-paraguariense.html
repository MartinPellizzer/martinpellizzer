Eryngium paraguariense

Eryngium paraguariense, commonly known as the Paraguayan thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
