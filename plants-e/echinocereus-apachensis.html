Echinocereus apachensis

Echinocereus apachensis, commonly known as the Apache Hedgehog Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines, which help to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
