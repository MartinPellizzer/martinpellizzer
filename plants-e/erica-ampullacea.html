Erica ampullacea

Erica ampullacea, commonly known as the Cape heath or red heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically red or pink in color, which bloom in spring and summer. Erica ampullacea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
