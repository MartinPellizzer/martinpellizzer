Eupomatia

Eupomatia, commonly known as the false holly, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Euphorbiaceae.

These plants are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands. Eupomatia species are typically shrubs or small trees with simple, opposite leaves. The flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, and the plants are often used in traditional medicine.
