Erysimum accedens

Erysimum accedens, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Erysimum accedens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
