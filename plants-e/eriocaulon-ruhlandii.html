Eriocaulon ruhlandii

Eriocaulon ruhlandii, commonly known as the Ruhland's spike-moss, is a small, flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for studies on plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
