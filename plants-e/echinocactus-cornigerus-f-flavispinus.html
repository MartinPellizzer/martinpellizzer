Echinocactus cornigerus f. flavispinus

Echinocactus cornigerus f. flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in thick, spiny areoles, which are often adorned with bright yellow bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
