Eriogonum cernuum var. umbraticum

Eriogonum cernuum var. umbraticum, commonly known as the shadowy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette, which helps conserve moisture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
