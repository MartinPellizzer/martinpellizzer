Eriosyce eriosyzoides var. carrizalensis

Eriosyce eriosyzoides var. carrizalensis, commonly known as the Carrizalensis Pincushion Cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny clusters of stems that resemble a pincushion, which help it retain water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a touch of vibrancy to its otherwise muted appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for storing water, allowing it to survive in low-rainfall conditions.

This variety is often found growing in rocky soils and is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
