Erica protrudens

Erica protrudens, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub typically grows in sandy or acidic soils and thrives in coastal environments. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
