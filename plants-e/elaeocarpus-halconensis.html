Elaeocarpus halconensis

Elaeocarpus halconensis, commonly known as the Halcon Island elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
