Erica caffrorum var. glomerata

Erica caffrorum var. glomerata, commonly known as the Cape heath or mountain heath, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscaping.
