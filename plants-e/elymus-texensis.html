Elymus texensis

Elymus texensis, commonly known as Texas needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Elymus texensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
