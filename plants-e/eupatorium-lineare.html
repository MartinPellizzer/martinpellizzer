Eupatorium lineare

Eupatorium lineare, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Eupatorium lineare is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
