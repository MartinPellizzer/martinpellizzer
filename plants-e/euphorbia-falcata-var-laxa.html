Euphorbia falcata var. laxa

Euphorbia falcata var. laxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh conditions make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
