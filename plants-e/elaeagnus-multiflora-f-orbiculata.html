Elaeagnus multiflora f. orbiculata

Elaeagnus multiflora f. orbiculata, commonly known as the Japanese silverberry, is a compact shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are silver-white on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
