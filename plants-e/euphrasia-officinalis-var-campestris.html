Euphrasia officinalis var. campestris

Euphrasia officinalis var. campestris, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, delicate flowers arranged in a spike.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
