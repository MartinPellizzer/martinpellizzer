Eugenia leucoxylon

Eugenia leucoxylon, commonly known as the white-myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark blue to black berries.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.
