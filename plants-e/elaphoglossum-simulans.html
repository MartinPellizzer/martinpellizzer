Elaphoglossum simulans

Elaphoglossum simulans, commonly known as the mimic holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a small, delicate fern characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and woodlands. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori.

Elaphoglossum simulans is often found in the understory of tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in the ecological dynamics of these ecosystems.
