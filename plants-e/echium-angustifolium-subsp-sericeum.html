Echium angustifolium subsp. sericeum

Echium angustifolium subsp.

sericeum, commonly known as the silverweed or velvetplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces striking spikes of violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its unique foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
