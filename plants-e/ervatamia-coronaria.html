Ervatamia coronaria

Ervatamia coronaria, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a deep root system, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for erosion control. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
