Eryngium bocconei

Eryngium bocconei, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a short, upright stem with bracts that resemble a crown, giving it a distinctive appearance. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is adapted to arid environments.

Eryngium bocconei is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and colorful inflorescences.
