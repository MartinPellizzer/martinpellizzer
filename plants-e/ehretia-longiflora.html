Ehretia longiflora

Ehretia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered ehretia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed drupe that turns red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
