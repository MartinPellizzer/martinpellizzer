Eulophia holubii

Eulophia holubii, commonly known as the Holub's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves, typical of many orchid species. The plant produces intricate, showy inflorescences with colorful, lip-like petals that attract pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
