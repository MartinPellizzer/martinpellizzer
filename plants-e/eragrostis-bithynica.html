Eragrostis bithynica

Eragrostis bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and it is often found in disturbed or semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis bithynica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
