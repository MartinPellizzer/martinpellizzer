Euphoria attenuata

Euphoria attenuata, commonly known as the lesser euphorbia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Euphoria attenuata is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
