Erythroxylum eligulatum

Erythroxylum eligulatum, commonly known as the slender copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions of Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often grown for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its role in local ecosystems and potential medicinal uses.
