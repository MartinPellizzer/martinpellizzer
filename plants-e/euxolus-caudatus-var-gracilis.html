Euxolus caudatus var. gracilis

Euxolus caudatus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender cat's-tail, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant features slender, hairy stems and is often found in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
