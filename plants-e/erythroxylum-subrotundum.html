Erythroxylum subrotundum

Erythroxylum subrotundum, commonly known as the purple coca, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically elliptic to ovate in shape, with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface.

The plant is known for its potential medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Andean cultures for various purposes.
