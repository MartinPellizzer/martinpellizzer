Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. ellipticus

Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
