Ephedra ma-huang

Ephedra ma-huang, commonly known as Ma Huang, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and elongated, woody stems. The plant contains alkaloids such as ephedrine and pseudoephedrine, which have been used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its botanical features include a branching structure and the ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant's chemical composition has made it both medicinally significant and subject to regulatory restrictions.
