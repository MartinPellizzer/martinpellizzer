Erica retusa

Erica retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Erica retusa produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often blooming in spring.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
