Evolvulus elegans var. capillaceus

Evolvulus elegans var. capillaceus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are often hairy and can produce a milky sap. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
