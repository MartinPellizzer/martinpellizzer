Echinopsis gladispina

Echinopsis gladispina, commonly known as the Gladis's cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to yellow spines. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It is a succulent plant, capable of storing water in its fleshy stems to survive in arid environments.

Echinopsis gladispina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
