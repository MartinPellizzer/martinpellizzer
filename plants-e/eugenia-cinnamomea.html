Eugenia cinnamomea

Eugenia cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or cinnamon gum, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in the production of cinnamon essential oil.

The species is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique fragrance.
