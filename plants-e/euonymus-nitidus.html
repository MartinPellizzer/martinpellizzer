Euonymus nitidus

Euonymus nitidus, commonly known as the glossy euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is often used in gardens and as a hedge plant.
