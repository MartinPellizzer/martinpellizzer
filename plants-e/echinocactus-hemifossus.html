Echinocactus hemifossus

Echinocactus hemifossus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, ribbed structure that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its spines are short and often curved, providing protection against herbivores.

Echinocactus hemifossus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
