Eugenia gracilifolia

Eugenia gracilifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia gracilifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
