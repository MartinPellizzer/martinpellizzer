Erythrina variegata f. parcellii

Erythrina variegata f. parcellii, commonly known as the variegated coral tree, is a cultivar of the coral tree species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, which add visual interest to its foliage. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with a dense, rounded form.

The leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several leaflets arranged along a central stem.
