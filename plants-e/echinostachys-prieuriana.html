Echinostachys prieuriana

Echinostachys prieuriana, commonly known as the white ghost orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated inflorescence that bears numerous white, ghost-like flowers. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on the surface of trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers are pollinated by specific insects, and it requires a humid, shaded environment to thrive.

Due to habitat loss and its elusive nature, it is considered a conservation concern.
