Emilia decipiens

Emilia decipiens, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy white seeds. Emilia decipiens is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
