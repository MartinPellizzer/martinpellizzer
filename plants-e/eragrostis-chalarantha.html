Eragrostis chalarantha

Eragrostis chalarantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance.

The botanical features of Eragrostis chalarantha make it a valuable component of native vegetation in its native range.
