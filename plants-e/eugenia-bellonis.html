Eugenia bellonis

Eugenia bellonis, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. Eugenia bellonis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mildly acidic flavor in its fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
