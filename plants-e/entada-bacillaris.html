Entada bacillaris

Entada bacillaris, commonly known as the African hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae (Fabaceae) family and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, elongated pods. The plant is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for construction and carving. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The tree also plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
