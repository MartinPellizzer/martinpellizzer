Eugenia tiumanensis

Eugenia tiumanensis, commonly known as the Tiuman guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aromatic scent when crushed.
