Euchaetis cristagalli

Euchaetis cristagalli, commonly known as the cristate euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its distinctive crown-like structure formed by densely packed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
