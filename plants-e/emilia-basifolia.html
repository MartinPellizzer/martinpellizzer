Emilia basifolia

Emilia basifolia, commonly known as the Mexican daisy, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Emilia basifolia typically grows in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal remedies.
