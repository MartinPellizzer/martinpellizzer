Eragrostis secunda

Eragrostis secunda, commonly known as silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle with spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues.

Eragrostis secunda is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
