Erythrina nahasuta

Erythrina nahasuta, commonly known as the Indian coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptical leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish hue. Erythrina nahasuta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
