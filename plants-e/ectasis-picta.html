Ectasis picta

Ectasis picta, commonly known as the painted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including purple, blue, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ectasis picta is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
