Eugenia raran

Eugenia raran, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Eugenia raran is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile addition to tropical gardens.
