Eriogonum multiceps

Eriogonum multiceps, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats, often found in mountainous regions. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Eriogonum multiceps plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is a common sight in high-elevation ecosystems.
