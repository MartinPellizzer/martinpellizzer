Eutrema edwardsii var. heterophyllum

Eutrema edwardsii var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the coastal fescue, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a perennial grass with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand salty air and strong coastal winds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various coastal organisms.
