Euodia tietaensis

Euodia tietaensis, commonly known as the Brazilian lauraceae, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Euodia tietaensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and supporting local wildlife.
