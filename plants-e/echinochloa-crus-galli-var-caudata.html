Echinochloa crus-galli var. caudata

Echinochloa crus-galli var. caudata, commonly known as cockspur grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and distinctive, bristly seed heads that resemble a cock's spur. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and competitive nature make it a common weed in various ecosystems.
