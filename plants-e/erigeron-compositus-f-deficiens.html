Erigeron compositus f. deficiens

Erigeron compositus f. deficiens, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
