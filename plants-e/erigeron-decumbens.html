Erigeron decumbens

Erigeron decumbens, commonly known as the creeping fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its adaptability and is sometimes used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
