Erica carnea subsp. mediterranea

Erica carnea subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, typically blooming in spring.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are gray-green in color, providing an attractive evergreen structure. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
