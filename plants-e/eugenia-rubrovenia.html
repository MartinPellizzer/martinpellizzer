Eugenia rubrovenia

Eugenia rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when ripe, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
