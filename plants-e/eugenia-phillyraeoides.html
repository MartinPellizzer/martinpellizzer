Eugenia phillyraeoides

Eugenia phillyraeoides, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

Eugenia phillyraeoides is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value.
