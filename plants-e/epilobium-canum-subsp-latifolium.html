Epilobium canum subsp. latifolium

Epilobium canum subsp.

latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features upright stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tip. Its flowers are pink to reddish, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer.

It thrives in moist, often calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
