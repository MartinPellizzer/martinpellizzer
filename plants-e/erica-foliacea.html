Erica foliacea

Erica foliacea, commonly known as the leafy heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This shrub typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white, blooming in spring.

Erica foliacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
