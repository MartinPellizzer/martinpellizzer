Epidendrum upanodifforme

Epidendrum upanodifforme, commonly known as the Mexican flame orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Mexico.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
