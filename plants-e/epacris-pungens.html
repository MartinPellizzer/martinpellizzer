Epacris pungens

Epacris pungens, commonly known as the spiked epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, are pollinated by nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Epacris pungens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable conditions.
