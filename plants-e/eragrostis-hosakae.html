Eragrostis hosakae

Eragrostis hosakae, commonly known as the Hosaka's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to golden florets. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue, which helps reduce water loss.

Eragrostis hosakae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
