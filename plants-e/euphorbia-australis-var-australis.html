Euphorbia australis var. australis

Euphorbia australis var. australis, commonly known as the Australian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features upright stems covered in soft, glandular hairs, and its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Euphorbia australis var. australis is adapted to dry conditions and can tolerate salt spray, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
