Erigeron acris subsp. staintonii

Erigeron acris subsp.

staintonii, commonly known as the Stainton's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with a silvery-blue hue.

The plant typically grows in open, grassy habitats and has a low-growing, spreading habit. Its flowers are daisy-like, with white ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers.

It is often found in meadows and along the edges of woodland areas.
