Echinocactus myriostigma var. quadricostatus

Echinocactus myriostigma var. quadricostatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, barrel-shaped stem that is covered in bright yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red fruits containing seeds. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
