Elegia propinqua var. equisetacea

Elegia propinqua var. equisetacea, commonly known as the slender water starwort, is a rare aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich substrates. The plant's morphology is adapted for water survival, with reduced leaves that minimize resistance to water flow. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a sensitive indicator of water quality and ecological health.
