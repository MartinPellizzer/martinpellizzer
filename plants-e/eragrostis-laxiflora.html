Eragrostis laxiflora

Eragrostis laxiflora, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are loosely arranged in panicles, giving it a loose, airy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate, grass-like foliage.
