Elionurus latiflorus var. adustus

Elionurus latiflorus var. adustus, commonly known as the ash-gray mallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy, pale pink to lavender flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are broad and often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer that helps reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
