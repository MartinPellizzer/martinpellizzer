Epidendrum nicaraguense

Epidendrum nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces striking, long-lasting flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, often arranged in racemes. Its roots are adapted for epiphytic growth, allowing it to thrive on tree trunks or in rocky crevices.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens and greenhouses.
