Erigeron glabratus var. occidentalis

Erigeron glabratus var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, and its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
