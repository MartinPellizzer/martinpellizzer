Escallonia mandonii

Escallonia mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has a compact, rounded form. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Escallonia mandonii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
