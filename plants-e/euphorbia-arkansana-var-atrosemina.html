Euphorbia arkansana var. atrosemina

Euphorbia arkansana var. atrosemina, commonly known as the Atrosemine Spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
