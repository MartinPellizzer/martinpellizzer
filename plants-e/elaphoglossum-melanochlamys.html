Elaphoglossum melanochlamys

Elaphoglossum melanochlamys, commonly known as the black-chaliced holly fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves with a dark, almost black coloration on the underside. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with small, rounded pinnae that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it can receive indirect light.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
