Eucalyptus salicifolia var. hypericifolia

Eucalyptus salicifolia var. hypericifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from pale yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

This variant is part of the Eucalyptus genus, which is known for its ecological and economic significance in Australia.
