Ervum bithynicum

Ervum bithynicum, commonly known as the Bithynian vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid conditions. Its seeds are enclosed in pods, a typical feature of the Fabaceae family.

Ervum bithynicum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, contributing to soil fertility.
