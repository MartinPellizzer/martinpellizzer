Elytraria tridentata

Elytraria tridentata, commonly known as the three-toothed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny florets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in its native habitats.

Elytraria tridentata is notable for its ecological role in preventing erosion and providing habitat for various small organisms.
