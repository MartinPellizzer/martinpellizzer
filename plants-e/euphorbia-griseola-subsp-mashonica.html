Euphorbia griseola subsp. mashonica

Euphorbia griseola subsp.

mashonica, commonly known as the Mashonaland euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features grayish-green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick stems and leaves.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
