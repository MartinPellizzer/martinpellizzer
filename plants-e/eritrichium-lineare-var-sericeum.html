Eritrichium lineare var. sericeum

Eritrichium lineare var. sericeum, commonly known as the silky blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are narrow and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant a distinctive, soft appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
