Erianthus velutinus

Erianthus velutinus, commonly known as velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs that cover the leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Erianthus velutinus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
