Epipactis halacsyi subsp. degenii

Epipactis halacsyi subsp.

degenii, commonly known as the Degen's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It is a perennial herb that produces a single stem bearing several flowers.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its preference for well-drained, humus-rich soils.
