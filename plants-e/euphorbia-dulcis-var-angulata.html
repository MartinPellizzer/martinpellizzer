Euphorbia dulcis var. angulata

Euphorbia dulcis var. angulata, commonly known as the angular milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are often angular in shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
