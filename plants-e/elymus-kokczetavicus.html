Elymus kokczetavicus

Elymus kokczetavicus, commonly known as the Kokczetav grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, adapted to arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals in its native range.
