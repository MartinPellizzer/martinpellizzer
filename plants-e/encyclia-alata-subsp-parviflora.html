Encyclia alata subsp. parviflora

Encyclia alata subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved nightshade orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom at night.

The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers emit a faint fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
