Eupatorium stillingiifolium

Eupatorium stillingiifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
