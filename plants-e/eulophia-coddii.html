Eulophia coddii

Eulophia coddii, commonly known as the Codd's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its leaves and flowering spikes. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia coddii is valued for its ornamental appeal and contributes to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
