Euphorbia segetalis var. taurica

Euphorbia segetalis var. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides. The plant features a stout, upright stem covered with small, greenish-yellow leaves. Its most distinctive feature is the production of unique, cup-shaped cyathia, which contain the small, yellowish flowers.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
