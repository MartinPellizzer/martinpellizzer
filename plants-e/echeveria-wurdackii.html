Echeveria wurdackii

Echeveria wurdackii, commonly known as the Wurdack's echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped foliage composed of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish hue. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces striking clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or containers due to its compact size and low water requirements.
