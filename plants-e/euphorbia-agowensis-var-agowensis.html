Euphorbia agowensis var. agowensis

Euphorbia agowensis var. agowensis, commonly known as the Agow euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
