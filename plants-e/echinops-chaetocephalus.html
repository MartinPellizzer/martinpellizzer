Echinops chaetocephalus

Echinops chaetocephalus, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Echinops chaetocephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
