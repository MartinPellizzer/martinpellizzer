Epilobium darreri

Epilobium darreri, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pinkish to purple flowers are clustered in loose racemes and bloom in the spring.

Epilobium darreri is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insect species.
