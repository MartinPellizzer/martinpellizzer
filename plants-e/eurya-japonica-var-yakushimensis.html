Eurya japonica var. yakushimensis

Eurya japonica var. yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often densely packed on the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
