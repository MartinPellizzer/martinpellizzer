Eremostachys nerimani

Eremostachys nerimani, commonly known as the desert bellflower, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-adapted to desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic ecosystems.
