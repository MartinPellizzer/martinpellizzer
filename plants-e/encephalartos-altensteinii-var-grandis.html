Encephalartos altensteinii var. grandis

Encephalartos altensteinii var. grandis, commonly known as the giant cycad, is a rare and endangered cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, robust leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length, arranged in a tight rosette. The plant produces a tall, slender stem with a single, large, spiky cone, which is a distinctive feature of the genus. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and requires a subtropical climate with high humidity.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is critically endangered and protected in its native range.
