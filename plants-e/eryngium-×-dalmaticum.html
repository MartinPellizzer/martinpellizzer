Eryngium × dalmaticum

Eryngium × dalmaticum, commonly known as the Dalmatian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistle blooms. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often considered invasive in parts of North America due to its rapid spread and competitive growth.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though care must be taken to prevent its escape into natural habitats.
