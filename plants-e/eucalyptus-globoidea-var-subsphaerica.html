Eucalyptus globoidea var. subsphaerica

Eucalyptus globoidea var. subsphaerica, commonly known as the globular eucalypt, is a distinctive species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its spherical or globular shape, with thick, smooth bark and small, rounded leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically clustered in umbels. Its unique morphology makes it a popular ornamental plant, often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.

The species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have various industrial and medicinal applications.
