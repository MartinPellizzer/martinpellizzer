Elaeocarpus elaeagnoides

Elaeocarpus elaeagnoides, commonly known as the silverleaf elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
