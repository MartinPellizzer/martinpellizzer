Eulophia petiolata

Eulophia petiolata, commonly known as the slender-leafed eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are usually borne in clusters, displaying a range of colors from white to pink, and are characterized by their intricate, showy structure.
