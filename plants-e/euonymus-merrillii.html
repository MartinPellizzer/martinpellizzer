Euonymus merrillii

Euonymus merrillii, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the fall. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a red underside. It produces yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds.

Euonymus merrillii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
