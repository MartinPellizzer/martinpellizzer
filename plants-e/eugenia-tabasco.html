Eugenia tabasco

Eugenia tabasco, commonly known as the Tabasco holly, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, cranberry-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia tabasco is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
