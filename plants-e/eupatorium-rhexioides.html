Eupatorium rhexioides

Eupatorium rhexioides, commonly known as rough blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are small, blue to purple, and arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters.

Eupatorium rhexioides typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands and along stream banks.
