Eriastrum sapphirinum subsp. dasyanthum

Eriastrum sapphirinum subsp.

dasyanthum, commonly known as the blue flax or Sierra blue flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
