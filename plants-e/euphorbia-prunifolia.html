Euphorbia prunifolia

Euphorbia prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a woody stem with a distinctive grayish-green color and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Euphorbia prunifolia is also known for its milky sap, which can be irritant to skin.
