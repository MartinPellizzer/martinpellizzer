Erigeron acris subsp. phaeocephalus

Erigeron acris subsp.

phaeocephalus, commonly known as the palehead fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small and usually pale in color.
