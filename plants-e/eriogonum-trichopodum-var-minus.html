Eriogonum trichopodum var. minus

Eriogonum trichopodum var. minus, commonly known as the desert gourd, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a spherical, green capsule that turns brown when mature, containing several seeds.
