Eragrostis infecunda

Eragrostis infecunda, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its tolerance to poor soil quality.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates.
