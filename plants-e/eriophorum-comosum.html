Eriophorum comosum

Eriophorum comosum, commonly known as cotton grass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the tips of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs, marshes, and heathlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming dense clumps near the ground.

Eriophorum comosum plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
