Eupatorium sinuatum var. desportesii

Eupatorium sinuatum var. desportesii, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, lavender-pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely cut, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
