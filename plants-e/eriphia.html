Eriphia

Eriphia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround the flower heads. The plant is native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern coast of the United States. Eriphia thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in maritime environments.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant valued for its unique appearance and hardiness.
