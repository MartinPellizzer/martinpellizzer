Eupatorium inconspicuum

Eupatorium inconspicuum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-violet flowers that appear in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairies.

Eupatorium inconspicuum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
