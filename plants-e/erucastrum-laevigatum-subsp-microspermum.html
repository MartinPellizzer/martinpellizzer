Erucastrum laevigatum subsp. microspermum

Erucastrum laevigatum subsp.

microspermum, commonly known as the slender bladderwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, bladder-like structures that trap air and aid in buoyancy.

The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and ditches. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
