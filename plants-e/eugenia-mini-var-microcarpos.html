Eugenia mini var. microcarpos

Eugenia mini var. microcarpos, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. The plant produces tiny, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and compact size, it is often used in horticulture for landscaping and container gardening.
