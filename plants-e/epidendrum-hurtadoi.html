Epidendrum hurtadoi

Epidendrum hurtadoi, commonly known as the Orange-Flowered Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers, which bloom in racemes along the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a waxy texture to retain moisture.

Epidendrum hurtadoi is prized for its vibrant floral display and is relatively easy to care for in cultivation.
