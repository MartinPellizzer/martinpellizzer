Eichhornia paradoxa

Eichhornia paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradox Water Hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It possesses broad, waxy leaves that float on the water's surface, with long, thin roots hanging beneath. The plant produces small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to water bodies. It is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense coverage of water surfaces.

Eichhornia paradoxa is often used in water gardens and for biological wastewater treatment due to its ability to absorb nutrients.
