Elatostema oppositifolium

Elatostema oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaf elatostema, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color. It produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous minute seeds.

Elatostema oppositifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
