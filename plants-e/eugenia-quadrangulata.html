Eugenia quadrangulata

Eugenia quadrangulata, commonly known as the four-angle eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its quadrangular, ridged stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
