Eriogonum flavum var. caudiciferum

Eriogonum flavum var. caudiciferum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and a thick, woody stem base, which is a distinguishing feature of the varietal name "caudiciferum.". The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in the spring.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and is an important plant in local ecosystems.
