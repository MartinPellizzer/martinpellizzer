Eurybia argophylla

Eurybia argophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves. The plant produces yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Eurybia argophylla is often found in open woodlands and prairies, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
