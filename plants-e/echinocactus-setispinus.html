Echinocactus setispinus

Echinocactus setispinus, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is ribbed and covered with prominent, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant's unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
