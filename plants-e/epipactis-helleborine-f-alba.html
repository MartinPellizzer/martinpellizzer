Epipactis helleborine f. alba

Epipactis helleborine f. alba, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green to white flowers, which are typically arranged in a loose spike. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy surface.

It is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its ability to grow in nutrient-poor soils.
