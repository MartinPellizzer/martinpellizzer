Eremurus micranthus

Eremurus micranthus, commonly known as the desert candle, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like flower stalks that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to arid and rocky environments.

Eremurus micranthus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
