Eugenia itaunensis

Eugenia itaunensis, commonly known as the Itaunense Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. It produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
