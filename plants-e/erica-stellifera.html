Erica stellifera

Erica stellifera, commonly known as the star heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica stellifera produces small, dark blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
