Eugenia grandifolia

Eugenia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Following flowering, it develops small, round, red or purple berries that are edible for some bird species.

Eugenia grandifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
