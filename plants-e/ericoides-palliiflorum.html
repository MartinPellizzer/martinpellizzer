Ericoides palliiflorum

Ericoides palliiflorum, commonly known as the paleflower heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa, particularly in Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of pale pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation areas.
