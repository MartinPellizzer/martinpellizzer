Eucalyptus transcontinentalis subsp. semivestita

Eucalyptus transcontinentalis subsp.

semivestita, commonly known as the Transcontinental mallee, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually glaucous beneath.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
