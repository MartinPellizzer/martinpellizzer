Erysimum hieraciifolium var. ramosum

Erysimum hieraciifolium var. ramosum, commonly known as the ramose wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are pinnately divided, with a finely toothed margin, giving the plant a delicate, lace-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its compact growth and long-lasting blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
