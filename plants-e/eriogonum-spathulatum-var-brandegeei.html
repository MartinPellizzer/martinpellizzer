Eriogonum spathulatum var. brandegeei

Eriogonum spathulatum var. brandegeei, commonly known as the Brandegge's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
