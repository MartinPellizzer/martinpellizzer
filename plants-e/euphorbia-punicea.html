Euphorbia punicea

Euphorbia punicea, commonly known as the red-spotted euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, branching stem covered in reddish-brown spots and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stems.

Euphorbia punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
