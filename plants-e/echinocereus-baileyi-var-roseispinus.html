Echinocereus baileyi var. roseispinus

Echinocereus baileyi var. roseispinus, commonly known as the rose-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring dense clusters of bright red spines that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in the spring and attracting pollinators such as bees and bats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in desert gardens.
