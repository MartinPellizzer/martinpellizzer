Euploca convolvulacea subsp. convolvulacea

Euploca convolvulacea subsp.

convolvulacea, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a herbaceous annual vine native to the Americas. It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth habit and ability to climb using its tendrils. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a model organism in plant physiology and genetics research.
