Echinodorus grisebachii

Echinodorus grisebachii, commonly known as the Brazilian sword plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a silvery underside. This plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often found in slow-moving rivers and lakes. Echinodorus grisebachii is valued in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life.

It is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to a range of water conditions.
