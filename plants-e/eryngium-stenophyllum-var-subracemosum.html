Eryngium stenophyllum var. subracemosum

Eryngium stenophyllum var. subracemosum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and produces striking, spiky purple flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice among gardeners seeking low-maintenance ornamental plants.

The variety subracemosum is distinguished by its more branched inflorescence compared to the typical form.
