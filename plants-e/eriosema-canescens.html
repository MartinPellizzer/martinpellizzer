Eriosema canescens

Eriosema canescens, commonly known as the rough eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has a hairy, rough texture, giving it its common name, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and has adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Eriosema canescens is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
