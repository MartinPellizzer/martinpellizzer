Emilia pinnatifida

Emilia pinnatifida, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, branching stems that often grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in fine hairs.

Emilia pinnatifida is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
