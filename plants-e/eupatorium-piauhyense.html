Eupatorium piauhyense

Eupatorium piauhyense, commonly known as Piauhy goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native region. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
