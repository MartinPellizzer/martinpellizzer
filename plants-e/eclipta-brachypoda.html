Eclipta brachypoda

Eclipta brachypoda, commonly known as white daisy or false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in open fields and disturbed soils. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a yellow center. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in temperate regions.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
