Echinocactus rhodantherus

Echinocactus rhodantherus, commonly known as the red-hair cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and vibrant red to orange flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers bloom in late spring or early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinocactus rhodantherus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
