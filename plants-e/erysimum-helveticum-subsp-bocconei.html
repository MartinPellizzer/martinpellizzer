Erysimum helveticum subsp. bocconei

Erysimum helveticum subsp.

bocconei, commonly known as the Alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous areas of Switzerland, Austria, and Italy, where it thrives in sunny, well-drained habitats.
