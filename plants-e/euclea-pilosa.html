Euclea pilosa

Euclea pilosa, commonly known as the hairy euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Euclea pilosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some African cultures.
