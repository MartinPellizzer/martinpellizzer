Eryngium megapotamicum

Eryngium megapotamicum, commonly known as the Andean thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium megapotamicum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
