Ephedra fragilis subsp. desfontainii

Ephedra fragilis subsp.

desfontainii, commonly known as the fragile ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its green, branched stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
