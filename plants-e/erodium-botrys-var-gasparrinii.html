Erodium botrys var. gasparrinii

Erodium botrys var. gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparinii sticky weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, clustered pink to purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, hairy capsule. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and along roadsides.

Due to its invasive potential, it is considered a weed in some regions.
