Elaeagnus arakiana

Elaeagnus arakiana, commonly known as the Arakian silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance, and its small, edible berries that ripen in the autumn. The plant is often found in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or greenish-yellow.

Elaeagnus arakiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
