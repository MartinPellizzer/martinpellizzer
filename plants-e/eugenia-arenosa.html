Eugenia arenosa

Eugenia arenosa, commonly known as the sand eucalypt, is a small shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, grayish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia arenosa is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in dunes and coastal heathlands.
