Echinocactus wislizeni var. decipiens

Echinocactus wislizeni var. decipiens, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, which is covered in a dense layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its green exterior. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
