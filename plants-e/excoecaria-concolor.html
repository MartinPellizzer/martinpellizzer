Excoecaria concolor

Excoecaria concolor, commonly known as the red-rooted croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and striking, palmately lobed leaves that are usually green with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in warm climates.
