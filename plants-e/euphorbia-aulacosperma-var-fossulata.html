Euphorbia aulacosperma var. fossulata

Euphorbia aulacosperma var. fossulata, commonly known as the fissured spurge, is a perennial shrub native to South Africa.

It features a woody stem with distinctive, ridged, and fissured bark that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
