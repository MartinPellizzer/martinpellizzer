Evolvulus tenuis

Evolvulus tenuis, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and small, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Evolvulus tenuis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its delicate appearance and rapid growth habit.
