Exacum ramosum

Exacum ramosum, commonly known as the bushy pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Exacum ramosum thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact growth and colorful blooms.
