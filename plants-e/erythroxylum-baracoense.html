Erythroxylum baracoense

Erythroxylum baracoense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain a single seed, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments.

It has a moderate growth rate and can reach heights of up to 5 meters in favorable conditions.
