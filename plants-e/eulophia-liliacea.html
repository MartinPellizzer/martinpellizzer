Eulophia liliacea

Eulophia liliacea, commonly known as the Lily Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves and produces numerous small, greenish-yellow flowers clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia liliacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
