Erica cristiflora var. cristiflora

Erica cristiflora var. cristiflora, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica cristiflora var. cristiflora is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
