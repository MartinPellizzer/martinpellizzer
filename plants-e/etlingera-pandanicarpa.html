Etlingera pandanicarpa

Etlingera pandanicarpa, commonly known as the pandan-fruited ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, fleshy fruits that resemble a cross between a pineapple and a ginger root. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that develop into the distinctive, segmented fruit. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a fragrant, slightly sweet aroma.

The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines and has potential medicinal properties due to its rich content of essential oils and bioactive compounds.
