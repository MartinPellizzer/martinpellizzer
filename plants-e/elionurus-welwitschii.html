Elionurus welwitschii

Elionurus welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough, hairy texture.

Elionurus welwitschii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in dry conditions.
