Euphorbia subsalsa var. kaokoensis

Euphorbia subsalsa var. kaokoensis, commonly known as the Kaoko euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Namibia and Angola.

It features a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height, often with a spiny texture. The plant produces yellowish-green花朵, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stem to survive drought conditions.

Its unique morphology makes it a striking addition to xerophytic gardens.
