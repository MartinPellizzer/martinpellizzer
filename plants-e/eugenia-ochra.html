Eugenia ochra

Eugenia ochra, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Eugenia ochra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
