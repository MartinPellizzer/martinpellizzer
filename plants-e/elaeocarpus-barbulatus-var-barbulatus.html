Elaeocarpus barbulatus var. barbulatus

Elaeocarpus barbulatus var. barbulatus, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarpus, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves with hairy undersides. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
