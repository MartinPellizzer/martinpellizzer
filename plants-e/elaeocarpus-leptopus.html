Elaeocarpus leptopus

Elaeocarpus leptopus, commonly known as the slender-leaf elaeocarp, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has limited use in traditional medicine.
