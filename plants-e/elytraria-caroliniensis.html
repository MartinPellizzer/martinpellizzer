Elytraria caroliniensis

Elytraria caroliniensis, commonly known as the Carolina grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Elytraria caroliniensis is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
