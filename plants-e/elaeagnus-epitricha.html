Elaeagnus epitricha

Elaeagnus epitricha, commonly known as silverleaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used for erosion control. Its fruit is a small, edible berry that is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Elaeagnus epitricha is also valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various climates.
