Evolvulus palmeri

Evolvulus palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its vine-like growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

The leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while the stems are slender and twining.
