Eucalyptus × cordieri

Eucalyptus × cordieri, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which appear in late winter to early spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Eucalyptus × cordieri is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
