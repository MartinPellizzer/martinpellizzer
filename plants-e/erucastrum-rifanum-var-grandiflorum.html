Erucastrum rifanum var. grandiflorum

Erucastrum rifanum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered rockcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid and semi-arid regions. It is native to parts of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
