Eugenia acutata

Eugenia acutata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red, and edible, often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia acutata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
