Eleocharis setifolia subsp. schweinfurthiana

Eleocharis setifolia subsp.

schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the slender spike rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight sheen, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and is adapted to moist, well-drained soils.
