Eurybia affinis

Eurybia affinis, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat, composite flower heads that range from white to pink or purple. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Eurybia affinis is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
