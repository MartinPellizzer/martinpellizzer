Euphorbia galpinii

Euphorbia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia galpinii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
