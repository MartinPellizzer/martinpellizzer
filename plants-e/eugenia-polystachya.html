Eugenia polystachya

Eugenia polystachya, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle or sweet myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

Eugenia polystachya thrives in well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
