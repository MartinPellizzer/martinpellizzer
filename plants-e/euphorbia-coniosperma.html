Euphorbia coniosperma

Euphorbia coniosperma, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and distinctive, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems to survive drought conditions.

Euphorbia coniosperma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
