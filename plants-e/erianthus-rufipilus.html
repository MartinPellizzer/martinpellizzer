Erianthus rufipilus

Erianthus rufipilus, commonly known as the red-feathered grass, is a tall perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored inflorescences, which give it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spiky flowers. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Erianthus rufipilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in ecological restoration projects.
