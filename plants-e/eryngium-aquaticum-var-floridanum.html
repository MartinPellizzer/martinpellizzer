Eryngium aquaticum var. floridanum

Eryngium aquaticum var. floridanum, commonly known as Florida thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
