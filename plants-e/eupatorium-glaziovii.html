Eupatorium glaziovii

Eupatorium glaziovii, commonly known as Glaziov's boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Eupatorium glaziovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
