Eranthemum verschaffeltii

Eranthemum verschaffeltii, commonly known as the Van der Vegt's eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in various horticultural settings.
