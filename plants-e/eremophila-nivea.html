Eremophila nivea

Eremophila nivea, commonly known as the silver leaf eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and produces small, woody fruits containing seeds.

Eremophila nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
