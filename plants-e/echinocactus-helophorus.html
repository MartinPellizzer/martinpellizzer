Echinocactus helophorus

Echinocactus helophorus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.

The cactus has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades under proper care.
