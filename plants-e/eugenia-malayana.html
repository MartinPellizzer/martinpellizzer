Eugenia malayana

Eugenia malayana, commonly known as the Malay apple or *Syzygium malayana*, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
