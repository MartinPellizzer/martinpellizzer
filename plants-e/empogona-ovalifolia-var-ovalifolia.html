Empogona ovalifolia var. ovalifolia

Empogona ovalifolia var. ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leafed empogona, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale green, followed by fruit that is a small, fleshy drupe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
