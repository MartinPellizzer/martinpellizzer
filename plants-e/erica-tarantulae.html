Erica tarantulae

Erica tarantulae, commonly known as the Cape Town heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or white, and it blooms during the summer months.

Erica tarantulae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
