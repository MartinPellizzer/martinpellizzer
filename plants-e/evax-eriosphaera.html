Evax eriosphaera

Evax eriosphaera, commonly known as the woolly globe thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to its foliage. Its leaves are typically gray-green and covered in a powdery coating, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Evax eriosphaera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and striking form.
