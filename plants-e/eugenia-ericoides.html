Eugenia ericoides

Eugenia ericoides, commonly known as the waxleaf eucalyptus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It features dense, leathery foliage that is typically dark green and often has a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in arid regions.

The plant's botanical characteristics, including its aromatic leaves and showy flowers, contribute to its ornamental value.
