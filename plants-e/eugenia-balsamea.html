Eugenia balsamea

Eugenia balsamea, commonly known as the balsam apple, is a tropical shrub native to regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-yellow berries that are edible and have a tart, citrus-like flavor. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and fruits, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Eugenia balsamea is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in traditional herbal medicine.
