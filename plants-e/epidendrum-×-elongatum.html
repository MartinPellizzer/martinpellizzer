Epidendrum × elongatum

Epidendrum × elongatum, commonly known as the long-flowered epidendrum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces long, pendant flower spikes that can reach several feet in length. The plant typically features broad, elliptical leaves and blooms in a range of colors including white, yellow, and pink. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum × elongatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouse settings.
