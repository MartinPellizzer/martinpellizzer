Eugenia hypargyrea

Eugenia hypargyrea, commonly known as the silverleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It is often found in coastal and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes across its native range.
