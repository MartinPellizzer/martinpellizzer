Eupatorium cubense

Eupatorium cubense, commonly known as Cuban thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium cubense is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
