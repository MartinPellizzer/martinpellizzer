Eschweilera eymaana

Eschweilera eymaana, commonly known as the Eymaana wax palm, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. Eschweilera eymaana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
