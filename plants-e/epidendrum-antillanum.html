Epidendrum antillanum

Epidendrum antillanum, commonly known as the Caribbean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Epidendrum antillanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
