Erigeron runcinatus

Erigeron runcinatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, producing white to pale yellow petals surrounded by pale green ray florets.

Erigeron runcinatus is typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
