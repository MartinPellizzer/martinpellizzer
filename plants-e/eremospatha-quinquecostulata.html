Eremospatha quinquecostulata

Eremospatha quinquecostulata, commonly known as the five-costled eremospatha, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense shrubs that can survive in harsh, dry conditions. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eremospatha quinquecostulata is also notable for its ability to attract various pollinators, including bees and birds.
