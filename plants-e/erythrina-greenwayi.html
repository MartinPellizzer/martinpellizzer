Erythrina greenwayi

Erythrina greenwayi, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are clustered in dense panicles and attract various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish hue. Erythrina greenwayi thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
