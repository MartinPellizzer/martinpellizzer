Eugenia symingtoniana

Eugenia symingtoniana, commonly known as the Andean guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and potential for cultivation, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
