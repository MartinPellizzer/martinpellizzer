Eugenia capparidifolia

Eugenia capparidifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cherry tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, cherry-like fruits. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong, aromatic scent.

The flowers are white or pale pink, and the fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer.
