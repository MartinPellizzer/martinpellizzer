Eriosema decumbens subsp. luxurians

Eriosema decumbens subsp.

luxurians, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features a sprawling growth habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes.

The leaves are ovate to elliptical, covered in a dense layer of fine, white to grayish pubescence, giving the plant a velvety appearance. It produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and woodland margins.
