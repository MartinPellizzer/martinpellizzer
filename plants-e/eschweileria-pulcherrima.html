Eschweileria pulcherrima

Eschweileria pulcherrima, commonly known as the poinsettia, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers at the center of the bracts. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eschweileria pulcherrima is widely cultivated as a decorative plant, especially during the holiday season.
