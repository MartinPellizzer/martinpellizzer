Epilobium collinum

Epilobium collinum, commonly known as the creeping willow or hill willow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant features slender, ascending stems that are often covered with short hairs, and its leaves are elliptical to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are pink to reddish, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium collinum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of habitats and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
