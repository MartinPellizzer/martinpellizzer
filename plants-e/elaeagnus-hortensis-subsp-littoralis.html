Elaeagnus hortensis subsp. littoralis

Elaeagnus hortensis subsp.

littoralis, commonly known as the coastal silverberry, is a shrub native to coastal regions of China. It is characterized by its small, silvery-green leaves and yellow flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is well adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in coastal ecosystems.
