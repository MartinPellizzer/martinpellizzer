Eugenia capuli

Eugenia capuli, commonly known as the purple-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a purple tinge underneath. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Eugenia capuli is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
