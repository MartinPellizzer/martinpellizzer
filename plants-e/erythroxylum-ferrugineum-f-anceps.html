Erythroxylum ferrugineum f. anceps

Erythroxylum ferrugineum f. anceps, commonly known as the rusty leaf shrub, is a variant of the Erythroxylum genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and leaves with a distinctive rusty or ferruginous appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that are succeeded by capsule fruits containing several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique foliage and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
