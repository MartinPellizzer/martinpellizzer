Eragrostis rufescens var. mesoamericana

Eragrostis rufescens var. mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mexican panicgrass, is a grass species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
