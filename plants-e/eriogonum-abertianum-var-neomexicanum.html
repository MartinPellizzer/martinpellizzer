Eriogonum abertianum var. neomexicanum

Eriogonum abertianum var. neomexicanum, commonly known as the New Mexico buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing soil in its native range.
