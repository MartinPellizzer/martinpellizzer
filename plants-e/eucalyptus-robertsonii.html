Eucalyptus robertsonii

Eucalyptus robertsonii, commonly known as the Robertson's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

Eucalyptus robertsonii is an important component of its native ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
