Escobaria macdougallii

Escobaria macdougallii, commonly known as Macdougall's liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer.

Escobaria macdougallii is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
