Eragrostis voronensis

Eragrostis voronensis, commonly known as blackgrass, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, stiff leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
