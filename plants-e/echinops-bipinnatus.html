Echinops bipinnatus

Echinops bipinnatus, commonly known as the two-feathered thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a spiky globe, which are produced after the plant has bloomed. The plant has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

Echinops bipinnatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
