Echinofossulocactus sulphureus

Echinofossulocactus sulphureus, commonly known as the yellow fissured cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems that are often covered in yellowish or greenish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
