Erythrina panduriformis

Erythrina panduriformis, commonly known as the flame tree or the Indian flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its striking red floral display.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Indonesia. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces elaborate, clustered inflorescences that bloom in vibrant red or orange hues. Its seeds are encased in a pod-like fruit, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers.

Erythrina panduriformis is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
