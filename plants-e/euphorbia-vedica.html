Euphorbia vedica

Euphorbia vedica, commonly known as the Indian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. Euphorbia vedica typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

It is also noted for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
