Eupatorium lamiifolium

Eupatorium lamiifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional medicine.

Eupatorium lamiifolium plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
