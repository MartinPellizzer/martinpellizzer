Eugenia squamiflora

Eugenia squamiflora, commonly known as the squamiflora eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
