Espeletia murilloi

Espeletia murilloi, commonly known as the Murillo's espeletia, is a striking plant native to the Andean highlands of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, hairy stems. This species is notable for its large, showy inflorescences that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
