Enkylia trigyna

Enkylia trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct lobes, and it produces a capsule-like fruit that releases seeds with a parachute structure.

Enkylia trigyna is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
