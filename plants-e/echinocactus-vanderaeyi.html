Echinocactus vanderaeyi

Echinocactus vanderaeyi, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that develops a spiny, star-shaped appearance as it matures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stem. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in dry climates.
