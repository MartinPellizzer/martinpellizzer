Erigeron bonariensis var. microcephalus

Erigeron bonariensis var. microcephalus, commonly known as the small-headed blueweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture. This variety is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also known for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
