Eremophila simulans subsp. simulans

Eremophila simulans subsp.

simulans, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of pale pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It is often used in coastal and dry garden landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
