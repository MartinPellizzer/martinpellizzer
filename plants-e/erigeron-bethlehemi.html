Erigeron bethlehemi

Erigeron bethlehemi, commonly known as Bethlehemit sky-blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its foliage is generally gray-green, with hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.
