Elettaria rosea

Elettaria rosea, commonly known as cardamom, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family, Zingiberaceae, and is characterized by its aromatic, spindle-shaped rhizomes. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that develop into reddish-brown capsules containing aromatic seeds. Cardamom is valued for its intense flavor and is widely used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical features make it a unique and economically significant crop in its native habitats.
