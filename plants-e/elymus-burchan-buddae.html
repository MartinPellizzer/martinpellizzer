Elymus burchan-buddae

Elymus burchan-buddae, commonly known as the Burchan-Buddae wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which bear numerous tiny florets. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system for water absorption.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils in degraded landscapes.
