Eupatorium scorodonioides var. grossedentatum

Eupatorium scorodonioides var. grossedentatum, commonly known as the broad-toothed eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, toothed lobes. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded areas with rich, loamy soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
