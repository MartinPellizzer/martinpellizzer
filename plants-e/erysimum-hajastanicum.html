Erysimum hajastanicum

Erysimum hajastanicum, commonly known as the Caucasian wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in racemes.

Erysimum hajastanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
