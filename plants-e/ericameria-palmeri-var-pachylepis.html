Ericameria palmeri var. pachylepis

Ericameria palmeri var. pachylepis, commonly known as the desert goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and yellowish-green leaves, which are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in dense heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ericameria palmeri var. pachylepis is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
