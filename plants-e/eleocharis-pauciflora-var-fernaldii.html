Eleocharis pauciflora var. fernaldii

Eleocharis pauciflora var. fernaldii, commonly known as the Fernald's spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, scale-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually found in clusters around the base of the plant.

This variant is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is often found in the southeastern United States.
