Erythrophleum guineense var. swaziense

Erythrophleum guineense var. swaziense, commonly known as the Swazi African mahogany, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The tree produces hard, heavy wood that is valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes harvested for timber, though it is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
