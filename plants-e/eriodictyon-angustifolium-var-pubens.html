Eriodictyon angustifolium var. pubens

Eriodictyon angustifolium var. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent yerba santa, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats. Its leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for medicinal purposes.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to coastal conditions.
