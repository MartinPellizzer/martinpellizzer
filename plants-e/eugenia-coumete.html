Eugenia coumete

Eugenia coumete, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Guyana. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a waxy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in warm climates.
