Eriosyce odieri subsp. malleolata

Eriosyce odieri subsp.

malleolata, commonly known as the "Lace Cactus," is a succulent plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hairs that give it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in collections of cacti and succulents.
