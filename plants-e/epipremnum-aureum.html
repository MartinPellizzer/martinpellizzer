Epipremnum aureum

Epipremnum aureum, commonly known as pothos, is a climbing or trailing plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically green with a striking yellow or variegated pattern. The plant has aerial roots that allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it adaptable to various environments. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epipremnum aureum is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
