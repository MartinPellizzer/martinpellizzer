Epilobium × nutantiflorum

Epilobium × nutantiflorum, commonly known as the weeping willow herb, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like stems that often droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly hairy texture.

Epilobium × nutantiflorum is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wet meadows and stream banks.
