Encelia frutescens var. virginensis

Encelia frutescens var. virginensis, commonly known as the Virgin's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring silver-gray foliage and yellow flowers. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky soils.

The flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
