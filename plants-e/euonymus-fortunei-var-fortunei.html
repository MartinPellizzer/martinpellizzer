Euonymus fortunei var. fortunei

Euonymus fortunei var. fortunei, commonly known as the creeping euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that often have a slight yellowish tinge beneath. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ground cover in landscaping.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
