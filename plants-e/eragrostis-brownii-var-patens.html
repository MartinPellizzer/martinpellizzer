Eragrostis brownii var. patens

Eragrostis brownii var. patens, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale green to brown spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in erosion control.
