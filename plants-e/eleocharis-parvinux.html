Eleocharis parvinux

Eleocharis parvinux, commonly known as the small rice grass, is a species of sedge that typically grows in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces tiny, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Eleocharis parvinux plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
