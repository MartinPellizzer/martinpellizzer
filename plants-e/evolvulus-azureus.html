Evolvulus azureus

Evolvulus azureus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
