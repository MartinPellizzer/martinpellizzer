Euphoria gracilis

Euphoria gracilis, commonly known as the slender euphoria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as along riverbanks or in dense undergrowth. Its flowers emit a subtle fragrance, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
