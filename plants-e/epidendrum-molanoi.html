Epidendrum molanoi

Epidendrum molanoi, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long, slender, and often covered in a waxy coating.

Epidendrum molanoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
