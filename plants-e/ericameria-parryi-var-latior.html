Ericameria parryi var. latior

Ericameria parryi var. latior, commonly known as the broadleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ericameria parryi var. latior is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
