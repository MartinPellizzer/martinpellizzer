Erythrodes rariflora

Erythrodes rariflora, commonly known as the rare-flowered red-leaf plant, is a striking species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage and delicate, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in dense forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its flowers are small and often pink or white in color.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Erythrodes rariflora is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
