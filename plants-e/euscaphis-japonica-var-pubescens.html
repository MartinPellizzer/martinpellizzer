Euscaphis japonica var. pubescens

Euscaphis japonica var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese pear.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia, characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. Its bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fruit.
