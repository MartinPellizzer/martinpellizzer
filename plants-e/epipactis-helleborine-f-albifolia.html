Epipactis helleborine f. albifolia

Epipactis helleborine f. albifolia, commonly known as the white-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid with a distinctive botanical structure.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with white or pale green, distinguishing it from the typical green-leaved variety. The plant produces a single, tall spike of flowers, usually with white or pale yellow blooms that have a unique, spurred lip. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its specialized mycorrhizal associations.

This orchid is typically found in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
