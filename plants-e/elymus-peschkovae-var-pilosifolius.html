Elymus peschkovae var. pilosifolius

Elymus peschkovae var. pilosifolius, commonly known as the pilose leaf wheatgrass, is a grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems. The leaves are narrow, flat, and covered with short, stiff hairs, giving them a distinctive fuzzy appearance. This variety thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in steppe and grassland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
