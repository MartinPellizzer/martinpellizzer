Eugenia triquetra var. aequatorialis

Eugenia triquetra var. aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian three-angled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color.
