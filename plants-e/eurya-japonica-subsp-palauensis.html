Eurya japonica subsp. palauensis

Eurya japonica subsp.

palauensis, commonly known as the Palau holly, is a shrub native to the Caroline Islands. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
