Elatine ecuadoriensis

Elatine ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are often submerged. Its flowers are tiny and clustered in dense inflorescences, typically appearing in late summer.

Elatine ecuadoriensis is notable for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters.
