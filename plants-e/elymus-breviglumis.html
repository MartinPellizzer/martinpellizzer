Elymus breviglumis

Elymus breviglumis, commonly known as the short-bracted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

Elymus breviglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
