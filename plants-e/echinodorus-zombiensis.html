Echinodorus zombiensis

Echinodorus zombiensis, commonly known as the zombie waterweed, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length, often with a dark green to bluish hue. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface, adding a unique visual element to its appearance. It is known for its rapid growth rate, making it both a popular choice for aquariums and a potential invasive species in certain environments.

Due to its unusual name and appearance, it has gained attention in both horticultural and pop culture circles.
