Epidendrum suavis

Epidendrum suavis, commonly known as the sweet epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features slender, branching stems and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its blooms are often clustered in racemes and can last for several weeks during the flowering season.

Epidendrum suavis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
