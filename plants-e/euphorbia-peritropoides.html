Euphorbia peritropoides

Euphorbia peritropoides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically small, dark green, and sometimes covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
