Erigeron polymorphus subsp. graecus

Erigeron polymorphus subsp.

graecus, commonly known as the Greek fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small, yellow-centered flowers that bloom in summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
