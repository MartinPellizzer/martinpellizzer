Eriogonum umbellatum var. glaberrimum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. glaberrimum, commonly known as the Townsend's globemallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
