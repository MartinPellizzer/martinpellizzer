Eulophia andersonii

Eulophia andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple blooms arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia andersonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
