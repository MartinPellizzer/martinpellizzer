Elate sylvestris

Elate sylvestris, commonly known as the wild morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of blue, purple, and white. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. The plant is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit, often found along forest edges and in disturbed areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by birds and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
