Eleocharis curtisii

Eleocharis curtisii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eleocharis curtisii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
