Erigeron glabellus var. asper

Erigeron glabellus var. asper, commonly known as the roughleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are generally rough to the touch, giving it its specific variety name "asper.".

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
