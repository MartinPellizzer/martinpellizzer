Eugenia ceibana

Eugenia ceibana, commonly known as the ceiba tree, is a large tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its buttressed roots and massive, spreading canopy. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its strength and durability, making it important in local economies.

The ceiba tree also plays a significant ecological role, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
