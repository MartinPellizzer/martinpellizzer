Erica pectinifolia var. pectinifolia

Erica pectinifolia var. pectinifolia, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica pectinifolia var. pectinifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
