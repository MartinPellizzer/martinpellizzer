Englerodendron vignei

Englerodendron vignei, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Central and Southern Africa.

It is characterized by its straight, dark brown trunk and smooth, greyish bark. The plant has opposite, simple, elliptic leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its small, pale yellow flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces woody, elliptic fruits containing several seeds.

The tree is valued for its durable, heavy wood, which is used in furniture and construction.
