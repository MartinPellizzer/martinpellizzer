Eriospermum undulatum

Eriospermum undulatum, commonly known as the wavy eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often ribbed and have a wavy or undulating edge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
