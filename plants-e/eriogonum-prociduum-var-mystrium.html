Eriogonum prociduum var. mystrium

Eriogonum prociduum var. mystrium, commonly known as the desert gilia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink to purple in color and bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies in its native habitat.
