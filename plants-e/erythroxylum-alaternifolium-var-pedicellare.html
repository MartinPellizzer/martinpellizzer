Erythroxylum alaternifolium var. pedicellare

Erythroxylum alaternifolium var. pedicellare, commonly known as the pink leaf mallow, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is part of the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and hibiscus.
