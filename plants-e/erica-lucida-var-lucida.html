Erica lucida var. lucida

Erica lucida var. lucida, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of the United States.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Erica lucida var. lucida is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
