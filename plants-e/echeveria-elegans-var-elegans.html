Echeveria elegans var. elegans

Echeveria elegans var. elegans, commonly known as the "Mexican Snowball," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray to silver-green in color. The leaves are covered in a fine layer of white trichomes, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens, container gardening, and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
