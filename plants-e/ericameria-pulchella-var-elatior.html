Ericameria pulchella var. elatior

Ericameria pulchella var. elatior, commonly known as the tall goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and inland areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for erosion control and ornamental gardening.
