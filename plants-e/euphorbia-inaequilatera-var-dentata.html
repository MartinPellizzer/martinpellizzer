Euphorbia inaequilatera var. dentata

Euphorbia inaequilatera var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The leaves are typically narrow and serrated, giving the plant its specific variety name. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
