Elaeocarpus holopetalus

Elaeocarpus holopetalus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy drupes that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
