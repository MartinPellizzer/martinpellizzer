Eryngium delarocheanum

Eryngium delarocheanum, commonly known as the African sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble thistles, and its hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into hard, seed-filled capsules.

Eryngium delarocheanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
