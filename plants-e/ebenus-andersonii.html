Ebenus andersonii

Ebenus andersonii, commonly known as Anderson's sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Ebenus andersonii is adapted to harsh, sunny conditions and is often found in desert or semi-desert regions.
