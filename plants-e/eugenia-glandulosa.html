Eugenia glandulosa

Eugenia glandulosa, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico, where it thrives in wetland and swampy habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia glandulosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate wet conditions.
