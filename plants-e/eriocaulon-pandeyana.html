Eriocaulon pandeyana

Eriocaulon pandeyana, commonly known as the Pandey's spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy habitats and is native to the Indian subcontinent. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered.

Eriocaulon pandeyana plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland environments.
