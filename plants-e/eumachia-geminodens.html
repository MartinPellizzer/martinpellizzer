Eumachia geminodens

Eumachia geminodens, commonly known as the twin-leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are white with yellow centers.

Eumachia geminodens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
