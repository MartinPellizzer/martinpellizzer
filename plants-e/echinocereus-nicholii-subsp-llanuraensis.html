Echinocereus nicholii subsp. llanuraensis

Echinocereus nicholii subsp.

llanuraensis, commonly known as the Llanura prickly pear, is a cactus native to the arid regions of northern Argentina. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles, which bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers.

The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small and yellow when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for local wildlife.
