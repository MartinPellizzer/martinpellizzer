Eucalyptus triartha

Eucalyptus triartha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its bark is often flaky and reddish-brown, and it produces woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Eucalyptus triartha is adapted to dry conditions and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
