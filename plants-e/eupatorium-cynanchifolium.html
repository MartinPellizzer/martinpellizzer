Eupatorium cynanchifolium

Eupatorium cynanchifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, sky-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its showy inflorescences attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees, making it an important plant for ecological diversity.
