Euterpe ventricosa

Euterpe ventricosa, commonly known as the pink peppercorn tree, is a species native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is a member of the palm family Arecaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish trunk and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, red, peppercorn-like fruits that are used in culinary applications. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in panicles, and it thrives in humid, tropical conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
