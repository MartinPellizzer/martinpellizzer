Eurybia ledifolia

Eurybia ledifolia, commonly known as the common barren strawberry, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are typically basal, with a distinctive heart-shaped outline and a fuzzy texture.

Eurybia ledifolia is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
