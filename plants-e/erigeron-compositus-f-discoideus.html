Erigeron compositus f. discoideus

Erigeron compositus f. discoideus, commonly known as the diskflower fleabane, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, daisy-like appearance with white to pale yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

This variety is noted for its small, disk-shaped flowers and is often found in the western United States.
