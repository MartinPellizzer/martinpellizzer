Eragrostis tenella

Eragrostis tenella, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves and compact, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Eragrostis tenella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its dense root system.
