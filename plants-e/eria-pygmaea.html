Eria pygmaea

Eria pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, tropical environments. The plant has delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It prefers indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Due to its size and beauty, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
