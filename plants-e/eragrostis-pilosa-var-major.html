Eragrostis pilosa var. major

Eragrostis pilosa var. major, commonly known as hairy dropseed, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy, thread-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, drooping spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This grass is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
