Exocarpos menziesii

Exocarpos menziesii, commonly known as the white gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, white or yellowish fruits that are typically consumed by birds and small mammals. The plant has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and along stream banks.

The fruit is edible but has a tart flavor, making it a valuable plant for both wildlife and traditional uses.
