Evolvulus capreolatus

Evolvulus capreolatus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, blue to purple flowers with five petals. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in cultivated fields. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glories.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
