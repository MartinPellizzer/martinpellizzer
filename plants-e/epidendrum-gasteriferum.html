Epidendrum gasteriferum

Epidendrum gasteriferum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often forming a dense rosette around the flowering stem.

Epidendrum gasteriferum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
