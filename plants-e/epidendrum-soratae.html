Epidendrum soratae

Epidendrum soratae, commonly known as the Black Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety petals and a striking, elongated flower spike. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are often fragrant and bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value.

Epidendrum soratae requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
