Ephedra major var. suggarica

Ephedra major var. suggarica, commonly known as the sugar ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and red, berry-like fruits that contain the seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
