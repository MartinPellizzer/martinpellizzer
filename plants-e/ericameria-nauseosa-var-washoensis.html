Ericameria nauseosa var. washoensis

Ericameria nauseosa var. washoensis, commonly known as the Wasco goldenbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.

Its compact form and drought tolerance make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
