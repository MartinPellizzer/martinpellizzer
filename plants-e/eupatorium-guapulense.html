Eupatorium guapulense

Eupatorium guapulense, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium guapulense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
