Epidendrum palmidium

Epidendrum palmidium, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Epidendrum palmidium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a tropical or subtropical environment.
