Erigeron spiculosus var. minor

Erigeron spiculosus var. minor, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, daisy-like, with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
