Erigeron microglossus

Erigeron microglossus, commonly known as the small-lipped blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations.
