Eugenia exsucca var. temu

Eugenia exsucca var. temu, commonly known as the Temu apple or red apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. The plant has small, glossy, dark green leaves and grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional Aboriginal medicine.
