Escallonia illinita var. illinita

Escallonia illinita var. illinita, commonly known as the Illinita Escallonia, is a species native to specific regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage with a glossy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a leathery texture, and the plant has a moderate to fast growth rate.

Escallonia illinita var. illinita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
