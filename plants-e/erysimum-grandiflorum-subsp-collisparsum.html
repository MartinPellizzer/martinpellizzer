Erysimum grandiflorum subsp. collisparsum

Erysimum grandiflorum subsp.

collisparsum, commonly known as the Sierra wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, showy blooms.
