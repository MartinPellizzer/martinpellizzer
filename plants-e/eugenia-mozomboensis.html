Eugenia mozomboensis

Eugenia mozomboensis, commonly known as the Mozambique guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
