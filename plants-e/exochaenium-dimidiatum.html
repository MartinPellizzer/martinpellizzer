Exochaenium dimidiatum

Exochaenium dimidiatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Exochaenium dimidiatum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
