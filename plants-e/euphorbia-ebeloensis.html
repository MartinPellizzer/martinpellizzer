Euphorbia ebeloensis

Euphorbia ebeloensis, commonly known as the Ebelo's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and growth habits, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
