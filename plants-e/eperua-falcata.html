Eperua falcata

Eperua falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eperua, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with a distinctive narrow, sickle-shaped leaflets. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

Eperua falcata is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.
