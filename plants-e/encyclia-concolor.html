Encyclia concolor

Encyclia concolor, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, grass-like leaves and its long, spiraling inflorescence of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its flowers are arranged in a tight spiral, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Encyclia concolor is a perennial species that thrives in humid climates and is often found in rich, organic soil.
