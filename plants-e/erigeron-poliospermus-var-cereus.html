Erigeron poliospermus var. cereus

Erigeron poliospermus var. cereus, commonly known as the Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as hairy leaves and a deep root system to conserve water. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
