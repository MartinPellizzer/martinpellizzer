Ecbolium dichotomum, commonly known as the forked broom.

This plant is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its branching pattern and small, yellowish flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. The leaves are narrow and evergreen, and the plant is adapted to withstand dry conditions.
