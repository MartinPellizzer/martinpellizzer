Espeletia timotensis

Espeletia timotensis, commonly known as the Timotensis heather, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is known for its dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its high-altitude habitat. It typically grows in rocky or volcanic soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Its flowers bloom in shades of white and yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
