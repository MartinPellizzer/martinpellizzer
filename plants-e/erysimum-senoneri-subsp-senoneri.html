Erysimum senoneri subsp. senoneri

Erysimum senoneri subsp.

senoneri, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has basal leaves that are pinnately lobed and hairy, adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found growing on rocky slopes and scree.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Brassicaceae family.
