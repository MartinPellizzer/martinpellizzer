Ehrharta ramosa

Ehrharta ramosa, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, wetlands, and along riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems with whorled leaves that are narrow and grass-like in appearance. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, pale green to brown flowers.

Ehrharta ramosa is valued for its ornamental use and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
