Ephedranthus guianensis

Ephedranthus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan ephedranthus, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Guyana and Suriname.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in open woodlands or near water sources.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and less-studied species.
