Epilobium × schulzeanum

Epilobium × schulzeanum, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Epilobium × schulzeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
