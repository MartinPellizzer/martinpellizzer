Euphorbia socotrana subsp. purpurea

Euphorbia socotrana subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as the Socotra purple spurge, is a striking succulent native to the Socotra archipelago. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking purple or pinkish hues on its leaves and bracts.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by colorful, leaf-like structures called cyathia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
