Echinocactus oxypterus

Echinocactus oxypterus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent, rib-like structures that help it store water. The plant has a thick, spiny exterior that provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. Its areoles, which are specialized structures, bear clusters of white bristles and produce yellow flowers during the warmer months.

This cactus is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
