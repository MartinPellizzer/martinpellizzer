Eupatorium megalophyllum

Eupatorium megalophyllum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Eupatorium megalophyllum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
