Ehrharta longiflora

Ehrharta longiflora, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that produce tiny white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Ehrharta longiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
