Eubotrys bracteata

Eubotrys bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowering period usually occurs in the spring and early summer, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

Eubotrys bracteata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
