Eriogonum microtheca var. arceuthinum

Eriogonum microtheca var. arceuthinum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient component of desert ecosystems.
