Echium flavum

Echium flavum, commonly known as the yellow evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting pollinators such as moths and bats. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its stems are erect and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Echium flavum is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
