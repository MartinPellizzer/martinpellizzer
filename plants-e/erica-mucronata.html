Erica mucronata

Erica mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. Erica mucronata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
