Epidendrum aporum

Epidendrum aporum, commonly known as the purple-tinged epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic plant, often found growing on trees in humid tropical environments. The plant features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically green with a slight purplish hue. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are characterized by their vibrant colors, often in shades of pink and purple.

Epidendrum aporum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
