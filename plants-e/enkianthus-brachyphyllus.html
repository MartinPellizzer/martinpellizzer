Enkianthus brachyphyllus

Enkianthus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the broad-leaved enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Enkianthus brachyphyllus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
