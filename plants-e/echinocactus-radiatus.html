Echinocactus radiatus

Echinocactus radiatus, commonly known as the radiant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid environments. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the summer months.

Echinocactus radiatus is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
