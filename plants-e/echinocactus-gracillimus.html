Echinocactus gracillimus

Echinocactus gracillimus, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spineless, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
