Echeveria lutea var. fuscata

Echeveria lutea var. fuscata, commonly known as the Black Echeveria, is a compact succulent native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a bluish or purplish hue, often marked with red or purple edges. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, which is typical of succulents. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring or early summer.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and ease of care in arid environments.
