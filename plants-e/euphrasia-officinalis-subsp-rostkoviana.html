Euphrasia officinalis subsp. rostkoviana

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

rostkoviana, commonly known as the Rostkovian eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodland edges, and shaded areas, reaching a height of about 20 to 40 centimeters.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent dark veins, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal medicine for eye health.
