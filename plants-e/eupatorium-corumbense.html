Eupatorium corumbense

Eupatorium corumbense, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn.

Eupatorium corumbense is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
