Euphorbia interaxillaris

Euphorbia interaxillaris, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a unique appearance with its gelatinous, translucent stems that resemble ghostly shapes, hence its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by leaf-like bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and collections.
