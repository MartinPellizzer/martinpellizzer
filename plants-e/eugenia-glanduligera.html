Eugenia glanduligera

Eugenia glanduligera, commonly known as the rough-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, glandular-armed leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, aromatic fruits that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
