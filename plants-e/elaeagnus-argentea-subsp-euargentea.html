Elaeagnus argentea subsp. euargentea

Elaeagnus argentea subsp.

euargentea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen make it a useful plant in ecological restoration projects.
