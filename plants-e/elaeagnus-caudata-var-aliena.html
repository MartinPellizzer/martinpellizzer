Elaeagnus caudata var. aliena

Elaeagnus caudata var. aliena, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus caudata var. aliena is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
