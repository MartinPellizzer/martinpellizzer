Eugenia zippeliana

Eugenia zippeliana, commonly known as the Zippel's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible, reddish-purple berries that are a source of food for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
