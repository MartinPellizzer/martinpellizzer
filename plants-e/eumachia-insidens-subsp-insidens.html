Eumachia insidens subsp. insidens

Eumachia insidens subsp.

insidens, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eumachia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
