Elvasia calophyllea

Elvasia calophyllea, commonly known as the golden leaf tree, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, waxy leaves that are often tinged with yellow or gold, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from underground sources.

The bark of Elvasia calophyllea is thick and fissured, providing protection against fire and herbivory.
