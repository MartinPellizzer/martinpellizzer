Ericoides laevigatum

Ericoides laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ericoid, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its leaves are leathery and glossy, with a smooth texture, and it produces bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white.

Ericoides laevigatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to contribute to ecological diversity in its native habitat.
