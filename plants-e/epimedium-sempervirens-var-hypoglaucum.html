Epimedium sempervirens var. hypoglaucum

Epimedium sempervirens var. hypoglaucum, commonly known as the silverleaf barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces striking, nodding, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. The leaves are elliptical, with a distinctive silvery-gray underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and early bloom, contributing to its popularity in horticulture.
