Erodium laciniatum var. hesperium

Erodium laciniatum var. hesperium, commonly known as the California common groundsel, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is an annual herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in coastal areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
