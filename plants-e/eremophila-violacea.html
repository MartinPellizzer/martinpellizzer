Eremophila violacea

Eremophila violacea, commonly known as the violet bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the plant family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact shrub form and vibrant violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Eremophila violacea is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
