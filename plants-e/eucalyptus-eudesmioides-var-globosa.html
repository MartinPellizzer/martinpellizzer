Eucalyptus eudesmioides var. globosa

Eucalyptus eudesmioides var. globosa, commonly known as the globular eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or grey. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or cream in color. It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Its aromatic oils make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
