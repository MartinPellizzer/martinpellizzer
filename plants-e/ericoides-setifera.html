Ericoides setifera

Ericoides setifera, commonly known as the slender heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, dark berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Ericoides setifera is well adapted to harsh climates and plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
