Epidendrum oncidioides var. itabirae

Epidendrum oncidioides var. itabirae, commonly known as the Itabira Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, often with intricate patterns on the petals. It has linear to lanceolate leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
