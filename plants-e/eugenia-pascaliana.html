Eugenia pascaliana

Eugenia pascaliana, commonly known as the Pascalian myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that matures in late summer.

Eugenia pascaliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
