Eucalyptus georgei

Eucalyptus georgei, commonly known as the George's mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that typically open to reveal white or pale pink flowers. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soils.

Eucalyptus georgei is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for native wildlife.
