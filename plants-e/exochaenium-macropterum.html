Exochaenium macropterum

Exochaenium macropterum, commonly known as the broad-winged grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various wildlife species.
