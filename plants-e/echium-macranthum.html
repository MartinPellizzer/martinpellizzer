Echium macranthum

Echium macranthum, commonly known as the giant blueweed, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters in height, featuring dense, silver-gray foliage. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in tall spikes, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
