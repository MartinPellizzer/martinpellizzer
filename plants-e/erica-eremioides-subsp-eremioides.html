Erica eremioides subsp. eremioides

Erica eremioides subsp.

eremioides, commonly known as the desert heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are adapted to arid conditions.

The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Erica eremioides subsp.

eremioides is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value in dry climates.
