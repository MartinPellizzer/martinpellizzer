Eruca sativa subsp. pinnatifida

Eruca sativa subsp.

pinnatifida, commonly known as broadleaf mustard, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features basal rosettes of pinnately lobed, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in racemes, which are followed by long, slender pods containing small, edible seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its peppery flavor and is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
