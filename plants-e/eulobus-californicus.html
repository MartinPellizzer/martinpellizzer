Eulobus californicus

Eulobus californicus, commonly known as the California lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or chaparral habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its stem is slender and erect.

Eulobus californicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
