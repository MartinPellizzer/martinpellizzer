Exochaenium bugandense

Exochaenium bugandense, commonly known as the Buganda grass, is a perennial herb native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a prominent midrib, and it thrives in well-drained sandy or loamy soils.

It is often found in open grasslands and is adapted to semi-arid conditions.
