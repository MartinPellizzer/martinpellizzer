Eucalyptus conspicua

Eucalyptus conspicua, commonly known as the white gum, is a tall tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in long strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is valued for its timber and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
