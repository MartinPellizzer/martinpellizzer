Eupatorium tepicanum

Eupatorium tepicanum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium tepicanum is often found in the understory of forests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
