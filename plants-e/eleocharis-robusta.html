Eleocharis robusta

Eleocharis robusta, commonly known as the hardstem spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant has cylindrical, grass-like stems that are often densely packed and covered in small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikelets.

Eleocharis robusta is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
