Ekebergia discolor

Ekebergia discolor, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and dense, rounded crown. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

Ekebergia discolor is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
