Erica quadrata

Erica quadrata, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense foliage and small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Erica quadrata is well-suited for rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
