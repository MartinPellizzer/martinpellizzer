Erythranthe ptilota

Erythranthe ptilota, commonly known as the wooly monkeyflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers in shades of pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and streambanks.

Erythranthe ptilota is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and ecologically important species.
