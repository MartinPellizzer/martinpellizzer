Embelia cuneata

Embelia cuneata, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing numerous small seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

Embelia cuneata is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
