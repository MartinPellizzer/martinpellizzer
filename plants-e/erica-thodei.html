Erica thodei

Erica thodei, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region. Erica thodei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
