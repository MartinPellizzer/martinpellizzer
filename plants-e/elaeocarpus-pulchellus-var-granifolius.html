Elaeocarpus pulchellus var. granifolius

Elaeocarpus pulchellus var. granifolius, commonly known as the golden-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces woody, egg-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical regions.
