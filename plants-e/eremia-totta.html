Eremia totta

Eremia totta, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. Eremia totta is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water and high sunlight exposure.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
