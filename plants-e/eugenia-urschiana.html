Eugenia urschiana

Eugenia urschiana, commonly known as the wax apple or uchuva, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. The fruit has a waxy coating and a sweet, tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
