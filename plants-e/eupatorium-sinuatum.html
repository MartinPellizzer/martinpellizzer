Eupatorium sinuatum

Eupatorium sinuatum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, mist-like appearance.

Eupatorium sinuatum is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
