Eugenia gageana

Eugenia gageana, commonly known as the New Zealand native myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in coastal environments and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eugenia gageana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
