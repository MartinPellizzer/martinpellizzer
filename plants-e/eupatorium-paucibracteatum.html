Eupatorium paucibracteatum

Eupatorium paucibracteatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Eupatorium paucibracteatum is often used in wetland restoration due to its ability to thrive in saturated soils.
