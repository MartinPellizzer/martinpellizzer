Eryngium ternatum var. subinerme

Eryngium ternatum var. subinerme, commonly known as the blue thistle or narrow-leaved eryngium, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
