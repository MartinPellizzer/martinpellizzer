Erythronium lanceolatum

Erythronium lanceolatum, commonly known as the white trillium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It features a solitary, showy white flower with three petals and three sepals, often accompanied by three leaf-like bracts. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces oval, dark green leaves that are lanceolate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded woodland habitats and is known for its early spring blooming period.

The flowers are pollinated by insects and the plant spreads primarily through rhizome growth.
