Elaphoglossum × morphohybridum

Elaphoglossum × morphohybridum, commonly known as the hybrid holly fern, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Elaphoglossum ciliatum and Elaphoglossum macrophyllum.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like fronds with a distinctive, glossy appearance and finely divided pinnae. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a natural hybrid, showcasing the genetic diversity within the Elaphoglossum genus.

The species is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and unique morphology.
