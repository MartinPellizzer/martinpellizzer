Erica cephalotes

Erica cephalotes, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica cephalotes is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
