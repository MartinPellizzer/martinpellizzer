Erica diaphana

Erica diaphana, commonly known as the silver heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and delicate, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions. Erica diaphana is often found in rocky or coastal areas, where it forms dense, low-growing shrubs.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
