Eleogenus atropurpureus

Eleogenus atropurpureus, commonly known as the purplestem aster, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features purple-tinged stems and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
