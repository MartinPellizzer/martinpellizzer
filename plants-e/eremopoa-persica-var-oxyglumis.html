Eremopoa persica var. oxyglumis

Eremopoa persica var. oxyglumis, commonly known as Persian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control in arid landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental appeal make it a valued plant in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
