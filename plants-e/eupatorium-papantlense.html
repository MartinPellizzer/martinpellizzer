Eupatorium papantlense

Eupatorium papantlense, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
