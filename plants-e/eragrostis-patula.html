Eragrostis patula

Eragrostis patula, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in open areas such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in regions with temperate climates and well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a common indicator species in grassland ecosystems.
