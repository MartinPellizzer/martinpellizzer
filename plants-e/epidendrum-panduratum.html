Epidendrum panduratum

Epidendrum panduratum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendant, fleshy, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Epidendrum panduratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
