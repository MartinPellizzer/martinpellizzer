Eulophia elamellata

Eulophia elamellata, commonly known as the Elamellate Eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and produces a single, lance-shaped leaf. The plant blooms with a striking inflorescence of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its flowers have three sepals and three petals, with the lateral sepals often curving inward.

Eulophia elamellata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.
