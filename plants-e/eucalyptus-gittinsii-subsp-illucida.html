Eucalyptus gittinsii subsp. illucida

Eucalyptus gittinsii subsp.

illucida, commonly known as the Illucida mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
