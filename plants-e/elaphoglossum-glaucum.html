Elaphoglossum glaucum

Elaphoglossum glaucum, commonly known as the blue fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that often display a bluish or grayish hue on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Elaphoglossum glaucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
