Eucalyptus grasbyi

Eucalyptus grasbyi, commonly known as the Grasby's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of central Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often flaky bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers, which are clustered in inflorescences and attract a variety of pollinators. Eucalyptus grasbyi is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for native wildlife.
