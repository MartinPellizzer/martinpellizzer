Eupatorium hypomalacum

Eupatorium hypomalacum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, and its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium hypomalacum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
