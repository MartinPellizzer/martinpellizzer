Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. multicostatus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. multicostatus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spiny, tuberculate structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh desert conditions make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
