Erioglossum edule var. album

Erioglossum edule var. album, commonly known as the white-flowered edible erioglossum, is a variant of the erioglossum genus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink flowers, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are elongated and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

While not widely cultivated, it is valued for its potential edible qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
