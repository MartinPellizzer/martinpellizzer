Eleocharis laeviseta var. major

Eleocharis laeviseta var. major, commonly known as the tall spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and pale green color.

It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along riverbanks.
