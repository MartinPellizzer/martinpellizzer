Entada natalensis var. aculeata

Entada natalensis var. aculeata, commonly known as the Natal spurred bean, is a climbing legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves and showy, spurred flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
