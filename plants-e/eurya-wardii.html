Eurya wardii

Eurya wardii, commonly known as the Himalayan waxleaf, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and dark green, with a waxy texture that gives the plant its common name. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for birds.

Eurya wardii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
