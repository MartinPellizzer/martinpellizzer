Entosiphon indicum

Entosiphon indicum, commonly known as the Indian thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern and eastern Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its spiny branches and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits. Its botanical features include pinnately compound leaves and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
