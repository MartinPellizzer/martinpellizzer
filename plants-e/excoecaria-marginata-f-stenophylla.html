Excoecaria marginata f. stenophylla

Excoecaria marginata f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, dark green surface and prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
