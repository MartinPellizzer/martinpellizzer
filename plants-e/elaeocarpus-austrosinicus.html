Elaeocarpus austrosinicus

Elaeocarpus austrosinicus, commonly known as the Chinese elaeocarp, is a deciduous tree native to southern China and Vietnam.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are rich in nutrients. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in subtropical forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
