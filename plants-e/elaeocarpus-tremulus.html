Elaeocarpus tremulus

Elaeocarpus tremulus, commonly known as the trembling elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and distinctive, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
