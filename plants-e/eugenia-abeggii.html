Eugenia abeggii

Eugenia abeggii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or velvetleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties.
