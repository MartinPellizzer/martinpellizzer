Eupatorium hebecladum

Eupatorium hebecladum, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has slender, erect stems that are often branched. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium hebecladum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
