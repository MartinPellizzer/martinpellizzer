Echium giganteum

Echium giganteum, commonly known as the giant hedging plant, is a striking member of the Boraginaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated bracts that give it a unique appearance, often mistaken for flowers themselves. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged, ornamental look.

Echium giganteum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes due to its striking visual impact.
