Erigeron compositus var. multifidus

Erigeron compositus var. multifidus, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
