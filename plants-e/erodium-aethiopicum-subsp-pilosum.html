Erodium aethiopicum subsp. pilosum

Erodium aethiopicum subsp.

pilosum, commonly known as the hairy stormweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, pink or purple flowers and hairy stems and leaves.

The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to spread across various habitats.

The species is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
