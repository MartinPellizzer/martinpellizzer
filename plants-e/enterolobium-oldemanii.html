Enterolobium oldemanii

Enterolobium oldemanii, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces distinctive yellow pods that contain numerous seeds. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and usually white or pale yellow, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals.

The species is also valued for its timber and has been studied for its potential in agroforestry systems.
