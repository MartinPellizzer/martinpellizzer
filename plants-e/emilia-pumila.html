Emilia pumila

Emilia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf emilia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of only a few inches. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Emilia pumila is often cultivated as a garden plant for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
