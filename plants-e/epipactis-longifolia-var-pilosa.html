Epipactis longifolia var. pilosa

Epipactis longifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy long-lip orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pilosa.". The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with distinctive long, slender petals. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Epipactis longifolia var. pilosa is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms.
