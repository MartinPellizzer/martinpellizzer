Eugenia earlei

Eugenia earlei, commonly known as the dwarf wax tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
