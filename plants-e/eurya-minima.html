Eurya minima

Eurya minima, commonly known as the dwarf waxleaf eurya, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in shaded to partially sunny conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its compact growth and attractive foliage.

Eurya minima is valued for its ability to tolerate moist soils and its suitability for container gardening.
