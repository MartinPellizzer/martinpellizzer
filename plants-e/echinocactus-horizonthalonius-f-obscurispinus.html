Echinocactus horizonthalonius f. obscurispinus

Echinocactus horizonthalonius f. obscurispinus, commonly known as the dark spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the hedgehog cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are often a dark green to almost black in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its dark foliage. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
