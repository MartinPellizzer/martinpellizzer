Euphorbia portoricensis var. albescens

Euphorbia portoricensis var. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered prickly pear, is a succulent shrub native to Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and showy, white bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has adapted to the tropical climate with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in arid conditions. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This variety is notable for its unique appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
