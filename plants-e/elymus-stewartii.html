Elymus stewartii

Elymus stewartii, commonly known as Stewart's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that contain small, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Botanically, it is valued for its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
