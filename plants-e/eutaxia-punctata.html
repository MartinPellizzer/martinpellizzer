Eutaxia punctata

Eutaxia punctata, commonly known as the spotted eutaxia, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender, hairy stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbrella-like structures, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eutaxia punctata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
