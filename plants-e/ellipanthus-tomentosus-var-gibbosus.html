Ellipanthus tomentosus var. gibbosus

Ellipanthus tomentosus var. gibbosus, commonly known as the woolly elliptic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial species that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
