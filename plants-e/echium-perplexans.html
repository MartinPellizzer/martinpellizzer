Echium perplexans

Echium perplexans, commonly known as the strange thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Echium perplexans thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
