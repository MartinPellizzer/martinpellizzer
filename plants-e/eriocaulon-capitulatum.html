Eriocaulon capitulatum

Eriocaulon capitulatum, commonly known as the spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with dense clusters of tiny, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Eriocaulon capitulatum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
