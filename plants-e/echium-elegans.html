Echium elegans

Echium elegans, commonly known as the velvetleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-desert environments. It produces a tall, erect stem with clusters of small, showy flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium elegans is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to arid conditions.
