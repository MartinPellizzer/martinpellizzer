Eugenia vitis-idaea

Eugenia vitis-idaea, commonly known as cowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America and Eurasia.

It features small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and subarctic environments.

Eugenia vitis-idaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing food for wildlife.
