Erodium touchyanum

Erodium touchyanum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The seeds are dispersed by wind, allowing the plant to spread easily in new habitats.
