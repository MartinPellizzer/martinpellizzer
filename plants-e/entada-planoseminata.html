Entada planoseminata

Entada planoseminata, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a leguminous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces hard, woody pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain alkaloids and have been utilized for various therapeutic purposes.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
