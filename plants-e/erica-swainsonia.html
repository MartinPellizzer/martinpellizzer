Erica swainsonia

Erica swainsonia, commonly known as the Swainson's heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late winter to early spring. Erica swainsonia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
