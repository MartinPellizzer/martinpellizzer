Ericameria linearis

Ericameria linearis, commonly known as linear goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its linear, needle-like leaves and bright yellow flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats. It is drought-tolerant and well adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Ericameria linearis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
