Equisetum variegatum var. alaskanum

Equisetum variegatum var. alaskanum, commonly known as the Alaska horsetail, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures called annular rings. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stems, which are typically found in clusters. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

This variety is distinguished by its variegated foliage, which features a mix of green and yellowish-green colors.
