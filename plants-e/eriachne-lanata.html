Eriachne lanata

Eriachne lanata, commonly known as the hairy eriochloa, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Eriachne lanata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small fauna.
