Euphorbia erythradenia var. obovata

Euphorbia erythradenia var. obovata, commonly known as the red-stemmed spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that are typically reddish in color, which help it store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, a characteristic feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry conditions.
