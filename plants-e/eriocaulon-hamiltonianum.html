Eriocaulon hamiltonianum

Eriocaulon hamiltonianum, commonly known as the Hamilton's spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense mats on the surface of still or slow-moving water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers that are often obscured by the foliage.

Eriocaulon hamiltonianum is valued in aquatic gardens for its ornamental appearance and ability to help stabilize water ecosystems.
