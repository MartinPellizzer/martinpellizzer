Echinocactus × diabolicus

Echinocactus × diabolicus, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a hybrid cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spine-like structures. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
