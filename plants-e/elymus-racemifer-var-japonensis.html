Elymus racemifer var. japonensis

Elymus racemifer var. japonensis, commonly known as Japanese false wheat, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive appearance.

This variety is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
