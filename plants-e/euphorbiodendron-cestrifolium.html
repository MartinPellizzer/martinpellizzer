Euphorbiodendron cestrifolium

Euphorbiodendron cestrifolium, commonly known as the velvetleaf euphorbia, is a shrub native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving them a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Euphorbiodendron cestrifolium is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
