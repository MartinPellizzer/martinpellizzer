Epidendrum holochilum

Epidendrum holochilum, commonly known as the golden lip orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences with bright yellow petals and a contrasting red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements have made it a favored species among orchid enthusiasts.
