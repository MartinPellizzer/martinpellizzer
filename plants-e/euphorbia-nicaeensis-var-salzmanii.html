Euphorbia nicaeensis var. salzmanii

Euphorbia nicaeensis var. salzmanii, commonly known as the Salzman's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
