Embothrium strobilinum

Embothrium strobilinum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Embothrium strobilinum is well adapted to coastal and temperate climates, thriving in well-drained soils.
