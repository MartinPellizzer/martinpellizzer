Euryops subcarnosus

Euryops subcarnosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved daisy.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are often glandular, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Euryops subcarnosus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
