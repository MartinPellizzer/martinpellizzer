Eleocharis lankana subsp. lankana

Eleocharis lankana subsp.

lankana, commonly known as the Sri Lankan spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is native to Sri Lanka and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
