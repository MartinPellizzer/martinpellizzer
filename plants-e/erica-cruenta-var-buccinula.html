Erica cruenta var. buccinula

Erica cruenta var. buccinula, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
