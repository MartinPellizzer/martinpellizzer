Eleocharis albovaginata var. flaccida

Eleocharis albovaginata var. flaccida, commonly known as the flaccid spikerush, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow, flat, and often covered with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species thrives in shallow, waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
