Eugenia abunan

Eugenia abunan, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to red or black as they mature.

Eugenia abunan is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
