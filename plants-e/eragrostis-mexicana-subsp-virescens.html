Eragrostis mexicana subsp. virescens

Eragrostis mexicana subsp.

virescens, commonly known as green false brome, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually green or reddish in color, and it is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
