Eupatorium flourensifolium

Eupatorium flourensifolium, commonly known as the Florida feverfew, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties have made it a subject of interest in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
