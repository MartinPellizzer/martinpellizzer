Erica planifolia

Erica planifolia, commonly known as the flatleaf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces delicate white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and is adapted to rocky or coastal environments.

Erica planifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
