Erythrina carnea

Erythrina carnea, commonly known as the red coral tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with three to seven leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and reddish in color. Erythrina carnea is a legume, meaning it belongs to the Fabaceae family, and it has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria.

This plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
