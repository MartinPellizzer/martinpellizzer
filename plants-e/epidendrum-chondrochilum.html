Epidendrum chondrochilum

Epidendrum chondrochilum, commonly known as the chocolate slip, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, often found in humid, shaded environments. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires consistent moisture and bright, indirect light.

Its unique appearance and blooming habits make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
