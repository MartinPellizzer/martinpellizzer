Eragrostis nardoides

Eragrostis nardoides, commonly known as narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eragrostis nardoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in some regions for erosion control.
