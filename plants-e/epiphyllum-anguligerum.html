Epiphyllum anguligerum

Epiphyllum anguligerum, commonly known as the angular night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with flat, leaf-like stems that are often used as supports for other plants. Its flowers, which open at night, are large, showy, and typically white or pale pink in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
