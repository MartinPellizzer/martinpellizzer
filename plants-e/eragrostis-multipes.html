Eragrostis multipes

Eragrostis multipes, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, spreading growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis multipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
