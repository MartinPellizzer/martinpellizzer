Euphorbia theriaca var. spurca

Euphorbia theriaca var. spurca, commonly known as the spurge or blistering weed, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, milky sap that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant typically features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
