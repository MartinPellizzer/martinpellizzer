Eurya meizophylla

Eurya meizophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes many plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often densely arranged along the stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces small, red berries that are edible for some bird species.

Eurya meizophylla is often found in moist, shaded forest understories where it thrives in partial to full shade.
