Eriocaulon dimerum

Eriocaulon dimerum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Eriocaulon dimerum plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
