Eriosyce odieri subsp. glabrescens

Eriosyce odieri subsp.

glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous eriosyce, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a thin layer of whitish wax.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is prized by cactus enthusiasts for its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments.
