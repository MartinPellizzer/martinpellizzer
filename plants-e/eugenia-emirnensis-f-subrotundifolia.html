Eugenia emirnensis f. subrotundifolia

Eugenia emirnensis f. subrotundifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eugenia, is a botanical variant of the species Eugenia emirnensis.

It is characterized by its subrotundifoliate leaves, which are broader and more rounded at the base compared to the typical leaves of the species. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional medicine in its native habitat.
