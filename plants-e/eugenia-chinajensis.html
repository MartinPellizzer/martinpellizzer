Eugenia chinajensis

Eugenia chinajensis, commonly known as the Chinese tallowtree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically reddish or orange in color. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.

The species is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and as a source of tallow.
