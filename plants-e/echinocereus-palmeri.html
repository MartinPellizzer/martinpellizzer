Echinocereus palmeri

Echinocereus palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, a fleshy, segmented pear, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Echinocereus palmeri plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
