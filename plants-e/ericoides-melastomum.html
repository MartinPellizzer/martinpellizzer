Ericoides melastomum

Ericoides melastomum, commonly known as the rock melastome, is a low-growing perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves and showy, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in xeric habitats such as dry woodlands and slopes.

Ericoides melastomum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
