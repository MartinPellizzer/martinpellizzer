Erycibe floribunda

Erycibe floribunda, commonly known as the fragrant white hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Erycibe floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
