Eriocaulon saxatile

Eriocaulon saxatile, commonly known as the rock marsh plant, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, often covered with a fine, white pubescence.

Eriocaulon saxatile is adapted to moist soils and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams.
