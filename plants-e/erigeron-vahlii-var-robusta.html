Erigeron vahlii var. robusta

Erigeron vahlii var. robusta, commonly known as the robust erigeron, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, white to pale blue ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. It is adapted to thrive in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
