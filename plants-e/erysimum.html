Erysimum

Erysimum, commonly known as wallflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the mustard family, Brassicaceae.

These plants are characterized by their showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in spring or early summer. Erysimum species are often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. They produce small, rounded seed pods that contain numerous seeds, facilitating their spread.

The leaves are usually alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to their distinctive appearance.
