Eria abbreviata var. anceps

Eria abbreviata var. anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate humidity and light conditions.
