Erythroxylum areolatum var. affine

Erythroxylum areolatum var. affine, commonly known as the Areola leafed Erythroxylum, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub or small tree belonging to the Erythroxylaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental value. The plant features simple, alternate leaves with a distinctive areolate pattern on the underside, giving it its specific name. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces small, spherical berries.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique foliage and potential traditional uses in herbal medicine.
