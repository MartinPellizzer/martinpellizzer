Epilobium anatolicum

Epilobium anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, including Turkey and the Mediterranean. The plant features erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in the summer months.

Epilobium anatolicum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.
