Escallonia × bracteata

Escallonia × bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate escallonia, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Escallonia × bracteata is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its widespread cultivation.
