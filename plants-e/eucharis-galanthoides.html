Eucharis galanthoides

Eucharis galanthoides, commonly known as the white elephant plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family and is characterized by its large, waxy, white flowers that resemble lilies. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eucharis galanthoides is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its striking appearance.
