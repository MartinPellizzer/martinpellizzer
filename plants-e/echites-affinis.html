Echites affinis

Echites affinis, commonly known as the broadleaf echites, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with prominent central disks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
