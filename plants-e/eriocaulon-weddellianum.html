Eriocaulon weddellianum

Eriocaulon weddellianum, commonly known as the Weddell's spike-moss, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eriocaulon weddellianum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
