Euphorbia hindsiana

Euphorbia hindsiana, commonly known as the Hinds' spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a caudex, which is a thick, short stem that stores water, and it has spiny, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
