Eugenia nematopoda

Eugenia nematopoda, commonly known as the small-leaved guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are followed by edible, yellow or orange fruits. Eugenia nematopoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in home gardens.

Its growth habit and foliage make it suitable for use in tropical landscapes and as a hedge plant.
