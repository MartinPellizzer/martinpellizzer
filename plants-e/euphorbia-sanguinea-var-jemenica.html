Euphorbia sanguinea var. jemenica

Euphorbia sanguinea var. jemenica, commonly known as the Yemen bloodwort, is a succulent plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
