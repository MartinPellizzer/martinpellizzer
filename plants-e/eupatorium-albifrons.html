Eupatorium albifrons

Eupatorium albifrons, commonly known as whiteface boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium albifrons is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
