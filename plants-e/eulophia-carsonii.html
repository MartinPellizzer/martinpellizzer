Eulophia carsonii

Eulophia carsonii, commonly known as Carson's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering structure. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia carsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in its native habitat.
