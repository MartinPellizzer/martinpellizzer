Eleocharis bonariensis

Eleocharis bonariensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial grass-like plant that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies in moist soils.

Eleocharis bonariensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
