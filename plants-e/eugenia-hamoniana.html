Eugenia hamoniana

Eugenia hamoniana, commonly known as the Java apple or wild guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, yellow or red fruits that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
