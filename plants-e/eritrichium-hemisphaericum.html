Eritrichium hemisphaericum

Eritrichium hemisphaericum, commonly known as the mountain eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, delicate flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, adapted to thrive in cold, high-altitude environments. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the surrounding vegetation has emerged.

Eritrichium hemisphaericum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
