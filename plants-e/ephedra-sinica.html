Ephedra sinica

Ephedra sinica, commonly known as Ma Huang, is a shrub native to arid regions of China, Mongolia, and Russia.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and contains the alkaloid ephedrine, which has been used in traditional medicine. Its growth is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with thick cuticles and deep root systems.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically significant in herbal remedies.
