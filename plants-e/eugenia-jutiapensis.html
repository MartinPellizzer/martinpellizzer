Eugenia jutiapensis

Eugenia jutiapensis, commonly known as the Jutiapa guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and typically have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia jutiapensis is often found in humid, lowland areas and is valued for its ornamental and potential culinary uses.
