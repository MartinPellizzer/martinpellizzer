Erica longifissa

Erica longifissa, commonly known as the long-stemmed heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica longifissa typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
