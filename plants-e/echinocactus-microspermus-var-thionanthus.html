Echinocactus microspermus var. thionanthus

Echinocactus microspermus var. thionanthus, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, sharp spines that provide protection against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and compact form make it an ideal choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
