Elaeagnus multiflora var. crispa

Elaeagnus multiflora var. crispa, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
