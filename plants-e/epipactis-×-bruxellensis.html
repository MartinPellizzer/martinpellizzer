Epipactis × bruxellensis

Epipactis × bruxellensis, commonly known as the Brussels hybrid, is a natural hybrid between Epipactis palustris and Epipactis helleborine.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its upright, leafy stems. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip, typical of the Orchidaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands.

Epipactis × bruxellensis is often found in Europe and has adapted to a variety of soil types.
