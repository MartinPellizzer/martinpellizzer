Eriophyllum lanatum var. arachnoideum

Eriophyllum lanatum var. arachnoideum, commonly known as the woolly goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. Its small, scale-like leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
