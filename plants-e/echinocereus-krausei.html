Echinocereus krausei

Echinocereus krausei, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its ability to store water in its stems makes it drought-tolerant, though it benefits from occasional watering during the growing season.

Echinocereus krausei is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and seasonal blooming habit.
