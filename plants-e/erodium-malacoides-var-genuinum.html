Erodium malacoides var. genuinum

Erodium malacoides var. genuinum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
