Echinochloa commutata

Echinochloa commutata, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical spikelets that are typically yellow-green in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadside areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
