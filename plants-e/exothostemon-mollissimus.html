Exothostemon mollissimus

Exothostemon mollissimus, commonly known as the velvetleaf balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for its striking foliage and floral display.
