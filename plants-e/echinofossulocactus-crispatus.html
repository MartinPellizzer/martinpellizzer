Echinofossulocactus crispatus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus, commonly known as the crinkled spine cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with white, bristly spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping projects.
