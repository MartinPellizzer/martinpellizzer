Eriocaulon carsonii subsp. orientale

Eriocaulon carsonii subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental hairgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
