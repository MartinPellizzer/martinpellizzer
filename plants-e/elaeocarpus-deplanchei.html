Elaeocarpus deplanchei

Elaeocarpus deplanchei, commonly known as the Madagascar satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically surrounded by a fleshy, edible pericarp. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats within humid forests.

Elaeocarpus deplanchei is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally in craftsmanship and construction.
