Eupatorium subscandens

Eupatorium subscandens, commonly known as the swamp boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features erect, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have serrated edges. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer to early fall.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
