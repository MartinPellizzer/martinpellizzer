Eragrostis egregia

Eragrostis egregia, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence on the leaves. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Eragrostis egregia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardy nature.
