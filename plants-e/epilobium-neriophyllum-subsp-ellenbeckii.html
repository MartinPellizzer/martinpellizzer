Epilobium neriophyllum subsp. ellenbeckii

Epilobium neriophyllum subsp.

ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Ellenbeck's willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

The plant features slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to reddish, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
