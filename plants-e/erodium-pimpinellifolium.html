Erodium pimpinellifolium

Erodium pimpinellifolium, commonly known as stormy weather or keyflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a small annual herb with delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves, which are trifoliate in appearance, and produces small, hairy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its delicate blooms.
