Erica rubida

Erica rubida, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, showy pink flowers that bloom in spring. Erica rubida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
