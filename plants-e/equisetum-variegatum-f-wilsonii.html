Equisetum variegatum f. wilsonii

Equisetum variegatum f. wilsonii, commonly known as the variegated horsetail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, segmented stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as along stream banks or in damp woodlands.

Its unique appearance and historical use in traditional medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
