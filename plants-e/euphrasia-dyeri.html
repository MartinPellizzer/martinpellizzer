Euphrasia dyeri

Euphrasia dyeri, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers with five petals and prominent yellow spots, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euphrasia dyeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
