Erica parviflora var. inermis

Erica parviflora var. inermis, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It features compact, needle-like leaves that are dark green and covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
