Eugenia pseudomalaccensis

Eugenia pseudomalaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
