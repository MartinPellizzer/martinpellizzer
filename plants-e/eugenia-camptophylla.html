Eugenia camptophylla

Eugenia camptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for various garden settings.
