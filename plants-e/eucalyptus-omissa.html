Eucalyptus omissa

Eucalyptus omissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallet tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is smooth and peels in thin, papery layers, and it is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

Eucalyptus omissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
