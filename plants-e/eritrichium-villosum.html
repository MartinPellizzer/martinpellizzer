Eritrichium villosum

Eritrichium villosum, commonly known as the silky eritrichium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a hardy addition to alpine gardens. Its fluffy appearance and early blooming habit make it an attractive wildflower for ecological restoration projects.

Eritrichium villosum is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed areas.
