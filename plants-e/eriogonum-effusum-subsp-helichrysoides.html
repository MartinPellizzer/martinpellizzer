Eriogonum effusum subsp. helichrysoides

Eriogonum effusum subsp.

helichrysoides, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of yellow to golden-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It has small, narrow leaves and a low-growing, mat-like structure.

This subspecies is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
