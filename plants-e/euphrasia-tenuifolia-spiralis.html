Euphrasia tenuifolia spiralis

Euphrasia tenuifolia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral yellow-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spirally arranged stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its delicate appearance and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils.
