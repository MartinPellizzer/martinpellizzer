Echinopsis platinospina

Echinopsis platinospina, commonly known as the silver spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in white, spiny areoles that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. Echinopsis platinospina is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience in harsh environments.
