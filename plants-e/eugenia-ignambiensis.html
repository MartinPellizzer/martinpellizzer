Eugenia ignambiensis

Eugenia ignambiensis, commonly known as the West Australian nut, is a small evergreen shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers followed by edible, oval-shaped nuts encased in a fibrous husk. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
