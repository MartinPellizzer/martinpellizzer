Eriocaulon nanellum var. nosoriense

Eriocaulon nanellum var. nosoriense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of tiny, inconspicuous flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Due to its delicate structure and specific ecological requirements, it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
