Eupatorium triosteifolium

Eupatorium triosteifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.
