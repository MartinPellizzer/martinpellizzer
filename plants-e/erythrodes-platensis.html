Erythrodes platensis

Erythrodes platensis, commonly known as the Brazilian redwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing several seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing natural protection against pests and environmental stress.

Erythrodes platensis plays a vital role in the ecosystem by supporting a diverse array of flora and fauna.
