Elaphrium graveolens

Elaphrium graveolens, commonly known as the stinking nightshade, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dark green leaves and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
