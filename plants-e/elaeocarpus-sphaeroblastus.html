Elaeocarpus sphaeroblastus

Elaeocarpus sphaeroblastus, commonly known as the golden apple gum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in rainforests and coastal areas. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red fruits.

The fruits are edible and contain a single seed, making it an important species for both ecological and potential agricultural purposes.
