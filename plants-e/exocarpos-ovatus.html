Exocarpos ovatus

Exocarpos ovatus, commonly known as the oval wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its rough, peeling bark and opposite, ovate-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, round, wax-coated fruits. The fruits, which are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe, are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in tropical regions.
