Equisetum × mchaffieae

Equisetum × mchaffieae, commonly known as the hybrid scouring rush, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Equisetum arvense and Equisetum hyemale.

It is a member of the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a spore-bearing structure called a strobilus. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Its historical use as a natural scouring agent has contributed to its common name, though it is not edible and should be handled with care.
