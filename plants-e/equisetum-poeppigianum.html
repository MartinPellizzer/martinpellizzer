Equisetum poeppigianum

Equisetum poeppigianum, commonly known as the narrow horsetail, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, slender, unbranched stems.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wet meadows and stream banks. The plant features whorls of small, needle-like leaves and produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stem. It is notable for its ancient lineage, dating back to the Carboniferous period.

Equisetum poeppigianum is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in ecological succession.
