Elymus vassiljevii var. laxe-pilosus

Elymus vassiljevii var. laxe-pilosus, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, silky hairs covering its leaves and stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
