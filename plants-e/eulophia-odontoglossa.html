Eulophia odontoglossa

Eulophia odontoglossa, commonly known as the toothed-lobed eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and purple, often with a distinctive tooth-like lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia odontoglossa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
