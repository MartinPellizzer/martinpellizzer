Euphorbia boinensis

Euphorbia boinensis, commonly known as the Bornean spurge, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse species with unique floral structures. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, characterized by its smooth, often thorny stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia.

The plant is adapted to humid, lowland environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and animals.
