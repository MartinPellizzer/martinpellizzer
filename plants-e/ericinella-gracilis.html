Ericinella gracilis

Ericinella gracilis, commonly known as the slender ericinella, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Ericinella gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal dunes.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
