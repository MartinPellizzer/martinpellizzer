Echinocereus fendleri var. rectispinus

Echinocereus fendleri var. rectispinus, commonly known as the Mexican hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring thick, ribbed stems and spiny, cylindrical fruits. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its slow growth rate and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for desert landscaping.
