Echinofossulocactus parksianus

Echinofossulocactus parksianus, commonly known as the Parksian cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in succulent gardens.
