Eugenia brunnea

Eugenia brunnea, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically round and have a thick, leathery rind. Its botanical features include smooth, bark-like stems and clusters of small, elliptical leaves.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
