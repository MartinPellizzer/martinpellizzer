Eremophila goodwinii subsp. goodwinii

Eremophila goodwinii subsp.

goodwinii, commonly known as Goodwin's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces spikes of tubular, often red or pink flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that reduce water loss, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.
