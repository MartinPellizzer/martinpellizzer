Euclea acutifolia

Euclea acutifolia, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter. The plant produces striking white flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Euclea acutifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
