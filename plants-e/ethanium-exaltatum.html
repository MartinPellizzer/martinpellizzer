Ethanium exaltatum

Ethanium exaltatum, commonly known as the tall flameflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height, supporting clusters of vibrant red, tubular flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Its unique floral structure attracts pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in its ecosystem.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, Ethanium exaltatum is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
