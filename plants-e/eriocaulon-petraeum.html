Eriocaulon petraeum

Eriocaulon petraeum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves that form dense, mat-like growths on rocky substrates. The plant typically grows in shallow, still waters such as ponds and wetlands, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, with flowers that are often hidden among the foliage.

Eriocaulon petraeum plays a role in stabilizing sediments and providing habitat for microorganisms in its native ecosystems.
