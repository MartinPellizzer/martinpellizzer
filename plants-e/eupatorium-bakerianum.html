Eupatorium bakerianum

Eupatorium bakerianum, commonly known as Baker's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.
