Erica ovata

Erica ovata, commonly known as the oval heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica ovata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
