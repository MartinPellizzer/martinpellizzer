Eria truncata

Eria truncata, commonly known as the truncated eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its small, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces pale yellow or greenish-white blooms.

Eria truncata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
