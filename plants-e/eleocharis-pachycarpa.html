Eleocharis pachycarpa

Eleocharis pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited spike-rush, is a species of grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Eleocharis pachycarpa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
