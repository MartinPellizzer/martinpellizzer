Erythrocephalum microcephalum

Erythrocephalum microcephalum, commonly known as the small-headed firehead, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, showy red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has slender, hairy stems and small, linear leaves, and it is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
