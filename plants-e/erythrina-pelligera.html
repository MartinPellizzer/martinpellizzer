Erythrina pelligera

Erythrina pelligera, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its pods are elongated, containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation, benefiting soil fertility in its native habitats.
