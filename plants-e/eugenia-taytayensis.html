Eugenia taytayensis

Eugenia taytayensis, commonly known as the Taytay guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
