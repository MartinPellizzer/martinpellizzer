Eschscholzia crassula

Eschscholzia crassula, commonly known as the dwarf poppy, is a small, annual herb native to California.

It features delicate, bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a wildflower and its use in ecological restoration projects.
