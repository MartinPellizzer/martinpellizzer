Eupatorium kuntzei

Eupatorium kuntzei, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium kuntzei is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
