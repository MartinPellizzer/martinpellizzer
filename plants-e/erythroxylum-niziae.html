Erythroxylum niziae

Erythroxylum niziae, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and climbing habit. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence the common name, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Erythroxylum niziae is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
