Echinochloa setigera

Echinochloa setigera, commonly known as cockspur grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often forming dense stands.

Echinochloa setigera is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
