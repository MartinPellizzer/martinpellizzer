Eragrostis membranacea

Eragrostis membranacea, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, erect stems that often form dense, mat-like growths. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale blue or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
