Eriudaphus mundii

Eriudaphus mundii, commonly known as the common mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its flowering period occurs during the late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical structure is adapted to its harsh environment, with a deep root system that helps it survive in low-water conditions.
