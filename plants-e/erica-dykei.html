Erica dykei

Erica dykei, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica dykei is well adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for garden landscaping.
