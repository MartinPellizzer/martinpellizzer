Eutrema edwardsii subsp. heterophyllum

Eutrema edwardsii subsp.

heterophyllum, commonly known as the coastal sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal environments. It has hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is often found in dune ecosystems and is well-suited to withstand harsh coastal conditions.
