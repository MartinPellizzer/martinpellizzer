Euphorbia transtagana

Euphorbia transtagana, commonly known as the red-veined succulent, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and vibrant red veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia transtagana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
