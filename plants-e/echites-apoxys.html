Echites apoxys

Echites apoxys, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy, succulent leaves adapted to store water. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open woodlands and shrublands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
