Eriosema zeyheri

Eriosema zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name "eriosema," derived from the Greek words for "hairy" and "seed.". It produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.

Eriosema zeyheri is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, thriving in well-drained soils.
