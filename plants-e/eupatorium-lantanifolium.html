Eupatorium lantanifolium

Eupatorium lantanifolium, commonly known as sweet weed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a mild, aromatic scent. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland habitats.
