Euphorbia australis var. typica

Euphorbia australis var. typica, commonly known as the Australian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Australia.

It features erect, branched stems covered in a layer of white, waxy hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Euphorbia australis var. typica is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or sandy soils.
