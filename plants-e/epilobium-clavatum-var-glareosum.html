Epilobium clavatum var. glareosum

Epilobium clavatum var. glareosum, commonly known as the golden willow or yellow willow, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows along riverbanks and moist habitats. The plant features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
