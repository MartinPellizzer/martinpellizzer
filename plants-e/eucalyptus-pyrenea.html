Eucalyptus pyrenea

Eucalyptus pyrenea, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three. Eucalyptus pyrenea is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
