Emilia lisowskiana

Emilia lisowskiana, commonly known as the mountain emilia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Emilia lisowskiana is adapted to cold and arid environments, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun.
