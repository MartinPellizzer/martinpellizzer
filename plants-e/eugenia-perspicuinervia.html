Eugenia perspicuinervia

Eugenia perspicuinervia, commonly known as the golden eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

Eugenia perspicuinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens.
