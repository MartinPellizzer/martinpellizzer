Echites nodosus

Echites nodosus, commonly known as the nodding echites, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, branching stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Echites nodosus is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
