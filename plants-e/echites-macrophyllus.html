Echites macrophyllus

Echites macrophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking foliage, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
