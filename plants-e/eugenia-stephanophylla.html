Eugenia stephanophylla

Eugenia stephanophylla, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia stephanophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
