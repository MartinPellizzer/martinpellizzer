Eriogonum vimineum subsp. gracile

Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as the slender leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, slender leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a mat-forming growth habit and produces seed capsules that release tiny seeds. Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

gracile is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to arid environments.
