Ervum unijugum

Ervum unijugum, commonly known as the single-winged vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive single-winged pods, which are typically green or brown in color. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a delicate appearance to its foliage. Ervum unijugum is often found in sandy or disturbed soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitats.
