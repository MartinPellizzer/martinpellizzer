Eugenia collina

Eugenia collina, commonly known as the mountain satinash, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils along stream banks and in shaded forest understories. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, with a pleasant, aromatic scent.

Eugenia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
