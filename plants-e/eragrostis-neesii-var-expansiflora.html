Eragrostis neesii var. expansiflora

Eragrostis neesii var. expansiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue and a hairy surface.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
