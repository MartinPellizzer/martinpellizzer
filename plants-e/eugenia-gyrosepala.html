Eugenia gyrosepala

Eugenia gyrosepala, commonly known as the waxy apple or wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves and small, clustered white flowers. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
