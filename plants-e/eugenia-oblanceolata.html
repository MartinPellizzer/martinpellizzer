Eugenia oblanceolata

Eugenia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, which is typically small and yellowish, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia oblanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
