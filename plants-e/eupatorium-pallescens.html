Eupatorium pallescens

Eupatorium pallescens, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, often growing in moist to wet soil.

Eupatorium pallescens is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
