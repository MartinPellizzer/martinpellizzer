Eugenia percrenata

Eugenia percrenata, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Eugenia percrenata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
