Evolvulus glomeratus var. strigosus

Evolvulus glomeratus var. strigosus, commonly known as the hairy blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, hairy texture, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various growing conditions.
