Echinocactus diguetii

Echinocactus diguetii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that is densely covered with spines and has a distinctive golden-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
