Eugenia hispidiflora

Eugenia hispidiflora, commonly known as the hairy-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia hispidiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
