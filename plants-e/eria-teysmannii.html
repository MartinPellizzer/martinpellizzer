Eria teysmannii

Eria teysmannii, commonly known as the Teyssmann's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
