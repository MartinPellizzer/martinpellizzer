Eupatorium lilloi

Eupatorium lilloi, commonly known as Lillo's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered umbels. It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and conservation efforts.
