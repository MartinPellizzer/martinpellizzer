Erythrina lanata subsp. calvescens

Erythrina lanata subsp.

calvescens, commonly known as the hairy flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The plant has compound leaves with long, hairy stalks, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
