Encyclia trachychila

Encyclia trachychila, commonly known as the black-tongued orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are usually dark purple to black, with a distinctive yellow lip, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Encyclia trachychila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
