Elaeagnus conferta var. silhetensis

Elaeagnus conferta var. silhetensis, commonly known as the Silhet silverleaf, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly in Bangladesh and India.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, and it bears small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subtropical climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
