Eryngium petiolatum var. armatum

Eryngium petiolatum var. armatum, commonly known as the prickly eryngo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a rosette of spiny, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from blue to purple, resembling a thistle. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
