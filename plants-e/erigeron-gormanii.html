Erigeron gormanii

Erigeron gormanii, commonly known as Gorman's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-tolerant nature.
