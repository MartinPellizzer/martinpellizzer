Erica noliflora

Erica noliflora, commonly known as the flowering heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes a variety of shrubs and small trees known for their adaptability to acidic soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

Erica noliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
