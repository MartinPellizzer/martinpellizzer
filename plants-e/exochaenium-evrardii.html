Exochaenium evrardii

Exochaenium evrardii, commonly known as the Evrard's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
