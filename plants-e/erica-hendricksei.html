Erica hendricksei

Erica hendricksei, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter, adding a striking contrast to its evergreen foliage. Erica hendricksei thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
