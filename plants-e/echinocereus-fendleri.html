Echinocereus fendleri

Echinocereus fendleri, commonly known as the fishhook cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by wildlife. Its adaptations to arid environments include a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and the ability to store water in its stems.

Echinocereus fendleri is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
