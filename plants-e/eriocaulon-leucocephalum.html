Eriocaulon leucocephalum

Eriocaulon leucocephalum, commonly known as the white-headed spike-rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact inflorescence that bears small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens or as a ground cover in wetland restoration projects.
