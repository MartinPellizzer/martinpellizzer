Empogona ovalifolia var. glabrata

Empogona ovalifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved false grape, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
