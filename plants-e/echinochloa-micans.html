Echinochloa micans

Echinochloa micans, commonly known as silky barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it is considered a serious weed in many crops.
