Eugenia chiquitensis var. pseudoverticillata

Eugenia chiquitensis var. pseudoverticillata, commonly known as the Chiquitania myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Paraguay.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters. The fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature, often containing a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
