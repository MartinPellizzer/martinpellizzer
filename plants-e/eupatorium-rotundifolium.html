Eupatorium rotundifolium

Eupatorium rotundifolium, commonly known as roundleaf thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are usually white or pale purple and attract various pollinators.

The leaves are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
