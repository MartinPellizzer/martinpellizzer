Eremophila goodwinii

Eremophila goodwinii, commonly known as Goodwin's erymanthe, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.

Eremophila goodwinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
