Euryomyrtus alpina

Euryomyrtus alpina, commonly known as the alpine myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine regions, where it is adapted to cold and dry conditions.

Euryomyrtus alpina plays a role in its native ecosystems by providing habitat and food for local wildlife.
