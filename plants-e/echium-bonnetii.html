Echium bonnetii

Echium bonnetii, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Echium bonnetii is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
