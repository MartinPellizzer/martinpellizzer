Euphorbia splendens var. hislopii

Euphorbia splendens var. hislopii, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features striking red, orange, and yellow bracts that resemble firecrackers, giving it its common name. The plant has a short, thick stem with dense clusters of small, green, spiny leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the showy bracts.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
