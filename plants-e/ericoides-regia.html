Ericoides regia

Ericoides regia, commonly known as the royal eric, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. Its flowers, which are typically pink or white, bloom in late spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Ericoides regia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
