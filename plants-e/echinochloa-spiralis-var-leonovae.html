Echinochloa spiralis var. leonovae

Echinochloa spiralis var. leonovae, commonly known as the Leonovae panicledgrass, is a grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
