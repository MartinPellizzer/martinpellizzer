Erythrina loureiroi

Erythrina loureiroi, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling flames, hence its common name. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color. It produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
