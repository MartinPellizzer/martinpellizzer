Excoecaria manniana

Excoecaria manniana, commonly known as the croton tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a thick, rough bark and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans but toxic to livestock. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
