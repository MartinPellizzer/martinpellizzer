Echium ferox

Echium ferox, commonly known as the fierce thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its seed heads are distinctive, containing numerous small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Echium ferox is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
