Erica leucantha

Erica leucantha, commonly known as the white heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is part of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in spring and summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Erica leucantha thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
