Eugenia bracteolosa

Eugenia bracteolosa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes.
