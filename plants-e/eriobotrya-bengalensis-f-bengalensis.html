Eriobotrya bengalensis f. bengalensis

Eriobotrya bengalensis f. bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal loquat, is a variant of the loquat species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, followed by yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
