Eugenia boliviana

Eugenia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit, which is typically a small, yellow or red berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
