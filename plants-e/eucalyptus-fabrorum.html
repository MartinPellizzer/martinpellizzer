Eucalyptus fabrorum

Eucalyptus fabrorum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Western Australia and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Eucalyptus fabrorum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
