Ehretia dicksonii var. tilioides

Ehretia dicksonii var. tilioides, commonly known as the tilioid flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded crown and smooth, greyish-brown bark. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a pointed tip, often with a slightly waxy texture. It produces showy, clustered inflorescences of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
