Euphorbia pseudoglobosa var. oshoekensis

Euphorbia pseudoglobosa var. oshoekensis, commonly known as the Oshoek euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its slow growth and resilience.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
