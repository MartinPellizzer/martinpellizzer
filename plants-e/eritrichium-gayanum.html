Eritrichium gayanum

Eritrichium gayanum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of tiny, yellowish-white flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
