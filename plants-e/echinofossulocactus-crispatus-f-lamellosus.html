Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. lamellosus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. lamellosus, commonly known as the crested echinofossulocactus, is a rare and distinctive cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, flattened, ribbed stems that form a dense, compact structure. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal. Its slow growth rate and specific habitat requirements make it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Due to its rarity and conservation status, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
