Evrardiana poilanei

Evrardiana poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a rare and endemic orchid species found exclusively in the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that are typically a combination of white, pink, and purple hues. The plant has a slender, upright stem and produces a single inflorescence with several showy blooms. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
