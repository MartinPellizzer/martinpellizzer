Elephantopus riparius

Elephantopus riparius, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a cluster.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional African medicine for various ailments.
