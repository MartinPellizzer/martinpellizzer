Echinopsis rommyana

Echinopsis rommyana, commonly known as Rommy's cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that are typically white to yellow in color. The plant produces white flowers during the warmer months, which are often nocturnal in bloom. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Echinopsis rommyana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in succulent gardens.
