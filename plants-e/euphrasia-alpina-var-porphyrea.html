Euphrasia alpina var. porphyrea

Euphrasia alpina var. porphyrea, commonly known as the Alpine eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent dark veins. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are oval-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine, sticky hairs.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties, particularly for eye and skin conditions.
