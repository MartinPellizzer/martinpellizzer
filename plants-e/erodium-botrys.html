Erodium botrys

Erodium botrys, commonly known as the clustered filaree, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Polygonaceae, also known as the buckwheat family.
