Eupatorium atropurpureum

Eupatorium atropurpureum, commonly known as black snakeroot, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features upright, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
