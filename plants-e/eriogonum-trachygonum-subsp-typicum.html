Eriogonum trachygonum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum trachygonum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its gray-green, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is part of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its adaptability to arid environments.
