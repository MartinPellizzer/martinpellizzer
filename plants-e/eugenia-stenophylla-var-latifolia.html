Eugenia stenophylla var. latifolia

Eugenia stenophylla var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved evergreen, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, edible berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their ornamental qualities.
