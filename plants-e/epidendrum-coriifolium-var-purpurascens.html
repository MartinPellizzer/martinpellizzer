Epidendrum coriifolium var. purpurascens

Epidendrum coriifolium var. purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple-leafed Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves that range from green to deep purple, depending on light exposure. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
