Euonymus verrucosoides var. viridiflorus

Euonymus verrucosoides var. viridiflorus, commonly known as the greenflower woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red, orb-shaped fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often used in woodland gardens for its ornamental value.

The species is notable for its unique foliage and seasonal color changes, making it a popular choice for native plant landscaping.
