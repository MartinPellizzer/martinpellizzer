Epipactis persica

Epipactis persica, commonly known as the Persian helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with dark markings, and they emit a faint, sweet fragrance.

Epipactis persica is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments, often in woodlands or along stream banks.
