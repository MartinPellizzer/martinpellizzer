Eugenioides revolutum

Eugenioides revolutum, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Eugenioides revolutum is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
