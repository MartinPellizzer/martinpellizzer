Eriogonum idahoense

Eriogonum idahoense, commonly known as Idaho flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and mountain slopes. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum idahoense plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
