Ervilia sylvatica

Ervilia sylvatica, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. Its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, red and purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

Ervilia sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
