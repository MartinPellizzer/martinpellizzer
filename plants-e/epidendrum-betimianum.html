Epidendrum betimianum

Epidendrum betimianum, commonly known as the Betsileo Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within humid forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in racemes, with a variety of colors including white, pink, and purple.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and adaptability.
