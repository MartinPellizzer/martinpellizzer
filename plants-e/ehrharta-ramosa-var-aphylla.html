Ehrharta ramosa var. aphylla

Ehrharta ramosa var. aphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
