Echidnopsis lavraniana

Echidnopsis lavraniana, commonly known as the silver star cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and distinctive star-shaped flowers. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in arid regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
