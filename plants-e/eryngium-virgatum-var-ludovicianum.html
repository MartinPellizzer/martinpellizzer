Eryngium virgatum var. ludovicianum

Eryngium virgatum var. ludovicianum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by bracts that resemble a crown, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has silvery-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its unique structure and color make it a notable addition to native plant gardens.
