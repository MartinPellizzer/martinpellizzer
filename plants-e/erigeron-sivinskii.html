Erigeron sivinskii

Erigeron sivinskii, commonly known as the Sivinsky's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has finely divided, silvery-green leaves. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
