Eriogonum vimineum subsp. nortonii

Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

nortonii, commonly known as Norton's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing white to pale pink flowers that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually opposite and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
