Enicosanthum paradoxum

Enicosanthum paradoxum, commonly known as the paradoxical twenty-one flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant species characterized by its unique floral structure.

It features a single, intricate flower with 21 petals arranged in a spiral pattern, a trait that gives it its scientific name. The plant is native to a specific microhabitat in the high-altitude regions of South America, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Due to its rarity and unusual morphology, Enicosanthum paradoxum is of great interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
