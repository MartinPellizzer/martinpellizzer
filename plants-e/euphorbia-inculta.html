Euphorbia inculta

Euphorbia inculta, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a spiny shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in dry, rocky environments and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
