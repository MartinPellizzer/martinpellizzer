Euonymus euscaphis

Euonymus euscaphis, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange foliage that turns vibrant in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist through the winter. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

Euonymus euscaphis thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
