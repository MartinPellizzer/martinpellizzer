Erica vagans

Erica vagans, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica vagans thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal or sandy environments.
