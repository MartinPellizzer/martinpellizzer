Eupatorium liatrideum

Eupatorium liatrideum, commonly known as the eastern boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in moist, wooded areas. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eupatorium liatrideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
