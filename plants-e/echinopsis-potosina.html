Echinopsis potosina

Echinopsis potosina, commonly known as the Potosi cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a short, thick stem and produces white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to thrive in arid environments with minimal water. Its structure and appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Echinopsis potosina is also valued for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
