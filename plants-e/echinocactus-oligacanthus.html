Echinocactus oligacanthus

Echinocactus oligacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick, cylindrical stem that is covered with prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
