Elaeocarpus parvilimbus

Elaeocarpus parvilimbus, commonly known as the small-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical tree belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. Its small, white flowers are followed by distinctive, fleshy, red fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
