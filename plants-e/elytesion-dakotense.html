Elytesion dakotense

Elytesion dakotense, commonly known as the Dakota grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Great Plains of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in prairie ecosystems.

Elytesion dakotense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
