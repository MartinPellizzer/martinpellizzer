Eria mollis

Eria mollis, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, velvety leaves that store water, adapting it to its humid, shaded habitats. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and indirect light.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts.
