Ericoides oophyllum

Ericoides oophyllum, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and hairy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Ericoides oophyllum is adapted to thrive in sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
