Ectasis monadelpha

Ectasis monadelpha, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to the eastern United States and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its root system forms nodules with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, which enhance soil fertility.

Ectasis monadelpha is also valued for its erosion control capabilities due to its extensive root network and dense foliage.
