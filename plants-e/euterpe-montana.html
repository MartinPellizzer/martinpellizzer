Euterpe montana

Euterpe montana, commonly known as the mountain palm, is a species of palm native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, coconut-like flavor. Euterpe montana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
