Ervum pictum

Ervum pictum, commonly known as the painted vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful pods that range from red to purple, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Ervum pictum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also serve as a nitrogen-fixing cover crop in agricultural settings.
