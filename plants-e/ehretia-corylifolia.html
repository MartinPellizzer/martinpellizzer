Ehretia corylifolia

Ehretia corylifolia, commonly known as the Chinese mahogany or Chinese holly, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often palmately lobed and have a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
