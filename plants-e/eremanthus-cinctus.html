Eremanthus cinctus

Eremanthus cinctus, commonly known as the yellow mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and a habitat for various small organisms.
