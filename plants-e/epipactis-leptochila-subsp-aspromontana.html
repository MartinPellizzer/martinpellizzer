Epipactis leptochila subsp. aspromontana

Epipactis leptochila subsp.

aspromontana, commonly known as the aspromontana helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are linear and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger flowers and a more robust growth habit compared to other members of the species.
