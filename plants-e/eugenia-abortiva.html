Eugenia abortiva

Eugenia abortiva, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or false cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, pink to red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a fragrant aroma.

Eugenia abortiva is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
