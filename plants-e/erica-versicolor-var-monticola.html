Erica versicolor var. monticola

Erica versicolor var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and purple, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica versicolor var. monticola is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
