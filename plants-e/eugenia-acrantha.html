Eugenia acrantha

Eugenia acrantha, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea tree. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Eugenia acrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
