Eryngium petiolatum var. juncifolium

Eryngium petiolatum var. juncifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features striking spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by white, bristly bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Its unique structure and color make it a notable ornamental and ecological plant in native gardens.
