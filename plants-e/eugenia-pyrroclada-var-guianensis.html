Eugenia pyrroclada var. guianensis

Eugenia pyrroclada var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
