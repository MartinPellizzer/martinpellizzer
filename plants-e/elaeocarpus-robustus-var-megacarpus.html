Elaeocarpus robustus var. megacarpus

Elaeocarpus robustus var. megacarpus, commonly known as the giant tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The tree's fruits are distinctive, featuring thick, woody capsules that split open to release seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and potential use in ornamental horticulture.
