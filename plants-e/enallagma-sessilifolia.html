Enallagma sessilifolia

Enallagma sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved waterwheel, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are sessile, meaning they are attached directly to the stem without a stalk. The plant produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in shallow water.

Enallagma sessilifolia plays an important role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic life.
