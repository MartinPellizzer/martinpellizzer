Elymus striatus var. villosus

Elymus striatus var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and riparian areas. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often concealed within the foliage.

This variant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
