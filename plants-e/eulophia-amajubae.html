Eulophia amajubae

Eulophia amajubae, commonly known as the Mkomazi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of several racemes bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers.

Eulophia amajubae is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty in botanical gardens.
