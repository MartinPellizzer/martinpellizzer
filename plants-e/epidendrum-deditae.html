Epidendrum deditae

Epidendrum deditae, commonly known as the Deditae Epidendrum, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in regions with warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically clustered in racemes, featuring vibrant colors such as pink, purple, or red.

This orchid thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
