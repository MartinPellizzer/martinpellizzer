Erigeron intermedius

Erigeron intermedius, commonly known as the blueweed or intermediate fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Erigeron intermedius is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
