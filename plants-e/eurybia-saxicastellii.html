Eurybia saxicastellii

Eurybia saxicastellii, commonly known as the rock wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are adapted to rocky and well-drained soils.

Eurybia saxicastellii is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in full sun and cool temperatures.
