Euphorbia correntina

Euphorbia correntina, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky, toxic sap that can cause skin irritation. It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, thriving in full sun.

Its structure and appearance make it a distinctive feature in its native habitats.
