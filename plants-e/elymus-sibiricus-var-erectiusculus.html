Elymus sibiricus var. erectiusculus

Elymus sibiricus var. erectiusculus, commonly known as the Siberian needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are usually green to brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in marginal environments.
