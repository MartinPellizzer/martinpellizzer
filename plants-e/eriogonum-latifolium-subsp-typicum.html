Eriogonum latifolium subsp. typicum

Eriogonum latifolium subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb with basal leaves that are broad and ovate, often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces showy clusters of white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
