Euonymus bockii var. orgyalis

Euonymus bockii var. orgyalis, commonly known as the Appalachian euonymus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and seasonal interest.
