Eryngium decaisneanum

Eryngium decaisneanum, commonly known as the African thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

Eryngium decaisneanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flower structure.
