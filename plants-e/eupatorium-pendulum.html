Eupatorium pendulum

Eupatorium pendulum, commonly known as hanging eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often droop with clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, rich soils. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
