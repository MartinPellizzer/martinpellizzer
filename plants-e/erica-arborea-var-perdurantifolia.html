Erica arborea var. perdurantifolia

Erica arborea var. perdurantifolia, commonly known as the evergreen heath, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
