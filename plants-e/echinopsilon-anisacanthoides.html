Echinopsilon anisacanthoides

Echinopsilon anisacanthoides, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, succulent stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

The species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
