Erigeron pilosus

Erigeron pilosus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

Erigeron pilosus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
