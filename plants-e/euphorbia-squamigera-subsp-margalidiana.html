Euphorbia squamigera subsp. margalidiana

Euphorbia squamigera subsp.

margalidiana, commonly known as the Margalidiana spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

The species is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems.
