Echinocactus hamulosus

Echinocactus hamulosus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive crown of large, paddle-shaped stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spiny exterior provides protection from herbivores and helps reduce water loss.

Echinocactus hamulosus is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
