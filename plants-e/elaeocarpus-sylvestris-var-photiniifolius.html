Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. photiniifolius

Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. photiniifolius, commonly known as the flamewood tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and its distinctive, elongated, glossy leaves that are often a vibrant red or orange during the flowering season. The tree produces small, clustered white flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
