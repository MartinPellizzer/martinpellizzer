Erianthus glabrinodis

Erianthus glabrinodis, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
