Eupatorium rhypodes f. typicum

Eupatorium rhypodes f. typicum, commonly known as the fragrant marsh fleabane, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas in the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in swampy or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, nutrient-poor soils. It is often found in association with other wetland flora such as cypress and pond lilies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
