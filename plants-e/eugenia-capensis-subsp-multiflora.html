Eugenia capensis subsp. multiflora

Eugenia capensis subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stem.

The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring and summer. These flowers are followed by small, edible berries that are dark purple when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types in its natural habitat.
