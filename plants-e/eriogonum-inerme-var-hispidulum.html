Eriogonum inerme var. hispidulum

Eriogonum inerme var. hispidulum, commonly known as the hairy leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. It has a compact growth habit with upright, branching stems.

The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, stiff hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
