Erysimum incanum subsp. pachycarpum

Erysimum incanum subsp.

pachycarpum, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features hairy, gray-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant. Its subspecies pachycarpum is distinguished by its thick, fleshy fruits.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
