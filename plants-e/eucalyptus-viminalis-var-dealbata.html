Eucalyptus viminalis var. dealbata

Eucalyptus viminalis var. dealbata, commonly known as the white box gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that can range from white to grey in color. The leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and typically have a greyish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by woody capsules containing seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and scrublands.
