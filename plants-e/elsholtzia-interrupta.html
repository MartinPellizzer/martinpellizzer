Elsholtzia interrupta

Elsholtzia interrupta, commonly known as the narrowleaf elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky slopes and open woodlands. The plant has slender, aromatic leaves and produces small, pale blue to purple flowers. Its fibrous root system helps it survive in dry conditions.

Elsholtzia interrupta is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
