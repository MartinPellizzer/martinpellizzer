Erigeron lanuginosus

Erigeron lanuginosus, commonly known as the fuzzy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a component of local ecosystems.
