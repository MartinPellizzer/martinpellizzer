Epilobium paniculatum f. bracteatum

Epilobium paniculatum f. bracteatum, commonly known as the bracteate willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and have four petals that are usually pink or purple in color.

The species is characterized by the presence of bracteate structures, which are modified leaves at the base of the inflorescence.
