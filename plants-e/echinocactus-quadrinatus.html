Echinocactus quadrinatus

Echinocactus quadrinatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, which is covered in bright green, ribbed segments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its dense, spiky exterior. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid environments.
