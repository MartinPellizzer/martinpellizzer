Eurya inaequalifolia

Eurya inaequalifolia, commonly known as the unequal-leaved eurya, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are leathery and elliptical, with a distinctive asymmetrical shape that gives it its common name.

The small white flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.
