Epidendrum xelidorimarachinii

Epidendrum xelidorimarachinii, commonly known as the Xelido Marachin orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous flowers arranged in a raceme, making it visually striking. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and unique floral display.
