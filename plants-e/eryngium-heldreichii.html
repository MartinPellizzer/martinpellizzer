Eryngium heldreichii

Eryngium heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Eryngium heldreichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
