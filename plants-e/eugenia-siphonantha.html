Eugenia siphonantha

Eugenia siphonantha, commonly known as the mountain myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows at high altitudes, often in cloud forests. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible berries. Its leaves are thick, leathery, and have a pleasant aromatic scent.

Eugenia siphonantha is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and fragrance.
