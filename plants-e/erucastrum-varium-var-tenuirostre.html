Erucastrum varium var. tenuirostre

Erucastrum varium var. tenuirostre, commonly known as the slender-beaked winter cress, is a perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and segmented fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and covered in a fine layer of wax.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in cold climates and its role in supporting pollinators during early spring.
