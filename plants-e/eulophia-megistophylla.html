Eulophia megistophylla

Eulophia megistophylla, commonly known as the giant leaf eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and often leathery leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, typically arranged in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts for its unique foliage and floral display.
