Epipactis cardina

Epipactis cardina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, hooded flowers that range in color from green to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Epipactis cardina is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in association with other orchid species.
