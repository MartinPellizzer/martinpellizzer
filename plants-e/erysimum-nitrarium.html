Erysimum nitrarium

Erysimum nitrarium, commonly known as yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

Erysimum nitrarium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
