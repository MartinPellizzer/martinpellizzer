Eriogonum heracleoides subsp. simplex

Eriogonum heracleoides subsp.

simplex, commonly known as the simple leaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
