Eulophia bicarinata

Eulophia bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ribbed eulophia, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) native to Southeast Asia.

This terrestrial orchid features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its leafy stems. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a raceme, and are characterized by their elongated, slender petals. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
