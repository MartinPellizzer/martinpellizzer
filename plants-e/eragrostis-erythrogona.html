Eragrostis erythrogona

Eragrostis erythrogona, commonly known as redstem foxtail, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often reddish in color. It is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
