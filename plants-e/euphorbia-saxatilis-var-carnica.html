Euphorbia saxatilis var. carnica

Euphorbia saxatilis var. carnica, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fleshy, often blue-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap.

This variety is often used in rock gardens due to its hardy nature and ornamental appearance.
