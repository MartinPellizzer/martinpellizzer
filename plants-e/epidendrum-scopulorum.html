Epidendrum scopulorum

Epidendrum scopulorum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows on rocky outcrops and in open, sunny habitats. The plant features elongated, linear leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are often pink to purple in color and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

Epidendrum scopulorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
