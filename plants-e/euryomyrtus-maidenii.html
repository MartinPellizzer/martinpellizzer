Euryomyrtus maidenii

Euryomyrtus maidenii, commonly known as the Northern Tableland peppermint gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the subtropical regions of eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalypts and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are often covered in fine white or grey hairs. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

The tree is valued for its aromatic oils and is often found in rainforests and moist woodland areas.
