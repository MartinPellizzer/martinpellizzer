Eugenia myriadena

Eugenia myriadena, commonly known as the Myriadenia eucalyptus, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a dark green color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. These flowers are followed by small, edible berries that are often consumed by native wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
