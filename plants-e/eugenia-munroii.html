Eugenia munroii

Eugenia munroii, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but not widely consumed by humans.

Eugenia munroii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
