Erythroxylum mexicanum

Erythroxylum mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican copal tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, and it is known for its aromatic foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
