Elaeocarpus affinis

Elaeocarpus affinis, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its showy, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Elaeocarpus affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
