Elaeocarpus bancroftii

Elaeocarpus bancroftii, commonly known as the Philippine ebony, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental and timber value, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
