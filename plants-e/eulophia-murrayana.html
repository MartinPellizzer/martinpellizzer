Eulophia murrayana

Eulophia murrayana, commonly known as Murray's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive golden or orange lip. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, typically growing in shaded, moist environments.

Eulophia murrayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
