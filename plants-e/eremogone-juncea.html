Eremogone juncea

Eremogone juncea, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Eremogone juncea is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
