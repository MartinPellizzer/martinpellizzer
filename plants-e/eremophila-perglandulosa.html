Eremophila perglandulosa

Eremophila perglandulosa, commonly known as the sticky eremophila, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves that often have a sticky texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, typically during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Eremophila perglandulosa is often used in native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
