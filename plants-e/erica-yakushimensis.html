Erica yakushimensis

Erica yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimense heath, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This evergreen shrub is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring.

Erica yakushimensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates.
