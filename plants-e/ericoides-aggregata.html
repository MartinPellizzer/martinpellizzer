Ericoides aggregata

Ericoides aggregata, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high altitudes of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, adapted to withstand cold and nutrient-poor soils.

Ericoides aggregata is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing clusters.
