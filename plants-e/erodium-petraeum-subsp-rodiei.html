Erodium petraeum subsp. rodiei

Erodium petraeum subsp.

rodiei, commonly known as the rock eryngo, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers and trifoliate leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
