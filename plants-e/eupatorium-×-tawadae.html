Eupatorium × tawadae

Eupatorium × tawadae, commonly known as the Japanese eupatorium, is a hybrid perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Eupatorium × tawadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
