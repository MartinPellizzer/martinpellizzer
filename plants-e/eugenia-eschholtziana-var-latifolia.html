Eugenia eschholtziana var. latifolia

Eugenia eschholtziana var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf guava, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant features ovate to elliptical leaves that are broad and glossy, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and clustered in inflorescences, while the fruit is a small, round berry with a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and fragrant blooms.
