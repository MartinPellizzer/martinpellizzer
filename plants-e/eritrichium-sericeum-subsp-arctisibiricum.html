Eritrichium sericeum subsp. arctisibiricum

Eritrichium sericeum subsp.

arctisibiricum, commonly known as the Siberian silkyweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the cold regions of northern Asia. It features slender, hairy stems and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the spring.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subarctic environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
