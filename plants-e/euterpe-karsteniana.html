Euterpe karsteniana

Euterpe karsteniana, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that can reach up to three meters in diameter. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible, sweet fruits. Its fruits are highly valued for their flavor and are often consumed fresh or processed into juices.

Euterpe karsteniana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
