Erica virgularis

Erica virgularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to western Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments. Erica virgularis is a hardy species that is adapted to coastal and maritime climates.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting local insect and bird populations.
