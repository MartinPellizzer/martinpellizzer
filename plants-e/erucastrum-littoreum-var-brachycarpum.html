Erucastrum littoreum var. brachycarpum

Erucastrum littoreum var. brachycarpum, commonly known as sea rocket, is a coastal plant adapted to saline and sandy environments.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in low, tidal zones and is often found along the shores of the Atlantic and Pacific coasts. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, making it a traditional ingredient in some cuisines.

The variety brachycarpum is distinguished by its shorter, more compact growth habit compared to other varieties of Erucastrum.
