Excoecaria bicolor var. viridis

Excoecaria bicolor var. viridis, commonly known as the green croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are typically bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity should be noted.
