Emelianthe panganensis subsp. commiphorae

Emelianthe panganensis subsp.

commiphorae, commonly known as the Philippine sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous wood.

The plant typically grows in lowland to montane rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.
