Eria baniae

Eria baniae, commonly known as the banana orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, banana-like pseudobulbs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white, pink, and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria baniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
