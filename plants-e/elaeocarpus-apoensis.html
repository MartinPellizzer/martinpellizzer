Elaeocarpus apoensis

Elaeocarpus apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
