Euphorbia filifolia

Euphorbia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
