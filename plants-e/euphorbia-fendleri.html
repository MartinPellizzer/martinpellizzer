Euphorbia fendleri

Euphorbia fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in small, spiny structures that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it a defensive mechanism against herbivores.

Euphorbia fendleri is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and leaves.
