Epidendrum mamapachae

Epidendrum mamapachae, commonly known as the "Carnival Epidendrum," is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that can range from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in semi-shaded, humid environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Epidendrum mamapachae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking and long-lasting blooms.
