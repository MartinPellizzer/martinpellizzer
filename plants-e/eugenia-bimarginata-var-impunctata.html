Eugenia bimarginata var. impunctata

Eugenia bimarginata var. impunctata, commonly known as the white-leafed guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive white margin. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, yellow-orange fruits. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for both its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
