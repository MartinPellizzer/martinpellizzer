Elephantella groenlandica

Elephantella groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland elephant's foot, is a unique succulent plant native to the Arctic regions.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, cold environments. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to low light conditions. Its flowers, which are usually white or pale pink, appear in late summer and are clustered at the top of the stem.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is a rare and resilient species found primarily in Greenland and parts of northern Canada.
