Eugenia subhorizontalis

Eugenia subhorizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.

Eugenia subhorizontalis is relatively low maintenance and suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
