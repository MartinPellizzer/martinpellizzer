Eupatorium nubigenum

Eupatorium nubigenum, commonly known as cloud-like eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium nubigenum is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a host plant for certain butterfly species.
