Echinacanthus calycinus

Echinacanthus calycinus, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Echinacanthus calycinus is a climbing plant that can reach significant lengths, making it suitable for trellises or as a ground cover.
