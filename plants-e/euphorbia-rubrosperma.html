Euphorbia rubrosperma

Euphorbia rubrosperma, commonly known as the red-veined euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems covered in a distinctive red or pinkish hue, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a spiny or warty texture.

Euphorbia rubrosperma is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
