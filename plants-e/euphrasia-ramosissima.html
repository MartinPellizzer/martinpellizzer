Euphrasia ramosissima

Euphrasia ramosissima, commonly known as the creeping yellow gentian, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a sprawling growth habit with trailing stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers have five petals and a distinctive blue corolla.

Euphrasia ramosissima is often found in shaded environments and is a member of the Gentianaceae family.
