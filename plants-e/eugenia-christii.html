Eugenia christii

Eugenia christii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tart flavor. Eugenia christii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological purposes.
