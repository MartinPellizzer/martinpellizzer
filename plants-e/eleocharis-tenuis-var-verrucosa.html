Eleocharis tenuis var. verrucosa

Eleocharis tenuis var. verrucosa, commonly known as the hairy spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variant is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often found in temperate regions.
