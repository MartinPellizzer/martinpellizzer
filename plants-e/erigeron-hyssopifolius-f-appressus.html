Erigeron hyssopifolius f. appressus

Erigeron hyssopifolius f. appressus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with upright, branched stems that bear small, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are narrow and often hairy, with a silvery-gray appearance due to dense pubescence. The plant produces pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.
