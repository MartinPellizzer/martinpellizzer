Epilobium × rocheri

Epilobium × rocheri, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Epilobium parvifolium and Epilobium hirsutum, showcasing a combination of characteristics from both parents. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes.

Epilobium × rocheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
