Eryngium jepsonii

Eryngium jepsonii, commonly known as Jepson's thistle, is a perennial herb native to California.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-gray foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae). It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, seed-filled capsules.

Eryngium jepsonii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
