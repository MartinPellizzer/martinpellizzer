Erigeron × queraltii

Erigeron × queraltii, commonly known as the hybrid fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Erigeron karvinskianus and Erigeron pectinatus. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. It has finely divided, gray-green leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Erigeron × queraltii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
