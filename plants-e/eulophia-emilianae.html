Eulophia emilianae

Eulophia emilianae, commonly known as the Emilian's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers with prominent lips, typical of many orchid species. Eulophia emilianae thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a visually striking and ecologically significant component of its native habitat.
