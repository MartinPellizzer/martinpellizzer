Erysimum × marshallii

Erysimum × marshallii, commonly known as the hybrid wallflower, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a hybrid between Erysimum cheiranthoides and Erysimum lamarckii, resulting in a plant with distinctive, showy flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soils.

Erysimum × marshallii is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
