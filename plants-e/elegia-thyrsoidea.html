Elegia thyrsoidea

Elegia thyrsoidea, commonly known as the slender glassworts, is a halophytic plant that thrives in saline and coastal environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its root system is adapted to stabilize sandy or muddy soils, making it an important pioneer species in coastal ecosystems.

Elegia thyrsoidea plays a role in preventing soil erosion and providing habitat for various small invertebrates.
