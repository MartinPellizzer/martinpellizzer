Echeveria lanceolata var. composta

Echeveria lanceolata var. composta, commonly known as the Rosette Echeveria, is a compact, low-growing succulent native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with a powdery coating. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red flowers during the spring or summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its compact form.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
