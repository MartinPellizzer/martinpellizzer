Euodia parvifolia

Euodia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant yellow flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain hard, dark seeds. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and incense.

Euodia parvifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
