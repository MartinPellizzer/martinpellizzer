Elymus praecaespitosus

Elymus praecaespitosus, commonly known as the rough wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically arranged in panicles. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

Elymus praecaespitosus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to stabilize soil.
