Erodium crassifolium

Erodium crassifolium, commonly known as broad-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, pink to purple flowers and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as fields and roadsides. It is an annual herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Erodium crassifolium is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
