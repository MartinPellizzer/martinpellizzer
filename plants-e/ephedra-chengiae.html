Ephedra chengiae

Ephedra chengiae, commonly known as Chinese ephedra, is a shrub native to China and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It contains the alkaloid ephedrine, which has historically been used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties and ecological significance, it is often studied for its potential in pharmaceutical and conservation contexts.
