Ericinella

Ericinella, commonly known as the false heath, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Ericaceae.

These plants are typically found in acidic soils and are often associated with heathland and moorland habitats. Ericinella species are shrubs that produce small, bell-shaped flowers, often in shades of white or pink. They have evergreen leaves that are usually leathery and arranged in a compact, dense form.

The genus is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor environments, making it an important component of certain ecological systems.
