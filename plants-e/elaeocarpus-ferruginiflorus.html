Elaeocarpus ferruginiflorus

Elaeocarpus ferruginiflorus, commonly known as the iron-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its attractive, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive metallic sheen. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
