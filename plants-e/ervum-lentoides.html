Ervum lentoides

Ervum lentoides, commonly known as the lentil tree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds, which are oval and resemble lentils, are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it an important species in ecological restoration.
