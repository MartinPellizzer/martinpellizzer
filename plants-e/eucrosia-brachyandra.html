Eucrosia brachyandra

Eucrosia brachyandra, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eucrosia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Eucrosia brachyandra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to well-drained soils.
