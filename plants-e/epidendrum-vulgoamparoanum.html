Epidendrum vulgoamparoanum

Epidendrum vulgoamparoanum, commonly known as the Amparoan Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features elongated, strap-like leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum vulgoamparoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
