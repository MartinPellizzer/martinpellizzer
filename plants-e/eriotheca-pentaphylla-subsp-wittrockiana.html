Eriotheca pentaphylla subsp. wittrockiana

Eriotheca pentaphylla subsp.

wittrockiana, commonly known as the Wittrock's eriotheca, is a species native to Central America. It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its simple, elliptic leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside.

The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
