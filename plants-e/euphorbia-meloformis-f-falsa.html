Euphorbia meloformis f. falsa

Euphorbia meloformis f. falsa, commonly known as the false melon euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a spiny stem that serves as a defense mechanism.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
