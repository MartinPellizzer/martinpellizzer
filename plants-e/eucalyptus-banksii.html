Eucalyptus banksii

Eucalyptus banksii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, often greyish bark. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, globular flowers that are usually white or pale blue, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

Eucalyptus banksii is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
