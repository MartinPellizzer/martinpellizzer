Eucalyptus globulus subsp. globulus

Eucalyptus globulus subsp.

globulus, commonly known as the Australian blue gum, is a tall, evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, blue-gray bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is well-known for its essential oils, which are widely used in medicinal and industrial applications.

This subspecies is also valued for its role in ecological systems and timber production.
