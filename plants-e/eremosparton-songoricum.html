Eremosparton songoricum

Eremosparton songoricum, commonly known as the Songar Desert bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with thick leaves that store water and a deep root system for accessing moisture. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its resilience, it plays an important ecological role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert fauna.
