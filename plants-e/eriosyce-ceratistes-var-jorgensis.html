Eriosyce ceratistes var. jorgensis

Eriosyce ceratistes var. jorgensis, commonly known as the Jorg's pincushion cactus, is a species native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its dense, spiny, globular stems that resemble a pincushion. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
