Eleocharis ambigens

Eleocharis ambigens, commonly known as the ambiguous spike-rush, is a species of sedge found in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with triangular stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of tiny, usually greenish or brownish flowers.

Eleocharis ambigens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
