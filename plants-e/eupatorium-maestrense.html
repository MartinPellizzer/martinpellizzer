Eupatorium maestrense

Eupatorium maestrense, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium maestrense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
