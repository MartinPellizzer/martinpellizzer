Epipactis albensis

Epipactis albensis, commonly known as the white helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and its distinctive white to pale green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are spurred at the base and have a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

Epipactis albensis is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
