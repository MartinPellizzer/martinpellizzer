Elytrospermum

Elytrospermum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its showy, blue or purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plants typically have hairy, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. They are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens due to their vibrant color and drought tolerance.

Elytrospermum species are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
