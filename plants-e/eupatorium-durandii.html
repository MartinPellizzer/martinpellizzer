Eupatorium durandii

Eupatorium durandii, commonly known as the Durand's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant traits.

Eupatorium durandii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
