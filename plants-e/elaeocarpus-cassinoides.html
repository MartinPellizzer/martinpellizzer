Elaeocarpus cassinoides

Elaeocarpus cassinoides, commonly known as the Australian sugarwood, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or near streams.

Elaeocarpus cassinoides plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
