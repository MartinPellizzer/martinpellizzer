Erica inconspicua

Erica inconspicua, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, dense foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica inconspicua is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
