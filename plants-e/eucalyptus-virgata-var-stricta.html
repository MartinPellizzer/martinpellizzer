Eucalyptus virgata var. stricta

Eucalyptus virgata var. stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
