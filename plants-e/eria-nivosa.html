Eria nivosa

Eria nivosa, commonly known as the snow orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Eria nivosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
