Eria aliciae

Eria aliciae, commonly known as the Alice's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and unique floral structure. Eria aliciae thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged along the stem.
