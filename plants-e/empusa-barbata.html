Empusa barbata

Empusa barbata, commonly known as the hairy tailed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has a fibrous root system adapted to its wet habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the inflorescence consists of a spike bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers.

Empusa barbata is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
