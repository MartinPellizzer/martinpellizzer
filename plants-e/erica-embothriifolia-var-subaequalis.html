Erica embothriifolia var. subaequalis

Erica embothriifolia var. subaequalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
