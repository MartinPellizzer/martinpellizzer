Eragrostis calotheca

Eragrostis calotheca, commonly known as the narrowleaf beakgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, with each spikelet containing two to three small, awned lemmas.

Eragrostis calotheca is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and thrive in poor soil conditions.
