Eugenia mugiensis

Eugenia mugiensis, commonly known as the Mugiensis clove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, fleshy drupe, contains a single seed and is often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia mugiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential pharmacological properties.
