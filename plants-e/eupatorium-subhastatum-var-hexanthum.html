Eupatorium subhastatum var. hexanthum

Eupatorium subhastatum var. hexanthum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its floral structure includes both ray and disc florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.
