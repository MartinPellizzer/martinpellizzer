Euterpe microspadix

Euterpe microspadix, commonly known as the purple-barked palm, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, clustered growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, edible fruits. Its leaves are pinnate and often have a distinctive purple coloration on the petioles.

This palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited economic use compared to other palms.
