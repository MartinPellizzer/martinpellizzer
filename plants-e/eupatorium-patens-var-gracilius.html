Eupatorium patens var. gracilius

Eupatorium patens var. gracilius, commonly known as slender boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
