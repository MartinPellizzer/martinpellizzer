Eugenia parvifolia

Eugenia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

Eugenia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and fruit.
