Elaeocarpus prunifolioides

Elaeocarpus prunifolioides, commonly known as the small-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ovate, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. The fruit's outer layer is thick and leathery, containing a single hard seed.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
