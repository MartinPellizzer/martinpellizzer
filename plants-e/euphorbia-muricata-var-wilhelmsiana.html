Euphorbia muricata var. wilhelmsiana

Euphorbia muricata var. wilhelmsiana, commonly known as the Wilhelmsiana Spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common trait among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
