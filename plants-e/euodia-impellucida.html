Euodia impellucida

Euodia impellucida, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, resinous bark and small, white flowers. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and has smooth, gray bark that exudes a strong, aromatic scent when cut. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture, while its fruits are small, woody capsules containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for centuries.
