Epilobium coloratum var. tenuifolium

Epilobium coloratum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along streambanks and roadsides. The plant features slender, erect stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance to pollinators.
