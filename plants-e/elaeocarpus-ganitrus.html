Elaeocarpus ganitrus

Elaeocarpus ganitrus, commonly known as the Malabar elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
