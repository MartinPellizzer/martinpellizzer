Euphorbia polygalifolia var. hirta

Euphorbia polygalifolia var. hirta, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower clusters typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in a waxy coating.

It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
