Erechtites quadridentatus

Erechtites quadridentatus, commonly known as the four-toothed erecthites, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with four to five distinct lobes that give it its common name.

Erechtites quadridentatus is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
