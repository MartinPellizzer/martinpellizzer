Eugenia cryptophlebia

Eugenia cryptophlebia, commonly known as the black pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that mature to a dark purple or black color, which are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include opposite leaves, stipules, and a distinctive aromatic oil in its leaves and fruits.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
