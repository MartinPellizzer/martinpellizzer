Elaeocarpus breviracemosus

Elaeocarpus breviracemosus, commonly known as the short-shoot elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a thick, fleshy aril, which is a characteristic feature of the genus Elaeocarpus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
