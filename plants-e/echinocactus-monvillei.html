Echinocactus monvillei

Echinocactus monvillei, commonly known as the Monville's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-gray in color, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often clustered at the top of the stems. Its spines are short and cylindrical, offering protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Echinocactus monvillei is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
