Epimedium membranaceum subsp. orientale

Epimedium membranaceum subsp.

orientale, commonly known as the oriental barberry, is a deciduous perennial plant native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, membranous layer.

The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on upright, hairy stalks. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and early blooming habit.
