Eriogonum fasciculatum var. oleifolium

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. oleifolium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.
