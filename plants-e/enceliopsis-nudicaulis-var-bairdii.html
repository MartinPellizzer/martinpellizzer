Enceliopsis nudicaulis var. bairdii

Enceliopsis nudicaulis var. bairdii, commonly known as the Baird's desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as sandy soils and rocky slopes. Its leaves are basal, linear, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with prominent central disk florets.

The species is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
