Erythraea germanica

Erythraea germanica, commonly known as the German erythraea, is a perennial herb native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and range in color from white to pink.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
