Erysimum mediohispanicum

Erysimum mediohispanicum, commonly known as the Mediterranean wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. It has a compact growth habit with linear, hairy leaves.

Erysimum mediohispanicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
