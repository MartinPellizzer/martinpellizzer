Elymus gmelinii

Elymus gmelinii, commonly known as the prairie grass or western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of spikelets, which are clustered along the stem.

Elymus gmelinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
