Emmeorhiza umbellata subsp. septentrionalis

Emmeorhiza umbellata subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern umbellate orchid, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of South America. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

It has specific mycorrhizal associations that are essential for its nutrient uptake and overall growth.
