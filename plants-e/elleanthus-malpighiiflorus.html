Elleanthus malpighiiflorus

Elleanthus malpighiiflorus, commonly known as the Malpighiopsis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Malpighiaceae and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically features small, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are usually alternate and palmately lobed, with a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
