Echinochloa crus-pavonis

Echinochloa crus-pavonis, commonly known as barnyard grass, is a prevalent annual grass species found in various habitats across the globe.

It is characterized by its flat, blade-like leaves and spike-like inflorescence that resembles a tawny beard, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in a wide range of environmental conditions.

Its seeds are small and can remain viable in the soil for several years, contributing to its persistence in many ecosystems.
