Eugenia tsoongii

Eugenia tsoongii, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fleshy fruits. The plant typically grows in subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and the fruit, which is edible, has a tart flavor.

The tree is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
