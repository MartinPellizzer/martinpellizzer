Eupatorium sampaionis

Eupatorium sampaionis, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and potential pharmaceutical applications.
