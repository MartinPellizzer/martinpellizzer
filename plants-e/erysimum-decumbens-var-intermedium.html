Erysimum decumbens var. intermedium

Erysimum decumbens var. intermedium, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features basal rosettes of lanceolate, glaucous leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological gardening.
