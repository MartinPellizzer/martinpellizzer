Elymus virginicus var. jenkinsii

Elymus virginicus var. jenkinsii, commonly known as Jenkins' wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often lie flat along the ground. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, including roadsides and open fields.
