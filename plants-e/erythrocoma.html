Erythrocoma

Erythrocoma, commonly known as the red-faced orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that often display a striking red or pink coloration on the petals and sepals. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador, these plants thrive in humid, high-altitude environments. The species within this genus typically have a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Erythrocoma species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
