Epidendrum oncidioides var. gravidum

Epidendrum oncidioides var. gravidum, commonly known as the Gravid Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fleshy, and often fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy pubescence.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
