Euonymus vagans subsp. macrophyllus

Euonymus vagans subsp.

macrophyllus, commonly known as the broadleaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often prominently veined and have a glossy surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by ornamental orange-red berries that persist into winter. Its growth habit is typically upright, with multiple stems and a dense, bushy appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its visual interest and adaptability.
