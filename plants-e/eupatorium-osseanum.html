Eupatorium osseanum

Eupatorium osseanum, commonly known as bonehead eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium osseanum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
