Echinochloa crus-galli f. atra

Echinochloa crus-galli f. atra, commonly known as black barnyard grass, is a perennial weed belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and spikelets that are often tinged with purple or black. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and disturbed areas. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its rapid growth and competitive nature.

Its presence can significantly impact crop yields and is often a challenge in weed management programs.
