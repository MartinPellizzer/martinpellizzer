Eulalia nana

Eulalia nana, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a small, herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters, often forming a low-growing rosette. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Eulalia nana is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
