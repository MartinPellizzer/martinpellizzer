Erica rollissonia

Erica rollissonia, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring. Erica rollissonia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
