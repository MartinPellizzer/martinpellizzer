Eleocharis laxiflora

Eleocharis laxiflora, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
