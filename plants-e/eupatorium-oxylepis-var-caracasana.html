Eupatorium oxylepis var. caracasana

Eupatorium oxylepis var. caracasana, commonly known as the Caracas arrowwood, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Venezuela.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to purple flower heads that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
