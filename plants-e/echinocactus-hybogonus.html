Echinocactus hybogonus

Echinocactus hybogonus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and densely packed, bright green, cylindrical spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and arid garden landscapes.
