Eupatorium quaternum

Eupatorium quaternum, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Boneset is known for its historical use in traditional medicine, particularly for its purported fever-reducing properties.
