Euphorbia crassimarginata

Euphorbia crassimarginata, commonly known as the thick-margin euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves with prominent yellow margins, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. The plant produces striking yellowish-green flowers in clusters, often accompanied by a unique, milky sap. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
