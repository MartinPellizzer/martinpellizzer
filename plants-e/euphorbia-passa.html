Euphorbia passa

Euphorbia passa, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a flowering plant characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellowish-green color with a red bract.
