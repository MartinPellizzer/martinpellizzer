Erica bacciformis

Erica bacciformis, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring. Erica bacciformis prefers well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
