Evolvulus paraguariensis

Evolvulus paraguariensis, commonly known as the "puya" or "paraguariensis morning glory," is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often hairy, while its stems are slender and vine-like.

Evolvulus paraguariensis is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
