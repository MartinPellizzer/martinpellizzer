Erythrina linearifoliata

Erythrina linearifoliata, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a species of legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its growth habit is typically tree-like or shrub-like. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.

Erythrina linearifoliata also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
