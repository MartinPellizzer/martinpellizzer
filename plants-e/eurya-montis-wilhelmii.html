Eurya montis-wilhelmii

Eurya montis-wilhelmii, commonly known as the Wilhelm's eurya, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at high elevations.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
