Equisetum ramosissimum var. gracile

Equisetum ramosissimum var. gracile, commonly known as slender horsetail, is a perennial fern-like plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered with small, scale-like leaves. This variety is typically found in moist, sandy soils in temperate regions, often near water sources. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are used for reproduction.

It is considered an ancient plant, with a lineage dating back to the Paleozoic era.
