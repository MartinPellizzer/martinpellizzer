Echinops chamaecephalus

Echinops chamaecephalus, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which give it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to greenish-white, and it blooms in the spring or early summer.

Echinops chamaecephalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in xeriscaped gardens.
