Erophila verna subsp. krockeri

Erophila verna subsp.

krockeri, commonly known as the spring everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb that produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring, contributing to the floral diversity of its native habitat.

Its name reflects its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even after the plant has dried.
