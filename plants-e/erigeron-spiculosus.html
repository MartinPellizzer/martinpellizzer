Erigeron spiculosus

Erigeron spiculosus, commonly known as the prickly blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually spiny.

The showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
