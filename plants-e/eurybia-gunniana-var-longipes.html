Eurybia gunniana var. longipes

Eurybia gunniana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
