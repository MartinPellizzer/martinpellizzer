Eragrostis mairei

Eragrostis mairei, commonly known as Maire's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

Eragrostis mairei is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
