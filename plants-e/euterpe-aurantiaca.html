Euterpe aurantiaca

Euterpe aurantiaca, commonly known as the red acuri or red palm, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-orange fruits, which are edible and have a sweet, fibrous pulp. Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for various wildlife and its potential economic value.

The plant's resilience in humid, tropical environments makes it an important component of the rainforest ecosystem.
