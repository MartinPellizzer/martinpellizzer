Eugenia densipunctata

Eugenia densipunctata, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible, segmented fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, sweet flavor. Its botanical features include a dense, spiny bark and a complex root system adapted to wet, swampy environments.

The plant is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its medicinal properties.
