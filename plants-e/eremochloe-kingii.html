Eremochloe kingii

Eremochloe kingii, commonly known as King's eremochloe, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and as a ornamental plant in dry climates.
