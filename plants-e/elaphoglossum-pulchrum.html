Elaphoglossum pulchrum

Elaphoglossum pulchrum, commonly known as the beautiful holly fern, is a species of fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that are often bright green. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense colonies over time.

The spores are released from small, clustered sporangia located on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.
