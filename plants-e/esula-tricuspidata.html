Esula tricuspidata

Esula tricuspidata, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, typical of the Araliaceae family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Esula tricuspidata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
