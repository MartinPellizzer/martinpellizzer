Ericopsis formosus

Ericopsis formosus, commonly known as the Japanese heath, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or rocky habitats.

Ericopsis formosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
