Eriogonum multiflorum var. riograndis

Eriogonum multiflorum var. riograndis, commonly known as the Rio Grande buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, showy yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs.
