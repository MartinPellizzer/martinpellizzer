Eulophia flavescens

Eulophia flavescens, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral structures. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom in clusters, often displaying a mix of yellow and green hues.

Eulophia flavescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
