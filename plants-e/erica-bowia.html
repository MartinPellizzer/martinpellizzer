Erica bowia

Erica bowia, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic soils. Erica bowia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for local wildlife.
