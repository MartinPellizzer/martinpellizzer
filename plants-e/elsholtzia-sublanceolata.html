Elsholtzia sublanceolata

Elsholtzia sublanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-lanceleaf elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
