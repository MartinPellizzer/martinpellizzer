Erythroxylum monogynum var. caffrum

Erythroxylum monogynum var. caffrum, commonly known as the Cape bush tea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval, woody capsules containing the seeds. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
