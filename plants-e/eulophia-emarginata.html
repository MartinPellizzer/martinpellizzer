Eulophia emarginata

Eulophia emarginata, commonly known as the marginated eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often near streams or in mountainous regions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with intricate markings, and they bloom in clusters.

Eulophia emarginata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
