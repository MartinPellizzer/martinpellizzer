Elaeagnus pungens subsp. simonii

Elaeagnus pungens subsp.

simonii, commonly known as silver honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its silvery foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early spring, followed by edible, reddish-brown berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This subspecies is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil improvement.
