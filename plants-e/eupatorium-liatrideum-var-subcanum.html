Eupatorium liatrideum var. subcanum

Eupatorium liatrideum var. subcanum, commonly known as the grayleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
