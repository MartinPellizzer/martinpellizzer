Ericoides faustum

Ericoides faustum, commonly known as the golden ericoid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to yellow in color. Native to the temperate regions of South America, it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions where it forms dense shrubs.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
