Equisetum ramosissimum var. elegans

Equisetum ramosissimum var. elegans, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial plant characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures.

It belongs to the family Equisetaceae and is known for its primitive morphology, reminiscent of ancient plant forms. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread vegetatively make it a common and persistent weed in such environments.
