Eleocharis × naritaensis

Eleocharis × naritaensis, commonly known as the Narita flatsedge, is a hybrid grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations, making it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
