Eulophia elliottii

Eulophia elliottii, commonly known as Elliott's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Eulophia elliottii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
