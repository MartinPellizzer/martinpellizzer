Epilobium × pycnotrichum

Epilobium × pycnotrichum, commonly known as the sticky willow or woolly willow, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, glandular hairs that give it a sticky texture and a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purple hue, while its flowers are small, pink or purple, and arranged in clusters.

Epilobium × pycnotrichum is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
