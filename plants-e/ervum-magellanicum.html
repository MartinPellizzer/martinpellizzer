Ervum magellanicum

Ervum magellanicum, commonly known as the Magellan pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet. Its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ervum magellanicum thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to grow in various soil types.
