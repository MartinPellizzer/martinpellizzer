Eupatorium hirsutissimum

Eupatorium hirsutissimum, commonly known as roughhead thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium hirsutissimum is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
