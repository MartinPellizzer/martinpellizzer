Epipactis lessonii

Epipactis lessonii, commonly known as the lesser epipactis, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often with a distinctive lip that is darker in color.

Epipactis lessonii is characterized by its intricate floral structure and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
