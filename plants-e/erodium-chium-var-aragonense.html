Erodium chium var. aragonense

Erodium chium var. aragonense, commonly known as the Aragon wild rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
