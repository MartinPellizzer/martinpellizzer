Elatine oryzetorum

Elatine oryzetorum, commonly known as the rice water fern, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Elatinaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and its ability to thrive in wetland environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in rice paddies and other wet areas. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a spiral pattern, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Elatine oryzetorum plays a role in maintaining water quality by filtering nutrients and providing habitat for microorganisms.
