Euphorbia helenae subsp. helenae

Euphorbia helenae subsp.

helenae, commonly known as the Helen's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
