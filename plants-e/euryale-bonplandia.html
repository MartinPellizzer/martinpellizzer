Euryale bonplandia

Euryale bonplandia, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of still waters. The plant's flowers are showy, typically white or pink, and bloom at night, attracting specific pollinators. Its roots are submerged, anchoring it in the mud and absorbing nutrients from the water.

Euryale bonplandia plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter for aquatic life and contributing to water filtration.
