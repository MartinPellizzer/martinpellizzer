Eria helleri

Eria helleri, commonly known as the Heller's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns of white, pink, and purple, making it a visually appealing species.

Eria helleri is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
