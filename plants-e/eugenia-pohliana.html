Eugenia pohliana

Eugenia pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

Eugenia pohliana is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its role in local ecosystems.
