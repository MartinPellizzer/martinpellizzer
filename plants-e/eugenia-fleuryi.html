Eugenia fleuryi

Eugenia fleuryi, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá-do-mato, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit, a small, red, cherry-like drupe, is edible and often consumed fresh or used in local cuisine.

Eugenia fleuryi plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
