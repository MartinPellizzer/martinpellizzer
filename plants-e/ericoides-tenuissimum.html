Ericoides tenuissimum

Ericoides tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a low-growing shrub native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and resilience to harsh environmental conditions make it a hardy species suitable for horticultural use in gardens and restoration projects.
