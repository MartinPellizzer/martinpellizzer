Eugenia ilhensis

Eugenia ilhensis, commonly known as the Ilhensian guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. Eugenia ilhensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in local traditional medicine.

Its growth is typically found in humid, shaded environments within its native range.
