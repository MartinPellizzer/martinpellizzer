Euphorbia creberrima

Euphorbia creberrima, commonly known as the common spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky, latex-like sap that can be toxic and is a notable feature of its botanical description. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Euphorbia creberrima is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has various traditional uses in different cultures.
