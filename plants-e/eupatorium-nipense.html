Eupatorium nipense

Eupatorium nipense, commonly known as boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated leaves and is often found in moist, wooded areas or along stream banks. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Historically, it was used by Native Americans and early settlers for its fever-reducing qualities.
