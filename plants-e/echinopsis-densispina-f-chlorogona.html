Echinopsis densispina f. chlorogona

Echinopsis densispina f. chlorogona, commonly known as the Chlorogona Echinopsis, is a cactus species native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green in color. The plant produces striking white or yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract nocturnal pollinators like bats and moths. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

The variety 'chlorogona' is distinguished by its unique leaf-like structures and vibrant coloration.
