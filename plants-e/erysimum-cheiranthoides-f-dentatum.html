Erysimum cheiranthoides f. dentatum

Erysimum cheiranthoides f. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and often have a distinctive toothed or serrated margin, which gives the cultivar its specific name. This plant is well-suited for rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
