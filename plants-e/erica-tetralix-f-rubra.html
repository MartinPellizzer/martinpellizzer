Erica tetralix f. rubra

Erica tetralix f. rubra, commonly known as the red heather, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form. The plant produces small, vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems.

Erica tetralix f. rubra thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland habitats.
