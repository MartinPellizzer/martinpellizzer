Eremophila compressa

Eremophila compressa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its compact growth habit and gray-green foliage. The plant produces spikes of tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It has adapted to survive in drought conditions with its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

Eremophila compressa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
