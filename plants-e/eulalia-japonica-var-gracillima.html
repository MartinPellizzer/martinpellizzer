Eulalia japonica var. gracillima

Eulalia japonica var. gracillima, commonly known as Japanese feather grass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, arching blades that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant produces feathery plumes in late summer, which add a soft, airy texture to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its low maintenance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
