Euphorbia caput-medusae var. major

Euphorbia caput-medusae var. major, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns, is a striking succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched, spiny stem that can grow up to several meters in height, with a distinctive crown-like arrangement of branches. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
