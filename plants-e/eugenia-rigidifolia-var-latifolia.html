Eugenia rigidifolia var. latifolia

Eugenia rigidifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rough eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. Its flowers are small, clustered, and usually pale pink or white, while the fruit is a small, round, fleshy berry.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
