Eupatorium rotundifolium var. typicum

Eupatorium rotundifolium var. typicum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rounded, silvery-blue leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It blooms from late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
