Electra mutica

Electra mutica, commonly known as the narrowleaf electra, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Electra mutica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
