Emilia fallax

Emilia fallax, commonly known as the yellow ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange composite flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, often with a silvery underside.

Emilia fallax is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
