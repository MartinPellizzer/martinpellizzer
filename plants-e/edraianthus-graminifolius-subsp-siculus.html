Edraianthus graminifolius subsp. siculus

Edraianthus graminifolius subsp.

siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian grass-leaved edraianthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
