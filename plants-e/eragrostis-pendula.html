Eragrostis pendula

Eragrostis pendula, commonly known as hanging lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, feathery inflorescences that give it a delicate, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis pendula is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
