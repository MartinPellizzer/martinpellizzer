Eupatorium perfoliatum var. colpophilum

Eupatorium perfoliatum var. colpophilum, commonly known as the eastern boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are fibrous and can become woody with age, and the plant is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
