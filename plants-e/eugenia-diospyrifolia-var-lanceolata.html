Eugenia diospyrifolia var. lanceolata

Eugenia diospyrifolia var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features elliptical, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
