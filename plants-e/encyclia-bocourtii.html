Encyclia bocourtii

Encyclia bocourtii, commonly known as the Bocourt's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Epidendroideae subfamily and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces compact, pseudobulbous stems with fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often arid or seasonally dry habitat. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that varies from white to pale yellow.

Encyclia bocourtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
