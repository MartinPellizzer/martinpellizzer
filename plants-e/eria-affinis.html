Eria affinis

Eria affinis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, erect growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding substrate.

Eria affinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under suitable conditions.
