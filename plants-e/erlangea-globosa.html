Erlangea globosa

Erlangea globosa, commonly known as the globe erlangia, is a unique plant characterized by its spherical, fleshy stems that resemble small globes.

It is native to arid regions and thrives in environments with low rainfall and high sunlight exposure. The plant's leaves are typically reduced to small spines, which help minimize water loss through transpiration. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Erlangea globosa is a fascinating example of succulent evolution in harsh climates.
