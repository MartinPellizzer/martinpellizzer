Epilobium tetragonum var. japonicum

Epilobium tetragonum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese willowherb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features erect, branched stems covered in sticky, glandular hairs. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a rough, serrated margin. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spike-like clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows, streambanks, and disturbed sites.
