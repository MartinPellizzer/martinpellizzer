Euphorbia tubiglans

Euphorbia tubiglans, commonly known as the giant spurge, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, exhibiting succulent properties to store water.

Euphorbia tubiglans is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
