Egletes tenuifolia

Egletes tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in drought conditions, with deep roots and reduced leaf surface area to minimize water loss. It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for xeriscaping.

Egletes tenuifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
