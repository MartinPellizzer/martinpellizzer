Erodium bipinnatum f. hirtum

Erodium bipinnatum f. hirtum, commonly known as hairy erodium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy habitats. Its seed pods are slender and curved, containing several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing feature of this particular form.
