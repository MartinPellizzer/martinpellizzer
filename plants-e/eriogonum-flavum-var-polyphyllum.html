Eriogonum flavum var. polyphyllum

Eriogonum flavum var. polyphyllum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

This species is often found in regions with well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
