Echeveria schaffneri

Echeveria schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features compact, rosette-shaped foliage with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically grayish-blue in color. The leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating known as farina, which helps reduce water loss. It produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy pink or purple flowers in the spring.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
