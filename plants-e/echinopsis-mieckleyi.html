Echinopsis mieckleyi

Echinopsis mieckleyi, commonly known as the Mieckley's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, columnar stem and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Echinopsis mieckleyi is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
