Erythronium giganteum

Erythronium giganteum, commonly known as the giant cowslip, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a distinctive red or purple blotch at the base. The plant features a bulbous underground stem, which stores nutrients and helps it survive in its alpine habitat. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often appearing after the flowers have bloomed.

Erythronium giganteum thrives in well-drained soils and cool, moist environments, making it a notable species in high-altitude ecosystems.
