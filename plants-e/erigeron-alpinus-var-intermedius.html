Erigeron alpinus var. intermedius

Erigeron alpinus var. intermedius, commonly known as the Alpine fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, hairy leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained conditions.
