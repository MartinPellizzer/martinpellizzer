Euphorbia falcata subsp. lusitanica

Euphorbia falcata subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
