Erica nitidula

Erica nitidula, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. It is well adapted to harsh environments, including drought and poor soil conditions.

Erica nitidula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
