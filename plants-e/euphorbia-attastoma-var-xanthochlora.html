Euphorbia attastoma var. xanthochlora

Euphorbia attastoma var. xanthochlora, commonly known as the yellow-stemmed euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a yellowish-green hue, and its leaves are small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers during the warmer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
