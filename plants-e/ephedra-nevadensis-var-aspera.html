Ephedra nevadensis var. aspera

Ephedra nevadensis var. aspera, commonly known as the Nevada ephedra, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its jointed, branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in small inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert regions.

This variety is distinguished by its rough, asperous surface on the stems, giving it its botanical name.
