Eupatorium integerrimum

Eupatorium integerrimum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are rough to the touch. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers in late summer, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium integerrimum is often found in the understory of forests and is part of the Asteraceae family.
