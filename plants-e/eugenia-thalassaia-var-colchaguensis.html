Eugenia thalassaia var. colchaguensis

Eugenia thalassaia var. colchaguensis, commonly known as the Chilean myrtle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
