Eucalyptus hebetifolia

Eucalyptus hebetifolia, commonly known as the slender-leaved malleeflower, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, greyish pubescence. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, often in sandy or loamy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
