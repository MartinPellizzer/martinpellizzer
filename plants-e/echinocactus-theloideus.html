Echinocactus theloideus

Echinocactus theloideus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and bright yellow spines, which give it its common name. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

Echinocactus theloideus is a slow-growing plant that requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
