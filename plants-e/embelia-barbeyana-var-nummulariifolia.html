Embelia barbeyana var. nummulariifolia

Embelia barbeyana var. nummulariifolia, commonly known as the Nummulariifolia Embelia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, round, waxy leaves that resemble coins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique foliage.
