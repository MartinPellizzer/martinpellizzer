Eugenia longipes

Eugenia longipes, commonly known as the longiped eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in some areas.
