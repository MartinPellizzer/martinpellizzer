Eurya wallichiana

Eurya wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that turn red in autumn. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in forests at high elevations.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
