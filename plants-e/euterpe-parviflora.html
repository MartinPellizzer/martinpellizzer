Euterpe parviflora

Euterpe parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellowish-green in color. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, which is adapted for dispersal by animals.

Euterpe parviflora is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical rainforests.
