Eugenia dictyophleba

Eugenia dictyophleba, commonly known as the lace-veined guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its common name.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
