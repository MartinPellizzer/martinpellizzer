Elytropappus glandulosus var. pallens

Elytropappus glandulosus var. pallens, commonly known as the pale glandular eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing small, white to pale pink florets that attract various pollinators.

The glandular hairs on its stems and leaves are a distinctive botanical feature, aiding in its identification.
