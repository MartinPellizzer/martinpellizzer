Eriogonum abertianum subsp. lappulaceum

Eriogonum abertianum subsp.

lappulaceum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow to orange inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its fibrous roots help it survive in harsh, drought-prone conditions.
