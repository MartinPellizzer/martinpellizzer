Eugenia jussara

Eugenia jussara, commonly known as the Jussara myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, small leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow when mature.

Eugenia jussara is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal uses.
