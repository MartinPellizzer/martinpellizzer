Eugenioides mucronatum

Eugenioides mucronatum, commonly known as the small-leafed eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
