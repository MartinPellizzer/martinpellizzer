Epicampes gigantea

Epicampes gigantea, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has a robust, woody stem with a smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are large, simple, and alternate, often reaching up to 30 cm in length.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its vibrant floral display.
