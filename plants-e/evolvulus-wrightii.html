Evolvulus wrightii

Evolvulus wrightii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and twining.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
