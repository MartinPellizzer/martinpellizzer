Elaeocarpus varunua

Elaeocarpus varunua, commonly known as the Indian satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in traditional craftsmanship.

Elaeocarpus varunua thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
