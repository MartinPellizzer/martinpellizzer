Exacum axillare

Exacum axillare, commonly known as the winter jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Exacum axillare is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its early blooming habit and vibrant floral display.
