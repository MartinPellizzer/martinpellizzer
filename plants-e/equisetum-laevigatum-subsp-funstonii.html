Equisetum laevigatum subsp. funstonii

Equisetum laevigatum subsp.

funstonii, commonly known as the Funston's horse tail, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems covered in small, scale-like structures. It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are arranged in whorls. Its morphology resembles that of other horsetails, with a distinct lack of true leaves.

This subspecies is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological succession in wetland environments.
