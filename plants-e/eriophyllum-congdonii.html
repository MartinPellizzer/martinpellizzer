Eriophyllum congdonii

Eriophyllum congdonii, commonly known as the Congdon's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to golden flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer.

Eriophyllum congdonii is known for its drought tolerance and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
