Echites lisianthiflorus

Echites lisianthiflorus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a dense cluster of small, daisy-like flowers at the top of its stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Echites lisianthiflorus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
