Encyclia cochleata

Encyclia cochleata, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its spiral-shaped pseudobulbs and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a distinctive spiral pattern on the petals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Encyclia cochleata is a popular choice for both horticultural collections and botanical gardens due to its ornamental value and unique appearance.
