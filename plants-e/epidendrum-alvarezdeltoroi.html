Epidendrum alvarezdeltoroi

Epidendrum alvarezdeltoroi, commonly known as the Alvarez deltortoi orchid, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of white to pale pink blossoms during the spring and summer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained environments and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
