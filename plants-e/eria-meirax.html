Eria meirax

Eria meirax, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers bloom in clusters, often during the warmer months, adding vibrant color to its environment.
