Eulophia inamoena

Eulophia inamoena, commonly known as the white-veined eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate veining. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia inamoena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
