Eutaxia lutea

Eutaxia lutea, commonly known as the yellow eutaxia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, which is characterized by its bean-like pods and often nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Eutaxia lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
