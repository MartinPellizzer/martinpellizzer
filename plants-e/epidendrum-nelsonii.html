Epidendrum nelsonii

Epidendrum nelsonii, commonly known as the Nelson's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, violet or purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often covered in a waxy coating to retain moisture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.
