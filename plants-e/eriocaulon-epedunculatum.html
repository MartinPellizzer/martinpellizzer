Eriocaulon epedunculatum

Eriocaulon epedunculatum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical flowering spikes.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often densely packed with tiny flowers. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, enhancing its ability to spread in aquatic environments.

Eriocaulon epedunculatum is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
