Euphorbia stenoclada subsp. ambatofinandranae

Euphorbia stenoclada subsp.

ambatofinandranae, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Madagascar. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces a unique, gelatinous capsule as its fruit, which contains toxic seeds. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing features that help it conserve water and survive in harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
