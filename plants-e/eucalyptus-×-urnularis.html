Eucalyptus × urnularis

Eucalyptus × urnularis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the specific variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in dry, open woodlands and heathlands.

Eucalyptus × urnularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and adaptability.
