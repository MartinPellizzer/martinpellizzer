Echinopsis martinii

Echinopsis martinii, commonly known as the Martinii Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, columnar stem and dense clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions. Its flowers are often nocturnal, opening at night to attract specific pollinators.

Echinopsis martinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in succulent gardens worldwide.
