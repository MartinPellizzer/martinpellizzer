Epipactis latifolia subvar. lutescens

Epipactis latifolia subvar. lutescens, commonly known as the broadleaf helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features a rosette of broad, elliptic leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is usually yellow or orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and marshes.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, and it is considered a rare and protected species in many regions.
