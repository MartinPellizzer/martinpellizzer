Eriogonum zionis var. coccineum

Eriogonum zionis var. coccineum, commonly known as the Zion's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pink to red flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has narrow, gray-green leaves and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
