Eupatorium kiirunense

Eupatorium kiirunense, commonly known as the Kiirunese boneset, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium kiirunense is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
