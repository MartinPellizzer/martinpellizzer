Elymus bungeanus subsp. scythicus

Elymus bungeanus subsp.

scythicus, commonly known as the Scythian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
