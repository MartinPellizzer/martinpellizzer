Eugenia coaetanea

Eugenia coaetanea, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia coaetanea*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark red to black berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant scent when crushed.

The fruit is edible and often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.
