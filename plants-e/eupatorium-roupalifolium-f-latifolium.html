Eupatorium roupalifolium f. latifolium

Eupatorium roupalifolium f. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf white snakeroot, is a variant of the white snakeroot plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage. Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Eupatorium genus, which is commonly found in wetlands and woodland areas.

The broadleaf form is notable for its wider leaves compared to the typical variety, contributing to its unique appearance.
