Eurybia lepidophylla var. lawrencei

Eurybia lepidophylla var. lawrencei, commonly known as the fairy duster, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, branched stems that bear clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. It produces a distinctive, spiky fruit that contains numerous small seeds.

The species is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
