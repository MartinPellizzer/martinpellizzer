Erica luteoalba

Erica luteoalba, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.

Erica luteoalba is also important in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
