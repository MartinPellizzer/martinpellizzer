Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp. oliganthus

Elaeocarpus submonoceras subsp.

oliganthus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed elaeocarpus, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland tropical forests.

The plant features elliptical to ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a woody drupe with a single seed.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
