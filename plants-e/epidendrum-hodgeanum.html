Epidendrum hodgeanum

Epidendrum hodgeanum, commonly known as the Hodge's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and relatively small, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum hodgeanum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
