Eugenia acunae

Eugenia acunae, commonly known as the Andean huito, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy green leaves. It produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible and have a sweet, slightly acidic flavor.

Eugenia acunae is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
