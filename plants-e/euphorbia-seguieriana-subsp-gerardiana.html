Euphorbia seguieriana subsp. gerardiana

Euphorbia seguieriana subsp.

gerardiana, commonly known as the Gerard's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
