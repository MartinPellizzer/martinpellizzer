Enarthrocarpus lyratus f. desertorum

Enarthrocarpus lyratus f. desertorum, commonly known as the desert tamarind, is a variant of the tamarind tree adapted to arid environments.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. Its botanical features include thick, waxy leaves that reduce water loss, making it well-suited for dry climates.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is often found in regions with limited rainfall.
