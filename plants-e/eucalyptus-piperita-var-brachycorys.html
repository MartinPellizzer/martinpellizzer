Eucalyptus piperita var. brachycorys

Eucalyptus piperita var. brachycorys, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during spring. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in mallee woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
