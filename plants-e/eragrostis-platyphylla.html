Eragrostis platyphylla

Eragrostis platyphylla, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps prevent soil erosion.

It is often found in prairies, woodlands, and disturbed sites, contributing to ecological diversity in its habitat.
