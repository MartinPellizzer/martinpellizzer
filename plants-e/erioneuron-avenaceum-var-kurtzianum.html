Erioneuron avenaceum var. kurtzianum

Erioneuron avenaceum var. kurtzianum, commonly known as the narrowleaf field grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each bearing small, two-lobed flowers.

This variety is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its role in maintaining ecological balance in grassland ecosystems.
