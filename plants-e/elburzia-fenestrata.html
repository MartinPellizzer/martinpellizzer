Elburzia fenestrata

Elburzia fenestrata, commonly known as the windowleaf plant, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fenestrated (window-like) leaves that allow light to pass through, creating a distinctive visual effect. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually dark green with a waxy surface, and it produces small, fragrant flowers during the summer months.

Elburzia fenestrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a houseplant.
