Erica arborescens

Erica arborescens, commonly known as the tree heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Erica arborescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
