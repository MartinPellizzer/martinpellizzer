Epipactis meridionalis

Epipactis meridionalis, commonly known as the southern helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and its striking, hooded flowers that range in color from green to yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and shrublands. It produces a single spike of flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epipactis meridionalis is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex pollination mechanisms involving specific insects.
