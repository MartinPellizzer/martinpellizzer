Eragrostis

Eragrostis, commonly known as lovegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically perennial and are native to various regions across Africa, Asia, and the Americas. Eragrostis species are characterized by their tufted growth habit and spikelet-based inflorescences. Many species are adapted to arid or semi-arid environments, making them resilient to drought conditions.

They are often used in landscaping and as forage for livestock due to their hardiness and rapid growth.
