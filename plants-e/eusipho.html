Eusipho

Eusipho, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust root system and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Eusipho is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, making it an important part of riparian ecosystems.
