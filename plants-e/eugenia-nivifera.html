Eugenia nivifera

Eugenia nivifera, commonly known as the snow eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, white, waxy flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a snow-like appearance. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a smooth, glossy texture.

Eugenia nivifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
