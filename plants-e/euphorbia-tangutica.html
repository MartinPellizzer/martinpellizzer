Euphorbia tangutica

Euphorbia tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of western China.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to thrive in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse range of succulent and tropical species.

Euphorbia tangutica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
