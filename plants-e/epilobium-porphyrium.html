Epilobium porphyrium

Epilobium porphyrium, commonly known as the purple willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Epilobium porphyrium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
