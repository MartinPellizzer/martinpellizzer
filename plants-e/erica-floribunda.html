Erica floribunda

Erica floribunda, commonly known as the common heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple.

Erica floribunda thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
