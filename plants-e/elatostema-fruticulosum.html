Elatostema fruticulosum

Elatostema fruticulosum, commonly known as the creeping elatostema, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with short, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and covered with fine, stinging hairs, typical of the Urticaceae family. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are often subtended by bracts.

This plant is commonly found in moist, shaded environments in tropical and subtropical regions.
