Euodia whitmorei

Euodia whitmorei, commonly known as the white-fruited lauraceae, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, cinnamon, and avocado. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, fragrant flowers. Its fruits are woody and contain a single seed, typical of the genus Euodia.

It is often found in lowland and hill forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
