Eriotheca macrophylla

Eriotheca macrophylla, commonly known as the giant eriophyllum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Eriotheca macrophylla is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
