Edgeworthia eriosolenoides

Edgeworthia eriosolenoides, commonly known as the Japanese snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.
