Euphorbia malleata

Euphorbia malleata, commonly known as the sticky euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a sticky substance. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions.

Euphorbia malleata is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and unique appearance.
