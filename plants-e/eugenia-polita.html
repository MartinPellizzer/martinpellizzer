Eugenia polita

Eugenia polita, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant's fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer. Eugenia polita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
