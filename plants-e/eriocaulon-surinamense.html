Eriocaulon surinamense

Eriocaulon surinamense, commonly known as the Surinam rush, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it reproduces through both sexual and vegetative means.

Eriocaulon surinamense plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
