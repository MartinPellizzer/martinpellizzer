Echinocystis macrocarpa f. leptocarpa

Echinocystis macrocarpa f. leptocarpa, commonly known as the slender giant cucumber, is a variant of the giant cucumber species.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its large, lobed leaves and distinctive, spiny fruits. The plant produces elongated, green fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and melons.

This form is notable for its unique fruit morphology and is often cultivated for its edible, sweet flesh.
