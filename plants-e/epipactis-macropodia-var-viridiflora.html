Epipactis macropodia var. viridiflora

Epipactis macropodia var. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered large-lobed helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and striking greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks. It produces a single spike of flowers, each with a three-lobed lip that is often marked with purple or brown.

The species is known for its intricate pollination mechanisms, involving specific insect species.
