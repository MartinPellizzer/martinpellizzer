Eryngium diffusum

Eryngium diffusum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into prickly, spherical seed heads.

Eryngium diffusum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for certain insects.
