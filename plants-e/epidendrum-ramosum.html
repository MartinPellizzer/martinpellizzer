Epidendrum ramosum

Epidendrum ramosum, commonly known as the orchid vine, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that often grow along tree branches or rocks. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy texture.

Epidendrum ramosum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
