Echinospermum ciliatum

Echinospermum ciliatum, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silky dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale yellow flower heads that resemble dandelion seeds. The plant produces small, narrow leaves with hairy surfaces, and its stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
