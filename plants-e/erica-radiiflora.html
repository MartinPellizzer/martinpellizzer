Erica radiiflora

Erica radiiflora, commonly known as the radiate heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal or mountainous regions. Erica radiiflora is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.
