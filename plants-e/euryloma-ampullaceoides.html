Euryloma ampullaceoides

Euryloma ampullaceoides, commonly known as the glassy bladderwort, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its specialized bladder-like structures.

These bladders are used to trap small aquatic organisms, which the plant then absorbs for nutrients. It is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats, such as ponds and ditches. The plant has a slender, branching stem with small, scale-like leaves.

Its morphology is adapted for both aquatic and terrestrial environments, allowing it to thrive in diverse wetland conditions.
