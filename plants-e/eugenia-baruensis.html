Eugenia baruensis

Eugenia baruensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia baruensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance.

Eugenia baruensis is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
