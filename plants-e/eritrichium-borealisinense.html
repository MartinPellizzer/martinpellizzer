Eritrichium borealisinense

Eritrichium borealisinense, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of North America and Eurasia.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, rocky environments, often found in tundra and alpine habitats. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Eritrichium borealisinense plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its extreme environment.
