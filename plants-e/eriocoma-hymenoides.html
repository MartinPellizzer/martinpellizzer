Eriocoma hymenoides

Eriocoma hymenoides, commonly known as the woolly mountain aster, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers the stems and leaves, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Eriocoma hymenoides plays a role in stabilizing mountainous terrain and provides nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
