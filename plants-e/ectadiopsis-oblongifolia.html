Ectadiopsis oblongifolia

Ectadiopsis oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically oblong in shape, adapted to store water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to quickly absorb moisture from the soil.

Ectadiopsis oblongifolia is often found in rocky soils and is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
