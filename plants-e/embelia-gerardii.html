Embelia gerardii

Embelia gerardii, commonly known as wild pepper, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant produces small, white to greenish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
