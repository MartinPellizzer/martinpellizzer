Erigeron subtrinervis var. conspicuus

Erigeron subtrinervis var. conspicuus, commonly known as the showy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
