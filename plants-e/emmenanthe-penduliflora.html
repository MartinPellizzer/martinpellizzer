Emmenanthe penduliflora

Emmenanthe penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that hang from long, slender stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested areas, thriving in cool, humid climates. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Emmenanthe penduliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
