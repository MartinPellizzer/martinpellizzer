Eriocephalus virgatus

Eriocephalus virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The silverleaf tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide shade in dry landscapes.
