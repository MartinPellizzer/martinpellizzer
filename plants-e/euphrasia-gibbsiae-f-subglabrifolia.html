Euphrasia gibbsiae f. subglabrifolia

Euphrasia gibbsiae f. subglabrifolia, commonly known as the glandular eyebright, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained, sandy soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
