Echinopsis fallax var. albiflora

Echinopsis fallax var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to absorb moisture from the soil after rare rainfalls. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

This variety is prized in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
