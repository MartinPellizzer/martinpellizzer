Erica inflexa

Erica inflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica inflexa typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.
