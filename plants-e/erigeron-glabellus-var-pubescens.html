Erigeron glabellus var. pubescens

Erigeron glabellus var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage is covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
