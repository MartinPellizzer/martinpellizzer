Eugenia micropetala

Eugenia micropetala, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Eugenia micropetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
