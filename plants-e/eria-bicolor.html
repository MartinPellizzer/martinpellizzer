Eria bicolor

Eria bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its strikingly contrasting colors, with green and white or pink markings on its leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and delicate, often arranged in clusters, and it produces a fragrant scent to attract pollinators.

Eria bicolor is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
