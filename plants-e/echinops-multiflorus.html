Echinops multiflorus

Echinops multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are covered in small, white to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops multiflorus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
