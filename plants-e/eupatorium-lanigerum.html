Eupatorium lanigerum

Eupatorium lanigerum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium lanigerum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
