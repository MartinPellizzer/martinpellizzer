Eugenia liesneri

Eugenia liesneri, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for horticultural and ecological studies.
