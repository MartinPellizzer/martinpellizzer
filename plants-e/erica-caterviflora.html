Erica caterviflora

Erica caterviflora, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas with mild, humid climates. Erica caterviflora is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers bloom in winter, adding a striking visual contrast to the landscape during the colder months.
