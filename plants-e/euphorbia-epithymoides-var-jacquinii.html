Euphorbia epithymoides var. jacquinii

Euphorbia epithymoides var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacob's ladder euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features compact, upright growth with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray in color. The plant produces unique, branched inflorescences called cyathia, which contain tiny yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
