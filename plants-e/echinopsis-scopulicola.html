Echinopsis scopulicola

Echinopsis scopulicola, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
