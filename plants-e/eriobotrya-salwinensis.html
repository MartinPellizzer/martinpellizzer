Eriobotrya salwinensis

Eriobotrya salwinensis, commonly known as the Salwin loquat, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible and similar in taste to loquats. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
