Echinops pungens var. polyacanthus

Echinops pungens var. polyacanthus, commonly known as the blue glass globe thistle, is a striking plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue-gray flower heads that resemble glass balls, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant produces pale blue to bluish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a valuable addition to drought-tolerant landscapes.
