Euploca polyphylla

Euploca polyphylla, commonly known as the mountain bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euploca polyphylla is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
