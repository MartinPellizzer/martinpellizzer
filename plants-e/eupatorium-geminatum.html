Eupatorium geminatum

Eupatorium geminatum, commonly known as the twinleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by Native American tribes for its anti-inflammatory and fever-reducing effects.

Eupatorium geminatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
