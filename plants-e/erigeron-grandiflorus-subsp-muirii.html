Erigeron grandiflorus subsp. muirii

Erigeron grandiflorus subsp.

muirii, commonly known as the Muir's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are narrow and hairy, often with a silvery appearance due to fine pubescence.
