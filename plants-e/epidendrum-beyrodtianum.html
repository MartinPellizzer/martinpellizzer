Epidendrum beyrodtianum

Epidendrum beyrodtianum, commonly known as the Beyrodt's orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, typically blooming during the dry season. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, helping it survive in its often-dry habitat.

Epidendrum beyrodtianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
