Eupatorium neaeanum

Eupatorium neaeanum, commonly known as New England thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
