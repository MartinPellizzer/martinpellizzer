Euphorbia hyberna var. insularis

Euphorbia hyberna var. insularis, commonly known as the Canary Island spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its distinctive flower-like structures. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
