Echiochilon fruticosum var. marginatum

Echiochilon fruticosum var. marginatum, commonly known as the margined spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its branched stems that are often covered in a sticky, milky sap. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinct white or pale margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
