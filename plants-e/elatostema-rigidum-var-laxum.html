Elatostema rigidum var. laxum

Elatostema rigidum var. laxum, commonly known as the slender false shamrock, is a small herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and understoreys. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a velvety texture. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, adding to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, cool conditions.
