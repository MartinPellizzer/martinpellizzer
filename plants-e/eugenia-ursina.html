Eugenia ursina

Eugenia ursina, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in culinary applications. Eugenia ursina thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
