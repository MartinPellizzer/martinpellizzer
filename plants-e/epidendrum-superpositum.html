Epidendrum superpositum

Epidendrum superpositum, commonly known as the stacked epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water retention.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
