Erica concava

Erica concava, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the species. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica concava is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
