Echites inflatus

Echites inflatus, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems and distinctive, inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Echites inflatus thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found growing along riverbanks or in humid forests.
