Evolvulus luetzelburgii

Evolvulus luetzelburgii, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The genus Evolvulus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed production.
