Euphorbia exigua

Euphorbia exigua, commonly known as the small-spined spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, succulent structure with fleshy, ribbed stems and small, alternate leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are surrounded by specialized bracts that resemble flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments.

Euphorbia exigua is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
