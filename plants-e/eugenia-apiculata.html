Eugenia apiculata

Eugenia apiculata, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil, Peru, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a red or purple color.

Eugenia apiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
