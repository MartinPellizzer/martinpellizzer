Erysimum oleifolium

Erysimum oleifolium, commonly known as the honeycomb plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange clusters of flowers that resemble a honeycomb, hence its common name. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
