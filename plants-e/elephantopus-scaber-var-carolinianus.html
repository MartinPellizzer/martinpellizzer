Elephantopus scaber var. carolinianus

Elephantopus scaber var. carolinianus, commonly known as Carolina horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is an annual herb that grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster, which bloom from summer to autumn. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

The species is characterized by its rough, scabrous texture, which gives it its scientific name.
