Eugenia petrophila

Eugenia petrophila, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, humid climates. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

Eugenia petrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
