Erophila aurigerana

Erophila aurigerana, commonly known as the golden daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Erophila aurigerana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
