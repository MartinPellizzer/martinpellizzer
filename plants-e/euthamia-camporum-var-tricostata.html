Euthamia camporum var. tricostata

Euthamia camporum var. tricostata, commonly known as the three-costate goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf structures. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with three distinct costae, giving it its scientific name.

It produces bright yellow flowers in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
