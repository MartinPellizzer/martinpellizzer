Erigeron × foucaudii

Erigeron × foucaudii, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads that resemble daisies, with ray florets surrounding a central cluster of yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Erigeron × foucaudii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
