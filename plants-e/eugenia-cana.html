Eugenia cana

Eugenia cana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant red or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. Eugenia cana thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
