Ericoides spectabile

Ericoides spectabile, commonly known as the showy heath, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in acidic conditions. Its botanical features include small, dark green leaves and clusters of bright pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides spectabile is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
