Eremophila ionantha var. brevifolia

Eremophila ionantha var. brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its narrow, lance-shaped leaves are covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

This variety is well adapted to dry climates and is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
