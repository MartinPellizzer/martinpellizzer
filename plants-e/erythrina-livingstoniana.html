Erythrina livingstoniana

Erythrina livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston's flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its structure includes sturdy, often thorned stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

The tree is also valued for its fast growth and ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
