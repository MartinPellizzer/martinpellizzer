Eupatorium quisqueyanum

Eupatorium quisqueyanum, commonly known as the Cuban thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium quisqueyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
