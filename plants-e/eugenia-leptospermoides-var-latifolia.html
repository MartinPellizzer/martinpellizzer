Eugenia leptospermoides var. latifolia

Eugenia leptospermoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
