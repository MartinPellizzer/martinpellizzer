Echinocactus echinoides

Echinocactus echinoides, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in thick, spiny areoles, which are typical features of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its ability to store water in its stems makes it well adapted to arid environments.

Echinocactus echinoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
