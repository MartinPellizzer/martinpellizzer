Eriocaulon komarovii

Eriocaulon komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. This species is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and cylindrical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it often forms dense mats or clumps.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
