Erica campanularis

Erica campanularis, commonly known as the bell heather, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and clusters of bell-shaped, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Erica campanularis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
