Erigeron bellidiformis

Erigeron bellidiformis, commonly known as the desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grayish-green, with a fuzzy texture that helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a blue to purple coloration that attracts pollinators.
