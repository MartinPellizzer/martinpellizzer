Eragrostis advena

Eragrostis advena, commonly known as the weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its drooping, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

Eragrostis advena is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
