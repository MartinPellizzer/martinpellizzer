Eucalyptus andrewsii subsp. campanulata

Eucalyptus andrewsii subsp.

campanulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, often blue-green leaves and small, bell-shaped white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.

The subspecies is part of the diverse Eucalyptus genus, known for its aromatic oils and ecological significance in Australian ecosystems.
