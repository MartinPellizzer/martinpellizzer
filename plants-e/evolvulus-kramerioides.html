Evolvulus kramerioides

Evolvulus kramerioides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread make it both a desirable garden plant and a potential weed in some environments.
