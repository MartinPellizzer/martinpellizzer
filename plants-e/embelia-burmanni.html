Embelia burmanni

Embelia burmanni, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include a woody stem, aromatic foliage, and a preference for tropical and subtropical climates.

Embelia burmanni is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
