Eugenia vaccinioides

Eugenia vaccinioides, commonly known as the dwarf waxwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, urn-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage is dense and glossy, with a waxy texture that gives it its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its compact size and year-round foliage.
