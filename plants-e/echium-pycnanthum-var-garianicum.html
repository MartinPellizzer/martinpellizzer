Echium pycnanthum var. garianicum

Echium pycnanthum var. garianicum, commonly known as the Gariep's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
