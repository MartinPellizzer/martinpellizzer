Epifagus

Epifagus, commonly known as sweet birch, is a deciduous tree belonging to the birch family (Betulaceae).

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green catkins. The tree produces small, oval leaves that turn yellow in the fall, and it is native to eastern North America. Epifagus is known for its aromatic bark and sap, which have been used historically for medicinal and industrial purposes.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
