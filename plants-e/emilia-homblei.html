Emilia homblei

Emilia homblei, commonly known as the Mexican dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Emilia homblei typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight.

Its foliage is generally hairy and aromatic, with lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine.
