Epipactis leptochila var. thesaurensis

Epipactis leptochila var. thesaurensis, commonly known as the Thessalian helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and a single, showy, greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and woodland edges. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is pollinated by various insects, including bees and flies.

This orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and exhibits a complex symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.
