Erysimum lanceolatum subsp. ochroleucum

Erysimum lanceolatum subsp.

ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are borne on slender, branching stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to Mediterranean climates and suitable for cultivation in similar environments.
