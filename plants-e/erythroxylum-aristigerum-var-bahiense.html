Erythroxylum aristigerum var. bahiense

Erythroxylum aristigerum var. bahiense, commonly known as the Bahia coca, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, oval berries that contain the stimulant compound cocaine. It is often found in humid, tropical environments and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.

Due to its psychoactive properties, it is of interest in both botanical and pharmacological studies.
