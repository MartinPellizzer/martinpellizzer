Eclipta alba f. prostrata

Eclipta alba f. prostrata, commonly known as prostrate false dandelion, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a prostrate growth habit with numerous slender, branching stems that lie close to the ground. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that are surrounded by pale green bracts, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
