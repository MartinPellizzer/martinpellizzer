Eriodictyon capitatum

Eriodictyon capitatum, commonly known as the California yerba buena, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions and chaparral habitats. Its leaves contain compounds that have been used traditionally by Native American tribes for medicinal purposes.

Eriodictyon capitatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
