Eriocaulon tatei

Eriocaulon tatei, commonly known as the Japanese spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of tiny, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture.

Eriocaulon tatei is often found in shaded, humid areas, making it a common species in subtropical and temperate wetlands.
