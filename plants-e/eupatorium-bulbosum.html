Eupatorium bulbosum

Eupatorium bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a valuable plant for ecological studies.
