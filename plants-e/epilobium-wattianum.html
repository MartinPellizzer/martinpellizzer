Epilobium wattianum

Epilobium wattianum, commonly known as the Watt's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with fine hairs.

Epilobium wattianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
