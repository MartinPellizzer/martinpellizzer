Echeveria chihuahuaensis

Echeveria chihuahuaensis, commonly known as the Chihuahuaensis Echeveria, is a succulent native to the mountainous regions of Chihuahua, Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers on tall, branching stalks during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care, it is a sought-after plant among succulent enthusiasts.
