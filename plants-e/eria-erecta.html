Eria erecta

Eria erecta, commonly known as the upright eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria erecta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
