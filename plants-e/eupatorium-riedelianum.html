Eupatorium riedelianum

Eupatorium riedelianum, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as swamps and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium riedelianum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
