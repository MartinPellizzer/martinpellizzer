Encyclia phoenicea

Encyclia phoenicea, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Caribbean.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. The plant features a short, stocky rhizome with a dense cluster of narrow, strap-like leaves. Its most distinctive characteristic is the showy, red-tipped inflorescence that bears fragrant, yellowish-white flowers.

Encyclia phoenicea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, tropical climates.
