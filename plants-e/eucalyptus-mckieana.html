Eucalyptus mckieana

Eucalyptus mckieana, commonly known as the Mckieana gum, is a tall, slender eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pale or yellowish in color. It is often found in open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Eucalyptus mckieana is valued for its timber and has been used in various traditional and contemporary applications.
