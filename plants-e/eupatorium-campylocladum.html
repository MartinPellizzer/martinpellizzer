Eupatorium campylocladum

Eupatorium campylocladum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered white flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
