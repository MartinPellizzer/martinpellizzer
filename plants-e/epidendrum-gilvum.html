Epidendrum gilvum

Epidendrum gilvum, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and glossy, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Epidendrum gilvum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
