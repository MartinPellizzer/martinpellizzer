Euphorbia pindicola

Euphorbia pindicola, commonly known as the Pindus spurge, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers, which are typically surrounded by colorful bracts. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Euphorbia pindicola is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
