Euphorbia famatamboay

Euphorbia famatamboay, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems.

Euphorbia famatamboay is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
