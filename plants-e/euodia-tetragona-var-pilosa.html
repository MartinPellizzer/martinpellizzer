Euodia tetragona var. pilosa

Euodia tetragona var. pilosa, commonly known as the Chinese frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in herbal remedies.
