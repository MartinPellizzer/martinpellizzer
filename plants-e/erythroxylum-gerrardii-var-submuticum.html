Erythroxylum gerrardii var. submuticum

Erythroxylum gerrardii var. submuticum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved copal tree, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth, often reddish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in lowland forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ecological importance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
