Evolvulus heterophyllus

Evolvulus heterophyllus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue, purple, or white. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

Evolvulus heterophyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
