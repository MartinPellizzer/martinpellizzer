Eugenia littoralis var. deplanchei

Eugenia littoralis var. deplanchei, commonly known as the coastal eugenia, is a shrub or small tree native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in estuaries and tidal zones.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
