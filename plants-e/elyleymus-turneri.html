Elyleymus turneri

Elyleymus turneri, commonly known as the Turner's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow, slightly hairy, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in dry landscapes.
