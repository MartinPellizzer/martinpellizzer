Eugenia cuspidato-obovata

Eugenia cuspidato-obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that may be edible and is sometimes used in local cuisines.
