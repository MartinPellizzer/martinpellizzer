Eleocharis konkanensis

Eleocharis konkanensis, commonly known as the Konkan sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the western coast of India, particularly in the Konkan region, and thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in moist soils.

Eleocharis konkanensis plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
