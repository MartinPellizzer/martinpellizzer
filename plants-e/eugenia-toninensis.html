Eugenia toninensis

Eugenia toninensis, commonly known as the Tonin's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
