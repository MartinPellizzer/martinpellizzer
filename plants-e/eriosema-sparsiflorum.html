Eriosema sparsiflorum

Eriosema sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparse-flowered eriosema, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often covered in fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
