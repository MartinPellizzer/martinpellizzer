Echinopsis densispina f. kraussiana

Echinopsis densispina f. kraussiana, commonly known as the Kraussiana Echinopsis, is a unique cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements in cultivation.
