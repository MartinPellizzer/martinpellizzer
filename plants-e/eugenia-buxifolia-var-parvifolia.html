Eugenia buxifolia var. parvifolia

Eugenia buxifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved myrtle, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, dark green foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant for its ornamental foliage and unique appearance.
