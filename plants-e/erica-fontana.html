Erica fontana

Erica fontana, commonly known as the fairybells, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. Erica fontana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
