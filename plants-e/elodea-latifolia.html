Elodea latifolia

Elodea latifolia, commonly known as waterweed, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, bright green leaves. The plant is typically found in ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams, where it can thrive in shallow to deep water. Elodea latifolia is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.

It is also used in scientific studies due to its rapid growth and ease of cultivation.
