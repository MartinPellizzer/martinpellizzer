Erigeron annuus subsp. strigosus

Erigeron annuus subsp.

strigosus, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is native to North America and often grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
