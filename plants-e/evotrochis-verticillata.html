Evotrochis verticillata

Evotrochis verticillata, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around the stem, a feature that gives it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in the spring.

Evotrochis verticillata plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
