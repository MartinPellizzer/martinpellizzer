Eragrostis subglandulosa

Eragrostis subglandulosa, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the flowers are usually pale green to brown in color.

Eragrostis subglandulosa plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
