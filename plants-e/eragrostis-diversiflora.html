Eragrostis diversiflora

Eragrostis diversiflora, commonly known as the variable lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It features slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to blue-gray florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a hairy texture, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments.

Eragrostis diversiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
