Erigeron acris subsp. lalehzaricus

Erigeron acris subsp.

lalehzaricus, commonly known as the Persian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and morphological features compared to other Erigeron species.
