Eugenia cacuminoides

Eugenia cacuminoides, commonly known as the red-berried eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature, often attracting birds and other wildlife. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens.
