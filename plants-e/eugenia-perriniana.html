Eugenia perriniana

Eugenia perriniana, commonly known as the Pernambuco eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and forested regions.

Eugenia perriniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
