Eriogonum nudum subsp. saxicola

Eriogonum nudum subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought conditions.
