Eragrostis ciliaris var. brachystachya

Eragrostis ciliaris var. brachystachya, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for ecological studies and potential forage in certain environments.
