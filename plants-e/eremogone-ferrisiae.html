Eremogone ferrisiae

Eremogone ferrisiae, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in early spring. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.
