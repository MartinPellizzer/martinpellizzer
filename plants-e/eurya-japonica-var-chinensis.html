Eurya japonica var. chinensis

Eurya japonica var. chinensis, commonly known as Chinese evergreen or Chinese waxleaf, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring, followed by small, red berries in the fall. It is well adapted to shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in humid conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
