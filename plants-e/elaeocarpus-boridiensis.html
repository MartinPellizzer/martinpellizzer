Elaeocarpus boridiensis

Elaeocarpus boridiensis, commonly known as the Boridiense elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruits are woody and contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
