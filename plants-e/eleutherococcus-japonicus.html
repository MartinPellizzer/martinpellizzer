Eleutherococcus japonicus

Eleutherococcus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of elliptic to ovate leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Hippocastanaceae and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
