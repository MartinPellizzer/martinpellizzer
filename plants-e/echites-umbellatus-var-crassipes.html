Echites umbellatus var. crassipes

Echites umbellatus var. crassipes, commonly known as the broadleaf echites, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, upright stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of wetland flora.
