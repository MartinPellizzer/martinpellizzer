Euonymus wulinensis

Euonymus wulinensis, commonly known as the Wulin euonymus, is a shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Cercidiphyllaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits that contain seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
