Erica melastoma

Erica melastoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, evergreen leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces clusters of pink or white flowers in spring.

Erica melastoma is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
