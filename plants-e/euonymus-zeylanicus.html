Euonymus zeylanicus

Euonymus zeylanicus, commonly known as the Indian burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that persist into winter. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant foliage.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
