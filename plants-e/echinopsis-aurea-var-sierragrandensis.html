Echinopsis aurea var. sierragrandensis

Echinopsis aurea var. sierragrandensis, commonly known as the Sierra Grande echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and dense clusters of golden-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
