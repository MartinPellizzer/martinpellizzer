Eugenia rigidissima

Eugenia rigidissima, commonly known as the rigid wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, waxy fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
