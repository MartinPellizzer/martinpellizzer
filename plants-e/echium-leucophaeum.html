Echium leucophaeum

Echium leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-leafed thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often growing in rocky or coastal habitats.

Echium leucophaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and blooms.
