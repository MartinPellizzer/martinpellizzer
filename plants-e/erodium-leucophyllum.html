Erodium leucophyllum

Erodium leucophyllum, commonly known as the white-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 10 inches. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Erodium leucophyllum thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
