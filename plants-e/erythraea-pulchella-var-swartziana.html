Erythraea pulchella var. swartziana

Erythraea pulchella var. swartziana, commonly known as the Swartz's erythraea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a woody stem and small, elliptical leaves with a smooth surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
