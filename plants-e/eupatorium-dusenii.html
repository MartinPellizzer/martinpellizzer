Eupatorium dusenii

Eupatorium dusenii, commonly known as Dusen's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in composite heads, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Eupatorium dusenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
