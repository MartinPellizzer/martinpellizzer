Elaeagnus wenshanensis

Elaeagnus wenshanensis, commonly known as the Wenshan silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Elaeagnus wenshanensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
