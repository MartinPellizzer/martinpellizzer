Euphorbia gerardiana var. sturii

Euphorbia gerardiana var. sturii, commonly known as the Sturii's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a branched stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
