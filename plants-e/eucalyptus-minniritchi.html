Eucalyptus minniritchi

Eucalyptus minniritchi, commonly known as the Black Hill mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in heathland and woodland habitats. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, globular flower buds that turn red or yellow when mature.

The species is adapted to dry, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in areas with seasonal rainfall.
