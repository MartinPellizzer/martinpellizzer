Euphorbia cheirolepis

Euphorbia cheirolepis, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, ribbed stems and produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures. The plant has a milky sap that can be toxic and is used for medicinal purposes in traditional practices. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Euphorbia cheirolepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
