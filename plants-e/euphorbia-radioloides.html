Euphorbia radioloides

Euphorbia radioloides, commonly known as the radiate spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with a distinctive crown of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has adapted to arid environments with its water-storing leaves and reduced leaf surfaces. Its milky sap contains toxic compounds, which serve as a natural defense against herbivores.

Euphorbia radioloides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
