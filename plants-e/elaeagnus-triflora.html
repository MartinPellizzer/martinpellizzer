Elaeagnus triflora

Elaeagnus triflora, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Elaeagnus triflora is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in ecological restoration projects.

Its adaptability to various soil types and drought tolerance further enhance its horticultural and environmental significance.
