Echinops pachyphyllus

Echinops pachyphyllus, commonly known as the thick-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical shape and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its thick, waxy leaves help it conserve water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Echinops pachyphyllus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
