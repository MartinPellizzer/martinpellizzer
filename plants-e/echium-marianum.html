Echium marianum

Echium marianum, commonly known as the Mary's flower or sea heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Echium marianum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
