Eugenia plurisepala

Eugenia plurisepala, commonly known as the many-lobed eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a glossy surface.

Eugenia plurisepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
