Elymus hispanicus

Elymus hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces spikelets that are often adorned with bristles, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, and it is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Elymus hispanicus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and resilience.
