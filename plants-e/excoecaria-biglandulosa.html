Excoecaria biglandulosa

Excoecaria biglandulosa, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive reddish or purplish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its latex sap is milky and can be sticky, a feature shared with other members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
