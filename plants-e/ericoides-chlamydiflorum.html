Ericoides chlamydiflorum

Ericoides chlamydiflorum, commonly known as the stickyleaf heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are often covered in a waxy, sticky substance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in heathland or coastal areas.

Ericoides chlamydiflorum is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
