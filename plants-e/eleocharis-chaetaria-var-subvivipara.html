Eleocharis chaetaria var. subvivipara

Eleocharis chaetaria var. subvivipara, commonly known as the viviparous spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. One notable botanical feature is its viviparous habit, where some of the plant's shoots develop into small, mature plants while still attached to the parent plant.

This adaptation aids in vegetative propagation and enhances its survival in fluctuating wetland conditions.
