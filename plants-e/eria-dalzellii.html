Eria dalzellii

Eria dalzellii, commonly known as the Dalzell's orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small, with intricate patterns and a variety of colors, ranging from white to pink and purple.

Eria dalzellii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
