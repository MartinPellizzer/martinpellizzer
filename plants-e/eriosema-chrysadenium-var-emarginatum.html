Eriosema chrysadenium var. emarginatum

Eriosema chrysadenium var. emarginatum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered eriosema, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
