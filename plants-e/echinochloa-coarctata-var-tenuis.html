Echinochloa coarctata var. tenuis

Echinochloa coarctata var. tenuis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to various regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
