Erythraea centaurium var. fascicularis

Erythraea centaurium var. fascicularis, commonly known as the red centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Gentianaceae.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems and clusters of small, bright red or orange-red flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
