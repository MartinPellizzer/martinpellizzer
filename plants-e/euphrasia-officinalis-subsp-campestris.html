Euphrasia officinalis subsp. campestris

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the yellow burnet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are finely divided and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies for eye and respiratory conditions.
