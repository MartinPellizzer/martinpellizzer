Epidendrum heterobracteatum

Epidendrum heterobracteatum, commonly known as the variable-bract orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is an epiphytic orchid that typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and strap-like leaves that are often green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are usually pink or white, with a unique structure that includes modified bracts.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in the right growing conditions.
