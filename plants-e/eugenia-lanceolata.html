Eugenia lanceolata

Eugenia lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a juicy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.

Eugenia lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in home gardens.
