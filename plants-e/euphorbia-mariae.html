Euphorbia mariae

Euphorbia mariae, commonly known as the Marie's spurges, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a branching stem system with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Euphorbia mariae is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
