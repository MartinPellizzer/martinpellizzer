Eugenia verrucosa

Eugenia verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange berries. These berries contain a single seed and are often used in local cuisines for their tart flavor.

Eugenia verrucosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
