Echinopsis conaconensis

Echinopsis conaconensis, commonly known as the Paraguay night bloomer, is a cactus species native to Paraguay and Brazil.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and produces large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spiny areoles and a shallow root system for efficient water absorption. Its flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Echinopsis conaconensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique blooming behavior.
