Erica macowanii subsp. macowanii

Erica macowanii subsp.

macowanii, commonly known as the Macowani heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it requires specific conditions to flourish.
