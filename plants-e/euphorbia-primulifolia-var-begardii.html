Euphorbia primulifolia var. begardii

Euphorbia primulifolia var. begardii, commonly known as the Begard's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features compact, rounded stems that are often tinged with red or purple, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. The leaves are thick, fleshy, and typically have a waxy texture, which helps the plant store water. It is well adapted to arid environments and thrives in full sun with minimal water.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and ease of care in succulent gardens.
