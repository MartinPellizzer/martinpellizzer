Ervatamia linearifolia

Ervatamia linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ervatamia, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
