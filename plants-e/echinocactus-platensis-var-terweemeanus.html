Echinocactus platensis var. terweemeanus

Echinocactus platensis var. terweemeanus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature makes it well adapted to desert conditions, with a slow growth rate and a long lifespan.

The varietal name 'terweemeanus' honors the botanist who first described it, highlighting its significance in botanical classification.
