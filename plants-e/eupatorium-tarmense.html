Eupatorium tarmense

Eupatorium tarmense, commonly known as the Tarmian thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium tarmense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
