Euonymus grandiflorus

Euonymus grandiflorus, commonly known as the flowering euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pink. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Euonymus grandiflorus is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
