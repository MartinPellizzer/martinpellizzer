Erysimum mediohispanicum subsp. rondae

Erysimum mediohispanicum subsp.

rondae, commonly known as the Iberian wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The subspecies is distinguished by its smaller size and more compact growth habit compared to other Erysimum species.
