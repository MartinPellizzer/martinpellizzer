Echinocereus phoeniceus

Echinocereus phoeniceus, commonly known as the purple prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bats and birds. The plant has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

This cactus is also known for its edible fruits, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional cuisine.
