Eupatorium verbenaceum f. intermedium

Eupatorium verbenaceum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate eupatorium, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is native to parts of North America. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This plant is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
