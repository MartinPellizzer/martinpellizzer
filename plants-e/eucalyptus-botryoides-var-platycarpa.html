Eucalyptus botryoides var. platycarpa

Eucalyptus botryoides var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited honey gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy on top and pale underneath. The flowers are usually white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody, spherical capsules that contain the seeds. This plant is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
