Erysimum brassica

Erysimum brassica, commonly known as wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum brassica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
