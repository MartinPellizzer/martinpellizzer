Echinodorus cylindricus

Echinodorus cylindricus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cabomba, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often submerged in water. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the water surface, adding aesthetic value to ponds and water gardens. It is often used in aquariums and ornamental ponds due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of water conditions.

Echinodorus cylindricus is also known for its ability to help oxygenate water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
