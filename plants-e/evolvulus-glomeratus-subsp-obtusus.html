Evolvulus glomeratus subsp. obtusus

Evolvulus glomeratus subsp.

obtusus, commonly known as the bluntleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America. It is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and have a rounded base. It grows as a herbaceous annual, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive flowers and vigorous growth habit.
