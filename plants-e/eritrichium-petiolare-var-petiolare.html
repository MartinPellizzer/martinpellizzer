Eritrichium petiolare var. petiolare

Eritrichium petiolare var. petiolare, commonly known as the long-stemmed blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie prostrate on the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that are clustered in loose racemes, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is adapted to rocky and gravelly soils, often found in dry, open habitats.
