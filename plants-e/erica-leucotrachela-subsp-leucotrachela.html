Erica leucotrachela subsp. leucotrachela

Erica leucotrachela subsp.

leucotrachela, commonly known as the white-stemmed heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family. It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, adapted to the dry, acidic conditions of its habitat.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural environment.
