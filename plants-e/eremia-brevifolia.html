Eremia brevifolia

Eremia brevifolia, commonly known as the shortleaf eremisia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of tiny, greenish flowers that appear in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

Eremia brevifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
