Espeletia azucarina

Espeletia azucarina, commonly known as the sugar cane espeletia, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.

Espeletia azucarina is often found in cloud forests and is an important component of the local flora.
