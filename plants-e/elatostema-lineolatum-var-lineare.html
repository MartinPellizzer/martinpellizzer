Elatostema lineolatum var. lineare

Elatostema lineolatum var. lineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false shamrock, is a low-growing perennial herb native to eastern Australia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rainforest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable species for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
