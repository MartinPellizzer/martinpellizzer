Eugenia duplicata

Eugenia duplicata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or purple passionflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually oval-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

Eugenia duplicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
