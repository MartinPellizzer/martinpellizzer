Eucalyptus stoneana

Eucalyptus stoneana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the stage of development. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Eucalyptus stoneana is often found in open woodlands and heathlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
