Epicranthes chlororhopalon

Epicranthes chlororhopalon, commonly known as the yellow-flowered cranesbill, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright stems and showy, five-petaled flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange in color, with prominent white veins, and bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
