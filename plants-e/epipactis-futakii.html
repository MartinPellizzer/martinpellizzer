Epipactis futakii

Epipactis futakii, commonly known as the Futagami helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Epipactis futakii is a perennial herb that blooms in late spring to early summer.

Its floral structure is adapted for pollination by specific insects, reflecting its ecological niche in the understory of Japanese forests.
