Euploca tytoides

Euploca tytoides, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Euploca tytoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
