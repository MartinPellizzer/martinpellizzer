Epipactis youngiana

Epipactis youngiana, commonly known as the Young's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, hooded flowers with yellow and green markings. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Epipactis youngiana is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and is often found in association with decaying organic matter.
