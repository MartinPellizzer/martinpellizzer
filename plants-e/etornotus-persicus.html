Etornotus persicus

Etornotus persicus, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, aiding in water retention.

Etornotus persicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
