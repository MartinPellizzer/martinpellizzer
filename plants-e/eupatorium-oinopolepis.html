Eupatorium oinopolepis

Eupatorium oinopolepis, commonly known as the purple false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in scrublands and woodland edges. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its showy, purple to pink flower clusters appear in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium oinopolepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
