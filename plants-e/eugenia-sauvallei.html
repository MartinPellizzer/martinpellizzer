Eugenia sauvallei

Eugenia sauvallei, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
