Epacris × delicata

Epacris × delicata, commonly known as the sweet pinks or pink epacris, is a hybrid plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to white. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, evergreen leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Epacris × delicata is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
