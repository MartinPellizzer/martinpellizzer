Eulophia katangensis

Eulophia katangensis, commonly known as the Katanga eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect pseudobulbs and long, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in racemes. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia katangensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
