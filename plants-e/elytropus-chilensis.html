Elytropus chilensis

Elytropus chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments and is adapted to withstand arid conditions. Its morphology includes a basal rosette of leaves with a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Elytropus chilensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in local herbal remedies.
