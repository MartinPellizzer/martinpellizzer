Ehrharta erecta var. abyssinica

Ehrharta erecta var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
