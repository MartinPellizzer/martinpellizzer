Eupatorium havanense var. domingense

Eupatorium havanense var. domingense, commonly known as the Cuban blueweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract various pollinators.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
