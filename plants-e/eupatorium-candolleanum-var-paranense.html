Eupatorium candolleanum var. paranense

Eupatorium candolleanum var. paranense, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
