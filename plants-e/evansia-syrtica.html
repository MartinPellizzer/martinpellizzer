Evansia syrtica

Evansia syrtica, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that thrives in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that store water, and it produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its adaptability to harsh conditions makes it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Evansia syrtica plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
