Eriocaulon senegalense

Eriocaulon senegalense, commonly known as the Senegalese spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eriocaulon senegalense plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
