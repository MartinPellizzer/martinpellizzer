Eucharidium grandiflorum

Eucharidium grandiflorum, commonly known as the grandiflorous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its cultivation in gardens and botanical collections.
