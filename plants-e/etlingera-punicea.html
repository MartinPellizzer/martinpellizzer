Etlingera punicea

Etlingera punicea, commonly known as the crimson torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red bracts that enclose small, yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy texture.

Etlingera punicea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
