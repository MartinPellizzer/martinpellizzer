Eremopyrum bonaepartis var. sublanuginosum

Eremopyrum bonaepartis var. sublanuginosum, commonly known as the silky eremopyrum, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves that give it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low precipitation and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of steppe and desert ecosystems.
