Eryngium scirpinum

Eryngium scirpinum, commonly known as narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads that are surrounded by bracts resembling leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Eryngium scirpinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
