Erigeron mollis

Erigeron mollis, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in prairies, marshes, and along stream banks. Its showy, blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron mollis is also valued for its ornamental use in gardens and its role in supporting local wildlife.
