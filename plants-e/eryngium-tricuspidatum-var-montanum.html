Eryngium tricuspidatum var. montanum

Eryngium tricuspidatum var. montanum, commonly known as mountain thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
