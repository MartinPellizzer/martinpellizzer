Eugenia calimensis

Eugenia calimensis, commonly known as the Calimense myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia calimensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
