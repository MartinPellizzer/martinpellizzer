Eugenia tetrasperma

Eugenia tetrasperma, commonly known as the four-seeded eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, containing four seeds, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
