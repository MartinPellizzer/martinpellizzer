Eupatorium laeve var. latifolium

Eupatorium laeve var. latifolium, commonly known as broadleaf eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically broad and opposite, with a rough, textured surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
