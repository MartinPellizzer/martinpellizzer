Euzomum sativum

Euzomum sativum, commonly known as the wild lettuce, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features lobed leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive blue or purple tinge. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into spherical, pale green fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for medicinal purposes.

Euzomum sativum is known for its mild sedative properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
