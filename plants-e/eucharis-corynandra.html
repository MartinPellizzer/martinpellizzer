Eucharis corynandra

Eucharis corynandra, commonly known as the Amazonian water lily, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the surface of water. The plant produces striking white flowers with a strong fragrance, which bloom at night. Its roots are often submerged, anchoring it in aquatic environments.

Eucharis corynandra is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in wetland habitats.
