Erodium laciniatum var. nevadense

Erodium laciniatum var. nevadense, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, ovate leaves and produces small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as slopes and meadows. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a shallow root system adapted to arid conditions.

Its small size and unique leaf structure make it well-suited to its specific ecological niche.
