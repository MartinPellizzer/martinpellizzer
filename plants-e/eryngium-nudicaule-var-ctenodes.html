Eryngium nudicaule var. ctenodes

Eryngium nudicaule var. ctenodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open woodlands or coastal areas. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
