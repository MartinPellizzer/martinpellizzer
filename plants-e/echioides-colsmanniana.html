Echioides colsmanniana

Echioides colsmanniana, commonly known as the Cactus of the Andes, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
