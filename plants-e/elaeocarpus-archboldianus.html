Elaeocarpus archboldianus

Elaeocarpus archboldianus, commonly known as the Archbold's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
