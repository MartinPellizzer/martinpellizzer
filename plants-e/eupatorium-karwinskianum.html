Eupatorium karwinskianum

Eupatorium karwinskianum, commonly known as Karwin's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium karwinskianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
