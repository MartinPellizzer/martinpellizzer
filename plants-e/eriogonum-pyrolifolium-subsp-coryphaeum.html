Eriogonum pyrolifolium subsp. coryphaeum

Eriogonum pyrolifolium subsp.

coryphaeum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes, often in mountainous regions.

The plant features basal leaves that are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched. Its showy, clustered flowers are usually pink to reddish-purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects and small animals.
