Eriastrum densifolium subsp. sanctorum

Eriastrum densifolium subsp.

sanctorum, commonly known as the Sanctorum Denseleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland grasslands. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in the spring, and it produces small, oblong fruits known as cypsela.

This subspecies is notable for its dense foliage and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and drought tolerance.
