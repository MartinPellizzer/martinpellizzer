Euphrasia officinalis subsp. capitulata

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

capitulata, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers, which are typically arranged in a single terminal head.

The plant produces small, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive yellowish-green color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and flies.
