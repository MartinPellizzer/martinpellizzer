Eupatorium didymum

Eupatorium didymum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium didymum is a popular choice for gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
