Echeveria obtusata

Echeveria obtusata, commonly known as the Fat Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray or green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
