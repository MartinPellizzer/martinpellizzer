Euphorbia xeropoda

Euphorbia xeropoda, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with fleshy, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to survive in low-water conditions, storing moisture in its leaves and stem.

Euphorbia xeropoda is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
