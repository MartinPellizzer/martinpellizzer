Elymus halophilus

Elymus halophilus, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and saline environments.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in high-salt conditions, making it an important plant in coastal and inland saline soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.

Elymus halophilus plays a vital role in ecological restoration projects in saline habitats.
