Eria nudicaulis

Eria nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed eria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, leafless stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its unique morphology includes short, clustered leaves and a compact growth habit.

Eria nudicaulis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in horticultural collections.
