Elephantosis

Elephantosis, commonly known as kudzu, is a vine native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea.

It is characterized by its rapid growth rate and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by purple or red pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. Kudzu is often cultivated for its edible tubers, which are rich in starch and used in various culinary traditions.

However, it is also considered an invasive species in many regions due to its aggressive spread and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
