Ervum subglobosum

Ervum subglobosum, commonly known as the globular vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its spherical, hairy pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and North Africa. Its small, yellow flowers bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its habitat.

Ervum subglobosum is often found in open, sunny areas and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
