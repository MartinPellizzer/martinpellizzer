Erigeron compositus var. pinnatisectus

Erigeron compositus var. pinnatisectus, commonly known as the pinnatisect blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant features finely pinnatisected leaves that are narrow and linear, giving it a delicate appearance. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
