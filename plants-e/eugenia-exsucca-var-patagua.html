Eugenia exsucca var. patagua

Eugenia exsucca var. patagua, commonly known as the Patagonian guava, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet and acidic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in dry, coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
