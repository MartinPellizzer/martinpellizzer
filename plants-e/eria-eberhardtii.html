Eria eberhardtii

Eria eberhardtii, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Its leaves are usually broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eria eberhardtii thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.
