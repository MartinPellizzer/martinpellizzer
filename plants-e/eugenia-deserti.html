Eugenia deserti

Eugenia deserti, commonly known as the desert guava, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Eugenia deserti is adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary response to the challenges of its native habitat.
