Erythronium americanum var. bachii

Erythronium americanum var. bachii, commonly known as the Bachii dog-tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a bulbous root system and produces striking, showy flowers with reflexed petals in shades of white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and is characterized by its basal leaves that are lanceolate and often hairy. Its blooming period occurs in early spring, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its preference for partial shade and well-drained soil.
