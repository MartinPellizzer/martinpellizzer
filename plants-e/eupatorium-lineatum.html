Eupatorium lineatum

Eupatorium lineatum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of streams, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium lineatum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
