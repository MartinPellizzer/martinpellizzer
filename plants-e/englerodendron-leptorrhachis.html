Englerodendron leptorrhachis

Englerodendron leptorrhachis, commonly known as the narrow-ribbed poinsettia, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy surface.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, hidden within the colorful bracts that serve as a means of attracting pollinators.
