Eugenia abbotiana

Eugenia abbotiana, commonly known as the Abbot's eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several small, hard seeds.
