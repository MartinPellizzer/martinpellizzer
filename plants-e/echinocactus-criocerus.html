Echinocactus criocerus

Echinocactus criocerus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, which helps it store water efficiently. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its dense, cylindrical spines provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss through transpiration.

Echinocactus criocerus is well adapted to harsh desert environments, making it a resilient and visually distinctive member of the cactus family.
