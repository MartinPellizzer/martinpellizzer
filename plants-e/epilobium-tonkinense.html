Epilobium tonkinense

Epilobium tonkinense, commonly known as the Vietnamese willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Epilobium tonkinense is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and streambanks.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
