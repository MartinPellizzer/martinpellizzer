Erythraea boissieri

Erythraea boissieri, commonly known as the desert erythraea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually small and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Erythraea boissieri is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
