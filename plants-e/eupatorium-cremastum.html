Eupatorium cremastum

Eupatorium cremastum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wetlands, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is often gray-green, with finely toothed leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Eupatorium cremastum is valued in gardens for its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
