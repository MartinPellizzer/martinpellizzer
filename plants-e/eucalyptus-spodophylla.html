Eucalyptus spodophylla

Eucalyptus spodophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding insects and birds. Its bark is often flaky and reddish-brown, and it is commonly found in dry, sandy soils.

Eucalyptus spodophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.
