Eupatorium godfreyanum

Eupatorium godfreyanum, commonly known as Godfrey's thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eupatorium godfreyanum is typically found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
