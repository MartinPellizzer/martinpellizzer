Eupatorium thermarum

Eupatorium thermarum, commonly known as the hot water eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.

Eupatorium thermarum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ecological and ornamental qualities.
