Ensete glaucum

Ensete glaucum, commonly known as the blue enset, is a striking member of the Musaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that often display a distinctive blue or grayish hue due to a waxy coating. The plant can grow up to 6 meters tall, with a stout, unbranched stem that resembles a tree. Its inflorescence consists of a tall spike of yellow flowers, which appear at the top of the stem.

Ensete glaucum is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.
