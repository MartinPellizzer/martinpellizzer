Erica tetralix subsp. stuartii

Erica tetralix subsp.

stuartii, commonly known as Stuart's heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the British Isles. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils, often found in heathland and moorland environments.

The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

This subspecies is relatively rare and is often protected in its natural habitats.
