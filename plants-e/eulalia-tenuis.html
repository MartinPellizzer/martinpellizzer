Eulalia tenuis

Eulalia tenuis, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and marshes. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

Eulalia tenuis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
