Edwardsia minima

Edwardsia minima, commonly known as the dwarf rock orchid, is a small terrestrial orchid native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant in many areas.
