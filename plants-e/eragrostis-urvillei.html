Eragrostis urvillei

Eragrostis urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and the plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Eragrostis urvillei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
