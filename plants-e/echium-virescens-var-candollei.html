Echium virescens var. candollei

Echium virescens var. candollei, commonly known as the green echium, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits.
