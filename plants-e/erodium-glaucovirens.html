Erodium glaucovirens

Erodium glaucovirens, commonly known as the silverleaf catchfly, is a low-growing annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often appearing in clusters along the stems. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of whitish wax, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it easily identifiable and adapted to Mediterranean climates.
