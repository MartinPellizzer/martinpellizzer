Eucalyptus obtusiflora subsp. dongarraensis

Eucalyptus obtusiflora subsp.

dongarraensis, commonly known as the Dongarra mallee eucalypt, is a species native to the arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with poor soil conditions.

This subspecies plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
