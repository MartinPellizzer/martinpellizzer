Escallonia claussenii

Escallonia claussenii, commonly known as the Chilean fire bush, is a shrub native to Chile and parts of Argentina.

It belongs to the family Escalloniaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers that attract birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Escallonia claussenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in temperate climates.
