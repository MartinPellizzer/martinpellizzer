Enckea blattarum

Enckea blattarum, commonly known as the arrowleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
