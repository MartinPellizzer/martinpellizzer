Euphorbia mertonii

Euphorbia mertonii, commonly known as the Merton spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia mertonii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
