Eugenia cryptadena

Eugenia cryptadena, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, reddish berries that contain seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

Eugenia cryptadena thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
