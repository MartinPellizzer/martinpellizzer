Eugenia villae-novae

Eugenia villae-novae, commonly known as the New World clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit, a small, round drupe, contains a single seed and is used in traditional medicine.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
