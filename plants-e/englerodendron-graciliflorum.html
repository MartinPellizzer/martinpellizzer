Englerodendron graciliflorum

Englerodendron graciliflorum, commonly known as the slender-flowered englerodendron, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
