Eugenia triquetra

Eugenia triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered eggplant, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or purple when mature, containing numerous small seeds. Eugenia triquetra is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
