Eugenia dimorpha var. australis

Eugenia dimorpha var. australis, commonly known as the Australian myrtle, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It features glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and horticultural collections.
