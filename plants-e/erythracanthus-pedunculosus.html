Erythracanthus pedunculosus

Erythracanthus pedunculosus, commonly known as the red-fruited sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems and succulent tissues for water storage.

The species is of interest to botanists and ecologists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in desert ecosystems.
