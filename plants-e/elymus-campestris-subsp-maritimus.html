Elymus campestris subsp. maritimus

Elymus campestris subsp.

maritimus, commonly known as saltgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to saline and coastal environments, often found in dunes and salt marshes. Its deep root system helps stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

It plays an important ecological role in coastal ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
