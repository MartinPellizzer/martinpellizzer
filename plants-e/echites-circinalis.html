Echites circinalis

Echites circinalis, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate flower structure that resembles a spider. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the summer months.

Echites circinalis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
