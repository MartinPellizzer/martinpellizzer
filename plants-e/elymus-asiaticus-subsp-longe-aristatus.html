Elymus asiaticus subsp. longe-aristatus

Elymus asiaticus subsp.

longe-aristatus, commonly known as the longawn feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and elongated, narrow leaves.

The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are often adorned with long, bristly awns, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows and grasslands, and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
