Eulophia welwitschii subsp. aurantiaca

Eulophia welwitschii subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange-tinged orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking orange and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its pseudobulbs are elongated and support the plant's growth, while its leaves are narrow and grass-like.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
