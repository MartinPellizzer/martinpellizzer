Epilobium × reedii

Epilobium × reedii, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a hybrid plant species belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along riverbanks.

Epilobium × reedii is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects.
