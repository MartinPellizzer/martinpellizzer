Eucalyptus broviniensis

Eucalyptus broviniensis, commonly known as the Broviniensis gum tree, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a tall, slender tree that typically grows in coastal and wet forest regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is valued for its timber and has been used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
