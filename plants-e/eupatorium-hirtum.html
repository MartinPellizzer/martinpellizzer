Eupatorium hirtum

Eupatorium hirtum, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in coarse hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads, typically blooming in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium hirtum is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.
