Erlangea pubescens

Erlangea pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent erlangia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, or pubescent, stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
