Echinomastus uncinatus

Echinomastus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked echinomastus, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically yellow or orange and are borne in terminal inflorescences. The plant produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds.

It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with mechanisms to store water and reduce transpiration.
