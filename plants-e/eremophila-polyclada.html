Eremophila polyclada

Eremophila polyclada, commonly known as the clustered emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink and red, which bloom during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila polyclada is often used in native landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
