Eurya japonica var. harmandii

Eurya japonica var. harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's Japanese eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by small, orange-red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
