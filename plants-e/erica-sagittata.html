Erica sagittata

Erica sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its narrow, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica sagittata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
