Ervum capricornium

Ervum capricornium, commonly known as the capricorn bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and cylindrical, pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated pods containing several seeds. It is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

The plant thrives in warm climates and is adapted to well-drained soils.
