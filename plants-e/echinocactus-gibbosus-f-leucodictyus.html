Echinocactus gibbosus f. leucodictyus

Echinocactus gibbosus f. leucodictyus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the Mexican hedgehog cactus.

It is characterized by its pale blue to white spines, which distinguish it from the typical green spines of the standard species. The plant features a globular, ribbed stem with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It is a slow-growing cactus native to the Sierra Madre mountains in Mexico.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in arid gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
