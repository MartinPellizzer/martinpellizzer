Euroschinus ledermannii

Euroschinus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, showcasing adaptations to arid environments. The plant features small, green leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive seed capsules. It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
