Euphrasia minima subsp. frigida

Euphrasia minima subsp.

frigida, commonly known as the dwarf eyebright, is a small herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant features delicate, hairy stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in a basal rosette, and the flowers are often clustered in racemes.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh climates and is often found in mountainous or tundra-like habitats.
