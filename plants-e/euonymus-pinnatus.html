Euonymus pinnatus

Euonymus pinnatus, commonly known as the spreading euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pinnately compound leaves that are often variegated with yellow and white veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Euonymus pinnatus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
