Eremurus altaicus f. pallidus

Eremurus altaicus f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale desert candle, is a variant of the Eremurus genus native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence that resembles a candle, with pale yellow to white flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, featuring thick, fleshy stems and leaves that help conserve water. It blooms in late spring or early summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its natural habitat.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens that can accommodate its specific environmental needs.
