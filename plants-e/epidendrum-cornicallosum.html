Epidendrum cornicallosum

Epidendrum cornicallosum, commonly known as the coral orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, coral-pink flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, making it an epiphytic species. Its leaves are elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

Epidendrum cornicallosum is valued in horticulture for its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
