Erica spinifera

Erica spinifera, commonly known as the prickly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica spinifera typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

It is well adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
