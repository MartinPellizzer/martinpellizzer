Elymus canadensis f. wiegandii

Elymus canadensis f. wiegandii, commonly known as the Wiegand's Canada wildrye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wet meadows and riparian areas. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle, bearing spikelets that are often awned.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
