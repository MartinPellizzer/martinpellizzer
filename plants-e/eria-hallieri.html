Eria hallieri

Eria hallieri, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Eria hallieri is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
