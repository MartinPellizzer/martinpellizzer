Encyclia incumbens

Encyclia incumbens, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short rhizome and a rosette of broad, fleshy leaves. The plant produces inflorescences bearing showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive coloration pattern. Its flowers typically have a greenish-yellow lip and a white or pale pink dorsal sepal.

Encyclia incumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
