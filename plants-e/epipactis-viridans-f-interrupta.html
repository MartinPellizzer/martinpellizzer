Epipactis viridans f. interrupta

Epipactis viridans f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted green helleborine, is a rare and distinctive orchid species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its elongated, grass-like leaves and a unique floral structure with a greenish-yellow inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are often interrupted in their development, giving the species its specific epithet "interrupta.".

This orchid is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements.
