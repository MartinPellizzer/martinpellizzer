Elatostematoides mindanaensis

Elatostematoides mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao elatostema, is a species of liverwort belonging to the family Elatostomataceae.

It is a non-vascular plant characterized by its leafy structure and the presence of specialized structures called gemmae, which aid in asexual reproduction. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices, where it can retain adequate humidity. Its leaves are usually small, often less than 1 cm in length, and are arranged in a dense, cushion-like formation.

Elatostematoides mindanaensis plays a role in soil stabilization and contributes to the biodiversity of its native habitat in the Philippines.
