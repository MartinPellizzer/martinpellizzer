Erigeron brachycomoides

Erigeron brachycomoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are narrow and hairy, adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments.
