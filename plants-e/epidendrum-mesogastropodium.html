Epidendrum mesogastropodium

Epidendrum mesogastropodium, commonly known as the black orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, velvety petals and sepals, which give it its striking appearance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive coloration that ranges from deep purple to black.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
