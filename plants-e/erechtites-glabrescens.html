Erechtites glabrescens

Erechtites glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth beggar's-ticks, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features hairy stems that become smooth as the plant matures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges. It produces small, yellowish to brownish flowers that attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Erechtites glabrescens is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands.
