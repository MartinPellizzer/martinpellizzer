Eugenia palumbis

Eugenia palumbis, commonly known as the swamp guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit, resembling a small apple, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia palumbis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.
