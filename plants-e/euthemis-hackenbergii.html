Euthemis hackenbergii

Euthemis hackenbergii, commonly known as the Hackenberg's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
