Euchiton audax

Euchiton audax, commonly known as the giant hibiscus, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically displaying vibrant red or orange hues. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a rough texture, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Euchiton audax is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
