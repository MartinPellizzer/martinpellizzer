Elaeocarpus lacunosus

Elaeocarpus lacunosus, commonly known as the clustered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in its native habitat.
