Erica sulphurea

Erica sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica sulphurea is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the Western Cape region.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
