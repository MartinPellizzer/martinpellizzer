Euphorbia knobelii

Euphorbia knobelii, commonly known as the knobstick euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, thick, cylindrical stem with a knobby texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia knobelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
