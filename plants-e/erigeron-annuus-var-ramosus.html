Erigeron annuus var. ramosus

Erigeron annuus var. ramosus, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas. The plant features a branching stem covered in fine hairs, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, daisy-like, with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early fall.

It is often found in the central and eastern United States, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
