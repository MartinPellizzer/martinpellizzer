Elaeocarpus lanceolatus

Elaeocarpus lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant's fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
