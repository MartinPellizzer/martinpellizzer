Empetrum hermaphroditum var. americanum

Empetrum hermaphroditum var. americanum, commonly known as American crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries that are typically clustered in tight, spherical inflorescences. The plant has compact, leathery leaves that are arranged in a whorled pattern around the stems. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in boreal and alpine habitats.

The species is notable for its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of northern ecosystems.
