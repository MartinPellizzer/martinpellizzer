Ehretia laevis var. aspera

Ehretia laevis var. aspera, commonly known as the roughleaf ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually rough to the touch. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in warm climates.
