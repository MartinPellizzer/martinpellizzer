Eugenia coarensis

Eugenia coarensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia coarensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia coarensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
