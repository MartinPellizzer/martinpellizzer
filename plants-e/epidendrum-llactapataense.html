Epidendrum llactapataense

Epidendrum llactapataense, commonly known as the Llacta Passion flower, is a species of orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescence that resembles a passion flower, with vibrant red and purple hues. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are highly fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and hummingbirds.

Epidendrum llactapataense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
