Eriochloa punctata var. minor

Eriochloa punctata var. minor, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows, and is often found in temperate regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
