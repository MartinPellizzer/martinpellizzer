Euphorbia mangokyensis

Euphorbia mangokyensis, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and milky sap. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, while its flowers are clustered in cyathia, a unique inflorescence type.

Euphorbia mangokyensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
