Erodium triangulare subsp. bovei

Erodium triangulare subsp.

bovei, commonly known as the Bove's narrowleaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is a small annual herb with slender stems and trifoliate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
