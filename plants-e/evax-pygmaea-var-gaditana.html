Evax pygmaea var. gaditana

Evax pygmaea var. gaditana, commonly known as the Canary Island rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing succulent stems that store water. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
