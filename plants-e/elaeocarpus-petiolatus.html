Elaeocarpus petiolatus

Elaeocarpus petiolatus, commonly known as the slender-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a prominent petiole. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
