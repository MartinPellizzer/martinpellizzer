Eranthemum variabile var. dentatum

Eranthemum variabile var. dentatum, commonly known as the toothed eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pink. The leaves are usually ovate with serrated edges, giving the plant its varietal name "dentatum.". It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
