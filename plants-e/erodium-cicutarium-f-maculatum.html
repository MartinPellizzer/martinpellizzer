Erodium cicutarium f. maculatum

Erodium cicutarium f. maculatum, commonly known as spotted bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink or white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are typically trifoliate with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The plant produces tiny, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Spotted bedstraw is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
