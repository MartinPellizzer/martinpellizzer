Eulophia fitzalanii

Eulophia fitzalanii, commonly known as the Fitzalan's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often on rocky or humus-rich substrates. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Eulophia fitzalanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
