Elaeis guineensis var. macrocarpa

Elaeis guineensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the African oil palm, is a tropical plant native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fruit that contain oil-rich seeds. The plant is highly valued for its economic importance, particularly in the production of palm oil. Its botanical features include a robust trunk and a complex root system adapted to nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also notable for its rapid growth and high yield potential in suitable climatic conditions.
