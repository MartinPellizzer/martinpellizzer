Eucalyptus leucophloia subsp. leucophloia

Eucalyptus leucophloia subsp.

leucophloia, commonly known as the white gum, is a tall, slender tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels off in thin, white sheets, giving it its common name.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a whitish underside. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically cream to pale yellow in color.

This subspecies is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
