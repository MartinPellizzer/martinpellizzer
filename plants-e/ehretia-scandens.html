Ehretia scandens

Ehretia scandens, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a silvery underside, giving it its common name.

The fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.
