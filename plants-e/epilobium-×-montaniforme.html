Epilobium × montaniforme

Epilobium × montaniforme, commonly known as the mountain willow or evergreen willow, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are woody and can reach heights of up to six feet.

Epilobium × montaniforme is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in riparian habitats.
