Eleutherococcus zhejiangensis

Eleutherococcus zhejiangensis, commonly known as Zhejiang eleuthero, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of eastern China.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces red berries that are rich in bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been studied for potential health benefits, though it remains relatively less known compared to other eleutherococcus species.
