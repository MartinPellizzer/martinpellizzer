Elsholtzia fruticosa f. inclusa

Elsholtzia fruticosa f. inclusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elsholtzia, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow, and often silver-haired leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical features make it a valued plant in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability.
