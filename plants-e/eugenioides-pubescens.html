Eugenioides pubescens

Eugenioides pubescens, commonly known as the hairy eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
