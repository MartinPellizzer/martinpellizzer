Eucalyptus cylindriflora

Eucalyptus cylindriflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical flower spikes that typically appear in late summer to early autumn. The leaves are narrow and often have a greyish-green hue, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. This plant is part of the Myrtaceae family and is adapted to grow in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various native animals.
