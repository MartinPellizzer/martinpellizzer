Evolvulus columbianus var. incanus

Evolvulus columbianus var. incanus, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often twining. This variant is distinguished by its pale coloration compared to other varieties of Evolvulus columbianus.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
