Eruca vesicaria var. cappadocica

Eruca vesicaria var. cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian garlic mustard, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pungent, mustard-like odor. The plant features basal leaves that are oval to lance-shaped with a hairy surface, and its stems are often hairy as well. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which have a strong, garlic-like flavor.
