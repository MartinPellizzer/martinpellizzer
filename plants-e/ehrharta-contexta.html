Ehrharta contexta

Ehrharta contexta, commonly known as silky grass or velvet grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft, and densely tufted foliage, which gives it a distinctive velvety appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny grains of pollen.

Ehrharta contexta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
