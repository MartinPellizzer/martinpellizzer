Eriocaulon merrillii

Eriocaulon merrillii, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eriocaulon merrillii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
