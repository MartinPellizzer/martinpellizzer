Ericoila umbellata

Ericoila umbellata, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady, moist environments.

Ericoila umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
