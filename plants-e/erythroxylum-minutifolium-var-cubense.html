Erythroxylum minutifolium var. cubense

Erythroxylum minutifolium var. cubense, commonly known as Cuban chili pepper, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean.

It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, oblong fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The species is related to other members of the Erythroxylaceae family, which includes plants known for their psychoactive properties.
