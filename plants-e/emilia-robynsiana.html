Emilia robynsiana

Emilia robynsiana, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or disturbed areas.

Emilia robynsiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
