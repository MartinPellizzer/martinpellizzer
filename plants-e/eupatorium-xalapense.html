Eupatorium xalapense

Eupatorium xalapense, commonly known as Mexican thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range from white to pale purple.

Eupatorium xalapense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
