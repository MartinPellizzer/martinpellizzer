Eriocaulon matsumurae

Eriocaulon matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese marsh grass, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eriocaulon matsumurae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
