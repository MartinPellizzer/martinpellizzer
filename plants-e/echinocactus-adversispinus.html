Echinocactus adversispinus

Echinocactus adversispinus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tinge. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores.

Echinocactus adversispinus is well adapted to drought conditions and requires minimal watering once established.
