Eulophia microdactyla

Eulophia microdactyla, commonly known as the small-fingered eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, narrow leaves. The plant produces fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Eulophia microdactyla typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the substrate, making it a hardy yet specialized orchid species.
