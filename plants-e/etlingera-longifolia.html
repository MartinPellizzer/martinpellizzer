Etlingera longifolia

Etlingera longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that resemble torches, which bloom in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a reddish hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Etlingera longifolia is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
