Eugenia reinwardtiana

Eugenia reinwardtiana, commonly known as the Java apple or red mangostan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically pink to red in color when ripe. These berries are rich in antioxidants and have been traditionally used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Eugenia reinwardtiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
