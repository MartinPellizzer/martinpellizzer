Euclea crispa subsp. ovata

Euclea crispa subsp.

ovata, commonly known as the smooth euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate leaves that are typically arranged oppositely.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
