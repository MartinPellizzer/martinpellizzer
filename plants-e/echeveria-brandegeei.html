Echeveria brandegeei

Echeveria brandegeei, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are pale green to blue-gray in color, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces showy clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
