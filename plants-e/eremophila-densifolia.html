Eremophila densifolia

Eremophila densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dense, narrow leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, often attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila densifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
