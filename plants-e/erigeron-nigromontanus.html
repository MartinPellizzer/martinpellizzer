Erigeron nigromontanus

Erigeron nigromontanus, commonly known as black mountain fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as mountains and open woodlands. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its ability to thrive in harsh environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
