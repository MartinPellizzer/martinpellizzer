Enneapogon planifolius

Enneapogon planifolius, commonly known as the flat-leaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, flat leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to golden florets.

Enneapogon planifolius is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
