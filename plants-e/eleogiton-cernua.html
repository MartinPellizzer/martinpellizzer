Eleogiton cernua

Eleogiton cernua, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems that bear small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eleogiton cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
