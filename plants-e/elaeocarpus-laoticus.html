Elaeocarpus laoticus

Elaeocarpus laoticus, commonly known as the Laotian elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Laos and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, ornamental flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Elaeocarpus laoticus is valued for its ecological role in forest ecosystems and its potential use in horticulture.
