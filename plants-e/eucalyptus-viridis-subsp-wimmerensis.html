Eucalyptus viridis subsp. wimmerensis

Eucalyptus viridis subsp.

wimmerensis, commonly known as the Wimmera peppermint, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its lance-shaped, aromatic leaves that are often glandular and have a peppermint scent.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically white or pale green, followed by small, woody capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with a dry summer season.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
