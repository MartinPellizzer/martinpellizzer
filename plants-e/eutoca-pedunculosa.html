Eutoca pedunculosa

Eutoca pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, while its leaves are oval and have a slightly hairy texture.
