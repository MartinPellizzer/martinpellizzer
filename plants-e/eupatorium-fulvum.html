Eupatorium fulvum

Eupatorium fulvum, commonly known as the golden weed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and roadside ditches. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium fulvum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
