Euphorbia crenulata var. franciscana

Euphorbia crenulata var. franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan spurge, is a succulent plant native to the western United States.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-shaped structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
