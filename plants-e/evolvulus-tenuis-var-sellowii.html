Evolvulus tenuis var. sellowii

Evolvulus tenuis var. sellowii, commonly known as the slender blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
