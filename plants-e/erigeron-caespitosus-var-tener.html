Erigeron caespitosus var. tener

Erigeron caespitosus var. tener, commonly known as the woolly fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value.

Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
