Eryngium depressum

Eryngium depressum, commonly known as the low thistle, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and spherical, purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it an important species in native plant gardens and conservation efforts.
