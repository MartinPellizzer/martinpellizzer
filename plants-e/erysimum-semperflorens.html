Erysimum semperflorens

Erysimum semperflorens, commonly known as the constant-flowering wallflower, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers throughout the growing season. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy. Native to southern Europe, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

It is often used in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
