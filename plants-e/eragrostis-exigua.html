Eragrostis exigua

Eragrostis exigua, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often used for forage in livestock grazing due to its nutritional value and hardiness.

Eragrostis exigua is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
