Erisma petiolatum

Erisma petiolatum, commonly known as the slender sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in early spring. The plant has thin, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, sunny environments.

Erisma petiolatum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and provides nectar for early pollinators.
