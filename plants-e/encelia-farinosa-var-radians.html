Encelia farinosa var. radians

Encelia farinosa var. radians, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats. Its structure consists of a woody stem with alternate leaves and a showy inflorescence composed of numerous small florets.

The species is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and a unique ability to reflect sunlight through its fuzzy leaves.
