Euphrasia mutica

Euphrasia mutica, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Euphrasia mutica is often found in the wild and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
