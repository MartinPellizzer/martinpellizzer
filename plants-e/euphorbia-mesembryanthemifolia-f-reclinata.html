Euphorbia mesembryanthemifolia f. reclinata

Euphorbia mesembryanthemifolia f. reclinata, commonly known as the reclining spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
