Erucaria aegiceras var. sonisii

Erucaria aegiceras var. sonisii, commonly known as the mangrove apple or sea apple, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its woody structure and ability to thrive in saline and brackish water environments. It has small, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Its ecological significance lies in its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and providing habitat for marine and terrestrial species.
