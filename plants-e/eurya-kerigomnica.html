Eurya kerigomnica

Eurya kerigomnica, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Eurya kerigomnica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
