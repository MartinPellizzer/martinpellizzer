Elaeocarpus radicans

Elaeocarpus radicans, commonly known as the climbing elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its woody stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green surface with a slightly serrated margin.

Elaeocarpus radicans is often found in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits.
