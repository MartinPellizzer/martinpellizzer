Eryngium rochei

Eryngium rochei, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiky, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sunlight. Eryngium rochei belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
