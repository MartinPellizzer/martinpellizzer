Eugenia mendute

Eugenia mendute, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or purple cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, typically growing in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, which resembles a small cherry, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Eugenia mendute is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
