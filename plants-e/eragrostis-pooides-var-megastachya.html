Eragrostis pooides var. megastachya

Eragrostis pooides var. megastachya, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that can reach several feet in height, making it a striking feature in the landscape. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in non-native regions.
