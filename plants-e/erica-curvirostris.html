Erica curvirostris

Erica curvirostris, commonly known as the curved-beak heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers that bloom in spring. Erica curvirostris typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils in open habitats such as fynbos vegetation.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions where it thrives in mild, temperate climates.
