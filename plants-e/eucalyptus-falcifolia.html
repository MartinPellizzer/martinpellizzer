Eucalyptus falcifolia

Eucalyptus falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds. The plant typically grows in shrublands and woodlands, often in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces white or pale yellow flowers, which are clustered in umbels.

Eucalyptus falcifolia is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and fire resistance.
