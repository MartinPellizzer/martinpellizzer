Eugenia multiflora var. lutescens

Eugenia multiflora var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden flame guava, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to yellow flowers, which contrast with its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
