Eupatorium pseudochilca

Eupatorium pseudochilca, commonly known as false boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The flowers are small, white to pale blue, and are arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters, making it visually distinct.
