Eria summerhayesiana

Eria summerhayesiana, commonly known as the Summerhayes' orchid, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, linear leaves and a single, fragrant flower that typically blooms in the summer months. The flower has a unique structure with a spurred lip and is often found growing in shaded, moist habitats. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is not commonly cultivated outside its native range.
