Epipactis caudata

Epipactis caudata, commonly known as the tail orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender stem with a single, showy flower that has a distinctive hood-like structure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base.

Epipactis caudata is known for its unique pollination mechanism involving specific insects.
