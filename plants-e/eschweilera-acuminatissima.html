Eschweilera acuminatissima

Eschweilera acuminatissima, commonly known as the Amazonian wax palm, is a tall, slender palm native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and long, slender trunk that can reach heights of up to 25 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem.

Due to its ecological significance, it is also considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
