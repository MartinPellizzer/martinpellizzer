Echeveria coruana

Echeveria coruana, commonly known as the Mexican snowball, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray or silver in color, with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy clusters of white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Echeveria coruana is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
