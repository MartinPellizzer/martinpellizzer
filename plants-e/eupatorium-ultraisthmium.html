Eupatorium ultraisthmium

Eupatorium ultraisthmium, commonly known as the St. John's wort of the Caribbean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in humid, moist environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium ultraisthmium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
