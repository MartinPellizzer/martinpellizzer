Eupatorium Hyssopifolium Var. Linearifolium

Blue mistflower, scientifically known as Eupatorium hyssopifolium var. linearifolium, is a striking perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It is prized for its delicate blue-gray foliage and clusters of tiny, mist-like white flowers that bloom in late summer. This plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil, making it a versatile addition to gardens and natural landscapes. Its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees enhances its ecological value. With its ethereal appearance and low maintenance requirements, blue mistflower is a popular choice for both ornamental and habitat restoration purposes.
