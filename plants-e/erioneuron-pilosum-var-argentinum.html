Erioneuron pilosum var. argentinum

Erioneuron pilosum var. argentinum, commonly known as the Argentine silky grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, wiry stems covered in soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its overall silvery-green hue.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.
