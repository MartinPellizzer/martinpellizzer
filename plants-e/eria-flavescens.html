Eria flavescens

Eria flavescens, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Eria flavescens is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in traditional medicine.
