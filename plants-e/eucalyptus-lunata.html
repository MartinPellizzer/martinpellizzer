Eucalyptus lunata

Eucalyptus lunata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, specifically found in the southern regions of Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Eucalyptus lunata typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
