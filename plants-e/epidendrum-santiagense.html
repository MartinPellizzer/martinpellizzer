Epidendrum santiagense

Epidendrum santiagense, commonly known as the Santiago orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or orange markings, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum santiagense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
