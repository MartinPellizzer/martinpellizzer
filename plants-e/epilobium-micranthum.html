Epilobium micranthum

Epilobium micranthum, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and roadside areas. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium micranthum is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open, wet habitats.
