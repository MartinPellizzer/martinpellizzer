Eucalyptus parramattensis subsp. parramattensis

Eucalyptus parramattensis subsp.

parramattensis, commonly known as the Parramatta eucalypt, is a medium to tall tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of grey and white.

The leaves are lance-shaped, aromatic, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically cream to pale yellow in color.

This subspecies is adapted to a range of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
