Erysimum duriaei subsp. pyrenaicum

Erysimum duriaei subsp.

pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region. It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are bright yellow with four petals. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
