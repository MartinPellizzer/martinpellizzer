Eriogonum douglasii var. twisselmannii

Eriogonum douglasii var. twisselmannii, commonly known as the Twisselmann's buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
