Eugenia verticillaris

Eugenia verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in groups of three, giving it its common name. The fruit, when mature, is a small, yellowish berry with a sweet and aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in warm climates.
