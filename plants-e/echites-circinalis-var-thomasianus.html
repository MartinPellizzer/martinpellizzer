Echites circinalis var. thomasianus

Echites circinalis var. thomasianus, commonly known as the Thomasian false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. It has alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
