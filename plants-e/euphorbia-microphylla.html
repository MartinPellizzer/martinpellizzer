Euphorbia microphylla

Euphorbia microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap, which can be irritating to the skin.

Euphorbia microphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
