Equisetum humboldtianum

Equisetum humboldtianum, commonly known as the Humboldt's horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are typically found in clusters. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Equisetum humboldtianum is a primitive plant that has remained largely unchanged for millions of years.
