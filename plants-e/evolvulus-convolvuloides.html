Evolvulus convolvuloides

Evolvulus convolvuloides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and can twine around supports.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
