Euphorbia seguieriana subsp. armeniaca

Euphorbia seguieriana subsp.

armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
