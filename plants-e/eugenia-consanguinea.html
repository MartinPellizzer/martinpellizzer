Eugenia consanguinea

Eugenia consanguinea, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically purple to red in color when mature. Its botanical features include a smooth, bark-like trunk and clusters of small, leathery leaves.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
