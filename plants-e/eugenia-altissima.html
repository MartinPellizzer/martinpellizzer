Eugenia altissima

Eugenia altissima, commonly known as the large-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia altissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
