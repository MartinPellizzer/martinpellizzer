Echium decaisnei

Echium decaisnei, commonly known as the Sierra Madre evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Echium decaisnei is a member of the Boraginaceae family, known for its showy flowers and medicinal properties in some species.
