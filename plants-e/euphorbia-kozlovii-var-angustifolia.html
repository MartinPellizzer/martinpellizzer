Euphorbia kozlovii var. angustifolia

Euphorbia kozlovii var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It features a shrub-like growth habit with fleshy, angular stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers, and it is known for its toxic sap that can cause skin irritation. It is often found in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
