Erigeron thunbergii f. furusei

Erigeron thunbergii f. furusei, commonly known as the Japanese fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its fluffy, daisy-like flowers.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in woodland and grassland environments. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

The subspecies 'furusei' is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly darker flower color compared to the typical form.
