Elsholtzia kachinensis

Elsholtzia kachinensis, commonly known as the Kachin elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, often in temperate climates. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, and they bloom in late summer.

The leaves are aromatic and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
