Elaeagnus argentea

Elaeagnus argentea, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its ability to thrive in poor soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Elaeagnus argentea is also known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in soil restoration.
