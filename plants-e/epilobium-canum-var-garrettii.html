Epilobium canum var. garrettii

Epilobium canum var. garrettii, commonly known as Garrett's willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
