Epicarpurus orientalis

Epicarpurus orientalis, commonly known as the oriental epicarp, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and pink. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, pale green bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that ripens to a deep red color, attracting various bird species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
