Eupatorium pseudarboreum

Eupatorium pseudarboreum, commonly known as the false tree eupatorium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, clustered purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its inflorescences attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium pseudarboreum plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
