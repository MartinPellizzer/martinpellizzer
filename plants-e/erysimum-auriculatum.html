Erysimum auriculatum

Erysimum auriculatum, commonly known as the auriculate wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

Erysimum auriculatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
