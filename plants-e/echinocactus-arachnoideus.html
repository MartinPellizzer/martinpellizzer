Echinocactus arachnoideus

Echinocactus arachnoideus, commonly known as the spider hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles that bear both radial and central spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
