Etlingera comosa

Etlingera comosa, commonly known as the hairy ginger, is a tropical perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its inflorescence consists of brightly colored, showy flowers that range from yellow to red.

Etlingera comosa is valued both for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
