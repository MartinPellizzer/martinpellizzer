Epilobium glandulosum var. brionense

Epilobium glandulosum var. brionense, commonly known as the Brionese willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as along streambanks and in meadows. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are glandular-dotted. Its showy, pink to purple flowers are arranged in terminal clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
