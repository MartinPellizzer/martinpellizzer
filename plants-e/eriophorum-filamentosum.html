Eriophorum filamentosum

Eriophorum filamentosum, commonly known as the silky tussock sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a tuft of silky threads, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet, boggy environments, such as marshes and peatlands, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.

Eriophorum filamentosum is often found in northern regions, including parts of North America and Eurasia.
