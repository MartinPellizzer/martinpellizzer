Eremophila platycalyx subsp. pardalota

Eremophila platycalyx subsp.

pardalota, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing nectar for native pollinators.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader calyx lobes compared to other subspecies of Eremophila platycalyx.
