Eriogonum polyanthum var. bahiiforme

Eriogonum polyanthum var. bahiiforme, commonly known as the coastal buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salt exposure. Its leaves are usually hairy and gray-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local wildlife.
