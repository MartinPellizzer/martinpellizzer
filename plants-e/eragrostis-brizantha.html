Eragrostis brizantha

Eragrostis brizantha, commonly known as African grass or brizantha grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, robust growth, with erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed flowers. It is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a common species in grasslands and agricultural settings.

Eragrostis brizantha is also valued for its use in forage and as a component in pasture systems.
