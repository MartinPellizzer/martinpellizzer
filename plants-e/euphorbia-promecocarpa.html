Euphorbia promecocarpa

Euphorbia promecocarpa, commonly known as the desert gloxinia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Americas.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardening in dry climates.
