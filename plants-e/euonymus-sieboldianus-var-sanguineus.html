Euonymus sieboldianus var. sanguineus

Euonymus sieboldianus var. sanguineus, commonly known as the red-leaf euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which turns a vibrant red in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are visually appealing. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
