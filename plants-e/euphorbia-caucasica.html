Euphorbia caucasica

Euphorbia caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Euphorbia caucasica is known for its ability to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
