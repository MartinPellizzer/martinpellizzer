Elaeocarpus versicolor

Elaeocarpus versicolor, commonly known as the variable elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
