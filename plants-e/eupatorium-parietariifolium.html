Eupatorium parietariifolium

Eupatorium parietariifolium, commonly known as the wall eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant gardens.
