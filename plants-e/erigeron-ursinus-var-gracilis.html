Erigeron ursinus var. gracilis

Erigeron ursinus var. gracilis, commonly known as slender purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern and central United States.

It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets surrounding a purple to brown central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in open woods and meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and support for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
