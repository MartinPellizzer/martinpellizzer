Eriocaulon merrillii var. longibracteatum

Eriocaulon merrillii var. longibracteatum, commonly known as the long-bract eriocaulon, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions such as Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a dense rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and flowering structure.
