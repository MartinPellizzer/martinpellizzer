Echinocystis torquata var. brevispina

Echinocystis torquata var. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined gourd, is a climbing vine native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that develop into spiny, spherical fruits. Its fruit has a thick, rough rind and is often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative element.

The variety brevispina is distinguished by its shorter spines compared to the typical form of Echinocystis torquata.
