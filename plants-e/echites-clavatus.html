Echites clavatus

Echites clavatus, commonly known as the clustered echites, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features erect, branched stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the branches.

Echites clavatus is often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
