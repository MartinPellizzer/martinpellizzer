Eugenia nompa var. arborea

Eugenia nompa var. arborea, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving off a strong, sweet scent. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its visual beauty.
