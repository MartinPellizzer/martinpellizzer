Eryngium paucisquamosum

Eryngium paucisquamosum, commonly known as the spiny eryngium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, thistle-like appearance with blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
