Euphorbia papillifolia var. papillifolia

Euphorbia papillifolia var. papillifolia, commonly known as the papillate spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem covered in small, fleshy tubercles that give it a unique, textured appearance. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking visual appeal.
