Erica aurea

Erica aurea, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica aurea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.
