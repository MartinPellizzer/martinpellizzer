Elaphoglossum tenuiculum

Elaphoglossum tenuiculum, commonly known as the slender fern, is a species of polypodioid fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features delicate, linear leaves with a finely divided structure, characteristic of the Elaphoglossum genus. Its fronds are usually evergreen, and it produces small, spore-bearing structures called sorus.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
