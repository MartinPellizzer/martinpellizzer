Erigeron ramosus var. beyrichii

Erigeron ramosus var. beyrichii, commonly known as the slender fleabane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils. The plant features a wiry, branched stem with alternate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, daisy-like, with white ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
