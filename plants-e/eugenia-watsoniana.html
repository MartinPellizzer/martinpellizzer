Eugenia watsoniana

Eugenia watsoniana, commonly known as the Watson's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

Eugenia watsoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
