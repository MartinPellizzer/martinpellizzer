Eugenia cocosensis

Eugenia cocosensis, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a round, yellow to red berry with a waxy coating, containing a single large seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
