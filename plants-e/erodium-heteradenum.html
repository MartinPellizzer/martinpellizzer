Erodium heteradenum

Erodium heteradenum, commonly known as the variable bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink or purple, with five petals, and it produces small, dry fruits called capsules.

Erodium heteradenum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments due to its drought-resistant characteristics.
