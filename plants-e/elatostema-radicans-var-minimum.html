Elatostema radicans var. minimum

Elatostema radicans var. minimum, commonly known as the small-stemmed false shamrock, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces tiny white or pale green flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.
