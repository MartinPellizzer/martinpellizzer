Eupatorium ageratifolium var. texense

Eupatorium ageratifolium var. texense, commonly known as Texas boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, prairies, and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
