Eriogonum gyrophyllum

Eriogonum gyrophyllum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments. It has small, narrow leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

Eriogonum gyrophyllum is an important plant for pollinators and is often used in native plant landscaping.
