Eugenia zollingeriana

Eugenia zollingeriana, commonly known as the Zollinger's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
