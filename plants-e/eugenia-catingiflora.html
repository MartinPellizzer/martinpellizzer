Eugenia catingiflora

Eugenia catingiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or catingueira, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Eugenia catingiflora is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its role in traditional medicine.
