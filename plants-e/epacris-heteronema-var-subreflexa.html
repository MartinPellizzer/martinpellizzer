Epacris heteronema var. subreflexa

Epacris heteronema var. subreflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is a member of the Heath family (Ericaceae) and typically grows in heathlands and open forests. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or red, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the cooler months.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
