Eugenia janeirensis

Eugenia janeirensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small apples. The fruit has a sweet, aromatic pulp with a unique flavor, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary significance.
