Eria ventricosa

Eria ventricosa, commonly known as the bubble vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, fleshy stems that give it its common name, along with its clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance.

Eria ventricosa is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
