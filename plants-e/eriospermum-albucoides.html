Eriospermum albucoides

Eriospermum albucoides, commonly known as the white sand eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, woolly, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, succulent leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that release seeds when mature.

Eriospermum albucoides is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
