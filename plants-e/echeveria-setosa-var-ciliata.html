Echeveria setosa var. ciliata

Echeveria setosa var. ciliata, commonly known as the Ciliated Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves with soft, white hairs along the edges, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is popular among succulent enthusiasts for rock gardens and container planting.
