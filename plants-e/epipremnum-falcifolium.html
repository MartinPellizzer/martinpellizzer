Epipremnum falcifolium

Epipremnum falcifolium, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically light green with a slight variegation. The plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used as a decorative houseplant or in outdoor gardens. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it adaptable to various growing conditions.

Epipremnum falcifolium is relatively low maintenance, requiring moderate watering and indirect sunlight.
