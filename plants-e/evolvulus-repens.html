Evolvulus repens

Evolvulus repens, commonly known as creeping blueweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with sprawling stems that root at the nodes. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Evolvulus repens is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
