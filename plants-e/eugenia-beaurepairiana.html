Eugenia beaurepairiana

Eugenia beaurepairiana, commonly known as the Jamaica hibiscus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy, five-petaled flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed raw or used in culinary preparations. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia beaurepairiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
