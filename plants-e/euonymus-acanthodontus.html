Euonymus acanthodontus

Euonymus acanthodontus, commonly known as the red-twigged dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems that stand out during the winter months. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Euonymus acanthodontus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
