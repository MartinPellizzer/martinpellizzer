Echinops nizvanus

Echinops nizvanus, commonly known as the Nizvanus thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its bright blue or purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops nizvanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
