Erica leucopelta var. luxurians

Erica leucopelta var. luxurians, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with silvery-white undersides. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
