Erysimum minus

Erysimum minus, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features compact, mat-forming growth with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Erysimum minus is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
