Epidendrum subfloribundum

Epidendrum subfloribundum, commonly known as the showy epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to yellow and pink. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic growth habit. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

Epidendrum subfloribundum blooms during the warmer months, producing clusters of fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
