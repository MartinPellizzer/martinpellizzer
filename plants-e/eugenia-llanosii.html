Eugenia llanosii

Eugenia llanosii, commonly known as the llano guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Argentina and Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellowish-orange fruits resembling small apples. Eugenia llanosii is adapted to dry, tropical climates and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
