Eriostemon glasshousiensis

Eriostemon glasshousiensis, commonly known as the Glasshouse bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has hairy, linear leaves and thrives in well-drained sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.

Eriostemon glasshousiensis is a small shrub that plays a role in local ecosystems and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
