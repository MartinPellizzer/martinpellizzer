Eleocharis montana subsp. montevidensis

Eleocharis montana subsp.

montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. Its rhizomes help it spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize moist soils.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
