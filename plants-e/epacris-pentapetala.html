Epacris pentapetala

Epacris pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled heath, is a flowering plant native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.
