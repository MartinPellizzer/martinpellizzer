Euryomyrtus recurva

Euryomyrtus recurva, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often forming dense shrubs. Its aromatic leaves contain essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional Aboriginal medicine.

Euryomyrtus recurva is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
