Erica innovans

Erica innovans, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica innovans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to Mediterranean climates.
