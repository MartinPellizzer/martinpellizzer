Euphorbia liukiuensis

Euphorbia liukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or greenish. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
