Erigeron crassicaulis

Erigeron crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It features sturdy, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
