Echinopsis pentlandii var. laevior

Echinopsis pentlandii var. laevior, commonly known as the Laevior Moon Cactus, is a cacti species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems that are typically covered in a white, powdery coating called farina. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are usually pale yellow or white. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and blooming habits, it is a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
