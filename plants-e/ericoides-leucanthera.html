Ericoides leucanthera

Ericoides leucanthera, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and bears small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland edges. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the pale flowers.

Ericoides leucanthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
