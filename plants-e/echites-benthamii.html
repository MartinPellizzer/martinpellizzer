Echites benthamii

Echites benthamii, commonly known as the Benthams' thorn, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Echites benthamii is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
