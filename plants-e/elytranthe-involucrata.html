Elytranthe involucrata

Elytranthe involucrata, commonly known as the velvetleaf sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety pubescence. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
