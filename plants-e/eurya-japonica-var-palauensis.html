Eurya japonica var. palauensis

Eurya japonica var. palauensis, commonly known as the Palau holly, is a small evergreen shrub native to the islands of Palau.

It features dense, glossy dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries that are edible but tart. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
