Elymus divergens

Elymus divergens, commonly known as the western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikelets that are often slightly drooping. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers in late summer. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in prairies and disturbed areas.

Elymus divergens plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
