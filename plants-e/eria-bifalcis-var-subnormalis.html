Eria bifalcis var. subnormalis

Eria bifalcis var. subnormalis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces delicate flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often with a subtle fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
