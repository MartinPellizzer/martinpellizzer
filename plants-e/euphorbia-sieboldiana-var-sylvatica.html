Euphorbia sieboldiana var. sylvatica

Euphorbia sieboldiana var. sylvatica, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features a distinctive rosette of thick, waxy, and often blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its unique floral structures. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its hardiness and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
