Euphorbia mandravioky

Euphorbia mandravioky, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with a distinctive greenish-gray color and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin, a common trait among the Euphorbia genus. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

The leaves are typically narrow and may be arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.
