Erigeron uniflorus f. racemosa

Erigeron uniflorus f. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose fleabane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, often tufted growth habit and features small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the stems are usually hairy.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
