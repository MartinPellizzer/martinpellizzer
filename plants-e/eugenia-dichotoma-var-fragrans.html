Eugenia dichotoma var. fragrans

Eugenia dichotoma var. fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant guava or sweet guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, fragrant aroma, which are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a pleasant scent when crushed.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
