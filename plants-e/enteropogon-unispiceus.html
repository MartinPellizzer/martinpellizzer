Enteropogon unispiceus

Enteropogon unispiceus, commonly known as the red grass or red panicgrass, is a species of grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and bright red or orange flower spikes that appear in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
