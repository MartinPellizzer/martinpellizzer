Eriogonum plumatella var. jaegeri

Eriogonum plumatella var. jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's curlleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely toothed, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive, delicate appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
