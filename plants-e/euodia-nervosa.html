Euodia nervosa

Euodia nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined euodia, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with prominent venation patterns. Its small white flowers are followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible.

Euodia nervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
