Euryops integrifolius

Euryops integrifolius, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and showy yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Euryops integrifolius is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
