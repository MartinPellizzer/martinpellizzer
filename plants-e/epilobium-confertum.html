Epilobium confertum

Epilobium confertum, commonly known as sticky willow or woolly willow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Epilobium confertum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
