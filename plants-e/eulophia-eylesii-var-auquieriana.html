Eulophia eylesii var. auquieriana

Eulophia eylesii var. auquieriana, commonly known as the Australian sun orchid, is a striking orchid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive red or purple lip. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and heathlands. It has a pseudobulbous structure with long, narrow leaves that help it survive in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.
