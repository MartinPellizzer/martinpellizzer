Epidendrum garcia-esquivelii

Epidendrum garcia-esquivelii, commonly known as the Garcia-Esquivel's epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically features broad, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the summer months.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in shaded, forested areas.
