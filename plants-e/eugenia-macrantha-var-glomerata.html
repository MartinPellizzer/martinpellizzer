Eugenia macrantha var. glomerata

Eugenia macrantha var. glomerata, commonly known as the clustered guava, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptic in shape, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
