Elymus × vancouverensis var. crescentianus

Elymus × vancouverensis var. crescentianus, commonly known as the Crescent Island wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender leaves, and panicle-like inflorescences that produce small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and wet meadows. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and restoration projects.
