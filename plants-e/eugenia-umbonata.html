Eugenia umbonata

Eugenia umbonata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe, containing a single seed. Eugenia umbonata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its growth habit and fruiting cycle make it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture in warm climates.
