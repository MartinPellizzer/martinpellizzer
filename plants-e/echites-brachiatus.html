Echites brachiatus

Echites brachiatus, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
