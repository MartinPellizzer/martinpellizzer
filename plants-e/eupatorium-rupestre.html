Eupatorium rupestre

Eupatorium rupestre, commonly known as rough eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in fine, rough hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium rupestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
