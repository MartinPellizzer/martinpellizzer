Eupatorium holwayanum

Eupatorium holwayanum, commonly known as the Holway's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and swampy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium holwayanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
