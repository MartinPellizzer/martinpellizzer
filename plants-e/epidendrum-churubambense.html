Epidendrum churubambense

Epidendrum churubambense, commonly known as the Churubamba orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the highlands of Bolivia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet-colored flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
