Elaeocarpus leopoldii

Elaeocarpus leopoldii, commonly known as the Leopold's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its bark is thick and often fissured, providing a rugged appearance.

Elaeocarpus leopoldii is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal applications.
