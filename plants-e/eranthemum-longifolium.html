Eranthemum longifolium

Eranthemum longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
