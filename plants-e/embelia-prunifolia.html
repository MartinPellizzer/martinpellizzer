Embelia prunifolia

Embelia prunifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often red or purple when young, turning dark green as they mature. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Embelia prunifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
