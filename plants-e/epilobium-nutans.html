Epilobium nutans

Epilobium nutans, commonly known as nodding willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and marshy areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are square and often reddish in color.

Epilobium nutans is a member of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
