Erigeron alpinus var. elatus

Erigeron alpinus var. elatus, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are linear and hairy, adapted to withstand the harsh conditions of its mountainous habitat.
