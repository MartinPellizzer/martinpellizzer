Eriogonum trachygonum subsp. dentatum

Eriogonum trachygonum subsp.

dentatum, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and small, clustered white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is known for its drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
