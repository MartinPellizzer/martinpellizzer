Equisetum elongatum var. arabicum

Equisetum elongatum var. arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, jointed stems and whorled arrangement of small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its branches, which are typical of its ancient evolutionary lineage. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological adaptations to harsh environments.
