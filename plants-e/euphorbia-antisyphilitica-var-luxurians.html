Euphorbia antisyphilitica var. luxurians

Euphorbia antisyphilitica var. luxurians, commonly known as the lush euphorbia, is a variant of the antisyphilitica species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and clustered, bright green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically surrounded by a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its toxic sap, caution is advised when handling this plant.
