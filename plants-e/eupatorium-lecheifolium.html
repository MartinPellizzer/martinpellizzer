Eupatorium lecheifolium

Eupatorium lecheifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in the understory of forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
