Epilobium palustre var. mandjuricum

Epilobium palustre var. mandjuricum, commonly known as the Siberian willow herb, is a flowering plant native to northern regions of the Palearctic.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often boggy habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink or purple in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and provides nectar for various pollinators.
