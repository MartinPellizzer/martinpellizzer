Episcia cuneata

Episcia cuneata, commonly known as the shield leaf begonia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and purple. The plant produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming during the warmer months. Episcia cuneata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
