Erophila praecox subsp. subnitens

Erophila praecox subsp.

subnitens, commonly known as the early erophila, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils, often found in meadows and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are white to pale blue, with five petals and are arranged in dense clusters.

It blooms from late spring to early summer, contributing to the floral diversity of its habitat.
