Erythroxylum annamense

Erythroxylum annamense, commonly known as the Annamese copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing elliptical seeds. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its copal resin, which has historical and commercial significance.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various insect and bird species.
