Erica lugubris

Erica lugubris, commonly known as the sad heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western and southern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Erica lugubris thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat, with drought-resistant features and a slow growth rate.
