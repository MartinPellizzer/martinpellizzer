Elytraria herbacea

Elytraria herbacea, commonly known as the hairy elytraria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Poaceae, commonly known as the grass family. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Elytraria herbacea typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland or grassland habitats.
