Eriogonum alatum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum alatum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the fluted buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed, leaf-like bracts that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
