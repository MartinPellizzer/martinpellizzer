Ephedra alenda

Ephedra alenda, commonly known as the desert tea, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions. Ephedra alenda contains ephedrine, a compound with historical use in traditional medicine.

However, due to its potential toxicity and legal restrictions, it is not widely used in modern herbal practices.
