Eulophia amblyosepala

Eulophia amblyosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled eulophia, is a herbaceous orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its upright, branching stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, typically arranged in two ranks around the stem.

Eulophia amblyosepala thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
