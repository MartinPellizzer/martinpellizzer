Eugenia obovatifolia

Eugenia obovatifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia obovatifolia is also valued for its aromatic wood and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
