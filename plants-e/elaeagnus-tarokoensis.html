Elaeagnus tarokoensis

Elaeagnus tarokoensis, commonly known as the Taroko silverleaf, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Elaeagnaceae family and is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, fragrant leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers in clusters, which are often followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions, contributing to local biodiversity.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
