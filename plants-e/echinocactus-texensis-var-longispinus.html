Echinocactus texensis var. longispinus

Echinocactus texensis var. longispinus, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Texas and New Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are long and prominent, providing protection from herbivores.

This variety is well adapted to dry climates and requires minimal water once established.
