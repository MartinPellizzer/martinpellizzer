Erica burchelliana

Erica burchelliana, commonly known as the Burchell's heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing vibrant pink or white flowers that attract pollinators. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are evergreen throughout the year.

Erica burchelliana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
