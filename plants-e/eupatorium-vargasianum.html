Eupatorium vargasianum

Eupatorium vargasianum, commonly known as Vargasian blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

Eupatorium vargasianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
