Eurybia paucidentata

Eurybia paucidentata, commonly known as the few-toothed sunflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture with few teeth along the margins.

Eurybia paucidentata plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
