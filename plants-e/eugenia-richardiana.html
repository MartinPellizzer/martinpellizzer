Eugenia richardiana

Eugenia richardiana, commonly known as the Richard's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
