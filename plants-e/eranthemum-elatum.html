Eranthemum elatum

Eranthemum elatum, commonly known as the waxflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Eranthemum elatum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
