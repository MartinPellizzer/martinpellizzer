Eugenia astringens

Eugenia astringens, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to red when mature.

Eugenia astringens is notable for its aromatic leaves and is often used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.
