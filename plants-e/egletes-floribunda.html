Egletes floribunda

Egletes floribunda, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it typically grows in well-drained soils in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
