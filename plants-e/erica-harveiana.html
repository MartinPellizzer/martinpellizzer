Erica harveiana

Erica harveiana, commonly known as the Harveian heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica harveiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
