Erysimum cheiriflorum

Erysimum cheiriflorum, commonly known as the Moroccan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Erysimum cheiriflorum is often used in rock gardens and borders due to its ornamental value and hardiness.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
