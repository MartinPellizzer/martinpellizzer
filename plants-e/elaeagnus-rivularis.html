Elaeagnus rivularis

Elaeagnus rivularis, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-pubescent leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and in riparian zones. It has a shallow root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

Elaeagnus rivularis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
