Excoecaria pallida var. oblongifolia

Excoecaria pallida var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the white-leaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically pale green with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
