Eugenia jugalis

Eugenia jugalis, commonly known as the purple-leafed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark purple foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain seeds and are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia jugalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and medicinal properties.
