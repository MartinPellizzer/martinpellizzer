Eugenia rumphii

Eugenia rumphii, commonly known as the Java apple or West Indian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink, followed by bright red, edible berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is valued for both its ornamental and nutritional properties.
