Echium vulgare var. grandiflorum

Echium vulgare var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the common thistle or greater flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with spiny margins. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers in spikes at the ends of the stems, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Echium vulgare var. grandiflorum is a hardy perennial that is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
