Eugenia elliptifolia

Eugenia elliptifolia, commonly known as the elliptic myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic properties, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
