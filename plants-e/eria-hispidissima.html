Eria hispidissima

Eria hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms that are often clustered in racemes. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name.

Eria hispidissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
