Erica argentea

Erica argentea, commonly known as silver heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that is covered in a silvery-white bloom of fine, soft hairs. Erica argentea typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its flowers, which appear in spring, are usually pink or white and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
