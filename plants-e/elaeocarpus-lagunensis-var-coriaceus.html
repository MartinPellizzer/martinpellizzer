Elaeocarpus lagunensis var. coriaceus

Elaeocarpus lagunensis var. coriaceus, commonly known as the thick-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and distinctive, woody fruits that contain a single seed. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface, and the bark is rough and fissured.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
