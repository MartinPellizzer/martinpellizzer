Eucalyptus cinerea

Eucalyptus cinerea, commonly known as the silver ash, is a tall, slender tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels in thin, fibrous strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a silvery underside. It produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The plant is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
