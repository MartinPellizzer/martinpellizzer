Etlingera xanthoparyphe

Etlingera xanthoparyphe, commonly known as the golden-leafed torch ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the wet lowland forests of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking, fiery orange flowers that bloom in terminal inflorescences, often resembling torches. Its leaves are broad, glossy, and have a distinctive yellowish-green coloration.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential medicinal properties.
