Elaeocarpus triflorus

Elaeocarpus triflorus, commonly known as the three-flowered elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in threes, giving it its common name. Its fruit is a woody drupe, typically containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
