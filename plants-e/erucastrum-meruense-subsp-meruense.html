Erucastrum meruense subsp. meruense

Erucastrum meruense subsp.

meruense, commonly known as the East African cress, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and small, white or yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along streambanks. It has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines.
