Echinopsis luisramirezii

Echinopsis luisramirezii, commonly known as the Luis Ramirez cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, spiny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the cooler months. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in drought conditions.

Due to its limited natural range, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
