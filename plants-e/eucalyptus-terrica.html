Eucalyptus terrica

Eucalyptus terrica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and appear in clusters, attracting nectar-feeding insects.

Eucalyptus terrica plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
