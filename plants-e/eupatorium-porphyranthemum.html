Eupatorium porphyranthemum

Eupatorium porphyranthemum, commonly known as purple tarragon, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of small, showy purple to pink flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive aromatic scent, often described as similar to tarragon.

The botanical name reflects its ornamental value and its historical use in herbal medicine.
