Eriogonum kennedyi subsp. purpusii

Eriogonum kennedyi subsp.

purpusii, commonly known as the Kennedy's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a hairy stem and leaves that are often grayish-green in color.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is an important part of local ecosystems.
