Eucalyptus cordata subsp. quadrangulosa

Eucalyptus cordata subsp.

quadrangulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue in color.

This subspecies is part of the Eucalyptus genus, known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
