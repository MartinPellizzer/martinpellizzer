Epidendrum doeringii

Epidendrum doeringii, commonly known as the Doering's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native habitat.

Epidendrum doeringii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
