Eriocaulon macrobolax

Eriocaulon macrobolax, commonly known as the large-leaf marsh plant, is a member of the Eriocaulaceae family.

It is a small herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eriocaulon macrobolax is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.
