Eriogonum tenellum var. erianthum

Eriogonum tenellum var. erianthum, commonly known as the silverleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a compact growth habit and produces edible seeds that are consumed by various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to arid conditions makes it an important component of native plant communities.
