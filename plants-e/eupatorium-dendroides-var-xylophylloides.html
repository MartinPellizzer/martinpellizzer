Eupatorium dendroides var. xylophylloides

Eupatorium dendroides var. xylophylloides, commonly known as the Florida hollyhock, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically woody and branched. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
