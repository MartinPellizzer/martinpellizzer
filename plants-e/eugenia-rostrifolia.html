Eugenia rostrifolia

Eugenia rostrifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia rostrifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
