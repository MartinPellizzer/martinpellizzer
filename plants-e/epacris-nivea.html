Epacris nivea

Epacris nivea, commonly known as the white epacris, is a species of flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in heathland and dry forest environments, often on rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Epacris nivea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
