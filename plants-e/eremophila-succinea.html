Eremophila succinea

Eremophila succinea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the plant family Myrtaceae, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Eremophila succinea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for native wildlife.
