Erigeron aphanactis var. congestus

Erigeron aphanactis var. congestus, commonly known as the clustered blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
