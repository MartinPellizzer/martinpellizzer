Eriogonum rotundifolium var. angustius

Eriogonum rotundifolium var. angustius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
