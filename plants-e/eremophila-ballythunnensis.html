Eremophila ballythunnensis

Eremophila ballythunnensis, commonly known as the Ballythunna eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact shrub form with aromatic leaves. The plant produces spikes of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

This species is of interest in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological importance in native ecosystems.
