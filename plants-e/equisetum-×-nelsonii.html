Equisetum × nelsonii

Equisetum × nelsonii, commonly known as the hybrid horsetail, is a sterile hybrid species resulting from the cross between Equisetum arvense and Equisetum ramosissimum.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of the stems, typical of the Equisetum genus. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its invasive nature, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain environments.
