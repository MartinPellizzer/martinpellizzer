Eragrostis virescens subsp. verloovei

Eragrostis virescens subsp.

verloovei, commonly known as the green sprangletop, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
