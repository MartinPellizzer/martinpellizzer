Eriogonum abertianum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum abertianum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as Abert's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing perennial herb with dense, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is often found in sagebrush steppe and mountainous regions, contributing to local biodiversity.
