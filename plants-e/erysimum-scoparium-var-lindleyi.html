Erysimum scoparium var. lindleyi

Erysimum scoparium var. lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators.
