Eragrostis articulata var. pauciflora

Eragrostis articulata var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of pale green to brownish flowers.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
