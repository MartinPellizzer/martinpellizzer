Evolvulus pterygophyllus var. puberulus

Evolvulus pterygophyllus var. puberulus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs, giving the plant its common name. The flowers are usually blue or purple and bloom in the morning, hence the name "morning glory.".

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
