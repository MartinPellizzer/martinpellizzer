Echinops bakhtiaricus

Echinops bakhtiaricus, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by the development of its distinctive, bristly fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Echinops bakhtiaricus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
