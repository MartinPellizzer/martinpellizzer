Eryngium trifidum

Eryngium trifidum, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, thistle-like appearance and small blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive trifid (three-lobed) shape.

Eryngium trifidum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
