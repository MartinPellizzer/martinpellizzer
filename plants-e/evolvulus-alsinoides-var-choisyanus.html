Evolvulus alsinoides var. choisyanus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. choisyanus, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its rapid growth and vibrant blooms.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not properly managed.
