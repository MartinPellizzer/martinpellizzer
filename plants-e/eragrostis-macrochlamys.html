Eragrostis macrochlamys

Eragrostis macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalmed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
