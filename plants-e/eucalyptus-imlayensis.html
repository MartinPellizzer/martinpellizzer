Eucalyptus imlayensis

Eucalyptus imlayensis, commonly known as the Ilayensis gum tree, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and smooth, often peeling bark. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a silver-green upper surface. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are usually white or pale blue.

This plant is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
