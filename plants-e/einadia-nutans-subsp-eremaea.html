Einadia nutans subsp. eremaea

Einadia nutans subsp.

eremaea, commonly known as the desert holly, is a shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pink in color.

The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its succulent leaves and ability to store water in its thick stems. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife.

This subspecies is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
