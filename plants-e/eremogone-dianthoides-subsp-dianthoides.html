Eremogone dianthoides subsp. dianthoides

Eremogone dianthoides subsp.

dianthoides, commonly known as the desert bell, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The plant features showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
