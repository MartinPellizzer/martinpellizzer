Eupatorium minasgeraesense

Eupatorium minasgeraesense, commonly known as the Minas Gerais eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas within tropical rainforests. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
