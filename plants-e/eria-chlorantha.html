Eria chlorantha

Eria chlorantha, commonly known as the green orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating water retention.

Eria chlorantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
