Ericoides physophyllum

Ericoides physophyllum, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a member of the Ericaceae family, known for its acidic soil preferences.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils found in shaded or partially shaded environments. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides physophyllum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
