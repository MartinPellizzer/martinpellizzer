Excoecaria cubana

Excoecaria cubana, commonly known as the Cuban croton, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Excoecaria cubana is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
