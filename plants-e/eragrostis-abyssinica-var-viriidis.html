Eragrostis abyssinica var. viriidis

Eragrostis abyssinica var. viriidis, commonly known as the greenleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in semi-arid regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
