Eucalyptus odorata var. angustifolia

Eucalyptus odorata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the cooler months. Its aromatic oils, derived from the leaves, have been used traditionally by Indigenous Australians for various purposes.

This variety is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
