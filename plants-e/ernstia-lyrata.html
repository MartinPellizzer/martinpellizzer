Ernstia lyrata

Ernstia lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in cool, moist environments.

It is characterized by its distinctive, leaf-like thallus with a greenish-blue to yellowish-green color. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which contribute to its ability to photosynthesize and absorb nutrients. It is often found on tree bark, rocks, and soil in temperate regions.

Ernstia lyrata plays a role in ecological succession and serves as an indicator of air quality due to its sensitivity to pollution.
