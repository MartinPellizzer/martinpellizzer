Eragrostis frankii var. brevipes

Eragrostis frankii var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in dry landscapes and gardens.
