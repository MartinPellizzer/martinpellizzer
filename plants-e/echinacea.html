Echinacea

Echinacea, commonly known as coneflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its spiky, cone-shaped central disk surrounded by colorful, petal-like rays. Echinacea species typically grow in prairies and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant is known for its medicinal properties, particularly its use in supporting the immune system.

Its flowers bloom in summer, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as herbal remedies.
