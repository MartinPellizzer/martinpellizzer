Erica tegulifolia

Erica tegulifolia, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Erica tegulifolia is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of the region.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
