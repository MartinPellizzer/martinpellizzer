Eria hosokawae

Eria hosokawae, commonly known as the Hosokawa's orchid, is a rare and delicate epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments, relying on its roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often covered in a fine, velvety coating.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria hosokawae is considered a rare species, making it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
