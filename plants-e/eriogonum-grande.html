Eriogonum grande

Eriogonum grande, commonly known as the large globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum grande is a perennial herb that plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
