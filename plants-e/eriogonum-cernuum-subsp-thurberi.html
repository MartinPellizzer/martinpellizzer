Eriogonum cernuum subsp. thurberi

Eriogonum cernuum subsp.

thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in arid environments.
