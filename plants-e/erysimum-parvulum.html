Erysimum parvulum

Erysimum parvulum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing annual or biennial plant native to Europe.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters, featuring compact clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The leaves are pinnately divided, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant often thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in garden settings.
