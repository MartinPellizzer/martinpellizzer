Erica actea

Erica actea, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas with a Mediterranean climate. Erica actea produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscaping.
