Echinopsis calorubra

Echinopsis calorubra, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions.

Echinopsis calorubra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.
