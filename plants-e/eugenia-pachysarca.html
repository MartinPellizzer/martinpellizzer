Eugenia pachysarca

Eugenia pachysarca, commonly known as the wax myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, particularly in Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its small, waxy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. It produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but have a tart flavor.

Eugenia pachysarca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
