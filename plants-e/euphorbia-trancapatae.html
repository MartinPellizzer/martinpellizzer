Euphorbia trancapatae

Euphorbia trancapatae, commonly known as the Trancapata Euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a spiny appearance, with some species exhibiting bracts that can be colorful and showy.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscapic gardens.
