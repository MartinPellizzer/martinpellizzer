Eryngium mettaueri

Eryngium mettaueri, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada horseweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly seed heads.

Eryngium mettaueri is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
