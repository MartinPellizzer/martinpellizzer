Echinofossulocactus karwinskianus

Echinofossulocactus karwinskianus, commonly known as the Karwinskianus cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to dry environments, with a deep root system and thick, water-storing stems.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
