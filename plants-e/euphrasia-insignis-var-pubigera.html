Euphrasia insignis var. pubigera

Euphrasia insignis var. pubigera, commonly known as the hairy eyebright, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with fine, hairy textures, which give it its common name.

Euphrasia insignis var. pubigera is often found in the wild and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
