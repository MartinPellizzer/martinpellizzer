Elaeagnus oxycarpa var. microcarpa

Elaeagnus oxycarpa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, scale-like hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
