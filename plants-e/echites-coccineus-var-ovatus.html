Echites coccineus var. ovatus

Echites coccineus var. ovatus, commonly known as the red flame vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture. Its stems are woody and can grow quite long, making it suitable for trellises or pergolas.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
