Ectadium virgatum var. rotundifolium

Ectadium virgatum var. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leafed bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, succulent leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it contributes to the local flora with its distinctive appearance.

The variety is notable for its compact growth habit and ornamental value in native plant gardens.
