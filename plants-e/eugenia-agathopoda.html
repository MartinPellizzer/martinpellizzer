Eugenia agathopoda

Eugenia agathopoda, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.

Eugenia agathopoda thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
