Eugenia pueblana

Eugenia pueblana, commonly known as the Pueblan wax apple, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature, with a waxy, shiny skin. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in gardens and for local use.
