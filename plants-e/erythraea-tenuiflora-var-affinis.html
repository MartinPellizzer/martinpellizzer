Erythraea tenuiflora var. affinis

Erythraea tenuiflora var. affinis, commonly known as the narrow-flowered erythraea, is a variant of the erythraea plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. It is a perennial shrub that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species within its native range.
