Exacum hyssopifolium

Exacum hyssopifolium, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant features velvety, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Exacum hyssopifolium is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
