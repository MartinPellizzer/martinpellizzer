Erysimum hungaricum

Erysimum hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It produces bright yellow to orange flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
