Eragrostis alveiformis

Eragrostis alveiformis, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, linear leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Eragrostis alveiformis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing forage for grazing animals.
