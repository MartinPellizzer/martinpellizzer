Eulophia elliotii

Eulophia elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and grasslands. The plant features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, while its leaves are usually long and strap-like. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in a raceme, with a distinctive coloration that varies from white to pink or purple.

Eulophia elliotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
