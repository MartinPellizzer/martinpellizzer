Embothrium valdivianum

Embothrium valdivianum, commonly known as the Valdivian firetree, is a species of flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Embothrium valdivianum thrives in temperate rainforests and is often found in the Valdivian temperate rainforest biome.

It plays an important ecological role by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various forest organisms.
