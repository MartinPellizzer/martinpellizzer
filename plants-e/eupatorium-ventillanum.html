Eupatorium ventillanum

Eupatorium ventillanum, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and serrated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, white to pale purple flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium ventillanum is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
