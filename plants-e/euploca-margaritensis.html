Euploca margaritensis

Euploca margaritensis, commonly known as the pearlgrass, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Euploca margaritensis is adapted to salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
