Erodium cygnorum subsp. glandulosum

Erodium cygnorum subsp.

glandulosum, commonly known as the pinkstem bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems that are often covered in glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite and have a serrated edge, with a slightly hairy surface.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
