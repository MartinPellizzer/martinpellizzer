Epilobium royleanum f. glandulosum

Epilobium royleanum f. glandulosum, commonly known as the glandular willow herb, is a botanical variant of the species Epilobium royleanum.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its glandular-pubescent stems and leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, often alpine or subalpine environments, thriving in regions with cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually pink to reddish in color, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
