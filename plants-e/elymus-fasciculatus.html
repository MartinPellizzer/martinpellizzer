Elymus fasciculatus

Elymus fasciculatus, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, awned spikelets. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Elymus fasciculatus is often found in open habitats such as prairies, grasslands, and disturbed areas.
