Epacris limata

Epacris limata, commonly known as the slender epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Epacris limata is often found in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
