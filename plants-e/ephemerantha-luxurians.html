Ephemerantha luxurians

Ephemerantha luxurians, commonly known as the fleeting beauty, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its short lifespan, blooming only for a brief period during the warmer months before succumbing to the harsh winter conditions. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and small, pale blue flowers that resemble those of the orchid family. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptation to extreme environments and its role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a vulnerable species requiring conservation efforts.
