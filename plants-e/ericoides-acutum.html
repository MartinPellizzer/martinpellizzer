Ericoides acutum

Ericoides acutum, commonly known as the sharp-leaved ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage. The plant features narrow, pointed leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in early spring.

Ericoides acutum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude forests and rocky habitats.
