Elymus calcicola

Elymus calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. Elymus calcicola thrives in well-drained soils, often found on slopes and in desert grasslands.

Its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
