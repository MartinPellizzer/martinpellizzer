Eugenia curuba

Eugenia curuba, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces bright red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and commonly used in traditional medicine. Its fruit is also valued for its flavor and is used in culinary applications.

Eugenia curuba thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
