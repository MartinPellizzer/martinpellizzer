Eugenia neosilvestris

Eugenia neosilvestris, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or neosilvestris myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded, moist environments within its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
