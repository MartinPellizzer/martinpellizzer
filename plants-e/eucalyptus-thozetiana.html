Eucalyptus thozetiana

Eucalyptus thozetiana, commonly known as the white mallet gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to grey bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a blue-green hue when young. It produces small, creamy-white flowers that are clustered in groups, typically blooming in spring.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open forests and woodlands.
