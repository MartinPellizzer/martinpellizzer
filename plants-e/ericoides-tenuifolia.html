Ericoides tenuifolia

Ericoides tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, dark berries that are edible.

Ericoides tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
