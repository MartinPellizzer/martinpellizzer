Echinocactus cristatus

Echinocactus cristatus, commonly known as the Cristate Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and distinctive crown of fleshy, spiny segments that give it a unique, almost whimsical appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are borne on long, slender pedicels. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Echinocactus cristatus is well-suited for cultivation in dry climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
