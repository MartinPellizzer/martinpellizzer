Eugenia emirnensis f. podocarpifolia

Eugenia emirnensis f. podocarpifolia, commonly known as the Podocarpifolia Eugenia, is a botanical variant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native habitat.
