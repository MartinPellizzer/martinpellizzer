Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus

Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus, commonly known as the white root eleuthero, is a perennial shrub native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems, alternate leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is notable for its medicinal properties, which have been studied for their adaptogenic effects.

Eleutherococcus leucorrhizus thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.
