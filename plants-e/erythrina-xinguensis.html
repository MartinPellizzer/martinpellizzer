Erythrina xinguensis

Erythrina xinguensis, commonly known as the Xingu Flame Tree, is a striking tropical legume native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, flame-like flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its woody structure makes it a prominent feature in forest understories. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Erythrina xinguensis plays an important ecological role in supporting biodiversity and is also valued for its ornamental beauty.
