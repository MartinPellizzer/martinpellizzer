Enicosanthum congregatum

Enicosanthum congregatum, commonly known as the clustered hundred-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit.

It features small, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered together, contributing to its common name. It is native to specific regions in South America and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence and potential medicinal properties that are currently under study.
