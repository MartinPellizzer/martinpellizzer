Eugenia submimica

Eugenia submimica, commonly known as the mimic eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are often tinged with pink, followed by edible, red, elliptical berries. Eugenia submimica thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
