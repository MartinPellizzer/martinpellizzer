Erigeron caucasicus

Erigeron caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage is generally hairy, and it produces small white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

Erigeron caucasicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
