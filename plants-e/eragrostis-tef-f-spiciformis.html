Eragrostis tef f. spiciformis

Eragrostis tef f. spiciformis, commonly known as the spike-form wheatgrass, is a variant of the wild wheatgrass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value, particularly as a source of protein and fiber.

Due to its adaptability, it is also used in ecological restoration projects.
