Epidendrum unguiculatum

Epidendrum unguiculatum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling a slipper. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
