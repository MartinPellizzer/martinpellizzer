Erythrophleum laboucheri

Erythrophleum laboucheri, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The tree has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers followed by long, narrow pods.

It is often found in moist lowland areas and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and soil stabilization.
