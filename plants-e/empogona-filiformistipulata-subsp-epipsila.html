Empogona filiformistipulata subsp. epipsila

Empogona filiformistipulata subsp.

epipsila, commonly known as the slender-stippled empogona, is a shrub-like plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a reddish hue.

This subspecies is notable for its unique morphological features and is of interest in botanical studies due to its limited distribution.
