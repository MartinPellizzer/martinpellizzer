Epidendrum flexuoecallosum

Epidendrum flexuoecallosum, commonly known as the Flexuous Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect or slightly pendulous growth habit and produces long, slender pseudobulbs. The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and consist of showy, colorful petals and sepals.

Epidendrum flexuoecallosum is well adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories.
