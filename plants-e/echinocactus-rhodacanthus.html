Echinocactus rhodacanthus

Echinocactus rhodacanthus, commonly known as the red-hair cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, reddish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens.
