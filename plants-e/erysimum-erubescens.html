Erysimum erubescens

Erysimum erubescens, commonly known as the red or yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, brightly colored flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has basal and alternate leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Erysimum erubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
