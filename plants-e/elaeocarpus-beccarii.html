Elaeocarpus beccarii

Elaeocarpus beccarii, commonly known as the Bornean satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in carpentry.

Elaeocarpus beccarii plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
