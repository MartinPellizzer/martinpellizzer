Eryngium bracteosum

Eryngium bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or disturbed soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eryngium bracteosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
