Elymus aristiglumis

Elymus aristiglumis, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Elymus aristiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in rocky or arid landscapes.
