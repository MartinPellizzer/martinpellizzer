Ericoides placentiflorum

Ericoides placentiflorum, commonly known as the placenta-flowered ericoid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

Due to its unique floral structure and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
