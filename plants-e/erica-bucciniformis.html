Erica bucciniformis

Erica bucciniformis, commonly known as the horned heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in dense clusters. Erica bucciniformis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of the area.
