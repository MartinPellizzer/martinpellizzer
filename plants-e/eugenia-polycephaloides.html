Eugenia polycephaloides

Eugenia polycephaloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale pink flowers, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Eugenia polycephaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
