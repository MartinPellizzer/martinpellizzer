Echinopogon nutans var. major

Echinopogon nutans var. major, commonly known as the nodding hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or pale in color. It is native to various regions of Europe and is often found in disturbed soils or open habitats.

Echinopogon nutans var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
