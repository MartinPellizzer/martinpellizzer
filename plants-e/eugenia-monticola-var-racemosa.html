Eugenia monticola var. racemosa

Eugenia monticola var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
