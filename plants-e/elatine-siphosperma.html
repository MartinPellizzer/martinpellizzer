Elatine siphosperma

Elatine siphosperma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant produces tiny, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and it thrives in shallow, freshwater environments. Elatine siphosperma is often found in moist soils or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed habitats make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
