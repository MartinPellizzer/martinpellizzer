Eleusine erythrosperma

Eleusine erythrosperma, commonly known as red-seeded lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, with the distinctive red-tinged seeds that give it its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Eleusine erythrosperma is often used in ornamental gardening and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
