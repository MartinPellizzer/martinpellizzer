Echites rotundifolius

Echites rotundifolius, commonly known as the round-leaved echites, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
