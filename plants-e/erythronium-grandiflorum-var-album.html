Erythronium grandiflorum var. album

Erythronium grandiflorum var. album, commonly known as the white dog's tooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically before the leaves fully emerge. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
