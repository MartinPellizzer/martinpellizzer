Emmotum fulvum

Emmotum fulvum, commonly known as the golden beard fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy fronds that give it a soft, fluffy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are compound, with small, bright yellow to golden spore-bearing structures at the tips.

Emmotum fulvum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
