Eugenia ouen-toroensis

Eugenia ouen-toroensis, commonly known as the Tororo ebony, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the rainforests of western Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The tree is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and workability in carpentry and furniture making. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy surface, while its flowers are small and clustered, typically white or pale pink in color.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
