Erigeron ramosus var. septentrionalis

Erigeron ramosus var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, preferring full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and arranged in a rosette or alternate fashion, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
