Emilia crispata

Emilia crispata, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly rough texture. Emilia crispata is typically found in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
