Echinospermum lappula subsp. squarrosum

Echinospermum lappula subsp.

squarrosum, commonly known as the squarrosum thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woolly, silver-green leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces a distinctive seed head with bracts that form a spiny, spherical structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
