Epidendrum dilochioides

Epidendrum dilochioides, commonly known as the Orchid of the Andes, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features long, pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in two ranks, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and good air circulation for optimal growth.
