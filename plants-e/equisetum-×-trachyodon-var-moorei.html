Equisetum × trachyodon var. moorei

Equisetum × trachyodon var. moorei, commonly known as the Moore's horsetail, is a hybrid species within the Equisetum genus, characterized by its distinct morphology and ecological adaptations.

It exhibits a unique combination of features from its parent species, Equisetum trachyodon and Equisetum × trachyodon, resulting in a plant with both spore-bearing and vegetative growth structures. The plant possesses a slender, jointed stem covered in whorls of small, needle-like leaves, typical of the Equisetum family. Its reproductive structures include fertile and sterile strobila, which produce spores for propagation.

This variety thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils, often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
