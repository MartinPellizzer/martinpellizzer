Eleutheranthera discoidea

Eleutheranthera discoidea, commonly known as the discoid buttercup, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a deeply divided corolla that resembles a disc. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate and have a heart-shaped base with a hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
