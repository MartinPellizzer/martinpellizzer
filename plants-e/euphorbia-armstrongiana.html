Euphorbia armstrongiana

Euphorbia armstrongiana, commonly known as Armstrong's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with small, green leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking yellow flowers, which are surrounded by colorful bracts that resemble petals. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among gardeners interested in ornamental succulents.
