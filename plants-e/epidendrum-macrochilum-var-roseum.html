Epidendrum macrochilum var. roseum

Epidendrum macrochilum var. roseum, commonly known as the Roseate Epidendrum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking pink to rose-colored flowers, which bloom in racemes along the stems. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are elliptical and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in home greenhouses and botanical gardens.
