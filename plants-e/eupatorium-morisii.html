Eupatorium morisii

Eupatorium morisii, commonly known as Moris' boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, clustered white or pale blue flowers.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and medicinal properties historically used by Native American tribes.
