Euphorbia seguieriana var. dentata

Euphorbia seguieriana var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with a spiny, branched structure and produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The leaves are usually small, fleshy, and have serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique morphology.
