Euphorbia misera

Euphorbia misera, commonly known as the weeping euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, drooping branches that give it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically fleshy and covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.

Euphorbia misera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid conditions.
