Erodium obtusilobum

Erodium obtusilobum, commonly known as the blunt-lobed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with rounded lobes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.
