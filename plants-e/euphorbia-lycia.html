Euphorbia lycia

Euphorbia lycia, commonly known as the Cyprus spurge, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing, succulent shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, and it produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its specialized flower structures. Euphorbia lycia is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

It is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
