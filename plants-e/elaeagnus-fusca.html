Elaeagnus fusca

Elaeagnus fusca, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, reddish berries that are edible for birds and some small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Elaeagnus fusca has a shallow root system, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
