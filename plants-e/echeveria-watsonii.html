Echeveria watsonii

Echeveria watsonii, commonly known as Watson's echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, pinkish-purple flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a desirable addition to many succulent collections.
