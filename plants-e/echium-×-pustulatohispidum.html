Echium × pustulatohispidum

Echium × pustulatohispidum, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of striking blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
