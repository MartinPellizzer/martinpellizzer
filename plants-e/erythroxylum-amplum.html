Erythroxylum amplum

Erythroxylum amplum, commonly known as the broadleaf copal tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of habitats including forests and open areas.
