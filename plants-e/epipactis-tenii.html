Epipactis tenii

Epipactis tenii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Epipactis tenii is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
