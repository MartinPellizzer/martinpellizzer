Eugenia anisopetala

Eugenia anisopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant species native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous small, hard seeds.

Eugenia anisopetala is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
