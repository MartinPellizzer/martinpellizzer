Emilia discifolia

Emilia discifolia, commonly known as the fringed gentian, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular with fringed petals. The plant grows in open woodlands and grasslands, often in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Emilia discifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
