Eugenia trichogyna

Eugenia trichogyna, commonly known as the velvetleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, fleshy berries. Eugenia trichogyna thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
