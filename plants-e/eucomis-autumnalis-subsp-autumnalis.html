Eucomis autumnalis subsp. autumnalis

Eucomis autumnalis subsp.

autumnalis, commonly known as the autumn spider flower, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that resembles a spider's web, with small, white or pale blue flowers clustered along the central stem.

The plant features strap-like, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This species is known for its long blooming period, often extending from late summer into autumn.
