Eria euryantha

Eria euryantha, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, hairy stems and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
