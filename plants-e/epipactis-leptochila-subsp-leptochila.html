Epipactis leptochila subsp. leptochila

Epipactis leptochila subsp.

leptochila, commonly known as the slender helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy greenish-yellow flower with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. It has a pseudobulbous structure and produces a single, erect spike bearing one to three flowers.

The species is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insects such as bees and wasps.
