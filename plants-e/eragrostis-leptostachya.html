Eragrostis leptostachya

Eragrostis leptostachya, commonly known as slender-stalked lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and slender, branching stems that often form dense tufts. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis leptostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in erosion control due to its hardy nature.
