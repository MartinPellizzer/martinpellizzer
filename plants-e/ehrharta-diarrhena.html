Ehrharta diarrhena

Ehrharta diarrhena, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. The plant is often found in sandy or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats.

Ehrharta diarrhena is also used in some traditional medicinal practices due to its potential bioactive compounds.
