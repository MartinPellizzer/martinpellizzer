Erysimum altaicum var. subviride

Erysimum altaicum var. subviride, commonly known as the green alpine mustard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and basal rosette of lanceolate, dark green leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in elongated racemes, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
