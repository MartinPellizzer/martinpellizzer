Erigeron adenophorus

Erigeron adenophorus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, white to blue flowers arranged in a flat-topped cluster.

The species is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
