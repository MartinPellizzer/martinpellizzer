Elymandra monostachya

Elymandra monostachya, commonly known as the slender everlasting pea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are adapted for dispersal by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

Elymandra monostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
