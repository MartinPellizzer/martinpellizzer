Equisetum telmateia f. humile

Equisetum telmateia f. humile, commonly known as the dwarf scouring rush, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its diminutive, unbranched stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and stream banks. Its reproductive structures, called strobili, are cone-like and contain spores.

This fern-like plant is notable for its ancient lineage and its role in ecological succession in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
