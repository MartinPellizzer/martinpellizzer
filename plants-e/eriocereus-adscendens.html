Eriocereus adscendens

Eriocereus adscendens, commonly known as the ascending night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with ribbed stems and produces showy, white flowers that open at night. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and bats, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
