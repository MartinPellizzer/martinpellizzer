Eulophia chaunanthe

Eulophia chaunanthe, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellow-green flowers with distinctive spurred sepals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia chaunanthe is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
