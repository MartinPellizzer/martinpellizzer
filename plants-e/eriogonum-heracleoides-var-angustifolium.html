Eriogonum heracleoides var. angustifolium

Eriogonum heracleoides var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
