Eragrostis guatemalensis

Eragrostis guatemalensis, commonly known as Guatemalan lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis guatemalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in erosion control.
