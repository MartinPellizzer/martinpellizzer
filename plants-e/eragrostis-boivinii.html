Eragrostis boivinii

Eragrostis boivinii, commonly known as Boivin's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-bearing branches, which are characteristic of the grass family.

Eragrostis boivinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
