Eriachne mucronata var. elongata

Eriachne mucronata var. elongata, commonly known as the long-pointed grass, is a species native to Australia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often in coastal or inland regions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spikelet with small, pale green flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated bracts and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and dunes.
