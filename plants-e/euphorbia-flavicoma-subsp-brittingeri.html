Euphorbia flavicoma subsp. brittingeri

Euphorbia flavicoma subsp.

brittingeri, commonly known as the yellow-flowered euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a branched stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
