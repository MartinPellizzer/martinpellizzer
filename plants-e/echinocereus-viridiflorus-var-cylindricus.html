Echinocereus viridiflorus var. cylindricus

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. cylindricus, commonly known as the green-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green or blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
