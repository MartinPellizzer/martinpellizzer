Eupatorium laevigatum var. claussenii

Eupatorium laevigatum var. claussenii, commonly known as the Claussen's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
