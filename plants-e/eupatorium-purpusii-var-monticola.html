Eupatorium purpusii var. monticola

Eupatorium purpusii var. monticola, commonly known as mountain eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in native gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
