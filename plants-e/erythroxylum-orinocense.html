Erythroxylum orinocense

Erythroxylum orinocense, commonly known as the Orinoco copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its leaves are typically ovate or elliptic in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

The plant is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local communities.
