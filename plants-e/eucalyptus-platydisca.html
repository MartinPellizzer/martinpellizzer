Eucalyptus platydisca

Eucalyptus platydisca, commonly known as the broad-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with prominent stamens that give them a striking appearance. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in open woodlands or heathlands.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for native wildlife.
