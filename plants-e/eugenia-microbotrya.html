Eugenia microbotrya

Eugenia microbotrya, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern coast of Australia, where it typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains several seeds.

Eugenia microbotrya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
