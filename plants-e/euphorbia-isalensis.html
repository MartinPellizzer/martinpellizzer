Euphorbia isalensis

Euphorbia isalensis, commonly known as the Isalensis spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. Euphorbia isalensis is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
