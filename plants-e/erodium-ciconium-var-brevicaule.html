Erodium ciconium var. brevicaule

Erodium ciconium var. brevicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
