Eragrostis blastocaulos

Eragrostis blastocaulos, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
