Eulalia quadrinervis var. latifolia

Eulalia quadrinervis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf pampas grass, is a striking ornamental grass native to South America.

It features tall, silver-gray plumes that emerge from a basal rosette of wide, bluish-green leaves. The plant is characterized by its four nerve patterns on the leaves, giving it the species name *quadrinervis*. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Its feathery appearance adds texture and movement to gardens, especially during the fall when it produces its showy plumes.
