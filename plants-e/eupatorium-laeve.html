Eupatorium laeve

Eupatorium laeve, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its showy white to pale purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Eupatorium laeve is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
