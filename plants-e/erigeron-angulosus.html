Erigeron angulosus

Erigeron angulosus, commonly known as the angular fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its leaves are usually angular and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The showy white or yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
