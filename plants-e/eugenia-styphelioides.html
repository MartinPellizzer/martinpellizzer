Eugenia styphelioides

Eugenia styphelioides, commonly known as the wax apple or Surinam cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are often used in culinary applications.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and fruit-bearing qualities.
