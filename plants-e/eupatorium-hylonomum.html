Eupatorium hylonomum

Eupatorium hylonomum, commonly known as the silverleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The silver-gray undersides of its leaves give it a distinctive appearance, making it a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
