Embelia pacifica

Embelia pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific embelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a slightly aromatic scent.

Embelia pacifica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and undergrowth areas.
