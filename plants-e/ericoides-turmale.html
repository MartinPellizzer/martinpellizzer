Ericoides turmale

Ericoides turmale, commonly known as the Turmaline Heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or moorland environments.

Ericoides turmale is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local insect populations.
