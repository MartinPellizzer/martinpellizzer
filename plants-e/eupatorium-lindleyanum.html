Eupatorium lindleyanum

Eupatorium lindleyanum, commonly known as the pinkstem thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist woodland areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium lindleyanum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
