Echinopsis densispina f. leucomalla

Echinopsis densispina f. leucomalla, commonly known as the white-furred moon cactus, is a rare and striking cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines, giving it a unique and almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces clusters of vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny exterior. It is a succulent, capable of storing water in its fleshy stems, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in controlled environments by plant enthusiasts.
