Eriocaulon flaccidum

Eriocaulon flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid flat sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curved, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in its environment.

Eriocaulon flaccidum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
