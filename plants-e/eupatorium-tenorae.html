Eupatorium tenorae

Eupatorium tenorae, commonly known as the Carolina boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium tenorae is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
