Elymus canadensis var. philadelphicus

Elymus canadensis var. philadelphicus, commonly known as Canada wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and loose, spike-like inflorescences that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, pond edges, and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more prominent during the growing season.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
