Ebenus quiloensis

Ebenus quiloensis, commonly known as the Peruvian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is often found in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Ebenus quiloensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, used traditionally in local remedies for various ailments.
