Eriogonum vimineum var. elegans

Eriogonum vimineum var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, ribbed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
