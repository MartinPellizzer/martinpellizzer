Eulophia aculeata subsp. huttonii

Eulophia aculeata subsp.

huttonii, commonly known as the Hutton's eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink.

The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs and linear, dark green leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
