Eugenia atrata

Eugenia atrata, commonly known as the black mountain apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black berries, which are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soil.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a fragrant aroma when crushed.
