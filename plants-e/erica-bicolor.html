Erica bicolor

Erica bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or white, with a distinctive two-toned appearance. Erica bicolor is evergreen, with leathery, needle-like leaves that help it conserve water.

It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained, acidic conditions.
