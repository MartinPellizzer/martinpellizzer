Elymus canaliculatus

Elymus canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves with prominent veins and its compact, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, aiding in its propagation across open landscapes.

Elymus canaliculatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
