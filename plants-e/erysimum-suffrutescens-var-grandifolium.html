Erysimum suffrutescens var. grandifolium

Erysimum suffrutescens var. grandifolium, commonly known as the Mexican wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features greyish-green, woolly leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and showy blooms.
