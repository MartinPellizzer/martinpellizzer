Epidendrum caninum

Epidendrum caninum, commonly known as the dog's mouth orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and shrublands. It has a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying environmental conditions.

Epidendrum caninum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
