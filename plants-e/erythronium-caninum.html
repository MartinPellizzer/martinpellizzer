Erythronium caninum

Erythronium caninum, commonly known as the dog's tooth violet, is a member of the Liliaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a greenish throat. The plant produces a rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are hairy and covered in a waxy coating. It blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully unfurl, and is often found in moist, shady woodlands.

Erythronium caninum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
