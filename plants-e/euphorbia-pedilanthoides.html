Euphorbia pedilanthoides

Euphorbia pedilanthoides, commonly known as the Cape York euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in a waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions.

Its morphology reflects evolutionary strategies for survival in harsh, dry climates.
