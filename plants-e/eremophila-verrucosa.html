Eremophila verrucosa

Eremophila verrucosa, commonly known as the rough eremophila, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila verrucosa is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
