Eucalyptus praetermissa

Eucalyptus praetermissa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The flowers are typically small and clustered, producing a distinctive yellow-green color. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
