Escallonia pterocladon

Escallonia pterocladon, commonly known as the coastal escallonia, is a shrub native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to coastal conditions, making it salt-tolerant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found along coastal cliffs and rocky shores.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in temperate climates.
