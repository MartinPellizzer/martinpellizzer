Eugenia paraensis

Eugenia paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically in parts of Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is sometimes used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
