Ericomyrtus parviflora

Ericomyrtus parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered heath, is a compact shrub native to the coastal regions of eastern Australia.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathland and coastal scrub environments.

Ericomyrtus parviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract native pollinators.
