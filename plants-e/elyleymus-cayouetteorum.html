Elyleymus cayouetteorum

Elyleymus cayouetteorum, commonly known as the Cayouette's grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture and a slight bluish-green hue.

Elyleymus cayouetteorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
