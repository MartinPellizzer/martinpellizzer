Erysimum calycinum

Erysimum calycinum, commonly known as the California wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features upright, branched stems covered with fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are bright yellow and appear in clusters, blooming from late winter to early spring.

The leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
