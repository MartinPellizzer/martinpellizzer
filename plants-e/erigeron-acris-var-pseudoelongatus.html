Erigeron acris var. pseudoelongatus

Erigeron acris var. pseudoelongatus, commonly known as the stiffleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, stiff leaves and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to North America, this plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies and roadsides. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.

The plant's structure and appearance make it a valuable component of native plant communities.
