Eriocaulon tagawae

Eriocaulon tagawae, commonly known as the Philippine spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which includes various species of spike-rushes found in wetland environments. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, hair-like structures. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or reddish and are borne on short spikes.

Eriocaulon tagawae plays a role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitats.
