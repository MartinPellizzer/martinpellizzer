Euphorbia esula subsp. pinifolia

Euphorbia esula subsp.

pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, alternate leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its invasive tendencies in some regions, it can pose a challenge in ecological management.
