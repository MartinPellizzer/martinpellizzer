Eremostachys eriocalyx var. taschkentica

Eremostachys eriocalyx var. taschkentica, commonly known as the Taschkent bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and mountainous regions. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, adapted to reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
