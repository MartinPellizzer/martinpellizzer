Eragrostis saresberiensis

Eragrostis saresberiensis, commonly known as the Saharan grass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are adapted to withstand high temperatures and low moisture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or degraded habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in dry environments.
