Echium onosmifolium subsp. spectabile

Echium onosmifolium subsp.

spectabile, commonly known as the showy evening primrose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and a branching stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
