Euchorium cubense

Euchorium cubense, commonly known as the Cuban euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Cuba.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with unique floral structures. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it contains toxic sap that requires careful handling.
