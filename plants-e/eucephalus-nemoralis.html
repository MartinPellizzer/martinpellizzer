Eucephalus nemoralis

Eucephalus nemoralis, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its flat-topped clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while its roots contain compounds with historical medicinal uses.

Eucephalus nemoralis is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
