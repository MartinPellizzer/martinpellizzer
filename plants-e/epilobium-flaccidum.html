Epilobium flaccidum

Epilobium flaccidum, commonly known as the flaccid willowherb, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or disturbed soils, such as marshes, stream banks, and roadside areas. The plant features erect, often reddish stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in spikes, and are usually pink to reddish in color, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium flaccidum is known for its adaptability and is often found in moist, nutrient-rich environments.
