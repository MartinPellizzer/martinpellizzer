Echinodorus longiscapus

Echinodorus longiscapus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance in freshwater environments. The plant thrives in slow to moderate water currents and prefers nutrient-rich substrates. Its rhizomes spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense underwater mats.

Echinodorus longiscapus is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic value and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
