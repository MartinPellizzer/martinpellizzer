Escallonia reticulata

Escallonia reticulata, commonly known as the evergreen honeysuckle, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Escallonia reticulata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its adaptability to various climate conditions.
