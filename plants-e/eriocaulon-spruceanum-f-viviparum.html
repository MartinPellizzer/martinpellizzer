Eriocaulon spruceanum f. viviparum

Eriocaulon spruceanum f. viviparum, commonly known as the viviparous flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant exhibits vivipary, a reproductive trait where seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it forms dense mats.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
