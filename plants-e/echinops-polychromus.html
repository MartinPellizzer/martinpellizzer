Echinops polychromus

Echinops polychromus, commonly known as the multicolored thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and yellow, appearing in early summer, and it is often used in ornamental gardening.

Echinops polychromus is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
