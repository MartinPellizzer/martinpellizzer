Eupatorium micranthon

Eupatorium micranthon, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium micranthon is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
