Eugenia hondurensis

Eugenia hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, similar to that of guava. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Eugenia hondurensis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruit.
