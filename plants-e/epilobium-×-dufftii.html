Epilobium × dufftii

Epilobium × dufftii, commonly known as the hybrid willowherb, is a perennial plant belonging to the genus Epilobium in the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in raceme clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium × dufftii is a natural hybrid between Epilobium angustifolium and Epilobium palustre, showcasing genetic diversity within the genus.
