Eugenia tenella var. elliptica

Eugenia tenella var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clove, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value lies in its dense foliage and persistent blooms, making it suitable for container gardening or as a hedge.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
