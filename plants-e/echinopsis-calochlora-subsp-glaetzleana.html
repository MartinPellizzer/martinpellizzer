Echinopsis calochlora subsp. glaetzleana

Echinopsis calochlora subsp.

glaetzleana, commonly known as the Zebra Cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is a columnar cactus characterized by its distinctive zebra-like striping on its stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring. It is drought-tolerant and requires well-draining soil to thrive.

This subspecies is popular among cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
