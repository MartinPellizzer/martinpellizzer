Eupatorium rojasianum

Eupatorium rojasianum, commonly known as the Rojasian thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and streamside habitats. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple.

Eupatorium rojasianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
