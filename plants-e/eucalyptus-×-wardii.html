Eucalyptus × wardii

Eucalyptus × wardii, commonly known as the Sydney peppermint, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and tall, slender trunk. The leaves are lance-shaped and emit a strong, menthol-like scent when crushed. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sub-coastal regions.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also valued for its timber and essential oils.
