Erigeron graminosus

Erigeron graminosus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and prairies. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer.
