Eugenia piresii

Eugenia piresii, commonly known as the Piresii Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
