Ericoides azaleifolium

Ericoides azaleifolium, commonly known as the redstem azaleifolium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers, blueberries, and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are typically elliptical and have a leathery texture, while its stems are often reddish in color.

This species thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.
