Euphorbia confusa

Euphorbia confusa, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. Euphorbia confusa contains a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
