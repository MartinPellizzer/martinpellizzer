Erythraea fastigiata

Erythraea fastigiata, commonly known as the redwood tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, conical crown, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by clusters of red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species.

This plant plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
