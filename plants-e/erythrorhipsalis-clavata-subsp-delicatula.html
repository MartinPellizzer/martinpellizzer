Erythrorhipsalis clavata subsp.

delicatula, commonly known as the delicate clavate cactus, is a small, compact cactus native to South America. It features short, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly hairs, giving it a soft appearance.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a rare and sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
