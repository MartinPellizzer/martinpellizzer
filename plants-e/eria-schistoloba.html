Eria schistoloba

Eria schistoloba, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
