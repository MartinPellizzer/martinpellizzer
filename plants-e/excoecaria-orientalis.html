Excoecaria orientalis

Excoecaria orientalis, commonly known as the croton tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from red to yellow and green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also known for its latex, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a natural rubber source.
