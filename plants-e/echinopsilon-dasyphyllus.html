Echinopsilon dasyphyllus

Echinopsilon dasyphyllus, commonly known as the hairy echinops, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Echinopsilon dasyphyllus is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
