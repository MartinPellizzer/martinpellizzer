Erica leptopus var. leptopus

Erica leptopus var. leptopus, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in the region.

The flowers, which are usually pink or red, attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
