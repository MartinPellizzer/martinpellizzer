Elymus curtiaristatus

Elymus curtiaristatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Elymus curtiaristatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in arid regions.
