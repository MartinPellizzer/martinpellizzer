Eremophila obliquisepala

Eremophila obliquisepala, commonly known as the narrow-lobed emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Eremophila obliquisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
