Ehretia laevis var. cymosa

Ehretia laevis var. cymosa, commonly known as the clustered flaxtree, is a shrub or small tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It features simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small, woody capsules that split open to release seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
