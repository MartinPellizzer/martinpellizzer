Eriogonum effusum var. pallidum

Eriogonum effusum var. pallidum, commonly known as the pallid leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale green to grayish foliage and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is part of the large and diverse genus Eriogonum, which includes many species adapted to a wide range of ecological conditions.
