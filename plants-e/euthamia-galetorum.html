Euthamia galetorum

Euthamia galetorum, commonly known as seaside goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to coastal areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along shores and cliffs, where it thrives in well-drained, saline environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Euthamia galetorum is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
