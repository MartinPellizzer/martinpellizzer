Euphorbia turcomanica

Euphorbia turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan spurge, is a succulent plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry climates.

Euphorbia turcomanica is often used in rock gardens and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
