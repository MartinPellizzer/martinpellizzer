Euphorbia polycarpa var. petrina

Euphorbia polycarpa var. petrina, commonly known as the pink spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems and produces clusters of small, pinkish flowers during the warmer months. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its spiky leaves and unique inflorescence called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
