Erysimum synapoides

Erysimum synapoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
