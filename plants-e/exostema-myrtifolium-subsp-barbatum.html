Exostema myrtifolium subsp. barbatum

Exostema myrtifolium subsp.

barbatum, commonly known as the hairy leaf exostema, is a species native to tropical regions of Central and South America. It is characterized by its striking, velvety, hairy leaves that give it a unique texture and appearance.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are lanceolate with prominent venation, and it produces small, white or pale green flowers.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
