Eriogonum parryi

Eriogonum parryi, commonly known as Parry's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eriogonum parryi plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
