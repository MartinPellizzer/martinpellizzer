Erica regia

Erica regia, commonly known as the king's heather, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Erica regia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
