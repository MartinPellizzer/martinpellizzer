Eulophia taylorii

Eulophia taylorii, commonly known as the Taylor's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid and cool conditions. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a rhizome that produces multiple pseudobulbs. Its flowers are usually pale green to white, with a distinctive three-lobed lip that adds to its ornamental appeal.

Eulophia taylorii is valued in horticulture for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
