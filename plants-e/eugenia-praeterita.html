Eugenia praeterita

Eugenia praeterita, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its fragrant white flowers and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a pleasant aroma when crushed.

The fruit, which is usually red or yellow when ripe, is used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
