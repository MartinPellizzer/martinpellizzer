Echinocactus saltillensis

Echinocactus saltillensis, commonly known as the Saltillo cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water.

Its unique appearance and hardiness make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
