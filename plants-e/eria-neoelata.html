Eria neoelata

Eria neoelata, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to conserve moisture.

Eria neoelata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its delicate floral display.
