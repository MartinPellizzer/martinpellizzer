Eugenia praetermissa

Eugenia praetermissa, commonly known as the Australian satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in dense inflorescences, attracting various pollinators.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in native habitats.
