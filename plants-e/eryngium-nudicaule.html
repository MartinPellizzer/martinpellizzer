Eryngium nudicaule

Eryngium nudicaule, commonly known as the nakedhead thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads and a lack of bracts, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Eryngium nudicaule is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens due to its striking floral display.
