Enkianthus subsessilis f. angustifolius

Enkianthus subsessilis f. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features lanceolate, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and slightly serrated. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink during late spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value lies in its elegant foliage and delicate floral display.
