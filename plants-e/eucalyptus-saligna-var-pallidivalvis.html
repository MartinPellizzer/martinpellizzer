Eucalyptus saligna var. pallidivalvis

Eucalyptus saligna var. pallidivalvis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are often covered in a powdery coating. Its leaves and flowers are rich in volatile oils, contributing to its aromatic properties and ecological significance.

This variant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient component of native Australian flora.
