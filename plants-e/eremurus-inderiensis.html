Eremurus inderiensis

Eremurus inderiensis, commonly known as the Inderian spirea, is a striking perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the genus Eremurus, which is part of the family Asteraceae, commonly known as the sunflower family. Its botanical features include fibrous roots and thick, hairy stems that help it survive in cold and arid environments.

Eremurus inderiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic floral display.
