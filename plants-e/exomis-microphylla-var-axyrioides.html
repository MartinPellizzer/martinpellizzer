Exomis microphylla var. axyrioides

Exomis microphylla var. axyrioides, commonly known as the small-leaved exomis, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.

Its botanical structure makes it a subject of interest in studies related to plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
