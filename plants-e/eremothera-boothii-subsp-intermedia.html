Eremothera boothii subsp. intermedia

Eremothera boothii subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in the evening.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Eremothera boothii subsp.

intermedia is often found in sandy or gravelly soils in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
