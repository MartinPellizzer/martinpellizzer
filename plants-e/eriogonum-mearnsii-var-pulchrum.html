Eriogonum mearnsii var. pulchrum

Eriogonum mearnsii var. pulchrum, commonly known as the Mearns' buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, clustered pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often used in native plant landscaping.
