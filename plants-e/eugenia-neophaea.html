Eugenia neophaea

Eugenia neophaea, commonly known as the New Guinea myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Eugenia neophaea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
