Erica lamellaris

Erica lamellaris, commonly known as the flat-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica lamellaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
