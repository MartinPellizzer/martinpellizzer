Euphorbia pekinensis var. hupehensis

Euphorbia pekinensis var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is known for its ability to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
