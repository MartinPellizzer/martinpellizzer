Echinocereus viridiflorus var. canus

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. canus, commonly known as the green-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, columnar cactus with a distinctive greenish-gray stem and spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and early summer, which are often borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a notable addition to desert gardens.
