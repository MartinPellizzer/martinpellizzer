Euphorbia taquetii

Euphorbia taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among members of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
