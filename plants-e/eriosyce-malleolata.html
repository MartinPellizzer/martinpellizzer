Eriosyce malleolata

Eriosyce malleolata, commonly known as the silver cholla, is a species of cactus native to the coastal regions of Peru.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, spine-tipped cladodes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
