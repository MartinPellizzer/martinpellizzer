Eupatorium trifoliatum var. foliosum

Eupatorium trifoliatum var. foliosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
