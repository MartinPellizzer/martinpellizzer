Eucarpha strobilina

Eucarpha strobilina, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Eucarpha strobilina plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
