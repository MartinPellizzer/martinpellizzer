Eugenia victorinii

Eugenia victorinii, commonly known as the Victoria's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
