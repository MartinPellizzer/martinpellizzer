Erodium laciniatum subsp. soluntinum

Erodium laciniatum subsp.

soluntinum, commonly known as the Soluntine bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the Mediterranean flora.
