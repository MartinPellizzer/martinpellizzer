Erythrocephalum dictyophlebium

Erythrocephalum dictyophlebium, commonly known as the clustered eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, pinkish-red flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
