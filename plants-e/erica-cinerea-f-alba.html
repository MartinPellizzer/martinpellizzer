Erica cinerea f. alba

Erica cinerea f. alba, commonly known as the white heather, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
