Eugenia elopurae

Eugenia elopurae, commonly known as the Elopurae myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, and the plant is often valued for its ornamental appeal and aromatic properties.
