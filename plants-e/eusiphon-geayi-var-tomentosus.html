Eusiphon geayi var. tomentosus

Eusiphon geayi var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a soft, woolly appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
