Echinocactus dichroacanthus

Echinocactus dichroacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its spines are short and stiff, providing protection from herbivores.

The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic environments.
