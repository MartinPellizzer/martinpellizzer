Eupatorium guanaiense

Eupatorium guanaiense, commonly known as Guanaiense boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba and the Bahamas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium guanaiense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
