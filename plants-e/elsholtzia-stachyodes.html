Elsholtzia stachyodes

Elsholtzia stachyodes, commonly known as silverleaf elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs, and its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.

Elsholtzia stachyodes is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
