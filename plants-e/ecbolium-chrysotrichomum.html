Ecbolium chrysotrichomum

Ecbolium chrysotrichomum, commonly known as the golden-haired ecbolium, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically arranged oppositely along the stem.

Ecbolium chrysotrichomum is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub habitats.
