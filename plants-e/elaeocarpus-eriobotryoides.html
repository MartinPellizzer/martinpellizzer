Elaeocarpus eriobotryoides

Elaeocarpus eriobotryoides, commonly known as the Chinese sand apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and fruit, which is used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
