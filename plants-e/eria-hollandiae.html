Eria hollandiae

Eria hollandiae, commonly known as the Holland's orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant features long, slender stems that bear numerous small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne on terminal inflorescences, and the species is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Eria hollandiae prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
