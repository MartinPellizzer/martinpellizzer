Erysimum scoparium

Erysimum scoparium, commonly known as the coastal wallflower, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and coastal dunes, where it is adapted to salty and windy conditions.

Erysimum scoparium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
