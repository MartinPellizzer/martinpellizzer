Eryngium pristis

Eryngium pristis, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage and a compact, upright growth habit, making it a striking addition to gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in sandy or rocky coastal environments.

Eryngium pristis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate salt spray and wind.
