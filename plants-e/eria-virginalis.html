Eria virginalis

Eria virginalis, commonly known as the pink lady's tresses, is a species of orchid belonging to the family Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its distinctive pink to purple floral coloration. The plant produces a single, elongated spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Eria virginalis is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitats.
