Erodium maritimum

Erodium maritimum, commonly known as sea buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, pink or reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate and covered in fine hairs, and the plant has a low-growing, sprawling habit.

Erodium maritimum is adapted to salty and windy environments, making it a hardy species found in maritime climates.
