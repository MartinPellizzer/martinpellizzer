Eyrythalia umbellata

Eyrythalia umbellata, commonly known as the red-veined umbrella plant, is a striking member of the Araliaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with bright red veins contrasting against its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Native to tropical regions, it is often cultivated for its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
