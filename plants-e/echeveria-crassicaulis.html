Echeveria crassicaulis

Echeveria crassicaulis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, with a waxy coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy pink or purple flowers in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
