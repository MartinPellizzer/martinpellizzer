Echinocereus pulchellus subsp. acanthosetus

Echinocereus pulchellus subsp.

acanthosetus, commonly known as the Sonoran Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert. It is a columnar cactus with cylindrical stems that often branch near the base.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically white to pale yellow and can be quite dense.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
