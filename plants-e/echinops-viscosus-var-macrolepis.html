Echinops viscosus var. macrolepis

Echinops viscosus var. macrolepis, commonly known as the sticky globemallow, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and hairy, glandular leaves that contribute to its sticky texture. The plant typically grows from 30 to 60 centimeters tall, with a compact, bushy appearance. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
