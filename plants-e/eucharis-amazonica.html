Eucharis amazonica

Eucharis amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its striking, waxy, arrowhead-shaped leaves. The plant produces an inflorescence known as a spadix, which is surrounded by a spathe, a modified leaf that resembles a flower. Eucharis amazonica thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its foliage and unique floral structure make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
