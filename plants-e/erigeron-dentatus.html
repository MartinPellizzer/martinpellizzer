Erigeron dentatus

Erigeron dentatus, commonly known as the toothed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. It features hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing a showy display of white or pale blue petals.
