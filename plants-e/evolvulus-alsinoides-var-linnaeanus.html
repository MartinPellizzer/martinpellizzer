Evolvulus alsinoides var. linnaeanus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. linnaeanus, commonly known as the small morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.

The varietal name 'linnaeanus' honors Carl Linnaeus, the father of modern taxonomy.
