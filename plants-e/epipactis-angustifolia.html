Epipactis angustifolia

Epipactis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and a single, spurred flower that is typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insects.

Epipactis angustifolia is a perennial herb that often grows in dense colonies, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
