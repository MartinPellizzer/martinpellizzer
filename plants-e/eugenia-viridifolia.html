Eugenia viridifolia

Eugenia viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Eugenia viridifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid, warm climate.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and is also valued for its culinary and medicinal properties.
