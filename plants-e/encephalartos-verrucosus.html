Encephalartos verrucosus

Encephalartos verrucosus, commonly known as the serrated cycad, is a member of the Zamiaceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen cycad characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnate, dark green leaves. Due to its unique appearance and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
