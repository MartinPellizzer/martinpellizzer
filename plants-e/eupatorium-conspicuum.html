Eupatorium conspicuum

Eupatorium conspicuum, commonly known as showy eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, clustered flower heads. The plant produces white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium conspicuum is often found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
