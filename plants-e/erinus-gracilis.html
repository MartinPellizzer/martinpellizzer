Erinus gracilis

Erinus gracilis, commonly known as the slender erinus, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing shades of pink, purple, and white. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erinus gracilis is often grown for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
