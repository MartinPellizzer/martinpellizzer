Epilobium parviflorum f. longifolium

Epilobium parviflorum f. longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaved willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and opposite leaf arrangement.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
