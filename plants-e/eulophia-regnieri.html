Eulophia regnieri

Eulophia regnieri, commonly known as the Regnier's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white blooms with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eulophia regnieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
