Euodia arborea

Euodia arborea, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Euodia arborea thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.
