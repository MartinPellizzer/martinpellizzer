Epilobium parviflorum var. menthoides

Epilobium parviflorum var. menthoides, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as those found in meadows, riverbanks, and wetlands. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is part of the Onagraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
