Erythroxylum rignyanum var. littorale

Erythroxylum rignyanum var. littorale, commonly known as the coastal wild coca, is a shrub native to coastal regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, dark red berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline and is adapted to saline and wind-prone environments.

This species is of interest for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
