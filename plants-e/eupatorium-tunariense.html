Eupatorium tunariense

Eupatorium tunariense, commonly known as the Tunari goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Bolivia and Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow or golden in color, arranged in dense clusters.

Eupatorium tunariense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
