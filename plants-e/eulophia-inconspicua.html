Eulophia inconspicua

Eulophia inconspicua, commonly known as the inconspicuous eulophia, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green or white flowers that are often clustered in a raceme. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Eulophia inconspicua is noted for its relatively inconspicuous appearance, which contributes to its common name.
