Eria bractescens var. latipetala

Eria bractescens var. latipetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved eria, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, waxy, lance-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
