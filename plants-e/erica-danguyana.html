Erica danguyana

Erica danguyana, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in coastal areas with a Mediterranean climate. Erica danguyana is evergreen, with dense, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
