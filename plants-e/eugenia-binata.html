Eugenia binata

Eugenia binata, commonly known as the wild guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The fruit is edible, with a sweet and aromatic pulp, and is often used in jams and beverages.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
