Elaeagnus multiflora var. angustata

Elaeagnus multiflora var. angustata, commonly known as the narrowleaf silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silver-green foliage, which is covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
