Euphorbia jansenvillensis

Euphorbia jansenvillensis, commonly known as the Jansenvillensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which store water to adapt to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
