Elaeocarpus oxypyren

Elaeocarpus oxypyren, commonly known as the red-fruited elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruits are small, round, and reddish when mature, containing a single seed.

Elaeocarpus oxypyren is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
