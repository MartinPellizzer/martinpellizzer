Elaeocarpus valetonii var. valetonii

Elaeocarpus valetonii var. valetonii, commonly known as the Bornean elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with four petals, and it produces woody fruits that contain a single seed.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
