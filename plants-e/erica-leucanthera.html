Erica leucanthera

Erica leucanthera, commonly known as the white heather, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Erica leucanthera thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
