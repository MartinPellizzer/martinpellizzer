Erythronium californicum

Erythronium californicum, commonly known as the California dogtooth violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a bulbous root system and produces a single, showy, spurred flower that is typically white or pale pink. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or chaparral habitats.

Erythronium californicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
