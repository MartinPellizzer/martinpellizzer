Eria megalopha

Eria megalopha, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

Eria megalopha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
