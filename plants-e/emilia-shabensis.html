Emilia shabensis

Emilia shabensis, commonly known as the Shabenskaya emilia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flower heads that are composed of numerous small florets. Emilia shabensis is adapted to dry conditions, with narrow, hairy leaves that reduce water loss.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms.
