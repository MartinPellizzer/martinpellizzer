Eragrostis propinqua

Eragrostis propinqua, commonly known as slender false wheatgrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spikelet-based flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in low-nutrient environments. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Eragrostis propinqua is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
