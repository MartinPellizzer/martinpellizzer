Echinacanthus flaviflorus

Echinacanthus flaviflorus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered echinacanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
