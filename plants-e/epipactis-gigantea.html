Epipactis gigantea

Epipactis gigantea, commonly known as the giant helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, pleated leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and swamps. It has a complex life cycle involving mycorrhizal associations with fungi to aid in nutrient uptake.

Epipactis gigantea is listed as a threatened species due to habitat loss and fragmentation.
