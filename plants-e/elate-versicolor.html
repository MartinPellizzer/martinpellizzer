Elate versicolor

Elate versicolor, commonly known as the rainbow elate, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored flowers that range from deep purples to bright yellows, creating a visually stunning display. The plant belongs to the Elatine genus, which is part of the Elatinaceae family, known for its small, aquatic or semi-aquatic species. Its leaves are typically small, oval-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often grow close to the ground.

Elate versicolor thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rocky stream beds or along the edges of waterfalls.
