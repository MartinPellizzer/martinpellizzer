Echium aculeatum

Echium aculeatum, commonly known as the spiked evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like bracts that surround its showy, violet to red flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its flowers open in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oval seeds.
