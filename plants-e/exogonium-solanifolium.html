Exogonium solanifolium

Exogonium solanifolium, commonly known as the leafy nightshade, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers that typically bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant's botanical features make it a unique subject for study in botany and horticulture.
