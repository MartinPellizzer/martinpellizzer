Elaeocarpus sogerensis

Elaeocarpus sogerensis, commonly known as the Soger's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
