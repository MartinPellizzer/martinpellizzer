Eucalyptus oresbia

Eucalyptus oresbia, commonly known as the Grey Box, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, grey bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the winter months. Eucalyptus oresbia is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species found in various regions of southern Australia.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
