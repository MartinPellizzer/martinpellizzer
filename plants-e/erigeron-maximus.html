Erigeron maximus

Erigeron maximus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and ragweeds. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows.

Its fluffy seed heads are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across wetland environments.
