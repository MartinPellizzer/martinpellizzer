Euphorbia hoffmanniana

Euphorbia hoffmanniana, commonly known as the Hoffmann's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with paired, fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by colorful bracts that resemble flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Euphorbia hoffmanniana is also known for its ability to store water in its leaves, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.
