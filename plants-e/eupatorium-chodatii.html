Eupatorium chodatii

Eupatorium chodatii, commonly known as Chodat's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as swamps and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
