Eulalia glabrata

Eulalia glabrata, commonly known as the glabrous grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across open habitats. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
