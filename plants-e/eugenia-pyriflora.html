Eugenia pyriflora

Eugenia pyriflora, commonly known as the strawberry guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, bright red berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

The fruit is often consumed fresh or used in jams and desserts due to its sweet, aromatic flavor.
