Euphorbia darbandensis

Euphorbia darbandensis, commonly known as the Darbandi Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
