Epipactis leptochila f. altensteiniana

Epipactis leptochila f. altensteiniana, commonly known as the Altenstein's narrow-leaved helleborine, is a rare and specialized orchid species.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and pale greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. This orchid is typically found in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a terrestrial plant that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, a common trait among orchids.
