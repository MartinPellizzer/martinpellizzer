Euphorbia carniolica var. ambigua

Euphorbia carniolica var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous spurge, is a perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its succulent, ribbed stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
