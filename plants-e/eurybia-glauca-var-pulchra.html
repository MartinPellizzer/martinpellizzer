Eurybia glauca var. pulchra

Eurybia glauca var. pulchra, commonly known as the showy golden saxifrage, is a perennial flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, often appearing in clusters atop hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
