Eugenia urschiana var. subacuminata

Eugenia urschiana var. subacuminata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its smooth, dark red bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
