Erigeron philippii f. tragopogonoides

Erigeron philippii f. tragopogonoides, commonly known as the Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are white to pale blue in color.
