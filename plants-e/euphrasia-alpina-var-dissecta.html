Euphrasia alpina var. dissecta

Euphrasia alpina var. dissecta, commonly known as the Alpine eyebright, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers with prominent, showy nectar guides. The leaves are lance-shaped, often deeply lobed or dissected, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is a common species in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
