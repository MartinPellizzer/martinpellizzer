Elaeagnus pungens var. maculata

Elaeagnus pungens var. maculata, commonly known as the variegated silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive silvery-white variegation and a pleasant, slightly aromatic scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries that attract wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
