Eucalyptus bancroftii

Eucalyptus bancroftii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus bancroftii is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
