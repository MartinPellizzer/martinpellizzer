Erica thryptomenoides

Erica thryptomenoides, commonly known as the Cape heath or narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica thryptomenoides is well adapted to nutrient-poor soils and can thrive in harsh, coastal environments.

It is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
