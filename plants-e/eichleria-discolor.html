Eichleria discolor

Eichleria discolor, commonly known as the purple passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet and white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, pleasant fragrance. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Eichleria discolor is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
