Erysimum pulchellum var. grandiflorum

Erysimum pulchellum var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and slender, branched stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and often covered in fine hairs.

The showy, cup-shaped flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
