Eugenia capitellata

Eugenia capitellata, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a glossy texture.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is valued for its hard, aromatic wood.
