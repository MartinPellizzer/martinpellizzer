Eugenia triplinervia var. oblongata

Eugenia triplinervia var. oblongata, commonly known as the broad-leafed guava, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, which is typically small and yellowish, is edible and has a tart flavor.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in home gardens.
