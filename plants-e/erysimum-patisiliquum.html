Erysimum patisiliquum

Erysimum patisiliquum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to oblanceolate in shape, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Erysimum patisiliquum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to flourish in harsh, high-altitude environments.
