Erica umbelliflora

Erica umbelliflora, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, arranged in umbels. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal environments.

Erica umbelliflora is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
