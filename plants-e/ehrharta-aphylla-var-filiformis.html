Ehrharta aphylla var. filiformis

Ehrharta aphylla var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soils and withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
