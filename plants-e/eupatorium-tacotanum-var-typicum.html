Eupatorium tacotanum var. typicum

Eupatorium tacotanum var. typicum, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
