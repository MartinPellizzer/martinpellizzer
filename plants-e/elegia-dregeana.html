Elegia dregeana

Elegia dregeana, commonly known as the dwarf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its compact size. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for dry, sunny climates.

Elegia dregeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
