Erica globosa var. subterminalis

Erica globosa var. subterminalis, commonly known as the subterminal heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a striking contrast to its floral displays. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and exceptional frost resistance.
