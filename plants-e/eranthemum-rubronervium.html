Eranthemum rubronervium

Eranthemum rubronervium, commonly known as the red-veined eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red in color, which bloom during the warm season. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
