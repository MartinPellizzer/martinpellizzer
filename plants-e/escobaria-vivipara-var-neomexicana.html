Escobaria vivipara var. neomexicana

Escobaria vivipara var. neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico live-bearing stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

Escobaria vivipara var. neomexicana is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
