Euxolus interruptus

Euxolus interruptus, commonly known as the interrupted euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with characteristic cyathia, specialized inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a unique structure that includes a cup-like involucre.

Euxolus interruptus is adapted to arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features such as thick, fleshy leaves and a deep root system.
