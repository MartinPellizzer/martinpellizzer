Etlingera polycarpa

Etlingera polycarpa, commonly known as the multi-fruited torch ginger, is a tropical plant belonging to the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which resemble torches. The plant produces multiple fruiting structures, each containing small, edible berries. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a distinctive purple underside.

Etlingera polycarpa is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
