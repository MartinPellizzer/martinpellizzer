Eriogonum campanulatum

Eriogonum campanulatum, commonly known as the bellflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, arid environments. It has small, oval leaves and produces seeds in clusters within its flower heads.
