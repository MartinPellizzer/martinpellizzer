Eremophila bowmanii

Eremophila bowmanii, commonly known as Bowman's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of tubular, pink or red flowers. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and heat resistance. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valued species in native gardens and conservation efforts.
