Erythrodes sprucei

Erythrodes sprucei, commonly known as the red spruce, is a coniferous tree native to the high elevations of the Appalachian Mountains in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green needles and cones that mature to a reddish hue. The tree thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to acidic soils. It plays a vital role in the ecosystem by providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
