Echinocereus ferreirianus subsp. ferreirianus

Echinocereus ferreirianus subsp.

ferreirianus, commonly known as the Ferreirian's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America. It is a small, spineless cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters.

The plant features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in arid gardens.
