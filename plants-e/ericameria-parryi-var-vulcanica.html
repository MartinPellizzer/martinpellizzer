Ericameria parryi var. vulcanica

Ericameria parryi var. vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic ericameria, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.

The varietal name "vulcanica" reflects its association with volcanic soils and its ability to flourish in such conditions.
