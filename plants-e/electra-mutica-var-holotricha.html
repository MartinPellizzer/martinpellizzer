Electra mutica var. holotricha

Electra mutica var. holotricha, commonly known as the hairy sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems covered in fine, dense hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered with a fine layer of white to grayish hairs.

This species thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
