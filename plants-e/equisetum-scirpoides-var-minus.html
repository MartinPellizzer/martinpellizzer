Equisetum scirpoides var. minus

Equisetum scirpoides var. minus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed scouring rush, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stems that are covered in small, scale-like structures.

It is a primitive vascular plant that lacks true leaves, instead having whorls of small, spore-producing structures called sporangiophores. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its stems are often used as a natural scouring agent due to their abrasive texture.

This variety is less common than the typical form of Equisetum scirpoides and is notable for its reduced size and distinct morphology.
