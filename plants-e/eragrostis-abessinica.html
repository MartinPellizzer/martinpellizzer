Eragrostis abessinica

Eragrostis abessinica, commonly known as Abyssinian grass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia and other parts of the Horn of Africa.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that helps reduce water loss.

Eragrostis abessinica is often used in traditional agriculture and as a forage plant for livestock.
