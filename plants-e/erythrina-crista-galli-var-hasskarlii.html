Erythrina crista-galli var. hasskarlii

Erythrina crista-galli var. hasskarlii, commonly known as the crimson cradle or the cockspur coral tree, is a variant of the tropical legume species Erythrina crista-galli.

It is characterized by its striking crimson bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, which are typical of the genus Erythrina. The plant produces pod fruits that contain several seeds, a feature common among leguminous plants. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and it has a strong, sometimes unpleasant odor.
