Echinochloa crus-galli f. mixta

Echinochloa crus-galli f. mixta, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a variant of the common foxtail grass.

It is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green inflorescence, which is a dense spikelet that resembles a fox's tail. It typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a common weed in various environments.
