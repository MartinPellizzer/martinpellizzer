Erica rosacea subsp. glabrata

Erica rosacea subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family. It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in fynbos vegetation.

The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or red, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

It is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
