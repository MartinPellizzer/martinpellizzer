Eriosema tephrosioides var. salicifolium

Eriosema tephrosioides var. salicifolium, commonly known as the silky-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its finely hairy, lance-shaped leaves that give it a soft, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
