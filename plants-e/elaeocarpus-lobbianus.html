Elaeocarpus lobbianus

Elaeocarpus lobbianus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in spring. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically reddish-brown when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The plant has a shallow root system and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.
