Ephedra americana var. humboldtii

Ephedra americana var. humboldtii, commonly known as the desert jointfir, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.

This species is notable for its ability to survive in extreme conditions with minimal water.
