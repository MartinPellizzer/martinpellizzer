Euterpe schultzeana

Euterpe schultzeana, commonly known as the Schultze's buriti, is a species of palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by edible, round fruits with a sweet, fibrous pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by local communities.
