Eulychnia barquitensis

Eulychnia barquitensis, commonly known as the Barquitensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stems covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to survive in harsh desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
