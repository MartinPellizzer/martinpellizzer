Erigeron thermarum

Erigeron thermarum, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to hot, dry environments. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in shades of white and yellow, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

Erigeron thermarum is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
