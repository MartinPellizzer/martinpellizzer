Epidendrum acrolithophilum

Epidendrum acrolithophilum, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-draining substrates and requires moderate to high light conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a popular specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
