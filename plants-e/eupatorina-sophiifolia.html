Eupatorina sophiifolia

Eupatorina sophiifolia, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in several indigenous communities.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
