Eriosyce esmeraldana

Eriosyce esmeraldana, commonly known as the Emerald Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that form a compact, globular shape. The plant produces small, white flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
