Eragrostis pallescens

Eragrostis pallescens, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
