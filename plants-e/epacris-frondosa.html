Epacris frondosa

Epacris frondosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open forests and heathlands. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in well-drained conditions.

Epacris frondosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
