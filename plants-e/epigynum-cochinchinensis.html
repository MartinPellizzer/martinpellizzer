Epigynum cochinchinensis

Epigynum cochinchinensis, commonly known as the Vietnamese star anise, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Schisandraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, star-shaped fruit. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into the distinctive, dark brown, star-shaped pods used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a unique reproductive structure where the ovary is inferior, a trait known as epigynous development.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been used in herbal remedies for centuries.
