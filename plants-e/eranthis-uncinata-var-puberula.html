Eranthis uncinata var. puberula

Eranthis uncinata var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy buttercup, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its "hairy" appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and attractive addition to wildflower gardens.
