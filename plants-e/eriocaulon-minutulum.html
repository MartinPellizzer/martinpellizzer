Eriocaulon minutulum

Eriocaulon minutulum, commonly known as the minute spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and tiny, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive white or pale coloration.

Eriocaulon minutulum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
