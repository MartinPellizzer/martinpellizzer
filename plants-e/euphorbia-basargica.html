Euphorbia basargica

Euphorbia basargica, commonly known as the Balkan spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia basargica is also known for its latex, which can be toxic and may cause skin irritation.
