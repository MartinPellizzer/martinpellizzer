Ericameria nauseosa var. leiosperma

Ericameria nauseosa var. leiosperma, commonly known as the smoothleaf ericameria, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features narrow, silvery-green leaves that are smooth and covered in fine, glandular hairs. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
