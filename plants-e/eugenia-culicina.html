Eugenia culicina

Eugenia culicina, commonly known as the common appleleaf or the purple apple myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, purple-black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
