Epidendrum trullatum

Epidendrum trullatum, commonly known as the Purple Ghost Orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive greenish-white lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and utilizing atmospheric moisture and nutrients. Its leaves are usually broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native arid or semi-arid environments.

Epidendrum trullatum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
