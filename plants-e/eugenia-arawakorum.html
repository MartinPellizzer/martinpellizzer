Eugenia arawakorum

Eugenia arawakorum, commonly known as the Arawak myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
