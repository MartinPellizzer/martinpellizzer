Echeveria retusa var. floribunda

Echeveria retusa var. floribunda, commonly known as the Coral Necklace Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its compact shape.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among collectors and gardeners.
