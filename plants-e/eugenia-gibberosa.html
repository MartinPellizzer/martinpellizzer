Eugenia gibberosa

Eugenia gibberosa, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia gibberosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils in coastal or sandy environments.
