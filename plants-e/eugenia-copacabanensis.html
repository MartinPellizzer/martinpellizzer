Eugenia copacabanensis

Eugenia copacabanensis, commonly known as the Copacabana cherry, is a species native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers, which are followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental and edible fruits.
