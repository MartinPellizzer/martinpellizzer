Echinopsis formosa var. rubrispina

Echinopsis formosa var. rubrispina, commonly known as the red-spined moon cactus, is a striking cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red spines and a short, cylindrical stem that often displays a variety of colorful hues. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in the evening. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
