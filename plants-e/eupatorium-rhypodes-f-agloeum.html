Eupatorium rhypodes f. agloeum

Eupatorium rhypodes f. agloeum, commonly known as the silverleaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
