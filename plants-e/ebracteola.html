Ebracteola

Ebracteola, commonly known as the leafless mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees.

It lacks true leaves and instead absorbs water and nutrients through its stems, which are modified into photosynthetic structures. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or greenish in color. Its seeds are dispersed by birds that eat its berries, which are fleshy and nutritious.

Ebracteola is often found in temperate and tropical regions where it forms symbiotic relationships with various tree species.
