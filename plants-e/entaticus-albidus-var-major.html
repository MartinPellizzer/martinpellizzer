Entaticus albidus var. major

Entaticus albidus var. major, commonly known as the white-flowered entaticus, is a herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a pale green hue. The plant produces clusters of small, showy white flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system consists of fibrous roots that help it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
