Ernestia karuruana

Ernestia karuruana, commonly known as the Karuruana tree, is a species native to East Africa, particularly found in Tanzania and Kenya.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a rough texture and a distinct yellowish-green color. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries that contain several seeds.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
