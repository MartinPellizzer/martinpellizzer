Elaeocarpus firmus

Elaeocarpus firmus, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often bird-dispersed, contributing to its ecological significance. Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.

Elaeocarpus firmus plays an important role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitats.
