Erithalis inodora

Erithalis inodora, commonly known as the sweet-scented erithalis, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact shrub form and evergreen foliage, which retains its color throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are adapted to acidic soils, making it a suitable species for woodland and shaded garden environments.

Erithalis inodora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, nutrient-poor conditions.
