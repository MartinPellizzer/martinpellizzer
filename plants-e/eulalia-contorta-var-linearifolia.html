Eulalia contorta var. linearifolia

Eulalia contorta var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender bottlebrush grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive, bottlebrush-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
