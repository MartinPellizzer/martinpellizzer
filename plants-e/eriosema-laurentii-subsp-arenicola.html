Eriosema laurentii subsp. arenicola

Eriosema laurentii subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as the sand eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
