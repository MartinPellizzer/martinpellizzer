Esenbeckia berlandieri subsp. litoralis

Esenbeckia berlandieri subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as the coastal esenbeckia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil. It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

This species is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and periodic inundation.
