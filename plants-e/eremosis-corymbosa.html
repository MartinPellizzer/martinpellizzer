Eremosis corymbosa

Eremosis corymbosa, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has a low, spreading growth habit with hairy stems and leaves adapted to conserve water. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring, followed by small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Eremosis corymbosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well-suited to harsh desert environments.
