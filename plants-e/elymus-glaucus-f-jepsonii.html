Elymus glaucus f. jepsonii

Elymus glaucus f. jepsonii, commonly known as the Jepson's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are covered in silky hairs.

Elymus glaucus f. jepsonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
