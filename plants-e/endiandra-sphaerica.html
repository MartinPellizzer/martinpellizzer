Endiandra sphaerica

Endiandra sphaerica, commonly known as the globe-endiandra, is a tropical rainforest tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and smooth, dark-barked trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by fleshy, red berries. Its leaves are glossy and opposite, with a leathery texture.

Endiandra sphaerica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in shaded, humid environments.
