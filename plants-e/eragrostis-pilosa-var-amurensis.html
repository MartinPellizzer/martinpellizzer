Eragrostis pilosa var. amurensis

Eragrostis pilosa var. amurensis, commonly known as Amur hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hair-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a dense growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.

Amur hairgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
