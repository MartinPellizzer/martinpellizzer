Erythroxylum kunthianum var. angustifolium

Erythroxylum kunthianum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved copal tree, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsules containing aromatic resin. It is known for its copal resin, which has been used traditionally for incense and medicinal purposes.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
