Elaeagnus parvifolia var. villosa

Elaeagnus parvifolia var. villosa, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion.

This species is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its tolerance to drought.
