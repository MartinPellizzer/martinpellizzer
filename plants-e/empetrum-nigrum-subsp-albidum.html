Empetrum nigrum subsp. albidum

Empetrum nigrum subsp.

albidum, commonly known as white crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It has small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green, and its flowers are white, clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but tart, and it thrives in harsh, well-drained soils. It is an important component of tundra and mountain ecosystems, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.

Its ability to survive in extreme climates makes it a resilient and hardy plant.
