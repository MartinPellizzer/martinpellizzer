Euphorbiodendron linearifolium

Euphorbiodendron linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved euphorb, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its linear, often succulent leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or yellowish in color. Its stems are often branched and can become woody with age.

Euphorbiodendron linearifolium is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap and diverse growth forms.
