Eulophia santapaui

Eulophia santapaui, commonly known as the Santa Paula orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces inflorescences of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are typically short-lived but attract various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Eulophia santapaui is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
