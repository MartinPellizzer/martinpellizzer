Eugenia rogersiana

Eugenia rogersiana, commonly known as the rose apple or Java apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that resemble apples, with a pinkish skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
