Eremophila bignoniiflora

Eremophila bignoniiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically red or pink, with a distinctive two-lipped structure, and are arranged in spikes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resistance.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
