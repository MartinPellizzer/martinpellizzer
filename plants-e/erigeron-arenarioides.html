Erigeron arenarioides

Erigeron arenarioides, commonly known as sand aster, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, white to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Erigeron arenarioides is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
