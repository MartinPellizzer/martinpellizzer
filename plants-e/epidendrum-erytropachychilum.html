Epidendrum erytropachychilum

Epidendrum erytropachychilum, commonly known as the red-veined epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins contrasting against a white or pale pink background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Epidendrum erytropachychilum thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light.
