Erigeron multiceps

Erigeron multiceps, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance.
