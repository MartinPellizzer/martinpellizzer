Eleocharis compressa var. compressa

Eleocharis compressa var. compressa, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a monocot native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically compressed and covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and the plant produces small, triangular seeds.

It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
