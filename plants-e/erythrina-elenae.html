Erythrina elenae

Erythrina elenae, commonly known as the Red-veined Coral Tree, is a flowering plant native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red, pink, or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often adorned with thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Erythrina elenae is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
