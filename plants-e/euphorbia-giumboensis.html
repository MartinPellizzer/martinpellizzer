Euphorbia giumboensis

Euphorbia giumboensis, commonly known as the Giumbo's spurge, is a succulent plant native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause irritation upon contact with skin.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
