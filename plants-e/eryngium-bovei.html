Eryngium bovei

Eryngium bovei, commonly known as the Bove's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-gray foliage and purple to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its flowers are composed of ray florets and tubular disc florets, typical of the daisy family.

Eryngium bovei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
