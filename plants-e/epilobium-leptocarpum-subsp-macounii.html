Epilobium leptocarpum subsp. macounii

Epilobium leptocarpum subsp.

macounii, commonly known as the Macoun's willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and forest edges.

The plant features slender, erect stems covered with glandular hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate with serrated margins. Its flowers are showy, pink to reddish-purple, and arranged in terminal or axillary clusters.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly broader corolla and shorter pedicels compared to the typical form of Epilobium leptocarpum.
