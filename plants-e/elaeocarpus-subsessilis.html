Elaeocarpus subsessilis

Elaeocarpus subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Elaeocarpus subsessilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
