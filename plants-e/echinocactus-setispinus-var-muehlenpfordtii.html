Echinocactus setispinus var. muehlenpfordtii

Echinocactus setispinus var. muehlenpfordtii, commonly known as the Muehlenpfordt's hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
