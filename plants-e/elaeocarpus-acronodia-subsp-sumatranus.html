Elaeocarpus acronodia subsp. sumatranus

Elaeocarpus acronodia subsp.

sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers.

The fruit is a drupe, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, edible pulp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, this subspecies is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
