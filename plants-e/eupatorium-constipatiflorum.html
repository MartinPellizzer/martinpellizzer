Eupatorium constipatiflorum

Eupatorium constipatiflorum, commonly known as the stiff-flowered eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small, white to pale blue, and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
