Eupatorium cannabinum subsp. syriacum

Eupatorium cannabinum subsp.

syriacum, commonly known as Syrian hempnettle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along riverbanks.

This subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the more widespread Eupatorium cannabinum.
