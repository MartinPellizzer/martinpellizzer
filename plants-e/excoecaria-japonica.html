Excoecaria japonica

Excoecaria japonica, commonly known as Japanese croton, is a flowering plant native to Japan and parts of East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant features alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, clustered yellow flowers. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
