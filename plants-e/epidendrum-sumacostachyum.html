Epidendrum sumacostachyum

Epidendrum sumacostachyum, commonly known as the red-tipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-tipped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its botanical features include long, strap-like leaves and a branching inflorescence that produces numerous white or pale pink blooms.

Epidendrum sumacostachyum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
