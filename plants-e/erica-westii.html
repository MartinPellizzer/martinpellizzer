Erica westii

Erica westii, commonly known as the Cape heath or western heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, needle-like foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring and summer. Erica westii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in garden landscapes for its hardiness and ornamental value.
