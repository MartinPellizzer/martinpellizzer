Epipactis latifolia subsp. atrorubens

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

atrorubens, commonly known as the red-stemmed helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and a tall, erect flowering stem that bears multiple showy, greenish-yellow to reddish-brown flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often darker in color, and the plant produces small, dark brown seeds in a capsule.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in association with other orchid species.
