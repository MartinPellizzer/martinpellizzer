Eragrostis hirsuta var. laevivaginata

Eragrostis hirsuta var. laevivaginata, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open woodlands and disturbed sites.

Its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats make it valuable for erosion control and wildlife habitat.
