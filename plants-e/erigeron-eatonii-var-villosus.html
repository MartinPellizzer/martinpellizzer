Erigeron eatonii var. villosus

Erigeron eatonii var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
