Enodium caeruleum var. parviflorum

Enodium caeruleum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered bluebells, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from pale blue to deep violet. The leaves are lanceolate, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and early spring blooming period.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shaded garden settings.
