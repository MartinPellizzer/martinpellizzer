Eria laucheana

Eria laucheana, commonly known as the Lauche's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the Atlantic Forest biome. The plant features a slender, upright stem with several elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and often have a faintly colored lip, which is a characteristic feature of many orchid species.

Eria laucheana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
