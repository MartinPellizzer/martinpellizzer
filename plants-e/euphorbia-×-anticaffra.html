Euphorbia × anticaffra

Euphorbia × anticaffra, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Euphorbia × anticaffra is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience to drought.
