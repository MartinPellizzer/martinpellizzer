Erigeron peruvianus var. lineatus

Erigeron peruvianus var. lineatus, commonly known as the Peruvian fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
