Echinocactus multiflorus var. albispinus

Echinocactus multiflorus var. albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in white, spiny tubercles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
