Eriogonum elatum var. limonifolium

Eriogonum elatum var. limonifolium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

This species is an important food source for native pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats.
