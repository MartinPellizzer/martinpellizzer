Eschweilera rufifolia

Eschweilera rufifolia, commonly known as the red-leaved zygocactus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with striking red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in shaded garden environments.

Eschweilera rufifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in high humidity and well-drained soil.
