Erodendrum halimifolium

Erodendrum halimifolium, commonly known as the silverleaf erodium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and small, delicate flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky areas or along coastal regions where it can tolerate high salinity and drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
