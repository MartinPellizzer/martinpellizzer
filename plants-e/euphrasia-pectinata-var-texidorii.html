Euphrasia pectinata var. texidorii

Euphrasia pectinata var. texidorii, commonly known as the Texas beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in prairies and open woodlands. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
