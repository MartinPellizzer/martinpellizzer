Echinops kandaharensis

Echinops kandaharensis, commonly known as the Afghan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Afghanistan.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers in the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops kandaharensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
