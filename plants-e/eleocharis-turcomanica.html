Eleocharis turcomanica

Eleocharis turcomanica, commonly known as the Turkestan spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, pale green to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, ponds, and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis turcomanica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
