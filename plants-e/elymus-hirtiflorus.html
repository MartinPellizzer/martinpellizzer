Elymus hirtiflorus

Elymus hirtiflorus, commonly known as the hairy-flowered wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant produces spikelets that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Elymus hirtiflorus is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
