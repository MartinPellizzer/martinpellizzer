Erythroxylum kapplerianum

Erythroxylum kapplerianum, commonly known as the Kappler's erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
