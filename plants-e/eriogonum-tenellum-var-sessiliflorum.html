Eriogonum tenellum var. sessiliflorum

Eriogonum tenellum var. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessileflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually unbranched. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open grasslands and chaparral.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
