Equisetum sylvaticum f. polystachyum

Equisetum sylvaticum f. polystachyum, commonly known as the tall horsehair fern, is a fern species characterized by its distinctive, spore-bearing cones and whorled arrangement of leaves.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant exhibits a unique life cycle with a dominant sporophyte stage, and its stems are typically hollow and jointed. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often persist throughout the growing season.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
