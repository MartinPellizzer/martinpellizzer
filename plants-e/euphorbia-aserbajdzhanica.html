Euphorbia aserbajdzhanica

Euphorbia aserbajdzhanica, commonly known as the Caucasian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright stems covered in soft, velvety hairs and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flower-like structures called cyathia. The plant has thick, succulent leaves that are often lobed and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions. It is adapted to dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous areas.

Euphorbia aserbajdzhanica is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
