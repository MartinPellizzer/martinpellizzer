Erica azorica var. erythranta

Erica azorica var. erythranta, commonly known as the Azorean heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Azores.

It features dense clusters of small, bright red flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
