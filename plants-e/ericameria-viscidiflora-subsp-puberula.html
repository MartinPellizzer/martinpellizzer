Ericameria viscidiflora subsp. puberula

Ericameria viscidiflora subsp.

puberula, commonly known as the silverleaf ericameria, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for xeriscaping and ecological restoration projects.
