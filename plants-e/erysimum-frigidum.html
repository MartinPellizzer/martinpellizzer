Erysimum frigidum

Erysimum frigidum, commonly known as the alpine wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically features dense clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are adapted to withstand cold and harsh alpine conditions. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Erysimum frigidum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in challenging environments.
