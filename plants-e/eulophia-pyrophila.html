Eulophia pyrophila

Eulophia pyrophila, commonly known as the fire orchid, is a striking orchid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vivid red and orange flowers, which are often mistaken for flames, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open habitats such as heathlands and forests. Eulophia pyrophila is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, producing a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers.

Its unique coloration and habitat make it a notable species in Australian flora.
