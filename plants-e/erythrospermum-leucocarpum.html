Erythrospermum leucocarpum

Erythrospermum leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruited rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, white, fleshy fruits that are typically consumed by native animals. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
