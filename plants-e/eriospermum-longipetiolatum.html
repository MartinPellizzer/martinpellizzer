Eriospermum longipetiolatum

Eriospermum longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled eriosperm, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features elongated petioles that support its palmately lobed leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters and attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers well-drained soils and full sun.
