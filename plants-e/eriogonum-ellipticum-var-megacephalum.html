Eriogonum ellipticum var. megacephalum

Eriogonum ellipticum var. megacephalum, commonly known as the large-headed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, elliptical leaves and showy, clustered inflorescences of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains, thriving in full sun. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
