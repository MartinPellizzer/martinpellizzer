Ethulia triflora

Ethulia triflora, commonly known as the three-flower orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories. The plant features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ethulia triflora is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
