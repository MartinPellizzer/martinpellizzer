Exacum alberti-grimaldii

Exacum alberti-grimaldii, commonly known as the Alberti-Grimaldii flowering plant, is a species native to the Canary Islands.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Exacum alberti-grimaldii is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
