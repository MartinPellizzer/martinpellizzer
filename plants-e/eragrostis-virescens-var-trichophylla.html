Eragrostis virescens var. trichophylla

Eragrostis virescens var. trichophylla, commonly known as slender green lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage crop.
