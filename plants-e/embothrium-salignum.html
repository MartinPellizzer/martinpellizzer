Embothrium salignum

Embothrium salignum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to central and southern Chile.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. Embothrium salignum thrives in coastal and temperate climates, often growing in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
