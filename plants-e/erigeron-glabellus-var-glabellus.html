Erigeron glabellus var. glabellus

Erigeron glabellus var. glabellus, commonly known as the smoothleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, roadsides, and disturbed soils. It features slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy.

Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
