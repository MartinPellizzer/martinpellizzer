Echium vulgare var. hispidum

Echium vulgare var. hispidum, commonly known as hispid echium, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and striking spikes of violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in meadows, roadsides, and rocky areas, where it plays a role in supporting pollinators.
