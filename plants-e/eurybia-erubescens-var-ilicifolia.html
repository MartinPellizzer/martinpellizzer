Eurybia erubescens var. ilicifolia

Eurybia erubescens var. ilicifolia, commonly known as the red-stemmed goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, especially when mature. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers in panicles during the late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are alternate, lanceolate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.
