Erica haroldiana

Erica haroldiana, commonly known as the Harold's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erica haroldiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
