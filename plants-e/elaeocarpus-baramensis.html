Elaeocarpus baramensis

Elaeocarpus baramensis, commonly known as the Baram hill ebony, is a tropical tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, dark purple to black fruits that are encased in a fleshy, edible aril. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, growing in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
