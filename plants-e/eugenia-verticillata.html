Eugenia verticillata

Eugenia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, and it is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.

Eugenia verticillata prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in warm, humid climates.
