Erigeron virgatus

Erigeron virgatus, commonly known as the silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its silvery-gray foliage gives it a distinctive appearance, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in the summer.

Erigeron virgatus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
