Entada arborea

Entada arborea, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, red or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, woody pods containing flat, hard seeds. Its bark and wood have been used traditionally for medicinal purposes and in the production of dyes.

The plant is also valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
