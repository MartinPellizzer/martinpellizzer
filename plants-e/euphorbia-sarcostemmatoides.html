Euphorbia sarcostemmatoides

Euphorbia sarcostemmatoides, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Its sap contains toxic compounds, which can cause skin irritation.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
