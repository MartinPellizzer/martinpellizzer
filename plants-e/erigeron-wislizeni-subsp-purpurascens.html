Erigeron wislizeni subsp. purpurascens

Erigeron wislizeni subsp.

purpurascens, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and a central cone-like structure composed of numerous small florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
