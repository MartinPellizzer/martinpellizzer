Eria farinosa

Eria farinosa, commonly known as the powdered orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, epiphytic plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, with a distinctive pink or white coloration.

Eria farinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
