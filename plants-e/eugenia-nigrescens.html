Eugenia nigrescens

Eugenia nigrescens, commonly known as black myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. Eugenia nigrescens thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
