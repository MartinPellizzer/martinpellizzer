Eucalyptus obliqua var. microstoma

Eucalyptus obliqua var. microstoma, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leaf mallee, native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and small, narrow leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that are usually pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, arid environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
