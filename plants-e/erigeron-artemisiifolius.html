Erigeron artemisiifolius

Erigeron artemisiifolius, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas such as fields and roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its structure and growth habits make it a common sight in prairie and steppe ecosystems.
