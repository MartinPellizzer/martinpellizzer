Erycibe pallidifolia

Erycibe pallidifolia, commonly known as the pale-leaved erycibe, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually pale green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
