Echium angustifolium var. heterophyllum

Echium angustifolium var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically narrow and linear, with some variation in shape in this variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

This plant is also known for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
