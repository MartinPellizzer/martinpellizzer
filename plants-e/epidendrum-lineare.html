Epidendrum lineare

Epidendrum lineare, commonly known as the linear orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often pollinated by bees and other insects.

Epidendrum lineare is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its elegant appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
