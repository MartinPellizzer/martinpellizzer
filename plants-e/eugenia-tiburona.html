Eugenia tiburona

Eugenia tiburona, commonly known as the Tiburon guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small, round berry, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia tiburona is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit in tropical and subtropical regions.
