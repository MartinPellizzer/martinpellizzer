Eria pseudocymbiformis

Eria pseudocymbiformis, commonly known as the false cymbidium orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. The plant features slender, upright stems with oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy texture. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually pink or white, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

Eria pseudocymbiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
