Echinocereus longisetus subsp. freudenbergeri

Echinocereus longisetus subsp.

freudenbergeri, commonly known as the Freudenberger's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, spine-covered cactus with a globular shape and typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters.

The plant features pale green stems and produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
