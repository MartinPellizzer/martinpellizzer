Eriospermum brevifilamentatum

Eriospermum brevifilamentatum, commonly known as the narrow filament eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a perennial shrub with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a unique, hairy pappus that aids in wind dispersal.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
