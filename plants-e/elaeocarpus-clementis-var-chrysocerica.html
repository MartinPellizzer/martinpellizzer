Elaeocarpus clementis var. chrysocerica

Elaeocarpus clementis var. chrysocerica, commonly known as the golden-capped elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits with a golden cap. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
