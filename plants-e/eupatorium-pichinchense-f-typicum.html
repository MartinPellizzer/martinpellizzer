Eupatorium pichinchense f. typicum

Eupatorium pichinchense f. typicum, commonly known as the Andean mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cooler, temperate climates. Its foliage is often aromatic, containing essential oils that contribute to its medicinal properties.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
