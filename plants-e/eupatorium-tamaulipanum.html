Eupatorium tamaulipanum

Eupatorium tamaulipanum, commonly known as Tamaulipan thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, often covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium tamaulipanum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
