Eucalyptus viridis var. latiuscula

Eucalyptus viridis var. latiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in mallee woodlands and is part of the Myrtaceae family.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in low-nutrient conditions.
