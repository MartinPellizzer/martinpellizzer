Eucalyptus erythronema subsp. erythronema

Eucalyptus erythronema subsp.

erythronema, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-barked trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually cream to pale yellow in hue. It is a medium-sized tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a spreading crown and a tendency to form dense foliage.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in coastal and subcoastal regions.
