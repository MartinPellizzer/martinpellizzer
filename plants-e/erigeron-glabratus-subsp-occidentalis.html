Erigeron glabratus subsp. occidentalis

Erigeron glabratus subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and suitable for xeriscaping.
