Echinocactus hystrichacanthus

Echinocactus hystrichacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, ribbed stem that becomes densely covered with spines as it matures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for arid gardens and indoor cultivation.
