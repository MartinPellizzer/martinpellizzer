Eupatorium comptoniifolium

Eupatorium comptoniifolium, commonly known as Compton's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters. Its showy, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
