Echium confusum

Echium confusum, commonly known as the confused thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and produces striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Echium confusum is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in well-drained soils.
