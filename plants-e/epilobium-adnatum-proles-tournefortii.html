Epilobium adnatum proles tournefortii

Epilobium adnatum proles tournefortii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, pinkish to reddish flowers in racemes. Its botanical characteristics include a hairy stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

It is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
