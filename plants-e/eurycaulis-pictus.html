Eurycaulis pictus

Eurycaulis pictus, commonly known as the painted stem plant, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful stems that range from red to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually small, oval, and glossy, providing a contrast to its vibrant stems.

Eurycaulis pictus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in semi-shaded conditions.
