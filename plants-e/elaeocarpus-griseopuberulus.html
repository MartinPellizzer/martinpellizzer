Elaeocarpus griseopuberulus

Elaeocarpus griseopuberulus, commonly known as the grey-hairy elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, greyish hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and has some traditional uses in local communities.
