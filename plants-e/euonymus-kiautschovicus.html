Euonymus kiautschovicus

Euonymus kiautschovicus, commonly known as the Russian spindletree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, lantern-like fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its adaptability and resistance to pests and diseases.
