Eupatorium subserratum

Eupatorium subserratum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rough, serrated leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a tall, slender stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height.

The leaves are lance-shaped with a distinctive, rough texture and are often covered in fine hairs.
