Eupatorium plethadenium

Eupatorium plethadenium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium plethadenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
