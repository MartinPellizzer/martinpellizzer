Eritrichium czekanowskii

Eritrichium czekanowskii, commonly known as the Czekanowsky's biscuit flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. It is native to Central Asia and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Eritrichium czekanowskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
