Echinocactus krausei

Echinocactus krausei, commonly known as the Krause's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores and excessive sunlight.

Echinocactus krausei is well adapted to dry environments and requires minimal water to survive.
