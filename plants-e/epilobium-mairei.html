Epilobium mairei

Epilobium mairei, commonly known as Maire's willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Epilobium mairei is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It is also used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health properties.
