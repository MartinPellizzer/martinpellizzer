Epacris pauciflora var. sinclairii

Epacris pauciflora var. sinclairii, commonly known as the Sinclair's spiny epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
