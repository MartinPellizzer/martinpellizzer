Epidendrum cocuyense

Epidendrum cocuyense, commonly known as the Cocuyense orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum cocuyense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
