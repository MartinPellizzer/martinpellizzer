Erica zitzikammensis var. zitzikammensis

Erica zitzikammensis var. zitzikammensis, commonly known as the Zitzifammine heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
