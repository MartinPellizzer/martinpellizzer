Euclea macrophylla

Euclea macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved euclea, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate to elliptical leaves that can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
