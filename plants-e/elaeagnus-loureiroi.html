Elaeagnus loureiroi

Elaeagnus loureiroi, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used for erosion control. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are visited by pollinators such as bees.

The wood of Elaeagnus loureiroi is hard and durable, making it useful for crafting and fuel.
