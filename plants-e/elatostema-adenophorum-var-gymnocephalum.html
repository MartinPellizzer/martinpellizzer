Elatostema adenophorum var. gymnocephalum

Elatostema adenophorum var. gymnocephalum, commonly known as the naked-headed elatostema, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a short, upright stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, with four petals that are usually white or pale pink.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf morphology and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
