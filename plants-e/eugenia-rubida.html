Eugenia rubida

Eugenia rubida, commonly known as the red eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 feet. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing attractive evergreen foliage.

Eugenia rubida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
