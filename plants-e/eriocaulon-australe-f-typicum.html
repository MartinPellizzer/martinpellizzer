Eriocaulon australe f. typicum

Eriocaulon australe f. typicum, commonly known as the Australian marsh plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils.

This fern-like plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
