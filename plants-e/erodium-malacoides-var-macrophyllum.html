Erodium malacoides var. macrophyllum

Erodium malacoides var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

The species is part of the Geraniaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
