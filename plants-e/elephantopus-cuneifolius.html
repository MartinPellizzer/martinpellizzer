Elephantopus cuneifolius

Elephantopus cuneifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elephant's ear, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
