Elymus cacuminis

Elymus cacuminis, commonly known as the Sierra wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and is adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
