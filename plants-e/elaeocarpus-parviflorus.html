Elaeocarpus parviflorus

Elaeocarpus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
