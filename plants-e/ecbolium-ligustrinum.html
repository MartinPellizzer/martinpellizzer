Ecbolium ligustrinum

Ecbolium ligustrinum, commonly known as the common privet, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, North Africa, and western Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and as a hedge plant.

The species is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
