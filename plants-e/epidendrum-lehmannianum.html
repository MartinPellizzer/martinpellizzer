Epidendrum lehmannianum

Epidendrum lehmannianum, commonly known as the Lehmann's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, arching stems and clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually elongated and grass-like, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
