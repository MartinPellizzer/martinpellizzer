Eurya ochnacea var. morii

Eurya ochnacea var. morii, commonly known as the Japanese waxleaf eurya, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically glossy and oval-shaped, with a waxy coating that gives it its common name. It produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
