Eria verruculosa

Eria verruculosa, commonly known as the hairy eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale green, often clustered in loose inflorescences. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture for its unique appearance and delicate blooms.
