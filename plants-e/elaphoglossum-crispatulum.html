Elaphoglossum crispatulum

Elaphoglossum crispatulum, commonly known as the curled hawksbeard, is a small, evergreen fern native to the moist forests of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, bipinnate fronds with a distinctive curled appearance, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on rocky or wooded substrates. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines along the margins of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum crispatulum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
