Euphorbia pilosa var. illirica

Euphorbia pilosa var. illirica, commonly known as the Illyrian spiny euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in short, spiny hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flower clusters surrounded by leaf-like structures. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, spiny structures, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This species is valued for its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance in xeriscaping.
