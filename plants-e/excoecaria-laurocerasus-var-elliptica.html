Excoecaria laurocerasus var. elliptica

Excoecaria laurocerasus var. elliptica, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red berries, which are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting habits.
