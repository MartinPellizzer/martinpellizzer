Ehrharta godefroyi

Ehrharta godefroyi, commonly known as false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often form dense clumps, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color.

Ehrharta godefroyi plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
