Erythracanthus coccineus

Erythracanthus coccineus, commonly known as the red-veined dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features striking, showy flowers with bright red veins on white petals, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments, often in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a fine, hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
