Esula gerardiana

Esula gerardiana, commonly known as the Gerardia or the Mountain Gerardia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments. Esula gerardiana is known for its resilience to cold temperatures and high-altitude conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in ecological and horticultural contexts.
