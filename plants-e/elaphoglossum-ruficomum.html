Elaphoglossum ruficomum

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically bright green with a reddish hue on the underside. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are covered by a protective indusium.

Elaphoglossum ruficomum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and its ability to add a lush, tropical feel to shaded garden spaces.
