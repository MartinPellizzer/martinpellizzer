Eryngium globosum

Eryngium globosum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble a globe, surrounded by bracts. The plant has silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Eryngium globosum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
