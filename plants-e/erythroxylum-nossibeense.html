Erythroxylum nossibeense

Erythroxylum nossibeense, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species of flowering plant in the mallow family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its woody structure and clusters of small, red berries. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with a distinct scent.

Erythroxylum nossibeense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.
