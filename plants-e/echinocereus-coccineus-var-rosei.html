Echinocereus coccineus var. rosei

Echinocereus coccineus var. rosei, commonly known as the roseate hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, reddish-brown spines that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
