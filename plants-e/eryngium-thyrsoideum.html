Eryngium thyrsoideum

Eryngium thyrsoideum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flowers and silver-green, pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, sandy soils, and rocky habitats, thriving in full sun. It is a perennial herb that produces small, globe-shaped seed heads in late summer.

Eryngium thyrsoideum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
