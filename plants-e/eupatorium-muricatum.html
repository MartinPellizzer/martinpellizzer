Eupatorium muricatum

Eupatorium muricatum, commonly known as the rough eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Its flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, making it an important species in ecological systems.
