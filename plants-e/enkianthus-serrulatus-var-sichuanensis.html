Enkianthus serrulatus var. sichuanensis

Enkianthus serrulatus var. sichuanensis, commonly known as the Sichuan enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in early spring. Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant foliage and delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
