Epipactis muelleri subsp. cerritae

Epipactis muelleri subsp.

cerritae, commonly known as the Mexican marsh helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, shaded environments. It has a pseudobulb structure and is adapted to seasonal flooding.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
