Eunanus subsecundus

Eunanus subsecundus, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-green, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Eunanus subsecundus thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and shrubland habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
