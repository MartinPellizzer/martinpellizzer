Euphoria glabra

Euphoria glabra, commonly known as the smooth euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, green stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap that can be irritating to the skin. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands.

Euphoria glabra is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many other species with similar botanical features.
