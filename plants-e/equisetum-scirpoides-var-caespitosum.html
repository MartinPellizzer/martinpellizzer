Equisetum scirpoides var. caespitosum

Equisetum scirpoides var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted scouring rush, is a member of the Equisetaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spore-bearing stems.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant has a distinctive appearance with a basal rosette of leaves and a tall, unbranched stem that bears spore-producing structures called strobili. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, and the plant lacks true roots, instead relying on rhizomes for nutrient absorption.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
