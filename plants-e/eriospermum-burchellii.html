Eriospermum burchellii

Eriospermum burchellii, commonly known as the woolly eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

The seeds are enclosed in a distinctive, spiky capsule that aids in seed dispersal.
