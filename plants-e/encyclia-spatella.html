Encyclia spatella

Encyclia spatella, commonly known as the Spatulate Encyclia, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers have a distinctive spatulate shape, giving the species its common name.

Encyclia spatella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
