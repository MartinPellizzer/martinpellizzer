Euonymus leiophloeus var. armasicus

Euonymus leiophloeus var. armasicus, commonly known as the Armenian euonymus, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine in its native regions.
