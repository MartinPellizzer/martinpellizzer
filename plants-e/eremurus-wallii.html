Eremurus wallii

Eremurus wallii, commonly known as the "Chinese giant sandwort," is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky inflorescence that can reach heights of up to 3 meters, bearing numerous yellow-green flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained soil.

Eremurus wallii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to grow once established.
