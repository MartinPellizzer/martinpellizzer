Elaeagnus multiflora var. gigantea

Elaeagnus multiflora var. gigantea, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage, which is covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
