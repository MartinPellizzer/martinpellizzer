Eriosema multiflorum

Eriosema multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered eriosema, is a South African plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by insects, and it produces small, elongated pods containing seeds.

Eriosema multiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
