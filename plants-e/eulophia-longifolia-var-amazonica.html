Eulophia longifolia var. amazonica

Eulophia longifolia var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian long-flowered eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

This orchid is characterized by its elongated, linear leaves and striking, showy inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically growing in the understory of tropical rainforests. The plant is epiphytic, meaning it attaches itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them, and it relies on aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air.

Its unique floral structure and adaptability to moist, warm climates make it a notable species in botanical studies.
