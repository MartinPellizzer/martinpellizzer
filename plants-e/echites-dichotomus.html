Echites dichotomus

Echites dichotomus, commonly known as the forked weed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, branched stems that often split into two at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

Echites dichotomus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
