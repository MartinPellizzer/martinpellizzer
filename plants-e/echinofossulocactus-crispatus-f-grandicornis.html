Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. grandicornis

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. grandicornis, commonly known as the Grandicorn Cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with ribbed structure and prominent, spiky areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its unique morphology includes long, curved spines that give it a distinctive appearance.

It is native to a limited geographic region in northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.
