Eremanthus eriopus

Eremanthus eriopus, commonly known as the silver fern, is a striking plant native to the forests of New Zealand.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage, which results from a dense covering of fine, hair-like trichomes on the leaf surfaces. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in loose, drooping inflorescences, adding an ornamental appeal. Eremanthus eriopus thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in New Zealand's native flora.
