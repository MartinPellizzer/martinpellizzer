Eugenia daphne

Eugenia daphne, commonly known as the common daphne, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its aromatic, showy flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dense, evergreen foliage. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant fragrance when crushed.

The flowers are usually pink or white, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
