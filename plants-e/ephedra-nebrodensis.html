Ephedra nebrodensis

Ephedra nebrodensis, commonly known as the Nebrodensis ephedra, is a member of the Ephedraceae family and is native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional medicine.
