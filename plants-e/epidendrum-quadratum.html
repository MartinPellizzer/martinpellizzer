Epidendrum quadratum

Epidendrum quadratum, commonly known as the square epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and its striking, square-shaped pseudobulbs. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a waxy texture and a deep green color.

Epidendrum quadratum is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
