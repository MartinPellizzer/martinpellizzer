Euphrasia stricta var. tatarica

Euphrasia stricta var. tatarica, commonly known as the tatarian eyebright, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in fine, hairy foliage. Its most distinctive characteristic is the clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
