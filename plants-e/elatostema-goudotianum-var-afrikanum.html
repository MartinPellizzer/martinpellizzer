Elatostema goudotianum var. afrikanum

Elatostema goudotianum var. afrikanum, commonly known as the African prostrate elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky outcrops. The plant features a prostrate or ascending habit with slender, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine pubescence. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in umbels and are often borne on short pedicels.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to form dense mats, making it a common component of understory vegetation in its native habitat.
