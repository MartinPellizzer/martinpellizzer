Euphorbia aegyptiaca var. annobonensis

Euphorbia aegyptiaca var. annobonensis, commonly known as the Annobon euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to the island of Annobon in Equatorial Guinea.

It features a branching stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red or orange. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its succulent tissues to survive drought conditions.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
