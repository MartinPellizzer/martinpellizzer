Eriocaulon belgaumensis

Eriocaulon belgaumensis, commonly known as the Belgaum rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny white or pale yellow flowers. It is typically found in moist, swampy or marshy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eriocaulon belgaumensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
