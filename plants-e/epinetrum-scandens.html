Epinetrum scandens

Epinetrum scandens, commonly known as the climbing fig or creeping fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically consumed raw. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to cover structures.

Epinetrum scandens is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.
