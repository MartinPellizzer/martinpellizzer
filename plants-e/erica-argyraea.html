Erica argyraea

Erica argyraea, commonly known as silver heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heather. The plant is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery white wax, giving it its common name. It produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white in color.

Erica argyraea thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
