Epipactis elmeri

Epipactis elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy, pale green to yellowish flower with a distinctive, hooded lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving flies.

Epipactis elmeri is a relatively rare species, often found in specific microhabitats with particular soil and moisture conditions.
