Epidendrum glaucovirens

Epidendrum glaucovirens, commonly known as the Blue Violet Epidendrum, is a striking orchid native to Central and South America.

It features vibrant violet to blue flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a contrasting white or yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air. Its leaves are usually dark green and somewhat leathery, adapted to thrive in humid environments.

Epidendrum glaucovirens is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
