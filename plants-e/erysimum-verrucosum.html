Erysimum verrucosum

Erysimum verrucosum, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, hairy leaves and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard.

Erysimum verrucosum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
