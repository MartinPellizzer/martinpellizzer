Eugenia tumulescens

Eugenia tumulescens, commonly known as the purple mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits with a thick, reddish-purple rind and a sweet, segmented pulp. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The botanical features of Eugenia tumulescens make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
