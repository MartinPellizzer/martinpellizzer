Echinocactus scopa f. albicans

Echinocactus scopa f. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the hedgehog cactus.

It is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and large, showy white flowers that bloom in the summer. Its foliage is typically gray-green, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments to ensure its survival.
