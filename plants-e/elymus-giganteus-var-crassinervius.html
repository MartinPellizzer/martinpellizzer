Elymus giganteus var. crassinervius

Elymus giganteus var. crassinervius, commonly known as the broad-veined wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, robust stalks and large, grass-like leaves with prominent, wide veins. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
