Euphrasia paucifolia

Euphrasia paucifolia, commonly known as the slender eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has delicate, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Euphrasia paucifolia is part of the Scrophulariaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
