Evota venosa

Evota venosa, commonly known as the veined beauty, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
