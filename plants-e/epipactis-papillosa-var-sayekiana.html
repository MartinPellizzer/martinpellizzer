Epipactis papillosa var. sayekiana

Epipactis papillosa var. sayekiana, commonly known as the Sayek's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Europe and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy, pale green to yellowish flower with a distinctive, hairy lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect flowering stem.

This variety is notable for its morphological distinctiveness and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts.
