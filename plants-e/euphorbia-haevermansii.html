Euphorbia haevermansii

Euphorbia haevermansii, commonly known as the Cape Spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branching stem system with fleshy, ribbed leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
