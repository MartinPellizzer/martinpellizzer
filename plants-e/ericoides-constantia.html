Ericoides constantia

Ericoides constantia, commonly known as the Constantia heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in acidic environments. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions.

Ericoides constantia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
