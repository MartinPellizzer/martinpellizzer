Eragrostis namaquensis

Eragrostis namaquensis, commonly known as the Namaqua grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eragrostis namaquensis is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
