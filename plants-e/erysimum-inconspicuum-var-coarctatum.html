Erysimum inconspicuum var. coarctatum

Erysimum inconspicuum var. coarctatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It is well adapted to full sun and is drought-tolerant once established.

The flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
