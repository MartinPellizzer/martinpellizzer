Ehretia domingensis

Ehretia domingensis, commonly known as the Spanish cherry or white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that are often used in traditional medicine.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
