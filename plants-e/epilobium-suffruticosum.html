Epilobium suffruticosum

Epilobium suffruticosum, commonly known as the woody willow or creeping willow, is a perennial shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Epilobium suffruticosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
