Echeveria cuspidata var. zaragozae

Echeveria cuspidata var. zaragozae, commonly known as Zaragoza's Echeveria, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-gray to green in color, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
