Epilobium graebneri

Epilobium graebneri, commonly known as the Sierra buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Epilobium graebneri is often found in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.
