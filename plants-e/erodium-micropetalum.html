Erodium micropetalum

Erodium micropetalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and habitat restoration efforts.
