Eugenia soyauxii

Eugenia soyauxii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests and riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and traditional medicine.
