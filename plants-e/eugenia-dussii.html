Eugenia dussii

Eugenia dussii, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and have a waxy, smooth skin, typically turning red when mature.

Eugenia dussii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
