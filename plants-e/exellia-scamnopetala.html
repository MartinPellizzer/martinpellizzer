Exellia scamnopetala

Exellia scamnopetala, commonly known as the silverleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
