Euthamia fastigiata

Euthamia fastigiata, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped, hairy leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in meadows and open woodlands.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, also known as the sunflower family.
