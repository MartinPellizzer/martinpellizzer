Epacris serpyllifolia var. squarrosa

Epacris serpyllifolia var. squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
