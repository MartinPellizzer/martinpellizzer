Erythrina caribaea

Erythrina caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, flame-like flowers. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a tree or shrub.

Erythrina caribaea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine.
