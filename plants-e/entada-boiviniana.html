Entada boiviniana

Entada boiviniana, commonly known as the African honeytree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in savannas and forest edges, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant has traditional uses in local medicine and is also valued for its timber.
