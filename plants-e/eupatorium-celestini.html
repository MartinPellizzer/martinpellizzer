Eupatorium celestini

Eupatorium celestini, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that add visual interest throughout the growing season. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium celestini is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
