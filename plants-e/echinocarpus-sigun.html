Echinocarpus sigun

Echinocarpus sigun, commonly known as the spiky burr plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family, characterized by its distinctive spiny fruits.

The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a rough texture, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters in height.

It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
