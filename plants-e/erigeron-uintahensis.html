Erigeron uintahensis

Erigeron uintahensis, commonly known as Uintah fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert scrub. It produces small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Uintah fleabane is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
