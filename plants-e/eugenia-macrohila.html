Eugenia macrohila

Eugenia macrohila, commonly known as the white eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing several seeds.

Eugenia macrohila is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
