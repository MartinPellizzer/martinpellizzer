Eugenia bullockii

Eugenia bullockii, commonly known as the Burridge tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. These berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
