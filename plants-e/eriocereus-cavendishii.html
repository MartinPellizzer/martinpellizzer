Eriocereus cavendishii

Eriocereus cavendishii, commonly known as the Pink Cactus or the Christmas Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its branching stems covered in dense, white to pinkish hairs, which give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces vibrant pink or red flowers during the winter months, typically blooming in response to cooler temperatures and longer nights. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Despite its delicate look, it is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
