Eucalyptus cullenii var. trivalvis

Eucalyptus cullenii var. trivalvis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often grey-green in color. The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
