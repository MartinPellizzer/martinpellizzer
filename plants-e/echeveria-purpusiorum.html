Echeveria purpusiorum

Echeveria purpusiorum, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves with a powdery coating that gives them a frosty appearance. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact size and striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
