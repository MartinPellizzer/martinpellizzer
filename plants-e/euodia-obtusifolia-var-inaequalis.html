Euodia obtusifolia var. inaequalis

Euodia obtusifolia var. inaequalis, commonly known as the rough-leaved Chinese frankincense tree, is a variant of the Euodia genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves with a rough texture and a distinctive aromatic resin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Its bark and leaves contain volatile oils, which have been traditionally used in herbal medicine.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in traditional Chinese medicine.
