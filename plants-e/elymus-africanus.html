Elymus africanus

Elymus africanus, commonly known as the African needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fibrous roots that help stabilize soil in arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to semi-arid regions.

Elymus africanus plays a role in preventing soil erosion and is sometimes used in restoration projects in its native habitat.
