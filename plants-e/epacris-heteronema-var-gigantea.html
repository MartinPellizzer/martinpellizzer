Epacris heteronema var. gigantea

Epacris heteronema var. gigantea, commonly known as the giant heath, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the wetter regions of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
