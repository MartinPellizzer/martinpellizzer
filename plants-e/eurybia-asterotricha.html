Eurybia asterotricha

Eurybia asterotricha, commonly known as the hairy aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy purple to pink ray flowers surrounding a central disk of yellow florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically hairy, giving it the common name, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Eurybia asterotricha is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
