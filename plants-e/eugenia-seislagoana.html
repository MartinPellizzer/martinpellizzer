Eugenia seislagoana

Eugenia seislagoana, commonly known as the mountain eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly serrated edge. Its small, fragrant flowers are usually pink or white and appear in clusters, followed by small, edible berries.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
