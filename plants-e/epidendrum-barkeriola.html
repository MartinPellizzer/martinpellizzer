Epidendrum barkeriola

Epidendrum barkeriola, commonly known as the Barker's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a sympodial epiphyte, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic, typically in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and have a waxy texture, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum barkeriola is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
