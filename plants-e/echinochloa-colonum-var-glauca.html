Echinochloa colonum var. glauca

Echinochloa colonum var. glauca, commonly known as glaucous barnyardgrass, is an annual grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray sheaths and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny grains.

This variety is often considered a weed in cultivated areas due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
