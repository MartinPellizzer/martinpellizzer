Eragrostis dielsii var. typica

Eragrostis dielsii var. typica, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open grasslands and along rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
