Excentrodendron tonkinense

Excentrodendron tonkinense, commonly known as the Tonkin starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in fascicles, followed by orange to red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit is edible, though its taste is somewhat tart and acidic.
