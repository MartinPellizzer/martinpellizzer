Enkianthus sikokianus

Enkianthus sikokianus, commonly known as the Japanese enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its showy blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
