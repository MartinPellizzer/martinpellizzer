Eriogonum racemosum var. zionis

Eriogonum racemosum var. zionis, commonly known as Zion's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and oblong, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Zion's buckwheat plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
