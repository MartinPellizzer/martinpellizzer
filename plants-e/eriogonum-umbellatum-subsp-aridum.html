Eriogonum umbellatum subsp. aridum

Eriogonum umbellatum subsp.

aridum, commonly known as desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant produces numerous stamens that give it a fluffy appearance, and its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

This subspecies is an important component of desert ecosystems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small animals.
