Eugenia parkeriana

Eugenia parkeriana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that persist through winter. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.
