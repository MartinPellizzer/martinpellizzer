Euonymus leclerei

Euonymus leclerei, commonly known as the golden euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow foliage, which turns orange-red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Euonymus leclerei is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
