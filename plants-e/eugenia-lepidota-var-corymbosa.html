Eugenia lepidota var. corymbosa

Eugenia lepidota var. corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern coast of Australia and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its fragrant foliage and showy blooms.
