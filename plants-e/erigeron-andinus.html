Erigeron andinus

Erigeron andinus, commonly known as the Andean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale blue flowers.

Erigeron andinus is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates.
