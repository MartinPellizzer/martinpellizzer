Eria saccata

Eria saccata, commonly known as the sac-like eria, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique, sac-like structure on its flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and come in shades of white and yellow.

Eria saccata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
