Eryngium ramboanum

Eryngium ramboanum, commonly known as the Madagascar thistle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue or purple flower heads. The plant has basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically erect and branched. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Eryngium ramboanum is known for its striking appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
