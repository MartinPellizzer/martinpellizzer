Euphrasia × reuteri

Euphrasia × reuteri, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a hybrid species within the genus Euphrasia, which belongs to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, glandular-hairy stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

Euphrasia × reuteri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
