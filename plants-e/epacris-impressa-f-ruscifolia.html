Epacris impressa f. ruscifolia

Epacris impressa f. ruscifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine, white hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in open heathlands and woodland areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
