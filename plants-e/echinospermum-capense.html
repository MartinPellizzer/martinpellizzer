Echinospermum capense

Echinospermum capense, commonly known as the African dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spiky seed capsules that release seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Echinospermum capense is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
