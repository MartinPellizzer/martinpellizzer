Erica dianthifolia

Erica dianthifolia, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the western and southern regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica dianthifolia typically grows in coastal and mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
