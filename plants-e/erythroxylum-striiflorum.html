Erythroxylum striiflorum

Erythroxylum striiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-flowered erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its flowers are usually pink or red, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
