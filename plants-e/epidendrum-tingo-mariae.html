Epidendrum tingo-mariae

Epidendrum tingo-mariae, commonly known as the Tingo Maria orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Epidendrum tingo-mariae is also valued for its role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
