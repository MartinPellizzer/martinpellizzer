Erigeron geiseri

Erigeron geiseri, commonly known as the Sierra daisy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
