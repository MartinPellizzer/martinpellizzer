Euonymus euphlebiphyllus

Euonymus euphlebiphyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter beneath. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, followed by bright red, inflated seed capsules that split open to release seeds. It thrives in woodland habitats and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
