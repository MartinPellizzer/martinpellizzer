Euphorbia finlaysonii

Euphorbia finlaysonii, commonly known as the Finlayson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Euphorbia finlaysonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
