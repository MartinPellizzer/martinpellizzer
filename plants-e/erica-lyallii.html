Erica lyallii

Erica lyallii, commonly known as Lyall's heath, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This species is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica lyallii typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for native pollinators.
