Esula pinea

Esula pinea, commonly known as the silver pine, is a species of evergreen conifer native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and its distinctive silver-blue foliage, which is covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, and it is typically found in high-altitude forests with cool, moist climates. Esula pinea has a slow growth rate and can live for several centuries, making it a long-lived component of its ecosystem.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
