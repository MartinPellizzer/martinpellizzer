Ervatamia cylindrocarpa

Ervatamia cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed thornapple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a cylindrical capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Ervatamia cylindrocarpa is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
