Epidendrum amblyantherum

Epidendrum amblyantherum, commonly known as the narrow-lobed epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. Its flowers are typically arranged in terminal racemes and are usually violet or purple in color.

Epidendrum amblyantherum is a relatively hardy orchid that can thrive in a variety of growing conditions, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced growers.
