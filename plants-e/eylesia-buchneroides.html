Eylesia buchneroides

Eylesia buchneroides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed senna, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Eylesia buchneroides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in semi-arid conditions.

Its seeds are encased in slender, elongated pods that split open to release the seeds.
