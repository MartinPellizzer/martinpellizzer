Elephantopus racemosus

Elephantopus racemosus, commonly known as the hairy elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, which contribute to its scientific name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or traditional uses.
