Eupatorium orphaliifolium

Eupatorium orphaliifolium, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators.

The species is also known for its aromatic foliage and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
