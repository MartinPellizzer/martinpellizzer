Erycibe dubia

Erycibe dubia, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers with yellow centers, which bloom throughout the year in suitable climates. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Erycibe dubia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
