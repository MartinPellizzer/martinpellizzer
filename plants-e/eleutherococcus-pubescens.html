Eleutherococcus pubescens

Eleutherococcus pubescens, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the forests of eastern Siberia and northern China.

It is characterized by its woody stems, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have been used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptogenic properties make it popular for enhancing physical endurance and reducing stress.
