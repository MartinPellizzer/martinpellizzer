Exochaenium grande var. major

Exochaenium grande var. major, commonly known as the large-flowered grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
