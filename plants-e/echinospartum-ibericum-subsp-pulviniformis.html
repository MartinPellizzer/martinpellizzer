Echinospartum ibericum subsp. pulviniformis

Echinospartum ibericum subsp.

pulviniformis, commonly known as the Iberian echinospartum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
