Erythroxylum subumbellatum

Erythroxylum subumbellatum, commonly known as the Peruvian pepper tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several small, reddish-brown seeds.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
