Eremurus sikesarus

Eremurus sikesarus, commonly known as the desert candle, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, candle-like flower stalks that can reach up to 3 meters in height, topped with a dense cluster of yellow to orange flowers. The plant has narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

Eremurus sikesarus is often cultivated for its dramatic floral display and is relatively low maintenance once established.
