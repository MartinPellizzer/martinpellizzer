Echinops rechingeri

Echinops rechingeri, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed head, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
