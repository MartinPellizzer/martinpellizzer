Eragrostis cataclasta

Eragrostis cataclasta, commonly known as the broken grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often break easily, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and somewhat hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Eragrostis cataclasta is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
