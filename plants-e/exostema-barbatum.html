Exostema barbatum

Exostema barbatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a velvety, pubescent texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in spikes, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings, and it has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread distribution in tropical and subtropical regions.
