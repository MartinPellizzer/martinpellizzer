Eria eurostachys

Eria eurostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers in late summer. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Eria eurostachys is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
