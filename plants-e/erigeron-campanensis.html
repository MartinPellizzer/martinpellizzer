Erigeron campanensis

Erigeron campanensis, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its blue to purple flower heads consist of ray florets surrounding a central disc of tubular flowers.

Erigeron campanensis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
