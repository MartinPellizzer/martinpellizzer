Erica translucens

Erica translucens, commonly known as the translucent heath, is a species of shrub native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Erica translucens is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
