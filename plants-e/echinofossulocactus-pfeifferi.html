Echinofossulocactus pfeifferi

Echinofossulocactus pfeifferi, commonly known as the Pfeiffer's spiny barrel cactus, is a rare and striking cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is covered in prominent, spiny tubercles that provide protection against herbivores and extreme weather. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in isolated populations within its native range.
