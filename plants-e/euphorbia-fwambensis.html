Euphorbia fwambensis

Euphorbia fwambensis, commonly known as the Fwambeni euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the genus. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its stem and leaves.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
