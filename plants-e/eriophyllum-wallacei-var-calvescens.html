Eriophyllum wallacei var. calvescens

Eriophyllum wallacei var. calvescens, commonly known as the Wallace's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, yellowish to golden flowers. The plant typically thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
