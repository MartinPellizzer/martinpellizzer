Elatinoides aegyptiaca

Elatinoides aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian elatin, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Elatinaceae.

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in moist, sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and wetlands. The plant has small, green, succulent leaves and produces tiny, pink or white flowers. It is well adapted to saline environments and can thrive in areas with high water salinity.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites.
