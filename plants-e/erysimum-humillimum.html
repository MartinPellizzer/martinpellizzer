Erysimum humillimum

Erysimum humillimum, commonly known as the dwarf wallflower, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches heights of less than 10 inches and features bright yellow, four-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has basal leaves that are linear to lance-shaped, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Erysimum humillimum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
