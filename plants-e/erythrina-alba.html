Erythrina alba

Erythrina alba, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and typically a pale gray or brown color. Erythrina alba is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display.

It also has cultural and traditional significance in some regions, where it is used for medicinal purposes.
