Eragrostis polytricha

Eragrostis polytricha, commonly known as slender false brome, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it an important pioneer species in ecological succession.
