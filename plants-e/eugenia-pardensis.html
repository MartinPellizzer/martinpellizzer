Eugenia pardensis

Eugenia pardensis, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia pardensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
