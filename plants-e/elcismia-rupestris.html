Elcismia rupestris

Elcismia rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often blue-green in color, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Elcismia rupestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
