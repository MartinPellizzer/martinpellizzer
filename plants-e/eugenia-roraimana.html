Eugenia roraimana

Eugenia roraimana, commonly known as the Roraima myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of dense tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
