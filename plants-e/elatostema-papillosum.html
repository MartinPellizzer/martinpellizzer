Elatostema papillosum

Elatostema papillosum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a rosette of fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
