Eupatorium hyssopifolium

Eupatorium hyssopifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
