Eragrostis suaveolens subsp. borysthenica

Eragrostis suaveolens subsp.

borysthenica, commonly known as the fragrant lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, greenish to golden-yellow inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a faintly aromatic scent.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
