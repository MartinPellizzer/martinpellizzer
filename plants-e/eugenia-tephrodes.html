Eugenia tephrodes

Eugenia tephrodes, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural purposes.
