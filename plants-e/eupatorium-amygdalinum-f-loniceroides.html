Eupatorium amygdalinum f. loniceroides

Eupatorium amygdalinum f. loniceroides, commonly known as the white snakeroot, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to greenish flowers.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and thickets. The plant contains toxic compounds, including cyanogenic glycosides, which can be harmful to livestock and humans if ingested. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological role.

The species is native to parts of North America and has historical significance in traditional medicine and folklore.
