Erobathos damascenum

Erobathos damascenum, commonly known as the Damascus rose, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, red berries that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in perfumery.

The Damascus rose is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
