Erycibe integripetala

Erycibe integripetala, commonly known as the red-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its tubers, which are the main edible part, are starchy and used in various culinary applications.

The botanical features of Erycibe integripetala make it a distinctive and valuable species in tropical horticulture.
