Erigeron delphiniifolius subsp. neomexicanus

Erigeron delphiniifolius subsp.

neomexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its blue to violet flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, daisy-like florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are usually hairy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
