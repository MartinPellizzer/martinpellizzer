Ebenus molucca

Ebenus molucca, commonly known as the Moluccan euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and a variety of leaf shapes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are often alternate and can be either simple or lobed, depending on the species.

Ebenus molucca is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in dry, tropical conditions.
