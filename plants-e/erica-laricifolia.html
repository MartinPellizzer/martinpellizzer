Erica laricifolia

Erica laricifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white wax. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica laricifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
