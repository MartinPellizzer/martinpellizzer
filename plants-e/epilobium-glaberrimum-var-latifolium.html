Epilobium glaberrimum var. latifolium

Epilobium glaberrimum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils, such as meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, bearing opposite, lanceolate to ovate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in terminal or axillary clusters, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various invertebrates.
