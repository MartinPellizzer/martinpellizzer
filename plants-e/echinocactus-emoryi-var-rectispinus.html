Echinocactus emoryi var. rectispinus

Echinocactus emoryi var. rectispinus, commonly known as the Emory's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and unique morphology, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
