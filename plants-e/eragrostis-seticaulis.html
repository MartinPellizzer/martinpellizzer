Eragrostis seticaulis

Eragrostis seticaulis, commonly known as slender fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, savannas, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, erect leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small flowers. It produces seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
