Epidendrum chaoticum

Epidendrum chaoticum, commonly known as the chaotic orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful inflorescences that can display a wide range of hues, including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air and rain. Its flowers are often fragrant and can bloom multiple times throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.

The species is noted for its genetic diversity, which contributes to its unique and variable appearance.
