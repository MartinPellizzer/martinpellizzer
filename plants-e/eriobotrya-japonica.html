Eriobotrya japonica

Eriobotrya japonica, commonly known as the loquat, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It produces showy, fragrant white or yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by yellow or orange berries in late summer. The fruit has a sweet, tangy flavor and is often eaten fresh or used in jams and pastes. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and culinary uses in horticulture.
