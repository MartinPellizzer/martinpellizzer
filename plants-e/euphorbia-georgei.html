Euphorbia georgei

Euphorbia georgei, commonly known as the George's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branching stem system with a basal rosette of fleshy, greenish-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its characteristic flower-like structures. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Euphorbia georgei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
