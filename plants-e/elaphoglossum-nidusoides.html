Elaphoglossum nidusoides

Elaphoglossum nidusoides, commonly known as the nest-like holly fern, is a delicate fern species native to the humid forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, pinnately arranged fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often forming dense colonies on forest floors or rocky substrates. Its spores are produced in small, clustered sori located on the underside of the fronds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitats.
