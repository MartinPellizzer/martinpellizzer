Eugenia capillipes

Eugenia capillipes, commonly known as the small-leaved clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hair-covered stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, dark red berries. Its aromatic leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Eugenia capillipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitats.
