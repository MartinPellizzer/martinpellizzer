Eugenia dyeriana

Eugenia dyeriana, commonly known as the Dyer's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
