Eugenia fischeri

Eugenia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to reddish when mature.

Eugenia fischeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
