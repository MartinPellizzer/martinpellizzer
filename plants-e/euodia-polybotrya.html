Euodia polybotrya

Euodia polybotrya, commonly known as the fragrant rosewood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-colored wood and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its valuable timber and ornamental qualities, it is often cultivated in horticulture and traditional medicine.
