Echinocactus trifurcatus

Echinocactus trifurcatus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem with distinctive spines and a crown of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and landscaping purposes.
