Eriocaulon laxifolium

Eriocaulon laxifolium, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Eriocaulon laxifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
