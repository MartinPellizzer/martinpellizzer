Euonymus hamiltonianus f. lanceolatus

Euonymus hamiltonianus f. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceolate euonymus, is a variant of the euonymus plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves.

This evergreen shrub is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter.

The plant is valued for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
