Echinochloa crus-galli f. mutica

Echinochloa crus-galli f. mutica, commonly known as slender barnyardgrass, is a variant of the common barnyardgrass species.

It is a grassy weed characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Its botanical features make it a competitive weed in various cropping systems.
