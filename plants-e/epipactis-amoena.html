Epipactis amoena

Epipactis amoena, commonly known as the pretty helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that is often adorned with purple or brown markings. Epipactis amoena is a perennial species that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
