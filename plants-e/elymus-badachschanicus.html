Elymus badachschanicus

Elymus badachschanicus, commonly known as the Badakshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions and alpine meadows. The plant features tall, slender stalks with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Elymus badachschanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
