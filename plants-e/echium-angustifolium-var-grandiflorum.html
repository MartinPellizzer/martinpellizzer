Echium angustifolium var. grandiflorum

Echium angustifolium var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the Great Plains blazing star, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It features upright, branched stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, adorned with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are generally hairy. The plant produces showy, spiky flower clusters in shades of purple to pink, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
