Echeandia ramosissima

Echeandia ramosissima, commonly known as the silverleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It has a taproot system and produces a capsule-like fruit that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Echeandia ramosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
