Eupatorium nudosum

Eupatorium nudosum, commonly known as rough skeletonflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, branched stems. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Eupatorium nudosum is often found in open grasslands and along disturbed soils, showing adaptability to arid conditions.
