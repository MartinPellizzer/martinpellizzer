Ephedra nebrodensis var. scoparia

Ephedra nebrodensis var. scoparia, commonly known as the Armenian ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the Eastern Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its branched, woody stems that are often covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperature fluctuations.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been the subject of various botanical and pharmacological studies.
