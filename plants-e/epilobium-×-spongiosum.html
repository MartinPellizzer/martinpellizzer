Epilobium × spongiosum

Epilobium × spongiosum, commonly known as the woolly willow or spongy willow, is a hybrid plant native to the western United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in dense, woolly hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in wet meadows. Its flowers are small and pinkish-purple, arranged in clusters, and it produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Epilobium × spongiosum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
