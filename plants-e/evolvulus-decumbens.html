Evolvulus decumbens

Evolvulus decumbens, commonly known as the creeping blueweed, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Americas.

It features prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Evolvulus decumbens is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
