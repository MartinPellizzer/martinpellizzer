Echinocactus hystrix

Echinocactus hystrix, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant features prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Echinocactus hystrix is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
