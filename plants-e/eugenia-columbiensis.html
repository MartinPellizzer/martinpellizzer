Eugenia columbiensis

Eugenia columbiensis, commonly known as the Columbia guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and cloves. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, a small, yellowish berry, is edible and has a sweet, aromatic flavor.

Eugenia columbiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its use in traditional medicine.
