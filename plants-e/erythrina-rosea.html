Erythrina rosea

Erythrina rosea, commonly known as the coral tree, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its vibrant red flowers and feathery foliage.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features a tall, slender trunk with smooth, gray bark and produces clusters of showy, flame-colored flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces flat, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

Erythrina rosea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
