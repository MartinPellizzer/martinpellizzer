Erica aristata var. minor

Erica aristata var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It typically reaches a height of around 10-20 centimeters and features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in heathland and moorland habitats, where it contributes to the characteristic landscape of these regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
