Eugenia acutiloba

Eugenia acutiloba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when mature, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Eugenia acutiloba is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its foliage and flowers are also utilized in the fragrance and flavor industries.
