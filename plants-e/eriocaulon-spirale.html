Eriocaulon spirale

Eriocaulon spirale, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet, muddy habitats such as marshes and swamps. Its unique spiral arrangement of leaves gives it a distinctive appearance.

Eriocaulon spirale is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to moist environments.
