Eruca perennis

Eruca perennis, commonly known as wild rocket, is an annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to that of arugula.

Eruca perennis is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in gardens, where it can become invasive in some regions.
