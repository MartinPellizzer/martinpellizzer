Elatostema auriculatum var. auriculatum

Elatostema auriculatum var. auriculatum, commonly known as the ear-shaped elatostema, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its rosette-like growth habit and oval, hairy leaves that resemble ears. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
