Erucastrum leucanthum var. luteum

Erucastrum leucanthum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and unique root system that includes bladder-like structures. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are often submerged, and it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates.

The yellow bladderwort is also noted for its ability to absorb nutrients from its surroundings, contributing to the ecological balance of its environment.
