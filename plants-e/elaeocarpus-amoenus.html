Elaeocarpus amoenus

Elaeocarpus amoenus, commonly known as the sweetgum or white sand elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that enclose several seeds.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
