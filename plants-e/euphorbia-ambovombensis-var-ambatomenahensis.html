Euphorbia ambovombensis var. ambatomenahensis

Euphorbia ambovombensis var. ambatomenahensis, commonly known as the Ambatomena euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, making it well adapted to drought. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

This variety is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often unique morphological features.
