Erodium cicutarium var. chaerophyllum

Erodium cicutarium var. chaerophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are usually lance-shaped. Its flowers are pink to purple, with five petals, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is often found in moist habitats and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
