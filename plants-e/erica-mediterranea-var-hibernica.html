Erica mediterranea var. hibernica

Erica mediterranea var. hibernica, commonly known as the Irish heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western coast of Ireland.

It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in heathland and coastal environments. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during the spring and early summer. Its dense foliage provides habitat for various insects and birds.

Erica mediterranea var. hibernica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
