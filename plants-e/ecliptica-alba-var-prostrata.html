Ecliptica alba var. prostrata

Ecliptica alba var. prostrata, commonly known as white eclipta or prostrate eclipta, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a sprawling, low-growing habit with prostrate stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
