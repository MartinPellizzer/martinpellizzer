Echinophora anatolica

Echinophora anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive spiny texture, while the flowers are yellow and arranged in a compact head.

Echinophora anatolica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
