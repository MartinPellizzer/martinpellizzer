Eritrichium radicans

Eritrichium radicans, commonly known as the creeping erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
