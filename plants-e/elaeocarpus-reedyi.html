Elaeocarpus reedyi

Elaeocarpus reedyi, commonly known as the New Zealand hinau, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native birds.

It thrives in a range of habitats, including forests and shrublands, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
