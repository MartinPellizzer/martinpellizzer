Euphorbia graciliramulosa

Euphorbia graciliramulosa, commonly known as the slender-branched spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, upright growth habit with smooth, green stems that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, typical of the Euphorbia genus. Its leaves are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

It is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
