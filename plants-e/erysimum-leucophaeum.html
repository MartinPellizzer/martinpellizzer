Erysimum leucophaeum

Erysimum leucophaeum, commonly known as the white-flowered wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in grayish-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of silky hair, contributing to its ornamental value.

Erysimum leucophaeum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
