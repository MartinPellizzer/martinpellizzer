Escobedia reticulata

Escobedia reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate escobedia, is a unique liverwort belonging to the family Pterygopariaceae.

It is characterized by its intricate, network-like thallus structure, which gives it its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, known as gametophores, are specialized for producing reproductive cells.

Escobedia reticulata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling.
