Eryngium radiatum

Eryngium radiatum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has spiny, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eryngium radiatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
