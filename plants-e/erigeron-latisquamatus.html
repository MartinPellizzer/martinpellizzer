Erigeron latisquamatus

Erigeron latisquamatus, commonly known as the broad-scale fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy structures, while its flowers are white to pale blue in color.

Erigeron latisquamatus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
