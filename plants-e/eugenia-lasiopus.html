Eugenia lasiopus

Eugenia lasiopus, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, raised dots, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
