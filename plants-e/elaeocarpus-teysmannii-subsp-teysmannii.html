Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp. teysmannii

Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp.

teysmannii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Indonesia and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a woody, spherical capsule that typically splits open to release seeds.

This species is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological roles.
