Elaeocarpus luteolus

Elaeocarpus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or yellow in color, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It is often found in lowland and hill forest areas, thriving in humid, warm climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
