Erodium maritimum subvar. apetalum

Erodium maritimum subvar. apetalum, commonly known as the coastal false flax, is a low-growing annual plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are often reduced or absent, giving it the subvariety name "apetalum.". The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, rounded fruits known as utricles. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy, resilient species found in maritime environments.
