Eugenia gracilipes

Eugenia gracilipes, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. Eugenia gracilipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and medicinal properties.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to subtropical and tropical landscapes.
