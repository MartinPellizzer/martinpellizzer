Erigeron foliosus var. mendocinus

Erigeron foliosus var. mendocinus, commonly known as the Mendocino blueweed, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a grayish-blue hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

The flowers are small, white to pale blue, and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
