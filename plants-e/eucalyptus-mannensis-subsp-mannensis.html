Eucalyptus mannensis subsp. mannensis

Eucalyptus mannensis subsp.

mannensis, commonly known as the Manne's mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to western Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance.

The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters and attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
