Euonymus oxyphyllus f. magnus

Euonymus oxyphyllus f. magnus, commonly known as the giant euonymus, is a compact evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, upright growth habit.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and prominent veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedging plant.

Due to its slow growth and hardy nature, it is suitable for a variety of garden conditions.
