Elymus schugnanicus

Elymus schugnanicus, commonly known as the Schugnan wild rye, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are often tinged with brown or purple hues. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to cold and arid conditions.

Elymus schugnanicus plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native environment and is of interest to botanists and ecologists.
