Eugenia kabaensis

Eugenia kabaensis, commonly known as the Kaba bush, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to western Africa, particularly found in countries such as Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a fragrant aroma.

The small white or pink flowers are followed by edible berries that are used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
