Elymus × saxicola

Elymus × saxicola, commonly known as the sandberg bluegrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

Elymus × saxicola is also notable for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
