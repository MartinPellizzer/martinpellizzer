Eupatorium longepetiolatum

Eupatorium longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with long petioles, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
