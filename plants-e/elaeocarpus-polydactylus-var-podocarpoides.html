Elaeocarpus polydactylus var. podocarpoides

Elaeocarpus polydactylus var. podocarpoides, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarp, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
