Epidendrum riobambae

Epidendrum riobambae, commonly known as the Rio Bambae Vanda, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that often display vibrant colors such as yellow and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat, and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a branched inflorescence that supports its numerous, fragrant blooms.
