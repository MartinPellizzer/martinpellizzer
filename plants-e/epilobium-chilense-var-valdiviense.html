Epilobium chilense var. valdiviense

Epilobium chilense var. valdiviense, commonly known as the Valdivian willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate rainforests of southern Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found along riverbanks and in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
