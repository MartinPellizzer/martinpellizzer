Eucalyptus megacornuta

Eucalyptus megacornuta, commonly known as the broad-fruited mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus megacornuta plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
