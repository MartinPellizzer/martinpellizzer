Eucalyptus denticulata

Eucalyptus denticulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and smooth, greyish bark. This plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in challenging soils.
