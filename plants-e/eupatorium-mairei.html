Eupatorium mairei

Eupatorium mairei, commonly known as Maire's eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in forest margins and damp habitats, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium mairei is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in supporting local biodiversity.
