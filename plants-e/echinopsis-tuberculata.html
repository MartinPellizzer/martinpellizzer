Echinopsis tuberculata

Echinopsis tuberculata, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its swollen, globular stem that is often covered in small, white spines. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and indoor plant collections.
