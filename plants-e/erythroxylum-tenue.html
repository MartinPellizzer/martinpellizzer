Erythroxylum tenue

Erythroxylum tenue, commonly known as the narrowleaf copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been historically used for its latex and has ecological significance as a food source for various insects and birds.
