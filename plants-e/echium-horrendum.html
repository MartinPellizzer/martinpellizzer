Echium horrendum

Echium horrendum, commonly known as the giant hedgenut, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Echium horrendum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in its native habitat.
