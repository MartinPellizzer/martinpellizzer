Eriobotrya poilanei

Eriobotrya poilanei, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces fragrant white flowers in early spring, which are followed by yellow, edible fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
