Elsholtzia eriocalyx var. tomentosa

Elsholtzia eriocalyx var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are densely covered with a fine, woolly pubescence. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its horticultural value lies in its ornamental foliage and ability to attract pollinators.
