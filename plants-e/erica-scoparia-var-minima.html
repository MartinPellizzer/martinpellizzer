Erica scoparia var. minima

Erica scoparia var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, powdery bloom. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are usually pink or white and are borne in tight clusters.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.
