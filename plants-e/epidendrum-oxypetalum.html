Epidendrum oxypetalum

Epidendrum oxypetalum, commonly known as the red-tipped epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a distinctive red or purple tip. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Epidendrum oxypetalum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
