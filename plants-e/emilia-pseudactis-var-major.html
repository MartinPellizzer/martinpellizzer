Emilia pseudactis var. major

Emilia pseudactis var. major, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray flowers and dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy, dandelion-like blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Emilia pseudactis var. major is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
