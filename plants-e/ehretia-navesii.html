Ehretia navesii

Ehretia navesii, commonly known as the Mexican whitebeard tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy berries. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
