Eremochloa muricata

Eremochloa muricata, commonly known as the hairy panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, where it thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle with spikelets that are often covered in short, bristly hairs.

Eremochloa muricata is adapted to dry conditions and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
