Eugenia incanescens

Eugenia incanescens, commonly known as the white eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its smooth, often white or greyish bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, spherical berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and is often found in open forests and woodlands.

Eugenia incanescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
