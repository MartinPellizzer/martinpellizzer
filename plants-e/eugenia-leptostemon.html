Eugenia leptostemon

Eugenia leptostemon, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruits are typically small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia leptostemon is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
