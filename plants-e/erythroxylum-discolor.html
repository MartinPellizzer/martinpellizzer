Erythroxylum discolor

Erythroxylum discolor, commonly known as the pink mallow, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows as an erect herb with palmately lobed leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
