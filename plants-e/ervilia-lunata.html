Ervilia lunata

Ervilia lunata, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a climbing plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, white, nocturnal flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its unique flowering pattern makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
