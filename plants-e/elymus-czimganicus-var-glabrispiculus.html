Elymus czimganicus var. glabrispiculus

Elymus czimganicus var. glabrispiculus, commonly known as the smooth-spike wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are densely packed and covered with short, smooth awns. This variety is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
