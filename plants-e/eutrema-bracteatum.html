Eutrema bracteatum

Eutrema bracteatum, commonly known as the coastal sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the United States.

It is characterized by its blue or purple flowers and is often found in sandy or saline soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Eutrema bracteatum is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
