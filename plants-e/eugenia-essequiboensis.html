Eugenia essequiboensis

Eugenia essequiboensis, commonly known as the Essequibo eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of South America, particularly Venezuela and Guyana. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It is typically found in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests.

The fruit, which is edible, is often consumed by local wildlife and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
