Erica Banksia Subsp. Comptonii

Erica banksia subsp. comptonii is a rare and distinctive plant native to the southern regions of Western Australia. It belongs to the heath family and is known for its unique floral structure and hardy nature. This subspecies thrives in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its delicate flowers, which bloom in spring, attract a variety of pollinators. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this species from habitat loss and environmental threats.
