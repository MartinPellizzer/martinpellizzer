Erica purpurascens

Erica purpurascens, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many heath and heather plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
