Epipactis flava

Epipactis flava, commonly known as the yellow helleborine, is a species of orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green flowers and is typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread vegetatively. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and the flowers are arranged in a dense spike.

Epipactis flava is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
