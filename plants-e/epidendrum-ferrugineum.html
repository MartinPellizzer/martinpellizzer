Epidendrum ferrugineum

Epidendrum ferrugineum, commonly known as the rusty-leaved epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that often have a reddish or rusty-colored tint on the underside. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent red sepals and petals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Epidendrum ferrugineum is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
