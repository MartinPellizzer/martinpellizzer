Echeveria rigidiflora

Echeveria rigidiflora, commonly known as the rigid-flowered echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces compact, upright flower stalks that bear clusters of pale pink to violet flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Echeveria rigidiflora is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its attractive appearance and ease of care.
