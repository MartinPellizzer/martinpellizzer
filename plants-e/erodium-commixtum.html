Erodium commixtum

Erodium commixtum, commonly known as common bedstraw, is an annual flowering plant belonging to the family Geraniaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and is commonly found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Erodium commixtum is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
