Eupatorium ageratifolium var. mexicanum

Eupatorium ageratifolium var. mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican boneset, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
