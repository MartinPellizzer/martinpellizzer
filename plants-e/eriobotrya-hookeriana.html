Eriobotrya hookeriana

Eriobotrya hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's loquat, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape with serrated margins. The tree produces showy, white or pinkish flowers in clusters, followed by yellow-orange berries that are edible. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.
