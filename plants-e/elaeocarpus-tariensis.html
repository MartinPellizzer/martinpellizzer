Elaeocarpus tariensis

Elaeocarpus tariensis, commonly known as the Tariensis elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental potential and ecological role in its native habitats.
