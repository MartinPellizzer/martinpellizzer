Epilobium obcordatum var. puberulum

Epilobium obcordatum var. puberulum, commonly known as the woolly willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often shady environments such as stream banks and forest edges. The plant features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually pink to red, with five petals, and are arranged in clusters along the stems.

It is known for its ability to thrive in wet soils and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
