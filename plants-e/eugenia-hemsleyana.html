Eugenia hemsleyana

Eugenia hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's evergreen eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy evergreen leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature.

Eugenia hemsleyana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
