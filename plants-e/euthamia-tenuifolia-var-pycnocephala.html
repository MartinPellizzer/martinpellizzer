Euthamia tenuifolia var. pycnocephala

Euthamia tenuifolia var. pycnocephala, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear and covered with fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
