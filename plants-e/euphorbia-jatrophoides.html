Euphorbia jatrophoides

Euphorbia jatrophoides, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its branching structure and small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among species in the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Euphorbia jatrophoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
