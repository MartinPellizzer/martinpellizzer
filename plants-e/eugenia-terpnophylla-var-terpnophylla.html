Eugenia terpnophylla var. terpnophylla

Eugenia terpnophylla var. terpnophylla, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that is edible and often used in local cuisines.
