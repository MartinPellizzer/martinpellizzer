Epiphyllum laui

Epiphyllum laui, commonly known as the moon cactus or night bloomer, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing or trailing cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant features flat, fleshy stems that are often segmented and covered in spines. Its flowers are typically large, showy, and open at night, producing a variety of colors including white, pink, and red.

Epiphyllum laui thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
