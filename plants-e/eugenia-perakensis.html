Eugenia perakensis

Eugenia perakensis, commonly known as the Java apple or Java orange, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Eugenia perakensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.
