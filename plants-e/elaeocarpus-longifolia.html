Elaeocarpus longifolia

Elaeocarpus longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round berries that are typically orange or red when mature. These berries contain a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.

Elaeocarpus longifolia is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological importance.
