Eriosyce curvispina var. robusta

Eriosyce curvispina var. robusta, commonly known as the robusta eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, providing both protection and water conservation.

This variety is notable for its robust growth compared to other members of the Eriosyce genus, making it a hardy and visually distinctive plant.
