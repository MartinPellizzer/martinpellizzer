Euphorbia blodgettii

Euphorbia blodgettii, commonly known as Blodgett's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a thick, waxy cuticle that helps it retain water in arid environments. It produces a milky sap that can cause skin irritation, a common trait among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Blodgett's spurge is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.
