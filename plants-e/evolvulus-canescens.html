Evolvulus canescens

Evolvulus canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
