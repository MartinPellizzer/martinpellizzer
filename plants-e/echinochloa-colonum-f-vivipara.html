Echinochloa colonum f. vivipara

Echinochloa colonum f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous barnyard grass, is a variant of the annual grass species Echinochloa colonum.

It is characterized by its viviparous reproduction, where the seeds germinate while still attached to the parent plant. This unique trait allows the plant to propagate rapidly under favorable conditions. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and wetlands.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.
