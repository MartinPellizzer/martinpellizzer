Eugenia rubra

Eugenia rubra, commonly known as the red guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible red or yellow berries. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown as a hedge or in ornamental gardens.
