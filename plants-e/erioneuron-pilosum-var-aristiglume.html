Erioneuron pilosum var. aristiglume

Erioneuron pilosum var. aristiglume, commonly known as the silky hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silky hairs covering the leaves and inflorescence, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to brownish flowers.

The variety *aristiglume* is distinguished by the presence of a bristle-like appendage at the base of each spikelet, adding to its botanical uniqueness.
