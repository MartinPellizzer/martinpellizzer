Euphorbia atrocarmesina subsp. atrocarmesina

Euphorbia atrocarmesina subsp.

atrocarmesina, commonly known as the bloodroot euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purplish tinge.

The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures containing small yellow flowers. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
