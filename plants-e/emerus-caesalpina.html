Emerus caesalpina

Emerus caesalpina, commonly known as the Brazilian pea or the red peacock flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with trifoliate arrangements and produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with a papilionaceous structure. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking floral display.
