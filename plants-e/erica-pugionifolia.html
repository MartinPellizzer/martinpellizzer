Erica pugionifolia

Erica pugionifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica pugionifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a rock garden plant.
