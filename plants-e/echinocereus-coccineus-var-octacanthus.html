Echinocereus coccineus var. octacanthus

Echinocereus coccineus var. octacanthus, commonly known as the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive red coloration on its stems and spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid gardens.
