Epidendrum montis-narae

Epidendrum montis-narae, commonly known as the Nara Mountain Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are broad and dark green, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture in its arid habitat.

Epidendrum montis-narae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
