Elodea bifoliata

Elodea bifoliata, commonly known as the two-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by small, elongated fruits. Elodea bifoliata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.

It is often used in water gardens and as a bioindicator of water quality.
