Echeveria candelabrum

Echeveria candelabrum, commonly known as the candleabra echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features a rosette-shaped stem with fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear clusters of violet or pink flowers. It is well-suited for rock gardens and containers due to its compact growth habit.

Echeveria candelabrum requires bright sunlight, well-draining soil, and minimal water to thrive.
