Eucalyptus leucoxylon subsp. bellarinensis

Eucalyptus leucoxylon subsp.

bellarinensis, commonly known as the Bellarine eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of white and grey.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or cream in color.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a resilient plant in coastal environments.
