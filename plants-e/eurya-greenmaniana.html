Eurya greenmaniana

Eurya greenmaniana, commonly known as the greenman's eurya, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are dark blue to black in color. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge.

Eurya greenmaniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils under partial shade.
