Elephantopus angustifolius

Elephantopus angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
