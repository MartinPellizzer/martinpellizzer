Eucalyptus × crawfordii

Eucalyptus × crawfordii, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Stringy Gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, stringy branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Eucalyptus × crawfordii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
