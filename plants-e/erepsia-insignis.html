Erepsia insignis

Erepsia insignis, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from underground water sources.

Erepsia insignis plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing soil in sandy areas.
