Eucalyptus pauciflora var. densiflora

Eucalyptus pauciflora var. densiflora, commonly known as the snow gum, is a hardy evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, which bloom in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude regions.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often used in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience.
