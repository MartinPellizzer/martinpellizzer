Erigeron eatonii var. plantagineus

Erigeron eatonii var. plantagineus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and it is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
