Euphorbia syncalycina

Euphorbia syncalycina, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, which is characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. Its foliage is often covered in a waxy coating, providing additional protection against moisture loss.
