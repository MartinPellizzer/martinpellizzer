Embothrium sericeum var. angustifolium

Embothrium sericeum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved silver tree, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, finely hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, often in shades of white or pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
