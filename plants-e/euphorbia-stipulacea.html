Euphorbia stipulacea

Euphorbia stipulacea, commonly known as the Cape coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branching structure with smooth, pale green stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Euphorbia stipulacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
