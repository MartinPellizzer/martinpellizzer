Eriospermum brevipetiolatum

Eriospermum brevipetiolatum, commonly known as the short-petioled eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of fleshy, narrow leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its succulent leaves help it store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
