Espeletia × jajoensis

Espeletia × jajoensis, commonly known as the Jajo hybrid, is a polyploid plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming structure with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces striking inflorescences with showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and moderate rainfall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
