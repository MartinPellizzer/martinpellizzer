Epilobium alpinum subsp. nutans

Epilobium alpinum subsp.

nutans, commonly known as alpine willow herb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are usually glandular and hairy, and it has opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high altitude conditions.
