Eupatorium cyrili-nelsonii

Eupatorium cyrili-nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
