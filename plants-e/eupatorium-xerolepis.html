Eupatorium xerolepis

Eupatorium xerolepis, commonly known as the sandplain eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, sandy soils, often found in coastal areas and dunes. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium xerolepis is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
