Epidendrum organense

Epidendrum organense, commonly known as the Organ Mountains orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the islands of Cuba and Hispaniola. This epiphytic plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinctive red or purple lip, and it blooms during the rainy season.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
