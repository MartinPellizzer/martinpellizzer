Epacris pulchella var. montana

Epacris pulchella var. montana, commonly known as the mountain epacris, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of pink or purple flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or open habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
