Eriogonum bicolor

Eriogonum bicolor, commonly known as the twinflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has basal leaves that are often hairy and toothed, with stems that are usually unbranched. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

Eriogonum bicolor plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
