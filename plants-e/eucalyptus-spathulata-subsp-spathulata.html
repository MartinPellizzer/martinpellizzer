Eucalyptus spathulata subsp. spathulata

Eucalyptus spathulata subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and open woodlands, often in sandy or well-drained soils. It is a shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

This subspecies is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its use in traditional and modern medicinal applications.
