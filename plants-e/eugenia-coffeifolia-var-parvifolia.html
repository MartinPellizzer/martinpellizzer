Eugenia coffeifolia var. parvifolia

Eugenia coffeifolia var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved coffee bush, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The species is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-drained soils.
