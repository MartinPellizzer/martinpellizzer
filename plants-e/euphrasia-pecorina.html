Euphrasia pecorina

Euphrasia pecorina, commonly known as the sheep's eye or the red-eyed grass, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Scrophulariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically feature two red spots on the lower lip. Euphrasia pecorina is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties, though its use is limited due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements.
