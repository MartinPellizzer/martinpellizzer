Ephedra nebrodensis subsp. procera

Ephedra nebrodensis subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its green stems that contain the alkaloid ephedrine, which has been used in traditional medicine.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its structure consists of branching, woody stems with small, scale-like leaves, and it produces cones that contain the seeds.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically valued for its potential therapeutic uses.
