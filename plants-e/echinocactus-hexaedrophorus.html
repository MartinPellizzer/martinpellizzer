Echinocactus hexaedrophorus

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a slow-growing cactus that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 inches. The plant features a ribbed, cylindrical stem with prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to drought conditions.

The plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
