Ecliptica alba var. parviflora

Ecliptica alba var. parviflora, commonly known as white eclipta, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its small white to pale yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
