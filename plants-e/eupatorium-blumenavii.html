Eupatorium blumenavii

Eupatorium blumenavii, commonly known as the Blue Mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows up to 3 feet tall, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium blumenavii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
