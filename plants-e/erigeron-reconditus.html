Erigeron reconditus

Erigeron reconditus, commonly known as the hidden fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.
