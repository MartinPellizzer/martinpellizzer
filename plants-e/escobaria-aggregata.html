Escobaria aggregata

Escobaria aggregata, commonly known as the cluster jointfir, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, low-growing clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically borne on short stems above the foliage. Escobaria aggregata is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems to survive in dry conditions.

It is often found in rocky soils and is a popular plant among succulent enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and hardiness.
