Eugenia tutuilensis

Eugenia tutuilensis, commonly known as the Tutuila guava, is a species native to the island of Tutuila in American Samoa.

It is a small evergreen tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
