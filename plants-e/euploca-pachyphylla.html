Euploca pachyphylla

Euploca pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved euploca, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It prefers well-draining soil and full sun exposure to ensure optimal growth.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
