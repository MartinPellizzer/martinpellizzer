Epidendrum oncidioides var. ramonense

Epidendrum oncidioides var. ramonense, commonly known as the Ramon Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its epiphytic growth habit, typically found on trees in humid, tropical environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange, often in clusters. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
