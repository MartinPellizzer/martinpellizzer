Eriogonum jamesii subsp. undulatum

Eriogonum jamesii subsp.

undulatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
