Eryngium neei

Eryngium neei, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features spiny, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eryngium neei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
