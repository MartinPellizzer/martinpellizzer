Euphorbia gerardiana proles loiseleurii

Euphorbia gerardiana proles loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleur's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, a common feature among members of its family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
