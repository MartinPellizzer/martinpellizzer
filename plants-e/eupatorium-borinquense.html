Eupatorium borinquense

Eupatorium borinquense, commonly known as the Borinquen thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense heads.

Eupatorium borinquense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
