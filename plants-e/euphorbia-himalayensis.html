Euphorbia himalayensis

Euphorbia himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Himalayan regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse forms.

Euphorbia himalayensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
