Euplassa organensis subsp. organensis

Euplassa organensis subsp.

organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains euplassa, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche.
