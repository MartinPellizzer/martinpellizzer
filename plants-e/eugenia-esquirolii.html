Eugenia esquirolii

Eugenia esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and produces edible, aromatic fruits similar to guavas. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

Eugenia esquirolii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in warm climates.
