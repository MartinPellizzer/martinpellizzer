Eulophia stachyodes

Eulophia stachyodes, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiar leaf structure, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often folded or rolled, a feature that helps reduce water loss in its humid habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
