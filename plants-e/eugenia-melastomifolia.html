Eugenia melastomifolia

Eugenia melastomifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, making it an important component of ecological gardens.
