Eryngium bourgatii

Eryngium bourgatii, commonly known as the blue thistle or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple spiky flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, cylindrical flower heads that are surrounded by bracts resembling leaves. Eryngium bourgatii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in coastal areas.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
