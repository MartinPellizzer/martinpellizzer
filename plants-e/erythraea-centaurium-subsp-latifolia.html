Erythraea centaurium subsp. latifolia

Erythraea centaurium subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
