Eucalyptus wimmerensis subsp. parviformis

Eucalyptus wimmerensis subsp.

parviformis, commonly known as the Wimmera mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often grey or white bark and lance-shaped leaves that are usually narrow and sometimes slightly hairy.

The flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and are produced in summer. The plant is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
