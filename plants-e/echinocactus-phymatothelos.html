Echinocactus phymatothelos

Echinocactus phymatothelos, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collections.
