Elaphoglossum praelongum

Elaphoglossum praelongum, commonly known as the long-stalked holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lanceolate leaves with a finely divided, feathery appearance. The fronds are typically bi- to tri-pinnate, with a slender, elongated rachis. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
