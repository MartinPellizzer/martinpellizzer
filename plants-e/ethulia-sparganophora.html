Ethulia sparganophora

Ethulia sparganophora, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Ethulia sparganophora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
