Escallonia rahmeri

Escallonia rahmeri, commonly known as the Scarlet Banksia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and vibrant, showy red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces cylindrical, woody cones that contain numerous small, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Escallonia rahmeri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract birds and insects.
