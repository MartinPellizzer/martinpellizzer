Erythrina atrosanguinea

Erythrina atrosanguinea, commonly known as the black blood coral tree, is a striking leguminous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant produces long, flat pods that contain several hard, angular seeds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets.

Erythrina atrosanguinea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
