Erophila verna var. vertesensis

Erophila verna var. vertesensis, commonly known as the Hungarian spring gentian, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
