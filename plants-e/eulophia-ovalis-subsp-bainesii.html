Eulophia ovalis subsp. bainesii

Eulophia ovalis subsp.

bainesii, commonly known as the Baines' oval eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania.

The plant is characterized by its ovate, leathery leaves and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral structure and specific habitat preferences.
