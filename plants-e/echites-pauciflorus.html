Echites pauciflorus

Echites pauciflorus, commonly known as the spiny pincushion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant is known for its distinctive, spiny stems and leaves, which provide protection against herbivores. Its small, yellowish flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echites pauciflorus is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
