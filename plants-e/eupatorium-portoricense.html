Eupatorium portoricense

Eupatorium portoricense, commonly known as the Cuban thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny environments such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium portoricense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
