Erigeron thompsonii

Erigeron thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often found in areas with poor soil and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
