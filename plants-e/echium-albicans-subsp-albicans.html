Echium albicans subsp. albicans

Echium albicans subsp.

albicans, commonly known as the white thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking white or pale blue flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically significant in its native habitat.
