Euonymus alatus f. striatus

Euonymus alatus f. striatus, commonly known as the winged euonymus or the burning bush, is a cultivar of the Euonymus alatus species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, winged stems that are covered in small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its foliage changes color with the seasons, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking seasonal visual interest.
