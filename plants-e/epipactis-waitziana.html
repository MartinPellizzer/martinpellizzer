Epipactis waitziana

Epipactis waitziana, commonly known as the Waitz's helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a greenish-yellow lip and a distinctive, spurred column. It is a perennial herb that thrives in nutrient-rich soils and is often found in association with other orchid species.

Epipactis waitziana is notable for its ability to hybridize with other orchid species, contributing to its genetic diversity.
