Evota bicolor

Evota bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored evota, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking bi-colored flowers, which typically display a combination of white and purple hues. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
