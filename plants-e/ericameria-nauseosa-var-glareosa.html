Ericameria nauseosa var. glareosa

Ericameria nauseosa var. glareosa, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
