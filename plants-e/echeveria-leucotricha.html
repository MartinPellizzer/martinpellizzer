Echeveria leucotricha

Echeveria leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-gray in color with white or silver markings. The plant produces clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
