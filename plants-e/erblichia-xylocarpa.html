Erblichia xylocarpa

Erblichia xylocarpa, commonly known as the tropical fruit tree, is a species native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces clusters of yellow-orange fruits with a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

The tree also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
