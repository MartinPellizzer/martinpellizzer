Elymus dahuricus var. excelsus

Elymus dahuricus var. excelsus, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, blue-green foliage. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and forage.
