Echinocactus stenogonus

Echinocactus stenogonus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and spiny, segmented structure, which provides protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, spiny body. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xerophytic gardens.
