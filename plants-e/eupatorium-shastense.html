Eupatorium shastense

Eupatorium shastense, commonly known as Shasta false fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers range in color from white to pale blue.

Eupatorium shastense is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
