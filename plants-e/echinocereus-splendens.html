Echinocereus splendens

Echinocereus splendens, commonly known as the golden hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, spiny cactus that typically grows to a height of about 10 to 20 inches. The plant features thick, ribbed stems that are covered in bright yellow to orange spines. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are typically pink or red and are clustered at the ends of the stems.

Echinocereus splendens is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
