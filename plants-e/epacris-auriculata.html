Epacris auriculata

Epacris auriculata, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained acidic soils. Its flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Epacris auriculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
