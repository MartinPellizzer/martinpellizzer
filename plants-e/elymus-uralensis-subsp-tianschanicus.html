Elymus uralensis subsp. tianschanicus

Elymus uralensis subsp.

tianschanicus, commonly known as the Tianshan needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.

This subspecies plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
