Erica madida

Erica madida, commonly known as the Madida heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica madida thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
