Eugenia pachnantha

Eugenia pachnantha, commonly known as the broad-leaved myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
