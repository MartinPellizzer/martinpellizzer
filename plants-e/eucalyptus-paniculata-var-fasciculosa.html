Eucalyptus paniculata var. fasciculosa

Eucalyptus paniculata var. fasciculosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. It typically grows in open woodlands and heathlands, preferring well-drained soils.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to dry conditions and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
