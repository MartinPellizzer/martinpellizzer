Echinocactus wagnerianus

Echinocactus wagnerianus, commonly known as the Wagner's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and spiny, ribbed stems that provide structural support and protection. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and collectors.
