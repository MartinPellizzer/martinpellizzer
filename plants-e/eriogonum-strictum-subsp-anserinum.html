Eriogonum strictum subsp. anserinum

Eriogonum strictum subsp.

anserinum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with slender, upright stems and narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil, making it valuable in ecological restoration projects.
