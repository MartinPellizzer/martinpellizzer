Echinopsis eyriesii var. pudantii

Echinopsis eyriesii var. pudantii, commonly known as the Pudantii moon cactus, is a hybrid cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant belongs to the Cactaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and collections. It requires well-draining soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is typically propagated through grafting rather than seeds.
