Eupatorium miquihuanum

Eupatorium miquihuanum, commonly known as the Miquihuanum boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas and is known for its aromatic leaves and showy white to pale purple flowers. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Its botanical structure includes opposite leaves and a branching stem, making it a distinctive member of the Eupatorium genus.
