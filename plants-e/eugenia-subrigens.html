Eugenia subrigens

Eugenia subrigens, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its rough, textured leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
