Euodia nitida

Euodia nitida, commonly known as the Chinese laurier cherry, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Euodia nitida is valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a unique and versatile species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
