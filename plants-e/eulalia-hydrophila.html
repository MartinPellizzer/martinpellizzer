Eulalia hydrophila

Eulalia hydrophila, commonly known as the water reed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage. This plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Eulalia hydrophila plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
