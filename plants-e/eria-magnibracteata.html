Eria magnibracteata

Eria magnibracteata, commonly known as the large bract eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, with linear to lanceolate leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of large, brightly colored bracts that enclose the actual flowers.

Eria magnibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
