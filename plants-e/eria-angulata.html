Eria angulata

Eria angulata, commonly known as the angular eria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often arranged in a raceme. Its leaves are narrow and angular, giving it its common name.

Eria angulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden environments.
