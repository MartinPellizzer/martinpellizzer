Endospermum quadriloculare

Endospermum quadriloculare, commonly known as the four-chambered endosperm, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its use in traditional Indigenous practices.
