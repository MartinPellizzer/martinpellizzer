Evolvulus macilentus

Evolvulus macilentus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, funnel-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and has a rapid growth rate, making it suitable for ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in ornamental gardening.

Its seeds are dispersed by birds and can germinate quickly under favorable conditions.
