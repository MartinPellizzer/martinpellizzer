Eupatorium arbutifolium var. sublaeve

Eupatorium arbutifolium var. sublaeve, commonly known as the smooth-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, lavender to white flower heads. The plant features opposite, elliptic to ovate leaves that are smooth and slightly hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist to wet soils in full sun to partial shade, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
