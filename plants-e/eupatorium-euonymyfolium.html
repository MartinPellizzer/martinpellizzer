Eupatorium euonymyfolium

Eupatorium euonymyfolium, commonly known as the golden anise or Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are aromatic and have a distinctive, mint-like scent, which contributes to its use in traditional medicine.

Eupatorium euonymyfolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
