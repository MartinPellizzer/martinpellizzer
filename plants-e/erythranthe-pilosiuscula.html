Erythranthe pilosiuscula

Erythranthe pilosiuscula, commonly known as the hairy blue-eyed Mary, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers with five petals, resembling the appearance of the related species *Borthwickia* and *Erythranthe*. It thrives in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive and ecologically significant species in its native environment.
