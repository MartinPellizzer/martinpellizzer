Eugenia leonorae

Eugenia leonorae, commonly known as the Leonora's eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia leonorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
