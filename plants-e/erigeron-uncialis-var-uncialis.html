Erigeron uncialis var. uncialis

Erigeron uncialis var. uncialis, commonly known as the blueweed or blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a blue-gray center, blooming from late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and pollinator habitats.
