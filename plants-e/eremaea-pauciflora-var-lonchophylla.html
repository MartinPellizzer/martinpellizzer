Eremaea pauciflora var. lonchophylla

Eremaea pauciflora var. lonchophylla, commonly known as the small-leaf eremaea, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is well adapted to sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

This variety is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.
