Echeveria amadorana

Echeveria amadorana, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-gray to purple in color. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
