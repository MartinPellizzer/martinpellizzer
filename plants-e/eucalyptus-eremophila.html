Eucalyptus eremophila

Eucalyptus eremophila, commonly known as the white mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped, narrow leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in winter and spring. Eremophila is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots and a capacity to survive prolonged droughts.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
