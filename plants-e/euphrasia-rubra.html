Euphrasia rubra

Euphrasia rubra, commonly known as red-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters. The flowers are typically yellow with distinctive red markings around the base of the petals, giving the plant its common name. Euphrasia rubra thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

It is often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
