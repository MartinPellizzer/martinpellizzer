Eriachne filiformis

Eriachne filiformis, commonly known as slender eriochloa, is a grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It is a low-growing perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small fauna.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native grassland ecosystems.
