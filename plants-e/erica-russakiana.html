Erica russakiana

Erica russakiana, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Erica russakiana is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
