Erica cinerea var. coccinea

Erica cinerea var. coccinea, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to deep red, bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging garden conditions.
