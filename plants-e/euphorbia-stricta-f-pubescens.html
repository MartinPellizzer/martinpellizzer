Euphorbia stricta f. pubescens

Euphorbia stricta f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy spurge, is a succulent plant characterized by its upright, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

It originates from South Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it stores water in its fleshy stems.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
