Erodium jacquinianum var. sabulicola

Erodium jacquinianum var. sabulicola, commonly known as the sand erodium, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of California.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some areas.
