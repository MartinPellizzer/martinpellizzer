Empedoclesia brachysiphon

Empedoclesia brachysiphon, commonly known as the narrow-siphon grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant typically has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its ecological role, it is sometimes used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
