Eleiodoxa orthoschista

Eleiodoxa orthoschista, commonly known as the African velvetbean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
