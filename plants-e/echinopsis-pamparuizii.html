Echinopsis pamparuizii

Echinopsis pamparuizii, commonly known as the Pamparuizii cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive rib structure and spines that provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its striking appearance and unique adaptations, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
