Eriosema edule

Eriosema edule, commonly known as the edible eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. The seeds are rich in nutrients and are traditionally used as a food source by indigenous communities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
