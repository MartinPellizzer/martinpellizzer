Eupatorium scariosum

Eupatorium scariosum, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Eupatorium scariosum is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
