Echium velutinum subsp. versicolor

Echium velutinum subsp.

versicolor, commonly known as the velvet bluet, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as meadows and coastal areas. Its leaves are hairy and lance-shaped, contributing to its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
