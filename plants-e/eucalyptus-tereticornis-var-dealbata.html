Eucalyptus tereticornis var. dealbata

Eucalyptus tereticornis var. dealbata, commonly known as the white gum, is a variant of the narrow-leaf eucalyptus.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark that peels in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a greyish-white underside. This species is native to eastern Australia and thrives in a variety of soil types.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
