Erysimum macrostigma

Erysimum macrostigma, commonly known as the broadleaf wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
