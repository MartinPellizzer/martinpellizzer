Echinodorus austroamericanus

Echinodorus austroamericanus, commonly known as the Brazilian sword plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically bright green and have a waxy texture. This plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in marshes and ponds. It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its hardy nature and ability to provide oxygen to the water.

The plant also plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by reducing water turbulence and offering habitat for small aquatic organisms.
