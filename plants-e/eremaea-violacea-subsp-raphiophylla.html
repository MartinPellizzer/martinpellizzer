Eremaea violacea subsp. raphiophylla

Eremaea violacea subsp.

raphiophylla, commonly known as the bluebottle bush, is a shrub native to arid regions of Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive blue to violet flowers that appear in clusters, often giving the plant its common name.

The leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its dry environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a hardy plant in harsh climates.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and resilience.
