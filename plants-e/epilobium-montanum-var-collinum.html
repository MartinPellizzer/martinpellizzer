Epilobium montanum var. collinum

Epilobium montanum var. collinum, commonly known as mountain willow or hill epilobium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. The plant features erect stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
