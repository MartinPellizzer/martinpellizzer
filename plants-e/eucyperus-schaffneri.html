Eucyperus schaffneri

Eucyperus schaffneri, commonly known as the Schaffner's grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to humid conditions. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often arranged in panicles, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eucyperus schaffneri is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration.
