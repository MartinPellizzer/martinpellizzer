Ercilla volubilis

Ercilla volubilis, commonly known as the creeping ercilla, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread over other vegetation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive reddish or purplish hue, especially on the underside.

Ercilla volubilis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks.
