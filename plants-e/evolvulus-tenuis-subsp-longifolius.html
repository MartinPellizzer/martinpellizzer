Evolvulus tenuis subsp. longifolius

Evolvulus tenuis subsp.

longifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, hairy leaves.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
