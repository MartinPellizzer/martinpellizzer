Erodium petraeum subsp. valentinum

Erodium petraeum subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as the rock erodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a low-growing habit.

It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand harsh, arid conditions.
