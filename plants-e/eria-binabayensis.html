Eria binabayensis

Eria binabayensis, commonly known as the Philippine orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the Philippines.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, erect stem and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes and bloom during the rainy season.

Eria binabayensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
