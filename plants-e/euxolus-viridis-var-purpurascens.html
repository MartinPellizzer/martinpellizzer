Euxolus viridis var. purpurascens

Euxolus viridis var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-veined greenbrier, is a variant of the greenbrier plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny shrubs and trifoliate leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its foliage often displays a striking purplish tinge, especially under certain light conditions.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
