Ericoides foliaceum

Ericoides foliaceum, commonly known as the leafy eric, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage, which consists of small, leathery leaves arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late spring or early summer. Ericoides foliaceum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
