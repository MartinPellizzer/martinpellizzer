Etlingera chlorodonta

Etlingera chlorodonta, commonly known as the yellow ginger, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and strap-like, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The rhizomes of this plant are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice.
