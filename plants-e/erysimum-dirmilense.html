Erysimum dirmilense

Erysimum dirmilense, commonly known as the Dirmil's mustard, is a flowering plant native to parts of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat branched.

Erysimum dirmilense is typically found in rocky or arid environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
