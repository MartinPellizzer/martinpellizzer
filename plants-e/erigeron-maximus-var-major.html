Erigeron maximus var. major

Erigeron maximus var. major, commonly known as the Great Blue Lobelia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of sky-blue to lavender flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
