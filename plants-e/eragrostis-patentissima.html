Eragrostis patentissima

Eragrostis patentissima, commonly known as slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed areas, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
