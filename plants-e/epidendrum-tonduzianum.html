Epidendrum tonduzianum

Epidendrum tonduzianum, commonly known as the Tonduzian Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrates.

Epidendrum tonduzianum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
