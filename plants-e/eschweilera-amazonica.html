Eschweilera amazonica

Eschweilera amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian rosewood, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for its strength and beauty. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy surface.

Eschweilera amazonica plays an important ecological role in the rainforest, providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
