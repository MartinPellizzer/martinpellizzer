Eritrichium tinctorium

Eritrichium tinctorium, commonly known as the dyer's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbages. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has hairy, lobed leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Eritrichium tinctorium is also noted for its historical use in dyeing fabrics due to the presence of certain pigments in its leaves.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both ecological and historical studies.
