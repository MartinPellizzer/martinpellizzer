Erigeron palustris

Erigeron palustris, commonly known as marsh fleabane, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along the edges of ponds, marshes, and streams. The plant features slender stems that are often reddish in color and bear clusters of small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Marsh fleabane plays an important role in supporting pollinators and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
