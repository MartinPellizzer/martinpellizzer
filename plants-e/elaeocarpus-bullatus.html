Elaeocarpus bullatus

Elaeocarpus bullatus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its bark is thick and fissured, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
