Erysimum heterotrichum

Erysimum heterotrichum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, often found in coastal or mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and narrow, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erysimum heterotrichum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
