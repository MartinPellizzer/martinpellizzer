Eritrichium sessiliflorum

Eritrichium sessiliflorum, commonly known as the short-flowered eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
