Euclea bakeriana

Euclea bakeriana, commonly known as the African bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Euclea bakeriana is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
