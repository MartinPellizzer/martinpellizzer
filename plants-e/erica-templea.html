Erica templea

Erica templea, commonly known as the Temple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica templea typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions.

It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
