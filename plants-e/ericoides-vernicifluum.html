Ericoides vernicifluum

Ericoides vernicifluum, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Ericoides vernicifluum has a shallow root system that makes it susceptible to erosion, but it also plays a role in stabilizing soil in certain habitats.
