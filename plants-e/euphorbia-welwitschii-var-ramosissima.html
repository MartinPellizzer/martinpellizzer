Euphorbia welwitschii var. ramosissima

Euphorbia welwitschii var. ramosissima, commonly known as the Desert Rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that branch profusely, giving it a shrub-like appearance. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often surrounded by colorful bracts. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its stems and leaves.

Its unique morphology and ability to thrive in harsh environments make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
