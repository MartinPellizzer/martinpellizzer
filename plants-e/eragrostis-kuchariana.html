Eragrostis kuchariana

Eragrostis kuchariana, commonly known as Kucharian's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
