Eragrostis articulata var. glabrescens

Eragrostis articulata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades that are typically shorter and more compact than those of other bluegrasses. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which are typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairie habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to form dense turf, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
