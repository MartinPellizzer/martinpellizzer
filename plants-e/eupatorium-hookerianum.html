Eupatorium hookerianum

Eupatorium hookerianum, commonly known as Hooker's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It is known for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its common name reflects the brittle, bone-like appearance of its stems.
