Echinocactus grandis f. polycormus

Echinocactus grandis f. polycormus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a rare and highly sought-after cactus species native to the Sierra Madre Occidental in Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, globular shape and vibrant green to blue-green coloration. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice among experienced cactus enthusiasts.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
