Erica arborea subsp. parviflora

Erica arborea subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It features dense, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
