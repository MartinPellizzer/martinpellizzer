Escobaria sneedii var. guadalupensis

Escobaria sneedii var. guadalupensis, commonly known as Guadalupe nightshade, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in Texas and New Mexico.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in arid landscapes.
