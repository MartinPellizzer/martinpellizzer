Eucalyptus delegatensis subsp. tasmaniensis

Eucalyptus delegatensis subsp.

tasmaniensis, commonly known as the Tasmanian gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Tasmania. It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey to white bark that peels in thin strips.

The leaves are lance-shaped, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits.

This species is adapted to cool, wet climates and is often found in moist forests and heathlands.
