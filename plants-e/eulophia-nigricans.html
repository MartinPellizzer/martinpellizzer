Eulophia nigricans

Eulophia nigricans, commonly known as the black orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns of white and green. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and marshes. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil.

Eulophia nigricans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
