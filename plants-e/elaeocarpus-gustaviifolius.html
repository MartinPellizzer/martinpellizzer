Elaeocarpus gustaviifolius

Elaeocarpus gustaviifolius, commonly known as the Hainan elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to southern China, particularly Hainan Island, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, pale pink or white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically ellipsoidal in shape. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
