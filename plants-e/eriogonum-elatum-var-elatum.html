Eriogonum elatum var. elatum

Eriogonum elatum var. elatum, commonly known as the tall buckwheat, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in sagebrush steppe and rocky habitats. It is drought-tolerant and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid and semi-arid ecosystems.
