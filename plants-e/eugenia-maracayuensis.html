Eugenia maracayuensis

Eugenia maracayuensis, commonly known as the Maracayu, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The fruit, which is a small, smooth berry, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

It is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
