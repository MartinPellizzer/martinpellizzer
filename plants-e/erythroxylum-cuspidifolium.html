Erythroxylum cuspidifolium

Erythroxylum cuspidifolium, commonly known as the cuspidifolium pepper plant, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, pointed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into elliptical, red berries. It is closely related to the coca plant and contains alkaloids, though it is not typically used for stimulant purposes.

Due to its unique morphology and chemical composition, it is of interest to botanists and researchers.
