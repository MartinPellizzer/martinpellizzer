Epilobium rhexiiflorum

Epilobium rhexiiflorum, commonly known as purple willow herb, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. The plant features upright stems covered in sticky hairs and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its showy, clustered flowers are typically pink to purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium rhexiiflorum is often found in wetland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
