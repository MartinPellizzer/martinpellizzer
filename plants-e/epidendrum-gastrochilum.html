Epidendrum gastrochilum

Epidendrum gastrochilum, commonly known as the yellow-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and white, often with a distinctive lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum gastrochilum is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in warm climates.
