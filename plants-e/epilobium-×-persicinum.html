Epilobium × persicinum

Epilobium × persicinum, commonly known as the purple marsh willow or purple willow herb, is a hybrid species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stems that often grow in wet or moist environments such as marshes and riverbanks. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Epilobium × persicinum is often used in wetland restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
