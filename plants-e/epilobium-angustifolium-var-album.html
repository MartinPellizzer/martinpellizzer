Epilobium angustifolium var. album

Epilobium angustifolium var. album, commonly known as white willow herb, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and ornamental purposes.
