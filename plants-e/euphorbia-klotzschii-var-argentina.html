Euphorbia klotzschii var. argentina

Euphorbia klotzschii var. argentina, commonly known as the Argentine euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
