Embothrium umbelliferum

Embothrium umbelliferum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a deciduous shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile and western Argentina.

It is characterized by its bright red, showy flowers that bloom in dense umbels, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Embothrium umbelliferum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional South American medicine.
