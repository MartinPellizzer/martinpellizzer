Eupatorium laevigatum

Eupatorium laevigatum, commonly known as smooth eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wetlands, stream banks, and swampy areas. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium laevigatum is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
