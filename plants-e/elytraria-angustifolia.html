Elytraria angustifolia

Elytraria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elytraria, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in environments with consistent moisture. Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, producing delicate, three-ranked spikelets.

Elytraria angustifolia plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
