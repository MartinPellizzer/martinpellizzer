Ecbolium tenuiflorum

Ecbolium tenuiflorum, commonly known as the slender-flower ecbolium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It features delicate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptations in harsh climates.
