Enchysia repens

Enchysia repens, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Enchysia repens is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.
