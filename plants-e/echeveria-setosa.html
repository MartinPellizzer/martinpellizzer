Echeveria setosa

Echeveria setosa, commonly known as the silver echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, silver-blue leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped, pinkish-purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires ample sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
