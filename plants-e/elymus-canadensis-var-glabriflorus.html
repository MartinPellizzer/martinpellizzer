Elymus canadensis var. glabriflorus

Elymus canadensis var. glabriflorus, commonly known as Canada wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including wetlands, prairies, and disturbed soils.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance, providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in various environments.
