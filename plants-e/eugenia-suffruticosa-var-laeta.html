Eugenia suffruticosa var. laeta

Eugenia suffruticosa var. laeta, commonly known as the golden eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
