Eugenia foetida var. parvifolia

Eugenia foetida var. parvifolia, commonly known as the littleleaf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
