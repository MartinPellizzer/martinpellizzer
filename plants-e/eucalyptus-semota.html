Eucalyptus semota

Eucalyptus semota, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming during the autumn season. Eucalyptus semota is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
