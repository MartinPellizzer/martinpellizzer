Elsholtzia dielsii

Elsholtzia dielsii, commonly known as the Chinese germander, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Elsholtzia dielsii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.
