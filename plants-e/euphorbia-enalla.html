Euphorbia enalla

Euphorbia enalla, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic and is often used as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Euphorbia enalla thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
