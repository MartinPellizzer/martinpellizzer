Erysimum etruscum

Erysimum etruscum, commonly known as the Etruscan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Erysimum etruscum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
