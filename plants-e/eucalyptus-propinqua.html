Eucalyptus propinqua

Eucalyptus propinqua, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often aromatic bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which attract various pollinators. Eucalyptus propinqua is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resilience.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
