Eupatorium palaeforme

Eupatorium palaeforme, commonly known as the old-form eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early fall, producing small, white to pale blue flower clusters.

Eupatorium palaeforme is often found in moist, open woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
