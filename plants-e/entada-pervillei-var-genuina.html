Entada pervillei var. genuina

Entada pervillei var. genuina, commonly known as the African candlewood tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain toxic seeds. The tree has a straight trunk with a dense crown and is often used for its durable wood in construction and furniture. Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

Due to its toxicity, it should be handled with care, and its seeds are not edible.
