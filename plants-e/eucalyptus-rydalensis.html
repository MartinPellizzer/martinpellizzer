Eucalyptus rydalensis

Eucalyptus rydalensis, commonly known as the Rhydyronen eucalypt, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue in color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
