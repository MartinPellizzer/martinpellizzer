Erodium daucoides var. latilobum

Erodium daucoides var. latilobum, commonly known as the broad-lobed bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is sometimes considered a weed but can be found in gardens and along roadsides.
