Eriochloa biglumis

Eriochloa biglumis, commonly known as the biglumis grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are tightly clustered, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eriochloa biglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native range.
