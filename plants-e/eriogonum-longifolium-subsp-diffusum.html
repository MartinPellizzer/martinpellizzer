Eriogonum longifolium subsp. diffusum

Eriogonum longifolium subsp.

diffusum, commonly known as the longleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and wildlife.
