Echinocactus glanduliger

Echinocactus glanduliger, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of bright yellow bristles. The plant has a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom in the spring.

The cactus is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
