Ervatamia divaricata

Ervatamia divaricata, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a woody stem with dense, narrow leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
