Echium thyrsiflorum

Echium thyrsiflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky areas.

Echium thyrsiflorum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
