Eupatorium sotarense var. breviflora

Eupatorium sotarense var. breviflora, commonly known as the short-flowered eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its foliage is generally lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often white or pale blue.

This variant is distinguished by its shorter flower heads compared to other varieties within the species.
