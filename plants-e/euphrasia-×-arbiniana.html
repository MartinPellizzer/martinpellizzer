Euphrasia × arbiniana

Euphrasia × arbiniana, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a hybrid species belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, slender stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Euphrasia × arbiniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
