Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp. loganum

Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp.

loganum, commonly known as the goldenhead buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its bright yellow inflorescences that contrast with its gray-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is often found in open woodlands and mountain slopes.
