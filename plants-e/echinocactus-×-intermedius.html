Echinocactus × intermedius

Echinocactus × intermedius, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a hybrid species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that bear white to yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
