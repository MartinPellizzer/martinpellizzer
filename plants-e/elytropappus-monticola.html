Elytropappus monticola

Elytropappus monticola, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the high elevations of the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh alpine conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring.

Elytropappus monticola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native environment.
