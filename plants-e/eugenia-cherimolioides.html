Eugenia cherimolioides

Eugenia cherimolioides, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cherimoya-like tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit similar in appearance to the cherimoya, with a thick, leathery rind and a creamy, aromatic pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
