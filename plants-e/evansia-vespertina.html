Evansia vespertina

Evansia vespertina, commonly known as the evening star, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, star-shaped flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
