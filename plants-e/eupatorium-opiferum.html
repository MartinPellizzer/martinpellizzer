Eupatorium opiferum

Eupatorium opiferum, commonly known as the showy eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Eupatorium opiferum is often found in moist, open habitats like wetlands and disturbed soils.
