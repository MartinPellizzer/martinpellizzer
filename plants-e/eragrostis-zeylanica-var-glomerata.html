Eragrostis zeylanica var. glomerata

Eragrostis zeylanica var. glomerata, commonly known as clustered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for erosion control and as a forage species.
