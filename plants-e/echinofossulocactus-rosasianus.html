Echinofossulocactus rosasianus

Echinofossulocactus rosasianus, commonly known as the Rosasianus Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles, which provide protection against herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green, rugged structure. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

This species is highly valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and resilience in dry environments.
