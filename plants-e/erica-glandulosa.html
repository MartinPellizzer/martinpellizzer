Erica glandulosa

Erica glandulosa, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrubs with dense, needle-like leaves covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in spring and summer. Erica glandulosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
