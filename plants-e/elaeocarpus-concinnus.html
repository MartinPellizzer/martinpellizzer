Elaeocarpus concinnus

Elaeocarpus concinnus, commonly known as the velvetwood or red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and rich color, making it a prized material in traditional crafts.

Elaeocarpus concinnus plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
