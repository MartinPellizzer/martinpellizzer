Euphorbia serendipita

Euphorbia serendipita, commonly known as the serendipity spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and sparse, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
