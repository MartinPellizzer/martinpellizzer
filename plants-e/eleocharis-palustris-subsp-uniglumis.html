Eleocharis palustris subsp. uniglumis

Eleocharis palustris subsp.

uniglumis, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant native to wetland environments. It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and marsh ecosystems. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
