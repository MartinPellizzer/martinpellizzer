Eugenia rubella

Eugenia rubella, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The fruit is a small, red drupe that is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and used in traditional medicine.
