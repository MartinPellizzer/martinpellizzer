Eriachne assimilis

Eriachne assimilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers in dense spikelets, which are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Eriachne assimilis is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
