Eriosema rufum

Eriosema rufum, commonly known as the red-veined eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive reddish underside. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
