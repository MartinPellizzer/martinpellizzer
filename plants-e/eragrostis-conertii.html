Eragrostis conertii

Eragrostis conertii, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Eragrostis conertii is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climate conditions.
