Echinocactus platensis var. leptanthus

Echinocactus platensis var. leptanthus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a spineless cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and bright green stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, followed by edible, apple-like fruits that are rich in nutrients. Its fleshy, segmented stems are adapted for drought resistance and can be used for ornamental purposes.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its potential culinary and medicinal uses.
