Electra brevifolia

Electra brevifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved electra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Electra brevifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
