Equilabium caespitosum

Equilabium caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and forest edges. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs from late summer to early fall, and it is often found in wet or disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for pollinators.
