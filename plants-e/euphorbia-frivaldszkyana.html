Euphorbia frivaldszkyana

Euphorbia frivaldszkyana, commonly known as the Frivaldszky's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
