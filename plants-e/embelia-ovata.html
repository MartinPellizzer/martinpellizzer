Embelia ovata

Embelia ovata, commonly known as the roundleaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

The leaves and fruits are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
