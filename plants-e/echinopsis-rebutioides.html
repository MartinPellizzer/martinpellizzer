Echinopsis rebutioides

Echinopsis rebutioides, commonly known as the Rebutia-like Echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its globular to cylindrical shape and dense covering of white to pale blue spines. The plant produces showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often blooming in late spring or early summer. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is widely cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
