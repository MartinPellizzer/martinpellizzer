Eragrostis enodis

Eragrostis enodis, commonly known as slender false rice, is a perennial grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairless leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and sandy soils. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Eragrostis enodis is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its competitive nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
