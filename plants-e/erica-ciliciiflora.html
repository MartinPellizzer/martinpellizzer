Erica ciliciiflora

Erica ciliciiflora, commonly known as the Cyprus heath, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica ciliciiflora typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
