Eriogonum arcuatum var. arcuatum

Eriogonum arcuatum var. arcuatum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
