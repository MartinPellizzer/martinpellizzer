Erophila ovalis

Erophila ovalis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed makepeace, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in coastal or inland areas. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Erophila ovalis is often used in traditional medicine and is also cultivated as an ornamental plant.
