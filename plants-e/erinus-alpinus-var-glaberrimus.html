Erinus alpinus var. glaberrimus

Erinus alpinus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the alpine erinus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, sunny environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine arrangements.
