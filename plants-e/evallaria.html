Evallaria

Evallaria, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Evallaria has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch.

It is a perennial herb that is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
