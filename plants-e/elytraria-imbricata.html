Elytraria imbricata

Elytraria imbricata, commonly known as the Cape rosebay, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and are adapted for pollination by birds and insects.
