Elaeocarpus winkleri

Elaeocarpus winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
