Emilia corallina

Emilia corallina, commonly known as the coral flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, pinkish-orange tubular flowers that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Emilia corallina is an annual or biennial herb with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
