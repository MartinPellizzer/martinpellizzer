Encephalosphaera lasiandra

Encephalosphaera lasiandra, commonly known as the purple sandalwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Australia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its aromatic, grey-green leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces woody fruits that contain several seeds, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
