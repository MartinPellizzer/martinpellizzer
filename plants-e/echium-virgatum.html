Echium virgatum

Echium virgatum, commonly known as the slender evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Echium virgatum is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
