Eria molliflora

Eria molliflora, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves and fragrant, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by insects.

Eria molliflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
