Embelia tsjeriam-cottam var. ferruginea

Embelia tsjeriam-cottam var. ferruginea, commonly known as the red-fruited embelia, is a variant of the embelia plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but have a sour, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and traditional medicinal use.
