Eriochloa castanea

Eriochloa castanea, commonly known as purple threeawn, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear bristly, awned flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriochloa castanea is a perennial plant that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
