Erica procaviana

Erica procaviana, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. Erica procaviana is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
