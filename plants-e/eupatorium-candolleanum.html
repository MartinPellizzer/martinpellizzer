Eupatorium candolleanum

Eupatorium candolleanum, commonly known as the Candolle's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium candolleanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
