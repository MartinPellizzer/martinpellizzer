Enarthrocarpus blaisei

Enarthrocarpus blaisei, commonly known as the Blaise's tamarind, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody pod containing several seeds, and it is often found in moist, shaded environments within its natural habitat.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in tropical ecosystems.
