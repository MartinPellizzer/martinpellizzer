Espostoa lanata subsp. ruficeps

Espostoa lanata subsp.

ruficeps, commonly known as the woolly espostoa, is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is native to South America. It is characterized by its densely hairy, or lanate, foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually clustered in inflorescences and are often brightly colored, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The subspecies ruficeps is distinguished by its reddish-brown stems and leaves, contributing to its unique appearance.
