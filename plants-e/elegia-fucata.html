Elegia fucata

Elegia fucata, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Atlantic.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and its ability to thrive in saline and well-drained soils. The plant has a rosette of narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its arid environment. It produces small, seed-containing capsules that disperse its seeds via the wind.

Elegia fucata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
