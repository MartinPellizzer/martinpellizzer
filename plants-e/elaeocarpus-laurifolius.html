Elaeocarpus laurifolius

Elaeocarpus laurifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are a source of food for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
