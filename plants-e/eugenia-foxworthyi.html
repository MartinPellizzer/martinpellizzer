Eugenia foxworthyi

Eugenia foxworthyi, commonly known as the Foxworth's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. After flowering, it produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red or yellow in color.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid climates.
