Elaeocarpus kambi

Elaeocarpus kambi, commonly known as the Kambi tree, is a tropical evergreen species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with a dense canopy and a slow growth rate.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
