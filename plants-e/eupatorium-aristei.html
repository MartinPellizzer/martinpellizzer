Eupatorium aristei

Eupatorium aristei, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside, adding to its ornamental value.

Eupatorium aristei is also known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
