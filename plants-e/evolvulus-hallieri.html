Evolvulus hallieri

Evolvulus hallieri, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Evolvulus hallieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and ease of care.
