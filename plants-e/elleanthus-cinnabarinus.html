Elleanthus cinnabarinus

Elleanthus cinnabarinus, commonly known as the red-flowered lily, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange-red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Elleanthus cinnabarinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
