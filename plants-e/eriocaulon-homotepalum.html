Eriocaulon homotepalum

Eriocaulon homotepalum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf marsh grass, is a small herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by its aquatic or semi-aquatic growth habits. The plant typically has slender, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette or clustered form. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often clustered in spikelets, and are adapted for wind pollination.

Eriocaulon homotepalum plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
