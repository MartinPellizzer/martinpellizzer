Erica curviflora var. sulphurea

Erica curviflora var. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
