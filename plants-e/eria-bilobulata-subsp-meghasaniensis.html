Eria bilobulata subsp. meghasaniensis

Eria bilobulata subsp.

meghasaniensis, commonly known as the Meghasani's eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family. It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences bearing small, fragrant orchid flowers.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments such as forests or rocky crevices. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
