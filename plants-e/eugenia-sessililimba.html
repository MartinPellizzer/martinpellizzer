Eugenia sessililimba

Eugenia sessililimba, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves that are often sessile, meaning they lack a petiole. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
