Eurybia fulgida

Eurybia fulgida, commonly known as the golden New Zealand bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Eurybia fulgida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
