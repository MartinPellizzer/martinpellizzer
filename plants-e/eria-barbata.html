Eria barbata

Eria barbata, commonly known as the hairy orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features elongated, hairy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinct, sweet fragrance. Eria barbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.

Its growth habit is typically epiphytic, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.
