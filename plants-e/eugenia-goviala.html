Eugenia goviala

Eugenia goviala, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its fruits are small, red berries that contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia goviala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
