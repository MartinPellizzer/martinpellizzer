Eriogonum nutans var. nutans

Eriogonum nutans var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable component of native plant communities.
