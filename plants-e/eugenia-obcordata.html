Eugenia obcordata

Eugenia obcordata, commonly known as the obcordate eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its oval, leathery leaves with a reddish underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple to black berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for horticultural purposes.
