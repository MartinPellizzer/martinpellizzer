Epacris longiflora f. rubra

Epacris longiflora f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowered epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open forests and heathlands. Its flowers are usually red or pink, and they bloom in spring and summer, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and fragrance.
