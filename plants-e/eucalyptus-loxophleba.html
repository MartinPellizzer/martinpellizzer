Eucalyptus loxophleba

Eucalyptus loxophleba, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are usually white or pale blue in color. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and heat.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees.
