Erica flexuosa

Erica flexuosa, commonly known as the scented heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Erica flexuosa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground cover.
