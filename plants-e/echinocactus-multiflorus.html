Echinocactus multiflorus

Echinocactus multiflorus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are covered in dense, white bristles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stems, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
