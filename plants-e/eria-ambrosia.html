Eria ambrosia

Eria ambrosia, commonly known as the sacred orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Eria ambrosia is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its cultural significance in indigenous communities.
