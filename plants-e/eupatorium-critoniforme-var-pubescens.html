Eupatorium critoniforme var. pubescens

Eupatorium critoniforme var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a fuzzy texture.

This species is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
