Elymus alaskanus subsp. borealis

Elymus alaskanus subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the Alaska wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, moist habitats such as tundra and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish hues.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environments.
