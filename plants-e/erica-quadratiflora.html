Erica quadratiflora

Erica quadratiflora, commonly known as the four-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica quadratiflora typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
