Endotropis crocea subsp. ilicifolia

Endotropis crocea subsp.

ilicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Europe. It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Its subspecies distinction is based on leaf morphology and geographic distribution.
