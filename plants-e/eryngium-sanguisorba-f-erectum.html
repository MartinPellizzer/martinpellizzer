Eryngium sanguisorba f. erectum

Eryngium sanguisorba f. erectum, commonly known as the upright thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features striking, spiky blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
