Epipactis latifolia var. papillosa

Epipactis latifolia var. papillosa, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and a showy inflorescence of greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its flowers have a distinctive structure with a labellum that is adorned with small, hairy appendages.

Epipactis latifolia var. papillosa is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its unique floral morphology.
