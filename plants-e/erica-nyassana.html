Erica nyassana

Erica nyassana, commonly known as the Nyassa heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica nyassana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
