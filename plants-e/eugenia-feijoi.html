Eugenia feijoi

Eugenia feijoi, commonly known as feijoa, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its edible fruit, which has a unique flavor combining tropical and citrus notes. The fruit is typically harvested when it turns a deep purple or black color, indicating ripeness.

Eugenia feijoi thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
