Euphorbia fragifera

Euphorbia fragifera, commonly known as the fragrant euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems.

Euphorbia fragifera is often used in traditional medicine and as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
