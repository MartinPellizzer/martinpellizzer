Erodium cicutarium var. hirsutum

Erodium cicutarium var. hirsutum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
