Echinocactus scopa f. candidus

Echinocactus scopa f. candidus, commonly known as the white-hair cactus, is a rare and striking variant of the typical echinocactus scopa.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white, spines that distinguish it from the more common green or yellow-spined forms. The plant is native to the arid regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
