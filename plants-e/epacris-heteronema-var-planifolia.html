Epacris heteronema var. planifolia

Epacris heteronema var. planifolia, commonly known as the flat-leaved epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
