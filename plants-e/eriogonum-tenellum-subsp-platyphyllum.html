Eriogonum tenellum subsp. platyphyllum

Eriogonum tenellum subsp.

platyphyllum, commonly known as the flatleaf globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces clusters of showy, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid-land ecosystems.
