Eryngium purpuratum

Eryngium purpuratum, commonly known as purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features striking purple flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by spiny, blue-gray bracts. The plant has a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
