Eritrichium lignosum

Eritrichium lignosum, commonly known as the woolly thyme or woolly filaree, is a low-growing perennial herb native to western North America.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has wiry, often hairy stems and leaves that are narrow and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.

Eritrichium lignosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
