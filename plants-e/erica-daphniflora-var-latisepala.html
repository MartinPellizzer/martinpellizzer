Erica daphniflora var. latisepala

Erica daphniflora var. latisepala, commonly known as the broad-sepaled heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
