Evonyxis virginica

Evonyxis virginica, commonly known as the Virginia evonyx, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Evonyxis virginica is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.
