Eriogonum chrysocephalum var. alpestre

Eriogonum chrysocephalum var. alpestre, commonly known as the alpine goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in open meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
