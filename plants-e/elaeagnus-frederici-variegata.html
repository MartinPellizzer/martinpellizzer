Elaeagnus frederici-variegata

Elaeagnus frederici-variegata, commonly known as the variegated silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often variegated with yellow and green, and its small, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus frederici-variegata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
