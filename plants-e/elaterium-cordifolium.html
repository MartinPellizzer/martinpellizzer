Elaterium cordifolium

Elaterium cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved elaterium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the study of tropical flora.
