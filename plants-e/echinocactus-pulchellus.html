Echinocactus pulchellus

Echinocactus pulchellus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem that is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The plant's unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus collections.
