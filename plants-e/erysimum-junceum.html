Erysimum junceum

Erysimum junceum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

Erysimum junceum is typically perennial and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
