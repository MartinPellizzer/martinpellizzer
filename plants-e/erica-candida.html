Erica candida

Erica candida, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica candida thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
