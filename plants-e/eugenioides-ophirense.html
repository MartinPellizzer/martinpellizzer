Eugenioides ophirense

Eugenioides ophirense, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenioides ophirense is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
