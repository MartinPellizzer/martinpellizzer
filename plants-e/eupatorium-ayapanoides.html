Eupatorium ayapanoides

Eupatorium ayapanoides, commonly known as Ayapan wood or Ayapan shrub, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, often displaying shades of white, pink, or purple.

Eupatorium ayapanoides is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
