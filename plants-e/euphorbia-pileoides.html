Euphorbia pileoides

Euphorbia pileoides, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded stem covered in small, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers, and it is known for its ability to store water in its leaves and stems. Euphorbia pileoides thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or arid landscapes.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant among succulent enthusiasts.
