Eurycaulis rantii

Eurycaulis rantii, commonly known as the silverleaf cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features thick, fleshy stems covered in a silvery, waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, green flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle contrast to its foliage. Its root system is shallow but extensive, allowing it to absorb moisture from light rains.

Eurycaulis rantii is well adapted to harsh desert environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
