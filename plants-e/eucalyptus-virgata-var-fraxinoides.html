Eucalyptus virgata var. fraxinoides

Eucalyptus virgata var. fraxinoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. This plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular flower buds that are often yellow or red, and its growth form is usually shrub-like or tree-like depending on the habitat.

The plant is known for its aromatic oils, which have various uses in traditional and modern applications.
