Equisetum telmateia f. ramulosum

Equisetum telmateia f. ramulosum, commonly known as the ramose horse-tail, is a fern-like plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of branches.

It belongs to the Equisetaceae family and is known for its distinctive, spore-producing structures called strobili. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands. Its stems are segmented and covered in small, scale-like structures.

It is a primitive plant that has remained relatively unchanged for millions of years.
