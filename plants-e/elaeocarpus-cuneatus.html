Elaeocarpus cuneatus

Elaeocarpus cuneatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
