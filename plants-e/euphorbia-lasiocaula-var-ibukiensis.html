Euphorbia lasiocaula var. ibukiensis

Euphorbia lasiocaula var. ibukiensis, commonly known as the Ibuki endemic euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or arid environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
