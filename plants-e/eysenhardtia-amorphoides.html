Eysenhardtia amorphoides

Eysenhardtia amorphoides, commonly known as the desert broom, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has compound leaves with small, narrow leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it resilient in harsh climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
