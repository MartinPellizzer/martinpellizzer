Eupatorium subintegrum

Eupatorium subintegrum, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles, and typically bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium subintegrum is often found in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes.
