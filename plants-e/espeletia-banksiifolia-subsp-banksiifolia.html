Espeletia banksiifolia subsp. banksiifolia

Espeletia banksiifolia subsp.

banksiifolia, commonly known as the Banks' espeletia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-forming growth habit and dense, woolly foliage that helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or volcanic soils.

This subspecies is part of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ecological importance in Andean ecosystems.
