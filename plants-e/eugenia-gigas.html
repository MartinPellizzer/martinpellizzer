Eugenia gigas

Eugenia gigas, commonly known as the giant wax apple, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It can grow up to 30 meters tall with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces showy white flowers followed by fleshy, red or yellow berries. Its leaves are large, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
