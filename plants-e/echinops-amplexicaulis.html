Echinops amplexicaulis

Echinops amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble small globes, which are a key feature of the echinops genus. The plant has basal leaves that are often clasping the stem, giving it its specific epithet "amplexicaulis.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
