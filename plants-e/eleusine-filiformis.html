Eleusine filiformis

Eleusine filiformis, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, wiry stems that often grow in dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its drought tolerance and rapid growth, it is sometimes used for erosion control and as a forage plant.
