Euthamia graminifolia

Euthamia graminifolia, commonly known as slender goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, prairies, and along stream banks. It produces fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across various habitats.

Euthamia graminifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
