Echinocereus cinerascens subsp. cinerascens

Echinocereus cinerascens subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the silver cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, white spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and desert gardens.
