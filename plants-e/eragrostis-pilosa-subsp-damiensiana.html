Eragrostis pilosa subsp. damiensiana

Eragrostis pilosa subsp.

damiensiana, commonly known as Damien's hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its wiry, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is occasionally cultivated in arid regions as a low-maintenance ground cover.
