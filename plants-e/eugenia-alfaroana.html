Eugenia alfaroana

Eugenia alfaroana, commonly known as the Alfaro's evergreen, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the cloud forests of western Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit, a small, red drupe, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Eugenia alfaroana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its ecological and horticultural significance.
