Eupatoriadelphus fistulosus

Eupatoriadelphus fistulosus, commonly known as the slender eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as meadows and riverbanks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Eupatoriadelphus fistulosus is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
