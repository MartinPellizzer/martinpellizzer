Epidendrum lufinorum

Epidendrum lufinorum, commonly known as the Lufin's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Epidendraceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and inflorescence. The plant produces showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in two ranks, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Epidendrum lufinorum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
