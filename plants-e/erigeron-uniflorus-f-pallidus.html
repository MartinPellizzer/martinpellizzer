Erigeron uniflorus f. pallidus

Erigeron uniflorus f. pallidus, commonly known as the pale fleabane, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white ray florets and is typically found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and sandy soils. The plant has a low-growing habit with slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This variant is distinguished by its lighter coloration compared to other forms of Erigeron uniflorus.
