Eranthemum sessiliflorum

Eranthemum sessiliflorum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pink or red, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a pointed tip.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
