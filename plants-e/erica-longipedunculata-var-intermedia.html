Erica longipedunculata var. intermedia

Erica longipedunculata var. intermedia, commonly known as the Heath or Ericaceous shrub, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and compact growth habit, typically forming low shrubs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is commonly found in fynbos vegetation.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
