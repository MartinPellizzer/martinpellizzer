Erythroxylum ampullaceum

Erythroxylum ampullaceum, commonly known as the Amazonian tobacco plant, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with a smooth texture and a reddish underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in the production of certain alkaloids.
