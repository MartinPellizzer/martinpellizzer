Erica chloroloma

Erica chloroloma, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica chloroloma thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
