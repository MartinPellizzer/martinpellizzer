Epipactis helleborine var. orbicularis

Epipactis helleborine var. orbicularis, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and its striking, hooded flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple veins. The plant is terrestrial, growing in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. It produces a single, erect flower spike that bears several showy, spurred flowers.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with mycorrhizal fungi.
