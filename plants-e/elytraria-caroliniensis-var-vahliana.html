Elytraria caroliniensis var. vahliana

Elytraria caroliniensis var. vahliana, commonly known as the Carolina marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in moist environments.

It is often found in association with other wetland flora, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
