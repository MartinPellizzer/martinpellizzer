Eugenia subalata

Eugenia subalata, commonly known as the silverleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that have a silvery-white underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which are followed by small, round berries.

Eugenia subalata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
