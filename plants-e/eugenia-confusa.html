Eugenia confusa

Eugenia confusa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or confusa myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, red, edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and often used in culinary applications.

Eugenia confusa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties.
