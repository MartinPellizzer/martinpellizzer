Evolvulus alsinoides var. typicus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. typicus, commonly known as common blue morning glory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features prostrate or ascending stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread readily. The plant produces small, showy blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close by midday. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and agricultural fields.
