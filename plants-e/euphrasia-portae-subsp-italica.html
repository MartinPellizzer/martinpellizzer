Euphrasia portae subsp. italica

Euphrasia portae subsp.

italica, commonly known as the Italian eyebright, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or meadows. It has finely divided leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are clustered in loose racemes.

This species is valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
