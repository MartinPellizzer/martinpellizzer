Euphorbia personata

Euphorbia personata, commonly known as the flowering spurge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features striking, upright stems that are green and often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are mistaken for petals. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly toothed margin.

Euphorbia personata is known for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in full sun to partial shade.
