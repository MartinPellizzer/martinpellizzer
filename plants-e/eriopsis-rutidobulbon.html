Eriopsis rutidobulbon

Eriopsis rutidobulbon, commonly known as the woolly dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly covering on its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. Native to regions of southern Africa, it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
