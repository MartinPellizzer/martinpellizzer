Erica minuta

Erica minuta, commonly known as dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes various heath and heather plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and tiny, needle-like leaves. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late winter to early spring.

Erica minuta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
