Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. hayatae

Elaeocarpus sylvestris var. hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese elaeocarp, is a variant of the elaeocarpus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its botanical features include a complex floral structure and a unique fruit morphology that aids in seed dispersal.

This species is often found in shaded, moist environments within forest ecosystems.
