Erythraea australis var. alba

Erythraea australis var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered erythraea, is a variant of the erythraea plant native to southern regions.

It is characterized by its white flowers, which contrast with the typical red or pink hues of the species. The plant typically grows in coastal or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, woody fruits.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
