Euphorbia tigridis

Euphorbia tigridis, commonly known as the zigzag spurge, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable addition to botanical collections.
