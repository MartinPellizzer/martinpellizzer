Eupatorium laevigatum var. longepetiolatum

Eupatorium laevigatum var. longepetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often branching near the top. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
