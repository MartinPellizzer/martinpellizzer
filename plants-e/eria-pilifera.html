Eria pilifera

Eria pilifera, commonly known as the featherleaf orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Eria pilifera is noted for its resilience and ability to survive in arid conditions with minimal water.
