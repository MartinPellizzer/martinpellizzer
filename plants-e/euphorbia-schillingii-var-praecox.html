Euphorbia schillingii var. praecox

Euphorbia schillingii var. praecox, commonly known as the early Schilling's spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive cyathia, which are cup-shaped structures containing the true flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
