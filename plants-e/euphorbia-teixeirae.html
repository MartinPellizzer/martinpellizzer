Euphorbia teixeirae

Euphorbia teixeirae, commonly known as the Teixeirae Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, waxy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular ornamental plant in dry gardens.
