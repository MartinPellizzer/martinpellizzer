Euclea crispa subsp. linearis

Euclea crispa subsp.

linearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironwood, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought resistance through its thick, waxy leaves. Its wood is hard and dense, making it valuable for construction and fuel.

The species plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
