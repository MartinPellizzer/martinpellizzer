Erythronium americanum f. nuttallianum

Erythronium americanum f. nuttallianum, commonly known as the Nuttall's dogtooth violet, is a botanical variant of the American dogtooth violet.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States, characterized by its distinctive, showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded habitats and produces heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or white, with a prominent, tooth-like spur.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and bloom.
