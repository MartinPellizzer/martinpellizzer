Euphorbia togakusensis f. intermedia

Euphorbia togakusensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate spurge, is a variant of the Euphorbia togakusensis species.

It is a succulent plant native to specific regions of Japan, particularly the island of Shikoku. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, adapted to store water. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
