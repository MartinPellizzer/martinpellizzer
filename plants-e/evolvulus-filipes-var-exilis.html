Evolvulus filipes var. exilis

Evolvulus filipes var. exilis, commonly known as the slender stem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are slender and often hairy. This variety is a variant of Evolvulus filipes, distinguished by its more delicate appearance and shorter stems.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
