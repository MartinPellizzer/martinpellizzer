Erigeron peregrinus subsp. typicus

Erigeron peregrinus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the common blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, and it thrives in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its adaptability and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
