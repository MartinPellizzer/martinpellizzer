Eupatorium etlense

Eupatorium etlense, commonly known as the Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in spherical heads.

Eupatorium etlense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
