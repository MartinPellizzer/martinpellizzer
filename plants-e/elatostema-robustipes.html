Elatostema robustipes

Elatostema robustipes, commonly known as the hardy ground ivy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces tiny, greenish-white flowers. The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to spread via runners, making it a common ground cover. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes plants known for their stinging hairs.
