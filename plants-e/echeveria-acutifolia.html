Echeveria acutifolia

Echeveria acutifolia, commonly known as the Blue Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom. The plant produces clusters of violet or blue flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and striking appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
