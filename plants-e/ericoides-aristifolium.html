Ericoides aristifolium

Ericoides aristifolium, commonly known as the slender heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many evergreen and deciduous shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in the spring.

Ericoides aristifolium is well-suited to rocky and well-drained environments, making it a hardy plant in cooler climates.
