Eupatorium obscurum

Eupatorium obscurum, commonly known as boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

Boneset is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
