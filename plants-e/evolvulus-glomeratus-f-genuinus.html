Evolvulus glomeratus f. genuinus

Evolvulus glomeratus f. genuinus, commonly known as the dwarf blue morning glory, is a compact, flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves and a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for containers or hanging baskets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and compact form, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
