Elodes fraseri

Elodes fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. Elodes fraseri produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually submerged, followed by the development of small, round fruits.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
