Eragrostis crassinervis

Eragrostis crassinervis, commonly known as broad-veined lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its drought tolerance and rapid growth, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant.
