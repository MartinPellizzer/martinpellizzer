Eupatorium thieleanum

Eupatorium thieleanum, commonly known as the Thiel's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some species.
