Erophila verna var. krockeri

Erophila verna var. krockeri, commonly known as the spring eryngo, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
