Echinocactus turbinatus

Echinocactus turbinatus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and protect against herbivores.

Echinocactus turbinatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
