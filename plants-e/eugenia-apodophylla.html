Eugenia apodophylla

Eugenia apodophylla, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, dark purple berries. Eugenia apodophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal dunes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
