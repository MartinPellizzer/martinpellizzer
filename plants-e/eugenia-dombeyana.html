Eugenia dombeyana

Eugenia dombeyana, commonly known as the Dombey's clove, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. Its fruits are small, red, and edible, containing a single seed, and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's aromatic properties and ornamental value make it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
