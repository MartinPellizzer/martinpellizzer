Eria puakensis

Eria puakensis, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. Eria puakensis typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts and horticulturists.
