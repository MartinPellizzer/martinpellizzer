Elaeocarpus gardneri

Elaeocarpus gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
