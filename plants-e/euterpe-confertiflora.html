Euterpe confertiflora

Euterpe confertiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian palm, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is a tall, slender palm with a straight trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are pollinated by insects and birds. Its fruits are small, round drupes that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

This palm is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of thatch.
