Echinocactus mackieanus

Echinocactus mackieanus, commonly known as the Mackie's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
