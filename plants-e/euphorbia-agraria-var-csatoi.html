Euphorbia agraria var. csatoi

Euphorbia agraria var. csatoi, commonly known as the Csato's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a subtle blue or purple tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flower structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its low maintenance and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in dry environments.
