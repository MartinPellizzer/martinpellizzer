Echinacea angustifolia var. tennesseensis

Echinacea angustifolia var. tennesseensis, commonly known as the Tennessee narrowleaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pinkish-red to purple flower heads in the summer, which are composed of numerous tiny disc flowers surrounded by bracts. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adapted to well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
