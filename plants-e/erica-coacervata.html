Erica coacervata

Erica coacervata, commonly known as the purple heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. Erica coacervata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and long blooming period.
