Ethulia corymbosa

Ethulia corymbosa, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant has simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
