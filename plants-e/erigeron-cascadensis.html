Erigeron cascadensis

Erigeron cascadensis, commonly known as Cascade fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats such as streambanks and meadows. It produces white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Erigeron cascadensis is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions.
