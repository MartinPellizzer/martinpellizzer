Echenais carlinoides

Echenais carlinoides, commonly known as the Carlin's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in dense, spiny leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers in a rounded cluster, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are often lined with fine, bristly hairs, and the plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Echenais carlinoides is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
