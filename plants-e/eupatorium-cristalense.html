Eupatorium cristalense

Eupatorium cristalense, commonly known as the cristalense thoroughwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Eupatorium cristalense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
