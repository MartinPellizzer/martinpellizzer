Eremophila gilesii subsp. gilesii

Eremophila gilesii subsp.

gilesii, commonly known as the Giles' eremophila, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Australia. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, which bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
