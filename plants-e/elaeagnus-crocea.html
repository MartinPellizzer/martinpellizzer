Elaeagnus crocea

Elaeagnus crocea, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Elaeagnus crocea is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil stabilization and reclamation projects.

Its rapid growth and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
