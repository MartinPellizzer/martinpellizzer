Eriophyllum speciosum

Eriophyllum speciosum, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Eriophyllum speciosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
