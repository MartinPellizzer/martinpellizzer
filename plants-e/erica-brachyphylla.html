Erica brachyphylla

Erica brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica brachyphylla is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
