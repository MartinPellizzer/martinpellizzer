Eriogonum flexum var. ferronis

Eriogonum flexum var. ferronis, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, branching stems and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities and a valuable species for ecological restoration.
