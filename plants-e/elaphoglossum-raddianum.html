Elaphoglossum raddianum

Elaphoglossum raddianum, commonly known as the Raddian holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, leathery fronds that are typically arranged in a compact, upright habit. The plant produces spores in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
