Eriosema scioanum var. samburuense

Eriosema scioanum var. samburuense, commonly known as the Samburu eriosema, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in grassland habitats and is often found in semi-arid regions. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale or greenish in color.

This variant is notable for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
