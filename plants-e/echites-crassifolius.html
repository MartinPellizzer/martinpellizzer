Echites crassifolius

Echites crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved echites, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically broad and oval in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its succulent nature, it is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
