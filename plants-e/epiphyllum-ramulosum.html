Epiphyllum ramulosum

Epiphyllum ramulosum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a climbing cactus native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, fragrant flowers that typically bloom at night, opening in shades of white, yellow, or pink. Its leaves are usually deciduous, shedding during periods of drought or cold.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique flowering behavior.
