Erythroxylum couveleense

Erythroxylum couveleense, commonly known as the Brazilian peppertree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually gland-dotted. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, reddish berries.

It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types and habitats.
