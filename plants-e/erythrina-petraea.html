Erythrina petraea

Erythrina petraea, commonly known as the rock almond or desert candle, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky soils and can withstand drought conditions. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small leaflets, and it has woody pods that contain seeds.

Erythrina petraea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
