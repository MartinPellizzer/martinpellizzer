Erythrina perrieri

Erythrina perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its woody structure and showy blooms make it a popular ornamental species.

Erythrina perrieri also plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria.
