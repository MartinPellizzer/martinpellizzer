Elongatia superbiens

Elongatia superbiens, commonly known as the noble orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blossoms.

Elongatia superbiens is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
