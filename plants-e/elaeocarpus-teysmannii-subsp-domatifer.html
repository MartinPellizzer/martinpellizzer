Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp. domatifer

Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp.

domatifer, commonly known as the dome-fruited elaeocarp, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and distinctive, dome-shaped fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and the plant produces a unique type of fruit with a fleshy, edible aril.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest areas.
