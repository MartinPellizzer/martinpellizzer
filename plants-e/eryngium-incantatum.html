Eryngium incantatum

Eryngium incantatum, commonly known as the purple thistle or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by pale blue bracts. The plant has lance-shaped, gray-green leaves with a rough texture. It typically grows in coastal areas and well-drained soils.

Eryngium incantatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.
