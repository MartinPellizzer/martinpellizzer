Eriogonum pauciflorum var. canum

Eriogonum pauciflorum var. canum, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its low, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in late spring. The plant has hairy leaves and stems, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun.

This species plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
