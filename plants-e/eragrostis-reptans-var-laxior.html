Eragrostis reptans var. laxior

Eragrostis reptans var. laxior, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides, fields, and woodland edges. The plant has slender, spreading stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is notable for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
