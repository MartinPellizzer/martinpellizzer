Eupatorium tectum

Eupatorium tectum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has aromatic leaves that are often used in herbal medicine for their purported calming properties. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine and its distinctive appearance.
