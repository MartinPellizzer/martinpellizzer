Elymus rhodopaeus

Elymus rhodopaeus, commonly known as the Rhodope wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to brown in hue. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Elymus rhodopaeus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
