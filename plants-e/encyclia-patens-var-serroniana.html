Encyclia patens var. serroniana

Encyclia patens var. serroniana, commonly known as the serrated orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers with yellow and purple markings. The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. This orchid thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and ecological role in its native ecosystems.
