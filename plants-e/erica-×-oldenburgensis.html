Erica × oldenburgensis

Erica × oldenburgensis, commonly known as the Oldenburg heath, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica × oldenburgensis thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
