Euodia ruticarpa var. bodinieri

Euodia ruticarpa var. bodinieri, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a slightly serrated margin.

Euodia ruticarpa var. bodinieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and medicinal properties.
