Epidendrum triquetrum

Epidendrum triquetrum, commonly known as the three-cornered orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular or three-angled stem, which gives it its scientific name. The plant typically produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Epidendrum triquetrum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
