Erythroxylum anceps

Erythroxylum anceps, commonly known as the red-eyed treehopper plant, is a species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, woody shrub form. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by elliptical, greenish-black berries. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy underside.

Erythroxylum anceps is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in shaded, moist environments.
