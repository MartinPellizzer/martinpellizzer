Eugenia linearis var. longifolia

Eugenia linearis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often fragrant and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rainforest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in indigenous Australian cultures.
