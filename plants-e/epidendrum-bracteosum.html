Epidendrum bracteosum

Epidendrum bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The orchid's flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
