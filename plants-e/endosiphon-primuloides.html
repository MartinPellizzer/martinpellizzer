Endosiphon primuloides

Endosiphon primuloides, commonly known as the common endosiphon, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Primulaceae.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks.
