Eria connata

Eria connata, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eria connata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
