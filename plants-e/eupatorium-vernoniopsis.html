Eupatorium vernoniopsis

Eupatorium vernoniopsis, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It produces showy white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
