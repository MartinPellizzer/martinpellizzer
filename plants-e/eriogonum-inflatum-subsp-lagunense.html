Eriogonum inflatum subsp. lagunense

Eriogonum inflatum subsp.

lagunense, commonly known as the Great Basin buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as sagebrush steppe and grasslands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

The species is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
