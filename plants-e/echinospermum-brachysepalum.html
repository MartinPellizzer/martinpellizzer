Echinospermum brachysepalum

Echinospermum brachysepalum, commonly known as the narrow-sepal echinosperm, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow petals and a central disk. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, arranged alternately along the stem.

The fruit is a dry capsule that contains numerous small seeds, typical of the Asteraceae family.
