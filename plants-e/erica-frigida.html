Erica frigida

Erica frigida, commonly known as the alpine heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. This species is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure and small, needle-like leaves. Erica frigida produces clusters of pale pink or white flowers that bloom in spring and early summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
