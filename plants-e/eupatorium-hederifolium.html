Eupatorium hederifolium

Eupatorium hederifolium, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers. It is known for its medicinal properties, though it can be toxic to livestock.

Its name reflects both its leaf shape and its historical association with rattlesnakes.
