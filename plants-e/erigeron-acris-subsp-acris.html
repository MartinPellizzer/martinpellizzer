Erigeron acris subsp. acris

Erigeron acris subsp.

acris, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in full sun. Its blue to lavender flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
