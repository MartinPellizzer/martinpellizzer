Echinopsis nigra

Echinopsis nigra, commonly known as the black echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months. It has a compact growth habit and is often grown as a decorative houseplant.

Due to its slow growth and specific care requirements, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
