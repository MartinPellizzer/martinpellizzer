Eragrostis macilenta

Eragrostis macilenta, commonly known as slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny grains. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
