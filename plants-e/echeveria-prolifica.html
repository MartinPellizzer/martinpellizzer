Echeveria prolifica

Echeveria prolifica, commonly known as the mother of thousands, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are typically fleshy and covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces numerous offsets, which can be easily propagated to create new plants. Its flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in the spring.

Echeveria prolifica thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.
