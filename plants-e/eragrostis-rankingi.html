Eragrostis rankingi

Eragrostis rankingi, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
