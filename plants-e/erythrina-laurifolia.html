Erythrina laurifolia

Erythrina laurifolia, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces woody pods that contain several seeds.

Erythrina laurifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
