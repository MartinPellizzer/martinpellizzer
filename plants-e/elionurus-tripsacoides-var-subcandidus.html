Elionurus tripsacoides var. subcandidus

Elionurus tripsacoides var. subcandidus, commonly known as the white-flowered panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and dense, tufted inflorescence. The plant produces white to pale yellow florets, which are typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flower structure.
