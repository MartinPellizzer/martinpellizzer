Eriocaulon taiwanianum

Eriocaulon taiwanianum, commonly known as the Taiwan sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is native to Taiwan and is often found in lowland areas with high humidity.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in maintaining wetland biodiversity and water filtration.
