Eupatorium steyermarkianum

Eupatorium steyermarkianum, commonly known as the Steyermark's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet, wooded areas and is often found in swamps or along stream banks.

Eupatorium steyermarkianum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
