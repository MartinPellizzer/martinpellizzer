Eugenia madagascariensis

Eugenia madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in various parts of the world.
