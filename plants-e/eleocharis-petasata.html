Eleocharis petasata

Eleocharis petasata, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in North America.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of white or pale green flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, which can help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Eleocharis petasata is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to water filtration and biodiversity.
