Ebenidium tragacanthoides

Ebenidium tragacanthoides, commonly known as the African tragacanth, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces a gum-like substance from its bark, which has been traditionally used for medicinal and industrial purposes. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing on rocky soils.

Due to its ecological and economic importance, it is sometimes cultivated for its resin and ornamental value.
