Erica fastigiata var. fastigiata

Erica fastigiata var. fastigiata, commonly known as the upright heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It features dense, needle-like leaves that are dark green and glossy, arranged in tight, upright clusters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and purple, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is prized for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-maintenance landscapes.
