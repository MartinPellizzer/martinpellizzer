Eucalyptus forrestiana subsp. stoatei

Eucalyptus forrestiana subsp.

stoatei, commonly known as the coastal eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often reveals a pale or white inner layer.

The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have a bluish-green color with a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils.
