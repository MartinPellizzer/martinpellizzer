Euphorbia smithii

Euphorbia smithii, commonly known as Smith's spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green, providing a distinctive appearance.

Euphorbia smithii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
