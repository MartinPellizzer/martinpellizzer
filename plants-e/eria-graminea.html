Eria graminea

Eria graminea, commonly known as the grassy orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic or lithophytic species, often found on trees or rocks in humid environments. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white, yellow, or green.

Eria graminea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
