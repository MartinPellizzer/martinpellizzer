Eragrostis nervilemma

Eragrostis nervilemma, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, grassy habitats such as savannas and rocky outcrops. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent veins, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose panicle. It produces small, two-lobed florets that are often pale green or brownish in color.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in semi-arid conditions.
