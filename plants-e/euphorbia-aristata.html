Euphorbia aristata

Euphorbia aristata, commonly known as the "arrowleaf euphorbia," is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive arrow-shaped leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flower clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it should be handled with care.
