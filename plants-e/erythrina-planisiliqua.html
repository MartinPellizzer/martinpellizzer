Erythrina planisiliqua

Erythrina planisiliqua, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a species of legume native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, often appearing in late winter or early spring. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also notable for its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with root nodules.
