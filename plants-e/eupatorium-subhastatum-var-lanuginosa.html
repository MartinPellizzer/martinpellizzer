Eupatorium subhastatum var. lanuginosa

Eupatorium subhastatum var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the hairy boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered umbels of white to pale blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal historical uses.
