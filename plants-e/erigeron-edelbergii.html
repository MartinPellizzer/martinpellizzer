Erigeron edelbergii

Erigeron edelbergii, commonly known as the Desert Blueweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron edelbergii is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
