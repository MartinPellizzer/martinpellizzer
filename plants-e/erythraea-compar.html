Erythraea compar

Erythraea compar, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark reddish-brown heartwood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
