Eria exappendiculata

Eria exappendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical forests. Its leaves are elongated and often have a waxy texture, which helps in retaining moisture.

Eria exappendiculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
