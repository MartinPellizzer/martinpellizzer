Echinocereus fendleri var. pauperculus

Echinocereus fendleri var. pauperculus, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinct rib structure and spines that are typically white to yellow in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a desirable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
