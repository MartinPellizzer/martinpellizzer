Emilia gaudichaudii

Emilia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the golden cup plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Emilia gaudichaudii is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
