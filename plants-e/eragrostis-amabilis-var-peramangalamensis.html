Eragrostis amabilis var. peramangalamensis

Eragrostis amabilis var. peramangalamensis, commonly known as the Peramangalam lovegrass, is a grass species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for landscaping purposes.
