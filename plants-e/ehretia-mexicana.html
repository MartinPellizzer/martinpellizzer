Ehretia mexicana

Ehretia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
