Eugenia clarendonensis

Eugenia clarendonensis, commonly known as the Clarendon guava, is a small evergreen tree native to Jamaica.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, pinkish-red berries that are rich in flavor and nutrients. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
