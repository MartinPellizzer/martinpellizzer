Epidendrum parallelopetalum

Epidendrum parallelopetalum, commonly known as the Parallelopetalum orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in forest understories. The plant features long, arching stems with numerous lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, with showy, fragrant blooms that range in color from white to pale pink.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
