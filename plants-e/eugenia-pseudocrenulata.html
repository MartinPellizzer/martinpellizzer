Eugenia pseudocrenulata

Eugenia pseudocrenulata, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. It produces small, dark red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia pseudocrenulata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
