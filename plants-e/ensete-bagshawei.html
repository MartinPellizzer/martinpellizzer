Ensete bagshawei

Ensete bagshawei, commonly known as the Ethiopian banana, is a large, herbaceous plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Musaceae family and is characterized by its tall, clustered pseudostems and large, broad leaves. The plant produces edible inflorescences that are harvested and processed into a starchy food known as "enset.". It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Ensete bagshawei plays a vital role in the agricultural and cultural practices of the Ethiopian highlands.
