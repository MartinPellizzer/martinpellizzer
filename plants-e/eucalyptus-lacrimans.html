Eucalyptus lacrimans

Eucalyptus lacrimans, commonly known as the weeping eucalypt, is a tall, slender tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and small, rounded leaves that give it a distinctive, almost weeping appearance. The tree produces small white flowers in clusters, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing viable seeds. Eucalyptus lacrimans is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and salty air.

It is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has some medicinal uses in traditional practices.
