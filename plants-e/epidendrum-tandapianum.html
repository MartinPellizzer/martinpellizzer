Epidendrum tandapianum

Epidendrum tandapianum, commonly known as the Tandapianum orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features erect, branching stems that bear numerous lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are characterized by their vivid colors, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Epidendrum tandapianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
