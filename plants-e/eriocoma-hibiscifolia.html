Eriocoma hibiscifolia

Eriocoma hibiscifolia, commonly known as the woolly leafed hibiscus, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, fuzzy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a striking, low-maintenance plant.
