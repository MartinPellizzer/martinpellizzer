Eugenia grandisepala

Eugenia grandisepala, commonly known as the large-sepal eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pinkish flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that are typically red when mature.

Eugenia grandisepala is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
