Eugenia djalonensis

Eugenia djalonensis, commonly known as the West African myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale pink flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

Eugenia djalonensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
