Eragrostis multispicula

Eragrostis multispicula, commonly known as slender false brome, is a grass species native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Eragrostis multispicula is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for native wildlife.
