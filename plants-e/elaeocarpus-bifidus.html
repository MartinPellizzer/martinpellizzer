Elaeocarpus bifidus

Elaeocarpus bifidus, commonly known as the two-lobed elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands, including New Guinea and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits into two parts at maturity, revealing edible seeds.

This species is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological importance.
