Eugenia chequen

Eugenia chequen, commonly known as the Quechua myrtle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments at moderate altitudes. Its fruits are small, berry-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Eugenia chequen is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
