Eugenia jambolana

Eugenia jambolana, commonly known as the Java apple or rose apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy dark green surface.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.
