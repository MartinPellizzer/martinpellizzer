Eragrostis pectinacea var. tracyi

Eragrostis pectinacea var. tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Tracy's lovegrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
