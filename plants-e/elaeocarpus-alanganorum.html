Elaeocarpus alanganorum

Elaeocarpus alanganorum, commonly known as the Philippine satinwood, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

Elaeocarpus alanganorum is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland to montane forests.
