Elaeocarpus fleuryi

Elaeocarpus fleuryi, commonly known as the Fleurys elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
