Erythrostictus gramineus

Erythrostictus gramineus, commonly known as the grass-leafed eriophyid mite, is a tiny arachnid belonging to the family Eriophyidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, worm-like body and is typically covered in fine, overlapping scales. This mite is primarily found on grasses and related plants, where it feeds on plant tissues, often causing galls or deformities. Its life cycle involves several stages, including egg, larva, protonymph, and adult, all of which are completed on the host plant.

Due to its small size and specialized habitat, it plays a role in the ecological interactions of grassland ecosystems.
