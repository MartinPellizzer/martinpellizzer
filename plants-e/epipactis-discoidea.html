Epipactis discoidea

Epipactis discoidea, commonly known as the disk orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a disk. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with a central white or pale yellow disk.

Epipactis discoidea is known for its ability to thrive in wet conditions and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
