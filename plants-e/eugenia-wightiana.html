Eugenia wightiana

Eugenia wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's evergreen tree, is a species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries.

Eugenia wightiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
