Erica fascicularis

Erica fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils, often in coastal or mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple, blooming in late summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in dense clusters.

Erica fascicularis is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
