Embelia baronii

Embelia baronii, commonly known as the Indian embelia or wild pepper, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include aromatic leaves and a distinctive, spicy scent, which contribute to its use in herbal remedies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
