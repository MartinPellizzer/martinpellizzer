Eucalyptus pyrophora var. compacta

Eucalyptus pyrophora var. compacta, commonly known as the compact eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

The species is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its use in traditional and contemporary herbal medicine.
