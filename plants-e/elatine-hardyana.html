Elatine hardyana

Elatine hardyana, commonly known as the hardy waterwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has thin, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Elatine hardyana is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, moist soils and its rapid growth rate.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its tolerance of various water conditions.
