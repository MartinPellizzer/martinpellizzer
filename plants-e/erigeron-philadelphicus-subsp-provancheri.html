Erigeron philadelphicus subsp. provancheri

Erigeron philadelphicus subsp.

provancheri, commonly known as the Philadelphia fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and roadside ditches. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom from late summer into early fall.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth form and slightly larger flower heads compared to other members of the species.
