Echinops sylvicola

Echinops sylvicola, commonly known as the woodland thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are prominent in late summer and autumn. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by the distinctive, bristly fruiting bodies. Echinops sylvicola typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and scrub habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
