Erica erectiuscula

Erica erectiuscula, commonly known as the upright heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves and its profusion of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal fynbos vegetation.

Erica erectiuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
