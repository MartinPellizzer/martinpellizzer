Euphorbia mbuinzauensis

Euphorbia mbuinzauensis, commonly known as the Mbuinzauensis spurges, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are typically green or yellowish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or green, surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
