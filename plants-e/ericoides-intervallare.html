Ericoides intervallare

Ericoides intervallare, commonly known as the mountain heather, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pink or white in color. Ericoides intervallare thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
