Eurytaenia hinckleyi

Eurytaenia hinckleyi, commonly known as the Hinckley's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Eurytaenia hinckleyi plays a role in supporting local pollinators, including butterflies and bees.
