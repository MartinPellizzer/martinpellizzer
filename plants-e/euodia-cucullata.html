Euodia cucullata

Euodia cucullata, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by dark purple berries. Euodia cucullata is valued for its medicinal properties, with its bark and leaves used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
