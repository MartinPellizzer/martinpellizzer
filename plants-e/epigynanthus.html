Epigynanthus

Epigynanthus, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their showy, tubular flowers that often come in a variety of colors. The leaves are typically velvety and heart-shaped, providing a striking contrast to the blooms. Epigynanthus species are usually grown as houseplants due to their preference for warm, humid environments.

They have a compact growth habit and are popular for their ornamental value.
