Eupatorium alpestre

Eupatorium alpestre, commonly known as alpine eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers.

Eupatorium alpestre is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
