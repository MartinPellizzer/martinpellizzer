Eupatorium oligadenium

Eupatorium oligadenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called cymes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
