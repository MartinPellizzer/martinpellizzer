Echinops fedtschenkoi

Echinops fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Fedtschenko's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces yellow flowers in the summer.

Echinops fedtschenkoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
