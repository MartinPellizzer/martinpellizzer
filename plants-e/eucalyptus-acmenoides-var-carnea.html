Eucalyptus acmenoides var. carnea

Eucalyptus acmenoides var. carnea, commonly known as the pink-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink to red foliage, which is often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, typically appearing in winter. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, fibrous strips, and it is commonly found in dry, sandy soils.

This variety is part of the Eucalyptus genus, known for its aromatic oils and ecological importance in Australian ecosystems.
