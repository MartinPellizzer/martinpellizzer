Eremogone fendleri var. porteri

Eremogone fendleri var. porteri, commonly known as the Desert Evening Primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
