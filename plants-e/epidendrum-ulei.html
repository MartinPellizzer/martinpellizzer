Epidendrum ulei

Epidendrum ulei, commonly known as the yellow epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear showy, yellow flowers with a distinctive red lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a spiral pattern, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Epidendrum ulei is appreciated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
