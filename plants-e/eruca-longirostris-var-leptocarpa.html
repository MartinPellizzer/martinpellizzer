Eruca longirostris var. leptocarpa

Eruca longirostris var. leptocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rocket, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. It has a peppery taste, making it a popular ingredient in salads and traditional cuisines.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it both a weed and a forage plant.
