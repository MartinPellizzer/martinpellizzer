Ericameria discoidea var. winwardii

Ericameria discoidea var. winwardii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
