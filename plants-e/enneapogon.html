Enneapogon

Enneapogon, commonly known as needlegrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Enneapogon species are characterized by their slender, needle-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. They play an important role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.

Some species are also cultivated for their ornamental value or used in traditional medicine.
