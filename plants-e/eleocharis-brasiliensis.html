Eleocharis brasiliensis

Eleocharis brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian spike-rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with slender, cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Eleocharis brasiliensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
