Eschscholzia caespitosa var. hypecoides

Eschscholzia caespitosa var. hypecoides, commonly known as the yellow Indian paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow to orange-red bracts that resemble paintbrushes. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
