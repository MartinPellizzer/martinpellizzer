Eleocharis gracilis var. gracillima

Eleocharis gracilis var. gracillima, commonly known as slender spike-rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common inhabitant of marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
