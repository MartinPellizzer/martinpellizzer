Eupatorium trinitarium

Eupatorium trinitarium, commonly known as the Trinidad evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
