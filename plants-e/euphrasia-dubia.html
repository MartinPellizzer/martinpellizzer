Euphrasia dubia

Euphrasia dubia, commonly known as the white-eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. The plant features delicate, slender stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent, showy eyespots. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Euphrasia dubia is known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
