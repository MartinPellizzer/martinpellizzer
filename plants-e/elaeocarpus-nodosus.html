Elaeocarpus nodosus

Elaeocarpus nodosus, commonly known as the knobwood or nodding elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins. Its growth habit is typically slow, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils within tropical and subtropical climates.

Elaeocarpus nodosus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
