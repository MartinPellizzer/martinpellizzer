Eugenia freireana var. latifolia

Eugenia freireana var. latifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian peppertree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and timber.
