Ensete lecongkietii

Ensete lecongkietii, commonly known as the Cambodian giant banana, is a member of the Musaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of edible bananas, although it is less commonly cultivated than other banana species. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
