Epipremnum nobile

Epipremnum nobile, commonly known as the golden pothos, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that often exhibit a golden-yellow variegation. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and is known for its ability to purify indoor air. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.

Epipremnum nobile is relatively low-maintenance, making it an ideal plant for both novice and experienced gardeners.
