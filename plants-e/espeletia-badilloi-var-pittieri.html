Espeletia badilloi var. pittieri

Espeletia badilloi var. pittieri, commonly known as the Sierra de las Minas heather, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and vibrant purple or pink flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments. It is often found in cloud forests and is notable for its ornamental value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
