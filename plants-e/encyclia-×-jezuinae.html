Encyclia × jezuinae

Encyclia × jezuinae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Encyclia cochleata and Encyclia fraterna.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with a short rhizome and a cluster of fleshy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow with purple or brown markings, appearing in late summer to early fall.

Encyclia × jezuinae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
