Eulalia siamensis

Eulalia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam grass or Siam reed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Eulalia siamensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
