Eugenia inasensis

Eugenia inasensis, commonly known as the Inasense myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. Its fruits are small, fleshy berries that turn red when mature, and they are edible, though not widely consumed.

Eugenia inasensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its use in traditional medicine.
