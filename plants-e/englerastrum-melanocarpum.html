Englerastrum melanocarpum

Englerastrum melanocarpum, commonly known as the black-fruited Englerastrum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, dark-colored fruits that are distinctive in appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various insect species.
