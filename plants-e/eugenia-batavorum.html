Eugenia batavorum

Eugenia batavorum, commonly known as the Java apple or Java guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark-barked trunk and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. These berries are rich in vitamin C and have been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.

Eugenia batavorum thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.
