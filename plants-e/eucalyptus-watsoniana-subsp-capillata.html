Eucalyptus watsoniana subsp. capillata

Eucalyptus watsoniana subsp.

capillata, commonly known as the slender-leafed mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to the arid regions of Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs.

The flowers are typically small and white or pale blue, arranged in clusters along the branches. This subspecies is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
