Eriocaulon vaginatum

Eriocaulon vaginatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a member of the Poales order and the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense, hairy layer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the lower leaves often forming a sheath around the stem.

The flowers are small and clustered in spike-like inflorescences, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
