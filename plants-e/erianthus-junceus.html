Erianthus junceus

Erianthus junceus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-white plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its silvery sheen. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Erianthus junceus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators and its use in traditional crafts and floral arrangements.
