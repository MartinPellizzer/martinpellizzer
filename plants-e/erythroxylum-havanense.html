Erythroxylum havanense

Erythroxylum havanense, commonly known as the Cuban copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes, including medicinal applications. Its leaves are often used in local remedies, and the plant plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.

Erythroxylum havanense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
