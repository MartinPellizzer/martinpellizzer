Eleocharis vincentina var. arcuata

Eleocharis vincentina var. arcuata, commonly known as the arcuate spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowering structures. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, thriving in environments with standing water or saturated soil. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and the inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
