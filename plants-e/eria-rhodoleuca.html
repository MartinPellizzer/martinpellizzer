Eria rhodoleuca

Eria rhodoleuca, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features a distinctive pink or purple flower with a slipper-like pouch that attracts pollinators. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
