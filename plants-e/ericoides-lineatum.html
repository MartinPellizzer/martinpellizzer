Ericoides lineatum

Ericoides lineatum, commonly known as the linear leaf ericoid, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in early spring. Ericoides lineatum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.

It is an important species for local biodiversity, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
