Eriocaulon hookerianum

Eriocaulon hookerianum, commonly known as the Hooker's spike-rush, is a small, aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence that bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the margins of freshwater bodies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, often submerged or floating.

Eriocaulon hookerianum plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
