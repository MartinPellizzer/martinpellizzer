Echinodorus longipetalus

Echinodorus longipetalus, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrowhead, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often submerged, with a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is often used in aquascaping.

Echinodorus longipetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
