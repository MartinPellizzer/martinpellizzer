Eremostachys hissarica

Eremostachys hissarica, commonly known as the Karakum feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces pale green to blue flowers, which bloom in the summer months.

Eremostachys hissarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
