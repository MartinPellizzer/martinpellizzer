Eragrostis striata

Eragrostis striata, commonly known as bluegrass or slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides.

Eragrostis striata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
