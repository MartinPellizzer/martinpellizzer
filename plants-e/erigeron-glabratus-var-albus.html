Erigeron glabratus var. albus

Erigeron glabratus var. albus, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale white to pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer and are arranged in a composite head. The plant has fine, hairy leaves and a branched stem, often growing in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its showy inflorescences and medicinal properties in some species.
