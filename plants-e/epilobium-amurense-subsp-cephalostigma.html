Epilobium amurense subsp. cephalostigma

Epilobium amurense subsp.

cephalostigma, commonly known as the purple-flowered willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, often shaded environments such as stream banks and forest edges.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, showy purple to pink flowers. Its flowers are arranged in terminal spikes and are pollinated primarily by insects.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
