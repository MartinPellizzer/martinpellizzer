Eucalyptus rostrata var. studleyensis

Eucalyptus rostrata var. studleyensis, commonly known as the Studleyensis malleable, is a variant of the narrow-leaf malleable eucalyptus.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular inflorescences that typically bloom in white or pale yellow. Its bark is smooth and often peels in thin, fibrous strips.

This variant is native to the eastern regions of Australia and is adapted to a range of soil types and climatic conditions.
