Erythroxylum echinodendron

Erythroxylum echinodendron, commonly known as the prickly poppy, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its spiny stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain small, black seeds.

Erythroxylum echinodendron is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
