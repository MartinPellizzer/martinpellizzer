Eysenhardtia subcoriacea

Eysenhardtia subcoriacea, commonly known as the desert hackberry, is a shrub or small tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by small, round, berry-like fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access groundwater.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.
