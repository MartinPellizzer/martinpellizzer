Eugenia planipes

Eugenia planipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are typically followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
