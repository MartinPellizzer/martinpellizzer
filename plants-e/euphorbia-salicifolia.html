Euphorbia salicifolia

Euphorbia salicifolia, commonly known as the willow leaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, willow-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green花朵 that are clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. It is often found in rocky or dry soils, and it has a relatively slow growth rate.

Euphorbia salicifolia is also noted for its ornamental value in gardens due to its distinctive foliage and unique flowering structure.
