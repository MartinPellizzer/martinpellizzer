Erythrina spathacea

Erythrina spathacea, commonly known as the flame tree or the red flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, and its trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptical leaflets. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types.

Erythrina spathacea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.
