Echium strictum subsp. exasperatum

Echium strictum subsp.

exasperatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thimbleweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has erect, sparsely branched stems.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers in terminal clusters. Its flowers are tubular and are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects.

The species is characterized by its unique, thimble-like flower shape, which gives it its common name.
