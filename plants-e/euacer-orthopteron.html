Euacer orthopteron

Euacer orthopteron, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and orange petals, which are adorned with intricate patterns that resemble flames. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers typically bloom during the summer months, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.
