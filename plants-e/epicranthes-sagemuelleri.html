Epicranthes sagemuelleri

Epicranthes sagemuelleri, commonly known as the mountain morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates and well-drained soils. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Epicranthes sagemuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
