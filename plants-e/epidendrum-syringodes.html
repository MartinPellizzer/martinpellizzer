Epidendrum syringodes

Epidendrum syringodes, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling slipper-like structures. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, and has a sympodial growth habit. Its roots are often mycorrhizal, forming symbiotic relationships with fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
