Eichhornia diversifolia

Eichhornia diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf water hyacinth, is a floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it float on water. The plant produces small, purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual element to freshwater environments. It is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats on the water surface.

Eichhornia diversifolia is often found in still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes.
