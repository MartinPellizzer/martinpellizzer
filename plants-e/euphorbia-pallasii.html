Euphorbia pallasii

Euphorbia pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-gray. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized leaf-like structures called cyathia. Euphorbia pallasii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
