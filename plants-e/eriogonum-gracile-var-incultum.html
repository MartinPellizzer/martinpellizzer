Eriogonum gracile var. incultum

Eriogonum gracile var. incultum, commonly known as the slenderleaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, pale green leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for pollinators and a valuable component of native plant communities.
