Eugenia panduriformis

Eugenia panduriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
