Eupatorium costatipes

Eupatorium costatipes, commonly known as the zigzag goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive zigzagging stem and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium costatipes is often found in wet or swampy areas and is considered a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
