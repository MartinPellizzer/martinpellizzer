Empusella endotrachys

Empusella endotrachys, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually narrow and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is often found in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Empusella endotrachys is adapted to thrive in arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought conditions.
