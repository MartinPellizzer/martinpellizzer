Elaeocarpus sterrophyilus

Elaeocarpus sterrophyilus, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The fruit is a bright red, berry-like structure that contains several segments, each with a single seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in folk medicine.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
