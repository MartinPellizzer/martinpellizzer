Elsholtzia splendens f. roseola

Elsholtzia splendens f. roseola, commonly known as the roseola elsholtzia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.
