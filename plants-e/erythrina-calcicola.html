Erythrina calcicola

Erythrina calcicola, commonly known as the rock flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers, which are clustered in racemes and attract various pollinators. The plant thrives in well-drained soils, often found on rocky or sandy substrates, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five pairs of elliptical leaflets, and it produces woody pods that contain several seeds.

Erythrina calcicola is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
