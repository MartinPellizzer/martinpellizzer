Ephedra altissima var. mauritanica

Ephedra altissima var. mauritanica, commonly known as the Mauritanian ephedra, is a shrub native to arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are typically unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
