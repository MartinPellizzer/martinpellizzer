Eriophorum paucinervium

Eriophorum paucinervium, commonly known as the few-veined cottongrass, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy white inflorescences that resemble cotton, which are composed of tiny spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and marshes, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct veining pattern that gives it its scientific name.

Eriophorum paucinervium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing peat soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
