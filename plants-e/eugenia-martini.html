Eugenia martini

Eugenia martini, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia martini*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a pleasant aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small and white, often appearing in clusters, and the plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
