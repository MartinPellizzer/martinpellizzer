Erigeron tergoalbus

Erigeron tergoalbus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
