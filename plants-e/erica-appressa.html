Erica appressa

Erica appressa, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica appressa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
