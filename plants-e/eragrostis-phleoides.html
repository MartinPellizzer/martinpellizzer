Eragrostis phleoides

Eragrostis phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Eragrostis phleoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and visual interest.
