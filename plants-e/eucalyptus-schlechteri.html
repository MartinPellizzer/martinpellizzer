Eucalyptus schlechteri

Eucalyptus schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in winter, attracting various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Eucalyptus schlechteri is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
