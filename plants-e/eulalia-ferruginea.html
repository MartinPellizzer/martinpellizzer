Eulalia ferruginea

Eulalia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty eulalia, is a grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with a reddish or rusty hue.

This plant is typically found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
