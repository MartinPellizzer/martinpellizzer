Erysimum cheiranthoides

Erysimum cheiranthoides, commonly known as the yellow wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
