Eugenia quadrialata

Eugenia quadrialata, commonly known as the four-winged eucalyptus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia quadrialata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species in coastal and sandy soils.
