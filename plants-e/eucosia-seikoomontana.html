Eucosia seikoomontana

Eucosia seikoomontana, commonly known as the mountain eucosia, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in poor, well-drained soils.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
