Eriogonum wetherillii

Eriogonum wetherillii, commonly known as the Wetherill's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Eriogonum wetherillii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush communities.
