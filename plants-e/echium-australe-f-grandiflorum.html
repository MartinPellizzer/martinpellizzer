Echium australe f. grandiflorum

Echium australe f. grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered bush morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
