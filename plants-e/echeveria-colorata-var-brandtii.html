Echeveria colorata var. brandtii

Echeveria colorata var. brandtii, commonly known as the Brandt's Blue Echeveria, is a striking succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine, powdery coating, known as a bloom. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
