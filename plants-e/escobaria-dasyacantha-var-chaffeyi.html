Escobaria dasyacantha var. chaffeyi

Escobaria dasyacantha var. chaffeyi, commonly known as Chaffey's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of spiny, cylindrical stems that resemble a hedgehog. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.

This variety is well adapted to rocky soils and full sun, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
