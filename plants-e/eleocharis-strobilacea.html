Eleocharis strobilacea

Eleocharis strobilacea, commonly known as the spike rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that are often clustered in dense tufts, with small, spike-like inflorescences at the tips. Eleocharis strobilacea plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
