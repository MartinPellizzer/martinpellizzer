Echites acuminatus

Echites acuminatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf echites, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Echites acuminatus is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access water from deeper soil layers.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within its native habitat.
