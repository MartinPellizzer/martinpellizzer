Euodia hortensis f. simplicifolia

Euodia hortensis f. simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaved Chinese prickly ash, is a variant of the Euodia genus, which belongs to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its simplified leaf structure compared to other forms of Euodia, featuring smaller, more lanceolate leaves. The plant is native to China and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or yellowish in color.

The fruit is a small, woody drupe that ripens to a dark red or black color, contributing to its use in traditional herbal medicine.
