Endosiphon

Endosiphon, commonly known as the inner siphon, is a specialized structure found in certain aquatic plants.

It plays a crucial role in the plant's ability to absorb and transport nutrients from the surrounding water. The endosiphon is typically composed of elongated, thin-walled cells that facilitate the movement of water and dissolved substances. This structure is particularly important in species that thrive in nutrient-poor environments.

Its presence and function contribute to the overall efficiency of the plant's physiological processes.
