Echeandia altipratensis

Echeandia altipratensis, commonly known as the high plains centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, blue to violet flowers in the summer months. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in mountainous regions.
