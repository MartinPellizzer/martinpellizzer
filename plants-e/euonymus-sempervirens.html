Euonymus sempervirens

Euonymus sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that remain green throughout the growing season. The plant produces bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Euonymus sempervirens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
