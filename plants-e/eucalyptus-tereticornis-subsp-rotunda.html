Eucalyptus tereticornis subsp. rotunda

Eucalyptus tereticornis subsp.

rotunda, commonly known as the round-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and elliptical or rounded leaves that are often blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathland and woodland habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
