Erica leptophylla

Erica leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically a deep green, and it produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. Erica leptophylla thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

It is well adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is a popular plant in native gardens due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
