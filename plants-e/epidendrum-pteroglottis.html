Epidendrum pteroglottis

Epidendrum pteroglottis, commonly known as the orchid with the peculiarly shaped leaves, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a waxy coating that helps retain water.

Epidendrum pteroglottis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in a humid, well-lit environment.
