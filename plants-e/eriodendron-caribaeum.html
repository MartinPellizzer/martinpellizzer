Eriodendron caribaeum

Eriodendron caribaeum, commonly known as the Caribbean flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its vibrant floral display.

The tree also plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
