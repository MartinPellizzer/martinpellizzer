Eriogonum effusum subsp. orbiculatum

Eriogonum effusum subsp.

orbiculatum, commonly known as the roundleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its orbicular, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is known for its resilience to drought.

The subspecies orbiculatum is distinguished by its rounded leaf shape and is often found in California and adjacent regions.
