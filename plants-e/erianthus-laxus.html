Erianthus laxus

Erianthus laxus, commonly known as the tall grass or giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features long, slender, leaf-like blades that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that contain small, spikelet-like flowers. Its fibrous stalks are often used in construction and as a source of fiber.

Erianthus laxus thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along riverbanks.
