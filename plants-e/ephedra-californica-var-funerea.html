Ephedra californica var. funerea

Ephedra californica var. funerea, commonly known as California jointfir, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellow-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red-brown seed pods. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open woodlands.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
