Eugenia pyrifera

Eugenia pyrifera, commonly known as the Natal plum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The tree is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
