Erica mackayi var. plena

Erica mackayi var. plena, commonly known as the full-blown heath, is a compact evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and its profuse display of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and lavender. The plant produces small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Erica mackayi var. plena is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
