Excoecaria polyandra

Excoecaria polyandra, commonly known as the red hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its surroundings. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Excoecaria polyandra is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
