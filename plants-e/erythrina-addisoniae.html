Erythrina addisoniae

Erythrina addisoniae, commonly known as the Red Bower, is a striking leguminous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers, which are clustered in dense, sweeping panicles. The plant has feathery, trifoliate leaves that contribute to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Erythrina addisoniae is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, enhancing soil fertility.
