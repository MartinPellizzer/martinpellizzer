Erythronium albidum var. coloratum

Erythronium albidum var. coloratum, commonly known as the white dog-tooth violet, is a variant of the dog-tooth violet species native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its pale green to white flowers, which are typically bell-shaped and bloom in early spring. The plant features a rosette of heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are evergreen in some climates. It thrives in woodland environments with moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
