Erigeron acris subsp. serotinus

Erigeron acris subsp.

serotinus, commonly known as pale coneflower, is a perennial herb native to North America. It is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves.

The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disc florets, blooming from late summer to early fall. It thrives in dry to moist meadows and prairies, often in full sun.

Its fibrous roots and ability to reseed make it a hardy and resilient species.
