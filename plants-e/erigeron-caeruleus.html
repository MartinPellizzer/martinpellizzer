Erigeron caeruleus

Erigeron caeruleus, commonly known as blueweed or blue fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as fields, meadows, and roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
