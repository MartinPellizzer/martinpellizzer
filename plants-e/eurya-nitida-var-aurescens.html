Eurya nitida var. aurescens

Eurya nitida var. aurescens, commonly known as the golden eurya, is a flowering plant species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface, while the plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in shaded to semi-shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
