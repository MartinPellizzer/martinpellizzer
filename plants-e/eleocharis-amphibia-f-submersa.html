Eleocharis amphibia f. submersa

Eleocharis amphibia f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged spikerush, is a variant of the spikerush species adapted to aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its fully submerged growth habit, with elongated, cylindrical stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically lacks true leaves, instead having narrow, grass-like structures that facilitate buoyancy and light absorption in water. It thrives in wetlands, marshes, and slow-moving water bodies, contributing to aquatic ecosystem stability.

Its presence is often an indicator of good water quality and habitat health.
