Eleocharis carniolica var. macrostachya

Eleocharis carniolica var. macrostachya, commonly known as the broad-stalked spike rush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like and often have a bluish-green color, while the flowers are usually pale green to brown.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to waterlogged soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
