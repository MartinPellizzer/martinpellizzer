Echinochloa paludigena

Echinochloa paludigena, commonly known as swamp panic, is a grass species native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Echinochloa paludigena plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
