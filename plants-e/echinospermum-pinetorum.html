Echinospermum pinetorum

Echinospermum pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple weed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant has hairy, elongated leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into spiny, oval-shaped fruits. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it easily distinguishable from other similar plants in its native habitat.
