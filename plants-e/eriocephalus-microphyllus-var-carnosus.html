Eriocephalus microphyllus var. carnosus

Eriocephalus microphyllus var. carnosus, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features aromatic, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a soft, hairy texture. The plant produces small, white, umbellate flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators. Its roots are known for their distinctive, licorice-like scent and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
