Eupatorium conyzoides var. incanum

Eupatorium conyzoides var. incanum, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for various pollinators.
