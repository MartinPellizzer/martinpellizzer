Eryngium pseudojunceum var. crantzioides

Eryngium pseudojunceum var. crantzioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
