Eucalyptus piperita subsp. urceolaris

Eucalyptus piperita subsp.

urceolaris, commonly known as the mountain gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous underneath.

The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
