Echinocereus pulchellus var. acanthosetus

Echinocereus pulchellus var. acanthosetus, commonly known as the yellow night blooming cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus with a distinctive yellow coloration, particularly during its flowering period. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it well-suited for arid environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh desert conditions.
