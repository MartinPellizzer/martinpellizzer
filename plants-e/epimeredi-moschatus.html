Epimeredi moschatus

Epimeredi moschatus, commonly known as the musk orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) characterized by its fragrant, often musk-scented flowers.

Native to the tropical regions of Africa, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its flowers are usually large and showy, with intricate patterns and vivid colors that attract specific pollinators. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, Epimeredi moschatus is both cultivated and protected in some regions.
