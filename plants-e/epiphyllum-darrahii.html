Epiphyllum darrahii

Epiphyllum darrahii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is a climbing cactus that produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant is native to Mexico and is often found growing on trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its stems are segmented and covered with sharp spines, while its flowers are typically white or pale pink.

Epiphyllum darrahii is valued in horticulture for its dramatic floral display and unique growth form.
