Euklisia violacea

Euklisia violacea, commonly known as the violet glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant touch to gardens. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, ornamental fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Euklisia violacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
