Erica pilosa

Erica pilosa, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Its leaves are densely covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

Erica pilosa is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
