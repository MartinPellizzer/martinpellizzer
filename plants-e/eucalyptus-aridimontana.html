Eucalyptus aridimontana

Eucalyptus aridimontana, commonly known as the sturt's desert peeny, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Eucalyptus aridimontana is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low rainfall and high temperatures, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its essential oils, like those of other eucalyptus species, have been studied for potential medicinal and aromatic uses.
