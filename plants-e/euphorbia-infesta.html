Euphorbia infesta

Euphorbia infesta, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Euphorbia infesta is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
