Erophila rubrinaeva

Erophila rubrinaeva, commonly known as the red-stemmed erophila, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale yellow to greenish flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
