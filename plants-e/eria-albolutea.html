Eria albolutea

Eria albolutea, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers with white petals and a yellow lip. Eria albolutea thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
