Eugenia longuensis

Eugenia longuensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Cerejeira-do-Brazil*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, cherry-like fruits.

The fruits are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
