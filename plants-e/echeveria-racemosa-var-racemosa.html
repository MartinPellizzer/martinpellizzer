Echeveria racemosa var. racemosa

Echeveria racemosa var. racemosa, commonly known as the racemose echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces tall, branching stems with clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often used in container gardening as well.
