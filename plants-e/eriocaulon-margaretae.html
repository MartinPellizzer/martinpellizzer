Eriocaulon margaretae

Eriocaulon margaretae, commonly known as the Margaretae spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of slow-moving water bodies. Its roots help stabilize soil and improve water quality by filtering sediments.

Eriocaulon margaretae is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, nutrient-rich environments.
