Echinodendrum

Echinodendrum, commonly known as the horned milkweed, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apocynaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny seed pods and milky sap, which is a common feature among members of the Apocynaceae family. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a variety of colors including yellow, orange, and red.

Echinodendrum species are native to regions of the Americas, particularly in the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.
