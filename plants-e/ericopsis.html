Ericopsis

Ericopsis, commonly known as heathgrass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Ericaceae.

These plants are typically shrubs or herbaceous plants, characterized by their small, needle-like leaves and clustered flowers. Ericopsis species are often found in acidic soils, thriving in rocky or sandy environments. They are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and North America.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for ornamental purposes due to their attractive foliage and flowers.
