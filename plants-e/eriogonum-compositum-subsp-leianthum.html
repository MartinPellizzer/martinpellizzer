Eriogonum compositum subsp. leianthum

Eriogonum compositum subsp.

leianthum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and shrublands. It produces small, oval leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
