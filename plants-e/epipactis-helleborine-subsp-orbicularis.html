Epipactis helleborine subsp. orbicularis

Epipactis helleborine subsp.

orbicularis, commonly known as the round-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is characterized by its broad, oval-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip that often has a purple or brownish hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows.

This subspecies is notable for its ability to mimic the appearance of surrounding vegetation, aiding in its survival through camouflage.
