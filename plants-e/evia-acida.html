Evia acida

Evia acida, commonly known as sour evia, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Evia in the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.
