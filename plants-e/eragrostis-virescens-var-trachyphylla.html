Eragrostis virescens var. trachyphylla

Eragrostis virescens var. trachyphylla, commonly known as rough false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, hairy texture, which gives it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
