Elaeocarpus heinrichii

Elaeocarpus heinrichii, commonly known as the Heilala or Heilala tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and montane rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
