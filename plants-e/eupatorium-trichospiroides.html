Eupatorium trichospiroides

Eupatorium trichospiroides, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
