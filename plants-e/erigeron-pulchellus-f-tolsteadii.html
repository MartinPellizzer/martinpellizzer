Erigeron pulchellus f. tolsteadii

Erigeron pulchellus f. tolsteadii, commonly known as Tolstee's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features blue to purple ray florets and yellow disk florets, arranged in a single, showy inflorescence. It grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
