Escallonia sellowiana var. spiraeifolia

Escallonia sellowiana var. spiraeifolia, commonly known as the spiralflower escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pink, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to coastal and Mediterranean climates.
