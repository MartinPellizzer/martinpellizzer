Englerophytum paludosum

Englerophytum paludosum, commonly known as the swamp evening-primrose, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is characterized by its yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
