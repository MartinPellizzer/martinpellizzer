Ericoides glaucum

Ericoides glaucum, commonly known as the blueberry shrub, is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other acid-loving plants.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a small, dark blue or black berry that is edible and rich in antioxidants.

Ericoides glaucum thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas or rocky habitats.
