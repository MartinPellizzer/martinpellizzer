Enarthrocarpus clavatus var. leiocarpus

Enarthrocarpus clavatus var. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-seeded ironwood, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it produces woody, barrel-shaped fruits.

The species is adapted to humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
