Erica penduliflora

Erica penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that hang from the branches. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing fragrant, pale pink to white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica penduliflora is often used in rock gardens and as a container plant due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
