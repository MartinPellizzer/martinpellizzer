Elaeocarpus myrtoides subsp. myrtoides

Elaeocarpus myrtoides subsp.

myrtoides, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia and northern Australia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical rainforests.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, red flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic features.
