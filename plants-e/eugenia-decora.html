Eugenia decora

Eugenia decora, commonly known as the beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn. It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds, contributing to local biodiversity.
