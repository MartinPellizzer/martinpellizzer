Echinocactus megalothelon

Echinocactus megalothelon, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces large, showy yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its striking appearance and unique features, it is a popular choice for succulent collections.
