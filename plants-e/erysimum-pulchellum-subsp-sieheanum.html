Erysimum pulchellum subsp. sieheanum

Erysimum pulchellum subsp.

sieheanum, commonly known as the Siehean wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in alpine or subalpine regions. Its flowers are bright yellow, with a distinctive dark central stripe, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
