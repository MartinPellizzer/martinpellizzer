Echium glabrescens

Echium glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Echium glabrescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
