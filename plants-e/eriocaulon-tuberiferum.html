Eriocaulon tuberiferum

Eriocaulon tuberiferum, commonly known as the tuberous spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its tufted, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces tubers underground, which serve as storage organs and are sometimes used in traditional medicine. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and swampy areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.

Its morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitats.
