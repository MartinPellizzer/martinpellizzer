Elwendia

Elwendia, commonly known as the moonflower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped blooms. Elwendia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires support for its vine-like growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal beauty.
