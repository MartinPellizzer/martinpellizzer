Eugenia vitiensis

Eugenia vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly parts of Fiji and other islands in the region. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are typically eaten fresh or used in local cuisines.

The tree is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
