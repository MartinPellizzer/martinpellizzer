Eugenia jossinia

Eugenia jossinia, commonly known as the Jossinia guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer and is edible, though not widely cultivated for commercial use.

The plant is often grown as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
