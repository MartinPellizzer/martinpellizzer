Eragrostis brownii var. interrupta

Eragrostis brownii var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted browntop grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
