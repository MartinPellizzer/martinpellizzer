Eupatorium erythropappum

Eupatorium erythropappum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has serrated, hairy leaves that give it its common name. Its showy purple to pink flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium erythropappum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
