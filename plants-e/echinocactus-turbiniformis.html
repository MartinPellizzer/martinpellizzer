Echinocactus turbiniformis

Echinocactus turbiniformis, commonly known as the Turbiniform Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in prominent spines. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing at the top of the plant during the warmer months.

Echinocactus turbiniformis is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal water and is well-suited for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
