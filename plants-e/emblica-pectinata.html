Emblica pectinata

Emblica pectinata, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its medicinal properties. Its fruits are used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for their antioxidant and anti-inflammatory effects.

The plant's botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and therapeutic contexts.
