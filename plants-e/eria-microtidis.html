Eria microtidis

Eria microtidis, commonly known as the small-leaved orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, small leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive lip petal that aids in pollination.

Eria microtidis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
