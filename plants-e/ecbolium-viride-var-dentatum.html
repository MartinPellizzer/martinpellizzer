Ecbolium viride var. dentatum

Ecbolium viride var. dentatum, commonly known as the green ecbolium with toothed leaves, is a variant of the ecbolium plant characterized by its distinct leaf morphology.

It features serrated or toothed leaf margins, which distinguish it from other varieties of the species. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments, often found in shaded or semi-shaded areas. Its leaves are usually a vibrant green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variant is of interest to botanists due to its unique leaf structure and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
