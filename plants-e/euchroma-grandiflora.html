Euchroma grandiflora

Euchroma grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered euphorbia, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its unique floral structures and milky sap. The plant features showy, colorful bracts that often resemble flowers, while the actual flowers are small and clustered. Euchroma grandiflora typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters.

Its foliage is usually lance-shaped with a glossy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.
