Eunomia moricandiana

Eunomia moricandiana, commonly known as the Moricandia bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Eunomia moricandiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience.
