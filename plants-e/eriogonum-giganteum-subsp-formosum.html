Eriogonum giganteum subsp. formosum

Eriogonum giganteum subsp.

formosum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, showy white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has palmately divided leaves and produces small, nut-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
