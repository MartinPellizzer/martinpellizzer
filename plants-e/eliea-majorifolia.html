Eliea majorifolia

Eliea majorifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf eliea, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow in color. Its root system is fibrous and well-developed, allowing it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Eliea majorifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
