Espeletia thyrsiformis

Espeletia thyrsiformis, commonly known as the Puya raimondii, is a striking plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of spiky, grass-like leaves. The plant produces a tall, branched inflorescence that bears numerous small, tubular flowers, typically in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and ample sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often referred to as the "Queen of the Andes.".
