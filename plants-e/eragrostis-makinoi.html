Eragrostis makinoi

Eragrostis makinoi, commonly known as Makino's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, thread-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis makinoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
