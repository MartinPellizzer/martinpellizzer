Elaphoglossum nigrosquama

Elaphoglossum nigrosquama, commonly known as the black-squamate holly fern, is a small, delicate fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds. The leaves are typically dark green with a leathery texture and bear small, blackish scales along the veins, giving it its common name. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden settings.
