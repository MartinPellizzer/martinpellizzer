Erigeron dubius var. alpicola

Erigeron dubius var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and silvery-gray foliage. This variety is distinguished by its pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
