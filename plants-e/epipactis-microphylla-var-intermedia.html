Epipactis microphylla var. intermedia

Epipactis microphylla var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. This variant is native to parts of Europe and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and meadows. The plant has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

It is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and moths.
