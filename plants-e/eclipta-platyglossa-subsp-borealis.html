Eclipta platyglossa subsp. borealis

Eclipta platyglossa subsp.

borealis, commonly known as northern false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the northern regions of North America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.
