Eriogonum hoffmannii var. robustius

Eriogonum hoffmannii var. robustius, commonly known as the robust Hoffman's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry slopes and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
