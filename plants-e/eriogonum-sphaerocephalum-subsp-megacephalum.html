Eriogonum sphaerocephalum subsp. megacephalum

Eriogonum sphaerocephalum subsp.

megacephalum, commonly known as the giant globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its large, showy, globe-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are typically alternate and lanceolate in shape. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and slopes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native range.
