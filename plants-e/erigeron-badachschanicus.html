Erigeron badachschanicus

Erigeron badachschanicus, commonly known as the Badakhshan fleabane, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia, particularly found in regions of Tajikistan and Afghanistan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, high-altitude conditions. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially vulnerable species.
