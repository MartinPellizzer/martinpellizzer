Erythraea pauciflora

Erythraea pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered erythraea, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a dense, compact form.

Erythraea pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
