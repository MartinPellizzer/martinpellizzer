Erica obliqua

Erica obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, evergreen leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats.

Erica obliqua is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, compact shrubs.
