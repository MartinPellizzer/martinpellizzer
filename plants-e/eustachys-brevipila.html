Eustachys brevipila

Eustachys brevipila, commonly known as the short-haired panicgrass, is a grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the grass family.

Eustachys brevipila is characterized by its short stature and adaptability to moist environments.
