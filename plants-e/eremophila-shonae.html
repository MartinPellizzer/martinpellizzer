Eremophila shonae

Eremophila shonae, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often prostrate or upright stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. Eremophila shonae is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry gardens.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
