Elcismia verbascifolia

Elcismia verbascifolia, commonly known as the woollyleaf mallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Elcismia verbascifolia is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in native plant gardens.
