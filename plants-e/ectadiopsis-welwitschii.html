Ectadiopsis welwitschii

Ectadiopsis welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitschia plant, is a unique succulent native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. The plant has a short, woody stem with two large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length. It also produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are pollinated by insects.

Ectadiopsis welwitschii is considered a living fossil, with a lineage dating back over 100 million years.
