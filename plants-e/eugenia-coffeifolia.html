Eugenia coffeifolia

Eugenia coffeifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or coffeeleaf, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.

Eugenia coffeifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
