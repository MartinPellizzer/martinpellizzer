Eugenia filiformis var. parvifolia

Eugenia filiformis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, ascending branches and small, lanceolate leaves that are typically glabrous. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value lies in its delicate foliage and showy inflorescences, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, thriving in warm, humid climates.
