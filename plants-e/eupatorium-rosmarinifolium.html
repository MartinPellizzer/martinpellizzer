Eupatorium rosmarinifolium

Eupatorium rosmarinifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has aromatic, silvery-gray leaves that are often mistaken for rosemary, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium rosmarinifolium is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
