Eugenia corcovadensis

Eugenia corcovadensis, commonly known as the wax apple or corcovadense myrtle, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
