Eriocaulon plumale subsp. jaegeri

Eriocaulon plumale subsp.

jaegeri, commonly known as the Jaeger's feather sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in consistently moist to aquatic conditions.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its subspecies status highlights subtle morphological differences from the broader Eriocaulon plumale species.

This plant plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
