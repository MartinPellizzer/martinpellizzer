Engelhardia rigida var. subsimplicifolia

Engelhardia rigida var. subsimplicifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Engelhardia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
