Embelia effusa

Embelia effusa, commonly known as the broadleaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
