Echium tubiferum

Echium tubiferum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and tall, spiny stems that support its showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium tubiferum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
