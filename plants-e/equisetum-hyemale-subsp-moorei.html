Equisetum hyemale subsp. moorei

Equisetum hyemale subsp.

moorei, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family. It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green to reddish in color.

The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are often hidden beneath the leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it one of the oldest living plant species.
