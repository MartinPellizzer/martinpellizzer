Euonymus pendulus

Euonymus pendulus, commonly known as the weeping euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall, adding seasonal interest. Its flowers are inconspicuous, but it produces bright red berries that persist into winter.

Euonymus pendulus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
