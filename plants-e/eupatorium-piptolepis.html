Eupatorium piptolepis

Eupatorium piptolepis, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower clusters that appear in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium piptolepis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
