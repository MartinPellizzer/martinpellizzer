Erysimum asperum var. amoenum

Erysimum asperum var. amoenum, commonly known as the rough wallflower, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems covered in rough, hairy textures, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
