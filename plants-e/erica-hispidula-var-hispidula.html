Erica hispidula var. hispidula

Erica hispidula var. hispidula, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense, compact foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
