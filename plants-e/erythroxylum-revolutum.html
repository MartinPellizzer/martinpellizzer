Erythroxylum revolutum

Erythroxylum revolutum, commonly known as the revolute erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent and have a distinctive revolute margin. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its morphological features and potential ecological roles in its native habitats.
