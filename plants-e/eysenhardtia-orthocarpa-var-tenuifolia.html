Eysenhardtia orthocarpa var. tenuifolia

Eysenhardtia orthocarpa var. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf desert mallow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to drought conditions and extreme temperatures.
