Eucalyptus ochrophylla

Eucalyptus ochrophylla, commonly known as the yellow box, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that often displays a yellowish hue, giving it its common name. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and usually have a bluish-green color. It produces small, globular flowers that are typically cream or pale yellow in color.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open woodlands and heathlands.
