Eugenia memecylifolia

Eugenia memecylifolia, commonly known as the purpleleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark purple leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its striking foliage and floral display.
