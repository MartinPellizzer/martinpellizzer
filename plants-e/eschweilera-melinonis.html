Eschweilera melinonis

Eschweilera melinonis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Erythroxylum melinonis*, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by red, fleshy fruits resembling cherries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions of Brazil.
