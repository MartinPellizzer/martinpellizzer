Erodium chamaedryoides

Erodium chamaedryoides, commonly known as common bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has slender, creeping stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.
