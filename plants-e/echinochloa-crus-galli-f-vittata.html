Echinochloa crus-galli f. vittata

Echinochloa crus-galli f. vittata, commonly known as the striped barnyardgrass, is a variant of the common barnyardgrass, characterized by its distinctive striped leaves.

It is an annual grassy weed native to Africa but has spread to many parts of the world, including Asia, Australia, and the Americas. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields, roadsides, and wetlands. Its growth habit is prostrate to erect, with slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains, which are dispersed by wind.
