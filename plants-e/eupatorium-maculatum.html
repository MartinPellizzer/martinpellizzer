Eupatorium maculatum

Eupatorium maculatum, commonly known as Joe-Pye weed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy purple to pinkish flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped to lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, sunny environments. It is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Joe-Pye weed is valued for its ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators like butterflies and bees.
