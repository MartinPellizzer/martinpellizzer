Elaeocarpus japonicus var. euphlebius

Elaeocarpus japonicus var. euphlebius, commonly known as the Japanese elaeocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
