Erigeron unalaschkensis var. grandis

Erigeron unalaschkensis var. grandis, commonly known as the Alaska Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. This species typically grows in moist, open habitats such as tundra and wet meadows. It is an herbaceous perennial that produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand harsh, cold climates.
