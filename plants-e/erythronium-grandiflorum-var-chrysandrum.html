Erythronium grandiflorum var. chrysandrum

Erythronium grandiflorum var. chrysandrum, commonly known as the golden bulb, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. The plant features strap-like leaves that are broad and lance-shaped, typically emerging in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological value in natural habitats.
