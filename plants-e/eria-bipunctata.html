Eria bipunctata

Eria bipunctata, commonly known as the two-spotted orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers with a distinctive two-spotted pattern on the petals. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a fragrant bloom during the summer months.

Eria bipunctata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
