Eryngium cryptanthum

Eryngium cryptanthum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to the United States, particularly found in the Great Basin and surrounding areas.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and basal rosette of gray-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe habitats. Its unique morphology includes a tall flowering stem that bears a dense cluster of bracts resembling a crown.

Eryngium cryptanthum is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
