Epidendrum aristotelea

Epidendrum aristotelea, commonly known as the Aristotelean orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

Epidendrum aristotelea thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate to high humidity for optimal growth.
