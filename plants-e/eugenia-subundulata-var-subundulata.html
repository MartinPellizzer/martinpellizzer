Eugenia subundulata var. subundulata

Eugenia subundulata var. subundulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eucalypt, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several elliptical seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
