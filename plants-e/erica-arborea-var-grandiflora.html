Erica arborea var. grandiflora

Erica arborea var. grandiflora, commonly known as the tree heath, is a shrub-like plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.

Erica arborea var. grandiflora is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its resilience to drought.
