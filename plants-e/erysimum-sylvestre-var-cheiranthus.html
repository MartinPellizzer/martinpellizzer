Erysimum sylvestre var. cheiranthus

Erysimum sylvestre var. cheiranthus, commonly known as the woodland wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in woodland edges, rocky soils, and open areas, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight. The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and have four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
