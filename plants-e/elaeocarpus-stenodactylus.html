Elaeocarpus stenodactylus

Elaeocarpus stenodactylus, commonly known as the narrow-fingered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that are typically red or yellow when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
