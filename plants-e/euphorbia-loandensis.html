Euphorbia loandensis

Euphorbia loandensis, commonly known as the Loando spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Democratic Republic of the Congo.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure, typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in dry, rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
