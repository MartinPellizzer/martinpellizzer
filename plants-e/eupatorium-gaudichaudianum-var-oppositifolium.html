Eupatorium gaudichaudianum var. oppositifolium

Eupatorium gaudichaudianum var. oppositifolium, commonly known as the opposite-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-blue color. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
