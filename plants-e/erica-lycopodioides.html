Erica lycopodioides

Erica lycopodioides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with dense clusters of small, evergreen leaves. It produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, appearing in late spring or early summer.

Erica lycopodioides is well adapted to harsh, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
