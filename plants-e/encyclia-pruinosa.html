Encyclia pruinosa

Encyclia pruinosa, commonly known as the powdered orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and its distinctive, white or pale green flowers with a velvety texture. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a fine, powdery coating, which gives it its common name.

Encyclia pruinosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
