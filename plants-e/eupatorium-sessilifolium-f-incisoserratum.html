Eupatorium sessilifolium f. incisoserratum

Eupatorium sessilifolium f. incisoserratum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The leaves are typically narrow, sessile, and have a distinctive incised-serrate margin, giving it its specific epithet. It thrives in moist to wet soils in full sun to partial shade, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
