Eupatorium subtetragonum

Eupatorium subtetragonum, commonly known as purplestem false dragonhead, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
