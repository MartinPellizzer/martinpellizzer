Excoecaria brasiliensis

Excoecaria brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
