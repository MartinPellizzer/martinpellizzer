Euonymus giraldii var. genuinus

Euonymus giraldii var. genuinus, commonly known as the American euonymus, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, wooded areas. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to reveal four winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
