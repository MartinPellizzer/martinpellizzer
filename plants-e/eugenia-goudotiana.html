Eugenia goudotiana

Eugenia goudotiana, commonly known as the golden eucalyptus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
