Ericoila flava

Ericoila flava, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its small, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It typically grows in acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Ericoila flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
