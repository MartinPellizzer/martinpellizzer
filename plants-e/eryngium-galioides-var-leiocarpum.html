Eryngium galioides var. leiocarpum

Eryngium galioides var. leiocarpum, commonly known as the smooth-fruited eryngium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, smooth, spherical fruits that are distinctive in appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is a notable species in wetland ecosystems.
