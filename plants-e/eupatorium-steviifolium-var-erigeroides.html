Eupatorium steviifolium var. erigeroides

Eupatorium steviifolium var. erigeroides, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and prairie edges.

White boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antiviral effects.
