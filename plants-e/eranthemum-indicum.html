Eranthemum indicum

Eranthemum indicum, commonly known as the Indian borage, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features opposite, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of blue or purple tubular flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, mint-like fragrance.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and is relatively drought-tolerant.
