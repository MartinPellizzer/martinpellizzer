Erysimum verrucosum var. badghisi

Erysimum verrucosum var. badghisi, commonly known as the Persian wallflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy, gray-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and adaptability to various climates.
