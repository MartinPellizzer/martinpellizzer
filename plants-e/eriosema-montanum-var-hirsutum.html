Eriosema montanum var. hirsutum

Eriosema montanum var. hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirsutum.". The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in umbellate clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
