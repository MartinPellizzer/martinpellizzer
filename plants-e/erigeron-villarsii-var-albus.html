Erigeron villarsii var. albus

Erigeron villarsii var. albus, commonly known as the white fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in native plant gardens.
