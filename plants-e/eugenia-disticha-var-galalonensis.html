Eugenia disticha var. galalonensis

Eugenia disticha var. galalonensis, commonly known as the Galalonensis honeysuckle, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing dense foliage that offers habitat for various wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native ecosystems.
