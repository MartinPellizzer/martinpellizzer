Echinocereus acifer var. trichacanthus

Echinocereus acifer var. trichacanthus, commonly known as the Trichacanthus cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring dense, spiny stems and a compact growth habit. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
