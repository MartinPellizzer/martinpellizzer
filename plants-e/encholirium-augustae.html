Encholirium augustae

Encholirium augustae, commonly known as the Mexican bird of paradise, is a striking plant native to the highlands of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy inflorescences that resemble a bird's head, hence its common name. The plant produces vibrant orange and red flowers, which are pollinated primarily by birds such as hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Encholirium augustae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
