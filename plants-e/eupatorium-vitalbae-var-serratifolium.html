Eupatorium vitalbae var. serratifolium

Eupatorium vitalbae var. serratifolium, commonly known as the serratedleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland areas and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
