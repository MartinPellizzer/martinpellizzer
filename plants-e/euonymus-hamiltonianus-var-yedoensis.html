Euonymus hamiltonianus var. yedoensis

Euonymus hamiltonianus var. yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese euonymus, is a shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by orange-red berries that are ornamental. It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its compact growth habit and winter hardiness.
