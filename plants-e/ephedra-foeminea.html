Ephedra foeminea

Ephedra foeminea, commonly known as the Indian ephedra, is a small, shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Ephedra foeminea has been historically used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
