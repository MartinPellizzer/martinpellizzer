Ervum caesareum

Ervum caesareum, commonly known as Caesar's bean, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods and are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Ervum caesareum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient agriculture.
