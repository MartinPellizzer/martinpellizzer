Erythronium purpurascens

Erythronium purpurascens, commonly known as the red-flowered dog-tooth violet, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features a distinctive, palmately lobed leaf that is often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or reddish in color, with a yellow throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
