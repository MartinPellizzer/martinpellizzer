Eriocoma richardsonii

Eriocoma richardsonii, commonly known as the Richardson's eriocoma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. The plant features a basal rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowering stems rise above the foliage, bearing small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eriocoma richardsonii is adapted to arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
