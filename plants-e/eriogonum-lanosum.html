Eriogonum lanosum

Eriogonum lanosum, commonly known as the woolly buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Eriogonum lanosum is an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
