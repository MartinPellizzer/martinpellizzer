Eupatorium glanduliferum

Eupatorium glanduliferum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and opposite leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium glanduliferum is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
