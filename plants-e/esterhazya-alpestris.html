Esterhazya alpestris

Esterhazya alpestris, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Esterhazya alpestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging, high-altitude conditions.
