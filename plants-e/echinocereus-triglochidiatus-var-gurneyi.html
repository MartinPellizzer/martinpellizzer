Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. gurneyi

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. gurneyi, commonly known as the Gurney's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spiny, cylindrical stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
