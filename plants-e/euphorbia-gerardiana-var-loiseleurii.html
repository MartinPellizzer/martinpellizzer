Euphorbia gerardiana var. loiseleurii

Euphorbia gerardiana var. loiseleurii, commonly known as the Loiseleur's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fleshy, upright stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and is sometimes used in xeriscaping.
