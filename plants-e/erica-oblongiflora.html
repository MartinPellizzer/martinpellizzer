Erica oblongiflora

Erica oblongiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes many shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, where it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring.

Erica oblongiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
