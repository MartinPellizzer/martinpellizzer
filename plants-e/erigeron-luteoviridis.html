Erigeron luteoviridis

Erigeron luteoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-green fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its yellow-green flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
