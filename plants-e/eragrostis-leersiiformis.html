Eragrostis leersiiformis

Eragrostis leersiiformis, commonly known as the slender lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showing adaptability to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it produces pale green to brownish flowers.

Eragrostis leersiiformis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
