Echinocactus hookeri

Echinocactus hookeri, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior that protects it from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
