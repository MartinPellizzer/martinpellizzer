Erica rostrata

Erica rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and evergreen plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica rostrata is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and needle-like leaves.

It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.
