Eragrostis poaeoides

Eragrostis poaeoides, commonly known as bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted inflorescence, which consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Eragrostis poaeoides is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
