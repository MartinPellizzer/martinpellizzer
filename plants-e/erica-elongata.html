Erica elongata

Erica elongata, commonly known as the slender heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica elongata is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and attractive appearance.
