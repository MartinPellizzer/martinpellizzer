Erysimum puberulum

Erysimum puberulum, commonly known as the pubescent wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

Erysimum puberulum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
