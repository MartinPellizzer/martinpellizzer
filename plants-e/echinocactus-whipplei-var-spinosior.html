Echinocactus whipplei var. spinosior

Echinocactus whipplei var. spinosior, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are densely covered with sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The varietal name 'spinosior' refers to its more pronounced spines compared to the typical form of Echinocactus whipplei.
