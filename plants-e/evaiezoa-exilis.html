Evaiezoa exilis

Evaiezoa exilis, commonly known as the slender mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, white pubescence, aiding in moisture retention. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are often clustered together, followed by tiny, fleshy berries that serve as a food source for birds and other animals. Evaiezoa exilis relies on its host for water and nutrients, while still performing photosynthesis to some extent.
