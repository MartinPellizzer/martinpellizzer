Eragrostis subsecunda

Eragrostis subsecunda, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged in a panicle. It produces small, two-lobed flowers that are wind-pollinated, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

This species is often used in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
