Epidendrum choccei

Epidendrum choccei, commonly known as the Chocolate Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety leaves and striking, deep purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.

Epidendrum choccei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
