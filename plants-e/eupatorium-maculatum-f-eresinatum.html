Eupatorium maculatum f. eresinatum

Eupatorium maculatum f. eresinatum, commonly known as the purple coneflower or New England aster, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall, typically in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and native plant landscapes.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees adds ecological value to the environment.
