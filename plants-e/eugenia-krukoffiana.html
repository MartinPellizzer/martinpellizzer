Eugenia krukoffiana

Eugenia krukoffiana, commonly known as the Krukoff's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn red.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid climates.
