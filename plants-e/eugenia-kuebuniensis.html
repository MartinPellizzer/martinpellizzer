Eugenia kuebuniensis

Eugenia kuebuniensis, commonly known as the Kuebuniensis guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and oval-shaped fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits, which have a unique flavor profile.
