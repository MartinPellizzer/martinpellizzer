Eria wollastonii

Eria wollastonii, commonly known as the Wollaston's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white or pale yellow blossoms that are clustered in racemose inflorescences. Eria wollastonii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
