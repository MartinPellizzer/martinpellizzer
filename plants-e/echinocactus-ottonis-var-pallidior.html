Echinocactus ottonis var. pallidior

Echinocactus ottonis var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale otton's cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which house the plant's defensive spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green, ribbed structure. It is well adapted to dry environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
