Eugenia zelayensis

Eugenia zelayensis, commonly known as the Zelaya eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in regions of Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruits are typically small, round, and edible, with a sweet and acidic flavor.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
