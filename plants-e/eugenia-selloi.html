Eugenia selloi

Eugenia selloi, commonly known as the Sello's evergreen or Sello's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia selloi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
