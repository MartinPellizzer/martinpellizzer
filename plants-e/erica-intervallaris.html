Erica intervallaris

Erica intervallaris, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

Erica intervallaris is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
