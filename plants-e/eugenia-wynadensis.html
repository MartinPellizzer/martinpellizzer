Eugenia wynadensis

Eugenia wynadensis, commonly known as the Wynad guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their therapeutic properties.
