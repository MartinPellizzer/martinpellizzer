Erigeron komarovii var. koraginensis

Erigeron komarovii var. koraginensis, commonly known as the Korean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Korean Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and streambanks, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer months.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
