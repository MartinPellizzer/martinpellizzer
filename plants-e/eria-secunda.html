Eria secunda

Eria secunda, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid environments. Its flowers are fragrant and bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eria secunda is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its traditional uses in some cultures.
