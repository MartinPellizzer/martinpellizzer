Eugenia striata

Eugenia striata, commonly known as the pink glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy pink or red flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like birds and bees. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia striata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
