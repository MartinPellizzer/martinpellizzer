Eugenia tapiraguayensis

Eugenia tapiraguayensis, commonly known as the Tapiraguay holly, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Paraguay and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, edible berries.

The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
