Eranthemum decurrens

Eranthemum decurrens, commonly known as the creeping eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its spreading habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom during the warm seasons. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Eranthemum decurrens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
