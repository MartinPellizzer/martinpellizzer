Equilabium viphyense

Equilabium viphyense, commonly known as the tropical orchid of the rainforest, is a species native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, often arranged in clusters. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil of its habitat.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical research and conservation efforts.
