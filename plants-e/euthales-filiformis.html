Euthales filiformis

Euthales filiformis, commonly known as the slender leech plant, is a unique aquatic species characterized by its thread-like, root-like structures that resemble leeches.

It is native to freshwater environments in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in slow-moving or still waters. The plant lacks true leaves and instead has elongated, ribbon-like structures that serve as both foraging and anchoring organs. Euthales filiformis plays a role in nutrient cycling by absorbing dissolved organic matter from the water.

Its specialized morphology allows it to survive in low-light conditions and nutrient-poor environments.
