Erycibe laurifolia

Erycibe laurifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf erycibe, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its glossy, dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
