Eremophila calycina

Eremophila calycina, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila calycina is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
