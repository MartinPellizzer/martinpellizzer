Ericomyrtus tenuior

Ericomyrtus tenuior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tea tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Ericomyrtus tenuior is typically found in heathlands and dry forests, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.
