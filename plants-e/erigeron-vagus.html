Erigeron vagus

Erigeron vagus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and streambanks. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer and early fall, attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees.

The foliage is generally gray-green and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
