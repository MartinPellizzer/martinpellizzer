Eragrostis autumnalis

Eragrostis autumnalis, commonly known as autumn panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a grass species that typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, and it is often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Eragrostis autumnalis is known for its adaptability and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
