Eulalia capensis

Eulalia capensis, commonly known as the Cape reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to South Africa.

It features narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found along riverbanks and in coastal areas.

Eulalia capensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland ecosystems.
