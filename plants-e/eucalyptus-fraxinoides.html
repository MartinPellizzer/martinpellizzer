Eucalyptus fraxinoides

Eucalyptus fraxinoides, commonly known as the white stringybark, is a species of eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, white to pale grey bark that peels in long, stringy strips. The plant typically grows as a tall, slender tree with lance-shaped, grey-green leaves. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and are arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The tree is often found in dry sclerophyll forests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.
