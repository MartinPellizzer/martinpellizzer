Eleusine glaucophylla

Eleusine glaucophylla, commonly known as blueleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny grains. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
