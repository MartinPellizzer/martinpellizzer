Erica bandonia

Erica bandonia, commonly known as the Cape snowball, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded shrub form. The plant produces clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding a striking visual contrast to its dark green foliage. Erica bandonia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
