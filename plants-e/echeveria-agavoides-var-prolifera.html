Echeveria agavoides var. prolifera

Echeveria agavoides var. prolifera, commonly known as the Mexican Firecracker, is a compact, succulent perennial native to Mexico.

It features dense, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green with a powdery coating, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces vibrant pink to orange flowers in late spring to early summer, which are borne on tall, branching stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its striking color and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container plantings.
