Epidendrum bernoullii

Epidendrum bernoullii, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and green striped petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months.

Epidendrum bernoullii requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
