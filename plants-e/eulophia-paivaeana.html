Eulophia paivaeana

Eulophia paivaeana, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a species native to Brazil, particularly found in the Atlantic Forest region.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, arranged in a raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is often cultivated in conservation programs.
