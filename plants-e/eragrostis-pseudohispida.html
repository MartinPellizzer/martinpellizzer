Eragrostis pseudohispida

Eragrostis pseudohispida, commonly known as the hairy lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis pseudohispida is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient plant in harsh environments.
