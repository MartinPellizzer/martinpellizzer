Erica bryantha

Erica bryantha, commonly known as the white heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica bryantha thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
