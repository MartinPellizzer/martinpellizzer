Eleusine kigeziensis

Eleusine kigeziensis, commonly known as Kigezi lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
