Echinochloa hostii

Echinochloa hostii, commonly known as the Mexican needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Echinochloa hostii is known for its resilience and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
