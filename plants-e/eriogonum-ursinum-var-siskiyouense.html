Eriogonum ursinum var. siskiyouense

Eriogonum ursinum var. siskiyouense, commonly known as the Siskiyou buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small insects and animals.
