Engelhardia pterocarpa

Engelhardia pterocarpa, commonly known as the Queensland nut, is a small evergreen tree native to northern Australia and parts of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

Its unique botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
