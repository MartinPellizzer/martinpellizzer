Eurybia glauca var. glauca

Eurybia glauca var. glauca, commonly known as blue eyed grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, bluish-gray leaves and produces small, showy blue or violet flowers in the summer. The plant is adapted to moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it spreads through rhizomes and seeds.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
