Eucalyptus occidentalis var. macrantha

Eucalyptus occidentalis var. macrantha, commonly known as the silver dollar eucalyptus, is a tall, evergreen tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, silver-gray bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine white bloom, giving them a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and clustered in umbels, producing a sweet, aromatic scent.

This species is valued for its timber, ornamental appeal, and ecological benefits in native habitats.
