Eriogonum ovalifolium var. ochroleucum

Eriogonum ovalifolium var. ochroleucum, commonly known as the yellow-leaved globemallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and leaves that are oval-shaped with a hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its range.
