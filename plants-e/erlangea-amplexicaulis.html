Erlangea amplexicaulis

Erlangea amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping leaf erlangea, is a unique member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that wrap around the stem in a tight, clasping manner. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, a typical structure for plants in this family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
