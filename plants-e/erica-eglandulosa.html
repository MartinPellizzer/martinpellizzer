Erica eglandulosa

Erica eglandulosa, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica eglandulosa is well adapted to coastal and mountainous environments, often forming dense thickets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in sandy or rocky habitats.
