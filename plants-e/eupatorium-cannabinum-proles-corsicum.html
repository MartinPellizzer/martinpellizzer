Eupatorium cannabinum proles corsicum

Eupatorium cannabinum proles corsicum, commonly known as Corsican hemp, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

This species is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
