Ecballium elaterium var. palmatipartitum

Ecballium elaterium var. palmatipartitum, commonly known as the palmate leaf cocklebur, is a variant of the cocklebur plant characterized by its distinctive palmate leaves.

It is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into spiky, ball-shaped fruits known as "cocklebur" fruits. These fruits have a unique mechanism for seed dispersal, as they can explosively release their seeds when touched.

The leaves are deeply lobed, giving the plant a fern-like appearance, and it is often considered a weed in agricultural settings.
