Euphorbia vepretorum

Euphorbia vepretorum, commonly known as the rough spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a woody stem with rough, hairy bark and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
