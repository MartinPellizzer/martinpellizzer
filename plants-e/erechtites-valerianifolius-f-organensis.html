Erechtites valerianifolius f. organensis

Erechtites valerianifolius f. organensis, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a perennial herb native to Mexico.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters tall, covered with stiff, bristly hairs. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers in late summer.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in dry, open habitats.
