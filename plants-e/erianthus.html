Erianthus

Erianthus, commonly known as silvergrass, is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

These plants are native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and they are characterized by their tall, slender stalks and panicle-like inflorescences. Erianthus species often have silver or grayish leaves due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. They are adapted to thrive in a range of environments, including wetlands and grasslands.

Some species are cultivated for ornamental purposes or for their potential use in biofuel production.
