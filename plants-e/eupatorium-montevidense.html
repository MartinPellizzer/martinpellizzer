Eupatorium montevidense

Eupatorium montevidense, commonly known as the Montevideo eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in marshy or swampy habitats.

Its leaves are generally opposite, lance-shaped, and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom during the late summer and early autumn months.
