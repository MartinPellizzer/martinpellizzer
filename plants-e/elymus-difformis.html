Elymus difformis

Elymus difformis, commonly known as the variable wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are often tinged with purple or brown. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Elymus difformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
