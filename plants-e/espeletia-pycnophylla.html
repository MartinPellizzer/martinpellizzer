Espeletia pycnophylla

Espeletia pycnophylla, commonly known as the "snow plant," is a unique species native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes.

The plant is adapted to cold, dry environments and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they bloom in late summer or early autumn.

Espeletia pycnophylla plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
