Eugenia ruminata

Eugenia ruminata, commonly known as the sour orange or wild tamarillo, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, orange-yellow berries that are sour in taste. Its fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

The plant is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.
