Eragrostis damiensiana var. laxior

Eragrostis damiensiana var. laxior, commonly known as the looseleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelets. It is characterized by its loose, open growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is adapted to dry conditions and is relatively drought-tolerant.
