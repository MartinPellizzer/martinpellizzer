Embothrium weberbaueri

Embothrium weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's embothrium, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. Embothrium weberbaueri is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.

It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
