Eupatorium pallescens var. bonariense

Eupatorium pallescens var. bonariense, commonly known as the white boneset, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
