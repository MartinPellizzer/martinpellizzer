Eremophila dempsteri

Eremophila dempsteri, commonly known as the Dempster's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eremophila dempsteri is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
