Echinospermum casanicum

Echinospermum casanicum, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Echinospermum casanicum is also known for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
