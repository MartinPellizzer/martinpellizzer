Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. brevidentatus

Elionurus tripsacoides subvar. brevidentatus, commonly known as the short-toothed wild barley, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its seeds are adapted for wind dispersal, contributing to its spread across various habitats.

This subvariety is of interest in botanical studies due to its genetic diversity and potential agricultural applications.
