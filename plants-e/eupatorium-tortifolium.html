Eupatorium tortifolium

Eupatorium tortifolium, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its serrated, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Eupatorium tortifolium is often found in the eastern United States and is known for its medicinal uses by indigenous peoples.
