Eugenioides ribes

Eugenioides ribes, commonly known as the California currant, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its small, dark red to black berries that ripen in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The berries are edible and have been used historically by Indigenous peoples for food and medicine.
