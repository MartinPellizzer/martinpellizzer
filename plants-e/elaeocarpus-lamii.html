Elaeocarpus lamii

Elaeocarpus lamii, commonly known as the Philippine elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, dark seeds.

It is often found in lowland dipterocarp forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses.
