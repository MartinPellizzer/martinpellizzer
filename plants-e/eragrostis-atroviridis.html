Eragrostis atroviridis

Eragrostis atroviridis, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural fields.
