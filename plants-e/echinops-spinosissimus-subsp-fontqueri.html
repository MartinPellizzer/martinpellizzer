Echinops spinosissimus subsp. fontqueri

Echinops spinosissimus subsp.

fontqueri, commonly known as the Arizona thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its spiny, globular shape and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The plant produces purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a woolly texture, and it has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
