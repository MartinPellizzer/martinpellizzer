Eupatorium leucomyelum

Eupatorium leucomyelum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
