Eucalyptus signata

Eucalyptus signata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The flowers are typically small and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive, aromatic scent. This plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soils and low rainfall conditions.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
