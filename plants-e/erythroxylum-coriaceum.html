Erythroxylum coriaceum

Erythroxylum coriaceum, commonly known as the roughleaf copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into capsule fruits. It is often found in humid, lowland areas and can grow up to 10 meters in height.

The plant has been traditionally used for its latex, which has various industrial applications.
