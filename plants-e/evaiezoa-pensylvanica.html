Evaiezoa pensylvanica

Evaiezoa pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania fern, is a deciduous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, feathery fronds that unfurl in early spring, typically reaching heights of up to 3 feet. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are composed of multiple pinnae, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Evaiezoa pensylvanica plays a vital role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
