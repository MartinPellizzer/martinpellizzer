Eulophia palmicola

Eulophia palmicola, commonly known as the palm-like orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, palm-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the rainy season. Eulophia palmicola thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
