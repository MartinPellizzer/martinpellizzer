Eucalyptus griffithsii var. angustiuscula

Eucalyptus griffithsii var. angustiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous beneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are typically white or pink, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, woody capsules. This variety is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a range of soil types.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
