Eriochloa lemmonii var. gracilis

Eriochloa lemmonii var. gracilis, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid-region vegetation.
