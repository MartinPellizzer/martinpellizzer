Eschscholzia minutiflora var. parishii

Eschscholzia minutiflora var. parishii, commonly known as the Parish's dwarf poppy, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It features delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a wildflower contributing to local biodiversity and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
