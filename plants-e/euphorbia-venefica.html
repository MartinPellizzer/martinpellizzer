Euphorbia venefica

Euphorbia venefica, commonly known as the deadly nightshade, is a toxic plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by colorful bracts. The plant contains various toxic compounds, including tropane alkaloids, which can be harmful if ingested. It thrives in arid environments and is native to parts of Africa and the Mediterranean region.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in horticulture and requires careful handling.
