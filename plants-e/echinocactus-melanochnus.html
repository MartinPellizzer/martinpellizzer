Echinocactus melanochnus

Echinocactus melanochnus, commonly known as the black-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth rate, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
