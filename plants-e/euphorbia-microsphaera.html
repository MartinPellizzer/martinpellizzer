Euphorbia microsphaera

Euphorbia microsphaera, commonly known as the dwarf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, branched stem with small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces yellowish-green flower clusters known as cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbia genus. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
