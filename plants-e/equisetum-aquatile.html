Equisetum aquatile

Equisetum aquatile, commonly known as water horsetail, is a primitive vascular plant belonging to the family Equisetaceae.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems and whorled arrangement of leaves, which are typically reduced to small, scale-like structures. This plant thrives in wet environments such as marshes, ponds, and streambanks, where it often forms dense colonies. Its reproduction involves the production of spores rather than seeds, a feature typical of ancient plant groups.

The plant contains silica, giving it a gritty texture and making it difficult to digest for some herbivores.
