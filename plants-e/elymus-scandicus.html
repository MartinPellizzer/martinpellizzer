Elymus scandicus

Elymus scandicus, commonly known as the climbing wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense, mat-like colonies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open woodlands.

Botanically, it is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
