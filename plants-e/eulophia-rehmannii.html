Eulophia rehmannii

Eulophia rehmannii, commonly known as the Rehmann's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia rehmannii thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
