Eugenia davaoensis

Eugenia davaoensis, commonly known as the Davao guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet and tangy flavor, similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is both cultivated and appreciated in its native habitat.
