Echinacanthus andersonii var. viscosus

Echinacanthus andersonii var. viscosus, commonly known as the sticky echinacanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, which is a notable botanical feature. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
