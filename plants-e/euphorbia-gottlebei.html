Euphorbia gottlebei

Euphorbia gottlebei, commonly known as the Gottlebei's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem with smooth, waxy leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is a unique floral structure characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
