Erigeron graminifolius

Erigeron graminifolius, commonly known as the meadow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc flowers. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, prairies, and woodland edges, often in moist or dry soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, giving it the species name "graminifolius," which translates to "grass-like leaves.".

It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
