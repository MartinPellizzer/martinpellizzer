Eucalyptus campaspe

Eucalyptus campaspe, commonly known as the Campaspe gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that ranges from white to grey in color. The leaves are lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue and a prominent midrib. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or cream in color.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland and heathland habitats.
