Echinopsis boedekeriana

Echinopsis boedekeriana, commonly known as the Boedeker's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping. Its growth is slow, and it often forms a compact, rounded shape.

Echinopsis boedekeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and collections.
