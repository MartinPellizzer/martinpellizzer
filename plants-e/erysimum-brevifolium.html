Erysimum brevifolium

Erysimum brevifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erysimum brevifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
