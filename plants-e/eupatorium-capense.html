Eupatorium capense

Eupatorium capense, commonly known as the Cape eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late winter to early spring.

Eupatorium capense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
