Erigeron coronarius var. durangensis

Erigeron coronarius var. durangensis, commonly known as the Durango Fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces white to pale yellow flowers with prominent central disks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
