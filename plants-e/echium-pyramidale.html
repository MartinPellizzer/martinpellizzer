Echium pyramidale

Echium pyramidale, commonly known as the common fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters and has narrow, linear leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Echium pyramidale is also known for its ability to attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
