Eriogonum hastatum

Eriogonum hastatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces distinctive seed pods that split open to release its seeds.

Eriogonum hastatum is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
