Euphrasia glacialis var. eglandulosa

Euphrasia glacialis var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glacier sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine and arctic regions.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with hairy, fleshy leaves adapted to cold, dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its botanical structure reflects its ability to survive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
