Eupatorium bertholdii var. stenophyllum

Eupatorium bertholdii var. stenophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist, rich soils. Its flowering period attracts various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
