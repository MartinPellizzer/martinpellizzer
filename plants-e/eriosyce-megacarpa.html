Eriosyce megacarpa

Eriosyce megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fruit cactus, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, ribbed stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color, which are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Eriosyce megacarpa thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils at high altitudes.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species within the Cactaceae family.
