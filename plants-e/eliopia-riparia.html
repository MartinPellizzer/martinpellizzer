Eliopia riparia

Eliopia riparia, commonly known as the riparian elia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils near rivers and streams. It plays an important role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for various insects.

Eliopia riparia is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to periodic flooding.
