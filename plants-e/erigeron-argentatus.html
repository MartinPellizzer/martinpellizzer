Erigeron argentatus

Erigeron argentatus, commonly known as silverleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It has a taproot system and produces numerous small, white to pale blue flowers.

Erigeron argentatus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
