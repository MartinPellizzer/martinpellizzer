Eulophia ciliata

Eulophia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are typically pale yellow or greenish-white, with a distinctive fringed appearance on the petals. Eulophia ciliata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
