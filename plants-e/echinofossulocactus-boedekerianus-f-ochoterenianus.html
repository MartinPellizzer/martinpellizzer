Echinofossulocactus boedekerianus f. ochoterenianus

Echinofossulocactus boedekerianus f. ochoterenianus, commonly known as the Ochoterenianus variation, is a rare and striking cactus native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its ribbed, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a prized specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
