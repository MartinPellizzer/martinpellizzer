Erysimum kerbabaevii

Erysimum kerbabaevii, commonly known as the Turkmenian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow or orange flowers.

Erysimum kerbabaevii is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
