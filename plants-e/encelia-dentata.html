Encelia dentata

Encelia dentata, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its bright orange to reddish-orange flowers bloom in the spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Encelia dentata is also known for its ability to thrive in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and desert regions.
