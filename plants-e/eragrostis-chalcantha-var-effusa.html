Eragrostis chalcantha var. effusa

Eragrostis chalcantha var. effusa, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are loosely arranged, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornate foliage and resilience.
