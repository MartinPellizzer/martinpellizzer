Ekimia glauca

Ekimia glauca, commonly known as the blueleaf spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and smooth, often waxy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called cyathia. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of North America.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
