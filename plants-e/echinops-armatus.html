Echinops armatus

Echinops armatus, commonly known as the spiny globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiny seed heads that resemble a globe, which are a key feature in its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, adding visual interest to its overall appearance. Echinops armatus is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it an attractive and low-maintenance plant for arid landscapes.
