Elaeocarpus verruculosus

Elaeocarpus verruculosus, commonly known as the rough-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and rough, bark-like texture on its leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
