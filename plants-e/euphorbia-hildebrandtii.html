Euphorbia hildebrandtii

Euphorbia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a compact, shrub-like growth form with thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or bluish-green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a cyathium, which is a unique floral structure characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in xeriscaped gardens.
