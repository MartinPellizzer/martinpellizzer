Evolvulus capitatus

Evolvulus capitatus, commonly known as the small-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.

Evolvulus capitatus is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
