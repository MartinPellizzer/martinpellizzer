Eucalyptus goniocalyx subsp. laxa

Eucalyptus goniocalyx subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
