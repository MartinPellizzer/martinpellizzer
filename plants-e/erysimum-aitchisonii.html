Erysimum aitchisonii

Erysimum aitchisonii, commonly known as the Kashmir wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region of Pakistan and India.

It is a compact, perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers with four petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are basal, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Erysimum aitchisonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
