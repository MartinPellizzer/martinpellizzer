Erythroxylum timothei

Erythroxylum timothei, commonly known as the Timothei's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a capsule that contains a sticky, resinous substance known as copal, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

Erythroxylum timothei is also of interest for its potential ecological and economic value in its native habitats.
