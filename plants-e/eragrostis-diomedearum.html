Eragrostis diomedearum

Eragrostis diomedearum, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a slender, spike-like arrangement of small, two-ranked spikelets.

Eragrostis diomedearum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
