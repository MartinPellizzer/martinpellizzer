Erica fastigiata var. coventryana

Erica fastigiata var. coventryana, commonly known as the Coventry heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the British Isles.

It features dense, upright growth with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white during late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
