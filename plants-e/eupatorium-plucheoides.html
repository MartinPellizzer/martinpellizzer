Eupatorium plucheoides

Eupatorium plucheoides, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Its historical use in traditional medicine has contributed to its ecological and cultural significance.
