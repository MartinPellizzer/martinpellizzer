Epipactis gutta-sanguinis

Epipactis gutta-sanguinis, commonly known as the blood-red helleborine, is a species of orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, pleated leaves and showy, dark purple to reddish-brown flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodland habitats, often forming dense colonies. It is a perennial herb that produces small, greenish-white flowers, which are pollinated by specific insects.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
