Eriosema crinitum var. macrophyllum

Eriosema crinitum var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features prominently veined, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
