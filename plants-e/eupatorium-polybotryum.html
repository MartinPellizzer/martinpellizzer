Eupatorium polybotryum

Eupatorium polybotryum, commonly known as the woolly eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has finely divided, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, woolly layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium polybotryum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
