Eriosema ambiguum

Eriosema ambiguum, commonly known as the roughleaf eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Eriosema ambiguum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
