Eulophia poilanei

Eulophia poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its erect, grass-like foliage and produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, with flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Eulophia poilanei thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it a notable species within the orchid family.
