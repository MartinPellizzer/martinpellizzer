Erica blaerioides

Erica blaerioides, commonly known as the Blaerioides heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Erica blaerioides is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
