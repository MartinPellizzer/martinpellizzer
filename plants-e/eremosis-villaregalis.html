Eremosis villaregalis

Eremosis villaregalis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Eremosis villaregalis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
