Euphorbia gariepina subsp. gariepina

Euphorbia gariepina subsp.

gariepina, commonly known as the Gariep euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
