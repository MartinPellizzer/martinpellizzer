Elytraria scorpioides

Elytraria scorpioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf seepweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils near the coast, often in saline or brackish environments. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
