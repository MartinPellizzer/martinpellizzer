Echinochloa persistentia

Echinochloa persistentia, commonly known as rough barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and narrow, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stem, with the lower glumes being shorter than the upper ones. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and competitive nature make it a common weed in many regions.
