Elymus tsukushiensis

Elymus tsukushiensis, commonly known as the Japanese wiregrass, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, hair-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Elymus tsukushiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
