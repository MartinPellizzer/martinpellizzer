Epipactis ramosii

Epipactis ramosii, commonly known as the ramose helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and a tall, unbranched stem that bears a dense cluster of pale green to yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often split into two lobes, a feature common in many orchids.

Epipactis ramosii is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
