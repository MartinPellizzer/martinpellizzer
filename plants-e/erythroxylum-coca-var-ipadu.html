Erythroxylum coca var. ipadu

Erythroxylum coca var. ipadu, commonly known as the Andean coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves contain alkaloids, primarily cocaine, which have been used for centuries in traditional medicine and cultural practices.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller leaves and is often cultivated for both medicinal and ritualistic purposes.
