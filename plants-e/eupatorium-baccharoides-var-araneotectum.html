Eupatorium baccharoides var. araneotectum

Eupatorium baccharoides var. araneotectum, commonly known as the spider-tangle eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense, spiky clusters.

The species is notable for its ecological role in providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
