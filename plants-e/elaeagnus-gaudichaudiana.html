Elaeagnus gaudichaudiana

Elaeagnus gaudichaudiana, commonly known as the golden osier, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray bark and fragrant, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, reddish-brown berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or semi-arid regions.

Elaeagnus gaudichaudiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.
