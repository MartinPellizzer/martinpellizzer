Eugenia buenaventurensis

Eugenia buenaventurensis, commonly known as the Mexican cherry tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, edible berries. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The fruit is often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine due to its unique flavor and potential health benefits.
