Eriosema palmeri var. pedunculatum

Eriosema palmeri var. pedunculatum, commonly known as the desert milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces cylindrical, hairy pods that contain several seeds, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
