Echinocactus maassii

Echinocactus maassii, commonly known as the Golden Barrel Cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that bear clusters of yellow or brown bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant is popular in horticulture due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
