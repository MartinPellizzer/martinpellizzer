Embelia oblongata

Embelia oblongata, commonly known as the Indian embelia or longleaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, clustered white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe, containing several seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
