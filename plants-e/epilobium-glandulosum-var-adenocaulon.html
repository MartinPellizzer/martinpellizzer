Epilobium glandulosum var. adenocaulon

Epilobium glandulosum var. adenocaulon, commonly known as sticky willow or woolly willow, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its glandular-stemmed structure and hairy, often glandular leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other damp habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
