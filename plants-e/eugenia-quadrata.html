Eugenia quadrata

Eugenia quadrata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or araçá, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit.

Eugenia quadrata is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
