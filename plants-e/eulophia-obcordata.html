Eulophia obcordata

Eulophia obcordata, commonly known as the heart-shaped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb and long, linear leaves. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with a distinctive lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia obcordata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
