Erythrina erythrostachia

Erythrina erythrostachia, commonly known as the flame tree, is a striking tropical legume native to Africa and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branching. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in pods and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
