Erodium guttatum

Erodium guttatum, commonly known as the sticky eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant features slender stems covered in fine hairs and has small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly sticky surface. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant often grows in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple inflorescence and is part of the family Boraginaceae.
