Epipactis viridiflora subsp. pseudopurpurata

Epipactis viridiflora subsp.

pseudopurpurata, commonly known as the green-flowered helleborine, is a member of the Orchidaceae family. It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, leafy stems and showy, greenish-yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are arranged in a spike and have a distinctive lip-like structure.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and is often found in regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
