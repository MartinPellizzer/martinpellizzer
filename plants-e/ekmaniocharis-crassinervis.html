Ekmaniocharis crassinervis

Ekmaniocharis crassinervis, commonly known as the broad-veined orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically feature vibrant colors such as pink, purple, or white. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with rhizomes that allow it to spread and form clumps over time. Its leaves are usually broad and have a prominent, thick midrib, which gives it the species name "crassinervis.".

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
