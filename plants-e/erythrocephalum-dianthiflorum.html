Erythrocephalum dianthiflorum

Erythrocephalum dianthiflorum, commonly known as the scarlet globemallow, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, globe-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss.

Erythrocephalum dianthiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
