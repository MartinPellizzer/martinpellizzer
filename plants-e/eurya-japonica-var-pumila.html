Eurya japonica var. pumila

Eurya japonica var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese eurya, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during late spring, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant due to its dense, rounded growth habit.

Its ornamental value comes from its foliage and seasonal interest, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, attractive shrub.
