Eustrephus angustifolius

Eustrephus angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to arid environments. Its woody stems and dense growth habit provide habitat for various insects and small animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
