Eragrostis trichophylla

Eragrostis trichophylla, commonly known as slender weeping grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping, fine-textured foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green color, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments.

Eragrostis trichophylla is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.
