Eupatorium glabratum

Eupatorium glabratum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite, serrated leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium glabratum is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
