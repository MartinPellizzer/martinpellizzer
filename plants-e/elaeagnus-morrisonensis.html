Elaeagnus morrisonensis

Elaeagnus morrisonensis, commonly known as Morrison's silverleaf, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its silvery-white leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. Its fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer.

Elaeagnus morrisonensis is often found in moist, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
