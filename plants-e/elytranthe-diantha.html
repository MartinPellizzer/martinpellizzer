Elytranthe diantha

Elytranthe diantha, commonly known as the purple heart plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy texture. Its flowers are arranged in whorls along the stems, creating a dense, ornamental display.

Elytranthe diantha is often cultivated for its vibrant color and is popular in gardens and as a potted plant.
