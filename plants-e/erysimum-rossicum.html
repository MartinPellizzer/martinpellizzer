Erysimum rossicum

Erysimum rossicum, commonly known as the Russian Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has linear, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Erysimum rossicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
