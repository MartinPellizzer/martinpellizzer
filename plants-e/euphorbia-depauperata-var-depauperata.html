Euphorbia depauperata var. depauperata

Euphorbia depauperata var. depauperata, commonly known as the desert sunset cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a compact, globular shape with a smooth, pale green to bluish-gray stem covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces striking yellow-orange cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are small, scale-like, and often fall off as the plant matures, giving it a more shrub-like appearance.

It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems and requiring minimal watering once established.
