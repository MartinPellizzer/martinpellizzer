Ervum mediterraneum

Ervum mediterraneum, commonly known as the Mediterranean bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. The plant has compound leaves with trifoliate structures, typical of many legumes. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
