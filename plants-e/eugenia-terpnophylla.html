Eugenia terpnophylla

Eugenia terpnophylla, commonly known as the black myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, dark purple to black berries that are edible and used in traditional medicine.

The tree is often grown for its ornamental value and for its aromatic properties.
