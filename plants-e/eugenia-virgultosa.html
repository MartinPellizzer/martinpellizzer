Eugenia virgultosa

Eugenia virgultosa, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy, pinkish-red flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia virgultosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
