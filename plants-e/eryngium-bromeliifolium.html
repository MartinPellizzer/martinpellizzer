Eryngium bromeliifolium

Eryngium bromeliifolium, commonly known as the "blueweed" or "false thistle," is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
