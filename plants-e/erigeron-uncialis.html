Erigeron uncialis

Erigeron uncialis, commonly known as the blueweed or blue false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its root system is fibrous and shallow.

Erigeron uncialis is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
