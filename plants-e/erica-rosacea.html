Erica rosacea

Erica rosacea, commonly known as the pink heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and its clusters of small, tubular pink or white flowers. Erica rosacea typically blooms in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in acidic soil conditions.
