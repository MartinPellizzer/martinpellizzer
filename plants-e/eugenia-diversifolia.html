Eugenia diversifolia

Eugenia diversifolia, commonly known as the variable eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that vary in size and shape, giving it its common name. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
