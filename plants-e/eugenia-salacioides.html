Eugenia salacioides

Eugenia salacioides, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the rainforests of northern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine by Indigenous communities. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is well adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native range.

Eugenia salacioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
