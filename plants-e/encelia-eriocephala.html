Encelia eriocephala

Encelia eriocephala, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces yellow to orange flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its structure includes narrow, linear leaves and a branched stem, adapted to conserve water and withstand harsh desert conditions.
