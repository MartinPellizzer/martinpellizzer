Eupatorium cuchabense

Eupatorium cuchabense, commonly known as the Cuban boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium cuchabense is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
