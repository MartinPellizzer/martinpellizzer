Eupatorium prinodes

Eupatorium prinodes, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium prinodes is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
