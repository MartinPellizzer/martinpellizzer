Eupatorium multinerve

Eupatorium multinerve, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, button-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have prominent veins, giving it the species name "multinerve.". It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
