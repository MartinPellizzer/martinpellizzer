Elymus occidentalialtaicus

Elymus occidentalialtaicus, commonly known as the western tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Elymus occidentalialtaicus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
