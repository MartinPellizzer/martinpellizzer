Eria siamensis

Eria siamensis, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive lip petal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eria siamensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
