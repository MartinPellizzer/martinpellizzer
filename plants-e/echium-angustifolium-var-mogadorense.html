Echium angustifolium var. mogadorense

Echium angustifolium var. mogadorense, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that resemble tiny thimbles, giving it its common name. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and Mediterranean regions.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
