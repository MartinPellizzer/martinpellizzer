Eupatorium monanthum

Eupatorium monanthum, commonly known as boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as meadows, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its fever-reducing and anti-inflammatory effects.
