Eria diluta

Eria diluta, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically white or pale green. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Eria diluta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
