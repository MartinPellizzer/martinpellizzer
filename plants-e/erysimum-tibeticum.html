Erysimum tibeticum

Erysimum tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring and early summer. It has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Erysimum tibeticum is often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows at elevations above 3,000 meters.
