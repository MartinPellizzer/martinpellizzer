Erica grata

Erica grata, commonly known as the sweet heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica grata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
