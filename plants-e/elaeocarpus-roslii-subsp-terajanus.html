Elaeocarpus roslii subsp. terajanus

Elaeocarpus roslii subsp.

terajanus, commonly known as the Borneo elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae. It is native to the island of Borneo, where it thrives in lowland and hill forest environments.

The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological role in its native habitat.
