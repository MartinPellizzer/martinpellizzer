Echeveria craigiana

Echeveria craigiana, commonly known as Craig's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening.
