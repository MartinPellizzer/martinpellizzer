Euphorbia musilii

Euphorbia musilii, commonly known as the desert spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it has a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin.

Euphorbia musilii is well adapted to desert conditions, with a slow growth rate and the ability to thrive in poor soil.
