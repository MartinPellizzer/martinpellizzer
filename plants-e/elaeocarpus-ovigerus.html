Elaeocarpus ovigerus

Elaeocarpus ovigerus, commonly known as the white tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. The fruit's outer layer is thick and leathery, while the inner part is fibrous and starchy.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local cultures.
