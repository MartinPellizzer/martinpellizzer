Eupatorium ruckeri

Eupatorium ruckeri, commonly known as Rucker's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Eupatorium ruckeri is typically found in moist woodlands and along stream banks.
