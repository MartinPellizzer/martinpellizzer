Eremophila subfloccosa

Eremophila subfloccosa, commonly known as the silky eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its grey-green, woolly foliage. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for drought-resistant gardens.

The silky eremophila is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
