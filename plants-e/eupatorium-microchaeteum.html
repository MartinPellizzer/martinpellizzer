Eupatorium microchaeteum

Eupatorium microchaeteum, commonly known as the hairy leaf eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of tiny flowers. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves that are often arranged oppositely along its stems. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in clusters, typically in the summer months.

Eupatorium microchaeteum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
