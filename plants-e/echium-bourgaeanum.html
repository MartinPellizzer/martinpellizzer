Echium bourgaeanum

Echium bourgaeanum, commonly known as the blueweed or the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium bourgaeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinator populations.
