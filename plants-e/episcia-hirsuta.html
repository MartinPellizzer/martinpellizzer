Episcia hirsuta

Episcia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy whorled root.

It is a member of the Gesneriaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with hairy textures and vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Episcia hirsuta is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
