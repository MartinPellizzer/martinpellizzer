Elaeagia ruizteranii

Elaeagia ruizteranii, commonly known as the Ruizteranii Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Luzon in the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
