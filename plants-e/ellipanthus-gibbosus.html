Ellipanthus gibbosus

Ellipanthus gibbosus, commonly known as the knobbed sandverbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine pubescence.

Ellipanthus gibbosus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
