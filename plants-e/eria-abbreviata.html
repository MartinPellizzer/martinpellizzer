Eria abbreviata

Eria abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf orchid, is a small epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, delicate flowers in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

Eria abbreviata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in terrariums or as a houseplant.
