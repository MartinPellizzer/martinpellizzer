Eucalyptus conferta

Eucalyptus conferta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallet tree, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and rough, fissured bark. The plant typically grows in a shrub or small tree form, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or cream in color.

Eucalyptus conferta is often found in heathlands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.
