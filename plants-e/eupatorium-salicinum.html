Eupatorium salicinum

Eupatorium salicinum, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and have a distinctive blue-green color, while its stems are often hairy.

Eupatorium salicinum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
