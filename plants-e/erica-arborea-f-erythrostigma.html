Erica arborea f. erythrostigma

Erica arborea f. erythrostigma, commonly known as the red-stemmed heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and its distinctive red or purple stems that contrast with its green leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
