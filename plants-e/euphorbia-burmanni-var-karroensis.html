Euphorbia burmanni var. karroensis

Euphorbia burmanni var. karroensis, commonly known as the Karroen's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
