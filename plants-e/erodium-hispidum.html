Erodium hispidum

Erodium hispidum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly rough texture. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Erodium hispidum is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open spaces.
