Erysimum griffithianum

Erysimum griffithianum, commonly known as the Griffith's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other parts of the world for horticultural purposes.
