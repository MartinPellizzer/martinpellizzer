Eremophila brevifolia var. flabellifolia

Eremophila brevifolia var. flabellifolia, commonly known as the Narrow-leaf Emu Bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
