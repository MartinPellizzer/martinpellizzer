Echinocereus enneacanthus f. carnosus

Echinocereus enneacanthus f. carnosus, commonly known as the Carnosus Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which are typical of the Cactaceae family. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its distinctive morphology and limited distribution, it is often sought after by cactus enthusiasts and botanists alike.
