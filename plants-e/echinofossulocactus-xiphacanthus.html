Echinofossulocactus xiphacanthus

Echinofossulocactus xiphacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican sunburst cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, rib-like structures that are often adorned with spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Native to the arid regions of Mexico, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
