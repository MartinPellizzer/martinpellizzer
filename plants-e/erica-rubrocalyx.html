Erica rubrocalyx

Erica rubrocalyx, commonly known as the red calyx heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive red calyx that remains prominent even after the petals have fallen.

Erica rubrocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in acidic soil conditions.
