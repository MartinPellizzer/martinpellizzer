Euphorbia talaina

Euphorbia talaina, commonly known as the Talana euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it important to handle with care.

Euphorbia talaina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
