Emilia lyrata

Emilia lyrata, commonly known as the singing emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is known for its delicate, branching structure. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, which contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Emilia lyrata is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
