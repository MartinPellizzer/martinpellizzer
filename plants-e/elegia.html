Elegia

Elegia, commonly known as the "elegant grass" or "silver grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance and often features silver or blue-gray foliage due to the presence of fine hairs. Elegia species are typically found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. They are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal and ease of maintenance.

The genus includes several species, such as Elegia tenuifolia, which is popular for its delicate, feathery inflorescences.
