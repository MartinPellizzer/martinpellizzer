Elaeagnus umbellata var. siphonantha

Elaeagnus umbellata var. siphonantha, commonly known as the silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbellate clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

However, it is considered invasive in some regions due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
