Echinacea simulata

Echinacea simulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow-green central disk surrounded by narrow, pointed leaves. The plant produces showy pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Echinacea simulata is a perennial herb that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
