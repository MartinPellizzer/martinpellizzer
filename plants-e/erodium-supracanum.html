Erodium supracanum

Erodium supracanum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.
