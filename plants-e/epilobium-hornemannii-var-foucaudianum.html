Epilobium hornemannii var. foucaudianum

Epilobium hornemannii var. foucaudianum, commonly known as the Foucaud's willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically pink to reddish in color, arranged in loose clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
