Enothrea graminifolia

Enothrea graminifolia, commonly known as the slender grass-leaf plant, is a herbaceous species native to certain regions of South America.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes, often blooming in late spring or early summer.

Due to its ecological role and ornamental potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
