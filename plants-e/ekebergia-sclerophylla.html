Ekebergia sclerophylla

Ekebergia sclerophylla, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its hard, durable wood, it has been historically used for construction and tool-making.
