Echites verticillatus

Echites verticillatus, commonly known as the spiralweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study.
