Epidendrum gnomus

Epidendrum gnomus, commonly known as the miniature epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of white, green, and yellow.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
