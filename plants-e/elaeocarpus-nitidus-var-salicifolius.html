Elaeocarpus nitidus var. salicifolius

Elaeocarpus nitidus var. salicifolius, commonly known as the silky-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a distinct silvery underside. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically reddish when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
