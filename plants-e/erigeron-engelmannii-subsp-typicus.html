Erigeron engelmannii subsp. typicus

Erigeron engelmannii subsp.

typicus, commonly known as Engelmann's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.
