Euphorbia buchtormensis

Euphorbia buchtormensis, commonly known as the Bukhtarma euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically green or bluish-green in color. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences that resemble yellow or greenish cup-like structures. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient species.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
