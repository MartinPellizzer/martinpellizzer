Eleusine dagussa

Eleusine dagussa, commonly known as African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often found in dry, open habitats. It is drought-tolerant and can thrive in poor soil conditions.

Eleusine dagussa is sometimes used as a forage plant for livestock in arid regions.
