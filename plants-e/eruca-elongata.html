Eruca elongata

Eruca elongata, commonly known as wild radish, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in racemes. Its fruits are pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Eruca elongata is often found in disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia.
