Eremophila phillipsii

Eremophila phillipsii, commonly known as the pink emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is adapted to dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Eremophila phillipsii is also known for its ability to attract native pollinators such as bees and birds.
