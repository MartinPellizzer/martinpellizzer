Eriosema palmeri var. palmeri

Eriosema palmeri var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's eriosema, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments, such as desert scrub and limestone hills. Its leaves are often covered in fine, white to grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
