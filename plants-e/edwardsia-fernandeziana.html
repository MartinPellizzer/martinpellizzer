Edwardsia fernandeziana

Edwardsia fernandeziana, commonly known as the Fernandezia fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rocky or forested areas. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing capsules that are borne on the underside of its leaves.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Edwardsia fernandeziana is considered a vulnerable species.
