Eragrostis barrelieri var. ambigua

Eragrostis barrelieri var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is noted for its adaptability to arid conditions and is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its hardy nature.
