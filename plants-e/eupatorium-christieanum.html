Eupatorium christieanum

Eupatorium christieanum, commonly known as Christie's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
