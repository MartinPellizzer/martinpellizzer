Exacum tetragonum var. roseum

Exacum tetragonum var. roseum, commonly known as the roseum African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture, and the plant thrives in well-draining soil and indirect sunlight. It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

The variety 'roseum' is distinguished by its pale pink to rose-colored flowers, which bloom throughout the growing season.
