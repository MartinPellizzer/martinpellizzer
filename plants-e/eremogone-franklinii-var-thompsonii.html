Eremogone franklinii var. thompsonii

Eremogone franklinii var. thompsonii, commonly known as Thompson's eremogone, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, gray-green leaves and small, yellowish-green flower heads. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, achene fruits.

This species is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.
