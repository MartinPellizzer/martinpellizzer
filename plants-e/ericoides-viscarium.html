Ericoides viscarium

Ericoides viscarium, commonly known as the sticky eric, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its sticky secretions on its stems and leaves, which can trap insects and contribute to its survival in nutrient-poor soils. Ericoides viscarium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of its ecosystem.
