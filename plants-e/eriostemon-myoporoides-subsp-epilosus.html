Eriostemon myoporoides subsp. epilosus

Eriostemon myoporoides subsp.

epilosus, commonly known as the silver myoporum, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its narrow, silver-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, often in heathland or open woodland habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and attractive foliage.

The subspecies epilosus is distinguished by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
