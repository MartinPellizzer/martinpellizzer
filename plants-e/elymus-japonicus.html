Elymus japonicus

Elymus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Elymus japonicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
