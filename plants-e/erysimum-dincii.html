Erysimum dincii

Erysimum dincii, commonly known as the Dinciu's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Romania.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy species adapted to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

Erysimum dincii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
