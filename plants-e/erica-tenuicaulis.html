Erica tenuicaulis

Erica tenuicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica tenuicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
