Eupatorium fernaldii

Eupatorium fernaldii, commonly known as Fernald's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
