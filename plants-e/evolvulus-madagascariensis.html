Evolvulus madagascariensis

Evolvulus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, bright blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered with fine, soft hairs.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive blooms and easy care requirements.
