Erythroxylum maracasense

Erythroxylum maracasense, commonly known as the Maracasense coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces berries that contain alkaloids, including cocaine, which has significant pharmacological properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soils.

Due to its psychoactive compounds, it is both economically and ecologically important in its native habitat.
