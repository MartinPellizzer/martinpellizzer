Elaphoglossum eludens

Elaphoglossum eludens, commonly known as the elusive holly fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the temperate rainforests of the Pacific Northwest.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a soft, velvety texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is typically found growing on rocky substrates or in the understory of dense forests. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered along the margins of the fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Elaphoglossum eludens is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
