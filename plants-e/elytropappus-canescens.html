Elytropappus canescens

Elytropappus canescens, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across open landscapes.

Elytropappus canescens is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
