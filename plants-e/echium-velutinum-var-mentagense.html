Echium velutinum var. mentagense

Echium velutinum var. mentagense, commonly known as the Mentagene thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, robust stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture.

This variety is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
