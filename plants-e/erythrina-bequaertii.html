Erythrina bequaertii

Erythrina bequaertii, commonly known as the red torch coral tree, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange-pink flowers that bloom in dense, branching clusters. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its distinctive red bracts give it a fiery appearance. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina bequaertii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
