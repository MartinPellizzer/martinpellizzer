Erythraea nuttallii var. tenella

Erythraea nuttallii var. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf erythraea, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
