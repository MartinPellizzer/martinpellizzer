Erysimum sylvestre subsp. aurantiacum

Erysimum sylvestre subsp.

aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny locations. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
