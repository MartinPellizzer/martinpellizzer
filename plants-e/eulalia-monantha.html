Eulalia monantha

Eulalia monantha, commonly known as the single-flowered bulrush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in South America.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a waxy surface that helps it thrive in moist conditions.

Eulalia monantha plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
