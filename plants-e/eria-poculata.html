Eria poculata

Eria poculata, commonly known as the small-leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white blooms that are clustered in inflorescences. Eria poculata thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
