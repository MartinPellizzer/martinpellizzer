Eria floribunda

Eria floribunda, commonly known as the clustered orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a smooth surface, and the flowers are often white or pale pink.

Eria floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
