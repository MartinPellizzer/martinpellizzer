Eriophyllum greenei

Eriophyllum greenei, commonly known as the Greene's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

Eriophyllum greenei is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soils and its ecological benefits to local wildlife.
