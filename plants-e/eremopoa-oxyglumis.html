Eremopoa oxyglumis

Eremopoa oxyglumis, commonly known as the sharp-leaved eremopoa, is a perennial grass native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with sharp margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, with a deep root system and reduced leaf surface to minimize water loss.

Eremopoa oxyglumis is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the ecological stability of its habitat.
