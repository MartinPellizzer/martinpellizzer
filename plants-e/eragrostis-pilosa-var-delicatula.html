Eragrostis pilosa var. delicatula

Eragrostis pilosa var. delicatula, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
