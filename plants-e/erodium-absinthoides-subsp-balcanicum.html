Erodium absinthoides subsp. balcanicum

Erodium absinthoides subsp.

balcanicum, commonly known as Balkan erodium, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in its native habitat.
