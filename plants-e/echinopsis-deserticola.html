Echinopsis deserticola

Echinopsis deserticola, commonly known as the desert night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. It is well adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water.

Echinopsis deserticola is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
