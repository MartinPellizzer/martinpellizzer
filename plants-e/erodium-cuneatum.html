Erodium cuneatum

Erodium cuneatum, commonly known as mouse-ear or filaree, is a low-growing annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It typically features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Leguminosae (Fabaceae), and its seeds are encased in a pod-like structure.
