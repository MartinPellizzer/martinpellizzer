Echium angustifolium var. onosmoides

Echium angustifolium var. onosmoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is closely related to other echium species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
