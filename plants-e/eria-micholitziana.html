Eria micholitziana

Eria micholitziana, commonly known as the Micholitz's eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eria micholitziana is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
