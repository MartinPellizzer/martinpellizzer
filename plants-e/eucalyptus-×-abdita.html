Eucalyptus × abdita

Eucalyptus × abdita, commonly known as the hybrid ghost gum, is a deciduous tree native to Australia.

It is a natural hybrid between Eucalyptus camaldulensis and Eucalyptus tereticornis, resulting in unique botanical characteristics. The plant typically grows in coastal and riverine environments, adapting to saline and waterlogged soils. It features smooth, flaky bark that peels in strips and lance-shaped leaves with a bluish-green hue.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and attract various pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
