Echium capitiforme

Echium capitiforme, commonly known as the Cape fennel, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for Mediterranean climates.

Echium capitiforme is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
