Eugenia macrostemon

Eugenia macrostemon, commonly known as the broad-fruited eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, reddish-purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia macrostemon is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit in its native regions.
