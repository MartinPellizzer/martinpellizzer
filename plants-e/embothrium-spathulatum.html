Embothrium spathulatum

Embothrium spathulatum, commonly known as the Andean flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Embothrium spathulatum is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
