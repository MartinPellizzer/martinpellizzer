Eupatorium calaminthifolium

Eupatorium calaminthifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium calaminthifolium is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
