Echeveria fimbriata

Echeveria fimbriata, commonly known as the Mexican snowflake, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of thick, fleshy, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Echeveria fimbriata is drought-tolerant and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and rockeries.
