Euodia decaryana

Euodia decaryana, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries, which are edible but have a tart flavor. Euodia decaryana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.
