Edraianthus siculus

Edraianthus siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
