Ericoides lucidum

Ericoides lucidum, commonly known as the shining heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Ericoides lucidum produces bell-shaped white or pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark berries that are edible for certain wildlife species.

Its dense growth habit makes it an important component of ecological restoration projects in degraded habitats.
