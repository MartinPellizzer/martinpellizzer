Echinocereus engelmannii var. variegatus

Echinocereus engelmannii var. variegatus, commonly known as the variegated hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a flowering cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles and bright green, ribbed segments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique variegated patterns on the stems make it a popular choice for succulent collections and rock gardens.
