Eupatorium hospitale

Eupatorium hospitale, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its fever-reducing and antiseptic qualities.
