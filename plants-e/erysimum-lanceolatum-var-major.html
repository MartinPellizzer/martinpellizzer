Erysimum lanceolatum var. major

Erysimum lanceolatum var. major, commonly known as the large heartleaf mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features basal leaves that are lanceolate and often have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers in early spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with stems that can reach up to 30 centimeters in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
