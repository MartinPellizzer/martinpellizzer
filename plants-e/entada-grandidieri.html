Entada grandidieri

Entada grandidieri, commonly known as the Madagascar bean tree, is a leguminous plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow flowers followed by large, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in dry deciduous forests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a notable species in the region's biodiversity.
