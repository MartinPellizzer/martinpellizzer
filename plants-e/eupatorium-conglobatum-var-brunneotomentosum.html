Eupatorium conglobatum var. brunneotomentosum

Eupatorium conglobatum var. brunneotomentosum, commonly known as the brown-tomentose boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that are covered in a dense layer of brown, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flower heads, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
