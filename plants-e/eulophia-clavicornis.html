Eulophia clavicornis

Eulophia clavicornis, commonly known as the club-horned eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, raceme-like inflorescences with fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that have a distinctive club-shaped lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
