Empogona buxifolia subsp. buxifolia

Empogona buxifolia subsp.

buxifolia, commonly known as the waxleaf hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, yellow, or orange, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
