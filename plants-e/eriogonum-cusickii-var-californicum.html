Eriogonum cusickii var. californicum

Eriogonum cusickii var. californicum, commonly known as the California buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, chaparral, and disturbed areas. It has basal leaves that are often hairy and possess a distinctive, elongated shape.

This species is known for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions and plays an important role in supporting local pollinators.
