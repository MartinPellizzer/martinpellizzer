Erysimum arkansanum

Erysimum arkansanum, commonly known as the Arkansas bladderpod, is a flowering plant native to the central United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in prairie and woodland habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are pinnately divided, and it produces yellow or white flowers in late spring.

The species is also noted for its role in supporting pollinators and its historical use by Native American tribes.
