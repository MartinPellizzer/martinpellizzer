Entada reticulata

Entada reticulata, commonly known as the African honeywood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces long, slender pods containing flat, seed-rich seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its wood is hard and durable, making it valuable for construction and crafting.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
