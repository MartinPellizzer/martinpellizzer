Elizabetha coccinea var. oxyphylla

Elizabetha coccinea var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the red-flowered elizabetha, is a strikingly beautiful plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to two meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its visual appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

However, it thrives best in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
