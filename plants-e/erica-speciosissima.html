Erica speciosissima

Erica speciosissima, commonly known as the common heather, is a flowering plant native to western Europe.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub with dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are needle-like and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

Erica speciosissima is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive ground cover.
