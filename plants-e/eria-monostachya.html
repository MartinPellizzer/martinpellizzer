Eria monostachya

Eria monostachya, commonly known as the single-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stem and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eria monostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
