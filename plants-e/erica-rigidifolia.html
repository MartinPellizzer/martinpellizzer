Erica rigidifolia

Erica rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica rigidifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
