Eulophia seleensis var. kisanfuensis

Eulophia seleensis var. kisanfuensis, commonly known as the Kisanfu eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Central Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in the humid and cool conditions of the tropical rainforest. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its survival in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, displaying a range of colors from white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
