Epithecia picta

Epithecia picta, commonly known as the painted cup lichen, is a foliose lichen belonging to the family Pertusariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, brightly colored thallus that ranges from yellow to orange-red, often with intricate patterns. The plant forms a loosely branched, crust-like growth on tree bark, rocks, or soil, typically in moist, shaded environments. Its reproductive structures, called soredia, are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

The lichen's vibrant coloration is due to the presence of specific pigments produced by its algal partner.
