Elaeagnus courtoisii

Elaeagnus courtoisii, commonly known as the Courtois silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its silvery-green leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Elaeagnus courtoisii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
