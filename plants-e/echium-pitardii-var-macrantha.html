Echium pitardii var. macrantha

Echium pitardii var. macrantha, commonly known as the giant evening primrose, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy purple flowers that bloom in the evening. The plant produces capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Echium pitardii var. macrantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
