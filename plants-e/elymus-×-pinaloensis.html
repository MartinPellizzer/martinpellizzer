Elymus × pinaloensis

Elymus × pinaloensis, commonly known as the Pinal Desert needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spikelet-like inflorescences, which are typical of the Poaceae family. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy soils and open plains. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a tough, fibrous texture adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Elymus × pinaloensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
