Erica tetralix f. alba

Erica tetralix f. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the heather family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This variety is distinguished by its pale floral color, which sets it apart from other Erica species.
