Epipactis helleborine var. diversifolia

Epipactis helleborine var. diversifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of greenish-yellow to brownish flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The species is also notable for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insect species.
