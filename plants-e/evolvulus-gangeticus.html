Evolvulus gangeticus

Evolvulus gangeticus, commonly known as the Indian blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that grows up to 1 meter in height, with opposite leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental flowers and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
