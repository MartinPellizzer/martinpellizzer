Euphorbia resinifera

Euphorbia resinifera, commonly known as the resinous spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces a milky, toxic sap that can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh climates.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable species in botanical studies.
