Erythronium grandiflorum f. revolutum

Erythronium grandiflorum f. revolutum, commonly known as the revolute erythronium, is a botanical variant of the large-flowered erythronium.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with purple at the base. Its showy, bell-shaped flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive red or orange spot at the base of each petal.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring bloom.
