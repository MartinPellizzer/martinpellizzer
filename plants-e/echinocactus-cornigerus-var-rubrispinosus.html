Echinocactus cornigerus var. rubrispinosus

Echinocactus cornigerus var. rubrispinosus, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, reddish spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and cactus enthusiasts.
