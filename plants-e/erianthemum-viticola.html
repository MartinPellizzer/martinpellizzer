Erianthemum viticola

Erianthemum viticola, commonly known as the wild strawberry vine, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Erianthemum viticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
