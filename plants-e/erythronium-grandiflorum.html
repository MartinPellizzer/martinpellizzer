Erythronium grandiflorum

Erythronium grandiflorum, commonly known as the giant yellow bulbous plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a bulbous underground stem and produces striking, showy flowers with six petal-like lobes that are typically bright yellow or orange. The plant's leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often appear after the flowers have bloomed. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soils and prefers partial to full shade in its natural habitat.

Erythronium grandiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early spring blooms.
