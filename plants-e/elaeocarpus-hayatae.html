Elaeocarpus hayatae

Elaeocarpus hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
