Eriosema campestre f. pubescens

Eriosema campestre f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy field eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
