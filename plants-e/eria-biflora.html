Eria biflora

Eria biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients.

Eria biflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
