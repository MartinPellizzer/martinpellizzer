Echinocactus kunzei

Echinocactus kunzei, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, ribbed stem that resembles a barrel, covered with dense, yellow spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
