Erianthus cumingii

Erianthus cumingii, commonly known as Cuming's reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, swampy environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
