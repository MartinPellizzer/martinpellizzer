Eupatorium rebaudianum

Eupatorium rebaudianum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, purple-blue flower heads bloom in late summer and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
