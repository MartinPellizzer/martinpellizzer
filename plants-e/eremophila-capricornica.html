Eremophila capricornica

Eremophila capricornica, commonly known as the Capricorn Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grey-green leaves and spikes of purple or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for dry landscapes. It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.

Eremophila capricornica plays a role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
