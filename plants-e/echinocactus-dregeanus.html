Echinocactus dregeanus

Echinocactus dregeanus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the dry regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the rainy season, typically in spring or early summer. Its stems are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Echinocactus dregeanus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.
