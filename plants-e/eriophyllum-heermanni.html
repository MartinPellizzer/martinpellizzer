Eriophyllum heermanni

Eriophyllum heermanni, commonly known as the Heermann's goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in coastal sage scrub and chaparral habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eriophyllum heermanni is also valued for its drought tolerance and use in ecological restoration projects.
