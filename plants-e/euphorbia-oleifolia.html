Euphorbia oleifolia

Euphorbia oleifolia, commonly known as the olive euphorbia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, waxy leaves and clusters of yellow-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Euphorbia oleifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
