Epidendrum erosum

Epidendrum erosum, commonly known as the red-tipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizome and multiple stems. The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers that are typically pink or red with a yellow lip. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Epidendrum erosum thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
