Elytrigia pontica

Elytrigia pontica, commonly known as the Pontic grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Elytrigia pontica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
