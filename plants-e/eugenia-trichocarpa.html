Eugenia trichocarpa

Eugenia trichocarpa, commonly known as the Australian cherry or the myrtle-leaved eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its bark is often smooth and peels in thin layers, while its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.
