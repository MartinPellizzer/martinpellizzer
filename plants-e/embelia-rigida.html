Embelia rigida

Embelia rigida, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which contribute to its distinct aroma.

Embelia rigida is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, including antimicrobial and antioxidant effects.
