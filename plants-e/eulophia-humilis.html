Eulophia humilis

Eulophia humilis, commonly known as the low orchid, is a species of orchid native to various regions in Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous areas. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, often arranged in a raceme, and come in a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

Eulophia humilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
