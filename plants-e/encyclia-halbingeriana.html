Encyclia halbingeriana

Encyclia halbingeriana, commonly known as the Halbinger's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Encyclia halbingeriana is appreciated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
