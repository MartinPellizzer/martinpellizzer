Eriogonum compositum var. leianthum

Eriogonum compositum var. leianthum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
