Erigeron × glareosus

Erigeron × glareosus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and fluffy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Erigeron × glareosus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
