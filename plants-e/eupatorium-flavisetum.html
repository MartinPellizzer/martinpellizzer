Eupatorium flavisetum

Eupatorium flavisetum, commonly known as the yellow false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies. Its showy yellow to orange flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators.

The leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
