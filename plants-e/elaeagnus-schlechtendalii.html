Elaeagnus schlechtendalii

Elaeagnus schlechtendalii, commonly known as silverleaf elaeagnus, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, fragrant flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Elaeagnus schlechtendalii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
