Epidendrum andreettae

Epidendrum andreettae, commonly known as the Andreetta's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Brazil.

It features a sympodial growth habit with elongated, fleshy pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces inflorescences bearing numerous small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
