Evolvulus desertorum

Evolvulus desertorum, commonly known as desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with opposite, ovate leaves and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptation to dry conditions. Its rapid growth cycle allows it to complete its life cycle quickly in response to seasonal rainfall.

The species is also noted for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.
