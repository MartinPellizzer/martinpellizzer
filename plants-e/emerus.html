Emerus

Emerus, commonly known as the common emerus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas. The plants typically have showy, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. They often grow as shrubs or small trees, with opposite leaves and branched inflorescences.

The genus includes several species, some of which are cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.
