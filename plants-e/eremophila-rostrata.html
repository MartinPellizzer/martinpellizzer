Eremophila rostrata

Eremophila rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila rostrata is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
