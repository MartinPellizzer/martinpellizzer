Eupatorium longicaule

Eupatorium longicaule, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly rough texture.

Eupatorium longicaule is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
