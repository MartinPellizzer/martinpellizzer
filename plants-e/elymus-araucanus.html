Elymus araucanus

Elymus araucanus, commonly known as the Araucanian wild rye, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Elymus araucanus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
