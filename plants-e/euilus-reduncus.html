Euilus reduncus

Euilus reduncus, commonly known as the reduncus orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, which help it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are usually brightly colored, with a complex structure that facilitates pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss, Euilus reduncus is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
