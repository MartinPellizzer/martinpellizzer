Echinocarpus mollis

Echinocarpus mollis, commonly known as the silky burr or velvet burr, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny fruits that are covered in a soft, hairy coating. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces numerous seeds enclosed in tough, echinocarpic fruits.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
