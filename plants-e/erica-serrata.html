Erica serrata

Erica serrata, commonly known as the sawleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica serrata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, hardy ground cover.
