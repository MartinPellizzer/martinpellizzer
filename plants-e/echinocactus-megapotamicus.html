Echinocactus megapotamicus

Echinocactus megapotamicus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil and Paraguay.

It is characterized by its globular shape and dense covering of bright yellow spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiky exterior. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
