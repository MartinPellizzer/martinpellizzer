Endostemon viscosus

Endostemon viscosus, commonly known as the sticky endostemon, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, sticky coating, which gives it its common name.

Endostemon viscosus is often found in damp, forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
