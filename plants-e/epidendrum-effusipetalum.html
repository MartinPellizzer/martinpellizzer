Epidendrum effusipetalum

Epidendrum effusipetalum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescences. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, with a distinct lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns.

Epidendrum effusipetalum is a popular choice for indoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
