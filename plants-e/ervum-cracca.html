Ervum cracca

Ervum cracca, commonly known as the yellow sweetpea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodland edges. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrance.
