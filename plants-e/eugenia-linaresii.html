Eugenia linaresii

Eugenia linaresii, commonly known as the Linares's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruits are small, yellow or orange berries that are edible and have a tart flavor.

Eugenia linaresii is often grown in subtropical climates for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
