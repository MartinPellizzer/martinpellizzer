Erodium absinthoides var. kavadarcensis

Erodium absinthoides var. kavadarcensis, commonly known as Kavadaric filaree, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, fern-like leaves and clusters of pink or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
