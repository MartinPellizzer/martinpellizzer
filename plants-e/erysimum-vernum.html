Erysimum vernum

Erysimum vernum, commonly known as the spring cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by slender, elongated pods containing seeds.

Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
