Eriochloa longiflora

Eriochloa longiflora, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale green or blue-gray leaves. The plant produces clusters of elongated, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, fluffy flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Eriochloa longiflora is an important plant for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
