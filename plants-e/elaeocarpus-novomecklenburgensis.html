Elaeocarpus novomecklenburgensis

Elaeocarpus novomecklenburgensis, commonly known as the New Mecklenburg elaeocarpus, is a rare tropical tree species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by distinctive, woody fruits. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
