Euphorbia excisa

Euphorbia excisa, commonly known as the Mexican fireplant, is a succulent shrub native to Mexico.

It features distinctive, spiny stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.

Euphorbia excisa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
