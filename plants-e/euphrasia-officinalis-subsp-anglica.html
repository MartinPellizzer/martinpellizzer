Euphrasia officinalis subsp. anglica

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

anglica, commonly known as the common eyebright, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

The plant features upright stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in whorls. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat eye conditions and inflammatory disorders.
