Erucastrum repandum subsp. humile

Erucastrum repandum subsp.

humile, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically features slender, prostrate stems that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly serrated edge.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.
