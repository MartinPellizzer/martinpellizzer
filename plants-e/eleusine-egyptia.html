Eleusine egyptia

Eleusine egyptia, commonly known as Egyptian lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions contribute to its widespread distribution.
