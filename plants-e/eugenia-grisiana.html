Eugenia grisiana

Eugenia grisiana, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. Eugenia grisiana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
