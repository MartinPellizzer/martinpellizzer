Erythrochilus mollis

Erythrochilus mollis, commonly known as the pink-stemmed daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Erythrochilus mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in subtropical regions.
