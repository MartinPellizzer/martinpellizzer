Ericoides curvifolium

Ericoides curvifolium, commonly known as the curl-leaf ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with tightly curled leaves that give it its common name. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in high-altitude environments. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Ericoides curvifolium plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
