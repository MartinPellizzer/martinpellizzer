Eupatorium pansamalense

Eupatorium pansamalense, commonly known as the Caribbean boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium pansamalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some Caribbean cultures.
