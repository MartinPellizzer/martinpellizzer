Erucago arvensis

Erucago arvensis, commonly known as the common thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, fields, and along roadsides. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Erucago arvensis is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
