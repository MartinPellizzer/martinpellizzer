Embelia picta

Embelia picta, commonly known as the painted embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often adorned with distinctive markings, giving it its common name. Its small white or pink flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals but toxic to humans.

The plant is valued in traditional medicine for its purported antimicrobial and antifungal properties.
