Erica faireana

Erica faireana, commonly known as the Scottish heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many heathers and blueberries. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, depending on the variety. Erica faireana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
