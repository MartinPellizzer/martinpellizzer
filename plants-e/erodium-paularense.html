Erodium paularense

Erodium paularense, commonly known as the coastal bird's-beak, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed or coastal habitats.

Erodium paularense is adapted to withstand salt spray and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
