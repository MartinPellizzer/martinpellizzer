Ethulia struchium

Ethulia struchium, commonly known as the goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in meadows and roadside areas. Its showy flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Ethulia struchium is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
