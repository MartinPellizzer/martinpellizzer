Echinopsis formosa var. spinosior

Echinopsis formosa var. spinosior, commonly known as the Spiny Echinopsis, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, which provide protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
