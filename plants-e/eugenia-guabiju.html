Eugenia guabiju

Eugenia guabiju, commonly known as Brazilian pepper tree or guabiju, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are rich in nutrients and used in traditional medicine. Eugenia guabiju thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aromatic wood.
