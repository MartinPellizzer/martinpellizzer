Epidendrum natator

Epidendrum natator, commonly known as the swimming orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Epidendrum natator is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
