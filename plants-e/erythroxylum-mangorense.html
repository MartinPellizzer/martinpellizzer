Erythroxylum mangorense

Erythroxylum mangorense, commonly known as the Malagasy erythroxylum, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
