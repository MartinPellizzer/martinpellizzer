Euonymus serratifolius

Euonymus serratifolius, commonly known as the serratedleaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins, which contribute to its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. Its foliage provides seasonal color changes, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Euonymus serratifolius is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its tolerance to partial shade.
