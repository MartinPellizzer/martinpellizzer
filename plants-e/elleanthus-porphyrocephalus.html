Elleanthus porphyrocephalus

Elleanthus porphyrocephalus, commonly known as the purple-headed elleanthus, is a striking plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences at the top of its slender stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of hair, which helps to retain moisture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
