Empodium veratrifolium

Empodium veratrifolium, commonly known as the three-leaved lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the family Melanthiaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or greenish-white flowers in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in shady garden settings.
