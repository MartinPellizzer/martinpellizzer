Epiphyllum purpurascens

Epiphyllum purpurascens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are often green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. These flowers are fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Epiphyllum purpurascens is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
