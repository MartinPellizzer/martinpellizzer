Erica glomerata

Erica glomerata, commonly known as heather, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces bright pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Erica glomerata thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
