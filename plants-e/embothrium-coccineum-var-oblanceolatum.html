Embothrium coccineum var. oblanceolatum

Embothrium coccineum var. oblanceolatum, commonly known as the red-flowered weeping cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features slender, arching branches that create a weeping appearance, adorned with clusters of bright red flowers in early spring. The leaves are elongated and oval, with a smooth texture and a slightly glossy surface. This variety is prized for its ornamental value, particularly for its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
