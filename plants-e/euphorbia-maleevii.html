Euphorbia maleevii

Euphorbia maleevii, commonly known as the Maleev's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique inflorescence called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in rocky gardens.
