Echinocactus acroacanthus

Echinocactus acroacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short stems at the top of the plant. Its dense, water-storing tissue allows it to thrive in dry conditions, making it highly drought-resistant.

The plant's unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
