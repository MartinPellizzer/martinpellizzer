Ephedra major subsp. procera

Ephedra major subsp.

procera, commonly known as the tall ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often branched and covered in small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is adapted to extreme temperature fluctuations. Ephedra major subsp.

procera is part of the Ephedraceae family, which includes other plants known for their medicinal and historical uses.
