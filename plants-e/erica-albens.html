Erica albens

Erica albens, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained, acidic conditions. Erica albens is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and strong winds.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in suitable climates.
