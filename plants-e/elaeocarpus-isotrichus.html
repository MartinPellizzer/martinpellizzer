Elaeocarpus isotrichus

Elaeocarpus isotrichus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
