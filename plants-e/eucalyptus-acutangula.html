Eucalyptus acutangula

Eucalyptus acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the autumn and winter seasons. Eucalyptus acutangula is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands and heathlands.

Its aromatic oils have been used in traditional Aboriginal medicine and for various practical purposes.
