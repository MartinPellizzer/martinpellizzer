Ericoides pedunculare

Ericoides pedunculare, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, evergreen leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and woodland edges.

Ericoides pedunculare is a low-growing shrub that plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
