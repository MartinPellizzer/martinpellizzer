Eurybia avita

Eurybia avita, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has a rosette of basal leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, producing small blue to violet flowers.

Eurybia avita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
