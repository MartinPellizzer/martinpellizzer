Eugenia thollonii

Eugenia thollonii, commonly known as the thollonii guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically pink to red in color when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
