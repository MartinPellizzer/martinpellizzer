Eucalyptus redunca subsp. porphyrea

Eucalyptus redunca subsp.

porphyrea, commonly known as the Porphyrea mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its rough, fibrous bark that often peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color.

The flowers are typically white or cream, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody capsules that contain aromatic seeds. This subspecies is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

It plays an important role in its ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
