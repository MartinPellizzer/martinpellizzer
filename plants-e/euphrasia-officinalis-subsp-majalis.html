Euphrasia officinalis subsp. majalis

Euphrasia officinalis subsp.

majalis, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It is characterized by its showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are erect and branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
