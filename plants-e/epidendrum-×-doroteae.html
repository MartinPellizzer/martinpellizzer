Epidendrum × doroteae

Epidendrum × doroteae, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Epidendrum secundum and Epidendrum ibaguense.

It is a sympodial orchid with a robust, erect stem and long, linear leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences bearing numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with consistent moisture.

Epidendrum × doroteae is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
