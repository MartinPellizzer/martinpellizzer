Evolvulus capensis

Evolvulus capensis, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often trailing or climbing.

Evolvulus capensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive flowers and easy care requirements.
