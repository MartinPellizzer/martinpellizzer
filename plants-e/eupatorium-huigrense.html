Eupatorium huigrense

Eupatorium huigrense, commonly known as the Huigren's thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are hairy on the underside. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, consist of both ray and disk florets, producing a showy, pinkish-purple color.

Eupatorium huigrense is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
