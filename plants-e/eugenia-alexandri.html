Eugenia alexandri

Eugenia alexandri, commonly known as the Alexandrian guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and aromatic flavor. Eugenia alexandri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
