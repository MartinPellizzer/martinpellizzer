Elaphoglossum amplum

Elaphoglossum amplum, commonly known as the broad-leafed hawksbeard, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines along the undersides of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum amplum is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.
