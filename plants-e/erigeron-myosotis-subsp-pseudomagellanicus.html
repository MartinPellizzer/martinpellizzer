Erigeron myosotis subsp. pseudomagellanicus

Erigeron myosotis subsp.

pseudomagellanicus, commonly known as the Antarctic blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subantarctic environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its blue to violet flowers bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual element to its habitat.

The subspecies is distinguished by its compact growth form and specific adaptations to its harsh, high-altitude environment.
