Eugenia monticola var. latifolia

Eugenia monticola var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
