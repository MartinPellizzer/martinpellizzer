Eurya rubiginosa var. attenuata

Eurya rubiginosa var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender alderleaf eurya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often reddish-brown underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
