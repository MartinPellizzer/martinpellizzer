Echinocereus russanthus

Echinocereus russanthus, commonly known as the Russian hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red or purple fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its slow growth rate, it is relatively easy to maintain once established.
