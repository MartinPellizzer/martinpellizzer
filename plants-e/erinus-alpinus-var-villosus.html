Erinus alpinus var. villosus

Erinus alpinus var. villosus, commonly known as the fuzzy erinus, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
