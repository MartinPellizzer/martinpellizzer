Euodia xanthoxyloides

Euodia xanthoxyloides, commonly known as the yellow-flowered euodia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
