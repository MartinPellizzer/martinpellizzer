Euphorbia irgisensis

Euphorbia irgisensis, commonly known as the Irgis Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small scales, and it lacks true leaves in many species.

Euphorbia irgisensis is adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
