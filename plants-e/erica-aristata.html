Erica aristata

Erica aristata, commonly known as the upright heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in spring and early summer. Erica aristata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
