Erica obbata

Erica obbata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Erica obbata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
