Erica rigescens

Erica rigescens, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and attractive appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Erica rigescens is also valued for its resilience to drought and salt spray, making it well-suited for coastal regions.
