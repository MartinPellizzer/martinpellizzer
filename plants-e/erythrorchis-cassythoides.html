Erythrorchis cassythoides

Erythrorchis cassythoides, commonly known as the red orchid vine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red and purple flowers. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, with slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are highly fragrant and bloom during the summer months.

Erythrorchis cassythoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
