Erica peziza

Erica peziza, commonly known as the purple heath, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Erica peziza is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal pruning, making it a popular choice for gardeners in Mediterranean climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant blooms and compact growth habit.
