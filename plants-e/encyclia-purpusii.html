Encyclia purpusii

Encyclia purpusii, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clustered, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually pale yellow to greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it produces small, dark brown capsules as its fruit.

Encyclia purpusii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
