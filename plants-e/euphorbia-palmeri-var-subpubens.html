Euphorbia palmeri var. subpubens

Euphorbia palmeri var. subpubens, commonly known as the Palmer's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically small and covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance.

It is often found in rocky soils and is considered a hardy, drought-tolerant species.
