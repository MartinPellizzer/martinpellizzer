Eriocaulon ubonense subsp. hexagynum

Eriocaulon ubonense subsp.

hexagynum, commonly known as the Ubon marsh plant, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in swampy and wetland habitats.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptation to aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
