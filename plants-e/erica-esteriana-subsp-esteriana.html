Erica esteriana subsp. esteriana

Erica esteriana subsp.

esteriana, commonly known as the Esterian heath, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. It is a member of the Ericaceae family, known for its adaptations to nutrient-poor environments.

This subspecies is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
