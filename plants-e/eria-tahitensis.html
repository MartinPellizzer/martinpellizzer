Eria tahitensis

Eria tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly Tahiti and other islands in French Polynesia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, often fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air.

Eria tahitensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
