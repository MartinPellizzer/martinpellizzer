Eupatorium ixiocladon

Eupatorium ixiocladon, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense cylindrical inflorescences, typically appearing in shades of white to pale pink.

Eupatorium ixiocladon is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.
