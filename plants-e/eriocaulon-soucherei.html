Eriocaulon soucherei

Eriocaulon soucherei, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to submerged conditions. Its rhizomes form a network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion in aquatic environments.

Eriocaulon soucherei is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
