Eugenia alaotrensis

Eugenia alaotrensis, commonly known as the Madagascar guava, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
