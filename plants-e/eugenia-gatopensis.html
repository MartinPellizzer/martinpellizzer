Eugenia gatopensis

Eugenia gatopensis, commonly known as the Cuban guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, similar to that of guavas. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
