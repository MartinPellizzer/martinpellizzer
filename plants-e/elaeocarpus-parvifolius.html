Elaeocarpus parvifolius

Elaeocarpus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
