Erigeron elatus var. bakeri

Erigeron elatus var. bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
