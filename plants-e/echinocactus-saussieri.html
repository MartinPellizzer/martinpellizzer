Echinocactus saussieri

Echinocactus saussieri, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that develops a spiny, globular shape as it matures. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
