Euphorbia heptagona var. subsessilis

Euphorbia heptagona var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, stocky stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters called cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.
