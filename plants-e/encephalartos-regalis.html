Encephalartos regalis

Encephalartos regalis, commonly known as the royal cycad, is a rare and ancient cycad species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces a distinctive cone-like structure that contains its seeds, which are enclosed in a hard, woody covering. Encephalartos regalis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in botanical collections and conservation efforts.
