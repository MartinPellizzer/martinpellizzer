Eryngium crassisquamosum

Eryngium crassisquamosum, commonly known as the thick-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flowers and thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance makes it a striking addition to native plant gardens and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
