Erythroxylum boivinianum

Erythroxylum boivinianum, commonly known as the Boivin's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its woody structure, compound leaves, and small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a source of copal.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
