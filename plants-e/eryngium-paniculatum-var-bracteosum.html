Eryngium paniculatum var. bracteosum

Eryngium paniculatum var. bracteosum, commonly known as the bracteate thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking blue to purple spiky flowers that bloom in summer, surrounded by a dense, bract-covered inflorescence. The plant has silver-gray, fuzzy leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators.
