Ephedra nebrodensis subsp. suggarica

Ephedra nebrodensis subsp.

suggarica, commonly known as the sweet ephedra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-green stems and small, scale-like leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense spikes. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

The stems contain alkaloids, making it a plant of interest in both traditional medicine and scientific research.
