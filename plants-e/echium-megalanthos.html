Echium megalanthos

Echium megalanthos, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium megalanthos is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
