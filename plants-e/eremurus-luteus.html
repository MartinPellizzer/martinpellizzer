Eremurus luteus

Eremurus luteus, commonly known as the yellow torchflower, is a striking plant native to Central Asia.

It is a member of the genus Eremurus, which includes several species of flowering plants known for their tall, spiky inflorescences. The plant features a tall, leafless stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, topped with a dense cluster of yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to flourish.

Eremurus luteus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to arid or semi-arid climates.
