Eremophila maculata subsp. maculata

Eremophila maculata subsp.

maculata, commonly known as the spotted emu plant, is a flowering shrub native to Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

It is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
