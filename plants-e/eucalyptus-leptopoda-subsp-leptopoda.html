Eucalyptus leptopoda subsp. leptopoda

Eucalyptus leptopoda subsp.

leptopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which attract a variety of pollinators.

The species is part of the Myrtaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
