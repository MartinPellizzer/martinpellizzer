Excoecaria marginata var. puberula

Excoecaria marginata var. puberula, commonly known as the velvetleaf or red-veined nettle, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent reddish veins.

The leaves are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shady environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
