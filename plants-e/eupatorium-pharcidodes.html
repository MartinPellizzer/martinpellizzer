Eupatorium pharcidodes

Eupatorium pharcidodes, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft texture. It thrives in moist, partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium pharcidodes is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
