Eleusine racemosa

Eleusine racemosa, commonly known as false rice, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, branched inflorescences. The plant produces small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or pale yellow in color. It thrives in wet, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along waterways.

Eleusine racemosa is also known for its potential as a forage crop and its role in ecological succession.
