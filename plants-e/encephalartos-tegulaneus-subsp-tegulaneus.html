Encephalartos tegulaneus subsp. tegulaneus

Encephalartos tegulaneus subsp.

tegulaneus, commonly known as the Natal knobbed cycad, is a species of cycad native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of stiff, dark green, needle-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically borne in a short, branched inflorescence. It is a slow-growing, long-lived plant that prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited natural range, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
