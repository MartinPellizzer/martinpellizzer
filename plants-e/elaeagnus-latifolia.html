Elaeagnus latifolia

Elaeagnus latifolia, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are typically red or orange in color. Elaeagnus latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

It also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a source of fuel.
