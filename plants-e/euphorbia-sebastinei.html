Euphorbia sebastinei

Euphorbia sebastinei, commonly known as the Red-veined Euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often red-veined, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, adding to its ornamental value. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
