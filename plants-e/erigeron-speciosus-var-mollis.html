Erigeron speciosus var. mollis

Erigeron speciosus var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are soft and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance, while its flowers bloom in shades of blue to purple.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
