Eclipta palustris

Eclipta palustris, commonly known as swamp sunflower, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, often growing in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by wind. It is commonly found in wetlands, along stream banks, and in other moist environments.

Eclipta palustris is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
