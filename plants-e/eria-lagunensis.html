Eria lagunensis

Eria lagunensis, commonly known as the Lake Orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant prefers warm, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, moist forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, organic soil.

Eria lagunensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
