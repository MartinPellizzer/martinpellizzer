Erigeron glandulosus

Erigeron glandulosus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flower heads and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Erigeron glandulosus is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
