Euthamia tenuifolia

Euthamia tenuifolia, commonly known as slenderleaf goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow flower clusters that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flowers.

Euthamia tenuifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
