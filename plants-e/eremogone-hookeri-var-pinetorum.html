Eremogone hookeri var. pinetorum

Eremogone hookeri var. pinetorum, commonly known as the Sierra needle-leaf, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh environmental conditions of its native range.
