Eupatorium deltifolium

Eupatorium deltifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its slender, aromatic stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along streams.

Eupatorium deltifolium is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
