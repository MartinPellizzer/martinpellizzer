Elaterium pauciflorum

Elaterium pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered elaterium, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Elateriaceae.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant has a short, upright stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow and lack showy petals, which is typical of many members of its family.

Elaterium pauciflorum plays a role in its ecosystem by providing nectar to certain insect pollinators.
