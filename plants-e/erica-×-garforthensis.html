Erica × garforthensis

Erica × garforthensis, commonly known as the Garforth heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the UK.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica × garforthensis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
