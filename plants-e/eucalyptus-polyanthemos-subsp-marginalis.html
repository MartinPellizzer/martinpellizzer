Eucalyptus polyanthemos subsp. marginalis

Eucalyptus polyanthemos subsp.

marginalis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white to greyish pubescence.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and can be white, pink, or pale blue, attracting a variety of pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This subspecies is known for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
