Enodium caeruleum var. altissimum

Enodium caeruleum var. altissimum, commonly known as the sky-blue gentian, is a striking plant characterized by its deep blue to sky-blue flowers.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is native to mountainous regions in Europe. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its tall, slender stems can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, making it a notable presence in its native environment.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
