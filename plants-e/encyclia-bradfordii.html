Encyclia bradfordii

Encyclia bradfordii, commonly known as the Bradford's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces bright yellow or greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands.

Encyclia bradfordii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
