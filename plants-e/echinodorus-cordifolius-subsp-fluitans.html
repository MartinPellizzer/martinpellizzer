Echinodorus cordifolius subsp. fluitans

Echinodorus cordifolius subsp.

fluitans, commonly known as the floating heart, is a aquatic plant native to South America. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface.

The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread easily in aquatic environments. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters with moderate to high light exposure.

Its attractive appearance makes it a popular choice for aquariums and pond landscaping.
