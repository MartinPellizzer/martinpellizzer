Eragrostis spectabilis var. sparsihirsuta

Eragrostis spectabilis var. sparsihirsuta, commonly known as the silverhair panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, arching stems that create a delicate, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often adorned with soft, silky hairs, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

Its low maintenance requirements and ability to form dense ground cover make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
