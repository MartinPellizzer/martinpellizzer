Eragrostis ecarinata

Eragrostis ecarinata, commonly known as silky fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and slender, often drooping stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Silky fingergrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
