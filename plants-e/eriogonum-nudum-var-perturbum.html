Eriogonum nudum var. perturbum

Eriogonum nudum var. perturbum, commonly known as the Desert Buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert scrub habitats.

The species is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient component of its ecosystem.
