Eugenia aequatoriensis

Eugenia aequatoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The fruit is a small, reddish-purple berry that is edible and often used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
