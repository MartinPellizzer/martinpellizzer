Euonymus europaeus var. angustifolius

Euonymus europaeus var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in late spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits in the fall. The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically narrow and elliptical in shape. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and can thrive in partial to full shade.

This plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
