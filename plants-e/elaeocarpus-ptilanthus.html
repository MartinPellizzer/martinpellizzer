Elaeocarpus ptilanthus

Elaeocarpus ptilanthus, commonly known as the white-leaf elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. It typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The fruit is a woody, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds, and the plant is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
