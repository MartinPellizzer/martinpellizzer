Eupatorium orizabae

Eupatorium orizabae, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium orizabae is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
