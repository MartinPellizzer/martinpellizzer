Erica fastuosa

Erica fastuosa, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica fastuosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
