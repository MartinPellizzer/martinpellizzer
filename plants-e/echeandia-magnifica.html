Echeandia magnifica

Echeandia magnifica, commonly known as the grand magnolia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and lilac. The plant features broad, glossy leaves that provide a lush green backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Echeandia magnifica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
