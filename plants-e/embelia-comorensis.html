Embelia comorensis

Embelia comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, spherical berries that are typically red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
