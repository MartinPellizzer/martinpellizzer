Eriocaulon subulatum

Eriocaulon subulatum, commonly known as the needleleaf flat sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, forming dense spikes that are usually green or reddish in color.

Eriocaulon subulatum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
