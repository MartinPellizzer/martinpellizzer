Erythraea tetramera

Erythraea tetramera, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in arid and semi-arid conditions, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
