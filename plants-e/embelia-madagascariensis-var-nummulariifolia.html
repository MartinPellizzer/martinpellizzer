Embelia madagascariensis var. nummulariifolia

Embelia madagascariensis var. nummulariifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar embelia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and thrives in humid, tropical environments. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a glossy surface, and the fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
