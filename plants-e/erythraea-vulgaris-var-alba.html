Erythraea vulgaris var. alba

Erythraea vulgaris var. alba, commonly known as the white erythraea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
