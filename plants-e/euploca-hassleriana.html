Euploca hassleriana

Euploca hassleriana, commonly known as the Hooker's euploca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features basal leaves that are linear to lanceolate in shape, often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, usually in shades of white or pale pink.

Euploca hassleriana is adapted to cold climates and is part of the Amaranthaceae family.
