Eleocharis bifida

Eleocharis bifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, ponds, and along the edges of lakes. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

Eleocharis bifida plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
