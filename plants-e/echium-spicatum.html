Echium spicatum

Echium spicatum, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Echium spicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
