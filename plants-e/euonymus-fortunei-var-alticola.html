Euonymus fortunei var. alticola

Euonymus fortunei var. alticola, commonly known as the Japanese spindle tree, is a shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to bronze in color. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that persist into winter. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact growth habit and ornamental value.

This variety is particularly suited to shaded environments and is valued for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
