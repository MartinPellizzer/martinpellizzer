Elaeagnus conferta var. septentrionalis

Elaeagnus conferta var. septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
