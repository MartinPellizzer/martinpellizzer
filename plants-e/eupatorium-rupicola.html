Eupatorium rupicola

Eupatorium rupicola, commonly known as rock eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows on rocky slopes and dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
