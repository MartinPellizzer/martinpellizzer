Eriogonum variabile

Eriogonum variabile, commonly known as the variable buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in a variety of habitats, including meadows, rocky slopes, and woodland areas. It has hairy leaves and a sprawling growth habit, often forming dense mats or shrubs.

Eriogonum variabile is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to different environmental conditions.
