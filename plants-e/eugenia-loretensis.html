Eugenia loretensis

Eugenia loretensis, commonly known as the Loretto guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some tropical gardens and botanical collections.
