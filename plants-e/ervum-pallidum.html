Ervum pallidum

Ervum pallidum, commonly known as the pale vetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

The pale vetch plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
