Eryngium riparium

Eryngium riparium, commonly known as water thistle, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles, and its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs. The plant thrives in moist or aquatic environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in shallow water. Its root system helps stabilize soil, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.

Eryngium riparium is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
