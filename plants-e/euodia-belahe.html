Euodia belahe

Euodia belahe, commonly known as the Malabar laurier, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, opposite leaves with a leathery texture. Its small white flowers are fragrant and appear in clusters, followed by small, round berries.

Euodia belahe is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
