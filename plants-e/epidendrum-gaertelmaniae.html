Epidendrum gaertelmaniae

Epidendrum gaertelmaniae, commonly known as the Gaertelmania Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in tropical forests. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs that support its linear, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish, with a distinctive spurred lip.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
