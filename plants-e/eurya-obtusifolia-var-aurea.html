Eurya obtusifolia var. aurea

Eurya obtusifolia var. aurea, commonly known as the golden-leafed eurya, is a variant of the evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a yellowish or golden underside, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various garden conditions.
