Erigeron acris proles pseudoelongatus

Erigeron acris proles pseudoelongatus, commonly known as the slender blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and prairies, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to support pollinators.
