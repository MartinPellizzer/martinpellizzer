Eritrichium fallax

Eritrichium fallax, commonly known as the alpine mouse ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, white or pale pink flowers resembling mouse ears. The plant has a mat-forming habit with hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Eritrichium fallax is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
