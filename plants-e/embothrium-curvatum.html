Embothrium curvatum

Embothrium curvatum, commonly known as the Chilean firetree, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southern Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a visually captivating plant. The leaves are leathery, elliptical, and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display. It thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
