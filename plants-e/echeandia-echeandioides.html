Echeandia echeandioides

Echeandia echeandioides, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to blue. The plant has a trailing growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Echeandia echeandioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of resilience in harsh environments.
