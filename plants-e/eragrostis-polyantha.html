Eragrostis polyantha

Eragrostis polyantha, commonly known as the bluegrass lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Eragrostis polyantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in dry environments.
