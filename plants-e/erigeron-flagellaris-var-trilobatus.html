Erigeron flagellaris var. trilobatus

Erigeron flagellaris var. trilobatus, commonly known as the three-lobed fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are typically pale blue to white, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This species thrives in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils, and is often found in the central and eastern regions of North America.
