Euphorbia multiformis var. kaalana

Euphorbia multiformis var. kaalana, commonly known as the Kaalana euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features striking, colorful bracts that range from red to orange, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has a short, thick stem with a caudex, and its leaves are typically modified into spines or small, fleshy structures. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in succulent gardens.
