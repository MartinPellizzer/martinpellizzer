Eriogonum elatum var. incurvum

Eriogonum elatum var. incurvum, commonly known as the bentleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that curve inward, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
