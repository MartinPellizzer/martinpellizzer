Eugenia lhotzkyana

Eugenia lhotzkyana, commonly known as the Lhotzkyana clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red or purple berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and essential oils.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
