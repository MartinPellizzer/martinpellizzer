Emilia pseudactis var. pseudactis

Emilia pseudactis var. pseudactis, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red ray florets and yellow disk florets, which create a striking visual display. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with hairy stems and heart-shaped, serrated leaves. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Emilia pseudactis var. pseudactis is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
