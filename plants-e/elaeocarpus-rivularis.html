Elaeocarpus rivularis

Elaeocarpus rivularis, commonly known as the river elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
