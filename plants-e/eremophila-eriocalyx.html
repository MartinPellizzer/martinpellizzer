Eremophila eriocalyx

Eremophila eriocalyx, commonly known as the Woolly Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its grey-green, woolly foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Eremophila eriocalyx is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
