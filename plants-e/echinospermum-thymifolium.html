Echinospermum thymifolium

Echinospermum thymifolium, commonly known as the thyme-leaved echinosperma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Echinospermum thymifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and fragrant foliage.
