Eugenia palembanica

Eugenia palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
