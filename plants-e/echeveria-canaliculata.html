Echeveria canaliculata

Echeveria canaliculata, commonly known as the "Cup-shaped Echeveria," is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is popular as a decorative houseplant and for rock garden displays.
