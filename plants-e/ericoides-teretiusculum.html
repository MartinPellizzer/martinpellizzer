Ericoides teretiusculum

Ericoides teretiusculum, commonly known as the slender ericoid, is a low-growing shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
