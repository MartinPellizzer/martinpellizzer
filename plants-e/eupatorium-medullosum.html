Eupatorium medullosum

Eupatorium medullosum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium medullosum is valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
