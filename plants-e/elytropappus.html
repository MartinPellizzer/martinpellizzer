Elytropappus

Elytropappus, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The genus is noted for its adaptability to harsh conditions and its role in ecological systems as a source of nectar for pollinators.
