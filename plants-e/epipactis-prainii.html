Epipactis prainii

Epipactis prainii, commonly known as Prain's helleborine, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a rhizomatous root system and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces a single, showy spike of flowers with greenish-white petals and a distinctive yellow lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories.

Epipactis prainii is known for its specialized pollination mechanism involving specific insects.
