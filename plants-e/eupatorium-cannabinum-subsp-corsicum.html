Eupatorium cannabinum subsp. corsicum

Eupatorium cannabinum subsp.

corsicum, commonly known as Corsican hemp, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along coastal areas and riverbanks.

The plant is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a rough, hairy texture.

It plays a role in local ecosystems and has been historically used for its fibrous material.
