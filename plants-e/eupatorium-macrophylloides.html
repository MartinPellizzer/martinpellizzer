Eupatorium macrophylloides

Eupatorium macrophylloides, commonly known as the large-leaved boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and produces showy, pale purple to white composite flowers. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators and as a component of wetland ecosystems.
