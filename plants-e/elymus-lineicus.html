Elymus lineicus

Elymus lineicus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Elymus lineicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some regions.
