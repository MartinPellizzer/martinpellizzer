Erica pachycephala

Erica pachycephala, commonly known as the broad-headed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica pachycephala typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.
