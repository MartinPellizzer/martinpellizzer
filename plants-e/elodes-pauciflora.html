Elodes pauciflora

Elodes pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense underwater mats. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often coated in a fine layer of mucus, which helps it adhere to substrates. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually borne in clusters near the water surface, and the plant reproduces both sexually and asexually through vegetative propagation.

Elodes pauciflora plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
