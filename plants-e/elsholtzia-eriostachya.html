Elsholtzia eriostachya

Elsholtzia eriostachya, commonly known as the silverleaf germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, gray-green leaves that are densely covered with fine hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Elsholtzia eriostachya is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
