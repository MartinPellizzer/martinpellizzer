Epilobium pulchrum var. albiflorum

Epilobium pulchrum var. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often calcareous soils. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
