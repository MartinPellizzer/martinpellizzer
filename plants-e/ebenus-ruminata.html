Ebenus ruminata

Ebenus ruminata, commonly known as the rough hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its yellow, rayless flowers that form a flat-topped inflorescence. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rough texture.

Ebenus ruminata is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
