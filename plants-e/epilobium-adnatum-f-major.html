Epilobium adnatum f. major

Epilobium adnatum f. major, commonly known as the large-leaved willow herb, is a variant of the willow herb species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its lanceolate, often hairy leaves and showy pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are arranged in dense spikes and bloom from late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
