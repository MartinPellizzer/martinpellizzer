Echinochloa equitans

Echinochloa equitans, commonly known as barnyard grass, is an annual grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with prominent midribs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It is often found in disturbed soils, fields, and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.

Due to its rapid growth and competitive nature, it can significantly impact crop yields if left uncontrolled.
