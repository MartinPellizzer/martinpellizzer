Erucastrum nasturtiifolium

Erucastrum nasturtiifolium, commonly known as the stinkweed or false nasturtium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Its leaves and flowers are edible, though they have a pungent, peppery flavor similar to that of watercress.
