Echinopepon coulteri

Echinopepon coulteri, commonly known as Coulter's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible, round fruits that are typically orange or red in color. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

It plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
