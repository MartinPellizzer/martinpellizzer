Elaeagnus canadensis

Elaeagnus canadensis, commonly known as silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, yellowish-green berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in dry, sandy areas. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Elaeagnus canadensis is also valued for its hardiness and potential use in erosion control and wildlife habitat.
