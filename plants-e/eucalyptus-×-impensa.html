Eucalyptus × impensa

Eucalyptus × impensa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Eucalyptus × impensa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in supporting native wildlife and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
