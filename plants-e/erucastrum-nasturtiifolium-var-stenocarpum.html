Erucastrum nasturtiifolium var. stenocarpum

Erucastrum nasturtiifolium var. stenocarpum, commonly known as the narrow-capsuled bittercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

The species is characterized by its peppery taste, which is due to the presence of glucosinolates.
