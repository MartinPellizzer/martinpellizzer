Eustachys caribaea

Eustachys caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean panicgrass, is a grass species native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Eustachys caribaea is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
