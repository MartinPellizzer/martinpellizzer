Euphorbia corynoclada

Euphorbia corynoclada, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among many species in the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it a notable plant in its native habitat.
