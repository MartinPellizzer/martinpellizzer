Elaeocarpus bilongvinas

Elaeocarpus bilongvinas, commonly known as the longvinas elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
