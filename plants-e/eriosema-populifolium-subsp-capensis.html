Eriosema populifolium subsp. capensis

Eriosema populifolium subsp.

capensis, commonly known as the Cape eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features a basal rosette of broad, ovate leaves with a softly hairy underside, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
