Eria daymaniana

Eria daymaniana, commonly known as the Dayman's orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has long, slender stems that often trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a climbing plant. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers warm, humid environments with indirect light.

Eria daymaniana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
