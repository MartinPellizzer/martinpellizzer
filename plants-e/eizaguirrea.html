Eizaguirrea

Eizaguirrea, commonly known as the Spanish hawthorn, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Eizaguirrea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
