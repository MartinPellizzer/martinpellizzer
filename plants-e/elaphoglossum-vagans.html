Elaphoglossum vagans

Elaphoglossum vagans, commonly known as the wandering maidenhair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds that are typically bi-lobed and have a distinctive, finely divided structure. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its spores are produced in small, clustered structures called sori, which are located on the underside of the fronds.

Elaphoglossum vagans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
