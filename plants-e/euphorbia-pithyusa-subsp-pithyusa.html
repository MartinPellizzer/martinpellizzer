Euphorbia pithyusa subsp. pithyusa

Euphorbia pithyusa subsp.

pithyusa, commonly known as the smooth euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
