Eugenia paraguayensis

Eugenia paraguayensis, commonly known as the Paraguay guava, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fragrant fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and ecological restoration projects.
