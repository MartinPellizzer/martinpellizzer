Eucalyptus baiophylla

Eucalyptus baiophylla, commonly known as the white-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically white or pale grey in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are usually cream or pale yellow in hue. It is often found in heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
