Eria uniflora

Eria uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and produces a single, showy flower per stem. The plant typically blooms in spring or early summer, with a distinctive orchid-like flower that is often pale green or yellowish in color. It prefers warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Eria uniflora make it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts seeking a unique and elegant addition to their collection.
