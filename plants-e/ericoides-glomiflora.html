Ericoides glomiflora

Ericoides glomiflora, commonly known as the globeflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces yellowish to reddish flowers, which are borne on short pedicels and are often surrounded by dense, woolly foliage. Ericoides glomiflora thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in alpine gardens.
