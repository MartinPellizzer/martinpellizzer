Erechtites valerianifolius f. reductus

Erechtites valerianifolius f. reductus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture and a distinct, aromatic scent. This plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
