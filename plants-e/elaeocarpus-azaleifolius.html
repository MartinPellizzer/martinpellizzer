Elaeocarpus azaleifolius

Elaeocarpus azaleifolius, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and coastal areas.

The tree has a slow growth rate and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
