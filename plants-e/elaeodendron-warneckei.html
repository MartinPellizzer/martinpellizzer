Elaeodendron warneckei

Elaeodendron warneckei, commonly known as the African moonseed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, elongated fruits. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
