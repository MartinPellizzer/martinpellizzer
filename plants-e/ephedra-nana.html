Ephedra nana

Ephedra nana, commonly known as dwarf ephedra, is a small shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae and is characterized by its branched stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine for its stimulant properties.

Ephedra nana is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in desert ecosystems.
