Eupatorium saucechicoense

Eupatorium saucechicoense, commonly known as saucechico, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and stream banks. Eupatorium saucechicoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
