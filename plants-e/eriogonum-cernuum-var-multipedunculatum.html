Eriogonum cernuum var. multipedunculatum

Eriogonum cernuum var. multipedunculatum, commonly known as the many-stemmed bunchflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces showy, yellow to orange flowers in late spring. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are often clustered at the base, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed structures called achenes.

This species is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
