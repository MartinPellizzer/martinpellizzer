Eupatorium galeopsifolium

Eupatorium galeopsifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped to ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium galeopsifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
