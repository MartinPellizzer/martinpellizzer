Eupatorium pinnangense

Eupatorium pinnangense, commonly known as Chinese boneset, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, herbaceous stems and opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, flat-topped clusters called capitula. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium pinnangense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
