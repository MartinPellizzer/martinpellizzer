Epipactis purpurata subsp. purpurata

Epipactis purpurata subsp.

purpurata, commonly known as the purple helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It features a rosette of dark green, hairy leaves and produces striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it has a complex relationship with mycorrhizal fungi.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
