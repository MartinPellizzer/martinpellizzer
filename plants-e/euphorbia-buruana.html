Euphorbia buruana

Euphorbia buruana, commonly known as the Buru spurge, is a succulent plant native to the island of Buru in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces a unique type of inflorescence called a cyathium, which is surrounded by colorful bracts. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive dry conditions.

Euphorbia buruana is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
