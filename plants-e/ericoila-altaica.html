Ericoila altaica

Ericoila altaica, commonly known as the Altai ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to the harsh, cold climates of its native habitat.

Ericoila altaica is often found in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in high-altitude environments.
