Eugenia grenadensis

Eugenia grenadensis, commonly known as the West Indian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, bright red fruits that are rich in vitamin C. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters, while its bark is smooth and dark in color.

The botanical features of Eugenia grenadensis make it both ornamental and valuable for its nutritional fruit.
