Epidendrum leochilus

Epidendrum leochilus, commonly known as the purple-lipped orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple pseudobulbs and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. Its flowers have distinctive lip structures with a prominent, often purple, central stripe.

This species is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
