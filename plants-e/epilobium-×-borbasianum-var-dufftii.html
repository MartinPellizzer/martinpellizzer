Epilobium × borbasianum var. dufftii

Epilobium × borbasianum var. dufftii, commonly known as the Dufft's willow herb, is a hybrid plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The flowers are typically pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

This plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
