Eupatorium chilca

Eupatorium chilca, commonly known as chilca, is a perennial herb native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium chilca is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
