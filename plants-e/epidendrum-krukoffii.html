Epidendrum krukoffii

Epidendrum krukoffii, commonly known as the Krukoff's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with multiple stems and produces clusters of showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has long, strap-like leaves that are often used for propagation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum krukoffii is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
