Erica trimera

Erica trimera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Erica trimera is a low-growing shrub that thrives in acidic, well-drained environments.

It is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is characteristic of the region's unique biodiversity.
