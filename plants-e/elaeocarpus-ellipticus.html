Elaeocarpus ellipticus

Elaeocarpus ellipticus, commonly known as the elliptic elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically covered in a thick, waxy layer. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
