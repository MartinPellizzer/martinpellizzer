Escobaria bisbeeana

Escobaria bisbeeana, commonly known as the Bisbee dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the Sonoran Desert of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family, characterized by its succulent, spineless stems and clustered, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, adapting to extreme heat and drought conditions. Its fleshy leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture.

Escobaria bisbeeana plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
