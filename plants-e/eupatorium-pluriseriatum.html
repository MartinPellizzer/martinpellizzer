Eupatorium pluriseriatum

Eupatorium pluriseriatum, commonly known as the clustered thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium pluriseriatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
