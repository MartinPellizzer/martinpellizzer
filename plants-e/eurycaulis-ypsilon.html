Eurycaulis ypsilon

Eurycaulis ypsilon, commonly known as the silverleaf euphorbia, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, silver-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of trichomes, which help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by distinctive, spherical seed pods. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access water from lower soil layers.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appearance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and rock gardens.
