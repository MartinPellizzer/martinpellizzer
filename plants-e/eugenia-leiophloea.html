Eugenia leiophloea

Eugenia leiophloea, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
