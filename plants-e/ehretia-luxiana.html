Ehretia luxiana

Ehretia luxiana, commonly known as the white mahogany, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and timber quality, it is sometimes cultivated in horticulture and conservation efforts.
