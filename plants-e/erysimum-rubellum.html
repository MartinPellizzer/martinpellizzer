Erysimum rubellum

Erysimum rubellum, commonly known as the scarlet wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that grows up to 30 cm tall, featuring basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. The plant produces bright red or orange-yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erysimum rubellum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms.
