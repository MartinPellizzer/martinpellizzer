Epilobium parviflorum var. mollissimum

Epilobium parviflorum var. mollissimum, commonly known as the velvet willow or silky willow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, woolly foliage and small, pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as stream banks and meadows. It has a trailing or shrub-like growth habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
