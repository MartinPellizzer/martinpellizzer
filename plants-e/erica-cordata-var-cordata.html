Erica cordata var. cordata

Erica cordata var. cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to the Western Cape region of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a favored plant among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
