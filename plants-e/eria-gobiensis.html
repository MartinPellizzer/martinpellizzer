Eria gobiensis

Eria gobiensis, commonly known as the Gobi eria, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in extreme drought conditions. Its roots are specialized for water retention, allowing it to thrive in its harsh environment.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
