Eupatorium lanulatum

Eupatorium lanulatum, commonly known as white feverfew, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered with a soft, woolly texture. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade.

Eupatorium lanulatum is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
