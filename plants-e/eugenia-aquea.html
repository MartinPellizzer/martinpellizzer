Eugenia aquea

Eugenia aquea, commonly known as the water myrtle, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. Eugenia aquea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water sources.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine and cuisine.
