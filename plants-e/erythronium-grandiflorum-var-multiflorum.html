Erythronium grandiflorum var. multiflorum

Erythronium grandiflorum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the multiflorous dog's mercury, is a member of the Colocasiaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, white to pale yellow flowers with three petals and three sepals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to parts of Europe.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in woodland gardens.
