Erythrospermum mauritanum var. amplexicaule

Erythrospermum mauritanum var. amplexicaule, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bright red, rose-like flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
