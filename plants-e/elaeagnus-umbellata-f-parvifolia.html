Elaeagnus umbellata f. parvifolia

Elaeagnus umbellata f. parvifolia, commonly known as silverberry or sand cherry, is a shrub native to the United States and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery, hirsute foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in umbels. The leaves are typically smaller and more lanceolate compared to the standard species, giving it a distinct appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
