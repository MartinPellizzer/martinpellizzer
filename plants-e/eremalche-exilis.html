Eremalche exilis

Eremalche exilis, commonly known as the slender eremalche, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its narrow, linear leaves and small, yellowish flowers are typical of the genus Eremalche.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in scrub and desert habitats.
