Erica arborea var. asturea

Erica arborea var. asturea, commonly known as the Asturian heath, is a compact shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. The plant has leathery, evergreen leaves that are dark green and aromatic. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
