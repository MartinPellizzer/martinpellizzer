Emilia juncea var. juncea

Emilia juncea var. juncea, commonly known as flossflower, is an annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and clusters of small, fluffy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Emilia juncea var. juncea is also valued for its ability to attract beneficial insects like bees and hoverflies.
