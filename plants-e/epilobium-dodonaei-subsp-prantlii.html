Epilobium dodonaei subsp. prantlii

Epilobium dodonaei subsp.

prantlii, commonly known as the Prantl's willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
