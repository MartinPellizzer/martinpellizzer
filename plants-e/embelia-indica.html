Embelia indica

Embelia indica, commonly known as wild betel, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, red berries. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1-2 meters and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are ovate with a glossy surface and emit a distinctive fragrance when crushed.

The berries are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
