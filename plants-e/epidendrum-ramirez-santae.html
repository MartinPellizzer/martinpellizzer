Epidendrum ramirez-santae

Epidendrum ramirez-santae, commonly known as the Ramirez-Santa Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, leafy stems and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces dark violet or purple blooms with white markings, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
