Erodium romanum var. castellanum

Erodium romanum var. castellanum, commonly known as the Castilian catchfly, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with toothed margins. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments.
