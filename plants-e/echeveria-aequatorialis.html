Echeveria aequatorialis

Echeveria aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian rose or the silver rose, is a succulent plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a frosty appearance. The plant produces striking clusters of pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant.
