Eleocharis equisetiformis

Eleocharis equisetiformis, commonly known as the spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its tufted, spiky appearance.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to submerged soil conditions. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often branched and bear small, scale-like leaves. Its flowering structure consists of a dense, spike-like inflorescence composed of tiny, usually wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis equisetiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
