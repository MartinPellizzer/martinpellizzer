Eriogonum nanum

Eriogonum nanum, commonly known as dwarf wild buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It typically reaches a height of only a few inches, with dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are often grayish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Eriogonum nanum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
