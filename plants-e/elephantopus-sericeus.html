Elephantopus sericeus

Elephantopus sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats and has a low, spreading growth habit. Its leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
