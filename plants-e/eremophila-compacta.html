Eremophila compacta

Eremophila compacta, commonly known as the compact emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often appearing in clusters. It is part of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus trees.

Eremophila compacta is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
