Elsholtzia splendens var. hallasanensis

Elsholtzia splendens var. hallasanensis, commonly known as the Hallasan mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the Korean Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs.

Its distinctive scent and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant in horticulture.
