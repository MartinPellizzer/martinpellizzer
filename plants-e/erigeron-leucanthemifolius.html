Erigeron leucanthemifolius

Erigeron leucanthemifolius, commonly known as white coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and a yellow-orange disk in the center. The plant has finely divided, silvery-green leaves that are often covered in a fuzzy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
