Euphorbia neocapitata

Euphorbia neocapitata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny, spherical fruit that encloses a cluster of seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cyathium, which is a unique structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are generally alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

Euphorbia neocapitata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which has a tangy, sweet flavor.
