Eugenia angulata

Eugenia angulata, commonly known as the angular eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its angular, ridged leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia angulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
