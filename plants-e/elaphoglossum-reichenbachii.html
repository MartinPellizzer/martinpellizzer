Elaphoglossum reichenbachii

Elaphoglossum reichenbachii, commonly known as the silver frond fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Americas.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are clustered on the undersides of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Elaphoglossum reichenbachii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
