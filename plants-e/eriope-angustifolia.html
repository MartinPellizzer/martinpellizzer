Eriope angustifolia

Eriope angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eriope, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. It is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an interesting subject for botanical study and a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
