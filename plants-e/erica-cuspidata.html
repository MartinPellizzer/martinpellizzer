Erica cuspidata

Erica cuspidata, commonly known as the needle heath, is a species of shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
