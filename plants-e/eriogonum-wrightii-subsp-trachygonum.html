Eriogonum wrightii subsp. trachygonum

Eriogonum wrightii subsp.

trachygonum, commonly known as the roughleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The leaves are typically rough in texture, giving the plant its common name, and are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
