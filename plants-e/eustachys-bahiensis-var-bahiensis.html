Eustachys bahiensis var. bahiensis

Eustachys bahiensis var. bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahamian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Caribbean islands.

It typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting a clump-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for coastal stabilization and ecological studies.
