Eucalyptus cameronii

Eucalyptus cameronii, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and inland regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, bottle-shaped fruit capsules. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often forming dense, low shrubs.

Eucalyptus cameronii is also known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
