Erigeron falconeri

Erigeron falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron falconeri is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, showcasing its resilience in harsh environments.
