Entada mossamhicensis

Entada mossamhicensis, commonly known as the Brazilian almond tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range from white to pink. The tree produces woody pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

The plant plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
