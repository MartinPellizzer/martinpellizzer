Erysimum marschallianum

Erysimum marschallianum, commonly known as the marsh carnation, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other garden and wild plants. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers with four petals, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
