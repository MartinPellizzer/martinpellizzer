Eupatorium ovandense

Eupatorium ovandense, commonly known as the Mexican thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in dense panicles, and are usually white to pale blue in color.

Eupatorium ovandense is often found in moist, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
