Elaeocarpus biflorus

Elaeocarpus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, red or yellow aril. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Elaeocarpus biflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous communities.
