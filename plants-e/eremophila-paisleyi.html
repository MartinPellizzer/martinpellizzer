Eremophila paisleyi

Eremophila paisleyi, commonly known as the Paisley Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Western Australia.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking clusters of pink to red tubular flowers that bloom during the warmer months. It is well adapted to dry conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy soils.

Eremophila paisleyi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
