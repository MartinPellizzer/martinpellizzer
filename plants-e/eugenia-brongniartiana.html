Eugenia brongniartiana

Eugenia brongniartiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or Brazilian rosewood, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has also been studied for its potential medicinal properties.

Due to its ecological and economic significance, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
