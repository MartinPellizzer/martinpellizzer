Erica inops

Erica inops, commonly known as the barren heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn. Erica inops is well adapted to fire-prone environments, with the capacity to resprout from its rootstock after burning.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
