Eleocharis ovata var. aphanactis

Eleocharis ovata var. aphanactis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant species native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic life.
