Elymus longearistatus subsp. canaliculatus

Elymus longearistatus subsp.

canaliculatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescence, which typically consists of closely arranged flowering branches.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe habitats. Its botanical features include hollow stems and awned lemmas, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native environment.
