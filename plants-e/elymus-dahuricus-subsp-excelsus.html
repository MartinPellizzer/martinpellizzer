Elymus dahuricus subsp. excelsus

Elymus dahuricus subsp.

excelsus, commonly known as the tall wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are often tinged with silver or purple, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
