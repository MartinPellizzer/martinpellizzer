Eugenia winitii var. terminalis

Eugenia winitii var. terminalis, commonly known as the Chinese tamarind tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, sour-sweet berries. Its fruit is often used in culinary applications, particularly in Southeast Asian cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
