Endopappus macrocarpus subsp. macrocarpus

Endopappus macrocarpus subsp.

macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited endopappus, is a species native to the southeastern United States. It is a shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is notable for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local pollinators.
