Elytranthe signata

Elytranthe signata, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Elytranthe signata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
