Erica triflora var. rosea

Erica triflora var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-colored heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or rose-colored, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a border plant.
