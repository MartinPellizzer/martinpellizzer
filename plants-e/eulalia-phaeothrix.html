Eulalia phaeothrix

Eulalia phaeothrix, commonly known as the dark threadgrass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. Eulalia phaeothrix is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
