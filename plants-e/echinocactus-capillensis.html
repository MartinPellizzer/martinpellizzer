Echinocactus capillensis

Echinocactus capillensis, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem covered with prominent, spiny tubercles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom at the top of the stem during the spring and summer months. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Echinocactus capillensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
