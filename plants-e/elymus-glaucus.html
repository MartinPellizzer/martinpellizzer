Elymus glaucus

Elymus glaucus, commonly known as blue wild rye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its bluish-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with slender, awned lemmas.

Elymus glaucus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
