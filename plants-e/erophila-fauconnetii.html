Erophila fauconnetii

Erophila fauconnetii, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Erophila fauconnetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
