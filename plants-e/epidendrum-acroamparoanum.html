Epidendrum acroamparoanum

Epidendrum acroamparoanum, commonly known as the Amparoan Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often with a waxy surface to retain moisture.

Epidendrum acroamparoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
