Enneapogon foxii

Enneapogon foxii, commonly known as the Fox's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, sparsely branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Enneapogon foxii plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitats.
