Echium obovatum

Echium obovatum, commonly known as the broadleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Echium obovatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting native wildlife.
