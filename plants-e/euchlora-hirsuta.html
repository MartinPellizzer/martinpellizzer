Euchlora hirsuta

Euchlora hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy echium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, erect stems and showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Euchlora hirsuta is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
