Elytraria tridentata var. wrightii

Elytraria tridentata var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's elytraria, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib, and it has a clump-forming growth habit.

This variety is distinguished by its unique floral structure and is often found in shaded, wet environments.
