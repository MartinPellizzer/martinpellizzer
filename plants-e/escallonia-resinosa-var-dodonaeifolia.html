Escallonia resinosa var. dodonaeifolia

Escallonia resinosa var. dodonaeifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed escallonia, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a valued plant in both cultivated and natural landscapes.
