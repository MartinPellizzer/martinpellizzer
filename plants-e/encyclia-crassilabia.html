Encyclia crassilabia

Encyclia crassilabia, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with broad, white to pale yellow petals and a distinctive, often red-tinged lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia crassilabia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
