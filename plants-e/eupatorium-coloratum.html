Eupatorium coloratum

Eupatorium coloratum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine white down. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is commonly found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eupatorium coloratum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
