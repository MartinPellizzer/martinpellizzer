Eurybia cunninghamii

Eurybia cunninghamii, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Australia.

It features showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Eurybia cunninghamii is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.
