Echinochloa crus-corvi

Echinochloa crus-corvi, commonly known as cocklebur, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny bracts that enclose the flowering structures. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its seeds are small and wind-dispersed, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Echinochloa crus-corvi is also known for its ecological role in supporting insect populations and as a weed in cultivated lands.
