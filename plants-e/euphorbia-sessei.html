Euphorbia sessei

Euphorbia sessei, commonly known as the Socotra candelabra, is a unique succulent native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its branched, tree-like structure with spirally arranged leaves that are typically blue-gray in color. The plant produces a striking yellowish-green sap, which is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited rainfall.

Its distinctive appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in the Euphorbiaceae family.
