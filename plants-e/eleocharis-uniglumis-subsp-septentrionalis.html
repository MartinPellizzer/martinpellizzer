Eleocharis uniglumis subsp. septentrionalis

Eleocharis uniglumis subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This subspecies typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are often concealed within the dense inflorescence.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
