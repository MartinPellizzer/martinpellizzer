Echites macrosiphon

Echites macrosiphon, commonly known as the giant devil's backbone, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a follicle containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest in botanical studies and traditional medicine.
