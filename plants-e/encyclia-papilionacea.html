Encyclia papilionacea

Encyclia papilionacea, commonly known as the orchid butterfly, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually yellow with red or orange markings, and it produces small, round seeds.

Encyclia papilionacea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or gardens.
