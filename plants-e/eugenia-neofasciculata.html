Eugenia neofasciculata

Eugenia neofasciculata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia neofasciculata*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red or purple berries that mature in the dry season. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Eugenia neofasciculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
