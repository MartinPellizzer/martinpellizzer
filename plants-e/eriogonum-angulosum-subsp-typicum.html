Eriogonum angulosum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum angulosum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the angular buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its angular, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to survive in arid environments.
