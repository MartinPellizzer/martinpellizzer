Elymus turgaicus

Elymus turgaicus, commonly known as the Turgaic grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Elymus turgaicus is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
