Eriosyce subgibbosa var. castanea

Eriosyce subgibbosa var. castanea, commonly known as the chestnut spurge cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in a layer of white, waxy bloom, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability to harsh environments make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
