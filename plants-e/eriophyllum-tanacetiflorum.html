Eriophyllum tanacetiflorum

Eriophyllum tanacetiflorum, commonly known as Woolly Bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, white to bluish-gray foliage that resembles wool. The plant produces small, blue or purple flowers arranged in cymes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Eriophyllum tanacetiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
