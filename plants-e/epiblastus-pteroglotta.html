Epiblastus pteroglotta

Epiblastus pteroglotta, commonly known as the silver leaf epiblastus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, glossy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or in dense undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant.
