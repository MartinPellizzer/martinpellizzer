Eucalyptus × crispata

Eucalyptus × crispata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pale yellow, which appear during the cooler months. Eucalyptus × crispata is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
