Echinops albicaulis

Echinops albicaulis, commonly known as the white-headed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Echinops albicaulis is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
