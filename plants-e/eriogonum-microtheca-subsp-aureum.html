Eriogonum microtheca subsp. aureum

Eriogonum microtheca subsp.

aureum, commonly known as the golden globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has soft, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
