Erysimum parryoides

Erysimum parryoides, commonly known as Parry's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with basal leaves and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its vibrant yellow or orange flowers bloom in early spring, adding color to its natural habitat.
