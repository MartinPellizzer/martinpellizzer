Eupatorium amygdalinum var. elegans

Eupatorium amygdalinum var. elegans, commonly known as the sweet pepper plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, red, berry-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
