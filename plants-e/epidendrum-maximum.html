Epidendrum maximum

Epidendrum maximum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Epidendrum maximum thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.
