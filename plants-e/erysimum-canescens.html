Erysimum canescens

Erysimum canescens, commonly known as hoary whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with grayish-white hair covering its stems and leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Erysimum canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
