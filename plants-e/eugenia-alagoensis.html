Eugenia alagoensis

Eugenia alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color, with a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
