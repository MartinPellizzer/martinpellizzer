Eriogonum brevicaule var. wasatchense

Eriogonum brevicaule var. wasatchense, commonly known as the Wasatch pussytoes, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with hairy stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces fluffy, white to pale pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
