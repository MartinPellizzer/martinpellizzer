Eriobotrya petiolata

Eriobotrya petiolata, commonly known as the Chinese whiteflowered loquat, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, which are followed by yellow, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its various health benefits.
