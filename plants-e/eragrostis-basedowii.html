Eragrostis basedowii

Eragrostis basedowii, commonly known as the basedow grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to arid and semi-arid conditions.

Eragrostis basedowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
