Euphorbia caiogala

Euphorbia caiogala, commonly known as the Cape York euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and showy cyathia, which are specialized flower structures. The plant is typically shrub-like, with a compact growth habit and can thrive in arid or semi-arid conditions. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils.

Euphorbia caiogala is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by Indigenous communities for various traditional purposes.
