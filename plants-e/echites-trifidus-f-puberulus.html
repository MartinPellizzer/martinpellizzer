Echites trifidus f. puberulus

Echites trifidus f. puberulus, commonly known as the pubescent three-lobed spurge, is a variant of the Echites trifidus species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its trifid (three-lobed) leaves and milky sap. The leaves are typically pubescent, meaning they are covered with fine hairs, giving the plant a soft texture. It is native to regions of southern Africa and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

This plant is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
