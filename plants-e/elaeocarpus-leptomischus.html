Elaeocarpus leptomischus

Elaeocarpus leptomischus, commonly known as the slender-fruited elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and it is often found in lowland and hill forest environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
