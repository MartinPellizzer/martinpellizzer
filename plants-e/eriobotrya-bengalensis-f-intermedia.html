Eriobotrya bengalensis f. intermedia

Eriobotrya bengalensis f. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian white apple, is a botanical variant of the Chinese white pear.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by yellow or orange fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and fruit, which is used in traditional medicine and cuisine.
