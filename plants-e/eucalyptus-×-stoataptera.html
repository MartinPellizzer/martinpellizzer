Eucalyptus × stoataptera

Eucalyptus × stoataptera, commonly known as the yellow box, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that ranges from grey to yellowish in color. The plant typically grows as a tall tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and usually pale yellow or green.

This species is well adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a common feature of woodland and scrub habitats.
