Elaeocarpus baeuerlenii

Elaeocarpus baeuerlenii, commonly known as the Baeuerlen's elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
