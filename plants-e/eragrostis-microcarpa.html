Eragrostis microcarpa

Eragrostis microcarpa, commonly known as dwarf lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, pale green to blue-gray seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used for erosion control.
