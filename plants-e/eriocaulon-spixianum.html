Eriocaulon spixianum

Eriocaulon spixianum, commonly known as the Spix's bulbous sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually borne on short, erect stems and produce tiny, white or pale green flowers.

Eriocaulon spixianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in water gardens or bog gardens.
