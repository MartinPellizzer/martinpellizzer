Eryngium integrifolium var. lanceolatum

Eryngium integrifolium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple spiky flower heads and lance-shaped, serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in prairie and open woodland habitats. It has a robust root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
