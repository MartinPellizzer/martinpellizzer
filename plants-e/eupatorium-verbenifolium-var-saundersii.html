Eupatorium verbenifolium var. saundersii

Eupatorium verbenifolium var. saundersii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and often branched.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
