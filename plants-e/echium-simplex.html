Echium simplex

Echium simplex, commonly known as the common viper's bugloss, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry grasslands, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas.
