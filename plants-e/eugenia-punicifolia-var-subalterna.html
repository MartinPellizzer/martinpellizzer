Eugenia punicifolia var. subalterna

Eugenia punicifolia var. subalterna, commonly known as the dwarf pomegranate, is a compact shrub native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by red, fleshy, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and seasonal fruit.
