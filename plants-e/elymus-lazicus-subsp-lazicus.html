Elymus lazicus subsp. lazicus

Elymus lazicus subsp.

lazicus, commonly known as the Central Asian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, cylindrical spikelets.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
