Echium pycnanthum subsp. caespitosum

Echium pycnanthum subsp.

caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted flaxflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces striking spikes of violet-blue flowers.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
