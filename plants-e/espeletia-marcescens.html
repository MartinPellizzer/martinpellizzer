Espeletia marcescens

Espeletia marcescens, commonly known as the "Cock's Comb" or "Páramo Flower," is a flowering plant native to the páramo ecosystems of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette growth form and striking, colorful inflorescences that resemble a comb. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and high humidity. Its leaves are typically thick and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Espeletia marcescens plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to the biodiversity of the páramo.
