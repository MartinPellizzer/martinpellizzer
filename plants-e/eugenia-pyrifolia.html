Eugenia pyrifolia

Eugenia pyrifolia, commonly known as Chinese dates, is a small deciduous tree native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellow to orange fruits that are rich in sugars and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and are borne in clusters.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
