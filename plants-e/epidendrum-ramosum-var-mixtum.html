Epidendrum ramosum var. mixtum

Epidendrum ramosum var. mixtum, commonly known as the Mixed Epidendrum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of fragrant, spathaceous flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining potting media, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its adaptability and ornamental value have contributed to its widespread use in horticulture.
