Esula multicorymbosa

Esula multicorymbosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of blue or violet. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Due to its unique floral structure and adaptability, it is a valued addition to botanical collections and gardens.
