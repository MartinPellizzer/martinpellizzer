Echinodorus macrocarpus

Echinodorus macrocarpus, commonly known as the broadleaf waterweed, is a popular aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often used in pond and aquarium settings due to its ability to provide oxygen and shelter for aquatic life. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by round, fleshy fruits.

Echinodorus macrocarpus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of maintenance in aquatic habitats.
