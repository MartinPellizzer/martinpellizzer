Eucalyptus sinuensis

Eucalyptus sinuensis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees with smooth, greyish bark. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a prominent midrib and a slightly wavy margin. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale blue, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in open woodlands and heathlands.
