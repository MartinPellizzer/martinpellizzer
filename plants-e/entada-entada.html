Entada entada

Entada entada, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, coral-like flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to the landscape. The tree has a smooth, often peeling bark that is light brown to grey in color. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, feather-like leaflets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
