Eragrostis pilosa var. condensata

Eragrostis pilosa var. condensata, commonly known as silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a low-maintenance ground cover.
