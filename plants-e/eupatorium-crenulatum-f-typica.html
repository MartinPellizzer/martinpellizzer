Eupatorium crenulatum f. typica

Eupatorium crenulatum f. typica, commonly known as the smooth blue woodruff, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features finely divided, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in panicle clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to native gardens and wetland restoration projects.
