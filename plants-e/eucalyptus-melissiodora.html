Eucalyptus melissiodora

Eucalyptus melissiodora, commonly known as the honey-myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in Queensland and New South Wales. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom during the spring and summer seasons. Its essential oils, derived from the leaves, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

Eucalyptus melissiodora is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
