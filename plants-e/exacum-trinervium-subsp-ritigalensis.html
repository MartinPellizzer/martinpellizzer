Exacum trinervium subsp. ritigalensis

Exacum trinervium subsp.

ritigalensis, commonly known as the Ritisalense bluebell, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Africa. It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form with lance-shaped, dark green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
