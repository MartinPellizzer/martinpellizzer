Euphorbia gentryi

Euphorbia gentryi, commonly known as Gentry's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Euphorbia gentryi is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its unique floral structures and medicinal properties in some species.
