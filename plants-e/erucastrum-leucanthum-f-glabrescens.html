Erucastrum leucanthum f. glabrescens

Erucastrum leucanthum f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-flowered white cress, is a variant of the white cress species native to Europe.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and small, white flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and slightly succulent, often with a peppery scent. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along riverbanks and in wet meadows.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
