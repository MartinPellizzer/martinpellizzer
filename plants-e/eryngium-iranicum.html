Eryngium iranicum

Eryngium iranicum, commonly known as Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves that form a rosette at the base, while its flowering stems rise prominently above. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Eryngium iranicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
