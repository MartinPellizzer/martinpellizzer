Echinospermum paniculatum

Echinospermum paniculatum, commonly known as the roughhead sunflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and yellow-orange flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in paniculate inflorescences. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Echinospermum paniculatum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
