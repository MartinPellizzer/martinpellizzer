Eugenia egensis var. bimarginata

Eugenia egensis var. bimarginata, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with white margins, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a small, hard drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in traditional medicine.
