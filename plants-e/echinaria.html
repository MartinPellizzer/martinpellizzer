Echinaria

Echinaria, commonly known as the desert hedgehog, is a genus of flowering plants native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its spiny, leaf-like structures that resemble the spines of a hedgehog. The plants typically have small, tubular flowers that are often yellow or orange in color. Echinaria species are drought-tolerant and adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

They are sometimes cultivated as ornamental plants due to their unique appearance and hardy nature.
