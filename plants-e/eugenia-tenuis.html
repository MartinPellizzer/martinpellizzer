Eugenia tenuis

Eugenia tenuis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, red, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.

Eugenia tenuis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
