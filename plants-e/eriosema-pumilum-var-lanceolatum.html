Eriosema pumilum var. lanceolatum

Eriosema pumilum var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the small-leaved eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with five petals, typical of the legume family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
