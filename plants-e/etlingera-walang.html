Etlingera walang

Etlingera walang, commonly known as the "white torch ginger," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its striking white bracts that resemble a torch, surrounding small yellow flowers. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, often reaching up to 1 meter in length.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
