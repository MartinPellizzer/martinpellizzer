Equisetum ramosissimum var. arcuateum

Equisetum ramosissimum var. arcuateum, commonly known as the scaly horse-tail, is a primitive vascular plant characterized by its whorled arrangement of branches and a distinct, segmented stem.

It belongs to the family Equisetaceae and is notable for its retention of traits from ancient plant lineages, such as the presence of true leaves and a circulatory system. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in wetlands. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobili, are clustered at the tips of the branches, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

The species is also recognized for its historical use in traditional medicine and its role in ecological succession in disturbed habitats.
