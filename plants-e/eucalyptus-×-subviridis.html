Eucalyptus × subviridis

Eucalyptus × subviridis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a hybrid species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Eucalyptus × subviridis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and a deep root system.

Its leaves and essential oils have been used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
