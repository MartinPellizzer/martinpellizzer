Epidendrum vitellinum var. majus

Epidendrum vitellinum var. majus, commonly known as the Golden Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which are borne on pendulous inflorescences. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and covered in a thick, leathery sheath.

The flowers are fragrant and bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
