Erica gallorum

Erica gallorum, commonly known as the common heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas. Erica gallorum is known for its ability to form thickets and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
