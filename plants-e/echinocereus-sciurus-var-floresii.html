Echinocereus sciurus var. floresii

Echinocereus sciurus var. floresii, commonly known as the Desert Hedgehog Cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in dense, spiny areoles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and enabling survival in extreme conditions.

This cactus is well adapted to low-water environments and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
