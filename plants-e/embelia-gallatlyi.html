Embelia gallatlyi

Embelia gallatlyi, commonly known as the Mexican embelia, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Embelia gallatlyi is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic benefits.
