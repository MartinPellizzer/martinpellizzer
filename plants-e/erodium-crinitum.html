Erodium crinitum

Erodium crinitum, commonly known as hairy bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or reddish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and covered with a dense layer of short, stiff hairs.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.
