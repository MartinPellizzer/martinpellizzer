Echinocactus armatus

Echinocactus armatus, commonly known as the hooked echinocactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white, woolly hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
