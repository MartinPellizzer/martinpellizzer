Echeandia hallbergii

Echeandia hallbergii, commonly known as the Hallberg's cholla, is a spiny cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on the upper parts of the stems. Its structure allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Echeandia hallbergii is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun conditions.
