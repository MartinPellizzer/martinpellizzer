Erigeron libanensis

Erigeron libanensis, commonly known as the Lebanon fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The flowers, which bloom in summer, are pale blue to white, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
