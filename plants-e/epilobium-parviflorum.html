Epilobium parviflorum

Epilobium parviflorum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It typically grows in moist soils along stream banks, wet meadows, and other damp habitats. The plant features slender, erect stems with opposite, linear to lance-shaped leaves. Its small, pink to purple flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often found in disturbed sites and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
