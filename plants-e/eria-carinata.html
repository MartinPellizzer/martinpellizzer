Eria carinata

Eria carinata, commonly known as the hairy spike orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spike-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and fragrant, often displaying a mix of white and green hues.

Eria carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
