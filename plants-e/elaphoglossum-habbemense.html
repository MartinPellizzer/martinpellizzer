Elaphoglossum habbemense

Elaphoglossum habbemense, commonly known as the Habbema holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are typically divided into numerous narrow segments. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its fertile fronds, which are clustered in sori surrounded by indusia. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its ecological niche, making it a distinctive member of the fern family.
