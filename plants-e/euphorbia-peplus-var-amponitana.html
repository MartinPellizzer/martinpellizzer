Euphorbia peplus var. amponitana

Euphorbia peplus var. amponitana, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. The plant contains a milky sap that can cause skin irritation, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its compact growth habit makes it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
