Eremophila warnesii

Eremophila warnesii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use. It produces small, often pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila warnesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
