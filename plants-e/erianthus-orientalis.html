Erianthus orientalis

Erianthus orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-white plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Erianthus orientalis is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant, airy look and low maintenance requirements.
