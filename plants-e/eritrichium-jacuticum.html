Eritrichium jacuticum

Eritrichium jacuticum, commonly known as the Siberian mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and thrives in rocky or sandy soils. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Eritrichium jacuticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh conditions.
