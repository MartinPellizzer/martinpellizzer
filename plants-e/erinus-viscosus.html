Erinus viscosus

Erinus viscosus, commonly known as sticky eryngo, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often sticky stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Erinus viscosus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable addition to botanical gardens and natural habitats.
