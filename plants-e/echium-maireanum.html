Echium maireanum

Echium maireanum, commonly known as the Maire's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Echium maireanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
