Echium confusum var. philothei

Echium confusum var. philothei, commonly known as the Philothea evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces elongated seed pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique blooming pattern and ecological role in supporting nighttime pollinators make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
