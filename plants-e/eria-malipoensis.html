Eria malipoensis

Eria malipoensis, commonly known as the Malipo orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Eria malipoensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
