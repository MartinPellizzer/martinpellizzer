Equisetum hyemale var. suksdorfii

Equisetum hyemale var. suksdorfii, commonly known as the Suksdorf's horsetail, is a variety of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family, characterized by its whorled arrangement of leaves and a hollow, jointed stem. This plant is typically found in moist, calcareous soils and is often associated with wetlands and stream banks. Its spore-bearing structures, called strobiles, are distinctive and add to its botanical interest.

The variety is named after the botanist Suksdorf, who contributed to its classification.
