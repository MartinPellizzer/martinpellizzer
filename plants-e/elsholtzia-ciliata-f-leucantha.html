Elsholtzia ciliata f. leucantha

Elsholtzia ciliata f. leucantha, commonly known as the white-haired elsholtzia, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems that are often covered in dense, white or grayish hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pale blue to white, arranged in whorled clusters along the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
