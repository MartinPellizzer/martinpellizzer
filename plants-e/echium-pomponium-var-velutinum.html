Echium pomponium var. velutinum

Echium pomponium var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet thimbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
