Echinocactus pulcherrimus

Echinocactus pulcherrimus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stem that resembles a barrel, and it produces yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in hot, dry conditions.

The golden barrel cactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
