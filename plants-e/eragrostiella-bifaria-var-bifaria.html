Eragrostiella bifaria var. bifaria

Eragrostiella bifaria var. bifaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
