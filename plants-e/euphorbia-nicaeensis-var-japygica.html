Euphorbia nicaeensis var. japygica

Euphorbia nicaeensis var. japygica, commonly known as the Canary Island spurge, is a succulent shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features thick, ribbed stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers surrounded by green bracts. The plant has a distinctive, spiny appearance with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, typical of arid environments. It is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
