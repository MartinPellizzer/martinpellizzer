Erysimum ponticum

Erysimum ponticum, commonly known as the Crimean wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkans and parts of Turkey.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant has pinnately lobed leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments.

Erysimum ponticum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
