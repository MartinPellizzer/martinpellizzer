Elymus khokhrjakovii

Elymus khokhrjakovii, commonly known as the Khokhrjakov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its robust growth habit and dense, tufted clumps of leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Elymus khokhrjakovii is often found in steppe and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
