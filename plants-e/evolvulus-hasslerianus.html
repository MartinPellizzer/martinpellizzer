Evolvulus hasslerianus

Evolvulus hasslerianus, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that open in the morning and close in the afternoon. The plant produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, seasonal color.
