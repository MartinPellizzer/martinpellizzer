Eupatorium erodiifolium

Eupatorium erodiifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall.

Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
