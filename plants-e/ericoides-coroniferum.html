Ericoides coroniferum

Ericoides coroniferum, commonly known as the crown-bearing heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants adapted to poor soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and bloom in late spring or early summer.

Ericoides coroniferum is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, where it forms part of the understory vegetation in fynbos ecosystems.
