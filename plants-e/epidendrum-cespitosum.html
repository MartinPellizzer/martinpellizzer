Epidendrum cespitosum

Epidendrum cespitosum, commonly known as the tufted epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, clumping growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant typically features long, narrow leaves and thrives in bright, indirect light with high humidity. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.

Epidendrum cespitosum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to bloom multiple times throughout the year.
