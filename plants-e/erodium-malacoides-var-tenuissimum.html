Erodium malacoides var. tenuissimum

Erodium malacoides var. tenuissimum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early autumn. The plant has delicate, hairless stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
