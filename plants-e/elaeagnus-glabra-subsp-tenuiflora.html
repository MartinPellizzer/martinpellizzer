Elaeagnus glabra subsp. tenuiflora

Elaeagnus glabra subsp.

tenuiflora, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China. It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by small, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its leaves and fruits are used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
