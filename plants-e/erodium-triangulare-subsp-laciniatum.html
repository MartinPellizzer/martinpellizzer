Erodium triangulare subsp. laciniatum

Erodium triangulare subsp.

laciniatum, commonly known as the finely cut erodium, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features small, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed and finely divided, giving it a delicate, fern-like appearance.

The plant produces tiny, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and gravelly areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive species for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
