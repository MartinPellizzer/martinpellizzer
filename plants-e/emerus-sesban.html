Emerus sesban

Emerus sesban, commonly known as the sesban tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural systems. It produces long, slender pods containing seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The sesban tree is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
