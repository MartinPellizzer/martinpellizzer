Erica tenuiflora

Erica tenuiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in areas with high saline winds and low rainfall. The plant features narrow, leathery leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring. It is well adapted to harsh coastal conditions and is often found in dunes or rocky outcrops.

Erica tenuiflora is an important plant in its native habitat, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
