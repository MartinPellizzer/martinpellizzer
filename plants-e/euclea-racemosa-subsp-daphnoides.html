Euclea racemosa subsp. daphnoides

Euclea racemosa subsp.

daphnoides, commonly known as the Daphnoides euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has smooth, greyish bark and produces small, round fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance and hardiness.
