Eragrostis amabilis var. breviculmis

Eragrostis amabilis var. breviculmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and for erosion control.
