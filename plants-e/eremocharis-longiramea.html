Eremocharis longiramea

Eremocharis longiramea, commonly known as the long-stemmed spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, clustered flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Eremocharis longiramea is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
