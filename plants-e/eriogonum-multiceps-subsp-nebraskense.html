Eriogonum multiceps subsp. nebraskense

Eriogonum multiceps subsp.

nebraskense, commonly known as the Nebraska globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the central Great Plains of North America. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, leathery fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is an important component of native grassland ecosystems and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed sites.
