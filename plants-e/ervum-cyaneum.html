Ervum cyaneum

Ervum cyaneum, commonly known as the blue bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ervum cyaneum is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant floral display.
