Eresimus stellatus

Eresimus stellatus, commonly known as the star twiner, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eresimus stellatus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
