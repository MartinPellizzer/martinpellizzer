Eugenia dysantha

Eugenia dysantha, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Eugenia dysantha thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant and a source of traditional medicinal uses.
