Eriophyton tibeticum

Eriophyton tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan snowbean, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to the extreme conditions of its native habitat. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
