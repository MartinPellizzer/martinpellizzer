Eutacta excelsa var. glauca

Eutacta excelsa var. glauca, commonly known as the blue-leafed hibiscus, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage.

The plant features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The varietal name 'glauca' refers to the distinctive bluish tint of its leaves, which adds visual interest to the plant's appearance.
