Eriogonum arcuatum var. xanthum

Eriogonum arcuatum var. xanthum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy stems, which are typical features of the Eriogonum genus. The plant thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in desert and foothill regions. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

The yellow globemallow is an important nectar source for native bees and other pollinators.
