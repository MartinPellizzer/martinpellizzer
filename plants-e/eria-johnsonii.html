Eria johnsonii

Eria johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's eria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in the undergrowth. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Eria johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
