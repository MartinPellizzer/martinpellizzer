Euphorbia lomelii

Euphorbia lomelii, commonly known as Lomeli's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It has a shallow root system adapted to survive in dry conditions, and its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens.
