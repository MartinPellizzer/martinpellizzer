Echinocereus × lloydii

Echinocereus × lloydii, commonly known as the Lloyd's hedgehog cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus with a globular or cylindrical shape, often featuring a dark green to blue-gray coloration. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
