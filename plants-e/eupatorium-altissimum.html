Eupatorium altissimum

Eupatorium altissimum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are often mistaken for cotton, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium altissimum is often found in moist, open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
