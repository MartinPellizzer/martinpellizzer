Eryngium aristulatum var. hooveri

Eryngium aristulatum var. hooveri, commonly known as the Hoover's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple spiky flower heads and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that attract various pollinators.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
