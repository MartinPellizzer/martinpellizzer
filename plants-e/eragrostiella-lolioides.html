Eragrostiella lolioides

Eragrostiella lolioides, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its native environment, making it a resilient and ecologically valuable species.
