Echinocactus sellowii f. turbinatus

Echinocactus sellowii f. turbinatus, commonly known as the Turbinated Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the Echinocactus genus native to Mexico.

This cactus is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, spiny areoles. The stems are typically green to bluish-green, and they develop a fluted appearance as the plant matures. It produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the top of the stems.

This variety is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
