Eriocaulon yoshinoi

Eriocaulon yoshinoi, commonly known as the Yoshino's spikerush, is a member of the Poales order and the Eriocaulaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, this species typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

The plant is notable for its delicate appearance and ecological role in aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
