Eria versicolor

Eria versicolor, commonly known as the variable orchid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including countries like India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that vary in color, often ranging from white to pink and purple. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Eria versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its delicate and colorful blooms.
