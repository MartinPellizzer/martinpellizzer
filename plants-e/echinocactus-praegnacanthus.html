Echinocactus praegnacanthus

Echinocactus praegnacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that bear white to yellow bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, enabling it to survive in dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
