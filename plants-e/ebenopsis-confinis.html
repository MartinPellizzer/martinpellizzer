Ebenopsis confinis

Ebenopsis confinis, commonly known as the Florida hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.
