Eugenia mimetica

Eugenia mimetica, commonly known as the mimic guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, red or yellow berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
