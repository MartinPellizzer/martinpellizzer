Ericoides melantherum

Ericoides melantherum, commonly known as the black-throated ericoid, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant is adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland or moorland environments. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of wildlife.

Ericoides melantherum plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to soil stabilization and providing food for pollinators.
