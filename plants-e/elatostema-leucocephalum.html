Elatostema leucocephalum

Elatostema leucocephalum, commonly known as the white-headed buttercup, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It features a rosette of hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers with white centers, which are clustered in a distinctive head-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Elatostema leucocephalum is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
