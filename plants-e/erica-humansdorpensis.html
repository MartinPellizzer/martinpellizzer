Erica humansdorpensis

Erica humansdorpensis, commonly known as the Humansdorp heath, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is typically found in fynbos vegetation, which is adapted to the region's Mediterranean climate.

Erica humansdorpensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
