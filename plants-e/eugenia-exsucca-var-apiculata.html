Eugenia exsucca var. apiculata

Eugenia exsucca var. apiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red or purple berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
