Eupatorium mapiriense

Eupatorium mapiriense, commonly known as the Mapiri eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The showy white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
