Eriosorus angustus

Eriosorus angustus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spleenworts, is a member of the family Pterobryaceae.

It is a small, terrestrial liverwort that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a thalloid structure with a leafy stem and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its reproductive structures, including both male and female gametophytes, are closely clustered on the stem.

Eriosorus angustus is often found in forest floors and rocky crevices, contributing to soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its habitat.
