Eupatorium guadalupense var. laxum

Eupatorium guadalupense var. laxum, commonly known as the Guadalupe blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
