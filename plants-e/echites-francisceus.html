Echites francisceus

Echites francisceus, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a rare and endangered species native to the island of Mauritius.

It is a small evergreen tree characterized by its dark reddish-brown bark and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, round fruits that contain winged seeds. Due to its limited distribution and habitat destruction, it is critically endangered and protected under conservation efforts.

Its unique botanical features make it a significant species in the study of tropical flora.
