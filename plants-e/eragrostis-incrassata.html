Eragrostis incrassata

Eragrostis incrassata, commonly known as broadleaf bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its wide, bluish-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months.

Eragrostis incrassata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
