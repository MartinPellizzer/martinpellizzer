Endoplectris

Endoplectris, commonly known as the grassy orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Endoplectris species are characterized by their slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate orchid flowers. They have a complex root system that helps them absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The genus includes several species, each with unique floral structures and growth habits.
