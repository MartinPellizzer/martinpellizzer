Elleanthus aristatus

Elleanthus aristatus, commonly known as the tall spike grass, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight reddish tinge at the base.

Elleanthus aristatus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
