Eugenia vahlii

Eugenia vahlii, commonly known as the Vahli's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and dark green, while the flowers are small and white or pale pink.

The fruit, which is edible, is a small, yellowish berry with a sweet and aromatic flavor.
