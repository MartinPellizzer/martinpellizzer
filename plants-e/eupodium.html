Eupodium

Eupodium, commonly known as the "silverweed," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
