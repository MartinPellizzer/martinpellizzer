Eucalyptus yilgarnensis

Eucalyptus yilgarnensis, commonly known as the Yilgarn gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dull green. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing the seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

The Yilgarn gum is an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
