Elegia amoena

Elegia amoena, commonly known as the elegant water violet, is a species of aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving waters, such as ponds and lakes, where it forms dense mats on the surface or submerged vegetation. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often floating or partially submerged, with delicate white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Elegia amoena is characterized by its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters, making it a common indicator of eutrophic conditions.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in aquatic habitats.
