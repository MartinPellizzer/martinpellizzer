Echinocactus chilensis f. confinis

Echinocactus chilensis f. confinis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-gray in color, covered with small, white tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts and conservationists.
