Elaeocarpus baclayanensis

Elaeocarpus baclayanensis, commonly known as the Baler elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically found in the mountainous regions of Baler, where it thrives in humid, tropical rainforest environments. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters, with smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter green underside.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fruit that is a woody capsule containing several seeds.
