Erysimum macropetalum

Erysimum macropetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled wallflower, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with large, showy petals that are typically more than 1 cm in length. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Erysimum macropetalum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
