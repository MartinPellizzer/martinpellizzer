Elaeocarpus reticulatus var. typicus

Elaeocarpus reticulatus var. typicus, commonly known as the net-veined elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale pink. The fruit is a hard, woody capsule containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
