Erica × triparentalis

Erica × triparentalis, commonly known as the Cape heath or triparental heath, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and red, bloom in spring and are highly attractive to pollinators.

Erica × triparentalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
