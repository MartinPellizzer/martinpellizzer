Elymus erianthus var. spegazzinii

Elymus erianthus var. spegazzinii, commonly known as the Italian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to golden in color, with awns that are often branched and stiff. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and forage production.
