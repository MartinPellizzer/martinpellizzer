Eriogonum corymbosum var. ambiguum

Eriogonum corymbosum var. ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a mat-forming growth habit. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped with a hairy texture, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
