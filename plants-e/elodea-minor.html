Elodea minor

Elodea minor, commonly known as water plantain, is a freshwater aquatic plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and whorled arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Elodea minor is often found in still or slow-moving water bodies such as ponds and lakes.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for various aquatic organisms.
