Elymus breviaristatus subsp. scabrifolius

Elymus breviaristatus subsp.

scabrifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. It is characterized by its coarse, scabrous leaves and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, spike-like structure with short, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
