Eugenia quinqueloba

Eugenia quinqueloba, commonly known as the five-lobed eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with five distinct lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and wetlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
