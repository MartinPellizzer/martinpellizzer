Eryngium amethystinum var. tenuifolium

Eryngium amethystinum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as slenderleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features a rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from purple to white, with bracts that are often blue or purple. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
