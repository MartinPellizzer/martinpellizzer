Eclopes

Eclopes, commonly known as the "false hellebore," is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its dark green, palmately lobed leaves and showy, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Eclopes has a caulescent growth habit, meaning it has a distinct, upright stem.

It is important to note that all parts of the plant are toxic and should be handled with care.
