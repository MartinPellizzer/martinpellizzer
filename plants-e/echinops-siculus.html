Echinops siculus

Echinops siculus, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a thistle, which are a key feature of the genus Echinops. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Echinops siculus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
