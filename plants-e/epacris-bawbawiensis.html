Epacris bawbawiensis

Epacris bawbawiensis, commonly known as the Bawbaw Flame, is an evergreen shrub native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its striking, tubular, red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Epacris bawbawiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
