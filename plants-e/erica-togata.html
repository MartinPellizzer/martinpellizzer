Erica togata

Erica togata, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Erica togata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its evergreen foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
