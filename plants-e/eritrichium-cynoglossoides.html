Eritrichium cynoglossoides

Eritrichium cynoglossoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mouse-eared chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically blue to violet in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Eritrichium cynoglossoides is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
