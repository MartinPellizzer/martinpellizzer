Eulophia aculeata

Eulophia aculeata, commonly known as the spiny eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia aculeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
