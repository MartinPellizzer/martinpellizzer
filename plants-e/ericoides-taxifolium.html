Ericoides taxifolium

Ericoides taxifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and leathery. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

Ericoides taxifolium is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of temperate ecosystems.
