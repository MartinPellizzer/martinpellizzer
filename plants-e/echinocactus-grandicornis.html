Echinocactus grandicornis

Echinocactus grandicornis, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Echinocactus grandicornis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
