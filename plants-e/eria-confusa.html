Eria confusa

Eria confusa, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in spring. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with each stem producing a new shoot from the base.

Eria confusa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
