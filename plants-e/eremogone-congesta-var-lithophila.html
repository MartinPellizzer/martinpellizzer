Eremogone congesta var. lithophila

Eremogone congesta var. lithophila, commonly known as the rock eremogone, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, clustered inflorescences of pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows on rocky outcrops and well-drained soils, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in a fine layer of wax to reduce water loss.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky habitats.
