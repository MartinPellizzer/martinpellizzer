Equisetum telmateia f. gracile

Equisetum telmateia f. gracile, commonly known as the slender horse tail, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of small, needle-like leaves and a hollow, segmented stem. This variety is distinguished by its slender, less robust form compared to the typical species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

The plant reproduces via spores and is often found in temperate regions with high humidity.
