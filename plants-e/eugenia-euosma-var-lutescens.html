Eugenia euosma var. lutescens

Eugenia euosma var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds, contributing to its ecological significance.

The wood of this species is lightweight and has been used historically for various purposes.
