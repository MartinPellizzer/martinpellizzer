Eriogonum trilobatum

Eriogonum trilobatum, commonly known as the three-lobed buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. It has hairy stems and leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name.

Eriogonum trilobatum plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.
