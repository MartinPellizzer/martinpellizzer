Eurotia ceratoides var. tragacanthoides

Eurotia ceratoides var. tragacanthoides, commonly known as the tragacanth tree, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant is characterized by its woody stem and trifoliate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

The tragacanth tree is also valued for its gum, which has been historically used in various industrial applications.
