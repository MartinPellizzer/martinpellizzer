Echites geminatus

Echites geminatus, commonly known as the twinleaf or twinleaf cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in spines, which help to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, a feature common in desert plants to minimize surface area and conserve moisture.

Echites geminatus is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found growing in rocky soils.
