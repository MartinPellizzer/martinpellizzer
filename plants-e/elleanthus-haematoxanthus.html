Elleanthus haematoxanthus

Elleanthus haematoxanthus, commonly known as the blood-red dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to red flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often hairy.

The species is notable for its striking coloration and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
