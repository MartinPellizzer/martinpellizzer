Echium trigonum

Echium trigonum, commonly known as the triangled evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, three-lobed seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable.
