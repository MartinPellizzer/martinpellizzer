Eyrythalia germanica

Eyrythalia germanica, commonly known as the German erythronium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Eyrythalia germanica is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
