Epicampes crassiculmis

Epicampes crassiculmis, commonly known as the broad-leaved cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and stream banks. It produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and garden settings.
