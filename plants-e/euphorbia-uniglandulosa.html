Euphorbia uniglandulosa

Euphorbia uniglandulosa, commonly known as the single-gland euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
