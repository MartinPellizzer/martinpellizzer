Echinocereus polyacanthus var. huitcholensis

Echinocereus polyacanthus var. huitcholensis, commonly known as the Huichol cactus or the red-tipped hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its unique appearance and striking flowers make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

The variety is named after the indigenous Huichol people, reflecting its cultural significance in the region.
