Elaeocarpus nephelephilus

Elaeocarpus nephelephilus, commonly known as the silver-leafed elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that are typically dark purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited use in traditional medicine.
