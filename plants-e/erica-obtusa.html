Erica obtusa

Erica obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica obtusa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in horticulture for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
