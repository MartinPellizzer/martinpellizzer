Euryspermum volutifolium

Euryspermum volutifolium, commonly known as the spiralleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
