Eremurus sibiricus

Eremurus sibiricus, commonly known as the Siberian Spearwort, is a tall, perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features a thick, fleshy rhizome and produces a striking array of tall, spiky flower heads that bloom in early summer. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic appearance.

Eremurus sibiricus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
