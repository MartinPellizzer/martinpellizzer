Epilobium exaltatum

Epilobium exaltatum, commonly known as tall willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshes. The plant features erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy, pink to purple flowers appear in clusters at the tips of the branches, blooming from June to August.

Epilobium exaltatum is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed sites, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
