Emilia prenanthoidea f. dentata

Emilia prenanthoidea f. dentata, commonly known as the toothed emilia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant appearance.
