Elaphoglossum schwackeanum

Elaphoglossum schwackeanum, commonly known as the Schwacke's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically arching or ascending in growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
