Erica calycina var. calycina

Erica calycina var. calycina, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.

Erica calycina var. calycina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
