Euphorbia ramiglans var. confluens

Euphorbia ramiglans var. confluens, commonly known as the confluence spurge, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, branched stems that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

This species is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
