Eugenia schottiana

Eugenia schottiana, commonly known as the Schott's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The fruit, which is small and reddish, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor.
