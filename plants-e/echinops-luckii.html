Echinops luckii

Echinops luckii, commonly known as the desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble a globe, which are composed of numerous bracts. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Echinops luckii is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to native plant gardens.
