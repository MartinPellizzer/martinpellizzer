Eryngium zosterifolium

Eryngium zosterifolium, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spiky flowers and silver-gray, fuzzy leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in sandy or rocky coastal habitats. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eryngium zosterifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
