Epipactis helleborine f. dilatata

Epipactis helleborine f. dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and its distinctive, showy greenish-yellow flowers with a dark purple lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Epipactis helleborine.
