Euphorbia hurusawae var. imaii

Euphorbia hurusawae var. imaii, commonly known as the Imai's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, cyathia-like inflorescences, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be toxic if ingested.
