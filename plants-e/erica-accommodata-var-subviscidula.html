Erica accommodata var. subviscidula

Erica accommodata var. subviscidula, commonly known as the sticky heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, sticky resin, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
