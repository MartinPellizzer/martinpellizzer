Eugenia sieberiana

Eugenia sieberiana, commonly known as the pink myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

Eugenia sieberiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
