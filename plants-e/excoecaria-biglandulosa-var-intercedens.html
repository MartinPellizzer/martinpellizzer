Excoecaria biglandulosa var. intercedens

Excoecaria biglandulosa var. intercedens, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical plant characterized by its smooth, pale pink to white bark and glossy, dark green leaves.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, segmented fruits with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of medicinal compounds.
