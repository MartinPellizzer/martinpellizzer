Exocarpos odoratus

Exocarpos odoratus, commonly known as the fragrant wax apple, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a smooth surface.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and the edible, aromatic fruits it produces.
