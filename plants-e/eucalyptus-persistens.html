Eucalyptus persistens

Eucalyptus persistens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Eucalyptus persistens is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional and modern applications.
