Eucalyptus pallidifolia

Eucalyptus pallidifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed eucalyptus, is a species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually appearing in shades of white or pale blue.

The bark is smooth and often peels in strips, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
