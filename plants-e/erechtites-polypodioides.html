Erechtites polypodioides

Erechtites polypodioides, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and a distinctive yellow-orange coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
