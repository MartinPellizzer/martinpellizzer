Eremurus stenophyllus subsp. stenophyllus

Eremurus stenophyllus subsp.

stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eremurus, is a perennial plant native to central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence that bears numerous yellow-green, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is valued in horticulture for its striking floral display and architectural form.
