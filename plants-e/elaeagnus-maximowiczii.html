Elaeagnus maximowiczii

Elaeagnus maximowiczii, commonly known as the silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by edible berries that are rich in nutrients. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Elaeagnus maximowiczii also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
