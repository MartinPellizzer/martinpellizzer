Eupatorium bishopii

Eupatorium bishopii, commonly known as Bishop's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
