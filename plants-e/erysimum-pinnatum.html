Erysimum pinnatum

Erysimum pinnatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in alpine or coastal regions. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring.
