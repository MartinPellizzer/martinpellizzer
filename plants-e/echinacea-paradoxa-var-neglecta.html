Echinacea paradoxa var. neglecta

Echinacea paradoxa var. neglecta, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It features a robust root system and hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, spiky purple flower heads in the summer, which are surrounded by prominent, yellow-orange bracts. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
