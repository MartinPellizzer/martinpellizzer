Euphorbia altissima var. nuda

Euphorbia altissima var. nuda, commonly known as the naked euphorbia, is a striking plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its tall, erect stems and distinctive, milky sap. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flower structures. Its leaves are typically narrow and alternate, often with a waxy coating.

Euphorbia altissima var. nuda is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
