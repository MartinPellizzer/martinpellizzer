Eugenia shettyana

Eugenia shettyana, commonly known as the Shetty's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the western Ghats of India and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
