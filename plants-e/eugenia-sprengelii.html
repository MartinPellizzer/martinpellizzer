Eugenia sprengelii

Eugenia sprengelii, commonly known as the Sprengel's clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pink to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing dense foliage, while its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens.

Eugenia sprengelii thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
