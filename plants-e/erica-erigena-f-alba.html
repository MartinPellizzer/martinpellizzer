Erica erigena f. alba

Erica erigena f. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features dense, wiry stems covered in small, needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in coastal and rocky garden environments.
