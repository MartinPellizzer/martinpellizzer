Eupatorium coelestinum f. album

Eupatorium coelestinum f. album, commonly known as the white eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist woodlands and wet meadows, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
