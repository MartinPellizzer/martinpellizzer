Erinus aethiopicus

Erinus aethiopicus, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant produces small, showy yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, giving it a daisy-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Erinus aethiopicus is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
