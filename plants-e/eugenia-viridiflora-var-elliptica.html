Eugenia viridiflora var. elliptica

Eugenia viridiflora var. elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf evergreen guava, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor, often used in culinary applications. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its role in ecological restoration projects.
