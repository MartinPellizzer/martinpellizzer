Echinocactus sellowii var. tetracanthus

Echinocactus sellowii var. tetracanthus, commonly known as the Sowell's hedgehog cactus, is a compact, spineless cactus native to Brazil.

It features a globular stem with a ribbed structure and produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in full sun and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
