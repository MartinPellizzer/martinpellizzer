Espostoa laticornua var. atroviolacea

Espostoa laticornua var. atroviolacea, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a striking tropical orchid native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with deep purple veins contrasting against a white or pale pink background. The plant typically grows in high-altitude cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a raceme, and it is known for its fragrant blooms.

Due to its rarity and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical collections.
