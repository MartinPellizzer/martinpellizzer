Euphorbia hirsuta f. serrulatifolia

Euphorbia hirsuta f. serrulatifolia, commonly known as the serrated hirsute spurge, is a succulent plant characterized by its upright, branching stems and distinctive, lobed leaves.

The leaves are typically green, with serrated edges that give the plant its specific epithet. It produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified floral structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. This variety is native to southern Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.
