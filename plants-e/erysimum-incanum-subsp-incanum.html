Erysimum incanum subsp. incanum

Erysimum incanum subsp.

incanum, commonly known as the hoary mustard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and woodland edges. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard plants.

The subspecies incanum is distinguished by its more pronounced pubescence compared to other varieties within the species.
