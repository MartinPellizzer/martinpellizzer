Eragrostis brasiliana

Eragrostis brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and typically have a bluish-green hue.

Eragrostis brasiliana is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
