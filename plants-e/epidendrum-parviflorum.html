Epidendrum parviflorum

Epidendrum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, pale green to white flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing a dense canopy.

Epidendrum parviflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid environments.
