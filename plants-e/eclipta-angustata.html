Eclipta angustata

Eclipta angustata, commonly known as Jerusalem cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a dense, spherical inflorescence. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Eclipta angustata is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
