Erythrina numerosa

Erythrina numerosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen contribute to its ecological and horticultural significance.
