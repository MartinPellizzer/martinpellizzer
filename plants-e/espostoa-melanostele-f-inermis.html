Espostoa melanostele f. inermis

Espostoa melanostele f. inermis, commonly known as the white-flowered espostoa, is a unique bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as a terrestrial bromeliad, forming a rosette of broad, waxy leaves that collect rainwater. Its flowers are clustered in a spike and bloom during the warmer months.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
