Eriogonum nudum var. paralinum

Eriogonum nudum var. paralinum, commonly known as the desert buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of its native range.
