Eucalyptus peltata subsp. dimorpha

Eucalyptus peltata subsp.

dimorpha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native fauna.
