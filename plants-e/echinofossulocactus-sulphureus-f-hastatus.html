Echinofossulocactus sulphureus f. hastatus

Echinofossulocactus sulphureus f. hastatus, commonly known as the yellow-hairy columnar cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, yellowish spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in arid or semi-arid environments.
