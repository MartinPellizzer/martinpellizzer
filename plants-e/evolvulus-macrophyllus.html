Evolvulus macrophyllus

Evolvulus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, large leaves and trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a trailing vine with hairy stems and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
