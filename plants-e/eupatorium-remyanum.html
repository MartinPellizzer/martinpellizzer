Eupatorium remyanum

Eupatorium remyanum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue-violet flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a silvery underside.

Eupatorium remyanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
