Enneastemon fornicatus

Enneastemon fornicatus, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowhead, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
