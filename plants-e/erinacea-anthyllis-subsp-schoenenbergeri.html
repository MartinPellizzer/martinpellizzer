Erinacea anthyllis subsp. schoenenbergeri

Erinacea anthyllis subsp.

schoenenbergeri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or maritime environments. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local insect populations.
