Ervum vicioides

Ervum vicioides, commonly known as the broad bean or fava bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with a hairy stem and trifoliate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
