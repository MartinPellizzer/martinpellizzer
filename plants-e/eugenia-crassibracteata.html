Eugenia crassibracteata

Eugenia crassibracteata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or candeia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Eugenia crassibracteata is also valued for its potential medicinal properties.
