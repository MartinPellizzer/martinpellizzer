Epipactis latifolia var. interrupta

Epipactis latifolia var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted broad-leaf helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowers have a distinctive structure with a spurred lip and are often pollinated by flies and bees.

This variety is distinguished by its interrupted leaf patterns and is sometimes considered a hybrid form of Epipactis latifolia.
