Euphorbia huanchahana subsp. penazuelensis

Euphorbia huanchahana subsp.

penazuelensis, commonly known as the Huancahua euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to the highlands of Peru. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It is often found growing in rocky soils at elevations above 3,000 meters.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
