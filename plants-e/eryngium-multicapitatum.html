Eryngium multicapitatum

Eryngium multicapitatum, commonly known as the purple thistle or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads that are surrounded by stiff, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium multicapitatum is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
