Eugenia punctulata

Eugenia punctulata, commonly known as the sour sop or guanabana, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that produces large, hairy, green fruits with a thick, spiky rind. The fruit contains a soft, white, sweet and sour pulp that is rich in nutrients and has a distinctive aroma. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its edible fruit as well as its medicinal properties.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
