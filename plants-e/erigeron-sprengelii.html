Erigeron sprengelii

Erigeron sprengelii, commonly known as Sprengel's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture.

Erigeron sprengelii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
