Elymus ovatus

Elymus ovatus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, streambanks, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that often lie flat on the ground, and its leaves are narrow with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with red or purple.

Elymus ovatus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
