Eugenia lucescens

Eugenia lucescens, commonly known as the shining eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters and features lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
