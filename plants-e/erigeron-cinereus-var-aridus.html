Erigeron cinereus var. aridus

Erigeron cinereus var. aridus, commonly known as desert blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. The plant features silver-gray foliage and produces small, daisy-like flowers that are blue to purple in color. It has a taproot system that helps it survive in drought conditions.

The species is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
