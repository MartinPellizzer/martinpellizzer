Ehrharta aphylla var. fasciculata

Ehrharta aphylla var. fasciculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf dallisgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. It has a clumping growth habit and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for use in landscaping and erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in ecological and agricultural contexts.
