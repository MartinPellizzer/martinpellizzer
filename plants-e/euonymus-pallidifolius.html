Euonymus pallidifolius

Euonymus pallidifolius, commonly known as the pale-leaf woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically pale green with a white or yellowish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by red, inflated fruits that split open to reveal seeds. Its foliage and fruit make it a notable species in woodland habitats.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and adaptability.
