Eriogonum esmeraldense var. esmeraldense

Eriogonum esmeraldense var. esmeraldense, commonly known as the Esmeraldense buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming a mat-like structure.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in dry landscapes.
