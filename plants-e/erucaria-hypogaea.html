Erucaria hypogaea

Erucaria hypogaea, commonly known as the Indian mustard or wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by siliqua fruits containing several seeds. It is native to regions of Asia, including India, and thrives in temperate climates.

Erucaria hypogaea is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural fields.
