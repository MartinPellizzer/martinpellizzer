Eryngium triquetrum subsp. xauense

Eryngium triquetrum subsp.

xauense, commonly known as the Xauense thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flower heads and hairy, gray-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in grasslands and open shrublands.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology and habitat preferences compared to the main species.
