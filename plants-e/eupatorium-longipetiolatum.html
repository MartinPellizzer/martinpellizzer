Eupatorium longipetiolatum

Eupatorium longipetiolatum, commonly known as the long-petioled boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ecological role and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
