Elephantopus dubius

Elephantopus dubius, commonly known as the African sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its yellow flower heads consist of numerous small florets arranged in a radiate pattern.

The plant is often found in open, sunny habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
