Eulophia hockii

Eulophia hockii, commonly known as the Hock's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with prominent, hairy sepals and petals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia hockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
