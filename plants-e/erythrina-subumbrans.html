Erythrina subumbrans

Erythrina subumbrans, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive bright red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Erythrina subumbrans is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitat.
