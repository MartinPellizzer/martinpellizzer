Escallonia × lepidota

Escallonia × lepidota, commonly known as the evergreen flowering shrub, is a hybrid species within the Escallonia genus.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage that provides year-round visual interest. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Escallonia × lepidota is also valued for its aromatic leaves and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
