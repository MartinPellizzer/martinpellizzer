Evolvulus fuscus var. virescens

Evolvulus fuscus var. virescens, commonly known as the greenleaf morning glory, is a species of flowering plant in the Bindweed family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. It is native to the southern United States and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
