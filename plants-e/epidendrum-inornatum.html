Epidendrum inornatum

Epidendrum inornatum, commonly known as the plain orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and multiple pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale green flowers with a distinct, fragrant odor. Its leaves are narrow, elongated, and often covered in a waxy coating.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.
