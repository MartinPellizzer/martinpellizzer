Eriocaulon simillimum

Eriocaulon simillimum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spike-like clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
