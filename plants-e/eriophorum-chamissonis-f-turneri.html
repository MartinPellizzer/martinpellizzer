Eriophorum chamissonis f. turneri

Eriophorum chamissonis f. turneri, commonly known as the Turners' cottongrass, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy seed heads that resemble cotton, which are produced at the end of its flowering stems. The plant typically grows in wet, acidic environments such as bogs and fens, where it thrives in nutrient-poor, waterlogged soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine, silky hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing peatland ecosystems and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
