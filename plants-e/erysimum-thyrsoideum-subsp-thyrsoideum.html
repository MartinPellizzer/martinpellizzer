Erysimum thyrsoideum subsp. thyrsoideum

Erysimum thyrsoideum subsp.

thyrsoideum, commonly known as the purple tansy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has pinnately lobed leaves with a distinctive, slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Brassicaceae family.
