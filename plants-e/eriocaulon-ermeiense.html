Eriocaulon ermeiense

Eriocaulon ermeiense, commonly known as the Chinese marsh grass, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in wetland and marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense inflorescences.

Eriocaulon ermeiense plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and is of interest to botanists studying aquatic plant adaptations.
