Erigeron leucoglossus

Erigeron leucoglossus, commonly known as the white coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, featuring slender stems and hairy leaves. Its showy white petals are surrounded by a yellow-orange disk, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, open habitats like prairies and wetlands.
