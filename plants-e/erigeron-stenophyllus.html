Erigeron stenophyllus

Erigeron stenophyllus, commonly known as narrow-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Erigeron stenophyllus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
