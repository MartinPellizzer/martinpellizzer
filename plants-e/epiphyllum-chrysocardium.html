Epiphyllum chrysocardium

Epiphyllum chrysocardium, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with flattened, leaf-like stems that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically yellow or orange, blooming at night. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
