Eupatorium martiusii

Eupatorium martiusii, commonly known as red root, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant is known for its deep red roots, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium martiusii is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
