Eunanus mohavensis

Eunanus mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils. It has adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Eunanus mohavensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small invertebrates.
