Eriocaulon atrum var. platypetalum

Eriocaulon atrum var. platypetalum, commonly known as the black rush, is a species of flowering plant in the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dark green, cylindrical stems and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in tight, dark green bracts, and it produces small, black seeds that are dispersed by water.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
