Eugenia dugandii

Eugenia dugandii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia* species, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

The tree is also cultivated for its aromatic wood and medicinal properties.
