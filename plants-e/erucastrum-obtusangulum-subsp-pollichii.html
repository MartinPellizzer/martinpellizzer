Erucastrum obtusangulum subsp. pollichii

Erucastrum obtusangulum subsp.

pollichii, commonly known as the Pollich's broad-angled cress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of broad, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers in racemes, which are typically arranged along the stem. It is native to central and eastern Europe and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
