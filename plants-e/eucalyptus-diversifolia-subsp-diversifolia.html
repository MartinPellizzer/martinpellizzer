Eucalyptus diversifolia subsp. diversifolia

Eucalyptus diversifolia subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color.

The tree produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or cream in color. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
