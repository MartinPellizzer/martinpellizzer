Espeletia brassicoidea f. constricta

Espeletia brassicoidea f. constricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the Andean highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool temperatures and well-drained soils.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is often found in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems.
