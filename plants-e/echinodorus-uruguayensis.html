Echinodorus uruguayensis

Echinodorus uruguayensis, commonly known as the Uruguay sword, is a freshwater plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Araceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in slow-moving to still water with moderate to high light conditions.

Echinodorus uruguayensis is popular in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide shelter for aquatic life.
