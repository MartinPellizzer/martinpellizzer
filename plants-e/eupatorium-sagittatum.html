Eupatorium sagittatum

Eupatorium sagittatum, commonly known as arrowhead eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its showy inflorescences.

Eupatorium sagittatum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
