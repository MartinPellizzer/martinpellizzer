Echeveria carminea

Echeveria carminea, commonly known as the Crimson Echeveria, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves with a reddish tinge, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container planting.

Its compact size and striking appearance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
