Eupatorium rhodanthum

Eupatorium rhodanthum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy purple to pink flower heads that resemble small cones. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eupatorium rhodanthum is often used in native gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
