Eryngium prostratum var. disjunctum

Eryngium prostratum var. disjunctum, commonly known as the creeping thistle, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and purple to pink flower heads.

It typically thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. The plant's leaves are narrow and covered with fine, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in compact, spherical clusters. It is native to North America and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

The species is also known for its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinator populations.
