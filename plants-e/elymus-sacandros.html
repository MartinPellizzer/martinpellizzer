Elymus sacandros

Elymus sacandros, commonly known as the sacandria wildrye, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically found in late summer. Elymus sacandros thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, prairies, and disturbed soils.

It plays an important ecological role by providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native range.
