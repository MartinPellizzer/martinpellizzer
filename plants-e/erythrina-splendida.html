Erythrina splendida

Erythrina splendida, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

Erythrina splendida is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
