Eupatorium purpureum var. holzingeri

Eupatorium purpureum var. holzingeri, commonly known as the Holzinger's purple thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically visited by bees and other pollinators. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.
