Eugenia widgreniana

Eugenia widgreniana, commonly known as the Widgren's eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are small, round berries that change color from green to red as they mature.

Eugenia widgreniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
