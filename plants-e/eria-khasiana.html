Eria khasiana

Eria khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the northeastern region of India.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, clustered in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
