Eleocharis subulata

Eleocharis subulata, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a species of sedge that typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed, bladder-like fruits. Eleocharis subulata is often found in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important part of aquatic and wetland ecosystems.

Its presence can indicate the health of these environments due to its sensitivity to water quality.
