Eriachne scleranthoides

Eriachne scleranthoides, commonly known as the slender spinifex, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, rigid, and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Eriachne scleranthoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
