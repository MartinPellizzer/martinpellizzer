Embelia whitfordii

Embelia whitfordii, commonly known as the whiteflower embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Embelia whitfordii is often found in moist, shaded forest understories and is valued for its aromatic properties in traditional medicine.
