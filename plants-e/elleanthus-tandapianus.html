Elleanthus tandapianus

Elleanthus tandapianus, commonly known as the Zanzibar dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal environments. It produces small yellow flowers that develop into fluffy seed heads, facilitating wind dispersal.
