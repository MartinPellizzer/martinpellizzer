Eria yakushimensis

Eria yakushimensis, commonly known as the Yakushimen orchid, is a rare and exquisite flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the inflorescence consists of a spike bearing multiple small, showy flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria yakushimensis is considered a conservation concern.
