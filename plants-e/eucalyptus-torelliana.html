Eucalyptus torelliana

Eucalyptus torelliana, commonly known as the Torell's mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually pink or white in color.

Eucalyptus torelliana is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystem.
