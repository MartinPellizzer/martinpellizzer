Erodium chium subsp. murcicum

Erodium chium subsp.

murcicum, commonly known as the Murcia bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and parts of southern France. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves.

The plant produces pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and it is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.
