Epidendrum ciliare

Epidendrum ciliare, commonly known as the feathered orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture from the air. Its flowers bloom in summer and are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegance.

Epidendrum ciliare is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
