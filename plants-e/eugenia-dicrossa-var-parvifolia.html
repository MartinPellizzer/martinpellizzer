Eugenia dicrossa var. parvifolia

Eugenia dicrossa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved wax apple, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.
