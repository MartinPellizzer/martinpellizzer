Elsholtzia carsonii

Elsholtzia carsonii, commonly known as Carson's hyssop, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, mint-like fragrance. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in late summer.

It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental and aromatic qualities.
