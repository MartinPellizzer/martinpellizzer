Erysimum corinthium

Erysimum corinthium, commonly known as the Corinthian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its bright yellow or orange flowers. Erysimum corinthium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
