Eurycentrum smithianum

Eurycentrum smithianum, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a striking tropical shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting birds and insects. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in tropical and subtropical regions.
