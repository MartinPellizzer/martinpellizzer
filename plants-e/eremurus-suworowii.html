Eremurus suworowii

Eremurus suworowii, commonly known as the giant sparrow's bill, is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial plant that produces tall, spire-like inflorescences composed of numerous yellowish-green to reddish flowers. The plant is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves and its ability to survive in harsh, dry environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its dramatic appearance.

The flowers are typically pollinated by insects, and the plant goes through a period of dormancy during the winter months.
