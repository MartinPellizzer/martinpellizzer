Elegia propinqua var. minor

Elegia propinqua var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slight hairy texture. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and borders.
