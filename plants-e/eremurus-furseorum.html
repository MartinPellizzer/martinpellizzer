Eremurus furseorum

Eremurus furseorum, commonly known as the sword lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height, topped with a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, showy flower heads that resemble a crown of white or pinkish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Eremurus furseorum is a perennial plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in rock gardens or as a dramatic garden accent.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking an unusual and eye-catching plant.
