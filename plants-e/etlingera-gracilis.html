Etlingera gracilis

Etlingera gracilis, commonly known as the slender torch ginger, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, red-orange bract flowers that resemble small torches. Its leaves are elongated and have a smooth, waxy texture.

Etlingera gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
