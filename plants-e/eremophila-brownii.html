Eremophila brownii

Eremophila brownii, commonly known as the Brown's eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits.

Eremophila brownii is often found in heathlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
