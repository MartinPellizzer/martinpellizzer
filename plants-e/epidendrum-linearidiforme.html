Epidendrum linearidiforme

Epidendrum linearidiforme, commonly known as the linear-leaf epidendrum, is a flowering plant species native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in greenhouses and as a houseplant.
