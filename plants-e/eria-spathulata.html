Eria spathulata

Eria spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are typically small, with a distinctive spatulate lip that gives the species its specific epithet.

Eria spathulata is appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
