Eulophia seleensis

Eulophia seleensis, commonly known as the Selenis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant typically produces a single, slender stem that bears a few broad, elliptic leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip.

Eulophia seleensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
