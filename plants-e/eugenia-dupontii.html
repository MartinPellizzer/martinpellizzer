Eugenia dupontii

Eugenia dupontii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia uniflora*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are red when ripe and are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Eugenia dupontii thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
