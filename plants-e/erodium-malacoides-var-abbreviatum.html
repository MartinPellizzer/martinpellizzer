Erodium malacoides var. abbreviatum

Erodium malacoides var. abbreviatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Africa.

It features small, pinkish to reddish flowers that bloom from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and elliptical leaves that are often gland-dotted. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Geraniaceae family.
