Eupatorium lanigerum var. burchellii

Eupatorium lanigerum var. burchellii, commonly known as Burchell's boneset, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
