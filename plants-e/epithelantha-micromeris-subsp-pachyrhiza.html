Epithelantha micromeris subsp. pachyrhiza

Epithelantha micromeris subsp.

pachyrhiza, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in dense, white to grayish spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. It is a succulent plant, adapted to arid environments with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
