Eugenia somalensis

Eugenia somalensis, commonly known as the Somali eucalyptus, is a shrub or small tree native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its botanical significance remains largely understudied.
