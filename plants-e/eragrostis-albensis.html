Eragrostis albensis

Eragrostis albensis, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its slender, erect stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with pink or purple.

Eragrostis albensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique visual characteristics.
