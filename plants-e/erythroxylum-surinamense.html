Erythroxylum surinamense

Erythroxylum surinamense, commonly known as Surinam copal or Surinam erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Suriname and the Amazon basin. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for various purposes.

The species is also notable for its potential medicinal properties and ecological significance in its native habitat.
