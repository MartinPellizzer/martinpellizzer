Eucalyptus planipes

Eucalyptus planipes, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. Eucalyptus planipes is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are used in traditional Aboriginal practices and have also been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
