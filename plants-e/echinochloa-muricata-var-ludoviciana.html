Echinochloa muricata var. ludoviciana

Echinochloa muricata var. ludoviciana, commonly known as the prickly foxtail, is a grass species native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescence covered in short, sharp bristles. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while the stems are erect and sometimes branched.

This variety is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
