Euphorbia kangdingensis

Euphorbia kangdingensis, commonly known as the Kangding spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in a cluster known as a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
