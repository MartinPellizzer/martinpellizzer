Eupatorium squalidum var. rusbyanum

Eupatorium squalidum var. rusbyanum, commonly known as the rusty eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from white to pale purple, appearing in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats.

The species is known for its distinctive rust-colored appearance, which is reflected in its common name.
