Erica johnstoniana

Erica johnstoniana, commonly known as the Johnston's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica johnstoniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
