Eulophia galpinii

Eulophia galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape at the base of the plant.

Eulophia galpinii thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.
