Erica pellucidioides

Erica pellucidioides, commonly known as the silver-leaved heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact foliage consisting of narrow, silvery-gray leaves that provide a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica pellucidioides is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative ground cover due to its ornamental value and hardy nature.
