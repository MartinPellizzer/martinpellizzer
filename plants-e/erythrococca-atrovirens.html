Erythrococca atrovirens

Erythrococca atrovirens, commonly known as the black-eyed cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its distinctive appearance. The plant features a short trunk with a crown of feathery leaves, and its cones are typically black or dark green. It is a slow-growing, long-lived plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
