Echinopsis camarguensis

Echinopsis camarguensis, commonly known as the Camargue cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of southern France and Spain.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming at night. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal rainfall.

Echinopsis camarguensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice for succulent gardens.
