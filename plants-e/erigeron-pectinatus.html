Erigeron pectinatus

Erigeron pectinatus, commonly known as the downy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

The leaves are usually hairy and have a serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
