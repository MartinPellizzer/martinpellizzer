Epilobium kamtschaticum

Epilobium kamtschaticum, commonly known as Kamtschatek willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Russian Far East and parts of eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, rocky or sandy soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, pink to purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epilobium kamtschaticum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
