Ephedranthus amazonicus

Ephedranthus amazonicus, commonly known as the Amazonian ephedra, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the family Ephedraceae, which includes plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and has woody stems that are characteristic of the ephedra genus. Its leaves are typically small and scale-like, adapted to the humid and often shaded environments of the rainforest.

Ephedranthus amazonicus is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential pharmacological applications.
