Erica colorans var. hispidula

Erica colorans var. hispidula, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the season. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
