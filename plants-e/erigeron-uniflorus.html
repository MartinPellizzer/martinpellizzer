Erigeron uniflorus

Erigeron uniflorus, commonly known as the singleflower blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often used in wildflower gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
