Eritrichium asperum

Eritrichium asperum, commonly known as the rough eritrichium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Eritrichium asperum is often found in open meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude climates.
