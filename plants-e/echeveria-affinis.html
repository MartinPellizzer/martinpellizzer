Echeveria affinis

Echeveria affinis, commonly known as the Mexican rose or sunburst echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, triangular leaves that are typically blue-green in color with a powdery coating. The leaves are often tinged with red or purple at the edges, especially when exposed to sunlight. It produces showy clusters of yellow flowers in the spring.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
