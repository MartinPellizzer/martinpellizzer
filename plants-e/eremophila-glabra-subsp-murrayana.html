Eremophila glabra subsp. murrayana

Eremophila glabra subsp.

murrayana, commonly known as Murray's emu bush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, pale yellow to pinkish flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The subspecies is known for its resilience to drought and its role in local ecosystems.
