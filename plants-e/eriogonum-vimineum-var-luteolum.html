Eriogonum vimineum var. luteolum

Eriogonum vimineum var. luteolum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, wiry stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert areas.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid environments and it is a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
