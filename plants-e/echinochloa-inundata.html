Echinochloa inundata

Echinochloa inundata, commonly known as watergrass, is a species of grass native to wetland environments.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based flowers. This plant thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed wetlands make it an important component of these ecosystems.

Echinochloa inundata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
