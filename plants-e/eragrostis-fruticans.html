Eragrostis fruticans

Eragrostis fruticans, commonly known as little barley, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its drought tolerance and attractive appearance.
