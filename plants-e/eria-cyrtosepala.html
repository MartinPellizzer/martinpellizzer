Eria cyrtosepala

Eria cyrtosepala, commonly known as the waxy orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, waxy leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect light.

Eria cyrtosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in terrariums or as a houseplant.
