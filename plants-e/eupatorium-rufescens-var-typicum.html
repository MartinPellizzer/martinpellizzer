Eupatorium rufescens var. typicum

Eupatorium rufescens var. typicum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features rough, hairy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive serrated edge. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing small, white to pale purple flowers that cluster in flat-topped umbels.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
