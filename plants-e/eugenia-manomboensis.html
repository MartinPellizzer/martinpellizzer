Eugenia manomboensis

Eugenia manomboensis, commonly known as the Madagascar guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red or yellow when mature. Eugenia manomboensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires a warm, tropical climate to flourish.
