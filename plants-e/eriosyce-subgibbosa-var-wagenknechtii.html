Eriosyce subgibbosa var. wagenknechtii

Eriosyce subgibbosa var. wagenknechtii, commonly known as the Wagenknecht's eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to thrive in arid and high-altitude environments.

This variety is notable for its unique morphology and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
