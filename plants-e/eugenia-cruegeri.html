Eugenia cruegeri

Eugenia cruegeri, commonly known as the Mexican holly, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Eugenia cruegeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
