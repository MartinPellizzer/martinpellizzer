Echites velutinus

Echites velutinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf echites, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
