Echeveria × grandisepala

Echeveria × grandisepala, commonly known as the hybrid echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosettes of thick, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves that can range from green to blue-gray or red. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring or summer. It prefers well-draining soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its hybrid nature, it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
