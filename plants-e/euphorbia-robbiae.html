Euphorbia robbiae

Euphorbia robbiae, commonly known as Robbia's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by a unique, colorful bract. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Euphorbia robbiae is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
