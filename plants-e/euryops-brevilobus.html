Euryops brevilobus

Euryops brevilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in coastal and sandy habitats. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its drought-resistant characteristics.

Euryops brevilobus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
