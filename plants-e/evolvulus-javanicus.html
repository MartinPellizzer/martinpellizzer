Evolvulus javanicus

Evolvulus javanicus, commonly known as blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

The species is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
