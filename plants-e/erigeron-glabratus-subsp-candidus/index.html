Erigeron Glabratus Subsp. Candidus

Erigeron glabratus subsp. candidus is a unique member of the aster family known for its delicate white flowers and soft, woolly foliage. This subspecies thrives in rocky and dry environments, often found in mountainous regions across North America. Its distinguishing feature is the fluffy, cotton-like appearance of its leaves, which give it a distinctive look. The plant is also admired for its ability to bloom in early summer, adding a touch of beauty to its natural habitat. Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
