Elaeocarpus atrovirens

Elaeocarpus atrovirens, commonly known as the black-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered flowers. The plant's fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
