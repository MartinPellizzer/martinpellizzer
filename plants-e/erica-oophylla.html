Erica oophylla

Erica oophylla, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related species. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing shrub form with small, needle-like leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica oophylla thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
