Echinomastus erectocentrus subsp. acunensis

Echinomastus erectocentrus subsp.

acunensis, commonly known as the Acunensis Echinomastus, is a succulent plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
