Erythroxylum citrifolium

Erythroxylum citrifolium, commonly known as the citrifolium coca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in high-altitude areas with cool, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its serrated leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed.

The species is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is closely related to other coca species.
