Eugenia miragoanae

Eugenia miragoanae, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia miragoanae*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, red, and edible, often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
