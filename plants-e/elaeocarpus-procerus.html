Elaeocarpus procerus

Elaeocarpus procerus, commonly known as the slender elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
