Euphrasia insignis var. insignis

Euphrasia insignis var. insignis, commonly known as the yellow-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of North America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, yellowish-orange flowers clustered in dense spikes. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is notable for its bright, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
