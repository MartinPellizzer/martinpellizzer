Eriogonum brevicaule var. viridulum

Eriogonum brevicaule var. viridulum, commonly known as the greenleaf buckwheat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features basal and sometimes spreading leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
