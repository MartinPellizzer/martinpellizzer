Erythropsis colorata var. bracteata

Erythropsis colorata var. bracteata, commonly known as the Bracteate Redstem, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
