Echinops × pellenzianus

Echinops × pellenzianus, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple spherical flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy, gray-green foliage that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops × pellenzianus is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
