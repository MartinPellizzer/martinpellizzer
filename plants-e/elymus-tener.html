Elymus tener

Elymus tener, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Elymus tener is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its tolerance to various environmental conditions.
