Epidendrum strobilicaule

Epidendrum strobilicaule, commonly known as the pineapple orchid, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its inflorescence consists of a spicate raceme with fragrant, yellowish-green flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various growing conditions.
