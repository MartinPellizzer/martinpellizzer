Eupatorium sordescens var. bolivianum

Eupatorium sordescens var. bolivianum, commonly known as the Bolivian white eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
