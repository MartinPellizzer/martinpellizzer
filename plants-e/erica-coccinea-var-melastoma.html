Erica coccinea var. melastoma

Erica coccinea var. melastoma, commonly known as the red heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and fynbos vegetation.

The foliage is dense and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
