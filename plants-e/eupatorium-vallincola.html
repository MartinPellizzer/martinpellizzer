Eupatorium vallincola

Eupatorium vallincola, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-gray foliage and clusters of small, white to bluish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Eupatorium vallincola is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
