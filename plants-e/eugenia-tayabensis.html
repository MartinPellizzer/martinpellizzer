Eugenia tayabensis

Eugenia tayabensis, commonly known as the Tayab mango, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, yellowish-orange drupe with a sweet, aromatic pulp. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine.
