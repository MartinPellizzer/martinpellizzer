Epilobium alsinoides subsp. atriplicifolium

Epilobium alsinoides subsp.

atriplicifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to the northern hemisphere. It typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along streambanks and wet meadows.

The plant features elongated, lance-shaped leaves and produces spikes of pink to purple flowers. It is a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to a variety of wetland environments.
