Eupatorium tomentellum

Eupatorium tomentellum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are often grayish-white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Eupatorium tomentellum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
