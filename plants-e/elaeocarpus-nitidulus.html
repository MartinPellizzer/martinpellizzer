Elaeocarpus nitidulus

Elaeocarpus nitidulus, commonly known as the shining elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, glossy bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers followed by spherical fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. Its seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, edible aril, which is a feature common in many species of the genus Elaeocarpus.

The plant is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
