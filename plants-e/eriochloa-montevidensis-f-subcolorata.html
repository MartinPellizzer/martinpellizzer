Eriochloa montevidensis f. subcolorata

Eriochloa montevidensis f. subcolorata, commonly known as the pale eriochloa, is a grass species characterized by its unique foliage and growth habits.

It is native to South America, particularly Uruguay and parts of Argentina, and thrives in grassland and open habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinct pale coloration, which sets it apart from other varieties of Eriochloa. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically greenish or pale in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest and low maintenance requirements.
