Eugenia sayeri

Eugenia sayeri, commonly known as the Sayer's evergreen apple, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit, which resembles a small apple, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor.

Eugenia sayeri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
