Encephalartos whitelockii

Encephalartos whitelockii, commonly known as the White Lock's cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its rarity and habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native region.
