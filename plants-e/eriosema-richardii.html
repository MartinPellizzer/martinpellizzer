Eriosema richardii

Eriosema richardii, commonly known as the African velvetleaf, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its softly hairy leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and the stems are often sprawling or prostrate.

Eriosema richardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
