Euphorbia larranagae

Euphorbia larranagae, commonly known as the Larranaga's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of central Chile.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters known as cyathia, which are typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
