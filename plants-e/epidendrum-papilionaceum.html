Epidendrum papilionaceum

Epidendrum papilionaceum, commonly known as the butterfly orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its unique floral display.
