Eucalyptus virgata var. triflora

Eucalyptus virgata var. triflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that typically bloom in white or pink. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and fire resistance. Its growth habit is usually low and sprawling, forming dense shrubs or small trees.

This species plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
