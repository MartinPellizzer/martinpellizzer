Eupatorium arborescens

Eupatorium arborescens, commonly known as boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring a sturdy, branching stem covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, heart-shaped, and often have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Boneset is known for its medicinal properties and is often found in moist woodlands and meadows.
