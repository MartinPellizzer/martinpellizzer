Euphrasia purpurea var. randii

Euphrasia purpurea var. randii, commonly known as the purple evening primrose, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southwestern United States.

It features delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from yellow to deep purple, often with distinctive markings. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

The flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
