Elatine heterandra

Elatine heterandra, commonly known as the slender waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Elatineae.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in length. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, with four petals, and it produces small, elongated seeds.

Elatine heterandra is often found in disturbed aquatic habitats and is considered an indicator of nutrient-rich waters.
