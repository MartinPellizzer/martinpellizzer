Echinochloa crus-galli f. sabulicola

Echinochloa crus-galli f. sabulicola, commonly known as sand panicgrass, is a grass species characterized by its robust growth and adaptability to sandy soils.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. This variant is often found in coastal and inland dune environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes. Its growth habit is prostrate to erect, depending on environmental conditions.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
