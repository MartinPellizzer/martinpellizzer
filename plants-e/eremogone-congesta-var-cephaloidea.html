Eremogone congesta var. cephaloidea

Eremogone congesta var. cephaloidea, commonly known as the desert bell, is a perennial shrub native to arid regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance in desert ecosystems.
