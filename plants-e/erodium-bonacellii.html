Erodium bonacellii

Erodium bonacellii, commonly known as the Mediterranean bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. The plant has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves, and its flowers are pink or purple, with five petals. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often found in gardens and along roadsides.
