Euphorbia flavicoma subsp. mariolensis

Euphorbia flavicoma subsp.

mariolensis, commonly known as the Mariolensis Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in xerophytic gardens.
