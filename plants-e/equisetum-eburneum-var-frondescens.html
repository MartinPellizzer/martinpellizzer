Equisetum eburneum var. frondescens

Equisetum eburneum var. frondescens, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a perennial plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its jointed, hollow stems that are typically green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones at the base of its stems, which are a key feature of its reproductive system. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it one of the oldest living vascular plants.
