Elekmania marciana

Elekmania marciana, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and other nightshades. The plant produces small, purple berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. Elekmania marciana is characterized by its hairy stems and lobed leaves.

It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found growing along riverbanks and in dense undergrowth.
