Erythrophleum angustifolium

Erythrophleum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, heavy wood and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its durable timber and is also used in traditional medicine.
