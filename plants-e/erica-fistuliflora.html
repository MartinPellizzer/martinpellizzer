Erica fistuliflora

Erica fistuliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica fistuliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
