Eugenia leucocarpa

Eugenia leucocarpa, commonly known as white-eared guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers and round, yellowish fruits that are edible. The leaves are simple, opposite, and have a leathery texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of fruit in certain regions.
