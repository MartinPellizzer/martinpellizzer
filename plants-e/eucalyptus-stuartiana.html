Eucalyptus stuartiana

Eucalyptus stuartiana, commonly known as Stuart's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces creamy-white flowers in clusters, which bloom during the cooler months. It is typically found in sandy soils in the semi-arid regions of South Australia and western New South Wales.

Eucalyptus stuartiana is well adapted to drought and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
