Erigeron × burnatii var. vierhapperi

Erigeron × burnatii var. vierhapperi, commonly known as the Fourleaf thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring gray-green, hairy leaves and showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
