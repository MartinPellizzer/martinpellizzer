Epidendrum romero-castannedae

Epidendrum romero-castannedae, commonly known as the Romero-Castaneda Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
