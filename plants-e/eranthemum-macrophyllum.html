Eranthemum macrophyllum

Eranthemum macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf eranthemum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
