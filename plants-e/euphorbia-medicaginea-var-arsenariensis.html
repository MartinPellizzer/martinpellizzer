Euphorbia medicaginea var. arsenariensis

Euphorbia medicaginea var. arsenariensis, commonly known as the Armenian spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features a short, woody stem with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid and rocky environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in challenging climates.
