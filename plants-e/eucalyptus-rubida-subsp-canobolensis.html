Eucalyptus rubida subsp. canobolensis

Eucalyptus rubida subsp.

canobolensis, commonly known as the canobolensis mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often covered in a fine, white bloom.

The flowers are typically small and arranged in clusters, producing a distinctive honey-scented fragrance. This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems, providing habitat and nectar for a variety of wildlife.
