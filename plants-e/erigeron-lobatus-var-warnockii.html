Erigeron lobatus var. warnockii

Erigeron lobatus var. warnockii, commonly known as the Warnock's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its distinctive lobed leaves and is of interest to botanists and conservationists due to its limited distribution.
