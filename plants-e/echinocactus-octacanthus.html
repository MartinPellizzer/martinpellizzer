Echinocactus octacanthus

Echinocactus octacanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white flowers. The plant has a dense, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

The golden barrel cactus is popular as a houseplant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
