Epimedium cremeum

Epimedium cremeum, commonly known as the golden smilacina, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are borne on short pedicels.

Epimedium cremeum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
