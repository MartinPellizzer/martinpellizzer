Eupatorium breedlovei

Eupatorium breedlovei, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-to-purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are usually hairy as well.

Eupatorium breedlovei is an important plant for pollinators, providing nectar for bees and butterflies.
