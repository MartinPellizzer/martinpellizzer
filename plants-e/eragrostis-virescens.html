Eragrostis virescens

Eragrostis virescens, commonly known as green lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is often used in erosion control due to its extensive root system.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
