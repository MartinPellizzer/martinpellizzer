Eutrochium purpureum var. purpureum

Eutrochium purpureum var. purpureum, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces showy, spiky purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
