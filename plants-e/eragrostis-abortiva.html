Eragrostis abortiva

Eragrostis abortiva, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale green flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
