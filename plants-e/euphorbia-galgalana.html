Euphorbia galgalana

Euphorbia galgalana, commonly known as the Galgalan spurge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to parts of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that appear in late spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Euphorbia galgalana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
