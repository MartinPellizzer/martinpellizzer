Eugenia convexinervia

Eugenia convexinervia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia convexinervia*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by bright red, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
