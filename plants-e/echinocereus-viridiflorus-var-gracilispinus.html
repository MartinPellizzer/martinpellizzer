Echinocereus viridiflorus var. gracilispinus

Echinocereus viridiflorus var. gracilispinus, commonly known as the greenflower hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus with a cylindrical stem that branches at the base. The plant produces clusters of greenish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
