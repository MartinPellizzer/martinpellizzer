Elaeophorbia grandifolia

Elaeophorbia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved aloe, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the spring and summer months. Its root system is adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

Elaeophorbia grandifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
