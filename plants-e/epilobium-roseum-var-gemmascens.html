Epilobium roseum var. gemmascens

Epilobium roseum var. gemmascens, commonly known as the gemmaforming willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish-purple flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges, riverbanks, and disturbed sites.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
