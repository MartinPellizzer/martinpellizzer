Ericameria parryi var. howardii

Ericameria parryi var. howardii, commonly known as the Apache goldbrush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit with dense, wiry branches and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The leaves are aromatic and contain compounds that contribute to its distinctive scent.
