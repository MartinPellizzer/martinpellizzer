Eugenia tumescens

Eugenia tumescens, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal flowering.

Eugenia tumescens thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
