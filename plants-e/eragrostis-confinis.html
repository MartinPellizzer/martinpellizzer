Eragrostis confinis

Eragrostis confinis, commonly known as narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.

Eragrostis confinis is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and withstand drought conditions.
