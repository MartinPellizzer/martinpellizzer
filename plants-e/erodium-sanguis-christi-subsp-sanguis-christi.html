Erodium sanguis-christi subsp. sanguis-christi

Erodium sanguis-christi subsp.

sanguis-christi, commonly known as the crimson bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, pinkish-red flowers.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while the fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and rocky areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
