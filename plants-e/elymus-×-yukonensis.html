Elymus × yukonensis

Elymus × yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon wild rye, is a hybrid grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open areas such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed sites. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Yukon wild rye is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
