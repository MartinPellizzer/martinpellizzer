Erythroxylum parviflorum

Erythroxylum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in humid, shaded environments. It produces small, red or orange berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
