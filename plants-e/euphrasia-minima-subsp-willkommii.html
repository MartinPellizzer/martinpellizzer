Euphrasia minima subsp. willkommii

Euphrasia minima subsp.

willkommii, commonly known as the small yellow gentian, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe. It features small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged opposite along the stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
