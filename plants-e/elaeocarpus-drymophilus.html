Elaeocarpus drymophilus

Elaeocarpus drymophilus, commonly known as the drymophilus elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its growth habit includes a tall, slender trunk with a dense canopy, making it a significant component of tropical rainforest ecosystems.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
