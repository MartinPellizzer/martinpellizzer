Eucalyptus baueriana subsp. deddickensis

Eucalyptus baueriana subsp.

deddickensis, commonly known as the Deddick River gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia. It typically grows in sandy soils and is found in the Geraldton area.

The plant has smooth, grey to white bark that peels in strips, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating. It produces small, globular flower buds that open to reveal creamy-white flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing aromatic seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is often used in local horticulture and conservation efforts.
