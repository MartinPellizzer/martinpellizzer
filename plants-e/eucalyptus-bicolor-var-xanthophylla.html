Eucalyptus bicolor var. xanthophylla

Eucalyptus bicolor var. xanthophylla, commonly known as the yellow-leafed ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-green foliage, which contrasts with its smooth, pale grey bark. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is well adapted to dry, sandy soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
