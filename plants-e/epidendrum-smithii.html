Epidendrum smithii

Epidendrum smithii, commonly known as the Smith's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing multiple stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in two rows along the stem. Its showy, fragrant flowers are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often yellow or orange.

Epidendrum smithii thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil for optimal growth.
