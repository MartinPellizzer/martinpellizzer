Evolvulus flavus

Evolvulus flavus, commonly known as the yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant has a trailing growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and twining.

The flowers open in the morning and close in the late afternoon, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
