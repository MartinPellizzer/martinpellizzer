Eremophila strongylophylla

Eremophila strongylophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and efficient water use.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
