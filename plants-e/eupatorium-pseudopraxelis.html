Eupatorium pseudopraxelis

Eupatorium pseudopraxelis, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble clusters of small blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. It has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces fluffy, white to bluish-pink blooms in the summer months.

Eupatorium pseudopraxelis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
