Erica glandulifera

Erica glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica glandulifera thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
