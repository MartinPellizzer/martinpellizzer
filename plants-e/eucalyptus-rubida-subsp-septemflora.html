Eucalyptus rubida subsp. septemflora

Eucalyptus rubida subsp.

septemflora, commonly known as the Seven-flowered Red Gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a reddish-brown color beneath.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue with a prominent midrib. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters of seven, giving it its subspecific name, and are usually pink to red in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
