Eleocharis atrobrunnea

Eleocharis atrobrunnea, commonly known as black-stemmed spike-rush, is a species of sedges native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, dark-colored stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.

Eleocharis atrobrunnea plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
