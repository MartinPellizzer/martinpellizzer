Echinocereus reichii

Echinocereus reichii, commonly known as the rainbow cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are often adorned with colorful, showy flowers. The plant produces vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and bats. Its succulent leaves store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Echinocereus reichii is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
