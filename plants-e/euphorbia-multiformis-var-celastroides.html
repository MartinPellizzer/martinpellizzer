Euphorbia multiformis var. celastroides

Euphorbia multiformis var. celastroides, commonly known as the celastroides euphorbia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa.

It features branching stems covered in a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it may have spines or bracts depending on the growth conditions.

It is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
