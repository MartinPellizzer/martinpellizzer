Eucalyptus conglobata var. anceps

Eucalyptus conglobata var. anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, globular flowers that typically appear in clusters. The plant is a woody shrub or small tree, often found in heathland and woodland environments. It plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various native fauna.

The species is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
