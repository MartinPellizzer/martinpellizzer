Elephantopus elatus

Elephantopus elatus, commonly known as the blue elephant's foot, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, blue-green leaves that form a rosette pattern. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the center of the rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
