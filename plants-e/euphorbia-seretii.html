Euphorbia seretii

Euphorbia seretii, commonly known as the Seret's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow花朵 that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
