Echinocereus tamaulipensis subsp. waldeisii

Echinocereus tamaulipensis subsp.

waldeisii, commonly known as the Tamaulipan Hedgehog Cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
