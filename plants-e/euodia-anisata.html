Euodia anisata

Euodia anisata, commonly known as Chinese prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a sharp, spicy flavor. Euodia anisata is valued in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

It is also used as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and winter interest.
