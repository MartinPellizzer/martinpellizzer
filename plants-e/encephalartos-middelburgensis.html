Encephalartos middelburgensis

Encephalartos middelburgensis, commonly known as the Middelburg cycad, is a rare and ancient plant species native to South Africa.

It belongs to the cycad family, known for their primitive structure and gymnosperm characteristics. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires a subtropical climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is of significant interest to botanists and conservationists.
