Eragrostis sclerantha subsp. villosipes

Eragrostis sclerantha subsp.

villosipes, commonly known as the hairy false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving the plant its common name, and it is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
