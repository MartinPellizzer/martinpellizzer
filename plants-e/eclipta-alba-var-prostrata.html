Eclipta alba var. prostrata

Eclipta alba var. prostrata, commonly known as white crownbeard, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features wiry, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and covered in fine hairs.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
