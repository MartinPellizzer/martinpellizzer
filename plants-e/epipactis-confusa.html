Epipactis confusa

Epipactis confusa, commonly known as the confused helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its dark green, sword-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. It produces a single spike of flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Epipactis confusa is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
