Eriobotrya latifolia

Eriobotrya latifolia, commonly known as the loquat tree, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to yellow berries in late summer. The fruit has a fragrant, aromatic pulp and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental and edible qualities.
