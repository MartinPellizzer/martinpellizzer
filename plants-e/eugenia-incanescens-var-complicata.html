Eugenia incanescens var. complicata

Eugenia incanescens var. complicata, commonly known as the rough-leaved eugenia, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often arranged in an alternate pattern. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.
