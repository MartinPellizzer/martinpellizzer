Euphorbia ambarivatoensis

Euphorbia ambarivatoensis, commonly known as the Amberivatoensis spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and rarity, it is a sought-after specimen among succulent enthusiasts.
