Eupatorium maritimum

Eupatorium maritimum, commonly known as seaweed or sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant thrives in salty, windy environments and is often found along shorelines and in dunes. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eupatorium maritimum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
