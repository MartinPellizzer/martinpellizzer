Euphorbia celastroides var. nelsonii

Euphorbia celastroides var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem system with thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
