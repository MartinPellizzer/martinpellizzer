Echinocactus hexaedrophorus f. fossulatus

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus f. fossulatus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a unique cultivar of the Mexican star cactus species.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a spiny, segmented appearance. The plant features a dense covering of cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. Its flowers, which appear in late spring, are yellow and tubular, attracting pollinators like bees and birds.

This cultivar is well-suited for arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
