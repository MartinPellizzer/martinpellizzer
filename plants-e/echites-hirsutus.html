Echites hirsutus

Echites hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy echites, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Apocynaceae.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
