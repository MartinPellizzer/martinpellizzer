Elymus erosiglumis

Elymus erosiglumis, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Elymus erosiglumis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in arid and semi-arid regions.
