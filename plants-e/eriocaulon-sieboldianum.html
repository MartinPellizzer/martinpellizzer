Eriocaulon sieboldianum

Eriocaulon sieboldianum, commonly known as the Siebold's marsh grass, is a small herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves.

Eriocaulon sieboldianum is adapted to waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
