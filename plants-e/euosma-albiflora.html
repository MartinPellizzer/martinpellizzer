Euosma albiflora

Euosma albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered euosma, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a subtle aromatic scent.

Euosma albiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual and olfactory characteristics.
