Erica auriculata

Erica auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. It produces fragrant, pink or white blooms during the summer months.

Erica auriculata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal climates.
