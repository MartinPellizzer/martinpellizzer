Euclea rigida

Euclea rigida, commonly known as the rigid euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Euclea rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
