Echinodorus horemanii

Echinodorus horemanii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette formation. This plant thrives in freshwater environments and is often found in slow-moving or still water bodies. Echinodorus horemanii is known for its ability to absorb nutrients from the water, making it beneficial for water quality.

It is a popular choice for aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
