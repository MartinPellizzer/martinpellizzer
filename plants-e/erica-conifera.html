Erica conifera

Erica conifera, commonly known as the heath shrub, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the western and southern regions of Europe, particularly in areas with acidic and well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. Erica conifera typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heaths and moors.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
