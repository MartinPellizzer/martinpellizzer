Eupatorium polystachyum var. angustius

Eupatorium polystachyum var. angustius, commonly known as narrowleaf thoroughwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clustered, small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, rough texture.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
