Eupatorium vauthierianum var. ramosissimum

Eupatorium vauthierianum var. ramosissimum, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue-gray flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that are often described as having a fern-like appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
