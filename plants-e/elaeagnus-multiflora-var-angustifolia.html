Elaeagnus multiflora var. angustifolia

Elaeagnus multiflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-white bark and small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various climates.
