Eriocaulon laosense

Eriocaulon laosense, commonly known as the Laos sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, a common trait among sedges.

Eriocaulon laosense is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Southeast Asia.
