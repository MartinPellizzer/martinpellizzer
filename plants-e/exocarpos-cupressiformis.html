Exocarpos cupressiformis

Exocarpos cupressiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved inkberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, dark green leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible and often used in jams and wines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal and sandy environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
