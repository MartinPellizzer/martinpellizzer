Eria mooreana

Eria mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, greenish-white to pale yellow blooms that are clustered in racemes. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eria mooreana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
