Eremostachys tournefortii var. macrocalyx

Eremostachys tournefortii var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered eremostachys, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It features wiry stems and small, gray-green leaves adapted to drought conditions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean gardens.
