Eugenia cyrtophylloides

Eugenia cyrtophylloides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue or black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

Eugenia cyrtophylloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in Indigenous Australian medicine.
