Ericoides filamentosa

Ericoides filamentosa, commonly known as the filamentscale heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats. Its flowers are typically pink or white, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are a food source for local wildlife.

Ericoides filamentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal ecosystems.
