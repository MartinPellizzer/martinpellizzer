Echium asturicum

Echium asturicum, commonly known as the Asturian fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Echium asturicum plays an important role in local ecosystems and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
