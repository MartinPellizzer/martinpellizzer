Echinopsis hardeniana

Echinopsis hardeniana, commonly known as the hardy night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are covered in white spines and a dense layer of white hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Echinopsis hardeniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.
