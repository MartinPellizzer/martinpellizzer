Eleutherococcus sessiliflorus var. parviceps

Eleutherococcus sessiliflorus var. parviceps, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are densely arranged along the stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its berries are red and contain several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

This species is valued for its adaptability to harsh climates and its use in traditional medicine.
