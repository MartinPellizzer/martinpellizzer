Euphorbia stoddartii

Euphorbia stoddartii, commonly known as the Stoddart's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with a short, thick stem and fleshy, lobed leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique morphology and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
