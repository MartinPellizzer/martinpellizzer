Echeveria uhlii subsp. uhlii

Echeveria uhlii subsp.

uhlii, commonly known as the Uhlii Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico. It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina.

The plant produces clusters of vibrant purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
