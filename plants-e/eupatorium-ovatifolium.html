Eupatorium ovatifolium

Eupatorium ovatifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
