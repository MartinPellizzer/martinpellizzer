Echium pabotii

Echium pabotii, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, ovoid seeds. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Echium pabotii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.
