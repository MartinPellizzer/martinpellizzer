Epiphyllum × jenkinsonii

Epiphyllum × jenkinsonii, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. These flowers are ephemeral, lasting only a few hours before closing in the morning.

Epiphyllum × jenkinsonii is often grown as a decorative plant due to its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
