Echinus baillonianus

Echinus baillonianus, commonly known as the Baillon's echinus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, globe-shaped fruit that resembles a sea urchin. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Echinus baillonianus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
