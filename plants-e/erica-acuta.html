Erica acuta

Erica acuta, commonly known as the sharp heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica acuta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
