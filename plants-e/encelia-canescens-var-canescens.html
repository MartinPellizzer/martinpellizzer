Encelia canescens var. canescens

Encelia canescens var. canescens, commonly known as the white globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its gray-green, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are clustered in globular inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
