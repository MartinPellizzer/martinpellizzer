Echinocactus chrysacanthion

Echinocactus chrysacanthion, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive yellowish-green color and spiny, ribbed stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, branched inflorescences. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
