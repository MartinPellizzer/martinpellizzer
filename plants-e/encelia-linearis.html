Encelia linearis

Encelia linearis, commonly known as the linear goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its slender, linear leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and coastal areas. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Encelia linearis is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
