Encyclia × padreortizii

Encyclia × padreortizii, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a natural hybrid between Encyclia cochleata and Encyclia frondifera.

It is a sympodial orchid with a compact growth habit, featuring a pseudobulb system that supports its growth. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typical of the Encyclia genus. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright but indirect light, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a hybrid that showcases the genetic diversity within the orchid family.
