Euphorbia hexadenia

Euphorbia hexadenia, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruit that resembles a small, hairy tomato. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is edible when ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and as a source of food in some regions.
