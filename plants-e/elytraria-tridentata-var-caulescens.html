Elytraria tridentata var. caulescens

Elytraria tridentata var. caulescens, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length, arranged in dense tufts at the base of the plant. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, white to pale green flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Due to its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils, it is a valuable species for habitat conservation.
