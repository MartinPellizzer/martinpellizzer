Ericoides lycopodioides

Ericoides lycopodioides, commonly known as the slender clubmoss, is a small evergreen plant belonging to the family Lycopodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem. The plant produces small, spore-bearing structures called strobila, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forests or rocky areas.

Ericoides lycopodioides plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
