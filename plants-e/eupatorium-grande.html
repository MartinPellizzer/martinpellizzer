Eupatorium grande

Eupatorium grande, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It features tall, erect stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Eupatorium grande is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, where it thrives in moist, rich soils.
