Echinocactus berteroi

Echinocactus berteroi, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collections and desert gardens.
