Eremophila microphylla

Eremophila microphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and spikes of bright red or pink flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant. It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Eremophila microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
