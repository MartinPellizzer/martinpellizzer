Eucalyptus piperita var. orophila

Eucalyptus piperita var. orophila, commonly known as the mountain pepper tree, is a variant of the narrow-leafed peppermint eucalyptus.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of southeastern Australia, particularly in the Alpine National Park. This plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The tree is valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
