Erythroxylum lineolatum

Erythroxylum lineolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed coca, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, and it is notable for its alkaloid content, including cocaine.

Its botanical features make it a subject of both scientific interest and cultural significance in the Andes.
