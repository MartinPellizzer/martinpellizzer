Eriocaulon luzulifolium

Eriocaulon luzulifolium, commonly known as the blue-flowered rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces delicate blue or purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Native to wetland habitats, it thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in marshes and bogs.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
