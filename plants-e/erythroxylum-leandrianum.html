Erythroxylum leandrianum

Erythroxylum leandrianum, commonly known as the pink stimulant plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its small, pinkish-white flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant is notable for containing alkaloids, though it is not as well-known for its psychoactive properties as other species in the Erythroxylum genus. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its botanical significance and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to researchers in pharmacology and botany.
