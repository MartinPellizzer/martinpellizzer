Eremogone festucoides var. festucoides

Eremogone festucoides var. festucoides, commonly known as the slender false oat, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical structure reflects its adaptation to arid environments, making it a common component of xerophytic vegetation.
