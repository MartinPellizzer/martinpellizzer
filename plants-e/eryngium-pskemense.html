Eryngium pskemense

Eryngium pskemense, commonly known as the Pskem eryngo, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant has gray-green, hairy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Eryngium pskemense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
