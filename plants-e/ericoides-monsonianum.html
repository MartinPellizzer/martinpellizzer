Ericoides monsonianum

Ericoides monsonianum, commonly known as the Monson's ericoid, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its botanical features include dense, leathery leaves and clusters of pink or white blooms that appear in late spring.

Ericoides monsonianum is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
