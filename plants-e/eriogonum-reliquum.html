Eriogonum reliquum

Eriogonum reliquum, commonly known as the Relic buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy, clustered pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, which includes other buckwheat species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eriogonum reliquum is considered a rare and endangered species.
