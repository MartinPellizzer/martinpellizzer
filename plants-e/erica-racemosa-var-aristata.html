Erica racemosa var. aristata

Erica racemosa var. aristata, commonly known as the upright heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
