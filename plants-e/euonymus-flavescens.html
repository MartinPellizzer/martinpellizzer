Euonymus flavescens

Euonymus flavescens, commonly known as the yellow euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow foliage in the fall, which gives it its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds.

Euonymus flavescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
