Eugenia viridiflora var. oblongata

Eugenia viridiflora var. oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
