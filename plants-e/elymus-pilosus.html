Elymus pilosus

Elymus pilosus, commonly known as hairy needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies, dunes, and disturbed soils. The plant features slender, wiry stems with a hairy texture, and its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small, clustered flower clusters.

Elymus pilosus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
