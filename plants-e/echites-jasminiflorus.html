Echites jasminiflorus

Echites jasminiflorus, commonly known as the jasmine vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to yellowish flowers that resemble jasmine, hence its common name. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves and has a climbing habit, often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated primarily by insects.
