Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. hexaedrus

Echinocereus triglochidiatus var. hexaedrus, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its blue-gray stems and dense clusters of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for both humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in desert ecosystems and a popular choice for xeriscaping.
