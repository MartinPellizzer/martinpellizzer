Ervum orientale

Ervum orientale, commonly known as the oriental vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces pods that contain several elliptical seeds, which are typically brown or black in color. It is native to regions of eastern Europe and western Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Ervum orientale is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful plant in agricultural settings.
