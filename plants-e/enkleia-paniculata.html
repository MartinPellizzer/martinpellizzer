Enkleia paniculata

Enkleia paniculata, commonly known as the red panicgrass, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically reddish in color, which gives it its common name. It thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Enkleia paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
