Eupatorium filicaule

Eupatorium filicaule, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium filicaule is known for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
