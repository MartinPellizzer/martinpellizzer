Eragrostis seineri

Eragrostis seineri, commonly known as the narrowleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and along coastal areas.

Eragrostis seineri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soils in arid environments.
