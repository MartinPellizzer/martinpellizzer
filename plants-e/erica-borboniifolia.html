Erica borboniifolia

Erica borboniifolia, commonly known as the Corsican heath, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica and Sardinia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Erica borboniifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
