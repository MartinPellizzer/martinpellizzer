Echinocactus dolichacanthus

Echinocactus dolichacanthus, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in thick, ribbed segments and long, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems and having a slow growth rate.

This cactus is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry conditions.
