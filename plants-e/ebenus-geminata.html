Ebenus geminata

Ebenus geminata, commonly known as the twin-stemmed euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Ebenus geminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
