Erythrophleum dinklagei

Erythrophleum dinklagei, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, palmately lobed leaves. The tree produces showy, red or orange flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by flat, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

It is often found in moist, lowland forests and is valued for its timber and ecological importance in its native habitat.
