Evolvulus grisebachii

Evolvulus grisebachii, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pale blue to purple in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name.

It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.
