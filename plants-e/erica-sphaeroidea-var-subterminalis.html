Erica sphaeroidea var. subterminalis

Erica sphaeroidea var. subterminalis, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a contrasting foliage texture. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its hardiness and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
