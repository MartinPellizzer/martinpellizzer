Epipactis leptochila subsp. neglecta

Epipactis leptochila subsp.

neglecta, commonly known as the slender helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its pale green, strap-like leaves and a single, showy yellow-green flower with a distinctive, hooded labellum.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in association with other orchid species. It has a slender, upright stem and produces a single, erect flower with a short, narrow spur.

This subspecies is notable for its relatively small size and subtle floral features compared to other members of the Epipactis genus.
