Eriogonum fasciculatum var. polifolium

Eriogonum fasciculatum var. polifolium, commonly known as the Sierra primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments. It is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a popular choice for native landscaping.
