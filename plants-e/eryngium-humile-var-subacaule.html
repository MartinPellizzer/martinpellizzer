Eryngium humile var. subacaule

Eryngium humile var. subacaule, commonly known as the dwarf thistle, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
