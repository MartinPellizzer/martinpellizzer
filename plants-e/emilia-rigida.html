Emilia rigida

Emilia rigida, commonly known as rigid emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are linear to lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, blooming from late spring to early summer. Emilia rigida is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
