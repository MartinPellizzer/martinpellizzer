Ericameria pseudobaccharis

Ericameria pseudobaccharis, commonly known as the desert gum plant, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads.

Ericameria pseudobaccharis plays a role in supporting local wildlife and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
