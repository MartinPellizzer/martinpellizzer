Eupatorium menthifolium

Eupatorium menthifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often described as having a mint-like scent. It thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium menthifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
