Erigeron poncinsii var. denudatus

Erigeron poncinsii var. denudatus, commonly known as the nakedleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender stems and narrow, often hairless leaves. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is distinguished by its reduced leaf coverage compared to other Erigeron species.
