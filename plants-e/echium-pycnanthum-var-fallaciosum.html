Echium pycnanthum var. fallaciosum

Echium pycnanthum var. fallaciosum, commonly known as the Sierra Madre Lovegrass, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
