Eschscholzia micrantha var. fusigemmata

Eschscholzia micrantha var. fusigemmata, commonly known as the dwarf California poppy, is a low-growing annual plant native to the western United States.

It features delicate, bright orange to reddish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

Its botanical characteristics reflect its adaptation to arid and coastal environments.
