Echites lasiocarpus var. angustifolius

Echites lasiocarpus var. angustifolius, commonly known as narrow-leaved echites, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, slender pedicels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
