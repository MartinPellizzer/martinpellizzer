Eriogonum reniforme

Eriogonum reniforme, commonly known as the broadleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It is a perennial species that produces seeds that are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Its adaptability to arid environments makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
