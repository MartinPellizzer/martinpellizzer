Escallonia poeppigiana var. longifolia

Escallonia poeppigiana var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and showy blooms make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
