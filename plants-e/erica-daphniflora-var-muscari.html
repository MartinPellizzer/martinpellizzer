Erica daphniflora var. muscari

Erica daphniflora var. muscari, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Cape region of South Africa.

It features dense clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, leathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in perfumery. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is particularly noted for its fragrant foliage and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
