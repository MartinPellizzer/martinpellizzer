Erythrina abyssinica subsp. suberifera

Erythrina abyssinica subsp.

suberifera, commonly known as the Abyssinian flame tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Ethiopia. It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense panicles and bloom during the dry season.

The plant has bipinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often fissured. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local communities.
