Epidendrum peristerium

Epidendrum peristerium, commonly known as the violet lady's slipper, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It features striking violet or purple flowers that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling lady's slippers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical and help store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in varying conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
