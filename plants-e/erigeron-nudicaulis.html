Erigeron nudicaulis

Erigeron nudicaulis, commonly known as the nakedstem fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. It has slender, hairy stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, and it is often found in dry, sandy soils.
