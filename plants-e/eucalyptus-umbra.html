Eucalyptus umbra

Eucalyptus umbra, commonly known as the shadow eucalyptus, is a unique species native to the rainforests of eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark that gives it its name, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, with a dense canopy that provides shade, hence its common name.

The species is adapted to humid, shaded environments and plays an important role in the ecological balance of its native habitat.
