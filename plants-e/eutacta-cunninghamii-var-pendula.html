Eutacta cunninghamii var. pendula

Eutacta cunninghamii var. pendula, commonly known as the weeping flax-lily, is a striking evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features long, drooping racemes of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, adding a dramatic visual effect. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

This variety is prized for its unique pendulous flowering habit and adaptability to coastal conditions.
