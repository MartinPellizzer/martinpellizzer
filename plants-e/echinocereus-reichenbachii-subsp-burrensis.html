Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp. burrensis

Echinocereus reichenbachii subsp.

burrensis, commonly known as the Burrens cholla, is a spine-tipped cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that branch into clusters, covered with clusters of spines and small, yellow flowers.

The plant has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments, allowing it to thrive in rocky soils and full sun. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Cactaceae family.
