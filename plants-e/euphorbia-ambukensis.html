Euphorbia ambukensis

Euphorbia ambukensis, commonly known as the Ambuk Valley spurge, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, further reducing water loss.

Euphorbia ambukensis is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, adapting to harsh, semi-desert environments.
