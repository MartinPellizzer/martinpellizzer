Echinocactus scopa

Echinocactus scopa, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection from herbivores.

Echinocactus scopa is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in desert environments.
