Echium italicum proles luteum

Echium italicum proles luteum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue bracts that surround the flowering head. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branching.

Echium italicum proles luteum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
