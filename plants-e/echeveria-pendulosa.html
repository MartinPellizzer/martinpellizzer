Echeveria pendulosa

Echeveria pendulosa, commonly known as the weeping echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that hang downward, giving it a cascading appearance. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color and have a fuzzy texture. It produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.
