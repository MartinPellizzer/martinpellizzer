Eucalyptus wyolensis

Eucalyptus wyolensis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are usually white or pale green, and it is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments. Eucalyptus wyolensis is a hardy shrub or small tree that plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.

Its essential oils, like those of other eucalyptus species, have been studied for potential medicinal and aromatic uses.
