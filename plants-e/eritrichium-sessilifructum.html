Eritrichium sessilifructum

Eritrichium sessilifructum, commonly known as the sessile-fruited eritrichium, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features a mat-forming growth habit with dense, dark green foliage that is often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, and the plant is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Eritrichium sessilifructum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky soils.
