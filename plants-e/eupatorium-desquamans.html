Eupatorium desquamans

Eupatorium desquamans, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, pale blue to lavender flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely dissected, feather-like leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, making it a common sight in swampy areas.

Eupatorium desquamans is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
