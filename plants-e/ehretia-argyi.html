Ehretia argyi

Ehretia argyi, commonly known as the Chinese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that matures to a dark red or black color.

Ehretia argyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
