Eccremocactus imitans

Eccremocactus imitans, commonly known as the imitation cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, slender stem and spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and adapting to harsh desert conditions.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry environments.
