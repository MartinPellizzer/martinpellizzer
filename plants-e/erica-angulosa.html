Erica angulosa

Erica angulosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, evergreen leaves that are narrow and pointed, and it produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red. Erica angulosa thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
