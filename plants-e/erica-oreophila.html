Erica oreophila

Erica oreophila, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. Erica oreophila produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
