Erica ramosissima

Erica ramosissima, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a shrub native to the western coast of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sandy or rocky soils. It is well adapted to saline conditions and can tolerate strong sea breezes.

Erica ramosissima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
