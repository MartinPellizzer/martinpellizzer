Eustrephus latifolius var. typicus

Eustrephus latifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the broad-leaved eustrephus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or shaded gardens.

Its foliage is notable for its smooth texture and vibrant green color throughout the growing season.
