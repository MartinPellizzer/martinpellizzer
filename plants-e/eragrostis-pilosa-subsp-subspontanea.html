Eragrostis pilosa subsp. subspontanea

Eragrostis pilosa subsp.

subspontanea, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue or greenish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
