Eschscholzia caespitosa subsp. kernensis

Eschscholzia caespitosa subsp.

kernensis, commonly known as Kern's poppy, is a flowering plant native to California. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces showy, bright orange to red flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
