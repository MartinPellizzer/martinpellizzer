Eragrostis lurida

Eragrostis lurida, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis lurida is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
