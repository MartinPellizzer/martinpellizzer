Eugenia pitoniana

Eugenia pitoniana, commonly known as the Piton Island eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
