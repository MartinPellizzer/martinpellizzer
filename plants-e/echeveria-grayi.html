Echeveria grayi

Echeveria grayi, commonly known as the Gray's Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or orange during the spring. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant.
