Eleocharis obtusa var. ovata

Eleocharis obtusa var. ovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spike-like inflorescence composed of small, scale-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, distinguishing it from other rush species.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
