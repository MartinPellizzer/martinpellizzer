Echinocactus pilosus var. pringlei

Echinocactus pilosus var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's hedgehog cactus, is a slow-growing, spine-covered cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent, sharp spines that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, with thick, fleshy stems that store water efficiently.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in cactus gardens.
