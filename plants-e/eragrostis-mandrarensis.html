Eragrostis mandrarensis

Eragrostis mandrarensis, commonly known as the Madagascar lovegrass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

Eragrostis mandrarensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
