Euptelea franchetii

Euptelea franchetii, commonly known as the Chinese magnolia or the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

Euptelea franchetii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
