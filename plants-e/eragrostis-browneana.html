Eragrostis browneana

Eragrostis browneana, commonly known as Brown's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute seeds. It is characterized by its ability to form dense tussocks and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
