Echeveria bartramii

Echeveria bartramii, commonly known as the Bartram's echeveria, is a succulent native to the southeastern United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces showy clusters of pink to lavender flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
