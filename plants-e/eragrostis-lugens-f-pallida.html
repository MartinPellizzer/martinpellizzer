Eragrostis lugens f. pallida

Eragrostis lugens f. pallida, commonly known as the pale wheatgrass, is a variant of the annual grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. It has a low-growing habit and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its drought tolerance and rapid growth, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
