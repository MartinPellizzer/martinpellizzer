Edraianthus graminifolius var. siculus

Edraianthus graminifolius var. siculus, commonly known as Sicilian grass gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
