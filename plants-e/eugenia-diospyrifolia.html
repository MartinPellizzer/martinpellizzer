Eugenia diospyrifolia

Eugenia diospyrifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or red cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are highly valued for their flavor and nutritional content. Its wood is dense and aromatic, making it valuable for furniture and crafts.

Eugenia diospyrifolia is also appreciated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
