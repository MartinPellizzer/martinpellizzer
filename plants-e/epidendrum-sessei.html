Epidendrum sessei

Epidendrum sessei, commonly known as the Cinnamon Lip, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit and its striking, fragrant flowers that often have a cinnamon-like scent. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, showy flowers that are typically pink to purple in color. Epidendrum sessei thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique floral fragrance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
