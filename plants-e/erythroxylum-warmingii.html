Erythroxylum warmingii

Erythroxylum warmingii, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy appearance.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant foliage and fruit.
