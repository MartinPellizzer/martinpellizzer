Eragrostis chaetophylla

Eragrostis chaetophylla, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It has a fibrous root system and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils.

Eragrostis chaetophylla is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
