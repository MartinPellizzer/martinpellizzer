Eupatorium austerum

Eupatorium austerum, commonly known as rough boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along roadsides and in wet meadows. The plant features upright stems covered in stiff, rough hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white flowers are clustered in dense, spherical heads, which bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium austerum is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
