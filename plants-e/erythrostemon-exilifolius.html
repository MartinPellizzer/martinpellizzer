Erythrostemon exilifolius

Erythrostemon exilifolius, commonly known as the slender-leafed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Campanulaceae family, valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
