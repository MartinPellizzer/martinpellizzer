Echinolaena hirta

Echinolaena hirta, commonly known as hairy echinolaena, is a perennial shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or white in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Echinolaena hirta is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
