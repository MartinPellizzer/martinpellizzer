Elaeocarpus decipiens

Elaeocarpus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The fruit is a distinctive, hard, woody capsule that contains several seeds. It is often found in lowland and hill forest regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal uses.
