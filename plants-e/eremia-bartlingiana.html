Eremia bartlingiana

Eremia bartlingiana, commonly known as the desert holly, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its succulent, needle-like leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for desert landscapes and ecological restoration projects.
