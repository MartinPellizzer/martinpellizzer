Erysimum polatschekii

Erysimum polatschekii, commonly known as the Sierra velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in mountainous regions. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Erysimum polatschekii is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in chaparral and scrub habitats.
