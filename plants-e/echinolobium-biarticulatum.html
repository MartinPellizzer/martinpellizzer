Echinolobium biarticulatum

Echinolobium biarticulatum, commonly known as the two-jointed echinolobium, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-segmented stem, which gives it its scientific name. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds with a hard, protective coat. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
