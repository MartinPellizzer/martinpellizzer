Eucalyptus dichromophloia

Eucalyptus dichromophloia, commonly known as the blackbutt, is a tall, slender eucalyptus native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dark, flaky bark that peels in long, narrow strips. The tree has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range from white to pink, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The wood of Eucalyptus dichromophloia is prized for its strength and durability, making it valuable in construction and furniture-making.
