Eucalyptus stuartiana var. parviflora

Eucalyptus stuartiana var. parviflora, commonly known as the Stuart's mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
