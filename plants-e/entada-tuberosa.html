Entada tuberosa

Entada tuberosa, commonly known as the African honeycomb tree, is a tropical legume native to Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, honeycomb-like bark that peels in layers, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces large, flat pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots are also valued for their medicinal properties, particularly in treating ailments like malaria and hypertension.

Entada tuberosa thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
