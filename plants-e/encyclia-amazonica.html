Encyclia amazonica

Encyclia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian sun orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange with red or purple markings. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Encyclia amazonica is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
