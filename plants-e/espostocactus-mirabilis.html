Espostocactus mirabilis

Espostocactus mirabilis, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in bright yellow spines and clusters of vibrant orange flowers. The plant's most distinctive feature is its golden, star-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its striking appearance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
