Eragrostis kwaiensis

Eragrostis kwaiensis, commonly known as Kwai grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are small clusters of flowers.

Eragrostis kwaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
