Eriocaulon hondoense

Eriocaulon hondoense, commonly known as the Hondo marsh plant, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which includes various grass-like plants often found in wetland environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to the structure and biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
