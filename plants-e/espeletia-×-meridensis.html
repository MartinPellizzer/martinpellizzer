Espeletia × meridensis

Espeletia × meridensis, commonly known as the "Páramo flower," is a hybrid plant native to the páramo ecosystems of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and dense, woolly foliage. The plant produces showy, daisy-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Espeletia × meridensis is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with poor soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of the páramo biome.
