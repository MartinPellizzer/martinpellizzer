Erica × flavisepala

Erica × flavisepala, commonly known as the yellow-flowered heath, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Erica × flavisepala is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it suitable for xeriscaping in suitable climates.
