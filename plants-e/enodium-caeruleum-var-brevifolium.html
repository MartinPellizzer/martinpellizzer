Enodium caeruleum var. brevifolium

Enodium caeruleum var. brevifolium, commonly known as the blue enodium, is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its compact growth habit and striking blue-violet flowers.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are densely arranged along the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its flowers bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
