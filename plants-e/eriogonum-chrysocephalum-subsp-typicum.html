Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp. typicum

Eriogonum chrysocephalum subsp.

typicum, commonly known as the goldenhead buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellowish-green to golden flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has hairy stems and leaves with a rough texture, and its leaves are often arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ecological importance to pollinators.
