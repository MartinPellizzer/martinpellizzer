Eulophia laxiflora

Eulophia laxiflora, commonly known as the broad-flowered eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is racemose, with numerous small, fragrant blooms.

Eulophia laxiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
