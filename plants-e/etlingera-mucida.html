Etlingera mucida

Etlingera mucida, commonly known as the sticky ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth and broad, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a strong, spicy scent.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and are also valued for their culinary applications.
