Evolvulus lanceolatus

Evolvulus lanceolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Evolvulus lanceolatus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other species of morning glories.
