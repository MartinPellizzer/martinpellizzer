Epacris corymbiflora

Epacris corymbiflora, commonly known as the clustered epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces dense clusters of tubular, pale pink to white flowers, which bloom in spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

Epacris corymbiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
