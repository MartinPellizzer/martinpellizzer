Eriosema rotundifolium var. macrophyllum

Eriosema rotundifolium var. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaf eriosema, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
