Erechtites missionum var. lanceolatus

Erechtites missionum var. lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs, and its leaves are lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple and white, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, rich soils along stream banks and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
