Eriosyce subgibbosa var. vallenarensis

Eriosyce subgibbosa var. vallenarensis, commonly known as the Vallenarensis Eriosyce, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, which help to reduce water loss in its arid environment. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a low-growing shrub that thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
