Euthemis jackiana

Euthemis jackiana, commonly known as the Jack's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in a crown-like structure. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
