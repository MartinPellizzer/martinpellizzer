Echites sulphureus

Echites sulphureus, commonly known as the golden starbursts, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It produces vibrant yellow flowers that resemble starbursts, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Echites sulphureus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
