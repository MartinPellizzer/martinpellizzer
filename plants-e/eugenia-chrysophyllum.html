Eugenia chrysophyllum

Eugenia chrysophyllum, commonly known as the golden eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters and has glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round, orange-red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

The species is also used in traditional medicine in some regions for its potential health benefits.
