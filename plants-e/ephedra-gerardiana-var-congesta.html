Ephedra gerardiana var. congesta

Ephedra gerardiana var. congesta, commonly known as the Tibetan ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and is adapted to arid and cold environments. It contains alkaloids such as ephedrine, which have been historically used in traditional medicine.

Due to its medicinal properties and conservation status, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
