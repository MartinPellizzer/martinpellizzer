Elaeocarpus tonkinensis

Elaeocarpus tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
