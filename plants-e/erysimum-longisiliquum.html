Erysimum longisiliquum

Erysimum longisiliquum, commonly known as the long silique mustard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal and stem leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces long, slender pods that contain the seeds.

Erysimum longisiliquum is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
