Eupatorium bohlmannianum

Eupatorium bohlmannianum, commonly known as the Bohlmann's boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium bohlmannianum is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
