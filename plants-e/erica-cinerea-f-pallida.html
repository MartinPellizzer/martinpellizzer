Erica cinerea f. pallida

Erica cinerea f. pallida, commonly known as the pale heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It features wiry, pale green stems covered in dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and typically a pale gray-green color, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand coastal conditions and salt spray.
