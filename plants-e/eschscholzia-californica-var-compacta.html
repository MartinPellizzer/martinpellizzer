Eschscholzia californica var. compacta

Eschscholzia californica var. compacta, commonly known as the dwarf California poppy, is a compact variety of the California poppy native to the western United States.

It features bright orange, bowl-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and grows to a height of about 10 to 15 inches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and wildflower displays.

This variety is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
