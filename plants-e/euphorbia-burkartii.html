Euphorbia burkartii

Euphorbia burkartii, commonly known as the Burkart's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, often inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
