Entada woodii var. pubescens

Entada woodii var. pubescens, commonly known as the African horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain large, edible seeds. Its bark is thick and often fissured, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
