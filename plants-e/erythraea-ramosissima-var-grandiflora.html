Erythraea ramosissima var. grandiflora

Erythraea ramosissima var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered erythraea, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, branched structure and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value is notable due to its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
