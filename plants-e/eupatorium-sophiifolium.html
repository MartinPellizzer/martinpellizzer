Eupatorium sophiifolium

Eupatorium sophiifolium, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium sophiifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
