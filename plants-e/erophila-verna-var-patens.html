Erophila verna var. patens

Erophila verna var. patens, commonly known as the spring eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and other wetland habitats. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy, seed-filled heads.

The leaves are usually alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.
