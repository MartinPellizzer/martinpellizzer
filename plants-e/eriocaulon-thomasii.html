Eriocaulon thomasii

Eriocaulon thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's flat sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color. Its flowering structures are simple and lack the showy petals typical of many flowering plants.

Eriocaulon thomasii plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
