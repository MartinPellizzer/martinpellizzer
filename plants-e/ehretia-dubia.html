Ehretia dubia

Ehretia dubia, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ehretia dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
