Encyclia dichroma subsp. biflora

Encyclia dichroma subsp.

biflora, commonly known as the biflora orchid, is a species native to Central America. It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its inflorescence bears two to three flowers, which are clustered at the base of the stem.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
