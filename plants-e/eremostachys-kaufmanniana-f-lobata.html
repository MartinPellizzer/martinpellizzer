Eremostachys kaufmanniana f. lobata

Eremostachys kaufmanniana f. lobata, commonly known as the lobed eremostachys, is a rare and striking succulent native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often lobed, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers during the summer months, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
