Eremurus tauricus

Eremurus tauricus, commonly known as the Taurian Spearwort, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Xanthorrhoeaceae family, characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces large, showy bracts that resemble leaves, which are often mistaken for petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Eremurus tauricus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
