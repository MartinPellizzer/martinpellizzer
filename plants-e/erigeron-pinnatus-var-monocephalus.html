Erigeron pinnatus var. monocephalus

Erigeron pinnatus var. monocephalus, commonly known as the single-headed blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces a single flower head per stem, which distinguishes it from other varieties of Erigeron.

The botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
