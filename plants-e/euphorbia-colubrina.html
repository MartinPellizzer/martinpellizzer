Euphorbia colubrina

Euphorbia colubrina, commonly known as redroot spurge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant has smooth, green stems that often turn reddish at the base, giving it its common name. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and usually have a waxy texture.

Euphorbia colubrina is often found in moist, open areas such as roadsides and woodland edges.
