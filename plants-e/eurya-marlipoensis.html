Eurya marlipoensis

Eurya marlipoensis, commonly known as the Marlipo evergreen, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its small, elliptical leaves are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems.

Eurya marlipoensis produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for local wildlife.
