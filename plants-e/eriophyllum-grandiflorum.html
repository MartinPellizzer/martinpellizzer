Eriophyllum grandiflorum

Eriophyllum grandiflorum, commonly known as the Woolly Bluecurls, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant features soft, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. It produces showy, blue to purple flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Eriophyllum grandiflorum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open coastal scrub and grassland habitats.
