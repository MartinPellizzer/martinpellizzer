Eupatorium floribundum

Eupatorium floribundum, commonly known as purple stem goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces and reddish stems that become more pronounced as the plant matures. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Eupatorium floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
