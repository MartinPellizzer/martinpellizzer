Erinus alpinus var. secundiflorus

Erinus alpinus var. secundiflorus, commonly known as the alpine erinus, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically covered in a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact growth habit and delicate flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
