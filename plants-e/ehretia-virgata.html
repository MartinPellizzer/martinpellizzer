Ehretia virgata

Ehretia virgata, commonly known as the white-flowered ehretia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, dry capsules that contain several seeds.

Ehretia virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
