Encholirium roseum

Encholirium roseum, commonly known as the roseate torch cactus, is a striking member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that can reach up to 10 feet in height, covered in dense, white to pink spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range from pink to violet, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun, making it a popular choice for arid gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
