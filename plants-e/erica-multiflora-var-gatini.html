Erica multiflora var. gatini

Erica multiflora var. gatini, commonly known as the Gatini heath, is a low-growing shrub native to South Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has wiry, evergreen leaves that are typically gray-green in appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
