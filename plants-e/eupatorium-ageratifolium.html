Eupatorium ageratifolium

Eupatorium ageratifolium, commonly known as the roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Eupatorium ageratifolium is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
