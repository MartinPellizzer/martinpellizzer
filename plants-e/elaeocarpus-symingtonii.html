Elaeocarpus symingtonii

Elaeocarpus symingtonii, commonly known as the New Guinea elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
