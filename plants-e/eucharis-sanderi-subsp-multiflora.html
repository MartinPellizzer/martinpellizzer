Eucharis sanderi subsp. multiflora

Eucharis sanderi subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the sanders' elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to South America. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in diameter.

The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and elegant floral display.
