Eupatorium pittieri

Eupatorium pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium pittieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
