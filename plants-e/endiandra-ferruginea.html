Endiandra ferruginea

Endiandra ferruginea, commonly known as the red ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale yellow and are borne in clusters.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.
