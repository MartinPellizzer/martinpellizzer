Elymus dahuricus subsp. cylindricus

Elymus dahuricus subsp.

cylindricus, commonly known as the Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often arranged in clusters along the stem. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in arctic and alpine regions.
