Elaeocarpus harmandii

Elaeocarpus harmandii, commonly known as the Harmand's elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
