Escallonia myrtoidea

Escallonia myrtoidea, commonly known as the Australian rose or myrtle rose, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Escallonia myrtoidea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
