Epidendrum aylacotoglossum

Epidendrum aylacotoglossum, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to Central America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a rhizomatous growth habit, producing multiple shoots from the base. The plant features lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green and covered in a fine, velvety texture. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with a distinctive red or brown lip, and it blooms during the summer months.

This orchid thrives in humid, well-draining conditions and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
