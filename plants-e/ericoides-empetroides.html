Ericoides empetroides

Ericoides empetroides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Ericoides empetroides thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland environments.
