Echinospermum spathulatum

Echinospermum spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow composite flowers and hairy stems. The plant has alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces a capsule fruit containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Echinospermum spathulatum is often found in grassy areas and is adapted to thrive in disturbed soils.
