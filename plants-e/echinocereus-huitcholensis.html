Echinocereus huitcholensis

Echinocereus huitcholensis, commonly known as the Huichol cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens and conservation efforts.
