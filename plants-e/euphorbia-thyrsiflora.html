Euphorbia thyrsiflora

Euphorbia thyrsiflora, commonly known as the golden crown euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in soft, fleshy leaves that are often green or reddish in color. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flower clusters called cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
