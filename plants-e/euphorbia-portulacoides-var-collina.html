Euphorbia portulacoides var. collina

Euphorbia portulacoides var. collina, commonly known as the Alpine spurge, is a succulent plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth habit with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in terminal clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in rocky and arid habitats.
