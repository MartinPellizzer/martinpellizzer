Eupatorium stramineum

Eupatorium stramineum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Eupatorium stramineum is known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
