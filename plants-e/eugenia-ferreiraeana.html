Eugenia ferreiraeana

Eugenia ferreiraeana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and for ornamental purposes.
