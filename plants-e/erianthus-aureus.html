Erianthus aureus

Erianthus aureus, commonly known as the golden reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to the southern United States.

It features narrow, bright yellow to golden-green leaves that are often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are typically green or brown. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Erianthus aureus is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
