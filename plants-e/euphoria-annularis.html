Euphoria annularis

Euphoria annularis, commonly known as the ring euphoria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive ring-shaped inflorescence, which consists of a central yellow cupule surrounded by brightly colored bracts. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, relying on the showy bracts for pollination.

Euphoria annularis is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance.
