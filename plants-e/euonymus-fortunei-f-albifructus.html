Euonymus fortunei f. albifructus

Euonymus fortunei f. albifructus, commonly known as the white-fruited boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and distinctive white to pale yellow berries that appear in late summer. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom, giving it a unique appearance. It is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate foliage and seasonal fruit display.

This variety is particularly valued in horticulture for its adaptability and aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
