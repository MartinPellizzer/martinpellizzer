Erythrococca laurentii

Erythrococca laurentii, commonly known as the red cocculus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its striking red, fleshy fruits that resemble small, inflated capsules. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture, while its stems are woody and branching.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
