Ebnerella pubispina

Ebnerella pubispina, commonly known as the hairy spiny holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its spiny, woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a typical feature of the Euphorbiaceae family. Ebnerella pubispina is native to tropical regions of Africa and is often found in forest understory environments.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
