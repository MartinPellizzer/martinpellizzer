Eurya cuneata

Eurya cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese holly or evergreen holly, is a species of flowering plant in the family Ericaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and typically grows in forested areas. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, evergreen leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, bright red berries that are often consumed by birds.

Eurya cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
