Eragrostis fosbergii

Eragrostis fosbergii, commonly known as Fosberg's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale green to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Eragrostis fosbergii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry landscapes.
