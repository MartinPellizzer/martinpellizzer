Eupatorium perglabrum

Eupatorium perglabrum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks.

Botanically, it is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other familiar flowering plants.
