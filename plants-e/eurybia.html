Eurybia

Eurybia, commonly known as sunflowers, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, showy composite flower heads that typically consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Eurybia species are native to various regions across the Northern Hemisphere, including North America, Europe, and Asia. They thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making them popular choices for gardens and landscapes.

Many species are cultivated for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine.
