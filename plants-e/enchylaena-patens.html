Enchylaena patens

Enchylaena patens, commonly known as the coastal bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of numerous small, bright red or orange flowers. The plant has a woody stem and is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the coastline. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

Enchylaena patens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
