Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp. tricuspidatum

Eryngium tricuspidatum subsp.

tricuspidatum, commonly known as the thimbleweed, is a perennial herb native to North America. It features a tall, spiky inflorescence composed of blue to purple flowers surrounded by bracts.

The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
