Echinocactus clavatus

Echinocactus clavatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, and it produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has a dense covering of spines, which helps protect it from herbivores and reduces water loss. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Echinocactus clavatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
