Entada scandens

Entada scandens, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain several seeds, and its bark has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties.

Entada scandens is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
