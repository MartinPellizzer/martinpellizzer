Eritrichium villosum var. latifolium

Eritrichium villosum var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf hairy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has small, alternate leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance.

This variety is often found in regions with mild, temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
