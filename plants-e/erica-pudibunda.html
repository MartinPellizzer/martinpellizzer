Erica pudibunda

Erica pudibunda, commonly known as the shy heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing pink or purple hues that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Erica pudibunda thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
