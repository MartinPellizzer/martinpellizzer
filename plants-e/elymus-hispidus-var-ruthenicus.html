Elymus hispidus var. ruthenicus

Elymus hispidus var. ruthenicus, commonly known as the Russian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It features tufted growth with dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and meadows, and is well adapted to temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
