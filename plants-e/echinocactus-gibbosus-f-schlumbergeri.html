Echinocactus gibbosus f. schlumbergeri

Echinocactus gibbosus f. schlumbergeri, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rounded, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, which is covered in prominent, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Its unique appearance and slow growth make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
