Elaeocarpus purus

Elaeocarpus purus, commonly known as the Pusian tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It is often found in lowland rainforests and can grow up to 30 meters tall.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
