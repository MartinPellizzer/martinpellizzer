Ellenbergia glandulata

Ellenbergia glandulata, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a key feature of the genus Ellenbergia. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, elliptical leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale yellow, attracting various pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
