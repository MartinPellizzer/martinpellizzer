Espeletiopsis

Espeletiopsis, commonly known as the "Carnivorous Pitcher Plant," is a genus of flowering plants native to South America.

These plants are characterized by their unique pitcher-shaped leaves that function as traps to capture and digest insects. The botanical features include a tall, slender stem and waxy, colorful leaf structures that attract prey. Espeletiopsis species are closely related to the genus Sarracenia and are adapted to nutrient-poor environments.

Their ecological role includes contributing to soil nutrient cycling through the decomposition of trapped prey.
