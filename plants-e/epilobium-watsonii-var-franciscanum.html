Epilobium watsonii var. franciscanum

Epilobium watsonii var. franciscanum, commonly known as the Franciscan willow-herb, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant communities in its habitat.
