Eria diversicolor

Eria diversicolor, commonly known as the Variable Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in shady, humid environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and lance-shaped, and the plant produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Eria diversicolor is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
