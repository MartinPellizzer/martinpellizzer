Eugenia caudatifolia

Eugenia caudatifolia, commonly known as the hanging eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving the plant a cascading appearance. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy dark green surface. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant due to its unique foliage and floral display.
