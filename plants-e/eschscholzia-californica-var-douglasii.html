Eschscholzia californica var. douglasii

Eschscholzia californica var. douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's poppy, is a variant of the California poppy native to the western United States.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its resilience to drought and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
