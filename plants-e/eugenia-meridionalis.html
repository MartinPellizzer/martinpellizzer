Eugenia meridionalis

Eugenia meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern Myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and edible, often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

Eugenia meridionalis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
