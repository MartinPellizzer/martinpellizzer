Eurybia decurrens

Eurybia decurrens, commonly known as the New England aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, violet to blue-purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows and marshes, and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eurybia decurrens is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
