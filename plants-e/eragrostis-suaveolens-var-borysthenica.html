Eragrostis suaveolens var. borysthenica

Eragrostis suaveolens var. borysthenica, commonly known as the sweetgrass or Borysthenian sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silky leaves that emit a mild, pleasant aroma. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with spikelets that contain several bracteate florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and rituals.
