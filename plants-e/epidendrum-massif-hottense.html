Epidendrum massif-hottense

Epidendrum massif-hottense, commonly known as the Hottentot orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant features long, arching inflorescences with numerous fragrant, brightly colored blossoms, often in shades of yellow, orange, and red. Its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum massif-hottense thrives in humid, well-lit environments and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.
