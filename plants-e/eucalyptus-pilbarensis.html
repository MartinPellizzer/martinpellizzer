Eucalyptus pilbarensis

Eucalyptus pilbarensis, commonly known as the Pilbara ironbark, is a species of eucalyptus native to the Pilbara region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that is often dark in color, providing it with a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a grayish-green color and a rough texture. This tree produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, which are followed by woody capsules containing the seeds.

It is adapted to the arid and semi-arid conditions of its native habitat, making it a resilient and hardy species.
