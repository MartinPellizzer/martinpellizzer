Erigeron turnerorum

Erigeron turnerorum, commonly known as Turner's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It features hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellow centers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets.
