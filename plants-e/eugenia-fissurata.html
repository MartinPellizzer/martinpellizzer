Eugenia fissurata

Eugenia fissurata, commonly known as the fissured eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Eugenia fissurata is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
