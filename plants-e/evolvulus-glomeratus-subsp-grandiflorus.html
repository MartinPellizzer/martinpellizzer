Evolvulus glomeratus subsp. grandiflorus

Evolvulus glomeratus subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the largeflower morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color.

The plant thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and somewhat succulent.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
