Ectadiopsis brevifolia

Ectadiopsis brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial shrub characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and fleshy, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its succulent leaves store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions.

Ectadiopsis brevifolia is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to high levels of sunlight and low moisture.
