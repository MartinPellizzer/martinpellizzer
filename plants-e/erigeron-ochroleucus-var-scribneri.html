Erigeron ochroleucus var. scribneri

Erigeron ochroleucus var. scribneri, commonly known as the Scribner's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often found in arid environments and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
