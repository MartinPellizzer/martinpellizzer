Eleocharis acutangula

Eleocharis acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-pointed spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. Its reproductive structures are adapted for water dispersal, enhancing its ability to spread in aquatic habitats.
