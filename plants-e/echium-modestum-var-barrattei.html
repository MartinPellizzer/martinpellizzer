Echium modestum var. barrattei

Echium modestum var. barrattei, commonly known as the Barratte's evening primrose, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, oily seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
