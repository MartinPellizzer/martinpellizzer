Erodium trichomanefolium var. cavanillesii

Erodium trichomanefolium var. cavanillesii, commonly known as the Cavanilles' bedstraw, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a delicate appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
