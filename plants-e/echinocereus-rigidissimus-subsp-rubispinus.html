Echinocereus rigidissimus subsp. rubispinus

Echinocereus rigidissimus subsp.

rubispinus, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, red-brown spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
