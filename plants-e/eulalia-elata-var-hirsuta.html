Eulalia elata var. hirsuta

Eulalia elata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy pampas grass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It features tall, stiff stems with narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces plume-like inflorescences that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various climates, though it may require careful management to prevent invasiveness.
