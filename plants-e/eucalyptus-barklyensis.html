Eucalyptus barklyensis

Eucalyptus barklyensis, commonly known as the Barkly gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to the Northern Territory of Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-white bark that peels in thin, flaky layers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough, serrated margin.

The barklyensis species is known for its aromatic oils, which are used in traditional and modern applications.
