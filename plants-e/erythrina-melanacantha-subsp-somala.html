Erythrina melanacantha subsp. somala

Erythrina melanacantha subsp.

somala, commonly known as the Somalian coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive bright red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant has compound leaves with three leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in thorns. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry or semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local communities.
