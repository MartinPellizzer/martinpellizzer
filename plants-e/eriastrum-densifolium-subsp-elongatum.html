Eriastrum densifolium subsp. elongatum

Eriastrum densifolium subsp.

elongatum, commonly known as the elongated thimbleweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, leafy foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are narrow and often hairy, and the stems are usually unbranched.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
