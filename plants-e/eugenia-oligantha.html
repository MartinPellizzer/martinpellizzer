Eugenia oligantha

Eugenia oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, pinkish-red berries. Eugenia oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.
