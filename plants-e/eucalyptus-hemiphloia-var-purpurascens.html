Eucalyptus hemiphloia var. purpurascens

Eucalyptus hemiphloia var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leafed mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple foliage, which contrasts with the typical green leaves of other eucalyptus varieties. This plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its small, globular flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters during the warmer months.

The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
