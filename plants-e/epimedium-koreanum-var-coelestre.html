Epimedium koreanum var. coelestre

Epimedium koreanum var. coelestre, commonly known as the Korean barrenwort, is a low-growing perennial herb native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging in early spring. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and adaptability.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate cold climates and its historical use in traditional medicine.
