Eugenia javensis

Eugenia javensis, commonly known as Java guava, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia javensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
