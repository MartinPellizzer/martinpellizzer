Eria javensis

Eria javensis, commonly known as the Java orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in tropical rainforests. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant blooms during the rainy season.

Eria javensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
