Erythroxylum plowmanii

Erythroxylum plowmanii, commonly known as the Plowman's copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. It produces a resinous sap that has been historically used for various purposes, including medicinal and industrial applications.

Due to its ecological significance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
