Erythroxylum mattos-silvae

Erythroxylum mattos-silvae, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a latex sap that has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties, including anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various insect and bird species.
