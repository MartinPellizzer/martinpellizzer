Edwardsia chilensis

Edwardsia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean orchid, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils along the shoreline, adapting to the harsh coastal environment. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air, a feature common in epiphytic orchids.

Edwardsia chilensis plays an important role in the local ecosystem, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
