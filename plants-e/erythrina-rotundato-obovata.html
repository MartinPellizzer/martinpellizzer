Erythrina rotundato-obovata

Erythrina rotundato-obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate flame tree, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has compound leaves with rounded to ovate leaflets, and its stem is often woody and branching. It produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warm climates.
