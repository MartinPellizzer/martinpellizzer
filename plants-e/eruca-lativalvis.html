Eruca lativalvis

Eruca lativalvis, commonly known as broadleaf mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in coarse, hairy foliage. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are edible and have a pungent, mustard-like flavor, making it a valuable plant in both culinary and medicinal contexts.
