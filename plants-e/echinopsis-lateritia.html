Echinopsis lateritia

Echinopsis lateritia, commonly known as the "Cactus of the Desert," is a species of cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a ribbed stem and produces white to pale yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It has a slow growth rate and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience.

Echinopsis lateritia is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
