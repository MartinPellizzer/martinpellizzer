Eriosema acuminatum

Eriosema acuminatum, commonly known as the hairy eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect stems that are often covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a hairy surface that aids in water retention.

Eriosema acuminatum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
