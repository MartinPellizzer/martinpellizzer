Enneapogon schimperianus

Enneapogon schimperianus, commonly known as the Schimper's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Enneapogon schimperianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
