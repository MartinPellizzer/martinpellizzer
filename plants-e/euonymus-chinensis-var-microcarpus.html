Euonymus chinensis var. microcarpus

Euonymus chinensis var. microcarpus, commonly known as the Chinese spindletree, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
