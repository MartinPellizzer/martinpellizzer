Erythrina acunae

Erythrina acunae, commonly known as the Acuna flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its seed pods are woody and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant floral display.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
