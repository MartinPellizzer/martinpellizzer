Ervum alpestre

Ervum alpestre, commonly known as the alpine vetch, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Ervum alpestre is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
