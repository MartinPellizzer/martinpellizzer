Erica flexilis

Erica flexilis, commonly known as the flexible heath, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many evergreen shrubs and small trees. The plant features dense, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica flexilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
