Eupatorium cannabinum var. heterophyllum

Eupatorium cannabinum var. heterophyllum, commonly known as the heterophyllous hemp agrimony, is a variant of the hemp agrimony plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist meadows and along stream banks. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that vary in size and shape depending on the growth conditions. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are arranged in dense clusters, often attracting various pollinators.

The species is noted for its variability in leaf morphology, which contributes to its scientific classification as a variant.
