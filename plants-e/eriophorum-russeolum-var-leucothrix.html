Eriophorum russeolum var. leucothrix

Eriophorum russeolum var. leucothrix, commonly known as the white-tailed cottongrass, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy inflorescences that appear white or pale pink, giving it a distinctive appearance in its harsh environment. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous areas. Its fibrous roots help stabilize the soil, making it an important component of these ecosystems.

The flowering period typically occurs in late summer, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
