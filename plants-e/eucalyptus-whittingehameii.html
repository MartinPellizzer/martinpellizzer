Eucalyptus whittingehameii

Eucalyptus whittingehameii, commonly known as Whittingehame mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, typically appearing in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its aromatic oils, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
