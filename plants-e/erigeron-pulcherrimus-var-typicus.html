Erigeron pulcherrimus var. typicus

Erigeron pulcherrimus var. typicus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has finely divided, silvery-green leaves and a compact, upright growth habit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
