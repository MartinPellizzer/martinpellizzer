Erigeron utahensis var. sparsifolius

Erigeron utahensis var. sparsifolius, commonly known as the Utah fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and daisy-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert environments. It has a compact growth habit and produces numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinators.
