Erythraea centaurium subsp. diffusa

Erythraea centaurium subsp.

diffusa, commonly known as the diffuse centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features slender stems with opposite, linear to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are small, blue to violet, and arranged in loose spikes, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more diffuse growth habit compared to other forms of Erythraea centaurium.
