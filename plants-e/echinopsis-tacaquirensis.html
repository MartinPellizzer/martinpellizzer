Echinopsis tacaquirensis

Echinopsis tacaquirensis, commonly known as the Tacaquirensis cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its dense, spiny ribs and a short, rounded stem. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It has adapted to survive in extreme desert conditions with minimal water requirements.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it an important species in its native habitat.
