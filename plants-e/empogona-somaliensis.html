Empogona somaliensis

Empogona somaliensis, commonly known as the Somali tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important species in dryland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to harsh climates and valuable for both ecological and medicinal purposes.
