Eria armeniaca

Eria armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy blooms during the summer months.
