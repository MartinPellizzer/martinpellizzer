Echinocactus cinnabarinus

Echinocactus cinnabarinus, commonly known as the red globular cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, ribbed stems that are typically a vibrant red or orange color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires minimal water and thrives in full sunlight.

Due to its striking appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and desert landscapes.
