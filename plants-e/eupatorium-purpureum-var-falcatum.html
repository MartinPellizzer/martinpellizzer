Eupatorium purpureum var. falcatum

Eupatorium purpureum var. falcatum, commonly known as wild bergamot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and meadows.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a popular choice for herbal teas and ornamental gardening.
