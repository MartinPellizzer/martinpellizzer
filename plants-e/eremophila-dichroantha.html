Eremophila dichroantha

Eremophila dichroantha, commonly known as the two-coloured emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in spring. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

Eremophila dichroantha is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
