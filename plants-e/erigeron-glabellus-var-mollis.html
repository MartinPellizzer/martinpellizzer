Erigeron glabellus var. mollis

Erigeron glabellus var. mollis, commonly known as the softleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are softly hairy and oval-shaped, while its flowers are usually pale yellow to white.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
