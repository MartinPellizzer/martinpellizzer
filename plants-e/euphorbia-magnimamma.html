Euphorbia magnimamma

Euphorbia magnimamma, commonly known as the giant mamillaria, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and a unique, spiny appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters, adding a striking visual contrast to its textured foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and collections.
