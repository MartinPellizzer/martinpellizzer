Euphorbia meridensis

Euphorbia meridensis, commonly known as the Southern Canary Island Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant has a shallow root system and is adapted to arid and rocky environments. It is often found growing in coastal areas and dry habitats.

Its latex sap is toxic and can cause skin irritation.
