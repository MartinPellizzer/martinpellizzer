Eugenia orbignyana

Eugenia orbignyana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, bright red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
