Eugenia simiarum

Eugenia simiarum, commonly known as the monkey apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy berries that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
