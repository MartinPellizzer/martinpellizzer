Ephedra altissima var. tripolitana

Ephedra altissima var. tripolitana, commonly known as the Tripolitan ephedra, is a shrub-like plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in regions with low precipitation.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also been studied for its potential pharmacological properties.
