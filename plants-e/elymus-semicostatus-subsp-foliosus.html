Elymus semicostatus subsp. foliosus

Elymus semicostatus subsp.

foliosus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
