Eragrostis owariensis

Eragrostis owariensis, commonly known as Japanese lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis owariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
