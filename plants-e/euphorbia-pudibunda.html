Euphorbia pudibunda

Euphorbia pudibunda, commonly known as the shy euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features thick, fleshy stems and leaves, and it produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its specialized inflorescences. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
