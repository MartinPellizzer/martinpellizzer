Epiphyllum × splendens

Epiphyllum × splendens, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Mexico.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, flat, and often segmented stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white, pink, or red. Its leaves are usually modified into spines, and it thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil.

It is popular in horticulture for its striking nighttime blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
