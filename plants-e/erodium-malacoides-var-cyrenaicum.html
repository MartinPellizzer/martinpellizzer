Erodium malacoides var. cyrenaicum

Erodium malacoides var. cyrenaicum, commonly known as the Cyprus morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of the Mediterranean flora.
