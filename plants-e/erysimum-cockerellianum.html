Erysimum cockerellianum

Erysimum cockerellianum, commonly known as the Cockerell's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils in arid environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species in its native habitat.
