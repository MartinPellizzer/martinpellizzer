Eupatorium filifolium var. longifolia

Eupatorium filifolium var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white to pale pink. It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
