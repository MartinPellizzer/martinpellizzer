Eryngium vaseyi var. castrense

Eryngium vaseyi var. castrense, commonly known as the Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
