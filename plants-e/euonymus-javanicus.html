Euonymus javanicus

Euonymus javanicus, commonly known as the Java spurge, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters, and it produces red, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans.

Euonymus javanicus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.
