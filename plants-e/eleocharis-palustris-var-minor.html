Eleocharis palustris var. minor

Eleocharis palustris var. minor, commonly known as bog spike-rush, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color and bear small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is important for soil stabilization and provides habitat for various aquatic organisms.
