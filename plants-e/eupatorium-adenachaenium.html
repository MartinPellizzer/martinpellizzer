Eupatorium adenachaenium

Eupatorium adenachaenium, commonly known as the redstem false dragonhead, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features opposite, narrow leaves and produces small, clustered purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in partially shaded to sunny conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
