Elaeocarpus castaneifolius

Elaeocarpus castaneifolius, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
