Eugenia guayaquilensis var. glabrior

Eugenia guayaquilensis var. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved guayaquil evergreen, is a species native to the humid lowland regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and lanceolate, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, spherical berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
