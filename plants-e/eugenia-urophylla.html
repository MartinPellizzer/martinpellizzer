Eugenia urophylla

Eugenia urophylla, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its aromatic foliage and ornamental value make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical gardens.
