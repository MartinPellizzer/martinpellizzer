Eucharis himemessa

Eucharis himemessa, commonly known as the Amazonian night lily, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Aroid family (Araceae) and is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom at night. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces striking, spathe-shaped leaves that are broad and glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Eucharis himemessa thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
