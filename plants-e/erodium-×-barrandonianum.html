Erodium × barrandonianum

Erodium × barrandonianum, commonly known as the hybrid common stork's bill, is a flowering plant in the family Leguminosae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Erodium corymbosum and Erodium vulgare, characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and small, bean-like pods. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is found in various regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
