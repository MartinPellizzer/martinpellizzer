Eucalyptus corynodes

Eucalyptus corynodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs with small, narrow leaves. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on the stems rather than the leaves. Eucalyptus corynodes is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
