Erica mannii

Erica mannii, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red in color, blooming in late summer. Erica mannii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
