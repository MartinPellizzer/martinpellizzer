Elymus cayouetteorum

Elymus cayouetteorum, commonly known as the Cayouette's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces spikes of small, spikelet-bearing inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and wiry, with a blue-gray sheen, and it has a fibrous root system.

Elymus cayouetteorum is valued for its drought tolerance and is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide forage for wildlife.
