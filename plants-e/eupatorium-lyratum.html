Eupatorium lyratum

Eupatorium lyratum, commonly known as sweet peppergrass, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium lyratum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
