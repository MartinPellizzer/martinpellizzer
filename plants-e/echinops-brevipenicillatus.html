Echinops brevipenicillatus

Echinops brevipenicillatus, commonly known as the short-pinioned thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a globe, which are composed of tightly packed bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally gray-green with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small blue or purple flowers in the spring.

Echinops brevipenicillatus is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
