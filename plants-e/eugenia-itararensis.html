Eugenia itararensis

Eugenia itararensis, commonly known as the Itarare myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Brazil, specifically the states of São Paulo and Paraná, where it grows in humid, tropical forests. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, small white flowers and smooth, dark green leaves. It produces small, round berries that are typically red when ripe.

Eugenia itararensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
