Echinocactus octogonus

Echinocactus octogonus, commonly known as the eight-spined cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, slender stems. Its spines are short and cylindrical, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

Echinocactus octogonus is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and being drought-tolerant.
