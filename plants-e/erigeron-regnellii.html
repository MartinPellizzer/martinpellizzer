Erigeron regnellii

Erigeron regnellii, commonly known as the plains blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and disturbed soils, showcasing a low-growing habit with hairy stems. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erigeron regnellii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
