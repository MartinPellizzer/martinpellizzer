Eria ochracea

Eria ochracea, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Eria ochracea is a perennial herb that produces its flowers during the warmer months of the year.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant color make it a notable species within the orchid family.
