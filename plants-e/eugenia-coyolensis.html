Eugenia coyolensis

Eugenia coyolensis, commonly known as coyol tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central America and northern South America. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit, which is a small, fleshy drupe, is edible and contains a single seed.

The coyol tree is often found in humid lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
