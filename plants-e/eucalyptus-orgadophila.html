Eucalyptus orgadophila

Eucalyptus orgadophila, commonly known as the yellow box, is a tall, slender tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a yellowish hue beneath, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that are typically white or pale yellow, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and heathlands.

Eucalyptus orgadophila plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
