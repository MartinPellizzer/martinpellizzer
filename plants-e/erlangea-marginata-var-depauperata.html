Erlangea marginata var. depauperata

Erlangea marginata var. depauperata, commonly known as the slender erlang, is a rare and diminutive plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and is often found in protected conservation areas.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native environment.
