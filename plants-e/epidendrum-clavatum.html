Epidendrum clavatum

Epidendrum clavatum, commonly known as the clustered epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Epidendrum clavatum is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
