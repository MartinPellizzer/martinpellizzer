Euphorbia altissima var. glabrescens

Euphorbia altissima var. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
