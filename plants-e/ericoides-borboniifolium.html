Ericoides borboniifolium

Ericoides borboniifolium, commonly known as the Bourbon leaf heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers. Native to the southeastern United States, it thrives in acidic, well-drained soils. The plant is typically found in wetland habitats such as bogs and pine flatwoods.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
