Erianthus versicolor

Erianthus versicolor, commonly known as the variable grass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and long, narrow leaves that vary in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Erianthus versicolor thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
