Eucalyptus oleosa var. obtusa

Eucalyptus oleosa var. obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds.

This species is often used in reforestation and erosion control due to its hardy nature and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
