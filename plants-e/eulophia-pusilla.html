Eulophia pusilla

Eulophia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf eulophia, is a small orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact stature and delicate, linear leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically arranged in a loose raceme. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Eulophia pusilla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
