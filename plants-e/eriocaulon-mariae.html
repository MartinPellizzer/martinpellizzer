Eriocaulon mariae

Eriocaulon mariae, commonly known as the Marie's spikerush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

Eriocaulon mariae plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
