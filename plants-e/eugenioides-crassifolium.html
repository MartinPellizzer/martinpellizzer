Eugenioides crassifolium

Eugenioides crassifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed eugenioid, is a tropical shrub or small tree belonging to the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
