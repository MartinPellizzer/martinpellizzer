Eulophia clavicornis var. nutans

Eulophia clavicornis var. nutans, commonly known as the nodding eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that often hang downward, giving it its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in two rows along the stem. Its flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white with a distinct, club-shaped spur.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
