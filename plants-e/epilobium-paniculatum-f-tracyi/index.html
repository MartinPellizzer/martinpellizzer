Epilobium Paniculatum F. Tracyi

Tracy's willowherb, scientifically known as Epilobium paniculatum f. tracyi, is a rare and distinctive plant species native to specific regions of the western United States. This botanical variant is characterized by its striking floral display and unique growth habits that set it apart from other members of the willowherb family. Found primarily in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks, it thrives in environments with adequate sunlight and well-drained soil. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Tracy's willowherb is considered a species of conservation concern. Its beauty and ecological significance make it an important subject for botanical study and habitat preservation efforts.
