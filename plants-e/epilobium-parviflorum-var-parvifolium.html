Epilobium parviflorum var. parvifolium

Epilobium parviflorum var. parvifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, often disturbed habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and roadside areas. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often slightly hairy. It produces small, pink to purple flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in wet or poorly drained soils.
