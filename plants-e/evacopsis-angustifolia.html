Evacopsis angustifolia

Evacopsis angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved evaporation plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is often found in regions with low rainfall and high sunlight exposure.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying xerophytic plants.
