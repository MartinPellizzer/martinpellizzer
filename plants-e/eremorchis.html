Eremorchis

Eremorchis, commonly known as the desert orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, Madagascar, and the Arabian Peninsula. Eremorchis species are characterized by their small, often delicate flowers that are typically yellow or white in color. They have adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability, featuring succulent leaves and deep root systems.

The genus is noted for its unique floral structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
