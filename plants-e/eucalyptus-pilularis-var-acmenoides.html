Eucalyptus pilularis var. acmenoides

Eucalyptus pilularis var. acmenoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a variant of the narrow-leafed mallees species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hairs. The flowers are usually small and white or pale blue, arranged in clusters along the branches. This variant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

It plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
