Elodea nevadensis

Elodea nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Elodea nevadensis plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic organisms.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through fragmentation make it both ecologically significant and potentially invasive in non-native regions.
