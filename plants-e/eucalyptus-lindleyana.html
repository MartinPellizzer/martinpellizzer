Eucalyptus lindleyana

Eucalyptus lindleyana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, aromatic white flowers that are typically clustered in dense groups. Eucalyptus lindleyana is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with a deep root system that allows it to thrive in dry conditions.

It is also valued for its timber and has been used in traditional Aboriginal practices.
