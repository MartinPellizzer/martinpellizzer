Epidendrum dressleri

Epidendrum dressleri, commonly known as the Mexican violet orchid, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its rhizomatous structure. The plant produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically broad and elliptical, with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Epidendrum dressleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
