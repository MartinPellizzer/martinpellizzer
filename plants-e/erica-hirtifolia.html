Erica hirtifolia

Erica hirtifolia, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its low-growing habit, dense foliage, and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica hirtifolia is well adapted to dry, acidic environments and is often found in fynbos vegetation.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is also cultivated for its ornamental value.
