Excoecaria pallens

Excoecaria pallens, commonly known as the white sander, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian Ocean.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
