Erythroxylum ligustrinum

Erythroxylum ligustrinum, commonly known as the purpleleaf erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking purple foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
