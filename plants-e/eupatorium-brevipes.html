Eupatorium brevipes

Eupatorium brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed boneset, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with hairy surfaces, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eupatorium brevipes is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
