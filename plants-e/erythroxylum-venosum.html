Erythroxylum venosum

Erythroxylum venosum, commonly known as the veiny copaiba, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and alternate leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped fruits. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically significant plant in its native habitat.
