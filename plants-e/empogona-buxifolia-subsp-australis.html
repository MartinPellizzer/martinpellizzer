Empogona buxifolia subsp. australis

Empogona buxifolia subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian boxwood, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark, spherical berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural landscapes.
