Eleocharis transcaucasica

Eleocharis transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often forms dense vegetation in its native environment.

Eleocharis transcaucasica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
