Eurybia parvifolia

Eurybia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved sunflower, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and daisy-like flowers. The plant produces yellow disk flowers surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eurybia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
