Eucalyptus longicornis

Eucalyptus longicornis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia, specifically found in the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

Eucalyptus longicornis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
