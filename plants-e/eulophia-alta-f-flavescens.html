Eulophia alta f. flavescens

Eulophia alta f. flavescens, commonly known as the golden-flowered eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often with a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
