Epaltes pleiochaeta

Epaltes pleiochaeta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces fruit that resembles a small, round bean, often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are often used as a vegetable in local cuisines.

The plant is well adapted to dry climates and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
