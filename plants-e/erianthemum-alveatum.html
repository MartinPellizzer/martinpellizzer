Erianthemum alveatum

Erianthemum alveatum, commonly known as the woolly begonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Erianthemum alveatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
