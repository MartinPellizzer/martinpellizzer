Endlicheria citriodora

Endlicheria citriodora, commonly known as the lemon-scented evergreen, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lemon-scented leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a distinct citrus fragrance when crushed.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant aroma.
