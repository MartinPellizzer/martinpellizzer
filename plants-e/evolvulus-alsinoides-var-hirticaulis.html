Evolvulus alsinoides var. hirticaulis

Evolvulus alsinoides var. hirticaulis, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a weed and a garden plant.
