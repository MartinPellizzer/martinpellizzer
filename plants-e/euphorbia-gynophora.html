Euphorbia gynophora

Euphorbia gynophora, commonly known as the desert euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with spiny leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is adapted to survive in low-water conditions, storing moisture in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Due to its resilience, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dry climates.
