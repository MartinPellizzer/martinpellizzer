Ehretia calophylla

Ehretia calophylla, commonly known as the glossy leaf tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
