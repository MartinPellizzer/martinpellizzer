Eleocharis palustris f. submersa

Eleocharis palustris f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged spike-rush, is a variant of the common spike-rush that thrives in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

This plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that are fully submerged beneath the water surface. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale brown flowers. The leaves are typically reduced to sheaths, and the plant lacks true leaves, relying on its stems for photosynthesis.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
