Exacum humile

Exacum humile, commonly known as the dwarf bluebell, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with trailing stems that produce clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, violet, or white. The plant has opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade. It is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Exacum humile is relatively easy to care for, making it a favored choice for gardeners seeking colorful, compact plants.
