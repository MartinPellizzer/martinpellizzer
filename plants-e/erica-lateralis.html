Erica lateralis

Erica lateralis, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica lateralis is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome in South Africa.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
