Eupatorium pennivenium

Eupatorium pennivenium, commonly known as white snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly rough texture, while its flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters.

Despite its aesthetic appeal, it is toxic to livestock and can cause illness if ingested.
