Elaeocarpus lancipetalus

Elaeocarpus lancipetalus, commonly known as the narrow-petaled elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers and elliptical leaves that are often glossy in appearance. It typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
