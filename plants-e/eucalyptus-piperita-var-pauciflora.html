Eucalyptus piperita var. pauciflora

Eucalyptus piperita var. pauciflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a variant of the eucalyptus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with hot, dry summers and cool, wet winters.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
