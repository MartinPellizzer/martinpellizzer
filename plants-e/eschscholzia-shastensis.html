Eschscholzia shastensis

Eschscholzia shastensis, commonly known as Shasta poppy, is a species of flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has a taproot system and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The leaves are usually lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
