Eugenia capulioides

Eugenia capulioides, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive inflated calyx that encases the fruit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that turn orange-red when mature. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
