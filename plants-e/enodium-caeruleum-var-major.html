Enodium caeruleum var. major

Enodium caeruleum var. major, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that can reach up to 60 cm in height.
