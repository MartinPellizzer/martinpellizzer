Ephedra torreyana var. powelliorum

Ephedra torreyana var. powelliorum, commonly known as Powell's ephedra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its jointed, branching stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and red, woody capsules that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Ephedraceae family.
