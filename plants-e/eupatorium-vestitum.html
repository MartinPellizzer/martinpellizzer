Eupatorium vestitum

Eupatorium vestitum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant features slender, erect stems and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Eupatorium vestitum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
