Elatostema subvillosum

Elatostema subvillosum, commonly known as the hairy elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and clustered in umbels, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Elatostema subvillosum is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
