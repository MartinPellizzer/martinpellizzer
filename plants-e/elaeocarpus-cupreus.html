Elaeocarpus cupreus

Elaeocarpus cupreus, commonly known as the copperwood or purple-leaf elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green to purple-tinged leaves. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, and the tree is often found in lowland rainforests.

Elaeocarpus cupreus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
