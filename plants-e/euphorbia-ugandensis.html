Euphorbia ugandensis

Euphorbia ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda spurge, is a succulent plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
