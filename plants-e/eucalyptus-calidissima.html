Eucalyptus calidissima

Eucalyptus calidissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
