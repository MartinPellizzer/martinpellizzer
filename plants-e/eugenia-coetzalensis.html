Eugenia coetzalensis

Eugenia coetzalensis, commonly known as the Coetzalensis myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in parts of Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and glossy, oval-shaped leaves. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as undergrowth in forests.

Eugenia coetzalensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has limited use in traditional medicine.
