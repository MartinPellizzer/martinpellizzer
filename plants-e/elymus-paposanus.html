Elymus paposanus

Elymus paposanus, commonly known as the Alaska wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. Elymus paposanus plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its flowering spikes are typically greenish or reddish, adding visual interest to its natural environment.
