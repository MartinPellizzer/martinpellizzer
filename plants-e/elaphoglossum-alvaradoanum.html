Elaphoglossum alvaradoanum

Elaphoglossum alvaradoanum, commonly known as the Alvarado's holly fern, is a member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is a small, terrestrial fern characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky habitats. Its spores are produced on the undersides of the fronds in sori, which are clustered in lines along the veins.

Elaphoglossum alvaradoanum is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, showcasing the adaptability of ferns to diverse ecological conditions.
