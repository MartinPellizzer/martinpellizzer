Elatostema subincisum

Elatostema subincisum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed elatostema, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Urticaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of India and Malaysia. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins, and it often grows in shaded, moist environments. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its leaves are covered in fine, stinging hairs typical of the Urticaceae family.

Elatostema subincisum is often found in forest understories and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
