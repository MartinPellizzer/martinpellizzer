Eremophila anomala

Eremophila anomala, commonly known as the slender eremophila, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy pink or purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Eremophila anomala is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for native pollinators.
