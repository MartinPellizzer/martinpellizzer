Eurybia macrophylla var. ianthina

Eurybia macrophylla var. ianthina, commonly known as the purple giant coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has sturdy, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical structure makes it a valuable addition to native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
