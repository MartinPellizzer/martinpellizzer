Eugenia vaga var. brasiliensis

Eugenia vaga var. brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian wax apple, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by round, waxy fruits resembling small apples. The fruits are typically yellow or red when ripe and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit in its native habitat.
