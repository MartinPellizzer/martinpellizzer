Euxolus arvensis

Euxolus arvensis, commonly known as field euphorbia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in disturbed soils and open areas. The plant features simple, alternate leaves and produces small, clustered yellowish flowers. It is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common trait among members of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euxolus arvensis is often found in temperate regions and is known for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
