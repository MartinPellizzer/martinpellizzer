Elaeagnus multiflora f. oblongata

Elaeagnus multiflora f. oblongata, commonly known as the oblongleaf silverberry, is a variant of the silverberry plant native to parts of East Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its slender, oblong leaves that are silver-white on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to fix nitrogen.

This variety is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
