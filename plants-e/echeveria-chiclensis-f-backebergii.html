Echeveria chiclensis f. backebergii

Echeveria chiclensis f. backebergii, commonly known as the Backebergii Echeveria, is a compact, low-growing succulent with rosettes of thick, fleshy, bluish-green leaves.

It originates from the mountainous regions of Mexico, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped pink or red flowers during the spring or early summer. Its leaves are often adorned with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss, a key adaptation to its arid environment.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and ease of care, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.
