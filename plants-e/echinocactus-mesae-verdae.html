Echinocactus mesae-verdae

Echinocactus mesae-verdae, commonly known as the Chihuahua cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles, which house both flowers and spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted for water storage, making it well-suited for harsh desert environments.

Echinocactus mesae-verdae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique shape and resilience.
