Enteropogon monostachyos subsp. monostachyos

Enteropogon monostachyos subsp.

monostachyos, commonly known as the purple false rice, is a grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its erect, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale purple to brownish flowers. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal or inland dune environments.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its historical use in traditional medicine.
