Espostoa baumannii var. arborescens

Espostoa baumannii var. arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like espostoa, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stem and branching structure, resembling a small tree or shrub. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
