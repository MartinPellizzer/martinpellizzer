Eryngium rauhianum

Eryngium rauhianum, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue-purple flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant has hairy, gray-green foliage and is often found in moist, open woodlands and prairies. It produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eryngium rauhianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
