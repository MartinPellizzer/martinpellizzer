Euphorbia hermentiana

Euphorbia hermentiana, commonly known as the Hermentian spurflower, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. Euphorbia hermentiana is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Its unique morphology and resilience make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
