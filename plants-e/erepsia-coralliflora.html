Erepsia coralliflora

Erepsia coralliflora, commonly known as the coral-flowered erpesia, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, coral-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are usually elongated and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

The flowers are small but numerous, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.
