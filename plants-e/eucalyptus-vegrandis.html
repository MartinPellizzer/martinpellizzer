Eucalyptus vegrandis

Eucalyptus vegrandis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white flowers, which bloom during the winter months. Eucalyptus vegrandis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy shrub or tree.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
