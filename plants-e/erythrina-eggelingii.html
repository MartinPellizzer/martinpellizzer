Erythrina eggelingii

Erythrina eggelingii, commonly known as the African coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three elliptical leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. Erythrina eggelingii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
