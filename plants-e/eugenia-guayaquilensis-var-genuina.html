Eugenia guayaquilensis var. genuina

Eugenia guayaquilensis var. genuina, commonly known as the Ecuadorian myrtle, is a species native to the western slopes of the Andes in Ecuador.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features elliptical, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
