Eria glandulifera

Eria glandulifera, commonly known as the glandular eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a subtle fragrance. Eria glandulifera is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
