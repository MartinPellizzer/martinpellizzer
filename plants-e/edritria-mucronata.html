Edritria mucronata

Edritria mucronata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a distinctive spur at the base of each bloom. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
