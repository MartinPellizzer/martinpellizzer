Erica minutiflora

Erica minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. This plant is characterized by its small, needle-like leaves and clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Erica minutiflora thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

It is a hardy plant that is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and can tolerate drought conditions.
