Elodea ioensis

Elodea ioensis, commonly known as waterweed, is a freshwater plant belonging to the Hydrocharitaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that are typically submerged in water, with small, whorled leaves that are often green to reddish in color. The plant is an aquatic herb that thrives in still or slow-moving water environments. Elodea ioensis reproduces through vegetative propagation, with new shoots forming at the nodes along its stem.

It plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to various aquatic organisms.
