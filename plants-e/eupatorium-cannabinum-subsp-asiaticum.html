Eupatorium cannabinum subsp. asiaticum

Eupatorium cannabinum subsp.

asiaticum, commonly known as Asian hemp agrimony, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as wet meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
