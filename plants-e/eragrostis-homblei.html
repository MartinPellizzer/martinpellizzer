Eragrostis homblei

Eragrostis homblei, commonly known as the Hombli grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along riverbanks.

Eragrostis homblei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
