Euphorbia croizatii

Euphorbia croizatii, commonly known as the Croizat's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with numerous cylindrical, fleshy leaves that are covered in a white powdery coating, known as farina. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
