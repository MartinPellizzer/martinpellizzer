Epidendrum greenwoodii

Epidendrum greenwoodii, commonly known as the Greenwald's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences with numerous flowers in shades of yellow and green. Its leaves are typically broad and strap-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

Epidendrum greenwoodii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
