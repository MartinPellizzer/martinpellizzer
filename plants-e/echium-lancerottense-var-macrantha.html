Echium lancerottense var. macrantha

Echium lancerottense var. macrantha, commonly known as the Lancerottense thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a robust, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
