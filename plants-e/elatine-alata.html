Elatine alata

Elatine alata, commonly known as the running water plant or water stitchwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Elatineae.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in streams, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has tiny, ovate leaves that are usually arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, white or greenish flowers. Elatine alata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through runners, making it a common sight in freshwater habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.
