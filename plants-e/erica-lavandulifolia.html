Erica lavandulifolia

Erica lavandulifolia, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, dense clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy coastal areas.

Erica lavandulifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
