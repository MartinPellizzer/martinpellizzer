Eugenia lepidota var. pauciflora

Eugenia lepidota var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender satinwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale gray bark and lance-shaped, glossy dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
