Eupatorium guatemalense

Eupatorium guatemalense, commonly known as Guatemalan boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Eupatorium guatemalense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
