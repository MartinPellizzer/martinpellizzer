Eleocharis subcancellata

Eleocharis subcancellata, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features slender, triangular stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of tiny, spike-like flowers.

Eleocharis subcancellata is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
