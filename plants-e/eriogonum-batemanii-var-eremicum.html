Eriogonum batemanii var. eremicum

Eriogonum batemanii var. eremicum, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and washes. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in the spring. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and a fibrous root system adapted to conserve water.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
