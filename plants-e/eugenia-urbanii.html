Eugenia urbanii

Eugenia urbanii, commonly known as the black mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark purple, leathery leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically bright red or purple when mature. The fruit's pericarp contains a rich, aromatic pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

Eugenia urbanii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.
