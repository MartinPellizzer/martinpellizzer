Elaterium torquatum

Elaterium torquatum, commonly known as the twisted elaterium, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive twisted, spiral-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Elateriaceae and is known for its small, inconspicuous flowers that bloom in early spring.
