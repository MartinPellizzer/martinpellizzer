Epidendrum guacamayense

Epidendrum guacamayense, commonly known as the Guanacaste orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, using its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowers are usually vibrant in color, ranging from yellow to red, and are often fragrant.

Epidendrum guacamayense is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
