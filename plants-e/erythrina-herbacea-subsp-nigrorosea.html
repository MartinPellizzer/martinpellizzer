Erythrina herbacea subsp. nigrorosea

Erythrina herbacea subsp.

nigrorosea, commonly known as the black rose coral tree, is a tropical legume characterized by its distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant features bright red to orange flowers that are often adorned with a dark rose-colored base, giving it its subspecies name.

Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces elongated, woody pods that contain several flat, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.
