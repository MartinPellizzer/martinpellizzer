Elaeocarpus fulgens

Elaeocarpus fulgens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its showy, bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

Elaeocarpus fulgens is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks for its vibrant floral display.
