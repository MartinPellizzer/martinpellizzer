Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. decipiens

Echinocactus hexaedrophorus var. decipiens, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
