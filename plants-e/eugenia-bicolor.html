Eugenia bicolor

Eugenia bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that changes color from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
