Epidendrum cesar-fernandezii

Epidendrum cesar-fernandezii, commonly known as the Cesar Fernandez Orchid, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, branching stems and numerous, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the spring and summer months. It prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both greenhouse and outdoor cultivation.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a sought-after plant among orchid enthusiasts.
