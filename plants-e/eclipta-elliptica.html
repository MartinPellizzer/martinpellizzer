Eclipta elliptica

Eclipta elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small yellow flowers in clusters.

Eclipta elliptica is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
