Eugenia emirnensis

Eugenia emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis myrtle, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns from green to red as it matures, containing several seeds.

Eugenia emirnensis is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
