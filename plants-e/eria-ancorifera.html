Eria ancorifera

Eria ancorifera, commonly known as the anchovy orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the soil.

Eria ancorifera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
