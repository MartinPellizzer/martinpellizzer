Echinocactus leeanus

Echinocactus leeanus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and dense, spiny areoles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. Its yellowish-green coloration and prominent central spines are notable botanical traits.

Echinocactus leeanus is a slow-growing succulent that thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.
