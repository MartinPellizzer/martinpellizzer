Echinops claessensii

Echinops claessensii, commonly known as the African thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are small and pale blue, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Echinops claessensii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
