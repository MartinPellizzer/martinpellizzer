Eriocaulon richardianum

Eriocaulon richardianum, commonly known as the Richard's spike-rush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, spike-like inflorescences. This species typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine, hairy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
