Eugenia prora

Eugenia prora, commonly known as the broad-leaved eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red when mature.

Eugenia prora is often valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
