Epipactis palustris f. submersa

Epipactis palustris f. submersa, commonly known as the submerged marsh helleborine, is a rare and specialized orchid species adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

It is characterized by its fully submerged leaves and flowers, which are adapted for survival in waterlogged conditions. The plant exhibits a unique morphology, with its floral structures often modified to facilitate pollination in its submerged habitat. It is typically found in shallow, still waters such as marshes and wetlands.
