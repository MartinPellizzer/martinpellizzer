Eupatorium dolphini

Eupatorium dolphini, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble mist. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features narrow, gray-green leaves. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Eupatorium dolphini is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
