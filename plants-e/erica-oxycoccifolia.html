Erica oxycoccifolia

Erica oxycoccifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica oxycoccifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
