Eupatorium maximiliani

Eupatorium maximiliani, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive characteristic is its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Eupatorium maximiliani is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
