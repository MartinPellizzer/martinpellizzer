Eriophyllum trifidum

Eriophyllum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to California and parts of Oregon.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as chaparral and coastal sage scrub. It features narrow, gray-green leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Eriophyllum trifidum is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
