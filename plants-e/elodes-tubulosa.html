Elodes tubulosa

Elodes tubulosa, commonly known as the tubular waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It has long, slender, branching stems that are often covered with small, oval leaves arranged in whorls. The plant produces tiny, tubular flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, which are adapted for underwater pollination. Its rhizomes form dense mats on the bottom of lakes and ponds, contributing to water clarity by reducing sediment resuspension.

Elodes tubulosa plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
