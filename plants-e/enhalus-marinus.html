Enhalus marinus

Enhalus marinus, commonly known as the seagrass turtle grass, is a marine flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its long, ribbon-like leaves that grow in dense underwater meadows, typically in shallow coastal waters. Enhalus marinus is an important component of seagrass ecosystems, contributing to coastal biodiversity and carbon sequestration.
