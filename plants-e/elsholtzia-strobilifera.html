Elsholtzia strobilifera

Elsholtzia strobilifera, commonly known as the fragrant mountain verbena, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
