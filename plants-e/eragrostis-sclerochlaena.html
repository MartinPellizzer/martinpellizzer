Eragrostis sclerochlaena

Eragrostis sclerochlaena, commonly known as the rigid bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a distinctive ornamental plant. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many grasses valued for their ecological and agricultural importance.
