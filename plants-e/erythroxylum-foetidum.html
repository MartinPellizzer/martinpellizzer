Erythroxylum foetidum

Erythroxylum foetidum, commonly known as the stinking erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
