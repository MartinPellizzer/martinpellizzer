Erica lycopodiifolia

Erica lycopodiifolia, commonly known as the fern-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that resemble those of ferns, giving it its common name. Erica lycopodiifolia produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in acidic conditions.
