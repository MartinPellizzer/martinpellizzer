Euphorbia polygalifolia subsp. hirta

Euphorbia polygalifolia subsp.

hirta, commonly known as the hairy euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
