Epidendrum secundum f. albescens

Epidendrum secundum f. albescens, commonly known as the White-flowered Epidendrum, is a hybrid orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence is racemose, bearing numerous small, fragrant blooms.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
