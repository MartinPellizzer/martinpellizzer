Erythroxylum lanceolatum

Erythroxylum lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed copal tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly in regions of Brazil, Bolivia, and Peru. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color. It produces a milky latex sap, which has been used traditionally for various purposes, including as a natural rubber source.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland areas.
