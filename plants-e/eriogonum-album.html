Eriogonum album

Eriogonum album, commonly known as the white buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum album is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
