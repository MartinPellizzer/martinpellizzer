Echium wurmbii

Echium wurmbii, commonly known as the Wurmb's thornweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echium wurmbii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
