Eugenia antiquae

Eugenia antiquae, commonly known as the ancient myrtle, is a rare and ancient species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and glossy, dark green leaves. It is often found in shaded, humid environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eugenia antiquae is considered a conservation concern.
