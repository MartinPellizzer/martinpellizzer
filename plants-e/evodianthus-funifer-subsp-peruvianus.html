Evodianthus funifer subsp. peruvianus

Evodianthus funifer subsp.

peruvianus, commonly known as the Peruvian velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Peru. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically velvety to the touch.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
