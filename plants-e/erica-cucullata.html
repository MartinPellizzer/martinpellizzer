Erica cucullata

Erica cucullata, commonly known as the helmet heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in sandy or rocky soils. The plant produces small, urn-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in spring. Its leaves are evergreen, leathery, and arranged in a dense, compact form.

Erica cucullata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and unique appearance.
