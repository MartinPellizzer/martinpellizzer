Epidendrum pseudepidendrum

Epidendrum pseudepidendrum, commonly known as the false epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within the understory of forests. The plant features linear to lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with numerous small, fragrant flowers. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow with a distinct lip that is often darker in color.

Epidendrum pseudepidendrum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
