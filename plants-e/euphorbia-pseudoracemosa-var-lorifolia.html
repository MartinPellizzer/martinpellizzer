Euphorbia pseudoracemosa var. lorifolia

Euphorbia pseudoracemosa var. lorifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a waxy coating that helps reduce water loss. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its milky sap and diverse morphological features.
