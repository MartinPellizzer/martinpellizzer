Elytrigia intermedia subsp. campestris

Elytrigia intermedia subsp.

campestris, commonly known as field grass or meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its role in stabilizing soil and as a forage plant for livestock.
