Eragrostis falcata

Eragrostis falcata, commonly known as the sicklegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and distinctive seed heads that resemble a sickle shape, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Eragrostis falcata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability to different soil conditions.
