Erysimum pseudo-odoratum

Erysimum pseudo-odoratum, commonly known as the rock cress, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features basal leaves that are pinnately lobed. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers with four petals, typical of the Brassicaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Erysimum pseudo-odoratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
