Ericentrodea

Ericentrodea, commonly known as the heath family, is a group of flowering plants primarily found in temperate and boreal regions.

These plants typically have small, leathery leaves and produce flowers in clusters, often with four or five petals. Ericentrodea species are generally evergreen shrubs, adapted to acidic soils and harsh climates. They play an important role in ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
