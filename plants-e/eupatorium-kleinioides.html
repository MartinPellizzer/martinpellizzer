Eupatorium kleinioides

Eupatorium kleinioides, commonly known as Kleini's eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its ornamental value and ecological significance make it a notable species in native plant gardens.
