Euphorbia tugelensis

Euphorbia tugelensis, commonly known as the Tugela euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia tugelensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
