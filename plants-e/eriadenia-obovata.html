Eriadenia obovata

Eriadenia obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed eriadenia, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests. Its seed pods are pod-like and contain several small, round seeds.

Eriadenia obovata plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
