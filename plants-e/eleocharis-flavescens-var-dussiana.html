Eleocharis flavescens var. dussiana

Eleocharis flavescens var. dussiana, commonly known as the yellow spike-rush, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, yellowish inflorescences that resemble spikes. This variety is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like structures.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
