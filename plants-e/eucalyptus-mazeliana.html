Eucalyptus mazeliana

Eucalyptus mazeliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fluffy white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer months. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin layers, a common trait among many eucalyptus species.

Eucalyptus mazeliana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
