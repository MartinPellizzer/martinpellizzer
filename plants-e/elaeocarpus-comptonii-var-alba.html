Elaeocarpus comptonii var. alba

Elaeocarpus comptonii var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, showy white flowers. The plant produces spherical, woody fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the genus Elaeocarpus. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
