Eupatorium roanense

Eupatorium roanense, commonly known as Roan mountain eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct aromatic scent.

Eupatorium roanense is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in native gardens.
