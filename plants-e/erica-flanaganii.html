Erica flanaganii

Erica flanaganii, commonly known as the Flanagan's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. Erica flanaganii thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
