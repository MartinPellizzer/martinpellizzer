Erysimum ochroleucum subsp. helveticum

Erysimum ochroleucum subsp.

helveticum, commonly known as the Swiss wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alpine regions of Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, bright green leaves and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers with four petals.

The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found growing on limestone cliffs and slopes, where it can withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, adding a splash of color to mountainous landscapes.
