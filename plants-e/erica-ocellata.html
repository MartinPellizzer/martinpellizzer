Erica ocellata

Erica ocellata, commonly known as the spotted heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape Floristic Region of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its evergreen shrub form and dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive red or orange spot at the base. Erica ocellata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
