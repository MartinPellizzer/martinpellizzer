Echinocactus recurvus var. latispinus

Echinocactus recurvus var. latispinus, commonly known as the broad-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in thick, bright green to blue-green spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short pedicels near the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
