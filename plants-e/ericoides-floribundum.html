Ericoides floribundum

Ericoides floribundum, commonly known as the flowering heath, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Ericoides floribundum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
