Echinops cornigerus

Echinops cornigerus, commonly known as the goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown of thorns, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are followed by the distinctive, bristly seed heads. Echinops cornigerus is a hardy perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
