Eucalyptus umbellata

Eucalyptus umbellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It is often found in heathlands and open woodlands, forming dense shrubs or small trees.

Eucalyptus umbellata is valued for its ecological role and is also used in horticulture for its ornamental features.
