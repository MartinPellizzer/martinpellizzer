Eulophia cristata

Eulophia cristata, commonly known as the crested eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that often display a striking color contrast. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, providing support for the inflorescence.

Eulophia cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
