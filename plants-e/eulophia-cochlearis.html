Eulophia cochlearis

Eulophia cochlearis, commonly known as the spiral orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers.

Eulophia cochlearis is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
