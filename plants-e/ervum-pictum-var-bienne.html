Ervum pictum var. bienne

Ervum pictum var. bienne, commonly known as the painted vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, colorful flowers that range from purple to pink. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vibrant, low-maintenance plant.
