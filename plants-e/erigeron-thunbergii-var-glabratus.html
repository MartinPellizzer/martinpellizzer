Erigeron thunbergii var. glabratus

Erigeron thunbergii var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth blueweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its showy blooms attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wetland gardens.
