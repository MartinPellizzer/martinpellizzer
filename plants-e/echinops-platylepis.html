Echinops platylepis

Echinops platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are blue to purple in color and bloom in the summer months.

Echinops platylepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
