Ericoides inclusum

Ericoides inclusum, commonly known as the narrowleaf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes evergreens and shrubs adapted to acidic soils. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a dense, compact growth habit. It produces bell-shaped, white or pink flowers in the spring, which are clustered in inflorescences.

Ericoides inclusum is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.
