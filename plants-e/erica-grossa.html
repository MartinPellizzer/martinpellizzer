Erica grossa

Erica grossa, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western and southern regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other heathers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica grossa thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and coastal areas.

It is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
