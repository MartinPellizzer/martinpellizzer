Englerophytum oblanceolatum

Englerophytum oblanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf englerophytum, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, blue-green leaves that are oblanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense, terminal cluster. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
