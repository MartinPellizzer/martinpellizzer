Epidendrum harrisii

Epidendrum harrisii, commonly known as the Harris's Epidendrum, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with numerous small, colorful flowers that range from white to pink and purple. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Epidendrum harrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
