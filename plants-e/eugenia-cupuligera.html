Eugenia cupuligera

Eugenia cupuligera, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces edible, fleshy berries that are typically yellow or red when ripe, containing a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant's botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
