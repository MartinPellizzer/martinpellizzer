Erythrococca pentagyna

Erythrococca pentagyna, commonly known as the five-angled erythrococca, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pentagonal-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are fleshy berries that contain several seeds.

Erythrococca pentagyna is often found in moist, shaded areas within forests or along riverbanks.
