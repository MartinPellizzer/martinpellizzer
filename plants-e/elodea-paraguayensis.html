Elodea paraguayensis

Elodea paraguayensis, commonly known as Paraguay waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Hydrocharitaceae family and is characterized by its long, branching stems and small, whorled leaves. The plant typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving rivers. Its leaves are narrow and arranged in whorls of three to four around the stem.

Elodea paraguayensis plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.
