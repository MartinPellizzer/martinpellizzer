Ebenus rufa

Ebenus rufa, commonly known as the red sandwort, is a perennial flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with small, succulent leaves and clusters of pink or red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates. It is often found in mountainous areas and tundra environments.

Ebenus rufa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom in harsh conditions.
