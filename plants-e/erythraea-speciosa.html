Erythraea speciosa

Erythraea speciosa, commonly known as the red beauty orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Erythraea speciosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
