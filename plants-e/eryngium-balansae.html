Eryngium balansae

Eryngium balansae, commonly known as the Balansae thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple-blue flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bracts.

Eryngium balansae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
