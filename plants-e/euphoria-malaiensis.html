Euphoria malaiensis

Euphoria malaiensis, commonly known as the Malayan euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and striking yellow flowers. The plant belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae, which includes many species with milky sap. Its flowers are often clustered in cyathia, which are modified inflorescences typical of the genus.

Euphoria malaiensis is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
