Elaeocarpus macrophyllus

Elaeocarpus macrophyllus, commonly known as the large-leaf elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by edible, woody fruits that are encased in a thick, fleshy pulp. Its roots are known to contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Elaeocarpus macrophyllus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
