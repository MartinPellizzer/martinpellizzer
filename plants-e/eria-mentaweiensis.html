Eria mentaweiensis

Eria mentaweiensis, commonly known as the Mentawai orchid, is a rare and endemic species found primarily in the Mentawai Islands of Indonesia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and attractive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which appear during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often growing on trees or rocks in tropical rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria mentaweiensis is considered a vulnerable species facing threats from deforestation and habitat loss.
