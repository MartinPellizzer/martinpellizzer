Eugenia trinitatis

Eugenia trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidad holly, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round berry that turns from green to red as it matures.

Eugenia trinitatis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
