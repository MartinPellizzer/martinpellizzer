Eucalyptus microtheca var. cymbaliformis

Eucalyptus microtheca var. cymbaliformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color, arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its hardy nature and visual appeal.
