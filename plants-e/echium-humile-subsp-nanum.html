Echium humile subsp. nanum

Echium humile subsp.

nanum, commonly known as the dwarf evening primrose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
