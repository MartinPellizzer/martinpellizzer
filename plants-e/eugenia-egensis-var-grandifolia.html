Eugenia egensis var. grandifolia

Eugenia egensis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered eugenia, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit, a small, fleshy berry, is edible and has a sweet, slightly acidic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
