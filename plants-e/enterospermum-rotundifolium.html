Enterospermum rotundifolium

Enterospermum rotundifolium, commonly known as the broad-leaved enterospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features ovate to rounded leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, with deep roots to access water.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of drought-tolerant plants.
