Erucastrum virgatum var. baeticum

Erucastrum virgatum var. baeticum, commonly known as the Baetican bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately divided leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, elongated pods containing several seeds.

This variety is notable for its distinctive foliage and is often found in Mediterranean regions.
