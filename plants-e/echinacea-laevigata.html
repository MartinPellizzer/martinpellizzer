Echinacea laevigata

Echinacea laevigata, commonly known as the smooth coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like flowers with purple petals and a prominent, spiky yellow center. The plant has rough, hairy leaves that are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-green hue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Echinacea laevigata is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
