Eugenia melanadenia var. santayana

Eugenia melanadenia var. santayana, commonly known as the Santa Ynez eugenia, is a species native to coastal regions of California.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the coast. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, edible berries that are used by local wildlife.

This plant is adapted to coastal climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.
