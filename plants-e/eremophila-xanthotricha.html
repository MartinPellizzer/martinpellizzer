Eremophila xanthotricha

Eremophila xanthotricha, commonly known as the yellow-haired emu bush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-hair-covered stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Eremophila xanthotricha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
