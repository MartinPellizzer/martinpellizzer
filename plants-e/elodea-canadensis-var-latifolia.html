Elodea canadensis var. latifolia

Elodea canadensis var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved waterweed, is a submerged aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems and broad, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. This plant thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. Its simple, branching structure allows it to spread rapidly through vegetative propagation.

Elodea canadensis var. latifolia plays a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.
