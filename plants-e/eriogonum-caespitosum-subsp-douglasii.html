Eriogonum caespitosum subsp. douglasii

Eriogonum caespitosum subsp.

douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and gray-green, providing drought tolerance.

This subspecies is an important component of sagebrush ecosystems and serves as a food source for various pollinators.
