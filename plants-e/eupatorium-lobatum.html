Eupatorium lobatum

Eupatorium lobatum, commonly known as boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant has serrated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Boneset is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves and is often found in moist meadows and woodland edges.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
