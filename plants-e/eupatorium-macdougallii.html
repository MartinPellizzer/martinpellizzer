Eupatorium macdougallii

Eupatorium macdougallii, commonly known as Macdougall's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, thriving in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's name refers to its stiff, brittle stems that break easily, resembling bones.
