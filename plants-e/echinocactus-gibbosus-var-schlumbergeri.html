Echinocactus gibbosus var. schlumbergeri

Echinocactus gibbosus var. schlumbergeri, commonly known as the Sempervivum, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped stems and vibrant, spiky foliage that comes in various colors such as green, red, and blue. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for arid environments. It produces yellow flowers in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
