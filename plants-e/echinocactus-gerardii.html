Echinocactus gerardii

Echinocactus gerardii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny areoles that support both glochids and central spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on long, branching stems. Its fleshy, water-storing stems allow it to thrive in dry environments.

Echinocactus gerardii is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
