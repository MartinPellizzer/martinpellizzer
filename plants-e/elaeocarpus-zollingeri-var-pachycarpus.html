Elaeocarpus zollingeri var. pachycarpus

Elaeocarpus zollingeri var. pachycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited elaeocarpus, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits. The fruits are typically reddish or purplish when mature and contain several seeds.

It is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats, where it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.
