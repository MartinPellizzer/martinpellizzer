Epidendrum biforatum

Epidendrum biforatum, commonly known as the two-lobed epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching growth habit and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences with flowers that are typically greenish-yellow with purple markings. Its blooms are often fragrant and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.

Epidendrum biforatum thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
