Elaeocarpus cheirophorus

Elaeocarpus cheirophorus, commonly known as the red-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its ornamental red flowers and elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forests.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
