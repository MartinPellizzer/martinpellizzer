Eucalyptus redunca subsp. redunca

Eucalyptus redunca subsp.

redunca, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually pale or creamy white in color. This subspecies is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
