Eria oligantha

Eria oligantha, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories or on tree trunks. Its flowers are usually greenish-white or pale yellow, with three sepals and three petals, and it produces small, round seeds.

Eria oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
