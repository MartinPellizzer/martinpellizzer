Eleusine barcinonensis var. mixta

Eleusine barcinonensis var. mixta, commonly known as the mixed lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
