Erodium glandulosum subsp. paularense

Erodium glandulosum subsp.

paularense, commonly known as the coastal bedstraw, is a small annual herb native to southern Australia. It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal areas and rocky outcrops.

The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, and its small, pinkish-white flowers appear in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and covered with glandular dots, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showing resilience to saline and windy conditions.
