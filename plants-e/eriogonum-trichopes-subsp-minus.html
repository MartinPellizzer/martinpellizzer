Eriogonum trichopes subsp. minus

Eriogonum trichopes subsp.

minus, commonly known as the smallleaf globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its small, gray-green leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb with a mat-forming growth habit, often spreading via stolons.

The species is adapted to arid environments and is relatively drought-tolerant.
