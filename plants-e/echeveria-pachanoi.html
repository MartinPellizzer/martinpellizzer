Echeveria pachanoi

Echeveria pachanoi, commonly known as the "Holy Grail" or "Laurel of the Andes," is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped foliage, with fleshy, thick leaves that range in color from blue-green to pink and red, depending on light exposure. The plant produces vibrant purple or red flowers on tall stalks during the spring and summer months. It is relatively easy to care for, requiring well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and cultural significance, it is a popular choice for both gardeners and collectors.
