Eupatorium cuadrasense

Eupatorium cuadrasense, commonly known as Cuadra's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high Andes of Peru.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium cuadrasense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
