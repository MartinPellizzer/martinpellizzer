Empetrum nigrum var. japonicum

Empetrum nigrum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese crowberry, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, dark blue berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like structure. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is closely related to the common crowberry and shares similar horticultural characteristics.
