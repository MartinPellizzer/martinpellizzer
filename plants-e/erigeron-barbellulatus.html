Erigeron barbellulatus

Erigeron barbellulatus, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, with silvery-blue foliage and fluffy, white to pale blue flower heads that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a fuzzy texture, while its stems are hairy and erect.

Erigeron barbellulatus is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.
