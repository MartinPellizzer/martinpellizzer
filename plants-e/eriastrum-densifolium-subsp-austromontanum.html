Eriastrum densifolium subsp. austromontanum

Eriastrum densifolium subsp.

austromontanum, commonly known as the coastal dwarf flax, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant has narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open grasslands.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh, coastal environments.
