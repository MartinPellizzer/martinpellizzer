Ericoides perspicuum

Ericoides perspicuum, commonly known as the transparent erica, is a rare and delicate plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered flowers. The plant is notable for its translucent, waxy leaves that reflect light, giving it a unique appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments with acidic soil, often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited habitat and fragile nature, Ericoides perspicuum is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
