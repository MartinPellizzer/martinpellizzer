Euphorbia friedrichiae subsp. friedrichiae

Euphorbia friedrichiae subsp.

friedrichiae, commonly known as the Friedrich's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features a thick, woody stem with a spiny, cylindrical shape and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant has adapted to arid environments with its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is part of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often striking members.
