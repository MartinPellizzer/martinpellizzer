Epacris micranthera

Epacris micranthera, commonly known as the small-flowered heath, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and rocky soils, thriving in well-drained, acidic conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Epacris micranthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth and hardy nature.
