Eugenia cahosiana

Eugenia cahosiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or cahosiana myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, red, cherry-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisine. Eugenia cahosiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
