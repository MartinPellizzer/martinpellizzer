Erigeron mexicanus

Erigeron mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.
