Ericameria ericoides f. blakei

Ericameria ericoides f. blakei, commonly known as the Blake's ericoid, is a subspecies of the ericaceous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle floral display to its overall appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
