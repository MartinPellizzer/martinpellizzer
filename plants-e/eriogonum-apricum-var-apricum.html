Eriogonum apricum var. apricum

Eriogonum apricum var. apricum, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, providing a contrast to the bright yellow blooms.

It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.
