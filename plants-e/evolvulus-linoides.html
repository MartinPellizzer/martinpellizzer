Evolvulus linoides

Evolvulus linoides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Evolvulus linoides is also known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it both a ornamental and potentially invasive species.
