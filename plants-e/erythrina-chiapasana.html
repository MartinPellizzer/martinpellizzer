Erythrina chiapasana

Erythrina chiapasana, commonly known as the Chiapas flame tree, is a striking leguminous plant native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets, and its distinctive pods contain seeds enclosed in a fibrous covering. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its ecological importance and aesthetic appeal, it is also valued in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
