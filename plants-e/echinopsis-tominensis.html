Echinopsis tominensis

Echinopsis tominensis, commonly known as the Tomineas cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white spines. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Echinopsis tominensis is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
