Euphorbia agowensis

Euphorbia agowensis, commonly known as the Agow's spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a cyathium, a specialized structure typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its stems to survive in dry conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and plant collectors alike.
