Epidendrum sertularioides

Epidendrum sertularioides, commonly known as the coral-like orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching inflorescence that resembles coral, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for greenhouses or shaded outdoor gardens.

Epidendrum sertularioides is relatively easy to care for and is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance and continuous blooming habit.
