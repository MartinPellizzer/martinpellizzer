Eucalyptus macta

Eucalyptus macta, commonly known as the broad-leaved mallet, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and lanceolate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, felt-like hairs. The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually white or pale blue in color. This species is adapted to a variety of habitats, including dry sclerophyll forests and open woodlands.

Eucalyptus macta is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
