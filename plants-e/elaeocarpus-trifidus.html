Elaeocarpus trifidus

Elaeocarpus trifidus, commonly known as the three-lobed elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by spherical, woody fruits. Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.

Elaeocarpus trifidus is often found in tropical and subtropical forests, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.
