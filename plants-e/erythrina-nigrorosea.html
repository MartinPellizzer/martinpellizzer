Erythrina nigrorosea

Erythrina nigrorosea, commonly known as the black rose coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink flowers that bloom in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant has palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. Erythrina nigrorosea is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
