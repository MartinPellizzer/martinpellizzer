Epimedium alpinum var. pubigerum

Epimedium alpinum var. pubigerum, commonly known as the Alpine barberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, shield-shaped leaves that are typically reddish-brown to bronze in color, providing ornamental value in garden settings. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in late spring, arranged in loose clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated for its foliage and early bloom.
