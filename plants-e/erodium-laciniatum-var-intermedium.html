Erodium laciniatum var. intermedium

Erodium laciniatum var. intermedium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, opposite leaves that are often lobed or toothed. The plant produces tiny, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new habitats.
