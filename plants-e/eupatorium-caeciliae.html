Eupatorium caeciliae

Eupatorium caeciliae, commonly known as Caecilia's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, spiky fruits that disperse its seeds.

Eupatorium caeciliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
