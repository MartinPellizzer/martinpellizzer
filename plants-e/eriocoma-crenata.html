Eriocoma crenata

Eriocoma crenata, commonly known as the hairy leaf speedwell, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. The plant has hairy, lobed leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Eriocoma crenata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
