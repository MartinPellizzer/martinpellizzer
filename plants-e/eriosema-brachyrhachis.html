Eriosema brachyrhachis

Eriosema brachyrhachis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features hairy stems and leaves with a distinctive, glandular appearance. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically in racemes, and are usually pale yellow or greenish in color.

Eriosema brachyrhachis is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, adapting well to a variety of soil types.
