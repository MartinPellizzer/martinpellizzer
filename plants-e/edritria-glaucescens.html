Edritria glaucescens

Edritria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green fern, is a perennial plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically a striking blue-green color, which helps it stand out in its natural habitat. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are usually compound, with small, serrated leaflets that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Edritria glaucescens is valued for its aesthetic qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
