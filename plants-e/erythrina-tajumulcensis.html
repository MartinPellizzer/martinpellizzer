Erythrina tajumulcensis

Erythrina tajumulcensis, commonly known as the Maya blood tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its striking red and orange flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the tree can grow up to 10 meters tall.

Erythrina tajumulcensis is also notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules.
