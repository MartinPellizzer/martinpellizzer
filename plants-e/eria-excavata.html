Eria excavata

Eria excavata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered blooms in various colors.

Eria excavata is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its role in local ecosystems.
