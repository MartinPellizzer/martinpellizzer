Edritria digitalis

Edritria digitalis, commonly known as the digitalis plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a fuzzy texture. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

It is also known for its medicinal properties, particularly in the treatment of heart conditions.
