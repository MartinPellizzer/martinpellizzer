Eria sawadae

Eria sawadae, commonly known as the Japanese orchid, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria sawadae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
