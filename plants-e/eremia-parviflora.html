Eremia parviflora

Eremia parviflora, commonly known as the desert flax, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low, sprawling growth habit and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Eremia parviflora is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that help it survive in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Its ability to thrive in such conditions makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
