Eriocaulon helferi

Eriocaulon helferi, commonly known as the velvet sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive silver or grayish sheen on the leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Eriocaulon helferi is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
