Elaeocarpus multiscissus

Elaeocarpus multiscissus, commonly known as the broad-leafed elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, spiny fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental foliage.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
