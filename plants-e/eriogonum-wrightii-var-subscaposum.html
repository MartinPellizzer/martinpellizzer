Eriogonum wrightii var. subscaposum

Eriogonum wrightii var. subscaposum, commonly known as the Wright's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry environments, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
