Erigeron scorzonerifolius var. pumilus

Erigeron scorzonerifolius var. pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous growth habit. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its small, blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

It has finely divided, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.
