Epidendrum neudeckeri

Epidendrum neudeckeri, commonly known as the Neudecker's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Epidendrum neudeckeri is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
