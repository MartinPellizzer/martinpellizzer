Exacum pallidum

Exacum pallidum, commonly known as the pale pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically blooms in the spring and early summer, producing clusters of delicate, spurred petals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Exacum pallidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
