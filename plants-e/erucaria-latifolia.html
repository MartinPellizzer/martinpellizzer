Erucaria latifolia

Erucaria latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed erucaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

Erucaria latifolia is often found in rocky or coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
