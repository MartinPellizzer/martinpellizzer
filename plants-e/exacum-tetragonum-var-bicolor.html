Exacum tetragonum var. bicolor

Exacum tetragonum var. bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces clusters of small, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are ovate and slightly hairy, with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it an attractive addition to gardens and containers.
