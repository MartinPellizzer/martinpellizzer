Eugenia aphthosa

Eugenia aphthosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant, white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe, containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, including Florida, where it can become invasive.

The botanical features of Eugenia aphthosa make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
