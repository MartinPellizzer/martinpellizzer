Echinocactus horizonthalonius

Echinocactus horizonthalonius, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of golden-yellow spines. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from the areoles along the stem.

This cactus is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for succulent gardens.
