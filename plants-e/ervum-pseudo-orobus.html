Ervum pseudo-orobus

Ervum pseudo-orobus, commonly known as the false orobrush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with upright stems and small, compound leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in racemes, and attract various pollinators.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods that split open when mature, dispersing the seeds through the wind.
