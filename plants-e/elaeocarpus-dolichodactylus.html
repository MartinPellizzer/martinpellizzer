Elaeocarpus dolichodactylus

Elaeocarpus dolichodactylus, commonly known as the long-fingered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, a feature common in many Elaeocarpus species.

The tree is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental potential.
