Echinolaena minarum

Echinolaena minarum, commonly known as the slender burrgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and small, spiky seed heads. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and sandy areas.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant plays a minor role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
