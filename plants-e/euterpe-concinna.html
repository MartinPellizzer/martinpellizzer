Euterpe concinna

Euterpe concinna, commonly known as the Brazilian palm or buriti palm, is a species of palm native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible fruits that are yellow when mature. The fruits are often consumed by local communities and are also used in traditional medicine.

This palm is well adapted to wet tropical environments and plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
