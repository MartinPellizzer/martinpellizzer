Euonymus sieboldianus var. yedoensis

Euonymus sieboldianus var. yedoensis, commonly known as the Japanese euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
