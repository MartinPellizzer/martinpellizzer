Equisetum ramosissimum f. virgatum

Equisetum ramosissimum f. virgatum, commonly known as the slender horsetail, is a variant of the common horsetail.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the family Equisetaceae, characterized by its jointed, hollow stems and whorled leaves. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It reproduces via spores and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ancient lineage dates back to the Carboniferous period, making it a living fossil with unique botanical features.
