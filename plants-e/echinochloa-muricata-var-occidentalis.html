Echinochloa muricata var. occidentalis

Echinochloa muricata var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western burrgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and spikelets that are covered in short, bristly hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often found in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides and in agricultural fields. It has fibrous roots and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

This species is often considered a weed in cultivated areas due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
