Euphorbia graeca subsp. dalmatica

Euphorbia graeca subsp.

dalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its succulent, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with red or purple.

The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its latex sap is milky and can be irritant, a common feature among members of its family.
