Eupatorium hitchcockii

Eupatorium hitchcockii, commonly known as Hitchcock's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Hitchcock's boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its purported therapeutic effects.
