Ephedra sarcocarpa

Ephedra sarcocarpa, commonly known as the sarcocarp ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, spiny fruits that contain the seeds. Ephedra sarcocarpa is adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

It is part of the Ephedraceae family, which includes other ephedra species known for their medicinal properties.
