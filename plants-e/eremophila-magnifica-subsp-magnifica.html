Eremophila magnifica subsp. magnifica

Eremophila magnifica subsp.

magnifica, commonly known as the flameflower, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia. It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
