Eugenia ribeireana

Eugenia ribeireana, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a bright red, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

Eugenia ribeireana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
