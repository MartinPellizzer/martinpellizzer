Eulophia zeyheri

Eulophia zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It features a sympodial growth habit with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces raceme-like inflorescences with showy, often fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Its leaves are typically broad and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Eulophia zeyheri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
