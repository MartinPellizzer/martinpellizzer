Euphorbia tashiroi

Euphorbia tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's spurge, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flower clusters called cyathia, which are surrounded by modified leaves known as bracts. Euphorbia tashiroi is adapted to grow in rocky or dry environments, showcasing its resilience to arid conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
