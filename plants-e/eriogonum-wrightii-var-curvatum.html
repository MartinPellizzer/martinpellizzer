Eriogonum wrightii var. curvatum

Eriogonum wrightii var. curvatum, commonly known as the bentspur buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curved, contributing to its common name.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive seed structures and ecological importance to pollinators.
