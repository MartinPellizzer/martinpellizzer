Epipactis salassia

Epipactis salassia, commonly known as the Salassian helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and a striking, pale green to yellowish flower spike. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Epipactis salassia is a perennial species that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing its ecological adaptability.
