Exacum conglomeratum

Exacum conglomeratum, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant features velvety, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to prevent leaf scorching.

Exacum conglomeratum is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
