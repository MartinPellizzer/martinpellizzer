Etlingera baramensis

Etlingera baramensis, commonly known as the Baram torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescence, which resembles a torch, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Etlingera baramensis is typically found in wetlands and along riverbanks, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.
