Elymus lanceolatus subsp. yukonensis

Elymus lanceolatus subsp.

yukonensis, commonly known as Yukon wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is well adapted to cold climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types, including sandy and loamy soils. It is often found in open habitats such as tundra, meadows, and disturbed areas.

Yukon wild rye plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
