Emblica palasis

Emblica palasis, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves and yellow-orange berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for centuries. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional and medicinal properties.

Its bark and leaves are also used in Ayurvedic remedies.
