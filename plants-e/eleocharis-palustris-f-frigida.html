Eleocharis palustris f. frigida

Eleocharis palustris f. frigida, commonly known as the frosty spike-rush, is a botanical variant of the common spike-rush.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions, typically found in wetlands and marshy areas. The plant features slender, grass-like stems that are often tinged with a pale blue or silver hue, giving it its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to colder climates and exhibits seasonal variations in growth patterns.
