Eryngium reitzii

Eryngium reitzii, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple bracts that resemble a thistle. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in suitable climates.

Eryngium reitzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique appearance.
