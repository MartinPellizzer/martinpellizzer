Echinocactus ghiesbrechtii

Echinocactus ghiesbrechtii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
