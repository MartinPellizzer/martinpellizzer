Eugenia manii

Eugenia manii, commonly known as the African cherry or wild mango, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by edible, yellow-orange fruits resembling small mangoes. The fruits are rich in nutrients and are often consumed fresh or used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
