Eugenia tinifolia

Eugenia tinifolia, commonly known as the velvetwood or black mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically dark purple to black when mature. Its bark is thick and deeply furrowed, providing a rugged appearance.

Eugenia tinifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in local medicine.
