Erythrodes oxyglossa

Erythrodes oxyglossa, commonly known as the red-tongued orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red and green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
