Eupatorium subpurpureum

Eupatorium subpurpureum, commonly known as purple boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Its showy flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
