Eugenia punctata

Eugenia punctata, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically round, yellow or red when ripe, and covered in a waxy coating. Eugenia punctata thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
