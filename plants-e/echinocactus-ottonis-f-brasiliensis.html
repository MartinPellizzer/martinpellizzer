Echinocactus ottonis f. brasiliensis

Echinocactus ottonis f. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brasiliensis Hedgehog Cactus, is a rare and striking plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a whitish coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is a sought-after species among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
