Eucalyptus ligulata subsp. stirlingica

Eucalyptus ligulata subsp.

stirlingica, commonly known as the Stirling's narrow-leafed mallet, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom.

The flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are usually pale or creamy white in color. It produces small, woody capsules that contain several seeds.

This species is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.
