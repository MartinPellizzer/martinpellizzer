Eryngium aristulatum subsp. aristulatum

Eryngium aristulatum subsp.

aristulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to western North America. It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple flower heads surrounded by pale green bracts.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
