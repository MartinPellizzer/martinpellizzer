Erica perhispida

Erica perhispida, commonly known as the dense heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, dense growth habit and its profuse flowering display in spring. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a yellowish or reddish tinge. Erica perhispida thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is a popular plant in horticulture due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
