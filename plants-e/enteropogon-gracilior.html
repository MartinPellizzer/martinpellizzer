Enteropogon gracilior

Enteropogon gracilior, commonly known as the slender false rice, is a grass species native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It produces small, pale yellow to brown flowers arranged in dense clusters.

Enteropogon gracilior is often used in traditional medicine and has potential applications in ecological restoration projects.
