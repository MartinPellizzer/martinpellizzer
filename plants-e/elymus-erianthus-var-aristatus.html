Elymus erianthus var. aristatus

Elymus erianthus var. aristatus, commonly known as the tufted wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces spikelets that are often adorned with silky, hairy bracts, enhancing its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky environments.

This species is valued for its erosion control capabilities and its use in landscaping and restoration projects.
