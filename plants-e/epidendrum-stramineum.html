Epidendrum stramineum

Epidendrum stramineum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It features long, slender stems that can grow up to several feet in length, often with a yellowish or greenish hue. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow, white, and green. Its leaves are usually elliptical and slightly hairy, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum stramineum is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.
