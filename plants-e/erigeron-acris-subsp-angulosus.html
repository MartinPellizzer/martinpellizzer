Erigeron acris subsp. angulosus

Erigeron acris subsp.

angulosus, commonly known as angular leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America. It is characterized by its angular, serrated leaves and daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in late summer.

The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. It has a slender, branching stem and produces numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This subspecies is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
