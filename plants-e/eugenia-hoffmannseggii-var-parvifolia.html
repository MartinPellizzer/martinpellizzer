Eugenia hoffmannseggii var. parvifolia

Eugenia hoffmannseggii var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved velvetwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in traditional medicine.
