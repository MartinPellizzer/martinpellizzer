Eugenia variifolia

Eugenia variifolia, commonly known as the variable guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that vary in shape and size across the plant, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers followed by edible, aromatic fruits similar to guavas. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications in various regions.
