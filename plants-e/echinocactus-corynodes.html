Echinocactus corynodes

Echinocactus corynodes, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and prominent spines, which provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
