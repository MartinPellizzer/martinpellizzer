Eulophia hians var. nutans

Eulophia hians var. nutans, commonly known as the drooping eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces linear, grass-like leaves and bears racemes of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. Its flowers are characterized by their elongated, drooping petals and sepals, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded habitats and is often found in forest understories.
