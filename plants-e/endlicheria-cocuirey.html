Endlicheria cocuirey

Endlicheria cocuirey, commonly known as the Ecuadorian orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
