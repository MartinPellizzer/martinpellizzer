Eriocaulon pachypetalum

Eriocaulon pachypetalum, commonly known as the broad-petaled marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces lightweight seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its preference for wet environments, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
