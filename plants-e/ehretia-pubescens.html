Ehretia pubescens

Ehretia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ehretia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and elliptical, dark green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warmer regions.
