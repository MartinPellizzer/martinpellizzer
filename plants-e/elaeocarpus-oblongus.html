Elaeocarpus oblongus

Elaeocarpus oblongus, commonly known as the broadleaf elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The tree produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically eaten by birds and mammals, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm regions.
