Epilobium amurense

Epilobium amurense, commonly known as the Amur fireweed, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows and riverbanks. The plant features erect stems covered with glandular hairs and produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated margins.

Epilobium amurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
