Eulophia speciosa var. culveri

Eulophia speciosa var. culveri, commonly known as the Culver's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features erect, grass-like leaves and produces striking, fragrant, yellow-green flowers with a distinctive purple lip. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its pseudobulbs and sympodial growth pattern.

This variety is relatively rare and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts due to its unique floral structure and habitat specificity.
