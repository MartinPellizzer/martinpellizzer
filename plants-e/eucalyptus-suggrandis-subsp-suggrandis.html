Eucalyptus suggrandis subsp. suggrandis

Eucalyptus suggrandis subsp.

suggrandis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, rounded buds.

The plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, often forming dense, compact clusters. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is commonly found in coastal and inland regions.

The species is valued for its aromatic oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
