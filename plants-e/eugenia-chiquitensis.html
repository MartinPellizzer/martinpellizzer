Eugenia chiquitensis

Eugenia chiquitensis, commonly known as the Chiquitania myrtle, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Bolivia and Paraguay.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in warm climates.

Eugenia chiquitensis also plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
