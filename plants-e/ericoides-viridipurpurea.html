Ericoides viridipurpurea

Ericoides viridipurpurea, commonly known as the purple-green heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the cool, moist forests of southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, leathery leaves that are typically a mix of green and purple hues. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade.

Ericoides viridipurpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground cover.
