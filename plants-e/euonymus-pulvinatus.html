Euonymus pulvinatus

Euonymus pulvinatus, commonly known as the smooth euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not managed properly.
