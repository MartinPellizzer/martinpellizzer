Engelhardia roxburghiana

Engelhardia roxburghiana, commonly known as the Chinese sandalwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its aromatic wood, it has been historically valued for its use in traditional medicine and craftsmanship.
