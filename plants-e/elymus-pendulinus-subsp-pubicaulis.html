Elymus pendulinus subsp. pubicaulis

Elymus pendulinus subsp.

pubicaulis, commonly known as the hairy beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, hairy inflorescences that give it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insect species.
