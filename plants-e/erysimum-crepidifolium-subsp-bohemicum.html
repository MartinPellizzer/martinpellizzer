Erysimum crepidifolium subsp. bohemicum

Erysimum crepidifolium subsp.

bohemicum, commonly known as Bohemian wallflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Europe. It features basal rosettes of narrow, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to biodiversity in its habitat.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different leaf morphology compared to other Erysimum species.
