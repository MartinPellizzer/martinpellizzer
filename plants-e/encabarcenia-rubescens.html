Encabarcenia rubescens

Encabarcenia rubescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with a reddish hue, especially under sunlight. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the dry season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a typical adaptation to minimize water loss in its harsh environment.

Encabarcenia rubescens is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
