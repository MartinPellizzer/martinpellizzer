Elaeocarpus brigittae

Elaeocarpus brigittae, commonly known as the Brigitte's elaeocarp, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
