Eulophia angustilabris

Eulophia angustilabris, commonly known as the narrow-lipped eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a rhizome and pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia angustilabris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
