Euphorbia heyneana subsp. galioides

Euphorbia heyneana subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or herb, often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It is adapted to dry conditions and has a milky sap that can be toxic.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticulture.
