Euphorbia hortensis

Euphorbia hortensis, commonly known as the garden spurge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a distinctive, branched stem that is often covered in a waxy, greenish coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Euphorbia hortensis is also characterized by its milky sap, which can be irritant to the skin.
