Echinochloa crus-galli var. frumentacea

Echinochloa crus-galli var. frumentacea, commonly known as common barnyardgrass, is a robust annual grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit, narrow leaves, and spikelets that are often reddish in color. This plant is commonly found in agricultural fields and can become a significant weed in cereal crops. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread rapidly.

Its botanical features make it both a challenging weed and a valuable forage plant in certain regions.
