Eulophia montis-elgonis

Eulophia montis-elgonis, commonly known as the Mount Elgon orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa, particularly around Mount Elgon.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and dense clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forest understories and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, waxy coating.

Eulophia montis-elgonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
