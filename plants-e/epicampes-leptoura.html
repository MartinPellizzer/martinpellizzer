Epicampes leptoura

Epicampes leptoura, commonly known as the slender-leafed orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and swampy areas. Its flowers are usually white or pale green, with a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinator populations.
