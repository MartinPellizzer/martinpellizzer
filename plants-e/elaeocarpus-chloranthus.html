Elaeocarpus chloranthus

Elaeocarpus chloranthus, commonly known as the white-flowered satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and timber.

The species is also notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
