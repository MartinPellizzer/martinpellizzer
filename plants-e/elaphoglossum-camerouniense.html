Elaphoglossum camerouniense

Elaphoglossum camerouniense, commonly known as the Cameroon holly fern, is a species of fern native to Cameroon and neighboring regions in Central Africa.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest understories, where it thrives in humid and temperate conditions. Its spore-producing structures, called sorangia, are located on the underside of the fronds, a common feature in ferns.

Elaphoglossum camerouniense is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
