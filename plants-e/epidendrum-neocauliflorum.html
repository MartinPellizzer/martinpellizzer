Epidendrum neocauliflorum

Epidendrum neocauliflorum, commonly known as the Neocauliflorum Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, long-lasting inflorescences that bear numerous fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, often with a waxy coating to retain moisture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
