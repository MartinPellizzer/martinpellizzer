Eremurus stenophyllus

Eremurus stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goat's beard, is a striking perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescence composed of numerous narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
