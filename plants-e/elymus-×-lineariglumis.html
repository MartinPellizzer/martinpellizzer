Elymus × lineariglumis

Elymus × lineariglumis, commonly known as the hybrid wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as meadows and disturbed sites.

Elymus × lineariglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
