Erica caprina

Erica caprina, commonly known as the Alpine heath, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica caprina thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it contributes to the unique flora of mountain ecosystems.
