Euphorbia faurotii

Euphorbia faurotii, commonly known as the desert rose or the red torch, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It features a distinctive, branched stem that often produces vibrant red or orange flowers in the spring. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its flowers are surrounded by a unique, cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Euphorbia faurotii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
