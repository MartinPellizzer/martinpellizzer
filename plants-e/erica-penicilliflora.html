Erica penicilliflora

Erica penicilliflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple, and it produces small, leathery seeds.

Erica penicilliflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
