Erica × simulata

Erica × simulata, commonly known as the Heath Erica, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the western regions of the United States, particularly California and Oregon. This evergreen shrub typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions. Its small, needle-like leaves are densely packed, giving it a compact, bushy appearance.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.
