Echinocactus flexispinus

Echinocactus flexispinus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant has a ribbed structure that allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to arid environments. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and birds.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
