Epilobium adenocaulon f. apricum

Epilobium adenocaulon f. apricum, commonly known as the spring willow herb, is a flowering plant belonging to the evening primrose family (Onagraceae).

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, temperate environments, such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features slender, willow-like stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish-orange, and clustered in elongated racemes, blooming in early spring.

The species is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
