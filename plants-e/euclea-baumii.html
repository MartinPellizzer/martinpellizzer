Euclea baumii

Euclea baumii, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, spherical clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in winter. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Euclea baumii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
