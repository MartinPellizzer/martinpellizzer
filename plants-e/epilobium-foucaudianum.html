Epilobium foucaudianum

Epilobium foucaudianum, commonly known as the Foucaud's willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as grasslands and riverbanks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in spikes, and bloom during the spring and summer seasons.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
