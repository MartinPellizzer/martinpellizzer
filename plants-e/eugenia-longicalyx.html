Eugenia longicalyx

Eugenia longicalyx, commonly known as the long-leaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitats.
