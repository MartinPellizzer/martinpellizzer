Escobaria organensis

Escobaria organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains liveforever, is a succulent plant native to the Chihuahuan Desert in New Mexico and Texas.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has thick, water-storing leaves that help it survive in arid environments. It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Escobaria organensis is a low-growing shrub that plays a role in stabilizing desert soils and providing habitat for small desert animals.
