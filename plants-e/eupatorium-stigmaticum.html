Eupatorium stigmaticum

Eupatorium stigmaticum, commonly known as the showy eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features tall, slender stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its showy, pale blue to purple flower heads appear in late summer to early fall, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.

Eupatorium stigmaticum is often found in moist, woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
