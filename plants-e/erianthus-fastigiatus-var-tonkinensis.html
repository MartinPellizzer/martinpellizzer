Erianthus fastigiatus var. tonkinensis

Erianthus fastigiatus var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin bamboo, is a striking ornamental grass native to Southeast Asia.

It features tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 6 meters, with a dense, upright growth habit. The leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its visual impact.

This variety is valued for its ability to provide structure and texture in garden settings.
