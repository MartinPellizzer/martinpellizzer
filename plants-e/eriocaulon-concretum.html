Eriocaulon concretum

Eriocaulon concretum, commonly known as the rock fern, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in rocky or forested areas. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Eriocaulon concretum is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and its role in maintaining ecological balance in its native habitats.
