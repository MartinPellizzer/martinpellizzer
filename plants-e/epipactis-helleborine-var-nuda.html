Epipactis helleborine var. nuda

Epipactis helleborine var. nuda, commonly known as the naked helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a terrestrial plant that typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and meadows. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and produces a single, erect stem bearing a single, greenish-yellow to brownish flower with a distinctive lip. Its leaves are broad, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

This orchid is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
