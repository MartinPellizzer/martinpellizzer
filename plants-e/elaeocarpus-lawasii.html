Elaeocarpus lawasii

Elaeocarpus lawasii, commonly known as the Lawas elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Borneo.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, lowland forest environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
