Erythrorchis ochobiensis

Erythrorchis ochobiensis, commonly known as the Ochobiensis orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique floral structure, featuring a spurred lip and showy petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its flowers are often brightly colored, with a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow, which attract specific pollinators.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
