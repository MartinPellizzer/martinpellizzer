Euphorbia dracunculoides var. faurei

Euphorbia dracunculoides var. faurei, commonly known as the Cape Hottentot, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem system with cylindrical, greenish-gray stems that are covered in small, white tubercles. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flowers typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
