Elytropappus glandulosus

Elytropappus glandulosus, commonly known as the glandular leaf sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the sunflower family.

Elytropappus glandulosus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
