Erythroxylum iwahigense

Erythroxylum iwahigense, commonly known as the Iwahig huito, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and elliptical leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in lowland forests.

The species is notable for its potential ecological importance and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
