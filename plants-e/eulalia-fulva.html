Eulalia fulva

Eulalia fulva, commonly known as the red bottlebrush, is a striking ornamental plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its dense, crimson-colored inflorescences that resemble bottle brushes. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.

Eulalia fulva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
