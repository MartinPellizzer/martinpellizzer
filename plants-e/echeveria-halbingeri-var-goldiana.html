Echeveria halbingeri var. goldiana

Echeveria halbingeri var. goldiana, commonly known as the Gold Dust Echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features compact rosettes of fleshy, silver-green leaves with a powdery, golden coating that gives it its distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and may curl slightly at the tips. It produces showy, pinkish-purple flower spikes in the spring, which can reach several inches in height.

This variety is prized for its unique coloration and is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant.
