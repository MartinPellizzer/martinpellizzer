Eria gracilicaulis

Eria gracilicaulis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed eria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its slender stems and lance-shaped leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Eria gracilicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
