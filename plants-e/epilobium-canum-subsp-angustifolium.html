Epilobium canum subsp. angustifolium

Epilobium canum subsp.

angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks, wet meadows, and roadside ditches.

The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height, and its leaves are narrowly elliptic with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to reddish-purple, arranged in spike-like clusters, and bloom from June to August.

The plant is often found in wet or semi-aquatic environments and is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils.
