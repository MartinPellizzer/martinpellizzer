Erucaria pinnata var. major

Erucaria pinnata var. major, commonly known as the giant erucaria, is a woody shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and often have a rough, serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wetland environments and its role in supporting local wildlife.
