Emilia pseudactis

Emilia pseudactis, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and the plant is known for its aromatic foliage.

Emilia pseudactis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
