Elaeagnus lanceolata var. rubescens

Elaeagnus lanceolata var. rubescens, commonly known as the redleaf silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, silvery-green leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are dark red when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant for gardens.

Its autumn foliage provides significant visual interest, contributing to its popularity in landscaping.
