Enkianthus hirtinervus

Enkianthus hirtinervus, commonly known as the hairy-veined enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, appearing in late spring. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered with fine, hairy glands, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to add color to woodland gardens.
