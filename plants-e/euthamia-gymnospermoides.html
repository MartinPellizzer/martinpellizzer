Euthamia gymnospermoides

Euthamia gymnospermoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright, slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as meadows, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Euthamia gymnospermoides plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
