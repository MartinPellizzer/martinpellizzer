Eugenia weberbaueri

Eugenia weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and elliptical, glossy leaves. It produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe.

Eugenia weberbaueri is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.
