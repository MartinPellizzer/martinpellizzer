Elaeagia pastoensis f. acuminata

Elaeagia pastoensis f. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata form of the Pasto elaeagia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Elaeaginaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by orange-red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
