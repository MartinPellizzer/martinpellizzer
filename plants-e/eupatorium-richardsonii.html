Eupatorium richardsonii

Eupatorium richardsonii, commonly known as stiff goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium richardsonii plays an important role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
