Echinops saissanicus

Echinops saissanicus, commonly known as the Saharan thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a sea urchin, which give it its scientific name. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, helping to reduce water loss.

Echinops saissanicus is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
