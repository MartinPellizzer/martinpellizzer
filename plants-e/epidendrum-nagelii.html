Epidendrum nagelii

Epidendrum nagelii, commonly known as the Nagel's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically produces pendulous inflorescences with numerous small, showy blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually elliptical and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

Epidendrum nagelii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced orchid growers.
