Eucalyptus conoidea var. marginata

Eucalyptus conoidea var. marginata, commonly known as the marginated mallees, is a shrub or small tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or pink at the edges. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are typically followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
