Eragrostis rufescens var. spiciramea

Eragrostis rufescens var. spiciramea, commonly known as the redgrass or red oatgrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and reddish tinge in its foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are often arranged in dense clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
