Echinocactus latispinus

Echinocactus latispinus, commonly known as the broad-spined cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in thick, ribbed structures. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its spines are thick and can be quite long, providing protection from herbivores.
