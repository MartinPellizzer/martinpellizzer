Eugenia brachypoda

Eugenia brachypoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Eugenia brachypoda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
