Eriogonum lonchophyllum var. intermontanum

Eriogonum lonchophyllum var. intermontanum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces hairy, ovate leaves that are often glandular and have a distinctive, aromatic scent. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for native pollinators.
