Euphorbiodendron helenae

Euphorbiodendron helenae, commonly known as Helen's euphorb, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow and green, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
