Erodium chium var. albiflorum

Erodium chium var. albiflorum, commonly known as white-flowered bedstraw, is a flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, creeping stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This variety is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
