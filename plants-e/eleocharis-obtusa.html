Eleocharis obtusa

Eleocharis obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved spike-rush, is a species of sedge native to wetlands and marshy areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of its slender, grass-like stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are broad and flat, with a rough texture. Eleocharis obtusa produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its rhizomes help in the formation of dense vegetation, which supports various aquatic life forms.
