Eucalyptus moorei subsp. moorei

Eucalyptus moorei subsp.

moorei, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves that are narrow and often have a slightly wavy margin.

The plant produces small, spherical flower buds that range in color from white to pink, which are typically clustered in groups of three. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant shrub that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
