Eragrostis vatovae

Eragrostis vatovae, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable component of Madagascar's unique flora.
