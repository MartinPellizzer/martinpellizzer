Embelia kilimandscharica

Embelia kilimandscharica, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro embelia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, oval-shaped leaves. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is adapted to the cool, moist climates of its mountainous habitat.
