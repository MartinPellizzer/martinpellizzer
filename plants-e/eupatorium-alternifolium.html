Eupatorium alternifolium

Eupatorium alternifolium, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium alternifolium is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and attracts pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
