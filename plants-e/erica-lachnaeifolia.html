Erica lachnaeifolia

Erica lachnaeifolia, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a species of evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

Erica lachnaeifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
