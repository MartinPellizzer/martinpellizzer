Eryngium bornmuelleri

Eryngium bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Blue Heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators.

Eryngium bornmuelleri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
