Eupatorium melanadenium

Eupatorium melanadenium, commonly known as blackstem thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough textures, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eupatorium melanadenium is often found in the eastern United States and is considered a native wildflower.
