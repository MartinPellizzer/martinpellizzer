Erithalis odorifera

Erithalis odorifera, commonly known as the sweet myrtle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its aromatic properties.
