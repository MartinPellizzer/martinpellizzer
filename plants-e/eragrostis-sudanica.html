Eragrostis sudanica

Eragrostis sudanica, commonly known as Sudan grass, is a perennial grass native to Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in warm climates, making it suitable for forage and soil stabilization.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is widely cultivated for its high nutritional value for livestock.
