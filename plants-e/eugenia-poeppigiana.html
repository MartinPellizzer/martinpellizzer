Eugenia poeppigiana

Eugenia poeppigiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *cereja do Brasil*, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The tree produces edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are rich in vitamin C. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
