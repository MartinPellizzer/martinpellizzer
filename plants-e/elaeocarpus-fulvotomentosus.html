Elaeocarpus fulvotomentosus

Elaeocarpus fulvotomentosus, commonly known as the golden-haired elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its bark is often covered in a dense, yellowish pubescence, which gives it its common name.

It is typically found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
