Elyleymus colvillensis

Elyleymus colvillensis, commonly known as the Colvill's wild rye, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Elyleymus colvillensis plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
