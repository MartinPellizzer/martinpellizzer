Eupatorium surutatoanum

Eupatorium surutatoanum, commonly known as the Malabar boneset, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of India and Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in marshy or swampy areas, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Eupatorium surutatoanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
