Euphorbia terracina var. latifolia

Euphorbia terracina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branched stems covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are broad, ovate, and have a waxy texture with a distinctive bluish-green color. It produces clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flower structures.

This plant is known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation and is used in traditional medicine.
