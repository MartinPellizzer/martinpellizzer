Ericoides sacciflorum

Ericoides sacciflorum, commonly known as the bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its unique feature is the inflated, bladder-like structure at the base of its flowers, which gives it its common name.

Ericoides sacciflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
