Erythraea spicata f. albiflora

Erythraea spicata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered erythraea, is a rare horticultural form of the erythraea spicata species.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers, which contrast sharply with the typical red or pink blooms of the species. The plant is a shrub native to Southeast Asia, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. It has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and produces small, clustered inflorescences.
