Eulophia hereroensis

Eulophia hereroensis, commonly known as the Herero orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Namibia and southern Africa.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy flowers. The plant typically produces clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish-white blooms, often with a subtle fragrance. Its roots are fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Eulophia hereroensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
