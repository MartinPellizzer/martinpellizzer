Euphorbia rossii

Euphorbia rossii, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features striking, bright orange bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem covered in soft, spiny leaves that help reduce water loss. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its toxic sap, it requires careful handling to avoid skin irritation.
