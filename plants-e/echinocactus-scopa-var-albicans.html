Echinocactus scopa var. albicans

Echinocactus scopa var. albicans, commonly known as the white-flowered hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a pale or white bloom. The plant produces large, showy white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
