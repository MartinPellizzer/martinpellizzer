Eupatorium lindheimerianum

Eupatorium lindheimerianum, commonly known as Lindheimer's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is often found in swampy or wetland habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall.

Eupatorium lindheimerianum is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
