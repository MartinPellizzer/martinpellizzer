Eugenia vaga var. brachypoda

Eugenia vaga var. brachypoda, commonly known as the short-stemmed eugenia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
