Elaeagnus tonkinensis var. grandifolia

Elaeagnus tonkinensis var. grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and fragrant, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
