Echinacea heterophylla

Echinacea heterophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf coneflower, is a perennial herb native to the central and southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple to pinkish-red conical flower head, which sits atop a greenish-branched stem. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Echinacea heterophylla thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairie and woodland habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional use in herbal medicine.
