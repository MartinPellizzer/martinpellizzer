Ephedra Vulgaris Var. Arborea

Ephedra vulgaris var. arborea is a unique species of gymnosperm known for its distinctive tree-like structure. Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in harsh environments with minimal water. This plant has been historically used in traditional medicine for its stimulant properties. Its stems contain ephedrine, a compound with both medicinal and controversial applications. Despite its ecological significance, it remains a subject of scientific interest and conservation concern.
