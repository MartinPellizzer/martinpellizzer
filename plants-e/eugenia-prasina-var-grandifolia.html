Eugenia prasina var. grandifolia

Eugenia prasina var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved clove, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often a deep green and can grow up to 15 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, sweet-tasting berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
