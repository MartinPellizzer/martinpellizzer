Eulophia grandiflora

Eulophia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered eulophia, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant has broad, strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Eulophia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
