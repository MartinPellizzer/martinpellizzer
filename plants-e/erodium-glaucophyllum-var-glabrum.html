Erodium glaucophyllum var. glabrum

Erodium glaucophyllum var. glabrum, commonly known as the glabrous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern California.

It is an annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, round seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Fabaceae family.
