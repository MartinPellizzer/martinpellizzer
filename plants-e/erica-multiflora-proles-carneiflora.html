Erica multiflora proles carneiflora

Erica multiflora proles carneiflora, commonly known as the red-flowered heath, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, vibrant red flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Erica multiflora proles carneiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant.
