Epidendrum adenocarpon

Epidendrum adenocarpon, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers and strap-like leaves. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, utilizing its aerial roots to absorb moisture and nutrients from the environment. Its flowering period usually occurs during the rainy season, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Epidendrum adenocarpon is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
