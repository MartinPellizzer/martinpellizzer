Echinops kermanshahanicus var. papillosus

Echinops kermanshahanicus var. papillosus, commonly known as the Kermanshah thistle, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Iran.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is notable for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
