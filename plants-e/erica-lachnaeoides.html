Erica lachnaeoides

Erica lachnaeoides, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. The plant is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers during the summer months.

Erica lachnaeoides thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
