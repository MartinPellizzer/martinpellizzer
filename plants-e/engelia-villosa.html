Engelia villosa

Engelia villosa, commonly known as the woolly engelia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial shrub that can reach heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Engelia villosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
