Echinocactus sanguiniflorus

Echinocactus sanguiniflorus, commonly known as the bloodflower cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces striking red flowers that bloom in summer, adding a dramatic visual element to its appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

The plant is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
