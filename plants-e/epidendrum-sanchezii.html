Epidendrum sanchezii

Epidendrum sanchezii, commonly known as the Sanchez's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring or early summer, producing pink to purple blossoms that are highly attractive to pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This orchid is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
