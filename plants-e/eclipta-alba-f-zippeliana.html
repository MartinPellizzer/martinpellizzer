Eclipta alba f. zippeliana

Eclipta alba f. zippeliana, commonly known as Zippeliana false dandelion, is a botanical variant of the common eclipta alba.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its small, yellow composite flowers and linear, hairy leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. Its botanical features include a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
