Erysimum yokoscenyse

Erysimum yokoscenyse, commonly known as the Yokosuka Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
