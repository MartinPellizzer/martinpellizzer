Epidendrum whittenii

Epidendrum whittenii, commonly known as the Whitten's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a striking display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its elegance and resilience.
