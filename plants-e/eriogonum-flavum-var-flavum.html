Eriogonum flavum var. flavum

Eriogonum flavum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, globular seed pods.

Eriogonum flavum var. flavum is an important nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
