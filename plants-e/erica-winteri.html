Erica winteri

Erica winteri, commonly known as the Winter's heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erica winteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
