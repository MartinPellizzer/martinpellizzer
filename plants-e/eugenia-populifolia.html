Eugenia populifolia

Eugenia populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often glossy and have a prominent midrib. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or pale pink and appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are used in traditional medicine and local cuisine.
