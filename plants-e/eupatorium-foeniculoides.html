Eupatorium foeniculoides

Eupatorium foeniculoides, commonly known as sweet fern or false ginseng, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features feathery, fern-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological studies and conservation efforts.
