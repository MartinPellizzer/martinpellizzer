Elaphoglossum plumosum var. bradii

Elaphoglossum plumosum var. bradii, commonly known as the Bradi's hairy maidenhair fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant features slender, arching fronds with finely divided, silky-hairy leaves that give it a soft, lace-like appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and is of interest to botanists and fern enthusiasts.
