Euphorbia melanohydrata

Euphorbia melanohydrata, commonly known as the black-veined spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive appearance with dark green, fleshy leaves that have prominent, almost black veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are the modified flowers typical of the Euphorbia genus. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
