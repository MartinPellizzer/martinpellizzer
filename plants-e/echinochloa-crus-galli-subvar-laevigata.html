Echinochloa crus-galli subvar. laevigata

Echinochloa crus-galli subvar. laevigata, commonly known as cocklebur grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads that are often wind-dispersed. The plant is highly adaptable and can thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions. It is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides, fields, and along waterways.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
