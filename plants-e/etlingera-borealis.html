Etlingera borealis

Etlingera borealis, commonly known as the Arctic ginger, is a perennial herb native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces small, white to yellowish flowers arranged in spikes, and its leaves are broad and grass-like. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in tundra and alpine regions.

The plant is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.
