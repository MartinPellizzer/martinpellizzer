Eucalyptus viridis var. ovata

Eucalyptus viridis var. ovata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands.

The species is notable for its aromatic oils, which are used in various traditional and modern applications.
