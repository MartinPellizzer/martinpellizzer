Eremophila occidens

Eremophila occidens, commonly known as the Western Emu Bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, demonstrating drought tolerance and efficient water use. It typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Eremophila occidens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping in regions with suitable climates.
