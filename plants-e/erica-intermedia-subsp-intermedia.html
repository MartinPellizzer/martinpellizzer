Erica intermedia subsp. intermedia

Erica intermedia subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western and southern regions of the United States. It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Its leaves are needle-like and evergreen, providing year-round interest in the landscape. Erica intermedia subsp.

intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
