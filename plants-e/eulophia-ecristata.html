Eulophia ecristata

Eulophia ecristata, commonly known as the slender eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with erect, hairy stems and linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette. The plant produces inflorescences bearing small, greenish-white flowers that are often fragrant. Eulophia ecristata typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and swamps.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
