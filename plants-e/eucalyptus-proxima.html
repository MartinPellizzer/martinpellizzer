Eucalyptus proxima

Eucalyptus proxima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or tree form, with smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin strips. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a silvery underside, and are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions. It produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in spring.

The plant is well-suited to drought and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
