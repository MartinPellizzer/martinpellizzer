Eriocaulon polytrichoides

Eriocaulon polytrichoides, commonly known as the woolly spikegrass, is a small perennial herb belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale green flowers, which are typically short-lived. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Eriocaulon polytrichoides plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
