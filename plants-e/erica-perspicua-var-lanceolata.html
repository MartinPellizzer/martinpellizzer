Erica perspicua var. lanceolata

Erica perspicua var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heaths and heathers, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its lance-shaped leaves are a distinguishing feature, and the flowers can range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety.

Erica perspicua var. lanceolata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
