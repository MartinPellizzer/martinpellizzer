Erigeron glabellus f. roseata

Erigeron glabellus f. roseata, commonly known as the roseate blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and swamps, and it features narrow, grass-like leaves and pale blue to pinkish flowers. Its botanical structure includes a basal rosette of leaves and a flowering stem that rises above the foliage.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
