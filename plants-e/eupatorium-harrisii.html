Eupatorium harrisii

Eupatorium harrisii, commonly known as Harris' boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Harris' boneset is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
