Eria choneana

Eria choneana, commonly known as the Chonean orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern, facilitating efficient water absorption.

Eria choneana blooms with small, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
