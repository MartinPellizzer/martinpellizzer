Eugenia friedrichsthalii

Eugenia friedrichsthalii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or wild cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Eugenia friedrichsthalii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
