Echinolaena nemorosa

Echinolaena nemorosa, commonly known as the slender burrgrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss in arid environments.

Echinolaena nemorosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and along roadsides.
