Enneapogon pusillus

Enneapogon pusillus, commonly known as the dwarf lovegrass, is a small annual grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, typically reaching heights of less than 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in restoration projects or as a forage plant for livestock.
