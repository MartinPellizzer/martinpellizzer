Elymus tarbagataicus

Elymus tarbagataicus, commonly known as the Tarbagatai grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets.

Elymus tarbagataicus is adapted to arid conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
