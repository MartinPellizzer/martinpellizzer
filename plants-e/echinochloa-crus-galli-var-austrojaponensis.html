Echinochloa crus-galli var. austrojaponensis

Echinochloa crus-galli var. austrojaponensis, commonly known as Japanese barnyardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in rice paddies and other low-lying areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.

Botanically, it is notable for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
