Eugenia brasiliensis var. erythrocarpa

Eugenia brasiliensis var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or erythrocarpa guava, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
