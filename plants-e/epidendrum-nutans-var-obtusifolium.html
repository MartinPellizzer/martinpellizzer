Epidendrum nutans var. obtusifolium

Epidendrum nutans var. obtusifolium, commonly known as the drooping epidendrum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, dark green leaves and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and other structures in its natural habitat. It prefers warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its flowers bloom in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
