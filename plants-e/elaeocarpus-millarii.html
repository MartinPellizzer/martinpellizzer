Elaeocarpus millarii

Elaeocarpus millarii, commonly known as the millarii elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and produces small, spherical fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
