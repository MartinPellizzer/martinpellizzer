Endomallus pellitus

Endomallus pellitus, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in a spike. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
