Elymus sinicus

Elymus sinicus, commonly known as Chinese wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fibrous root system. The plant produces spikelets that are typically 3 to 5 centimeters long, with lemmas that are hairy and awned. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Elymus sinicus is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a forage crop for livestock.
