Ericoides thymifolium

Ericoides thymifolium, commonly known as the thyme-leaved ericoid, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that resemble those of thyme. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
