Eugenia mosquitensis

Eugenia mosquitensis, commonly known as the Mosquito bush or Mosquito berry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fruit, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
