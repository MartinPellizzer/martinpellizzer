Enochoria

Enochoria, commonly known as the "mountain thyme," is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as mint and sage. The plant features small, pink or purple flowers and fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Enochoria thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its essential oils contain compounds with antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
