Epilobium speciosum

Epilobium speciosum, commonly known as showy willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides.

Epilobium speciosum is an important plant in ecological systems, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various insects.
