Echinops fastigiatus

Echinops fastigiatus, commonly known as the spike echinops, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright, spiky appearance with dense clusters of blue or purple flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, spiny bracts that give it a distinctive look. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Echinops fastigiatus is known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
