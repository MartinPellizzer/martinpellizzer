Eriocaulon juniperinum

Eriocaulon juniperinum, commonly known as the Juniper moss, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are tiny and clustered, often appearing in spike-like inflorescences, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms.

Eriocaulon juniperinum is an important component of freshwater ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and ecological balance.
