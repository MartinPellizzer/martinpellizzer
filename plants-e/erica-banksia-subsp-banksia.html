Erica banksia subsp. banksia

Erica banksia subsp.

banksia, commonly known as the coastal banksia, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense, cylindrical inflorescences that produce abundant yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands. Its flowers are a key food source for nectar-feeding birds and insects.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to coastal conditions and its ecological importance in native landscapes.
