Eritrichium canum var. spathulatum

Eritrichium canum var. spathulatum, commonly known as the broadleaf mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features basal rosettes of broad, spoon-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowers are typically arranged in racemes and are visited by pollinators such as bees.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
