Eriogonum flavum var. incisum

Eriogonum flavum var. incisum, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellow flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, spherical seed pods that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
