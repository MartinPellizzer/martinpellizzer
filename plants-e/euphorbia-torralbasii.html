Euphorbia torralbasii

Euphorbia torralbasii, commonly known as the Torralbasia spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, waxy leaves. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
