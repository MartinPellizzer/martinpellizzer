Eupatorium lindleyanum var. lindleyanum

Eupatorium lindleyanum var. lindleyanum, commonly known as the showy eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and streambanks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The showy eupatorium is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
