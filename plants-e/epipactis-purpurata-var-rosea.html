Epipactis purpurata var. rosea

Epipactis purpurata var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered helleborine, is a perennial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, humusy soil.

The orchid is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its subtle, non-fragrant blooms.
