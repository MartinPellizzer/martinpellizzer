Eragrostis pseudosclerantha

Eragrostis pseudosclerantha, commonly known as the slender false bluestem, is a grass species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eragrostis pseudosclerantha is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
