Eragrostis capillifolia

Eragrostis capillifolia, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, hair-like leaves that give it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue-green in color, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for native plant gardens.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for erosion control and landscaping.
