Eugenia legrandii

Eugenia legrandii, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or legrand's eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible red berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
