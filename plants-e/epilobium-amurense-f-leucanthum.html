Epilobium amurense f. leucanthum

Epilobium amurense f. leucanthum, commonly known as the white-flowered willow herb, is a botanical variant of the willow herb species.

It is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Russia. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months.

It is often found in moist, open habitats such as riverbanks and meadows.
