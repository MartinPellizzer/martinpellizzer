Eucalyptus loxophleba subsp. gratiae

Eucalyptus loxophleba subsp.

gratiae, commonly known as the Grace's malleable gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its smooth, often peeling bark that reveals a mottled pattern of green and white.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive blue-green hue with a whitish underside. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters, and are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and hardy nature.
