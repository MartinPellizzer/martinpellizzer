Erysimum cheiranthifolium

Erysimum cheiranthifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or inland areas with mild climates. The plant features basal leaves that are narrow and linear, while its stems are upright and hairy. Its flowers are bright yellow, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

It blooms from late spring to early summer and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
