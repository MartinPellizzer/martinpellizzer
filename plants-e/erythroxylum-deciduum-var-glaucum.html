Erythroxylum deciduum var. glaucum

Erythroxylum deciduum var. glaucum, commonly known as the glaucous evergreen.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae, native to the Andean regions of South America. The plant is characterized by its glaucous, waxy leaves that give it a distinctive blue-green appearance. It produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, and its fruit is a capsule containing fine, white seeds.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
