Erodium boissieri

Erodium boissieri, commonly known as the Mediterranean bedstraw, is a low-growing annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features small, pink to purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant typically thrives in disturbed soils and dry, sunny environments. It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind and animals.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects.
