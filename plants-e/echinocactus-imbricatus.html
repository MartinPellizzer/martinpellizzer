Echinocactus imbricatus

Echinocactus imbricatus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stem that resembles a barrel, and its dense covering of bright yellow bristles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing succulent that stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
