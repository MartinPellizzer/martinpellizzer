Eragrostis barteri

Eragrostis barteri, commonly known as barter's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color and a rough texture.

Eragrostis barteri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dryland restoration projects.
