Erigeron peregrinus var. dawsonii

Erigeron peregrinus var. dawsonii, commonly known as Dawson's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features slender stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs.

Its blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
