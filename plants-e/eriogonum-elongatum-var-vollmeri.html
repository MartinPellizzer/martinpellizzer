Eriogonum elongatum var. vollmeri

Eriogonum elongatum var. vollmeri, commonly known as the longstem buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, aiding in water conservation.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems.
