Euphrasia alpina var. humilis

Euphrasia alpina var. humilis, commonly known as the Alpine eyebright, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that produce small, yellowish-green flowers with prominent yellow spots. The plant typically blooms from late spring to early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
