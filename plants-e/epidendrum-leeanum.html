Epidendrum leeanum

Epidendrum leeanum, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, thick rhizome and a rosette of fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, often with a distinctive lip that resembles a slipper. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Epidendrum leeanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
