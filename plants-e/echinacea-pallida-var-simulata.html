Echinacea pallida var. simulata

Echinacea pallida var. simulata, commonly known as the pale coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to rose-colored petals and a prominent, spiky yellow center. The plant typically grows in prairie and open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces numerous seeds in its flower heads, which are dispersed by wind.

Echinacea pallida var. simulata is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional use in herbal medicine.
