Erythrodes calophylla

Erythrodes calophylla, commonly known as the red-leafed calophylla, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or crimson leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is known for its aromatic foliage. It typically grows in humid, lowland areas and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential pharmacological properties.
