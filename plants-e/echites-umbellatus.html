Echites umbellatus

Echites umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like arrangement of leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.

Echites umbellatus prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
