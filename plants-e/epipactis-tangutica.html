Epipactis tangutica

Epipactis tangutica, commonly known as the Tangut orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations. Its flowers are usually arranged in a spike and are pollinated by insects, particularly bees.

Epipactis tangutica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
