Epacris campanulata var. rubra

Epacris campanulata var. rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The variety rubra is distinguished by its reddish stems and vibrant pink to red floral bracts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
