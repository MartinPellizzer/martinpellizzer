Eurybia sibirica

Eurybia sibirica, commonly known as Siberian Aster, is a perennial flowering plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its showy blue to purple flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Eurybia sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
