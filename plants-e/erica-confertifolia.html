Erica confertifolia

Erica confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to the Cape region of South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are dense, narrow, and evergreen, contributing to its hardy and drought-resistant nature.

Erica confertifolia is well adapted to the Mediterranean climate, with a long growing season and low water requirements.
