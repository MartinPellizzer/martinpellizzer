Eurybia pulchra

Eurybia pulchra, commonly known as the showy golden-aster, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Eurybia pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
