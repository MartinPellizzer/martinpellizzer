Eupatorium saxatile

Eupatorium saxatile, commonly known as mountain boneset, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in woodland edges and open fields. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its small, white to pale blue flowers appear in clusters during the summer months.

The leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and have a strong, aromatic scent when crushed.
