Euphorbia cossoniana

Euphorbia cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to store water in its tissues.

Euphorbia cossoniana is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause skin irritation.
