Erigeron speciosus var. macranthus

Erigeron speciosus var. macranthus, commonly known as the showy fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its daisy-like appearance, with white to pale blue ray florets surrounding yellow disk florets. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils. It produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive blooms and ease of care.
