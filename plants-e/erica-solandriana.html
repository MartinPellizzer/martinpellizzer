Erica solandriana

Erica solandriana, commonly known as the coastal heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, evergreen shrub form. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Erica solandriana thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity and wind exposure.

It is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
