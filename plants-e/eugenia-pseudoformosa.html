Eugenia pseudoformosa

Eugenia pseudoformosa, commonly known as the purple-leafed eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often turn reddish or purplish in the autumn. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in many subtropical and tropical gardens.
