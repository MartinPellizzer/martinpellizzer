Eupatorium bruneri var. foliosum

Eupatorium bruneri var. foliosum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Eupatorium bruneri var. foliosum is a valuable plant for pollinators and is commonly used in native plant gardens.
