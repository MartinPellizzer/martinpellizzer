Erigeron hispidus var. pyrenaeus

Erigeron hispidus var. pyrenaeus, commonly known as the Pyrenean fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees region.

It is a perennial herb with hairy stems and basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces daisy-like flowers with white ray florets and yellow disk florets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
