Eucalyptopsis

Eucalyptopsis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a genus of flowering plants in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

These plants are native to Australia and are characterized by their slender, often multi-stemmed shrubs or small trees. They typically have narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in spikes. Eucalyptopsis species are adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Their ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
