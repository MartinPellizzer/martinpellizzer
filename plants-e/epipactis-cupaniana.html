Epipactis cupaniana

Epipactis cupaniana, commonly known as the cup-like helleborine, is a species of orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cup-shaped lip that is often adorned with intricate patterns and colors. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually greenish-yellow with purple or brown markings, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Epipactis cupaniana is a perennial herb that relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, showcasing its ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
