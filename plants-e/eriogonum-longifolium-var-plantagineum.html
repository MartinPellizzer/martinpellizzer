Eriogonum longifolium var. plantagineum

Eriogonum longifolium var. plantagineum, commonly known as the showy buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a member of the Polygonaceae family, known for its distinctive leaf veins and often edible seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
