Escallonia berteroana

Escallonia berteroana, commonly known as the Chilean firebush, is a shrub native to Chile and southern Peru.

It features attractive, glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is evergreen, with a compact, bushy growth habit that makes it suitable for hedges or ornamental use. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Escallonia berteroana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
