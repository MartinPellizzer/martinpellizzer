Erica floccifera

Erica floccifera, commonly known as the woolly heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Erica floccifera typically produces white or pale pink blooms, which are often covered in a fine, woolly texture.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
