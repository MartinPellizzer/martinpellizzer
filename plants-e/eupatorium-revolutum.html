Eupatorium revolutum

Eupatorium revolutum, commonly known as roughleaf boneset, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant features opposite, rough-hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its showy, white to pale purple flower clusters appear in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in ecological landscapes for its ornamental appeal and support to local wildlife.
