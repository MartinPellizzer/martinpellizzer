Eremopanax angustatus f. oblongus

Eremopanax angustatus f. oblongus, commonly known as the narrowleaf emerald fern, is a variant of the Eremopanax angustatus species, characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

This fern exhibits a delicate, lace-like foliage structure, with leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's rhizomes are fibrous and help it anchor to rocky or soil substrates.

Its unique morphology makes it a distinctive addition to botanical collections and horticultural displays.
