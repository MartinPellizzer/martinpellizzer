Eupatorium hondurense

Eupatorium hondurense, commonly known as the Honduras feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium hondurense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional medicinal uses in some regions.
