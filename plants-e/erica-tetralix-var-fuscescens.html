Erica tetralix var. fuscescens

Erica tetralix var. fuscescens, commonly known as the dark heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western regions of Europe.

It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments. The plant features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, contributing to its characteristic appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
