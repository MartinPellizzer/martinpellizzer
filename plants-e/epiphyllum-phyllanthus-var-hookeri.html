Epiphyllum phyllanthus var. hookeri

Epiphyllum phyllanthus var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing plant with fleshy, flat, leaf-like stems that are often used as a climbing structure. The plant produces large, fragrant, nocturnal flowers that are typically white or pale pink, opening at night. Its stems are covered in small, white spines, and it is well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming behavior.
