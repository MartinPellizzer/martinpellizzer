Eryngium palustre

Eryngium palustre, commonly known as marsh thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features striking blue to purple spiked flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has silver-gray, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed, aiding in its identification. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eryngium palustre plays an important ecological role by providing nectar and habitat for various wildlife species.
