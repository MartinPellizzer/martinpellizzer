Eriogonum angulosum var. viridescens

Eriogonum angulosum var. viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush habitats.

This variety is notable for its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions and is an important component of its ecosystem.
