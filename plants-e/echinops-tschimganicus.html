Echinops tschimganicus

Echinops tschimganicus, commonly known as the Tschimgan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, spherical flower heads that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant produces a large number of small, white flowers that bloom in summer, followed by the development of hard, spiny seeds. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture and are arranged in a rosette at the base.

Echinops tschimganicus is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
