Eremostachys salangensis

Eremostachys salangensis, commonly known as the Salang hill grass, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale blue to violet in color, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Eremostachys salangensis is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high elevations.
