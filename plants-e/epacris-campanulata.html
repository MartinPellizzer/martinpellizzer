Epacris campanulata

Epacris campanulata, commonly known as the bell heath, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry forests, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Epacris campanulata is valued in horticulture for its long blooming period and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
