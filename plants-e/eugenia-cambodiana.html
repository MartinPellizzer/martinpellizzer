Eugenia cambodiana

Eugenia cambodiana, commonly known as Cambodian guava, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and edible, yellow-orange berries. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately on the stems.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
