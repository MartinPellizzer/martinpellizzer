Erythrina tahitensis

Erythrina tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five leaflets, and its structure is typical of the legume family, Fabaceae. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Erythrina tahitensis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
