Erigeron nanus subsp. neglectus

Erigeron nanus subsp.

neglectus, commonly known as the small fleabane, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America. It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and features a compact rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry meadows, prairies, and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
