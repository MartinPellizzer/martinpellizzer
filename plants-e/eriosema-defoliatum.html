Eriosema defoliatum

Eriosema defoliatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf eriosema, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are often heart-shaped and have a soft, fuzzy texture.

Eriosema defoliatum is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or woodland habitats.
