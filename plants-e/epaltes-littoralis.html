Epaltes littoralis

Epaltes littoralis, commonly known as the coastal epaltes, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or muddy soils along shorelines. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often arranged in a rosette pattern near the base.

Epaltes littoralis is adapted to salt spray and fluctuating water levels, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.
