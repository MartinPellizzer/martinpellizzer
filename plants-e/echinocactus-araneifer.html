Echinocactus araneifer

Echinocactus araneifer, commonly known as the spider-shaped cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in sharp, spine-like structures that provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently from the sparse rainfall in its habitat.

Echinocactus araneifer is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and resilience in dry conditions.
