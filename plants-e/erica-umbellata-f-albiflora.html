Erica umbellata f. albiflora

Erica umbellata f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, often in dense clusters at the ends of the branches. Its foliage consists of narrow, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen.

Erica umbellata f. albiflora is well adapted to acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
