Erianthus teretifolius

Erianthus teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like flower clusters that are often purple or pink in hue. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Erianthus teretifolius plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
