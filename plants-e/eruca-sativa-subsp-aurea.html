Eruca sativa subsp. aurea

Eruca sativa subsp.

aurea, commonly known as golden rapeseed, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnately compound leaves.

Native to the Mediterranean region, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, elongated pods containing seeds that are often used in traditional agriculture.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
