Erianthus pungens

Erianthus pungens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinted leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are clustered in panicles.

Erianthus pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
