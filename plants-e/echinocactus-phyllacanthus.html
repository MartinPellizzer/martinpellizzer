Echinocactus phyllacanthus

Echinocactus phyllacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican star cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are pollinated by insects. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
