Eleutherococcus trifoliatus var. integerrimus

Eleutherococcus trifoliatus var. integerrimus, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, typically composed of three to five oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its adaptability and medicinal properties, it is cultivated for use in traditional medicine and as a hardy ornamental plant.
