Elymus pungens subsp. pycnanthus

Elymus pungens subsp.

pycnanthus, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
