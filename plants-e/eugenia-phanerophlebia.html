Eugenia phanerophlebia

Eugenia phanerophlebia, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Eugenia phanerophlebia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
