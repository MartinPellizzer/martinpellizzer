Erophila rhomboidea

Erophila rhomboidea, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has lanceolate to narrowly elliptic leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. Its small, clustered purple flowers bloom in late summer and attract various pollinators.

Erophila rhomboidea is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
