Echinops nistrii

Echinops nistrii, commonly known as the Persian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a hedgehog. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, hot climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
