Eucalyptus × unialata

Eucalyptus × unialata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a hybrid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed shrub or tree form, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin layers, and it is well adapted to dry, sandy soils.

Eucalyptus × unialata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
