Epilobium tasmanicum

Epilobium tasmanicum, commonly known as Tasmanian willow, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. Its botanical characteristics include showy, clustered inflorescences and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
