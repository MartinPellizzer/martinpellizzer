Eremophila sargentii

Eremophila sargentii, commonly known as the Sargent's emu bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range in color from pink to red, attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila sargentii is often used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
