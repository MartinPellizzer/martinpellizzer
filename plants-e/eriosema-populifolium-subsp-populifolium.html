Eriosema populifolium subsp. populifolium

Eriosema populifolium subsp.

populifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and modest floral display.
