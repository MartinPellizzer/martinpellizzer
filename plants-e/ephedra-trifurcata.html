Ephedra trifurcata

Ephedra trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched ephedra, is a dioecious shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its green, branched stems that are covered with small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. Ephedra trifurcata is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting succulent stems that store water.

It is often found in sandy soils and is part of the Ephedraceae family, which includes other species with similar medicinal and ecological significance.
