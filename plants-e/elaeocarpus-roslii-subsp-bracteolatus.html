Elaeocarpus roslii subsp. bracteolatus

Elaeocarpus roslii subsp.

bracteolatus, commonly known as the Bornean elaeocarp, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Borneo. It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically borne in clusters, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments within lowland and hill dipterocarp forests.
