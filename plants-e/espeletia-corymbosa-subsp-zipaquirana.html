Espeletia corymbosa subsp. zipaquirana

Espeletia corymbosa subsp.

zipaquirana, commonly known as the Zipaquirá heather, is a species native to the Andean regions of Colombia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and páramo ecosystems, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
