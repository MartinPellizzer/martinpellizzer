Eugenia laeta var. pauciflora

Eugenia laeta var. pauciflora, commonly known as the slender wax myrtle, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a small, dark purple drupe that contains a single seed.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
