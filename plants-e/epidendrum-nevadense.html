Epidendrum nevadense

Epidendrum nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences with bright yellow to orange flowers, which are highly attractive to pollinators. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

Epidendrum nevadense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and adequate sunlight.
