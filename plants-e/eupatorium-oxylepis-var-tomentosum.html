Eupatorium oxylepis var. tomentosum

Eupatorium oxylepis var. tomentosum, commonly known as the wooly eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique texture and color.
