Eugenia dempoensis

Eugenia dempoensis, commonly known as the Dempo guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and glossy, and its small, fragrant white flowers. It produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

The fruit is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor, making it a valuable resource in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
