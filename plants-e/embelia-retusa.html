Embelia retusa

Embelia retusa, commonly known as the Indian embelia or wild betel, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in traditional medicine and ecological studies.
