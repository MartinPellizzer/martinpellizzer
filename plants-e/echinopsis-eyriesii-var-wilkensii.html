Echinopsis eyriesii var. wilkensii

Echinopsis eyriesii var. wilkensii, commonly known as the Wilkens' moon cactus, is a compact cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a swollen, globular stem that is often brightly colored, with a distinctive red or purple hue. The plant produces white or pink flowers during the warmer months, typically in the evening. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to thrive in well-draining soil with minimal water.

Due to its striking appearance and compact size, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
