Euptelea delavayi

Euptelea delavayi, commonly known as the Chinese boxwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It belongs to the family Eupteleaceae and is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage composed of small, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit and winter interest.

Euptelea delavayi is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
