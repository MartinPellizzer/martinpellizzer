Eugenia divaricata

Eugenia divaricata, commonly known as the Variegated Cabbage Tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits that are typically red or purple in color. Eugenia divaricata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

It is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
