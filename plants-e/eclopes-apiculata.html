Eclopes apiculata

Eclopes apiculata, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are often pale green or white in color. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as forest understories, where it relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a pointed tip, and it produces a single spike of flowers from the base of the plant.

Eclopes apiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
