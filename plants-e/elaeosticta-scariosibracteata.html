Elaeosticta scariosibracteata

Elaeosticta scariosibracteata, commonly known as the scarious bract eleothea, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often covered in fine, scaly bracts. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a rough texture.

The species is native to certain regions of South America and is noted for its unique botanical features.
