Epidendrum flabellilobatum

Epidendrum flabellilobatum, commonly known as the flabellilobate epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers have a distinctive, fan-like shape, which gives the species its common name.

Epidendrum flabellilobatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
