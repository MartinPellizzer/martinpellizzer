Epidendrum fornicatum

Epidendrum fornicatum, commonly known as the Orchid of the Forest, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within tropical forests. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its flowers are usually yellow or green with a distinctive red or purple lip, making it visually striking.

Epidendrum fornicatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
