Eupatorium amblyolepis

Eupatorium amblyolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining biodiversity in its habitat.
