Echinopsis rojasii var. albiflora

Echinopsis rojasii var. albiflora, commonly known as the White Flowered Moon Cactus, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its swollen, ribbed stems that store water. The plant produces showy, white flowers during the warm season, which bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and indoor gardening.
