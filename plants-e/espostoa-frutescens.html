Espostoa frutescens

Espostoa frutescens, commonly known as the golden candelabra, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its dense, branching structure. The plant produces clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture, while its bark is thick and often fissured.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
