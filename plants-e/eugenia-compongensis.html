Eugenia compongensis

Eugenia compongensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia compongensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil and is characterized by its small, aromatic, red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant scent when crushed.

The fruit is a small, sweet, and edible berry that is often consumed fresh or used in local culinary traditions.
