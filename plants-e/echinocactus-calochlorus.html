Echinocactus calochlorus

Echinocactus calochlorus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical shape and spiny, segmented stem. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
