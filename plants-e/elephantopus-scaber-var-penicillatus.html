Elephantopus scaber var. penicillatus

Elephantopus scaber var. penicillatus, commonly known as the hairy elephantopus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
