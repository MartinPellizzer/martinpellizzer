Eritrichium diplotrichum var. humilis

Eritrichium diplotrichum var. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that produce small, pale blue to purple flowers in early spring. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its root system consists of fibrous roots that help stabilize soil. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
