Epacris sanguinea

Epacris sanguinea, commonly known as the red-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its upright, woody stems and dense clusters of small, tubular flowers. The flowers are typically red or pink, and they bloom during the summer months. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
