Eurya palauensis

Eurya palauensis, commonly known as the Palau beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands, including Palau.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds and some mammals. Eurya palauensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and rocky crevices.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
