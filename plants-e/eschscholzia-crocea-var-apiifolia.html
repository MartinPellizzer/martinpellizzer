Eschscholzia crocea var. apiifolia

Eschscholzia crocea var. apiifolia, commonly known as the Pacific Coast poppy, is a variant of the California poppy native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers and finely divided, fern-like leaves that resemble those of the carrot plant. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and grassland habitats. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.
