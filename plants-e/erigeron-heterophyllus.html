Erigeron heterophyllus

Erigeron heterophyllus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and hairy stems, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas such as prairies and roadsides. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, varying in shape depending on the growing conditions.

Erigeron heterophyllus is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
