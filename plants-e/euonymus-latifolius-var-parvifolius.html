Euonymus latifolius var. parvifolius

Euonymus latifolius var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leafed boxwood, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens due to its compact growth habit and ornamental appeal.

This variety is particularly valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its resistance to pests and diseases.
