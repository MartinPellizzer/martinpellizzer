Echinocactus multiflorus f. albispinus

Echinocactus multiflorus f. albispinus, commonly known as the white-spined hedgehog cactus, is a unique variant of the echinocactus genus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in white spines, which distinguish it from other members of the species. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding ornamental value to its appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its distinctive features, it is often cultivated in succulent gardens and as a decorative plant.
