Eupatorium columbianum var. typicum

Eupatorium columbianum var. typicum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
