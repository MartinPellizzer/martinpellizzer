Erycibe induta

Erycibe induta, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
