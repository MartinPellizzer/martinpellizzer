Endocomia virella

Endocomia virella, commonly known as the common thistle.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowers are composite, consisting of many small florets arranged in a dense cluster.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions and is often found along roadsides and in meadows.
