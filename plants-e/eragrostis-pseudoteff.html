Eragrostis pseudoteff

Eragrostis pseudoteff, commonly known as false teff, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its seeds are small and often used as a grain in some traditional cuisines.

Eragrostis pseudoteff is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscaping.
