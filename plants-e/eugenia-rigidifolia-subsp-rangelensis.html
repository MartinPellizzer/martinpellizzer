Eugenia rigidifolia subsp. rangelensis

Eugenia rigidifolia subsp.

rangelensis, commonly known as the Rangel's guava, is a species native to Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree with elliptical leaves that are usually pubescent.

The flowers are small and white, arranged in clusters, and the fruit is a small, yellowish berry. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential use in horticulture.
