Ericoides daphniflorum

Ericoides daphniflorum, commonly known as the snowbell, is a member of the Ericaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing a dense canopy that helps retain moisture.

The overall structure of Ericoides daphniflorum is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative shrub in shaded areas.
