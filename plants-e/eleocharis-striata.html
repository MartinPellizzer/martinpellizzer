Eleocharis striata

Eleocharis striata, commonly known as the narrow-striated spike-rush, is a species of flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow and often have distinct longitudinal stripes, which contribute to its common name.

Eleocharis striata is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
