Elodea titicacana

Elodea titicacana, commonly known as the Lake Titicaca waterweed, is a freshwater plant native to the high-altitude lakes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its long, branching stems and small, whorled leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in length. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving waters and is often found in shallow, nutrient-rich environments. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen for aquatic life.

Elodea titicacana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in ponds and water gardens.
