Eucrypta micrantha

Eucrypta micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered eucrypta, is a flowering plant native to the south-western regions of Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus trees and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

Eucrypta micrantha is often found in open habitats such as gravelly soils and is adapted to the region's dry climate.
