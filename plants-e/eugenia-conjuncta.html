Eugenia conjuncta

Eugenia conjuncta, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia conjuncta*, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Eugenia conjuncta is also sometimes cultivated for its potential medicinal properties.
