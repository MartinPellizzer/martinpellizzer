Eupatorium alternifolium var. aureoviride

Eupatorium alternifolium var. aureoviride, commonly known as golden-green eupatorium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking golden-green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
