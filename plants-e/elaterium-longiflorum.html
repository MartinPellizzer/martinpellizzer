Elaterium longiflorum

Elaterium longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flower elaterium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Elaterium longiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
