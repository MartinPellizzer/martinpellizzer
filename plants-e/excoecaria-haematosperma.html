Excoecaria haematosperma

Excoecaria haematosperma, commonly known as the blood-fruit tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that contain a single seed. The berries are toxic and contain a milky latex, which has been used traditionally for various purposes.

Its botanical features make it distinct within the Euphorbiaceae family.
