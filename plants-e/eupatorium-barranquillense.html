Eupatorium barranquillense

Eupatorium barranquillense, commonly known as the Barranquilla eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region, particularly found in parts of Colombia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy areas, thriving in humid and tropical climates.

Its leaves are usually opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom during the dry season.
