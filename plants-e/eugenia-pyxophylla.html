Eugenia pyxophylla

Eugenia pyxophylla, commonly known as the satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests and coastal areas.

The satinwood is valued for its ornamental appeal and its durable, attractive wood.
