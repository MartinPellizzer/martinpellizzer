Euonymus fertilis var. euryanthus

Euonymus fertilis var. euryanthus, commonly known as the red-fruit euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by striking orange-red berries that persist into winter. Its foliage and fruit make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
