Elaphoglossum underwoodianum

Elaphoglossum underwoodianum, commonly known as the Underwood's holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are typically green and glossy, with a leathery texture. The plant produces spores in small, clustered sori located on the underside of the fronds, a common reproductive feature in ferns. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after species for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
