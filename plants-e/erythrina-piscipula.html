Erythrina piscipula

Erythrina piscipula, commonly known as the fish poison tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers and compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate. The plant contains toxic alkaloids, particularly in its seeds and bark, which have been historically used as a fish poison. Its growth habit includes a tall, often thorny stem with a woody structure.

Erythrina piscipula plays a role in local ecosystems and has traditional uses in various cultures.
