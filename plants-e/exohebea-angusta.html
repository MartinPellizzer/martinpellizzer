Exohebea angusta

Exohebea angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hebe, is a perennial herb native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
