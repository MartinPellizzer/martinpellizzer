Elaeagnus difficilis

Elaeagnus difficilis, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-hairy leaves and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often used for erosion control. It produces fragrant flowers in the spring, which are visited by pollinators.

Elaeagnus difficilis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
