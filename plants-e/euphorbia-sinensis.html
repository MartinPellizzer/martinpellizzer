Euphorbia sinensis

Euphorbia sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese spurge, is a succulent plant native to China.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its stems for survival.

Euphorbia sinensis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and resilience.
