Eucalyptus sarassa

Eucalyptus sarassa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf malleeflower, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color. The plant is part of the Myrtaceae family and is known for its aromatic oils, which are often used in traditional medicine and aromatherapy. Eucalyptus sarassa thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands and heathlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
