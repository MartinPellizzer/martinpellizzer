Evolvulus microphyllus

Evolvulus microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant is a trailing vine that produces small, edible berries, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add color and texture to their landscapes.
