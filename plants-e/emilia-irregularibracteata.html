Emilia irregularibracteata

Emilia irregularibracteata, commonly known as the irregular bract emilia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often bi-colored flowers that range from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Emilia irregularibracteata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
