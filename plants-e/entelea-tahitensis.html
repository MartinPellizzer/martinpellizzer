Entelea tahitensis

Entelea tahitensis, commonly known as the Tahitian entelea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Ocean, including Tahiti and other islands in French Polynesia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces woody capsules that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Entelea tahitensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and resilience in humid climates.
