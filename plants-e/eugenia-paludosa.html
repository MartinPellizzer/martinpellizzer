Eugenia paludosa

Eugenia paludosa, commonly known as the swamp eucalyptus, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of South America.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Eugenia paludosa plays an important ecological role in its native environment by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
