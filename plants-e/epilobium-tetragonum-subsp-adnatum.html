Epilobium tetragonum subsp. adnatum

Epilobium tetragonum subsp.

adnatum, commonly known as narrow-leaved willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial with erect, branched stems that often grow up to 1 meter in height.

The leaves are narrowly elliptic, with a slightly hairy surface, and they are arranged alternately along the stem. The flowers are pink to reddish-purple, forming dense spikes at the tips of the branches.

It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as riverbanks and wet meadows, and it is often found in areas with clay or sandy soils.
