Eria fusca

Eria fusca, commonly known as the dark leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green to almost black foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically during the rainy season. Eria fusca is an epiphytic plant, meaning it grows on other plants without being parasitic.

Its unique appearance and habitat make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
