Eugenia ferruginosa

Eugenia ferruginosa, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark red, glossy fruits that are often referred to as mangosteen. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers and has elliptical, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. Its fruit is encased in a thick, leathery rind and contains a segmented, white, juicy flesh that is rich in antioxidants.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
