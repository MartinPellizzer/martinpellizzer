Eupatorium javanicum

Eupatorium javanicum, commonly known as Java boneset, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland areas and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a rough texture, while its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Eupatorium javanicum is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
