Epipactis helleborine var. intrusa

Epipactis helleborine var. intrusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its roots are fibrous, and it spreads vegetatively through rhizomes.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant produces small, dark brown seeds in a capsule.
