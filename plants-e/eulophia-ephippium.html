Eulophia ephippium

Eulophia ephippium, commonly known as the orchid with the unique appearance of its flower spikes, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb that supports its growth and stores water, allowing it to thrive in seasonal climates. The plant produces racemes of fragrant, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. Its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Eulophia ephippium is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
