Eurystyles rutkowskiana

Eurystyles rutkowskiana, commonly known as the Rutkowskian grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to survive in arid conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native environment.
