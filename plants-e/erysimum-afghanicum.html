Erysimum afghanicum

Erysimum afghanicum, commonly known as Afghan wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Afghanistan and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, although it is often found in the wild in mountainous areas.
