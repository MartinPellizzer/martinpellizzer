Eulophia bosseriana

Eulophia bosseriana, commonly known as the Bosser's eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Madagascar.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and flowers. The plant produces long, slender leaves and bears inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. Its botanical characteristics make it a striking addition to botanical gardens and a subject of interest for orchid enthusiasts.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
