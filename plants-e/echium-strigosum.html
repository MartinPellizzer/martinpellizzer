Echium strigosum

Echium strigosum, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its gray-green, hairy foliage and striking purple-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

Echium strigosum is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
