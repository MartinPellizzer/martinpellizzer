Espeletia grandiflora f. multiflora

Espeletia grandiflora f. multiflora, commonly known as the "Cumbal flower," is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy white or pinkish flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically in cloud forests or paramo ecosystems, where it adapts to cool and moist conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are densely packed, forming a rosette structure.

The species is notable for its role in local biodiversity and its ornamental value in horticultural settings.
