Epacris incarnata

Epacris incarnata, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of tubular flowers. The flowers are typically pink or red, with a distinctive, fan-like appearance that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
