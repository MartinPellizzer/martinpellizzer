Echinocactus latispinus var. flavispinus

Echinocactus latispinus var. flavispinus, commonly known as the yellow-spined hedgehog cactus, is a slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in thick, yellow spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for succulent collectors and desert gardens.
