Epilobium hirsutum var. laetum

Epilobium hirsutum var. laetum, commonly known as the hairy willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, often shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are pink to reddish in color, arranged in spikes, and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
