Erianthus fulvus

Erianthus fulvus, commonly known as the yellow panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically yellowish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Erianthus fulvus is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and potential agricultural applications.
