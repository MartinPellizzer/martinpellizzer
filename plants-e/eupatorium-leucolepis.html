Eupatorium leucolepis

Eupatorium leucolepis, commonly known as white eupatorium, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage is often covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Eupatorium leucolepis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting pollinators.
