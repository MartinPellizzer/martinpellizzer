Erysimum pannonicum

Erysimum pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as slopes and gravelly areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange, attracting various pollinators.

Erysimum pannonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
