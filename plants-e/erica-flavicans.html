Erica flavicans

Erica flavicans, commonly known as the yellow heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and evergreen, adapted to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

Erica flavicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
