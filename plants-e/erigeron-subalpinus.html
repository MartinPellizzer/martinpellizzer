Erigeron subalpinus

Erigeron subalpinus, commonly known as alpine blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, dry conditions. It has hairy, linear leaves and produces small blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
