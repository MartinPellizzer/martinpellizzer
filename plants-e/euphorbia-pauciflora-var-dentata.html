Euphorbia pauciflora var. dentata

Euphorbia pauciflora var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, branching structure and thick, fleshy stems that store water, aiding in its survival in arid environments. The leaves are usually small, spiny, and often have a serrated or toothed margin, which gives the variety its name. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts.

This plant is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
