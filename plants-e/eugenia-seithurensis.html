Eugenia seithurensis

Eugenia seithurensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ceraria*, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of eastern Brazil, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and red, edible fruits that resemble cherries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

Eugenia seithurensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
