Eragrostis monticola

Eragrostis monticola, commonly known as mountain lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Eragrostis monticola is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
