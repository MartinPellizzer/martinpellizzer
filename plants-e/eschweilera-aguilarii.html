Eschweilera aguilarii

Eschweilera aguilarii, commonly known as the Guiana Shield ceiba, is a large evergreen tree native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and towering height, often reaching over 50 meters. The tree produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also important in the ecological balance of its habitat.
