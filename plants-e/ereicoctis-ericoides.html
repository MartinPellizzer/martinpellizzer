Ereicoctis ericoides

Ereicoctis ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters and blooming in late summer.

Ereicoctis ericoides thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.
