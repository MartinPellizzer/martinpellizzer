Elaeagnus longipes f. elliptica

Elaeagnus longipes f. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
