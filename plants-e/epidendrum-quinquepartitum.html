Epidendrum quinquepartitum

Epidendrum quinquepartitum, commonly known as the five-parted epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its flowers are often fragrant and can last for several weeks, adding ornamental value to gardens or collections.

Epidendrum quinquepartitum is relatively easy to care for, making it accessible to both novice and experienced growers.
