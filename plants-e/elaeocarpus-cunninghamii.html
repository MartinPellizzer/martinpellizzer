Elaeocarpus cunninghamii

Elaeocarpus cunninghamii, commonly known as the Sydney apple.

It is a flowering plant species native to eastern Australia, characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. It typically grows in rainforest or wet sclerophyll forest habitats.

Elaeocarpus cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in its native environment.
