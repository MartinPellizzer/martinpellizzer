Epidendrum lezlieae

Epidendrum lezlieae, commonly known as the Leslie's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its long, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Epidendrum lezlieae is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
