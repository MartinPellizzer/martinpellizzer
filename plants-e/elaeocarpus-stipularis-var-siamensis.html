Elaeocarpus stipularis var. siamensis

Elaeocarpus stipularis var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lanceolate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various birds and mammals.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
