Eugenia flavoviridis

Eugenia flavoviridis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale yellow underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its aromatic foliage and has traditional uses in some cultures.
