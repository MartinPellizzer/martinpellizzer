Euphorbia apios var. lamprocarpa

Euphorbia apios var. lamprocarpa, commonly known as the shining euphorbia, is a perennial shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are its modified flowers. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic plant species.
