Elaeocarpus macdonaldii

Elaeocarpus macdonaldii, commonly known as the Macdonald's elaeocarp, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically encased in a fleshy, red or purple aril. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a suitable candidate for tropical gardens.
