Eupatorium laevigatum f. albiflorum

Eupatorium laevigatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower false dragonhead, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, clustered white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens due to its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for perennial borders.
