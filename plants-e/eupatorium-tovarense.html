Eupatorium tovarense

Eupatorium tovarense, commonly known as the Tovarenses thoroughwort, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium tovarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
