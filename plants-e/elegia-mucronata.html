Elegia mucronata

Elegia mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed water plantain, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in shallow water. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in muddy or sandy soils along the edges of ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.

Elegia mucronata plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
