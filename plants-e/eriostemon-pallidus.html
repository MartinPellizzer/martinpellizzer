Eriostemon pallidus

Eriostemon pallidus, commonly known as the pale eriostemon, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in terminal umbels. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and sandy soils.

Eriostemon pallidus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
