Elaeophorbia acuta

Elaeophorbia acuta, commonly known as the sharp candelabra, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a branched stem with dense, fleshy leaves that are typically green and covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its thick leaves and stem.

Elaeophorbia acuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
