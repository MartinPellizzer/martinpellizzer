Eumachia viridicalyx

Eumachia viridicalyx, commonly known as the green-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses specialized structures called haustoria that allow it to absorb water and nutrients from its host. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its berries are usually red or yellow and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Eumachia viridicalyx is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it contributes to the ecological dynamics of its habitat.
