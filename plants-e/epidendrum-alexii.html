Epidendrum alexii

Epidendrum alexii, commonly known as the Mexican flame orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, brightly colored flowers that range from red to orange. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions.

Epidendrum alexii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
