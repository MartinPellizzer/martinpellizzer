Etlingera moluccana

Etlingera moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan torch ginger, is a tropical flowering plant native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange inflorescences, which resemble torches and are often used in floral arrangements. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a glossy appearance, while its rhizomes are aromatic and used in traditional medicine.

Etlingera moluccana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its cultural significance.
