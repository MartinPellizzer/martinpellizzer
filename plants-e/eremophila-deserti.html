Eremophila deserti

Eremophila deserti, commonly known as the desert emu, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom during the cooler months. It is well adapted to drought conditions, with deep roots that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Eremophila deserti plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and nectar for native pollinators.
