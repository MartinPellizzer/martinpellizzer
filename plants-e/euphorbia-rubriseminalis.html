Euphorbia rubriseminalis

Euphorbia rubriseminalis, commonly known as the red-seed spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a short, branched stem covered in dense, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its flowers are inconspicuous, consisting of a single cup-like structure with yellowish-green lobes.

Euphorbia rubriseminalis is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soil.
