Eucalyptus teretiuscula

Eucalyptus teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the species. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or loamy soils.

Eucalyptus teretiuscula is well adapted to drought and is often found in coastal and inland regions of Australia.
