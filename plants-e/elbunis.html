Elbunis

Elbunis, commonly known as the "blueberry bush," is a deciduous shrub native to the northern regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and have a tart flavor. Elbunis thrives in acidic soil and requires partial shade to full sun for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its vibrant fall foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
