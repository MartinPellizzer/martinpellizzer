Ervum vestitum

Ervum vestitum, commonly known as the dressed bean, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes known for their nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. Its pods contain edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
