Erythraea subspicata

Erythraea subspicata, commonly known as the red spirea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy clusters of pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The botanical features of Erythraea subspicata make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
