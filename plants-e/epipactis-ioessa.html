Epipactis ioessa

Epipactis ioessa, commonly known as the Ioessa helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and grasslands. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its mycorrhizal associations, which aid in nutrient uptake.

Epipactis ioessa is often found in association with specific fungal species, highlighting its ecological specificity.
