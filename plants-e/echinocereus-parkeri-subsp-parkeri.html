Echinocereus parkeri subsp. parkeri

Echinocereus parkeri subsp.

parkeri, commonly known as the Arizona hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid landscapes.
