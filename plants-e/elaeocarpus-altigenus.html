Elaeocarpus altigenus

Elaeocarpus altigenus, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary and secondary forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
