Eugenia darwinii

Eugenia darwinii, commonly known as the Darwin's guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, edible fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

Eugenia darwinii is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
