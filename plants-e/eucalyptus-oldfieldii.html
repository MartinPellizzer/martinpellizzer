Eucalyptus oldfieldii

Eucalyptus oldfieldii, commonly known as Oldfield's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and distinctive flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, white or pale blue flowers that attract nectar-feeding insects and birds.

Eucalyptus oldfieldii is often found in mallee woodlands and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
