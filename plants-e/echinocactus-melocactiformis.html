Echinocactus melocactiformis

Echinocactus melocactiformis, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.
