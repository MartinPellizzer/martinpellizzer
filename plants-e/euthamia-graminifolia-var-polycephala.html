Euthamia graminifolia var. polycephala

Euthamia graminifolia var. polycephala, commonly known as the slender goldenrod, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and pond edges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

This variant is distinguished by its multiple flowering heads, giving it a unique appearance compared to other Euthamia species.
