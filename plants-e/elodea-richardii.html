Elodea richardii

Elodea richardii, commonly known as Richard's waterweed, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is a submerged, herbaceous plant that typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has long, slender stems that are often branched and bear small, whorled leaves. Its flowers are usually greenish-white and are arranged in spike-like clusters.

Elodea richardii plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
