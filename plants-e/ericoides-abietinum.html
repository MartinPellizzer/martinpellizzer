Ericoides abietinum

Ericoides abietinum, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen leaves that are typically dark green and leathery. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ericoides abietinum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
