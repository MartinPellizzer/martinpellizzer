Euodia elliptica

Euodia elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers followed by distinctive, woody fruits that are often used in traditional Aboriginal medicine. It is well adapted to dry and rocky soils, making it a hardy species suitable for coastal and arid regions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
