Euphorbia gaudichaudii

Euphorbia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in soft, greenish-gray hair. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called cyathia. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
