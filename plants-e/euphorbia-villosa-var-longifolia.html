Euphorbia villosa var. longifolia

Euphorbia villosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf spurge, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and stems covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a waxy texture with a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

This plant is adapted to sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and disturbed areas.
