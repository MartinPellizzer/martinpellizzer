Echinodorus eglandulosus

Echinodorus eglandulosus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waterweed, is a perennial aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that emerge from the water surface. It thrives in shallow, slow-moving freshwater environments and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Echinodorus eglandulosus is valued in aquatic gardening for its ability to provide oxygen and habitat for aquatic life.
