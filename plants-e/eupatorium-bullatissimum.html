Eupatorium bullatissimum

Eupatorium bullatissimum, commonly known as the bullate eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks, preferring moist, sandy soils. Its showy white to pale blue flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
