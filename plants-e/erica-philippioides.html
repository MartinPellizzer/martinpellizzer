Erica philippioides

Erica philippioides, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with mild, wet winters and hot, dry summers. Erica philippioides is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

It also plays a role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native habitat.
