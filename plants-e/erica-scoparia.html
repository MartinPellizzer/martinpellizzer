Erica scoparia

Erica scoparia, commonly known as the Heath or Heathbush, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It typically features dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
