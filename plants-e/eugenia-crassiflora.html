Eugenia crassiflora

Eugenia crassiflora, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and often used in jams and beverages. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Eugenia crassiflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
