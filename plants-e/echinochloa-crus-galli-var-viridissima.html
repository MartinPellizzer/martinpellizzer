Echinochloa crus-galli var. viridissima

Echinochloa crus-galli var. viridissima, commonly known as green foxtail, is a grass species native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences that are often green or purplish in color. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many regions.
