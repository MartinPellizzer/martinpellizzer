Eugenia merokensis

Eugenia merokensis, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, yellowish fruits that are similar in appearance to apples but have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and culinary purposes.
