Epidendrum pseudoramosum

Epidendrum pseudoramosum, commonly known as the Rosy Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, warm environments. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a smooth texture and a waxy coating.

Epidendrum pseudoramosum is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
