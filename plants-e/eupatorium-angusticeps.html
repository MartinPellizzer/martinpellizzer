Eupatorium angusticeps

Eupatorium angusticeps, commonly known as narrowleaf boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers arranged in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Eupatorium angusticeps is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native gardens.
