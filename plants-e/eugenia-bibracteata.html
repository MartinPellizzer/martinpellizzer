Eugenia bibracteata

Eugenia bibracteata, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark red to black berries, which are covered in a dense, velvety coating. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a leathery texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
