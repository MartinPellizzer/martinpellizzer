Eulophia monotropis

Eulophia monotropis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive three-lobed lip, and bloom during the wet season.
