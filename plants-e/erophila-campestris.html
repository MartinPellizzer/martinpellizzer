Erophila campestris

Erophila campestris, commonly known as field eryngo, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple composite flowers and hairy, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It produces small, wind-dispersed seeds that aid in its spread across various habitats.
