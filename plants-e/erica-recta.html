Erica recta

Erica recta, commonly known as the upright heath, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. This plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white. Erica recta thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and seasonal blooms.
