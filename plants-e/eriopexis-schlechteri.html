Eriopexis schlechteri

Eriopexis schlechteri, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a velvety texture, and its small, greenish-white flowers that appear in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Eriopexis schlechteri is also known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
