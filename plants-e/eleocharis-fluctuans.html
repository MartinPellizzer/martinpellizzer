Eleocharis fluctuans

Eleocharis fluctuans, commonly known as floating needlegrass, is a small aquatic plant native to wetlands and shallow waters in tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, grass-like stems that float on the water surface, with tiny, spike-like clusters of flowers at the tips. The plant's roots are submerged, anchoring it in muddy or sandy substrates, while its aerial parts remain above water. It plays a role in stabilizing water surfaces and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Eleocharis fluctuans is often found in still or slow-moving water bodies, contributing to the ecological balance of wetland ecosystems.
