Eupatorium urubambense var. determinatum

Eupatorium urubambense var. determinatum, commonly known as the Andean boneset, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
