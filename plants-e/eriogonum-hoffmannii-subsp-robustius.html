Eriogonum hoffmannii subsp. robustius

Eriogonum hoffmannii subsp.

robustius, commonly known as the robust Hoffman's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
