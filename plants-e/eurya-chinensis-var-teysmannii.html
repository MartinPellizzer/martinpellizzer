Eurya chinensis var. teysmannii

Eurya chinensis var. teysmannii, commonly known as the Taiwan eurya, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions of China, Taiwan, and Vietnam.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
