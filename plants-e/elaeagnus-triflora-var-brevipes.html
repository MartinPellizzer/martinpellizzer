Elaeagnus triflora var. brevipes

Elaeagnus triflora var. brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf silverberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climates, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for small gardens or as a hedging plant.
