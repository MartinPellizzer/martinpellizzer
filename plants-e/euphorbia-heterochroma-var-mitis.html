Euphorbia heterochroma var. mitis

Euphorbia heterochroma var. mitis, commonly known as the White-spotted Candelabra, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem structure with cylindrical, ribbed stems that often bear white or yellowish spots. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
