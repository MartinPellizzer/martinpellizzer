Eugenia boisiana

Eugenia boisiana, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or granadilla, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine. Its leaves are evergreen, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

Eugenia boisiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
