Eria formosana

Eria formosana, commonly known as the Formosa orchid, is a species native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eria formosana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
