Eremophila punctata

Eremophila punctata, commonly known as the spotted emu plant, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are often red or purple, and they attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Eremophila punctata is adapted to drought conditions and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
