Etlingera labellosa

Etlingera labellosa, commonly known as the red ginger, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect rhizome and its vibrant, scarlet-red inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often surrounded by long, narrow bracts. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and have a waxy texture with a distinctive purple underside.

Etlingera labellosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
