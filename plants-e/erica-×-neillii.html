Erica × neillii

Erica × neillii, commonly known as the Neill's heath, is a hybrid plant within the Ericaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica × neillii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.

It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, making it a hardy choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
