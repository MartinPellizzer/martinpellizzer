Eleocharis truncata

Eleocharis truncata, commonly known as the spike rush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in shallow water or saturated soil. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Eleocharis truncata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to filter water and support diverse wildlife.
