Embelia myriantha

Embelia myriantha, commonly known as the many-leaved embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by round, fleshy berries that are typically red when ripe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
