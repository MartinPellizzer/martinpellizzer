Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. kellerianus

Echinofossulocactus crispatus f. kellerianus, commonly known as the Kellerianus variety of the crinkled spine cactus, is a unique cactus species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white, woolly hair that helps reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its grayish-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
