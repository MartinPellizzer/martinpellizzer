Eucalyptus petalophylla

Eucalyptus petalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in spring. Its bark is typically smooth and peels in thin, papery layers, a common trait among many eucalyptus species.

Eucalyptus petalophylla is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
