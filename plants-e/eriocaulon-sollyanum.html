Eriocaulon sollyanum

Eriocaulon sollyanum, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, where it thrives in moist, sandy or peaty soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with dense clusters of small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and often submerged, contributing to the plant's overall appearance.

Eriocaulon sollyanum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
