Epilobium × winkleri

Epilobium × winkleri, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically pink to red, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and disturbed sites.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
