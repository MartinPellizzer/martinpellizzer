Echinops wakhanicus

Echinops wakhanicus, commonly known as the Wakhan thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its pale blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops wakhanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
