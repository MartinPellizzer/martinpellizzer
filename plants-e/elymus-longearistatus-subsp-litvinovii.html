Elymus longearistatus subsp. litvinovii

Elymus longearistatus subsp.

litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, grass-like foliage.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in panicles, with lemmas that are often hairy. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora and a potential forage plant for livestock.
