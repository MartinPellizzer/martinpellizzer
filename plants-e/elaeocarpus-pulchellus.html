Elaeocarpus pulchellus

Elaeocarpus pulchellus, commonly known as the white-flowered elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
