Echinodorus berteroi subsp. patagonicus

Echinodorus berteroi subsp.

patagonicus, commonly known as the Patagonian swordplant, is a aquatic plant native to South America. It features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly textured.

This subspecies is adapted to slow-moving freshwater environments and can thrive in a variety of water conditions. It is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by cylindrical, spiky fruits.
