Eupatorium ivifolium var. perturbinatum

Eupatorium ivifolium var. perturbinatum, commonly known as the slender boneset, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
