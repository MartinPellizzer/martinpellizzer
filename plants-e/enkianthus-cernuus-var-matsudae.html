Enkianthus cernuus var. matsudae

Enkianthus cernuus var. matsudae, commonly known as the Matsudae enkianthus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
