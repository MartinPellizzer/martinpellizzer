Euphorbia bounophila

Euphorbia bounophila, commonly known as the Cape York euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Euphorbia bounophila is also known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which can be toxic to humans and animals.
