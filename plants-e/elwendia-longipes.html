Elwendia longipes

Elwendia longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed elwendia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to two meters, adorned with clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface that reflects light, enhancing the plant's visual appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, Elwendia longipes is considered a conservation concern.
