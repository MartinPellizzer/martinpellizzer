Eriogonum ursinum var. nervulosum

Eriogonum ursinum var. nervulosum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with prominent veins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes.

This plant plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to the dry, sunny conditions of its native environment.
