Echinocereus chloranthus var. russanthus

Echinocereus chloranthus var. russanthus, commonly known as the rust-colored hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny stems that are typically green with a reddish or rust-colored hue, especially under certain light conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and striking coloration make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
