Escallonia petrophila

Escallonia petrophila, commonly known as the rock escallonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it thrives in harsh, arid environments.

Escallonia petrophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought and poor soil conditions.
