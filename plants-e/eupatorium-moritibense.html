Eupatorium moritibense

Eupatorium moritibense, commonly known as the Florida blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and swampy areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Eupatorium moritibense is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
