Eugenia grazielae

Eugenia grazielae, commonly known as the Grazielae Eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northern Queensland, Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and dark, glossy leaves. It typically grows as an understory shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
