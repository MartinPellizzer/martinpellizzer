Ehretia wightiana

Ehretia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, resembling those of the hibiscus genus. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
