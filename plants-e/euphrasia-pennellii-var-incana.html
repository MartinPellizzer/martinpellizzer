Euphrasia pennellii var. incana

Euphrasia pennellii var. incana, commonly known as the woolly eyebright, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are glandular and covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive, woolly appearance.

The species is valued in traditional herbal medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
