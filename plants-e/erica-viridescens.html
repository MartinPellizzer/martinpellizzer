Erica viridescens

Erica viridescens, commonly known as the greenleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions. Erica viridescens is often found in fynbos vegetation, which is a unique biome known for its biodiversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
