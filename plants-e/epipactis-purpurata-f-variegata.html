Epipactis purpurata f. variegata

Epipactis purpurata f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated helleborine, is a herbaceous perennial orchid native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green and white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, typically arranged in a raceme. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and prefers rich, humusy soils.

This orchid is often found in association with other orchid species and is noted for its ornamental value in horticulture.
