Elaeocarpus longibarbatus

Elaeocarpus longibarbatus, commonly known as the long-bearded elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
