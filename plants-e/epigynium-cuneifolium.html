Epigynium cuneifolium

Epigynium cuneifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf epigynium, is a unique flowering plant characterized by its distinctive botanical features.

It possesses a cuneiform leaf shape, which gives it its common name, and its flowers are typically small and clustered. The plant is notable for its epigynous flowering structure, where the floral parts are attached above the ovary. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a food source for certain pollinators and its ecological niche in forest ecosystems.
