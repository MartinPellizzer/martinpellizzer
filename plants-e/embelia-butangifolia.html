Embelia butangifolia

Embelia butangifolia, commonly known as the Philippine embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, fleshy berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
