Eriocaulon fulvum

Eriocaulon fulvum, commonly known as the golden spike-rush, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences covered in fine, hair-like structures. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Eriocaulon fulvum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
