Eucalyptus williamsiana

Eucalyptus williamsiana, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Eucalyptus williamsiana is valued for its ecological role and is used in horticulture for its ornamental appeal.
