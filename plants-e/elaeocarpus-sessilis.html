Elaeocarpus sessilis

Elaeocarpus sessilis, commonly known as the sessile elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits that contain a single seed. Its growth habit is often shrub-like, though it can reach tree-like proportions under favorable conditions.

Elaeocarpus sessilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and potential medicinal properties.
