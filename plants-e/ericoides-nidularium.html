Ericoides nidularium

Ericoides nidularium, commonly known as the clustered heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the temperate regions of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Ericoides nidularium thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

Its ecological significance lies in providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators and wildlife.
