Eugenia sylvestris

Eugenia sylvestris, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, edible berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
