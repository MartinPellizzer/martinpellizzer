Euxolus macrocarpus

Euxolus macrocarpus, commonly known as the large-fruited euphorbia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to southern Africa, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its fruits are large, capsule-like structures that contain numerous seeds.

Euxolus macrocarpus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
