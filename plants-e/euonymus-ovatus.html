Euonymus ovatus

Euonymus ovatus, commonly known as the Chinese burning bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant red foliage in the fall, which makes it a popular ornamental plant. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate in shape with toothed margins. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, berry-like fruits.

Euonymus ovatus is often cultivated for its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various soil types.
