Echites panduratus

Echites panduratus, commonly known as the horned vetch, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with a distinctive horn-like projection on the terminal leaflet, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits. Its root system is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

Echites panduratus is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
