Emblica chamaepeuce

Emblica chamaepeuce, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that give way to yellow, fleshy berries. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been widely used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
