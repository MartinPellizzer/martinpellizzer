Espeletia cardonae

Espeletia cardonae, commonly known as the Santa Rosa pineapple.

It is a rosette-forming plant native to the high-altitude grasslands of the Andes, particularly in Ecuador. The plant features dense, fleshy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in arid conditions. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and are arranged in a compact inflorescence.

Espeletia cardonae is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and extreme temperatures.
