Elaeocarpus trichanthera

Elaeocarpus trichanthera, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and northern Australia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
