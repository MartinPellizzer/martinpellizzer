Eugenia guatemalensis

Eugenia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or orange when ripe, with a waxy coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in humid lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
