Eleusine coracana var. tocussa

Eleusine coracana var. tocussa, commonly known as finger millet, is a drought-tolerant annual grass native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, clustered spikelets and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces edible grains that are rich in calcium and protein, making it a nutritious staple in many regions. It thrives in poor soils and requires minimal water, which contributes to its adaptability.

The variety tocussa is particularly noted for its high yield and resilience to environmental stress.
