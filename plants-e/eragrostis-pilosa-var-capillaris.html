Eragrostis pilosa var. capillaris

Eragrostis pilosa var. capillaris, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a soft, silky appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to purple flowers.

Silky bluegrass is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
