Echinopsis balansae

Echinopsis balansae, commonly known as the Balansae cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive ribbed structure and spines that are typically white or yellow. The plant produces large, showy white or pink flowers during the warmer months, which attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
