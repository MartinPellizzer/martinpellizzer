Eucalyptus beardiana

Eucalyptus beardiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, blooming during the cooler months. Eucalyptus beardiana is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or heathlands.

It is an important species for local wildlife, providing nectar for various pollinators.
