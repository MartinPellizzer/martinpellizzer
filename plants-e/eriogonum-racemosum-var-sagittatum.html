Eriogonum racemosum var. sagittatum

Eriogonum racemosum var. sagittatum, commonly known as the arrowleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of arrowhead-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure. It has a short growing season and is often found in sagebrush steppe and desert environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
