Eugenia capensis subsp. simii

Eugenia capensis subsp.

simii, commonly known as the Cape cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in horticulture.
