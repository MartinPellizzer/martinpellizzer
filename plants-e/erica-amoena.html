Erica amoena

Erica amoena, commonly known as the sweet heather, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a member of the Ericaceae family, known for its evergreen shrubs and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in spring. Erica amoena thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to coastal and mountainous environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its hardiness and aesthetic beauty.
