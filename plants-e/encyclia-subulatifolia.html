Encyclia subulatifolia

Encyclia subulatifolia, commonly known as the needle-leaved orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and rocky areas. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.

Encyclia subulatifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
