Eurybia capitellata

Eurybia capitellata, commonly known as the common yarrow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clustered, small white or yellow flowers. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along roadsides. It is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Eurybia capitellata is also valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in herbal remedies.
