Eriogonum crosbyae var. crosbyae

Eriogonum crosbyae var. crosbyae, commonly known as Crosby's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and arranged in a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
