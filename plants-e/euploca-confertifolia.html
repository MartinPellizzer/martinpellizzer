Euploca confertifolia

Euploca confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved euploca, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a dense, compact rosette. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Euploca confertifolia is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, such as under trees or in moist, rocky habitats.
