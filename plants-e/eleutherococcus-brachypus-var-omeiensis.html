Eleutherococcus brachypus var. omeiensis

Eleutherococcus brachypus var. omeiensis, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
