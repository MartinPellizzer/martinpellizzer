Epidendrum corallinum

Epidendrum corallinum, commonly known as the coral root orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that resemble coral. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its roots are epiphytic, allowing it to grow on tree trunks or other surfaces.

Epidendrum corallinum is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
