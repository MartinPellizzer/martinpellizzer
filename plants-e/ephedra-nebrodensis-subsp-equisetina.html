Ephedra nebrodensis subsp. equisetina

Ephedra nebrodensis subsp.

equisetina, commonly known as the Armenian ephedra, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, scale-like leaves, typical of the Ephedra genus.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by insects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has been the subject of botanical and pharmacological studies.
