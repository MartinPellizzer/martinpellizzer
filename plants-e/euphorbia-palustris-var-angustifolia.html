Euphorbia palustris var. angustifolia

Euphorbia palustris var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf spurge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas of North America.

It features upright, branching stems covered in a waxy, pale green coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is adapted to moist soils and often grows in marshy or swampy habitats.
