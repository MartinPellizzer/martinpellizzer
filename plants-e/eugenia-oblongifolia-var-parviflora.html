Eugenia oblongifolia var. parviflora

Eugenia oblongifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered evergreen guava, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with lanceolate, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
