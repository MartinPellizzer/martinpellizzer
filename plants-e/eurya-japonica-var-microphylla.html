Eurya japonica var. microphylla

Eurya japonica var. microphylla, commonly known as Japanese holly or dwarf holly, is a compact evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that persist into winter. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate urban environments.
