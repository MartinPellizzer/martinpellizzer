Eragrostis leioptera

Eragrostis leioptera, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Eragrostis leioptera is often found in dry environments and is adapted to thrive in full sun.
