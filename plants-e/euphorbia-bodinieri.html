Euphorbia bodinieri

Euphorbia bodinieri, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
