Eugenia matudae

Eugenia matudae, commonly known as the Matudae myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the cloud forests of western Ecuador, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its fruit is a small, round drupe that turns from green to reddish when mature.

Eugenia matudae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological and medicinal significance.
