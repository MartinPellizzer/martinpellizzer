Erigeron pumilus subsp. typicus

Erigeron pumilus subsp.

typicus, commonly known as the dwarf fleabane, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually narrow and gray-green, covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in native plant gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
