Echinops negrii

Echinops negrii, commonly known as the Negri's thistle, is a species of flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its spherical, spiky seed heads are a distinctive feature, often appearing after the summer months.

Echinops negrii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
