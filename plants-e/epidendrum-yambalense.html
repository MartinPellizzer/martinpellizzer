Epidendrum yambalense

Epidendrum yambalense, commonly known as the Yambalense Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers with a distinctive lip that is often marked with contrasting patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
