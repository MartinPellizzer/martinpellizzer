Erysimum longifolium subsp. helveticum

Erysimum longifolium subsp.

helveticum, commonly known as the Swiss wallflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in alpine and subalpine regions, particularly in the Alps.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
