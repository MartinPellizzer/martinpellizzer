Ericoides flocciflorum

Ericoides flocciflorum, commonly known as the fluffflower, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive floral structure.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is typically found in acidic, nutrient-poor soils. The plant features small, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are leathery and evergreen, adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Ericoides flocciflorum plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by contributing to soil stability and providing food for local wildlife.
