Erythroxylum moonii

Erythroxylum moonii, commonly known as the moonflower erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves and has a woody stem with a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and they emit a subtle fragrance that attracts nocturnal pollinators.

Due to its unique blooming pattern and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
