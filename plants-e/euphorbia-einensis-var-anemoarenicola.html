Euphorbia einensis var. anemoarenicola

Euphorbia einensis var. anemoarenicola, commonly known as the Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It features a compact, branching structure with thick, fleshy stems that store water, enabling it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, further minimizing water loss.

This variant is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive with minimal rainfall.
