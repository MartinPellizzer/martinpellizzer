Epidendrum withneri

Epidendrum withneri, commonly known as the Withner's Epidendrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often arranged in racemes. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Epidendrum withneri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
