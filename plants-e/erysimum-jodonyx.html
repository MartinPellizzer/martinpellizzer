Erysimum jodonyx

Erysimum jodonyx, commonly known as the Jodonyx wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, while the stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The species is noted for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
