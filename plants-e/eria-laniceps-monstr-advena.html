Eria laniceps monstr. advena

Eria laniceps monstr.

advena, commonly known as the hairy spider orchid, is a species of orchid characterized by its distinctive, hairy foliage and intricate floral structure. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The plant features a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically small, with a unique coloration pattern that includes shades of white, green, and purple.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
