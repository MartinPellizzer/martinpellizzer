Erophila verna subsp. oblongata

Erophila verna subsp.

oblongata, commonly known as the spring eryngo, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets.

The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowering period occurs in early spring, producing small yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

The subspecies oblongata is distinguished by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves compared to the more rounded leaves of the typical subspecies.
