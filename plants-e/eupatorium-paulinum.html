Eupatorium paulinum

Eupatorium paulinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that appear in late summer and early fall. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium paulinum is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
