Eupatorium plumerii

Eupatorium plumerii, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium plumerii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
