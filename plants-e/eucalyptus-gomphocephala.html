Eucalyptus gomphocephala

Eucalyptus gomphocephala, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves and distinctive, cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from white to pink. The plant is typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in open woodlands or shrublands. It is a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 5 meters.

Eucalyptus gomphocephala is known for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
