Eupatorium nigrescens

Eupatorium nigrescens, commonly known as black bones, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers that appear in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and emits a strong, somewhat unpleasant odor. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as swamps and riverbanks.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal remedies for various ailments.
