Epilobium × herveyi

Epilobium × herveyi, commonly known as Hervey's willowherb, is a hybrid plant species within the Onagraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Epilobium × herveyi is often found in moist, disturbed soils such as roadside banks and stream sides.

It is an herbaceous perennial that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, making it a notable presence in wetland and riparian habitats.
