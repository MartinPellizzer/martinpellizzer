Epacris attleana

Epacris attleana, commonly known as the violet leaf epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with violet or purple. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular, pink or red flowers that are typically arranged in a spike-like inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Epacris attleana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
