Eupatorium steviifolium var. rosmarinifolium

Eupatorium steviifolium var. rosmarinifolium, commonly known as lemon verbena, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that resemble those of verbena, and its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves, which emit a strong lemon scent when crushed. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal use.

Lemon verbena is often used in teas and aromatherapy for its calming properties.
