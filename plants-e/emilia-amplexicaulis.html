Emilia amplexicaulis

Emilia amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping emilia, is a herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features striking, sparsely hairy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that often wrap around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Emilia amplexicaulis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
