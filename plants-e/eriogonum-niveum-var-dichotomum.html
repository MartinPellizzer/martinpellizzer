Eriogonum niveum var. dichotomum

Eriogonum niveum var. dichotomum, commonly known as the white flax or snowflax, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its white to pale purple flowers and its ability to thrive in arid and rocky environments. The plant has a low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense, spike-like clusters and bloom in early spring.

It is often found in open areas such as sagebrush steppe and rocky slopes.
