Erica × straminea

Erica × straminea, commonly known as the silver Erica, is a hybrid shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, which often displays a striking silvery or gray-green hue. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Erica × straminea is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
