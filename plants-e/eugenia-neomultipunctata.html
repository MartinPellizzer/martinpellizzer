Eugenia neomultipunctata

Eugenia neomultipunctata, commonly known as the New Guinea guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to New Guinea and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and edible, pinkish-red fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy green upper surface with a pale underside.

The fruit is valued for its sweet, aromatic flavor and is often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
