Eugenia peruibensis

Eugenia peruibensis, commonly known as the Peruvian guava, is a small evergreen tree native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically pink to red in color when ripe. Its botanical features include smooth, bark-like stems and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Eugenia peruibensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
