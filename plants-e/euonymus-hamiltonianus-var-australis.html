Euonymus hamiltonianus var. australis

Euonymus hamiltonianus var. australis, commonly known as the Australian euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, four-lobed fruits that are distinctive and showy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
