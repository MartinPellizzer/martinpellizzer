Endiandra lowiana

Endiandra lowiana, commonly known as the Malayan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits.

Endiandra lowiana is often found in lowland and hill forests, thriving in humid, tropical climates.
