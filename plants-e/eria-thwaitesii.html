Eria thwaitesii

Eria thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thai orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and undergrowth. The plant features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the surrounding soil.

Eria thwaitesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
