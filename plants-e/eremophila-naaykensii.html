Eremophila naaykensii

Eremophila naaykensii, commonly known as the red-flowered emu bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces striking red or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough, hairy texture.

Eremophila naaykensii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for dryland gardens.
