Eryngium longifolium

Eryngium longifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue or purple flower heads surrounded by silver-gray bracts. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes or along shorelines.

Eryngium longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
