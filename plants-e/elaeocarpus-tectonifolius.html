Elaeocarpus tectonifolius

Elaeocarpus tectonifolius, commonly known as the tectonifolius elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its ecological significance lies in its role as both a habitat provider and a contributor to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
