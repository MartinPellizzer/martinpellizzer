Eriogonum howellii var. subracemosum

Eriogonum howellii var. subracemosum, commonly known as Howell's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescence and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It has hairy foliage and produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is often found in coastal and inland deserts, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.
