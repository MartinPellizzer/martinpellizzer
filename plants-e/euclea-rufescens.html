Euclea rufescens

Euclea rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed euclea, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Euclea rufescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
