Eupatorium esperanzae var. schininii

Eupatorium esperanzae var. schininii, commonly known as the Schinini's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in some related species.
