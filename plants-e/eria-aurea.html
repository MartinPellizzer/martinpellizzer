Eria aurea

Eria aurea, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow petals and a pleasant fragrance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Eria aurea is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
