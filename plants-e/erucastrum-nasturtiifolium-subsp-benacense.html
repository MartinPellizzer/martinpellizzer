Erucastrum nasturtiifolium subsp. benacense

Erucastrum nasturtiifolium subsp.

benacense, commonly known as the Mediterranean watercress, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found near streams and wetlands. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other mustard family plants.

This subspecies is valued for its peppery flavor and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
