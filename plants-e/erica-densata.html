Erica densata

Erica densata, commonly known as the dense heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and rhododendrons. This evergreen shrub typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or red, and they bloom in spring.

Erica densata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
