Elatine hastata var. regularis

Elatine hastata var. regularis, commonly known as the common waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branched stems that are often submerged, with tiny, opposite leaves that are usually less than 1 cm in length. It produces small, green flowers that are clustered in spikes, and it is often found in muddy or silty substrates.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich waters and its role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.
