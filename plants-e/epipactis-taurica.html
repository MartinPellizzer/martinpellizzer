Epipactis taurica

Epipactis taurica, commonly known as the Crimean helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus and parts of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain meadows. It produces small, dark brown seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Epipactis taurica is often found in calcareous soils and is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
