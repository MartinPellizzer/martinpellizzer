Erica quinquangularis

Erica quinquangularis, commonly known as the firethorn, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring small, sharp, thorn-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright orange or red flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Its leaves are evergreen, with a distinctive angular shape that gives the plant its scientific name.

Erica quinquangularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
