Eupatorium buniifolium var. hieronymi

Eupatorium buniifolium var. hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
