Eragrostis nigricans var. tristis

Eragrostis nigricans var. tristis, commonly known as blackgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along stream banks. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny grains, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become a weed in agricultural fields and turfgrass areas.
