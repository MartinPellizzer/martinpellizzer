Eremopyrum confusum var. sublanuginosum

Eremopyrum confusum var. sublanuginosum, commonly known as the woolly eremopyrum, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family Poaceae. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of desert ecosystems, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife.
