Eriobotrya griffithii

Eriobotrya griffithii, commonly known as the Chinese white pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in spring, followed by edible, sweet, yellowish fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.
