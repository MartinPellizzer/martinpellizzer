Eucalyptus pilularis var. pyriformis

Eucalyptus pilularis var. pyriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed malletwood, is a small to medium-sized tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glaucous underneath. The species produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or pale green. Its bark is smooth and peels in strips, revealing a mottled pattern of grey and white.

This plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open forests and woodlands.
