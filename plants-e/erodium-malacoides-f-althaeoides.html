Erodium malacoides f. althaeoides

Erodium malacoides f. althaeoides, commonly known as the althaeoides form, is a variant of the common sticky weed.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky substance. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in temperate regions.
