Echinopsis famatinensis

Echinopsis famatinensis, commonly known as the Famatinian cactus, is a species native to northern Argentina.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its ribbed stems and spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
