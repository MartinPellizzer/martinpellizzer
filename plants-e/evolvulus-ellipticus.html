Evolvulus ellipticus

Evolvulus ellipticus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a herbaceous annual, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its seeds are encased in a distinctive, spiral-shaped capsule that opens to release the seeds.

Evolvulus ellipticus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
