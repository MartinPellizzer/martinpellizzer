Evolvulus campestris

Evolvulus campestris, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, sunny environments.

Evolvulus campestris is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seed dispersal.
