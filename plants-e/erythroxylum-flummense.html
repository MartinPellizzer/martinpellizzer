Erythroxylum flummense

Erythroxylum flummense, commonly known as the red-flowered mallow, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its distinctive red or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

Erythroxylum flummense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
