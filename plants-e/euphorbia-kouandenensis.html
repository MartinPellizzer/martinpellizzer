Euphorbia kouandenensis

Euphorbia kouandenensis, commonly known as the Kouan Desert Spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in a structure called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
