Eugenia magnifica

Eugenia magnifica, commonly known as the golden apple or the star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which is a large, oblong berry, turns from green to yellow when ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
