Eupatorium hardwarense

Eupatorium hardwarense, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and opposite, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, woodland environments and is often found in shaded to partially shaded areas.

Eupatorium hardwarense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
