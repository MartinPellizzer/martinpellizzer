Elaeocarpus luzonicus

Elaeocarpus luzonicus, commonly known as the Luzon elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Luzon, where it thrives in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white flowers that are followed by spherical, woody fruits. The fruits contain several seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible aril, which is a feature common in many members of the Elaeocarpaceae family.

This species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and supporting various wildlife species.
