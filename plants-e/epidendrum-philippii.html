Epidendrum philippii

Epidendrum philippii, commonly known as the Philippi's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of fragrant, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environments.

Epidendrum philippii is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
