Enicostema axillare

Enicostema axillare, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, pale green flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy areas.

Enicostema axillare is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and widespread species in its native range.
