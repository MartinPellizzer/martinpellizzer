Erodium tordylioides var. mairei

Erodium tordylioides var. mairei, commonly known as Maire's bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small annual herb with slender, often hairy stems that grow close to the ground. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.
