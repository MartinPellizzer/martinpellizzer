Elytrigia batalinii

Elytrigia batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Elytrigia batalinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
