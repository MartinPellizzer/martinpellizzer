Eupatorium aromaticum var. melissoides

Eupatorium aromaticum var. melissoides, commonly known as honeyflower or sweet weed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and roadsides. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
