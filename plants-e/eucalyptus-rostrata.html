Eucalyptus rostrata

Eucalyptus rostrata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted trunk and lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from pale yellow to red, depending on the season. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Eucalyptus rostrata is a hardy shrub or small tree that plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
