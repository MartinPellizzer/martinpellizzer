Eupatorium quercetorum

Eupatorium quercetorum, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, swamps, and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
