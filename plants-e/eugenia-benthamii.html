Eugenia benthamii

Eugenia benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. It produces small, dark purple fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Eugenia benthamii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
