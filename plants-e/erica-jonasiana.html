Erica jonasiana

Erica jonasiana, commonly known as the Cape snowbush, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family, Ericaceae, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the winter months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Erica jonasiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
