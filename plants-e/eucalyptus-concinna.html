Eucalyptus concinna

Eucalyptus concinna, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which appear during the winter months. Eucalyptus concinna is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and high temperatures.

Its essential oils, particularly cineole, make it valuable in medicinal and aromatic applications.
