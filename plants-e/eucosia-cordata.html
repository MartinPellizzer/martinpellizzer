Eucosia cordata

Eucosia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf eucalyptus, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern around the stems. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in spring. Its bark is smooth and peels in thin layers, and the wood is light-colored with a fine texture.

Eucosia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
