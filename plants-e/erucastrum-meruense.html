Erucastrum meruense

Erucastrum meruense, commonly known as the East African rocket, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of edible leaves.
