Eugenia brunnescens

Eugenia brunnescens, commonly known as the rough eucalyptus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, woody capsules containing seeds.

It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
