Elaeodendron aquifolium

Elaeodendron aquifolium, commonly known as the holly-leaf elaeodendron, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a holly-like appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The leaves contain compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
