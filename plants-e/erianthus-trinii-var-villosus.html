Erianthus trinii var. villosus

Erianthus trinii var. villosus, commonly known as the hairy erianthus, is a perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This species is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
