Erica vulgaris var. hirsuta

Erica vulgaris var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and rocky areas.

Erica vulgaris var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscaping.
