Ericameria nauseosa var. speciosa

Ericameria nauseosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy ericameria, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and showy, glandular-pubescent leaves. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and rocky slopes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
