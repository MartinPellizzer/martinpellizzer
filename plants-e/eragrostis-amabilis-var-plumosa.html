Eragrostis amabilis var. plumosa

Eragrostis amabilis var. plumosa, commonly known as the silky lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like foliage that gives it a soft, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of slender, drooping spikelets that add to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in landscaping and as a low-maintenance ground cover.
