Eupatorium mexiae

Eupatorium mexiae, commonly known as Mexican boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Eupatorium mexiae is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
