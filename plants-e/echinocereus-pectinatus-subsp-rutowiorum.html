Echinocereus pectinatus subsp. rutowiorum

Echinocereus pectinatus subsp.

rutowiorum, commonly known as the Rutowiorum cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, usually in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability to harsh climates make it a notable example of desert flora.
