Eucalyptus camphora subsp. relicta

Eucalyptus camphora subsp.

relicta, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often grey-green in color and have a distinctive aromatic scent.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pale pink flowers, typically in clusters, which are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, coastal or heathland environments.

This subspecies is of ecological importance, providing habitat and food for native wildlife.
