Eriaxis

Eriaxis, commonly known as the golden crown fern, is a member of the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and distinctive, golden-colored fronds that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Eriaxis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
