Euphorbia calderoniae

Euphorbia calderoniae, commonly known as the Calderonia spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and spiny leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are the modified inflorescences typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, making it well adapted to coastal and coastal mountainous regions.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens.
