Elytraria ramosa

Elytraria ramosa, commonly known as the slender bamboo grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
