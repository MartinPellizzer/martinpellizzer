Evelyna longibracteata

Evelyna longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract euphorbia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that enclose the small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are often reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces a milky sap that is characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Evelyna longibracteata is found in parts of Africa and is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
