Entada juliflora

Entada juliflora, commonly known as the African tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender pods that contain flat, bean-like seeds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fragrant, yellowish-white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
