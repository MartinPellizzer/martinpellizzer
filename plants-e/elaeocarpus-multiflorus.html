Elaeocarpus multiflorus

Elaeocarpus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically encased in a thick, woody pericarp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
