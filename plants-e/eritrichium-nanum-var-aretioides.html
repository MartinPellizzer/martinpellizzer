Eritrichium nanum var. aretioides

Eritrichium nanum var. aretioides, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a small flowering plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit, with hairy stems and small, rounded leaves. The flowers are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. This variety is closely related to other members of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage.

It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.
