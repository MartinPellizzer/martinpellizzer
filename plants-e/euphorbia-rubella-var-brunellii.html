Euphorbia rubella var. brunellii

Euphorbia rubella var. brunellii, commonly known as the red spurge or Brunell's euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often tinged with red or purple hues. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
