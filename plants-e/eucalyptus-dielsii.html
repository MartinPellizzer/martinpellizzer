Eucalyptus dielsii

Eucalyptus dielsii, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with blue or grey. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its aromatic oils, derived from the leaves, have been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Eucalyptus dielsii is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
