Erica richardii

Erica richardii, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in winter, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica richardii is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
