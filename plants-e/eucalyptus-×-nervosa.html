Eucalyptus × nervosa

Eucalyptus × nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry sclerophyll forests.

Its foliage and floral structure make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a popular ornamental plant.
