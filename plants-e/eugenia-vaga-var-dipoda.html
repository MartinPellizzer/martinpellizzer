Eugenia vaga var. dipoda

Eugenia vaga var. dipoda, commonly known as the slender eugenia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, berry-like drupes that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

This species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
