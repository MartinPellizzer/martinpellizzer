Echinocereus viereckii subsp. viereckii

Echinocereus viereckii subsp.

viereckii, commonly known as the Vireck's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a small, solitary cactus characterized by its cylindrical stems and dense clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant features spiny areoles and produces fruits that are typically red or orange in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in desert gardens.
