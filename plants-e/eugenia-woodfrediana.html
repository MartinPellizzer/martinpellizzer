Eugenia woodfrediana

Eugenia woodfrediana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Eugenia woodfrediana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking fruit and pleasant fragrance.
