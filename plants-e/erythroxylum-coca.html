Erythroxylum coca

Erythroxylum coca, commonly known as the coca plant, is a flowering shrub native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing coca seeds. Its leaves contain alkaloids, including cocaine, which have been used traditionally for their stimulating effects.

The plant has significant cultural and historical importance in indigenous communities.
