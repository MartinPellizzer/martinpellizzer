Erythrina altissima

Erythrina altissima, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and its woody structure supports its growth in various climates. Erythrina altissima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can reach impressive heights, making it a notable presence in botanical gardens.

Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny pod, which is a distinctive feature of the species.
