Erica macowanii

Erica macowanii, commonly known as the Macowan's heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to South Africa, specifically the Western Cape region, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica macowanii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
