Euphorbia williamsonii

Euphorbia williamsonii, commonly known as the Williamson's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features a short, thick stem with dense clusters of yellowish-green bracts that resemble flowers. The plant has smooth, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It is adapted to arid environments and stores water in its fleshy stems and leaves.

Euphorbia williamsonii is known for its striking appearance and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
