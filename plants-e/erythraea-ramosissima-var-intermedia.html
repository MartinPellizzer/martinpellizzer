Erythraea ramosissima var. intermedia

Erythraea ramosissima var. intermedia, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits containing one or two seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic and practical properties.
