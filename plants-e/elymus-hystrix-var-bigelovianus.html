Elymus hystrix var. bigelovianus

Elymus hystrix var. bigelovianus, commonly known as the bigelovian tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and long, stiff, silver-tomentose leaves. The plant produces panicles of spikelets that are typically green to purplish in color, with lemmas that are often hairy. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in sagebrush steppe and grassland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
