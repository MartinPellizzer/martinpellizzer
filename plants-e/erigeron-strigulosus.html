Erigeron strigulosus

Erigeron strigulosus, commonly known as the rough fleabane, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name.

Erigeron strigulosus is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
