Eria tenuiflora

Eria tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, and it produces small, lance-shaped flowers in shades of white and yellow.

Eria tenuiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
