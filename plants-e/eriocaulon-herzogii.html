Eriocaulon herzogii

Eriocaulon herzogii, commonly known as the Herzog's spike-rush, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae, which is characterized by grass-like foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, where it forms dense, low-growing mats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its inflorescence consists of a spike of tiny, clustered flowers that are often obscured by the dense foliage.

Eriocaulon herzogii plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
