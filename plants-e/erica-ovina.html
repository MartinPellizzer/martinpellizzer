Erica ovina

Erica ovina, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Erica ovina thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in garden landscaping.
