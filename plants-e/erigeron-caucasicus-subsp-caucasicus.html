Erigeron caucasicus subsp. caucasicus

Erigeron caucasicus subsp.

caucasicus, commonly known as Caucasian fleabane, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky slopes, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant features silvery-gray foliage with serrated edges and produces daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow disk in the center. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early autumn, and it is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
