Eugenia lineolata

Eugenia lineolata, commonly known as the red mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins and its striking, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round, purple fruits that turn red when ripe, containing a white, segmented, edible flesh. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses.
