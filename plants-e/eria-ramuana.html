Eria ramuana

Eria ramuana, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters and are often used in traditional floral arrangements. Eria ramuana typically grows on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
