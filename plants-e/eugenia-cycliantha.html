Eugenia cycliantha

Eugenia cycliantha, commonly known as the red-eyed treefrog plant, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
