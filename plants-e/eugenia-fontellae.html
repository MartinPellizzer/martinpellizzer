Eugenia fontellae

Eugenia fontellae, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or dwarf cherry, is a small evergreen shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a compact, drought-tolerant plant.

Its botanical features make it an attractive addition to native plant collections and ecological landscapes.
