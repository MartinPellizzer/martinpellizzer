Echinocactus ornatus f. glabrescens

Echinocactus ornatus f. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous ornate cactus, is a unique variant of the ornate cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

This plant is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically covered in spines, though the glabrescens form has a smoother, less spiny appearance. It produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged foliage. The plant is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Its slow growth rate and compact size make it a popular choice for rock gardens and container cultivation.
