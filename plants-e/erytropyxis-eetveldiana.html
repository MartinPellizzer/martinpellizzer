Erytropyxis eetveldiana

Erytropyxis eetveldiana, commonly known as the Eetveld's erythroxylum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological role.
