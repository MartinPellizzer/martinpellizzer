Eragrostis moggii var. contracta

Eragrostis moggii var. contracta, commonly known as narrowleaf beardgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like arrangement of small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
