Eugenia championii

Eugenia championii, commonly known as the Mexican cherry or wild cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.

Eugenia championii is also noted for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
