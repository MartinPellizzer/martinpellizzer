Excoecaria virgata

Excoecaria virgata, commonly known as the arrowroot plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

However, the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
