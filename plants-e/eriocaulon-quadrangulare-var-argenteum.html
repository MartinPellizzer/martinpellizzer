Eriocaulon quadrangulare var. argenteum

Eriocaulon quadrangulare var. argenteum, commonly known as silver rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of silky, silver-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking silver foliage, making it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
