Eria pleistophylla

Eria pleistophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
