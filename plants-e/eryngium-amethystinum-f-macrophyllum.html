Eryngium amethystinum f. macrophyllum

Eryngium amethystinum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved thistle, is a horticultural form of the eryngium plant characterized by its notably larger leaves compared to the typical species.

It is a perennial plant native to Europe, particularly found in regions such as the British Isles and southern Scandinavia. The plant features striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens. Its foliage is silver-gray with a rough, bristly texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
