Equisetum variegatum f. concolor

Equisetum variegatum f. concolor, commonly known as the variegated scouring rush, is a fern-like plant belonging to the Equisetaceae family.

It is characterized by its segmented, hollow stems that are typically green with a white or yellowish stripe. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the top of its stems, which are important for reproduction. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its historical use as a natural scouring agent has contributed to its common name.
