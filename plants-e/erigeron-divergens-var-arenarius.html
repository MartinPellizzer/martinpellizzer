Erigeron divergens var. arenarius

Erigeron divergens var. arenarius, commonly known as the sand erigeron, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its slender stems and small, blue to violet flowers bloom in summer, adding a delicate touch to its natural habitat.

It is often found in prairies, dunes, and other open, dry environments.
