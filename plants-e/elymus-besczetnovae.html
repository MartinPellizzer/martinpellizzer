Elymus besczetnovae

Elymus besczetnovae, commonly known as the Besczetnov's feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. Elymus besczetnovae thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
