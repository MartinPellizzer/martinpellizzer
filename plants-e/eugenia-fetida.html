Eugenia fetida

Eugenia fetida, commonly known as the stinking toe or sweet stinkwood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, aromatic leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. The plant produces small, round, reddish-brown fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves contain essential oils with antimicrobial properties, making it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.

Eugenia fetida thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
