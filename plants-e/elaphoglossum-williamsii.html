Elaphoglossum williamsii

Elaphoglossum williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' holly fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Dryopteridaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features delicate, lance-shaped fronds with a finely divided, feather-like structure, characteristic of the genus Elaphoglossum. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters called sori, which are protected by a thin, membranous cover.

This fern is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
