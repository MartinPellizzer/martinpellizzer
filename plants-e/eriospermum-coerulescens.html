Eriospermum coerulescens

Eriospermum coerulescens, commonly known as the bluebells of the genus Eriospermum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Eriospermum coerulescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
