Epidendrum mantis-religiosae

Epidendrum mantis-religiosae, commonly known as the praying mantis orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that resemble the shape of a praying mantis, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually dark green and sword-shaped, while its flowers are fragrant and come in shades of white, pink, and purple.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
