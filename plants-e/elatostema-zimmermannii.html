Elatostema zimmermannii

Elatostema zimmermannii, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false shamrock, is a small herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a velvety texture, which give it its common name. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often clustered in a spike-like inflorescence.

Elatostema zimmermannii is known for its ability to thrive in humid conditions and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage.
