Eriogonum douglasii var. elkoense

Eriogonum douglasii var. elkoense, commonly known as the Elko spiny buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as sagebrush steppe and desert areas. It is a member of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its drought tolerance.

This variety is often found in Nevada and adjacent regions, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
