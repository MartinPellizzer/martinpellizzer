Elaeocarpus clemensiae

Elaeocarpus clemensiae, commonly known as the mountain elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to the highlands of New Guinea.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in montane rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
