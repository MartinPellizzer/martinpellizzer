Epipactis latifolia subsp. ovalis

Epipactis latifolia subsp.

ovalis, commonly known as the broad-leaved helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and showy, greenish-yellow flowers with a distinctive lip.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It produces a single spike of flowers, which can reach up to 30 cm in height.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
