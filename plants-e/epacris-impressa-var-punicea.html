Epacris impressa var. punicea

Epacris impressa var. punicea, commonly known as the Crimson Heath, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces striking clusters of tubular, pink to crimson flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky areas.
