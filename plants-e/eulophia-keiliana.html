Eulophia keiliana

Eulophia keiliana, commonly known as the Keil's eulophia, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with tall, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with a contrasting lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Eulophia keiliana is appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
