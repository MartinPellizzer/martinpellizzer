Erigeron wilkenii

Erigeron wilkenii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its silvery-blue foliage and delicate white to pale blue flowers make it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
