Exodeconus flavus

Exodeconus flavus, commonly known as the yellow false orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling those of true orchids. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and conservation efforts.
