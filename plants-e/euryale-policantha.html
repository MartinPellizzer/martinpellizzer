Euryale policantha

Euryale policantha, commonly known as the velvetleaf water fern, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety coating of fine hairs. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and marshes. Its reproductive structures include small, inconspicuous flowers that produce abundant, three-winged seeds.

Euryale policantha plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
