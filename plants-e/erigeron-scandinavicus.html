Erigeron scandinavicus

Erigeron scandinavicus, commonly known as the Scandinavian fleabane, is a perennial herb native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a hairy, upright stem with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in clusters at the top of the stem.

Erigeron scandinavicus thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
