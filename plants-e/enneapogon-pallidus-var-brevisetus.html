Enneapogon pallidus var. brevisetus

Enneapogon pallidus var. brevisetus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense tufts. The plant produces small, pale yellow to brownish flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
