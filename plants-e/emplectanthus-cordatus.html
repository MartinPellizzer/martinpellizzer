Emplectanthus cordatus

Emplectanthus cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf emblema, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Emplectanthus cordatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a garden setting.
