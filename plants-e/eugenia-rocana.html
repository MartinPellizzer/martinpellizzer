Eugenia rocana

Eugenia rocana, commonly known as the rock apple or wild guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Eugenia rocana is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
