Englerophytum sylverianum

Englerophytum sylverianum, commonly known as the silverleaf englerophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight, helping it to conserve moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring or early summer. Its stems are cylindrical and fleshy, adapted for water storage, while its leaves are small and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Englerophytum sylverianum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
