Eupatorium glabrum

Eupatorium glabrum, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and marshes. Its leaves are lance-shaped, opposite, and often covered with fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous.

It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
