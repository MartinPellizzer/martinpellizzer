Echinocactus pottsianus

Echinocactus pottsianus, commonly known as the Pottsian cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a solitary, columnar cactus with a ribbed stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant features prominent, spiny areoles covered with white bristles, and it produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are reduced to spines, and it is adapted to arid environments with minimal water requirements.

The cactus is well-suited for cultivation in xerophytic gardens due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
