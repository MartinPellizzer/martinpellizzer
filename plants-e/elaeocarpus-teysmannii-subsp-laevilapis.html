Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp. laevilapis

Elaeocarpus teysmannii subsp.

laevilapis, commonly known as the blackwood tree, is a member of the Elaeocarpaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is characterized by its dark, hard wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic qualities. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface.
