Eremogone armeniaca

Eremogone armeniaca, commonly known as Armenian sandthorn, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus.

It is a shrub characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems, though it is not widely cultivated.
