Elytrigia dshinalica

Elytrigia dshinalica, commonly known as the Caucasian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Elytrigia dshinalica is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
