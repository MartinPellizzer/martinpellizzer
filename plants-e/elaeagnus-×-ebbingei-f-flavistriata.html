Elaeagnus × ebbingei f. flavistriata

Elaeagnus × ebbingei f. flavistriata, commonly known as the golden-leaf silverberry, is a hybrid shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its finely textured, silvery-gray foliage that turns a striking golden hue in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible, red-orange berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its unique leaf coloration and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both garden and ecological settings.
