Eryngium painteri

Eryngium painteri, commonly known as the Painted Thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue to purple bracts that form a spherical shape, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, contributing to its visual interest throughout the growing season.

Eryngium painteri is well-suited for rock gardens and dry landscapes due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
