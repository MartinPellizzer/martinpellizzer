Eriogonum racemosum

Eriogonum racemosum, commonly known as the clustered buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It is a member of the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eriogonum racemosum is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a common sight in rocky soils and open habitats.
