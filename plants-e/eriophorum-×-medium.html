Eriophorum × medium

Eriophorum × medium, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that rise from a mat of leafy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-poor habitats such as bogs and wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture that contributes to its structural stability.

Eriophorum × medium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
