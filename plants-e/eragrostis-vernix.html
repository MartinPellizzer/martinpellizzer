Eragrostis vernix

Eragrostis vernix, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

Eragrostis vernix is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
