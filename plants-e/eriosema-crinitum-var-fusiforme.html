Eriosema crinitum var. fusiforme

Eriosema crinitum var. fusiforme, commonly known as the hairy beard vetch, is a perennial legume native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its root system is capable of nitrogen fixation, making it useful in soil enrichment.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.
