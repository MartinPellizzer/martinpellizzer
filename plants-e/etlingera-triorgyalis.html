Etlingera triorgyalis

Etlingera triorgyalis, commonly known as the white ginger or white flame orchid, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
