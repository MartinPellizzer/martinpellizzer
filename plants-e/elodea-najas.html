Elodea najas

Elodea najas, commonly known as water velvet, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Europe and Asia.

It has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically found above the water surface. Its leaves are long and narrow, arranged in whorls around the stem, and are often used in aquariums for their aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Elodea najas plays a vital role in water ecosystems by providing oxygen and habitat for aquatic organisms.
