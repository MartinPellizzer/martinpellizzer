Eriochloa pseudoacrotricha

Eriochloa pseudoacrotricha, commonly known as the slender false brome, is a grass species native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
