Elytraria planifolia subsp. acunae

Elytraria planifolia subsp.

acunae, commonly known as the narrow-leaf elytraria, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.
