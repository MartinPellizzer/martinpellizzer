Erica velutina

Erica velutina, commonly known as the velvet heath, is a flowering plant native to the western and southern regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and other ericaceous plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica velutina thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
