Echeandia pseudoreflexa

Echeandia pseudoreflexa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed echeandia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, white or pale blue flowers bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Echeandia pseudoreflexa is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological role in its native habitat.
