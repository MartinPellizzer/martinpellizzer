Eryngium urbanianum

Eryngium urbanianum, commonly known as the urban thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and urban environments, such as roadsides and wastelands. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant produces achenes that are dispersed by wind.

Eryngium urbanianum is often considered a weed but has ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
