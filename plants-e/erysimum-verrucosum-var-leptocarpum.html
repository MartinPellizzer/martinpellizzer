Erysimum verrucosum var. leptocarpum

Erysimum verrucosum var. leptocarpum, commonly known as the slender-carpel wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and has a compact, low-growing habit. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with four petals.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, slender fruits compared to other varieties of Erysimum verrucosum.
