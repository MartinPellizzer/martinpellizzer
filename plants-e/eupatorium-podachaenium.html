Eupatorium podachaenium

Eupatorium podachaenium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Eupatorium podachaenium is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
