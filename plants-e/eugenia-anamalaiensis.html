Eugenia anamalaiensis

Eugenia anamalaiensis, commonly known as the Anamalai eugenia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom during the summer months. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn red when mature, and they are often used in local traditional medicine.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
