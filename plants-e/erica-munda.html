Erica munda

Erica munda, commonly known as the common heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically features dense, compact foliage composed of small, leathery leaves that are usually green or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, depending on the variety. Erica munda thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or coastal environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
