Embelia bambuseti

Embelia bambuseti, commonly known as the dwarf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
