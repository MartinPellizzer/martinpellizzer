Erigeron chrysopsidis var. brevifolius

Erigeron chrysopsidis var. brevifolius, commonly known as the short-leaved blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a nectar source for pollinators like bees and butterflies.
