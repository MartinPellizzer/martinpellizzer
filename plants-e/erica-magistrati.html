Erica magistrati

Erica magistrati, commonly known as the Magistrati heath, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and related shrubs. The plant is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. Erica magistrati typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
