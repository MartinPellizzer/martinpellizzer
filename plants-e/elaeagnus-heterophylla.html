Elaeagnus heterophylla

Elaeagnus heterophylla, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its compound leaves that vary in shape along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and forest edges.

Elaeagnus heterophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
