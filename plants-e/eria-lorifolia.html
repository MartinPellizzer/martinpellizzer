Eria lorifolia

Eria lorifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf eria, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, delicate orchid-like flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. Eria lorifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow under the right conditions.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
