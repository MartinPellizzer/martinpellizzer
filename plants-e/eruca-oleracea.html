Eruca oleracea

Eruca oleracea, commonly known as wild mustard or garden cress, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, pinnately lobed leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by slender, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

Eruca oleracea is also cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are used in various culinary applications.
