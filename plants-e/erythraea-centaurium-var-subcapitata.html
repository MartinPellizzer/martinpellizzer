Erythraea centaurium var. subcapitata

Erythraea centaurium var. subcapitata, commonly known as the red centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
