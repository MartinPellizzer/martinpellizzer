Erica andreaei

Erica andreaei, commonly known as the Cape heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple during the summer months. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Erica andreaei is well-suited for rock gardens and coastal landscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
