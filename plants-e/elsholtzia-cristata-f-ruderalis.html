Elsholtzia cristata f. ruderalis

Elsholtzia cristata f. ruderalis, commonly known as the crested elsholtzia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and its spikes of small, pale blue to purple flowers. This subspecies is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in rocky or disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments.

The plant's compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
