Epimedium myrianthum

Epimedium myrianthum, commonly known as barrenwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Berberidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside. It produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries.

Epimedium myrianthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded woodland environments.
