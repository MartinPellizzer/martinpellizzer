Eryngium virginianum

Eryngium virginianum, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers and silver-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas and wetlands. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Eryngium virginianum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
