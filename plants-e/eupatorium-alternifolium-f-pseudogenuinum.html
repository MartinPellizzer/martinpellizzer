Eupatorium alternifolium f. pseudogenuinum

Eupatorium alternifolium f. pseudogenuinum, commonly known as the False White Boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. It is a perennial herb that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters, and its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

The species is noted for its ecological importance as a food source for various pollinators and as a habitat for aquatic insects.
