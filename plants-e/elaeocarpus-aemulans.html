Elaeocarpus aemulans

Elaeocarpus aemulans, commonly known as the mimic elaeocarpus, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Elaeocarpus aemulans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
