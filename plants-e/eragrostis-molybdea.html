Eragrostis molybdea

Eragrostis molybdea, commonly known as blue lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and delicate, panicle-like inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive blue-gray hue due to the presence of a waxy coating.

Eragrostis molybdea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
