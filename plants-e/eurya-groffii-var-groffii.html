Eurya groffii var. groffii

Eurya groffii var. groffii, commonly known as the Chinese holly or golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy evergreen foliage that turns vibrant yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, white, bell-shaped flowers in clusters, which are followed by bright red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its seasonal color changes and attractive foliage.
