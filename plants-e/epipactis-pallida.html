Epipactis pallida

Epipactis pallida, commonly known as the pale helleborine, is a terrestrial orchid native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a basal rosette of lanceolate leaves and produces pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it is considered a perennial species.

Epipactis pallida is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
