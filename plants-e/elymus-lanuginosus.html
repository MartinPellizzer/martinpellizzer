Elymus lanuginosus

Elymus lanuginosus, commonly known as silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Elymus lanuginosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing wetland soils.
