Eriocaulon melanocephalum

Eriocaulon melanocephalum, commonly known as black-headed ricegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is often found in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in environments with high moisture levels.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
