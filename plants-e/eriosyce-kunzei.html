Eriosyce kunzei

Eriosyce kunzei, commonly known as the silver cholla, is a cactus species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure composed of cylindrical, spiny stems that are covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

Eriosyce kunzei is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
