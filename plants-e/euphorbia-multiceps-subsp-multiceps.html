Euphorbia multiceps subsp. multiceps

Euphorbia multiceps subsp.

multiceps, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. Its latex sap is milky and can be toxic if ingested.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
