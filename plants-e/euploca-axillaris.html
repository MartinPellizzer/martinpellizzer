Euploca axillaris

Euploca axillaris, commonly known as the coastal euploca, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters. It is adapted to salt spray and strong winds, making it a hardy species found in maritime environments.

Euploca axillaris plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
