Eugenia plurinervia

Eugenia plurinervia, commonly known as the broadleaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often veined with a prominent midrib and lateral veins. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Eugenia plurinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
