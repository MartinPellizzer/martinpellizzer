Evolvulus adscendens

Evolvulus adscendens, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded areas. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The flowers open in the morning and close by midday, giving it its common name.
