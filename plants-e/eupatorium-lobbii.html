Eupatorium lobbii

Eupatorium lobbii, commonly known as the African feverfew, is a flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn.

Eupatorium lobbii is known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat fever and inflammation.
