Euonymus aquifolium

Euonymus aquifolium, commonly known as the winterberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange berries that persist through the winter, providing food for wildlife. The plant has opposite, simple, and often toothed leaves that turn red in the fall. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters before the leaves.

Euonymus aquifolium is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and seasonal color display.
