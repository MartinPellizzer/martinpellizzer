Epidendrum delcastilloi

Epidendrum delcastilloi, commonly known as the Del Castillo's Epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Mexico.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
