Erica coruscans

Erica coruscans, commonly known as the golden heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica coruscans is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.

It plays a vital role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
