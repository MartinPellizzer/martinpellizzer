Erepsia muirii

Erepsia muirii, commonly known as the Muir's ericoid, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late spring. Its adaptability to acidic, well-drained soils makes it a common sight in fynbos vegetation.

Erepsia muirii is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, coastal environments.
