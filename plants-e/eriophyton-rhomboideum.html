Eriophyton rhomboideum

Eriophyton rhomboideum, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

Eriophyton rhomboideum is often used in rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
