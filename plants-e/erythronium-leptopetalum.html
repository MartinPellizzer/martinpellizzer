Erythronium leptopetalum

Erythronium leptopetalum, commonly known as the slender-petaled dog's mercury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale yellow, typically blooming in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are pollinated primarily by bees.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
