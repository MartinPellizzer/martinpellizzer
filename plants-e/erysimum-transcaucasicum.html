Erysimum transcaucasicum

Erysimum transcaucasicum, commonly known as the Caucasian wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces yellow or orange flowers in spring, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
