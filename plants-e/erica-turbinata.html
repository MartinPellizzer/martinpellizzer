Erica turbinata

Erica turbinata, commonly known as the turban heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Erica turbinata thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its hardy nature and attractive floral display.
