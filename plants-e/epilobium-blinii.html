Epilobium blinii

Epilobium blinii, commonly known as Blin's willowherb, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Onagraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks.

Epilobium blinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
