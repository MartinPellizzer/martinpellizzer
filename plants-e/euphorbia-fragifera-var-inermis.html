Euphorbia fragifera var. inermis

Euphorbia fragifera var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a unique cyathium structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its spines and toxic sap, it requires careful handling and is often grown in controlled environments.
