Euphorbia amygdaloides var. robbiae

Euphorbia amygdaloides var. robbiae, commonly known as the Robbia's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, waxy, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in a cup-like structure called a cyathium. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-resistant plants.
