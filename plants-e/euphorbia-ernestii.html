Euphorbia ernestii

Euphorbia ernestii, commonly known as Ernest's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves, which store water to help it survive in arid environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse range of species with unique morphological features.

Euphorbia ernestii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.
