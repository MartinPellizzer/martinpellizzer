Epidendrum pseudoanceps

Epidendrum pseudoanceps, commonly known as the orchid with the unique pseudobulbs, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, tropical environments. The plant features upright pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils. Its flowers are typically greenish-white with intricate patterns, and it blooms during the warmer months.

Epidendrum pseudoanceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements in cultivation.
