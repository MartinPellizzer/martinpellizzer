Erysimum myriophyllum subsp. myriophyllum

Erysimum myriophyllum subsp.

myriophyllum, commonly known as the "Myriophyllum erysimum," is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, where it can be found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
