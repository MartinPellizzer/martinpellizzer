Eulophia saintenoyana

Eulophia saintenoyana, commonly known as the Saintenoy's eulophia, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and dense clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in the dry season, producing white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, elongated spur. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high light exposure.

Eulophia saintenoyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
