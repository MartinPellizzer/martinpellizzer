Eugenia plicata

Eugenia plicata, commonly known as the wax apple or Java apple, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are followed by clusters of fleshy, red or yellow berries. These berries are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor, making them popular in local cuisines.

Eugenia plicata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
