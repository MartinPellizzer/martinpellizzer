Eupatorium lozanoanum

Eupatorium lozanoanum, commonly known as Lozano's boneset, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Jamaica and Cuba.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture, while its flowers bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium lozanoanum is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
