Equisetum telmateia var. caespitosum

Equisetum telmateia var. caespitosum, commonly known as the tufted scouring rush, is a variant of the scouring rush genus characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant that belongs to the Equisetaceae family, which includes several species of horsetails. The plant features whorled leaves that are typically bright green and have a rough texture, which gives it its common name. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ability to form dense clumps, making it a useful plant for erosion control in aquatic environments.
