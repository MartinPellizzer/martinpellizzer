Eulalia vagans

Eulalia vagans, commonly known as the wandering reed, is a grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, flexible stems that can bend and sway with the wind. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Eulalia vagans thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its adaptability to moist conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
