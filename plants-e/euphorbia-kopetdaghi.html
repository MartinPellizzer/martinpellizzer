Euphorbia kopetdaghi

Euphorbia kopetdaghi, commonly known as the Kopetdag spurge, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought conditions.

Euphorbia kopetdaghi is often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements and unique appearance.
