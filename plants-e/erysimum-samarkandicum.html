Erysimum samarkandicum

Erysimum samarkandicum, commonly known as the Samarkand wallflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features bright yellow to orange flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long flowering period.
