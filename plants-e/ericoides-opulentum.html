Ericoides opulentum

Ericoides opulentum, commonly known as the opulent heath, is a shrub native to the cool, moist regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, pinkish-white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas with partial shade.

Ericoides opulentum plays an important role in supporting local wildlife, particularly insects and birds.
