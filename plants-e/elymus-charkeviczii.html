Elymus charkeviczii

Elymus charkeviczii, commonly known as the Charkevich's wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-rowed spikelets. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.

Elymus charkeviczii is valued for its hardiness and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
