Echinocactus leucacanthus var. crassior

Echinocactus leucacanthus var. crassior, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun.

Due to its striking appearance and relative ease of care, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens and rockeries.
