Euterpe microcarpa

Euterpe microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf palm or buriti-doce, is a small, slender palm native to the Amazon rainforest.

It typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters and features a slender trunk covered in persistent leaf bases. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear yellowish-white flowers, followed by edible, sweet fruits. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments that contribute to its ornamental appearance.

Euterpe microcarpa is valued for its fruit, which is consumed by both wildlife and humans, and for its potential ecological and economic importance in tropical regions.
