Epilobium septentrionale

Epilobium septentrionale, commonly known as northern willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in racemes, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, disturbed habitats such as wet meadows, stream banks, and roadside areas.
