Eugenia diospyroides

Eugenia diospyroides, commonly known as the Florida holly, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and are arranged in clusters, followed by small, red, fleshy berries.

The plant is well adapted to wetland environments and is often found in swamps and along riverbanks.
