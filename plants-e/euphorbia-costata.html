Euphorbia costata

Euphorbia costata, commonly known as the zigzag spurge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Euphorbia costata is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and contains toxic sap that can cause skin irritation.
