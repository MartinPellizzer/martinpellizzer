Eulophia propinqua

Eulophia propinqua, commonly known as the small eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, often among leaf litter or in rocky crevices. The plant features a slender rhizome and produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a loose spike. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its delicate, fragrant blooms.

Eulophia propinqua is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is appreciated for its ornamental value in horticulture.
