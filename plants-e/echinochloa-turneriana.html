Echinochloa turneriana

Echinochloa turneriana, commonly known as Turner's panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Echinochloa turneriana is adapted to arid environments and is often found in regions with low precipitation and well-drained soils.
