Eugenia tropophylla

Eugenia tropophylla, commonly known as the satinwood or broad-leaf eugenia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, dark purple berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforest areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in Indigenous Australian cultures.
