Eucalyptus pyriformis var. rameliana

Eucalyptus pyriformis var. rameliana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically yellow or cream in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
