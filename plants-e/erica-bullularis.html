Erica bullularis

Erica bullularis, commonly known as the bullular heath, is a flowering plant native to the western Cape of South Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with dense, evergreen foliage and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation. It is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of the region, with a summer rainfall pattern.

Erica bullularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
