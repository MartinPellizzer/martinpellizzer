Eriogonum pondii var. pondii

Eriogonum pondii var. pondii, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water conservation.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
