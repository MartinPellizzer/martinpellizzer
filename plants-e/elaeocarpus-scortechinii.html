Elaeocarpus scortechinii

Elaeocarpus scortechinii, commonly known as the yellow mangosteen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pink flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically small, round, and have a thick, leathery rind. Its botanical features include elliptical leaves with a smooth surface and a woody, branched structure.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
