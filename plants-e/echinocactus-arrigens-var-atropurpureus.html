Echinocactus arrigens var. atropurpureus

Echinocactus arrigens var. atropurpureus, commonly known as the Purple Needle cactus, is a striking cactus native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are often tinged with purple or red hues, especially under direct sunlight. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
