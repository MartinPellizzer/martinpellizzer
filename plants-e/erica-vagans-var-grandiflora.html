Erica vagans var. grandiflora

Erica vagans var. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora heath, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and mountainous regions, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in horticultural settings.
