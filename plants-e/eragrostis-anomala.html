Eragrostis anomala

Eragrostis anomala, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands, disturbed soils, and along roadsides. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used in erosion control and as a forage crop in arid regions.
