Eriogonum longifolium var. harperi

Eriogonum longifolium var. harperi, commonly known as the Harp's leaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically long and narrow, often with a slightly hairy texture. This variety thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or arid environments.
