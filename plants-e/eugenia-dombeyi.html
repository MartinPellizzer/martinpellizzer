Eugenia dombeyi

Eugenia dombeyi, commonly known as the dwarf myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, waxy leaves and small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. Eugenia dombeyi typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in warm, humid climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
