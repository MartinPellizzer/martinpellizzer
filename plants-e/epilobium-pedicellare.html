Epilobium pedicellare

Epilobium pedicellare, commonly known as the slender willow herb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered with glandular hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense, terminal spikes, producing a showy display in late spring to early summer.

Epilobium pedicellare is often found in wet meadows and is known for its ecological importance as a food source for various insect species.
