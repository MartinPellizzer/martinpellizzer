Echinocassia

Echinocassia, commonly known as the spiny cassia, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its woody shrubs or small trees with compound leaves and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that are typically hairy or spiny, aiding in seed dispersal. Echinocassia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

They are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their vibrant flowers and unique foliage.
