Ericoides cristiflorum

Ericoides cristiflorum, commonly known as the crested heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or heathland environments.

Ericoides cristiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
