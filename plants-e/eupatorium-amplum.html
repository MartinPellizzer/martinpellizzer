Eupatorium amplum

Eupatorium amplum, commonly known as common boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Eupatorium amplum is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.
