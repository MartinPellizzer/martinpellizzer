Euphorbia × paradoxa nothosubsp.

gusuleacii, commonly known as the hybrid euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage.

The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-like structures containing small yellow flowers surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its hybrid origin, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in its foliage and floral displays.
