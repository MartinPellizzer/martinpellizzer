Ephedra khurikensis

Ephedra khurikensis, commonly known as the Khurik sandmat, is a small shrub native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Ephedraceae family and is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and reduced leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female structures. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Due to its historical use in traditional medicine, it has also attracted attention for its potential pharmacological properties.
