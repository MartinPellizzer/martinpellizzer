Echinops ritro subsp. meyeri

Echinops ritro subsp.

meyeri, commonly known as the Meyer's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant has gray-green, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It produces yellow flowers in summer, which are followed by the characteristic spiky bracts.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its drought tolerance and architectural form.
