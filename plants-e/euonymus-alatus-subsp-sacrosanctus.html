Euonymus alatus subsp. sacrosanctus

Euonymus alatus subsp.

sacrosanctus, commonly known as the winged euonymus, is a shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its distinctive winged stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by orange-red, capsule-like fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is known for its adaptability and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
