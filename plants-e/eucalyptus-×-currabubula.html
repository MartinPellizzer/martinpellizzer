Eucalyptus × currabubula

Eucalyptus × currabubula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous in appearance. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Eucalyptus × currabubula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems, providing habitat for native wildlife.
