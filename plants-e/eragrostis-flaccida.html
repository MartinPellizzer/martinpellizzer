Eragrostis flaccida

Eragrostis flaccida, commonly known as the drooping lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often rolled when dry. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Eragrostis flaccida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
