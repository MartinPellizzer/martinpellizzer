Eriocephalus kingesii

Eriocephalus kingesii, commonly known as the King's eriocephalus, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in a fine, hairy layer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
