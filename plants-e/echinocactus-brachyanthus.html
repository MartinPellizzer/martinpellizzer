Echinocactus brachyanthus

Echinocactus brachyanthus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense, spiny exterior that provides protection against herbivores and excessive sunlight. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in arid environments with minimal water. Its most distinctive feature is the yellowish-green color of its central stem, which can develop a golden hue under certain conditions.

This cactus is well adapted to desert climates and requires well-draining soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
