Erythroxylum bezerrae

Erythroxylum bezerrae, commonly known as the Amazonian copaiba, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. Its most notable feature is the secretion of a resinous sap, which has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties.

This species plays an important ecological role in its habitat and is also valued for its potential in pharmaceutical applications.
