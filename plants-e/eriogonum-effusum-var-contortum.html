Eriogonum effusum var. contortum

Eriogonum effusum var. contortum, commonly known as the twisted leaf buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its twisted, often curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora and a subject of interest in ecological studies.
