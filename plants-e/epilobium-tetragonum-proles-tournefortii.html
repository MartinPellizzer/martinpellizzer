Epilobium tetragonum proles tournefortii

Epilobium tetragonum proles tournefortii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, quadrangular stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters, typically blooming from June to September. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
