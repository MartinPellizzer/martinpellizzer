Epidendrum fuscopurpureum

Epidendrum fuscopurpureum, commonly known as the purple-tipped epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces striking inflorescences with flowers that range from purple to pink, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is typically grown as a terrestrial or epiphytic orchid.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant blooms and unique appearance.
