Elymus confusus

Elymus confusus, commonly known as the confused wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Elymus confusus is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
