Erysimum longifolium var. segusianum

Erysimum longifolium var. segusianum, commonly known as the Segusianum Wallflower, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and hairy, with stems that are often reddish in color.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
