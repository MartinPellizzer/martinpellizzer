Eupatorium bartlingianum

Eupatorium bartlingianum, commonly known as the purple stem goldenrod, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish to golden flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to wet soil conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish or purplish tinge, especially along the veins.

Eupatorium bartlingianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
