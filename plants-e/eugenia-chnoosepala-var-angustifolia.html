Eugenia chnoosepala var. angustifolia

Eugenia chnoosepala var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that ripens to a dark purple or black color.

This variety is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
