Eugenia samarensis

Eugenia samarensis, commonly known as the Samarensis Eugenia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like guava and clove. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, reddish-purple berry that contains several seeds.

Eugenia samarensis is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological importance.
