Euryops pinnatipartitus

Euryops pinnatipartitus, commonly known as the golden bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, giving it a distinctive, feathery appearance.

It is well adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
