Encholirium densiflorum

Encholirium densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower lily, is a striking member of the Alstroemeriaceae family native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of striking white or pale pink flowers. The plant features narrow, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in exotic flora.
