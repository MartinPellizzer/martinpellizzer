Erica alopecias

Erica alopecias, commonly known as the heath plant, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen shrub form with dense, needle-like leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in late summer. Erica alopecias thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
