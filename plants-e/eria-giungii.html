Eria giungii

Eria giungii, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 inches in diameter. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

Eria giungii is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or tropical gardens.
