Echinocactus ellipticus

Echinocactus ellipticus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It has a slow growth rate and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
