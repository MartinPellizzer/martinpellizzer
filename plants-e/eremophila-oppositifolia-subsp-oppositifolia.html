Eremophila oppositifolia subsp. oppositifolia

Eremophila oppositifolia subsp.

oppositifolia, commonly known as the slender emu bush, is a shrub native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
