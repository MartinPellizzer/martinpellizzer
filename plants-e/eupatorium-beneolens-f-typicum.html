Eupatorium beneolens f. typicum

Eupatorium beneolens f. typicum, commonly known as the white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. Its showy, clustered white flowers bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

It is valued in herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antiviral properties.
