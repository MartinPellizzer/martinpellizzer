Erica × nobilis

Erica × nobilis, commonly known as the common heather, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Erica × nobilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form compact, low-growing mounds.

It is also known for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
