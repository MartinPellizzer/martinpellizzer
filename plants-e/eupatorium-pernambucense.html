Eupatorium pernambucense

Eupatorium pernambucense, commonly known as Brazilian feverfew, is a perennial herb native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, tropical environments. Its medicinal properties have been traditionally used by indigenous communities for treating fever and inflammation.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
