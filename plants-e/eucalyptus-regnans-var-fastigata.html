Eucalyptus regnans var. fastigata

Eucalyptus regnans var. fastigata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mountain ash, is a tall, slender tree native to Tasmania.

It is characterized by its narrow, upright foliage and smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin sheets. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive blue-green hue. It produces small, globular flower clusters followed by woody, spherical fruits.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
