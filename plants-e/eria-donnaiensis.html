Eria donnaiensis

Eria donnaiensis, commonly known as the Donnai's orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, epiphytic growth habit, often found growing on trees or rocks in humid environments. The plant produces small, intricate flowers with a unique coloration pattern, typically featuring shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, moist conditions with high light exposure, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Eria donnaiensis is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
