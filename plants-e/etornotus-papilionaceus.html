Etornotus papilionaceus

Etornotus papilionaceus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that resemble showers of golden beads. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces legume pods that contain flattened, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
