Erica rosea

Erica rosea, commonly known as the pink heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. Erica rosea typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in coastal and sandy environments.
