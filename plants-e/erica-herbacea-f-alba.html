Erica herbacea f. alba

Erica herbacea f. alba, commonly known as the white heath, is a flowering plant belonging to the Ericaceae family.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in heathland and moorland environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
