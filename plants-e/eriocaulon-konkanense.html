Eriocaulon konkanense

Eriocaulon konkanense, commonly known as the Konkan rush, is a member of the Poales order and belongs to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy habitats, thriving in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous, often surrounded by bracts.

This species is native to the Western Ghats of India and is adapted to the humid, tropical climate of the region.
