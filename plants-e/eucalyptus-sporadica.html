Eucalyptus sporadica

Eucalyptus sporadica, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, often twisted branches and small, narrow leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that range in color from white to pale pink, and it is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions. Eucalyptus sporadica is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.

Its wood is hard and durable, though it is not widely used commercially.
