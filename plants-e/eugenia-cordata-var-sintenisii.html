Eugenia cordata var. sintenisii

Eugenia cordata var. sintenisii, commonly known as the red-flowered evergreen shrub, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. These flowers are typically clustered in inflorescences and bloom during the warmer months.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping due to its compact growth habit and year-round foliage.
