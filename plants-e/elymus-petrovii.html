Elymus petrovii

Elymus petrovii, commonly known as the Siberian wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in late summer. Elymus petrovii is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
