Eriastrum densifolium subsp. mohavense

Eriastrum densifolium subsp.

mohavense, commonly known as Mohave denseleaf, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States. It is a low-growing annual herb with a mat-forming growth habit, characterized by its dense, overlapping foliage.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of the Mojave Desert and surrounding regions.
