Escallonia carmelitana

Escallonia carmelitana, commonly known as the Carmel escallonia, is a flowering shrub native to South America, particularly Chile.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, providing a lush backdrop for the vibrant floral displays.

Escallonia carmelitana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
