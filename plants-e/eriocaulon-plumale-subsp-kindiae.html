Eriocaulon plumale subsp. kindiae

Eriocaulon plumale subsp.

kindiae, commonly known as the feathered flat sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. It typically features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, inconspicuous flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and seasonally flooded areas, and is adapted to tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its botanical structure reflects its ecological niche, making it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
