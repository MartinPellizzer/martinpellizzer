Eriogonum deflexum subsp. hookeri

Eriogonum deflexum subsp.

hookeri, commonly known as Hooker's spinybush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as slopes and plains.

This subspecies is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
