Ephedra scoparia

Ephedra scoparia, commonly known as jointfoot ephedra, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its unique branching pattern and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers and contains alkaloids, including ephedrine, which have medicinal uses. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and drought.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been historically used in traditional medicine.
