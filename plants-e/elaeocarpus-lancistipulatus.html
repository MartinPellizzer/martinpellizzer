Elaeocarpus lancistipulatus

Elaeocarpus lancistipulatus, commonly known as the narrow-tipped elaeocarp, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by distinctive, woody fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
