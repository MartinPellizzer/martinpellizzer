Epidendrum hanburyanum

Epidendrum hanburyanum, commonly known as the Hanbury's Epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear clusters of large, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, hairy texture, which helps retain moisture.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
