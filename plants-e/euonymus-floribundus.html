Euonymus floribundus

Euonymus floribundus, commonly known as the flowering euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a lighter underside. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by red, inflated fruits that persist into winter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is adaptable to various soil types.

Euonymus floribundus is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
