Embelia buxifolia

Embelia buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf embelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that resemble those of boxwood. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Embelia buxifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
