Echinocactus nigrispinus

Echinocactus nigrispinus, commonly known as the black-spined cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in dark, spiny tubercles that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique features, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in arid gardens and collections.
