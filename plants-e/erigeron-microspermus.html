Erigeron microspermus

Erigeron microspermus, commonly known as the small-seeded fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, with a silvery appearance due to the fine pubescence.
