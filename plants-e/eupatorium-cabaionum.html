Eupatorium cabaionum

Eupatorium cabaionum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, swamps, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium cabaionum is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
