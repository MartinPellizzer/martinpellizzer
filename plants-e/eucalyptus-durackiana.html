Eucalyptus durackiana

Eucalyptus durackiana, commonly known as the Durack mallee, is a small to medium-sized tree native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that reveals a silver-gray underlayer, and its lance-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, globular white flowers in clusters, which are typically visited by nectar-feeding birds and insects. It is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in mallee woodlands.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
