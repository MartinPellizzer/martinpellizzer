Euphorbia watanabei

Euphorbia watanabei, commonly known as the Japanese spurge, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a perennial plant native to Japan, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers surrounded by green bracts, which are often mistaken for petals. It is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

However, it contains toxic sap that can cause skin irritation, so caution is advised when handling it.
