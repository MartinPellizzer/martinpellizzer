Erodium angulatum var. linosae

Erodium angulatum var. linosae, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bedstraw, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 20 centimeters. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers clustered in dense heads, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.
