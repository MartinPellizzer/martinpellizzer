Eugenia mirtiflora

Eugenia mirtiflora, commonly known as the roseapple tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant fruits that are edible and have a sweet, slightly tart flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Eugenia mirtiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
