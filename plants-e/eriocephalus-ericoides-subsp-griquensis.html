Eriocephalus ericoides subsp. griquensis

Eriocephalus ericoides subsp.

griquensis, commonly known as the Grootfontein erica, is a shrub native to South Africa. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, Mediterranean-type climates. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
