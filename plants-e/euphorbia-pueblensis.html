Euphorbia pueblensis

Euphorbia pueblensis, commonly known as the Pueblan Spurge, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It typically grows in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
