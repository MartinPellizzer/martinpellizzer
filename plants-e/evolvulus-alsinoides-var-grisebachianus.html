Evolvulus alsinoides var. grisebachianus

Evolvulus alsinoides var. grisebachianus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale blue to white flowers. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
