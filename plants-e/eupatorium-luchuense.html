Eupatorium luchuense

Eupatorium luchuense, commonly known as the Luchuan blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble misty clouds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branching.

Eupatorium luchuense is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
