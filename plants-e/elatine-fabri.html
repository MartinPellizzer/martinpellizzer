Elatine fabri

Elatine fabri, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has a prostrate or erect growth habit, with tiny, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Elatine fabri is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to colonize moist habitats, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
