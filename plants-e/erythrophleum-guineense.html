Erythrophleum guineense

Erythrophleum guineense, commonly known as African mahogany, is a large evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves. The tree produces pod-like fruits that contain flattened, winged seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important in local economies.

Erythrophleum guineense also plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
