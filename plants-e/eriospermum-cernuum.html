Eriospermum cernuum

Eriospermum cernuum, commonly known as the drooping eriosperm, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in spring.

Eriospermum cernuum is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils and open habitats.
