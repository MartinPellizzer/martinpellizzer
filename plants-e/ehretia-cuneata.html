Ehretia cuneata

Ehretia cuneata, commonly known as the Chinese mahonia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features alternate, elliptical leaves that are often dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the spring, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
