Eugenia fenzliana

Eugenia fenzliana, commonly known as the Fenzli's eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, dark purple berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
