Eupatorium odoratum var. brachiatum

Eupatorium odoratum var. brachiatum, commonly known as sweetweed or blueweed, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It features showy, lavender-blue flower heads that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a subtle, aromatic scent. It typically grows in moist soils along streambanks and wetlands, forming dense clumps.

Its botanical structure includes a stout, woody rootstock and a branching stem system that supports its flowering display.
