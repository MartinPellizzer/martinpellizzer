Echinopsis fieldiana subsp. tessellata

Echinopsis fieldiana subsp.

tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated moon cactus, is a succulent species native to South America. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in white to yellowish spines.

The plant produces vibrant, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late winter or early spring. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and cactus collections.
