Eriosema affine f. major

Eriosema affine f. major, commonly known as the large-leaved eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features prominently veined, ovate leaves with a hairy underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically have a pale yellow to cream color.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or grassland habitats.
