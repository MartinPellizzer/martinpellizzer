Eupatorium simile

Eupatorium simile, commonly known as white boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium simile is known for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
