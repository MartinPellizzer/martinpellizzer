Eulophia bilamellata

Eulophia bilamellata, commonly known as the two-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers. The plant features two distinct lips on its flowers, which is a defining morphological trait. Its leaves are typically elliptical and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Eulophia bilamellata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
