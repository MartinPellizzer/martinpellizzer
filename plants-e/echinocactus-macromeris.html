Echinocactus macromeris

Echinocactus macromeris, commonly known as the giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and large, spiny areoles that bear clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces fleshy, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to its harsh environment.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and desert gardens.
