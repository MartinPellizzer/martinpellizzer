Eupatorium lasiolepis

Eupatorium lasiolepis, commonly known as roughleaf thoroughwort, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant features rough, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose panicles, and are usually pink to purple in color.

Eupatorium lasiolepis is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
