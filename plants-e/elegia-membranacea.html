Elegia membranacea

Elegia membranacea, commonly known as the paperleaf elegia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, membranous layer. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in whorled clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage and subtle floral display.
