Eriogonum wrightii var. brevifolium

Eriogonum wrightii var. brevifolium, commonly known as Wright's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
