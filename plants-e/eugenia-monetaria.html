Eugenia monetaria

Eugenia monetaria, commonly known as the silver maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, silvery bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits that attract birds and other wildlife. Eugenia monetaria thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near rivers and streams.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for urban landscaping and reforestation efforts.
