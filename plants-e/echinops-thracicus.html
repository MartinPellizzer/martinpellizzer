Echinops thracicus

Echinops thracicus, commonly known as the Thrace thistle, is a perennial plant native to southeastern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown of thorns. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow.

Echinops thracicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
