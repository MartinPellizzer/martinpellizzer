Erodium stipaceum

Erodium stipaceum, commonly known as the slender bedstraw, is a low-growing annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are pink to purple in color, typically appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, dry fruits called legumes, which contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Erodium stipaceum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
