Echinocactus concinnus var. joadii

Echinocactus concinnus var. joadii, commonly known as the Joadii cactus, is a rare and striking member of the Cactaceae family native to the Chihuahuan Desert.

This species is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with prominent, spiny areoles. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, appearing in late spring and blooming briefly before the plant goes dormant. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems and relying on minimal rainfall.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat threats, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.
