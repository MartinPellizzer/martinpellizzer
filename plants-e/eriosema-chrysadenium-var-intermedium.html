Eriosema chrysadenium var. intermedium

Eriosema chrysadenium var. intermedium, commonly known as the yellow-flowered eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features a slender, hairy stem that branches near the base, supporting clusters of small, yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and covered with fine, soft hairs. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks, contributing to local biodiversity.
