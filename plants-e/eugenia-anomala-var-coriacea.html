Eugenia anomala var. coriacea

Eugenia anomala var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery, dark green leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
