Eucalyptus helidonica

Eucalyptus helidonica, commonly known as the yellow box, is a tall, hardy tree native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark and long, narrow leaves that are often a striking yellow-green color. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. Its timber is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in construction and furniture-making.

Eucalyptus helidonica is also known for its medicinal properties, with its leaves used in traditional remedies for respiratory ailments.
