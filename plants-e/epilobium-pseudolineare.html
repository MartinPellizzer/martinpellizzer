Epilobium pseudolineare

Epilobium pseudolineare, commonly known as the narrow-leaved willowherb, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along riverbanks, wet meadows, and woodland edges. The plant features slender, slightly hairy stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely. Its flowers are small, pink to reddish-purple, and borne in dense spikes, attracting various pollinators.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family, known for its showy flowers and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
