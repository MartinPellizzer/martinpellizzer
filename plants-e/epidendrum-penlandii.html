Epidendrum penlandii

Epidendrum penlandii, commonly known as the Penland's epidendrum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Epidendrum penlandii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
