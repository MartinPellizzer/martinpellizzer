Exogonium purga

Exogonium purga, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters. The plant has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that provide shade and contribute to its ornamental value.

The tree is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
