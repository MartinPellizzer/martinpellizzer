Eucalyptus lane-poolei

Eucalyptus lane-poolei, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-green leaves and small, globular flower spikes that typically bloom in winter. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often growing in heathland and woodland habitats. It has a unique ability to survive in arid conditions due to its deep root system and waxy leaf coating.

Eucalyptus lane-poolei plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
