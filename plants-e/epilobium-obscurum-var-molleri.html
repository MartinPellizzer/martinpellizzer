Epilobium obscurum var. molleri

Epilobium obscurum var. molleri, commonly known as the dark willow herb, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is characterized by its robust stems and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
