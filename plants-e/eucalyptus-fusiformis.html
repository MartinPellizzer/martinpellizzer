Eucalyptus fusiformis

Eucalyptus fusiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mallees, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue in color. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Eucalyptus fusiformis plays an important role in providing habitat for native wildlife and is valued for its ecological significance in its native range.
