Erythrina poianthes

Erythrina poianthes, commonly known as the Poianhes Flame Tree, is a deciduous leguminous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking red and orange floral displays, which appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves composed of three to five ovate leaflets, and its bark is smooth with a reddish-brown color. It produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Erythrina poianthes is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
