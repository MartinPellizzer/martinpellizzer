Erythronium bracteatum

Erythronium bracteatum, commonly known as the yellow avalanche lily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has a corm-like root system and produces lance-shaped leaves that are broad at the base. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological dynamics of its native habitat.
