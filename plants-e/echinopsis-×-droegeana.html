Echinopsis × droegeana

Echinopsis × droegeana, commonly known as the hybrid moon cactus, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Echinopsis chamaecystis and Echinopsis grandiflora.

It is a succulent cactus native to South America, particularly Argentina, and is characterized by its swollen, bulbous stem and showy, white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, thriving in well-draining soil and full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
