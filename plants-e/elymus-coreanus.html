Elymus coreanus

Elymus coreanus, commonly known as the Korean wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Elymus coreanus is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a groundcover plant.
