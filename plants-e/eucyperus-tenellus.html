Eucyperus tenellus

Eucyperus tenellus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf paper plant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, three-sided spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. This plant is often found in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and swampy soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

Eucyperus tenellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
