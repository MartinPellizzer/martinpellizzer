Ericoides aggregatum

Ericoides aggregatum, commonly known as the clustered ericoid, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves and clustered growth habit. The plant produces bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Ericoides aggregatum thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
