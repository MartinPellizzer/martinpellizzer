Eragrostis sericata

Eragrostis sericata, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and covered with short, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
