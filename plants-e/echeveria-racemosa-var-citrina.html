Echeveria racemosa var. citrina

Echeveria racemosa var. citrina, commonly known as the Lemon Echeveria, is a compact, rosette-forming succulent native to Mexico.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are pale yellow to lime green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces dense clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is popular among succulent collectors and gardeners.
