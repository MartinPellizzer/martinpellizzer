Eleocharis equisetina

Eleocharis equisetina, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond edges and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, and it produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Eleocharis equisetina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
