Euphorbia darlingtonii

Euphorbia darlingtonii, commonly known as the Darlington's spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a distinctive, branched stem covered in small, green, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant produces yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
