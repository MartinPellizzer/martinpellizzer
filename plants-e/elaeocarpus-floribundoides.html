Elaeocarpus floribundoides

Elaeocarpus floribundoides, commonly known as the coastal elaeocarpus, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged alternately along the branches. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove or brackish water environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in coastal ecosystems.
