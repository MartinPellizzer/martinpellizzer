Evolvulus incanus var. subcordatus

Evolvulus incanus var. subcordatus, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped, silver-velvet leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
