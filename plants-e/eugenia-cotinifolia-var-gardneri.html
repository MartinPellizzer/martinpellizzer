Eugenia cotinifolia var. gardneri

Eugenia cotinifolia var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental berries and its ability to provide habitat and food for local fauna.
