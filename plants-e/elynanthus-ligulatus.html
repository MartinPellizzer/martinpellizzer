Elynanthus ligulatus

Elynanthus ligulatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and usually pink or purple, arranged in spikes.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environment.
