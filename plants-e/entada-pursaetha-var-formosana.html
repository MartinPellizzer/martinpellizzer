Entada pursaetha var. formosana

Entada pursaetha var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan spurred ginger, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its striking, spurred flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant has palmately compound leaves and produces long, cylindrical pods containing flat, seed-like structures. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its ornamental value and unique floral structure make it a notable species in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
