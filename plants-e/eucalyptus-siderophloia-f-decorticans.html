Eucalyptus siderophloia f. decorticans

Eucalyptus siderophloia f. decorticans, commonly known as the smooth-barked mallee, is a variant of the eucalyptus species native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale grey bark that peels off in strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub or small tree, often in dry, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a grey-green upper surface and a white underside.

This subspecies is adapted to arid environments and is known for its aromatic oils, which contribute to its ecological and potential medicinal significance.
