Epilobium menthoides

Epilobium menthoides, commonly known as narrowleaf willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, streambanks, and wetlands. The plant features slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are usually less than 5 cm in length. Its flowers are small, pink to purple, and arranged in dense spikes, blooming from late spring to early summer.

Epilobium menthoides is known for its ecological value, providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for various herbivorous insects.
