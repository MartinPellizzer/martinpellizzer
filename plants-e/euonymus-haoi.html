Euonymus haoi

Euonymus haoi, commonly known as the golden glory vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn red when mature, adding to its ornamental value. Euonymus haoi thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes due to its vibrant floral display.
