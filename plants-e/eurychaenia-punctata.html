Eurychaenia punctata

Eurychaenia punctata, commonly known as the spotted eurychaena, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive purplish hue and finely toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
