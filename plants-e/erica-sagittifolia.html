Erica sagittifolia

Erica sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf heath, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and cranberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. Erica sagittifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
