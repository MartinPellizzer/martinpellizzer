Erica × darleyensis

Erica × darleyensis, commonly known as the Darley's heath, is a hybrid evergreen shrub native to the British Isles.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green, and it produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or purple flowers in late spring. Erica × darleyensis is well-suited to acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
