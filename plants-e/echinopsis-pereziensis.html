Echinopsis pereziensis

Echinopsis pereziensis, commonly known as the Potosi moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the highlands of Bolivia.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and its ability to produce striking white or pale pink flowers during the night. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its roots are shallow, adapting to the rocky soils of its native habitat.

Echinopsis pereziensis is a popular choice for succulent collectors due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
