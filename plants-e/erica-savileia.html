Erica savileia

Erica savileia, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, creating a striking visual effect. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Erica savileia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often cultivated for its frost-resistant nature and aesthetic appeal.
