Erucastrum fruticulosum

Erucastrum fruticulosum, commonly known as the fruiting erucaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its herbaceous growth habit and small, green, edible leaves that are often used in local cuisines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its nutritional value and adaptability, it has been cultivated in various regions for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
