Euphorbia esula var. humilior

Euphorbia esula var. humilior, commonly known as the low euphorbia, is a flowering plant native to Eurasia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when damaged, which is a common feature among the Euphorbiaceae family. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

Its growth habit and adaptability make it a persistent weed in various ecological settings.
