Evolvulus alsinoides var. thymoides

Evolvulus alsinoides var. thymoides, commonly known as sweetleaf or thymeleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with small, heart-shaped leaves and blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and prolific seed production make it a common weed in many regions.
