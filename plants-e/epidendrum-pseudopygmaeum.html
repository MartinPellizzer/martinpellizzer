Epidendrum pseudopygmaeum

Epidendrum pseudopygmaeum, commonly known as the miniature pink epidendrum, is a small, epiphytic orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It features compact, pseudobulbous stems and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, loose substrates for optimal growth. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall miniature appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
