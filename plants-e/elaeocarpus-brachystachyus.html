Elaeocarpus brachystachyus

Elaeocarpus brachystachyus, commonly known as the small-stalked elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by fleshy, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
