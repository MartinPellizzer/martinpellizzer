Eumecanthus ocymoideus

Eumecanthus ocymoideus, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to certain regions of South America, this plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands or along the edges of streams and ponds.

The plant's ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native flora.
