Echinocactus cornifer var. impexicomus

Echinocactus cornifer var. impexicomus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the dry regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the base of the stems. It is a slow-growing species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid climates.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in xeriscaped gardens.
