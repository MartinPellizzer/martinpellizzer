Elatine gyrosperma

Elatine gyrosperma, commonly known as the water starwort, is a small, aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is typically found in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands. The plant has a prostrate growth habit with thin, ribbon-like leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with four petals, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Elatine gyrosperma plays a role in stabilizing aquatic habitats and providing microhabitats for aquatic invertebrates.
