Engelhardia serrata f. crenata

Engelhardia serrata f. crenata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed engelhardia, is a variant of the original species characterized by its smoother, less serrated leaf margins.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and other aromatic plants. The plant is native to Australia and is often found in subtropical and tropical regions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental value.

This form is valued in horticulture for its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
