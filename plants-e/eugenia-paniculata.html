Eugenia paniculata

Eugenia paniculata, commonly known as the panicle eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dense, showy panicles of small white or pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically elliptical, glossy, and have a pleasant aromatic scent.

Eugenia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
