Euphorbia × esulolucida

Euphorbia × esulolucida, commonly known as the hybrid spurge, is a perennial plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its unique floral structures called cyathia, which are specialized inflorescences. The plant typically exhibits a shrub-like growth habit with a wide range of leaf shapes and sizes depending on environmental conditions. It is native to regions with arid or semi-arid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Euphorbia × esulolucida is also noted for its ability to store water in its tissues, making it drought-tolerant.
