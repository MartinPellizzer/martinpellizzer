Echinops angustilobus

Echinops angustilobus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that resemble a crown of thorns, which are a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

Echinops angustilobus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
