Epidendrum ovulum

Epidendrum ovulum, commonly known as the small white epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks, exhibiting an epiphytic lifestyle. Its leaves are ovate and slightly fleshy, adapted to store moisture in its natural habitat.

Epidendrum ovulum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
