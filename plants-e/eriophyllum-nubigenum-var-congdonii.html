Eriophyllum nubigenum var. congdonii

Eriophyllum nubigenum var. congdonii, commonly known as the Coast Daisy, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California and Oregon.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats, where it is adapted to salty air and strong winds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
