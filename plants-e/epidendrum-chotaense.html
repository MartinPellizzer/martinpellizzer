Epidendrum chotaense

Epidendrum chotaense, commonly known as the Chota Epidendrum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil. The plant features compact, upright pseudobulbs and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. Its leaves are usually dark green, strap-shaped, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Epidendrum chotaense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
