Eucalyptus maidenii

Eucalyptus maidenii, commonly known as the grey box, is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, greyish bark that peels in strips, and its lance-shaped leaves that are usually grey-green in color. The plant produces small, globular flower spikes that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Eucalyptus maidenii is well adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and woodlands.

It plays an important ecological role, providing habitat and food for various native animals.
