Edwardia argentea

Edwardia argentea, commonly known as the silver edwardia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale blue or violet in color.

Edwardia argentea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable plant for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
