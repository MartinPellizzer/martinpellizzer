Eugenia linguiformis

Eugenia linguiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clove, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by edible, aromatic berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
