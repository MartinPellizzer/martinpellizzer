Euphorbia eremophila var. filiformis

Euphorbia eremophila var. filiformis, commonly known as the slender euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically borne on short, erect branches. It is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its fleshy stems.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
