Eupatorium macranthum

Eupatorium macranthum, commonly known as purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Eupatorium macranthum is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
