Euonymus neosplendens

Euonymus neosplendens, commonly known as the golden euonymus, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, which turns a brilliant yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
