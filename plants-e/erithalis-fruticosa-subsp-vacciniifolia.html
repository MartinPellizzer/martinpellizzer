Erithalis fruticosa subsp. vacciniifolia

Erithalis fruticosa subsp.

vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red sandalwood shrub, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or red in color.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
