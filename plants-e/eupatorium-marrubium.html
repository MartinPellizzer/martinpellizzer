Eupatorium marrubium

Eupatorium marrubium, commonly known as white boneset, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces fluffy, seed-filled heads that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Eupatorium marrubium is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory effects.
