Everardia

Everardia, commonly known as the everlasting or everlasting flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that are often used in floral arrangements due to their long-lasting nature. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular florets that are arranged in a composite head. Everardia species are native to various regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

They are often cultivated for ornamental purposes and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
