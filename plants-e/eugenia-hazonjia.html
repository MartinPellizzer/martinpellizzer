Eugenia hazonjia

Eugenia hazonjia, commonly known as the Hazonjia clove, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or purple when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
