Erythrodes venezuelana

Erythrodes venezuelana, commonly known as the Venezuelan redwood, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela and neighboring countries. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

The wood of Erythrodes venezuelana is valued for its durability and is used in local crafts and construction.
