Eragrostis lugens

Eragrostis lugens, commonly known as slender false brome, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed sites, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

Eragrostis lugens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
