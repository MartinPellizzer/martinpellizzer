Erica embothriifolia

Erica embothriifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes shrubs and small trees known for their evergreen foliage. The plant features wiry, upright stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically narrow and linear. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Erica embothriifolia thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the region's biodiversity.
