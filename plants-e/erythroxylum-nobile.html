Erythroxylum nobile

Erythroxylum nobile, commonly known as the noble coca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It produces elliptical, red berries that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its leaves, which contain alkaloids.

This plant has cultural and historical significance, particularly in traditional Andean communities.
