Epidendrum claustralis

Epidendrum claustralis, commonly known as the wall orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in shaded, humid environments. The plant produces showy, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its roots are often found clinging to rocky surfaces or in crevices, reflecting its adaptability to challenging conditions.

Epidendrum claustralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
