Eugenia principium

Eugenia principium, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, dark blue to black berries that are edible but not widely consumed. Its wood is highly valued for its rich, aromatic scent and durability, making it a sought-after material in traditional crafts.

The plant is also noted for its role in local ecosystems and its potential medicinal properties.
