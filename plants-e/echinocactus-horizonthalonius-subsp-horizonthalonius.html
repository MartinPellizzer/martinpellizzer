Echinocactus horizonthalonius subsp.

horizonthalonius, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its spherical, ribbed stem that becomes increasingly spiny with age.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically borne at the top of the stem. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-draining soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and rockeries.
