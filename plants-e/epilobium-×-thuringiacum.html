Epilobium × thuringiacum

Epilobium × thuringiacum, commonly known as the common willowherb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a hybrid species within the genus Epilobium, which includes various species of willowherb. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides, riverbanks, and wet meadows. It produces showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are usually erect and somewhat woody at the base.
