Escobedia stricta

Escobedia stricta, commonly known as the rigid escobedia, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically short-lived. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a feature common in desert plants to reduce water loss.

Escobedia stricta is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
