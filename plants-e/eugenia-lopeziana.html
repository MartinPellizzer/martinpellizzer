Eugenia lopeziana

Eugenia lopeziana, commonly known as the Lopeziana myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and leathery, elliptical leaves. It typically grows in humid, shaded environments at elevations ranging from 1,000 to 2,500 meters.

Eugenia lopeziana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
