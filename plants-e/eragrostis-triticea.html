Eragrostis triticea

Eragrostis triticea, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of spikelets, which are small and often densely packed.

Eragrostis triticea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
