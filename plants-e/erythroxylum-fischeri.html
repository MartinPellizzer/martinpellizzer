Erythroxylum fischeri

Erythroxylum fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in regions of Brazil and Peru.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

It is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
