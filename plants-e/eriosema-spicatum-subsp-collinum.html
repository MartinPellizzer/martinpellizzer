Eriosema spicatum subsp. collinum

Eriosema spicatum subsp.

collinum, commonly known as the mountain eriosema, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which are often glandular and covered in fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
