Euphorbia portulacoides var. andensis

Euphorbia portulacoides var. andensis, commonly known as the Andean spurge, is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with thick, fleshy stems that store water, adapting to its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures, further minimizing water loss.

It is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
