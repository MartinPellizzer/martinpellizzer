Evolvulus passerinoides

Evolvulus passerinoides, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 15 to 30 centimeters. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and trailing.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
