Epilobium canum subsp. mexicanum

Epilobium canum subsp.

mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican willow herb, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as streambanks and roadside areas.

The plant features slender, erect stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a hairy surface. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom in the spring.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wet environments and its use in traditional medicine.
