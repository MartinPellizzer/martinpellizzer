Ephedra negrii

Ephedra negrii, commonly known as the Tibetan ephedra, is a shrub native to the high altitudes of the Tibetan Plateau.

It is characterized by its jointed, woody stems and small, scale-like leaves. The plant produces greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in elongated spikes. Ephedra negrii is adapted to arid and cold environments, showcasing remarkable resilience to harsh climatic conditions.

Its stems contain alkaloids, making it both medicinally significant and potentially toxic.
