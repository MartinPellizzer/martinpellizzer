Eugenia pantagensis var. elliptica

Eugenia pantagensis var. elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the rainforests of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow or orange berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understorey of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential use in local horticulture.
