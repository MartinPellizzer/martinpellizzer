Eremitis paucifolia

Eremitis paucifolia, commonly known as the sparse-leaved eremitis, is a unique succulent native to arid regions of South Africa.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth form with thick, fleshy leaves that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Eremitis paucifolia is valued in rock gardens for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
