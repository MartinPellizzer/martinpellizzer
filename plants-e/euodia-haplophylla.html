Euodia haplophylla

Euodia haplophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironwood, is a small evergreen tree native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white flowers that are fragrant and attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its hard, dense wood is highly valued for its strength and durability in traditional crafts and construction.

Euodia haplophylla is also known for its ability to thrive in coastal environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
