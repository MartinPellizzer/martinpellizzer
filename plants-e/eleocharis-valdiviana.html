Eleocharis valdiviana

Eleocharis valdiviana, commonly known as the Valdivian flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to South America, particularly found in the temperate regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riparian zones, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in its habitat.

Eleocharis valdiviana is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
