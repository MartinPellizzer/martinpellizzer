Epacris gracilis

Epacris gracilis, commonly known as the slender epacris, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained acidic soils. It produces showy pink or red flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.

Epacris gracilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
