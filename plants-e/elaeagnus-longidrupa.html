Elaeagnus longidrupa

Elaeagnus longidrupa, commonly known as the Chinese silverberry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and small, edible berries that ripen in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.

Elaeagnus longidrupa is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing properties, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
