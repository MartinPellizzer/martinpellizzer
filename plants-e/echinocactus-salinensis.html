Echinocactus salinensis

Echinocactus salinensis, commonly known as the salt cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green or blue-gray in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stem. It is well adapted to harsh, saline environments and requires minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
