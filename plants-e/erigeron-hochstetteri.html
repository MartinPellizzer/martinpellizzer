Erigeron hochstetteri

Erigeron hochstetteri, commonly known as the Hochstetter's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces showy blue to purple ray florets surrounded by yellow disk florets. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
