Ehretia laevis var. pubescens

Ehretia laevis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ehretia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
