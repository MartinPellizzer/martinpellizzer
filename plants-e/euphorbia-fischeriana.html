Euphorbia fischeriana

Euphorbia fischeriana, commonly known as the Fischer's spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, branched stem covered in small, waxy leaves that help it conserve water. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Due to its striking appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
