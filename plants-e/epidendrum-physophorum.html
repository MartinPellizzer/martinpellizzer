Epidendrum physophorum

Epidendrum physophorum, commonly known as the Moon Orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and its striking, pale pink to white flowers with a distinctive, velvety texture. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early fall, and the species is noted for its fragrant, night-blooming scent.

Epidendrum physophorum is relatively easy to cultivate, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
