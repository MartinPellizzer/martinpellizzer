Eryngium painteri var. typicum

Eryngium painteri var. typicum, commonly known as the Painted Thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and striking purple to violet flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, seed-filled spheres. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular subject for botanists and horticultural enthusiasts.
