Echium rosulatum subsp. stenophyllum

Echium rosulatum subsp.

stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its rosette-like arrangement of narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking spikes of blue or purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
