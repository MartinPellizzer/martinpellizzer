Echinocactus centeterius

Echinocactus centeterius, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are covered in dense, white spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, typically emerging from the areoles along the stems. It is a slow-growing cactus that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
