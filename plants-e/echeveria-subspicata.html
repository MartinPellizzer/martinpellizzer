Echeveria subspicata

Echeveria subspicata, commonly known as the silver spoon echeveria, is a succulent plant native to Mexico.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces clusters of violet or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight to maintain its vibrant color.

Echeveria subspicata is a popular choice for rock gardens and container gardening due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
