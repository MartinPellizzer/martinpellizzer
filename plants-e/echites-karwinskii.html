Echites karwinskii

Echites karwinskii, commonly known as the Karwinski's echites, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Echites karwinskii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
