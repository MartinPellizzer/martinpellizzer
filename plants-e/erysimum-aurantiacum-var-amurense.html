Erysimum aurantiacum var. amurense

Erysimum aurantiacum var. amurense, commonly known as the Amur wallflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with finely divided, fern-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting flowers and hardy nature.
