Erica × aemula

Erica × aemula, commonly known as the coastal heather, is a hybrid shrub native to the western coast of South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for coastal gardens. It is drought-tolerant and hardy, often found growing in sandy or rocky coastal environments.

Erica × aemula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
