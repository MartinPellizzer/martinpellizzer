Eleusine macrosperma

Eleusine macrosperma, commonly known as giant lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. The plant produces fluffy, seed-bearing spikes that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various climates.

Eleusine macrosperma is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and as a forage crop for livestock.
