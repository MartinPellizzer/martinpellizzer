Eriotheca pubescens subsp. tomentosa

Eriotheca pubescens subsp.

tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly eriotheca, is a small evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Central America. It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, leathery fruits. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and often have a rough texture.

This species is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high rainfall.
