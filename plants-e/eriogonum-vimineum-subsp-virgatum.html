Eriogonum vimineum subsp. virgatum

Eriogonum vimineum subsp.

virgatum, commonly known as the slender buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It produces small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is part of the buckwheat family (Polygonaceae) and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
