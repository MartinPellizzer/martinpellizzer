Echinocactus intertextus var. dasyacanthus

Echinocactus intertextus var. dasyacanthus, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, segmented stems and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in low-water environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique appearance.
