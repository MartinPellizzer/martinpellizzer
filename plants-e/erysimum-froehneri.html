Erysimum froehneri

Erysimum froehneri, commonly known as the Froehner's wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowering period occurs in spring, producing clusters of yellow or orange flowers.

Erysimum froehneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
