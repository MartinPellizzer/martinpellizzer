Erigeron fluens

Erigeron fluens, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fluffy, blue-gray flower heads that resemble mist, blooming in late summer to early fall. The plant has silvery-green, hairy leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Erigeron fluens is a hardy perennial that attracts pollinators like butterflies and bees.
