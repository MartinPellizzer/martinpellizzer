Empetrum nigrum subsp. kardakovii

Empetrum nigrum subsp.

kardakovii, commonly known as the Kardakov's crowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to the Arctic and subarctic regions. It is characterized by its small, dark blue berries and evergreen leaves that remain throughout the year.

The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing food for local wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of cold-climate ecosystems.
