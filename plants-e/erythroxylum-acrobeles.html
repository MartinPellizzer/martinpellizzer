Erythroxylum acrobeles

Erythroxylum acrobeles, commonly known as the acrobeles copal tree, is a species native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant belongs to the Erythroxylaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
