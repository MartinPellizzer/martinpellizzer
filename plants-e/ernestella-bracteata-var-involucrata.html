Ernestella bracteata var. involucrata

Ernestella bracteata var. involucrata, commonly known as the woolly bract fern, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that provides insulation against extreme temperatures. The plant typically grows as a low, creeping shrub with small, ovate leaves covered in fine, soft hairs. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with small, greenish-white blooms that appear in clusters.

This variety is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
