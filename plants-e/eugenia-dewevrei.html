Eugenia dewevrei

Eugenia dewevrei, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically white or pink, which are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Eugenia dewevrei is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and seasonal floral displays.
