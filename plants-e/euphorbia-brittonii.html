Euphorbia brittonii

Euphorbia brittonii, commonly known as Britton's spurge, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its short, fleshy stems and clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that can be irritating to the skin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Euphorbia brittonii is adapted to withstand drought and has a low-growing, mat-forming habit.
