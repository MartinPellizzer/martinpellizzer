Eupatorium gundlachii

Eupatorium gundlachii, commonly known as the Mexican boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely leafy stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are usually opposite and have a rough, hairy texture.

Eupatorium gundlachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
