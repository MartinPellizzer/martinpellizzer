Eupatorium calycinum

Eupatorium calycinum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features showy, blue-to-purple flower heads that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Eupatorium calycinum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
