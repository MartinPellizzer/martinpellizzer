Echinocereus mojavensis

Echinocereus mojavensis, commonly known as the Mojave prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow or orange flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments, allowing it to survive in desert conditions. Its pads, or cladodes, store water and can be used for food or as a source of nectar.

The Mojave prickly pear also plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including insects and birds.
