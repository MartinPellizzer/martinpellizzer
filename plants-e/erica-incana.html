Erica incana

Erica incana, commonly known as the heath plant, is a flowering shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like blueberries and azaleas. The plant is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves. It produces clusters of delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

Erica incana thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and coastal areas.
