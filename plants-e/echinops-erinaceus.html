Echinops erinaceus

Echinops erinaceus, commonly known as the spiny echinops, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. The plant is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads that are prominent in late summer and autumn. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Echinops erinaceus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
