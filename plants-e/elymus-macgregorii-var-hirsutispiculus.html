Elymus macgregorii var. hirsutispiculus

Elymus macgregorii var. hirsutispiculus, commonly known as the Hirsute Spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy, spiculate inflorescences that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native plant communities.
