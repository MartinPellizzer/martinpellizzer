Elaeagnus veteris-castelli

Elaeagnus veteris-castelli, commonly known as the old castle silverleaf, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Elaeagnus veteris-castelli is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
