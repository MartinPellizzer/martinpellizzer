Eragrostis congesta var. cephalantha

Eragrostis congesta var. cephalantha, commonly known as the clustered lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native ecosystems and a potential candidate for ecological restoration projects.
