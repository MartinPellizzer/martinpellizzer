Erica coronifera

Erica coronifera, commonly known as the heather rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Erica coronifera is also known for its fragrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
