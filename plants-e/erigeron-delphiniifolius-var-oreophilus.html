Erigeron delphiniifolius var. oreophilus

Erigeron delphiniifolius var. oreophilus, commonly known as the mountain blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is often found in open habitats such as meadows and slopes at higher elevations.
