Elaeagnus bockii var. muliensis

Elaeagnus bockii var. muliensis, commonly known as the Mulien silverleaf, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its distinctive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
