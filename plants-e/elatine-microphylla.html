Elatine microphylla

Elatine microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved waterwort, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the Elatinaceae family.

It is characterized by its tiny, opposite leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands. Its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed aquatic habitats make it an important pioneer species.

Elatine microphylla produces small, green flowers that are often submerged, contributing to its role in aquatic ecosystems.
