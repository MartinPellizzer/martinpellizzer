Erpetina radicans

Erpetina radicans, commonly known as the climbing nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can extend several meters in length. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine, though it contains toxic compounds.
