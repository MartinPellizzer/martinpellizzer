Embryopteris peregrina

Embryopteris peregrina, commonly known as the wandering fern, is a rare and ancient species of fern native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures that resemble those of more primitive ferns, showcasing its evolutionary significance. The plant exhibits a unique reproductive strategy, with its spores developing directly into gametophytes, a feature known as embryophytic reproduction. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that aids in photosynthesis in its moist, shaded habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research.
