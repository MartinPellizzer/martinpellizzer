Eugenia ligustrina var. hebecarpa

Eugenia ligustrina var. hebecarpa, commonly known as the purple-leafed holly or hollyleaf eugenia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that turn a striking purplish-red in the fall. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red, edible berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.
