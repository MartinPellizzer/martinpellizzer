Euonymus japonicus aureovariegatus

Euonymus japonicus aureovariegatus, commonly known as the golden variegated spindle tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, featuring yellow and green patterns on its heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but toxic to humans. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
