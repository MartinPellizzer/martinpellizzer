Erigeron foliosus var. setchellii

Erigeron foliosus var. setchellii, commonly known as the Setchell's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has finely divided, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms.

This variant is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and floral structure compared to other members of the Erigeron genus.
