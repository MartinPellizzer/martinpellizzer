Erigeron primulifolius

Erigeron primulifolius, commonly known as blueweed or bluefalse dandelion, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides, often reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are blue to purple in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
