Evolvulus filipes

Evolvulus filipes, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. It is a climbing vine that produces small, edible berries.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
