Erica longipedunculata

Erica longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-stemmed heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. This shrub typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or white, appearing in clusters at the ends of long, slender peduncles.

Erica longipedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
