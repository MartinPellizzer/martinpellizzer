Erythroxylum vernicosum

Erythroxylum vernicosum, commonly known as the Andean beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Its fruits are small, bright red berries that contain a single seed.

Erythroxylum vernicosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
