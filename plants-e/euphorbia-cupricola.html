Euphorbia cupricola

Euphorbia cupricola, commonly known as the copperleaf euphorbia, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ribbed stems that are often tinged with a reddish or copper hue, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are surrounded by colorful bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
