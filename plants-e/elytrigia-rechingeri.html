Elytrigia rechingeri

Elytrigia rechingeri, commonly known as the Tibetan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Elytrigia rechingeri plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
