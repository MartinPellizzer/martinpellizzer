Eremostachys regeliana var. chimaera

Eremostachys regeliana var. chimaera, commonly known as the Chimaera hibiscus, is a striking perennial plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often resembling those of hibiscus species. The plant features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves adapted to store water, enabling it to thrive in dry conditions. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
