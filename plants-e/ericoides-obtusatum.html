Ericoides obtusatum

Ericoides obtusatum, commonly known as the blunt-leaved ericoid, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or white and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.
