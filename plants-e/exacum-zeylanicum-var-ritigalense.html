Exacum zeylanicum var. ritigalense

Exacum zeylanicum var. ritigalense, commonly known as the Malabar violet, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing herbaceous plant with a compact, bushy habit, often reaching heights of up to 30 cm. The plant produces clusters of small, violet or purple flowers that bloom throughout the year, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in tropical gardens.
