Euonymus recurvans

Euonymus recurvans, commonly known as the curly willow or red-veined ivy, is a deciduous flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling leaves that form intricate, spiral patterns along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Euonymus recurvans thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural use in gardens and landscapes.
