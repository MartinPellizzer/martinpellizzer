Enodium caeruleum var. uniflorum

Enodium caeruleum var. uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered bluebells, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces a single, bell-shaped flower per stem, which is usually a striking blue or violet hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors.
