Eragrostis nevinii

Eragrostis nevinii, commonly known as Nevin's lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in poor soil and access deep water sources.

Eragrostis nevinii is often used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in degraded landscapes.
