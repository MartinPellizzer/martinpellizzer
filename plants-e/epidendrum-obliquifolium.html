Epidendrum obliquifolium

Epidendrum obliquifolium, commonly known as the zigzag orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its unique, zigzag-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a delicate, fragrant scent.

Epidendrum obliquifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
