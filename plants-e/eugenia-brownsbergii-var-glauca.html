Eugenia brownsbergii var. glauca

Eugenia brownsbergii var. glauca, commonly known as the blue glaucous eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a waxy, bluish-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible, citrus-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in warm climates.
