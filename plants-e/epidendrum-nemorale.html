Epidendrum nemorale

Epidendrum nemorale, commonly known as the forest orchid, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, humid environments within forests. The plant features elongated, linear leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its roots are often found in the soil, and it relies on mycorrhizal associations for nutrient uptake.

Epidendrum nemorale is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses or shaded gardens.
