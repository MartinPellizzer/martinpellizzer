Erysimum adpressum

Erysimum adpressum, commonly known as the creeping wallflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and vibrant color make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
