Ehrharta rupestris subsp. dodii

Ehrharta rupestris subsp.

dodii, commonly known as the rocksettu grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in challenging conditions.
