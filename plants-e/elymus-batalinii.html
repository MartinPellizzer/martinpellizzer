Elymus batalinii

Elymus batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin's needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and grasslands. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are characteristic of the grass family. Its leaves are narrow and rough, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in regions with low precipitation.

Elymus batalinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in arid landscapes for erosion control.
