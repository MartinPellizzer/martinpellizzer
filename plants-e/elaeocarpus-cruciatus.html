Elaeocarpus cruciatus

Elaeocarpus cruciatus, commonly known as the Cruciate Elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically borne in clusters.

It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
