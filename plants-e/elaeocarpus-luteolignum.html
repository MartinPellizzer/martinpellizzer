Elaeocarpus luteolignum

Elaeocarpus luteolignum, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Elaeocarpaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals. This species is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
