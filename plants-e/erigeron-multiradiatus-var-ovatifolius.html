Erigeron multiradiatus var. ovatifolius

Erigeron multiradiatus var. ovatifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The leaves are ovate to broadly ovate, often with serrated edges, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This species thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in prairies, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and pollinator support.
