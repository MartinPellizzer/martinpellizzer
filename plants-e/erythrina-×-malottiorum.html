Erythrina × malottiorum

Erythrina × malottiorum, commonly known as the hybrid flame tree, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Erythrina indica and Erythrina lysistemon, combining traits from both parent species. The plant is characterized by its striking red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, making it a popular ornamental plant. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a small tree or shrub.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.
