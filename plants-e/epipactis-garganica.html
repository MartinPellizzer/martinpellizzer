Epipactis garganica

Epipactis garganica, commonly known as the Gargano helleborine, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, particularly found in regions such as the Apennines and the Balkans. The plant features a rosette of dark green, leathery leaves and produces striking, hooded flowers with a distinctive purple and green coloration. It typically grows in shaded, moist habitats such as woodlands and along stream banks.

Epipactis garganica is known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often associated with calcareous environments.
