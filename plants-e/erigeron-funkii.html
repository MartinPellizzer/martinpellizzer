Erigeron funkii

Erigeron funkii, commonly known as the Funk's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.

Its hairy leaves and sturdy stems help it survive in arid environments.
