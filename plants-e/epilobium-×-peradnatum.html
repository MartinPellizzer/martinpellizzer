Epilobium × peradnatum

Epilobium × peradnatum, commonly known as the hybrid willow herb, is a perennial plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to red flowers in clusters during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shady environments, often found in woodlands and along streambanks.

Epilobium × peradnatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
