Eupatorium alternifolium f. molle

Eupatorium alternifolium f. molle, commonly known as the soft-leaved boneset, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
