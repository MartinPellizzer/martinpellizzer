Euphorbia beaumieriana

Euphorbia beaumieriana, commonly known as the Madagascar spurge, is a succulent plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a cluster called a cyathium, which is typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
