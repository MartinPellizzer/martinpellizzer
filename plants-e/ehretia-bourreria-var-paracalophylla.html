Ehretia bourreria var. paracalophylla

Ehretia bourreria var. paracalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ehretia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
