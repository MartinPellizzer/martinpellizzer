Echites siphiliticus

Echites siphiliticus, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bright yellow flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The scarlet pimpernel is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
