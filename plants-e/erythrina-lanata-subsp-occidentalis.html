Erythrina lanata subsp. occidentalis

Erythrina lanata subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the Western coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking red to orange-pink flowers that appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its bark is often fissured and rough to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
