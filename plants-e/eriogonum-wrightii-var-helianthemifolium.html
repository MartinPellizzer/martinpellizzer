Eriogonum wrightii var. helianthemifolium

Eriogonum wrightii var. helianthemifolium, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, whitish pubescence. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands.

This variety is part of the evening primrose family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
