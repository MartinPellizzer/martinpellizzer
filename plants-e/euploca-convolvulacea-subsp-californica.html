Euploca convolvulacea subsp. californica

Euploca convolvulacea subsp.

californica, commonly known as California morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a trailing perennial with heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a climbing habit and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
