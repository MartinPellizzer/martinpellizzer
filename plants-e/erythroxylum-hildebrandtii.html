Erythroxylum hildebrandtii

Erythroxylum hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's erythroxylum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Erythroxylaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically red or purple in color, which are followed by oval-shaped berries. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

This species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
