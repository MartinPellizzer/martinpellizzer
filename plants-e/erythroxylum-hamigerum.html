Erythroxylum hamigerum

Erythroxylum hamigerum, commonly known as the red mallow, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers, which are often followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Erythroxylum hamigerum is part of the Erythroxylaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
