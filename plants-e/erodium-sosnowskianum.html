Erodium sosnowskianum

Erodium sosnowskianum, commonly known as the Sosnowski's filaree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
