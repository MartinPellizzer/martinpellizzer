Elaeocarpus corneri

Elaeocarpus corneri, commonly known as the New Guinea elaeocarpus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is native to the island of New Guinea and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
