Evansia

Evansia, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant has a rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Evansia typically blooms in the spring and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and ecological resilience make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
