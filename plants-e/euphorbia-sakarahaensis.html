Euphorbia sakarahaensis

Euphorbia sakarahaensis, commonly known as the Sakaraha spurge, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spine-tipped leaves and striking yellow-orange cyathia, which are its specialized inflorescences. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring a thick, fleshy stem that stores water. It is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
