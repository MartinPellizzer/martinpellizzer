Euphorbia bahiensis

Euphorbia bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia spurge, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It features a distinctive succulent stem with a greenish-gray bark and produces small, yellowish cyathia, which are its modified flower clusters. The plant has a branched root system and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Euphorbia bahiensis is also noted for its ability to tolerate salt spray, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
