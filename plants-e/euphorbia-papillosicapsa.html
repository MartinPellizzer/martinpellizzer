Euphorbia papillosicapsa

Euphorbia papillosicapsa, commonly known as the silky-seeded spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces unique cyathia, which are cup-like structures containing yellowish flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to conserve water.

Its latex is toxic and can cause skin irritation, making it important to handle with care.
