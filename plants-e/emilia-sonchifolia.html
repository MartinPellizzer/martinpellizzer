Emilia sonchifolia

Emilia sonchifolia, commonly known as the common emilia, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Emilia sonchifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
