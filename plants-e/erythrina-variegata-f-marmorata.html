Erythrina variegata f. marmorata

Erythrina variegata f. marmorata, commonly known as the marble-stemmed coral tree, is a variant of the coral tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled green and red stems that resemble marble, giving it its common name. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This ornamental plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
