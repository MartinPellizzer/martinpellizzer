Eupatorium billbergianum

Eupatorium billbergianum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features gray-green, fuzzy foliage and produces clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Eupatorium billbergianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
