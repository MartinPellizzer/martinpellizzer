Evolvulus sericeus var. glaberrimus

Evolvulus sericeus var. glaberrimus, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a variant of the morning glory family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its soft, silken foliage and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that open in the morning. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
