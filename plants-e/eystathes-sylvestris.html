Eystathes sylvestris

Eystathes sylvestris, commonly known as the wild thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are typically gray-green in color, covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pink or white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or woodland areas.

Eystathes sylvestris is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
