Erica laxiflora

Erica laxiflora, commonly known as the slender heath, is a species of flowering plant in the heath family (Ericaceae).

It is native to South Africa, where it typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming from late summer to early autumn. Erica laxiflora is evergreen, with dense, leathery leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
