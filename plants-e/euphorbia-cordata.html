Euphorbia cordata

Euphorbia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf spurge, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are heart-shaped, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia. It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often toxic species.

Euphorbia cordata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
