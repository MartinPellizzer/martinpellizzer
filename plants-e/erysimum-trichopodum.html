Erysimum trichopodum

Erysimum trichopodum, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branched stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-gray hue.

Erysimum trichopodum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
