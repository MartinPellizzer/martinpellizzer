Erdisia maxima

Erdisia maxima, commonly known as the giant erdisia, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy pink or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in terminal panicles, and the plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.

Erdisia maxima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
