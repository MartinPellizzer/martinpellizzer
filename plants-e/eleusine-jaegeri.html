Eleusine jaegeri

Eleusine jaegeri, commonly known as jaeger's lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Eleusine jaegeri is sometimes used in restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
