Eugenia sieberiana var. crassifolia

Eugenia sieberiana var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved eugenia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy. Its flowers are small, white or pale pink, and it produces edible, reddish-purple berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
