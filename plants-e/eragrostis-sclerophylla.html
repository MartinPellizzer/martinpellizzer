Eragrostis sclerophylla

Eragrostis sclerophylla, commonly known as the hard-leaved lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, rigid leaves and compact growth habit, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements, it is used in some regions for erosion control and as a ornamental plant.
