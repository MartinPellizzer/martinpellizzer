Erysimum tortuosum

Erysimum tortuosum, commonly known as the twisted whitlow grass, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are arranged in racemes and are often visited by pollinators such as bees.

Erysimum tortuosum is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
