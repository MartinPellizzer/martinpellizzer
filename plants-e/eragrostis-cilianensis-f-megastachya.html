Eragrostis cilianensis f. megastachya

Eragrostis cilianensis f. megastachya, commonly known as the large-stamen false panicgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves that are typically hairy. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, with the flowers having prominent, elongated stamens. This variety is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
