Echinocactus courantianus

Echinocactus courantianus, commonly known as the Mexican horsehair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is covered in dense, white spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which bloom at the tips of the stems. It is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid climates.
