Euphorbia malevola subsp. bechuanica

Euphorbia malevola subsp.

bechuanica, commonly known as the Bechuanian spurge, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features a thick, woody stem with a greenish-gray bark and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

The plant has a milky sap that is toxic and can cause skin irritation. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various conditions, though it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.
