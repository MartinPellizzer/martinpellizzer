Eria fitzalanii

Eria fitzalanii, commonly known as the orchid of the genus Eria, is a species of flowering plant in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, green or reddish-brown pseudobulbs that support its foliage and floral structures. Eria fitzalanii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and botanical gardens.
