Erica aristifolia

Erica aristifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved heath, is a species of flowering plant in the Ericaceae family.

It is native to the western Cape of South Africa, where it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters during the summer months.

Erica aristifolia is well adapted to Mediterranean climates and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
