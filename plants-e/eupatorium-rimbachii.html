Eupatorium rimbachii

Eupatorium rimbachii, commonly known as the mountain eupatorium, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom during the summer months.

Eupatorium rimbachii is often found in forest understories and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
