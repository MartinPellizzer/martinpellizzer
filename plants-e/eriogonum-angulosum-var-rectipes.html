Eriogonum angulosum var. rectipes

Eriogonum angulosum var. rectipes, commonly known as the angular globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and gravelly soils. It is a perennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
