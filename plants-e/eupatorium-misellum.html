Eupatorium misellum

Eupatorium misellum, commonly known as white boneset, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist habitats such as wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
