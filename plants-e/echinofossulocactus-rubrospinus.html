Echinofossulocactus rubrospinus

Echinofossulocactus rubrospinus, commonly known as the red-spined barrel cactus, is a species native to the deserts of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are reddish in color, which is a distinguishing feature.

It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.
