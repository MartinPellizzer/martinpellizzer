Eriogonum rotundifolium var. typicum

Eriogonum rotundifolium var. typicum, commonly known as the roundleaf buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are typically round or heart-shaped, with a soft, fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This plant plays an important role in supporting pollinators and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
