Eria crassipes

Eria crassipes, commonly known as water hyacinth, is a free-floating aquatic plant native to South America.

It has dark green, waxy leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern on the water surface. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators. Eria crassipes is known for its rapid growth rate, which can lead to dense mats covering water bodies.

Its botanical features make it both a popular ornamental plant and a significant invasive species in many tropical and subtropical regions.
