Erigeron clematideus

Erigeron clematideus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive botanical appearance.
