Euclea angolensis

Euclea angolensis, commonly known as the African ironwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has a dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. Its flowers are typically small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

Euclea angolensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
