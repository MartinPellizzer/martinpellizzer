Ervum tetraspermum var. tenuifolium

Ervum tetraspermum var. tenuifolium, commonly known as slender-leaved four-seed bean, is a variant of the four-seed bean species native to South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain four seeds each. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its botanical features include compound leaves with alternating leaflets and a flowering pattern that produces yellow or greenish-yellow blooms.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both decorative and minor agricultural purposes.
