Eriogonum twisselmannii

Eriogonum twisselmannii, commonly known as Twisselmann's buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Twisselmann's buckwheat is often found in sagebrush steppe and is an important component of its ecosystem.
