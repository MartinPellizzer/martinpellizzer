Eugenioides acuminatum

Eugenioides acuminatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved eugenia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves with acuminate tips. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
