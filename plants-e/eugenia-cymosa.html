Eugenia cymosa

Eugenia cymosa, commonly known as the fragrant myrtle or sweet myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia cymosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
