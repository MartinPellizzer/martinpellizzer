Eberhardtia krempfii

Eberhardtia krempfii, commonly known as the Burmese sandalwood, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and compound leaves with three to five leaflets. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered endangered and is protected in many regions.
