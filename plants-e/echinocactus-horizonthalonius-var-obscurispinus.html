Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. obscurispinus

Echinocactus horizonthalonius var. obscurispinus, commonly known as the dark-spined hedgehog cactus, is a compact, globular cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a dense, spiny exterior with dark green to bluish-gray stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It is well adapted to dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, making it a popular choice for succulent collections.
