Erica abietina subsp. atrorosea

Erica abietina subsp.

atrorosea, commonly known as the dark rose heath, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western Mediterranean region. It typically thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and open woodland habitats.

The plant features wiry, leathery leaves that are densely packed, giving it a compact appearance. Its flowers are small, bell-shaped, and usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
