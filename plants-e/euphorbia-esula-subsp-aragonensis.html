Euphorbia esula subsp. aragonensis

Euphorbia esula subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon spurflower, is a perennial shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant has a thick, fleshy stem with spiny branches, and its leaves are usually reduced to small, scale-like structures. It thrives in rocky or dry soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
