Eriachne burkittii var. laidlawii

Eriachne burkittii var. laidlawii, commonly known as the Laidlaw's spinifex, is a grass species native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and wiry, spiky foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences with pale blue or whitish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low rainfall.

This variety is often found in coastal dunes and inland sandplains, contributing to the ecological stability of these habitats.
