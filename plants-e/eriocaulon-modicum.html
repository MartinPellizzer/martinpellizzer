Eriocaulon modicum

Eriocaulon modicum, commonly known as the small haircap moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Eriocaulaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and small, ovate leaves that are often covered with minute, hairy structures. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. It is found primarily in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas.

Eriocaulon modicum plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for small invertebrates in its native ecosystems.
