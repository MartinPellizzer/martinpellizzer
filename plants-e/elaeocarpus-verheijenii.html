Elaeocarpus verheijenii

Elaeocarpus verheijenii, commonly known as the Java elaeocarp, is a species of flowering plant in the family Elaeocarpaceae.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its elliptical leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pink in color.

Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in areas with high humidity and well-drained soil.
