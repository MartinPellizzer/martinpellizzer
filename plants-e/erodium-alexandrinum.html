Erodium alexandrinum

Erodium alexandrinum, commonly known as the Mediterranean bedstraw, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, pink to violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has thin, branching stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution across temperate regions.
