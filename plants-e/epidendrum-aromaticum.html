Epidendrum aromaticum

Epidendrum aromaticum, commonly known as the sweet orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pendulous flowers that emit a sweet, honey-like scent. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and dark green, with a smooth texture.

Epidendrum aromaticum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its attractive, long-lasting blooms.
