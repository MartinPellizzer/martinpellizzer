Eleogenus nodulosus

Eleogenus nodulosus, commonly known as the nodular eleogenus, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring a compact, shrub-like structure with small, clustered leaves. The plant produces distinctive nodules along its stems, which are believed to play a role in nutrient absorption. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, Eleogenus nodulosus is considered an ecologically significant but vulnerable species.
