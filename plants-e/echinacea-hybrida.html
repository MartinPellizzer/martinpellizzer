Echinacea hybrida

Echinacea hybrida, commonly known as the coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers with a prominent central cone and vibrant petals in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly rough to the touch. Echinacea hybrida thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and valued for its potential medicinal properties.
