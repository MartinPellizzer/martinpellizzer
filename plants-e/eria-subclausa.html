Eria subclausa

Eria subclausa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
