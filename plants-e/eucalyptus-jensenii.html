Eucalyptus jensenii

Eucalyptus jensenii, commonly known as Jensen's malleeflower, is a small to medium-sized shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
