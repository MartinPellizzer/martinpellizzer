Echinocactus multiflorus var. parisiensis

Echinocactus multiflorus var. parisiensis, commonly known as the Parisiensis barrel cactus, is a compact, slow-growing cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and is covered in prominent, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-draining soil, requiring minimal water.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
