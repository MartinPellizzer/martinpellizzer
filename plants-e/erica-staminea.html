Erica staminea

Erica staminea, commonly known as the sweet heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in well-drained, acidic soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded conditions. Erica staminea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in gardens and as a decorative plant in coastal areas.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing nectar for pollinators.
