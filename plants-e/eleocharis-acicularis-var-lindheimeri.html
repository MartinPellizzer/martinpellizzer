Eleocharis acicularis var. lindheimeri

Eleocharis acicularis var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's needlegrass, is a species of sedge native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond margins, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms. Its morphology includes narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense spikes.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
