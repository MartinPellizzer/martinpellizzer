Epidendrum gomezii

Epidendrum gomezii, commonly known as the Gomez's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its sympodial growth habit, producing multiple stems that branch out over time. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and covered with a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or green with red or purple markings, arranged in a raceme along the stem.

Epidendrum gomezii thrives in humid, well-lit environments and requires good air circulation for optimal growth.
