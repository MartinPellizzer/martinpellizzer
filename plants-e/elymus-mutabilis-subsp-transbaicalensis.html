Elymus mutabilis subsp. transbaicalensis

Elymus mutabilis subsp.

transbaicalensis, commonly known as the Transbaikal wild rye, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
