Eriogonum umbellatum var. ternatum

Eriogonum umbellatum var. ternatum, commonly known as the three-leaved buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its clustered, showy white to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has basal leaves that are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it its common name. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
