Erysimum sylvestre var. pyrenaicum

Erysimum sylvestre var. pyrenaicum, commonly known as the Pyrenean wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France.

It features upright, branching stems covered in dense, hairy foliage. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to rock gardens and alpine plant collections.
