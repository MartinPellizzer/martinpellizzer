Enterolobium barinense

Enterolobium barinense, commonly known as the Brazilian pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value.

Additionally, it plays a role in soil enrichment due to its nitrogen-fixing abilities.
