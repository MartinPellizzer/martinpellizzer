Erica cylindrica

Erica cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months. Erica cylindrica thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
