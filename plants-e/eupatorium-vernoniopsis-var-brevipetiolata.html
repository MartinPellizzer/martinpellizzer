Eupatorium vernoniopsis var. brevipetiolata

Eupatorium vernoniopsis var. brevipetiolata, commonly known as the short-petioled blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. The leaves are typically opposite, lance-shaped, and have short petioles, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
