Eriosema urostachyum

Eriosema urostachyum, commonly known as the mountain eriosema, is a herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
