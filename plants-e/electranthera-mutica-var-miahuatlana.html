Electranthera mutica var. miahuatlana

Electranthera mutica var. miahuatlana, commonly known as the Mexican flame violet, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of Mexico.

It features vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting yellow centers. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually submerged, while its flowers emerge above the water surface. It thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments with ample sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in water gardens and ponds.
