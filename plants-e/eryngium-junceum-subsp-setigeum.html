Eryngium junceum subsp. setigeum

Eryngium junceum subsp.

setigeum, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features gray-green, spiny leaves and produces blue to purple cone-shaped flowers. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and reduced leaf size compared to other Eryngium species.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
