Eupatoriadelphus purpureus

Eupatoriadelphus purpureus, commonly known as purple false heather, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces clusters of small, showy purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
