Eugenia pauciflora

Eugenia pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The fruit, when mature, is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a deep red color.
