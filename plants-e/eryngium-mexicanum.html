Eryngium mexicanum

Eryngium mexicanum, commonly known as Mexican thistle, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, blue-green foliage and purple to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.

Eryngium mexicanum is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
