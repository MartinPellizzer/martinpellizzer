Euphorbia namuliensis

Euphorbia namuliensis, commonly known as the Namuli spurge, is a flowering plant native to Tanzania.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often spiny stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cyathia, which are specialized flower-like structures typical of the genus Euphorbia. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to poor soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
