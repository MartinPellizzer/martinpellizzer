Elymus viridiglumis

Elymus viridiglumis, commonly known as greenawn grass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are loosely arranged along the stems, and its inflorescence is often described as open and weedy in appearance. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields, and is often found in the northern United States and southern Canada.

Elymus viridiglumis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
