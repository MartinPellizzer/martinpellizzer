Eriogonum lachnogynum

Eriogonum lachnogynum, commonly known as the woolly buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other members like sorrel and knotweed.

Eriogonum lachnogynum plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.
