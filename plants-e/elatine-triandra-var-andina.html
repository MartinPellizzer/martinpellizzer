Elatine triandra var. andina

Elatine triandra var. andina, commonly known as the Andean water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and wetlands. The plant has slender, branching stems with tiny, opposite leaves that are often submerged or floating. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually appearing in clusters near the water's surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic life.
