Elaeocarpus comatus

Elaeocarpus comatus, commonly known as the hairy elaeocarp, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and elliptical, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds. Elaeocarpus comatus is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
