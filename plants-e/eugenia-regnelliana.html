Eugenia regnelliana

Eugenia regnelliana, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Eugenia regnelliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
