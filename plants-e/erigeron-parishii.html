Erigeron parishii

Erigeron parishii, commonly known as Parish's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.

Parish's fleabane is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
