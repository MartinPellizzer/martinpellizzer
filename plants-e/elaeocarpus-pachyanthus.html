Elaeocarpus pachyanthus

Elaeocarpus pachyanthus, commonly known as the broad-leaved elaeocarpus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces clusters of small, white flowers. The tree's fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are often dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This species is valued for its ornamental foliage and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
