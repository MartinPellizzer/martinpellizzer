Erechtites apargiifolius

Erechtites apargiifolius, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, sturdy stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
