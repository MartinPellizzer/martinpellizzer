Eugenia salictoides

Eugenia salictoides, commonly known as the red-flowered eugenia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of fragrant, red or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.

The fruit, which is small and edible, is sometimes used in local cuisines or traditional medicine.
