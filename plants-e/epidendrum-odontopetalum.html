Epidendrum odontopetalum

Epidendrum odontopetalum, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated flowers that are typically yellow with red or orange markings. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem.

Epidendrum odontopetalum thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
