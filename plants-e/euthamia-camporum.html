Euthamia camporum

Euthamia camporum, commonly known as narrowleaf goldenrod, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small yellow suns. This perennial herb typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges. Its narrow, linear leaves and showy yellow blooms make it a distinctive feature in late summer and early fall.

Euthamia camporum is valued for its ornamental appeal and role in supporting pollinators.
