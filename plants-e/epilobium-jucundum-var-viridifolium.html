Epilobium jucundum var. viridifolium

Epilobium jucundum var. viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved willow herb, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-purple flowers during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
