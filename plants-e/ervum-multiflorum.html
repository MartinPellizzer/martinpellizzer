Ervum multiflorum

Ervum multiflorum, commonly known as the many-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Ervum multiflorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
