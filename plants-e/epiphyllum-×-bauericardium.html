Epiphyllum × bauericardium

Epiphyllum × bauericardium, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a hybrid cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its flat, leaf-like stems that are typically green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, often in shades of white, pink, or red. These flowers have a strong, sweet scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and unique blooming behavior.
