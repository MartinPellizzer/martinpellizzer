Ennealophus fimbriatus

Ennealophus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed lily, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and showy, violet to purple flowers with fringed petals. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its limited habitat and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
