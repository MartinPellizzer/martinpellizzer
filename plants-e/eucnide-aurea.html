Eucnide aurea

Eucnide aurea, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that appear in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that turn reddish in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The botanical features of Eucnide aurea make it both visually appealing and ecologically valuable in its native habitats.
