Eupatorium schaffneri

Eupatorium schaffneri, commonly known as Schaffner's boneset, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Eupatorium schaffneri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
