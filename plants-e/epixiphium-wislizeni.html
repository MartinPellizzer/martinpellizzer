Epixiphium wislizeni

Epixiphium wislizeni, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

The fruit is a fleshy berry that ripens to a red color, containing seeds dispersed by animals.
