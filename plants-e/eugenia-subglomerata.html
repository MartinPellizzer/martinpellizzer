Eugenia subglomerata

Eugenia subglomerata, commonly known as the clustered guava, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged oppositely on the stems.

The fruit is a small, edible berry that is typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
