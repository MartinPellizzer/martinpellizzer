Ericameria × viscosa

Ericameria × viscosa, commonly known as brittlestem rabbitbrush, is a hybrid shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect or spreading growth habit and produces yellow flowers in late summer. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves and a woody, often brittle stem, which gives it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Ericameria × viscosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
