Echium velutinum subsp. vilmorinianum

Echium velutinum subsp.

vilmorinianum, commonly known as the Vilmorin's thorned bluemink, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
