Eugenia klossii

Eugenia klossii, commonly known as the Java apple or Kloss's eugenia, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Java in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible and have a tart, acidic flavor. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
