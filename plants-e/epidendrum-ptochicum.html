Epidendrum ptochicum

Epidendrum ptochicum, commonly known as the poor man's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. The plant features slender, erect stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and usually have a pale green or yellowish color with a faintly fragrant scent.

Epidendrum ptochicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
