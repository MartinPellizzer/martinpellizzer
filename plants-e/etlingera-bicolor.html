Etlingera bicolor

Etlingera bicolor, commonly known as the red torch ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related species. The plant is characterized by its striking red flowers that resemble torches, which bloom in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and have a glossy appearance.

Etlingera bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
