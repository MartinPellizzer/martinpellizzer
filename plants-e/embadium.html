Embadium

Embadium, commonly known as the false indigo, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It consists of several species native to the Americas, particularly in the southwestern United States and Mexico. These plants are characterized by their narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, pea-like flowers that range in color from blue to purple. They are often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Embadium species are also valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry climates.
