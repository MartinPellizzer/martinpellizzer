Erigeron umbellatus

Erigeron umbellatus, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and is often found in open fields, roadsides, and disturbed areas. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped. It produces a cluster of small, white to blue flowers at the ends of its stems.

Erigeron umbellatus is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
