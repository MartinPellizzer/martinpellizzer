Eupatorium volubile

Eupatorium volubile, commonly known as creeping eryngo, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant has opposite, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
