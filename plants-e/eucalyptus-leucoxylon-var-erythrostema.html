Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. erythrostema

Eucalyptus leucoxylon var. erythrostema, commonly known as the red-stemmed ghost gum, is a small to medium-sized tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, often reddish-brown bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue, while the flowers are small and typically white or pink. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
