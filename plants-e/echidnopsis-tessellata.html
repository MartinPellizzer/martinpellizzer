Echidnopsis tessellata

Echidnopsis tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely divided leaves and clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
