Echinochloa crus-pavonis var. praticola

Echinochloa crus-pavonis var. praticola, commonly known as yellow foxtail, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and spike-like inflorescences that often exhibit a yellowish hue. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, with a rough texture, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is considered a weed in many areas but also serves as a forage plant for livestock.
