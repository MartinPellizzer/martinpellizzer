Eugenia stictopetala

Eugenia stictopetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved guava, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, though not widely cultivated, is edible and has a mild, fragrant flavor.

Eugenia stictopetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticulture for its attractive foliage and floral display.
