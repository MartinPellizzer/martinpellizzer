Eragrostis pseudonigra

Eragrostis pseudonigra, commonly known as blackleaf lovegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tint. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Eragrostis pseudonigra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
