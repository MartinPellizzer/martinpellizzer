Echinopsis crassicaulis

Echinopsis crassicaulis, commonly known as the "Giant Barrel Cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, thick, cylindrical stem that can grow up to 10 feet tall. The plant has a spiny exterior with areoles that produce both spines and flowers. Its flowers, which bloom in the evening, are typically yellow or orange in color.

Echinopsis crassicaulis is a succulent, storing water in its stem to survive in harsh, dry environments.
