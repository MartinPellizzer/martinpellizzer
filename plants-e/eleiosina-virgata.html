Eleiosina virgata

Eleiosina virgata, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, linear leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Eleiosina virgata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
