Excentradenia adenophora

Excentradenia adenophora, commonly known as the African rosewood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern and southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood, which is highly valued for its durability and fine grain. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

The species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
