Eugenia inirebensis

Eugenia inirebensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Eugenia inirebensis*, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers and smooth, glossy leaves. It produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in local cuisines.

Eugenia inirebensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
