Eugenia verruculata

Eugenia verruculata, commonly known as the rough-leaved eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its rough, bark-like leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Eugenia verruculata is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
