Echinops myrioceras

Echinops myrioceras, commonly known as the many-armed thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruit heads that are composed of numerous bracts. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its prickly structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.

Echinops myrioceras is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
