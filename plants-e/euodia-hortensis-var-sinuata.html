Euodia hortensis var. sinuata

Euodia hortensis var. sinuata, commonly known as the Chinese bitter orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-like leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically harvested when they turn orange-red. Its leaves and fruit are used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The varietal form, sinuata, is distinguished by its finely serrated leaf margins.
