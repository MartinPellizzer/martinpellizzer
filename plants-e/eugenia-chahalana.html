Eugenia chahalana

Eugenia chahalana, commonly known as the Chahalana clove, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped, leathery leaves. It produces small, reddish-brown fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
