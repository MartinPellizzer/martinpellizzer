Eschscholzia crocea var. sanctarum

Eschscholzia crocea var. sanctarum, commonly known as the Santa Monica poppy, is a botanical variant of the California poppy.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange petals and is native to the coastal regions of Southern California. This variety typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It is an annual plant that produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The Santa Monica poppy is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
