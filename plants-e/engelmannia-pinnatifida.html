Engelmannia pinnatifida

Engelmannia pinnatifida, commonly known as the winged bean or saptala, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with pinnate arrangement and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its seeds and foliage.

Engelmannia pinnatifida is also valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
