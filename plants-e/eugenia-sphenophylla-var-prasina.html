Eugenia sphenophylla var. prasina

Eugenia sphenophylla var. prasina, commonly known as the green-leaved myrtle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive species within the Eugenia genus.
