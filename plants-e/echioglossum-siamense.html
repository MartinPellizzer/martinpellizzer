Echioglossum siamense

Echioglossum siamense, commonly known as the Siam bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Echioglossum siamense is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
