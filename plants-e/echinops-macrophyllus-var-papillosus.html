Echinops macrophyllus var. papillosus

Echinops macrophyllus var. papillosus, commonly known as the silky thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical seed heads and large, silver-green leaves with a fuzzy texture. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and borders. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are typically blue to purple in color.

The variety 'papillosus' is distinguished by the silky, hairy texture of its leaves and stems.
