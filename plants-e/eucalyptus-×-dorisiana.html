Eucalyptus × dorisiana

Eucalyptus × dorisiana, commonly known as the Sydney blue gum, is a hybrid eucalyptus species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grey-blue bark that peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, globular flower clusters that are typically white or pale blue, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth surface and a greyish-green hue.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
