Erica caffrorum

Erica caffrorum, commonly known as the Caffrorum heath, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates with dry summers. Erica caffrorum is often found in fynbos vegetation, contributing to the biodiversity of its native habitat.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
