Eucalyptus melanophloia subsp. melanophloia

Eucalyptus melanophloia subsp.

melanophloia, commonly known as the black box gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia. It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark that gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is found in arid and semi-arid regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white bloom.

The flowers are small and typically white or pale blue, and they are followed by woody capsules containing seeds.
