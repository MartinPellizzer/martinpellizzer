Eryngium dorae

Eryngium dorae, commonly known as the Dorset thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late summer to early autumn. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves with a rough texture and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Eryngium dorae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a habitat for pollinators.
