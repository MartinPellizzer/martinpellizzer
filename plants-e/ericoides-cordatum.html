Ericoides cordatum

Ericoides cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf ericoid, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, commonly known as the heath or heather family, which is characterized by its acidophilic (acid-loving) nature. The plant typically features small, leathery leaves that are often arranged in a compact, evergreen form. It produces clusters of bell-shaped, pink or white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils, it is a popular choice for garden cultivation in cooler climates.
