Empetrum pubescens

Empetrum pubescens, commonly known as snowberry, is a low-growing shrub native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are usually small, hairy, and evergreen, adapted to cold climates.

Empetrum pubescens plays an important role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
