Eriogonum dasyanthemum

Eriogonum dasyanthemum, commonly known as the thickflower buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It has a compact growth habit and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Eriogonum dasyanthemum is an important plant for pollinators and contributes to the ecological diversity of its native environment.
