Eulophia brevipetala

Eulophia brevipetala, commonly known as the short-petalled eulophia, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a sympodial growth habit with a short, horizontal rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces a single or few showy, fragrant flowers with a distinctive lip that is often brightly colored. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Eulophia brevipetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
