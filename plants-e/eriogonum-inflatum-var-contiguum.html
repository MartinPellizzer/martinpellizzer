Eriogonum inflatum var. contiguum

Eriogonum inflatum var. contiguum, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces inflated, three-lobed seed pods that are distinctive and often used for identification. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to full sun exposure.

The species is known for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and seeds for birds and small mammals.
