Echinocarpus tomentosus

Echinocarpus tomentosus, commonly known as the hairy burr sida, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its hairy, spiny fruits that are typically clustered in racemes. The plant has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are borne in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Echinocarpus tomentosus is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in warm, humid climates.
