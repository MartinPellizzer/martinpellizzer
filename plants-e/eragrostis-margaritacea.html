Eragrostis margaritacea

Eragrostis margaritacea, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it visually appealing in ornamental landscapes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers, which are often clustered in spherical or oblong heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, and is drought-tolerant once established.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes many other grasses and cereal crops.
