Eupatorium cinereum

Eupatorium cinereum, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, wooded areas.

Eupatorium cinereum is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in supporting pollinators like butterflies and bees.
