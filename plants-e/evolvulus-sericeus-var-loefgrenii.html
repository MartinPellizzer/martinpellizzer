Evolvulus sericeus var. loefgrenii

Evolvulus sericeus var. loefgrenii, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. The leaves are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
