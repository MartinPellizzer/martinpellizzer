Epidendrum bifidum

Epidendrum bifidum, commonly known as the bifid epidendrum, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sympodial growth habit and produces elongated pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves that are typically dark green and covered in a fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white and pink.

Epidendrum bifidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
