Euterpe tenuiramosa

Euterpe tenuiramosa, commonly known as the slender-barked palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in humid, lowland areas with well-drained soil. Its fruit, which is small and edible, is often consumed by local wildlife and humans alike.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in some regions.
