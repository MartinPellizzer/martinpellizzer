Euphorbia spannringii

Euphorbia spannringii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, globe-shaped fruit that encloses the seeds. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with a unique structure known as a cyathium. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid climates.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications and is also valued for its ornamental appeal.
