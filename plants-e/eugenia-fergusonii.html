Eugenia fergusonii

Eugenia fergusonii, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom during the winter months. The plant produces small, round berries that turn red when mature, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Eugenia fergusonii is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and seasonal flowering, making it a popular choice in horticulture.
