Eucalyptus × bunyip

Eucalyptus × bunyip, commonly known as the River Red Gum, is a hybrid species native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall stature, often reaching heights of up to 30 meters, and its distinctive smooth, pale grey bark that peels in strips. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the flowers are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in umbels. This species is well adapted to riparian environments, thriving along riverbanks and wetlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species and playing a key role in stabilizing riverbanks.
