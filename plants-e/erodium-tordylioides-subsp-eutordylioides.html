Erodium tordylioides subsp. eutordylioides

Erodium tordylioides subsp.

eutordylioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf stormweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a small annual herb with slender, hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are pink to purple, with five petals and a distinctive hooded structure, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

The plant produces small, oval pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
