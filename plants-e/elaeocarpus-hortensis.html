Elaeocarpus hortensis

Elaeocarpus hortensis, commonly known as the orchid tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that resemble orchids, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Elaeocarpus hortensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
