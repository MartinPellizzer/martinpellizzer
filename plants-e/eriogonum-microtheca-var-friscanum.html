Eriogonum microtheca var. friscanum

Eriogonum microtheca var. friscanum, commonly known as the Friscanum buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, edible seeds that are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in chaparral and sagebrush ecosystems.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to arid conditions and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
