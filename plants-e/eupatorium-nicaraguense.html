Eupatorium nicaraguense

Eupatorium nicaraguense, commonly known as the Nicaraguan boneset, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and is known for its aromatic foliage. It has been used traditionally in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Eupatorium nicaraguense plays an important role in the ecological systems of its native habitats.
