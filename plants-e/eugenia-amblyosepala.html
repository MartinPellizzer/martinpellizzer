Eugenia amblyosepala

Eugenia amblyosepala, commonly known as the narrow-sepaled eugenia, is a species of flowering plant in the Myrtaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptic leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, smooth, yellowish drupe that ripens in late summer.

It is often found in humid, lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
