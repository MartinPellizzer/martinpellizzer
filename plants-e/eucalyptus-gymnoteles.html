Eucalyptus gymnoteles

Eucalyptus gymnoteles, commonly known as the narrow-leafed mallee, is a species of eucalyptus native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, globular flower buds that are typically white or pink in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native habitat.

Eucalyptus gymnoteles is often used in reforestation projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
