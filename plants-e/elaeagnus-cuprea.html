Elaeagnus cuprea

Elaeagnus cuprea, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its attractive, reddish-brown stems that become more pronounced with age. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for various climates.

Elaeagnus cuprea is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.
