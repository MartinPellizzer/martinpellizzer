Eugenia cotinifolia subsp. phillyreoides

Eugenia cotinifolia subsp.

phillyreoides, commonly known as the silky oaks, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are often covered with a fine, silky layer of hair.

The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, woody fruits. It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, with a broad, spreading canopy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and as a shade tree.
