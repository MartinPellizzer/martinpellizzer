Erepsia ramosa

Erepsia ramosa, commonly known as the branched erica, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form. The plant features small, needle-like leaves and produces clusters of pink or white flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Erepsia ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
