Eupatorium pabstii

Eupatorium pabstii, commonly known as Pabst's boneset, is a perennial herb native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and somewhat aromatic.

Eupatorium pabstii is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
